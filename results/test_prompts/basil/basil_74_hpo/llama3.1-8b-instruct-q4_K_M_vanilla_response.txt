SUMMARY:
Kathy Griffin has sparked controversy with a photo shoot featuring her holding a fake, bloodied head of President Donald Trump. The image was shot by celebrity photographer Tyler Shields and intended as an expression of art inspired by Trump's comments about Megyn Kelly. Griffin took to Twitter to explain her motivations, stating that she does not condone violence but rather "mocking the Mocker in Chief." However, after backlash, Griffin issued a formal apology on Twitter, saying she had "gone too far."

The Secret Service has also responded to the controversy, issuing a statement confirming that they monitor social media and open-source reporting for threats against protectees. The agency's Twitter account stated that threats receive the highest priority of all investigations.

Griffin's career has been marked by shock value and pushing boundaries, but this photo shoot may have gone too far. Griffin's apology suggests she understands the gravity of her actions and is attempting to move forward from the controversy.
