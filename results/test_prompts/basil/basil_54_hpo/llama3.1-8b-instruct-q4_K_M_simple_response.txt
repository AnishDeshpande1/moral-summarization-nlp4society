SUMMARY:
Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson signed a revised version of the Religious Freedom Reformation Act, which addresses concerns about discrimination against LGBT individuals. Initially, Hutchinson refused to sign an earlier version of the bill due to its potential to allow businesses to discriminate against customers based on their sexual orientation or gender identity. The amended legislation now mirrors the federal RFRA and prohibits such discrimination. This change came after immense national backlash, with politicians, business leaders, and celebrities speaking out against the original bill. Hutchinson cited pressure from his own son, who signed a petition asking him to veto the bill, as well as criticism from Walmart CEO Doug McMillon. The revised legislation is seen as a compromise between those who believe in religious freedom and those who advocate for LGBT rights.

END OF SUMMARY
