SUMMARY: Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson (R) ultimately signed a softened version of the state’s Religious Freedom Reformation Act after initially refusing to endorse a version that would have permitted businesses to discriminate against LGBT people. The Republican‑controlled legislature rewrote the bill at Hutchinson’s request, aligning it with the federal RFRA and removing the discriminatory language. Hutchinson cited personal moral conflict, noting that his son Seth signed a petition urging a veto and that “families—and there’s a generational difference of opinion on these issues”—highlighting a moral duty to protect vulnerable groups. Business pressure also played a role; Walmart CEO Doug McMillon warned the original bill “threatens to undermine the spirit of inclusion.” A similar moral reckoning unfolded in Indiana, where lawmakers added explicit language to their RFRA to forbid discrimination based on sexual orientation or gender identity after nationwide outrage. The revisions reflect a broader moral stance that religious liberty should not be used as a pretext for denying equal treatment to LGBT individuals. END OF SUMMARY.
