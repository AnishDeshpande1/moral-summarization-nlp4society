SUMMARY: Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson (R) signed a revised Religious Freedom Reformation Act (RFRA) on Thursday after initially refusing to sign an earlier version that could have permitted discrimination against LGBT individuals. The Republican legislature amended the bill at Hutchinson’s request, aligning it with the federal RFRA and removing language allowing businesses to refuse service to LGBT customers. Hutchinson cited personal pressure, noting his son Seth’s petition to veto the original bill and highlighting generational differences on the issue. He also faced opposition from Walmart CEO Doug McMillon, who warned the bill threatened inclusion. Simultaneously, Indiana lawmakers altered their recently enacted RFRA, adding language that explicitly prohibits discrimination based on sexual orientation or gender identity, responding to widespread national criticism from politicians, business leaders, and celebrities.

END OF SUMMARY.
