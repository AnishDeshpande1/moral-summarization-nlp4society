SUMMARY:
Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson (R) exercised his executive authority to sign a revised Religious Freedom Reformation Act after rejecting an earlier version that critics said would permit businesses to discriminate against LGBT people. At the governor’s request, the Republican‑controlled legislature amended the bill to mirror the federal RFRA and explicitly forbid discrimination based on sexual orientation or gender identity, thereby restoring a sense of fairness and preventing cheating of marginalized groups. Hutchinson highlighted a generational subversion of his original stance, noting that his son Seth signed a petition urging a veto and that families are divided on the issue. He also faced pressure from powerful authorities such as Walmart’s CEO Doug McMillon, who warned that the original bill threatened inclusion. Parallel changes in Indiana’s RFRA further illustrate the national pushback against laws that could subvert equal treatment. By signing the revised bill, Hutchinson re‑asserted his authority while aligning it with broader expectations of fairness and nondiscrimination.  

END OF SUMMARY.
