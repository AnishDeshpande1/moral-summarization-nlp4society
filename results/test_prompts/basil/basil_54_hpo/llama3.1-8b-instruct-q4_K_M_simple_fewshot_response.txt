SUMMARY:

Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson (R) signed a revised version of the state's Religious Freedom Reformation Act, which addresses concerns about discrimination against lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender individuals. Earlier versions of the bill sparked nationwide backlash, with critics arguing that it would allow businesses to discriminate against LGBT customers. In response to the criticism, Hutchinson initially refused to sign an earlier version of the bill, citing a "generational gap" in opinion on these issues. His son had signed a petition urging him to veto the legislation, and top employers like Walmart also called for its rejection. The revised bill now mirrors the federal RFRA, but critics argue that it still does not go far enough to protect LGBT individuals from discrimination. Hutchinson's move is seen as an attempt to strike a balance between religious freedom and non-discrimination.
