SUMMARY:
Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson signed a revised Religious Freedom Reformation Act after initially refusing the original version that critics said would let businesses discriminate against LGBTQ + individuals. At the governor’s request, the Republican‑controlled legislature removed the contentious provisions, aligning the bill with the federal Religious Freedom Restoration Act. Hutchinson cited a “generational difference” in values, noting that his son Seth signed a petition urging him to veto the first draft. He also faced pressure from Walmart’s CEO, who warned the bill threatened “the spirit of inclusion.” At the same time, Indiana lawmakers amended their own RFRA, adding language that explicitly forbids discrimination based on sexual orientation or gender identity, responding to a nationwide backlash from politicians, business leaders and celebrities. Both states’ revisions reflect a push to balance religious liberty with protections for LGBTQ + people.

END OF SUMMARY.
