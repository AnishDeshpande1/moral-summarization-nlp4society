SUMMARY: Arkansas Gov. Asa Hutchinson (R) signed a revised version of the state's Religious Freedom Reformation Act after refusing to sign an earlier bill that would have *allowed* businesses to *discriminate* against lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender individuals. At the governor’s request, the Republican‑controlled legislature *amended* the *legislation* to mirror the federal RFRA and to remove language that could be used for *discrimination*. Hutchinson cited personal pressure, noting his son Seth’s petition to *veto* the original *bill* and highlighting a generational shift in opinion. He also faced corporate pressure from Walmart, whose CEO warned that the original proposal would *undermine* the “spirit of inclusion.” Meanwhile, Indiana legislators *amended* their own RFRA, adding language that explicitly forbids *discrimination* based on sexual orientation or gender identity, responding to a wave of national backlash. Both states illustrate how political leaders are navigating *legislation* that balances religious liberty claims with protections against *discrimination*. END OF SUMMARY.
