**SUMMARY:**  
Arkansas Gov. Asa Hutchinson signed a revised Religious Freedom Reformation Act after rejecting an earlier version that many said would permit discrimination against LGBT people. The governor cited personal opposition and a “generational” difference, noting his son Seth’s petition to veto the bill. Pressure from Walmart’s CEO, who warned the original text “threatens” the spirit of inclusion, also influenced the change. The Republican legislature rewrote the bill to mirror the federal RFRA and remove discriminatory provisions. In Indiana, lawmakers similarly amended their RFRA to state explicitly that it does not allow discrimination based on sexual orientation or gender identity, responding to a national backlash. Both states’ adjustments reflect growing political opposition to policies that could sanction discrimination while emphasizing inclusion.
