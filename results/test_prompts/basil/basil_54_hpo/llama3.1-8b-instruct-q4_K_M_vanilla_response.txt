SUMMARY:

Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson signed a revised version of the state's Religious Freedom Reformation Act on Thursday, which had been amended to remove provisions that allowed businesses to discriminate against LGBT customers. The changes were made in response to national backlash and pressure from politicians, business leaders, and celebrities. Hutchinson initially refused to sign an earlier version of the bill due to concerns about discrimination, but after a revised version was passed by the legislature, he signed it into law.

Meanwhile, Indiana's governor, Mike Pence, faced similar backlash over his state's RFRA bill, which had been signed into law last week. In response, the Indiana general assembly passed new language explicitly stating that the law does not allow discrimination based on sexual orientation or gender identity.

The revisions in both states were made after intense pressure from various groups, including Walmart, whose CEO called on Hutchinson to veto the Arkansas bill.

END OF SUMMARY.
