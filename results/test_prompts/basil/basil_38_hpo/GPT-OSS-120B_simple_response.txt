SUMMARY: The Treasury Department announced that, for federal tax purposes, the state where a same‑sex couple was married now outweighs the state where they reside, extending full marriage‑based tax benefits nationwide. Prompted by the Supreme Court’s overturn of DOMD, the policy corrects the historic injustice that forced couples like Edie Windsor to pay estate taxes denied to opposite‑sex spouses, and it grants same‑sex spouses access to filing status, deductions, credits, and benefits previously blocked by DOMA. While the change also subjects some couples to the “marriage penalty,” it eliminates the discriminatory denial of rights and affirms that “committed and loving gay and lesbian married couples will now be treated equally under our nation’s federal tax laws.” The move is framed as a moral advance for LGBTQ + equality, aligning federal law with the principle that all Americans deserve equal protection and benefits regardless of state recognition. END OF SUMMARY.
