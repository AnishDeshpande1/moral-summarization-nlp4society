SUMMARY: The Treasury Department’s new ruling **provides** uniform federal tax treatment for legally married same‑sex couples, **recognizing** their marriage regardless of the state where they reside. Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew **assured** couples that moving across state lines will not change their filing status, **treating** them equally under tax law. The decision **extends** the benefits denied under the former Defense of Marriage Act, eliminating the **burden** that once **forced** couples like Edie Windsor to pay estate taxes. All tax provisions that consider marriage—including filing status, employee benefits, IRA contributions, child tax credits, and estate taxes—now **apply** to same‑sex spouses, though the **act** also means they may face the “marriage penalty.” The policy **has** no effect on domestic partnerships or civil unions. Advocates hail the move as a moral correction, **having** finally granted gay and lesbian married couples the tax rights and protections previously **denied** to them.  

END OF SUMMARY.
