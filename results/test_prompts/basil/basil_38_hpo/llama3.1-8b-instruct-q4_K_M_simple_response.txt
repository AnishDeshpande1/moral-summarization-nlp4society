SUMMARY:

The U.S. Treasury Department announced a new policy recognizing same-sex couples' marriages for federal tax purposes, even if they live in a state that does not. This decision follows the Supreme Court's ruling to overturn the Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA) and marks another step towards equality for the LGBTQ+ community. The policy states that the state where the couple was married will determine their federal tax status, providing certainty and clear guidance for same-sex couples nationwide. This move is significant as it addresses the burden of federal tax law on same-sex couples, which prompted the legal challenge to DOMA in the first place. Edie Windsor, the plaintiff in the DOMA case, hailed the decision as a crucial step towards equality.

The new policy applies to all federal tax provisions where marriage is a factor, including filing status, employee benefits, and income taxes. While same-sex couples will now be eligible for crucial tax benefits and protections, they will also be subject to the "marriage penalty," in which joint filers incur a higher tax burden than if they filed as single people.

END OF SUMMARY.
