SUMMARY:
The Treasury Department announced that the IRS will recognize same‑sex marriages for federal tax purposes even when couples live in states that do not, aligning tax treatment with the Supreme Court’s decision to strike down DOM A. Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew said the rule gives “certainty and clear, coherent tax filing guidance” and guarantees that legally married same‑sex couples can move across the country without losing federal filing status. The policy extends all marriage‑related tax benefits and obligations—filing status, credits, deductions, estate and gift taxes—to same‑sex couples nationwide, while also subjecting them to the same “marriage penalty” that heterosexual couples face. Advocates hailed the move as a long‑overdue correction of the discrimination that prompted the original DOM A lawsuit, noting that it follows other Obama‑administration actions expanding federal benefits to gay couples in non‑recognizing states. Edie Windsor, whose case overturned DOM A, praised the decision as the exact remedy she sought. This step marks another significant advance toward equal treatment of LGBTQ + families under federal law.  
END OF SUMMARY.
