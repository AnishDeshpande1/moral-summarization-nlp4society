**SUMMARY:**  
The U.S. Treasury announced a new policy that recognizes same‑sex marriages for federal tax purposes, even when couples reside in states that do not. Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew said the ruling provides “certainty and clear, coherent tax filing guidance” and ensures that “committed and loving gay and lesbian married couples will now be treated equally” under federal law. The decision marks a significant step of *progress* for the LGBT community, removing the *discriminatory* barriers that once denied them *benefits*, *protections*, and *access* to tax advantages. It also means couples can move *freely* across the country without their federal filing status changing, though they will now face the same *marriage penalty* that applies to many heterosexual couples. Human Rights Campaign President Chad Griffin called the change “crucial” for *justice* and *fair* treatment, and plaintiff Edie Windsor hailed it as the *remedy* she sought. The policy extends *rights* and *equal* treatment to all legally married same‑sex couples nationwide.
