SUMMARY:

The U.S. Department of the Treasury announced that it will recognize same-sex couples' marriages for tax purposes, even if they live in a state that does not. This decision follows the Supreme Court's ruling to overturn the Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA) and marks another step towards equality for the lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender community. The policy, which was prompted by Edie Windsor's lawsuit against DOMA, provides certainty and clear tax filing guidance for all legally married same-sex couples nationwide. Under this new policy, federal tax provisions where marriage is a factor will apply to same-sex couples regardless of their state of residence. This decision ensures that committed and loving gay and lesbian married couples will now be treated equally under federal tax laws. The Treasury's actions are part of a series of LGBT policies offered by agencies within the Obama administration following the DOMA ruling, aimed at providing equal rights and benefits to same-sex couples.

END OF SUMMARY.
