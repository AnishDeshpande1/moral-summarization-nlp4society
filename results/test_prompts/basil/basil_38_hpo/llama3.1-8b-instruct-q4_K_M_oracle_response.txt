SUMMARY:

The U.S. Treasury Department's decision provides certainty and clear tax filing guidance for same-sex married couples nationwide, ensuring they are treated equally under federal tax laws. The ruling assures these couples that their federal filing status will not change even if they move to a state that does not recognize their marriage. This decision marks the latest progress in the lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender community's fight for equality.

The Treasury's actions follow the overturning of the Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA) by the U.S. Supreme Court, which forced same-sex couples to pay higher taxes due to the act's discriminatory provisions. This decision extends federal benefits to gay couples in states that don't recognize their marriages, a crucial step towards equality.

Edie Windsor, the plaintiff who challenged DOMA, hailed the decision as the precise remedy she sought, ensuring that committed and loving gay and lesbian married couples will be treated equally under federal tax laws. The Treasury's actions are part of a broader effort to extend federal benefits to same-sex couples, recognizing their marriages and providing them with access to crucial tax benefits.

END OF SUMMARY.
