The U.S. Department of the Treasury has announced that it will recognize same-sex couples' marriages even if they live in a state that does not, marking a significant step towards equality for the LGBTQ+ community. The decision, prompted by the Supreme Court's ruling to overturn DOMA, provides clarity and consistency in federal tax laws for all legally married same-sex couples nationwide. Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew stated that this ruling "provides access to benefits, responsibilities and protections under federal tax law that all Americans deserve." This move is seen as a victory for equality, with Edie Windsor, the plaintiff in the DOMA case, hailing it as the precise remedy she sought when challenging the constitutionality of DOMA. The policy applies to married couples, not those in domestic partnerships or same-sex unions, and comes on the heels of other LGBT policies offered by agencies within the Obama administration following the DOMA ruling.
