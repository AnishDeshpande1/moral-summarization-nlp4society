SUMMARY:

The U.S. Department of the Treasury announced that it will recognize same-sex couples' marriages for tax purposes, even if they live in a state that does not. This decision was prompted by the Supreme Court's ruling to overturn the Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA) and provides clarity on how to handle federal taxes for same-sex married couples who move to a different state. The new policy states that the state where the couple got married will determine their federal tax status, rather than the state they currently reside in. This decision affects all federal tax provisions related to marriage, including filing status, employee benefits, and income taxes. The Treasury's actions are seen as a significant step forward for the lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender community, providing access to crucial tax benefits and protections previously denied to them.

END OF SUMMARY.
