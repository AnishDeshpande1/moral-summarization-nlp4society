SUMMARY:
Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson warned lawmakers that the pending “religious‑freedom” bill – criticized for potentially legalising discrimination against gay people – may need to be recalled or rewritten. The governor’s own son, Seth Hutchinson, a labor organizer and LGBTQ‑rights advocate, wrote to his father urging him to consider the bill’s harmful impact on Arkansas’s reputation and economy. Their public disagreement highlights a classic subversion of parental authority: the younger Hutchinson challenges the governor’s traditional, authority‑based stance by invoking care for marginalized citizens and fairness toward LGBTQ Arkansans. Seth’s story – from low‑wage jobs to activism after witnessing injustice toward a gay teacher – underscores a moral appeal to protect vulnerable people (care/harm) and to demand equal treatment (fairness/cheating). While the governor signals openness to revising the legislation, he attributes his reconsideration to broader pressure, not solely his son’s influence. The family’s ongoing dialogue reflects a broader political clash between established authority and a call for more inclusive, fair policies.

END OF SUMMARY.
