SUMMARY: Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson announced that he may ask legislators to recall or rewrite portions of the controversial “religious freedom” bill, which opponents say would permit discrimination against LGBTQ people. In a rare public note, Hutchinson revealed that his son, Seth Hutchinson—a Texas State Employees Union organizer and longtime political dissenter—had written to him warning that the measure could harm the state’s reputation and economy. Despite their close personal relationship, the father‑son duo have long held divergent political views; Seth, a former Young Democratic Socialist and gay‑rights activist, broke from his family’s conservative tradition after early work experiences highlighted economic and social injustices. He has previously voiced opposition to the bill and to other Republican policies, while maintaining regular family contact. The governor credited broader pressure, not his son’s influence, for his reconsideration of the legislation. END OF SUMMARY.
