SUMMARY:
Asa Hutchinson, Republican governor of Arkansas, revealed on Wednesday that his son Seth had expressed opposition to the state's proposed "religious freedom" bill. The bill has been criticized for potentially allowing discrimination against gay people. Seth, a labor activist and organizer for the Texas State Employees Union, wrote to his father warning that the proposal could harm Arkansas' reputation and economy. This is not the first time the two have disagreed on politics; they have had discussions about social issues since high school. Seth's views began to diverge from his family's when he was 15 and working in low-paying jobs, leading him to become interested in gay rights. Despite their differences, the father-son relationship remains close, with regular communication and family gatherings.

END OF SUMMARY.
