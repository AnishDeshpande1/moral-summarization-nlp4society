SUMMARY:
Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson said his son, Seth Hutchinson, a Texas State Employees Union organizer and self‑identified “black sheep” of the family, warned him that the state’s pending religious‑freedom bill could hurt Arkansas’s reputation and economy. Seth, who grew up on the campaign trail but shifted toward progressive views after working low‑wage jobs and witnessing discrimination against gay people, has long debated politics with his father. He told the governor that his concerns stem from a broader sense of justice, not personal influence, and noted that families often disagree on politics while staying close. The governor, acknowledging his son’s letter, said he will ask lawmakers to recall or rewrite parts of the bill, citing pressure from all sides. The episode highlights a personal moral clash within a prominent Arkansas family over a proposal critics say would legalize discrimination against LGBTQ + individuals.
