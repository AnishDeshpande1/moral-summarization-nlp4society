SUMMARY: Asa Hutchinson, the Republican Governor of Arkansas, is facing opposition from his own son, Seth, over a proposed bill that critics argue would legalize discrimination against gay people. Despite their differing views, the family maintains a close relationship, with Seth having grown up on the campaign trail and initially following his parents' values. However, as he worked various jobs and began to see injustices in low-paying employment and mistreatment of LGBTQ+ individuals, his views changed. Seth joined groups like the Young Democratic Socialists and rallied for gay rights during his college years. His activism did not sway his father's initial stance on the bill, but rather the pressure from all sides prompted Asa Hutchinson to reconsider. On Wednesday, he announced that he would ask lawmakers to either recall or rewrite portions of the bill. Seth refused to take credit, attributing his father's change in heart to the mounting opposition. The family's disagreement highlights the importance of justice and equality, and the impact that individual voices can have on public policy.

END OF SUMMARY.
