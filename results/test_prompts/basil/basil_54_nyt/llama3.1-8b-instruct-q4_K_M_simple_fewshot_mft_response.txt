SUMMARY:

Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson's son, Seth Hutchinson, has been vocal in his opposition to the state's proposed religious freedom bill, which critics argue could legalize discrimination against gay people. In a letter to his father, Seth expressed concerns that the bill would damage Arkansas' reputation and economy. The younger Hutchinson, an organizer for the Texas State Employees Union, has long been involved in labor activism and gay rights advocacy. His views diverged from his father's when he was about 15 years old, after witnessing older workers struggling with low-paying jobs and observing the treatment of a gay art teacher. Despite their differing opinions, Seth claims that he did not sway his father's decision to reconsider the bill, attributing it instead to the "pressure coming at him from all sides." The governor has now asked lawmakers to either recall or rewrite portions of the bill, in an effort to strike a balance between religious freedom and non-discrimination.
