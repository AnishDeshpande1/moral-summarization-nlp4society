SUMMARY:

A bill dubbed the "religious freedom" bill in Arkansas, which critics argue would allow for discrimination against gay people, has sparked opposition from an unexpected source: Governor Asa Hutchinson's own son, Seth Hutchinson. Despite being raised on the campaign trail by his father, a former congressman, Seth developed liberal views at a young age and began to disagree with his father's conservative stances. He became involved in labor activism and rallied for gay rights during his time in college. The younger Hutchinson has maintained a close relationship with his father despite their differing views, and has been in regular communication with him about the bill. On Wednesday, Governor Hutchinson announced that he would ask lawmakers to either recall or rewrite parts of the bill, citing pressure from various groups. Seth attributed his father's change of heart to external pressure rather than his own influence.

END OF SUMMARY.
