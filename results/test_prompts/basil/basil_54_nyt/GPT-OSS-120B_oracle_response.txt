SUMMARY: Governor Asa Hutchinson of Arkansas defended a religious‑freedom bill that critics say would legalize discrimination against gay people. In a surprising twist, his son, Seth Hutchinson – a labor activist and former Young Democratic Socialist – wrote to his father warning that the proposal could damage Arkansas’s reputation and economy. Despite their love and close family ties, the two have long debated justice and policy, often arguing fiercely but staying connected. Seth’s views shifted after working low‑wage jobs and witnessing an arrest of a gay teacher who was only loitering because he could not be out. He later joined gay‑rights rallies and urged his father to ask lawmakers to recall or rewrite portions of the bill. While Seth refuses credit for any change, he says his father is now reconsidering the measure amid pressure from all sides. The episode highlights a personal justice struggle within a politically influential Arkansas family. END OF SUMMARY.
