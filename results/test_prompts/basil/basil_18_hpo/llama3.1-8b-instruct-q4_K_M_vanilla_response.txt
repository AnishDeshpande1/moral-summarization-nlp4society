SUMMARY:

Republican presidential candidate Rick Perry has released a new television ad in Iowa, pledging to end what he calls "Obama's war on religion". In the ad, Perry criticizes Obama for allowing open service of gays and lesbians in the military and prohibiting prayer in public schools. He also condemns the Obama administration's decision to use foreign aid to promote gay rights abroad, calling it a "war with people of faith". Perry argues that faith is essential to America's strength and that he will fight against liberal attacks on religious heritage as president. The ad is part of Perry's effort to boost his campaign in Iowa ahead of the January 3 caucuses, where he currently lags behind other Republican candidates with just 6% support among likely caucus-goers.

END OF SUMMARY.
