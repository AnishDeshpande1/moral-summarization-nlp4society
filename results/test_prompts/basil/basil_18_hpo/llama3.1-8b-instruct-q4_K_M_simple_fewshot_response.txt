SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry has released a new television ad in Iowa, appealing to hard-right conservatives by contrasting Christianity with homosexuality. He vows to end what he calls "Obama's war on religion" and highlights his criticism of overturning the "don't ask, don't tell" policy for gays serving in the military. Perry claims that it's wrong that gays can serve openly in the military while children can't openly celebrate Christmas or pray in school. The ad is part of Perry's multimillion-dollar television budget, targeting Iowa's Christian evangelicals. However, his message has been met with criticism from the Log Cabin Republicans, who argue that being a person of faith does not mean one cannot support equality. This latest attack on Obama by Perry poses a challenge to the President's campaign, particularly among conservative voters.

END OF SUMMARY.
