SUMMARY:

Republican presidential candidate Rick Perry has released a new television ad in Iowa, pledging to end what he calls "Obama's war on religion". Perry claims that Obama's policies, including allowing open service of gays and lesbians in the military and prohibiting prayer in public schools, are an attack on people of faith. He vows to fight against liberal attacks on religious heritage and restore America's strength through faith. The ad is part of Perry's efforts to appeal to conservative voters in Iowa, where he has recently stepped up his attacks on Obama for cultural issues. Perry's campaign has also invested heavily in the state, with plans to bring influential Texans to Iowa in December. However, a recent poll shows that Perry still lags behind other Republican candidates among likely caucus-goers.
