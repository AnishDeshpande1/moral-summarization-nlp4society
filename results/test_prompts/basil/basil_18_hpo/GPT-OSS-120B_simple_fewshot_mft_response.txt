SUMMARY:
Republican nominee Rick Perry launched a $1 million TV ad in Iowa that frames President Obama as a subversive force attacking America’s religious authority. Perry declares he will “end Obama’s war on religion,” positioning himself as the defender of traditional, sacred values against a liberal administration that he says degrades morality by allowing gays to serve openly in the military and banning public prayer and Christmas celebrations in schools. He portrays Obama’s use of foreign aid to promote LGBTQ rights abroad as an assault on the sanctity of faith‑based life, accusing the president of mistaking tolerance for endorsement of a “degraded” lifestyle. By invoking “faith made America strong,” Perry casts his campaign as a restoration of rightful authority and moral purity, contrasting his Christian identity with what he depicts as Obama’s subversive, secular agenda. Despite the aggressive moral messaging, a Des Moines Register poll shows Perry garnering only six percent support among likely Republican caucus voters. END OF SUMMARY.
