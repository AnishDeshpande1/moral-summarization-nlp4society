SUMMARY:
Republican nominee Rick Perry launched a new TV ad in Iowa that frames President Barack Obama as waging a “war on religion.”  Perry, unabashedly Christian, attacks the Obama administration’s support for open service by gays and lesbians in the military and its ban on public school prayer, arguing that faith is the foundation of America’s strength.  He pledges to end what he calls liberal assaults on the nation’s religious heritage and to stop the use of foreign aid to promote LGBTQ rights abroad, which he says betrays “America’s tolerance” for an endorsement of those lifestyles.  The ad, part of a $1 million broadcast buy ahead of the Jan. 3 caucuses, is accompanied by a plan to bring hundreds of influential Texans to Iowa.  Despite the moral‑focused messaging, a Des Moines Register poll shows Perry with only about six percent support among likely Republican caucus voters.  
END OF SUMMARY.
