SUMMARY: Republican presidential hopeful Rick Perry launched a television ad in Iowa pledging to **end** what he calls President Obama’s “war on religion.” Perry denounces the **open** service of gays and lesbians in the military and the ban on **prayer** and **celebrate**‑ing Christmas in public schools, saying he will **fight** liberal **attacks** on America’s **faith**‑based heritage. He claims “faith made America strong” and promises to **fight** to restore it. Perry also **attacks** the Obama administration for **investing** foreign aid to **promote** gay‑rights abroad, labeling it **objectionable** and a **mistaken** endorsement of a lifestyle many believers reject. The ad, bought for $1 million ahead of the Jan. 3 caucuses, is part of a broader push that includes bringing “more than 600 influential Texans” to Iowa. Despite the aggressive messaging, a Des Moines Register poll shows Perry with only six percent support among likely Republican caucus‑goers. END OF SUMMARY.
