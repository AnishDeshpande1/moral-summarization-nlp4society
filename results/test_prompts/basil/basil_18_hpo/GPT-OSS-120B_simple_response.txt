SUMMARY:  
Republican presidential hopeful Rick Perry launched a TV ad in Iowa condemning what he calls “President Barack Obama’s war on religion.” Perry frames his campaign as a moral crusade to defend Christian values against liberal policies he sees as attacks on faith: the open service of gays and lesbians in the military, bans on public‑school prayer, and U.S. foreign aid that promotes LGBTQ rights abroad. He declares openly that he is proud to be a Christian and argues that America’s strength once came from its religious heritage—a strength he vows to restore. Perry has intensified cultural attacks on Obama, accusing the administration of using taxpayer money to endorse lifestyles that many believers find “deeply objectionable.” He has purchased $1 million in TV spots ahead of the Iowa caucuses and plans a large Texas‑based outreach to Iowa voters, though a recent Des Moines Register poll shows he trails with only about six percent support among likely Republican caucus participants.  

END OF SUMMARY.
