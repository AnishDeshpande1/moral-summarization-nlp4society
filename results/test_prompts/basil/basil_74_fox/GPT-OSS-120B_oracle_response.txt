SUMMARY:  
Kathy Griffin admitted she “went too far” with a photo shoot by controversial photographer Tyler Shields, holding a blood‑splattered mask of President Trump. She called the image “disturbing” and said it “wasn’t funny,” acknowledging it was “wrong” and that she “understands how it offends people.” After backlash—including criticism from Chelsea Clinton who called the picture “vile and wrong” and mentions of past “threats” and “abuse” linked to Shields’ work—Griffin said she would take the picture down. Shields defended the piece as art, arguing that “freedom” of expression is “allowed” and that provocative images are meant to make viewers uncomfortable. He noted the photo is not real, likening it to a movie scene, and said he does not regret the collaboration, despite previous controversies. Griffin’s apology and the removal of the image reflect the moral outcry surrounding the stunt.  

END OF SUMMARY.
