SUMMARY:

Kathy Griffin apologized for her recent photo shoot with photographer Tyler Shields, where she held a bloodied mask of President Trump. Griffin admitted that the image was "too disturbing" and "not funny," saying she had "made a lot of mistakes in my career." The photo shoot sparked widespread outrage on social media, with Chelsea Clinton calling it "vile and wrong."

Shields defended the photo as art, stating that it was meant to make a statement about politics. He claimed that Griffin was hoping to create a bold message and that the image was not meant to be taken literally.

Reps for Griffin did not comment on the matter, but Shields said he didn't regret collaborating with her. The controversy is not new for Shields, who has faced backlash in the past over his photos. Griffin's apology comes as the photo shoot continues to generate heated debate and criticism.

END OF SUMMARY.
