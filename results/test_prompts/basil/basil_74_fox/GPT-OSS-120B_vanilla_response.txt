SUMMARY: Comedian Kathy Griffin apologized on Twitter for a controversial photo shoot with photographer Tyler Shields, in which she is depicted holding a blood‑stained mask of President Donald Trump. Describing the image as “too disturbing” and “not funny,” Griffin said she “went too far” and will remove the picture, acknowledging it offends many. The shoot, intended as a bold political statement, drew criticism from the public and figures such as Chelsea Clinton, who called it “vile and wrong.” Shields defended the work as artistic expression, saying art should provoke mixed reactions and that no real violence occurred. He noted technical issues caused a behind‑the‑scenes video to be temporarily removed. This is not Shields’ first controversy; he previously faced backlash for a photo of “Glee” actress Heather Morris. Griffin’s representatives have not commented further. END OF SUMMARY.
