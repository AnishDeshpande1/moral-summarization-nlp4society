President Donald Trump renewed his attack on the Justice Department, labeling it a “deep state” and calling for the jailing of top Hillary Clinton aide Huma Abedin. Trump posted an early‑morning tweet after the State Department released emails that showed Abedin forwarding sensitive messages, including passwords, to a personal account. He demanded that the Justice Department act on “Comey and others,” referencing former FBI Director James Comey’s Russia investigation. Trump has repeatedly undermined the Justice Department and FBI, criticizing Attorney General Jeff Sessions for recusing himself and asserting his absolute right to direct investigations. By framing the Justice Department as corrupt and partisan, Trump portrays his actions as defending loyalty to the nation and exposing betrayal within the “deep state.” The president’s rhetoric emphasizes authority, subversion of perceived bias, and a claim of fairness against a supposedly cheating bureaucracy.
