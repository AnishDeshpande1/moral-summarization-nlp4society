SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump intensified his assault on the Justice Department, labeling it the “deep state,” and called for the imprisonment of former Hillary Clinton aide Huma Abedin after a batch of her State Department emails was released. The emails, obtained through a Judicial Watch FOIA lawsuit, showed Abedin had forwarded official messages—some containing passwords—to a personal account during the 2016 campaign, a detail Trump and his right‑wing allies have repeatedly highlighted. In the same tweet, Trump demanded that the Justice Department act on “Comey and others,” referencing the ousted FBI director who was overseeing the Russia‑collusion probe. The president has continuously undermined the FBI and Justice Department, criticizing Attorney General Jeff Sessions for recusing himself and asserting that he has an “absolute right” to direct investigations. The moral framing suggests Trump is abusing presidential power to weaponize the justice system against political opponents, eroding institutional independence and the rule of law.

END OF SUMMARY.
