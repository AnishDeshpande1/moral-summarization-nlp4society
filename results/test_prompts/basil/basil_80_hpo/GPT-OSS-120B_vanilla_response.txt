SUMMARY: President Donald Trump renewed his attack on the Justice Department, labeling it part of the “deep state,” and called for the imprisonment of former Clinton aide Huma Abedin. Trump’s criticism came after the State Department released Abedin’s emails, which had been obtained through a Judicial Watch FOIA lawsuit and were tied to the 2016 FBI probe of Hillary Clinton’s private email server. The disclosed emails showed Abedin forwarding State Department messages, some containing passwords, to a personal account. In the same tweet, Trump urged the Justice Department to act on “Comey and others,” referencing former FBI Director James Comey, who was overseeing the Russia investigation until his May dismissal. Trump has repeatedly sought to undermine the Justice Department and FBI, accusing Attorney General Jeff Sessions of overstepping after Sessions recused himself from the Russia probe, and claiming he has the “absolute right” to direct investigations. END OF SUMMARY.
