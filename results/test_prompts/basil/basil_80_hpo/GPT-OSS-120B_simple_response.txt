SUMMARY: President Donald Trump renewed his crusade against what he calls the “deep state” Justice Department, publicly demanding the imprisonment of former Clinton aide Huma Abedin after a batch of her State Department emails was released. The emails—obtained through a Judicial Watch FOIA lawsuit and tied to the 2016 FBI probe of Hillary Clinton’s private server—show Abedin allegedly forwarded sensitive messages, including passwords, to a personal account. Trump used the disclosure to vilify the Justice Department, urging it to act on “Comey and others,” a thinly veiled attack on the FBI’s former director who was overseeing the Russia‑collusion investigation before being fired. The president’s tweets and recent statements continue to delegitimize the Justice Department and FBI, accusing them of partisan bias and insisting he has an “absolute right” to direct their investigations. This rhetoric reflects a broader pattern of Trump’s attempts to undermine independent law‑enforcement institutions for political gain. END OF SUMMARY.
