SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump renewed his attack on the "deep state" Justice Department, calling for the jailing of top Hillary Clinton aide Huma Abedin. This came after emails from Abedin were released by the State Department, showing that she had forwarded sensitive information to her personal account. Trump also targeted former FBI Director James Comey and Attorney General Jeff Sessions, claiming they should be held accountable. In a tweet, Trump stated that the Justice Department must act on "Comey and others," fueling speculation about his motives.

Trump's criticism of the Justice Department is part of a larger effort to undermine the Russia investigation and assert his control over government investigations. He has repeatedly claimed that he has the right to direct government investigations himself, despite constitutional checks on presidential power. The move marks another escalation in Trump's feud with the Justice Department and FBI, which has intensified as the Russia probe continues.

END OF SUMMARY.
