SUMMARY: House Speaker Nancy Pelosi says she isn’t upset by Rep. Rashida Tlaib’s vulgar pledge to “impeach that motherfucker,” viewing the outburst as a free‑speech issue rather than a matter for censorship. In a town‑hall interview with Joy Reid, Pelosi admits she personally dislikes the language but refuses to police colleagues’ words, noting that even President Trump has used far cruder insults that have real‑world impact. Pelosi argues Tlaib’s expletive, while noisy, does not affect House business and should be protected as an individual member’s expression, not a Democratic caucus stance. Tlaib, speaking to supporters after becoming one of the first Muslim women in Congress, used the profanity to rally her base around impeachment, prompting cheers. Pelosi cautions the remark may energize Trump’s supporters but carries little substantive weight. Trump later mocked the comment on Twitter, claiming Democrats want to impeach him because they can’t win in 2020. END OF SUMMARY.
