SUMMARY: House Speaker Nancy Pelosi said she isn’t upset over Rep. Rashida Tlaib’s vulgar “motherfucker” comment about impeaching President Donald Trump, framing it as a matter of freedom of speech. In a town‑hall interview with Joy Reid, Pelosi admitted she dislikes the expletive but refuses to censor colleagues, noting the remark is “no worse than what the president has said.” She pointed to Trump’s infamous leaked 2005 tape where he bragged about grabbing women “by the pussy,” and his repeated insults toward other nations and political foes, arguing his remarks have real impact while Tlaib’s do not. Tlaib, the new Muslim congresswoman, used the expletive at a MoveOn event, rallying supporters with chants to impeach the “motherfucker.” Pelosi warned the comment might energize Trump’s base but said it does not affect House business. Trump responded on Twitter, claiming they want to impeach him because they can’t win in 2020. END OF SUMMARY.
