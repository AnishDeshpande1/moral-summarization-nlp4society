SUMMARY: House Speaker Nancy Pelosi said she is not upset about Rep. Rashida Tlaib’s vulgar vow to “impeach that motherfucker,” referring to former President Donald Trump, framing it as a free‑speech issue rather than censorship. In a town‑hall interview with Joy Reid, Pelosi acknowledged she personally dislikes the language but does not police colleagues’ speech, noting Trump’s own profane remarks have been far more impactful. Pelosi argued Tlaib’s comment is unlikely to affect House business, though it may energize Trump’s base. Tlaib, one of the first Muslim women in Congress, used the expletive while celebrating her victory at a MoveOn event and promising impeachment. Trump responded on Twitter, claiming Democrats want to impeach him because they can’t win in 2020. Pelosi emphasized that the controversy concerns an individual member’s speech, not the Democratic caucus’s stance. END OF SUMMARY.
