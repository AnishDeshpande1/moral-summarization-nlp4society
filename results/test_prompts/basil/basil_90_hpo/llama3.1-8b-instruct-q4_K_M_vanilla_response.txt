SUMMARY:

House Speaker Nancy Pelosi defended Rep. Rashida Tlaib's (D-MI) use of profanity when discussing impeaching President Donald Trump, calling it a free speech issue. Tlaib had used the phrase "impeach the motherfucker" during an event hosted by MoveOn on Thursday. Pelosi stated that while she doesn't like the language, she believes it's no worse than Trump's past comments, which have been widely criticized. She noted that Trump's comments can impact people's lives, but Tlaib's comment does not.

Pelosi also argued that any controversy over the comment is about individual freedom of speech, not the stance of the Democratic caucus. Trump appeared to respond to Tlaib on Twitter, suggesting that Democrats only want to impeach him because they know they can't win in 2020. Pelosi said she doesn't think Tlaib's comment will make much of a difference in House business.

END OF SUMMARY.
