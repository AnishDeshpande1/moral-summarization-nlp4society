SUMMARY:

House Speaker Nancy Pelosi has defended Michigan Rep. Rashida Tlaib's controversial comment about impeaching President Donald Trump, calling it a "free speech" issue. Tlaib had vowed to "impeach that motherfucker" in a speech to supporters, using an expletive that sparked cheers from the crowd. Pelosi acknowledged that she doesn't like the language, but noted that Trump has made similar insults, including bragging about grabbing women "by the pussy" and referring to certain nations as "shithole countries". Pelosi argued that while Trump's comments have the power to "impact people's lives", Tlaib's comment does not. She also reasoned that any controversy over Tlaib's comment concerns the "freedom of speech of an individual member", not the stance of the Democratic caucus. Trump responded on Twitter, claiming that Democrats only want to impeach him because they know they can't win in 2020.

END OF SUMMARY.
