SUMMARY:
President Trump lashed out at former Clinton aide Huma Abedin, accusing her of “disregarding basic security protocols” and of handing classified passwords to foreign agents. Citing the recent release of Abedin’s emails—some marked classified and allegedly forwarded to her personal Yahoo account and to her estranged husband’s laptop—Trump demanded that the “Deep State” Justice Department finally hold her accountable, likening her treatment to the harsh punishment of Navy sailor Kristian Saucier, who was jailed for taking unauthorized submarine photos. The tweet framed the episode as a stark double standard: while a low‑level sailor was imprisoned, “Crooked Hillary” and her top aide have escaped any consequences despite alleged mishandling of classified material. By invoking the “Deep State” and calling for jail, Trump positioned himself as the champion of national‑security integrity and law‑and‑order, casting the Clinton camp as reckless and above the law. The moral narrative underscores a call for equal accountability and warns against partisan leniency for political elites.  
END OF SUMMARY.
