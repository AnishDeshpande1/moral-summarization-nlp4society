SUMMARY:
President Trump attacked former Clinton aide Huma Abedin, labeling her “crooked” and demanding the “Deep State” Justice Department prosecute her for allegedly mishandling classified material. He framed the issue as a stark example of double standards, contrasting Abedin’s alleged security breaches—sending classified passwords to foreign agents and forwarding sensitive State Department emails to a personal Yahoo account—with the relatively light treatment of others, such as the sailor jailed for photographing a submarine. By invoking “Deep State” actors and calling for jail, Trump positions himself as defending legitimate authority against subversive insiders. He accuses Abedin of betraying loyalty to the nation and to Hillary Clinton, while portraying the lack of punishment for Clinton’s circle as an unfair cheating of the rules. The tweet underscores a narrative that the establishment protects its own (Clinton allies) and punishes outsiders, urging a reassertion of proper authority, equitable justice, and loyalty to the country. END OF SUMMARY.
