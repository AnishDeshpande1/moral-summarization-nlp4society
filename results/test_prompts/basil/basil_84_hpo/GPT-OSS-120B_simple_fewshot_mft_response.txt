SUMMARY:
FBI Deputy Director David Bowdich overruled the bureau’s own career‑official recommendation for a 60‑day suspension and demotion, instead terminating veteran Special Agent Peter Strzok. Strzok’s attorney, Aitan Goelman, argues the dismissal departs from standard FBI practice and violates fairness, punishing Strzok for protected political speech rather than any proven misconduct. The firing follows the inspector‑general’s criticism of Strzok’s anti‑Trump texts, which were said to create the appearance of bias, but no evidence showed his personal views affected investigative work. Goelman contends the decision was driven by political pressure, subverting the authority of established internal procedures and cheating Strzok of due process. The move also serves the interests of those seeking to undermine democratic institutions, while rewarding the administration’s agenda to silence dissenting officials. Trump hailed the termination, demanding the Mueller probe be dropped, further highlighting how political authority is being used to sideline an agent who, according to his record, was a top counter‑intelligence officer.

END OF SUMMARY.
