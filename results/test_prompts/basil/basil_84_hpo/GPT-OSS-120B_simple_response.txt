SUMMARY:  
FBI Special Agent Peter Strzok was dismissed by Deputy Director David Bowdich after a career‑official recommendation for a 60‑day suspension and demotion. Strzok’s attorney, Aitan Goelman, argues the firing departs from normal Bureau practice, violates FBI Director Chris Wray’s promise of due process, and reflects political pressure to punish Strzok for protected speech. He stresses that extensive investigations and congressional testimony found no evidence that Strzok’s personal views influenced his work, and that the termination rewards those who seek to undermine the country and weaken democracy.  

Strzok, a 21‑year veteran and noted counter‑intelligence officer, exchanged anti‑Trump texts with former FBI official Lisa Page, which led to his removal from the Mueller team once the messages surfaced. The DOJ Inspector General, while “deeply troubled” by the texts and by Strzok’s use of personal email for FBI business, also noted a text suggesting willingness to affect the election. The precise reason for Bowdich’s decision remains unclear, though Strzok’s extramarital affair with Page may have been a factor. President Trump celebrated the firing, calling for the Trump investigation to be dropped and the Clinton probe to be redone.  

The case raises concerns that political retaliation, rather than impartial accountability, drove the termination, posing a moral threat to democratic norms.  

END OF SUMMARY.
