SUMMARY: FBI Special Agent Peter Strzok, removed from the Mueller team after his anti‑Trump texts were uncovered, was fired on Friday by Deputy Director David Bowdich despite a career FBI official’s recommendation for a 60‑day suspension and demotion. Strzok’s attorney, Aitan Goelman, called the termination a break from standard practice and a politically‑motivated punishment for protected speech. The Inspector General’s report had flagged Strzok’s texts as creating an appearance of bias and cited misuse of personal email and an extramarital relationship with former FBI official Lisa Page as concerns. While the report found no evidence that Strzok’s personal views affected his investigative work, it noted the potential for perceived bias. FBI Director Chris Wray had previously pledged to follow normal disciplinary procedures. President Donald Trump praised the firing on Twitter, urging the end of the Russia probe and a redo of the Clinton investigation. END OF SUMMARY.
