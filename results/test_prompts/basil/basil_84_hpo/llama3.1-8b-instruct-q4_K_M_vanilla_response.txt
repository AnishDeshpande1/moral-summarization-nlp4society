SUMMARY:

FBI Special Agent Peter Strzok was fired from the bureau despite recommendations for a 60-day suspension and demotion. His attorney, Aitan Goelman, stated that the decision was "a departure from typical Bureau practice" and contradicted FBI Director Chris Wray's earlier guarantee to follow regular procedures. Strzok had been involved in investigations surrounding the Clinton and Trump campaigns during the 2016 election and joined the Mueller team before leaving due to his anti-Trump texts. The inspector general report criticized Strzok for his use of personal email to conduct FBI business and potentially biased investigative decisions.

The precise justification for FBI Deputy Director David Bowdich's decision is unclear, but it may have been influenced by Trump's disdain for Strzok. Goelman called the decision "a political move" that punishes Strzok for protected speech under the First Amendment. Trump celebrated the firing on Twitter, calling for the Mueller investigation to be dropped and the Clinton investigation to be redone.

END OF SUMMARY.
