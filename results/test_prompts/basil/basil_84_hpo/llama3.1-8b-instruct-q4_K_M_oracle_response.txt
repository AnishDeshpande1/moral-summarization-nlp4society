SUMMARY:

FBI Special Agent Peter Strzok, who was involved in the investigations surrounding the Clinton and Trump campaigns during the 2016 election, has been fired from the bureau despite a recommendation for a 60-day suspension and demotion. Strzok's attorney claims that the decision to terminate him is a departure from typical Bureau practice and contradicts FBI Director Chris Wray's earlier guarantee of following regular process. The decision was taken in response to political pressure, rather than on the basis of facts, and is seen as an attempt to punish Strzok for his protected First Amendment speech. This decision may harm our country and weaken our democracy by allowing those who seek to undermine it to prevail. Despite a lengthy investigation and Congressional testimony, no evidence was found that Strzok's personal views impacted his work on the Clinton or Trump investigations. The inspector general report criticized Strzok for his use of personal email and an anti-Trump text, but Strzok's attorney claims that this is an attempt to take official action against him in response to his speech.

END OF SUMMARY.
