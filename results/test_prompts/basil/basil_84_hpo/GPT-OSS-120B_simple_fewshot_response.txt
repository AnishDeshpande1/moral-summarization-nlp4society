SUMMARY:
FBI Special Agent Peter Strzok, a 21‑year veteran who helped lead both the Clinton email probe and the Mueller investigation, was dismissed by Deputy Director David Bowdich despite an internal recommendation for a 60‑day suspension and demotion. Strzok’s lawyer, Aitan Goelman, denounced the firing as a break from standard Bureau practice, arguing it was driven by political pressure and punished Strzok’s protected speech rather than any proven misconduct. The Justice Department’s inspector‑general had flagged Strzok’s anti‑Trump texts – including one suggesting he might take “official action to impact the presidential candidate’s electoral prospects” – and his use of personal email for FBI business, but found no evidence his personal views affected his investigative work. The decision, critics say, rewards those who seek to undermine democratic institutions and sets a dangerous precedent of politicizing the FBI. President Trump praised the termination and urged that the Russia probe be dropped, further highlighting the clash between partisan loyalty and the principle of an impartial, accountable law‑enforcement agency.  

END OF SUMMARY.
