SUMMARY:

FBI Special Agent Peter Strzok was fired from the bureau despite a career official's recommendation for a 60-day suspension and demotion. Strzok's attorney, Aitan Goelman, claims that the decision was taken in response to political pressure, rather than on the basis of evidence that his personal views affected his work. The FBI's Office of Professional Responsibility had recommended disciplinary action against Strzok, but Deputy Director David Bowdich overruled this recommendation and fired him. This decision has been criticized by Goelman as a "departure from typical Bureau practice" and a attempt to punish Strzok for his political speech protected by the First Amendment.

Strzok's firing is seen as a response to his criticism of President Trump, with Trump himself celebrating the news on Twitter. The incident raises concerns about the politicization of the FBI and the impact of external pressure on disciplinary decisions within the agency. Goelman warns that this decision will "produce only one winner - those who seek to harm our country and weaken our democracy."
