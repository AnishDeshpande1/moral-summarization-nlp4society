SUMMARY:
President Trump denounced the criminal case against former national security adviser Michael Flynn as “very unfair,” claiming that both he and Hillary Clinton “lied” to the FBI but only Flynn suffered consequences. By portraying Flynn as a victim of a partisan “double standard,” Trump amplified his own narrative of victimhood while ignoring the serious nature of Flynn’s guilty plea for false statements about Russia contacts. The president’s weekend tweet about firing Flynn “because he lied” sparked legal concerns that he might be incriminating himself in obstruction of justice, yet he dismissed the issue and blamed his lawyer for the wording. Meanwhile, senators from both parties warned that Trump’s public comments on an active investigation jeopardize the rule of law. The episode underscores a troubling moral imbalance: Trump’s allies are defended and excused, whereas his political opponents are portrayed as above the law, eroding accountability and public confidence in the justice system. END OF SUMMARY.
