SUMMARY: President Trump criticized the criminal case against former national security adviser Michael Flynn, calling it “very unfair” and alleging a double standard compared to Hillary Clinton, who he said also lied to the FBI without consequence. Trump expressed sympathy for Flynn, who pleaded guilty to false statements about his Russia contacts, and reiterated his earlier tweet questioning the fairness of the investigation. The tweet sparked legal concerns about possible obstruction of justice, as it suggested Trump knew Flynn had lied when he allegedly asked former FBI Director James Comey to halt the probe and later fired him. Trump’s personal lawyer John Dowd claimed he authored the tweet and did not intend to break news. Senate Republicans warned Trump to be cautious about commenting on ongoing investigations, while Senate Judiciary Committee Democrat Dianne Feinstein indicated a potential obstruction of justice case is forming. Flynn is now cooperating with Special Counsel Robert Mueller’s Russia investigation, though his future disclosures remain uncertain. END OF SUMMARY.
