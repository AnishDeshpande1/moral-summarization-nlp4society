SUMMARY:

President Trump defended former national security adviser Michael Flynn by claiming that both he and Hillary Clinton lied to the FBI, but only Flynn faced consequences. Trump argued that this was unfair, stating that "Hillary Clinton lied many times to the FBI, nothing happened to her. Flynn lied and they destroyed his life." This moral double standard highlights a lack of fairness in how Trump views the situation. By comparing himself to Clinton, who also lied to the FBI but did not face severe consequences, Trump is attempting to deflect attention from his own potential wrongdoing.

The real concern here is whether Trump's actions are driven by a desire for fairness and justice or a desire to cheat the system. Flynn's guilty plea has sparked questions about Trump's involvement in the investigation, with some suggesting that he may have obstructed justice. The contrast between Trump's words and actions raises concerns about his commitment to fairness and his willingness to bend the rules to suit his own interests.

END OF SUMMARY.
