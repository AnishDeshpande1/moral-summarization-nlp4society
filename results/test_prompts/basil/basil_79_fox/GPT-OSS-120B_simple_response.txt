SUMMARY: President Trump denounced the criminal case against former national security adviser Michael Flynn as “very unfair,” framing it as a moral double‑standard. He argued that both he and Hillary Clinton lied to the FBI, yet only Flynn suffered consequences, saying “Clinton lied many times…nothing happened to her. Flynn lied and they ruined his life.” Trump’s comments, made while heading to a policy announcement in Utah, echoed a weekend tweet questioning the fairness of the investigation. The president’s moral narrative casts Flynn as a victim of a partisan justice system and Clinton as a similarly culpable figure who escaped punishment. Critics, including Sen. Lindsey Graham and Sen. Dianne Feinstein, warned that Trump’s public remarks and a controversial tweet about firing Flynn could expose him to obstruction‑of‑justice accusations. While Flynn has pleaded guilty to false statements and is cooperating with Special Counsel Mueller, Trump’s rhetoric seeks to portray the legal actions against him as an unjust, politically motivated assault, reinforcing his broader claim of bias against his administration. END OF SUMMARY.
