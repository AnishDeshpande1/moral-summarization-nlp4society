SUMMARY:

President Trump weighed in on the case of former national security adviser Michael Flynn, calling it "very unfair" that Flynn faced consequences for lying to the FBI while Hillary Clinton did not face similar repercussions. Trump claimed that both he and Clinton "lied" to the FBI but only Flynn was held accountable. He also defended his decision to fire Flynn, saying he did so because Flynn lied to Vice President Pence about Russia contacts.

The president's comments come after a weekend tweet sparked controversy, with some experts suggesting it may have exposed him to obstruction of justice allegations. Trump denied asking former FBI Director James Comey to stop investigating Flynn, but Special Counsel Robert Mueller is still investigating the matter. Flynn has pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI and is cooperating with investigators.

The issue has raised questions about a potential double standard in how government officials are treated when they lie to investigators. The president's comments have been criticized by some lawmakers, including Sen. Dianne Feinstein, who said the Senate Judiciary Committee is looking into obstruction of justice allegations against Trump.

END OF SUMMARY.
