SUMMARY:

President Trump weighed in on the Michael Flynn case, calling it "very unfair" and claiming that Hillary Clinton lied to the FBI but faced no consequences. Trump stated that he feels "badly" for Flynn, who pleaded guilty to giving false statements about his Russia contacts. The president pointed out that Clinton was interviewed by the FBI about her private email system but did not face charges. Trump's comments come after a weekend tweet sparked controversy, with some legal experts suggesting it could expose him to obstruction of justice allegations.

Senator Lindsey Graham warned Trump to be cautious when commenting on ongoing investigations. Senator Dianne Feinstein said that the Senate Judiciary Committee is investigating potential obstruction of justice against Trump. Flynn has begun cooperating with Special Counsel Robert Mueller's investigators in the Russia meddling case, raising questions about what he might reveal about Trump and his associates. The White House has faced criticism over Trump's tweets and comments on the case.

END OF SUMMARY.
