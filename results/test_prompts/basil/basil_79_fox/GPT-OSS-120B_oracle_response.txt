SUMMARY: President Trump denounced the criminal case against former national security adviser Michael Flynn as “very unfair,” arguing that both he and Hillary Clinton **lied** to the FBI but only Flynn suffered consequences. Trump claimed Clinton’s numerous **lies** during her FBI interview resulted in no punishment, while Flynn’s false statements **ruined** his life and were **destroyed** by investigators. He repeated the charge of a **double standard**, saying “nothing happened” to Clinton but Flynn was **ruined**. The president’s weekend tweet about firing Flynn for lying sparked concerns of possible **obstruction** and **lying** about his knowledge of the investigation. Legal analysts warned that the tweet could expose Trump to **obstruction** charges, while his lawyer John Dowd said he merely drafted the message. Senate leaders, including Sen. Lindsey Graham and Sen. Dianne Feinstein, cautioned that Trump’s public **actions** and comments could be seen as **putting** together a case of **obstruction of justice**. Flynn has now **cooperated** with Special Counsel Mueller, leaving open what he may reveal about Trump’s **actions**.
