SUMMARY:
President Trump denounced the criminal case against former national security adviser Michael Flynn as “very unfair,” accusing the Justice Department of applying a double standard. He claimed both he and Hillary Clinton “lied” to the FBI, yet only Flynn was punished, suggesting a cheating of the rules that should apply equally to all. Trump’s remarks, made while departing for a policy event in Utah, echoed a weekend tweet questioning whether a “double standard” existed. Legal experts warned that his public comments and a controversial tweet about firing Flynn could implicate him in obstruction of justice, highlighting the seriousness of the alleged unfair treatment. Senate Republicans cautioned the president against commenting on ongoing investigations, while Democrats pointed to a growing obstruction‑of‑justice case. The framing underscores a perceived breach of fairness: Flynn’s guilty plea and sentencing are portrayed as a punitive outlier compared with Clinton’s lack of consequences, casting the administration’s actions as cheating the principle of equal justice. END OF SUMMARY.
