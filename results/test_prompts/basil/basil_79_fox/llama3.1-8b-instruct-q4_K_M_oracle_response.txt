SUMMARY: President Trump claims the criminal case against former national security adviser Michael Flynn is "very unfair," stating that both Flynn and Hillary Clinton "lied" to the FBI, but only Flynn faced consequences. Trump believes Flynn's life was "destroyed" by the investigation, while Clinton's actions were merely "unfairly" overlooked. The president notes that Clinton "lied many times" during her FBI interview in 2016 without facing repercussions, echoing his previous weekend tweet about a "double standard." However, legal experts warn that Trump's tweets may have exposed him to charges of obstruction of justice, as they suggest he was aware of Flynn's lies when he allegedly asked Comey to lay off the investigation. Meanwhile, Sen. Dianne Feinstein says her committee is building a case against Trump for obstruction of justice, citing Flynn's cooperation with Special Counsel Robert Mueller's investigators in the Russia meddling case. END OF SUMMARY.
