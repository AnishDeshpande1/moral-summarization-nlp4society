SUMMARY:

President Trump announced that former Democratic Sen. Joe Lieberman is his leading contender to replace James Comey as FBI director. Lieberman, 75, met with Trump at the White House on Wednesday and has law enforcement experience from his two-term tenure as Connecticut's attorney general. He also ran for vice president in 2000 and became an independent after losing the Democratic primary in 2006.

Trump had previously considered several other candidates, including current acting FBI Director Andrew McCabe and former Oklahoma Gov. Frank Keating. However, Lieberman is now the frontrunner, with some Republican senators calling his nomination "intriguing". Senate Majority Whip John Cornyn praised Lieberman's bipartisan appeal, saying he could get 100 votes in the Senate.

Not everyone is supportive of Lieberman's candidacy, however. Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer argued against a career politician leading the FBI, and Sen. Claire McCaskill called it a "mistake" to nominate him.

END OF SUMMARY.
