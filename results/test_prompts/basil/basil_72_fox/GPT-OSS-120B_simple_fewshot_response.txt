SUMMARY:
President Trump announced he is “very close” to naming a new FBI director and identified former Democratic‑turned‑independent Senator Joe Lieberman as the front‑runner. Lieberman, a former Connecticut attorney general and 2000 vice‑presidential candidate, met with Trump and other candidates, including acting director Andrew McCabe, former Gov. Frank Keating and FBI veteran Richard McFeely. While a handful of Republican senators called the prospect “intriguing” and noted Lieberman’s bipartisan appeal, Democrats warned that selecting a career politician threatens the FBI’s independence and erodes public confidence. Sen. Claire McCaskill called the move a “mistake,” and Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer argued against any partisan figure heading the bureau. The moral framing underscores that the FBI should be led by career law‑enforcement professionals, not by politicians whose partisan histories could compromise the agency’s impartiality.

END OF SUMMARY.
