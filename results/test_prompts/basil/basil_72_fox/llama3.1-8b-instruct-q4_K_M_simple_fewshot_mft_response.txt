SUMMARY:

President Trump is close to naming a new FBI director, with former Democratic Sen. Joe Lieberman as his top choice. Lieberman's background in law enforcement dates back to his tenure as Connecticut's attorney general from 1983-1989. However, some Democrats are raising concerns about Lieberman's nomination, citing his history of switching parties and endorsing Republican candidates, including John McCain in the 2008 presidential campaign. Sen. Claire McCaskill called the idea a "mistake," while Senate Majority Whip John Cornyn praised Lieberman as someone who could get 100 votes in the Senate. The loyalty and betrayal concerns surrounding Lieberman's nomination are evident, with some Democrats questioning his commitment to their party. Despite these reservations, Trump appears committed to selecting Lieberman as the new FBI director.
