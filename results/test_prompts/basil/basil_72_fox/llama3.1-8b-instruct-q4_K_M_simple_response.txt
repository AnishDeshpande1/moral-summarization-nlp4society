SUMMARY:

President Trump is close to naming a new FBI director, with former Democratic Sen. Joe Lieberman as the leading contender. Lieberman, who served in the Senate from 1989-2013 and was a vice presidential candidate on Al Gore's ticket, met with Trump at the White House on Wednesday. Despite being a Democrat-turned-independent, Lieberman has expressed support for Republican candidates, including John McCain and Hillary Clinton. Some Republicans view him as an intriguing choice, while Democrats see it as a mistake.

Senate Majority Whip John Cornyn praised Lieberman's ability to get bipartisan support in the Senate, but not everyone is convinced. Sen. Claire McCaskill called the idea "a mistake," and Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer expressed concern about appointing a career politician to lead the FBI. The choice of Lieberman as FBI director has raised questions about partisanship and the politicization of the agency.

END OF SUMMARY.
