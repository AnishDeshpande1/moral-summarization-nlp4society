SUMMARY: President Trump announced he is “very close” to naming a new FBI director to replace James Comey, with former Democratic Senator Joe Lieberman emerging as the leading candidate. Lieberman, 75, met with Trump at the White House and has a background as Connecticut’s attorney general (1983‑1989) and a long Senate career, during which he switched from Democrat to independent and endorsed both John McCain and Hillary Clinton in past elections. Trump also consulted acting FBI Director Andrew McCabe, former Oklahoma Governor Frank Keating, and FBI official Richard McFeely. Republicans see Lieberman as a potentially bipartisan choice; Senate Majority Whip John Cornyn noted he might secure unanimous Senate support, while Senator Claire McCaskill and Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer expressed reservations about a career politician leading the FBI. Cornyn, previously on the shortlist, withdrew his own candidacy. END OF SUMMARY.
