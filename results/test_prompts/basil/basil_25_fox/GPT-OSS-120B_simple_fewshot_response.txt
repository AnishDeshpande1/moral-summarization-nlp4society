SUMMARY:
Paul Ryan went on the offensive, using a Fox News interview and a new TV ad to cast President Obama’s health‑care law as a “piggy‑bank raid” on Medicare, while portraying the Romney‑Ryan budget as a plan that will “save, protect and strengthen” the entitlement for current and near‑retirees. The ad warns seniors that Obama has cut $716 billion from Medicare to pay for Obamacare. The Obama campaign slammed the ad as “dishonest and hypocritical,” pointing out that Ryan’s own budget also relies on Medicare savings and would shift future retirees to voucher‑style payments, effectively ending the guaranteed benefits they enjoy today. Both sides selectively frame the other’s proposals to appear either noble protectors of seniors or ruthless budget cutters. The exchange underscores a moral battle over honesty and responsibility for Medicare’s future, with Republicans claiming they are defending seniors and Democrats accusing them of the very cuts they denounce. END OF SUMMARY.
