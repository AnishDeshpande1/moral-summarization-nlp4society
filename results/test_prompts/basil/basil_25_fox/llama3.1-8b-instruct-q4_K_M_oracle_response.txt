SUMMARY:

Paul Ryan, the Republican vice-presidential candidate, is defending his Medicare plan against criticism from Democrats, who claim it would end the entitlement program as we know it. In an interview with Fox News and a new TV ad, Ryan argued that President Obama's health care law has damaged Medicare for current seniors by cutting $716 billion from the program to finance its own costs. He said the "Romney-Ryan" plan would preserve Medicare by restoring those cuts and preventing what he called a raid on the program. The Obama campaign disputed this argument, calling Ryan's plan dishonest and hypocritical because it also proposes significant changes to Medicare that would slow the rate of benefit growth for seniors. Ryan claimed his plan is necessary to save Medicare from itself and prevent its eventual collapse due to unsustainable costs. He said the Obama administration's cuts are a form of fraud that hurts the very people the program is meant to help.

END OF SUMMARY.
