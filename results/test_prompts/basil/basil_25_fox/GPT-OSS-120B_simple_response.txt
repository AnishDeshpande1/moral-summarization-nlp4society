SUMMARY: In a heated moral showdown over Medicare, Paul Ryan defended the Romney‑Ryan ticket as the side “saving” and “protecting” the cherished entitlement, accusing President Obama of turning it into a “piggy bank” to fund Obamacare. Ryan’s Fox News interview and a new TV ad portray the GOP plan as a promise to keep benefits for current and near‑retirement seniors while modestly reforming future payments, casting Obama’s health law as a ruthless raid on seniors’ savings. The Obama campaign fired back, labeling Ryan’s ad “dishonest and hypocritical,” warning that the Republican budget would ultimately replace guaranteed Medicare benefits with vouchers, effectively ending the program as people know it. Both camps selectively framed the other’s proposals, each claiming the moral high ground: Republicans as guardians of Medicare, Democrats as defenders of seniors against covert cuts. The exchange also highlighted broader moral accusations—Ryan blamed Obama for job‑killing borrowing and “failure,” while Obama dismissed the Republican agenda as “wrong.” The dispute underscores a classic political morality play: who truly protects the vulnerable and who is willing to sacrifice them for partisan goals. END OF SUMMARY.
