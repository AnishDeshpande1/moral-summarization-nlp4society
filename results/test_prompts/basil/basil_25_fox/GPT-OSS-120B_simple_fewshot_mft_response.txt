SUMMARY:
Paul Ryan went on the offensive, using a Fox News interview and a new TV ad to portray the Obama administration as subverting authority and harming seniors by “raiding” Medicare like a “piggy bank.” He claimed the Romney‑Ryan plan will **protect** and **strengthen** Medicare for current and near‑retirees, presenting the GOP as the rightful authority defending a cherished entitlement. The Obama campaign countered, labeling Ryan’s ad “dishonest and hypocritical” and accusing the Republican budget of **cheating** seniors by delivering the same savings that would erode guaranteed benefits, effectively ending Medicare as it exists. Both sides selectively frame the debate: Republicans emphasize care for seniors and fairness in preserving benefits, while Democrats stress the harm of Republican cuts and the unfairness of misleading claims. The clash underscores a broader struggle over who holds legitimate authority over health‑care policy, who is caring for vulnerable Americans, and who is being fair or deceptive in the fight over Medicare’s future. END OF SUMMARY.
