SUMMARY:

Paul Ryan responded to criticism of his Medicare plan in an interview with Fox News, claiming that President Obama's health care law treats the program like a "piggy bank" by cutting $716 billion from it. Ryan argued that his own plan would preserve Medicare for current seniors and those retiring soon, while making changes for those retiring a decade from now. The Romney campaign released a TV ad accusing Obama of cutting Medicare to pay for his health care law.

The Obama campaign disputed the claims, calling the new Romney-Ryan ad "dishonest and hypocritical" because Ryan's own proposals would end Medicare as we know it. Both sides are selectively describing each other's plans, with Ryan saying his proposal is to preserve Medicare, not end it. The debate over Medicare is likely to continue as a key issue in the election.

END OF SUMMARY.
