SUMMARY:  
In his first solo interview since being named Mitt Romney’s running mate, Paul Ryan defended the Republican health‑care plan, claiming it protects Medicare while President Obama’s Affordable Care Act “raids” the program like a piggy bank. Ryan highlighted a new TV ad that accuses Obama of cutting $716 billion from Medicare to fund Obamacare, and asserted that the GOP proposal would preserve benefits for current and near‑retirement seniors, reserving changes only for those retiring a decade away. Obama’s campaign responded, calling the ad “dishonest and hypocritical,” and warned that the Ryan‑Romney budget would replace guaranteed Medicare benefits with vouchers. Both sides selectively framed each other’s proposals. Ryan also attacked Obama’s job record and borrowing, and recounted how he and Romney built rapport during a Wisconsin campaign tour. He mentioned his early interest in economics sparked by Ayn Rand’s *Atlas Shrugged*. Obama, speaking in Iowa, praised Ryan’s character but dismissed his and Romney’s tax and health‑care policies as wrong.  

END OF SUMMARY.
