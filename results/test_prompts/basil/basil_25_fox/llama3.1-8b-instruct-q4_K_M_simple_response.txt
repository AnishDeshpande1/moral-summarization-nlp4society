SUMMARY:

House Budget Committee Chairman Paul Ryan responded to criticism over his Medicare plan in an interview with Fox News, claiming that President Obama's health care law treats the program like a "piggy bank" and that the Romney-Ryan plan preserves it. Ryan argued that the Obama administration is damaging Medicare for current seniors by cutting $716 billion from the program to pay for the Affordable Care Act. The Romney campaign released an ad accusing Obama of raiding Medicare, while the Obama campaign called the new ad "dishonest and hypocritical." Both sides are being selective in how they describe each other's plans, with Ryan arguing that his proposal would strengthen Medicare by giving seniors government payments to purchase private plans or stay within the current system.

Ryan also criticized Obama for his economic policies, saying that his heavy borrowing is dragging down growth. The Romney campaign has been touting their plan as a way to create jobs and save the country from a debt crisis. END OF SUMMARY.
