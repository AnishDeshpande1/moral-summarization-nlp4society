SUMMARY:

Representative Paul Ryan defended his Medicare plan on Fox News, claiming that President Obama's health care law treats the program like a "piggy bank" to finance Obamacare. Ryan stated that the Romney-Ryan plan preserves Medicare for current seniors and those retiring soon, while Obama's plan damages it. The Obama campaign disputes this argument, calling the new Romney-Ryan ad "dishonest and hypocritical." Both sides are being selective in how they describe each other's plans, with Ryan acknowledging that his own plan achieves "savings" for Medicare in the long run.

The moral framing of this issue is centered on Authority/Subversion, as both parties argue over who has the right to make decisions about Medicare. The Care/Harm dimension is also relevant, as the fate of current seniors and those retiring soon hangs in the balance. Additionally, Fairness/Cheating plays a role, with both sides accusing each other of being dishonest or hypocritical in their descriptions of each other's plans.

END OF SUMMARY.
