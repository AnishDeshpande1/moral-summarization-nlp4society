SUMMARY:
Paul Ryan defended his Medicare plan in an interview with Fox News, claiming that President Obama's health care law treats Medicare like a "piggy bank" and that the Romney-Ryan plan preserves it. Ryan argued that their plan would strengthen Medicare by giving seniors government payments to purchase private plans or stay within the current system, while Obama's plan cuts Medicare benefits. However, Democrats dispute this argument, pointing out that Ryan's own proposals for Medicare would slow the rate of benefit growth and potentially leave beneficiaries with only a voucher in place of guaranteed benefits. Both sides are being selective in how they describe each other's plans, with Ryan accusing Obama of "raiding" Medicare to pay for ObamaCare and Obama calling Ryan's plan a threat to Medicare as we know it.
