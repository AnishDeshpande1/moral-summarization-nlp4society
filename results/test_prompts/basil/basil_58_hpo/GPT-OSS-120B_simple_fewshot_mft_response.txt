SUMMARY:
New Jersey Governor Chris Christie announced his 2016 presidential bid, positioning himself as an authority willing to challenge a broken political hierarchy. He condemned both parties for “cheating” Americans with partisan gridlock, arguing that the nation’s tradition of compromise has been betrayed and that voters deserve fair, effective governance. Christie framed his candidacy as a loyal defense of the people, citing his leadership during Hurricane Sandy and his work with the Republican Governors Association as proof of his commitment to the public good. At the same time, he acknowledged past controversies—most notably the Bridgegate scandal—as instances where subordinates abused authority and betrayed public trust, urging a return to honest, accountable leadership. By calling for “courage” to stand up against a corrupt establishment, Christie casts his run as a moral corrective that restores rightful authority, ensures fairness, and reaffirms loyalty to the nation’s core values.  

END OF SUMMARY.
