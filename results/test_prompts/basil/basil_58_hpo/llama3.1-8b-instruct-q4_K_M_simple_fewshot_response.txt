New Jersey Governor Chris Christie announced his candidacy for President in 2016, citing his desire to fight for the American people. In a speech at his alma mater, Christie criticized lawmakers in Washington and both parties for failing to compromise and lead the country effectively. He emphasized the need for courage and leadership in politics, referencing his own experience as governor of New Jersey during Hurricane Sandy. Christie's favorability ratings have been mixed due to controversies such as Bridgegate and his reversal on Common Core education standards. Despite these challenges, Christie remains a contender among Republican presidential candidates. His background as a tough-talking former federal prosecutor and his leadership through the storm damage has garnered him attention. However, voters may be wary of his reputation for bullying and reversing positions.
