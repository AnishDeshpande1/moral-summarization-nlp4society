SUMMARY:
New Jersey Governor Chris Christie announced his candidacy for President in 2016, citing his desire to fight for the American people. He criticized both parties for failing to deliver on their promises and creating an atmosphere of anxiety among Americans. Christie praised his home state's resilience after Hurricane Sandy and highlighted his leadership during that time. He also acknowledged past controversies, including the "Bridgegate" scandal and reversing his position on Common Core education standards.

Christie's favorability ratings have been affected by these issues, but he remains a viable candidate in the GOP field. His announcement comes as he seeks to rebrand himself and appeal to a wider audience. Critics argue that Christie's tough-talking style may alienate some voters, while supporters see him as a straight shooter who is willing to take on the establishment.

Christie's presidential bid is likely to be marked by his efforts to balance his reputation for being blunt with his need to attract broad support. He will need to navigate these challenges in order to succeed in the primaries and general election.
