SUMMARY:  
New Jersey Gov. Chris Christie (R) announced his 2016 presidential bid, telling supporters he is “ready to **fight** for the American people.”  In a speech at his alma mater, he praised his home state and claimed his experience as governor—especially his **leadership** during Hurricane Sandy—has **led** him to the White House race.  Christie castigated Washington, saying both parties have **failed** the nation and turned compromise into a “dirty word,” urging citizens to demand courage and **lead** change.  He highlighted his earlier **responsiveness** to disaster relief and his role as chairman of the Republican Governors Association, where he helped other GOP governors win in 2014.  Yet his reputation was tarnished by the **Bridgegate** **plot** that used lane closures on the George Washington Bridge to punish a political rival, a scandal that damaged his favorability.  Despite the controversy and a recent flip‑flop on Common Core, polls place him in the middle of the GOP field.
