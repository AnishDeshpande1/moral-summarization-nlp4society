SUMMARY: Chris Christie announced his presidential bid, stating he's ready to "fight for the people of the United States of America." He criticized lawmakers in Washington, claiming both parties have failed the country by prioritizing partisan interests over compromise. Christie emphasized the need for leaders who can stand up and say "enough" to gridlock. As governor, he was praised for his responsiveness and leadership during Hurricane Sandy, a moment that showcased his ability to put politics aside and lead. However, his reputation suffered a blow with the Bridgegate scandal, which involved a plot by members of his administration to cause traffic jams as revenge against a mayor who didn't support Christie's re-election. Despite this controversy, Christie remains committed to leading and has vowed to restore America's values of compromise and productivity.

END OF SUMMARY
