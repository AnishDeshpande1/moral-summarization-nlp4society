SUMMARY:
New Jersey Governor Chris Christie announced his 2016 presidential bid, positioning himself as a “fighter” for the American people and castigating both parties for a lack of compromise. While he praised his home state and highlighted his leadership during Hurricane Sandy, the announcement glosses over the controversies that have tarnished his reputation. Christie’s blunt style and reputation for political bullying resurfaced with the lingering “Bridgegate” scandal, in which members of his administration engineered traffic jams as retaliation against a mayor who refused to endorse him. His recent flip‑flop on Common Core standards and a history of putting personal ambition above party unity further suggest that his campaign is driven more by self‑interest than genuine reform. By framing the bipartisan gridlock as a moral failing of Washington, Christie attempts to cast himself as the antidote, yet the same tactics that once earned him favorability now raise questions about his integrity and motives.

END OF SUMMARY.
