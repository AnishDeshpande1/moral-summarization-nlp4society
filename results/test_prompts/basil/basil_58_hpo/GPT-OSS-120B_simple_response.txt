SUMMARY:
New Jersey Governor Chris Christie (R) announced his 2016 presidential bid, framing his candidacy as a moral crusade against a stagnant, partisan Washington. Speaking at his alma mater, Christie declared he is “ready to fight for the people of the United States” and condemned both parties for betraying America’s tradition of compromise, urging citizens to summon the courage to say “enough.” He praised his governorship—particularly his leadership during Hurricane Sandy—and positioned his state‑level service as proof of his commitment to the public good. Christie’s blunt, confrontational style, which he admits makes him “not everybody’s cup of tea,” is presented as a necessary honesty in a political climate he portrays as anxiety‑inducing and unproductive. The announcement follows a career marked by high favorability that was later tarnished by the “Bridgegate” scandal and a recent reversal on Common Core, yet he remains a central figure among GOP hopefuls. By casting his run as a moral stand for accountability and decisive leadership, Christie seeks to rally voters who feel abandoned by partisan gridlock.

END OF SUMMARY.
