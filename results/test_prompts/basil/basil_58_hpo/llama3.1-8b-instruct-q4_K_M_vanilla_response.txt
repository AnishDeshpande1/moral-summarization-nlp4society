SUMMARY:
New Jersey Gov. Chris Christie announced his candidacy for President in 2016, citing his experience as governor and his desire to fight for the American people. In his public announcement, Christie praised his home state and criticized lawmakers in Washington for their lack of productivity. He also took aim at both parties, saying that compromise is a "dirty word" and that Americans are suffering from anxiety due to Congress's inability to work together.

Christie has been preparing for a potential White House run since 2013, when he was elected chairman of the Republican Governors Association. Despite facing criticism for his role in the George Washington Bridge lane-closing scandal (known as "Bridgegate") and his reversal on Common Core education standards, Christie remains a viable candidate in the GOP primary.

According to recent polls, Christie is in the middle of the pack of potential candidates. His favorability rating among voters has been high, particularly after he led New Jersey through the aftermath of Hurricane Sandy in 2012.

END OF SUMMARY.
