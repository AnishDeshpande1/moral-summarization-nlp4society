SUMMARY: New Jersey Governor Chris Christie announced his candidacy for the 2016 Republican presidential nomination, making the public declaration at his alma mater, Livingston High School. In his speech, Christie criticized both parties and President Obama for congressional gridlock, urging Americans to demand change. The former federal prosecutor, who gained national attention for his leadership after Hurricane Sandy and his role as chairman of the Republican Governors Association, has a mixed record: praised for crisis management but tarnished by the 2014 “Bridgegate” scandal and recent flip‑flopping on Common Core standards. Despite these controversies, polls place him in the middle of the GOP field. Christie emphasized his readiness to fight for the country and his willingness to be a blunt, decisive leader. END OF SUMMARY.
