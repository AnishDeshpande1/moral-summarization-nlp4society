SUMMARY:
All nine Republican members of the House Intelligence Committee have demanded that Rep. Adam Schiff resign as chairman, accusing him of perpetuating a “demonstrably false” narrative that the Trump campaign colluded with Russia despite the Mueller report’s finding of no direct conspiracy. The GOP letter slammed Schiff for leading a “well‑orchestrated media campaign” and for allegedly leaking information, calling his continued claims “alarming” and “incompatible” with his oversight duties. President Trump echoed the demand, tweeting that Schiff should be forced out of Congress for “unlawfully lying and leaking.” Schiff, however, doubled down, insisting that “undoubtedly there is collusion” and labeling any failure to pursue the issue as immoral, unethical, unpatriotic, and corrupt. Democrats, meanwhile, continue to press for the full Mueller report’s release, noting that while the report does not exonerate the president, it also does not conclude he committed a crime. The clash underscores a partisan battle over the truth of the Russia investigation and the proper role of congressional oversight.

END OF SUMMARY.
