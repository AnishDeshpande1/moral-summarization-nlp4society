SUMMARY:  
All nine Republican members of the House Intelligence Committee have demanded that Democratic Chairman Rep. Adam Schiff resign, accusing him of perpetuating a “demonstrably false” Russia‑collusion narrative despite the Mueller report’s failure to find a criminal conspiracy by President Trump. In a Thursday letter, the GOP members charged Schiff with leading a “well‑orchestrated media campaign” and leaking information, calling his continued claims “alarming.” President Trump echoed the demand, tweeting that Schiff should be forced out of Congress for “unlawfully lying and leaking.” Schiff, however, maintains that “undoubtedly there is collusion,” and insists the investigation must continue to determine whether the president or his associates are compromised by a hostile foreign power. He framed his stance in moral terms, describing the refusal to pursue the issue as “immoral, unethical, unpatriotic, corrupt and evidence of collusion.” Democrats, while urging the Justice Department to release the full Mueller report, have defended Schiff’s right to oversight. The clash centers on competing moral judgments about truth‑telling, accountability, and loyalty to the nation.  
END OF SUMMARY.
