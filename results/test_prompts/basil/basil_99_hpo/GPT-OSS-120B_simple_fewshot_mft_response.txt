SUMMARY:
All nine Republican members of the House Intelligence Committee have demanded that Rep. Adam Schiff resign as chairman, accusing him of subverting the Committee’s authority by pushing a “demonstrably false” narrative of Trump‑Russia collusion. In their letter they claim Schiff’s continued promotion of the claim betrays the public trust and the loyalty owed to the institution, framing his actions as a cheat on the truth and a betrayal of his duty to provide impartial oversight. President Trump echoed the charge, tweeting that Schiff is “unlawfully lying and leaking” and should be forced out of Congress entirely. While Mueller’s report did not conclude the president committed a crime, Republicans argue it refutes Schiff’s assertions, portraying his insistence on further investigation as a disloyal, unethical challenge to legitimate authority. Schiff, however, insists the issue is moral, patriotic, and essential, accusing his opponents of corruptly undermining accountability. The clash highlights a partisan battle over who holds rightful authority, who is being fair or cheating the public, and where loyalty to the nation and to institutional norms truly lies.  
END OF SUMMARY.
