SUMMARY:
President Trump attacked Facebook and Twitter for what he described as unfair, discriminatory treatment of Republican and conservative voices, claiming the platforms are “closing down” right‑leaning opinions while ignoring violations by left‑wing users. He warned that “too many voices are being destroyed, some good & some bad,” and urged that all participants be allowed to speak so the public can judge the content themselves. The tweet echoed earlier complaints about “shadow banning” of top GOP figures such as Ronna McDaniel, which Twitter later blamed on an algorithmic glitch. Both companies defended their ranking practices as neutral and denied political bias, while a Facebook executive apologized for mishandling pro‑Trump personalities Diamond and Silk. Trump also condemned censorship as “dangerous,” juxtaposing it with his criticism of “fake news” networks CNN and MSNBC, which he says he tolerates with “a grain of salt.” The overall framing presents the tech giants’ actions as a breach of fairness—cheating conservatives out of equal treatment and free expression. END OF SUMMARY.
