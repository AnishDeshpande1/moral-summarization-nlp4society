SUMMARY:

President Trump accused social media tech giants Facebook and Twitter of "discriminating" against Republicans and conservatives, claiming that too many voices are being destroyed. He stated that his administration would not allow this to happen and called for equal treatment on the platforms. This comes after allegations of censorship by both companies, with some users reporting that their accounts were given limited visibility or even shadow banned.

Twitter denied the allegations, stating that it does not discriminate based on political viewpoints and that its algorithmic glitch was fixed last month. Facebook apologized for mistreating pro-Trump personalities Diamond and Silk, but Trump remains critical of social media censorship. He noted that while he disapproves of CNN and MSNBC's content, he does not demand that it be taken off the air.

The controversy has been ongoing for years, with conservatives claiming that tech giants are biased against them. Trump's administration has promised to address the issue, but no concrete actions have been announced yet. END OF SUMMARY.
