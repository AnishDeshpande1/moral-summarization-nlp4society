SUMMARY:
President Trump has accused social media tech giants Facebook and Twitter of discriminating against Republicans and conservatives, claiming that "too many voices are being destroyed". He argues that these platforms are censoring conservative accounts while ignoring liberal ones, calling this a "dangerous thing". The controversy escalated after Vice News reported that top Republican accounts were being given limited visibility on Twitter. Both companies have denied allegations of bias, with Twitter stating that its ranking models aim to make tweets relevant and timely, but may not always show content from certain accounts. Facebook has also apologized for mishandling communications with pro-Trump personalities Diamond and Silk.

Trump's comments come after his administration has been criticized for its handling of social media regulation. He suggests that the platforms should allow all voices to participate, regardless of their quality or ideology. The issue has sparked debate about free speech online and the role of tech giants in regulating content. Trump has used Twitter extensively during his presidency, and his campaign was credited with using Facebook effectively in 2016.

END OF SUMMARY.
