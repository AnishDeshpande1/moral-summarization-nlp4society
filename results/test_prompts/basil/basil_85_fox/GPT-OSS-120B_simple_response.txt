SUMMARY: President Trump condemned social‑media giants for “discriminating” against Republicans, warning that “too many voices are being destroyed” and pledging his administration will prevent such censorship. He framed the issue as a moral fight for free expression, insisting that all viewpoints—good or bad—must be allowed to compete in the public square. Trump cited alleged “shadow‑banning” of GOP figures like Ronna McDaniel and the mishandling of pro‑Trump personalities Diamond and Silk, while accusing platforms of silencing right‑wing opinions yet tolerating left‑wing media such as CNN and MSNBC. Twitter and Facebook responded by denying political bias, attributing visibility problems to algorithmic glitches and ranking practices, and apologizing for communication errors. Trump portrayed censorship as a dangerous threat to democratic discourse, contrasting it with his personal tolerance of “fake news” on cable news, which he says he simply ignores. END OF SUMMARY.
