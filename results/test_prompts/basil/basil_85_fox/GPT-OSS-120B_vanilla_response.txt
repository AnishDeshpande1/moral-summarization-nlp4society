SUMMARY: President Trump criticized major social‑media platforms, accusing them of discriminating against Republicans and conservatives by “destroying” many voices. In a lengthy Twitter post, he warned that his administration would not tolerate such censorship and urged that all viewpoints, good or bad, be allowed. Trump’s claims echo longstanding conservative complaints of “shadow banning,” a term he has previously called illegal. Recent controversy intensified after Vice News reported limited visibility for top GOP accounts on Twitter, which the company attributed to a temporary algorithmic glitch and denied any political bias. Facebook also faced scrutiny after mishandling pro‑Trump personalities Diamond and Silk, prompting an apology from a senior executive but no admission of systemic bias. Trump contrasted social‑media censorship with what he termed “fake” coverage by CNN and MSNBC, stating he does not demand their removal. He concluded that suppressing opinions is dangerous and must be stopped. END OF SUMMARY.
