SUMMARY:
President Trump used Twitter to condemn Facebook and Twitter for “discriminating” against Republicans, claiming that “too many voices are being destroyed” and urging that all viewpoints, “good & bad,” be allowed to circulate. He framed the tech firms’ moderation policies as a dangerous form of censorship that silences conservative voices while ignoring violations by left‑wing users. The article notes that the companies deny bias, describing the issue as an algorithmic glitch or a standard ranking system, and cites recent apologies from Facebook executives over mishandling pro‑Trump personalities. Trump also attacked mainstream media, labeling CNN and MSNBC as “fake news” yet saying he does not demand their removal. The moral framing presents Trump’s stance as a defense of free speech against alleged partisan suppression, while questioning the responsibility of social‑media platforms to curb misinformation and the inconsistency of demanding censorship only for opposing viewpoints.
