SUMMARY:

President Trump has vowed to end birthright citizenship, claiming it costs the country billions of dollars and is unfair to citizens. He argues that the 14th Amendment does not cover birthright citizenship, citing the phrase "subject to the jurisdiction thereof." Trump also referenced a 1993 statement by former Senate Democratic Leader Harry Reid, who said that no sane country would award citizenship to children of illegal immigrants. However, Reid later apologized and reversed his stance. Critics of birthright citizenship argue that it encourages illegal immigration, while many scholars believe it would take a constitutional amendment to change the policy. Republicans are divided on the issue, with some opposing the use of an executive order to impose such a significant change. Democrats have united in condemning Trump's move, arguing that it exceeds his authority.

END OF SUMMARY.
