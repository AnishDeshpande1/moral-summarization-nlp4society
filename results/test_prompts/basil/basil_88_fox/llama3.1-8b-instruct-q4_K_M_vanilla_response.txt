SUMMARY:
President Trump announced on Wednesday that he plans to end birthright citizenship, which grants US citizenship to children born in the country to non-citizen or illegal immigrant parents. He claimed it would be ended "one way or the other" and cited costs and fairness as reasons. The policy has been a contentious issue for years, with some arguing it encourages illegal immigration. Trump's plan is to use an executive order, but critics point out that this would likely face challenges in court and could even violate the 14th Amendment.

Speaker Paul Ryan said the amendment is "pretty clear" and that an executive order cannot end birthright citizenship. Senate Judiciary Chairman Chuck Grassley also expressed skepticism about the plan. Democrats have universally condemned Trump's move, with House Minority Leader Nancy Pelosi calling it "just more of the same" of his previous immigration policies. The issue may ultimately be decided by the Supreme Court.

END OF SUMMARY.
