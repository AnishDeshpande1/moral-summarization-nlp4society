SUMMARY: 
President Trump vowed to end birthright citizenship, which grants US citizenship to children born in the country of non-citizen or illegal immigrant parents. He cited the cost to the country and what he considers unfair treatment of citizens as reasons for ending the policy. The move has sparked disagreement among Republicans and condemnation from Democrats.

Trump suggested using an executive order to end birthright citizenship, but this was met with skepticism by some lawmakers who pointed out that such a significant change would require a constitutional amendment. Speaker Paul Ryan said the 14th Amendment is "pretty clear" and cannot be changed by executive order alone.

The debate has been ongoing for years, with critics arguing that the policy encourages illegal immigration. However, others argue that it is a long-standing practice in the US and other countries like Canada and Latin American nations.

Trump's move was seen as an attempt to restrict immigration and build on his "America First" agenda. Democrats were united in their condemnation of the proposal, with House Minority Leader Nancy Pelosi calling it another example of Trump's disregard for authority.

END OF SUMMARY.
