SUMMARY: President Trump declared that “so‑called birthright citizenship” – which he claims “costs our Country billions of dollars and is very unfair to our citizens” – will be ended “one way or the other.” He argues the 14th Amendment does not apply because of the phrase “subject to the jurisdiction thereof,” and he invoked former Senate Democrat Harry Reid’s 1993 remarks that granting citizenship to children of illegal immigrants is a “reward” for breaking the law. Trump’s push, first aired in an Axios interview, frames the policy as a moral injustice that encourages illegal entry and imposes a burden on law‑abiding Americans. While Republicans such as Paul Ryan and Chuck Grassley warn that an executive order cannot overturn the Constitution and call for congressional action, others like Lindsey Graham pledge legislation, calling birthright citizenship a “magnet for illegal immigration.” Democrats uniformly condemn the move as an overreach, with Nancy Pelosi dismissing Trump’s claims as unrelated to his authority. The debate is presented as a clash between protecting the nation’s moral and fiscal integrity versus preserving a constitutional right.  

END OF SUMMARY.
