SUMMARY:

President Trump has vowed to end birthright citizenship, a policy that he claims is "unfair" to US citizens and costs the country "billions of dollars". He believes that the 14th Amendment does not cover this policy, citing the phrase "subject to the jurisdiction thereof". Trump's push to end birthright citizenship has sparked disagreement among Republicans and condemnation from Democrats. He argues that the policy encourages "crime" and is a "magnet for illegal immigration". Trump initially suggested using an executive order to end the policy, but later shifted his stance, acknowledging that the matter "will be settled by the United States Supreme Court". Democrats have united in condemning Trump's move, with House Minority Leader Nancy Pelosi stating that his remarks have "no relationship to what his authority is". The debate surrounding birthright citizenship has long been a contentious issue in the US, with critics arguing that it encourages "illegal immigrants" to "come" to the country and claim residency.
