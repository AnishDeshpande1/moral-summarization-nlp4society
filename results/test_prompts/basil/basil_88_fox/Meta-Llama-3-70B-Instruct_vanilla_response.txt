SUMMARY:

President Trump has vowed to end birthright citizenship, claiming it costs the country billions of dollars and is unfair to citizens. He tweeted that the 14th Amendment does not cover birthright citizenship due to the phrase "subject to the jurisdiction thereof." Trump cited a 1993 comment by former Senate Democratic Leader Harry Reid, who said that no sane country would award citizenship to children of illegal immigrants. Trump wants to use an executive order to end the practice, but many scholars argue it would require a constitutional amendment. Republicans are divided on the issue, with some opposing the use of an executive order, while Democrats are united in their condemnation of Trump's move. The issue is contentious, with critics arguing it encourages illegal immigration, while others argue it is a constitutional right.

END OF SUMMARY.
