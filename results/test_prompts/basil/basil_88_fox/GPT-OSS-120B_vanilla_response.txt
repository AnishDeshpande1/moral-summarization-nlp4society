SUMMARY: President Donald Trump pledged to end “birthright citizenship,” claiming it is not protected by the 14th Amendment and saying it costs the nation billions. He referenced 1993 remarks by former Senate Democratic Leader Harry Reid, who once argued against granting citizenship to children of illegal immigrants, though Reid later apologized and reversed his stance. Trump announced plans to use an executive order to terminate the practice, sparking disagreement among Republicans and condemnation from Democrats. GOP leaders such as Speaker Paul Ryan and Senate Judiciary Chairman Chuck Grassley argued that the Constitution’s language is clear and that only Congress—or the Supreme Court—should decide the issue, while Sen. Lindsey Graham promised legislation. Democrats, including Nancy Pelosi, denounced Trump’s proposal as beyond his authority. Legal scholars note that overturning birthright citizenship would likely require a constitutional amendment or a Supreme Court ruling. END OF SUMMARY.
