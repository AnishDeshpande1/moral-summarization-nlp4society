SUMMARY: President Trump pledged that “so‑called birthright citizenship” will be **ended** “one way or the other,” claiming the policy is **unfair** and a **magnet for illegal immigration** that costs the country billions and fuels **crime**. He argued the 14th Amendment does not cover children **entering** the U.S. illegally because they are not “subject to the jurisdiction thereof,” and cited former Senate Democratic Leader Harry Reid’s 1993 remarks as proof that the practice rewards illegal **entering** and **coming** to the United States. Trump suggested an executive order could **restrict** the practice, though Republicans such as Speaker Paul Ryan and Sen. Chuck Grassley warned that only Congress or the Supreme Court can **end** it. Sen. Lindsey Graham announced he will be **introducing** legislation to address the issue. Democrats, including Nancy Pelosi, condemned the move as beyond presidential authority. The debate centers on whether the Constitution allows the **coming** of a child born to non‑citizen parents to automatically receive citizenship, or whether the policy should be **ended** to curb illegal **immigration** and associated **crime**. END OF SUMMARY.
