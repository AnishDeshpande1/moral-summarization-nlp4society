SUMMARY:

President Trump has vowed to end birthright citizenship, claiming it costs the country billions of dollars and is unfair to citizens. He tweeted that the policy will be ended "one way or the other," citing the 14th Amendment. Trump's push to end the practice has sparked disagreement among Republicans and condemnation from Democrats. Critics argue that the policy encourages illegal immigration, while many scholars believe it would take a constitutional amendment to change the policy. Republican lawmakers, including Speaker Paul Ryan and Senate Judiciary Chairman Chuck Grassley, have expressed reservations about Trump's plan to use an executive order to impose the change. Democrats, on the other hand, have united in condemning Trump's move, with House Minority Leader Nancy Pelosi saying it has no relationship to his authority. The issue is a contentious one in the US, with many seeking to restrict illegal immigration.

END OF SUMMARY.
