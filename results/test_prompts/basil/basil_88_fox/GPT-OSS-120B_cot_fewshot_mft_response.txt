**SUMMARY:**  
President Trump vowed that “so‑called birthright citizenship” – which he called “unfair” and a “ridiculous” loophole that “costs our country billions” – will be ended “one way or the other.” He argued the policy is not covered by the 14th Amendment because of the phrase “subject to the jurisdiction thereof,” and he accused former Senate leader Harry Reid of promoting “insane” open‑border “stuff” that “rewards” illegal immigrants with citizenship and public benefits. Trump suggested an executive order could end the practice, but Republican leaders warned that such a move would subvert constitutional authority and should be handled by Congress. Senate Judiciary Chairman Chuck Grassley said he will review the proposal, while Sen. Lindsey Graham called birthright citizenship a “magnet for illegal immigration.” Democrats condemned the plan, with Nancy Pelosi calling it an overreach of presidential authority and an attempt to cheat the nation out of fairness. The dispute is expected to be settled by the Supreme Court.
