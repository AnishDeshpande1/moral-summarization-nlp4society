SUMMARY:
President Trump vowed to end “birthright citizenship” – the constitutional right that grants citizenship to anyone born on U.S. soil – claiming it “costs our country billions” and is “unfair” to citizens. He suggested an executive order could achieve this, invoking a disputed interpretation of the 14th Amendment and attacking former Senate Democratic leader Harry Reid for once supporting a ban on the practice. Trump’s rhetoric framed the policy as a “reward” for illegal immigration and a magnet for crime, despite the fact that many other nations, including Canada and several Latin American countries, also grant citizenship by birth. Legal scholars warn that only a constitutional amendment, not an executive order, could overturn the rule, and Republican leaders such as Speaker Paul Ryan and Senator Chuck Grassley warned that Congress, not the president, should handle the issue. Democrats uniformly condemned Trump’s move as an overreach and a distraction from real immigration reform. The episode highlights a morally charged battle over the nation’s founding promise of birthright citizenship and the limits of presidential power.

END OF SUMMARY.
