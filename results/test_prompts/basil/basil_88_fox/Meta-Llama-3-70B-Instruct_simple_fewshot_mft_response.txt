SUMMARY:

President Trump has vowed to end birthright citizenship, claiming it's unfair to US citizens and costs the country billions of dollars. He tweeted that the policy will be ended "one way or the other," citing the 14th Amendment's phrase "subject to the jurisdiction thereof" as justification. Trump's move has sparked disagreement among Republicans and condemnation from Democrats, with many arguing that it would take a constitutional amendment to change the policy. Critics of birthright citizenship claim it encourages illegal immigration, while others argue it's a fundamental right. Trump's attempt to use an executive order to impose such a significant immigration change has been met with skepticism, with lawmakers from both parties questioning its legality. Democrats have united in condemning Trump's move, with House Minority Leader Nancy Pelosi dismissing it as an overreach of authority.

END OF SUMMARY.
