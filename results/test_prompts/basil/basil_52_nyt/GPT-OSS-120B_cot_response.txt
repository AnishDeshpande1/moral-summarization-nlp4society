**SUMMARY:**  
Governor Mike Pence of Indiana faced a fierce backlash after signing the state’s Religious Freedom Restoration Act, a law he says protects religious liberty and freedom from government burden. Critics argue the bill enables discrimination against gays and lesbians, questioning whether tolerance is a two‑way street. Thousands protested at the Statehouse, and businesses—including Salesforce, Angie’s List, and the city of Seattle—announced travel halts and boycotts of Indiana. The NCAA expressed concern about the law’s impact on upcoming events. Pence, surprised by the negative reaction, promised to seek clarification legislation in the General Assembly but reiterated that expanding protections for sexual orientation is not on his agenda. He maintains the intent is to protect genuine religious convictions, not to sanction discrimination.
