SUMMARY:
Indiana Governor Mike Pence, reacting to the nationwide outcry over the state’s newly‑signed Religious Freedom Restoration Act, said he will push legislation to “clarify the intent” of the law and rejected claims that it authorizes discrimination against gay and lesbian people. Pence told the Indianapolis Star and appeared on ABC’s “This Week” insisting the bill protects genuine religious liberty, not bigotry, and that “tolerance is a two‑way street.” He promised to explain the measure to those who “don’t understand it” and to seek any needed amendments in the General Assembly, while refusing to add sexual‑orientation protections to the statute. The law, modeled on earlier federal and state statutes, is intended to shield individuals and businesses from being compelled to act against their sincerely held religious beliefs. Critics and business leaders have responded with protests and travel bans, and the controversy has cast a shadow over upcoming NCAA events in Indianapolis. Pence maintains the legislation is morally correct and essential for safeguarding faith‑based freedom. END OF SUMMARY.
