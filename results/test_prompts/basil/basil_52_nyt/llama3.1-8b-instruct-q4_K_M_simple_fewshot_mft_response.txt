SUMMARY:

Indiana Governor Mike Pence has faced a growing backlash over his signing of the Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which critics argue sanctions discrimination against gays and lesbians. Despite assertions that the law was not intended to discriminate, Pence acknowledged that it could be clarified to address concerns. The governor stated that he supports religious liberty but also wants to "amplify what this bill really is" in the legislative process. Proponents of the law claim it allows Christian vendors to refuse service to same-sex couples without fear of reprisal. However, critics note that the law includes different wording than similar laws in other states and does not protect sexual orientation as a protected class in Indiana. Pence has refused to add such protections, stating they are "not on my agenda." The controversy has led several businesses to halt travel to Indiana and the N.C.A.A. to express concern about the implications of the law.
