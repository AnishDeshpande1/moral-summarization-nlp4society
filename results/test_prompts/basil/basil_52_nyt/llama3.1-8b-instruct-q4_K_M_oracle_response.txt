SUMMARY: Governor Mike Pence of Indiana is facing a growing backlash over the state's Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which critics say sanctions discrimination against gays and lesbians. In response to the controversy, the governor announced that he would seek legislation to clarify the intent of the law, aiming to protect individuals or companies from being forced to take actions that impose a substantial burden on their religious beliefs. However, opponents argue that the law could open the door to wider discrimination, as it does not provide protections for sexual orientation under civil rights law in Indiana. The controversy has led businesses and governments to halt business with the state and city of Indianapolis, including Salesforce.com and Angie's List abandoning deals with the state. Pence defended the law, citing its origins in federal legislation, but refused to add protections for gays and lesbians to his agenda. The debate highlights a clash between competing values of liberty and equality. END OF SUMMARY.
