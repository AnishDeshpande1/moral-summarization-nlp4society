SUMMARY: Governor Mike Pence of Indiana is addressing the backlash over the Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which he signed into law last week. The law aims to protect individuals or companies from being forced to take actions that impose a substantial burden on their religious beliefs. However, critics argue that it sanctioned discrimination against gays and lesbians by allowing businesses to refuse service to same-sex couples. Pence rejected these assertions, saying the law is about protecting tolerance and not about imposing wider discrimination. He announced plans to introduce legislation to clarify the intent of the law, which he claims was misunderstood. Businesses and governments have begun to boycott Indiana in protest, with some even halting travel to the state. Critics point out that Indiana's law includes different wording than other states' versions, potentially opening the door to wider discrimination. Pence defended the law, saying it was not about discrimination, but rather about protecting religious liberty. END OF SUMMARY.
