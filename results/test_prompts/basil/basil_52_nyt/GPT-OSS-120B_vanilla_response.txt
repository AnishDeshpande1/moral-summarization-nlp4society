SUMMARY: Indiana Governor Mike Pence, facing a political firestorm over the state’s newly enacted Religious Freedom Restoration Act, said he will push for legislation to clarify the law’s intent and denied that it permits discrimination against gays and lesbians. Pence expressed surprise at the backlash, which has triggered protests, corporate travel bans, and threats to cancel business deals, as well as concerns from the NCAA about upcoming events in Indianapolis. While affirming his support for religious liberty, he did not specify how the law would affect cases like a Christian florist refusing service to a same‑sex couple, and he rejected adding sexual orientation protections. Critics argue the Indiana version differs from similar statutes in other states and could enable broader discrimination. Pence indicated discussions with legislative leaders to amend the bill but offered no details, maintaining that the law is not about discrimination but about mutual tolerance. END OF SUMMARY.
