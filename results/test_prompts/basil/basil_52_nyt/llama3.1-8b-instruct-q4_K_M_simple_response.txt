SUMMARY:

Indiana Governor Mike Pence is facing backlash over the state's new Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which critics say sanctions discrimination against gays and lesbians. Despite assertions that the law protects individuals or companies from being forced to act against their religious beliefs, protesters gathered at the Statehouse, businesses halted travel to Indiana, and governments announced a ban on city employees traveling to the state for work. Pence has rejected claims of discrimination, saying that the issue is about tolerance being a two-way street. He plans to introduce legislation to clarify the intent of the law, but critics argue that the wording could open the door to wider discrimination. The governor also stated that he does not intend to add protections for gays and lesbians as a protected class in Indiana. Pence's handling of the issue has become a political headache, particularly with his potential presidential bid in 2016.

END OF SUMMARY.
