SUMMARY: Indiana Governor Mike Pence, surprised by the backlash to the state’s Religious Freedom Restoration Act, said he will seek legislation to **clarify** the bill’s **intent** and denied that it **sanctions discrimination** against gays and lesbians. Pence reiterated his support for **religious liberty** and said the law is meant to protect individuals and businesses from a **substantial burden** on their beliefs, not to **ban** service to same‑sex couples. He told the Indianapolis Star he expects a clarifying bill in the General Assembly this week, though he gave few details and did not directly answer whether a Christian florist could **refuse service** to a gay couple. The law has prompted thousands to **protest**, and companies such as Salesforce, Angie’s List and city officials in Seattle have announced a **halt** to travel and a **ban** on using public funds for trips to Indiana, with some **abandon**ing deals with the state. Critics argue the wording **opens** the door to broader **discrimination**, noting that Indiana lacks **protected** status for sexual orientation. Pence said expanding **protections** for gays is not on his **agenda**. END OF SUMMARY.
