SUMMARY:
Indiana Governor Mike Pence has faced backlash over his signing of the Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which critics argue sanctions discrimination against gays and lesbians. Despite assertions that the law protects individuals from being forced to act against their religious beliefs, Pence acknowledged the need for clarification on the law's intent. He plans to introduce legislation this week to address concerns, but stopped short of directly answering whether the law would protect a Christian florist who refused service to a gay couple. The controversy has sparked protests and led several businesses to halt travel to Indiana. As Governor, Pence said he supports religious liberty, but did not indicate a willingness to add protections for gays and lesbians in Indiana's civil rights laws.
