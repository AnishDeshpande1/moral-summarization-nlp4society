SUMMARY:
Indiana Governor Mike Pence, defending his role as the state’s authority, said he will push legislation to clarify the recently enacted Religious Freedom Restoration Act after a wave of criticism that the law could enable discrimination against gay and lesbian people. Pence insisted the bill was intended to protect sincere religious convictions, not to sanction bias, and argued that “tolerance is a two‑way street.” He rejected claims that the measure is a mistake, while acknowledging that the public and business leaders are reacting strongly, viewing the law as unfair to LGBTQ individuals. Critics accuse the governor of subverting equal protection by allowing businesses to refuse service on religious grounds, noting that Indiana lacks sexual‑orientation protections. In response, Pence said expanding such protections is not on his agenda, emphasizing his authority to shape the law while framing the backlash as a misunderstanding of fairness rather than an endorsement of discrimination. The controversy has spurred protests, corporate boycotts, and concerns from the NCAA about future events in the state. END OF SUMMARY.
