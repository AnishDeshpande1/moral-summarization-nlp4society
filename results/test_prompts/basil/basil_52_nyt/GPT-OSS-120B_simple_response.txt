SUMMARY: Indiana Governor Mike Pence, facing a firestorm over the state’s new Religious Freedom Restoration Act, vowed to push for clarifying legislation while denying that the law sanctions anti‑gay discrimination. Pence told the Indianapolis Star he was surprised by the backlash and expects the General Assembly to act quickly, but he offered no specifics. He reiterated support for “religious liberty,” suggesting the law lets Christian vendors refuse service to same‑sex couples, yet he avoided a direct answer on whether a florist could deny a gay couple. Critics argue the bill’s wording could legitimize discrimination, especially since Indiana does not protect sexual orientation as a civil right. The controversy sparked massive protests, corporate boycotts (Salesforce, Angie’s List) and travel bans from cities like Seattle, while the NCAA warned the law might affect future events. Pence framed the dispute as a question of “two‑way tolerance,” insisting the intent, not the impact, matters, and he rejected adding gay‑rights protections to his agenda. The moral tension lies between claimed religious freedom and the risk of institutionalizing prejudice against LGBTQ people. END OF SUMMARY.
