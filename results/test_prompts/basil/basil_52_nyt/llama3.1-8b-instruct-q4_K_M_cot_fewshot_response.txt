Indiana Governor Mike Pence has been facing backlash over the state's Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which he signed into law last week. The law aims to protect individuals or companies from being forced to take actions that impose a substantial burden on their religious beliefs. However, critics argue that it sanctions discrimination against gays and lesbians. Pence rejected assertions of discrimination, saying that the issue is about "tolerance" and whether it's a "two-way street." He acknowledged that the law was drafted from a version that President Obama voted for when he was a state senator in Illinois, but said that the Indiana law includes different wording that could open the door to wider discrimination. The backlash has been an unexpected political headache for Pence, who is juggling whether to run for re-election or for president in 2016. He stated that he would not add protections for gays and lesbians to his agenda, which critics say is a missed opportunity.
