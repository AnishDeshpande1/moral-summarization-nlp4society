Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed the first major Supreme Court challenge to Colorado’s 2012 marijuana legalization, arguing that the state’s network of recreational shops creates a **dangerous gap** in federal drug‑control policy. The lawsuit claims that marijuana **flows** across state lines, **undermining** neighboring bans, **draining** treasuries and placing **stress** on criminal‑justice systems. Border sheriffs report a **problem** of increased arrests, impounded vehicles and higher jail costs, describing the influx as **strained** resources. Colorado’s Attorney General John Suthers calls the challenge “without **merit**” and vows to defend the law. Advocates warn that shutting down legal retailers could empower **violent criminal** organizations and revive the black market. The case targets the commercial side of legalization, leaving the personal‑use provision untouched, and highlights concerns over untracked sales and the lack of buyer background checks.
