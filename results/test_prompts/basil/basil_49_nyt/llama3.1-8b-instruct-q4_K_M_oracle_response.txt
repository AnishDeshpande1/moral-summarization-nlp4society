SUMMARY:

Two heartland states, Nebraska and Oklahoma, have filed a lawsuit against Colorado's marijuana legalization, arguing that it is creating a "dangerous gap" in the federal drug-control system by allowing marijuana to flow into neighboring states. They claim that this influx has drained their treasuries, placed stress on their criminal justice systems, and created a problem for law enforcement agencies. The suit accuses Colorado officials of participating in a scheme that cultivates, packages, and distributes marijuana, ignoring federal laws and regulations. Nebraska and Oklahoma argue that Colorado's lack of tracking and background checks allows criminals to acquire marijuana inventory directly from retail stores. If they succeed in shutting down Colorado's recreational marijuana shops, they warn it could put violent criminal organizations back in charge. Colorado's attorney general has vowed to defend the state's laws against this challenge. The lawsuit is aimed at striking down key parts of Colorado's 2012 voter-approved measure that legalized marijuana for adult use.

END OF SUMMARY.
