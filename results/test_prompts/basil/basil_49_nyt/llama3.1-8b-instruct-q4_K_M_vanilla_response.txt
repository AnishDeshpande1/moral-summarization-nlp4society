SUMMARY:

Attorneys general in Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a lawsuit against Colorado's recreational marijuana law, arguing that it allows the state to sell marijuana into neighboring states where it is still illegal. The suit claims that Colorado's regulated system has created a "dangerous gap" in federal drug control laws, leading to an influx of marijuana into surrounding states. Nebraska and Oklahoma say this has resulted in more arrests, higher costs for law enforcement, and strain on their resources. Colorado's attorney general disputes the claim, saying the challenge is without merit. If the lawsuit succeeds, it could lead to the shutdown of recreational marijuana retailers, potentially boosting the black market. Marijuana advocates warn that a shutdown would put violent criminal organizations back in charge. The case has been filed directly with the US Supreme Court and may have significant implications for states with legalized marijuana.

END OF SUMMARY.
