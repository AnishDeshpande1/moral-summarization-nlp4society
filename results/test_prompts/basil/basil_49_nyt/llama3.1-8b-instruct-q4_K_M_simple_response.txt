SUMMARY:

Attorneys general from Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a lawsuit against Colorado's recreational marijuana law, arguing that it is creating a "dangerous gap" in the federal drug-control system by allowing marijuana to flow into neighboring states. They claim that this influx of marijuana is undermining their own laws, draining their treasuries, and straining their criminal justice systems. The lawsuit argues that Colorado's lack of regulations on tracking marijuana once it is sold and its failure to require background checks for buyers are contributing to the problem. Nebraska and Oklahoma are seeking to strike down key parts of Colorado's law, which they claim allows commercial enterprises to cultivate, package, and distribute marijuana in violation of federal laws.

Colorado's attorney general has defended the state's law, saying it is "without merit." Marijuana advocates have warned that if the challenge succeeds, it could boost the black market. The lawsuit does not seek to overturn the portion of Colorado's law that made marijuana legal for personal use and possession.

END OF SUMMARY.
