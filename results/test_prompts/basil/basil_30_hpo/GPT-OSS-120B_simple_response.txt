SUMMARY: President Barack Obama unveiled the most ambitious gun‑control effort of a generation, framing it as a moral imperative to protect children and judge the nation by its willingness to act. He outlined a four‑part plan—law‑enforcement resources, tighter limits on dangerous firearms and ammunition, school safety, and mental‑health services—calling for universal background checks, reinstating the assault‑weapons ban, a 10‑round magazine limit, banning armor‑piercing rounds, expanded mental‑health care in schools, more police officers, and a federal gun‑trafficking statute. Estimated at $500 million, the proposal is paired with 23 immediate executive actions, such as data sharing for background checks and improved active‑shooter training. Obama stressed that “if even one life can be saved, we have an obligation to try,” urging public demand to overcome strong gun‑rights opposition. The NRA condemned the plan as overreach, while gun‑control advocates praised its breadth. The administration emphasized that the measures target future production, not existing weapons, and aim to give law‑enforcement tools to stop illegal transfers while providing schools and communities resources to prevent violence. The success now hinges on congressional action and public pressure. END OF SUMMARY.
