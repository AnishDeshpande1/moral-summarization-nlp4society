SUMMARY: President Barack Obama announced the most comprehensive gun‑control initiative in a generation, outlining a four‑part plan covering law enforcement, firearm availability, school safety, and mental health. Key proposals include universal background checks, reinstating the assault‑weapons ban, restoring a 10‑round magazine limit, banning armor‑piercing rounds, expanding school mental‑health services, funding more police officers, and creating a federal gun‑trafficking statute. The package is estimated to cost about $500 million, partly from existing budget funds, and is paired with 23 immediate executive actions such as data sharing for background checks, enhanced training for active‑shooter responses, and CDC research on gun violence. Obama will nominate Byron Todd Jones as permanent ATF director. While gun‑control advocates praise the effort, critics warn it may face stiff opposition in Congress and from the NRA, which decries the proposals as overreach. The administration emphasizes that the measures target future production of assault weapons and high‑capacity magazines, not existing stock, and stresses the need for public demand to drive legislative success. END OF SUMMARY.
