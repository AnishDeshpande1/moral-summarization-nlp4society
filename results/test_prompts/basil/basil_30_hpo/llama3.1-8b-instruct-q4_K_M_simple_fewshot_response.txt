The plan aims to address law enforcement, availability of dangerous firearms and ammunition, school safety, and mental health. While the proposal faces opposition from the NRA and other gun rights groups, Obama emphasized that it is a moral imperative to keep children safe. He called on the American people to demand action and expressed confidence that with public support, the plan can become a reality.

The White House proposals are not a cure-all for mass shootings, but they would provide law enforcement with mechanisms to go after illegal transfers of weapons and prevent them from falling into the wrong hands.
