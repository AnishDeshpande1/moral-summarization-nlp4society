SUMMARY:
President Trump announced he is preparing an executive order to eliminate birthright citizenship, claiming the United States is “the only country” that grants citizenship to babies born to non‑citizens and insisting the move must end. Legal experts and scholars quickly rebuffed the claim, noting that the 14th Amendment’s citizenship guarantee can only be changed through a constitutional amendment or a two‑thirds congressional vote, not by unilateral presidential action. This proposal was condemned as an overreach of authority, with House Speaker Paul Ryan and several Republicans warning that the president cannot “eviscerate” the Constitution and likening the plan to past abuses of executive power. Civil‑rights groups and immigration advocates warned the order would harm children of undocumented parents, creating a class of second‑class citizens, inflaming racial tensions and violating basic fairness. Even members of Trump’s own party, such as Senate candidate Bob Hugin, denounced the idea as un‑American and unjust. Legal scholars predict inevitable court challenges, and the White House has offered no clear legal basis for the executive order.  

END OF SUMMARY.
