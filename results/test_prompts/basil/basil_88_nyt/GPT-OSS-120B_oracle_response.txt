SUMMARY: President Trump announced he is preparing an executive order that would *eviscerate* the long‑standing constitutional *grant* of birthright citizenship, a move he framed as ending an *absurdity* that “a baby is essentially a citizen … for 85 years.” Legal scholars say the 14th Amendment’s *jurisdiction* clause makes such a change *unconstitutional* and would require a constitutional *convention* or a two‑thirds vote in Congress, not a simple *executive* action. Even members of his own party, including House Speaker Paul Ryan, warned the president is *wrong* to try to *strip* citizenship and warned of inevitable *litigation*. Critics called the proposal *illegal*, *offensive*, and likened it to *ethnic cleansing*, saying it would *exacerbate* racial *tensions*, fuel *fears* and deepen political *polarization* at a time when *unity* and *inclusion* are needed. Immigration advocates argue the plan would *create* a permanent class of *second‑class* citizens and would *double* the unauthorized population by 2050, *charging* future generations with *crimes* for simply being born. The president’s advisers claim a *clarification* from the White House Counsel suggests the order can be issued without a constitutional amendment, but the consensus remains that only a formal amendment can *re‑authorize* such a fundamental change. END OF SUMMARY.
