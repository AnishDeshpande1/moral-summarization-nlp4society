SUMMARY: President Trump announced he is drafting an executive order to strip the long‑standing constitutional guarantee of birthright citizenship, framing the move as a moral crusade against a “ridiculous” benefit that he claims rewards illegal immigration. Legal experts and many Republicans quickly warned that the 14th Amendment cannot be overridden by a presidential decree, emphasizing the constitutional rule of law and warning of inevitable court battles. Critics denounced the proposal as unconstitutional, ethically abhorrent, and akin to “ethnic cleansing,” arguing it would create a class of second‑class citizens, deepen racial tensions, and betray America’s historic identity as a nation of immigrants. Conservative commentator Michael Anton tried to justify the idea on historical and philosophical grounds, but scholars highlighted the 1898 Wong Kim Ark decision that affirms citizenship for anyone born on U.S. soil, regardless of parents’ status. Policy analysts warned that abolishing birthright citizenship would dramatically increase the undocumented population, undermining the very immigration control the president claims to pursue. The episode illustrates how political posturing ahead of the midterms can weaponize immigration issues, sacrificing constitutional principles and social cohesion for short‑term electoral gain. END OF SUMMARY.
