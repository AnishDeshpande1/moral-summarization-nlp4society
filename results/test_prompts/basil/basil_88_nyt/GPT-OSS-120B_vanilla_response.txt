SUMMARY: President Trump announced he is preparing an executive order to end birthright citizenship under the 14th Amendment, claiming it unfairly grants citizenship to children of undocumented immigrants. Legal experts and many Republicans, including Speaker Paul Ryan, say the Constitution cannot be overridden by an executive order and would require a constitutional amendment or congressional action. The White House Counsel allegedly told Trump he could act unilaterally, though no legal basis has been provided. Critics, such as civil‑rights groups and some GOP senators, denounce the proposal as unconstitutional, inflammatory, and politically risky ahead of the midterm elections. Historical precedent, notably the 1898 Wong Kim Ark decision, affirms citizenship for anyone born on U.S. soil regardless of parents’ status. Scholars warn that revoking birthright citizenship would create a large class of stateless individuals and could double the undocumented population by 2050. The plan faces expected legal challenges and highlights Trump’s strategy of bold, often unfeasible actions to energize his anti‑immigrant base before voting.

END OF SUMMARY.
