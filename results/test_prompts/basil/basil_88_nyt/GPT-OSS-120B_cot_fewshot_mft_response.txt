**SUMMARY:**  
President Trump announced he is preparing an executive order to nullify the long‑standing constitutional guarantee of birthright citizenship, claiming the policy is “ridiculous” and must end. Legal scholars and many Republicans, including Speaker Paul Ryan, warned that such a move would subvert the 14th Amendment and exceed presidential authority, labeling it an illegal and unconstitutional abuse of power. Civil‑rights leaders called the proposal offensive, dangerous and akin to “ethnic cleansing,” arguing it would exacerbate racial tensions, exploit fear, and deepen national polarization. Even members of Trump’s own party pushed back, emphasizing the need for a constitutional amendment rather than a unilateral executive order. Critics stressed the unfairness of stripping citizenship from children of undocumented immigrants, highlighting the potential harm to millions and the risk of creating a permanent class of second‑class citizens. While some conservatives argue the amendment has been misread, the consensus remains that any change requires a lengthy constitutional process, not a presidential decree. The debate underscores deep conflicts over authority, care for vulnerable populations, and fairness in America’s immigration system.
