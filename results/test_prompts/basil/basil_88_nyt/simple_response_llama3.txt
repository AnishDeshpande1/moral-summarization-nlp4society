SUMMARY:

President Trump announced plans to issue an executive order nullifying birthright citizenship in the United States, sparking backlash from lawmakers and legal scholars. Birthright citizenship grants automatic U.S. citizenship to children born on American soil, regardless of their parents' immigration status. Legal experts argue that President Trump cannot unilaterally change this constitutional guarantee without a lengthy constitutional process or congressional approval. The 14th Amendment's language is clear: "All persons born or naturalized in the United States... are citizens of the United States and of the State wherein they reside." Some conservatives argue that birthright citizenship was meant to apply only to citizens and legal permanent residents, not undocumented immigrants.

END OF SUMMARY.
