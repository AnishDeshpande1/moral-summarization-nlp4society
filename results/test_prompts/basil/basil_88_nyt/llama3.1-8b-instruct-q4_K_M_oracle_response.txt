SUMMARY:

President Trump's latest move is an attempt to eviscerate the constitutional guarantee of birthright citizenship in the United States, claiming it's a benefit that should not be granted to children born to undocumented immigrants. However, this idea has been met with swift pushback from even his own party members, who argue that such a decision would be an abuse of presidential power and unconstitutional.

The 14th Amendment clearly states that any child born in the United States is considered a citizen, regardless of their parents' immigration status. Legal scholars agree that attempting to deny birthright citizenship through an executive order would lead to litigation and exacerbate racial tensions and fears.

Critics argue that such a decision would be a form of ethnic cleansing, stripping away the equal rights and inclusion that citizens deserve. The proposal is also counterproductive, as it would create a permanent group of second-class citizens and increase the unauthorized population in the United States.

The idea has been met with outrage from civil rights groups, who see it as an attempt to exploit fears and drive polarization across the country.
