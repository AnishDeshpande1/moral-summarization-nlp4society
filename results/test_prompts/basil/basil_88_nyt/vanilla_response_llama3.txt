SUMMARY:

President Trump announced plans to revoke birthright citizenship, which grants automatic US citizenship to children born in the country, regardless of their parents' immigration status. He claimed that only the United States grants such citizenship and that it's "ridiculous" and needs to end. However, at least 30 other countries also grant birthright citizenship. House Speaker Paul Ryan and others have pushed back against the plan, saying it would require a lengthy constitutional process and cannot be done through an executive order. Legal scholars agree that Trump lacks the authority to unilaterally change the Constitution's 14th Amendment, which guarantees birthright citizenship.

Many experts argue that scrapping birthright citizenship would create a permanent group of second-class citizens, exacerbate racial tensions, and drive further polarization. The proposal has also sparked outrage among civil rights groups, who call it "ethnic cleansing." Some Republicans have rejected the idea, highlighting the political risks for the party in stoking anti-immigrant sentiment.

END OF SUMMARY.
