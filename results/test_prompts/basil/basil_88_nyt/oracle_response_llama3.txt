SUMMARY:

President Trump's suggestion to nullify birthright citizenship through an executive order is met with swift pushback from legal scholars, lawmakers, and civil rights groups. The move would create a false narrative that a child born in the US is not automatically a citizen. However, at least 30 countries grant automatic birthright citizenship, including Canada and Mexico. Birthright citizenship is enshrined in the 14th Amendment to the Constitution, which cannot be overridden by presidential action. The idea has been met with moral outrage, with critics calling it "ethnic cleansing" and "offensive." The proposal would also exacerbate racial tensions, exploit fears, and drive further polarization across the country. It is illegal, unconstitutional, and would create a permanent group of second-class citizens.

END OF SUMMARY.
