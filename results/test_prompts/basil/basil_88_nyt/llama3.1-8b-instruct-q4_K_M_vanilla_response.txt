SUMMARY:

President Trump announced plans to sign an executive order that would nullify birthright citizenship in the US, a move that would require him to override the 14th Amendment to the Constitution. However, this plan has been met with swift pushback from even his own party members, including House Speaker Paul Ryan, who stated that the president "obviously" cannot eviscerate birthright citizenship by executive order. Legal scholars agree that Trump's proposal would be a constitutional overreach and could prompt legal challenges. The White House Counsel's Office has advised Trump that an executive order could be used to make the change, but the grounds for this are unclear.

The move is seen as an attempt to energize his anti-immigrant base ahead of the midterm elections. Civil rights groups have condemned the idea, calling it "ethnic cleansing" and illegal. Some Republicans have also rejected the suggestion, indicating the potential risks for the party in stoking anti-immigrant sentiment. The proposal has sparked debate over the 14th Amendment's application to children of undocumented immigrants.

END OF SUMMARY.
