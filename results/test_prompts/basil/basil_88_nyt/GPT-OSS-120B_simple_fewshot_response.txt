SUMMARY:
President Trump announced he is drafting an executive order to end birthright citizenship, claiming the United States is “the only country” that grants citizenship to babies born to undocumented parents. Legal experts and many Republicans quickly rebuked the notion, noting that the 14th Amendment enshrines citizenship for anyone born on U.S. soil and can only be changed through a constitutional amendment, not a unilateral order. House Speaker Paul Ryan, Vice President Mike Pence, and several GOP lawmakers warned that such a move would be unconstitutional and invite costly court battles. Civil‑rights groups condemned the proposal as “ethnic cleansing,” illegal, and likely to deepen racial division. Conservative commentator Michael Anton argued the amendment is misread, but scholars and immigration advocates countered that stripping birthright citizenship would create a permanent underclass and could double the unauthorized population by 2050. The plan appears aimed at energizing Trump’s anti‑immigrant base ahead of the midterm elections, despite widespread legal and political opposition.  
END OF SUMMARY.
