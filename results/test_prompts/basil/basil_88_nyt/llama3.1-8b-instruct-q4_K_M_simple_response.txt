SUMMARY:

President Trump has announced plans to issue an executive order that would nullify birthright citizenship in the United States, citing concerns about immigrants taking advantage of American benefits. However, this claim is disputed by experts, who point out that at least 30 other countries grant automatic birthright citizenship. House Speaker Paul Ryan and other Republicans have pushed back against the idea, arguing that it cannot be done through executive order and would require a lengthy constitutional process.

Critics of Trump's plan argue that it would create a permanent underclass of second-class citizens and exacerbate racial tensions. Experts also note that denying birthright citizenship to children of undocumented immigrants could lead to more unauthorized immigration, as families might bring their children with them in search of better lives.

The proposal has sparked widespread outrage from civil rights groups, who have labeled it "ethnic cleansing." Some Republicans have also rejected the idea, highlighting the potential risks for the party. The move is seen by many as an attempt to energize Trump's anti-immigrant base ahead of midterm elections.
