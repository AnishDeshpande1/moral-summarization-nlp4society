SUMMARY:

President Trump has suggested that he is preparing an executive order to nullify the constitutional guarantee of birthright citizenship in the United States, stating that it is "ridiculous" and needs to end. However, this move would be a subversion of the Constitution and the 14th Amendment, which clearly states that any child born in the US is considered a citizen. Trump's proposal has been met with swift pushback from even his own party members, including House Speaker Paul Ryan, who stated that it cannot be done by executive order and would require a constitutional process. Critics argue that this move would cause harm to children of undocumented immigrants, leaving them as second-class citizens without rights or protections. The proposal is also seen as unfair and discriminatory, targeting a specific group of people based on their immigration status. Trump's suggestion has sparked outrage among civil rights groups and some Republicans, highlighting the moral implications of such a move.

END OF SUMMARY.
