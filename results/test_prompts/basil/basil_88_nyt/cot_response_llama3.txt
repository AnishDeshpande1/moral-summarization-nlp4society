SUMMARY:
President Trump's plan to nullify birthright citizenship, a constitutional guarantee, is met with swift pushback from even his own party. His assertion that the US is the only country where a person can come in and have a baby, making the child essentially a citizen for 85 years, has been labeled as "ridiculous" by many. The plan is based on a false claim, as at least 30 other countries grant automatic birthright citizenship. The idea of doing away with birthright citizenship for children of undocumented immigrants has been framed as an attempt to test the limits of presidential power and undermine the fundamental principle of equality before the law.

The notion that being born within US geographical limits automatically confers US citizenship is considered "absurdity" by some. Others see it as a move aimed at creating a "permanent group of second-class citizens" and exacerbating racial tensions. The proposal has been met with outrage from civil rights groups, who call it "offensive" and an attempt to drive further polarization across the country.
