SUMMARY:
New documents reveal that the Obama administration, led by State Department officials, ignored repeated, classified warnings about a growing Islamist threat in Benghazi. Draft talking‑points show spokeswoman Victoria Nuland trying to erase references to prior attacks so Congress could not “beat up” the department, a clear attempt to subvert accountability. A secret August 2012 cable—signed by Ambassador Chris Stevens—alerted Secretary of State Clinton that ten Islamist militias and AQ training camps operated in the city and that the 17 February Brigade meant to protect the consulate was compromised. Despite daily requests for police protection, the warnings went unanswered, exposing U.S. staff to grave harm. When asked, Clinton testified she never saw the cable, a claim that senior military leaders found “surprising,” suggesting a breach of fairness and possible cheating by senior officials who failed to act on the “cry for help.” President Obama later admitted the staff were “not in a position where they were adequately protected.” The accumulated evidence points to negligent leadership, a betrayal of duty, and an effort to shield the department from scrutiny, violating the moral duties of authority, care for personnel, and honest, fair governance.  
END OF SUMMARY.
