SUMMARY:

New documents reveal that the Obama administration's initial story-line on the Benghazi attack was misleading, highlighting State Department concerns about leadership failure to act on documented warnings and security incidents. A classified cable from August 2012 warned of a severe Islamist threat in Libya, including Al Qaeda and Ansar al-Sharia training camps near the consulate. The cable also expressed concerns about the ability to defend the post in case of a coordinated attack due to limited resources. Former diplomatic security agents have described this as a "cry for help." Secretary of State Hillary Clinton claimed she had no knowledge of the warning, contradicting the testimony of Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Gen. Martin Dempsey. The revelations raise questions about the negligence of State Department leadership and whether they were aware of the growing threat in Libya. The controversy highlights concerns about the administration's handling of security warnings and its response to the attack.

END OF SUMMARY.
