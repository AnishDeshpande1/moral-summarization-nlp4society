SUMMARY:
New documents and leaked “talking points” reveal that the Obama administration deliberately down‑played and edited references to earlier attacks and intelligence warnings about Benghazi, hoping to shield the State Department from congressional scrutiny. State Department spokeswoman Victoria Nuland is shown urging the removal of any mention of prior security incidents, arguing the administration should not give Congress “ammunition to beat up” on her agency. Classified State Department cables, now public, detail a August 2012 emergency meeting in which the CIA warned that ten Islamist militias and al‑Qaida training camps operated in Benghazi and that the 17 February Brigade meant to protect the consulate was compromised. Requests for a daily police presence went unanswered, and senior officials, including Secretary of State Hillary Clinton, testified they never saw the cable—a claim that military leaders found “surprising.” A former diplomatic‑security veteran says the cascade of warnings constitutes a “cry for help” ignored by senior leadership, amounting to negligence, if not misconduct. The record underscores a pattern of deception and failure to protect U.S. personnel in Libya under Obama’s watch.

END OF SUMMARY.
