SUMMARY: New documents reveal that the State Department ignored multiple, explicit warnings about the escalating Islamist threat in Benghazi before the 2012 attack. Draft talking points show spokeswoman Victoria Nuland tried to remove references to prior attacks to protect the department from congressional criticism. A classified August 2012 cable, signed by Ambassador Chris Stevens, warned Secretary of State Hillary Clinton that ten Islamist militias and Al Qaeda training camps operated in Benghazi, that the security brigade was compromised, and that requests for a daily police presence were unanswered. Clinton testified she never saw the cable, a claim that surprised senior military officials. Additional records show over 200 violent incidents in Libya from mid‑2011 to mid‑2012 and a June 2012 security review noting a spike in attacks on foreigners. A former diplomatic security officer called the pattern of warnings “a compelling argument” of negligence by State Department leadership. An internal review later acknowledged senior officials lacked proactive leadership but found no misconduct. President Obama admitted the staff were not adequately protected. END OF SUMMARY.
