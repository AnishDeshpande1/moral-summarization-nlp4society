SUMMARY:

New revelations have emerged about the Obama administration's handling of the Benghazi attack, highlighting concerns over State Department leadership's failure to act on documented warnings and security incidents. Draft talking points from the time show that State Department spokeswoman Victoria Nuland tried to remove references to prior attacks in Benghazi, citing a desire not to give Congress "ammunition" to criticize her department.

A classified cable sent by Ambassador Chris Stevens in August 2012 warned of a severe Islamist threat and Al Qaeda presence in Benghazi, including the groups ultimately blamed for the attack. The cable was ignored by Secretary Clinton, who claimed she had no knowledge of it during congressional hearings. Former diplomatic security agents have described the warning as a "cry for help."

The controversy has sparked debate over whether State Department leadership was negligent in responding to repeated security requests. An internal review found that certain officials demonstrated a lack of proactive leadership and management ability, but did not find any misconduct.

END OF SUMMARY.
