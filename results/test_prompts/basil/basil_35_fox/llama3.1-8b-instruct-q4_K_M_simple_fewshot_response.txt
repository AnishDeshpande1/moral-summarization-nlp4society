SUMMARY:

Newly obtained documents reveal that the Obama administration was aware of the growing Islamist threat in Benghazi, Libya before the 2012 attack on the US diplomatic compound. State Department records show that officials had documented warnings and security incidents, including a classified cable warning of a severe and growing Islamist threat. Despite these warnings, the administration failed to act on them, with Secretary of State Clinton claiming she was unaware of the specific cable. The controversy has raised questions about the leadership's failure to protect American personnel in Benghazi. A former diplomatic security agent argues that the multiple warnings present a compelling case of negligence by State Department leadership. The incident has led to renewed criticism of the Obama administration's handling of national security and its initial storyline on the attack.

END OF SUMMARY.
