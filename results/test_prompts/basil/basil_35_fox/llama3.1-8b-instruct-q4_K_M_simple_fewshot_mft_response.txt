SUMMARY:

New evidence has emerged that the Obama administration deliberately misled the public about the Benghazi attack, stripping out references to prior attacks in Libya from draft talking points. State Department spokeswoman Victoria Nuland argued that this was necessary to avoid giving Congress "ammunition" to criticize her department. The administration's failure to act on documented warnings and security incidents has been widely criticized as a dereliction of duty. A classified State Department cable, marked SECRET, warned of the Islamist threat in Benghazi and foreshadowed the attack itself. Despite this warning, Ambassador Chris Stevens was not adequately protected, and the consulate was vulnerable to attack. The administration's negligence and lack of proactive leadership have been criticized as a betrayal of trust by those who rely on them for security. The controversy surrounding the Obama administration's handling of the Benghazi attack underscores the need for transparency and accountability in government.

END OF SUMMARY.
