SUMMARY:  
New documents and leaked “talking points” reveal that the Obama administration, especially State Department officials, down‑played earlier warnings about the escalating Islamist threat in Benghazi. Victoria Nuland pushed to erase references to prior attacks to avoid giving Congress “ammunition” against the department. A classified August 2012 cable, signed by Ambassador Chris Stevens, warned Secretary Hillary Clinton that ten Islamist militias and Al Qaeda‑linked camps operated in the city, that the local security brigade was compromised, and that requests for a daily police presence were ignored. Clinton later testified she never saw the cable, a claim that surprised senior military leaders. The record shows over 200 violent incidents in Libya before the February 2012 assault and a clear pattern of ignored security requests, leading a former diplomatic‑security veteran to label the leadership’s inaction as negligent. An internal review admitted senior officials showed “a lack of proactive leadership and management ability,” though it stopped short of finding misconduct.  

END OF SUMMARY.
