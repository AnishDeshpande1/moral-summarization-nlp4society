SUMMARY:
Donald Trump condemned Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg for publicly criticizing his presidential candidacy, labeling her remarks as “highly inappropriate” and a disgrace to the Supreme Court. He argued that a judge should not subvert the institution’s authority by entering a political campaign, urging Ginsburg to apologize and even leave the bench. Trump framed his response as defending the proper hierarchy and impartiality of the judiciary, portraying Ginsburg’s comments as a breach of respected authority. He then shifted to campaign matters, noting he would soon announce his vice‑presidential pick, emphasizing that his selection will rely on instinct and long‑standing loyalty rather than external pressures. By highlighting his own decisive authority in choosing a running mate and dismissing the need for external validation, Trump reinforces a narrative of upholding traditional authority while casting Ginsburg’s dissent as subversive. END OF SUMMARY.
