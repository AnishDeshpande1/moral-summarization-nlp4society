SUMMARY:

Donald Trump criticized Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg for her involvement in the presidential campaign, calling her comments "highly inappropriate" and suggesting she owed other justices an apology. Trump felt that Ginsburg's remarks were unbefitting a member of the Supreme Court, stating it was a "disgrace to the court." He implied that Ginsburg's actions were unprofessional and unworthy of her position.

Meanwhile, Trump announced that he would be selecting his running mate by Friday, with five candidates in consideration. He based his decision on instinct rather than polling or fund-raising prowess, saying "my whole life has been about gut." Trump mentioned Gov. Chris Christie, Newt Gingrich, and Gov. Mike Pence as potential picks, but did not give further details.

Trump's comments sparked controversy over the Supreme Court's role in politics, with some arguing that Ginsburg's involvement was justified given the stakes of the election. Others saw her actions as a threat to the independence of the judiciary.
