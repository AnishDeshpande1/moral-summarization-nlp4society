SUMMARY:

Donald Trump lashed out at Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg for her comments about his candidacy, calling them "highly inappropriate" and demanding an apology from her to the court. Trump's remarks came after Ginsburg expressed her fears about what the country would be like if Trump were elected, saying she couldn't imagine the consequences. Trump deemed Ginsburg's comments unbefitting a Supreme Court judge, saying they only energized his base and suggesting she should step down from the court as soon as possible.

In other news, Trump revealed he would announce his running mate by Friday, choosing from a pool of five known candidates and two unknown ones. He emphasized that his decision would be based on instinct, rather than fundraising prowess or campaign trail performance.

Trump's criticism of Ginsburg raises questions about the boundaries of judicial impartiality and the appropriate role of the Supreme Court in political discourse. His response also highlights his tendency to lash out at critics and opponents, rather than engaging in constructive dialogue.

END OF SUMMARY.
