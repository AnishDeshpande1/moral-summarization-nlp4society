SUMMARY:
Donald Trump blasted Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg for publicly expressing fear that the nation would suffer under a Trump presidency, calling her comments “highly inappropriate” and a disgrace to the Court. In a telephone interview with The Times, Trump demanded that Ginsburg apologize to her fellow justices and even suggested she should leave the bench. He framed her political remarks as beneath the dignity of the high court and said they only “energize my base.” The interview then shifted to Trump’s own campaign, where he announced he would pick a running mate within days, emphasizing gut instinct over polls or fundraising. He listed potential candidates—Gov. Chris Christie, former Speaker Newt Gingrich, Gov. Mike Pence, retired Lt. Gen. Mike Flynn, and Sen. Joni Ernst—offering brief, favorable character sketches. The moral thrust of the piece underscores Trump’s condemnation of a judge stepping into politics while he himself navigates the political arena with a focus on personal intuition.

END OF SUMMARY.
