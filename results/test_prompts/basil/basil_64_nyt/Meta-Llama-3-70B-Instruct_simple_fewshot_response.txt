SUMMARY:

Donald Trump lashed out at Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg, calling her comments about his candidacy "highly inappropriate" and demanding an apology. Ginsburg had expressed fear about a Trump presidency, saying it could be disastrous for the country and the court. Trump deemed her remarks unbefitting a Supreme Court justice, claiming they only energize his base. He also announced that he would soon choose a running mate, narrowing down his options to five candidates. Trump emphasized that his decision would be based on instinct, rather than fundraising prowess or polling. He praised several potential candidates, including Gov. Chris Christie, Retired Lt. Gen. Mike Flynn, Sen. Joni Ernst, Gov. Mike Pence, and Newt Gingrich. Trump's criticism of Ginsburg highlights the tension between the judiciary and the political sphere, raising concerns about the independence and impartiality of the Supreme Court.

END OF SUMMARY.
