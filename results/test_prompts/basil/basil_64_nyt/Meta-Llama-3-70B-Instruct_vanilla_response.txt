SUMMARY:

Donald Trump criticized Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg for speaking out against his presidential candidacy, calling her remarks "highly inappropriate" and suggesting she owes an apology to the court. Ginsburg had expressed fear about what the country would be like if Trump were elected, joking about moving to New Zealand. Trump said her comments were unbefitting a Supreme Court judge and only energized his base. Trump also announced that he would reveal his running mate by Friday, saying the decision would be based on instinct rather than fundraising prowess. He mentioned several potential candidates, including Gov. Chris Christie, Newt Gingrich, and Gov. Mike Pence, praising their qualities. Trump also suggested he would consider polling in Democratic states like Connecticut, believing he could put them in play in November.

END OF SUMMARY.
