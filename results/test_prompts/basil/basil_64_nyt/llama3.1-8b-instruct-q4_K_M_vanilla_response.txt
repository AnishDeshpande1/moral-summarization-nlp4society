SUMMARY:
Donald Trump criticized Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg for speaking out on the presidential race, calling her comments "highly inappropriate" and suggesting she owed other justices an apology. Trump said Ginsburg's remarks were unbefitting a member of the high court and that she should apologize to the court. He also joked about moving to New Zealand if he lost the election.

Trump announced that he will announce his vice-presidential running mate by Friday, narrowing down his choices from 7 candidates. He indicated that his decision was largely based on instinct and personal relationships with the candidates. Trump praised Gov. Mike Pence as a strong contender for the position.

The controversy surrounding Ginsburg's comments has sparked debate about the role of Supreme Court justices in politics. Trump's criticism of Ginsburg comes at a time when he is preparing to announce his running mate, a decision that could be crucial in the election.

END OF SUMMARY.
