SUMMARY:
Donald Trump blasted Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg for her “highly inappropriate” comments that the nation would be worse off if he became president, calling her remarks a disgrace to the Court and demanding an apology. He argued that a justice should not wade into partisan politics, suggesting she should even leave the bench. Trump used the controversy to rally his base, portraying himself as the victim of a biased judiciary. He then turned to campaign business, teasing his vice‑presidential pick, saying he will choose from a short list that includes Chris Christie, Newt Gingrich, Mike Flynn, Joni Ernst and Mike Pence, emphasizing that his decision will be driven by instinct and “gut.” He dismissed fundraising or polling as primary factors, insisting his long‑standing relationships with the candidates give him confidence. The interview underscores Trump’s narrative that the courts are politicized against him and that he will appoint a loyal running mate to further his agenda. END OF SUMMARY.
