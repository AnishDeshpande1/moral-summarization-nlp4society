SUMMARY:

Donald Trump criticized Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg for her "unbefitting" remarks about his presidential candidacy, calling them "highly inappropriate" and suggesting that she owed the other justices an apology. Trump found it "beneath the court" for Ginsburg to make such statements, which he believes will only "energize" his base. Ginsburg had expressed fear about what the nation would look like if Trump were elected, jokingly suggesting she might move to New Zealand. Trump's comments came as he is finalizing his choice of a running mate, with a decision expected by Friday. He emphasized that his selection would be based on instinct, rather than fundraising prowess or potential. Trump also hinted that he might consider polling in traditionally Democratic states, such as Connecticut.

END OF SUMMARY.
