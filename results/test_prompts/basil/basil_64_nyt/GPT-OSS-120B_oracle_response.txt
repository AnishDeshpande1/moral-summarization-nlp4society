SUMMARY:  
Donald J. Trump condemned Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg’s comments about his candidacy as “highly inappropriate,” labeling the Supreme Court justice’s political remarks as unbefitting of her office. He argued that a judge should not be *involved* in a campaign and said Ginsburg owed an apology to her fellow justices, even suggesting she should leave the bench promptly. Trump also discussed his upcoming vice‑presidential pick, noting he has five known candidates plus two unknowns and will decide in the next few days based largely on instinct and “gut.” He praised potential picks—including Gov. Chris Christie, former Speaker Newt Gingrich, Lt. Gen. Mike Flynn, Sen. Joni Ernst, and Gov. Mike Pence—using brief, favorable descriptors. The former president reiterated his confidence that his selection will benefit many Americans and hinted at targeting traditionally Democratic states in the general election.  

END OF SUMMARY.
