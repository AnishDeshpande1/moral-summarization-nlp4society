SUMMARY: Former President Donald Trump criticized Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg for publicly expressing fear that his election would harm the nation, calling her comments “highly inappropriate” and urging her to apologize and possibly leave the bench. In a telephone interview with The Times, Trump said her remarks were a disgrace to the Court and would energize his supporters. He also announced that he expects to reveal his vice‑presidential pick within a few days, mentioning a shortlist that includes Gov. Chris Christie, former Speaker Newt Gingrich, retired Lt. Gen. Mike Flynn, Sen. Joni Ernst, and Gov. Mike Pence, describing each in positive terms. Trump said the decision will be driven largely by instinct and gut feeling, not fundraising or polling, though he noted he might consider states like Connecticut. He will appear on Tuesday night in Indiana with Pence. END OF SUMMARY.
