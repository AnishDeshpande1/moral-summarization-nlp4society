SUMMARY:

US Presidential candidate Donald J. Trump criticized Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg for speaking out against his candidacy, calling her remarks "highly inappropriate" and suggesting she owed the court an apology. Trump argued that a judge's role is to remain impartial, not to take sides in politics. In response to Ginsburg's comments about being frightened by the prospect of a Trump presidency, Trump dismissed them as unbefitting a member of the high court and said they only energized his base.

Trump also discussed his vice-presidential selection process, stating that he has narrowed it down to several candidates and will make a decision in the coming days. He emphasized that his choice is based on instinct rather than polling or fund-raising prowess.

The exchange highlights the tensions between Trump's campaign and the judiciary, with Trump viewing Ginsburg's comments as an overstep of judicial boundaries and Ginsburg expressing concern about the implications of a Trump presidency for the country and the court.

END OF SUMMARY.
