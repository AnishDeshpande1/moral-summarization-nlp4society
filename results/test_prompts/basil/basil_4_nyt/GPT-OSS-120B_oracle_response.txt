SUMMARY:  
Republican National Committee chairman Michael Steele drew fierce criticism and calls for his resignation after a Connecticut fund‑raiser speech in which he labeled the United States’ involvement in Afghanistan as “a war of Obama’s choosing” and warned that history shows a nation should not **engage** in a **land war** there. Steele’s remarks suggested the conflict was a **battle** the president was **flipping** by **demonizing** Iraq while pushing the **war** in Afghanistan, a stance that Democrats called **betting** against troops and **rooting** for failure. Conservative commentator William Kristol rebuked Steele, noting the war was **prosecuted** by both Presidents Bush and Obama and that any criticism should not come from the party’s chairman. Steele later issued a statement affirming that America must win the **war on terror**, stressing the **security** of the free world and recalling the **tragedy** of the September 11 **attacks**. He reiterated support for increased troop levels and for Gen. David Petraeus’s confirmation, arguing that success in Afghanistan is essential.  

END OF SUMMARY.
