SUMMARY:  
Michael Steele, the Republican National Committee chair, sparked a firestorm after a Connecticut fundraiser when he called the U.S. involvement in Afghanistan “a war of Obama’s choosing” and suggested the United States was on the wrong side of history. His remarks were condemned as a betrayal of the troops and an irresponsible moral judgment about a conflict that began under President George W. Bush after 9/11 and was continued by President Obama. Prominent conservatives, including Weekly Standard editor William Kristol, demanded Steele’s resignation, arguing that his gaffe not only misrepresents history but also undermines the moral duty to support the men and women fighting abroad. Democrats echoed the outrage, accusing Steele of “betting against our troops.” Steele later tried to soften the blow, affirming that America must win the war on terror and supporting the troop surge and Gen. Petraeus’s confirmation, but he did not step down. The episode highlights the moral expectation that political leaders honor the sacrifices of soldiers and speak responsibly about ongoing wars.  

END OF SUMMARY.
