Michael Steele, the Republican National Committee chairman, sparked fierce criticism and a call for his resignation after declaring at a Connecticut fund‑raiser that the United States was on the wrong side of history in Afghanistan, labeling the conflict “a war of Obama’s choosing.” Steele’s critique of President Obama’s strategy – calling it “cute” and “demonizing” Iraq while shifting focus to Afghanistan – prompted conservatives like William Kristol to demand his ouster, citing Steele’s pattern of gaffes and embarrassments. Democrats accused Steele of betting against the troops and rooting for failure, while Republican officials defended his right to comment. Steele later issued a statement insisting America must win the war, calling the post‑9/11 conflict both a necessary and tragic fight, and reaffirming his support for increased troop levels and General Petraeus’s confirmation. The controversy underscores the moral stakes parties attach to the Afghanistan war and the intense pressure on leaders whose words are seen as either supportive or undermining of the nation’s security.
