SUMMARY:
Republican National Committee Chairman Michael Steele sparked a firestorm after a fundraiser speech in which he called the Afghanistan conflict “a war of Obama’s choosing” and suggested the United States was on the wrong side of history. Conservatives quickly condemned the remarks as a betrayal of the troops and an unacceptable political gaffe, with prominent voices such as Weekly Standard editor William Kristol demanding Steele resign, noting his tenure has been riddled with embarrassments and that no party leader should question the war effort. Democrats seized on the comments, accusing Steele of “betting against our troops.” Steele later tried to backtrack, asserting that America must win the war on terror and supporting the troop surge and Gen. Petraeus’s confirmation, but he did not address calls for his resignation. The moral judgment is clear: Steele’s statements undermine support for the soldiers fighting in Afghanistan and warrant his removal from party leadership. END OF SUMMARY.
