SUMMARY:

Michael Steele, chairman of the Republican National Committee, faced criticism and calls for his resignation after stating that the US was on the wrong side of history in its conflict in Afghanistan, calling it "a war of Obama's choosing." He claimed President Obama shifted the focus from Iraq to Afghanistan and made a series of criticisms about Obama's military strategy. The comments were made at a party fund-raiser in Connecticut.

Steele later issued a statement clarifying his remarks, stating that America must win the war on terror, but did not directly address calls for his resignation. William Kristol, editor of the Weekly Standard, called for Steele's resignation, saying the war was prosecuted under Presidents Bush and Obama, not just Obama.

The Democratic National Committee criticized Steele, calling him "betting against our troops and rooting for failure in Afghanistan." Republican officials disputed this characterization but did not dispute the authenticity of a video recording of Steele's remarks. The controversy marks another instance of a high-profile gaffe by Steele as party chairman. END OF SUMMARY.
