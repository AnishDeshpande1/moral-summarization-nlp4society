SUMMARY: Republican National Committee Chairman Michael Steele faced intense criticism and calls for his resignation after a Connecticut fund‑raiser speech in which he claimed the United States was on the “wrong side of history” in Afghanistan and called it “a war of Obama’s choosing.” Steele later clarified that America must win the war on terror and supported the troop surge and Gen. Petraeus’s confirmation. Critics, including conservative commentator William Kristol and the Democratic National Committee, denounced his remarks as undermining the troops and mischaracterizing the conflict, which began under President George W. Bush after 9/11 and continued under President Obama. While Republican officials accepted the video’s authenticity, they disputed its portrayal. Steele has not resigned but reiterated support for the mission in Afghanistan. END OF SUMMARY.
