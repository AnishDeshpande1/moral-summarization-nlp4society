SUMMARY:
Republican National Committee Chairman Michael Steele sparked a storm of criticism and resignation calls after labeling the Afghanistan conflict “a war of Obama’s choosing” and saying the United States was on the wrong side of history. By publicly questioning the legitimacy of a war that has been pursued under both Bush and Obama, Steele was accused of subverting established authority and betraying the party’s duty to support the troops. Critics emphasized the moral harm of his remarks, arguing they undermine morale and endanger the lives of service members fighting in Afghanistan. Conservative leaders such as William Kristol and several GOP senators demanded accountability, framing Steele’s comments as an unfair abuse of his leadership position that jeopardizes the principle of honest, responsible governance. Steele attempted to mitigate the backlash by reaffirming that America must win the war on terror and must fully support its troops, but his initial statement was portrayed as a betrayal of both authority and the care owed to soldiers, and as an unjust, self‑serving deviation from the party’s shared values. The episode underscores the intertwined concerns of authority, care for troops, and fairness in political leadership.  
END OF SUMMARY.
