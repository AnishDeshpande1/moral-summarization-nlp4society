SUMMARY:

Michael Steele, Chairman of the Republican National Committee, sparked controversy by stating that the US is on the wrong side of history in its conflict in Afghanistan, calling it "a war of Obama's choosing". He claimed that President Bush started the war and Obama shifted focus to Afghanistan, implying that Obama is responsible for the ongoing conflict. This statement drew fierce criticism from Democrats, who accused Steele of rooting for failure in Afghanistan. Even a prominent conservative commentator, William Kristol, called for Steele's resignation as party leader, stating that the war in Afghanistan was not "a war of Obama's choosing".

Steele later clarified his remarks, saying that America must win the war on terror and that he supported increasing troop forces in Afghanistan. However, his initial statement has raised questions about his leadership and understanding of US foreign policy.

END OF SUMMARY.
