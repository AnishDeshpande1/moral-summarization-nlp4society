SUMMARY:

Republican National Committee Chairman Michael Steele drew criticism for his comments at a party fund-raiser, where he declared that the US is on the wrong side of history in Afghanistan, calling it "a war of Obama's choosing." Steele claimed that President Obama was trying to be cute by flipping a script demonizing Iraq while saying the battle should be in Afghanistan. This statement sparked outrage among conservatives and calls for his resignation. William Kristol, editor of the Weekly Standard, said Steele's comments were unacceptable and that he should resign as party leader. The war in Afghanistan was started by former President George W. Bush in response to the 9/11 attacks, and Obama shifted the focus from Iraq to Afghanistan. Steele's remarks were seen as a betrayal of the troops and the country's efforts in Afghanistan, with some calling it "betting against our troops and rooting for failure." Steele attempted to clarify his statement by saying that Afghanistan is now Obama's war, but the backlash continues.

END OF SUMMARY.
