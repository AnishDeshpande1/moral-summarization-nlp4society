SUMMARY: Michael Steele, chairman of the Republican National Committee, sparked controversy by declaring that the US is on the wrong side of history in Afghanistan, calling it "a war of Obama's choosing". He claimed the conflict was not something the US had actively prosecuted or wanted to engage in, but rather a battle that President Obama flipped by demonizing Iraq while shifting focus to Afghanistan. This statement drew fierce criticism and calls for his resignation from prominent Republicans like William Kristol. Kristol argued that Steele's remarks were a betrayal of the Republican Party's stance on the war. The Democratic National Committee also weighed in, accusing Steele of rooting for failure in Afghanistan. Despite the backlash, Steele maintained that the US must engage in the fight against terrorism and support its troops to ensure the security of the free world. He argued that winning this war is a necessary task after the tragedy of 9/11.

END OF SUMMARY.
