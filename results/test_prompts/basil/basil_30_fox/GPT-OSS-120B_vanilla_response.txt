SUMMARY: President Obama announced a comprehensive gun‑control plan that includes new restrictions on assault weapons and a 10‑round limit on magazines, along with calls for Congress to pass universal background checks, bans on armor‑piercing ammunition, and stricter gun‑trafficking laws. He also urged legislation to require background checks for most private sales and gun‑show transactions, and proposed $4 billion for hiring more police and funding school safety and mental‑health services. In parallel, Obama signed nearly two dozen executive actions directing agencies to improve background‑check data sharing, expand CDC research on gun violence, and enhance law‑enforcement tracing of firearms. The plan faces strong opposition from Republicans, the NRA, and gun‑rights groups, who argue it threatens the Second Amendment and fails to address the primary sources of gun deaths. Senate Republican Marco Rubio and Texas Governor Rick Perry criticized the proposals, while Senate Democrats and Harry Reid expressed support for considering all options. The White House also nominated Todd Jones as the permanent ATF director. END OF SUMMARY.
