SUMMARY: President Obama called for new restrictions on assault weapons and a 10‑round limit on magazines as part of a broader plan to **curb** gun **violence**. He urged Congress to pass measures that would **prohibit** armor‑piercing bullets, expand universal **background checks**, and ban **straw purchasing**, arguing that even one life **saved** justifies the effort. The proposal also seeks to **improve** mental‑health services, enhance school safety, and fund up to 1,000 resource officers and counselors. Obama pledged to **use** the weight of the presidency to push the legislation, emphasizing the duty of **protecting** the nation’s most vulnerable after the **Sandy Hook shooting** that **killed** 20 children. Critics claim the plan **undermines** the **law‑abiding** right to bear arms and is **misguided**, noting most **killings** involve handguns, not assault rifles. The administration also directed agencies to share data with the federal background‑check system and to trace firearms used in criminal **investigations**, while **helping** schools develop emergency plans. **END OF SUMMARY.**
