SUMMARY: President Obama proposed a comprehensive plan to curb gun violence, which includes a new assault-weapons ban, a 10-round limit on magazines, and universal background checks for nearly all gun sales. He called on Congress to pass these measures, citing the need to protect the most vulnerable among us from violence. The president emphasized that "if there's even one life that can be saved, then we've got an obligation to try." Obama also proposed improving access to mental health services and enhancing school safety through emergency plans, resource officers, and mental health professionals.

The plan aims to use executive action to improve background checks, research causes of gun violence, and trace guns from criminal investigations. However, the administration's proposals have sparked controversy among lawmakers and gun-rights groups, with some accusing Obama of undermining Americans' constitutional right to bear arms. The National Rifle Association plans to launch a major ad campaign against the plan. Despite the opposition, the White House is committed to helping schools improve their emergency plans and beef up mental health coverage.

END OF SUMMARY.
