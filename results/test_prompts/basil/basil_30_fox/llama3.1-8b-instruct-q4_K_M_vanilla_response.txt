SUMMARY:

President Obama unveiled a comprehensive plan to curb gun violence, calling for stricter regulations on assault weapons and high-capacity magazines. He proposed a new ban on semi-automatic firearms and a 10-round limit on magazine capacity. Additionally, he suggested legislation to bar the possession and importation of armor-piercing bullets and require universal background checks for nearly all gun sales.

The plan also includes measures to improve access to mental health services and enhance school safety. Obama signed executive actions to share information with the federal background check system and require law enforcement to trace guns from criminal investigations.

Gun rights groups, including the NRA, have expressed opposition to the proposals, while some lawmakers have vowed to resist federal action. The plan's most contentious elements include universal background checks and the assault weapons ban, which face significant opposition from Republican lawmakers.

END OF SUMMARY.
