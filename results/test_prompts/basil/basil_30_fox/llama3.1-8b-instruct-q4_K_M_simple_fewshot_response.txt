SUMMARY:
President Barack Obama unveiled a comprehensive plan to curb gun violence, calling for new restrictions on assault weapons and magazines, as well as universal background checks for nearly all gun sales. The plan includes 23 executive actions that will take effect immediately, such as requiring federal agencies to share information with the federal background check system and directing the Centers for Disease Control to research causes of gun violence. Obama also called on Congress to pass legislation to bar the possession and importation of armor-piercing bullets and to require criminal background checks for nearly all gun sales. The president emphasized that "if there's even one life that can be saved, then we've got an obligation to try" and urged lawmakers to act now. Despite opposition from gun-rights advocates and Republican lawmakers, the administration is pressing forward with its plan to address gun violence.
