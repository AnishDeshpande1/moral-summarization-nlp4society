SUMMARY:
President Obama announced a new gun‑control plan that calls on Congress to enact tougher restrictions on assault weapons, high‑capacity magazines, armor‑piercing bullets and to expand background checks to nearly all sales. Framing the measures as a moral duty to protect “the most vulnerable,” Obama said the nation has an “obligation” to act and pledged to use “whatever weight this office holds” to push the proposals. The plan also includes executive actions on data sharing, gun tracing, CDC research and mental‑health services, and a $4 billion commitment to hire more police. While Democrats argue all options should be on the table, Republicans and the NRA accuse the president of subverting the Constitution and targeting law‑abiding citizens, claiming the measures are unfair to gun owners and will not stop mass shootings. The debate thus pits authority‑based appeals to presidential power and public safety against claims of constitutional subversion and fairness to gun‑rights holders.  

END OF SUMMARY.
