SUMMARY:
President Obama announced a bold gun‑control plan, urging Congress to adopt a tougher assault‑weapons ban, a 10‑round magazine limit, bans on armor‑piercing bullets, and near‑universal background checks. He framed the initiative as a moral duty to protect the nation’s most vulnerable, especially children, and pledged to use “whatever weight this office holds” to save lives. The proposal is paired with 23 executive actions—enhancing background‑check data sharing, directing CDC research, expanding school safety resources, and boosting mental‑health services—while nominating Todd Jones to lead the ATF. Obama highlighted the need for more police officers and stricter gun‑trafficking laws, presenting the measures as common‑sense steps to curb gun violence. Critics, including Sen. Marco Rubio, Gov. Rick Perry, and the NRA, argue the plan infringes on Second‑Amendment rights and will not stop mass shootings, noting most killings involve handguns. Nonetheless, the administration insists that moral responsibility demands immediate action to prevent further tragedies.  
END OF SUMMARY.
