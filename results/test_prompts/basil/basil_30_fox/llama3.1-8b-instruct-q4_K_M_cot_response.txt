**SUMMARY:**

President Obama unveiled a comprehensive plan to curb gun violence, which includes a new and tougher assault-weapons ban, a 10-round limit on magazines, and universal background checks. The president emphasized that if there's even one life that can be saved, then we have an obligation to try. He urged Congress to pass several major changes to the country's current gun laws, including legislation to bar the possession and importation of armor-piercing bullets and to require criminal background checks for nearly all gun sales. The plan also calls for a range of measures to improve access to mental health services and improve safety at America's schools. Critics argue that the proposal is misguided and targets law-abiding citizens instead of focusing on keeping guns out of the hands of criminals and the mentally ill.
