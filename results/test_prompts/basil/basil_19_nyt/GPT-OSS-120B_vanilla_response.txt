SUMMARY: Donald J. Trump announced he will no longer moderate the Newsmax Republican presidential debate, but he reiterated he may still run as an independent if he feels the party’s nominee is unsuitable. He emphasized the importance of choosing the “right Republican candidate” to defeat the Obama administration, yet warned he won’t relinquish his right to run. Critics within the GOP condemned his decision to link debate moderation with a possible independent bid. The debate was already in trouble, with only Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum agreeing to attend while Mitt Romney, Rick Perry, Michele Bachmann, Jon Huntsman Jr., and Ron Paul declined, undermining Trump’s claim as a party kingmaker. Trump, speaking through lawyer Michael Cohen, said he must keep all options open to “make America great again.” END OF SUMMARY.
