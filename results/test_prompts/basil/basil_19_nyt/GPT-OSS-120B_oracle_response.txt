SUMMARY:  
Donald J. Trump announced he is withdrawing from moderating a Newsmax presidential debate, but he made clear he will not disappear from politics. He warned that if the Republican nominee he deems “the right Republican candidate” fails to meet his standards, he will keep the option to run as an independent. Trump faced criticism for even considering moderating a debate that only Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum would attend, while other GOP figures—Mitt Romney, Rick Perry, Michele Bachmann, Jon M. Huntsman Jr. and Ron Paul—declined, undermining his self‑styled role as the party’s kingmaker. In his statement, Trump labeled the Obama administration “failed and very destructive” and reaffirmed his mission to “make America great again,” insisting he must leave all options open. The episode highlights the tension between the Republican establishment and Trump’s willingness to threaten an independent run if the party does not choose a candidate he considers capable of defeating the “failed and destructive” legacy of the previous administration.  

END OF SUMMARY.
