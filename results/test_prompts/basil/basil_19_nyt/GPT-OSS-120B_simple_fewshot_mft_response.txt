SUMMARY:
Donald J. Trump announced he is withdrawing as moderator of the Newsmax Republican debate, but he framed the move as a principled stand against party authority that might block the “right” candidate. Citing the need to keep “all of my options open” to defeat the “failed and very destructive Obama administration,” Trump warned he will not relinquish his right to run as an independent if the GOP selects a weak nominee. His decision drew sharp criticism from Republican leaders who saw his dual role as moderator and potential candidate as a subversive challenge to party order. With only Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum willing to attend, the debate highlighted the party establishment’s refusal to embrace Trump’s self‑styled king‑maker status. By stepping down, Trump positions himself as a rebel authority, insisting that true loyalty to America means refusing to be constrained by party decisions and keeping the option to “make America great again” alive. END OF SUMMARY.
