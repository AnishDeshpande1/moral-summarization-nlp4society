SUMMARY:
Donald J. Trump has dropped plans to moderate a presidential debate hosted by Newsmax, but he still intends to stay involved in the Republican primary. In his statement, Trump emphasized that he wants the right candidate to defeat the Obama administration and hinted at running as an independent if he's not satisfied with the Republican nominee. This decision comes after several high-profile Republicans declined to attend the debate, including Mitt Romney, Rick Perry, and Michele Bachmann. By dropping out of moderating the debate, Trump may have been trying to avoid a potentially awkward situation, but his statement suggests that he's still considering an independent run. The move has sparked criticism from the Republican establishment, who see him as a divisive figure. With Trump's involvement still uncertain, it remains to be seen how this will impact the Republican primary.
