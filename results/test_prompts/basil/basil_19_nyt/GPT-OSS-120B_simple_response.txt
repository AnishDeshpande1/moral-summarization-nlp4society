SUMMARY:  
Donald J. Trump announced he will not moderate the Newsmax Republican debate, but he warned he will not abandon his “right to run as an independent” if the party’s nominee does not meet his standards. By stepping back, Trump exposed his lingering ambition to reshape the race and left the GOP with a leadership vacuum. His decision drew sharp rebuke from party elites, who condemned his willingness to flirt with a candidacy while still holding a public forum hostage. The debate itself collapsed, with only Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum agreeing to appear, while the rest of the Republican field—Mitt Romney, Rick Perry, Michele Bachmann, Jon Huntsman Jr., and Ron Paul—refused, underscoring Trump’s fragile “kingmaker” reputation. In a lawyer‑issued, exclamation‑point‑filled statement, Trump insisted he must keep all options open “because, above all else, we must make America great again!” The episode highlights a moral critique: Trump’s self‑appointed role as arbiter of Republican virtue threatens party unity and betrays a pattern of personal ambition over collective responsibility.
