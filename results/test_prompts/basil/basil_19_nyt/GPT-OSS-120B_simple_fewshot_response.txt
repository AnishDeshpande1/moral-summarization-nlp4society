SUMMARY:
Donald J. Trump has withdrawn from moderating the Newsmax Republican presidential debate, but his exit underscores his continued willingness to intervene in the GOP’s future. In a force‑filled statement, Trump warned that if the party does not select “the right Republican” to defeat the “failed and very destructive Obama administration,” he will not abandon his right to run as an independent. By refusing to preside over a debate that only Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum were willing to attend, Trump signaled that the party’s elite have marginalized its true kingmaker. His decision drew criticism from establishment Republicans for appearing to keep a back‑door campaign option open, yet it also highlights his commitment to “make America great again” above personal ambition. Trump’s departure may have handed the GOP a lesson: shunning a figure who claims to champion the nation’s revival could cost them the decisive influence they need to choose a candidate capable of delivering on the promises of the 2016 movement.  

END OF SUMMARY.
