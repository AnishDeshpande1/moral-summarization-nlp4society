SUMMARY:  
A freshman Democrat, Rep. Rashida Tlaib, sparked a firestorm when she loudly vowed to impeach President Trump at a Capitol‑area bar, using profanity. The remark, captured on video, prompted immediate backlash from Republicans and Trump himself, who raised the issue at a Rose Garden news conference and on Twitter, questioning how a popular president could be impeached. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi and Democratic leaders stressed that impeachment was premature, emphasizing the need to wait for investigations by Special Counsel Robert Mueller and New York prosecutors before any formal action. While some House Democrats, including Reps. Brad Sherman and Al Green, have already introduced impeachment articles alleging obstruction of justice, senior Democrats warn that a partisan impeachment lacking Senate support could backfire in 2020. Pelosi defended Tlaib’s right to speak but reiterated that impeachment should not be driven by politics. Republicans, led by Rep. Kevin McCarthy, condemned Tlaib’s language and questioned Democratic decorum. The episode highlights growing pressure from the liberal wing for impeachment versus the party leadership’s cautious strategy.  

END OF SUMMARY.
