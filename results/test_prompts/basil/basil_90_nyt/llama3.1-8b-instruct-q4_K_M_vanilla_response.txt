SUMMARY:

Freshman House Democrat Representative Rashida Tlaib sparked controversy when she promised to impeach President Trump at a celebration near the Capitol on Thursday night, using profanity in her remarks. Her comments were widely criticized by Republicans and some Democrats, who view impeachment as premature. However, many Democrats are pushing for investigations into Trump's administration, with several articles of impeachment already introduced. Representative Jerrold Nadler, chairman of the House Judiciary Committee, said it is too early to talk about impeachment.

Trump seized on Tlaib's comments, asking in a tweet how one can impeach a president who has won a great election and stating that he cannot be impeached because he is doing a good job. Speaker Nancy Pelosi defended Tlaib but also emphasized the importance of following facts before taking any action. The Constitution grants the House power to impeach officials for high crimes and misdemeanors, but what constitutes such crimes is traditionally defined by the majority party.

END OF SUMMARY.
