SUMMARY:  
Within 24 hours of the 116th Congress swearing‑in, freshman Democrat Rep. Rashida Tlaib’s profanity‑filled promise to **impeach** President Trump shattered the new Congress’s tentative **reopening** spirit and forced the president to ask Speaker Nancy Pelosi if Democrats intended to **impeach** him. Republicans seized on Tlaib’s remarks, **criticizing** her and, in some outlets, **referring** to her with racially tinged slurs, while Trump **threatened** a prolonged **shutdown** unless he received border‑wall funding. Pelosi and senior Democrats warned that premature **impeachment** talk was politically dangerous, insisting they would wait for the Mueller **investigation** and any evidence of **crime**, **treason**, **bribery**, or other **high crimes and misdemeanors**. Nonetheless, other House members, including Rep. Brad Sherman, introduced articles alleging Trump **obstructed** justice by firing James Comey, accusing him of **obstruction**, **collaborated** with Russia, and other **wrongdoing**. Democrats argued they must not **impeach** for purely **political** reasons nor avoid it for the same, fearing a **martyr** narrative that could **threaten** the nation’s democratic institutions. Republicans dismissed the **behavior**, demanding censure of Tlaib and questioning the new majority’s **conduct**.  
END OF SUMMARY.
