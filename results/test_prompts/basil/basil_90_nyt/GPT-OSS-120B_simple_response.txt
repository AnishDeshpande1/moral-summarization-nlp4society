SUMMARY:  
The article frames the impeachment debate as a moral clash over the proper use of constitutional power versus political expediency. Freshman Democrat Rashida Tlaib’s profane vow to “impeach the motherfucker” ignited a firestorm, prompting President Trump to publicly question whether a president who “wins” can be removed. Republicans seized on Tlaib’s remarks to paint Democrats as hostile and even invoked racial and religious slurs, while Democratic leaders cautioned that impeachment is a grave responsibility that must be grounded in facts, not partisan fervor. House leaders stress that impeachment should address “treason, bribery or other high crimes” and warn that a premature, politically motivated effort could backfire, strengthening Trump’s base. Yet they also refuse to silence dissent, defending Tlaib’s right to “speak truth to power” and pledging investigations into alleged wrongdoing. The moral framing underscores a tension: the duty to defend democratic institutions against alleged abuses versus the risk of weaponizing impeachment for electoral gain.  

END OF SUMMARY.
