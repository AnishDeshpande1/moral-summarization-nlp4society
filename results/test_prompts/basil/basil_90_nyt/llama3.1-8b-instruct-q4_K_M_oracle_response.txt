SUMMARY:

A new Congress began, but the impeachment threat loomed large over Washington. Representative Rashida Tlaib's expletive-laden promise to impeach President Trump sparked outrage and criticism from Republicans, who sought to portray Democrats as out to destroy his presidency. Trump himself invoked the issue, asking if Democrats planned to impeach him during a meeting with Speaker Nancy Pelosi. Despite her denial of any intention to impeach, the president's frank embrace of the topic was unprecedented.

Meanwhile, Tlaib stood by her comments, stating that she would "always speak truth to power." Her remarks were at odds with Democratic talking points, which emphasize waiting for the findings of Robert Mueller's investigation before considering impeachment. However, many Democrats are privately harboring suspicions about Trump's actions, including obstruction of justice and collaboration with Russia.

As investigations into wrongdoing around the president begin, Republicans seized on Tlaib's comments to threaten her credibility and that of the Democratic Party. The stage is set for a potentially divisive and contentious process, with both sides accusing each other of politicizing impeachment.

END OF SUMMARY.
