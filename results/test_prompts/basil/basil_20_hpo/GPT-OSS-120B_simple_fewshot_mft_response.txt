SUMMARY:
In a combative CBS interview, Rick Santorum reinforced his attacks on President Obama’s “phony theology,” asserting that the president’s worldview subverts traditional Christian authority and places the Earth above human beings. Santorum framed his criticism of federal policies as a defense of moral sanctity, arguing that mandatory prenatal testing—by leading to abortions and “culling” disabled lives—violates the care owed to the unborn and degrades societal values. He further claimed that forcing health insurers to cover such testing is unfair, cheating taxpayers out of honest choice, and that federally‑run public education is an outdated, unjust system that disrespects parental authority and the sanctity of local control. Obama campaign aide Robert Gibbs condemned Santorum’s remarks as “well over the line,” accusing the GOP of a race‑to‑the‑bottom mindset that disqualifies opponents and harms civil discourse. The exchange underscores deep moral divisions over authority, care for the vulnerable, fairness in public policy, and the perceived degradation of sacred principles.  

END OF SUMMARY.
