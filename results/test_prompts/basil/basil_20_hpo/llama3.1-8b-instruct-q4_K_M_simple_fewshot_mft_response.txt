SUMMARY:

Rick Santorum defended his controversial remarks on President Obama's policies, claiming they are not based on the Bible. He stated that Obama's worldview prioritizes the Earth's welfare over human well-being, and that humans should use resources wisely but not serve the Earth as the primary objective. Santorum also doubled down on his claims that prenatal testing results in abortions, and that federally provided education is "anachronistic." He suggested that health insurance providers should not be forced to cover prenatal testing, and that local communities and parents should control public education, rather than the federal government.

The Obama campaign responded strongly, calling Santorum's remarks a personal affront and an attack on the President's character and faith. They argued that it's time to focus on debating political positions, not questioning each other's faith or worldview. The exchange highlights the divisive tone of the GOP primary, with some critics accusing Santorum of attempting to centralize power and limit individual freedom.

The issue at hand raises concerns about Authority/Subversion, as Santorum is challenging Obama's policies and authority. It also touches on Care/Harm, as Santorum suggests that prenatal testing results in more abortions and harm to the disabled. The debate over education policy also involves Fairness/Cheating, with Santorum arguing that local communities should have control over public education rather than the federal government. Finally, the discussion of Obama's policies being "anachronistic" relates to Sanctity/Degradation, as it implies a lack of respect for established norms and values.
