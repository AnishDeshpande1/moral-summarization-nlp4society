SUMMARY:

In an interview on CBS's "Face the Nation," Republican presidential candidate Rick Santorum defended his recent comments on abortion, education, and President Obama's policies. He claimed that prenatal testing leads to more abortions, and argued that federally provided education is "anachronistic." Santorum also stated that Obama's policies are not based on the Bible, saying they are a "phony ideal" of environmentalism. When asked about his comments, Santorum insisted that he was not questioning Obama's faith, but rather criticizing his worldview.

Santorum's remarks have been criticized by the Obama campaign, with aide Robert Gibbs calling them "well over the line." Gibbs accused the Republican presidential hopefuls of engaging in a "race to the bottom" and making disqualifying comments about each other. Santorum's interview on CBS came after he made similar comments at a Tea Party event in Ohio, where he dismissed Obama's policies as being based on "some phony theology."

END OF SUMMARY.
