SUMMARY:
In a Sunday interview on CBS’s “Face the Nation,” Rick Santorum doubled down on his controversial remarks, defending his claim that free prenatal testing leads to more abortions, arguing that government‑run public education is “anachronistic,” and insisting President Obama’s policies are rooted in a “phony theology” not based on the Bible. Santorum reiterated that he does not question Obama’s faith, but criticizes the president’s worldview as a “phony ideal” and a “radical” environmentalist agenda that places the Earth above man. He described prenatal testing as a “fact” that results in abortions and urged that insurers not be forced to cover it. Santorum also likened federally controlled schools to a poorly designed car, saying local communities and parents should steer education. Obama campaign aide Robert Gibbs condemned the rhetoric as “well over the line” and warned that the GOP primary has become a “race to the bottom.” END OF SUMMARY.
