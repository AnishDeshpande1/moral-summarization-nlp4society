SUMMARY:  
In a Sunday interview on CBS’s “Face the Nation,” Republican Senator Rick Santorum reiterated several controversial statements. He defended his claim that prenatal testing leads to more abortions and argued that health‑insurance plans should not be required to cover such tests. Santorum also described federally provided public education as “anachronistic,” suggesting it should be customized by local communities and parents, likening it to buying a car. He criticized President Obama’s policies as rooted in a “phony theology” not based on the Bible, though he said he does not question Obama’s personal faith. Santorum linked radical environmentalism to this “phony ideal,” asserting that humanity’s purpose is to steward resources, not serve the earth. Obama campaign spokesman Robert Gibbs condemned Santorum’s remarks as “well over the line,” accusing the GOP of a “race to the bottom.”  

END OF SUMMARY.
