SUMMARY:
Rick Santorum doubled down on a series of controversial, religiously‑tinged positions in a Sunday interview on CBS’s “Face the Nation.” He reiterated that he does not doubt President Obama’s professed Christianity, but condemned the president’s “worldview” as a “phony theology” that, in Santorum’s view, puts the Earth above people. Santorum also claimed that federally‑run public education is “anachronistic” and should be handed to local parents, likening schools to cars that must be customized for the consumer. Most provocatively, he argued that free prenatal testing leads to more abortions and the “culling” of disabled children, insisting that insurers should not be required to cover such tests. Obama campaign aide Robert Gibbs denounced Santorum’s rhetoric as “well over the line,” accusing the GOP of a “race to the bottom” that disqualifies opponents on character rather than policy. Santorum’s remarks raise serious moral concerns about the devaluation of disability, the politicization of faith, and the erosion of constructive public discourse.  

END OF SUMMARY.
