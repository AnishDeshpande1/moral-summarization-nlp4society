SUMMARY: In a sharply moralized interview on CBS’s “Face the Nation,” Republican hopeful Rick Santorum doubled down on a series of controversial, religion‑laden statements. He defended his claim that prenatal testing leads to abortions, arguing that “free prenatal testing ends up in more abortions and therefore culls the ranks of the disabled,” and urged that insurers not be forced to cover such tests. Santorum also denounced federally run public schools as “anachronistic,” insisting that education should be locally controlled and tailored like a consumer‑chosen car. He attacked President Obama’s worldview, calling it a “phony theology” not based on the Bible, and dismissed environmental stewardship as a misguided ideal that places the earth above humanity. While Santorum insisted he does not question Obama’s personal faith, he framed the president’s policies as morally inferior to a biblically grounded vision for America. Obama campaign spokesman Robert Gibbs condemned Santorum’s rhetoric as “well over the line,” warning that such moralizing drives the GOP race “to the bottom.” The exchange highlighted a stark moral clash over faith, government responsibility, and the value of human life. END OF SUMMARY.
