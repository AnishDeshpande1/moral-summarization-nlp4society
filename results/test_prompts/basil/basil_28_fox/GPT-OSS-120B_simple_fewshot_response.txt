SUMMARY:
In the vice‑presidential debate, Joe Biden adopted an aggressive, almost combative style to make up for President Obama’s muted performance in the prior presidential debate. Biden peppered Paul Ryan with grins, snickers and interruptions, repeatedly attacking the Romney‑Ryan ticket on issues ranging from the “47 percent” comment to Medicare, foreign policy and the Libya attack. He framed the Republicans as out‑of‑touch elitists who would raise costs for seniors and resort to military solutions, while positioning himself as the fierce defender of the middle class and a “happy warrior” for ordinary Americans. Ryan countered by accusing Obama of weak foreign policy, a shoddy economic recovery and turning Medicare into a “piggy bank” for ObamaCare, but Biden dismissed these points as “malarkey.” The debate was portrayed as a crucial reset for the Obama campaign, with Biden’s feisty approach cast as a moral stand against the Republican ticket’s alleged indifference and policy failures. The outcome of the debate could influence a race that is already tightening in the polls.  
END OF SUMMARY.
