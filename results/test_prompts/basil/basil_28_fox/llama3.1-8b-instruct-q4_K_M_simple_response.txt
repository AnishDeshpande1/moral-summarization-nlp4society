SUMMARY:

Vice President Biden clashed with Republican running mate Paul Ryan in the vice presidential debate, setting an aggressive tone against Romney's criticisms of Obama's policies. Biden went on offense, attacking Romney for his comments about the "47 percent" of Americans who don't pay federal income taxes and criticizing the administration's handling of the Libya terror attack. Ryan shot back, accusing Obama of projecting weakness with his foreign policy and presiding over a shoddy recovery.

The debate focused on key issues such as the economy, budget deficit, and foreign policy, with both candidates appealing to the middle class. Biden defended the Obama administration's policies while Ryan criticized them for being ineffective. The pressure was on Biden to recapture momentum after Obama's lackluster performance in the first presidential debate, but it remains to be seen whether the vice presidential debate will alter the course of the election.

END OF SUMMARY.
