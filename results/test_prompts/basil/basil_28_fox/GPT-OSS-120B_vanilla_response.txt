SUMMARY: In the vice‑presidential debate, Vice President Joe Biden adopted an aggressive, confrontational style, aiming to counter the criticism of President Obama’s muted performance in the earlier presidential debate. Biden repeatedly challenged Republican running mate Paul Ryan and the Romney ticket, attacking Mitt Romney’s “47 percent” remark and accusing the GOP of jeopardizing Medicare and the middle class. Ryan defended the Republican stance, criticizing Obama’s foreign‑policy handling of the Benghazi attack, the economic recovery, and warning of a looming debt crisis. The debate was marked by frequent interruptions, sharp exchanges on the economy, taxes, foreign policy, and social issues such as abortion and Iran. Polls show Romney gaining ground on Obama, and both campaigns view the vice‑presidential showdown as pivotal ahead of the remaining presidential debates in October. END OF SUMMARY.
