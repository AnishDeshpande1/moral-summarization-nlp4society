SUMMARY:

Vice President Biden went on offense against Paul Ryan, trying to hit the reset after President Obama's lackluster performance in the previous debate. Biden attacked Romney over his comment about the "47 percent" of Americans who don't pay federal income taxes, saying he's had it up to here with those kinds of statements. Ryan shot back, accusing Obama of projecting weakness on foreign policy, particularly in response to the terror attack on the U.S. Consulate in Libya. He also accused the administration of presiding over a shoddy recovery and a growing deficit.

The debate turned into an uncharacteristically feisty affair, with both candidates frequently interrupting each other. Biden repeatedly suggested that Romney and Ryan would too readily choose to strike at Iran's nuclear program, while Ryan warned of a coming debt crisis if the administration doesn't take drastic action. The moderator criticized the Obama administration for trying to shift the blame for the Libya attack from the terrorists to Romney's criticism.

The debate will likely have little impact on the overall campaign, but it may alter the tone and direction of the remaining debates. The polls have started to swing in Romney's favor, with him now leading in some battleground states.

END OF SUMMARY.
