SUMMARY:

Vice President Joe Biden took on a more aggressive tone in the vice presidential debate, trying to make up for President Obama's lackluster performance last week. Biden attacked Paul Ryan and the Romney/Ryan ticket on various issues, including taxes, Libya, and foreign policy. He criticized Romney's comment about the "47 percent" of Americans who don't pay federal income taxes, saying it was a personal attack. Ryan shot back, accusing Obama of projecting weakness in his response to the terror attack on the U.S. Consulate in Benghazi.

The debate was marked by frequent interruptions and banter between the two candidates. Biden defended the administration's handling of Libya, while Ryan criticized its response as weak. The two candidates also clashed on issues like Medicare and abortion. Analysts said Biden bordered on being too hot at times, but he managed to challenge Ryan's points effectively.

The debate's outcome is uncertain, with polls showing Romney gaining momentum in recent days.
