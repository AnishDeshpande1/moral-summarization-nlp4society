SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump defended his former national security adviser Michael Flynn, portraying Flynn as a loyal servant whose life was destroyed after he “lied” to the FBI. By contrasting Flynn’s fate with that of his 2016 rival Hillary Clinton—whom Trump claims “lied many times to the FBI” without consequence—Trump frames the episode as a stark fairness issue, accusing the justice system of cheating and applying a double standard. He invokes his presidential authority to dismiss concerns about obstruction, citing his lawyer John Dowd’s claim that a president cannot obstruct justice because the Constitution makes him the chief law‑enforcement officer. This appeal to authority subverts congressional and investigative oversight, while emphasizing care for a trusted ally and denigrating a political opponent as undeserving of punishment. The narrative casts Trump’s defense of Flynn as an act of loyalty, painting the investigation as an unjust attack on a faithful insider and a biased assault that harms the loyal while sparing the disloyal. The article thus intertwines authority, care, fairness, and loyalty to critique the legal scrutiny of both Flynn and the president.

END OF SUMMARY.
