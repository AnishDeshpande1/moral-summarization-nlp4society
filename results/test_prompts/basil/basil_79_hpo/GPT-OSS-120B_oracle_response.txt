SUMMARY: President Donald Trump defended former national security adviser Gen. Michael Flynn, saying Flynn’s life has been “ruined” and “destroyed” after he pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI. Trump contrasted Flynn’s fate with Hillary Clinton’s “use” of a private email server, claiming Clinton “lied many times” to the FBI and “nothing happened” to her, calling the disparity “unfair.” Flynn’s guilty plea marked the first “charges” against a former administration official in Special Counsel Robert Mueller’s “collusion” probe. The indictment makes it harder for Trump to “distance” himself from the investigation, especially after Flynn admitted he “misled” the transition team about his “contacts” with Russian officials, including discussions of “sanctions” and a “backchannel” to the Kremlin. Trump and lawyer John Dowd now argue the president cannot “obstruct justice” because, as chief law‑enforcement officer, he can “express” his view of any case. The president also previously said he “fired” FBI Director James Comey with “this Russia thing” in mind, and Comey testified that Trump asked him to “end” the Flynn investigation. END OF SUMMARY.
