SUMMARY: 
President Donald Trump defended former national security adviser Michael Flynn, saying his life was "ruined" due to lying to the FBI. Trump contrasted this with Hillary Clinton's use of a private email server, claiming she lied many times but faced no consequences. This defense comes as Flynn pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI in special counsel Robert Mueller's investigation into possible collusion between Trump's campaign and Russia. The president's words raise questions about his own involvement in the scandal, as he and his lawyer John Dowd claim that Trump already knew about Flynn's lies and that he had the right to express his views on the case.

Dowd argued that as President, Trump cannot be guilty of obstructing justice because he is the chief law enforcement officer. This statement has sparked further debate over whether Trump's actions constitute obstruction of justice, particularly given his previous statements about firing FBI Director James Comey due to the Russia investigation.

END OF SUMMARY.
