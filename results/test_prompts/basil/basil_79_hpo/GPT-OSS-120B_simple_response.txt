SUMMARY: President Donald Trump publicly defended former national security adviser Michael Flynn by casting his legal troubles as a moral injustice, contrasting Flynn’s ruined life with former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton’s apparently untouchable status despite her own lies to the FBI. Trump lamented that “Flynn lied, and it destroyed his life,” while accusing Clinton of lying “many times … and nothing happened to her,” framing the situation as a stark double standard. The article notes that Flynn had just pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI during the Mueller investigation, the first former administration official to be charged, and that his false statements involved misleading the transition team about contacts with Russian officials. Trump and his lawyer, John Dowd, have repeatedly denied any personal involvement in the Russia probe, now arguing that the president cannot obstruct justice because, as chief law‑enforcement officer, he is constitutionally entitled to express his views on any case. This moral framing highlights Trump’s narrative of selective accountability and an appeal to presidential immunity. END OF SUMMARY.
