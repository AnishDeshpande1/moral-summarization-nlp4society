SUMMARY:
President Trump defended his former national security adviser, Michael Flynn, by invoking Hillary Clinton's use of a private email server. Trump claimed that Clinton lied to the FBI and faced no consequences, while Flynn's lies "destroyed his life." However, this narrative ignores the fact that Flynn pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI in special counsel Robert Mueller's investigation into possible collusion between Trump's campaign and Russia. Trump's defense raises questions about whether he obstructed justice by firing then-FBI Director James Comey with the "Russia thing" in mind, and requesting that Comey end the agency's investigation into Flynn. Trump's lawyer, John Dowd, attempted to justify this action by claiming that as president, Trump has the right to express his view on any case, but this argument is legally dubious. The incident highlights the ongoing controversy surrounding Trump's involvement in the Russia investigation and his potential interference with justice.
