SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump again tried to shield former national security adviser Michael Flynn by portraying his guilty plea for lying to the FBI as a tragic, one‑sided injustice. Trump repeatedly claimed that Hillary Clinton “lied many times to the FBI and nothing happened to her,” using the comparison to suggest a double standard that “ruined” Flynn’s life. The article notes that Flynn’s plea ties him directly to the Mueller probe into Russian interference and raises fresh questions about whether Trump himself obstructed justice, especially after Trump’s own statements about firing FBI Director James Comey and his lawyer John Dowd’s claim that a president cannot obstruct justice. By framing Trump’s remarks as a deflection that pits a former opponent against a convicted official, the piece underscores a pattern of blame‑shifting that seeks to undermine the integrity of the investigation and the rule of law.

END OF SUMMARY.
