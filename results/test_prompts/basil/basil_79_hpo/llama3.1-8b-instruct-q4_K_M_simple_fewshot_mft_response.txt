SUMMARY:

President Trump defended his former national security adviser Michael Flynn, claiming that Hillary Clinton lied many times to the FBI without consequences. Trump argued that this is unfair, stating that Flynn's life has been "ruined" due to his guilty plea for lying to the FBI. However, Trump's defense raises questions about his own involvement in the Russia investigation and potential obstruction of justice. The president's lawyer, John Dowd, attempted to clarify that Trump isn't guilty of obstructing justice because he is the chief law enforcement officer under Article II of the Constitution.

The moral framing of this article highlights concerns about Authority/Subversion, as Trump's actions appear to undermine the authority of institutions like the FBI and the special counsel investigation. The article also touches on Fairness/Cheating, as Trump implies that Clinton received preferential treatment while Flynn was held accountable for his lies. Furthermore, the article raises questions about Loyalty/Betrayal, particularly in regards to Flynn's betrayal of trust by lying to the transition team and the FBI.

The article also subtly mentions Care/Harm, as Flynn's life has been "ruined" due to his guilty plea, highlighting the consequences of his actions. Overall, Trump's defense of Flynn is seen as an attempt to deflect attention from his own potential wrongdoing and to undermine the legitimacy of the investigation.
