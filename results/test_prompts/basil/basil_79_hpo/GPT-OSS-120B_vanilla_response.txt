SUMMARY: President Donald Trump defended former national security adviser Michael Flynn, lamenting that Flynn’s life was “ruined” after pleading guilty to lying to the FBI during the Mueller investigation. Trump juxtaposed Flynn’s punishment with former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton’s alleged false statements to the FBI about her email server, claiming Clinton faced no consequences. Flynn’s guilty plea marks the first indictment of a former administration official in the probe into possible Trump‑Russia collusion. The case complicates Trump’s attempts to distance himself from the investigation, especially as he and his lawyer John Dowd argue that the president cannot obstruct justice because, as chief law‑enforcement officer, he has the right to express his views on any case. The article also references prior allegations that Trump fired FBI Director James Comey to halt the Russia probe and that he asked Comey to stop the Flynn investigation. END OF SUMMARY.
