SUMMARY:  
The House Intelligence Committee’s first hearing after the release of Robert Mueller’s 300‑page report descended into partisan conflict. Republicans demanded Chairman Adam Schiff resign, likening his actions to McCarthy‑era witch hunts and accusing him of promoting false information. Schiff refused, insisting the committee must continue investigating possible Russian interference and alleged misconduct by President Trump, his campaign, and associates such as Jared Kushner and Paul Manafort. Attorney General William Barr’s brief summary of Mueller’s findings was deemed insufficient by Democrats, who argue the report shows a troubling pattern of wrongdoing. Republicans, including Kevin McCarthy and Michael Turner, claimed Mueller had cleared Trump of conspiracy, while Democrats, led by Pelosi and Jim Himes, defended Schiff and pledged to keep probing. The hearing featured experts warning that Putin’s aggression will intensify. In contrast, the Senate Intelligence Committee, chaired by Richard Burr, proceeds with a steadier pace and has scheduled another Kushner interview.  

END OF SUMMARY.
