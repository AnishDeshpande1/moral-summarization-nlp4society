SUMMARY:

The House Intelligence Committee's hearing on Russia's intervention in the 2016 presidential election turned into a heated debate, fueled by Republican demands for Chairman Adam Schiff's resignation. Representative Kevin McCarthy compared Schiff to Joseph R. McCarthy, accusing him of "chasing after Russian collusion" and undermining faith in US government institutions. Schiff defended himself, stating that he was not about to step down or absolve President Trump's campaign from conduct that is considered immoral, unethical, and unpatriotic.

The committee's investigation revealed disturbing evidence of wrongdoing by Trump's associates, including a secret back channel with Russia and direct contact with Russian intelligence hackers. Schiff condemned these actions as corrupt and unacceptable, saying they undermined US national security. Democrats backed him, while Republicans accused Schiff of abusing his position to promote false information.

The Senate Intelligence Committee's chairman, Senator Richard M. Burr, said he aimed to finish witness interviews in the coming weeks and issue reports on the panel's findings. Meanwhile, the House committee's investigation continues, with new evidence emerging that Russia's threat to US democracy is bolder than ever.

END OF SUMMARY.
