SUMMARY:  
In a sharply divided House Intelligence Committee hearing, Republicans immediately demanded Chairman Adam Schiff’s resignation, likening his pursuit of the Russia‑2016 probe to “McCarthyism.”  Schiff refused, insisting that the undisclosed details of Mueller’s 300‑page report demand a full inquiry and condemning alleged Trump‑campaign contacts with Russian intelligence as “immoral, unethical, unpatriotic and corrupt.”  Democrats rallied behind Schiff, praising his “calm, professional, patriotic” stance and pledging to protect the committee’s work, while Republicans accused him of abusing his position and spreading falsehoods.  President Trump joined the attack, calling for Schiff’s removal.  Amid the clash, experts warned that Putin’s aggression against Western democracies is intensifying, underscoring the stakes of the investigation.  The Senate Intelligence Committee, by contrast, continued its probe quietly and methodically.  The hearing highlighted a moral battle: Democrats framing the inquiry as a necessary defense of democratic integrity, Republicans casting it as a partisan witch‑hunt.  
END OF SUMMARY.
