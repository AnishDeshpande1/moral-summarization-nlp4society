SUMMARY:
The House Intelligence Committee’s first post‑Mueller hearing erupted into a partisan showdown, exposing a deep moral divide in Congress over the Russia investigation. Republicans, led by Kevin McCarthy, demanded Chairman Adam Schiff’s resignation, likening his pursuit of alleged collusion to “McCarthyism,” and accusing him of abusing his position and spreading falsehoods. By contrast, Schiff refused to step down, condemning the Trump campaign’s secret contacts with Russian officials as “immoral, unethical, unpatriotic and corrupt.” He argued that the committee’s role is to uncover foreign compromise, not wait for criminal prosecutions, and warned that the president’s son‑in‑law’s covert back‑channel with Moscow was a grave breach of national security. Democrats rallied behind Schiff, praising his “calm, professional, patriotic” conduct and framing the Republicans’ attack as an attempt to shield the president from accountability. The episode underscores a moral battle: Republicans portray Mueller’s report as exoneration and accuse Democrats of a political witch‑hunt, while Democrats contend that the truth about Russian interference and Trump’s conduct must be pursued despite partisan pressure. END OF SUMMARY.
