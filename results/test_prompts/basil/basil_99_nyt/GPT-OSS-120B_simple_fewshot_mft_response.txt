SUMMARY:
The House Intelligence Committee’s first post‑Mueller hearing quickly descended into a battle over authority, with Republicans demanding Chairman Adam Schiff resign and accusing him of subverting legitimate oversight. Republicans framed their demand as a defense of institutional integrity, likening Schiff’s pursuit of “Russian collusion” to McCarthy‑era witch‑hunts, while portraying themselves as loyal guardians of the nation against partisan abuse. Schiff, in turn, condemned the alleged Russian contacts of Trump’s son‑in‑law and associates as immoral, unethical, unpatriotic and corrupt—invoking a sanctity narrative that the president’s circle had degraded American values. Democrats defended Schiff’s authority, calling his conduct patriotic and fair, and warned that Republican attacks betrayed the committee’s mission and the public’s trust. President Trump joined the subversive chorus, demanding Schiff’s resignation as punishment for “lying and leaking.” The clash highlighted competing claims of fairness versus cheating, loyalty to party versus loyalty to the Constitution, and a struggle over who holds the moral high ground in protecting the nation’s integrity. END OF SUMMARY.
