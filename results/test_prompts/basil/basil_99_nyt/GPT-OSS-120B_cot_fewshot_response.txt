**SUMMARY:**  
During the first House Intelligence Committee hearing after the Mueller report, Republicans moved to force Chairman Adam Schiff’s resignation, accusing him of **lying**, **false** claims and **abuse** of his chairmanship. Rep. Kevin McCarthy likened Schiff to Senator Joseph McCarthy, while Rep. Michael Turner warned that Schiff was “chasing after Russian collusion” with **immoral**, **unethical**, **unpatriotic** and **corrupt** tactics. Schiff rejected the demand, insisting the president’s son‑in‑law’s secret back‑channel with Russia and contacts with the GRU were **not OK** and calling such conduct **immoral**, **unethical**, **unpatriotic** and **corrupt**. Democrats defended Schiff, labeling the Republican charge as **irresponsible** and bordering on **criminal** behavior, and praised his “calm, professional, patriotic” leadership. President Trump joined the attack, calling Schiff’s actions **unlawful** and demanding his resignation. The showdown highlighted the deep partisan split over the Mueller findings, with each side accusing the other of **abuse**, **corruption**, and **irresponsibility** while the Senate panel continued its own investigation.
