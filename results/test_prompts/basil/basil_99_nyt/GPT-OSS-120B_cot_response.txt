**SUMMARY:**  
At the House Intelligence Committee’s first hearing after the release of Mueller’s 300‑page report, Republicans stormed the room demanding Chairman Adam Schiff’s resignation, accusing him of “McCarthyism,” abuse of his position and spreading false, unpatriotic, unethical and even criminal narratives. Schiff rebuffed the attacks, calling the alleged Trump‑Kushner‑Russia contacts immoral, corrupt and a breach of national security, and refused to step down until the full report was examined. Democrats rallied behind Schiff, labeling his work “patriotic” and “professional,” while President Trump and GOP leaders called for his removal, branding his actions as lies and abuse. The hearing, attended by experts warning of a growing “Putinism” threat, highlighted the deep partisan divide, with the Senate panel continuing its investigation quietly. The clash underscored competing moral frames: Republicans portraying Schiff as a dangerous conspiracist, Democrats depicting him as a defender of integrity against corrupt, unethical conduct.
