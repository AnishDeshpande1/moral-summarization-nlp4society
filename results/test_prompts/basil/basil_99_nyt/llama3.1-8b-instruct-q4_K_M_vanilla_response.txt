SUMMARY:

The House Intelligence Committee's hearing on Russia's intervention in the 2016 presidential election turned into a heated debate between Democrats and Republicans. Republicans demanded Chairman Adam Schiff's resignation, accusing him of promoting false information about President Trump's campaign. Schiff refused to step down, saying he would not absolve Trump or his campaign from wrongdoing without seeing Mueller's report first.

The hearing was marked by acrimony, with Representative Kevin McCarthy comparing Schiff to Senator Joseph R. McCarthy and calling his actions "McCarthyism." Democrats backed Schiff, accusing Republicans of turning a blind eye to Russian interference.

The episode highlighted the deep divide in Congress on the issue of Russian election interference. The Senate Intelligence Committee, led by Chairman Richard M. Burr, has continued its investigation with a bipartisan approach, while the House committee remains deeply divided.

END OF SUMMARY.
