SUMMARY: In a Council on Foreign Relations speech, Senator Marco Rubio unveiled a “Rubio Doctrine” that frames U.S. foreign policy as a moral duty to protect American strength, economic liberty, and core values. He pledges to keep the military fully funded even in “peaceful” times, positioning the United States as a vigilant defender against violations of international waters, airspace, cyberspace and outer space, as well as against economic aggression. Rubio’s broader vision stresses the spread of economic and political freedom, reinforcement of alliances, and resistance to great‑power bullying of smaller nations. He also commits to transparent foreign aid and the protection of vulnerable groups—especially women and religious minorities—arguing that America must hear the cries of the oppressed and champion their freedom. The moral framing casts U.S. power as a righteous force for liberty and humanitarian relief worldwide. END OF SUMMARY.
