SUMMARY:
Senator Marco Rubio, a potential presidential candidate, outlined his foreign policy approach in a speech before the Council on Foreign Relations. His doctrine has three main pillars: ensuring American strength, protecting the economy in a globalized world, and preserving America's core values. Rubio emphasized the need to "adequately fund" the military, even in times of peace, due to the unstable world we live in today. He also promised to use American power to oppose international law violations, including economic disruptions caused by invasions.

Rubio's vision for America's role in the world involves supporting the spread of freedom and economic prosperity, reinforcing alliances, resisting large powers' attempts to dominate smaller neighbors, and advancing human rights. Specifically, he pledged to protect women and minority groups from persecution, demonstrating a commitment to moral clarity in foreign policy. By highlighting his defense hawk credentials and vision for American leadership, Rubio aims to differentiate himself in the crowded primary field. END OF SUMMARY.
