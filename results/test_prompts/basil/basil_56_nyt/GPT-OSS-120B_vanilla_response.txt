SUMMARY: Senator Marco Rubio will present his foreign‑policy doctrine to the Council on Foreign Relations, positioning it as a “saltier” version of the Obama approach and a contrast to the Bush Doctrine’s preventive strikes. Rubio’s plan rests on three pillars: maintaining American military strength, protecting the U.S. economy in a globalized world, and upholding America’s core moral values. He pledges to fully fund the military even in peacetime, oppose violations of international waters, airspace, cyberspace and outer space, and counter economic disruption from invasions. Rubio also promises to promote economic and political freedom abroad, strengthen alliances, resist large‑power aggression toward smaller nations, ensure transparent foreign aid, and champion the rights of vulnerable groups, including women and religious minorities. The speech aims to portray him as a defense hawk and differentiate him in the Republican primary field. END OF SUMMARY.
