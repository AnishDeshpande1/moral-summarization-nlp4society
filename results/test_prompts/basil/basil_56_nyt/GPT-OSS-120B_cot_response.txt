In a Council on Foreign Relations speech, Senator Marco Rubio outlined a “Rubio Doctrine” built on three pillars: ensuring American **strength**, protecting the American economy, and preserving the **moral** **clarity** of America’s core **values**. He pledged to **adequately fund** the military even in “times of peace and stability,” emphasizing a robust **defense** posture to **oppose** violations of international waters, airspace, cyberspace, and outer space. Rubio’s broader vision calls for spreading **economic and political freedom**, reinforcing alliances, resisting large powers that seek to **subjugate** smaller neighbors, and maintaining transparent foreign‑aid programs. He stressed advancing the **rights** of the **vulnerable**, including **women** and religious minorities who are often **persecuted**, so that the afflicted peoples of the world hear their **cries**, see their **suffering**, and know the **truth**: America hears, sees, and desires their **freedom**.
