SUMMARY:
Senator Marco Rubio will use his Council on Foreign Relations speech to unveil a “Rubio Doctrine” that builds on the Bush Doctrine’s focus on self‑defense while adding a clear moral compass. He frames his foreign‑policy vision around three pillars: preserving American strength, protecting the U.S. economy in an interconnected world, and upholding the nation’s core values. Rubio vows to “adequately fund” the military even in peacetime, arguing that the world is far from stable. He pledges to employ U.S. power to defend international waters, airspace, cyberspace and outer space, and to counter economic disruption caused by aggressor states. Beyond hard power, Rubio emphasizes a moral mission: spreading economic and political freedom, reinforcing alliances, resisting great‑power domination of smaller nations, and championing the rights of vulnerable groups—women and religious minorities—so that “the afflicted peoples of the world know the truth.” By casting himself as a defense hawk with a compassionate, liberty‑driven agenda, Rubio seeks to differentiate himself in the Republican primary and present a vision of America as both the world’s strongest protector and its moral beacon.  
END OF SUMMARY.
