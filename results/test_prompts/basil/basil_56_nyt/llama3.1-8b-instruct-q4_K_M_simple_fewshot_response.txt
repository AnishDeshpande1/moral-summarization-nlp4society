SUMMARY:

Senator Marco Rubio outlined his foreign policy approach, which he calls the "Rubio Doctrine". According to Rubio, the key pillars of this doctrine are ensuring American strength, protecting the US economy in a globalized world, and preserving moral clarity. He emphasized the need for a strong military, promising to adequately fund it even in times of peace. Rubio also vowed to use American power to oppose any violations of international waters, airspace, or cyberspace. His approach is marked by a hawkish tone, with a focus on American exceptionalism and a willingness to take decisive action. Rubio's vision for the US role in the world involves supporting the spread of economic and political freedom, reinforcing alliances, and advancing human rights.

Rubio's speech was seen as a departure from the Obama administration's cautious approach to foreign policy, which he criticized for eroding America's moral leadership. His stance on key issues such as Cuba and Iran was also emphasized, with a strong emphasis on maintaining the embargo and distrusting their nuclear programs.
