SUMMARY:

Senator Marco Rubio's foreign policy approach, outlined in a speech to the Council on Foreign Relations, prioritizes American strength, economic protection, and moral clarity. Rubio's doctrine emphasizes the need for a robust military, even in times of peace, to maintain American authority and prevent harm to its citizens and allies. He vows to use American power to resist international violations, protect smaller nations from subjugation, and advance the rights of vulnerable groups, including women and religious minorities. Rubio's stance is rooted in a strong sense of moral obligation, fairness, and sanctity, positioning the US as a beacon of freedom and defender of the oppressed. His approach is a clear departure from the Obama Doctrine, which Rubio implies is too passive, and a more assertive alternative to the Bush Doctrine's focus on preventive strikes. Rubio's speech is a bid to establish himself as a defense hawk and a champion of American exceptionalism.

END OF SUMMARY.
