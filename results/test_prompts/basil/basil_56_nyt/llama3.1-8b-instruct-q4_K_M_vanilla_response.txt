SUMMARY:

Senator Marco Rubio will outline his approach to foreign policy in a speech at the Council on Foreign Relations, building on the Bush Doctrine of preventive strikes for self-defense. His three main pillars are: ensuring American strength; protecting the economy in a globalized world; and preserving America's core values. Rubio will prioritize funding the military and using American power to oppose international violations, including economic disruption caused by invasions.

Rubio's speech will also outline his vision for America's role in the world, including supporting the spread of economic and political freedom, reinforcing alliances, and advancing the rights of vulnerable populations such as women and religious minorities. He will emphasize that America hears the cries of the afflicted and desires their freedom. Rubio, a defense hawk and member of the Senate Foreign Relations and Intelligence committees, is positioning himself in the crowded primary field with a strong foreign policy stance.

END OF SUMMARY.
