SUMMARY:  
Senator Rick Santorum clarified that his criticism of President Obama’s “phony theology” was aimed at the president’s environmental policies, not his Christian faith. Santorum argued that radical environmentalists prioritize the Earth over human welfare, describing this view as a false ideal that undermines stewardship of resources. The Obama campaign condemned the remarks as a personal attack, with former press secretary Robert Gibbs calling them “destructive” and urging a focus on policy rather than character. Deputy press secretary Ben LaBolt labeled Santorum’s comments as the “latest low” in a Republican primary marked by negativity, contrasting Obama’s focus on jobs and middle‑class security. Santorum, who has recently overtaken Mitt Romney in GOP polls, reiterated that his use of “theology” referred to a worldview that elevates the planet above people and politicizes issues like global warming to expand government power. He maintained respect for Obama’s faith while disputing his approach to environmental and economic matters.  

END OF SUMMARY.
