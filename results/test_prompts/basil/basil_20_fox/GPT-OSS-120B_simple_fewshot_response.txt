SUMMARY:
Rick Santorum defended his recent remarks about President Obama’s “phony theology,” insisting he was not attacking Obama’s faith but criticizing what he called a “radical environmentalist” worldview that places the Earth above people. Santorum argued that humans should be stewards of resources, not servants of the planet, and framed his comments as a critique of policies he sees as unscientific and aimed at expanding government power. The Obama campaign, however, condemned the remarks as a personal affront and an example of the “ugliness, distortion and negativity” that has come to define the Republican primary. Former press secretary Robert Gibbs called the comments “over the line,” “destructive,” and urged a return to debate on ideas rather than attacks on character and faith. Deputy press secretary Ben LaBolt labeled Santorum’s statement “the latest low” and contrasted it with Obama’s focus on jobs and middle‑class security. While Santorum maintains he never questioned Obama’s Christianity, his language has intensified the moral criticism that the GOP race is descending into personal vilification and divisive rhetoric. END OF SUMMARY.
