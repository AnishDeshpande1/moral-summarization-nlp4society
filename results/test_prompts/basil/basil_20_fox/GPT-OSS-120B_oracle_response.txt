SUMMARY:
Republican candidate Rick Santorum told voters that President Obama’s “phony theology” reflects a radical environmentalist worldview that puts the Earth above people. Santorum stressed he does not doubt Obama’s Christian faith, but he condemned the “politicization” of climate policy as an “attempt” to “centralize” power and give the government control over resources. He argued that man should be the objective, a steward of the Earth, not its servant. The Obama campaign called Santorum’s remarks “well over the line,” labeling them a personal affront and an example of the “nastiness, divisiveness, ugliness, and distortions” that have marked the GOP primary. Press secretary Robert Gibbs said such negativity shows a mindset that disqualifies opponents rather than fostering debate. Deputy press secretary Ben LaBolt praised Obama’s focus on “creating jobs and restoring economic security” for the middle class, contrasting it with the pessimism of Santament’s attack. Both sides warned that the exchange underscores a race to the bottom in political discourse.  

END OF SUMMARY.
