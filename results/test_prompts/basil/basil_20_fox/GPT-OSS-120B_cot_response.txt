**SUMMARY:**  
Rick Santorum told voters that President Obama’s “phony theology” reflects a “radical” view that puts the Earth above people, a stance he called a “phony ideal” and “destructive.” He insisted he does not doubt Obama’s Christian faith, but criticized what he sees as a “world view” that elevates the planet over human welfare. The Obama campaign blasted Santorum’s remarks as a personal affront, labeling them “the latest low” in a primary marked by “distortions,” “ugliness,” “pessimism,” and “negativity.” Press secretary Robert Gibbs warned that such “nastiness” and “divisiveness” push the race to the bottom, urging a debate on policy rather than attacks on character or faith. Santorum, who has surged ahead of Mitt Romney in polls, maintained his focus is on stewardship of resources for humanity, not serving the Earth.
