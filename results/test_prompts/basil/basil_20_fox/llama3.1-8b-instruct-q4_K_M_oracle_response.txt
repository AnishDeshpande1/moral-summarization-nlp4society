SUMMARY:

Rick Santorum's comments about President Obama's "phony theology" have been met with strong criticism from the president's campaign. According to Robert Gibbs, Santorum's remarks are "well over the line" and constitute a personal affront. The Obama campaign sees Santorum's comments as part of a larger trend of negativity and pessimism in the Republican primary. In contrast, President Obama is focused on creating jobs and restoring economic security for the middle class.

Santorum clarified that he was not questioning the president's faith, but rather challenging his world view, which he believes prioritizes the Earth over human welfare. Santorum argued that this approach is "phony" and that man should be seen as the objective, not the Earth. The politicization of global warming has been a contentious issue in recent years, with some arguing that it is an attempt to centralize power and give more authority to the government.

The campaign's response highlights the ugliness and divisiveness that have characterized the Republican primary so far. Santorum's comments have been seen as a stark contrast to Obama's approach, which emphasizes creating opportunities for all Americans. END OF SUMMARY.
