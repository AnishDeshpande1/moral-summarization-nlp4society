SUMMARY:
Senator Rick Santorum sparked a controversy in the GOP primary by accusing President Obama of promoting a “phony theology” that elevates the Earth above people. Santorum insisted he was not questioning Obama’s Christian faith, but was condemning what he called a radical environmentalist worldview that, in his view, subverts proper human stewardship and unfairly shifts power to government. The Obama campaign rebuked the remarks as a personal attack that crosses the line of civil discourse, labeling them destructive, dishonest and an attempt to cheat the public out of honest debate. Press secretary Robert Gibbs warned that such subversive accusations undermine respect for authority and fairness in politics, while deputy press secretary Ben LaBolt called the comments the “latest low” in a campaign marked by distortion and negativity. Santorum defended his stance as a critique of policy, not character, arguing that the president’s agenda unfairly prioritizes an unproven environmental agenda over jobs and middle‑class security. The exchange highlights a clash over authority and the perceived fairness of each side’s political tactics.  
END OF SUMMARY.
