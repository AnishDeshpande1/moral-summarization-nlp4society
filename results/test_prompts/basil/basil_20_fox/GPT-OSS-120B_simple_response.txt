SUMMARY:  
Sen. Rick Santorum defended his criticism of President Obama’s “phony theology,” insisting he was not attacking Obama’s Christian faith but rather condemning what he calls a “radical environmentalist” worldview that places the Earth above human welfare. Santorum argued that humans should be stewards of resources, not servants of the planet, and labeled the environmental agenda a misguided, unscientific attempt to expand government power. Obama’s campaign responded with moral outrage, calling Santorum’s remarks a personal affront that crossed the line into character assassination and contributed to the “ugliness” and “negativity” of the GOP primary. Press officials urged a return to policy debate without questioning each other’s faith, emphasizing that the president’s focus is on jobs and middle‑class security, not on any “phony” ideology. The exchange highlighted a moral clash: Santorum framed his stance as a defense of human dignity and responsible stewardship, while the Obama team framed his comments as a destructive, faith‑based attack that undermines civil discourse.  

END OF SUMMARY.
