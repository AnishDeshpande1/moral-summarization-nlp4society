SUMMARY:

Rick Santorum, a Republican presidential candidate, sparked controversy by referring to President Obama's "phony theology" in regards to environmental policies. However, Santorum clarified that he was not questioning the president's Christian faith but rather challenging his world view on the relationship between humans and the Earth. He believes that Obama's approach prioritizes the welfare of the planet over human needs.

The Obama campaign responded strongly, calling Santorum's remarks a personal affront and "well over the line." They argued that it's time to focus on policy debates rather than questioning each other's character and faith.

Santorum countered by saying that he was challenging Obama's radical environmentalist views, which he believes elevate the Earth above human needs. He emphasized his commitment to responsible resource use and good stewardship of the Earth, but not at the expense of human welfare.

The exchange highlights the increasingly divisive tone of the Republican primary campaign and raises questions about the role of faith in public discourse.

END OF SUMMARY.
