SUMMARY:

Rick Santorum sparked controversy when he referred to President Obama's "phony theology" over the weekend, but clarified that he was actually challenging the president's environmental policies, not questioning his faith. According to Santorum, Obama's policies prioritize the Earth above human welfare, which he believes is a misguided approach. The Obama campaign responded forcefully, calling Santorum's remarks "well over the line" and "destructive." Santorum maintained that he respects Obama's Christianity but disagrees with his world view on environmental issues, suggesting that it amounts to an attempt to centralize power in government. This exchange highlights the increasingly bitter tone of the Republican primary campaign, with both sides accusing each other of personal attacks and distortions.
