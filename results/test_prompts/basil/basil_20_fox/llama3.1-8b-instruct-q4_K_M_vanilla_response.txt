SUMMARY:

Rick Santorum clarified his comments about President Obama's "phony theology" on Sunday, stating that he was actually challenging the president's environmental policies, not questioning his faith. Santorum said he believes Obama is a Christian and was referring to a "world view that elevates the Earth above man." He argued that this world view is based on unproven scientific claims about global warming and is an attempt to centralize power in government.

The Obama campaign responded forcefully, calling Santorum's remarks a personal affront and a low point in the Republican primary. They accused Santorum of questioning the president's character and faith, while also criticizing the tone of the entire GOP primary.

Santorum maintained that he was simply debating policy and not attacking the president's faith or values. However, his comments have sparked controversy and highlighted the increasingly negative tone of the 2012 Republican presidential primary.

END OF SUMMARY.
