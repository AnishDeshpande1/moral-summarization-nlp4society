SUMMARY:

Washington Gov. Jay Inslee and Seattle Mayor Ed Murray have banned government-funded travel to Indiana in response to the state's new Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which they claim allows businesses to discriminate against gay individuals and others. State lawmakers are trying to clarify the law, but critics argue that it would enable discrimination, citing examples such as a baker refusing to serve a gay couple for a wedding cake. The controversy has sparked boycott threats from cities and states across the country, with several major corporations and celebrities voicing their opposition.

The backlash against Indiana's law highlights concerns about the erosion of civil rights protections for marginalized communities. Critics argue that the law would create a "license to discriminate" and undermine progress towards equality. Proponents of the law claim it is necessary to protect religious freedom, but opponents say it would have the opposite effect by allowing businesses to deny services based on personal beliefs.

END OF SUMMARY.
