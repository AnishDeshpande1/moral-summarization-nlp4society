SUMMARY:

Washington Governor Jay Inslee and Seattle Mayor Ed Murray have imposed an administration-wide ban on state-funded travel to Indiana, citing the state's new Religious Freedom Restoration Act as "disturbing" and discriminatory towards gays and others. They join a growing list of cities and states opposing the law, which allows businesses to assert their right to "the free exercise of religion." However, lawmakers in Indiana are trying to clarify the language, with Senate President Pro Tem David Long stating that the law does not discriminate and will not be allowed to do so. The primary concern is whether businesses can now legally refuse services to certain individuals based on their religious beliefs. Other states, including Oklahoma, South Carolina, and Texas, have introduced legislation to amend or put similar laws in place. The backlash has prompted boycott threats from corporate America, Hollywood, and even the NCAA, which could impact revenue for Indianapolis-area hotels and businesses.

END OF SUMMARY.
