SUMMARY:  
Backlash surged after Indiana enacted a new Religious Freedom Restoration Act that many say could **discriminate** against gay people. Washington Gov. Jay Inslee called the law **disturbing** and **opposed** it, issuing an administration‑wide **ban** on state‑funded travel to Indiana. Seattle Mayor Ed Murray, who is openly gay, issued a similar **ban**, directing city departments to **clarify** contracts and avoid doing business with Indiana firms, saying “discrimination has no place in our city.” Critics fear businesses could **refuse** service to gay couples, invoking a religious **exercise** of belief. Indiana Republicans, including Senate President Pro Tem David Long and House Speaker Brian Bosma, defended the statute, insisting it does not **discriminate** and promising to **clarify** any ambiguous language. They warned that the law should not **burden** religious practice, even as the NCAA and corporations voice concerns about potential economic **burden** and boycotts. The controversy highlights a clash between expanding civil‑rights **embracing** and claims of religious liberty.
