SUMMARY:

Indiana's new religious freedom law, which allows businesses to assert their right to "the free exercise of religion," is facing backlash from politicians, corporations, and celebrities. Washington state Governor Jay Inslee and Seattle Mayor Ed Murray have banned government-funded travel to Indiana due to concerns that the law may allow discrimination against gay residents. State lawmakers are trying to clarify the legislation, which they claim does not discriminate. However, critics argue that it could permit businesses to deny services to certain groups. The NCAA has expressed concern about the law's implications on future events and workforce, while corporations such as Angie's List have put plans to expand in Indiana on hold. Celebrities like George Takei and Ashton Kutcher are also calling for a boycott.

END OF SUMMARY.
