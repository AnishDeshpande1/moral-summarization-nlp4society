SUMMARY: Indiana’s new Religious Freedom Restoration Act has sparked a wave of backlash, prompting Washington Governor Jay Inslee and Seattle Mayor Ed Murray to ban state‑funded travel to Indiana and review contracts with Indiana‑based companies. Critics argue the law could permit businesses to refuse services to gay individuals, such as a baker denying a wedding cake. Republican leaders, including Senate President Pro Tem David Long and House Speaker Brian Bosma, defended the legislation, saying it does not discriminate and pledging to clarify its language. They cited similar federal and state statutes, though Indiana’s version uniquely allows businesses to claim a “free exercise of religion.” The controversy has drawn national attention, with the NCAA, corporations, and celebrities like George Takei and Ashton Kutcher calling for boycotts. Fifteen other states are considering comparable religious‑freedom bills. Lawmakers aim to amend the act before the upcoming NCAA Final Four in Indianapolis, fearing economic fallout from potential boycotts. END OF SUMMARY.
