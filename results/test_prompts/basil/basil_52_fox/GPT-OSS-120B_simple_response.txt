SUMMARY:  
The Indiana Religious Freedom Restoration Act has ignited a moral outcry, prompting Washington Governor Jay Inslee and Seattle Mayor Ed Murray—who is openly gay—to ban state‑funded travel to Indiana and to scrutinize contracts with Indiana‑based firms. Both leaders condemn the law as “disturbing” and contrary to the growing national commitment to civil rights, insisting that “discrimination has no place” in their jurisdictions. Critics fear the statute could legally permit businesses to refuse service to gay couples, exemplified by the baker‑cake scenario. Indiana Republicans, including Senate President Pro Tem David Long and House Speaker Brian Bosma, defend the measure, claiming it does not discriminate and pledging to “clarify” its language, while Governor Mike Pence avoided direct answers about its impact on LGBTQ customers. The controversy has spurred broader backlash: the NCAA worries about its athletes and staff, Angie’s List paused expansion, and celebrities like George Takei and Ashton Kutcher call for boycotts. Over 15 other states are pursuing similar “religious‑freedom” bills, heightening the national debate over whether such laws mask sanctioned discrimination.  

END OF SUMMARY.
