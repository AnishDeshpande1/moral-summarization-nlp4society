SUMMARY:

Governor Jay Inslee of Washington and Seattle Mayor Ed Murray have banned government-funded travel to Indiana in response to the state's new Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which critics argue could allow businesses to discriminate against individuals based on their sexual orientation. The law, signed by Governor Mike Pence, allows a business to assert a right to "the free exercise of religion," sparking concerns that it may permit discrimination. State lawmakers have attempted to clarify the legislation, but acknowledge that they are unclear about its implications. Critics argue that the law undermines equality and fairness, with Seattle Mayor Ed Murray stating that "discrimination has no place in our city." The backlash against the law has gained national attention, with figures in corporate America and Hollywood voicing their opposition. The NCAA has expressed concern about the potential impact on future events, and several businesses have announced plans to boycott or reevaluate their involvement in Indiana.
