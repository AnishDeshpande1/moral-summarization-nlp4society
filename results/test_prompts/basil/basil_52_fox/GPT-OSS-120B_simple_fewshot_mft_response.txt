SUMMARY:
The backlash to Indiana’s new Religious Freedom Restoration Act has intensified as Washington state officials denounce the law as a subversion of civil‑rights authority and a threat to fairness. Governor Jay Inslee and Seattle Mayor Ed Murray—who is openly gay—issued executive orders banning state‑funded travel to Indiana and urging reviews of contracts with Indiana‑based firms, framing the measure as “discrimination” that violates “equality 101” and the sacred principle that all citizens deserve equal treatment. Lawmakers and Governor Mike Pence defend the statute by invoking the authority of the 1993 federal law and the rights of religious institutions, but Republican leaders David Long and Brian Bosma admit the language is unclear and pledge rapid clarification to prevent businesses from legally refusing service to gay couples. Critics argue the bill degrades the sanctity of inclusive public life by allowing religious excuses for exclusion, while supporters claim it upholds religious sanctity. The controversy has sparked national boycotts, corporate pull‑backs, and concerns from the NCAA and Hollywood, highlighting a clash between traditional authority and emerging standards of fairness and moral purity.  

END OF SUMMARY.
