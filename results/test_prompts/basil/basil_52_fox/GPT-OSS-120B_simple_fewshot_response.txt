SUMMARY:
Washington Governor Jay Inslee and Seattle Mayor Ed Murray have banned state‑funded travel to Indiana in protest of the state’s new Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which critics say could permit businesses to refuse service to gay people. Murray, an openly gay mayor, also ordered a review of city contracts with Indiana‑based companies, calling discrimination “equality 101.” Indiana lawmakers and Governor Mike Pence defend the law, pointing to the 1993 federal statute and similar measures in 19 other states, but acknowledge the need to “clarify” language that might allow refusals of service. Senate President Pro Tem David Long and House Speaker Brian Bosma said they will act quickly to prevent discrimination and to address concerns raised by the NCAA, corporate leaders and entertainers calling for a boycott. The backlash threatens Indiana’s economy ahead of the NCAA Final Four and adds Indiana to a growing list of states grappling with the balance between religious liberty and civil‑rights protections.

END OF SUMMARY.
