Washington Governor Jay Inslee and Seattle Mayor Ed Murray have banned state‑funded travel to Indiana, denouncing the state’s new Religious Freedom Restoration Act as “disturbing” and a threat to civil rights. Both leaders framed the law as a potential source of discrimination against gay residents, contradicting the values of equality and inclusion they champion. Indiana Republicans, including Senate President Pro Tem David Long and House Speaker Brian Bosma, defended the legislation, insisting it does not permit prejudice and pledging to clarify its language. The controversy has sparked a broader boycott movement, with the NCAA, corporate leaders, and celebrities like George Takei and Ashton Kutcher voicing opposition and warning of economic repercussions for Indianapolis. As other states consider similar religious‑freedom bills, the clash highlights a national debate over religious liberty versus the protection of individual rights.
