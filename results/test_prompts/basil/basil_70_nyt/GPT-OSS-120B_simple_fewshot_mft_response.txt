SUMMARY:
Donald Trump’s inauguration marked a dramatic subversion of traditional political authority, as the outsider‑businessman declared a “hostile takeover” of Washington and pledged to dismantle the “empty talk” of career politicians. Emphasizing care for the nation’s suffering, he painted a bleak picture of mothers and children trapped in poverty, rusted‑out factories and drug‑ridden streets, promising to fight “American carnage” and restore jobs, borders, wealth and dreams. He framed his agenda as a fight against systemic cheating, accusing a small elite in the capital of hoarding government rewards while ordinary Americans bore the costs, and vowed to return power to the people. Loyalty was invoked repeatedly, urging citizens to rally behind the new “populist uprising” and to reject the betrayals of both parties. The ceremony featured both supportive crowds chanting “Trump” and hostile protesters, highlighting the deep division over his challenge to established authority. Amidst limited Democratic participation and sporadic violence, Trump’s inaugural address combined appeals to protect the vulnerable, punish perceived unfairness, and demand unwavering loyalty to his vision of a reclaimed America.  
END OF SUMMARY.
