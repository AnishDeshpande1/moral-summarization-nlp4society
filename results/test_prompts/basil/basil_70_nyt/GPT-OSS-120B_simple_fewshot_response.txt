SUMMARY:
Donald Trump was inaugurated as the 45th president, framing his ascent as a populist “hostile takeover” that will end “American carnage” and restore lost greatness. In a stark 16‑minute address he painted a grim picture of poverty, rusted factories and crime, then vowed to “fight for you with every breath,” promising to bring back jobs, borders, wealth and dreams. He positioned himself as the ultimate outsider, unbound by Washington’s elite, and declared that “the time for empty talk is over.” The ceremony featured rain‑soaked supporters chanting “Make America Great Again,” while modest protests turned occasionally violent. Trump immediately began rolling back Obama policies, issuing orders to freeze new regulations and ease the Affordable Care Act. He thanked the Obamas and Clinton modestly, but Democrats dismissed his dark rhetoric as out of touch. The inauguration highlighted a stark division: enthusiastic crowds versus a boycotted, sparsely attended National Mall, underscoring the new president’s promise to return power to the people and reshape the nation’s future.  
END OF SUMMARY.
