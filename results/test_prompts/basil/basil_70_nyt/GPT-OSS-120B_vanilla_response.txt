SUMMARY: Donald J. Trump was inaugurated as the 45th U.S. president, delivering a stark, populist inaugural address that framed the nation as suffering “American carnage” and promised to “make America great again” by restoring jobs, borders, and wealth. He emphasized his outsider status, vowed to roll back Obama-era policies, and pledged protectionist, anti‑terrorism measures. The ceremony was marked by heavy rain, large but smaller crowds than previous inaugurations, and sporadic protests, including arrests and property damage. Vice President Mike Pence was sworn in shortly before Trump, who placed his hand on two historic Bibles. The day featured limited bipartisan outreach, with brief thanks to the Obamas and a nod to Hillary Clinton. Cabinet confirmations began with Defense Secretary James Mattis and Homeland Security Secretary John Kelly; the CIA nomination faced Democratic delay. Former presidents and political figures attended, while many Democrats and Washington residents boycotted. Former President Obama and Vice President Biden departed for personal trips but indicated plans to remain engaged. Trump’s ascent, as a wealthy real‑estate mogul with no prior public office, represents an unprecedented shift in American political history. END OF SUMMARY.
