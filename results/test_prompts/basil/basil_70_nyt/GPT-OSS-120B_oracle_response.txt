SUMMARY:  
Donald J. Trump was inaugurated as the 45th president, promising to **restore** a nation he described as suffering from **ravages** of economic decline, **poverty**, and **carnage**. In a rain‑soaked ceremony he pledged **action**, saying he would **bring** back jobs, borders, wealth and dreams, and would **eradicate** terrorism. His **disruption** of the political order was framed as a hostile takeover of a capital long **reaped** by elites. Immediately, he began rolling back Obama policies, issuing orders to **buy** and **hire** American workers and to **respond** to the “stolen” lives lost to drugs and crime. Protests erupted; **violence** broke out as demonstrators **smashed** windows and **burned** a limousine, prompting police to use tear gas and **arrest** over 200 people. Trump’s inaugural rhetoric mixed **truth**, **justice**, **humor** and **love** with warnings of **interference** and a pledge that power was being returned from Washington to the people. While supporters chanted “Trump! Trump!” opponents shouted “Not my president,” highlighting the deep division as the new administration promised **changes** that would end the **carnage** and reshape America.  
END OF SUMMARY.
