SUMMARY:

Donald Trump was inaugurated as the 45th President of the United States, promising to restore America's greatness and bring back its lost wealth, jobs, and borders. He described a nation ravaged by economic dislocation, foreign exploitation, and crime, vowing to eradicate terrorism and "bring back our dreams." The inauguration marked a disruption in Washington politics, with Trump positioning himself as an outsider not beholden to the current system. In his inaugural address, he accepted responsibility for the country's problems, promising action and change. However, his vision was met with criticism from Democrats, who saw it as a dark portrayal of America's failures.

Protests erupted outside the Capitol, with some violence and arrests. Trump responded by issuing executive orders, including one to freeze new regulations. He also pledged to "buy American and hire American," while vowing to eradicate terrorism. The inauguration was marked by a sense of division and tension, with many Americans feeling that their interests were being ignored.

END OF SUMMARY.
