**SUMMARY:**  
Donald J. Trump was inaugurated as the 45th U.S. president, presenting himself as an outsider ready to shatter the “established order” and end the nation’s “American carnage.” In a rain‑soaked ceremony he painted a grim picture of poverty, rusted‑out factories, crime and a dark, failing America, promising to fight for the people and bring back jobs, borders, wealth and dreams. He vowed to transfer power from a hostile Washington elite back to the citizens, declaring there is “no room for prejudice.” The day featured peaceful protests that turned violent, with animosity and hostility evident as demonstrators clashed with police and some shouted “Not my president.” Trump’s first actions included rolling back Obama’s policies and issuing orders to ease the Affordable Care Act. Cabinet nominations were partially confirmed, while Democrats boycotted parts of the event. The inauguration highlighted deep national division, with supporters chanting “Make America Great Again” and opponents expressing shock at the dark tone of the address.
