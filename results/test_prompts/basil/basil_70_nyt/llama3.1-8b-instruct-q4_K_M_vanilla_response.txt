SUMMARY:

Donald Trump was inaugurated as the 45th President of the United States on Friday, promising to "shatter the established order" and restore America's greatness. In his inaugural address, he painted a grim picture of the country, citing poverty, crime, and decay in inner cities and rust-belt areas. He vowed to bring back jobs, borders, wealth, and dreams, and to fight for Americans with every breath in his body.

Trump's ascension marked a hostile takeover of Washington, with him rejecting politicians who are "all talk and no action." He signed executive orders freezing new regulations and easing the burden on the Affordable Care Act. Protesters gathered across the city, with over 200 people arrested, but Trump's supporters were also out in force.

Trump's inauguration was notable for its dark tone and emphasis on protectionism, with him promising to "buy American and hire American." He also pledged to eradicate Islamic terrorism and denied that he demonized Mexicans and Muslims. The ceremony marked the beginning of a new era in Washington, one that is expected to be marked by significant change and upheaval.

END OF SUMMARY.
