SUMMARY:

Susan E. Rice, President Obama's national security adviser, sharply criticized Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu of Israel for his plans to address Congress without consulting the White House. Ms. Rice stated that Mr. Netanyahu's actions have injected partisanship into the relationship between the US and Israel, which is "unfortunate" and "destructive." The White House has also expressed its displeasure with Mr. Netanyahu's visit, with President Obama saying he will not meet with him to avoid any appearance of influencing the Israeli elections.

The controversy has damaged US-Israeli relations, with top administration officials hinting at their displeasure in recent days. Vice President Biden and Secretary of State Kerry are both absent from Washington during Mr. Netanyahu's visit, and a representative from the White House will not attend his speech to the American Israel Public Affairs Committee. Ms. Rice emphasized that the US wants the relationship between the two countries to be strong and unaffected by politics.

END OF SUMMARY.
