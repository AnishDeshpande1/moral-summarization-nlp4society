SUMMARY:
National Security Adviser Susan Rice harshly rebuked Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu for scheduling a partisan‑laden speech to a joint session of Congress just weeks before Israel’s elections. Rice argued that the invitation, arranged by Speaker John Boehner and the Israeli ambassador without White House clearance, violated diplomatic protocol and “injected a degree of partisanship… destructive of the fabric of the relationship.” The administration framed the episode as a breach of proper authority and a subversive move that threatens the longstanding, bipartisan U.S.–Israel alliance. President Obama echoed Rice’s concerns, refusing to meet Netanyahu to avoid the appearance of influencing the election and labeling the episode a “weakening” of the relationship. Democratic senators offered a private, bipartisan meeting to preserve fairness and balance, which Netanyahu declined, further fueling accusations of unfair, politically motivated conduct. The moral takeaway stresses that disregarding established authority and fair, non‑partisan process undermines trust and damages the essential U.S.–Israel partnership.  
END OF SUMMARY.
