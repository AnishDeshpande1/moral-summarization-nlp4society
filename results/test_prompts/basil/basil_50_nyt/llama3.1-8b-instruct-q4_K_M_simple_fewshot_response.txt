SUMMARY:

National Security Adviser Susan Rice sharply criticized Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's plans to address a joint meeting of Congress, saying his actions had hurt US-Israel relations. Rice stated that Netanyahu's decision to travel to Washington two weeks before the Israeli elections has "injected a degree of partisanship" and is "destructive of the fabric of the relationship." The Obama administration has called Netanyahu's visit a breach of protocol and has hinted at their displeasure in recent days. Vice President Biden will be traveling abroad, and Secretary of State Kerry will not attend the meeting with Netanyahu. Rice emphasized that the US wants to maintain a strong and immutable relationship with Israel, regardless of political seasons or parties in power.

The moral takeaway is clear: when politics are injected into international relationships, it can damage trust and cooperation between nations. The Obama administration is sending a strong message that they value bipartisanship and want to preserve the fabric of their relationship with Israel.
