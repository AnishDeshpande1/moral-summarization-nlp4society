SUMMARY:
National security adviser Susan E. Rice sharply rebuked Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu for planning a congressional address two weeks before Israel’s elections, saying his move “injects partisanship” and harms U.S.–Israeli relations. The speech, arranged by Speaker John Boehner and the Israeli ambassador without White House input, is seen as a breach of protocol and a political attempt to oppose the Iran nuclear deal favored by President Obama. Obama will not meet Netanyahu during the visit to avoid influencing the election, and Vice President Biden will be abroad. Secretary of State John Kerry will be in Switzerland negotiating with Iran. Democratic senators offered Netanyahu a private meeting to balance the partisan invitation, which he declined, citing concerns about perceived partisanship. Rice emphasized that the U.S.–Israel relationship must remain strong and non‑partisan, regardless of political cycles. The White House echoed Rice’s view, stating the episode weakens the bilateral bond.

END OF SUMMARY.
