SUMMARY:  
Susan E. Rice, President Obama’s national security adviser, publicly rebuked Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu for turning his upcoming Washington speech into a partisan stunt ahead of Israel’s elections. Rice warned that the timing and the way the address was arranged—without White House input and at the invitation of Speaker Boehner—“injects a degree of partisanship… destructive of the fabric of the relationship” between the United States and Israel. The Obama administration, echoing Rice’s view, said the episode breaches protocol, risks politicizing a traditionally bipartisan alliance, and weakens diplomatic ties. President Obama will not meet Netanyahu during the visit to avoid the appearance of influencing the Israeli election, and Vice President Biden will be abroad. Netanyahu insists the trip is necessary to oppose a nuclear deal with Iran, but Democrats criticize his refusal to meet privately, seeing it as further politicizing the issue. The episode underscores a moral stance that U.S.–Israeli relations should remain “unquestionably strong, immutable,” regardless of electoral politics.  

END OF SUMMARY.
