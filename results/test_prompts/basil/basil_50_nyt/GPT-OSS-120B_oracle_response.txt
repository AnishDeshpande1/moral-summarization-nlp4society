SUMMARY:
Susan E. Rice sharply rebuked Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, saying his decision to address a joint meeting of Congress injected partisan politics that have hurt the U.S.–Israel relationship. Rice called the move a breach of protocol, noting it is destructive to the fabric of the alliance and has already damaged bilateral trust. The speech, arranged by Speaker Boehner and the Israeli ambassador without White House input, was scheduled two weeks before Israel’s election, raising concerns about an obligation to influence voters. Netanyahu refused a private meeting with Democratic senators, a refusal that Durbin called disappointing and that could create a misperception of partisanship. Rice emphasized that the United States must keep its relationship with Israel strong and immutable, regardless of political seasons or which party is in power. The Obama administration echoed her assessment, warning that allowing the relationship to be subjected to party politics weakens it. Netanyahu maintains his address is meant to “prevent” a nuclear deal with Iran, not to gain political favor.  
END OF SUMMARY.
