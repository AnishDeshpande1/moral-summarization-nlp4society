SUMMARY:

Susan E. Rice, President Obama's national security adviser, criticized Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu of Israel for his plans to address a joint meeting of Congress next week. She stated that his actions had hurt the US-Israel relationship and injected partisanship into the relationship. The White House agreed with her assessment, saying that allowing party politics to influence the relationship weakens it.

The controversy began when Netanyahu accepted an invitation from Speaker John Boehner without consulting the White House, which called it a breach of protocol. President Obama will not meet with Netanyahu during his visit to avoid influencing Israeli elections. Top administration officials have hinted at their displeasure in recent days, and Vice President Biden is traveling abroad during Netanyahu's visit.

Netanyahu has defended his decision to speak to Congress, saying he wants to prevent a nuclear agreement with Iran and that the US Congress may be the final brake on such an agreement. His refusal to meet with Democratic senators has been criticized as partisanship.

END OF SUMMARY.
