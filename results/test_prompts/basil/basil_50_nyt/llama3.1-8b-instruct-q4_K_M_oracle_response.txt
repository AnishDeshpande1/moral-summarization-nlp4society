SUMMARY: 
The US-Israeli relationship has been damaged due to Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's actions, which have injected partisanship into the relationship. This breach of protocol has hurt the nation's relationship with the United States, according to Susan E. Rice, President Obama's national security adviser. Ms. Rice stated that Mr. Netanyahu's decision to address a joint meeting of Congress two weeks before Israeli elections is destructive of the fabric of the relationship. The White House has acknowledged this damage and expressed their displeasure through top officials' comments.

The episode marks a low point in the tense relationship between President Obama and Prime Minister Netanyahu, with both sides engaged in sniping and finger-pointing. Mr. Netanyahu's refusal to meet with Democratic senators for a private meeting has been seen as disappointing, while his decision to address Congress has created a misperception of partisanship regarding his upcoming visit. The White House has emphasized the importance of maintaining a strong relationship between the two nations, regardless of political seasons or parties in charge.

END OF SUMMARY.
