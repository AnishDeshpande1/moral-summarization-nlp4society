SUMMARY:
National Security Adviser Susan Rice blasted Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s planned Congress address, warning that the trip—arranged by Speaker John Boehner without White House input and timed just weeks before Israel’s elections—has “injected a degree of partisanship” that is “destructive of the fabric” of the U.S.–Israel relationship. Rice emphasized that the bilateral alliance must remain strong and immutable, free from political maneuvering, echoing President Obama’s own concerns. The White House confirmed the assessment, noting the breach of protocol and the risk that party politics will weaken the partnership. Netanyahu, insisting his visit is to oppose a nuclear deal with Iran, declined a private meeting with Democratic senators, further fueling accusations of politicizing the alliance. The moral takeaway is clear: allowing partisan games to dictate foreign‑policy engagements endangers a vital, historically bipartisan relationship and compromises diplomatic integrity. END OF SUMMARY.
