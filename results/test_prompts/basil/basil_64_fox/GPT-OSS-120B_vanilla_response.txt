SUMMARY: Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg sparked bipartisan outrage after publicly criticizing presumptive GOP presidential nominee Donald Trump, calling him a “faker” and questioning his fitness for office. Her remarks, first published in The New York Times and later reiterated on CNN, prompted calls for her resignation, including a harsh tweet from Trump demanding she step down. Republican leaders such as Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell and House Speaker Paul Ryan condemned her comments as inappropriate and damaging to the Court’s perceived impartiality. Even Democrats expressed discomfort, noting that justices should not air political views publicly. Editorial boards from The Washington Post and The New York Times echoed the criticism, citing judicial conduct rules that prohibit endorsing or opposing candidates. Amid the backlash, Senator Bernie Sanders offered limited support, agreeing Trump is an opportunist but not weighing in on the propriety of Ginsburg’s statements. The controversy highlights concerns over the politicization of the Supreme Court and the boundaries of judicial commentary. END OF SUMMARY.
