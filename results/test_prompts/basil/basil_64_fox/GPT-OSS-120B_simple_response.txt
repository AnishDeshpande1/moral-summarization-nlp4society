SUMMARY:  
Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg sparked a bipartisan outcry after publicly denouncing the presumptive GOP nominee, Donald Trump, as a “faker” and unfit for the presidency. Her remarks—first to The New York Times and later on CNN—were condemned as inappropriate for a sitting justice, violating the judicial code that forbids public endorsement or opposition of candidates. President Trump retaliated on Twitter, calling Ginsburg’s statements “very dumb” and demanding her resignation. Republican leaders, including Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell and House Speaker Paul Ryan, labeled her comments “totally inappropriate” and warned that such bias threatens the Court’s impartiality. Even Democratic senators expressed discomfort, noting that justices should keep political views private. Major newspapers echoed this moral judgment, stressing that politicizing the bench erodes public trust. The only notable defense came from Senator Bernie Sanders, who, while not endorsing Ginsburg’s conduct, attacked Trump’s character. The episode underscores a widely held belief that a Supreme Court justice must remain above partisan fray to preserve the integrity of the judiciary.  
END OF SUMMARY.
