SUMMARY:
Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg drew a bipartisan backlash after publicly calling the presumptive GOP nominee a “faker” and saying he is unqualified to be president. Her remarks, first published in The New York Times and repeated on CNN, were condemned as “inappropriate” by Donald Trump, who accused her of being “very dumb” and urged her to resign. Republican leaders, including Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell and House Speaker Paul Ryan, warned that a justice who is “involved” in political debate threatens the Court’s “objective” role and appears “biased,” thereby “undermining” public confidence. Democrats also expressed discomfort, noting that judges should not “endorse” or “oppose” candidates and that such partisan statements are “out of the realm” of judicial conduct. Major newspapers echoed the criticism, citing the judicial Code of Conduct that bars judges from public political advocacy. Amid the criticism, Vermont Senator Bernie Sanders offered qualified support, labeling Trump a “total opportunist” who “lies” frequently.  
END OF SUMMARY.
