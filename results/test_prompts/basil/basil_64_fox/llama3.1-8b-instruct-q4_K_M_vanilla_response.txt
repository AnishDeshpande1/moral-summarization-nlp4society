SUMMARY:

Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg faced backlash for her comments criticizing presumptive GOP nominee Donald Trump, calling him a "faker" who is unqualified to be president. Trump responded by saying Ginsburg's comments were "highly inappropriate" and that she should resign. Republican leaders, including Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell and House Speaker Paul Ryan, also criticized Ginsburg's comments, saying they raised questions about the Supreme Court's objectivity.

Even some Democratic lawmakers expressed concern about Ginsburg's comments, with Sen. Chris Murphy saying they were "unusual" for a Supreme Court justice to make. Mainstream media outlets also chimed in, with The Washington Post's editorial board calling her comments "much too political." However, Vermont Sen. Bernie Sanders gave qualified support for Ginsburg's comments, agreeing that Trump is an opportunist who lies frequently.

Ginsburg initially made the comments in an interview with The New York Times and doubled down on them in a CNN interview. Her statements sparked a heated debate about the role of judges in politics.

END OF SUMMARY.
