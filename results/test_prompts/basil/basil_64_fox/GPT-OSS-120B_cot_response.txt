**SUMMARY:**  
Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg ignited a bipartisan backlash after publicly calling the presumptive GOP nominee a “faker” and “unqualified” for the presidency. Her remarks, deemed “out of line” and “inappropriate” for a high‑court judge, prompted Donald Trump to label her comments “dumb,” say her “mind is shot,” and demand her resignation as a “disgrace” to the Court. Republican leaders Mitch McConnell and Paul Ryan echoed the criticism, warning that such statements reveal bias and undermine the Court’s impartiality. Even Democrats expressed discomfort, noting that justices have political views but should not air them so overtly. Major newspapers, including The Washington Post and The New York Times, warned that politicization erodes public faith in the judiciary. Amid the storm, Senator Bernie Sanders offered qualified support, calling Trump an “opportunist” who “lies a whole lot.” The episode highlights the tension between free expression and the ethical expectation of judicial neutrality.
