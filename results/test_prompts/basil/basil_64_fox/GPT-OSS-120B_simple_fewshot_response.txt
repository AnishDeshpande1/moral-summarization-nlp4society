SUMMARY:
Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg faced a swift, bipartisan backlash after reiterating her harsh criticism of presumptive GOP nominee Donald Trump, calling him a “faker” and unfit for the presidency. Even allies warned that her remarks were inappropriate for a Supreme Court justice. Trump, Republican leaders including Mitch McConnell and Paul Ryan, and major newspapers condemned the comments as a breach of judicial impartiality, urging Ginsburg to apologize or resign. Democrats also expressed discomfort, noting that justices should not air overt political views. The criticism highlighted a shared moral belief that the judiciary must remain neutral to preserve public confidence, and that Ginsburg’s politicized statements undermine that principle. Bernie Sanders offered limited support for Ginsburg’s substance while acknowledging the controversy. The episode underscores the ethical expectation that judges refrain from partisan commentary, regardless of personal convictions.

END OF SUMMARY.
