SUMMARY: Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg's comments about Donald Trump have sparked a bipartisan backlash, with many calling them inappropriate and undermining the impartiality of the court. Trump himself responded by saying she should resign, while Republican leaders Mitch McConnell and Paul Ryan echoed his sentiments, labeling her comments "totally inappropriate" and "inherently biased." Even some Democrats were critical, with Sen. Chris Murphy stating that justices should not air their political views in public. Mainstream media outlets also weighed in, calling her comments a politicization of the court that undermines public faith in its impartiality. Ginsburg's use of words like "faker" and "ego" was seen as crossing a line, and Trump's critics argue that he has been treated too gently by the press regarding his refusal to release his tax returns. The controversy highlights concerns about the objectivity of the court and whether justices should remain neutral in public discourse. END OF SUMMARY.
