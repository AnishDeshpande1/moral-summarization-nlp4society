SUMMARY:
Rep. Paul Ryan, R-Wis., is set to speak at Georgetown University on Thursday despite criticism from nearly 90 professors and administrators who argue he has misused Catholic doctrine to support his budget plan. In a letter, they claim Ryan's invocation of the principle of subsidiarity, which emphasizes local-level solutions, is being used as a "rationale for gutting government programs." The writers point out that this teaching requires higher levels of government involvement and cannot be used to justify disproportionate cuts in essential services to the poor. The U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops has also spoken out against Ryan's budget plan, which they say would harm the most vulnerable members of society.

Georgetown University's Public Policy Institute is hosting Ryan's lecture, but the event remains scheduled despite the criticism. A spokesman for Ryan said he looks forward to affirming his commitment to a preferential option for the poor and addressing the debt crisis. The controversy has sparked debate about the intersection of faith and politics, with some arguing that Ryan's budget plan is at odds with Catholic teaching.

END OF SUMMARY.
