SUMMARY:

GOP Representative Paul Ryan is set to speak at Georgetown University, but his Catholic social teaching credentials are being questioned by nearly 90 professors and administrators at the Jesuit-founded school. They sent him a letter criticizing his misuse of Catholic doctrine to support his budget plan, which they say decimates food programs for struggling families and weakens protections for the elderly and sick. The letter argues that Ryan misappropriated the Catholic teaching of "subsidiarity" as a rationale for gutting government programs, and quotes from the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops' letters to Congress saying budgets cannot rely on disproportionate cuts in essential services to the poor. The Georgetown University group is calling on Ryan to revisit Catholic social teaching and uphold the values of compassion and love embodied in the gospel of Jesus Christ. They aim to "uphold the values of our faith" by challenging Ryan's budget plan, which they see as harming the poor and vulnerable.
