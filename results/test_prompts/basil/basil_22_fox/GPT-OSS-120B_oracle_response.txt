SUMMARY:  
A group of nearly 90 professors and administrators from the Jesuit‑founded Georgetown University sent Rep. Paul Ryan, R‑Wis., a letter accusing him of **misusing** Catholic doctrine to justify a GOP budget that **decimates** food programs, **weakens** protections for the elderly and sick, and **gives** tax breaks to the wealthiest. The letter praises Ryan’s willingness to discuss Catholic social teaching but says his **misappropriated** use of the principle of **subsidiarity** turns a call for local‑level solutions into a **gutting** of government aid. It argues that subsidiarity is not a free pass to **dismantle** programs and that higher levels of government must still act. Ryan, a Catholic and House Budget Committee chairman, replied that the letters do not represent all U.S. bishops and reiterated his commitment to a “preferential option for the poor,” which he says does **not** mean a **preferential option for bigger government**. He defended the budget on Fox News, claiming it follows bipartisan policy and will **avert** a fiscal **crisis** that would **hurt** the poor. Georgetown’s Public Policy Institute confirmed the Thursday lecture will proceed as planned.  
END OF SUMMARY.
