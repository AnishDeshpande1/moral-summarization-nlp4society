SUMMARY:

A group of nearly 90 professors and administrators at Georgetown University have sent a letter to Representative Paul Ryan, criticizing his misuse of Catholic doctrine to support his budget plan. They argue that Ryan's interpretation of "subsidiarity" is flawed, as it ignores the need for higher levels of government to provide help when local communities face problems beyond their means. The scholars point out that Ryan's budget would decimate food programs and weaken protections for the elderly and sick, while giving more tax breaks to the wealthiest few. They also quote from the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops' letters to Congress, which state that budgets cannot rely on disproportionate cuts in essential services to the poor. The scholars emphasize that Ryan's actions are not only morally wrong but also harm vulnerable populations.

Ryan has defended his budget by saying it follows through on bipartisan policies, but the Georgetown scholars argue that he is misusing Catholic teaching to justify his plan. They urge him to revisit Catholic social teaching and prioritize care for the most vulnerable members of society.
