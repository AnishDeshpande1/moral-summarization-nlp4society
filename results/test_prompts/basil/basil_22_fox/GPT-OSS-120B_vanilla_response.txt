SUMMARY: A group of about 90 professors and administrators from Georgetown University, a Jesuit‑affiliated institution, sent Rep. Paul Ryan (R‑Wis.) a letter criticizing his use of Catholic social teaching to justify his GOP budget proposal. The letter acknowledges Ryan’s willingness to discuss Catholic doctrine but argues he is misusing the principle of subsidiarity to defend cuts to food aid, elder‑care, and other social programs, and to expand tax breaks for the wealthy. While recognizing that subsidiarity encourages local solutions, the scholars emphasize that Catholic teaching also requires higher‑level government support and opposes disproportionate cuts to services for the poor, citing U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops statements. Ryan, a Catholic and chair of the House Budget Committee, responded that the bishops’ conference does not represent all bishops and reiterated his commitment to a “preferential option for the poor” without expanding government. Georgetown’s Public Policy Institute confirmed Ryan’s scheduled speech will proceed. END OF SUMMARY.
