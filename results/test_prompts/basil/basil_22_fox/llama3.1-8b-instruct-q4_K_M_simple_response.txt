SUMMARY:

A group of nearly 90 professors and administrators at Georgetown University have written a letter to Rep. Paul Ryan (R-Wis.), criticizing his misuse of Catholic doctrine to support his deficit-reducing budget plan. They argue that his interpretation of "subsidiarity" as a rationale for gutting government programs is incorrect, and that it requires higher levels of government to address problems effectively. The letter also cites the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops' letters to Congress, which state that budgets cannot rely on disproportionate cuts in essential services to the poor. Ryan has defended his budget by saying that he is following through on bipartisan-supported policies and affirming a "preferential option for the poor." However, the Georgetown professors argue that this interpretation ignores the bishops' warnings about disproportionately cutting services to the poor.

END OF SUMMARY.
