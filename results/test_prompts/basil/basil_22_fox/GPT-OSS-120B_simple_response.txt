SUMMARY:  
A coalition of nearly 90 Georgetown‑affiliated professors and administrators has publicly rebuked GOP Rep. Paul Ryan, R‑Wis., for weaponizing Catholic doctrine to justify his aggressive deficit‑cutting budget. In a letter to Ryan, they acknowledge his willingness to discuss Catholic social teaching but condemn his “misuse” of the principle of subsidiarity to defend cuts that would cripple food assistance, weaken protections for the elderly and sick, and hand tax breaks to the wealthy. While Ryan cites subsidiarity as a call for “government closest to the people,” the Georgetown scholars argue that Catholic teaching does not grant a free pass to dismantle essential government programs; it demands that higher levels of government step in when local solutions fall short. They cite U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops’ guidance that budgets must not rely on disproportionate cuts to services for the poor. Ryan’s office maintains the Georgetown lecture will proceed, asserting a “preferential option for the poor” that does not require “bigger government.” The exchange highlights a moral clash: Catholic educators accuse Ryan of distorting faith‑based values to advance a fiscal agenda that harms the most vulnerable.  

END OF SUMMARY.
