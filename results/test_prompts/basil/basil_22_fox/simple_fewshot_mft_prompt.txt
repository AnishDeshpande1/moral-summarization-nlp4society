The scholars point out that the budget's cuts to food programs and Pell Grants will have devastating impacts on the poor and low-income students. They correct Ryan's misunderstanding of the Catholic concept of "subsidiarity," emphasizing that it requires higher levels of government to provide help when local communities face problems beyond their means. The scholars urge Ryan to revisit Catholic social teaching, citing the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops' critique of his budget.
