SUMMARY:
A coalition of nearly 90 Georgetown professors and administrators—many of them Jesuit priests and scholars—has publicly rebuked Rep. Paul Ryan for what they deem a subversive misuse of Catholic authority. In a letter sent before his Georgetown speech, they argue that Ryan’s budget “decimates food programs for struggling families, weakens protections for the elderly and sick, and hands tax breaks to the wealthiest,” thereby harming the most vulnerable and violating the Church’s preferential option for the poor. The faculty contend that Ryan’s invocation of the Catholic principle of subsidiarity is a distortion that turns a doctrine meant to guide compassionate, fair governance into a justification for gutting government aid. Citing the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops, they stress that a just budget cannot rely on disproportionate cuts to essential services, framing Ryan’s stance as both an abuse of religious authority and an unfair, cheating of the poor. Ryan’s office maintains the invitation stands, claiming his policy reflects “government closest to the people” without abandoning a commitment to the poor, but the Georgetown scholars maintain that true Catholic teaching demands care for the needy and equitable treatment, not the erosion of social safety nets.  
END OF SUMMARY.
