SUMMARY:
Nearly 90 Georgetown University professors and administrators, many of them Jesuit‑affiliated, have publicly rebuked Rep. Paul Ryan for invoking Catholic social teaching to justify a budget that slashes food aid, weakens protections for the elderly and sick, and hands tax breaks to the wealthiest. The letter praises Ryan’s willingness to discuss Catholic ideas but condemns his “misuse” of the doctrine of subsidiarity, arguing that true Catholic teaching calls for higher levels of government to step in when local communities cannot meet basic needs, not a free‑hand dismantling of public programs. Citing the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops, the scholars stress that a just budget cannot rely on disproportionate cuts to services for the poor. Ryan’s office maintains the invitation stands and claims the budget reflects a “preferential option for the poor,” but the Georgetown faculty contend that his interpretation betrays the gospel’s call to protect the most vulnerable, turning Catholic doctrine into a cover for austerity. END OF SUMMARY.
