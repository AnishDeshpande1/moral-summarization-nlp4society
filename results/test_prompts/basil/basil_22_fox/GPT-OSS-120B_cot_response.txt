**SUMMARY:**  
A group of nearly 90 Georgetown professors and administrators wrote to GOP Rep. Paul Ryan, accusing him of **misusing** Catholic doctrine to justify a **budget** that **decimates** food aid for **poor** families, **radically weakens** protections for the **elderly** and **sick**, and hands **tax breaks** to the **wealthiest**. While Ryan cites the Catholic **principle** of **subsidiarity**—favoring local **community** solutions and limited **government**—the letter argues that this teaching is **misappropriated** when it becomes a **rationale** for **gutting** essential programs. The scholars invoke the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops, warning against **disproportionate cuts** that betray Catholic **social teaching** of **justice**, **solidarity**, and a **preferential option for the poor**. Ryan, the House Budget Committee chairman, defended his plan as a necessary response to an **urgent** debt crisis, insisting it reflects a **preferential** concern for the **poor** without expanding **government**. The Georgetown Public Policy Institute confirmed Ryan’s speech will proceed as scheduled.
