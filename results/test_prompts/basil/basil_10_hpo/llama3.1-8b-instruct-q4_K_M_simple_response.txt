SUMMARY:

In a move seen as a key tenet of their effort to reduce government size, the Republican-led House read the US Constitution on its second day in power. Volunteers recited the seven articles and 27 amendments that make up the nation's governing document. The GOP has made strict adherence to the Constitution a priority, with new rules requiring lawmakers to cite constitutional authority for proposed legislation. This move is seen as a fulfillment of tea party activists' demands to return to the founding principles of government. However, some have questioned the practical impact of this rule, pointing out that all congressional action is initially presumed valid and that interpreting the Constitution's text can be subjective.

The estimated cost of reading the document on the House floor is $1.1 million, which has been criticized as excessive given the chamber's current inactivity. Republican leaders argue that their actions demonstrate a commitment to respecting the Constitution and reducing government spending.
