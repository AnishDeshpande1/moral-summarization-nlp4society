SUMMARY:
On its second day of Republican rule, the House publicly read the Constitution, a move framed as a moral commitment to “enact” legislation only when it can be tied to explicit constitutional authority. GOP leaders say the new rule will “institute” a requirement that every bill include a “Constitutional Authority Statement,” a symbolic but potentially “controversial” and “contentious” shift in legislative practice. While critics note that most laws are already vetted for constitutionality, supporters argue the measure underscores a genuine respect for the nation’s founding principles. The reading ceremony, estimated to cost about $1.1 million, coincides with the House’s first “spending” cut proposal—a 5 percent “trim” of leadership and committee office budgets that Republicans claim will save $35 million over nine months. Speakers such as John Boehner and Michele Bachmann framed these actions as a return to constitutional fidelity and fiscal responsibility. END OF SUMMARY.
