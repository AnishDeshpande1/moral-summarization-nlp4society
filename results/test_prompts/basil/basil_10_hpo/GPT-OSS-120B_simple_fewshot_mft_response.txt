SUMMARY:
On its first day in power, the Republican‑controlled House staged a full reading of the Constitution, a symbolic act meant to assert the party’s authority over legislative process and to signal a subversion of the previous congressional culture. GOP leaders announced that every new bill must include a “constitutional authority statement,” positioning the move as a fair safeguard against legislative overreach and portraying past practices as cheating the public by ignoring the nation’s founding rules. Speakers such as Speaker John Boehner and Rep. Michele Bachmann framed the initiative as a return to proper constitutional obedience, casting Democrats’ prior procedures as subversive and unfair. Critics noted that the reading—costing about $1.1 million—and the new rule may create endless disputes over interpretation, but Republicans billed the measures as a fair correction of a system they claim has been cheated of its original limits. The same session also introduced a 5 percent cut to congressional office budgets, presented as another fair, fiscally responsible step toward the “end of reckless spending” promised by voters.  

END OF SUMMARY.
