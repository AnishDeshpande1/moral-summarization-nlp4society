SUMMARY:
On its second day in power, the Republican‑led House staged the first full reading of the Constitution, a symbolic act championed by Tea Party activists who view strict constitutional adherence as a moral imperative for shrinking federal government. GOP leaders announced a new rule requiring every bill to include a “constitutional authority statement” from its author, arguing that lawmakers truly oath only to the Constitution. Speakers such as Speaker John Boehner and Rep. Michele Bachmann framed the measure as a fulfillment of voters’ demand for fiscal responsibility and a return to founding principles. While critics note that most legislation is already presumed constitutional and that the rule may spark costly disputes, Republicans highlighted the $1.1 million cost of the reading as a worthwhile investment in transparency. The same day, the House moved to cut its own spending by 5 %, projected to save $35 million in nine months, reinforcing the party’s moral narrative of cutting waste and honoring the nation’s founding document.  
END OF SUMMARY.
