SUMMARY:  
On their first day in power, House Republicans staged a full reading of the Constitution, turning the nation’s founding charter into a public ritual and a benchmark for legislation.  They plan to require every new bill to include a “constitutional authority statement” from its author, framing strict constitutional adherence as a moral duty to curb federal overreach and honor the oath lawmakers swear to the Constitution, not merely to their constituents.  Tea‑Party activists celebrate this as a return to the country’s founding principles, while critics note the symbolic nature of the move and warn that constitutional interpretations will inevitably spark controversy.  The effort, costing about $1.1 million, is presented as a responsible use of taxpayer money, alongside a proposed 5 % cut to congressional office budgets that Republicans claim will save $35 million in nine months.  The moral narrative positions constitutional fidelity as the ethical foundation for responsible governance and fiscal restraint.  
END OF SUMMARY.
