SUMMARY:

On the second day of Republican rule in the House, members read the Constitution aloud for the first time in history. This move is seen as a key tenet of their effort to reduce the federal government's size and adhere strictly to the document. The House GOP will now require lawmakers to include a statement citing constitutional authority for any new legislation. Tea party activists have praised this development, but experts are skeptical about its impact. According to estimates, reading the Constitution cost $1.1 million. Republicans also passed their first spending cut measure, a 5% reduction in leadership and member office budgets, which is expected to save $35 million over nine months.

Newly-minted Speaker John Boehner said that voters called for an end to reckless spending and a renewed commitment to the Constitution, while Rep. Michele Bachmann stated that these new rules demonstrate Republicans' seriousness about respecting the document.

END OF SUMMARY.
