SUMMARY:
On its second day of Republican control, the U.S. House of Representatives read the entire Constitution aloud, a first in its 221‑year history. The GOP plans to require every new bill to include a “constitutional authority statement” citing the specific constitutional basis for the legislation. The move reflects the party’s pledge to strict constitutional adherence and aligns with Tea Party demands for smaller government. Critics note that most legislation is already presumed constitutional and vetted by legal staff, so the new requirement may generate controversy without substantive impact. The reading and related initiatives are estimated to cost about $1.07 million. Speaker John Boehner and Rep. Michele Bachmann highlighted the effort as a commitment to the Constitution following voter calls for reduced spending. On the same day, the House will consider a 5 % budget cut for leadership and committee offices, projected to save $35 million over nine months.  
END OF SUMMARY.
