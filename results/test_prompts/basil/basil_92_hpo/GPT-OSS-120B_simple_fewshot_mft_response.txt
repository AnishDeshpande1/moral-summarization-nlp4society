SUMMARY:
A rare statement from special counsel Robert Mueller’s team on Friday contested BuzzFeed’s explosive Thursday report that President Donald Trump ordered Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about a Moscow‑tower deal. Spokesman Peter Carr said the outlet’s “description of specific statements… and characterization of documents and testimony… are not accurate,” but did not directly refute the core claim that Trump directed the false testimony. BuzzFeed’s editor‑in‑chief Ben Smith defended the reporting and urged the counsel to clarify the dispute; a BuzzFeed spokesperson later said the story had been “re‑confirmed” and showed no sign of inaccuracy.

Democratic leaders seized on the story as evidence of presidential subversion of lawful authority and a breach of fairness, calling for Mueller to disclose what he knows and threatening impeachment if the allegations prove true. Senator Dianne Feinstein and Rep. Adam Schiff framed the issue as a serious abuse of power—Trump allegedly cheating the public and Congress by ordering a lie, undermining the rule of law and the integrity of the investigative process. Trump denied the accusations, accusing Cohen of lying to the special counsel to lessen his sentence.

END OF SUMMARY.
