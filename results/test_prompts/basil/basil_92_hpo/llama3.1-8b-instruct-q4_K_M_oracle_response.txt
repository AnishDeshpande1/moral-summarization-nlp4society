SUMMARY:

The special counsel Robert Mueller's team disputed BuzzFeed's report that President Donald Trump directed Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about the Trump Tower deal in Moscow. Mueller's office stated that the description of specific statements and characterization of documents were inaccurate, but did not directly dispute the allegations. The report claimed that Trump ordered Cohen to lie, which Cohen then acknowledged during his interviews with Mueller's office.

The controversy has sparked strong reactions from Democrats on Capitol Hill, who are calling for action against Trump if the allegations prove true. Sen. Dianne Feinstein stated that if Trump committed a crime by directing Cohen to lie, he must be held accountable through impeachment or other means. Chairman of the House Intelligence Committee Rep. Adam Schiff revealed that his committee is already investigating the matter and working to secure witness testimony and documents.

The dispute between Mueller's office and BuzzFeed has added confusion to the ongoing investigation into Trump's ties to Russia, but one thing remains clear: if the allegations are true, it would be a serious crime that demands action.

END OF SUMMARY.
