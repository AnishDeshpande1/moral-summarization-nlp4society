SUMMARY:

Special Counsel Robert Mueller's team disputed BuzzFeed's report that President Donald Trump directed attorney Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about negotiations over a Trump Tower in Moscow. Peter Carr, a spokesman for the special counsel, stated that BuzzFeed's description of statements and documents obtained by Mueller's office was inaccurate. However, the statement did not directly dispute that Trump had directed Cohen to lie or mention the Trump Tower deal.

BuzzFeed Editor-in-Chief Ben Smith stood by their reporting and asked the special counsel to clarify its statement. The news site claimed it had re-confirmed its reporting and seen no indication of inaccuracy. Top Democrats, including Sen. Dianne Feinstein and Rep. Adam Schiff, called for serious action, including impeachment, if the allegations proved true.

The controversy has added confusion to Mueller's investigation into Trump's ties to Russia. Trump has maintained his innocence, accusing Cohen of lying to reduce his jail time. The dispute highlights the ongoing battle between the special counsel's office and news outlets over what information is accurate in the investigation.

END OF SUMMARY.
