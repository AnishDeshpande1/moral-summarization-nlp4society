SUMMARY:  
In a fraught clash over truth and accountability, Special Counsel Robert Mueller’s team publicly challenged BuzzFeed’s explosive claim that President Donald Trump ordered his lawyer Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about a Moscow‑tower deal.  Mueller’s spokesman said BuzzFeed’s description of statements, documents and testimony was “not accurate,” though he stopped short of denying the core allegation.  BuzzFeed stood by its reporting, insisting its sources were solid and asking the counsel to specify the dispute.  The dispute sharpened the moral stakes: Democrats framed the story as a possible act of obstruction and a breach of constitutional duty, urging impeachment and demanding full disclosure.  Senators such as Dianne Feinstein warned that if Trump indeed instructed Cohen to deceive lawmakers, he “committed a crime and must be held accountable.”  House Intelligence Chair Adam Schiff pledged to chase every witness and document, portraying the investigation as a vital safeguard against abuse of power.  Trump, meanwhile, denied wrongdoing and cast Cohen as the one lying to protect himself.  The episode underscores a broader ethical battle over truth, responsibility, and the rule of law in American governance.  
END OF SUMMARY.
