SUMMARY:

Special counsel Robert Mueller's team disputed BuzzFeed's report that President Donald Trump directed attorney Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about negotiations over a Trump Tower in Moscow. In a rare statement, Mueller's spokesman said BuzzFeed's description of statements and documents obtained by his office was inaccurate. However, the statement did not directly dispute that Trump had directed Cohen to lie to Congress.

BuzzFeed Editor-in-Chief Ben Smith stood by their reporting and asked Mueller's office to clarify its statement. A BuzzFeed spokesman later confirmed that they had re-confirmed their reporting and seen no indication of inaccuracies. Democrats on Capitol Hill reacted strongly to the allegations, with some calling for impeachment or criminal charges if the allegations prove true.

The controversy has added more confusion to Mueller's investigation into Trump's ties to Russia. Rep. Adam Schiff (D-Calif.) said his committee is working to confirm BuzzFeed's report and follow the evidence wherever it may lead.

END OF SUMMARY.
