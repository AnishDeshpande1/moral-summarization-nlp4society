SUMMARY:
In a rare Friday night rebuttal, special counsel Robert Mueller’s team told BuzzFeed that its “description” of statements, documents and testimony related to Michael Cohen’s congressional testimony was “not accurate.” The clarification adds further confusion to Mueller’s ongoing probe of President Donald Trump’s Russia ties. BuzzFeed had reported, citing two federal officials, that Trump ordered Cohen to lie to Congress about the Trump Tower Moscow negotiations, citing internal emails, texts and witness interviews. The outlet stood by its story and urged the special counsel to specify what it was disputing, while a BuzzFeed spokesperson later said the reporting had been “re‑confirmed” with no sign of inaccuracy. Democrats seized on the report, with Sen. Dianne Feinstein and Rep. Adam Schiff warning that if the allegations are true the president has committed a crime and could face impeachment. Trump continues to deny any wrongdoing, accusing Cohen of lying to the special counsel to lessen his own sentence. The episode underscores the deep partisan clash over accountability and the integrity of the investigation.  
END OF SUMMARY.
