SUMMARY: The Treasury Department and IRS announced that, effective for the 2013 tax year, all legally married same‑sex couples will be recognized for federal tax purposes regardless of their state of residence. Couples must file as “married filing jointly” or “married filing separately,” not as single individuals, and can amend prior returns for possible refunds. The rule applies to any marriage performed in the U.S. or abroad, but not to civil unions or domestic partnerships. While the change ensures uniform federal filing status, it creates complications for couples in states that do not recognize their marriage, as they may have to file federal returns as married while filing state returns as individuals. Tax experts note potential “marriage penalty” impacts and a slight increase in federal revenue, offset by added spending on spousal benefits. The decision follows the Supreme Court’s June ruling striking down DOMA and is part of broader federal efforts to align benefits, though other agencies (e.g., Social Security) are using differing standards, leading to a patchwork of regulations. END OF SUMMARY.
