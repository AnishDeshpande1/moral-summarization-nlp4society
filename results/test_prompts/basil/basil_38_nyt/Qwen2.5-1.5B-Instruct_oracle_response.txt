SUMMARY: The U.S. government is recognizing same-sex couples' legal marriages for federal tax purposes, according to the Treasury Department and the Internal Revenue Service (IRS). This ruling follows the landmark Supreme Court decision that struck down the 1996 Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA), ensuring same-sex couples can file federal tax returns as "married filing jointly" or "married filing separately," provided both partners are legally married. However, same-sex couples living in states that do not recognize their marriages will still need to file separate tax returns for their state taxes. The ruling is part of efforts to make same-sex couples' tax treatment equivalent to that of heterosexual couples, addressing concerns over unequal benefits and potential tax penalties. While the decision aims to provide clarity and equality, it may lead to increased administrative complexity and varying interpretations across different states regarding same-sex marriage recognition. The ruling's implementation reflects the evolving landscape of LGBTQ+ rights and the ongoing challenge of balancing social justice with practical considerations in taxation. END OF SUMMARY.
