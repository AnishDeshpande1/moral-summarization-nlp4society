SUMMARY:

The Treasury Department's decision provides certainty and clear guidance for all legally married same-sex couples nationwide, treating them equally under federal tax laws regardless of their state of residence or where they were married. This ruling assures that same-sex spouses can move freely throughout the country without worrying about changes in their federal filing status. The decision is a sign of how quickly the government is moving to recognize and treat gay couples with fairness, following the landmark Supreme Court decision that struck down the Defense of Marriage Act.

The ruling has implications for many gay couples' tax liabilities, but also provides access to crucial benefits and protections previously denied to them under discriminatory laws. Same-sex couples will now be able to file amended returns for certain prior tax years, potentially leading to refunds. While this is a step forward in ensuring equal treatment, it creates complexities for same-sex couples living in states that do not recognize their marriages.

END OF SUMMARY.
