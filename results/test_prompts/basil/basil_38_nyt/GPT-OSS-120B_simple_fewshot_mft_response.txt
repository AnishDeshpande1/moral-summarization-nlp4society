SUMMARY:
The Treasury Department and IRS announced that all legally married same‑sex couples will be recognized for federal tax purposes, regardless of the state in which they live, implementing the Supreme Court’s decision that struck down DOMA. By requiring these couples to file jointly or separately as “married” rather than as single individuals, the rule affirms federal authority to treat gay marriages equally and curbs subversive state refusals to recognize them. Advocacy groups praised the move as a matter of fairness, granting equal tax benefits and protections that were previously denied under a discriminatory law. At the same time, the guidance aims to protect same‑sex families from financial harm by providing clear filing status and the option to amend past returns for possible refunds, while acknowledging that some may face a “marriage penalty.” Experts note the change will create administrative challenges for states that do not recognize these unions, but the federal stance underscores a commitment to equitable treatment and the well‑being of LGBTQ + families. END OF SUMMARY.
