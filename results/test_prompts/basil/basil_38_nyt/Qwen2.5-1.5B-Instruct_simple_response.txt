SUMMARY: On April 4, 2013, the U.S. Treasury Department and the Internal Revenue Service announced a sweeping change regarding the recognition of same-sex marriages for federal tax purposes. Under the new rules, all legally married same-sex couples will be treated as if they were legally married in the state where they reside. This includes those who marry outside of the United States or in states that recognize same-sex marriages. However, the new rules still leave room for confusion and potential complications, particularly for same-sex couples living in states that do not recognize their marriages. The change is part of broader efforts by the U.S. government to ensure equal treatment of same-sex couples under federal law.
END OF SUMMARY.
