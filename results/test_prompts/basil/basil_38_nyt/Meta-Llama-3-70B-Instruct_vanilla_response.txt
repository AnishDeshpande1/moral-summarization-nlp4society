SUMMARY:

The US Treasury Department and Internal Revenue Service (IRS) have announced that all same-sex couples who are legally married will be recognized as such for federal tax purposes, regardless of the state they live in. This means that same-sex couples will no longer be able to file federal tax returns as single individuals, but must file jointly or separately as married couples. The ruling applies to all legal marriages made in the US or foreign countries, but does not extend to civil unions or registered domestic partnerships. Gay and civil rights groups have praised the ruling, saying it provides certainty and equal treatment under federal tax laws. However, the ruling may have implications for tax liabilities, and couples living in states that do not recognize their marriages may face complications. The Treasury decision is part of a broader effort by the federal government to conform to the Supreme Court's landmark decision striking down the Defense of Marriage Act.

END OF SUMMARY.
