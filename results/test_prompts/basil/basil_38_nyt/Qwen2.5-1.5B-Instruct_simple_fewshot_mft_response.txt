SUMMARY:

The U.S. Treasury Department and Internal Revenue Service announced that all same-sex couples who are legally married will be recognized as such for federal tax purposes, regardless of whether the state where they live recognizes their union. This ruling follows the Supreme Court's decision in June, which found that same-sex couples are entitled to federal benefits but left open the question of how the government would actually administer them. The ruling aims to provide clarity and assurance to all legally married same-sex couples, ensuring that they are treated the same as straight couples for tax purposes. However, the ruling could have implications for tax liabilities and potentially lead to increased costs for same-sex couples living in states that do not recognize their marriages. The ruling is part of a broader effort by the government to align with the Supreme Court's decision and ensure that gay and lesbian couples are treated equally under federal law. END OF SUMMARY.
