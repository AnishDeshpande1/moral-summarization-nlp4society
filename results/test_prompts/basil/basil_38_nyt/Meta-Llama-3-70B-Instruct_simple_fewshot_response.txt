SUMMARY:

The Treasury Department and the Internal Revenue Service have announced that all same-sex couples who are legally married will be recognized as such for federal tax purposes, regardless of the state where they live. This ruling is a significant step towards implementing the landmark Supreme Court decision that struck down the Defense of Marriage Act, ensuring that gay couples are treated equally under federal law. The ruling provides certainty and clear tax-filing guidance for all legally married same-sex couples nationwide, allowing them to move freely throughout the country without changing their federal filing status. Gay and civil rights groups have praised the ruling, which will provide access to crucial tax benefits and protections previously denied to them. However, the ruling may have ramifications for many gay couples' tax liabilities, and states may respond with changes of their own. The Obama administration has pushed federal agencies to ensure the Supreme Court's ruling is carried out quickly and smoothly, promoting equality and fairness for all families.

END OF SUMMARY.
