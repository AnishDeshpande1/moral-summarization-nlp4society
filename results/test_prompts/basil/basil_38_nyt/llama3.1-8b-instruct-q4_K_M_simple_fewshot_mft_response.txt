SUMMARY:

The Treasury Department and Internal Revenue Service have announced that same-sex couples who are legally married will be recognized as such for federal tax purposes, regardless of where they live. This decision marks a significant step towards implementing the Supreme Court's ruling in June, which struck down the Defense of Marriage Act. The new rule change ensures that same-sex spouses can file their federal tax returns together as "married filing jointly" or individually as "married filing separately," without regard to the state where they live. While this decision provides clarity and consistency for same-sex couples, it also creates a patchwork of regulations affecting gay and lesbian couples across different federal agencies, raising questions about discrimination and fairness in the distribution of benefits.

Gay and civil rights groups have praised the ruling, saying that committed and loving gay and lesbian married couples will now be treated equally under federal tax laws. However, the rule change may also create complications for same-sex couples who live in states that do not recognize their marriages, requiring them to navigate a cumbersome workaround.
