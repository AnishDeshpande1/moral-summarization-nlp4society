SUMMARY:
The Treasury Department announced that all legally married same‑sex couples will be recognized for federal tax purposes, regardless of the state in which they live. Effective for the 2013 tax year, such couples must file as “married filing jointly” or “married filing separately,” receiving the same treatment as opposite‑sex spouses and gaining certainty that moving across state lines will not alter their federal filing status. Gay‑rights groups praised the rule as a long‑overdue correction of the discriminatory Defense of Marriage Act, granting equal tax benefits and protections. While the change may create paperwork challenges for couples in states that do not recognize their marriage and could trigger a modest “marriage penalty” for some, it also allows many to claim refunds for prior years. The ruling is part of a broader federal effort to align regulations with the Supreme Court’s decision, though inconsistencies remain across agencies such as Medicare and Social Security. Overall, the policy advances equal treatment for gay and lesbian families under federal law.  
END OF SUMMARY.
