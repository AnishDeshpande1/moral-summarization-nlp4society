SUMMARY:
As part of the effort to align with the Supreme Court's ruling striking down the Defense of Marriage Act, the Treasury Department and the Internal Revenue Service clarified that all legally married same-sex couples will be treated the same for federal tax purposes, regardless of where they reside. This includes allowing same-sex couples to file their federal tax returns as if they were married, rather than filing as individual taxpayers. However, same-sex couples still face challenges in different states, where benefits may vary depending on whether they consider residency or celebration standards. The ruling aims to streamline the application of federal benefits while addressing the complexities faced by same-sex couples seeking equality. END OF SUMMARY.
