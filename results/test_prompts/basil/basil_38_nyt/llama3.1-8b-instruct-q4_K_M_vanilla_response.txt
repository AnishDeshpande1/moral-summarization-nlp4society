SUMMARY:

The Treasury Department and Internal Revenue Service announced that all same-sex couples who are legally married will be recognized as such for federal tax purposes, regardless of the state where they live. This ruling applies to couples who got married in any state or foreign country and is a result of the Supreme Court's decision to strike down the Defense of Marriage Act. Same-sex couples must file their federal tax returns together as "married filing jointly" or individually as "married filing separately." The ruling also allows couples to file amended returns for prior tax years, which could lead to refunds.

Gay rights groups praised the ruling, but tax experts noted that it may create complexities and a paperwork burden for same-sex couples living in states that do not recognize their marriages. The Treasury decision is part of a patchwork of regulations emerging from federal agencies as they adjust to the Supreme Court's ruling.
