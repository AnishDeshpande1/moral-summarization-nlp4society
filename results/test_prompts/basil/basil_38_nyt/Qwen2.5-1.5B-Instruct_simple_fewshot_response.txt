The U.S. Treasury Department and Internal Revenue Service announced that all same-sex couples who are legally married will be recognized as such for federal tax purposes, regardless of the state where they reside. This is a significant change to the previous situation, where same-sex couples were taxed as singles unless they lived in a state that recognized their marriage. The decision aims to provide clarity and consistency in treating same-sex couples fairly. However, it raises concerns about potential tax liabilities and inconsistencies across different states.
