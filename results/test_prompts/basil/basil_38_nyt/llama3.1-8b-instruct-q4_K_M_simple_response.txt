SUMMARY:

In response to the landmark Supreme Court decision striking down the Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA), the Treasury Department and Internal Revenue Service have issued new rules recognizing same-sex couples as married for federal tax purposes, regardless of their state of residence. This ruling applies to all legally married same-sex couples in the US or foreign countries, providing them with equal access to federal benefits and protections. The decision also allows same-sex couples to file amended returns for prior tax years, potentially resulting in refunds for some couples.

Gay rights groups have praised the ruling as a major step towards equality and fairness for same-sex couples. However, experts note that the new rules may create complexities and complications for couples living in states that do not recognize their marriages, particularly with regards to state taxes. The Obama administration's push for consistent regulations has been met with varying responses from different federal agencies, raising questions about discrimination and fairness in the distribution of benefits.

END OF SUMMARY.
