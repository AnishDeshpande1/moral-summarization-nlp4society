SUMMARY: Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio endorsed Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination, praising Perry’s “superior border security record” and pledging support for tougher immigration enforcement. Arpaio highlighted Perry’s plans for increased border personnel, fencing, and surveillance from Brownsville to San Diego. Perry thanked Arpaio, emphasizing a shared goal of securing the southern border to protect national security, public safety, and jobs. The endorsement may aid Perry, who has faced criticism from conservatives for supporting DREAM‑act beneficiaries and for policies seen as attracting undocumented immigrants. Perry’s rivals, including former Massachusetts Governor Mitt Romney, have attacked his immigration stance, accusing him of creating a “magnet” for illegal immigration. Perry maintains he opposes amnesty but advocates extending work visas and promises to secure the border within a year as president. END OF SUMMARY.
