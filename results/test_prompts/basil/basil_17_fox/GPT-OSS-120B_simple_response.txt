SUMMARY:  
Arizona’s hard‑line sheriff, Joe Arpaio, publicly backed Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination, praising Perry as the candidate most committed to “tough‑on‑crime” border enforcement. Arpaio, famed for reviving chain gangs and backing Arizona’s SB 1070 immigration law, argued that Perry’s record and plan to fund full‑time border security—from Brownsville to San Diego—make the nation safer. Perry echoed the sentiment, emphasizing that a secure southern border protects national security, public safety and jobs, and pledged to deploy “thousands more federal boots on the ground, strategic fencing and aerial resources” within a year. The endorsement aims to bolster Perry, who has faced criticism from the GOP right for supporting DREAM‑age students and for policies that some, like Mitt Romney, claim attract undocumented immigrants. Perry maintains he opposes amnesty but supports expanded work visas, positioning himself as the candidate who will aggressively curb illegal immigration.  

END OF SUMMARY.
