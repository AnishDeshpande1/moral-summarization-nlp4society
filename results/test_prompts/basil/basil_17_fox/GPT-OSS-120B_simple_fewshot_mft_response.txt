SUMMARY:
Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio, the self‑styled “America’s Toughest Sheriff” and a longtime champion of harsh immigration enforcement, publicly endorsed three‑term Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination during a New Hampshire campaign swing. Arpaio praised Perry as the strongest authority on border security, claiming he has done more than any other candidate to protect the nation from illegal immigration and to uphold law‑and‑order. Both men framed the endorsement as a moral duty to safeguard public safety and jobs, arguing that a tough‑on‑crime president must fund full‑time border operations, increase personnel, fencing and surveillance, and secure the border within a year. The article also notes intra‑party disputes: Perry’s support for DREAM‑child policies and work‑visa extensions has drawn criticism from fellow Republican Mitt Romney, who accuses him of “magnetizing” undocumented immigrants and undermining fair immigration rules. The moral framing underscores respect for legitimate authority, the duty to protect citizens from harm, and the fairness imperative of enforcing immigration laws without granting preferential treatment.  

END OF SUMMARY.
