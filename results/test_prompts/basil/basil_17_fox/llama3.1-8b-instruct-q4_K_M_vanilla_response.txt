SUMMARY:

Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio, known for his tough stance on illegal immigration, endorsed Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination. Arpaio praised Perry's record on border security and plan to fund full-time operations from Brownsville to San Diego. Perry accepted the endorsement, stating that a secure border is vital to protecting America's national security, public safety, and jobs. The endorsement may help Perry address criticism from the Right about his stance on immigration, particularly regarding DREAM children.

Perry has faced clashes with Mitt Romney over immigration, who accused him of creating a magnet for undocumented immigrants by supporting in-state tuition rates for them. Perry maintains that he opposes amnesty or a path to citizenship and plans to secure the border within a year through additional federal boots, fencing, and aerial resources.

The endorsement from Arpaio could help Perry counter criticism from Romney on immigration and appeal to conservative voters who value tough stances on crime and security.

END OF SUMMARY.
