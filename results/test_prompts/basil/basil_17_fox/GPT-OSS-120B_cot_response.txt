**SUMMARY:**  
Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio, the self‑styled “America’s Toughest Sheriff,” endorsed Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination, praising Perry’s “tough‑on‑crime” stance and “superior border security record.” Arpaio argued that a “secure border” is essential for America’s “national security, public safety and jobs,” and pledged support for Perry’s plan to fund full‑time border operations, fencing, and surveillance from Brownsville to San Diego. Perry welcomed the endorsement, emphasizing his commitment to “make our border and our nation safer” and to deploy “thousands more federal boots on the ground” within a year. The endorsement may help Perry counter criticism from the right over his support for DREAM children and accusations from Mitt Romney that his policies act as a “magnet” for undocumented immigrants. Perry maintained he opposes “amnesty” or a “path to citizenship,” while still advocating for expanded work visas.
