SUMMARY:
Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio, the outspoken “America’s Toughest Sheriff” who revived chain gangs and backed Arizona’s hard‑line SB 1070 law, endorsed three‑term Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination during a New Hampshire swing. Arpaio praised Perry as the candidate who has done the most to combat illegal immigration, calling for a “tough‑on‑crime” president who will fund full‑time border security from Brownsville to San Diego. Perry echoed this moral stance, saying a secure border protects national security, public safety and jobs, and pledged to deploy thousands of federal agents, fencing and aerial surveillance within a year. The endorsement may aid Perry, who has faced criticism from the right for supporting DREAM‑child tuition and for his “magnet” comments about undocumented immigrants, while clashing with Mitt Romney over immigration policy. The moral framing presented underscores the urgency of strong border enforcement as a matter of protecting American lives, safety and prosperity.

END OF SUMMARY.
