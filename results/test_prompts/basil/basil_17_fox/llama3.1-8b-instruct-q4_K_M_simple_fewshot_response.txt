Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio, known for his tough stance on immigration, has endorsed Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination. Arpaio praised Perry's border security record and plan to secure the border within a year through increased personnel, fencing, and surveillance. The endorsement comes despite criticism of Perry's immigration platform, which opposes amnesty or a path to citizenship but supports extending work visas for undocumented immigrants. This partnership between Arpaio and Perry highlights the importance of border security in their vision for America. However, critics argue that Perry's stance on immigration is not tough enough, particularly with regards to his support for DREAM children.

The moral framing of this issue raises questions about the treatment of undocumented immigrants and the balance between national security and compassion. While Arpaio and Perry frame their proposal as a necessary measure to protect America's borders, others see it as overly punitive and inhumane. The debate surrounding immigration policy highlights the complexities of balancing competing values such as security, fairness, and human dignity.
