SUMMARY:
North Carolina Democratic Rep. Bob Etheridge issued a “profound” apology after an online video showed him physically confronting a young student who asked if he supported the “Obama agenda.” The footage captures Etheridge seizing the man’s wrist and neck, demanding to know his identity, and refusing to release him even when asked. By reacting with aggression, Etheridge subverted the expected authority and decorum of a congressman, behaving in a way that many observers described as “unbecoming” of a public official. The incident also inflicted clear harm on the student, prompting Etheridge to acknowledge that “no matter how partisan…that’s no excuse for my response.” He added that he would apologize directly if he knew the youths’ identities. The episode, occurring after a long day and outside a Pelosi‑headlined fundraiser, has been highlighted by critics as evidence of abusive conduct by a lawmaker who has supported controversial Obama‑era policies, raising concerns about both respect for institutional authority and the physical safety of constituents. END OF SUMMARY.
