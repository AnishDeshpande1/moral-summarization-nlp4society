SUMMARY:  
Three days after a YouTube video showed North Carolina Rep. Bob Etheridge in a physical **confrontation** with a young man, the Democrat issued a written apology, saying he “profoundly” regrets the **tussle**. In the amateur clip, the student‑aged man asks Etheridge whether he supports the “Obama agenda,” and Etheridge **grabs** the man’s wrist and later his **neck** while trying to seize the cell‑phone camera. The man pleads “please let go,” but Etheridge’s **response** is to **grab** tighter, prompting a bystander to explain they are “just here for a project.” Etheridge told reporters he cannot apologize personally because he “doesn’t know who they are,” though he acknowledged having been **spat** at, **pushed**, and **threatened** before, and that none of that justifies his **conduct**. Critics called the episode an **assault** and “unbecoming of a member of Congress.” The incident occurs as Etheridge, a long‑time incumbent, runs for re‑election against Republican nurse Renee Ellmers.  

END OF SUMMARY.
