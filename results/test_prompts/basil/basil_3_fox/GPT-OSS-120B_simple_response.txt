SUMMARY:  
In a video that quickly went viral, North Carolina Democratic Rep. Bob Etheridge was caught physically confronting a young man who asked him about his support for the “Obama agenda.” Etheridge seized the student’s wrist, tried to snatch his phone, and even grabbed his neck, demanding to know the man’s identity. After the incident, Etheridge issued a written statement saying he “profoundly regrets” his actions and acknowledged that partisan anger is no excuse for violence. He claimed he could not apologize personally because he does not know the youths’ names, but reiterated that past threats against him do not justify the assault. Critics, including conservative blogger Mike Flynn, condemned the episode as “unbecoming of a member of Congress” and highlighted the dissonance between Etheridge’s policy votes—supporting Obama’s health‑care overhaul and climate legislation—and his aggressive conduct toward a citizen. The episode comes as Etheridge, a seven‑term incumbent, seeks re‑election against Republican nurse Renee Ellmers, with political analysts expecting him to retain his seat despite the controversy.  

END OF SUMMARY.
