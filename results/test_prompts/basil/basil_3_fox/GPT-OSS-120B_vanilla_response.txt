SUMMARY:  
Three days after a YouTube video showed North Carolina Rep. Bob Etheridge physically confronting a young man on a Washington street, the Democrat issued a written apology, saying he “profoundly” regrets his actions. The amateur footage captures Etheridge grabbing the student’s wrist and neck after the man asked if he supported the “Obama agenda” and tried to take Etheridge’s phone. Etheridge later explained he did not apologize to the individuals because he does not know who they are, and he cited a “long day” and prior harassment as context, though he admitted no excuse. Critics, including conservative blogger Mike Flynn, condemned the incident as unbecoming of a congressman and highlighted Etheridge’s support for Obama’s health‑care and climate legislation. The confrontation occurred near a fundraiser headlined by Speaker Nancy Pelosi. Etheridge, a seven‑term incumbent on the Ways and Means and Budget committees, is seeking re‑election in November against Republican nurse Renee Ellmers, with observers expecting him to retain his seat.  

END OF SUMMARY.
