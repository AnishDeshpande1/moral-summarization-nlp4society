SUMMARY:
North Carolina Democratic Rep. Bob Etheridge issued a “profound” apology after an amateur video showed him grabbing a young man’s wrist and neck during a street confrontation about the “Obama agenda.” The footage, posted to YouTube, captures Etheridge trying to seize the student’s phone, demanding to know his identity, and then holding the man by the neck despite pleas to let go. Etheridge said he regrets the incident, acknowledging that partisan tension is no excuse, and explained he cannot apologize personally because he does not know who the men are. Critics, including conservative commentator Mike Flynn, condemned the behavior as unbecoming of a congressman and linked it to Etheridge’s support for Obama’s health‑care and climate legislation. The episode occurred near a Pelosi‑headlined fundraiser and comes as Etheridge, a veteran of the Ways and Means and Budget committees, prepares for a re‑election race against Republican nurse Renee Ellmers, though observers expect him to retain his seat. The incident underscores the need for civility and respect in political discourse.  
END OF SUMMARY.
