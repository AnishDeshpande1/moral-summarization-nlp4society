SUMMARY:

North Carolina Representative Bob Etheridge has apologized for physically confronting a young man who asked him about his support for President Obama's agenda. A video of the incident, which shows Etheridge grabbing the man's wrist and neck, was widely circulated online. Etheridge said he is "profoundly" sorry for his response, stating that it was unacceptable regardless of how partisan or difficult things get. He emphasized that he has always tried to treat people with respect, but acknowledged that his behavior in this incident fell short. The young man involved in the confrontation identified himself as a student, and another man present claimed they were there for a project. Etheridge's actions have been widely criticized by Republicans, who say his conduct is "unbecoming of a member of Congress." The incident has sparked controversy and raised questions about the importance of respecting others' opinions, even in the face of disagreement.

END OF SUMMARY.
