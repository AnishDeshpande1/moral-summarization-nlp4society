SUMMARY:

North Carolina Rep. Bob Etheridge was involved in a physical confrontation on a Washington street, as captured on an internet video. A young man approached Etheridge and asked if he supported the "Obama agenda," leading to a tussle between the two. The congressman grabbed the young man's wrist and neck, refusing to let go despite being told to release him. Etheridge later apologized for his actions, stating that they were unbecoming of a member of Congress. He cited a long day as an excuse for his behavior, but acknowledged that it was no justification for physical aggression. The incident has sparked criticism from conservative bloggers and observers, who argue that Etheridge's conduct is unacceptable. The congressman is running for re-election in November against Republican Renee Ellmers.

END OF SUMMARY.
