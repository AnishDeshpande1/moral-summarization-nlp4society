Three days after a YouTube video showed North Carolina Rep. Bob Etheridge in a physical tussle with a young man, the Democrat issued a statement saying he is “profoundly” sorry and deeply regrets his actions. Etheridge acknowledged that “no matter how partisan or difficult things get, that is no excuse for my response,” and called his conduct “unbecoming” of a member of Congress. The video captured Etheridge grabbing the student’s wrist and neck after the man asked about the “Obama agenda,” prompting the congressman to demand, “Who are you? I have a right to know.” Etheridge said he would apologize if he knew the men’s identities, noting he has been spat at, pushed and threatened before, but that is no justification. Critics labeled the episode a “bad” assault, while Etheridge pledged to uphold a more civil and respectful tone moving forward.
