SUMMARY:

North Carolina Rep. Bob Etheridge has apologized for a physical confrontation caught on video, in which he grabbed the wrist and neck of an unidentified young man who asked him about his support for the "Obama agenda". The incident occurred outside a fundraiser last week, and was posted to YouTube by a bystander. Etheridge said he is "profoundly" sorry for his response, but claimed he didn't know the identity of the young men involved. He said he had been having a long day and that the confrontation was not an excuse for his actions. The incident has sparked criticism from political opponents, with one blogger calling it "unbecoming of a member of Congress". Etheridge is running for re-election in November against Republican Renee Ellmers.

END OF SUMMARY.
