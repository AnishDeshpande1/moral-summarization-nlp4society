SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump, speaking from his Asia trip, said he does not want “a mere allegation… from many years ago” to ruin a person’s life, but he added that if the accusations against Alabama GOP Senate nominee Roy Moore are true, “Judge Moore will do the right thing and step aside.” Vice‑president Mike Pence called the allegations “disturbing” and said they would disqualify anyone from office if proven. Moore’s campaign rejected the Washington Post’s report—based on interviews with more than 30 sources and four women—as “fake news.” Republican lawmakers on Capitol Hill urged Moore to withdraw if the claims are true. The piece also recalled Trump’s own history of dismissing sexual‑misconduct accusations against himself. After Moore won the 2016 primary, Trump initially backed establishment candidate Luther Strange, then switched support to Moore, tweeting that he was “a really great guy.” The moral thrust of the story is clear: serious allegations of sexual abuse demand accountability, and deflecting or minimizing them undermines the integrity of public office. END OF SUMMARY.
