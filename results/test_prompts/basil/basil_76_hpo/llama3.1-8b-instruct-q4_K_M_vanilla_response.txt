SUMMARY:

The White House responded to allegations that Alabama GOP Senate nominee Roy Moore sexually molested a 14-year-old girl in 1979. President Trump's press secretary, Sarah Huckabee Sanders, said the president believes that allegations from many years ago shouldn't destroy someone's life, but if true, Moore should step aside. Vice President Mike Pence also expressed concern, stating that if the allegations are true, they would disqualify anyone from serving in office. Moore's campaign denied the allegations, calling them "fake news." However, numerous Republican lawmakers on Capitol Hill called for Moore to drop out of the race if the allegations are true.

Trump has faced similar allegations himself and dismissed them as "locker room talk" during his 2016 campaign. In contrast, he backed a different candidate, Sen. Luther Strange, in the primary, but later disavowed that endorsement and tweeted support for Moore after he won. The allegations have sparked widespread concern among Republicans about Moore's candidacy.

END OF SUMMARY.
