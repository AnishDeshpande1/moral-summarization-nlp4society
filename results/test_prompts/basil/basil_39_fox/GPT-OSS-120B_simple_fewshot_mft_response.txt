SUMMARY:
Mary Cheney, an openly lesbian daughter of former Vice President Dick Cheney, used Facebook to condemn her sister Liz Cheney’s opposition to same‑sex marriage, arguing that denying marriage equality harms families and infringes on their right to love and protection. Mary stressed that “freedom means freedom for everyone” and that all families, no matter how they are formed, deserve equal rights, privileges and safeguards. Liz Cheney countered that marriage should be decided by voters in each state, not by judges, and said she is “not pro‑gay marriage,” a stance Mary labeled as wrong and harmful to LGBTQ Americans. The clash highlights a moral conflict over caring for marginalized families versus upholding a restrictive view of marriage. Their father, former Vice President Dick Cheney, has voiced support for same‑sex marriage, echoing the care‑focused argument that all families should be protected. The dispute unfolds as Liz Cheney prepares to run against incumbent Senator Mike Enzi in the 2014 Wyoming Republican primary.

END OF SUMMARY.
