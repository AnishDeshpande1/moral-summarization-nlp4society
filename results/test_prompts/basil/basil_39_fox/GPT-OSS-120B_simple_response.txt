SUMMARY: Mary Cheney, an openly lesbian daughter of former Vice President Dick Cheney, publicly rebuked her sister Elizabeth Cheney’s anti‑same‑sex‑marriage stance on Facebook. Mary emphasized that “freedom means freedom for everyone,” arguing that all families deserve equal rights and protections. Elizabeth, a Wyoming Senate candidate, had said marriage should be decided by voters state‑by‑state, not by judges, and clarified she is “not pro‑gay marriage.” Mary countered, calling her sister “dead wrong” and urging supporters to like and share her post. The dispute highlights a familial clash over the moral principle of equal liberty, with the younger Cheney championing universal marriage rights while the older Cheney maintains a traditional, states‑rights approach. Their father, former Vice President Dick Cheney, has voiced support for same‑sex marriage but also favors state‑level decisions, mirroring Elizabeth’s position. The public feud underscores how personal convictions about freedom and equality can divide even close family members in the political arena. END OF SUMMARY.
