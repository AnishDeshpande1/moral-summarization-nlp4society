SUMMARY:

Mary Cheney, daughter of former Vice President Dick Cheney, publicly criticized her sister Elizabeth's stance on same-sex marriage on Facebook. Elizabeth, a Wyoming Senate candidate, believes the issue should be decided by voters in each state, rather than judges or legislators. Mary, who is openly lesbian and married, disagreed, stating that all families should have equal rights and protections regardless of their composition. She wrote that freedom means freedom for everyone, including those with same-sex relationships.

Their father, Dick Cheney, has publicly supported same-sex marriage and agrees with Elizabeth's state-by-state approach. The disagreement highlights the complexities within the Republican party on social issues. Elizabeth is running against incumbent Senator Mike Enzi in the 2014 primary. Mary's post sparked a backlash from some, who called for her to support her sister, but others praised her for speaking out.

END OF SUMMARY.
