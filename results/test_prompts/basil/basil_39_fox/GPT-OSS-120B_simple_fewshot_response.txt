SUMMARY:
Mary Cheney, an openly lesbian daughter of former Vice President Dick Cheney, publicly rebuked her sister Liz Cheney’s opposition to same‑sex marriage on Facebook. Mary asserted that “freedom means freedom for everyone” and that all families, no matter how they are formed, deserve equal rights, privileges and protections. Liz Cheney, a 2014 Wyoming Senate candidate, argued that marriage should be decided by voters in each state rather than by judges or legislators, stating she is “not pro‑gay marriage.” The sisters’ clash highlights a family dispute over core values of equality and personal liberty, with their father having voiced support for marriage equality. The moral framing underscores the importance of extending freedom and equal rights to all families, regardless of sexual orientation, and questions the role of personal belief versus universal human rights in public policy.

END OF SUMMARY.
