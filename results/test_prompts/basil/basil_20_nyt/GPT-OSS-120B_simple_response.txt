SUMMARY:  
Rick Santorum tried to soften the backlash after accusing President Obama of following a “phony theology,” insisting he meant Obama’s secular view of humanity, not his personal faith. Obama aide Robert Gibbs condemned Santorum’s remarks as “wrong” and “destructive,” highlighting the ugly, divisive tone of the Republican primary. Santorum also argued that public schools are an outdated, “factory‑style” system, urging a return to locally‑run, parent‑centered education while still allowing limited government support. On health care, he defended his claim that covering prenatal testing leads to more abortions and thus “culls” disabled lives, pointing to his own daughter’s severe chromosomal disorder and accusing Obama of a poor record on disability and abortion. Rival Ron Paul mocked Santorum’s focus on social issues, questioning the political viability of opposing Obama on matters like birth‑control funding amid larger national crises. The episode underscores a moral clash: Santorum frames his positions as protecting family responsibility and societal health, while opponents portray his rhetoric as hateful, divisive, and morally insensitive.  
END OF SUMMARY.
