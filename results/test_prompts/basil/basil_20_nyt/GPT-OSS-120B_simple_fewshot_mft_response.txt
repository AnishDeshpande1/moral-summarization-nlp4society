SUMMARY:
Rick Santorum tried to soften his earlier jab that President Obama follows a “phony theology,” insisting he was attacking the president’s worldview, not his Christian faith. The comment sparked condemnation from Obama adviser Robert Gibbs, who labeled the attack on character and faith as “over the line,” highlighting the divisive, subversive tone of the Republican primary. Santorum also denounced federal and state control of public schools as anachronistic, arguing that education should be locally run and parent‑led, a claim framed as restoring proper authority and fairness to families. On health policy, he defended his criticism of the Obama health‑care mandate for prenatal testing, asserting that free testing leads to more abortions and “culls” disabled children, thereby invoking concerns about societal harm and the sanctity of life. Santorum’s remarks on abortion and disability were unrepentant, portraying Obama as morally deficient on these issues. Rival Ron Paul mocked Santorum’s focus on social issues, suggesting it distracts from larger threats to civil liberties and national debt, underscoring the moral clash over authority, care, fairness, and sanctity in the campaign.  

END OF SUMMARY.
