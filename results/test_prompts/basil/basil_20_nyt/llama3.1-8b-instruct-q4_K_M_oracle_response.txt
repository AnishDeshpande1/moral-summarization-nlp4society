SUMMARY: 
Rick Santorum's comments about President Obama's "phony theology" have been criticized as crossing the line and being destructive, reflecting the nastiness and divisiveness of the Republican nominating process. Mr. Gibbs, a political adviser to President Obama, condemned such attacks on character and faith, calling them wrong. Mr. Santorum clarified that he was referring not to the president's faith but to his view of man's relationship to the world, saying it is a different ideal based on a secular worldview rather than a biblical one. He also defended his criticism of the president's healthcare policy, which requires insurance companies to cover prenatal testing, stating that this provision is an attempt to save money by culling the ranks of the disabled in society through abortion. Mr. Santorum has been criticized for focusing on social issues like abortion and contraception rather than addressing pressing concerns like civil liberties, wars, and debt. His stance has been seen as undermining the moral framework that prioritizes human dignity and well-being.

END OF SUMMARY.
