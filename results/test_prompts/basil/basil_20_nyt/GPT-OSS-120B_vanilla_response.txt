SUMMARY:  
Rick Santorum attempted to clarify his “phony theology” comment about President Obama, saying he was criticizing Obama’s worldview rather than his faith. Obama adviser Robert Gibbs condemned the remarks as “over the line” and indicative of Republican divisiveness. Santorum also argued that federal or state control of schools is “anachronistic,” advocating locally‑run, parent‑focused education while acknowledging a role for government funding. He defended his criticism of the Obama health‑care mandate on prenatal testing, claiming it encourages abortions of disabled fetuses, and reiterated his view that Obama has a poor record on abortion and disabled children. Ron Paul mocked Santorum’s focus on social issues, questioning the electoral viability of such a strategy amid concerns over civil liberties, wars, and the national debt.  

END OF SUMMARY.
