SUMMARY:

Rick Santorum sparked controversy on Saturday when he referred to President Obama's agenda as having a "phony theology" that is not based on the Bible. He clarified on Sunday that he meant to say that Obama's view of man's relationship to the world is different from his own, but still a valid belief system. Santorum also criticized public education and the federal government's role in it, suggesting that parents should be in charge of their children's education.

Santorum defended his criticism of President Obama's healthcare policy, which requires insurance companies to cover prenatal testing. He argued that this is done to save money by reducing the number of disabled children born, a claim that was disputed by some critics. Representative Ron Paul criticized Santorum for focusing on social issues rather than more pressing concerns like civil liberties and national debt.

Robert Gibbs, an adviser to President Obama, called out Santorum's comments as "nastiness" and "destructive".
