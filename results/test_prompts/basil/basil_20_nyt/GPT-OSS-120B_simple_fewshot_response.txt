SUMMARY:
Rick Santorum sought to clarify his “phony theology” remark about President Obama, insisting he was attacking Obama’s worldview—not his Christian faith. He argued that Obama’s agenda elevates the Earth above humanity, a “radical environmentalist” stance that Santorum deems misguided. Obama adviser Robert Gibbs condemned the comment as a personal attack, calling it “destructive” and emblematic of the “nastiness, divisiveness, ugliness” of the GOP primary. Santorum also warned that federal or state control of public schools is “anachronistic,” advocating locally‑run, parent‑centered education while acknowledging a limited role for government funding. He defended his criticism of the Obama health‑care mandate on prenatal testing, linking it to abortion and the treatment of disabled children, and refused to soften his view of Obama’s record on those issues. Rival Ron Paul mocked Santorum’s focus on social issues, questioning the electoral viability of such a strategy amid broader concerns about civil liberties, wars and the national debt. The episode underscores the heated moral framing of the Republican contest, with Santorum positioning himself as a defender of traditional values against what he portrays as Obama’s “phony” ideology.  
END OF SUMMARY.
