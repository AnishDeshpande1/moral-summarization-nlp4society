SUMMARY:

Rick Santorum faced criticism for his comments on President Obama's faith, which he referred to as a "phony theology". Santorum claimed that Obama's views were not based on the Bible, but rather a different ideology. Robert Gibbs, an adviser to Obama, called such attacks on character and faith "wrong" and "destructive".

Santorum also faced scrutiny for his views on education, stating that public schools are an "anachronistic" concept and suggesting that parents should be in charge of their children's education. He defended his criticism of the president's health-care policy, which he claimed was aimed at saving money by reducing prenatal testing and subsequent abortions.

Santorum's comments sparked a debate among Republican rivals, with Ron Paul criticizing him for focusing on social issues rather than more pressing concerns such as civil liberties and national debt. Santorum's stance has been criticized as divisive and lacking in compassion, particularly given his own experiences as a father of a child with a serious chromosomal disorder.

END OF SUMMARY.
