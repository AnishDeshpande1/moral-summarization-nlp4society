SUMMARY:  
Rick Santorum tried to clarify his remark that President Obama follows a “phony theology,” insisting he was attacking the president’s view of humanity rather than his personal faith. Obama adviser Robert Gibbs condemned the comment as an attack that crossed the line, calling it wrong, destructive and a symptom of divisiveness in the Republican nominating charge. Santorum also denounced federal and state control of schools as anachronistic, arguing that education should be locally run and that parents ought to optimize their children’s potential in partnership with nearby districts. He defended his criticism of the health‑care law’s prenatal‑testing requirement, claiming it encourages abortions and thus “culls” disabled lives, a stance he said reflects the president’s lack of compassion for the disabled. Rival Ron Paul mocked Santorum’s focus on social issues, warning that such emphasis undermines civil liberties while the nation faces wars and a $16 trillion debt.  

END OF SUMMARY.
