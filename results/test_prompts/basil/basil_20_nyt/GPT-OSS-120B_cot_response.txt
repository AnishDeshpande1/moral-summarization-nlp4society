**SUMMARY:**  
Rick Santorum tried to clarify his criticism of President Obama’s “phony theology,” saying it referred to the president’s view of man’s relationship to the world, not to his personal faith. Obama adviser Robert Gibbs condemned the remarks as “wrong,” “destructive,” and emblematic of the “nastiness, divisiveness, ugliness” of the Republican nominating process. Santorum also called federal and state control of schools “anachronistic,” arguing that education should be locally run and parents should lead. While he does not oppose government financing of schools, he insists parents must partner with local districts to tailor learning for each child. Santorum defended his comment that prenatal testing saves money because “free prenatal testing ends up in more abortions and therefore less care… we cull the ranks of the disabled,” accusing Obama of a “bad record” on abortion and disabled children. Rival Ron Paul mocked Santorum’s focus on abortion and contraception, questioning the strategy amid concerns about civil liberties, wars, and the $16 trillion debt.
