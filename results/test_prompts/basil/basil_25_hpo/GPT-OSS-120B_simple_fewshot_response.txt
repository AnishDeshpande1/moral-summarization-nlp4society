SUMMARY:
The debate over Medicare has turned into a blame‑the‑other‑party moral showdown. After Mitt Romney picked Paul Ryan as his running mate, the Romney campaign accused President Obama of “stealing” $700 billion from Medicare to fund Obamacare, painting Obama as the one with “blood on his hands.” In response, the Obama team points out that the Republican plan would turn Medicare into a quasi‑voucher system, effectively cutting benefits for seniors. Both sides invoke past campaign attacks—Obama’s 2008 attacks on John McCain and the 2010 GOP charge that Obamacare raided Medicare—to bolster their moral narratives. Fact‑checkers note that the alleged cuts are largely savings from reduced waste, fraud and slower growth, not direct benefit reductions, but the political rhetoric frames the issue as a stark moral choice: Republicans promise to “protect” Medicare, while Democrats accuse them of planning to “raid” and undermine seniors’ guarantees. This moral framing fuels the controversy and discourages substantive policy discussion.

END OF SUMMARY.
