SUMMARY:

The politics surrounding Medicare have become increasingly complicated as the Romney and Obama campaigns trade blows over which candidate would do more harm to people's benefits. The Romney campaign claims that Obama took $700 billion out of Medicare to fund Obamacare, while the Obama team disputes this characterization, saying the money was used to limit waste, fraud, and abuse in the program. However, it is revealed that Obama himself used similar language against his 2010 opponent John McCain, accusing him of cutting Medicare by $800 billion.

The issue has sparked a debate over the accuracy of both campaigns' claims, with fact-checkers and analysts disputing the Romney campaign's characterization. The back-and-forth has contributed to a reluctance to discuss Medicare reform substantively, out of fear that it will invite future attacks. The article notes that this development could complicate the political dialogue even further.
