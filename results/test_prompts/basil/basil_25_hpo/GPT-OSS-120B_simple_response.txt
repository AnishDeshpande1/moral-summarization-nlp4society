SUMMARY:  
The article frames the Medicare fight as a moral showdown over who is “stealing” from seniors. Romney’s campaign accuses President Obama of draining $700 billion from Medicare to fund Obamacare, painting Obama as having “blood on his hands.” In response, fact‑checkers note that the ACA’s savings come from cutting waste, fraud and abuse—not from cutting benefits. The piece recalls that Obama himself used similar rhetoric against John McCain in 2008, claiming McCain’s plan would slash Medicare by $800‑$882 billion, a claim also debunked as misleading. Both parties have weaponized Medicare rhetoric to cast opponents as heartless toward seniors, while each side touts their own “savings” as protective of elder health care. The constant back‑and‑forth creates a moral narrative that any proposal threatening Medicare is an attack on the nation’s “golden years,” making substantive policy discussion difficult and encouraging political fear‑mongering.  

END OF SUMMARY.
