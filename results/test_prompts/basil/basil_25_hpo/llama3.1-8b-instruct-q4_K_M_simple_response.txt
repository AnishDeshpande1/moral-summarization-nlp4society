SUMMARY:

In the US presidential election, both candidates have been accused of harming Medicare benefits. Mitt Romney's campaign has criticized President Obama for taking $700 billion from Medicare to fund his health care overhaul, known as Obamacare. However, independent fact-checkers and news analysts have disputed this characterization, stating that the money was taken by limiting waste, fraud, and abuse in the program.

In 2010, President Obama used similar language against Senator McCain, accusing him of cutting $800 billion from Medicare to pay for his health care plan. This attack was rated as "false" by FactCheck.org, which found that McCain's proposal aimed to limit waste and slow the rate of growth, not cut benefits.

The debate over Medicare has become increasingly complicated, with both candidates accused of being dishonest about their proposals. The issue has led to a reluctance to discuss Medicare reform substantively, due to concerns that it will invite future attacks.
