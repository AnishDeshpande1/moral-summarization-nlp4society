SUMMARY:  
The 2012 presidential race has turned Medicare into a political flashpoint. After Mitt Romney selected Paul Ryan as his running mate, the Obama and Romney campaigns began trading accusations over who would harm Medicare benefits more. Republicans claim President Obama “stole” $700 billion from Medicare to fund the Affordable Care Act (ACA), arguing the law reduces future program growth by cutting waste, fraud, and Medicare Advantage costs. Fact‑checkers dispute this, noting the savings are modest and intended to subsidize ACA coverage, not slash benefits. The tactic echoes earlier attacks: Obama accused John McCain in 2008 of cutting $800‑$882 billion from Medicare, while McCain’s team defended those claims as efficiency reforms rather than benefit reductions. FactCheck.org labeled Obama’s ads false. Both parties have previously used Medicare‑related rhetoric to score political points, complicating substantive policy discussion. Analysts warn that the recurring mud‑slinging may deter serious debate on Medicare reform.

END OF SUMMARY.
