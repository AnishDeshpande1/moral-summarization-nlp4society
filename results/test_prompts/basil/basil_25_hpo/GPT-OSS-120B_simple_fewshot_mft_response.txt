SUMMARY:
The debate over Medicare has turned into a partisan battle over who is harming seniors and subverting responsible authority. The Romney campaign, invoking RNC Chair Reince Priebus, accuses President Obama of “stealing $700 billion” from Medicare to fund Obamacare, framing the president as a cheater who endangers the vulnerable. In response, the Obama team points out that the Republican plan would convert Medicare into a voucher system, potentially reducing benefits and betraying the duty of government to protect seniors—an act they label as harmful and unfair. Both sides invoke past attacks: Obama once warned that John McCain’s plan would cut Medicare by $800‑$882 billion, while Republicans recall that Obama’s own savings come from cutting waste, not benefits. Fact‑checkers dispute the exaggerated claims, emphasizing that the real dispute is over authority to manage Medicare responsibly, the care owed to seniors, and the fairness of each party’s accounting. The moral framing highlights authority versus subversion, the care of seniors versus harm to their benefits, and accusations of cheating through misleading fiscal rhetoric. END OF SUMMARY.
