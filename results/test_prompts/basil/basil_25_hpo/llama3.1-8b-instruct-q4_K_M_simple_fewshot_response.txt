SUMMARY:

The politics of Medicare have become increasingly complicated in the wake of Mitt Romney's choice of Paul Ryan as his vice presidential nominee. Both campaigns are accusing each other of doing more harm to people's benefits. The Romney campaign has pointed out that President Obama took $700 billion out of Medicare as part of his health care overhaul, while the Obama team disputes this characterization and argues that the savings come from limiting waste, fraud, and abuse. This debate is not new - in 2010, then-Senator Obama used similar language to attack Senator McCain's plan, which proposed cutting $800 billion from Medicare. The Romney campaign has responded by accusing Obama of raiding Medicare, a charge that originated with a Wall Street Journal article. Fact-checkers have disputed this characterization, but it remains a potent political issue.

END OF SUMMARY.
