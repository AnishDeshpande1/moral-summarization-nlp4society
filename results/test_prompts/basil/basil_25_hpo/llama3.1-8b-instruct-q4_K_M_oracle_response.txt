SUMMARY:

The Romney campaign's claim that Obama stole $700 billion from Medicare to fund Obamacare is being disputed by independent fact-checkers, who argue that the money was taken out of the system to limit waste, fraud, and abuse, mostly in Medicare Advantage. The savings are budgeted to help subsidize insurance for people under the ACA, which would provide them with access to preventive care and save them $600 a year on prescription drugs.

The Romney campaign's characterization of Obama's actions is being seen as a rejoinder that may do more harm to people's benefits than good. Republicans have used similar attacks in the past, including during the 2010 congressional elections when Senator Obama accused Senator McCain of cutting Medicare by $800 billion and making drastic cuts to pay for his health care plan.

The current debate over Medicare proposals has become complicated due to the reuse of these attack lines, which may contribute to the reluctance to talk substantively about Medicare reform out of a concern that it merely invites future attacks. The Romney campaign's refusal to specify how they will pay for their tax cuts and reforms may also raise questions about their commitment to protecting Medicare benefits.

END OF SUMMARY.
