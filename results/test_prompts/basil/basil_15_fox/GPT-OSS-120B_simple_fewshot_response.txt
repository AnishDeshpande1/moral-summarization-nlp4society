SUMMARY:
Rep. Michele Bachmann has refused to confront Newsweek over its “Queen of Rage” cover, but the magazine is being condemned as sexist and misogynistic. NOW President Terry O’Neill argued the title would never be applied to a man—citing Gloria Steinem’s test—and said it paints a serious presidential contender as a “nut‑job,” something not done to male politicians like Howard Dean. Newsweek defended the close‑up photo as one of several flattering images. Bachmann, who has not seen the cover, brushed off the story while campaigning in Iowa. O’Neill said her group opposes the depiction because it discourages women from running for office, even though they aim to defeat Bachmann politically. Conservative media watchdog Brent Bozell called the cover an example of long‑standing journalistic dishonesty toward conservatives and urged conservatives to demand editorial oversight. The moral of the story is that the press must avoid gender‑based slurs and biased portrayals, ensuring fair treatment of all candidates regardless of sex or ideology.  
END OF SUMMARY.
