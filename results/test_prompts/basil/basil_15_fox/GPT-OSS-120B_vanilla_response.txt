SUMMARY: Rep. Michele Bachmann has avoided commenting on Newsweek’s “The Queen of Rage” cover, which features an unflattering close‑up of the Minnesota Republican. The National Organization for Women (NOW) President Terry O’Neill called the cover sexist, arguing that a comparable label such as “King of Rage” would never be applied to a man and citing Gloria Steinem’s test for gender bias. NOW says the portrayal casts Bachmann as a “nut job” and could discourage women from seeking office. Newsweek defended the image, noting that similar photos of Bachmann were taken and that other, more flattering pictures appear on its site. Conservative commentator Brent Bozell said the cover reflects a longstanding bias against conservatives in mainstream magazines and warned that such “journalistic dishonesty” makes those outlets unreliable. Bachmann, campaigning in Iowa, said she had not seen the cover but would look at it. END OF SUMMARY.
