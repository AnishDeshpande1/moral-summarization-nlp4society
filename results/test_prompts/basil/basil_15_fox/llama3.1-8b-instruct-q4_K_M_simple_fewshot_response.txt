SUMMARY:

Rep. Michele Bachmann's presidential campaign was targeted by Newsweek magazine, which featured her on its cover as "The Queen of Rage" accompanied by an unflattering photo. However, critics have accused the magazine of sexism and bias, pointing out that such a headline would never be applied to a male politician. The National Organization for Women President Terry O'Neill called the cover "sexist," while Brent Bozell of the Media Research Center described it as "sheer journalistic dishonesty." Despite disagreeing with Bachmann on many issues, NOW is defending her against what they see as misogynistic attacks, emphasizing that women should be encouraged to run for office without facing such biased coverage. The incident highlights the need for fairness and accuracy in media representation, particularly when it comes to powerful female politicians.
