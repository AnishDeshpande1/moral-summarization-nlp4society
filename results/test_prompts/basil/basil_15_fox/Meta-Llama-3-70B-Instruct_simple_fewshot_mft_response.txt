SUMMARY:

Newsweek's latest cover featuring Michele Bachmann has sparked controversy, with critics accusing the magazine of sexism and bias. The cover, dubbed "The Queen of Rage," shows an unflattering photo of Bachmann, which many argue is a deliberate attempt to portray her as unstable and irrational. The National Organization for Women President Terry O'Neill condemned the cover as "sexist," arguing that a similar depiction would never be applied to a male candidate. The magazine's defense of the photo rings hollow, as it is clear that the image was chosen to perpetuate a biased narrative. The moral of the story is that the media has a responsibility to present fair and balanced representations of public figures, rather than sensationalized and biased portrayals that can be damaging to their reputation and the public's perception of them. By engaging in such journalistic dishonesty, Newsweek undermines its credibility and perpetuates a culture of unfairness and cheating.
