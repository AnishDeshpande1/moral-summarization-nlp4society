SUMMARY:

The cover of Newsweek's latest edition, featuring Rep. Michele Bachmann as "The Queen of Rage," has been deemed sexist by the National Organization for Women President Terry O'Neill. The depiction is seen as misogynistic and an attack on a serious contender for president. O'Neill explained that if a man were to be referred to in such a way, it would not be considered acceptable. Newsweek defended the photo, but many are calling it out of bounds. Rep. Bachmann herself declined to comment on the cover, saying she hadn't seen it. However, NOW is standing up for Bachmann, despite disagreeing with her on most issues, because they want to protect women from such attacks and promote a culture where women feel empowered to run for office. Brent Bozell of the Media Research Center agrees that Newsweek's depiction is dishonest and calls for editorial oversight to prevent such sexist and misogynistic portrayals in the future.

END OF SUMMARY.
