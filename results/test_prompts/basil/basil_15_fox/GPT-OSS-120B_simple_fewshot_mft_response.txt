SUMMARY:
Rep. Michele Bachmann refused to engage with Newsweek over its “Queen of Rage” cover, which critics say unfairly maligns the Republican presidential hopeful. NOW President Terry O’Neill called the depiction sexist and a double standard, asking whether a man would ever be labeled “King of Rage” and noting that Howard Dean never received a similar slur. She argued that such misogynistic treatment cheats women of a fair chance to run for office, warning that biased media portrayals discourage female candidates. The magazine defended the photo as one of several images, but media watchdog Brent Bozell accused Newsweek of a long‑standing pattern of dishonest, unfair coverage of conservatives. The controversy highlights concerns that Newsweek is cheating the principles of fair, unbiased journalism by singling out a female candidate with a demeaning label and image. END OF SUMMARY.
