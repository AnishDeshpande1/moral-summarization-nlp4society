SUMMARY:
The controversy centers on Newsweek’s cover labeling Republican presidential hopeful Michele Bachmann “The Queen of Rage” with an unflattering close‑up photo. National Organization for Women president Terry O’Neill condemned the cover as sexist, invoking Gloria Steinem’s test—no magazine would call a man “The King of Rage.” She argued the depiction reduces a serious female candidate to a “nut‑job” and warns that such misogynistic attacks discourage women from seeking office. Newsweek defended the image, noting multiple similar photos and posting flattering alternatives online. Bachmann, who said she had not seen the cover, brushed off the criticism during a campaign stop in Iowa. Conservative commentator Brent Bozell echoed the criticism of mainstream weeklies’ bias against conservatives, calling the practice “journalistic dishonesty” and urging conservatives to avoid or demand oversight from such outlets. The dispute highlights broader moral concerns about gender bias in media and its chilling effect on women’s political participation.  
END OF SUMMARY.
