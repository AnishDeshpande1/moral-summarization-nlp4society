Newsweek's latest cover featuring Michele Bachmann has sparked controversy, with critics accusing the magazine of sexism and bias against conservatives. The cover shows Bachmann with an unflattering photo and the headline "THE QUEEN OF RAGE," which has been deemed "sexist" by the National Organization for Women President Terry O'Neill. O'Neill argues that the magazine would not have used a similar headline for a male candidate, and that the depiction of Bachmann as "crazy" is unfair and damaging to her reputation. The moral of the story is that the media has a responsibility to present fair and balanced representations of public figures, rather than sensationalized and biased portrayals that can be damaging to their reputation and the public's perception of them. By standing up against such attacks, we can promote a more respectful and inclusive political discourse that encourages women to run for office. END OF SUMMARY.
