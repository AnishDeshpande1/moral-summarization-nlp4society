**SUMMARY:**  
Newsweek’s cover calling Republican presidential hopeful Michele Bachmann “The Queen of Rage” sparked accusations of sexism and misogyny. NOW President Terry O’Neill condemned the image as a “sexist, misogynistic attack,” arguing that no man would be labeled “King of Rage” and that such depictions cast women as “nut‑job” or “crazy.” The magazine defended the photo as one of many flattering shots, but critics like Brent Bozell called the cover an example of journalistic dishonesty toward conservatives. Bachmann, who said she hadn’t seen the cover, brushed off the controversy while campaigning in Iowa. O’Neill stressed that misogynistic media treatment discourages women from running for office, even as she vows to defeat Bachmann politically.
