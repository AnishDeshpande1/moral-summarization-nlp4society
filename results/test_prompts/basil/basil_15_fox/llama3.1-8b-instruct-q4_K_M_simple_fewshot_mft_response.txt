SUMMARY:

Newsweek's latest cover featuring Michele Bachmann has sparked controversy, with critics accusing the magazine of sexism and bias against conservatives. The National Organization for Women President Terry O'Neill called the cover "sexist" and argued that the same image would not be applied to a man in a similar position. The magazine defended the photo by saying it was an extreme close-up taken from several angles, but many photos of Bachmann posted on their website are flattering. Brent Bozell, head of the Media Research Center, accused Newsweek of "journalistic dishonesty" and suggested that conservatives should demand editorial oversight or steer away from such venues altogether. The controversy highlights concerns about fairness and bias in media representation, particularly when it comes to women in politics. As NOW's Terry O'Neill stated, if good women are discouraged from running for office due to unfair treatment by the media, it would be a significant loss for democracy.
