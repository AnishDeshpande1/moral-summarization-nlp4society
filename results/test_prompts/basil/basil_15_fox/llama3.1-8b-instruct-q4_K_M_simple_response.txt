SUMMARY:

Newsweek magazine's cover story on Michele Bachmann, calling her the "Queen of Rage," has been criticized for being sexist by the National Organization for Women (NOW) President Terry O'Neill and others. They argue that a man would not be referred to as the "King of Rage" in a similar situation, and that Newsweek is unfairly casting Bachmann as a "nut job." NOW's Gloria Steinem explained that she uses a test to determine if a description is sexist: would it be applied to a man? If not, then it is. Newsweek has defended the photo, but many are calling out the magazine for its depiction of Bachmann.

Critics argue that this kind of treatment can discourage women from running for office. NOW's President Terry O'Neill said that her group will defend Bachmann against these "misogynistic attacks" because they want to encourage women to run for public office. The Media Research Center's Brent Bozell also criticized Newsweek, saying it is an example of journalistic dishonesty and that conservatives should demand editorial oversight from the magazine.

END OF SUMMARY.
