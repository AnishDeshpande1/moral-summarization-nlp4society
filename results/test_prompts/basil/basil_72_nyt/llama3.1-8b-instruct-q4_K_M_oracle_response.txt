SUMMARY:

President Trump is facing criticism for his rapid embrace of Joseph I. Lieberman, the former Democratic senator, as a finalist for F.B.I. director. Administration officials have expressed concerns that Mr. Lieberman's lack of federal law enforcement experience and his age (75) could be problematic. Despite these reservations, Mr. Trump has indicated that he cherishes the F.B.I. and wants to appoint a "great" director. The president's decision-making process has been closely held to avoid leaks and speculation.

The selection of an F.B.I. director is seen as critical due to the damage caused by James B. Comey's sudden firing and the ouster of his predecessor, William S. Sessions. Mr. Trump's staff members have urged him to take more time to make a decision, but he appears increasingly likely to leave for a foreign trip without making a choice.

The White House remains divided on the issue, with some officials questioning whether Mr. Lieberman would be an appropriate choice given his law enforcement experience as Connecticut attorney general and his ties to Marc E. Kasowitz, one of Trump's lawyers.

END OF SUMMARY.
