SUMMARY: President Trump, facing a self‑imposed deadline to name a new FBI director, indicated that former Democratic Senator and 2000 vice‑presidential nominee Joseph I. Lieberman is a leading finalist. While Trump praised the FBI and hinted at Lieberman’s candidacy, senior staff members warned that the choice is unconventional—Lieberman lacks federal law‑enforcement experience, is 75, and works for a firm linked to Trump’s lawyer, raising conflict‑of‑interest concerns. Some officials suggest promoting from within, naming Acting Director Andrew G. McCabe, Special Agent Adam S. Lee, or former senior official Richard A. McFeely as alternatives. Other candidates include former Gov. Frank Keating, former Rep. Mike Rogers (a former FBI agent), and judges Michael J. Garcia and Henry E. Hudson. The White House remains divided, and Trump may postpone the decision during a planned foreign trip, risking further criticism from Democrats and concerns about agency morale after Comey’s firing. END OF SUMMARY.
