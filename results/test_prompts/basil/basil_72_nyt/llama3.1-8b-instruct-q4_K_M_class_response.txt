SUMMARY:

President Trump is under pressure to appoint a new F.B.I. director, but his staff is urging him to take more time due to concerns about the rapid selection of Joseph I. Lieberman, a former Democratic senator with no federal law enforcement experience. The president's team fears that Mr. Lieberman's appointment could cause further damage to the bureau's reputation and morale, which was already hurt by James B. Comey's sudden firing in May. Members of Trump's staff are urging him to repair some of this damage by choosing a candidate with more relevant expertise.

Despite these concerns, Mr. Trump has expressed enthusiasm for Mr. Lieberman, calling him "very close" to being selected. However, several senior officials have expressed serious doubts about his suitability for the job, citing his age and potential conflict-of-interest accusations. Other candidates, including Adam S. Lee, Richard A. McFeely, and Andrew G. McCabe, are still under consideration.

The White House remains divided on the issue, with some officials pushing for a more experienced candidate to lead the F.B.I.

END OF SUMMARY.
