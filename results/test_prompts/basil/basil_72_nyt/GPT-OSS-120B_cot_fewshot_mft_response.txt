President Trump, just 24 hours before his self‑imposed deadline to name a new FBI director, told reporters he was “very close” to a choice and confirmed former Democratic senator Joseph I. Lieberman as a finalist. Staff members, alarmed by the rapid embrace of the 75‑year‑old, charming political operator with no federal law‑enforcement background, urged a more careful, critical process to avoid damage to FBI morale and the agency’s apolitical reputation. Trump emphasized his “emphatic” support for the FBI, saying he “cherish[es]” the bureau and its special role, even as he faces a firestorm of criticism and accusations of conflict‑of‑interest. Democrats pushed back hard, warning of a potential betrayal of fairness and loyalty, while some Republicans, including John McCain, offered friendly support. The White House remains divided, with serious doubts about Lieberman’s age, credibility, and possible short‑term tenure, and other candidates—including internal agents and former officials—still under consideration.
