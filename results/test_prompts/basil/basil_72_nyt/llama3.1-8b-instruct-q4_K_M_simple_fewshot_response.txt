SUMMARY:
President Trump is close to choosing a new F.B.I. director, with Joseph I. Lieberman, a former Democratic senator and vice-presidential nominee, emerging as a front-runner. However, members of Trump's staff are urging him to take more time to make the critical hire, citing concerns about Lieberman's lack of federal law enforcement experience and potential conflicts of interest. Despite these reservations, Trump has expressed enthusiasm for Lieberman, praising his "bipartisan reputation" and suggesting that he would be a good choice to restore credibility to the F.B.I. after the controversy surrounding James Comey's firing. Other candidates, including former Gov. Frank Keating and Rep. Mike Rogers, are also under consideration. The search process has been marked by a lack of transparency, with Trump keeping the decision-making closely held to avoid leaks.
