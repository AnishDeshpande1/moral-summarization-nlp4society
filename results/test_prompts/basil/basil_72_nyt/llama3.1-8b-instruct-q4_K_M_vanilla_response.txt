SUMMARY:

President Trump is reportedly close to choosing a new F.B.I. director, with Joseph I. Lieberman, the former Democratic senator and vice-presidential nominee, as a finalist. However, members of Trump's staff are urging him to take more time to make the decision, citing concerns about Lieberman's lack of federal law enforcement experience. Administration officials describe the search process as fluid, but acknowledge that the president is keeping it closely held to avoid leaks.

Trump has expressed his fondness for the F.B.I. and its reputation, saying "the F.B.I. is special." He also acknowledged that Lieberman was among the finalists when asked by reporters, sparking speculation about whether he will ultimately choose him. Other contenders include Adam S. Lee, a special agent in charge of the Richmond field office, and Richard A. McFeely, a former senior official at the F.B.I.

The White House remains divided on the selection process, with some officials expressing doubts about Lieberman's suitability for the role due to his age and potential conflicts of interest.

END OF SUMMARY.
