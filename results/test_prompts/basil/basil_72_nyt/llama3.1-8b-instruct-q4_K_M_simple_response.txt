SUMMARY:

President Trump is expected to make a decision on a new F.B.I. director, but his staff is urging him to take more time due to concerns about the candidate's qualifications. Joseph I. Lieberman, a former Democratic senator and vice-presidential nominee, has been named as a finalist, but many officials are skeptical of his ability to lead the agency. Lieberman has no federal law enforcement experience, which raises questions about whether he is the right choice for the job.

The decision on who to choose as F.B.I. director is being kept closely held by the administration in an effort to avoid leaks and speculation. However, Trump's comments suggest that Lieberman may be his top pick. Many officials are urging Trump to hire from within the agency to repair morale after the sudden firing of James B. Comey.

The selection process has been fluid, with several candidates being considered, including Adam S. Lee, Richard A. McFeely, and Andrew G. McCabe, who is currently acting director. The decision will have significant implications for the agency's reputation and effectiveness.
