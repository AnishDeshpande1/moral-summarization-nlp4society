SUMMARY: The U.S. House of Representatives passed the Democrats’ sweeping anti‑corruption and voting‑rights bill, the For the People Act (H.R. 1), by a 234‑193 vote. The nearly 700‑page measure seeks to expand voting access—making Election Day a federal holiday, instituting automatic voter registration, and restoring voting rights to former felons—while curbing big‑money influence through a matching‑funds system for small donors and new disclosure rules for super‑PACs and dark‑money groups. It also targets ethical lapses in the executive branch, requiring presidents, vice presidents and major candidates to release ten years of tax returns, mandating disclosure of inaugural committee spending, and prohibiting federal funds from being used at businesses owned by top officials. Additional provisions would increase transparency of presidential travel costs and ban political appointees from using private flights for official business. Republicans, led by Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell and House GOP leader Kevin McCarthy, condemned the bill and vowed not to consider it in the Senate, labeling it a “Democrat Politician Protection Act.” Despite procedural attacks and criticism from groups like the ACLU over First Amendment concerns, Democrats held the caucus together and passed the legislation, though it faces slim chances of Senate approval. END OF SUMMARY.
