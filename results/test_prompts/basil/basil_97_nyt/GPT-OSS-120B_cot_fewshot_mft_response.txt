SUMMARY:
The House passed the Democrats’ sweeping anti‑corruption and voting‑rights package, H.R. 1, framing it as a fight for fairness and a check on authority that has been subverted by “big‑money” influence. Democrats described the bill as a necessary “power grab” for the American people to restore integrity, curb corruption, and end cheating by special interests. Key provisions include a federal election‑day holiday, automatic voter registration, restoration of voting rights for former felons, and a six‑to‑one matching system that forces disclosure of donors and penalizes illegal contributions. The legislation also seeks to hold presidents, vice‑presidents and cabinet officials to higher ethics standards by mandating tax‑return releases and prohibiting federal funds from benefiting businesses owned by the administration, aiming to prevent unfair violations of the public trust. Republicans denounced the measure as a massive federal takeover that would undermine election integrity and devalue the franchise of U.S. citizens. Despite procedural attacks, Democrats kept the bill intact. END OF SUMMARY.
