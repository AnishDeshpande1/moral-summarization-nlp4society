SUMMARY:
The House overwhelmingly approved the sweeping “For the People Act,” a 700‑page anti‑corruption and voting‑rights package that seeks to make elections more inclusive, transparent, and ethical. By designating Election Day a federal holiday, auto‑registering voters, restoring voting rights for formerly incarcerated citizens, and tightening disclosure of political donations, the bill aims to curb the outsized influence of money and restore public trust in government. Democrats framed the legislation as a moral imperative to protect democracy, hold leaders to higher standards, and ensure every citizen’s voice is heard. Republicans denounced it as a partisan power grab and warned it would expand federal control and threaten free‑speech rights. While the measure is unlikely to survive the Senate, its passage signals a commitment to clean up Washington, increase accountability, and uphold the principle that government should serve the people, not special interests. END OF SUMMARY.
