**SUMMARY:**  
The House passed the Democrats’ showcase anti‑*corruption* and *voting rights* bill, a sweeping effort to *clean* up Washington and restore *integrity* to the nation’s *democracy*. Proponents call it a “*power grab* for the American people,” aiming to end *undue* *influence* of big money, increase *transparency*, and enforce stricter *ethical* standards on federal officials. Key provisions include making Election Day a federal holiday, automatic voter registration, restoring voting rights for former felons, and a matching‑funds system to curb high‑donor contributions. The bill also demands disclosure of *dark‑money* donors, requires presidents and candidates to release ten years of tax returns, and bans federal spending at businesses tied to top officials—measures framed as essential for *accountability* and *justice*. Republicans denounce it as a federal takeover that threatens *freedom* and *fair* elections, branding it the “Democrat Politician Protection Act.” Despite procedural attacks, Democrats held firm, emphasizing that the legislation is needed to protect the *rights* of citizens and prevent future *abuse*.
