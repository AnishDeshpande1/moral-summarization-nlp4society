SUMMARY:  
The House overwhelmingly approved the Democrats’ sweeping anti‑corruption and voting‑rights package, H.R. 1, positioning it as a moral crusade to restore power to ordinary Americans and cleanse Washington of “big‑money” influence. Proponents frame the bill as a promise‑keeping effort – a “power grab for the American people” – that would make Election Day a federal holiday, auto‑register voters, return voting rights to former felons, and match small donations to candidates who reject large contributions. The legislation also seeks transparency by forcing disclosure of dark‑money donors and requiring top officials, including the president, to release ten years of tax returns, arguing that ethical lapses have eroded public trust.  

Republicans denounce the measure as a partisan overreach, labeling it the “Democrat Politician Protection Act” and warning that it threatens election integrity and free speech. Despite fierce partisan battles and procedural attacks, Democrats held firm, arguing that without confronting special‑interest influence and holding leaders to higher standards, the nation cannot address its biggest challenges.  

The bill now faces an almost certain Senate defeat, but its passage reflects a moral framing of democracy as a right to be protected from corruption and elitist control.  

END OF SUMMARY.
