Etheridge said he “deeply and profoundly regrets” his **actions** and affirmed his long‑standing commitment to treat all viewpoints with **respect**. He pledged to **promote** civil public discourse and warned that partisan politics do not excuse a poor **conduct**. The incident, captured by a passerby’s camera and later posted by a conservative website, drew criticism from Republican officials, who called the congressman’s **conduct** “unbecoming.” Etheridge, who won his seat by a large margin in a conservative district, faces a re‑election challenge but remains out of the top Republican target list. The episode coincided with Vice President Joe Biden’s renewed push for gun‑control legislation. END OF SUMMARY.
