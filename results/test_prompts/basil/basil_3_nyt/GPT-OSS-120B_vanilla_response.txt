SUMMARY: Representative Bob Etheridge (D‑NC) apologized after a video showed him forcibly grabbing a young man’s hand, arm, and neck while the man asked if Etheridge supported President Obama’s agenda. Etheridge said he deeply regretted his reaction and emphasized his commitment to civil discourse. The incident, captured by the man’s camera and later posted on conservative site Breitbart, was amplified by the National Republican Congressional Committee, prompting criticism from Republican spokesperson Jon Thompson. Etheridge, a seven‑term incumbent in a conservative district south of Raleigh, won his last election by a large margin, though the district voted for Obama by 6 %. The video has drawn political attention amid his upcoming re‑election bid, which is not a top Republican target. The article also notes that Vice President Joe Biden resumed a White House gun‑control campaign, highlighting executive actions and urging congressional legislation. END OF SUMMARY.
