SUMMARY:

Representative Bob Etheridge, a Democrat from North Carolina, apologized on Monday for his behavior in a video that surfaced showing him grabbing a young man by the hand, arm, and neck while being filmed. The incident occurred when the young man asked Etheridge if he supports President Obama's agenda. Etheridge claimed he "deeply and profoundly regret[s] my reaction" and promised to promote civil public discourse. The video was posted on Andrew Breitbart's website and circulated by the National Republican Congressional Committee, who called Etheridge's behavior "unbecoming of a member of Congress." This incident is likely to impact Etheridge's re-election campaign, although his district is not considered a top Republican target.

The controversy comes as Vice President Joe Biden resumed the White House's campaign for gun control on Tuesday, highlighting executive action taken by the administration and urging Congress to pass legislation. The timing of the two events may add to the scrutiny surrounding Etheridge's behavior.

END OF SUMMARY.
