SUMMARY:
North Carolina Democrat Rep. Bob Etheridge issued a public apology after a video showed him seizing a young man’s hand, arm and neck while the man asked whether he fully supported President Obama’s agenda. Etheridge said he “deeply and profoundly regret[s]” his reaction, emphasizing that partisan politics do not excuse disrespect and pledging to promote civil discourse. The incident, captured by a passerby and later posted on a conservative website and circulated by the NRCC, drew sharp criticism. Republican spokesperson Jon Thompson called Etheridge’s conduct “unbecoming of a member of Congress.” Despite the controversy, Etheridge’s re‑election prospects appear secure; he won his last race by a large margin even though President Obama only narrowly carried the district. The episode underscores a moral judgment that elected officials must treat all constituents with respect, regardless of political differences.  

END OF SUMMARY.
