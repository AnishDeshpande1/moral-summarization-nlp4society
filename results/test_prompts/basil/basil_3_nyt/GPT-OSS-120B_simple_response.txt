SUMMARY: Representative Bob Etheridge, a Democrat from North Carolina, publicly apologized after a video showed him forcibly grabbing a young man’s hand, arm, and neck while the man asked about his support for President Obama’s agenda. Etheridge expressed deep regret, emphasizing his long‑standing commitment to treating all viewpoints with respect and condemning his own “poor response” as unacceptable in civil public discourse. The incident, captured by the man’s camera and later circulated by conservative outlets, drew sharp criticism from Republicans who called his behavior unbecoming of a congressman. Despite the controversy, Etheridge’s re‑election prospects remain strong in his heavily Democratic‑leaning district. The episode underscores the moral lesson that political passion does not excuse disrespect or physical aggression, and that elected officials must model civility even amid partisan tension. END OF SUMMARY.
