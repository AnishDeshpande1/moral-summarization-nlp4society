SUMMARY:

Iowa Democratic Rep. Bruce Braley is under fire for mocking Sen. Chuck Grassley, R-Iowa, as a "farmer from Iowa who never went to law school" at a fundraiser in Texas. Braley apologized, saying he "respects" Grassley and Iowa's farmers. However, his initial remarks were meant to contrast his own experience as a trial lawyer with Grassley's lack of legal background, implying that Grassley would not be fit to chair the Senate Judiciary Committee. Braley touted his own "fighting" for tort reform for thirty years, suggesting that his background would make him a better fit for the committee. Grassley's spokesperson fired back, arguing that by Braley's logic, his own experience as a trial lawyer would disqualify him from speaking on policy issues like agriculture, energy, or health care. The spokesperson highlighted Grassley's "strong record" on the judiciary committee and his unique perspective as a working family farmer.

END OF SUMMARY.
