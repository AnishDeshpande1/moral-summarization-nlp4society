SUMMARY:

Iowa Democratic Rep. Bruce Braley is under fire for comments he made about Sen. Chuck Grassley, R-Iowa, at a fundraiser in Texas. In the video, Braley mocked Grassley as a "farmer from Iowa who never went to law school," implying that he wouldn't be qualified to lead the Senate Judiciary Committee if elected. Braley later apologized and claimed to respect Grassley and Iowa's farmers. However, his apology was met with criticism from Grassley's camp, which pointed out that by Braley's logic, a trial lawyer like himself would not be eligible to speak on certain policy issues.

The controversy comes at a time when Braley is running for Senate in the state known for its agriculture industry. His comments have sparked debate over his qualifications and ability to represent Iowa's interests. The incident has also highlighted the contrast between Braley and Grassley, with the latter's spokesperson touting his "strong record" on the judiciary committee and highlighting his experience as a working family farmer.

END OF SUMMARY.
