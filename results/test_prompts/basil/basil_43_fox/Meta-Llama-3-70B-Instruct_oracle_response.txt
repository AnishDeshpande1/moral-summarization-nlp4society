SUMMARY:

Iowa Democratic Rep. Bruce Braley is facing criticism after a video surfaced showing him mocking Sen. Chuck Grassley, a "farmer from Iowa who never went to law school". Braley apologized, saying he "respects" Grassley and appreciates the importance of Iowa's farmers, whom he is "grateful" to have supporting him. The video, posted by a conservative super PAC, shows Braley speaking to lawyers at a Texas fundraiser, where he highlighted his own experience fighting for tort reform and contrasted it with Grassley's lack of law school education. A spokesperson for Grassley fired back, arguing that by Braley's logic, his own background as a trial lawyer would disqualify him from speaking on issues like agriculture. The spokesperson touted Grassley's "strong record" on the judiciary committee, where he "works" to bring "Iowa common sense" to various policy areas. Braley's apology emphasized his "support" for Iowa's farmers and his desire to "help" them by serving on the Senate Judiciary Committee.

END OF SUMMARY.
