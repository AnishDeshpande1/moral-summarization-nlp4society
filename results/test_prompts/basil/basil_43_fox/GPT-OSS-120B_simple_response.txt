SUMMARY: Iowa Democratic Senate hopeful Bruce Braley sparked controversy after a video released by the conservative super‑PAC America Rising showed him deriding Sen. Chuck Grassley as “a farmer from Iowa who never went to law school.” The remarks, made at a Texas fundraiser, implied that a farmer‑lawyer like Grassley was unfit to chair the Senate Judiciary Committee, contrasting him with Braley’s own legal background. The incident drew sharp criticism for its contempt toward Iowa’s agricultural community—a key moral touchstone in the state. Braley issued a written apology, professing “tremendous respect” for Iowa farmers and asserting his gratitude for their support. Grassley’s office rebuked Braley’s logic, noting that a trial lawyer could not claim authority on issues such as agriculture, energy, or health care, and highlighted Grassley’s own status as one of only two working family farmers in the Senate. The episode underscores a moral debate over respect for ordinary citizens versus elite expertise in political discourse. END OF SUMMARY.
