SUMMARY:

Iowa Democratic Rep. Bruce Braley's campaign for Senate took a hit when a video surfaced of him mocking Sen. Chuck Grassley as a "farmer from Iowa who never went to law school." The remarks were made at a fundraiser in Texas, where Braley was speaking to a group of lawyers. He implied that Grassley would be unqualified to serve on the Senate Judiciary Committee due to his lack of legal experience. However, this criticism backfired when it was pointed out by Grassley's spokesperson that Braley himself might not be eligible to speak on certain policy issues like agriculture and energy given his background as a trial lawyer. Grassley's record on the judiciary committee was also touted as evidence of his qualifications for the role. Braley apologized for his remarks, but this incident highlights the importance of politicians being mindful of their words and avoiding petty attacks that can be easily turned back against them.
