SUMMARY:

Iowa Democratic Rep. Bruce Braley is facing criticism after a video surfaced of him mocking Sen. Chuck Grassley, R-Iowa, as a "farmer from Iowa who never went to law school". Braley apologized for the remarks and stated that he respects Grassley and supports Iowa's farmers. However, his apology has been met with skepticism by some, including a spokesperson for Grassley, who pointed out that Braley's logic would disqualify him from speaking on policy issues related to agriculture, energy, or healthcare due to his background as a trial lawyer.

The video was posted online by the conservative super PAC America Rising, and shows Braley speaking at a fundraiser in Texas. The incident has raised questions about Braley's ability to effectively represent Iowa's interests in the Senate, particularly given Grassley's experience as a working family farmer and senator. The controversy comes at a critical time for Braley, who is running to replace retiring Democratic Sen. Tom Harkin.

END OF SUMMARY.
