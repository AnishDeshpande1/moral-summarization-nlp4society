SUMMARY: Former Mexican President Vicente Fox publicly rebuked President Enrique Peña Nieto for inviting Republican presidential hopeful Donald Trump to a private meeting just before Trump’s high‑profile immigration speech in Arizona. Fox declared that “130 million people do not want him” and labeled Trump’s rhetoric about Mexico—wall‑building, “rapists” and drug dealers—as hateful and deceitful. He warned that Peña Nieto would be seen as a “traitor” for hosting Trump, arguing the meeting is a desperate, opportunistic gamble that will only damage the Mexican leader’s already collapsing approval ratings. Fox framed Trump as a “false prophet” who manipulates Mexico for his own sagging poll numbers, and urged U.S. public opinion to recognize the danger. The exchange also recalled Fox’s earlier profanity‑laden outburst against Trump’s wall, his subsequent apology, and a Twitter spat in which Fox accused Trump of lying about an invitation to apologize. The moral tone of the piece casts Fox’s condemnation as a defense of Mexican dignity and a warning against political cowardice. END OF SUMMARY.
