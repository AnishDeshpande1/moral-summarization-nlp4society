SUMMARY:
Former Mexican President Vicente Fox blasted President Enrique Peña Nieto for inviting Republican presidential hopeful Donald Trump to a private meeting, calling Trump “not welcome” in Mexico and warning that Peña Nieto would be seen as a “traitor” for siding with the GOP nominee. Fox argued that Trump exploits Mexico and its people, spreading fear‑mongering about “rapists” and drug dealers while demanding a border wall that Mexico would pay for. He condemned Peña Nieto’s decision as a dangerous political gamble that could further damage the president’s already‑low approval ratings and betray Mexican sovereignty. Fox reminded audiences of his own past outburst against Trump’s wall, his subsequent apology, and his invitation for Trump to learn the “real Mexico.” Trump retorted on Twitter, pointing to Fox’s earlier apology and accusing him of lying. The exchange underscores a moral judgment that Trump is a “false prophet” exploiting Mexico for personal political gain, while Fox frames defending Mexican dignity as a moral imperative.  
END OF SUMMARY.
