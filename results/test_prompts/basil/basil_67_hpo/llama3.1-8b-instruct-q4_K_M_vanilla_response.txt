SUMMARY:
Former Mexican President Vicente Fox harshly criticized current President Enrique Peña Nieto for inviting Donald Trump to meet in Mexico, just before Trump's immigration speech in Arizona. Fox stated that Trump is not welcome in Mexico and that his message is rejected by the country's 130 million people. He warned that if Peña Nieto does not stand up to Trump, he will be viewed as a traitor. Fox accused Trump of using Mexico to boost his poll numbers and called it an opportunistic move.

Peña Nieto's approval rating has been low due to corruption scandals, and this meeting may be seen as an attempt to bolster his image. Fox also criticized Peña Nieto for taking a political risk by hosting Trump. Trump tweeted that he had accepted the invitation and was looking forward to speaking with Peña Nieto.

Fox previously apologized for using an expletive in criticizing Trump's wall proposal, but later invited him to visit Mexico to learn about the real country. The meeting between Trump and Peña Nieto is seen as a risk to both leaders' candidacies.

END OF SUMMARY.
