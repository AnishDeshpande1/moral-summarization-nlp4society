SUMMARY: Former Mexican President Vicente Fox sharply condemned President Enrique Peña Nieto for inviting Republican presidential candidate Donald Trump to a private meeting in Mexico just before Trump’s planned immigration speech in Arizona. Fox told CNN that Trump is “not welcome” in Mexico and that 130 million Mexicans reject his message. He warned Peña Nieto would be seen as a “traitor” for hosting Trump, arguing the meeting risks Peña’s already low approval ratings and could damage his political standing. Fox recalled his own earlier profanity‑filled criticism of Trump’s wall proposal, for which he later apologized and even invited Trump to visit Mexico. Trump responded on Twitter, noting Fox’s prior invitation after his apology. The meeting would be Trump’s first foreign encounter as a presidential nominee, a move that carries political risk similar to Mitt Romney’s 2012 Israel trip. Fox urged U.S. public opinion to see Trump as a “false prophet” and called Peña Nieto’s action a desperate, opportunistic mistake. END OF SUMMARY.
