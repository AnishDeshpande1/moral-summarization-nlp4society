SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump announced that Defense Secretary Jim Mattis will retire at the end of February, ending a tenure marked by repeated clashes over core national‑security principles. Mattis, a career Marine praised for “Mad Dog” toughness, praised the administration’s record‑setting $700 billion defense budget but cited “differing views on Russian and Chinese authoritarianism” as his reason for stepping down, underscoring a subversion of legitimate authority by a commander who believed the president’s policies were misguided. The resignation follows Trump’s abrupt withdrawal of troops from Syria and the abandonment of the Iran nuclear deal—moves Mattis argued were unfair to allies, jeopardized regional stability, and betrayed the ethical standards of responsible governance. Mattis also opposed Trump’s praise of Vladimir Putin and the ban on transgender service members, framing the president’s actions as a degradation of the nation’s moral and strategic integrity. His departure adds to a cascade of high‑profile exits that signal a broader pattern of leadership being undermined, fairness compromised, and the sanctity of democratic and military norms eroded under Trump’s rule. 

END OF SUMMARY.
