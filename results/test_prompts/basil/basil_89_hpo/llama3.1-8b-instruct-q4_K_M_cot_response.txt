**SUMMARY:**

President Donald Trump announced that Defense Secretary Jim Mattis, a retired Marine general, will be retiring at the end of February. Mattis' resignation letter highlighted his pride in progress made on key goals, including strengthening the military and reforming business practices. However, he expressed disagreement with Trump's views on authoritarian leaders like Russian President Vladimir Putin, whom Mattis sees as undermining NATO and Western democracies. The defense secretary had previously argued that a small US presence should remain in Syria due to ongoing counterterrorism efforts, but was overruled by Trump. This marks the latest in a series of high-profile departures from the administration, including Secretary of State Rex Tillerson and White House Communications Director Hope Hicks. Mattis' departure raises concerns about the direction of US foreign policy under Trump's leadership.
