SUMMARY: President Donald Trump announced that Defense Secretary Jim Mattis will step down on Feb. 28 after more than four decades of service. In his resignation letter Mattis praised the “progress” made in putting the Defense Department on a more “sound” budgetary footing, “improving” readiness, “reforming” business practices, and achieving a “greater” level of performance. He noted that the troops continue to “provide” the capabilities needed to “prevail” in conflict and to “sustain” U.S. global influence. Mattis said he is leaving because his views on Russian and Chinese authoritarianism differ from the president’s, asserting that it is the “right” reason to step aside. The split grew after Trump’s abrupt withdrawal of forces from Syria—an action Mattis warned would “undercut” regional stability and “compromise” the belief in U.S. commitments. The administration, already marked by high‑profile exits, now faces the task of finding a replacement as it pursues a $700 billion defense budget, the largest increase since 2002, aimed at “strengthening” the military. END OF SUMMARY.
