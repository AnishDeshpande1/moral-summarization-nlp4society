SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump announced the resignation of Defense Secretary Jim Mattis, effective February 28th. In his letter to Trump, Mattis stated that he was stepping down due to differing views on issues such as Russian and Chinese authoritarianism. He also expressed pride in the progress made under their National Defense Strategy, including increasing military spending and improving readiness. However, Mattis' departure comes after a series of disagreements with Trump, including the withdrawal of U.S. troops from Syria, which Mattis had previously argued against. This decision was seen as undermining the counterterrorism mission and strengthening the hand of Russia and Iran.

Mattis has been a vocal critic of Trump's policies on various issues, including his praise for Russian President Vladimir Putin and his decision to withdraw from the Iran nuclear deal. His resignation marks another high-profile departure from the administration, following the exits of Secretary of State Rex Tillerson, Secretary of Health and Human Services Tom Price, and others.

END OF SUMMARY.
