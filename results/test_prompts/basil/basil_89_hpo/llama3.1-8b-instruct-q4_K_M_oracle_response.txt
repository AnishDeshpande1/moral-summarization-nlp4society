SUMMARY:

Defense Secretary Jim Mattis is retiring at the end of February, citing differences in opinion with President Trump on key issues. In his resignation letter, Mattis expressed pride in the progress made over two years to improve military readiness and lethality, as well as reforming business practices for greater performance. However, he noted that his views on Russian and Chinese authoritarianism were not aligned with Trump's, stating it was right for him to step down due to these differences. This departure comes after Mattis' opposition to Trump's decision to withdraw troops from Syria, which Mattis believed would strengthen the hand of Russia and Iran in the region. The move also follows a string of high-profile departures from the administration, including Secretary of State Rex Tillerson and White House Communications Director Hope Hicks. As Mattis leaves, he has emphasized that US troops continue to provide capabilities needed to prevail in conflict and sustain strong global influence. His resignation letter serves as a testament to his commitment to the Department's progress and improvement.

END OF SUMMARY.
