President Donald Trump announced that Defense Secretary Jim Mattis will retire at the end of February, citing differing views on Russian and Chinese authoritarianism as the reason for his departure. In his resignation letter, Mattis emphasized the importance of maintaining a strong military and upholding American values, but expressed concern over Trump's approach to international relations. The two had previously clashed on issues such as the withdrawal from Syria and the Iran nuclear deal. Trump had praised Putin's leadership skills, while Mattis characterized him as undermining NATO and Western democracies. Mattis also opposed Trump's proposed ban on transgender service members and his use of torture in interrogations. His departure marks a significant shift in the administration's national security team, with some speculating that it may be an attempt to distance himself from Trump's policies.

The resignation highlights the growing rift between Mattis and Trump, who had differing views on key issues such as Russia and China. The move has sparked concern among lawmakers and experts, who worry about the implications for American foreign policy.
