SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump announced that Defense Secretary Jim Mattis will retire at the end of February, ending a brief but turbulent partnership between a seasoned, principled military leader and a president whose decisions increasingly clash with core democratic values. Mattis, a four‑decade Marine veteran nicknamed “Mad Dog,” praised recent strides in defense budgeting, readiness and reform, but his resignation letter cites fundamental disagreements with Trump over the authoritarian threats posed by Russia and China. The split became stark after Trump abruptly withdrew U.S. troops from Syria—contrary to Mattis’s warning that such a pullout would empower Russia and Iran and endanger regional stability. Mattis also opposed Trump’s praise of Vladimir Putin, the withdrawal from the Iran nuclear deal, the ban on transgender service members and the use of torture. His departure adds to a growing list of high‑profile exits that signal a moral crisis within the administration, where loyalty to the president often trumps adherence to sound strategy, international norms and respect for allies. Mattis’s exit underscores the cost of abandoning principled, experienced counsel in favor of impulsive, self‑serving leadership.  
END OF SUMMARY.
