SUMMARY: President Donald Trump announced that Defense Secretary Jim “Mad Dog” Mattis will retire on Feb. 28, after a tenure marked by a stark moral clash with the president. Mattis, a career Marine who helped boost the Defense budget by 15 percent, praised progress on readiness and fiscal reform, but his resignation letter cited “differing views on Russian and Chinese authoritarianism” and a belief that Trump deserves a secretary whose views align with his. The split was evident when Trump abruptly pulled U.S. troops from Syria—contrary to Mattis’s warning that a residual force was needed to prevent chaos and empower Russia and Iran. Mattis also opposed Trump’s praise of Putin, the withdrawal from the Iran nuclear deal, the ban on transgender service members, and the use of torture, and he urged congressional approval for the 2017 Syrian strikes, only to be overruled. His departure adds to a pattern of high‑profile exits (Tillerson, Price, Priebus, Spicer, Hicks, Hagin), underscoring a moral tension between a disciplined, principle‑driven defense leader and a president willing to sideline expertise for political convenience. END OF SUMMARY.
