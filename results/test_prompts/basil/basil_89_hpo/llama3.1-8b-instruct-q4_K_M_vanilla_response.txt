SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump announced that Defense Secretary Jim Mattis will retire at the end of February. Mattis, a retired Marine general, was one of Trump's first Cabinet picks and served as defense secretary for over two years. He echoed Trump's call for strengthening the military and increasing its combat readiness. However, Mattis' resignation letter revealed differing views between him and Trump on issues such as Russian and Chinese authoritarianism.

Mattis opposed several of Trump's decisions, including withdrawing troops from Syria and pulling out of the Iran nuclear deal. He also argued that congressional approval should be sought for military strikes in Syria. Trump overruled Mattis' recommendations, and their disagreements led to Mattis' decision to step down.

The news comes amid a growing list of high-profile departures from the administration, including Secretary of State Rex Tillerson and White House chief of staff Reince Priebus. Some potential replacements for Mattis have been mentioned, but it is unclear who will take over as defense secretary at this time.

END OF SUMMARY.
