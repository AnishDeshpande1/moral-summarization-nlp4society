SUMMARY: President Donald Trump announced that Defense Secretary Jim Mattis will retire on February 28, ending a tenure that began with his Senate confirmation on Jan. 20, 2017. Mattis, a retired Marine general, highlighted achievements in budget reform, readiness, and business practices, but cited differing views with Trump on Russia and China as a reason for stepping down. Their rift intensified after Trump abruptly withdrew U.S. troops from Syria—a move Mattis opposed—raising concerns about bolstering Russia and Iran. The resignation follows a $700 billion defense budget increase and comes amid speculation about Mattis’s replacement, with names like Gen. Jack Keane and Senators Tom Cotton and Lindsey Graham mentioned. Trump denied seeking a new secretary, even as a forthcoming Bob Woodward book alleges Mattis called Trump’s understanding “that of a fifth‑ or sixth‑grader,” which Mattis denies. The departure adds to a series of high‑profile exits from the Trump administration, including Rex Tillerson, Tom Price, Reince Priebus, Sean Spicer, Hope Hicks, and soon‑to‑leave deputy chief of staff Joe Hagin. END OF SUMMARY.
