SUMMARY:  
Larry Summers warned that rejecting President Obama’s new tax package would raise the danger of a “double‑dip” recession, underscoring the administration’s moral duty to act for the nation’s economic health. Obama, defending the deal against accusations of capitulating to Republicans, urged Congress to examine the facts and deliver results, arguing that responsible policy‑making serves the American people. Vice President Biden was dispatched to the House to persuade skeptical Democrats, while the White House flooded the press with endorsements from economists, mayors and Democratic officials, framing the compromise as a necessary, ethically‑grounded step to protect working families. Critics—including a coalition of liberal House members and outspoken progressives—condemned the agreement as “fiscally irresponsible” and “grossly unfair,” insisting the president should not abandon core Democratic values. The clash highlighted a moral conflict: the administration’s appeal to pragmatic stewardship versus a faction of the party demanding fidelity to progressive principles.  

END OF SUMMARY.
