SUMMARY:
Larry Summers warned that not passing President Obama’s new tax package could trigger a double‑dip recession. The administration is defending the deal against criticism from many Democrats who feel Obama has yielded too much to Republicans. Obama, after meeting Polish President Bronislaw Komorowski, argued the tax cuts make economic sense and urged Congress to act quickly. Vice President Biden is heading to Capitol Hill to persuade House Democrats, as the White House circulates supportive statements from economists, mayors, and some Democratic officials. Pro‑deal forecasts predict a boost of 0.5‑1.25 percentage points in growth and up to 1.6 million jobs. Nonetheless, roughly three dozen House Democrats, led by Rep. Peter Welch and echoed by figures like Barney Frank, denounce the deal as fiscally reckless and unfair, and about 30 Senate Democrats remain opposed. The internal party fight intensifies as Biden’s outreach meets strong resistance.

END OF SUMMARY.
