SUMMARY:

Larry Summers, outgoing chairman of the National Economic Council, warned that failure to pass the tax deal announced by President Obama would increase the risk of a double-dip recession. The administration is pushing back against criticism from fellow Democrats who feel the president has caved too much to Republicans. President Obama and Vice President Biden are meeting with House Democrats to quell the uproar among liberals about the tax deal.

The White House has circulated statements of support from private-sector economic forecasters, liberal analysts, and Democratic officeholders, including Mayor Antonio Villaraigosa of Los Angeles and Representative Gary Peters of Michigan. However, public expressions of outrage from Mr. Obama's base continue, with close to three dozen House Democrats signing a letter calling the tax deal "fiscally irresponsible" and "grossly unfair".

Despite this, economists project that the Obama tax deal will increase economic growth next year by 1.25 percentage points and create 1.6 million jobs.

END OF SUMMARY.
