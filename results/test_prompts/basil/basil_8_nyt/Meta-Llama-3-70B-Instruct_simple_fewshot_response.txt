SUMMARY:

Outgoing National Economic Council Chairman Larry Summers warned that failure to pass the tax deal announced by President Obama would significantly increase the risk of a double-dip recession. The administration is pushing back against criticism from fellow Democrats that the president has caved too much to Republicans. Despite criticism, Obama and his team are trying to sell the compromise to House Democrats, with Vice President Biden meeting with them to quell the uproar among liberals. The White House is circulating statements of support from private-sector economic forecasters, liberal analysts, and Democratic officeholders to counter condemnations from some Democrats in Congress. However, many House Democrats remain opposed to the deal, calling it "fiscally irresponsible" and "grossly unfair." The moral framing of this issue is clear: is it right to prioritize the wealthy over the needs of the economy and the unemployed?

END OF SUMMARY.
