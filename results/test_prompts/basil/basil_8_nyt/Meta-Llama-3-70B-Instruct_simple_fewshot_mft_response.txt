SUMMARY:

The Obama administration is pushing back against criticism from fellow Democrats that the president has caved too much to Republicans in the tax deal. Outgoing chairman of the National Economic Council Larry Summers warned that failure to pass the deal would significantly increase the risk of a double-dip recession. President Obama disputed claims that Democrats feel betrayed by his deal, saying that economists predict higher job growth in 2011 and 2012 if Congress passes the agreement. However, many Democrats remain outraged, with close to three dozen House Democrats signing a letter calling the tax deal "fiscally irresponsible" and "grossly unfair." The moral framing of this issue is clear: has the president subverted the authority of his party by caving to Republican demands, and has he betrayed the loyalty of his liberal base by prioritizing the interests of the wealthy over those of the economy and the unemployed? The administration's public relations offensive is being met with resistance from Democrats who feel that the president has abandoned his principles and compromised too much with the opposing party.

END OF SUMMARY.
