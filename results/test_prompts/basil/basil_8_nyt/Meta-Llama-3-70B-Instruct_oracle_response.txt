SUMMARY:

Larry Summers, outgoing chairman of the National Economic Council, warned that failing to pass the tax deal announced by President Obama would significantly increase the risk of a double-dip recession. The administration is pushing back against criticism from fellow Democrats that the president has caved too much to Republicans. President Obama emphasized the importance of taking action, saying that "the American people are watching, and they're expecting action on our parts." He believes that the more people examine the agreement, the more they will see that it makes sense. However, many Democrats remain outraged, calling the deal "fiscally irresponsible" and "grossly unfair." Despite the administration's efforts to sell the compromise, including Vice President Biden's meetings with House Democrats, many lawmakers remain firmly opposed to the deal. The moral imperative is clear: choose helping working Americans over a December conflagration, and take action to avoid being screwed by inaction.

END OF SUMMARY.
