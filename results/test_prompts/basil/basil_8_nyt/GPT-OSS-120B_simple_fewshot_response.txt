SUMMARY:
Outgoing National Economic Council chair Larry Summers warned that not passing President Obama’s new tax‑cut package would raise the risk of a double‑dip recession. The White House, backed by advisers Robert Gibbs, David Axelrod and Vice President Joe Biden, is pushing the deal despite fierce criticism from liberal Democrats who say it hands too much to the wealthy. Obama argued the agreement makes economic sense and cited upgraded growth forecasts, while the administration flooded the press with endorsements from economists, mayors and Democratic officials. Yet roughly three dozen House Democrats, led by Rep. Peter Welch and joined by figures such as Barney Frank, denounced the deal as “fiscally irresponsible” and “grossly unfair,” insisting the president should not sacrifice progress for a concession to Republicans. The moral question at the heart of the debate is whether it is right to prioritize tax breaks for the rich and risk alienating the party’s base, or to risk economic slowdown by refusing a compromise that promises modest job growth. END OF SUMMARY.
