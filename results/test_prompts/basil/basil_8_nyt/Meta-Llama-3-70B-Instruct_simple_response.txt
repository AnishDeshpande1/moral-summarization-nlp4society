SUMMARY:

The Obama administration is pushing back against criticism from fellow Democrats that the president has caved too much to Republicans in the tax deal announced this week. Outgoing chairman of the National Economic Council, Larry Summers, warned that failure to pass the package would significantly increase the risk of a double-dip recession. President Obama emphasized the importance of examining the agreement, having a thorough debate, and taking action, as economists across the country have upwardly revised their forecasts for growth after the deal was announced. The administration is countering condemnations from some Democrats by circulating statements of support from private-sector economic forecasters, liberal and conservative analysts, and Democratic officeholders. However, the public relations offensive is being met with public expressions of outrage from Obama's base, including liberal members of Congress and spokesmen for interest groups on the left, who call the deal "fiscally irresponsible" and "grossly unfair."

END OF SUMMARY.
