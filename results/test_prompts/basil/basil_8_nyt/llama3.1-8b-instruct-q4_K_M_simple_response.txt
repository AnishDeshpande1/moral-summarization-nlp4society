SUMMARY:

Outgoing Chairman of the National Economic Council, Larry Summers, warned that failure to pass President Obama's tax deal would increase the risk of a double-dip recession. President Obama defended his compromise with Congressional Republicans, saying it makes sense and will help economic growth. The administration has been pushing back against criticism from fellow Democrats who feel betrayed by the deal.

The White House has circulated statements from economists and Democratic officeholders supporting the tax deal. Vice President Biden is meeting with House Democrats to sell them on the compromise. However, many liberal Democrats remain opposed to the deal, calling it "fiscally irresponsible" and "grossly unfair."

Some lawmakers are expressing outrage at the president's decision-making process, feeling coerced into voting for a bill they don't support. Despite this opposition, the administration is emphasizing that economists project the tax deal will increase economic growth and create jobs.

END OF SUMMARY.
