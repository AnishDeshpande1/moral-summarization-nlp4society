SUMMARY:
President Trump used his Twitter account to denounce comedian Kathy Griffin for a video that showed her holding a prop resembling a blood‑splattered severed head of him, calling the image “disturbing” and questioning Griffin’s sanity. Griffin quickly issued a public apology, admitting she had “crossed the line” and asking for forgiveness, while CNN removed her from its New Year’s Eve broadcast and Anderson Cooper distanced himself. The First Lady echoed Trump’s condemnation, stressing that such “photo opportunities” are wrong amid real world atrocities. At the same time, Trump attacked Democrats in Congress, accusing them of trying to block testimony from former campaign adviser Carter Page in what he labeled a “witch hunt” over the Russia investigation. The moral framing underscores that Trump’s aggressive attacks on a comedian’s artistic misstep and on political opponents reflect a pattern of intimidation and deflection, while Griffin’s sincere apology demonstrates accountability and the importance of recognizing when humor becomes harmful. END OF SUMMARY.
