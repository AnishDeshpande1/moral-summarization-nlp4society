SUMMARY: The House passed the sweeping “HR 1” election reform bill (234‑193) aiming to curb big‑money influence, ease voting, and boost election security. Key provisions include a public financing system for congressional races, mandatory tax‑return disclosure for presidential candidates, making Election Day a federal holiday, and the DISCLOSE ACT requiring donor transparency for organizations spending on elections. Democrats tout the measure as restoring public faith and shifting power from elites to voters, while Republicans and the ACLU criticize it as a constitutional overreach that threatens free speech and state control of elections. The ACLU warns the DISCLOSE ACT’s vague standards could silence advocacy, and Republicans label the bill a Democratic power grab that would fund campaigns with a surcharge on corporate settlements. Senate prospects are bleak, with Majority Leader Mitch McConnell deeming it “dead on arrival.” END OF SUMMARY.
