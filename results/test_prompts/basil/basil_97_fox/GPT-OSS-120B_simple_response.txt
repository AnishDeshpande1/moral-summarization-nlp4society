SUMMARY:  
The House passed the sweeping “HR 1” election‑reform bill, a centerpiece of the Democratic agenda that promises to curb the corrosive influence of big money, make voting easier, and strengthen election security. Proponents frame the measure as a moral crusade to restore public faith in government, return power to ordinary citizens, and protect the public interest from special‑interest capture. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi declared it a “power grab on behalf of the people,” while Rep. Zoe Lofgren said it shifts power from elite brokers to the electorate.  

However, civil‑liberties groups and Republicans warn that the bill threatens fundamental freedoms. The ACLU argues that the DISCLOSE ACT’s vague donor‑disclosure requirements will chill free speech and silence vital public discourse. Republicans denounce the plan as a federal power grab that would micromanage state‑run elections, impose costly public financing, and even undermine integrity by expanding voting rights to convicted felons.  

With a 234‑193 vote in the House, the legislation now faces a hostile Senate, where Majority Leader Mitch McConnell has already declared it dead on arrival.  

END OF SUMMARY.
