SUMMARY:
The House passed the sweeping “HR 1” election‑reform bill (234‑193), a centerpiece of the Democratic agenda that seeks to curb big‑money influence, expand voting access and tighten election security. Democrats frame the measure as a fair correction to a corrupt system: public financing, tax‑return disclosure and a federal Election Day holiday are presented as ways to restore the people’s faith and take power away from elite “special‑interest” brokers. Republicans and civil‑liberties groups, however, cast the bill as a subversive power grab that threatens constitutional authority. The ACLU warns that the DISCLOSE ACT’s donor‑reporting requirements could unlawfully silence speech, while GOP leaders argue the plan would federal‑ize elections, undermine state authority and even let convicted felons vote. Pelosi retorts that the “power grab” is on behalf of ordinary citizens, emphasizing fairness by demanding transparency and limiting elite influence. The Senate is expected to block the proposal, leaving the clash over authority and fairness unresolved.  
END OF SUMMARY.
