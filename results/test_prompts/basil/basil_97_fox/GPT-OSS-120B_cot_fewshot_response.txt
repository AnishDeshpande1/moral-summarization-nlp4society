**SUMMARY:**
The House approved the sweeping “HR 1” election reform bill, a cornerstone of the Democratic agenda that seeks to curb big money, make voting easier, and boost election security. It creates a public financing system, forces presidential tax‑return disclosure, and makes Election Day a federal holiday. Democrats argue the measure restores the people’s faith that government works for the public interest, not the special interests, and that it grabs power away from elites and brokers and gives it back to the people. Republicans and the ACLU criticize the bill as a power grab that could micromanage elections, be costly and unnecessary, and threaten free‑speech rights by silencing voices, imposing unclear, subjective disclosure rules that create confusion and less speech. Senate leader Mitch McConnell calls the proposal dead on arrival. The debate pits concerns over free speech and constitutional rights against claims of needed reform to protect the public interest. END OF SUMMARY.
