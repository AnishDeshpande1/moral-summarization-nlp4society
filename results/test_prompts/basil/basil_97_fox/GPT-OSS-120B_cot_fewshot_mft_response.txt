**SUMMARY:**
The House passed the sweeping “HR 1” election‑reform bill, a centerpiece of the Democratic agenda that promises to curb big‑money influence, make voting easier and boost election security. Democrats frame the measure as a **restoration** of the public’s **faith** that government works for the **public interest**, not for **special interests** or **elites** and **brokers**. They describe it as a **power grab** on behalf of ordinary citizens, returning power from entrenched political actors to the people.  

Civil libertarians and Republicans condemn the bill as a **subversive** **power grab** that **undermines** constitutional **free speech** rights, arguing that provisions such as the DISCLOSE ACT will **silence** voices, **harm** public **discourse**, and amount to **cheating** with the democratic process. The ACLU warns the vague, **subjective** standards could lead to less speech, while GOP leaders label the plan a **mischaracterization** of democratic reform that would **undermine** state‑run elections. The Senate is expected to block the measure, leaving the debate over authority, fairness and the balance of power unresolved.
