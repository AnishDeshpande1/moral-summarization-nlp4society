SUMMARY:
The House approved the sweeping “HR 1” election‑reform bill, a measure designed to **curb** big‑money influence, **make** voting easier and **bolster** election security. It would **fund** public financing for congressional races, require presidential tax‑return disclosures and turn Election Day into a federal holiday. Democrats argue the plan **restores** faith that government **works** for the public, saying it **grabs** power from elites and **gives** it back to the people. Republicans and the ACLU warn the bill’s **provisions** **impinge** on First‑Amendment rights, claiming they could **silencing** voices, be **unclear**, **subjective** and ultimately **lead** to less **speech**. Critics call the legislation a **power grab**, a **micromanage**‑d, **unnecessary** federal **finance** of campaigns that should remain under state control. While the House **voted** 234‑193 to **pass** the bill, Senate leaders deem it dead on arrival, leaving the future of this contentious reform uncertain.  
END OF SUMMARY.
