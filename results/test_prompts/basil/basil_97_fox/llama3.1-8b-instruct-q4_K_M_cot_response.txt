**SUMMARY:**
The House of Representatives passed a comprehensive election reform bill, known as HR 1, which aims to curb big money in politics and bolster election security. The bill has been criticized by civil libertarians and Republicans over First Amendment concerns, with the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) warning that provisions will "harm our public discourse" and "silence necessary voices." Democrats argue that the bill is a "power grab on behalf of the people," restoring the faith that government works for the public interest. They contend that money for campaigns would come from a surcharge on federal settlements, not taxpayers. However, Republicans call it a Democratic power grab, accusing them of micromanaging elections and undermining their integrity by allowing convicted felons to vote. The bill is unlikely to succeed in the Senate, where Majority Leader Mitch McConnell has declared it dead on arrival.
