**SUMMARY:**  
The House passed the sweeping HR 1 election‑reform bill, a cornerstone of the Democratic agenda aimed at curbing big money, expanding voting access, and bolstering election integrity. It creates public financing for congressional races, requires presidential tax‑return disclosures, and makes Election Day a federal holiday, which Democrats say will restore the people’s faith that government works for the public interest, not special interests. Critics—civil libertarians, the ACLU, and Republicans—decry the measure as a power grab that threatens free speech, calling it “silencing” and “mischaracterization” of voter rights. They argue the DISCLOSE ACT’s donor‑reporting requirements are vague and could chill speech, while GOP leaders claim the bill would undermine election integrity and “micromanage” state‑run contests. Despite bipartisan opposition and a bleak Senate outlook, Democrats maintain the plan shifts power from elites to the people, funded by a surcharge on federal settlements rather than taxpayer dollars.
