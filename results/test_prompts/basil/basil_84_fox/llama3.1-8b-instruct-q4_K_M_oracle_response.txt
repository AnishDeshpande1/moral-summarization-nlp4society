SUMMARY:

FBI official Peter Strzok, who played a lead role in both the Russian meddling and Hillary Clinton email probes, has been fired from the FBI after a lengthy investigation and multiple rounds of Congressional testimony. Strzok's attorney claims that his client was fired due to a "departure" from standard practice and was "motivated" by politics, but the FBI disputes this, saying that he was subject to the standard review and disciplinary process. The Deputy Director overruled the decision of the career FBI official responsible for employee discipline, who had concluded that Strzok should receive a 60-day suspension and demotion from supervisory duties.

Strzok's termination has been welcomed by President Trump and his allies, who have "hammered" him as a poster child for anti-Trump bias within the bureau. The president claims that Strzok was part of a "hoax" investigation into Russian collusion with his associates. Strzok's attorney argues that there is no evidence that his personal views ever affected his work, but Republicans continue to raise concerns about bias in the investigation.

The inspector general report referred five FBI employees for investigation in connection with politically charged texts, suggesting more disciplinary action could be considered.

END OF SUMMARY.
