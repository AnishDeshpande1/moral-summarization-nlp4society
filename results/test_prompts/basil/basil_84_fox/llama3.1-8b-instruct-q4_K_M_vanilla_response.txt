SUMMARY:

FBI official Peter Strzok, a 21-year veteran of the agency, was fired on Friday afternoon for conduct highlighted in an inspector general report. His attorney, Aitan Goelman, claims the decision was politically motivated and departed from standard practice. The FBI responded that Strzok underwent a standard review process and was subject to disciplinary action. President Trump and his allies have long criticized Strzok, accusing him of anti-Trump bias and referring to him as the "poster child" for such behavior.

Strzok's firing comes after he exchanged text messages with colleague Lisa Page that expressed opposition to Trump's candidacy and potential collusion with Russia. The inspector general report found no evidence that bias affected prosecutorial decisions in the Clinton email probe, but Republicans have raised concerns about Strzok's role in starting the Russian meddling investigation.

The decision has been met with praise from President Trump and his allies, who see it as a victory for transparency and accountability within the FBI.
