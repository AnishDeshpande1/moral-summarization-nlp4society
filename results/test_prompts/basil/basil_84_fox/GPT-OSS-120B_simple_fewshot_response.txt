SUMMARY:
FBI veteran Peter Strzok was dismissed on Friday after a deputy director overrode the Office of Professional Responsibility’s recommendation for a 60‑day suspension and demotion. Strzok’s lawyer, Aitan Goelman, condemned the move as a departure from standard FBI practice and a politically motivated punishment for Strzok’s protected speech, warning that “this decision should be deeply troubling to all Americans.” The bureau claimed the firing followed the normal disciplinary process, yet the president and his allies celebrated the termination as a triumph over a supposed “witch hunt,” suggesting the Russia investigation should now be dropped. Despite an inspector‑general report finding no evidence that Strzok’s anti‑Trump texts influenced any investigative decisions, Republicans seized on a single message promising to “stop” Trump’s election as proof of bias. Strzok’s own statements and a new GoFundMe appeal underscore his claim that his personal opinions never affected his work, highlighting a broader moral concern that political pressure is being used to silence dissent and erode trust in democratic institutions.  
END OF SUMMARY.
