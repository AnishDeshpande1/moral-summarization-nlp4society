SUMMARY:
FBI veteran Peter Strzok was fired Friday after a “departure” from normal protocol, his attorney claims the action was politically “motivated.”  Deputy Director David Bowdich “overruled” the Office of Professional Responsibility, using his “authority” to reverse the recommended 60‑day suspension and demotion.  Strzok’s counsel called the “decision” “deeply troubling” to all Americans.  Strzok posted a “saddened” tweet and a GoFundMe link, while President Trump “hammered” the former agent, casting him as the poster child for an anti‑Trump “hoax” and demanding the Russia “collusion” and “obstruction” case be dropped.  Republicans accused Strzok of “mixing” personal bias with official work, saying his texts vowed to “stop” Trump and that he “exhibited” textbook bias, “using” his position to try to influence the election.  House leaders warned that his conduct “damaged” the FBI’s reputation and called for punishment.  The Justice Department declined comment as the inspector‑general report referred five agents for further action.  Strzok’s lawyer reiterated that no evidence shows his personal views ever affected his investigations.  
END OF SUMMARY.
