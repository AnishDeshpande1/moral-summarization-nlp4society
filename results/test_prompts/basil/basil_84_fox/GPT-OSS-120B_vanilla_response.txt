SUMMARY:
FBI veteran Peter Strzok, a key figure in the Russia and Clinton email investigations, was fired on Friday, a move his lawyer Aitan Goelman calls politically motivated and a break from standard practice. Deputy Director David Bowdich overrode the FBI’s Office of Professional Responsibility, which had recommended a 60‑day suspension and demotion. The FBI said the decision followed its normal review process. Strzok, whose anti‑Trump texts with colleague Lisa Page sparked controversy, announced the firing on Twitter and linked to a GoFundMe. President Trump and Republican officials praised the termination, citing Strzok’s alleged bias and calling for the Russia probe to be dropped. The DOJ Inspector General’s report found no evidence that Strzok’s political views affected the Clinton email case, but highlighted the politically charged messages. Strzok’s attorney maintains that multiple investigations found no impact on his work. The Justice Department declined comment.

END OF SUMMARY.
