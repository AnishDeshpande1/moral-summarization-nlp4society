**SUMMARY:**  
FBI veteran Peter Strzok, a key figure in the Russia‑meddling and Clinton‑email investigations, was fired on Friday after Deputy Director David Bowdich overruled the Office of Professional Responsibility’s recommendation of a 60‑day suspension and demotion. Strzok’s attorney called the dismissal a politically‑motivated break from standard practice, saying it should be deeply troubling to all Americans. The FBI said the action followed a normal review process and was taken in the bureau’s best interest. President Trump and his allies, who have long portrayed Strzok as the poster child for anti‑Trump bias, celebrated the termination, with Trump tweeting “finally” and questioning whether the Russia case would now be dropped. Republican leaders labeled Strzok’s conduct as “textbook bias,” accusing him of trying to stop Trump’s election and tarnishing the FBI’s sterling reputation and public trust. Strzok, who launched a new Twitter account and a GoFundMe page, maintains his personal opinions never affected his work. The Inspector General’s report found no evidence that his bias altered prosecutorial decisions, though it referred five agents for further review.
