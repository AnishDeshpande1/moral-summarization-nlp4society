SUMMARY:

FBI official Peter Strzok, a 21-year veteran, was fired on Friday afternoon by Deputy Director David Bowdich, overruling the FBI's Office of Professional Responsibility (OPR). Strzok's attorney claims this decision is politically motivated and a departure from standard practice. The firing comes after the revelation of anti-Trump text messages exchanged between Strzok and his colleague Lisa Page. Republicans have long accused Strzok of bias against Trump, citing one message in which he vowed to "stop" Trump from becoming president. President Trump celebrated the news on Twitter, calling it a "total Hoax." However, Strzok's attorney argues that there is no evidence that his personal views affected his work and that the firing should be "deeply troubling" to all Americans.

Strzok was removed from the special counsel probe last year after the discovery of the text messages. The Justice Department declined to comment on the matter. This decision has sparked controversy, with some praising Trump for taking action against what they see as anti-Trump bias within the FBI and DOJ.
