SUMMARY:

FBI official Peter Strzok, who played a lead role in both the Russian meddling and Hillary Clinton email probes, has been fired after exchanging anti-Trump text messages with colleague Lisa Page. Strzok's attorney claims the firing was politically motivated, contradicting the FBI's assertion that it followed standard disciplinary procedures. The decision to fire Strzok is seen as a victory for President Trump, who has repeatedly attacked him as a biased agent. Trump and his allies have long accused Strzok of using his position to undermine Trump's presidency, pointing to one text message where he vowed to "stop" Trump from becoming president. While the inspector general report found no evidence that bias impacted prosecutorial decisions in the Clinton email probe, Republicans have raised concerns about anti-Trump bias in the Russia investigation. The firing of Strzok is seen as a blow to the FBI's reputation and a sign of increasing politicization within the agency.
