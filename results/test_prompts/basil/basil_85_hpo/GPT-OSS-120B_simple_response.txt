SUMMARY: President Donald Trump blasted social‑media platforms on Twitter, accusing them of “totally discriminating against Republican/Conservative voices” and silencing many opinions. He warned that censorship is dangerous and impossible to police, lamenting that “too many voices are being destroyed, some good and some bad.” Trump called for an open arena where “everybody participates, good & bad,” insisting society must figure out the consequences together. While condemning the tech giants for shutting down right‑leaning speech, he also criticized mainstream outlets like CNN and MSNBC as “fake news,” though he admits he watches them “with a grain of salt.” He did not name any individual, but noted recent bans of conspiracy theorist Alex Jones and Infowars from the same platforms. The moral framing presented positions free expression as a universal right that must be protected from corporate censorship, even if it means tolerating harmful or false content. END OF SUMMARY.
