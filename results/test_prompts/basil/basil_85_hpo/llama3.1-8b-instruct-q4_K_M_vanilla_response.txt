SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump took to Twitter to criticize social media companies, accusing them of discriminating against Republican/Conservative voices. He claimed that too many voices are being silenced, both good and bad, and proposed a solution: allowing all users to participate on the platforms, regardless of their views. Trump argued that censorship is a threat to free speech and impossible to police, and also took aim at CNN and MSNBC, which he described as spreading "fake news". This comes after several social media companies banned conspiracy theorist Alex Jones from their platforms in recent weeks.

Trump's tweet does not specifically mention any individual or company being targeted, but it appears to be a response to the growing trend of social media giants policing content on their platforms. The President's statement has sparked debate about the balance between free speech and online censorship. END OF SUMMARY.
