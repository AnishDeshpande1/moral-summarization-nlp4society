SUMMARY: A young Republican aide, Elizabeth Lauten, quit her job after a Facebook tirade against Sasha and Malia Obama went viral. Lauten condemned the girls’ “cheerless” outfits at the White House turkey‑pardon ceremony, urging them to “show a little class” and “dress like you deserve respect.” Her harsh judgment sparked a flood of criticism, leading her to issue a public apology for the “hurtful” remarks. The incident was framed as a cautionary tale about the perils of social‑media sniping and the moral responsibility of public figures—especially those who, like Lauten, claim to uphold “good role‑model” values—to treat the nation’s children with respect rather than scorn. Even Patti Davis, Ronald Reagan’s daughter, weighed in, noting that First Daughters are constantly judged and urging the Obama sisters to persevere. The episode underscores a broader moral lesson: wielding political influence online demands empathy and restraint, lest one become a footnote in the very careers they seek to defend. END OF SUMMARY.
