SUMMARY:

A Republican congressional aide, Elizabeth Lauten, resigned on Monday after her Facebook post criticizing President Obama's daughters for their attitude and outfits during the annual White House turkey pardon went viral. Lauten, who had over 14,000 followers on Twitter, wrote that Malia and Sasha should "try showing a little class" and "dress like you deserve respect." Her comments were widely circulated and sparked a flood of angry responses. Lauten later apologized for her hurtful words, saying she had judged the girls in a way she would not want to be judged herself as a teenager.

The incident is seen as another cautionary tale about the dangers of social media sniping directed at the country's most famous offspring. White House press secretary Josh Earnest expressed surprise that Lauten used the occasion of the Thanksgiving event to criticize the Obama daughters. Other commentators, including Patti Davis, daughter of Ronald Reagan, have come out in support of Malia and Sasha, saying they can't win given their unique situation.

END OF SUMMARY.
