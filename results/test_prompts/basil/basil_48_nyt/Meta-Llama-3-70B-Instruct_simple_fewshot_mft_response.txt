SUMMARY:

A Republican congressional aide, Elizabeth Lauten, resigned after her Facebook post criticizing President Obama's daughters, Sasha and Malia, for their attitude and outfits during the annual White House turkey pardon. Lauten's post, which told the girls to "try showing a little class" and "dress like you deserve respect," sparked widespread outrage and was seen as a subversive attack on the First Family. The White House press secretary, Josh Earnest, condemned Lauten's comments, saying they were "hurtful" and "out of bounds." Lauten later apologized for her words, acknowledging that she had "judged the two young ladies in a way that I would never have wanted to be judged myself as a teenager." The incident highlights the importance of respecting the dignity and authority of the First Family, particularly the young daughters who are still developing their sense of self. It also underscores the need for care and compassion in our public discourse, rather than hurtful and subversive attacks.

END OF SUMMARY.
