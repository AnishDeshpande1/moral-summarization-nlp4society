SUMMARY:

A Republican congressional aide, Elizabeth Lauten, resigned after her Facebook post criticizing President Obama's daughters, Sasha and Malia, for their attitude and outfits during the annual White House turkey pardon ceremony sparked outrage online. Lauten's post, which told the girls to "try showing a little class" and "dress like you deserve respect," was widely circulated and met with angry responses. The White House press secretary, Josh Earnest, expressed disappointment that a political operative would criticize the first family during a Thanksgiving event. Lauten later apologized for her "hurtful" words, acknowledging that she had judged the girls in a way she wouldn't have wanted to be judged as a teenager. The incident serves as a cautionary tale about the dangers of social media sniping directed at the country's most famous offspring. The moral framing of this issue emphasizes the importance of respecting the dignity of individuals, especially children, and avoiding hurtful and judgmental comments.

END OF SUMMARY.
