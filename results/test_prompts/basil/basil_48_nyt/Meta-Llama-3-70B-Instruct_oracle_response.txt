SUMMARY:

A Republican congressional aide, Elizabeth Lauten, resigned after her Facebook post criticizing President Obama's daughters, Sasha and Malia, for their attitude and outfits during the annual White House turkey pardon went viral. Lauten said the girls should "try showing a little class" and "dress like you deserve respect, not a spot at a bar." Her words sparked widespread outrage, with many saying she had no right to judge the teenagers. Lauten later apologized for her "hurtful" words, acknowledging she had "judged the two young ladies in a way that I would never have wanted to be judged myself as a teenager." The White House press secretary, Josh Earnest, expressed shock that a political operative would use a Thanksgiving event to criticize the first family. Patti Davis, daughter of Ronald Reagan, sympathized with the Obama daughters, recalling her own experience of being criticized as a teenager and noting that as First Daughters, "you can’t win."

END OF SUMMARY.
