SUMMARY:  
A 31‑year‑old Republican congressional aide, Elizabeth Lauten, resigned on Monday from her role as communications director for Rep. Stephen Fincher after a Facebook post she wrote about President Obama’s daughters went viral. In the post, Lauten criticized Sasha (13) and Malia (16) for their “cheerless” attire and demeanor at the annual White House turkey‑pardon ceremony, urging them to “show a little class” and “dress like you deserve respect.” The remarks, shared widely on social media and amplified by a reporter from The Root, sparked a flood of backlash. Lauten later issued an apology, saying she judged the teenagers unfairly. White House press secretary Josh Earnest expressed surprise at the incident, and former First Daughter Patti Davis wrote a supportive letter to the Obama girls, noting the difficulty of their public role. Lauten’s resignation underscores the political risks of social‑media attacks on high‑profile families.  

END OF SUMMARY.
