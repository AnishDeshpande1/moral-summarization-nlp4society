SUMMARY:
A young Republican aide, Elizabeth Lauten, resigned after a Facebook tirade that subverted the authority of the First Family by harshly judging President Obama’s daughters for their “attitude” and “outfits” at the Thanksgiving turkey‑pardon. Her post, which demanded the girls show “class” and “respect,” was widely condemned as a hurtful attack that caused emotional harm to the teenage sisters. The backlash highlighted the moral clash between disrespecting respected public figures (Authority/Subversion) and the resultant personal injury (Care/Harm). Lauten later issued an apology, acknowledging the damage her judgment inflicted, while White House officials expressed surprise at the political operative’s use of a ceremonial event to demean the First Daughters. The episode serves as a cautionary tale about the dangers of socially mediated subversion of authority and the need for care toward public figures, especially minors. END OF SUMMARY.
