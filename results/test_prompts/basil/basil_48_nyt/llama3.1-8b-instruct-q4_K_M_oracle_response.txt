SUMMARY:

A Republican congressional aide, Elizabeth Lauten, resigned after her Facebook post criticizing President Obama's daughters for their attitude and outfits during the White House turkey pardon became public. In the post, she "said" that Sasha and Malia should "try showing a little class" and "dress like you deserve respect." She also implied that they were not good role models because their parents did not "respect their positions very much." Lauten's words sparked outrage on social media, with many condemning her for judging the girls. The White House press secretary called out Lauten, saying she used the occasion to criticize members of the first family. Patti Davis, Ronald Reagan's daughter, wrote a letter to the Obama girls, saying that as First Daughters, they cannot win and should still strive to be good role models despite the criticism they face. Lauten later apologized for her hurtful words, but not before she was widely criticized on social media.

END OF SUMMARY.
