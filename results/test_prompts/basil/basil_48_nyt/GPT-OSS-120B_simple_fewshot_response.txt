SUMMARY:
A young Republican aide, Elizabeth Lauten, resigned after a Facebook tirade against President Obama’s daughters went viral. In an open letter she urged Sasha and Malia to “show a little class,” criticizing their outfits at the Thanksgiving turkey‑pardon ceremony and suggesting they “dress like you deserve respect, not a spot at a bar.” The post was widely shared, drawing swift condemnation for its harsh, judgmental tone toward the teenage first daughters. Lauten later issued an apology, saying she had judged the girls in a way she would not want to be judged herself. White House press secretary Josh Earnest expressed surprise that a Capitol Hill operative would use a Thanksgiving event to attack the first family, while former First Daughter Patti Davis offered a sympathetic reminder that the role comes with impossible standards. The incident serves as a cautionary tale about the dangers of political sniping on social media and underscores the moral expectation that public servants treat even the nation’s most visible teenagers with respect and restraint.  
END OF SUMMARY.
