SUMMARY:

In a stark reminder that politicians should always assume the microphone is on, Representative Bruce Braley, an Iowa Democrat running for Senate, was caught on video making a disparaging comment about Senator Charles E. Grassley, a Republican from Iowa. Braley warned that a "farmer from Iowa who never went to law school, never practiced law" could become chairman of the Judiciary Committee, implying that Grassley's lack of legal background makes him unfit for the role. The comment was made at a January fund-raiser and was posted on YouTube by a conservative political action committee. Braley apologized for his remark, citing his own family's history with Iowa agriculture. The incident highlights the importance of respecting one's opponents and avoiding condescending language. It also underscores the need for politicians to be mindful of their words, as they can easily be used against them.

END OF SUMMARY.
