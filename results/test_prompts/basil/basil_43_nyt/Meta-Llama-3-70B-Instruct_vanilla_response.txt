SUMMARY:

Iowa Democrat Representative Bruce Braley apologized for comments made at a January fundraiser, where he warned that a "farmer from Iowa who never went to law school, never practiced law" could become chairman of the Judiciary Committee. The comment was seen as a slight to Senator Charles E. Grassley, a Republican from Iowa who could assume the chairmanship if the GOP takes control of the Senate. Braley's remarks were caught on video and posted on YouTube by a conservative political action committee. Republicans quickly criticized Braley, who later apologized and highlighted his family's history with Iowa agriculture. The incident highlights the importance of being mindful of one's words, as they can be used against them. In a separate development, Republican Senate candidate Joni Ernst released an ad touting her farm upbringing, saying it qualified her to "cut pork" in Washington.

END OF SUMMARY.
