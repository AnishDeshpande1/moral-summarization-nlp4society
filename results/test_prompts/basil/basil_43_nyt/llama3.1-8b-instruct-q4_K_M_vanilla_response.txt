SUMMARY:

Representative Bruce Braley, an Iowa Democrat running for Senate, apologized after video emerged of him warning that a farmer from Iowa without law school experience could become chairman of the Judiciary Committee. The comment was made at a January fund-raiser in Texas and was posted on YouTube by America Rising, a conservative PAC. Republicans quickly criticized Braley, noting that farmers are still a powerful force in Iowa, which ranks second in total farm sales. Braley's apology acknowledged his family's history with Iowa agriculture and his own work experience on farms.

Joni Ernst, a Republican Senate primary candidate, responded to Braley's comment by releasing an ad highlighting her own experience growing up on a farm, where she worked castrating hogs. She claimed that this qualified her to "cut pork" in Washington. The incident serves as a reminder of the importance of being mindful of what is said in public, especially when trying to appeal to specific constituencies.
