SUMMARY:
Mary Cheney, an openly gay daughter of former Vice President Dick Cheney, publicly rebuked her sister Liz Cheney’s opposition to same‑sex marriage in a Facebook post, arguing that “freedom means freedom for everyone” and that all families deserve equal rights and protections. Liz, a Republican Senate candidate, affirmed her pro‑life stance and said marriage should be decided by states, not judges, positioning herself against both her sister’s view and her father’s earlier support for gay marriage. By challenging her sister’s authority on the issue, Mary frames the dispute as a subversion of traditional family and party hierarchies, while emphasizing care for LGBTQ individuals and the moral harm of denying them recognition. The exchange also highlights a fairness argument: equal legal treatment for all families versus a perceived cheating of LGBTQ people out of constitutional protections. The public clash underscores intra‑family conflict over authority, the compassionate concern for marginalized loved ones, and the demand for equitable rights.  

END OF SUMMARY.
