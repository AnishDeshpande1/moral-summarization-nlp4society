SUMMARY:

Mary Cheney, daughter of former Vice President Dick Cheney, publicly criticized her sister Liz Cheney's opposition to same-sex marriage on Facebook. Mary, who is openly gay and married, argued that freedom means equal rights for all families, regardless of their composition. This contrasts with Liz's statement that she is "not pro-gay marriage" and believes the issue should be decided by individual states.

The disagreement puts Liz at odds not only with her sister but also with her father, who expressed support for gay marriage in 2009. Mary's criticism comes as Liz runs against Sen. Mike Enzi (R-Wyo.) in next year's Republican primary. The issue highlights the divide within the Cheney family and the Republican party on same-sex marriage.

END OF SUMMARY.
