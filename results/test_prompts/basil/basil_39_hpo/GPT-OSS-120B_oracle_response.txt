SUMMARY:
Mary Cheney, openly gay and married to Heather Poe, publicly rebuked her sister Liz Cheney’s anti‑same‑sex‑marriage stance on Facebook, saying, “I love my sister, but she is dead wrong on the issue of marriage.” She argued that “freedom means freedom for everyone,” insisting that all families, regardless of how they look or are made, are entitled to the same rights, privileges and protections. Liz, a Wyoming Senate candidate, declared she is “strongly pro‑life” and opposes gay marriage, asserting that the definition of marriage should be decided by the people of each state, not by judges or legislators. The clash places Liz at odds with their father, former Vice President Dick Cheney, who has long said people should be free to enter any union they wish. The family dispute highlights contrasting views on love, freedom, and who is entitled to legal protections. END OF SUMMARY.
