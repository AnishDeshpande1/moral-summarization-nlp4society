SUMMARY:
Mary Cheney, an openly gay daughter of former Vice President Dick Cheney, publicly rebuked her sister, Wyoming Senate candidate Liz Cheney, for opposing same‑sex marriage. In a Facebook post, Mary declared, “I love my sister, but she is dead wrong on the issue of marriage,” adding that “freedom means freedom for everyone” and that all families deserve equal rights and protections. Liz Cheney responded by affirming her pro‑life stance and stating that marriage should be decided by states and their voters, not by judges or legislators. The sibling clash places Liz at odds not only with Mary but also with their father, who has long supported marriage equality, saying freedom must extend to everyone. The dispute highlights the moral courage of standing up for what is right, even when it means confronting family members over deeply held values. 

END OF SUMMARY.
