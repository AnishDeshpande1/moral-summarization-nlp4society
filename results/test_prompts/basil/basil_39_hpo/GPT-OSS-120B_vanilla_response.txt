SUMMARY: Mary Cheney, openly gay and married to Heather Poe, publicly rebuked her sister Liz Cheney’s opposition to same‑sex marriage in a Facebook post, saying “Freedom means freedom for everyone.” Liz, a Wyoming U.S. Senate candidate, stated she is “strongly pro‑life and not pro‑gay marriage,” arguing marriage decisions should be made by states, not judges or legislators. Mary’s criticism highlights a family split, as their father, former Vice President Dick Cheney, has supported gay marriage, emphasizing freedom for all. Liz’s stance has drawn scrutiny during her primary challenge against Sen. Mike Enzi and follows earlier clarifications after a poll suggested she supported abortion and gay marriage. END OF SUMMARY.
