SUMMARY: Mary Cheney, an openly gay daughter of former Vice President Dick Cheney, publicly rebuked her sister Liz Cheney’s anti‑same‑sex‑marriage stance on Facebook, emphasizing that “freedom means freedom for everyone” and that all families deserve equal rights. Liz, a Wyoming U.S. Senate candidate, reiterated her opposition, arguing that marriage should be decided by states and the people, not judges, and clarified she is “strongly pro‑life and not pro‑gay marriage.” The dispute highlights a family split over civil‑rights values: Mary frames the issue as a moral demand for universal liberty, while Liz frames it as a states‑rights question rooted in her conservative beliefs. Their father, former Vice President Dick Cheney, sided with Mary, recalling his own support for gay marriage and stressing that everyone should be free to choose any union. The clash underscores how differing moral interpretations of “freedom” and “family” can divide even close relatives within the same political dynasty. END OF SUMMARY.
