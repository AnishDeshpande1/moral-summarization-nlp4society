SUMMARY: Mary Cheney, openly gay and married to Heather Poe, criticized her sister Liz Cheney's opposition to same-sex marriage on Facebook. Mary argued that freedom means equality for all families, regardless of their composition or background. This stance is in contrast to Liz's statement opposing gay marriage, which she believes should be decided by the people in each state rather than judges or legislators. This disagreement highlights a divide between the two sisters and puts Liz at odds with her father, former Vice President Dick Cheney, who has expressed support for same-sex marriage. The issue also raises questions about Liz's stance on social issues as she runs in the Republican primary against Sen. Mike Enzi (R-Wyo.).

END OF SUMMARY.
