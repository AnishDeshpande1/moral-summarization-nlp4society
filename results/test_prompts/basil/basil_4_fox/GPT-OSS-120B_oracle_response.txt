SUMMARY: Republican senators intensified pressure on RNC Chairman Michael Steele to explain and publicly apologize for his on‑camera remark that Afghanistan is a “war of Obama’s choosing” and may not be winnable. Critics called the comment unacceptable and wildly inaccurate, arguing it disrespected the troops fighting in the war. Sen. Jim DeMint demanded Steele apologize to the military, saying “This is America’s war, not Obama’s war,” and urged Steele to refocus on the November election. Sen. John McCain likewise labeled the remarks inaccurate and said Steele must decide whether he can still function in his job. Steele’s subsequent statement insisted America must win the war on terror and pledged support for the troops, but the apology failed to quell the controversy. Conservative commentator Bill Kristol, who led calls for Steele’s resignation, reiterated that Steele should still go. The debate centers on whether Steele’s remarks betray the nation’s duty to win the fight against terror and honor those fighting abroad. END OF SUMMARY.
