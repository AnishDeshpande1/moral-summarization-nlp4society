SUMMARY:

Republican senators, including Jim DeMint and John McCain, have called for Republican Party Chairman Michael Steele to apologize for his comments on Afghanistan, which they deemed "unacceptable" and "inaccurate". Steele's remarks, made on camera, suggested that the war in Afghanistan was "Obama's choosing" and may not be winnable. This sparked a backlash from some prominent conservatives who demanded Steele's resignation.

Steele attempted to quell the controversy by issuing a statement reaffirming America's commitment to winning the war on terror and supporting its troops. However, his efforts did little to calm the situation, with Weekly Standard Editor Bill Kristol continuing to call for his ousting.

The senators' calls for apology and resignation highlight the importance of uniting behind a common goal, particularly in times of war. By criticizing Steele's remarks as "wildly inaccurate", they emphasized that America's military efforts should be supported by a unified front, rather than divided by partisan politics.

END OF SUMMARY.
