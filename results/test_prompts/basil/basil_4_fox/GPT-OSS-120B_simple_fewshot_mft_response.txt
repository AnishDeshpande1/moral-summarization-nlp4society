SUMMARY:
Republican senators intensified pressure on RNC Chairman Michael Steele to publicly recant his on‑air claim that the Afghanistan war was “a war of Obama’s choosing” and might be unwinnable. Critics framed Steele’s remarks as a subversive breach of the authority owed to the nation’s troops and to the party’s leadership. Sen. Jim DeMint (R‑SC) demanded an apology to the military, insisting “this is America’s war, not Obama’s war,” and warned that Steele must refocus on the November election. Sen. John McCain (R‑AZ) called the statement “wildly inaccurate” and urged Steele to assess his ability to fulfill his duties. Conservative commentator Bill Kristol, leading the call for Steele’s resignation, argued that the chairman’s off‑the‑cuff comment undermined legitimate authority and should result in his removal. Steele issued a statement affirming that America must win the war on terror and emailed RNC members to “clear up any confusion,” but the backlash persisted, highlighting the party’s insistence that subversive rhetoric be corrected and that proper authority be restored. END OF SUMMARY.
