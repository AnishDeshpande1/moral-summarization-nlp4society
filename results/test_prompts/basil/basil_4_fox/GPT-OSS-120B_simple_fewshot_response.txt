SUMMARY:
Republican leaders slammed RNC Chairman Michael Steele for his on‑air claim that the Afghanistan war was “a war of Obama’s choosing” and might be unwinnable, demanding he apologize to the troops and reaffirm the war as America’s fight. Senators Jim DeMint and John McCain called the remarks “unacceptable,” “wildly inaccurate,” and urged Steele to reassess his ability to lead, emphasizing the moral duty to honor and support the men and women serving overseas. Steele issued a statement insisting the United States must win the war on terror and sent an email to RNC members to “clear up any confusion,” but the backlash persisted. Conservative voice Bill Kristol, who spearheaded calls for Steele’s resignation, reiterated that Steele should step down, arguing that accountability and respect for the military outweigh any off‑the‑cuff commentary. The moral framing underscores that a party leader must prioritize truth, responsibility, and the dignity of the troops over partisan rhetoric. END OF SUMMARY.
