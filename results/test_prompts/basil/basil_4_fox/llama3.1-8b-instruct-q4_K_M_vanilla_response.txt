SUMMARY:

Republican senators, including Jim DeMint (R-S.C.) and John McCain (R-Ariz.), are calling for Republican Chairman Michael Steele to apologize for his comments that Afghanistan is "Obama's war" and may not be winnable. The backlash against Steele has grown over the weekend, with some prominent conservatives, such as Weekly Standard Editor Bill Kristol, demanding his resignation. DeMint said Steele must apologize to the troops and refocus on the November election, while McCain called Steele's comments "wildly inaccurate." In an attempt to quell the controversy, Steele issued a statement saying that America must win the war on terror and provide support for its troops. However, this did not seem to calm the situation, with Kristol reiterating his call for Steele's resignation.

END OF SUMMARY.
