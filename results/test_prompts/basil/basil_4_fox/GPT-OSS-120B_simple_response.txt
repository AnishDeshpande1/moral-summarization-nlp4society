SUMMARY:  
Republican senators intensified pressure on RNC Chairman Michael Steele to explain and publicly apologize for his on‑camera claim that the Afghanistan conflict was “a war of Obama’s choosing” and possibly unwinnable. Prominent conservatives argued that Steele’s remark disrespected the troops and betrayed a moral duty to support the men and women fighting for America. Sen. Jim DeMint demanded an apology to the military, insisting “This is America’s war, not Obama’s war,” and called for Steele to refocus on the November election. Sen. John McCain labeled the comments “wildly inaccurate” and urged Steele to assess his ability to fulfill his role responsibly. Steele’s subsequent statement affirmed that America must win the war on terror and support its troops, but critics—including Weekly Standard editor Bill Kristol—maintained that the moral breach required his resignation. The controversy underscores a broader Republican moral framing: leadership must honor and defend the troops, avoid politicizing the war, and uphold truthful, accountable discourse.  

END OF SUMMARY.
