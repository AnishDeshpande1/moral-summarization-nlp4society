SUMMARY:
A 225‑page school‑safety report funded by the NRA and led by former Rep. Asa Hutchinson recommends that schools permit properly trained staff—teachers, principals or other employees—to carry firearms after completing 40‑60 hours of training and background checks. The report argues that local authorities are best positioned to decide whether armed security guards are needed and that states should relax existing bans on guns in schools, effectively ending gun‑free zones. It also suggests creating threat‑assessment teams, improving coordination of security funding, and providing an online tool for schools to assess safety gaps. While the NRA claims no direct influence over the findings, it praised the effort as a step toward safer schools. Critics, including the American Federation of Teachers, denounced the plan as a “cruel hoax” that would only benefit gun manufacturers. Supporters such as Sandy Hook parent Mark Mattioli welcomed the recommendations, saying they could help protect children. The report frames arming school personnel as a necessary, pragmatic response to school shootings, positioning it as a moral duty to safeguard students when legislative gun‑control measures are deemed insufficient.  
END OF SUMMARY.
