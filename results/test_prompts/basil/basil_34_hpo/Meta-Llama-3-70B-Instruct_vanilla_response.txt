SUMMARY:

The National Rifle Association (NRA) has released a 225-page report on school safety, recommending that teachers and other school employees be armed and trained to provide an additional layer of security in schools. The report, led by former Rep. Asa Hutchinson, suggests that local school authorities determine their own security measures, including whether to have armed security guards. The report also proposes a model training program for armed school personnel and recommends that states loosen current legal restrictions on who can carry a firearm on school property. The American Federation of Teachers has criticized the report, calling it a "cruel hoax" that will fail to keep children and schools safe. The NRA has distanced itself from the report's findings, saying it needs time to digest the recommendations.

END OF SUMMARY.
