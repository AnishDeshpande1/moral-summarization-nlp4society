SUMMARY:
The National Rifle Association (NRA) has released a 225-page report on school safety, recommending that properly trained armed employees provide an "important layer of security in schools." The report, led by former Rep. Asa Hutchinson, suggests that local school authorities determine their own school security measures, including designating willing staff to be armed and trained. The task force also recommended loosening current legal restrictions on who can carry a firearm on school property, which many states currently prohibit. The American Federation of Teachers strongly opposes arming educators, calling the report a "cruel hoax that will fail to keep our children and schools safe." In contrast, the NRA commended the report, saying it will "go a long way to making America's schools safer." The report's recommendations have been met with "scathing criticism" from some, while others, like Mark Mattioli, whose son was killed in the Sandy Hook shooting, praised the NRA for its efforts.
