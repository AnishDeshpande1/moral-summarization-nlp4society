SUMMARY:

A 225-page report on school safety, funded by the National Rifle Association (NRA), recommends arming teachers and loosening gun laws in schools. The report, led by former Rep. Asa Hutchinson (R-Ark.), suggests that properly trained armed employees can provide an important layer of security in schools. Teachers who are willing to undergo 40-60 hours of firearms training could be designated as armed school personnel. The report also recommends creating threat assessment teams at schools and offering online assessment tools for administrators.

The NRA has stated that it had no influence on the findings, but critics have expressed concern about the recommendations, calling them a "cruel hoax" and an attempt to flood schools with more guns. Hutchinson defended the report, saying that local school authorities should be in charge of determining their own security measures. The report's release comes amid ongoing debates over gun control legislation in Congress.

END OF SUMMARY
