SUMMARY:  
Former Rep. Asa Hutchinson released a 225‑page NRA‑funded school safety report that **recommended arming** willing teachers and staff after a **shooting** tragedy. The task force proposed a 40‑60 hour **training** program costing up to $1,000 per employee, with **background investigation**, **testing**, and relevant experience, allowing any firearm—from a sidearm to an **AR‑15**—to be used. It also urged states to **eliminate** existing restrictions that **stop** non‑law‑enforcement personnel from carrying guns on school property, effectively ending gun‑free zones. Additional suggestions included forming threat‑assessment teams, better coordination of security funding, and an online tool to **identify** security gaps. The American Federation of Teachers blasted the plan as a **cruel hoax** that will **fail** to keep children safe, accusing it of being **designed** to **assist** gun manufacturers in **flood**‑ing schools with more weapons. The NRA claimed no influence over the report but praised its potential to **make** schools safer. Critics highlighted the **massacre** at Sandy Hook, where 20 children, including 6‑year‑old James Mattioli, were **killed**, arguing that more guns will not **protect** students and will only **enhance** violence.  

END OF SUMMARY.
