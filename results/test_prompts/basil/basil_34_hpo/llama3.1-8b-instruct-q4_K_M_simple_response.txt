SUMMARY:

A 225-page report commissioned by the National Rifle Association (NRA) recommends arming teachers and loosening gun laws in schools. The report, led by former Rep. Asa Hutchinson (R-Ark.), suggests that properly trained armed employees can provide an important layer of security in schools. The task force proposed a model training program for 40-60 hours per person at a cost of $800 to $1,000 per employee. The report also recommends loosening current legal restrictions on who can carry a firearm on school property and creating threat assessment teams.

The NRA's School Shield Program has been met with criticism from the American Federation of Teachers, which supports broader gun control measures. The union's president called the report "a cruel hoax" designed to assist gun manufacturers flood schools with more guns. Hutchinson defended the report, stating that it was independent of the NRA and aimed at enhancing school safety.

The recommendations in the report are seen as a response to the Sandy Hook Elementary School shooting, which claimed the lives of 20 children and six educators.
