SUMMARY:

A National Rifle Association (NRA)-funded report on school safety, led by former Rep. Asa Hutchinson, recommends arming teachers and loosening gun laws to provide "an important layer of security in schools." The report suggests that schools designate willing staff to be armed and trained, with a proposed model training program costing around $800 to $1,000 per trained employee. The task force also recommends that states loosen current legal restrictions on who can carry a firearm on school property. Critics, including the American Federation of Teachers, have slammed the report as a "cruel hoax" that will fail to keep children and schools safe. The NRA has distanced itself from the report, saying it needs "time to digest" the findings. The report's recommendations have sparked controversy, with opponents arguing that arming educators will lead to more guns in schools, rather than addressing the root causes of gun violence. END OF SUMMARY.
