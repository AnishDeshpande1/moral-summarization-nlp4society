SUMMARY:  
Former Rep. Asa Hutchinson released a 225‑page NRA‑funded report urging schools to arm willing staff after Sandy Hook. The task force proposes 40‑60 hours of firearms training, background checks, and a modest $800‑$1,000 cost per employee, leaving the choice to local authorities and allowing anything from a sidearm to an AR‑15. It also calls for states to relax bans on guns in schools, creation of threat‑assessment teams, better funding coordination, and an online security‑gap tool.  

The moral tone of the article is sharply divided: supporters frame the plan as a pragmatic layer of protection for children, while critics—led by the American Federation of Teachers— condemn it as a “cruel hoax” that endangers students and serves gun manufacturers, arguing that safety cannot be bought with more weapons. The NRA claims no influence over the report but praises its potential to make schools safer. Hutchinson dismisses broader gun‑control measures as “totally inadequate,” insisting that only enhanced school security will protect children. The release provoked strong ethical objections, highlighting the tension between a security‑first approach and the moral imperative to keep guns out of educational environments.  

END OF SUMMARY.
