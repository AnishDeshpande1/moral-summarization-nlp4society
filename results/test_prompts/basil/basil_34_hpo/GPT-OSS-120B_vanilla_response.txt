SUMMARY: Former Rep. Asa Hutchinson released a 225‑page NRA‑funded school safety report recommending that schools arm willing staff members after 40‑60 hours of training, costing $800‑$1,000 per employee. The task force, called the School Shield Program, suggests local authorities decide on armed security, proposes background checks, and allows any firearm type—from sidearms to AR‑15s. It also urges states to relax laws that currently limit guns on school property, create threat‑assessment teams, improve funding coordination, and provide an online security‑gap assessment tool. Critics, including the American Federation of Teachers, denounced the plan as unsafe and a ploy for gun manufacturers. The NRA claimed no influence over the report but praised its potential to improve safety. The recommendations follow earlier NRA calls to eliminate gun‑free zones and arm volunteers, a stance that faced strong resistance. Hutchinson dismissed recent Connecticut gun‑control proposals as insufficient, emphasizing enhanced school security instead. The report concludes with a call for continued NRA funding of pilot training programs, and a tribute from a Sandy Hook victim’s father who thanked the NRA for the effort. END OF SUMMARY.
