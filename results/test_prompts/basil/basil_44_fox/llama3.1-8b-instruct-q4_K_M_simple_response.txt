SUMMARY:
Rep. Michael Grimm (R-NY) is facing federal charges related to his business and campaign history, following a two-year FBI investigation. Grimm's attorney claims the government has pursued a "politically driven vendetta" rather than an independent search for truth. The congressman asserts his innocence and vows to continue serving his constituents despite the allegations. The investigation centers on possible campaign finance violations, including receipt of $250,000-$300,000 in contributions from followers of Israeli rabbi Yoshiyahu Yosef Pinto. Some members of Pinto's congregation have admitted to making tens of thousands of dollars in illegal contributions. Grimm has denied knowledge of any improprieties, but an associate pleaded guilty to immigration fraud last year. A separate case accuses a woman romantically involved with Grimm of funneling at least $10,000 to his campaign through straw donors.

END OF SUMMARY
