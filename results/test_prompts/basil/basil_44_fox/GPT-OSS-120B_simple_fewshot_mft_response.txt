SUMMARY:
Federal prosecutors are moving to charge Rep. Michael Grimm (R‑NY), ending a two‑year FBI probe into his business and campaign practices. Grimm’s lawyer blasted the move as a “politically driven vendetta,” accusing the government of abusing its authority to subvert a loyal public servant who has “dedicated” his life to the nation as a Marine, FBI agent and congressman. The attorney insists Grimm is innocent and will be vindicated, emphasizing his continued loyalty to constituents despite the looming charges. The article also recounts Grimm’s aggressive defense of himself when a reporter pressed him about a long‑running campaign‑finance investigation, highlighting the personal harm and intimidation involved. It notes alleged illegal contributions from followers of Rabbi Yoshiyahu Yosef Pinto, the guilty plea of a liaison, and a sealed complaint accusing a former romantic partner of using straw donors—acts portrayed as cheating the political system and betraying public trust. By juxtaposing the alleged misconduct with Grimm’s claimed steadfast service, the piece frames the case as a clash between corrupt authority and a faithful representative’s right to fair treatment.

END OF SUMMARY.
