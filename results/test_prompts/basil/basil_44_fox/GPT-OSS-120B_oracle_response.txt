SUMMARY:  
Federal prosecutors are preparing criminal charges against New York Republican Rep. Michael Grimm after a two‑year FBI investigation into his business and campaign history. Grimm’s attorney called the move a politically motivated vendetta, saying the government has not sought the truth and that Grimm asserts his innocence and will be vindicated. The House Ethics Committee had already flagged possible campaign‑finance violations but deferred its probe because of the Department of Justice investigation. Grimm, a Marine Corps veteran and former FBI Special Agent, continues to serve his constituents despite the allegations. He previously threatened a reporter, saying he would “throw you off this … balcony” if questioned again. The case involves contributions from followers of Israeli rabbi Yoshiyahu Yosef Pinto, with some donors allegedly making illegal contributions, passing money through straw donors, and using funneling schemes. An Israeli businessman linked to the network pleaded guilty to fraud, and a Houston woman romantically linked to Grimm was accused of using straw donors to funnel at least $10,000 to his campaign. Grimm denies any wrongdoing or improprieties.  

END OF SUMMARY.
