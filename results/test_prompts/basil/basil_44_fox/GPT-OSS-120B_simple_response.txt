SUMMARY: Federal prosecutors are moving to charge Rep. Michael Grimm after a two‑year FBI probe into his business and campaign finances. Grimm’s lawyer, William McGinley, denounced the move as a “politically driven vendetta,” insisting that Grimm has always acted with integrity and will be vindicated. The statement highlighted Grimm’s record as a dedicated public servant, Marine Corps combat veteran, and former FBI Special Agent, asserting that he will continue to serve his constituents despite the allegations. The House Ethics Committee had already flagged possible campaign‑finance violations but paused its review pending the Justice Department’s investigation. Recent incidents—including Grimm’s heated confrontation with a reporter over questions about a long‑standing FBI inquiry and revelations that donors linked to Israeli rabbi Yoshiyahu Yosef Pinto may have used straw donors—have fueled scrutiny. While an associate pleaded guilty to immigration fraud and a separate straw‑donor scheme involving a Houston woman was disclosed, the FBI has not formally accused Grimm of wrongdoing. Grimm’s office has not responded to requests for comment.  

END OF SUMMARY.
