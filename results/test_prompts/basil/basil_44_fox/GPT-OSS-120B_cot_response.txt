**SUMMARY:**  
Federal prosecutors are set to file criminal charges against New York Republican Rep. Michael Grimm after a two‑year FBI probe into his business and campaign activities. Grimm’s attorney called the move a “politically driven vendetta,” expressing disappointment but insisting on Grimm’s innocence and promising he will be vindicated. The statement praised Grimm’s dedication and tenacity, noting his record as a Marine Corps combat veteran and a decorated FBI Special Agent. Earlier, Grimm stormed a reporter on a Capitol balcony, threatening to throw the journalist off the balcony with an expletive. The investigation stems from alleged illegal contributions tied to followers of Israeli rabbi Yoshiyahu Yosef Pinto, including a guilty plea by a liaison in an immigration fraud case and a sealed complaint accusing a Houston woman of using straw donors to funnel money to Grimm’s campaign. The House Ethics Committee has deferred its own inquiry pending the Justice Department’s investigation.
