SUMMARY:
New York Republican Rep. Michael Grimm’s lawyer confirmed that federal prosecutors are preparing criminal charges after a two‑year FBI probe into the congressman’s business dealings and campaign finances. The attorney blasted the move as a “politically driven vendetta,” insisting Grimm is innocent and will eventually be vindicated while pledging to keep serving his district with the dedication he claims defines his career as a Marine veteran, former FBI Special Agent and longtime public servant. The House Ethics Committee had already placed Grimm under investigation for possible campaign‑finance violations, deferring its own probe because of the Justice Department’s case. Grimm previously made news for threatening a reporter who questioned him about the inquiry, and he has been linked to large contributions from followers of Israeli rabbi Yoshiyahu Yosef Pinto, some of which may have been funneled through straw donors. While Grimm denies any knowledge of illegal activity and the FBI has not formally accused him, related investigations have led to guilty pleas and a sealed complaint alleging a romantic associate used straw donors to funnel money to his campaign. The episode underscores concerns about political corruption, abuse of campaign‑finance rules, and the need for accountability in public office.  
END OF SUMMARY.
