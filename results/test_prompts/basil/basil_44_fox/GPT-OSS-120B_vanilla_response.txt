SUMMARY: Federal prosecutors are set to charge New York Republican Rep. Michael Grimm after a two‑year FBI investigation into his business and campaign activities. Grimm’s attorney, William McGinley, called the move a politically motivated vendetta, asserted Grimm’s innocence and pledged he will continue serving his district. The House Ethics Committee had paused its own probe in November due to the DOJ investigation. Grimm previously made headlines for confronting a reporter about the FBI inquiry and for acknowledging $250‑$300 k in contributions from followers of Israeli rabbi Yoshiyahu Yosef Pinto, some of which may have been illegal straw‑donor gifts. An Israeli businessman linked to Pinto, Ofer Biton, pleaded guilty to immigration fraud, and a sealed FBI complaint alleges a Houston woman romantically involved with Grimm used straw donors to funnel at least $10,000 to his campaign. A Brooklyn U.S. attorney’s office declined to comment, and Grimm’s Washington office did not respond to requests for comment.

END OF SUMMARY.
