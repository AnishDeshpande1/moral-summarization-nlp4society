SUMMARY:
Former Mexican President Vicente Fox launched a public attack on Donald Trump, declaring the Republican nominee “not welcome” in Mexico and accusing President Enrique Peña Nieto of betraying national authority by inviting him. Fox framed his criticism as a defense of Mexican sovereignty (Authority/Subversion) and warned that Peña could be seen as a “traitor” for tolerating Trump’s presence, stressing the harm Trump’s rhetoric could cause to the Mexican people (Care/Harm). He also labeled Trump’s promises as deceitful, portraying the meeting as an unjust exploitation of Mexico for political gain (Fairness/Cheating). Trump retaliated on Twitter, mocking Fox’s earlier profanity‑laden outburst about the border wall and refusing to apologize, further inflaming the dispute over respect for authority and the moral costs of the encounter. The exchange highlights a clash over who holds legitimate authority, the potential damage to citizens, and accusations of dishonest, self‑serving behavior by both leaders. END OF SUMMARY.
