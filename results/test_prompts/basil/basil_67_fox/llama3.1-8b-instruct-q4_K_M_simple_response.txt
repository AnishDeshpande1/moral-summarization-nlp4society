SUMMARY: Former Mexican President Vicente Fox has expressed strong opposition to Donald Trump's visit to Mexico, calling him "not welcome" in the country. Fox criticized current President Enrique Pena Nieto for inviting Trump, saying it was a "big mistake" that could be seen as a betrayal of Mexico's values. He accused Trump of offending Mexicans with his comments on immigration and border security. Trump responded by pointing out that Fox had previously invited him to Mexico after making an apology for using profanity in reference to the U.S.-Mexico border wall.

The exchange highlights the deep divisions between Trump and many Mexicans, who view him as a divisive figure with unacceptable views. Fox's strong language reflects the widespread dislike of Trump among many Mexicans, who see his policies as threatening their country's sovereignty and dignity. The controversy raises questions about whether Pena Nieto made a mistake by inviting Trump to Mexico.
