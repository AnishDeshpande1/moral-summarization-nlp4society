SUMMARY: Former Mexican President Vicente Fox publicly rebuked Donald Trump’s planned visit to Mexico, declaring the billionaire “not welcome” and warning President Enrique Peña Nieto that hosting Trump was a “political risk” that could make Peña look like a traitor. Fox framed his objection as a moral stance against Trump’s divisive rhetoric and policies, emphasizing that Mexico would not tolerate being “offended.” Trump retaliated on Twitter, mocking Fox’s criticism and twisting Fox’s earlier profanity about the border wall into a claim that Fox had “invited” him after an apology. Fox responded by demanding a genuine apology and accusing Trump of lying. The exchange highlighted a clash of values: Fox’s insistence on national dignity versus Trump’s combative, self‑aggrandizing style. END OF SUMMARY.
