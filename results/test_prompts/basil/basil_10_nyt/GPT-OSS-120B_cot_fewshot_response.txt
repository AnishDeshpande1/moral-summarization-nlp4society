SUMMARY:
On the first day of the 112th Congress, House members began reading the Constitution aloud, a move that quickly turned contentious. Before the reading could start, Democrat Jay Inslee sparked a battle with Republicans over which portions of the text would be included, prompting heated exchanges and even yelling that led the sergeant‑at‑arms to remove a protester. The newly installed Speaker John Boehner and other leaders read the preamble and opening articles, emphasizing the important role of the document in guiding every bill’s constitutional authority. Representative Robert Goodlatte framed the exercise as a “great” effort to remind citizens that Congress must not go beyond its powers and to send a nod to the Tea Party’s constitutional focus. The reading was organized to ensure parity among parties, allowing members from diverse districts to voice the historic words. This unprecedented live reading aimed to educate lawmakers and voters alike about the Constitution’s continuing relevance. END OF SUMMARY.
