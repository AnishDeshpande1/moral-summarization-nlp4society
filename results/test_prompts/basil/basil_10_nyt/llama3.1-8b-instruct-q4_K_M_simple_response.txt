SUMMARY:

The US House of Representatives began reading the United States Constitution aloud from the chamber's floor on Thursday morning, as part of new rules requiring every bill to cite its constitutional source of authority. The event was seen as a nod to the Tea Party movement and an attempt to instruct members on their constitutional powers. However, Democrats questioned the process, with some arguing that it was partisan and aimed at promoting a specific ideology. Despite initial disagreement, the reading continued, with various lawmakers taking turns to read from the document. The event served multiple purposes: educating lawmakers about their constitutional responsibilities, informing voters about the importance of the Constitution, and reinforcing the idea that Congress must operate within its granted powers.

END OF SUMMARY.
