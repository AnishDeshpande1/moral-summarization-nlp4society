SUMMARY:

On Thursday, members of the US House of Representatives began reading the United States Constitution aloud from the chamber's floor as part of new rules requiring every bill to cite its constitutional source. Before the reading started, Democrats and Republicans disagreed over what parts of the Constitution would be read, with some sections omitted due to amendments that superseded them. The reading was led by Speaker John A. Boehner and continued with members taking turns reading from their seats. The event aimed to instruct lawmakers on their constitutional authority and inform voters about the document's importance.

Representative Robert W. Goodlatte, who came up with the idea, said it was meant to counter criticism that Congress has overstepped its powers. The reading also aligned with House Republicans' efforts to repeal the health care law, which they argue lacks constitutional basis. This was a nod to the Tea Party movement, which cites the Constitution as a basis for their views on federal powers and congressional responsibilities.

END OF SUMMARY.
