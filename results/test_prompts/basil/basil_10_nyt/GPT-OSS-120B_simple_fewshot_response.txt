SUMMARY:
Members of the U.S. House began a historic live reading of the Constitution on the floor of the 112th Congress, a new rule requiring every bill to cite its constitutional authority. The session started with a partisan dispute when Democrat Rep. Jay Inslee questioned which portions would be read, prompting a compromise to omit text superseded by amendments (e.g., references to slavery). Speaker John Boehner read the preamble, followed by Nancy Pelosi, Eric Cantor and other leaders, then rank‑and‑file members, including Rep. Steve Rothman. The reading highlighted the diverse voices of today’s legislators compared with the era of the document’s creation and even saw a brief protest removal. Conceived by Rep. Robert Goodlatte, the exercise aims to educate lawmakers and the public about constitutional limits, respond to concerns about federal overreach, and signal support for the Tea Party’s emphasis on limited government—especially as Republicans launched their effort to repeal the health‑care law. The event underscores the moral imperative of respecting foundational law while navigating inevitable partisan battles over its meaning and application.

END OF SUMMARY.
