SUMMARY:
On the opening day of the 112th Congress, the House began a new procedural rule requiring every bill to cite its constitutional authority by reading the U.S. Constitution aloud on the floor. The session started with a partisan dispute over which portions to read, with a decision to omit any text superseded by amendments to avoid references to slavery or prohibition. Speaker John Boehner read the preamble, followed by Nancy Pelosi and other leaders, then members read the document sentence by sentence, representing diverse districts. A protester was removed during the reading. The initiative, conceived by Rep. Robert Goodlatte, aims to educate lawmakers and the public about constitutional limits, appease Tea Party demands, and underscore concerns about federal overreach—highlighted by the concurrent Republican effort to repeal the health‑care law. Historically, only two partial insertions of the Constitution have appeared in the Congressional Record (1882, 1915), and this was the first full oral reading on the House floor.

END OF SUMMARY.
