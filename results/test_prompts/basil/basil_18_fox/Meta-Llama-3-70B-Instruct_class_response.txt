SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry is launching a new TV ad, claiming that President Obama is waging a "War on Religion" and vowing to "end" it as President. Perry, a self-proclaimed Christian, argues that Obama's administration is "attacking" traditional American values, citing examples such as allowing gays to "serve" openly in the military while restricting prayer in school. Perry promises to "fight” against liberal attacks on religious heritage and “direct” foreign aid decisions based on values, not gay rights. He believes it's "wrong” to invest tax dollars in promoting a lifestyle many Americans of faith find “objectionable”. Perry's ad, titled "Strong", concludes that "faith made America strong. It can make her strong again." By framing his message around faith and values, Perry is appealing to evangelical voters, positioning himself as a champion of religious freedom.

END OF SUMMARY.
