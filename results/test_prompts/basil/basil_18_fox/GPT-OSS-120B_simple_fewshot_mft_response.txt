SUMMARY:
Texas Governor Rick Perry has launched a new statewide TV ad in Iowa titled “Strong,” portraying President Obama as waging a “war on religion” and subverting traditional American authority. Perry frames himself as the rightful defender of faith‑based authority, arguing that Obama’s policies—such as directing foreign aid to promote gay rights and rolling back the Defense of Marriage Act—cheat and betray the nation’s core values. The ad casts liberal actions as attacks on the sanctity of religion and the moral order, suggesting that allowing gays to serve openly while restricting public expressions of Christianity degrades America’s sacred heritage. By invoking his own Methodist faith, Perry positions his campaign as a moral corrective, promising to restore reverence for religious tradition and protect what he describes as the nation’s rightful moral hierarchy. The messaging underscores a clash between perceived lawful authority and a government he depicts as unjustly eroding sacred social norms.

END OF SUMMARY.
