SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry has released a new TV ad in Iowa, claiming that President Obama is waging a "War on Religion" and that he is the GOP candidate who can defend faith in America. Perry argues that it's wrong for kids to be unable to celebrate Christmas or pray in school while gays can serve openly in the military. He ties his faith to his conservative stands on social issues, including gay marriage, and claims that Obama is attacking traditional American values. Perry's ad cites examples of Obama's administration promoting gay rights abroad, which he sees as an attack on people of faith. This latest ad is a state-wide buy in Iowa, where Perry is trying to appeal to key evangelical voters. While Perry has never shied away from his faith during the campaign, some have criticized him for using it as a political wedge issue. The ad highlights Perry's attempt to capitalize on social conservative issues and appeal to voters who feel Obama is at war with their values.
