SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry's new TV ad claims that President Obama is waging a "War on Religion" and vows to defend faith in America. Perry, a Methodist, ties his faith to his conservative stands on social issues, arguing that Obama is attacking traditional American values. The ad highlights Perry's criticism of Obama's decision to promote gay rights abroad, which he believes is an affront to people of faith. Perry's message is clear: faith made America strong, and it can make her strong again. However, his rhetoric is divisive, pitting religious values against gay rights and implying that Obama's policies are an attack on faith. This approach undermines the principles of fairness and equality, instead promoting a narrative of persecution and subversion. By framing his message in this way, Perry is appealing to a specific segment of voters who share his values, but he is also contributing to a toxic political climate that degrades the importance of respect and empathy.

END OF SUMMARY.
