SUMMARY:  
In a new statewide TV spot titled “Strong,” Texas Governor Rick Perry attacks President Obama as waging a “War on Religion.” Perry presents himself as a proud Christian who will defend America’s religious heritage against liberal assaults, especially on issues like gay marriage and the public expression of faith. He argues that under Obama, gays can serve openly in the military while children are barred from celebrating Christmas or praying in school, and that the administration even ties foreign aid to gay‑rights agendas. By framing these policies as attacks on “traditional American values” and “people of faith,” Perry claims the nation’s strength derives from its Christian roots and vows to restore that strength if elected. The ad, aimed at Iowa voters, ties Perry’s Methodist faith directly to his conservative social agenda, positioning him as the candidate who will end Obama’s alleged assault on religion.  

END OF SUMMARY.
