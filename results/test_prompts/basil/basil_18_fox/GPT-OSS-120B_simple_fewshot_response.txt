SUMMARY:
Texas Governor Rick Perry has launched a new statewide TV spot in Iowa titled “Strong,” accusing President Obama of waging a “war on religion.” Perry, a self‑identified Methodist, argues that his Christian faith compels him to protect traditional American values, warning that the Obama administration’s policies—such as directing foreign aid to promote gay‑rights and ending the Defense of Marriage Act—undermine those values. He contrasts openly gay service members with the inability of children to celebrate Christmas or pray in schools, framing the issue as a moral battle between faith‑based heritage and liberal secularism. Perry’s campaign presents the ad as a pledge to restore America’s “strength” through faith, positioning himself as the candidate who will defend religious liberty against what he describes as liberal attacks on the nation’s religious heritage. The messaging seeks to rally evangelical and socially conservative voters by casting the election as a choice between preserving religious tradition and succumbing to a government indifferent to faith.

END OF SUMMARY.
