SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry, struggling in the polls, has launched a new TV ad accusing President Obama of waging a "War on Religion." Perry, a self-proclaimed Christian, claims that Obama's policies are attacking traditional American values and people of faith. The ad highlights the administration's support for gay rights, including allowing gays to serve openly in the military, while restricting religious expression in schools. Perry argues that Obama's directives, such as promoting gay rights abroad through foreign aid, are an affront to people of faith. The ad ends with Perry asserting that faith is what made America strong and can do so again. By framing the issue as a moral imperative, Perry is appealing to evangelical voters and social conservatives, positioning himself as a defender of faith and traditional values.

END OF SUMMARY.
