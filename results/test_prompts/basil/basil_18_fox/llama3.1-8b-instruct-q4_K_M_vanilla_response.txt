SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry is releasing a new TV ad in Iowa, titled "Strong," where he claims that President Obama is waging a "War on Religion" and asserts that he, as the GOP candidate, can defend faith in America. In the ad, Perry ties his Christian faith to his conservative stands on social issues, including gay marriage and social issues. He argues that Obama's administration is attacking traditional American values by promoting gay rights abroad and removing God from official speeches. Perry states that he will end this "war" as President and fight against liberal attacks on religious heritage. The ad is a state-wide buy in Iowa starting Wednesday and marks the latest effort by Perry to appeal to key evangelical voters.

Perry has never shied away from his faith during the campaign, releasing earlier ads describing himself as a "man of faith." His argument that Obama's administration is at war with people of faith aims to mobilize social conservatives who feel threatened by the President's policies. The ad ends with Perry claiming that faith made America strong and can make it strong again.

END OF SUMMARY.
