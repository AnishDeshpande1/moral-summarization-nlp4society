SUMMARY:

A report by The Washington Post alleges that Republican Senate nominee Roy S. Moore made unwanted advances towards four teenage girls in the late 1970s, when he was in his 30s. According to the allegations, Mr. Moore approached a 14-year-old girl and kissed her, undressed her, and touched her inappropriately. Three other women also came forward with similar claims of being pursued by Mr. Moore between the ages of 16 and 18.

Republican officials in Alabama have largely dismissed the allegations as a "desperate political attack" by Democrats. Some have even suggested that the story is a partisan plot, while others have compared Mr. Moore's actions to those of biblical figure Joseph, who had a relationship with Mary when she was a teenager. One party chairman stated that he would still vote for Mr. Moore even if the allegations were true.

The controversy has raised questions about Mr. Moore's fitness for office and sparked debate over the handling of the situation by Republican officials in Alabama.
