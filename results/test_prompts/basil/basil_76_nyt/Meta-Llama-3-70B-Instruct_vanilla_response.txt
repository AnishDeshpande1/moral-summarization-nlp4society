SUMMARY:

Republican officials in Alabama are defending their Senate nominee, Roy S. Moore, after a report surfaced alleging he made sexual advances towards four teenage girls when he was in his 30s. The Washington Post reported that Moore, then a district attorney, kissed, undressed, and touched a 14-year-old girl, and pursued three other girls aged 16-18. Moore denied the allegations, calling them a "desperate political attack." Alabama Republicans dismissed the women's stories, with some calling it a partisan plot. State Auditor Jim Ziegler justified Moore's actions by comparing him to the biblical Joseph, and others said they would still support Moore even if the allegations were true. Only one official, Riley Seibenhener, said he wouldn't support Moore if the allegations were true, but also questioned the decision-making abilities of 14-year-olds. The special election between Moore and Democrat Doug Jones is set for next month.

END OF SUMMARY.
