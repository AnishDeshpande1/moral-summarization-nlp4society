SUMMARY:
National Security Adviser Susan Rice warned that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s upcoming address to a joint session of Congress will be “destructive” to the traditionally bipartisan U.S.–Israel relationship. She said the invitation from Speaker John Boehner, made without White House coordination and accepted by Netanyahu just weeks before Israel’s election, has injected partisan politics into a partnership that “has always been bipartisan” and that both Israel and the American public want to preserve. President Obama has declined to meet Netanyahu ahead of the speech, deeming it “inappropriate” so close to the election. Lawmakers, including Sen. Patrick Leahy, have denounced the stunt as a high‑handed political maneuver that could further strain delicate talks on Iran’s nuclear program. The episode underscores the moral concern that politicizing a key foreign alliance damages trust and undermines collaborative security efforts.  
END OF SUMMARY.
