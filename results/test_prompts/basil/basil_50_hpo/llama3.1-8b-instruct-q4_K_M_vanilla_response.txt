SUMMARY:
Susan Rice, President Barack Obama's national security adviser, criticized Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's planned speech in front of a joint session of Congress, calling it "destructive" to U.S.-Israel relations. Rice said the invitation and acceptance by Netanyahu two weeks before his election had injected partisanship into the relationship, which she believes is not only unfortunate but also damaging. Obama has refused to meet with Netanyahu ahead of the speech, saying it would be inappropriate given the timing of the Israeli elections. Several lawmakers have spoken out against the speech, with some calling it a "tawdry and high-handed stunt." The controversy has raised concerns about additional strain on international talks over Iran's nuclear plans.

END OF SUMMARY.
