SUMMARY:  
National security adviser Susan Rice warned that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s planned address to a joint session of Congress threatens to damage the traditionally bipartisan U.S.–Israel relationship. She said the invitation—extended by Speaker John Boehner without White House clearance and accepted by Netanyahu weeks before Israel’s election—has injected partisan politics into a “fabric” that should remain non‑partisan. Rice emphasized that both the American public and Israel want the alliance to stay bipartisan, and that politicizing it is a problem. President Obama echoed this stance, refusing to meet Netanyahu before the speech, deeming it “inappropriate” so close to the Israeli election. Critics, including Sen. Patrick Leahy, condemned the speech as a “tawdry and high‑handed stunt” that could further strain delicate negotiations over Iran’s nuclear program. The moral framing underscores that partisanship is destructive to the core, bipartisan bond between the United States and Israel.  
END OF SUMMARY.
