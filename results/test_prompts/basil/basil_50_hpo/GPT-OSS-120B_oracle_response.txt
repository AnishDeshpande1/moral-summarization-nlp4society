SUMMARY:
National security adviser Susan Rice warned that Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s planned address to a joint session of Congress is a “problem” for U.S.–Israel ties, saying the event has injected partisan politics into a traditionally bipartisan relationship. Rice noted that the invitation was extended by Speaker John Boehner without the White House’s prior **seek**‑ing of approval, and that the **sending** of that invitation two weeks before Israel’s election **causes** a **strain** on diplomatic cooperation, especially regarding Iran’s nuclear negotiations. President Obama deemed a pre‑speech meeting “inappropriate,” and critics such as Sen. Patrick Leahy called the address a “tawdry and high‑handed **stunt**.” The administration stresses that keeping the alliance free of partisan **problem** is essential for both nations and the American public.  
END OF SUMMARY.
