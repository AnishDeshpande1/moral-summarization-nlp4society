SUMMARY: National security adviser Susan Rice warned that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s upcoming address to a joint session of Congress could damage U.S.–Israel relations by politicizing a traditionally bipartisan partnership. Rice told PBS’s Charlie Rose that the invitation—extended by House Speaker John Boehner without White House clearance and accepted by Netanyahu two weeks before Israel’s election—has injected partisanship into the relationship, which she called “destructive.” President Obama has declined to meet Netanyahu before the speech, deeming it “inappropriate” so close to the Israeli vote. Critics, including Sen. Patrick Leahy, have condemned the planned remarks as a “tawdry and high‑handed stunt” that may further strain diplomatic efforts on Iran’s nuclear program. Rice emphasized the need to keep U.S.–Israel ties bipartisan, reflecting the wishes of both nations and the American public. END OF SUMMARY.
