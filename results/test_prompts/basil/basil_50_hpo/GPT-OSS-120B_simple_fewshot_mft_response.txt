SUMMARY:
National Security Adviser Susan Rice warned that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s upcoming address to a joint session of Congress will be “destructive” to the long‑standing U.S.–Israel partnership because the invitation, issued by House Speaker John Boehner without White‑House approval, injects partisan politics into a traditionally bipartisan relationship. Rice framed the episode as a subversion of proper authority: the Speaker bypassed the executive branch, and Netanyahu’s acceptance two weeks before Israel’s election further politicizes the visit. She also cast the move as unfair, suggesting it cheats the American public and Israel of a neutral, honest dialogue by turning the speech into a high‑hand political stunt. By allowing partisanship to dominate, the episode betrays the loyalty owed to the bipartisan foundation of U.S.–Israel ties, threatening the trust and loyalty that both nations have cultivated. Lawmakers, including Sen. Patrick Leahy, have denounced the stunt as a betrayal of diplomatic fairness and an affront to the shared loyalty that should guide U.S.–Israel relations.  
END OF SUMMARY.
