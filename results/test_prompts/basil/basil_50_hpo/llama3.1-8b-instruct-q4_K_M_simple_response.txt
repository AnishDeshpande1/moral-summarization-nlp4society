SUMMARY: Susan Rice, former national security adviser under President Barack Obama, has expressed concerns that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's planned speech in front of a joint session of Congress will damage the US-Israel relationship. According to Rice, the partisan nature of the invitation and acceptance of the speech is "destructive" and undermines the bipartisan tradition of US-Israel relations. The White House has also been critical of Netanyahu's decision to speak before Congress, with President Obama stating that it would be "inappropriate" to meet with him two weeks before Israel's elections.

Critics argue that the speech could strain international talks on Iran's nuclear plans and inject partisanship into an important diplomatic relationship. Several lawmakers have spoken out against the speech, calling it a "tawdry and high-handed stunt." The controversy highlights concerns about the potential consequences of politicizing diplomatic relationships and undermining bipartisan cooperation.

END OF SUMMARY.
