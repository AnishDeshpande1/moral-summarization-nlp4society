SUMMARY:
President Trump denounced the FBI for “destroying” the life of former national‑security adviser Michael Flynn after Flynn pleaded guilty to lying about his contacts with the Russian ambassador, while claiming that Democratic rival Hillary Clinton “lied many times” to the bureau and faced no repercussions. Trump framed the episode as a stark double‑standard, portraying Flynn as a loyal servant unfairly punished and Clinton as a victim of a lenient establishment. He warned that presidents normally stay out of ongoing investigations, but insisted his empathy for Flynn was personal, not political. By juxtaposing Flynn’s guilty plea with Clinton’s cleared email‑server probe, Trump cast the FBI’s actions as partisan and unjust, reinforcing his narrative that the justice system is being weaponized against his allies while protecting his opponents. This moral framing suggests a bias in law‑enforcement that harms loyal conservatives and spares liberal figures, urging scrutiny of the special‑counsel probe’s fairness.  

END OF SUMMARY.
