SUMMARY: President Donald Trump criticized the FBI on Monday, claiming it ruined the life of his former national security adviser Michael Flynn, who pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI about his contacts with the Russian ambassador. Trump expressed sympathy for Flynn, whom he fired in February for misleading Vice President Pence, and contrasted Flynn’s fate with that of Democratic presidential candidate Hillary Clinton, alleging she lied to the FBI “many times” without consequence. Trump did not specify Clinton’s alleged falsehoods; she had voluntarily interviewed with the FBI in July 2016 regarding her use of a private email server, after which FBI Director James Comey recommended no charges. The remarks come as Special Counsel Robert Mueller’s investigation intensifies scrutiny of Trump’s inner circle. Trump’s public comments on an ongoing federal investigation are unusual for a president. END OF SUMMARY.
