SUMMARY: President Trump publicly condemned the FBI for what he described as the ruin of former national security adviser Michael Flynn’s life after Flynn pleaded guilty to lying to the bureau about his contacts with the Russian ambassador. Trump contrasted Flynn’s fate with that of his Democratic rival, Hillary Clinton, whom he claimed “lied many times to the FBI” yet faced no consequences. He expressed personal sympathy for Flynn, noting the former general “led a very strong life,” and framed the FBI’s actions as a moral injustice. The article notes that Clinton’s voluntary interview about her private email server led then‑FBI Director James Comey to recommend no charges. By juxtaposing Flynn’s punishment with Clinton’s lack of repercussions, Trump’s comments carry a moral judgment that the investigative system is unevenly applied, rewarding allies and punishing enemies. END OF SUMMARY.
