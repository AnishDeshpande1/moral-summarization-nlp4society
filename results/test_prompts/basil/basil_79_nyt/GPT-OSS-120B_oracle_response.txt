SUMMARY: President Trump lamented that the FBI “destroyed” the life of former national security adviser Michael Flynn after he pleaded guilty to lying to agents, while noting that Hillary Clinton “lied many times” to the bureau and faced no consequences. Trump said he feels “very badly” for Flynn, whom he fired for lying to the vice president, and urged that officials avoid any appearance of influencing the special‑counsel investigation into Russia’s 2016 election interference. He did not cite specific statements by Clinton, only recalling her voluntary interview during the email‑server probe that led FBI Director James Comey to recommend no charges. The article highlights the contrast between Flynn’s fate—his career destroyed after he lied—and Clinton’s, where nothing “happened” despite alleged falsehoods. END OF SUMMARY.
