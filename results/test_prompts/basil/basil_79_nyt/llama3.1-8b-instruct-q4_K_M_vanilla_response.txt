SUMMARY:
President Trump expressed sympathy for his former national security adviser, Michael T. Flynn, who pleaded guilty to lying to the F.B.I. about conversations with the Russian ambassador. Trump said he felt "very badly" for Flynn and contrasted his treatment with that of Hillary Clinton, whom he claimed had lied to the F.B.I. on multiple occasions but faced no consequences. However, Trump did not specify what Clinton's alleged lies were. The comments come as the special counsel investigation led by Robert S. Mueller III focuses intensely on Trump's inner circle. It is unusual for a president to weigh in on federal investigations, as it can be seen as influencing the outcome.

END OF SUMMARY.
