SUMMARY:
President Trump again framed the Flynn case as a moral injustice, portraying the FBI’s prosecution of former national‑security adviser Michael Flynn as a harmful act that “ruined” his life. By contrast, Trump claimed Democratic rival Hillary Clinton “lied many times” to the FBI yet faced no consequences, presenting a stark fairness gap. The article notes Flynn’s guilty plea for lying about his contacts with the Russian ambassador and his dismissal for deceiving the vice president, while emphasizing Trump’s expressed sympathy and the president’s suggestion that the investigation is punishing Flynn unfairly. The piece underscores the care/harm dimension—Flynn’s alleged victimisation—and the fairness/cheating dimension—Clinton’s alleged deceit paired with a lack of punishment—highlighting the perceived double standard in how the justice system treats the two figures. This moral framing casts the FBI’s actions as both harmful to an individual and unjustly selective.  

END OF SUMMARY.
