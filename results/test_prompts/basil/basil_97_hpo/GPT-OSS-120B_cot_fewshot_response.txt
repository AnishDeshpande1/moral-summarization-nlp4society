**SUMMARY:**
Democrats celebrated a historic, pivotal day as the House passed the For The People Act, a bill they say will make a real difference by taking power from elites, special interests and the 1 percent and giving it back to the American people. Leaders called the vote an opportunity to be on the right side of history and to combat endemic corruption in money‑in‑politics. Republicans offered hardened opposition, branding the legislation a federal power grab that threatens free speech and imposes unnecessary benefits for lazy workers. Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell, the biggest opponent of campaign‑finance reform, signaled the bill will not advance, noting that Republican‑led restrictions would be overturned by the act.
