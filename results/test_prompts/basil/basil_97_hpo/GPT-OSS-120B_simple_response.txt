SUMMARY:  
Democrats celebrated a moral victory on Friday when the House passed the For The People Act (234‑193), a sweeping reform bill designed to wrest power from “elites, special interests and the 1 percent” and return it to ordinary Americans. The legislation seeks to expand voting access, restore rights to ex‑felons, end partisan gerrymandering, create publicly funded House elections, and tighten executive ethics—measures framed as a fight against corruption and inequity. Leaders such as Speaker Nancy Pelosi, Rep. Zoe Lofgren, and the late Rep. John Lewis hailed the vote as a historic, pivotal step on the “right side of history,” urging citizens to act now. The Democratic Democracy Reform Task Force, backed by a coalition of candidates who pledged to prioritize these reforms, crafted the bill despite years in minority. Republicans opposed it as a federal power grab that threatens free speech and benefits Democrats. The Senate is unlikely to act, and President Trump has been warned of a veto, but Democrats plan to push individual provisions and continue the broader push for voting‑rights protections. The House vote is presented as the first moral step in a long‑term battle to cleanse American politics.  
END OF SUMMARY.
