SUMMARY: The Democratic‑led House passed the For the People Act on Friday, 234‑193, fulfilling a top pledge from its 2018 election win. The sweeping reform package would expand voting access, restore voting rights to ex‑felons, create publicly funded House races, increase transparency of campaign money, strengthen executive ethics rules, and end partisan gerrymandering. Democrats framed it as a fight against elite influence and voter suppression, citing broad support from candidates and activists. Republicans opposed the bill, calling it a federal power grab that threatens free speech and benefits Democrats. The Senate, led by Majority Leader Mitch McConnell, is unlikely to consider the measure, and President Trump has been told to veto it. A Senate companion bill is being drafted, and Democrats plan to pursue related reforms such as a Voting Rights Act reauthorization and automatic voter registration. END OF SUMMARY.
