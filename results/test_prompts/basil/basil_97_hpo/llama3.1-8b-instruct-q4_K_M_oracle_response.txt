SUMMARY:

The House of Representatives passed the For The People Act, a landmark campaign finance, voting rights, and ethics reform bill, in a historic vote. This legislation aims to expand voting access, enhance transparency of money in politics, restore voting rights to millions of ex-felons, strengthen executive branch ethics laws, and end partisan gerrymandering. Democrats' top priority since winning control of the House in 2018, the bill seeks to stem the inequality of campaign funding by empowering small donors with matching contributions from public funds. The legislation also aims to change politics in this country by prioritizing the needs of the American people over special interests and elites.

Republicans opposed the bill, claiming it is a federal power grab designed to help Democrats win elections. However, Democrats believe that the reform package is popular and plan to pursue individual pieces of it to keep issues of corruption and voting rights front and center. The bill now faces an uncertain future in the Senate, where Majority Leader Mitch McConnell has signaled opposition.

END OF SUMMARY.
