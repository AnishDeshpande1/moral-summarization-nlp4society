SUMMARY:  
Democrats celebrated a historic House victory as the For The People Act passed 234‑193, a bill that **expands** voting access, **enhances** transparency of money in politics, **restores** voting rights to ex‑felons and **strengthens** executive‑branch ethics rules while aiming to end partisan **gerrymandering**. Supporters framed the legislation as a fight against **attacks** on democracy, promising to **stem** the growing **inequality** of campaign funding by letting small donors **take** power from the **1 percent** and **give** it back to ordinary Americans. The party **prioritized** the reforms, making the agenda **clear** after a long‑term **corruption** narrative. Republicans denounced the measure as a federal **grab**, claiming it **assaults** free‑speech rights, creates unnecessary **benefits**, and would **restrict** election integrity, while promising to **veto** and keep existing **restrictions** that the bill would **overturn**. Senate leader Mitch McConnell signaled the bill will not move forward in the Senate, though a companion measure is being drafted. Democrats say this is only the first step, pledging further **re‑authorization** of voting rights and a broader **change** in American politics.  
END OF SUMMARY.
