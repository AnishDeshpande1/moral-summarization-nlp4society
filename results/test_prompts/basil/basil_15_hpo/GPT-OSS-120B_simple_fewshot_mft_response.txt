SUMMARY:
Newsweek’s newest issue places Republican presidential hopeful Michele Bachmann on its cover with the caption “THE QUEEN OF RAGE,” a label that does not appear in the accompanying profile but suggests a hostile, sensationalized portrayal.  Conservative observers argue the cover is a deliberate act of bias that cheats Bachmann and her supporters of fair treatment, portraying her as “crazy” and mocking the Tea Party’s “radical” stance.  The article notes that this is not an isolated incident; previous covers have similarly singled out conservatives—Mitt Romney as a Broadway character and Sarah Palin in 2009—fueling accusations that the mainstream media routinely unfairly targets right‑wing figures.  By highlighting these patterns, the piece frames Newsweek’s editorial choice as a breach of journalistic fairness, effectively cheating conservatives out of an unbiased representation.  Readers are invited to judge whether the cover crosses the line of acceptable critique.
