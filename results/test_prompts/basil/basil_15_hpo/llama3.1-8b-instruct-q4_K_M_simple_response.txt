SUMMARY:
Newsweek's latest issue features Michele Bachmann on its cover, labeled as the "QUEEN OF RAGE". However, the word "rage" does not appear in the magazine's actual profile of Bachmann, where author Lois Romano critiques the "radical" nature of the Tea Party. Conservative websites are criticizing the cover, calling it biased and suggesting that it portrays Bachmann in a negative light. This is not the first time Newsweek has faced conservative backlash for its covers, having previously drawn criticism for portraits of Mitt Romney and Sarah Palin.

The controversy raises questions about the role of media bias in shaping public perception and whether certain groups are unfairly targeted or portrayed in a negative light. The use of provocative language like "RAGE" on the cover may be seen as sensationalistic or inflammatory by some, while others may see it as a legitimate critique of Bachmann's politics.

END OF SUMMARY.
