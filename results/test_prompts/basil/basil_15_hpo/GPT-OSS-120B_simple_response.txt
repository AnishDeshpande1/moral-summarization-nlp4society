SUMMARY:  
Newsweek’s newest issue spotlights Rep. Michele Bachmann with a provocative cover that labels her “THE QUEEN OF RAGE.” The image—Bachmann staring wide‑eyed at the camera against a stark blue background—does not actually use the word “rage” in the accompanying profile, though writer Lois Romano condemns the “radical” Tea Party she backs. Conservative outlets have decried the cover as an attempt to portray Bachmann as “crazy,” and a blogger questioned whether the mainstream media can honestly claim neutrality toward conservatives. This backlash echoes earlier controversies, such as Newsweek’s 2012 Mitt Romney “Book of Mormon” cover and the infamous 2009 Sarah Palin cover, both of which sparked accusations of left‑wing bias. The piece invites readers to judge whether the magazine has crossed a line in its portrayal of a conservative figure.  

END OF SUMMARY.
