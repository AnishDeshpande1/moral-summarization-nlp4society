SUMMARY:
Newsweek’s newest issue spotlights Republican presidential hopeful Michele Bachmann on its cover, branding her “THE QUEEN OF RAGE” with a wide‑eyed stare against a stark blue backdrop. Although the article by Lois Romano never uses the word “rage,” it denounces the “radical” Tea Party that Bachmann champions. Conservative blogs have already decried the image as sexist and evidence of mainstream media bias, with commentators asking how anyone can claim the press is neutral toward conservatives. The backlash echoes previous controversies, such as the magazine’s 2009 Sarah Palin cover and a 2012 depiction of Mitt Romney as a Broadway character. Critics argue the cover crosses a line by mocking a female candidate, while Newsweek defends its editorial choices. The episode reignites the debate over media fairness and the treatment of women in politics.  
END OF SUMMARY.
