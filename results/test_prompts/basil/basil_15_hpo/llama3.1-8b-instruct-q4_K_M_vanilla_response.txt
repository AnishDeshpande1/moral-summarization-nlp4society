SUMMARY:
Newsweek's latest issue features Michele Bachmann on the cover, titled "THE QUEEN OF RAGE". However, the article itself does not use the word "rage" to describe Bachmann, but rather criticizes the "radical" nature of the Tea Party she supports. Conservative websites are criticizing the cover, saying it makes Bachmann look "crazy" and accusing Newsweek of bias against conservatives. This is not the first time Newsweek has faced backlash for a cover; they previously drew criticism for their Mitt Romney and Sarah Palin covers.

The article notes that while some may see the cover as inflammatory, others may view it as an honest representation of Bachmann's views and demeanor. The controversy surrounding the cover highlights the ongoing debate about media bias and how news outlets portray public figures. Newsweek's decision to use this provocative title has sparked a discussion about the limits of free speech and the responsibility of journalists in presenting their subjects.

END OF SUMMARY.
