SUMMARY:

A bitter feud between Republican presidential candidates Ted Cruz and Donald Trump has escalated, with both men accusing each other of personal attacks on their wives. After the National Enquirer published a story alleging that Cruz had extramarital affairs, Cruz accused Trump of orchestrating the smear campaign and said he would not support Trump if he wins the nomination. Trump's campaign responded by saying the article was "garbage" and a "tabloid smear." The feud began when an anti-Trump super PAC posted ads featuring Melania Trump in her underwear from a British GQ photo shoot, prompting Trump to threaten to "spill the beans" about Cruz's wife Heidi. Cruz called Trump a "coward" for attacking his wife and accused him of being a "classless" politician.

The exchange reflects a growing trend in this year's presidential campaign, where personal attacks and insults have become increasingly common. The debate has raised questions about the limits of acceptable behavior in politics and whether candidates should engage in such tactics to win votes.

END OF SUMMARY.
