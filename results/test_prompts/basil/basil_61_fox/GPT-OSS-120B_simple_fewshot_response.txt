SUMMARY:
Sen. Ted Cruz publicly rebuked Donald Trump after the former president’s campaign launched personal attacks on Cruz’s wife, Heidi, and after a National Enquirer story—blamed on Trump’s “henchmen”—falsely claimed Cruz had extramarital affairs. Cruz declared he would no longer back Trump if the billionaire secures the GOP nomination, emphasizing that he “doesn’t make a habit of supporting people who attack my wife and attack my family.” He denounced the tabloid allegations as “garbage” and “complete and utter lies.” Trump’s spokeswoman Katrina Pierson dismissed the Enquirer story as “stupidity on all levels,” while Trump himself threatened to “spill the beans” on Heidi, calling Cruz a liar and a coward. The feud escalated when an anti‑Trump super PAC ran ads using a nude photo of Melania Trump, prompting Trump to accuse Cruz of exploiting the image. Both sides traded personal insults, breaking the usual campaign norm that spouses are off‑limits. The episode highlights the corrosive moral cost of weaponizing family members in political battles, even as Trump maintains a sizable delegate lead and Cruz fights to stay viable.  
END OF SUMMARY.
