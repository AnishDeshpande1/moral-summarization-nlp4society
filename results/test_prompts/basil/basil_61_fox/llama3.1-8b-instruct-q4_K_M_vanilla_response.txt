SUMMARY:

Sen. Ted Cruz has distanced himself from Donald Trump, citing attacks on his wife Heidi as a reason for backing away from his pledge to support Trump if he wins the GOP nomination. The feud began when an anti-Trump super PAC posted ads featuring Melania Trump in a compromising position, prompting Trump to threaten to "spill the beans" about Cruz's wife. Cruz responded by calling Trump a "coward" and accusing him of attacking his family. Trump has denied any involvement with the National Enquirer story that alleged Cruz had extramarital affairs, which Cruz called a "tabloid smear." The back-and-forth has escalated into personal attacks, with both candidates trading barbs on social media.

Trump's campaign has released statements denying any wrongdoing and calling the allegations "stupidity on all levels." The dispute comes as Trump holds a substantial lead in the delegate chase for the GOP nomination. Cruz may face an uphill battle to catch up, but is vowing to continue his campaign despite the personal attacks.

END OF SUMMARY.
