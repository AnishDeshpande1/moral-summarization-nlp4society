SUMMARY:  
Sen. Ted Cruz publicly rebuked Donald Trump for dragging their families into the GOP primary fight, insisting he will not back a candidate who “attacks my wife and attacks my family.” Cruz denounced the National Enquirer’s false affair story as a “tabloid smear” orchestrated by Trump and his allies, and warned Trump that any further assault on his spouse Heidi would reveal Trump’s own cowardice. Trump’s camp dismissed the tabloid claims as “stupidity,” while Trump himself threatened to “spill the beans” on Cruz’s wife after a super‑PAC ran a provocative ad using a vintage Melania Trump photo. Cruz responded that the ad was not his campaign’s work and called Trump “classless” and a “coward” for targeting women. The feud underscores a moral line the author highlights: personal attacks on spouses and families are unethical and betray the decorum of political contests. Despite Trump’s delegate lead, Cruz’s challenge hinges on keeping the campaign’s tone respectable and refusing to condone attacks on women.  

END OF SUMMARY.
