SUMMARY:
Sen. Ted Cruz publicly denounced Donald Trump after a National Enquirer story—widely dismissed as a fabricated smear—claimed Cruz had multiple affairs. Cruz condemned the tabloid attack as “garbage” and “complete and utter lies,” accusing Trump and his “henchmen” of degrading his family’s honor. He warned that he will not support a candidate who harms his wife or betrays basic decency, stating “Donald Trump is not going to be the Republican nominee.” The feud escalated when a super‑PAC ran a nude‑Melania ad, prompting Trump to threaten to “spill the beans” on Cruz’s wife Heidi, a threat Cruz called cowardly and classless. Both sides invoked loyalty to the party—citing a prior pledge not to run as independents—and framed the exchanges as betrayals of that loyalty. The personal attacks were portrayed as morally degrading, violating norms of respect (Sanctity) and fairness, while the candidates’ defenses highlighted concern for family welfare (Care) and the injustice of false accusations (Fairness). Cruz’s stance underscores a demand for honest, respectful politics and loyalty to party values over personal vilification.
