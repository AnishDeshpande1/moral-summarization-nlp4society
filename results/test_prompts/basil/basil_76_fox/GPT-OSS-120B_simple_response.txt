SUMMARY: President Trump, speaking via Press Secretary Sarah Sanders, said he would hope Roy Moore steps aside if the sexual‑misconduct allegations are true, but cautioned against destroying a life on a decades‑old claim. Moore, a staunch Christian conservative, denied the accusations and attacked the “liberal media” as “forces of evil” while vowing to keep fighting for his faith‑based values. The Washington Post reported that a woman, then 14, said Moore, then a prosecutor, made unwanted sexual advances and touched her inappropriately, though she said no intercourse occurred. Additional women recalled similar unwanted attention. Republican leaders, including McConnell and Gardner, said Moore must withdraw if the allegations prove true, while Moore’s camp called the story “baseless” and “fake news.” Alabama Governor Kay Ivey called the claims “deeply disturbing” but pledged to wait for facts. The moral tension centers on the duty to protect vulnerable youths and uphold truth versus the principle of due process and not condemning a man without proven guilt. END OF SUMMARY.
