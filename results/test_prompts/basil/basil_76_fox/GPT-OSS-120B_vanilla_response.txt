SUMMARY: President Trump said he expects Alabama Senate candidate Roy Moore to step aside if the sexual‑misconduct allegations against him are true, while also urging caution against rushing to judgment. White House Press Secretary Sarah Sanders echoed this stance, noting the president does not want an allegation to destroy a life but that Moore should resign if the claims are verified. The Washington Post reported that Leigh Corfman, now 53, alleged Moore, then 32 and an assistant district attorney, made sexual advances toward her when she was 14, including kissing, partial undressing, and touching her undergarments, though she says intercourse did not occur. The story also cited three other women who said Moore pursued them as teenagers. The allegations have prompted calls from GOP senators, including Mitch McConnell and John McCain, for Moore to withdraw from the Dec. 12 special Senate race against Democrat Doug Jones. Moore’s campaign dismissed the report as “baseless” and “fake news.” Alabama Governor Kay Ivey called the claims “deeply disturbing” and said she will await the facts. END OF SUMMARY.
