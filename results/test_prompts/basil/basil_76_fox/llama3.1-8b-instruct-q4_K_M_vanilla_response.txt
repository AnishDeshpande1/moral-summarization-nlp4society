SUMMARY:

President Trump's White House stated that if allegations of sexual misconduct against Alabama Senate candidate Roy Moore are true, he will do the right thing and step aside. Moore has denied the claims, calling them "baseless" and part of a "liberal media lapdog" conspiracy. The Washington Post reported on a woman's claim that Moore initiated a sexual encounter with her when she was 14 in 1979. Three other women also came forward claiming Moore pursued them as teenagers. Republican senators are calling for Moore to drop out if the allegations are true, including Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell and Sen. Cory Gardner.

Moore's campaign claims the report is "fake news" and that he will not give up the fight. The Post notes that even if the allegations are true, they may not be prosecutable due to the statute of limitations having passed. Alabama Governor Kay Ivey responded by saying the allegations are "deeply disturbing" but she will hold judgment until the facts come out.

END OF SUMMARY.
