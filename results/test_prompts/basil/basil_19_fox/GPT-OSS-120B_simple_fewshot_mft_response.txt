SUMMARY:
Donald Trump announced he is withdrawing as moderator of the Dec. 27 Newsmax Republican debate, saying he must avoid a “conflict of interest” in case he chooses to run as an independent. By stepping aside, Trump frames his move as a safeguard for the party’s authority to select the “right” candidate to defeat the “failed” Obama administration, while preserving his own subversive option to challenge the party’s establishment. He cited RNC Chair Reince Priebus’ concerns and emphasized that he will keep his options open, still planning to endorse the eventual nominee despite threatening a third‑party bid. Trump praised Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum for their “courage, conviction, and confidence” in attending, and warned that if Republicans pick the “wrong” candidate he will not relinquish his right to run independently. His withdrawal underscores a tension between party hierarchy and his willingness to subvert it to protect his own political ambitions.  

END OF SUMMARY.
