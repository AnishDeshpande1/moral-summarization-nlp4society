SUMMARY:

Donald Trump announced that he is withdrawing as moderator for a Republican presidential debate on December 27, citing concerns about potential conflicts of interest if he decides to run as an independent candidate next year. Trump had previously flirted with a possible Republican bid before shelving the idea, and some candidates expressed concern that he would not rule out running as a third-party candidate. Trump said he wants to keep his options open in case the Republicans choose the wrong candidate or the economy remains bad. He also stated that he plans to endorse a Republican candidate despite his threatened independent run. The debate was sponsored by Newsmax, but it's unclear if it will proceed without Trump. Trump expressed confidence that the debate would have received high ratings with him as moderator.

END OF SUMMARY.
