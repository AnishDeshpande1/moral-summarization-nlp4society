SUMMARY:

Donald Trump announced he will not moderate a Republican presidential debate due to concerns about conflict of interest if he decides to run as a third-party candidate next year. He stated that his decision is meant to keep his options open, including an independent bid, which he claims is necessary in case the Republicans choose the "wrong" candidate and the economy remains bad. Trump also expressed his desire to ensure the right Republican candidate is chosen to defeat President Obama's administration, implying that one of the current candidates may not be up to the task.

Trump praised Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum for agreeing to attend the debate, while other Republican candidates declined due to concerns about Trump's potential third-party run. The Newsmax debate was initially sponsored by Newsmax but became heavily associated with Trump after he was named moderator. With Trump out of the picture, it is unclear whether the debate will still take place.

END OF SUMMARY.
