SUMMARY: Former President Donald Trump announced he will step down as moderator of the Newsmax-sponsored Republican presidential debate scheduled for Dec. 27, citing the need to avoid a “conflict of interest” should he decide to run as a third‑party candidate. Trump said his withdrawal keeps his options open amid concerns that the GOP might nominate the “wrong” candidate and a worsening economy. He reiterated he is still weighing an independent bid and will endorse the Republican nominee he deems suitable. The decision follows several GOP hopefuls pulling out of the debate, leaving only Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum remaining as participants. Trump referenced RNC Chair Reince Priebus’ worries about his involvement and expressed confidence that the debate would have drawn high ratings. He praised Gingrich and Santorum for their willingness to attend. END OF SUMMARY.
