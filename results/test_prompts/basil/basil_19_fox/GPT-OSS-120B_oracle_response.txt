SUMMARY:
Donald Trump announced Tuesday that he is withdrawing as moderator of the upcoming Newsmax‑sponsored Republican debate after most candidates chose not to attend. He framed the move as a way to avoid any “conflict of interest” and to keep his options open in case he decides to run as an independent and help ensure the “right” Republican candidate is chosen to defeat what he called the failed and destructive Obama administration. Trump told Fox News he is still looking at an independent bid, warning that if Republicans pick the wrong candidate and the economy continues to slide, he may step in. He praised Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum for having the courage, conviction, and confidence to attend, and said he will still endorse the eventual nominee while preserving his right to run. Citing RNC Chair Reince Priebus’ concerns, Trump said he must leave all options open because, above all else, “we must make America great again.” END OF SUMMARY.
