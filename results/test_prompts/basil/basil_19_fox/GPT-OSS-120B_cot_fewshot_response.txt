SUMMARY:
Donald Trump announced he is withdrawing as moderator of the Dec. 27 Newsmax Republican debate after most candidates declined to attend. Citing a potential “conflict” of interest, he said he must keep his options “open” in case the “right” Republican candidate is not chosen and the economy turns “bad.” Trump framed his exit as a move to ensure the “right” candidate defeats the “failed” and “destructive” Obama administration, while still pledging to endorse the eventual nominee. He praised Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum for their “courage, conviction and confidence,” and reiterated his trademark “confidence” that the debate would have drawn great ratings. By stepping aside, Trump says he can preserve his ability to run as an independent and continue to work toward making America “great” again. END OF SUMMARY.
