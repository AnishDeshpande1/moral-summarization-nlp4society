SUMMARY:
Donald Trump announced he is withdrawing as moderator of the Dec. 27 Newsmax Republican debate, saying he must avoid a “conflict of interest” in case he decides to run as an independent candidate. He framed the move as keeping his options open to endorse the “right” Republican while still weighing a third‑party bid, claiming the GOP might pick the “wrong” nominee and the economy will stay weak. After Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum confirmed attendance, the rest of the field bowed out, partly over worries that Trump could still launch a third‑party run. Trump cited RNC Chair Reince Priebus’ concerns and expressed confidence the debate would have drawn huge ratings. He praised Gingrich and Santorum for their “courage, conviction, and confidence,” but ultimately left the stage to preserve his own political ambitions. The episode highlights how Trump prioritizes personal strategic calculations over the public’s need for a robust, inclusive primary debate, raising questions about his commitment to the democratic process and the principle of putting the nation’s interests before his own.  
END OF SUMMARY.
