SUMMARY: Donald Trump announced he is withdrawing as moderator of the upcoming Newsmax‑sponsored Republican debate, citing a desire to avoid a “conflict of interest” should he decide to run as a third‑party candidate. He framed the move as a principled effort to keep his options open and ensure the “right” Republican wins, warning that a poor nominee and a faltering economy could force him to run independently. Trump praised the few candidates who stayed (Gingrich and Santorum) for their “courage, conviction, and confidence,” while criticizing the others for bowing out over concerns that he might not rule out a third‑party bid. He invoked the legacy of the “failed and very destructive Obama administration” to justify his stance, insisting that preserving the chance to run as an independent is essential to “make America great again.” The moral framing portrays Trump as putting personal ambition above party unity, yet presenting his withdrawal as a self‑sacrificial act to protect the GOP’s future. END OF SUMMARY.
