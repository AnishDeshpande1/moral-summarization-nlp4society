SUMMARY:

The special counsel, Robert S. Mueller III, disputed a report by BuzzFeed News that President Trump directed his lawyer, Michael D. Cohen, to lie to Congress about his role in negotiations to build a skyscraper in Moscow. The report alleged that Trump pressured Cohen to commit perjury, which would be a damning revelation in the investigation into Russia's attempts to sabotage the 2016 presidential election. If true, it could be grounds for initiating impeachment proceedings against Trump. The White House and Trump's lawyers vigorously denied the report, calling it "categorically false." However, Cohen did correct his false statements about the Moscow tower to prosecutors working for Mueller, and provided "useful information" about his contacts with "persons connected to the White House" in 2017 and 2018. The report highlights concerns about Trump's involvement in the project and his attempts to conceal the details from senior administration officials. The incident adds to a chaotic week for the White House, which has faced questions about recent revelations that FBI counterintelligence agents began investigating Trump in 2017.

END OF SUMMARY.
