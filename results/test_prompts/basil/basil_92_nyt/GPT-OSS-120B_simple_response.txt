SUMMARY:  
A heated dispute erupted over a BuzzFeed News report that claimed President Trump ordered his fixer Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about the Trump Tower Moscow deal. The special counsel’s office, via spokesman Peter Carr, bluntly denied the article’s accuracy, marking a rare public challenge to a media story. Democrats in Congress seized on the allegation, warning that if true it would amount to subornation of perjury and obstruction of justice—crimes that could trigger impeachment or resignation. The White House and Trump’s lawyers dismissed the claim as “categorically false.”  

The moral stakes are clear: if the president indeed pressured a witness to commit perjury, it would represent a grave abuse of power, undermining the rule of law and the integrity of democratic institutions. Conversely, the special counsel’s denial and the media’s insistence on its sources highlight the tension between accountability and the need for factual certainty. The episode underscores a broader ethical battle over truth‑telling, responsibility, and the limits of presidential authority.  

END OF SUMMARY.
