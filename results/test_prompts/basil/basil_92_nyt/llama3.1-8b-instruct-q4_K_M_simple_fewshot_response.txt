SUMMARY:
The special counsel investigating Russian interference in the 2016 election disputed a report that President Trump directed Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about his role in negotiations to build a skyscraper in Moscow. The rare public statement by Robert Mueller's office challenged the facts of an article published by BuzzFeed News, which reported that Cohen told prosecutors he was pressured by the president before his congressional testimony. The White House and Trump's lawyers denied the report even before the special counsel's office weighed in. If true, the allegations could be grounds for initiating impeachment proceedings against Trump. Representative Adam Schiff said the allegations "would constitute both the subornation of perjury as well as obstruction of justice." The report adds to a chaotic week for the White House, which has had to fend off questions about recent revelations that F.B.I. counterintelligence agents began investigating Trump in 2017.

END OF SUMMARY.
