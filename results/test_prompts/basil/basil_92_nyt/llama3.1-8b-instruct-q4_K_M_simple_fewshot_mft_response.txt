SUMMARY:

The special counsel investigating Russian interference in the 2016 election disputed a report that President Trump directed Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about his role in negotiations to build a skyscraper in Moscow. The rare public statement by the special counsel's spokesman challenged the facts of an article published by BuzzFeed News, saying that their description of specific statements and characterization of documents and testimony were "not accurate". If true, the allegations could be grounds for initiating impeachment proceedings against Trump. Senior members of Congress have called for investigations, with some even suggesting that Trump must resign or be impeached if the allegations are proven. The White House and lawyers for Trump have vigorously denied the BuzzFeed report, but the special counsel's statement has added another jolt to a chaotic week for the White House.

The dispute highlights concerns about presidential authority and accountability, as well as issues of fairness and honesty in government. The special counsel's statement suggests that Trump may be trying to subvert the truth and undermine the investigation, while the BuzzFeed report raises questions about the president's involvement in potential perjury and obstruction of justice.

END OF SUMMARY.
