SUMMARY:
A rare public statement from Special Counsel Robert Mueller’s office disputed BuzzFeed News’ claim that President Trump ordered his lawyer Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about the Trump Tower Moscow deal. The spokesperson said BuzzFeed’s description of statements and documents was “not accurate.” While the White House and Trump’s lawyers called the story “categorically false,” Democrats in Congress warned that, if true, the allegation would amount to subornation of perjury and obstruction of justice, prompting calls for impeachment or resignation. The article notes that Cohen’s prior false testimony was part of a broader pattern of deception, though he later corrected himself and cooperated with investigators. The dispute underscores the ongoing political battle over the president’s alleged attempts to pressure witnesses, highlighting concerns about abuse of power and threats to democratic accountability.

END OF SUMMARY.
