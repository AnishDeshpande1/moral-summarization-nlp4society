**SUMMARY:**  
The special counsel’s office publicly disputed BuzzFeed News’ claim that President Trump pressured Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about the Trump Tower Moscow deal, calling the report “not accurate.” The allegation—that the president directed Cohen to commit perjury—would be a **damning** piece of evidence of **obstruction** and **subornation** of perjury, potentially triggering **impeachment** or a call for the president to **resign**. Democrats on the House Intelligence Committee, including Rep. Adam Schiff and Rep. Joaquin Castro, said the story, if true, would constitute serious **obstruction of justice**. The White House and Trump’s lawyers labeled the BuzzFeed story **categorically false**. The controversy revives earlier findings that Cohen repeatedly **lied** to Congress and that his false testimony was driven by **greed** and **deceptive** loyalty, a pattern prosecutors described as a “path of **darkness** over **light**.” Barr, testifying before his Attorney‑General nomination, affirmed that coaching a witness to lie would be a crime under obstruction statutes.
