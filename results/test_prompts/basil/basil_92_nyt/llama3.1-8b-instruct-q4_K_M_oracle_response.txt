SUMMARY:

The special counsel investigating Russian interference in the 2016 election disputed a report that President Trump directed his lawyer, Michael Cohen, to lie to Congress about negotiations for a Moscow skyscraper. The report, published by BuzzFeed News, alleged that Trump pressured Cohen to commit perjury, which could be grounds for impeachment proceedings. However, the special counsel's office challenged the accuracy of the report, saying it was "categorically false." If true, the allegations would constitute obstruction of justice and subornation of perjury. Senior members of Congress, including Representative Adam Schiff, called for an investigation into the matter, citing concerns about Trump's attempt to conceal details from senior administration officials about his interactions with Russian President Vladimir Putin.

The report added to a chaotic week for the White House, which has had to fend off questions about recent revelations that F.B.I. counterintelligence agents began investigating Trump in 2017 and that he has tried to conceal the details of his interactions with Putin. The investigation into Trump's conduct continues, with the special counsel working to uncover evidence of obstruction of justice.

END OF SUMMARY.
