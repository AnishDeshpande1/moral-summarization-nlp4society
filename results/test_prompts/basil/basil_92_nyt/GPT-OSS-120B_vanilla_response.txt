SUMMARY: The special counsel’s office, via spokesman Peter Carr, publicly disputed a BuzzFeed News report that claimed President Trump ordered lawyer Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about the Trump Tower Moscow deal. Carr said BuzzFeed’s description of statements and documents was “not accurate.” The BuzzFeed story had sparked calls from Democrats in Congress for impeachment, with Rep. Adam Schiff and Rep. Joaquin Castro warning that the allegations, if true, would constitute subornation of perjury and obstruction of justice. The White House and Trump’s lawyers called the report “categorically false.” BuzzFeed’s editor Ben Smith stood by the reporting and urged clarification from the special counsel. The dispute comes amid ongoing investigations into Russian interference, Cohen’s guilty plea for lying to Congress and campaign‑finance crimes, and a pending Senate Intelligence Committee review. Prosecutors have previously detailed Cohen’s false testimony about the Moscow negotiations, which continued into 2016, and his later cooperation with investigators. The controversy adds pressure on the Trump administration as it faces scrutiny over potential obstruction of justice.  

END OF SUMMARY.
