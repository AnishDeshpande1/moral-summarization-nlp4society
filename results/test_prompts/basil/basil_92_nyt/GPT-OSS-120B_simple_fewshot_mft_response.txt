SUMMARY:
A rare public statement from the Mueller special‑counsel’s office disputed BuzzFeed’s claim that President Trump ordered Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about the Moscow tower deal, challenging the story’s factual basis. The White House and Trump’s lawyers slammed the report as “categorically false,” while BuzzFeed stood by its sources and urged clarification. Democrats on the House and Senate Intelligence Committees warned that, if true, the allegation would amount to subornation of perjury and obstruction of justice—serious breaches of fairness and an abuse of presidential authority. Representative Adam Schiff said the panel would pursue further testimony, and Rep. Joaquin Castro warned that a true story would require Trump’s resignation or impeachment. The dispute underscores a clash between the president’s attempts to shield his power and the demand for honest, lawful conduct, framing the episode as a contest between subversive misuse of authority and the fair application of justice.  

END OF SUMMARY.
