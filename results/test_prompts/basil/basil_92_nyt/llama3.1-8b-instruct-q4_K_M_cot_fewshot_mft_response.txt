SUMMARY:
The special counsel investigating Russian interference in the 2016 election disputed a report that President Trump had directed Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about his role in negotiations to build a skyscraper in Moscow. The special counsel's office challenged the facts of the article, saying that BuzzFeed's description of specific statements and characterization of documents and testimony were "not accurate." If true, the allegations would constitute both the subornation of perjury as well as obstruction of justice, which could be grounds for initiating impeachment proceedings against Mr. Trump. The president himself responded on Twitter, calling the special counsel's statement a "great day for our Country," but the controversy has sparked a debate about journalism and credibility. The investigation continues to uncover new evidence, including the fact that Cohen initially lied to prosecutors working for Mueller after he pleaded guilty in August to campaign finance violations and financial crimes.
