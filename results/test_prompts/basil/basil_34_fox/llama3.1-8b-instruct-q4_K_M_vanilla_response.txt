SUMMARY:

The National Rifle Association (NRA) has released a report calling for states to allow more school personnel to carry weapons on school grounds, provided they undergo extensive training. The report, led by former Arkansas Rep. Asa Hutchinson, recommends creating a 40-60 hour training program for armed officers and proposing a "model" state law allowing certain personnel to carry guns in schools. While the NRA initially faced criticism for focusing on school security rather than gun control measures, the report also suggests conducting safety assessments and assessing mental health as a pre-indicator of violence. The proposal comes as Congress and some states consider gun control legislation, but the NRA argues that improving school security is crucial.

The report's lead author, Hutchinson, emphasized that this plan does not involve arming all teachers, but rather allowing certain personnel to carry guns in schools after receiving specialized training. The NRA's CEO, Wayne LaPierre, has argued that armed officers are necessary to protect students and that "the only thing that stops a bad guy with a gun is a good guy with a gun."

END OF SUMMARY.
