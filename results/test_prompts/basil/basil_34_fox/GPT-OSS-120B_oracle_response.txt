SUMMARY:
The National Rifle Association urged state legislatures to allow more armed officers to **carry** weapons on school grounds after extensive training, framing the proposal as essential **protection** against future **shooting** tragedies. NRA CEO Wayne LaPierre and former Arkansas Rep. Asa Hutchinson argued that “the only thing that stops a bad guy with a gun is a good guy with a gun,” insisting that schools need **improving** security measures. Hutchinson’s 225‑page report called for a 40‑60‑hour training program and a “model” state law permitting certain personnel—not teachers, who should **teach**—to be armed. The report also recommended safety assessments and a pilot program to use mental‑health indicators to prevent **killing**. While Democrats have **rejected** the NRA’s focus and pushed for **measures** such as **bans**, **strengthening** background checks, and **restrict**ing assault‑weapon access, some states have **approved** and **enacted** legislation allowing armed teachers. The debate continues as Connecticut moves forward with a sweeping gun‑control package, but the NRA maintains that **protecting** schools through armed officers is the only way to **stop** future tragedies.  
END OF SUMMARY.
