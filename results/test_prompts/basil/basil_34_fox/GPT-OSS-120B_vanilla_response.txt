SUMMARY: The National Rifle Association (NRA) urged state legislatures to permit more school personnel to carry weapons after extensive training, concluding a weeks‑long review sparked by the Newtown mass shooting. NRA CEO Wayne LaPierre previously called for armed officers in every school, while former Arkansas Rep. Asa Hutchinson, who led the review, softened the stance, saying each school should have at least one armed officer but decisions should be locally determined. Hutchinson proposed a 40‑60‑hour training program and a model state law allowing trained staff—excluding teachers—to carry guns on campus. The 225‑page report also recommends mandatory school safety assessments and a pilot program to use mental‑health indicators for violence prevention. The NRA’s recommendations come as Congress and states debate gun‑control measures, including assault‑weapon bans and stronger background checks. Connecticut is moving forward with a comprehensive gun‑control package, while South Dakota has already allowed armed teachers. LaPierre reiterated that school security must match protections in banks and Congress, asserting that “the only thing that stops a bad guy with a gun is a good guy with a gun.”  
END OF SUMMARY.
