SUMMARY:
The National Rifle Association urged state legislatures to let more school personnel carry firearms after extensive training, presenting a 225‑page report that caps its review of the Newtown tragedy. NRA CEO Wayne LaPierre and former Arkansas Rep. Asa Hutchinson argued that every school should have at least one armed officer, though decisions should be made locally and teachers should focus on teaching. The proposal includes a 40‑60‑hour training program and a “model” state law permitting qualified staff to be armed, as well as safety assessments and a pilot mental‑health screening program. The NRA’s stance frames armed protection as the moral duty to safeguard children, echoing LaPierre’s claim that “the only thing that stops a bad guy with a gun is a good guy with a gun.” The report received praise from Newtown victim’s father Mark Mattioli and comes as many states debate broader gun‑control measures, highlighting the NRA’s belief that responsible armed security is the ethical solution to prevent future school shootings.  
END OF SUMMARY.
