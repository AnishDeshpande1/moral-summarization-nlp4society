SUMMARY:
The National Rifle Association (NRA) is urging state legislatures to enact laws that would let specially trained school personnel carry firearms on campus, framing the proposal as an exercise of legitimate authority to safeguard children. NRA CEO Wayne LaPierre and former Rep. Asa Hutchinson argue that empowering “good guys with guns” restores order and counters the subversive threat of armed attackers, while emphasizing that the training program—40 to 60 hours—ensures responsible use of force. The NRA’s 225‑page review, released after the Newtown tragedy, calls for each school to have at least one armed officer, a safety assessment, and a pilot mental‑health initiative to detect warning signs, positioning these steps as caring measures to protect students from harm. The plan deliberately excludes teachers from carrying weapons, reinforcing the authority of trained security staff while preserving teachers’ instructional role. Support from Newtown victim’s father Mark Mattioli underscores the moral appeal to protect children, even as Congress and states debate broader gun‑control reforms. By presenting armed protection as both an authoritative response to lawlessness and a compassionate duty to prevent future violence, the NRA frames its recommendations as a necessary, caring assertion of order in schools.  
END OF SUMMARY.
