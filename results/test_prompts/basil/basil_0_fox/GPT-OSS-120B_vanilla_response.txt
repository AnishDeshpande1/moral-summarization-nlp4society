SUMMARY: Fox News host Glenn Beck closed the Conservative Political Action Conference (CPAC) with a keynote that blamed “progressivism” for America’s troubles, using his signature chalkboard to emphasize the point. While audience members shouted “President Obama,” Beck argued the issue was more complex and rejected expanding the Republican Party to include progressives. He framed America as an “idea that sets people free” and shared personal reflections on life’s unfairness. The speech capped three days of panels featuring potential 2012 Republican presidential candidates, but many attendees came primarily for Beck, who is viewed as a leading voice in the new conservative movement. Beck also highlighted his 9/12 Project—nine values and twelve principles he says define the nation—which inspired a large Washington march earlier in September. Attendee Cynthia Burgiss, a 22‑year‑old university student, praised Beck as relatable and credited him for mobilizing diverse crowds. END OF SUMMARY.
