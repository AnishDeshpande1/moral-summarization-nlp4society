SUMMARY:
Glenn Beck delivered the keynote speech at the Conservative Political Action Conference (CPAC), blaming "progressivism" for America's problems. He used his trademark chalkboard to emphasize his point, writing "Progressivism" on it and explaining that it's not just President Obama who is responsible, but rather a broader ideology. Beck rejected the idea of expanding the Republican Party to include progressives, saying "America is not a clown show." He also opened up about his troubled past, stating that life is not fair and sometimes the bad guy wins.

Beck's speech was met with enthusiasm from the crowd, who saw him as a leader in the new conservative movement. His popularity has coincided with the rise of the Tea Party Movement and populist rage directed at Washington. He also launched the 9/12 Project, which sets forth nine values and 12 principles that he believes America was founded upon.

Attendees praised Beck for being relatable and an "everyday guy" who inspired a major march in Washington in September. The speech capped off three days of panel discussions and high-profile speakers at CPAC.

END OF SUMMARY.
