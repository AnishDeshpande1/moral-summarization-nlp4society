SUMMARY: Fox News host Glenn Beck closed CPAC with a fiery keynote, wielding his familiar chalkboard to blame “progressivism” for America’s ills. He dismissed any attempt by the Republican Party to broaden its tent to include progressives, calling such a move a “circus” and insisting that America is an idea that “sets people free.” Beck mixed political critique with personal confession, reminding the audience that “life is not fair” and “the bad guy sometimes wins.” His speech reflected the surge of Tea Party‑style populism and introduced his 9/12 Project—nine values and twelve principles he claims define the nation’s founding—recently showcased in a large Washington march. Attendees, like 22‑year‑old Cynthia Burgiss, hailed Beck as a relatable, everyday figure and credited him with energizing the new conservative movement. The moral framing presents progressivism as a moral threat to American freedom, while portraying Beck’s message as a righteous call to return to the nation’s true principles. END OF SUMMARY.
