SUMMARY:
Fox News host Glenn Beck delivered the keynote speech at the Conservative Political Action Conference (CPAC), blaming "progressivism" for America's problems. He used his trademark chalkboard to emphasize that progressivism is the underlying issue, rather than President Obama or any specific individual. Beck rejected the idea of expanding the Republican Party to include progressives, stating that America is an idea that sets people free, not a circus. He also shared his personal story of overcoming adversity, emphasizing that life is not always fair and that good often does not prevail.
Beck's speech was met with enthusiasm from the crowd, who see him as a leader in the new conservative movement. His 9/12 Project, which promotes nine values and 12 principles, inspired a major march in Washington last September. Attendees praised Beck for being a relatable and everyday guy who is making a difference.
END OF SUMMARY.
