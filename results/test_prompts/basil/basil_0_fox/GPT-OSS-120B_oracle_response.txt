SUMMARY:  
Fox News host Glenn Beck closed CP AC with his trademark chalkboard, writing “Progressivism” to argue that the ideology is harming a nation that should be **free**. He dismissed calls to broaden the Republican tent, likening such a move to a circus, and reminded the crowd that “life is not **fair**; the **bad guy** sometimes **wins**.” Beck’s speech, which highlighted his new 9/12 Project and its call for the country’s founding values, resonated with attendees who saw him as a relatable, everyday voice for the emerging conservative movement. Many, like 22‑year‑old Cynthia Burgess, attended both the 9/12 march and CP AC because they believed Beck could help bring people of all races, religions, and political affiliations **together** under a vision of America that truly **sets people free**.  
END OF SUMMARY.
