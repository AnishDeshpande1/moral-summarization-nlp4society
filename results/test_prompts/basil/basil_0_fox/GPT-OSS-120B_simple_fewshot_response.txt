SUMMARY:
Fox News host Glenn Beck closed CPAC with a fiery keynote that framed “progressivism” as the root of America’s ills and warned Republicans against diluting their message by inviting left‑leaning elements into the party. Using his signature chalkboard, Beck declared that the nation’s problems are “not that simple” and dismissed the idea of a “big‑tent” approach, likening a mixed‑ideology party to a circus. He appealed to the audience’s sense of moral duty, urging them to protect the founding “idea that sets people free” and to reject the unfairness of a world where “the bad guy sometimes wins.” Beck’s speech, steeped in personal testimony of struggle and redemption, positioned him as a leading voice of the new conservative movement and the 9/12 Project, which promotes nine core values and twelve founding principles. Attendees, such as university student Cynthia Burgiss, praised Beck as a relatable, everyday figure whose activism they credit with galvanizing cross‑demographic support for a purist, freedom‑focused America.  
END OF SUMMARY.
