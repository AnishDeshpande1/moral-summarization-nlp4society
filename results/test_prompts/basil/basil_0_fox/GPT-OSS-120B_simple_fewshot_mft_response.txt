SUMMARY:
Fox News host Glenn Beck closed CPAC with a fiery keynote that framed progressivism as a cheating force undermining America’s fairness. Using his trademark chalkboard, Beck blamed “progressivism” for the nation’s ills, portraying it as an unjust agenda that steals opportunities from ordinary citizens. He rejected calls for a broader Republican tent, insisting that true conservatives must remain loyal to the party’s core principles and reject any alliance with progressive ideas. Beck’s personal story of hardship underscored his message that life is unfair when “the bad guy sometimes wins,” reinforcing the need for a loyal, united front against what he depicted as a deceitful, unfair political movement. Attendees, many drawn to Beck as a relatable leader of the new conservative wave, praised his call for steadfast loyalty to the GOP and his condemnation of progressive “cheating” that threatens America’s founding values.  
END OF SUMMARY.
