SUMMARY:
Glenn Beck delivered the keynote speech at CPAC, blaming "progressivism" for America's problems. He emphasized that America is not a clown show, but an idea that sets people free, and that it's not about expanding the Republican Party to include progressives. Beck shared his personal story of overcoming adversity, stating that life is not fair, and sometimes the bad guy wins. However, he emphasized the importance of working together for a common goal. The 9/12 Project, which Beck founded, aims to restore the country's founding values and principles, promoting unity among people from diverse backgrounds.

Beck's speech resonated with the audience, who saw him as a leader in the new conservative movement. His popularity has coincided with the rise of the Tea Party Movement and populist rage against Washington. The 9/12 Project inspired a major march in Washington this year, where people came together for different reasons but shared a common goal. Beck's message emphasizes the importance of freedom, fairness, and unity.

END OF SUMMARY.
