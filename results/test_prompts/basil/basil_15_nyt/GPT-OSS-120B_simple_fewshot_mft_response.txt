SUMMARY:
Newsweek’s latest cover depicts Republican presidential hopeful Michele Bachmann with exaggerated, wide‑eyed intensity and the caption “The Queen of Rage,” prompting a wave of criticism that the magazine is harming her reputation through a biased, disrespectful portrayal. Conservative bloggers denounced the image as deliberately demeaning, while the National Organization for Women called it sexist, arguing that such a caricature would never be applied to a man—an unfair double standard that harms the subject’s dignity. Newsweek attempted to defend itself by releasing additional photos showing Bachmann in similarly intense poses, but the backlash highlights concerns that the outlet is cheating readers of a fair, balanced representation and inflicting moral injury on a political figure. The episode follows previous controversial covers, reinforcing the view that the media’s sensationalist choices can cause real damage and violate principles of fairness and care. END OF SUMMARY.
