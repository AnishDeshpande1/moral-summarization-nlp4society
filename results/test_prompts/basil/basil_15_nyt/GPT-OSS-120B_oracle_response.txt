SUMMARY:  
Newsweek’s cover featuring Rep. Michele Bachmann with a wide‑eyed stare and the caption “The Queen of Rage” sparked accusations that the magazine **puts** a conservative woman in a **crazy**, **sexist**, and **disrespectful** light. Conservative bloggers called the image **biased** and complained it **done** to a woman in a way that would never be **done** to a man. Even the National Organization for Women’s president called it **sexist**, invoking Gloria Steinem’s test about whether such treatment would ever be applied to a man. Newsweek defended the choice by releasing an “outtakes” gallery showing Bachmann in similar poses and noting the cover accompanied a profile by Lois Romano on her Tea Party role. The controversy echoes earlier Newsweek covers that were deemed **creepy** or **sexist**, such as the 2009 Sarah Palin running‑shorts photo and a computer‑generated image of Princess Diana with the Duchess of Cambridge.  

END OF SUMMARY.
