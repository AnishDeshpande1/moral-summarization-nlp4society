SUMMARY:

The latest Newsweek magazine cover features Representative Michele Bachmann, labeled as the "Queen of Rage," which many conservatives have deemed "disrespectful" and "biased" against their views. The photo shows Bachmann with an intense gaze, sparking outrage among some in the conservative blogosphere who claim it makes her look "crazy." Some unexpected critics, including Terry O'Neill from the National Organization for Women, argue that the cover is also "sexist," pointing out that a man would not be depicted in such a way. Newsweek responded to the backlash by posting an "outtakes" gallery on its website, showcasing other photos of Bachmann with similar intensity, but no outrage seems to have been sparked by the article itself. This incident joins a list of controversies surrounding Newsweek's cover choices, including a 2009 photo of Sarah Palin that was deemed "sexist" and a computer-generated image of Princess Diana that some found "creepy." The magazine's editors seem to be courting controversy with their bold and often provocative covers.

END OF SUMMARY.
