SUMMARY:  
Newsweek’s latest cover features Republican presidential hopeful Michele Bachmann with a wide‑eyed, almost manic stare and the caption “The Queen of Rage.” Conservatives blasted the image as a deliberate smear, accusing the magazine of anti‑conservative bias, while feminist groups called it sexist, arguing such a demeaning portrayal would never be used on a man. The magazine defended the choice by releasing additional photos that show Bachmann in similarly intense poses, and noted the cover accompanies a profile of her role in the Tea Party. This controversy echoes earlier Newsweek incidents—most notably a 2009 Palin cover deemed sexist and a recent, criticized CGI collage of the Duchess of Cambridge with Princess Diana—highlighting ongoing concerns about media sensationalism and gendered double standards.  

END OF SUMMARY.
