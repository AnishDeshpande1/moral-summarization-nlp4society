SUMMARY:

Newsweek's recent cover featuring Representative Michele Bachmann, dubbed "The Queen of Rage," has sparked controversy among conservatives and some feminists alike. Critics argue that the image is sexist and implies that Bachmann is unstable or unhinged. Newsweek responded to the backlash by posting an outtakes gallery on its website, showcasing other photos taken for the article, which also feature Bachmann with a wide-eyed intensity. The move has been seen as an attempt to downplay the controversy.

Feminist groups have come to Bachmann's defense, arguing that the image would not be used if she were a man and is therefore sexist. This incident follows other controversies surrounding Newsweek's cover choices, including a photo of Sarah Palin in running shorts and a computer-generated image of Princess Diana's ghost. The debate raises questions about media bias and the objectification of women in politics.

END OF SUMMARY.
