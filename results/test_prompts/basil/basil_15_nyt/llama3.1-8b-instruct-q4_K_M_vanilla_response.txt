SUMMARY:

Newsweek's latest cover features Representative Michele Bachmann, a Republican presidential candidate and Tea Party favorite, with the caption "The Queen of Rage". Many conservatives have criticized the photo, calling it biased and sexist. Some bloggers on conservative websites, such as News Busters and Freedom's Lighthouse, accused Newsweek of trying to make Bachmann look crazy or unstable. Even some unlikely allies, including Terry O'Neill from the National Organization for Women, joined in criticizing the cover, saying it was sexist because it would never be done to a man.

In response to the controversy, Newsweek posted an "outtakes" gallery on its Daily Beast website, showing other photos of Bachmann taken during the same shoot. The article accompanying the cover, written by Lois Romano, seems to have sparked less outrage than the photo itself. This is not the first time Newsweek has faced criticism over its cover choices, having previously been accused of sexism in 2009 and of being insensitive with a computer-generated image of Princess Diana's ghost.

END OF SUMMARY.
