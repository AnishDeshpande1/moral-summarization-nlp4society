SUMMARY:
Newsweek’s latest cover features Republican presidential hopeful Michele Bachmann with a wide‑eyed stare and the caption “The Queen of Rage,” prompting a wave of criticism that the magazine is portraying her as irrational and sexist. Conservative bloggers denounced the image as biased against conservatives, while the National Organization for Women called it sexist, asking if a man would ever be depicted the same way. Newsweek defended the choice by releasing additional photos showing Bachmann in similar poses, but the backlash revived concerns about media outlets using sensationalized visuals that damage reputations and reinforce gender stereotypes. The episode echoes previous controversies over Newsweek’s covers of Sarah Palin and a CGI Princess Diana, underscoring a recurring moral lesson: the press has a duty to present public figures fairly and without demeaning caricature, respecting both political balance and gender equity. END OF SUMMARY.
