SUMMARY:

Washington Governor Jay Inslee has banned state-funded travel to Indiana due to its new Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which critics argue could allow businesses to discriminate against gay and lesbian individuals for religious reasons. Inslee joins other states and cities, including Connecticut, San Francisco, and Seattle, in opposing the law. Business leaders such as Apple's Tim Cook and Salesforce's Marc Benioff have also spoken out against the measure, with some canceling events in Indiana. The executives of nine Indiana-based companies have sent a letter to Governor Mike Pence expressing their concerns about the impact on their employees and the state's reputation. Despite Pence's claims that the law has been "grossly misconstrued," critics argue it is a license for discrimination against certain groups. State lawmakers are seeking to clarify the language to address these concerns, but many view the law as morally repugnant and discriminatory.
