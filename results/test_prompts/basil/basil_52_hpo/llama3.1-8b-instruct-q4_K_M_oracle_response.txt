SUMMARY:

Washington Gov. Jay Inslee has imposed an administration-wide ban on state-funded travel to Indiana, citing the disturbing new law that could allow businesses to deny service to gay and lesbian individuals for religious reasons. This move is part of a growing trend of opposition to the Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which has sparked national backlash over concerns it provides justification for discrimination against LGBT individuals. Inslee emphasized Washington's commitment to equality, stating "We in Washington stand for equality" and inviting companies that promote equality and opportunity to visit and do business in the state. Other states and cities, including Connecticut, San Francisco, and Seattle, have also imposed bans on travel to Indiana. Business leaders, such as Apple's Tim Cook and Salesforce's Marc Benioff, have spoken out against the law, calling it "dangerous." The governor of Indiana has defended his decision to sign the bill, but legislative leaders are seeking to clarify that the law does not protect discrimination.

END OF SUMMARY.
