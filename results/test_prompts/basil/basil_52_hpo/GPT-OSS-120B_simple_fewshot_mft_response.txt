SUMMARY:
Washington Governor Jay Inslee announced a ban on state‑funded travel to Indiana, condemning the state’s new Religious Freedom Restoration Act as a threat to civil‑rights protections and a potential source of harm to LGBT people. Citing a recent case in which a Washington florist was fined for refusing service to a gay couple, Inslee framed the ban as a defense of care for vulnerable citizens and a stand for fairness against a law he views as subverting equal treatment. Connecticut Governor Dan Malloy, several city leaders and business figures—including Tim Cook and Marc Benioff—joined the protest, labeling the Indiana measure as “dangerous” and a possible license to discriminate. Indiana Governor Mike Pence defended the legislation, claiming it merely mirrors federal law and does not intend to permit discrimination, invoking the Golden Rule as moral guidance. State Senate President Pro Tem David Long pledged to clarify the statute to ensure it cannot be used to cheat or harm anyone. The coordinated backlash underscores a moral clash over authority, the protection of vulnerable groups, and the demand for equitable treatment.

END OF SUMMARY.
