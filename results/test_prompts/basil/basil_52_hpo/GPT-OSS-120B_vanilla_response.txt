SUMMARY: Washington Governor Jay Inslee announced a ban on state‑funded travel to Indiana in protest of the state’s new Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which critics say could permit businesses to refuse service to LGBT individuals on religious grounds. Inslee joined other states and cities—including Connecticut, San Francisco, Seattle, and the Twin Cities—in condemning the law, citing recent discrimination cases such as a fined Washington florist. Business leaders like Apple’s Tim Cook and Salesforce’s Marc Benioff have also spoken out, with several Indiana companies urging Governor Mike Pence to address concerns. Pence defended the law, claiming it mirrors a 1993 federal statute and does not target sexual orientation, while Indiana legislators promised to clarify that the measure does not authorize discrimination. The controversy has sparked a broader national backlash against the bill. END OF SUMMARY.
