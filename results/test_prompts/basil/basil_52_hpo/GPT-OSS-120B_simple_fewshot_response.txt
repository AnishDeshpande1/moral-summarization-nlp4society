SUMMARY:
Washington Governor Jay Inslee announced a statewide ban on government‑funded travel to Indiana, condemning the state’s new Religious Freedom Restoration Act as “disturbing” and a threat to civil‑rights progress for LGBT people. Inslee cited a recent case of a Washington florist fined for refusing service to a gay couple and urged businesses that value equality to choose Washington instead of Indiana. Connecticut Governor Dan Malloy, San Francisco and Seattle mayors, and the Twin Cities’ mayors have taken similar actions, while tech leaders such as Apple’s Tim Cook and Salesforce’s Marc Benioff have publicly denounced the law. Indiana executives from companies like Angie's List and Anthem wrote to Governor Mike Pence expressing concern over the law’s impact on employees and the state’s reputation. Pence defended the measure, claiming it is mischaracterized and mirrors the 1993 federal law signed by President Clinton, while Republican Senate President Pro Tem David Long promised a swift clarification to ensure the law does not permit discrimination. The backlash underscores a broad, bipartisan moral stance that discrimination has no place in modern America.  
END OF SUMMARY.
