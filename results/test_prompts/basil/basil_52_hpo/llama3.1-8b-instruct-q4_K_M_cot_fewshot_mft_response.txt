Washington Gov. Jay Inslee announced a ban on state-funded travel to Indiana due to the new Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which critics argue could allow businesses to discriminate against gay and lesbian individuals for religious reasons. Inslee called the law "disturbing" and said it goes against the values of embracing civil rights for everyone. He cited the case of a Washington-based florist who was recently ordered to pay a fine for discriminating against a gay couple, emphasizing that "we in Washington stand for equality." Many business leaders have spoken out against the law, including Apple's Tim Cook and Salesforce's Marc Benioff, who have canceled events or expressed concerns about its impact on employees. The executives of nine Indiana-based companies also sent a letter to Gov. Mike Pence outlining their worries. Pence has defended his decision, but the governor and legislative leaders say they will seek to clarify that the law does not protect discrimination. END OF SUMMARY.
