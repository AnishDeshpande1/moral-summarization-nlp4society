SUMMARY: Washington Governor Jay Inslee condemned Indiana’s newly‑passed Religious Freedom Restoration Act as “disturbing” because it could permit businesses to refuse service to gay and lesbian people on religious grounds. Inslee announced a statewide ban on government‑funded travel to Indiana, urging companies that value equality to choose Washington instead. Connecticut Governor Dan Malloy, the cities of San Francisco and Seattle, and Minneapolis‑St. Paul joined the protest, also restricting public travel to Indiana. Business leaders—including Apple’s Tim Cook and Salesforce’s Marc Benioff—publicly denounced the law; nine Indiana‑based firms wrote to Governor Mike Pence, warning that the measure harms employees and the state’s reputation. Pence defended the bill, claiming it has been mischaracterized and does not mention sexual orientation, but Indiana Senate President Pro Tem David Long pledged to amend the law to prevent discrimination. The backlash underscores a broader moral argument that civil‑rights protections for LGBTQ people must not be undermined by religious‑freedom statutes.  

END OF SUMMARY.
