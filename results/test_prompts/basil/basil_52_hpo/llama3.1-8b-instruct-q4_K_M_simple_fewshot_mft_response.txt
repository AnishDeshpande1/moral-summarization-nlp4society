SUMMARY:

Washington Governor Jay Inslee announced a ban on state-funded travel to Indiana, citing concerns that the state's new Religious Freedom Restoration Act could lead to discrimination against gay and lesbian individuals. Inslee joins other states and cities in opposing the law, which critics argue provides legal justification for such discrimination. The law has sparked national backlash, with business leaders and politicians speaking out against it. Apple CEO Tim Cook called the law "dangerous," while Salesforce's Marc Benioff canceled events in Indiana. Nine Indiana-based companies also sent a letter to Governor Mike Pence expressing their concerns. Pence defended the law, claiming it was "grossly misconstrued" as a license to discriminate, but acknowledged that he and legislative leaders would seek to clarify that the law does not protect discrimination. The controversy highlights the tension between authority and subversion, with some arguing that the law undermines equal rights for all individuals. Others see it as an attempt to undermine fairness and promote harm towards marginalized communities.

END OF SUMMARY.
