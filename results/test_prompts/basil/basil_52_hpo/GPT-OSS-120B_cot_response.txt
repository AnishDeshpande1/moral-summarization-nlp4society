**SUMMARY:**  
Washington Gov. Jay Inslee condemned Indiana’s new Religious Freedom Restoration Act as “disturbing” and a threat to civil rights, announcing a ban on state‑funded travel to the state. He framed Washington’s stance around equality and openness, inviting businesses that value opportunity and the “Golden Rule” to visit instead. Connecticut’s Gov. Dan Malloy, San Francisco, Seattle, and the Twin Cities also imposed travel bans, joining business leaders who called the law dangerous and a “license to discriminate.” Apple’s Tim Cook and Salesforce’s Marc Benioff publicly denounced the measure, while Indiana executives warned of reputational damage. Gov. Mike Pence defended the bill, saying it is not intended to permit discrimination and that he “abhor discrimination,” but legislative leaders pledged to clarify the law to prevent any misuse. The backlash reflects a broader national debate over religious freedom versus LGBTQ + rights.
