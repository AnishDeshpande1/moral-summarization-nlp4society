SUMMARY: Washington Gov. Jay Inslee announced that his administration will **impose** a **ban** on state‑funded travel to Indiana, calling the state’s new Religious Freedom Restoration Act **disturbing** and a **dangerous** **justification** for **discrimination** against gay and lesbian people. Inslee said Washington **stands** for **equality**, **abhor**s **discriminating** actions, and will **open** its doors to businesses that **promote** inclusion. Connecticut Gov. Dan Malloy took a similar **opposing** stance, and cities such as San Francisco, Seattle, Minneapolis and St. Paul have also **imposed** travel **bans**. Business leaders, including Tim Cook and Marc Benioff, have **spoken** out, with some **refusing** to hold events in Indiana. Indiana Gov. Mike Pence defended the **law**, claiming it is **misconstrued** and does not **allow** a **license** to **discriminate**, saying he would **veto** any bill that **legalized** discrimination. State Senate President Pro Tem David Long said a **fix** will come soon to ensure the **law** does not **permit** **discrimination**. Executives from Indiana firms have warned of the **impact** on employees and the state’s reputation.  

END OF SUMMARY.
