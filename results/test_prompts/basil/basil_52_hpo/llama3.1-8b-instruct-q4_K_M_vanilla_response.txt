SUMMARY:

Washington Governor Jay Inslee announced a ban on state-funded travel to Indiana due to the state's new Religious Freedom Restoration Act, which critics say allows businesses to discriminate against gay and lesbian individuals for religious reasons. Connecticut Governor Dan Malloy also took a similar stand against the law. San Francisco and Seattle have imposed bans on city-funded travel to Indiana, and several business leaders, including Apple's Tim Cook and Salesforce's Marc Benioff, have spoken out against the law. The executives of nine Indiana-based companies sent a letter to Governor Mike Pence expressing their concerns about the law's impact on employees and the state's reputation.

Governor Pence has defended his decision to sign the bill, claiming it has been "grossly misconstrued." However, he and legislative leaders say they will seek to clarify that the law does not protect discrimination. The mayors of Minneapolis and St. Paul have condemned the measure, and several states and cities are joining in opposition.

END OF SUMMARY.
