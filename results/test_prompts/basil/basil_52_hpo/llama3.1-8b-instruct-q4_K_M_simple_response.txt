SUMMARY:

Washington Gov. Jay Inslee has banned state-funded travel to Indiana due to the state's new law allowing businesses to deny service to gay and lesbian individuals for religious reasons. Inslee called the law "disturbing" and said Washington stands for equality, inviting companies that oppose the law to visit his state instead. Other states and cities, including Connecticut, San Francisco, and Seattle, have also imposed bans on travel to Indiana. Business leaders such as Apple's Tim Cook and Salesforce's Marc Benioff have spoken out against the law, citing concerns about its impact on employees and the state's reputation. Indiana Gov. Mike Pence has defended the law, claiming it has been "grossly misconstrued," but legislative leaders say they will seek to clarify that the law does not protect discrimination.

The controversy highlights the ongoing debate over civil rights and equality in the US, with many arguing that laws like Indiana's threaten to roll back progress made towards LGBTQ+ rights. Inslee and other governors are standing firm against the law, demonstrating their commitment to promoting equality and inclusivity in their states.

END OF SUMMARY.
