SUMMARY:
Governor Rick Perry has released a multimillion‑dollar TV ad aimed at hard‑right conservatives, framing his campaign as a defense of traditional authority and religious sanctity against what he calls “Obama’s war on religion.” Perry asserts his Christian identity and argues that allowing gays to serve openly in the military subverts sacred American values, while denying public prayer and Christmas celebrations harms children’s moral upbringing. He pledges to overturn liberal policies, portraying the Obama administration as a corrupting force that degrades the nation’s religious heritage. The ad was condemned by the Log Cabin Republicans, who argue that Perry’s appeal to authority and sanctity unjustly discriminates against gay people, violating fairness and the principle of equal liberty. Their executive director warned that Perry seeks to become a “theocrat‑in‑chief,” using religious authority to cheat LGBTQ Americans out of equal treatment and to inflict moral harm on a vulnerable community. The controversy highlights a clash between claims of protecting sacred tradition and accusations of unfair, harmful subversion of civil rights.  
END OF SUMMARY.
