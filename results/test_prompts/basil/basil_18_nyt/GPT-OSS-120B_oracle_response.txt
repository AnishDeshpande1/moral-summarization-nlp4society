SUMMARY:  
In a new television ad aimed at hard‑right conservatives, Texas Governor Rick Perry contrasts Christianity with homosexuality and vows to end “Obama’s war on religion.” He admits he is a Christian and claims that while gays can serve openly in the military, children cannot celebrate Christmas or pray in school. Perry promises to fight liberal attacks on the nation’s religious heritage, arguing that faith made America strong and can make it strong again. The ad, funded by a multimillion‑dollar budget focused on evangelical voters in Iowa, follows Perry’s criticism of the Obama administration’s plan to use diplomacy and foreign aid to promote gay rights abroad. The Log Cabin Republicans denounced the ad, saying Perry’s stance denies that a person of faith can support  equality, and warned that the nation was built on liberty and individual responsibility, not theocracy.  

END OF SUMMARY.
