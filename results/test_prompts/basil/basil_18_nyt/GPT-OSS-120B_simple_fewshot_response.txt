SUMMARY:
Texas Governor Rick Perry has released a new TV ad aimed at hard‑right conservatives, portraying a clash between Christianity and homosexuality and pledging to halt what he calls “Obama’s war on religion.” Perry declares his pride in being a Christian and argues that the nation is wrong when gays serve openly in the military while children cannot openly celebrate Christmas or pray in school. The ad, targeting evangelical voters in Iowa, also attacks the Obama administration’s plan to use diplomacy and foreign aid to promote gay rights worldwide. Perry promises to fight “liberal attacks on our religious heritage,” asserting that faith made America strong and can do so again. The Log Cabin Republicans condemned the spot, saying a person of faith can still support equality and warning that Perry is campaigning to be “commander‑in‑chief, not theocrat‑in‑chief.” They emphasized that individual liberty and responsibility, including open service by gay service members, align with the nation’s founding principles.  
END OF SUMMARY.
