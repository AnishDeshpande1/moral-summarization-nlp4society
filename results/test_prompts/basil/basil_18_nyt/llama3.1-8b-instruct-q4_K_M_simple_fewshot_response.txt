SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry has launched a new campaign ad targeting hard-right conservatives, contrasting Christianity with homosexuality and vowing to end what he calls “Obama’s war on religion.” In the ad, Perry criticizes President Obama's decision to allow openly gay soldiers in the military, stating that this policy is an attack on Christian values. He also promises to fight against liberal attacks on religious heritage, claiming that faith made America strong and can do so again. The ad has been denounced by the Log Cabin Republicans, a gay rights group, who argue that Perry's message is exclusionary and at odds with American founding principles of individual liberty and responsibility.

The ad reflects Perry's efforts to appeal to Christian evangelicals in Iowa, where large numbers of Republican caucus-goers identify as such. The campaign has been criticized for its divisive rhetoric, with some arguing that it reinforces a false narrative about the Obama administration waging war on religion. The ad's message is likely to resonate with hard-right conservatives, but may alienate more moderate voters.
