SUMMARY:

Gov. Rick Perry of Texas has launched a new campaign ad that appeals to hard-right conservatives by contrasting Christianity with homosexuality. Perry vows to end what he calls “Obama’s war on religion” and highlights his criticism of overturning the “don’t ask, don’t tell” policy for gays serving in the military. He claims that faith made America strong and can do so again. The ad has been denounced by the Log Cabin Republicans, who argue that Perry is assuming being a person of faith does not afford one to support equality. They also point out that open service by gay and lesbian servicemembers is directly in line with the vision of our founding fathers. Perry's campaign has focused on Iowa, where large numbers of Republican caucusgoers describe themselves as Christian evangelicals. By framing his message in this way, Perry is attempting to tap into a sense of moral outrage among conservative voters.

END OF SUMMARY.
