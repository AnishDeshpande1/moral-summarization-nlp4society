SUMMARY: Texas Governor Rick Perry has released a new campaign ad targeting hard‑right conservatives, juxtaposing Christianity with homosexuality and pledging to end “Obama’s war on religion.” In the ad, Perry declares his Christian faith and criticizes policies that allow gays to serve openly in the military while restricting public Christmas celebrations and school prayer. The ad, part of a multimillion‑dollar TV push focused on Iowa’s evangelical voters, follows Perry’s recent condemnation of the Obama administration’s plan to use diplomacy and aid to promote gay rights abroad. Perry promises to protect “our religious heritage” and restore America’s strength through faith. The Log Cabin Republicans condemned the ad, stating that faith does not preclude support for equality and warning that Perry is positioning himself as a “theocrat‑in‑chief” rather than a commander‑in‑chief. END OF SUMMARY.
