SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry has released a new campaign ad targeting hard-right conservatives, which contrasts Christianity with homosexuality and vows to end what he calls “Obama’s war on religion.” The ad criticizes the Obama administration's decision to promote gay rights globally and allows gays to serve openly in the military. Perry claims that his kids can't celebrate Christmas or pray in school due to liberal attacks on religious heritage. He promises to fight against these "liberal attacks" as president.

The Log Cabin Republicans, a group of gay men and lesbian Republicans, have denounced the ad, calling it "wrong" for Perry to assume being a person of faith means opposing equality. They argue that open service by gay and lesbian servicemembers aligns with the vision of the founding fathers. The ad is part of Perry's multimillion-dollar television budget focused on Iowa, where many Republican caucusgoers are Christian evangelicals.

END OF SUMMARY.
