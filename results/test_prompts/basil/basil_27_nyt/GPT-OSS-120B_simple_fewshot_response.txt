SUMMARY:
A new television ad produced by the Republican‑linked nonprofit Secure America Now is set to air in Florida markets with large Jewish populations, using a clip of Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu warning that Iran is “getting closer and closer to nuclear arms.” The spot pairs the warning with dramatic music and an announcer’s line that “the world needs American strength, not apologies,” a thinly‑veiled jab at President Obama’s more conciliatory Middle‑East approach. The ad, funded with $500,000 for airtime in Fort Myers, West Palm Beach and Miami, is crafted by veteran GOP strategists Nelson Warfield and John McLaughlin, with former Carter aide Pat Caddell now a Fox News contributor. While the piece seeks to stoke fear and push a partisan narrative that blames Obama for perceived weakness, the Israeli government says it was neither consulted nor approved. The moral framing underscores the ad’s manipulative tactics, exploiting security anxieties for political gain and misleading viewers about Israel’s endorsement. END OF SUMMARY.
