SUMMARY:
A new advertisement by Secure America Now, a group run by Republican strategists, aims to stoke anxiety over American policies in the Middle East, particularly in areas of Florida with large Jewish populations. The ad features Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu warning of the risks of a nuclear Iran, emphasizing that "every day that passes, Iran gets closer and closer to nuclear arms." The ad's dramatic soundtrack and unseen announcer conclude that "the world needs American strength, not apologies," a not-so-veiled swipe at President Obama's foreign policy, which has been criticized by Republicans, including Mitt Romney, as apologetic towards volatile countries like Iran. The Israeli government was not consulted on the ad and did not approve it. The ad has reserved $500,000 of airtime in Fort Myers, West Palm Beach, and Miami. END OF SUMMARY.
