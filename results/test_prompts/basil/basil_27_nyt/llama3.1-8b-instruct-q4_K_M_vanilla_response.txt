SUMMARY:

A new advertisement will run in areas of Florida with large Jewish populations, targeting American policies in the Middle East. The ad features a clip of Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu warning about the risks of a nuclear Iran. A soundtrack plays as Netanyahu says "the world tells Israel to wait" and an announcer concludes that "the world needs American strength, not apologies." This is seen as a swipe at President Obama's foreign policy, which Republicans have criticized as apologetic towards volatile countries like Iran.

The ad was produced by Secure America Now, a group run by two Republican strategists and Pat Caddell, a former aide to Jimmy Carter who now works for Fox News. The group has reserved $500,000 of airtime in Fort Myers, West Palm Beach, and Miami. However, the Israeli government says they were not consulted on the ad and did not approve it.

END OF SUMMARY.
