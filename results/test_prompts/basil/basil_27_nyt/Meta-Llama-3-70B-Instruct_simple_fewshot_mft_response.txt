SUMMARY:

A new advertisement by Secure America Now, a group run by Republican strategists, is stoking anxiety over American policies in the Middle East, particularly with regards to Iran's nuclear program. The ad features Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu warning of the risks of a nuclear Iran, with a dramatic soundtrack and a narrator concluding that "the world needs American strength, not apologies." This is a clear swipe at President Obama's foreign policy, which has been criticized by Republicans as apologetic. The ad is airing in areas of Florida with large Jewish populations, with a budget of $500,000. However, a senior Israeli official has stated that the government was not consulted on the ad and did not approve it. The ad's message is one of authority and strength, implying that the Obama administration is weak and ineffective in its dealings with Iran. This framing undermines the authority of the current administration and creates a sense of harm and vulnerability. The ad's use of Netanyahu's words also raises questions about the subversion of the US political process by foreign interests.

END OF SUMMARY.
