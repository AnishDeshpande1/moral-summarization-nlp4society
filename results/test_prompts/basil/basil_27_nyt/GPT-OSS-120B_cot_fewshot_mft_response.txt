**SUMMARY:**  
A new Secure America Now advertisement targeting Florida’s large Jewish communities dramatizes Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s warning that “every day that passes, Iran gets closer and closer to nuclear arms,” pairing the clip with tense, “Homeland‑style” music. The spot amplifies anxiety about a volatile, nuclear Iran and frames the issue as a matter of American strength versus Obama’s “apologetic” foreign‑policy approach, a clear swipe that critics label as subversive to established authority. Produced by longtime Republican strategists Nelson Warfield and John McLaughlin, with former Carter aide Pat Caddell, the ad reserves $500,000 for airtime in Fort Myers, West Palm Beach and Miami. A senior Israeli official said the Israeli government was not consulted and did not approve the message, underscoring concerns about care for accurate representation and the potential harm of politicizing security threats. The ad thus pits authority against subversion and invokes care/harm language to rally support for a tougher U.S. stance in the Middle East.
