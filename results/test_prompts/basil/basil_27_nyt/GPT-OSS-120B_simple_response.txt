SUMMARY:  
A new political ad targeting Florida’s large Jewish communities exploits fears about Iran’s nuclear ambitions by replaying a clip of Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu warning that “every day that passes, Iran gets closer and closer to nuclear arms.” Set to dramatic “Homeland‑style” music, the spot frames the issue as an urgent security crisis and ends with the line, “The world needs American strength, not apologies,” a thinly‑veiled jab at President Obama’s more diplomatic approach to volatile states like Iran. Produced by the right‑leaning group Secure America Now—run by veteran Republican strategists and a former Carter aide now on Fox News—the ad purchases $500,000 of airtime in Fort Myers, West Palm Beach and Miami. While the ad seeks to rally support for a hard‑line stance, an Israeli official confirmed the Israeli government was neither consulted nor approved the message. The piece underscores how Middle‑East policy is weaponized in U.S. presidential politics, using fear‑inducing tactics to pressure voters toward a more confrontational foreign‑policy agenda.  

END OF SUMMARY.
