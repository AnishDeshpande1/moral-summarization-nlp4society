SUMMARY:  
A new political ad targeting Florida’s large Jewish communities **attempts** to **stoke** fear about U.S. policy toward the Middle East by replaying a clip of Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu warning that Iran is getting **closer** to nuclear weapons. The spot pairs Netanyahu’s urgent plea—“The world tells Israel, ‘Wait. There’s still time.’ And I say wait for what? Wait until when?”—with dramatic music reminiscent of “Homeland,” and ends with an unseen voice declaring, “The world needs American strength, not **apologies**.” The message is a thinly‑veiled criticism of President Obama’s approach, echoing Republican complaints that his foreign‑policy stance is overly conciliatory toward volatile regimes like Iran. The ad was produced by Secure America Now, a group run by veteran Republican strategists and a former Carter aide now on Fox News, which has bought $500,000 of airtime in Fort Myers, West Palm Beach and Miami. An Israeli official said the Israeli government was not consulted and did not approve the advertisement.  
END OF SUMMARY.
