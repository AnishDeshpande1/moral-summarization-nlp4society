SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump is close to selecting an interim FBI director, with former Sen. Joe Lieberman as his top choice. However, the nomination has sparked severe reservation among Democrats, who argue that a politician in charge of the FBI would undermine the agency's independence and confidence in its integrity. They claim it would be contrary to the FBI's mission to have a non-law enforcement official leading the agency. Sen. Claire McCaskill called it a "mistake" to select an elected official for the post, while Sen. Brian Schatz said it was shocking and contrary to everything the FBI is about.

The concerns are fueled by allegations that Trump interfered with the investigation into his administration's ties to Russia. The nomination of Lieberman, who has supported an independent commission to investigate these ties, would only exacerbate these issues. Republicans, on the other hand, have praised Lieberman and expressed confidence in his ability to get confirmed.

The need for a new FBI director arose after Trump fired James Comey last week, amid concerns that he may have interfered with the investigation. The selection of Lieberman as interim director would be a highly controversial move that could undermine public trust in the agency.

END OF SUMMARY.
