SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump is close to selecting an interim candidate to replace former FBI Director James Comey, with former Sen. Joe Lieberman as his top choice. However, the selection of Lieberman has sparked concerns among Democrats about undermining the independence and credibility of the FBI. Critics argue that a politician, especially one with no federal law enforcement experience, should not be in charge of the agency. They point out that selecting an elected official would create a perception of partisanship and undermine public trust in the agency's ability to investigate without influence.

In contrast, Republicans praise Lieberman's qualifications and express confidence that he would receive widespread support from the Senate. The selection process has been contentious, with Democrats warning that it would be a "mistake" and could even lead to a split vote in the chamber. Trump's decision to choose an interim candidate amid controversy over his own handling of the Russia investigation raises concerns about his commitment to preserving the integrity of the agency.

END OF SUMMARY.
