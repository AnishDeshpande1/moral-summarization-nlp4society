SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump announced he is “very close” to naming former Democratic‑turned‑independent Senator Joe Lieberman as an interim FBI director, despite Lieberman’s lack of law‑enforcement experience and his recent political ties. The move has sparked sharp condemnation from Democrats, who argue that appointing a politician undermines the FBI’s independence and erodes public confidence in the agency. Senators Claire McCaskill, Brian Schatz, Dick Durbin and others called the choice a “mistake” and “shocking,” warning it would likely face bipartisan opposition in the Senate. By contrast, several Republicans praised Lieberman, with Marco Rubio, John Cornyn and John McCain expressing admiration and confidence in his confirmation prospects. The controversy intensifies after Trump’s recent firing of former FBI Director James Comey amid the ongoing Russia investigation, raising concerns that the president is seeking to place a politically loyal figure at the helm of the nation’s premier law‑enforcement agency to shield himself from scrutiny.

END OF SUMMARY.
