SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump is close to selecting an interim FBI director, with former Sen. Joe Lieberman as his top choice. Lieberman, a centrist Democrat with no federal law enforcement experience, has sparked severe reservation among Democrats, who argue that a politician should not head the FBI. They claim that Lieberman's background in politics undermines the independence of the agency and that there are many more qualified candidates. Republicans, on the other hand, have praised Lieberman, with some predicting that he could get 100 confirmation votes. The need for a new FBI director arose after Trump fired James Comey, amid an ongoing investigation into the Trump campaign's ties to Russia. The controversy surrounding Lieberman's potential nomination highlights the concerns about Trump's interference with the investigation and the need for an independent and impartial FBI director.

END OF SUMMARY.
