SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump is close to selecting an interim FBI director, with former Sen. Joe Lieberman as his top choice. However, Democrats are reacting with severe reservation, calling it a "mistake" to select an elected official to the post. Sen. Claire McCaskill argued that the FBI director should be a law enforcement official, not a politician. Sen. Brian Schatz deemed the possible nomination "shocking," stating that it would "undermine" the public's confidence in the agency's independence. A top Senate Democratic aide concurred, saying it would be a "terrible" choice. Republicans, on the other hand, praised Lieberman, with Sen. John McCain saying he would be "overjoyed" if Lieberman were picked. The need for a new FBI director arose after Trump fired James Comey, a move that has raised concerns about Trump's interference with the investigation into his campaign's ties to Russia. The selection of a politician like Lieberman would be "contrary" to the FBI's purpose and could further erode trust in the agency.

END OF SUMMARY.
