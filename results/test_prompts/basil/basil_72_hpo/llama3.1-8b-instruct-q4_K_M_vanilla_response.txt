SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump is close to selecting an interim candidate to replace former FBI Director James Comey. His top choice is former Senator Joe Lieberman, who has no federal law enforcement experience but has been a centrist Democrat and supported an independent commission to investigate ties between Trump's administration and Russia. Democrats are strongly opposing the nomination, calling it a "mistake" and "shocking," citing concerns that a politician would undermine the independence of the agency. Senate Democratic aide said there couldn't possibly be a worse time to appoint a politician to lead the FBI.

Republicans, on the other hand, have praised Lieberman's experience and suggested he could get 100 confirmation votes. Trump met with four candidates, including current acting FBI Director Andrew McCabe and former governor Frank Keating. The need for a new director arose after Trump fired Comey last week amid an ongoing investigation into his campaign's ties to Russia.

END OF SUMMARY.
