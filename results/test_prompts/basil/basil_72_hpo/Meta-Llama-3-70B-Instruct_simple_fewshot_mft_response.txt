SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump is close to selecting an interim FBI director, with former Sen. Joe Lieberman as his top choice. Lieberman's lack of federal law enforcement experience and political background have sparked severe reservation among Democrats, who argue that the FBI director should be a law enforcement official, not a politician. This decision raises concerns about the integrity of the FBI and the potential for political interference in the agency's investigations. The choice of Lieberman, a centrist Democrat, also undermines the independence of the agency and erodes public confidence. In contrast, Republicans have praised Lieberman, with some even speculating that he could get 100 confirmation votes. The need for a new FBI director comes after Trump's controversial firing of James Comey, which has heightened concerns about Trump's interference with the investigation into his campaign's ties to Russia. This development has sparked outrage and calls for an independent commission to investigate these ties.

END OF SUMMARY.
