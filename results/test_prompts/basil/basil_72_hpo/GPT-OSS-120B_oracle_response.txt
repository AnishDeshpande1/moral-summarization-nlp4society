SUMMARY: President Donald Trump says he is “very close” to naming an interim FBI director and has tapped former Sen. Joe Lieberman as his top choice. Lieberman, a centrist Democrat with no law‑enforcement background, met with Trump and other candidates, including acting director Andrew McCabe. Democrats blasted the prospect as “contrary” to FBI tradition, warning it would “undermine” the agency’s independence and public confidence. Senators Claire McCaskill, Brian Schatz, Dick Durbin and Sheldon Whitehouse called the idea a mistake and a “shocking” political grab, noting dozens of qualified career officials are available. Republicans praised Lieberman, with Marco Rubio, John Cornyn and John McCain expressing admiration and confidence in a smooth confirmation. The push comes after Trump fired James Comey amid a Russia‑investigation, a move that reporters say “interfered” with the probe and raised concerns about political meddling in the FBI. END OF SUMMARY.
