SUMMARY: President Donald Trump said he is “very close” to naming an interim FBI director, with former Senator Joe Lieberman as his leading candidate. Trump met Lieberman alongside acting director Andrew McCabe, former Oklahoma governor Frank Keating, and former FBI executive Richard McFeely. Lieberman, a centrist Democrat with no law‑enforcement background, would be an unconventional pick; he has previously supported an independent Russia probe. Democrats reacted strongly against the idea, calling it a mistake to appoint a politician, warning it would undermine FBI independence and likely face Senate opposition. Senators Claire McCaskill, Brian Schatz, Dick Durbin, and Sheldon Whitehouse publicly criticized the choice. Republicans praised Lieberman, with Marco Rubio, John Cornyn, and the late John McCain expressing support. The nomination comes after Trump fired former FBI Director James Comey amid the ongoing Russia investigation and reports that Trump asked Comey to halt the probe into Michael Flynn. END OF SUMMARY.
