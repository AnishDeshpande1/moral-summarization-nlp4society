SUMMARY: New Jersey Governor Chris Christie announced he will enter the 2016 Republican presidential race, making the declaration from his hometown of Livingston at 11 a.m. Despite being an early establishment favorite, Christie now faces low poll numbers, a fundraising shortfall, and waning support from major donors, many of whom are shifting to rivals such as Jeb Bush. Critics cite scandals and declining popularity in New Jersey as obstacles. Nonetheless, New York Times columnist Maggie Haberman argues that Christie’s combative, charismatic style—particularly his town‑hall performances—could make his personality a key asset in the campaign. END OF SUMMARY.
