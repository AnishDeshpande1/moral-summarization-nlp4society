SUMMARY:
New Jersey Governor Chris Christie is expected to announce his candidacy for the White House in 2016, despite facing an uphill climb for the Republican nomination. Christie's popularity has plummeted, and he faces a significant fundraising deficit. However, some argue that his "brash, challenging" personality could be his main asset in the campaign. According to Maggie Haberman of The New York Times, Christie's ability to connect with voters through town hall meetings could set him apart from other candidates.

Christie's status as the establishment favorite has vanished due to scandals and plummeting popularity in his home state. However, he still has a chance to succeed, especially if he can leverage his personality to appeal to voters. As Haberman notes, Christie's ability to connect with people is genuine, and some even find him relatable.

END OF SUMMARY.
