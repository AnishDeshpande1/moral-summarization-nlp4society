SUMMARY:
New Jersey Governor Chris Christie announced today that he will enter the 2016 Republican presidential race, launching his campaign from his hometown of Livingston.  Despite sagging poll numbers, a fundraising shortfall, and a loss of support from establishment donors after a series of scandals that have damaged his reputation in his own state, Christie is portrayed as a resilient figure who refuses to be written off.  Commentators note that his appeal rests on a distinctly personal brand—his brash, combative style and his knack for town‑hall confrontations that elicit strong emotional reactions, from anger to laughter and hope.  By foregrounding his personality as his chief asset, Christie aims to rally voters who value authenticity and boldness, positioning himself as a candidate willing to confront challenges head‑on despite moral criticisms and political setbacks.  The piece frames his candidacy as a test of personal integrity and the moral courage to persist in the face of adversity.

END OF SUMMARY.
