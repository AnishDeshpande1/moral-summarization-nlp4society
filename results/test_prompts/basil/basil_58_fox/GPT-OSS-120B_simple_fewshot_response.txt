SUMMARY:
New Jersey Governor Chris Christie announced today that he will enter the 2016 Republican presidential race, despite a steep uphill climb. Scandals and sagging approval at home have stripped him of his “establishment favorite” status and sent many mega‑donors to rival Jeb Bush. Nevertheless, Christie’s campaign is framed as a story of personal resilience and boldness. Maggie Haberman notes that his greatest asset is his brash, combative personality, which he wields like a weapon in town‑hall battles—leaving some voters angry, but many more laughing, weeping, or hoping for a handshake. The moral framing casts Christie as a gritty underdog who refuses to be written off, betting that his fierce, personable style can overcome poll deficits and fundraising woes to win the GOP nomination. His launch from his hometown underscores a narrative of hometown grit and determination to fight for the White House against the odds.  
END OF SUMMARY.
