SUMMARY:
New Jersey Governor Chris Christie is expected to announce his candidacy for the 2016 White House race today at 11am from his hometown of Livingston, NJ. Despite facing an uphill climb due to low poll numbers and a fundraising deficit, Christie's team believes he can win over voters with his unique personality. According to Maggie Haberman in The New York Times, Christie's "brash, challenging, and often combative" nature could be his main asset in the campaign. He has gained experience hosting town hall meetings in New Jersey, where he takes questions from residents and has a reputation for being relatable and empathetic. However, Christie also faces criticism for scandals and declining popularity in his home state, which may deter some donors and voters.

END OF SUMMARY.
