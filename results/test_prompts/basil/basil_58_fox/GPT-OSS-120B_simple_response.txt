SUMMARY:  
New Jersey Governor **Chris Christie** announced his 2016 presidential bid from his hometown of Livingston, positioning himself as a **bold, combative outsider** despite a steep climb ahead.  While the **Washington Post** notes his fading establishment support—plagued by scandals, low state approval, and a shrinking donor base—**the New York Times** frames Christie’s candidacy as a **testament to his resilient personality**.  He is portrayed as a **“maestro of the town‑hall,”** using his brash style to engage voters, provoke strong reactions, and inspire hope.  The article suggests that, even with poor polls and fundraising gaps, Christie’s **charismatic, confrontational appeal** could still make him a **significant force** in the GOP race.
