**SUMMARY:**  
Sen. Marco Rubio invoked President John F. Kennedy to argue that American **strength**—not **weakness**—is the best way to prevent **danger** and secure **peace**. He claimed the United States remains a **benevolent** global force and warned that **weakness** invites the **enemy**. Rubio’s “Rubio Doctrine” rests on three pillars: **American strength**, protection of the U.S. economy, and **moral clarity** about core values. He criticized President Obama for what he sees as a soft, disengaged approach, and attacked Hillary Clinton’s record as “ineffective…dangerously negligent.” While rejecting the 2003 Iraq invasion after learning Saddam lacked WMDs, he contrasted his stance with Jeb Bush’s continued support. Rubio’s speech positioned a robust, assertive foreign policy as essential to safeguarding **peace** and confronting **danger**.
