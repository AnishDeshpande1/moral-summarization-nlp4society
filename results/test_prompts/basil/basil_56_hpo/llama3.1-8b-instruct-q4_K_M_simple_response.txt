SUMMARY:
US Senator Marco Rubio outlined his foreign policy vision, invoking President John F. Kennedy's approach to assertive American power abroad. Rubio emphasized that US strength is essential for preventing war and promoting peace, criticizing President Obama's policies as weak and ineffective. He proposed the "Rubio Doctrine," guided by three principles: American strength, economic protection, and moral clarity regarding core values. Rubio also attacked Democratic presidential hopeful Hillary Clinton's tenure as secretary of state, calling it "ineffective at best and dangerously negligent at worst." When questioned about invoking Kennedy and the Cold War era, Rubio argued that his statements represented timeless truths.

Rubio's vision for American leadership emphasizes a more assertive role abroad and a stronger military budget. He also took aim at Jeb Bush, who recently supported the 2003 invasion of Iraq despite knowing it was based on faulty intelligence. Rubio claimed he would not have authorized the invasion if he had known Hussein did not possess weapons of mass destruction.

END OF SUMMARY.
