SUMMARY: U.S. Sen. Marco Rubio used a Council on Foreign Relations speech to outline a “Rubio Doctrine” that calls for a larger defense budget and a more assertive global role, invoking President John F. Kennedy’s Cold‑War ethos. He argued that American strength deters war, criticized President Obama for “softening” U.S. posture, and said the nation should protect its economy and uphold moral clarity. Rubio, a Senate Foreign Relations Committee member, highlighted his record opposing Obama’s Cuba policy, Iran nuclear talks, and advocating for Syrian rebels and increased aid to Ukraine. He also attacked Hillary Clinton’s tenure as Secretary of State as ineffective. When pressed about the Kennedy reference, he called its principles “timeless.” Rubio said he would not have supported the 2003 Iraq invasion had he known Saddam lacked WMDs, contrasting his view with Jeb Bush’s recent comments supporting the war. END OF SUMMARY.
