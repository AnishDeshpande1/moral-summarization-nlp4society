SUMMARY:  
Sen. Marco Rubio invoked President John F. Kennedy to argue that American strength is a means of **preventing** war, while **weakness** is the **friend** of **danger** and the **enemy** of peace. He **disagrees** with President Obama, saying the president **believes** the U.S. is too soft on adversaries and should **cede** leadership, a view Rubio calls **negligent**. Rubio’s “Rubio Doctrine” rests on three pillars: **American strength**, **protection** of the U.S. economy, and **moral clarity** about core values. He **took** a hard line against Obama’s diplomatic **building** with Cuba, Iran, Syria and Ukraine, warning that **help** to rivals would only **promote** tyranny. In a interview, Rubio said Kennedy’s **understood** truths are **timeless**, and he would not have **favored** the 2003 Iraq **destruction** had he known the weapons claim was false. He also criticized Hillary Clinton’s tenure as “ineffective at best and dangerously **negligent** at worst,” and warned that other leaders risk being **trapped** in the past.  

END OF SUMMARY.
