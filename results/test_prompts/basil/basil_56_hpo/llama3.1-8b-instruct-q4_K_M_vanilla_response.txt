SUMMARY:

US Senator Marco Rubio (R-Fla.) outlined his foreign policy vision at the Council on Foreign Relations, invoking President John F. Kennedy's philosophy of projecting American power abroad to promote peace and prevent war. Rubio argued that a stronger US military and assertive global presence are necessary to maintain America's influence as a benevolent force. He criticized President Obama's approach, saying it has led to weakness and emboldened adversaries. Rubio also launched a critique of Hillary Clinton's tenure as Secretary of State, calling it "ineffective at best and dangerously negligent at worst." In contrast, Rubio presented his own principles: American strength, protection of the economy in a globalized world, and moral clarity regarding core values. The speech marked another opportunity for Rubio to differentiate himself from President Obama and other Republican contenders, including Jeb Bush.

END OF SUMMARY.
