SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry has taken a hardline stance on illegal immigration, calling for the detention and deportation of all undocumented immigrants apprehended by authorities. He criticized the Obama administration's "catch-and-release" policy, which allows some nonviolent illegal immigrants to be released back into the public. Perry's stance has earned him an endorsement from Sheriff Joe Arpaio, known for his tough stance on immigration. However, Arpaio is a polarizing figure, and his endorsement may not help Perry in New Hampshire, where immigration is not a top concern for voters. Perry's position is a reversal from his previous support for a state plan that provided in-state college tuition to children of illegal immigrants, which was criticized as too soft on immigration. Perry has apologized for calling opponents of his previous stance "heartless." His tough stance on immigration may be an attempt to win back Republican primary voters who abandoned him.

END OF SUMMARY.
