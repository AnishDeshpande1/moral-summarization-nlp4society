SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry has announced his plan to detain and deport all undocumented immigrants arrested or detained, in an effort to draw a contrast with the Obama administration's "catch-and-release" policy. Perry claimed that this policy allows nonviolent illegal immigrants to be released back into the general public, calling it a "horrific policy". He also received an endorsement from Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio, who praised Perry's efforts to interdict illegal crossings at the Texas-Mexico border. However, Perry has faced criticism for his support of a state plan that gives in-state college tuition to some children of undocumented immigrants, and it remains to be seen how much the endorsement will help him with Republican primary voters. The issue of immigration is not as prominent among likely Republican voters in New Hampshire, where Perry trails Mitt Romney.
