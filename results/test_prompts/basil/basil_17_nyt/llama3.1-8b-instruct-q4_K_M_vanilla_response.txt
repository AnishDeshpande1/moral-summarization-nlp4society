SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry announced his support for a federal policy that would detain and deport all undocumented immigrants apprehended by authorities, in an effort to draw a contrast with the Obama administration's "catch-and-release" policy. Perry claimed this policy would allow officials to focus on deporting serious criminals and threats to public safety. He also received an endorsement from Sheriff Joe Arpaio of Arizona, who praised Perry's efforts to interdict illegal crossings at the Texas-Mexico border.

Perry has been criticized for his previous support of in-state college tuition for children of undocumented immigrants, which he later apologized for calling "heartless." The endorsement by Arpaio may not boost Perry's chances in New Hampshire, where immigration is a relatively low priority for Republican voters. In fact, one recent poll showed that only 4% of likely Republican voters cited immigration as the most important issue.

Perry also signaled that he would drop the lawsuit against Arizona's controversial immigration law if elected president.

END OF SUMMARY.
