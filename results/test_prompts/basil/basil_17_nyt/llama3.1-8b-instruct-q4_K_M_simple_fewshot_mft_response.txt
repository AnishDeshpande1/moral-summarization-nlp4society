SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry has vowed to detain and deport every illegal alien apprehended, citing the Obama administration's "catch-and-release" policy as a "horrific" practice that prioritizes nonviolent offenders over public safety. Perry's stance is supported by Sheriff Joe Arpaio of Maricopa County, Arizona, who praised Perry's efforts to interdict illegal crossings and criticized the federal government for suing his department over alleged racial profiling. Perry has faced criticism from within his own party for supporting in-state tuition for children of undocumented immigrants, which he later apologized for calling "very inappropriate." The endorsement from Arpaio may help Perry shore up his credentials on immigration ahead of the New Hampshire primary. However, the issue remains a low priority for Republican voters, with only 4% citing it as their top concern.

The moral framing of this article highlights Perry's emphasis on authority and order in enforcing immigration laws, contrasting with what he sees as the Obama administration's leniency towards undocumented immigrants. The article also touches on fairness and cheating, suggesting that Perry's stance is more just than the current policy, which prioritizes nonviolent offenders over public safety.
