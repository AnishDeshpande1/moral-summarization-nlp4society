SUMMARY: In a stark moral appeal, Texas Governor Rick Perry pledged to back a federal policy that would **detain and deport every illegal alien caught**—a hard‑line stance he presents as a righteous correction to what he calls the Obama administration’s “horrific” catch‑and‑release approach. Perry frames the issue as a matter of public safety and national interest, arguing that swift removal of undocumented detainees protects citizens. To bolster his moral credibility, he secured the endorsement of Sheriff Joe Arpaio, the controversial “toughest sheriff” who boasts a record of aggressive immigration enforcement and claims to act without fear of racial‑profiling accusations. Perry’s earlier support for in‑state tuition for children of undocumented immigrants drew criticism; he now apologizes, labeling his past remarks as “inappropriate,” and re‑positions himself as compassionate yet firm. While immigration ranks low (4 %) among Republican voters, the moral narrative pits “hard‑working Americans” against “illegal entrants,” casting lenient policies as heartless. Yet the endorsement is risky: Arpaio faces a DOJ lawsuit over alleged civil‑rights violations, and recent Arizona backlash shows that extreme immigration tactics can alienate moderate voters. Perry’s moral framing seeks to portray his stance as a necessary defense of the nation’s integrity, even as political realities question its electoral payoff.  
END OF SUMMARY.
