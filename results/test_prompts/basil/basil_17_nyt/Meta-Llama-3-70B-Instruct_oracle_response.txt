SUMMARY: Texas Governor Rick Perry has taken a tough stance on illegal immigration, calling for a federal policy to "detain and deport every illegal alien that we apprehend." This policy would involve expediting hearings for those arrested or detained and found to be undocumented, with the goal of deporting them from the country. Perry criticized the Obama administration's "catch-and-release" policy, which allows some nonviolent illegal immigrants to be released back into the general public, calling it "horrific." Perry's stance has earned him an endorsement from Sheriff Joe Arpaio, known for his tough stance on immigration. However, Arpaio's own controversial policies, including allegations of racial profiling, have raised concerns. Perry's efforts to shore up his credentials on immigration come as he faces criticism from within his own party for supporting a state plan that gives some children of illegal immigrants in-state college tuition. END OF SUMMARY.
