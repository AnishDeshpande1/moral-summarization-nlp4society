SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Rick Perry has announced his support for a federal policy that would detain and deport all undocumented immigrants who are arrested or detained, following an expedited hearing. This stance is in contrast to the Obama administration's "catch-and-release" policy, which allows nonviolent undocumented immigrants to be released back into the general public. Perry's proposal aims to focus resources on deporting serious offenders and those who pose a threat to national security.

Perry has gained an endorsement from Sheriff Joe Arpaio of Arizona, known for his tough stance on immigration, but this may not necessarily boost his credentials in the Republican primary. Arpaio is a polarizing figure, with some criticizing his methods as racially motivated. Perry's position on immigration has been criticized by some Republicans who see him as too soft.

The policy has sparked controversy, with some arguing that it would unfairly target nonviolent immigrants and create more problems than solutions. The Justice Department has filed a lawsuit against Arpaio's department for allegedly violating the rights of Hispanics.
