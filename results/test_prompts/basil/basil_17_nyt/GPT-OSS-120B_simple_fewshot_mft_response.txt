SUMMARY:
Governor Rick Perry pledged to back a federal policy that would detain and deport every illegal immigrant caught, framing the Obama administration’s “catch‑and‑release” approach as a subversive, unfair compromise that cheats the nation of security. Perry positioned himself as the authority who will enforce strict border rules, contrasting his tough stance with what he called the “horrific” policies of his political rivals. He secured the endorsement of Sheriff Joe Arpaí­o, a similarly hard‑line authority figure, who praised Perry’s actions against illegal crossings and cast opposition as weak or heartless. At the same time, Perry’s earlier support for in‑state tuition for some children of undocumented immigrants was portrayed as a fairness violation, prompting criticism from within his party and an apology for calling critics “heartless.” The article underscores the clash between authoritative enforcement of immigration laws and accusations that lenient policies constitute cheating the American public out of safety and equitable treatment. Perry’s appeal seeks to restore perceived lawful order while accusing opponents of undermining fairness.  

END OF SUMMARY.
