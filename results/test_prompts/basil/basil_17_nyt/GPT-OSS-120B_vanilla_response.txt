SUMMARY: Texas Governor Rick Perry, a Republican presidential candidate, called for a federal policy to detain and deport every illegal immigrant caught, emphasizing expedited hearings for those found undocumented. He criticized the Obama administration’s “catch‑and‑release” approach and sought to bolster his immigration credentials ahead of the New Hampshire primary, securing an endorsement from controversial Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio, who praised Perry’s border enforcement actions. Perry faced backlash from within his party for supporting in‑state tuition for some children of illegal immigrants and for previously calling opponents “heartless,” for which he later apologized. Despite Arpaio’s support, immigration ranks low among Republican voters, and Perry trails Mitt Romney in polls. Arpaio, under a Justice Department lawsuit over alleged civil‑rights violations, remains a polarizing figure, highlighted by recent defeats of similarly hard‑line Arizona lawmakers. Perry also indicated he would not continue the federal lawsuit against states with strict immigration laws if elected president. END OF SUMMARY.
