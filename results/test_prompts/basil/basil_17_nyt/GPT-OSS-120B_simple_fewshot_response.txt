SUMMARY:
Texas Governor Rick Perry used a New Hampshire stop to pledge federal “detention and deportation of every illegal alien we apprehend,” condemning the Obama administration’s “catch‑and‑release” approach as “horrific.” He clarified that the policy would apply only to those arrested, detained and later found undocumented, with expedited hearings. Perry sought to reinforce his immigration credentials ahead of the New Hampshire primary, securing an endorsement from Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio, who praised Perry’s actions to interdict illegal crossings and send Texas Rangers to the border. Perry reiterated his apology for earlier calling opponents “heartless,” trying to win back Republican voters who view him as too soft on immigration. While immigration ranks low among Republican concerns in the state, Perry’s hard‑line stance and Arpaist’s polarizing support aim to portray a strong, law‑and‑order alternative to the Obama administration and to opponents like Mitt Romney. Perry also signaled he would halt federal lawsuits against states that enforce strict immigration measures if elected.

END OF SUMMARY.
