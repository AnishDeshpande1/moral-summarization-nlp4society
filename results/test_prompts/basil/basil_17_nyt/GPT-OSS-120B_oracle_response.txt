SUMMARY: Texas Governor Rick Perry vowed to back a federal **policy** that would **detain** and **deport** every illegal alien caught, calling the Obama administration’s “catch‑and‑release” approach “horrific.” He argued that swift **deport** actions would let officials focus on **crimes** and threats to public safety. To bolster his immigration stance before the New Hampshire primary, Perry secured the endorsement of Sheriff Joe Arpaí­o, who praised Perry’s efforts to **force** border interceptions and praised his willingness to **do** more than just talk. Perry, previously criticized for supporting in‑state tuition for children of undocumented immigrants, apologized for calling his opponents “heartless.” While Arpaí­o is a polarizing figure—facing a Justice Department **lawsuit** for alleged racial profiling and **cooperating** failures—he remains popular in Arizona. Recent setbacks for hard‑line immigration advocates, such as the recall of Senator Russell Pearce, suggest shifting voter attitudes. Perry said he would not **cooperate** with federal suits against states that enact strict immigration **policy** if elected president. END OF SUMMARY.
