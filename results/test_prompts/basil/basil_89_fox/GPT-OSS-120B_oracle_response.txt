SUMMARY: President Trump announced on Sunday that Defense Secretary James Mattis will leave the administration on January 1, weeks earlier than his planned February exit, after Mattis’ resignation letter revealed stark policy disagreements. Trump praised Deputy Secretary Patrick Shanahan’s “long list of accomplishments” while serving as Deputy and at Boeing, saying Shanahan will be “great” as Acting Secretary. The sudden change has heightened tension in the Defense Department, with sources saying Mattis quit in protest over national‑security policies and that further resignations could follow. At the same time, the White House is moving to pull all 2,000 troops out of Syria, a decision Trump downplayed despite criticism that the withdrawal could lead to ISIS re‑emerging and the abandoning of Kurdish allies. Trump boasted that the U.S. has defeated ISIS in Syria and framed the pull‑out as a sacrifice that will bring troops home “happy & healthy.” Similar criticism surrounds the plan to withdraw half of the U.S. force from Afghanistan, where more than 2,400 American soldiers have died. The administration’s actions are being portrayed as protecting national interests while the debate over war, combat, and defeat intensifies. END OF SUMMARY.
