SUMMARY:

President Trump's announcement that Defense Secretary James Mattis will leave the administration on January 1, weeks earlier than planned, has sparked tension in the Defense Department and Washington. This move comes after Mattis' resignation letter made clear his policy disagreements with the White House. The president has appointed Patrick Shanahan as Acting Secretary of Defense, citing his accomplishments and service to the Pentagon. However, this decision has been criticized for being a sacrifice to Trump's national security policies.

The administration's plans to pull all 2,000 troops out of Syria have sparked controversy, with Mattis warning that abandoning the region could allow ISIS to reemerge. The White House has defended its decision, saying it is bringing its troops back home after defeating ISIS in the region. However, critics argue that this move will sacrifice America's role as a global leader and put innocent lives at risk.

The situation has escalated, with more resignations expected in the coming days. Mattis' departure is seen as a defeat for Trump's policies, but the president downplayed the news, calling it a "nothing event." END OF SUMMARY.
