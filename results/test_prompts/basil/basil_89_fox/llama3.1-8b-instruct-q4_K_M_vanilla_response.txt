SUMMARY:

President Trump announced Sunday that Defense Secretary James Mattis will leave the administration on January 1, weeks earlier than planned. This comes just days after Mattis' bombshell resignation letter revealed his policy disagreements with the White House. Deputy Secretary of Defense Patrick Shanahan will assume the title of Acting Secretary of Defense starting January 1.

Mattis' departure date was initially set for February 28, but it's unclear why it was moved up. Sources say Mattis quit "in protest" over Trump's national security policies and more resignations could be coming. The White House is making plans to pull all 2,000 troops out of Syria, with Trump claiming the US has defeated ISIS in the region.

Brett McGurk, the US envoy for the global coalition to defeat Islamic State, also resigned, effective December 31. The president downplayed the news, calling McGurk a "grandstander" and criticizing the Fake News Media for making too much of the story.

END OF SUMMARY.
