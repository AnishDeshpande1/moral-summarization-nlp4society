SUMMARY: President Trump announced that Defense Secretary James Mattis will depart on January 1, 2019—earlier than his own February resignation letter indicated—citing the appointment of Deputy Secretary Patrick Shanahan as Acting Secretary. Mattis’ resignation, the first cabinet-level protest over policy in four decades, underscored deepening conflict between the Pentagon and the White House over Trump’s aggressive “America First” security agenda, including the planned withdrawal of all 2,000 U.S. troops from Syria and a rapid pull‑out from Afghanistan. Sources suggest Mattis quit in “protest” of the president’s decisions, warning that abandoning Syrian forces could revive ISIS and betray Kurdish allies. The turmoil intensified as U.S. envoy Brett McGurk, who had overseen the anti‑ISIS coalition, also resigned, accusing the administration of premature disengagement. Trump dismissed both departures as media exaggeration, framing the troop withdrawals as fulfilling campaign promises and protecting American lives. The cascade of high‑level resignations highlights a moral clash: military leaders argue that reckless pull‑outs jeopardize regional stability and betray allies, while the president portrays them as courageous, patriotic actions.

END OF SUMMARY.
