SUMMARY:
President Trump announced that Defense Secretary James Mattis will leave the administration on Jan. 1, accelerating his departure after Mattis’ resignation letter exposed deep policy rifts with the White House. Trump praised Deputy Secretary Patrick Shanahan, a former Boeing executive, as the new Acting Secretary. Mattis, the first cabinet member in four decades to resign over a policy disagreement, had originally planned to stay until Feb. 28 to ensure a smooth transition for key events such as NATO meetings. Sources say Mattis quit “in protest” over Trump’s national‑security choices, and more resignations may follow. The move comes as the administration pushes to pull all 2,000 U.S. troops from Syria—a decision long warned against by military leaders for risking an ISIS resurgence and abandoning Kurdish allies. Simultaneously, U.S. envoy Brett McGurk, who warned that ISIS was not yet defeated, also resigned. Trump dismissed both exits as “fake news” and boasted about “defeating ISIS,” while critics argue the rapid withdrawals betray commitments to allies and endanger regional stability, raising serious moral concerns about the administration’s disregard for responsible foreign‑policy and the lives of service members and partners. 
END OF SUMMARY.
