SUMMARY: President Donald Trump announced that Defense Secretary James Mattis will leave the administration on January 1, 2019, earlier than his previously stated February 28 exit. Mattis had resigned in protest over Trump’s national‑security policies, marking the first Cabinet resignation over a policy dispute in 40 years. Deputy Secretary of Defense Patrick Shanahan, a former Boeing executive, will become Acting Secretary of Defense. The accelerated departure follows heightened tension in the Pentagon and reports that more resignations may follow. Concurrently, Trump’s administration plans to withdraw all 2,000 U.S. troops from Syria, claiming ISIS has been defeated, despite criticism that the pullout could revive the group and abandon Kurdish allies. U.S. envoy Brett McGurk, who coordinated the anti‑ISIS coalition, also resigned effective December 31, a move Trump downplayed on Twitter. Additionally, the White House intends to reduce its Afghan force by about 7,000 troops this summer, a decision that has drawn criticism given the ongoing reliance of Afghan security forces on U.S. support. END OF SUMMARY.
