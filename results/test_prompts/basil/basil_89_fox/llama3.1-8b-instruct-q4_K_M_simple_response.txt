SUMMARY:

President Trump announced that Defense Secretary James Mattis will leave the administration on January 1, weeks earlier than planned. This comes just days after Mattis' resignation letter revealed his policy disagreements with the White House. Patrick Shanahan, a former Boeing executive, will assume the role of Acting Secretary of Defense. The sudden departure of Mattis and other officials, including Brett McGurk, the US envoy for the global coalition to defeat ISIS, has sparked concern about the future of national security policies.

Trump's decision to pull troops out of Syria and Afghanistan has been met with criticism from military leaders and experts. Mattis' resignation letter hinted at his disagreement with Trump's policies, which have led to increased tension in the Defense Department. The White House's plans for troop withdrawals have raised concerns about the long-term consequences for regional security and the potential re-emergence of ISIS.

END OF SUMMARY
