SUMMARY:

Mary Cheney, sister of Wyoming Senate candidate Liz Cheney, publicly criticized her sister's stance on same-sex marriage on Facebook. Mary, who is gay and married to her partner, wrote that Liz is "dead wrong" on the issue and that freedom means equality for all families, regardless of their composition. This comes after Liz revealed she is not in favor of gay marriage, but rather believes it should be decided by individual states through a vote. Mary's post was aimed at encouraging others to share her views, using the phrase "Please like and share if you agree." The disagreement between the two sisters reflects a broader split among Republicans on same-sex marriage, with some seeing support for equality as a way to attract younger voters and others sticking to traditional values.

END OF SUMMARY.
