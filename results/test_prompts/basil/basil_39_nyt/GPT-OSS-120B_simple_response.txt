SUMMARY: Mary Cheney publicly rebuked her sister, Senate candidate Liz Cheney, for opposing same‑sex marriage, framing the dispute as a matter of universal freedom and equal rights. In a Facebook post, Mary — herself gay and married to her partner — declared “Freedom means freedom for everyone,” insisting that all families deserve the same legal protections regardless of their composition. She condemned Liz’s “state‑by‑state” approach as a “show of hands” that fails to protect fundamental liberties, urging readers to “like and share” if they agree. The clash reflects a deeper moral divide within the Republican Party: while some, like former Vice President Dick Cheney, champion marriage equality as a civil right, others cling to traditional marriage as a cornerstone of family values and fear electoral backlash. Mary’s appeal casts the issue not merely as political strategy but as a moral imperative to extend freedom and dignity to every individual and family. END OF SUMMARY.
