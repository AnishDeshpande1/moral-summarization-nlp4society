SUMMARY:

Mary Cheney, sister of Wyoming Senate candidate Liz Cheney, publicly criticized her sister's stance on same-sex marriage. Mary, who is gay and married to her partner, wrote on Facebook that she loves her sister but disagrees with her views on the issue. She echoed their father Dick Cheney's support for same-sex marriage, stating that "freedom means freedom for everyone" and that all families should have equal rights and protections.

Liz Cheney had previously stated that she is not pro-gay marriage, but believes the issue should be decided by the states and their voters. Mary called this position inadequate, saying it's not something to be decided by a show of hands. The disagreement reflects a broader split within the Republican party on same-sex marriage, with some members seeing support for equality as a way to attract younger voters, while others view traditional marriage as a core value.

The issue is particularly relevant in Wyoming, where incumbent Senator Mike Enzi opposes legalizing same-sex marriage. Mary's public statement highlights the challenge Liz Cheney faces in balancing her own views on social issues with the party's conservative base.
