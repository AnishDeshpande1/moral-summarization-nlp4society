SUMMARY:
Mary Cheney, an openly gay woman and the younger sister of Wyoming Senate candidate Liz Cheney, publicly rebuked her sister’s opposition to same‑sex marriage on Facebook, urging friends to “like and share” her call for equal rights. Mary quoted their father, former Vice President Dick Cheney, asserting that “freedom means freedom for everyone” and that all families deserve the same legal protections regardless of how they are formed. Liz Cheney responded that she is “not pro‑gay marriage” and believes the issue should be decided by each state’s voters, not by judges or legislators. The sibling clash highlights a broader rift within the Republican Party between traditionalist conservatives and those recognizing shifting public opinion on marriage equality. The moral framing underscores the importance of standing up for equality, freedom, and the dignity of all families, even when it means confronting close family members and party orthodoxy.  
END OF SUMMARY.
