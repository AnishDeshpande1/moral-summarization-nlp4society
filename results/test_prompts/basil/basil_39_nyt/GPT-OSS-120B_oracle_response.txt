SUMMARY: Mary Cheney, who is gay and married, publicly rebuked her sister Liz Cheney’s stance that marriage “must be decided by the states” and not guaranteed for all. On Facebook Mary wrote that “freedom means freedom for everyone,” insisting that all families are entitled to the same rights, privileges and protections regardless of how they look. She urged friends to like and share the post, arguing that marriage is not something to be decided by a simple show of hands. Liz, a Wyoming Senate candidate, said she is not pro‑gay marriage and left the issue to voters, a position that Mary called “dead wrong.” The family clash reflects a broader Republican split: some members fear abandoning traditional marriage could cost them younger voters, while others, like incumbent Senator Mike Enzi, continue to oppose same‑sex marriage, seeing it as a core family value. END OF SUMMARY.
