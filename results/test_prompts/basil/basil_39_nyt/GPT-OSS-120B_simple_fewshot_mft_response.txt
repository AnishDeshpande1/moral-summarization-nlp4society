SUMMARY:
Mary Cheney publicly rebuked her sister Liz’s opposition to same‑sex marriage, calling it “dead wrong” and urging her Facebook friends to share the post. By invoking their father’s support and the slogan “Freedom means freedom for everyone,” Mary framed Liz’s stance as an unfair denial of equal rights to families of all shapes, portraying it as a betrayal of the principle that all citizens deserve the same legal protections. Liz defended a “state‑by‑state” approach, arguing that marriage should be decided by voters rather than judges, but Mary dismissed this as a “show of hands” that cheats the LGBTQ+ community out of basic fairness. The intra‑family clash highlights a broader Republican split, with some candidates risking political loss by denying equal marriage rights, while others cling to traditional definitions that many view as unjust and discriminatory. The narrative underscores the moral charge that Liz’s position cheats the ideal of equal treatment under the law. END OF SUMMARY.
