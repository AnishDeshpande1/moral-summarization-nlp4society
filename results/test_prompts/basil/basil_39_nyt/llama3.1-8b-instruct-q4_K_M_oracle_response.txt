SUMMARY:

Mary Cheney, a gay woman and sister of Wyoming Senate candidate Liz Cheney, criticized her sister's stance on same-sex marriage, calling it "dead wrong" and urging others to share their disagreement. Liz Cheney had revealed that she is not in favor of same-sex marriage, but instead believes the issue should be decided by the people in each state through a vote. Mary Cheney disagreed with this approach, stating that freedom means freedom for everyone, including all families regardless of how they are made or who they are composed of. She emphasized that everyone is entitled to the same rights, privileges, and protections as every other family. The debate mirrors the broader disagreement among Republicans on same-sex marriage, with some advocating for support and others opposing it due to concerns about losing conservative voters. Mary Cheney's stance highlights the risk posed by full-throated support for same-sex marriage in a Republican primary.

END OF SUMMARY.
