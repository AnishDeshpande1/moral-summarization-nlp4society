SUMMARY:
Mary Cheney publicly rebuked her sister, Wyoming Senate candidate Liz Cheney, for opposing same‑sex marriage. In a Facebook post, Mary—who is gay and married her partner—said Liz is “dead wrong” and called for equal marriage rights, echoing their father Dick Cheney’s supportive stance. Liz responded to a push poll by saying marriage should be decided by state voters, not judges or legislators, a position she framed as a compromise. Mary dismissed this, urging followers to like and share her post. The sisterly dispute reflects a wider split within the Republican Party, where some see support for gay marriage as essential to attract younger voters, while others view traditional marriage as a core conservative value. In Wyoming, incumbent Republican Senator Mike Enzi also opposes same‑sex marriage, highlighting the political risk Liz faces in a primary.  
END OF SUMMARY.
