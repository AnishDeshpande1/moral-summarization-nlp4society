SUMMARY:

New Jersey Governor Chris Christie announced his candidacy for president, relying on his personality and charisma to overcome his lack of advantages in the Republican field. He criticized his rivals, including Senators Marco Rubio and Ted Cruz, for their lack of experience running a state government. Christie also took aim at President Obama's foreign policy and Hillary Clinton, calling her "Barack Obama's second mate." Despite his vulnerabilities, Christie is hoping to appeal to voters with his honesty and willingness to tell difficult truths.

Christie's announcement was marked by a mix of humor and seriousness, as he joked about his father's accounting business and described the decision to enter politics as a "coin toss" between him and his wife. He plans to focus on town hall meetings in New Hampshire, where he hopes to connect with voters directly. With his campaign facing significant challenges, including a crowded field of better-financed rivals, Christie must rely on his personality to win over Republican voters.

END OF SUMMARY.
