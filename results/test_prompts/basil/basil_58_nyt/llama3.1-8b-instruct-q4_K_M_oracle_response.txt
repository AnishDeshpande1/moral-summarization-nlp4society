SUMMARY:

New Jersey Governor Chris Christie launched his presidential campaign, relying on his personality to overcome his waning popularity and reputation for decision-making. He vowed to run the country with strength and authority, but his record as governor has been marred by economic calamity, including credit downgrades and anemic growth. His administration descended into scandal, mired in lawsuits over missed payments to state pension systems. Christie blamed Congress and President Obama's foreign policy for dysfunction and gridlock, warning that Hillary Clinton would only perpetuate a weak stance on the world stage. He reserved his strongest criticism for Washington, accusing it of turning its back on the American people and being under the influence of special interests. With two pillars of his campaign - his record and judgment - looking wobbly, Christie must now rely on his personality to turn his candidacy around.

END OF SUMMARY.
