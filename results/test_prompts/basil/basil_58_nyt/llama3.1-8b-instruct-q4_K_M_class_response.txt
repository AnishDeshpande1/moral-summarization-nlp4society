SUMMARY:

New Jersey Governor Chris Christie declared his candidacy for president, relying on his personality and decision-making skills to run the country. He criticized Washington's dysfunction and gridlock, saying that compromise is a dirty word that would keep America under the crown of England if it were not for historical figures like Washington, Adams, and Jefferson. Christie vowed to campaign and govern with strength, authority, and candor, even if it means making difficult truths uncomfortable for voters.

Christie's candidacy faces significant challenges, including low approval ratings in his home state and a lackluster record on issues like economic recovery and ethics. He has been criticized for his role in the "Bridgegate" scandal and for not adequately addressing the state pension system crisis. Despite these vulnerabilities, Christie is pinning his hopes on his personality and plans to hold town hall meetings in New Hampshire, where he will focus on building a connection with voters.

Christie's record and judgment have been called into question, but he remains defiant, vowing to fix the problems facing America and put an end to the lying and stealing that has contributed to the country's economic calamity. END OF SUMMARY.
