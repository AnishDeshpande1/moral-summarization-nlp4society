SUMMARY:

Glenn Beck delivered a keynote speech at CPAC 2010, demanding that Republicans prioritize ideological purity over expanding their coalition. Beck used his trademark chalkboard to emphasize the dangers of "progressivism," which he believes is "eating the Constitution." He took swipes at Democrats, but focused primarily on urging conservatives to get their own house in order. Beck's speech was a call to action, urging the GOP to embark on a 12-step program of recovery from their addiction to spending and big government. He shared personal anecdotes, including his struggles with addiction and lack of formal education, to illustrate the importance of limited government and individual freedom. The crowd was enthralled, but Beck's message may not be what the conservative movement needs to hear, as it prioritizes ideological purity over broadening appeal to voters. END OF SUMMARY.
