SUMMARY:

Glenn Beck delivered a speech at the CPAC conference, urging Republicans to focus on their own house rather than trying to win over Democratic voters. He criticized the idea of a "big tent" approach, saying that America is not a circus and should be based on ideological purity. Beck argued that conservatism needs to succeed on its own terms, rather than simply being seen as less bad than progressivism. He used his personal story of recovery from addiction to make the point that the Republican Party needs to acknowledge its problems and embark on a path of reform.

Beck also criticized Republicans who are willing to compromise with Democrats, saying that it's not enough just to "not suck as much" as the other side. He emphasized the need for the GOP to stand up for conservative principles and reject big government and spending. The speech was well-received by the CPAC crowd, but some have questioned whether Beck's message is what the party needs to hear in order to win elections.
