SUMMARY:
Glenn Beck closed CPAC 2010 with a fervent call for the Republican Party to submit to higher authority – the Constitution – and purge the “progressive” corruption he says is degrading the nation’s core values. He warned that the GOP has betrayed its own principles by tolerating “big‑government” habits and urged a collective “Come‑to‑Jesus” moment, framing the party’s current path as a moral failing that must be corrected. Beck appealed to loyalty, demanding that Republicans abandon any compromise with Democratic ideas and remain faithful to a pure, traditional conservatism. He invoked sanctity by likening the party’s excesses to an addiction, proposing a 12‑step recovery to restore the sacred integrity of American governance. Through personal testimony of struggle and redemption, Beck positioned himself as a moral authority urging the party to reject subversive influences, reaffirm its loyalty to founding ideals, and protect the sanctity of the Constitution.  
END OF SUMMARY.
