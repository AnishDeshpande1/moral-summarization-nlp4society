SUMMARY:

Glenn Beck's keynote speech at CPAC 2010 emphasized the need for Republicans to prioritize ideological purity over expanding their coalition. Beck argued that "progressivism" is a threat to the Constitution and that conservatives must succeed, rather than just opposing Democrats. He demanded that Republicans acknowledge their own problems, particularly their addiction to spending and big government, and embark on a 12-step program of recovery. Beck's message was clear: supporting Democratic agendas would no longer be tolerated in the GOP. The speech was intertwined with Beck's personal story of struggle and redemption, which added to its emotional appeal. The crowd was enthralled, but Beck's emphasis on ideological purity over coalition-building may not be the most effective strategy for conservatives. By framing the issue in terms of Authority/Subversion, Loyalty/Betrayal, and Sanctity/Degradation, Beck positioned himself as a champion of conservative values and a critic of Republican compromise. END OF SUMMARY.
