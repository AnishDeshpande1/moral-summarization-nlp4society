SUMMARY:

Conservative talk show host Glenn Beck addressed the CPAC conference, urging Republicans to prioritize ideological purity over broadening their coalition. Beck argued that supporting policies similar to those of Democrats would not suffice and instead advocated for a "Come-To-Jesus moment" where Republicans acknowledge their addiction to spending and big government. He proposed a 12-step program of recovery, modeled after his own experience with alcoholism, in which lawmakers would publicly apologize for their actions.

Beck emphasized that America is an idea that sets people free, rather than a circus or clown show. His speech was characterized by hand gestures, impersonations, and chalkboard illustrations, as well as personal anecdotes about his struggles with addiction and lack of formal education. Beck's message was clear: the Republican Party must undergo a transformation to regain its conservative values and principles.

END OF SUMMARY.
