SUMMARY:

Conservative talk show host Glenn Beck delivered a speech at the CPAC conference, urging Republicans to focus on ideological purity rather than trying to expand their coalition. Beck argued that the party needs to get its own house in order before trying to govern again, and that supporting Democratic-like policies will no longer be tolerated. He used personal anecdotes, including his struggles with addiction, to illustrate the need for the GOP to admit its problems and embark on a "12-step program of recovery" from its addiction to spending and big government. Beck's message was met with enthusiasm from the crowd, but it goes against the traditional political strategy of expanding one's coalition to win elections. Instead, Beck emphasized the importance of ideological purity, saying that America is "an idea that sets people free," not a circus or a clown show.

END OF SUMMARY.
