SUMMARY: Glenn Beck closed the 2010 CPAC with a lengthy speech urging the Republican Party to cleanse itself before seeking power. While criticizing Democrats and “progressivism” for eroding the Constitution, Beck focused on internal GOP reform, calling for ideological purity and a “Come‑to‑Jesus” moment. He warned against any policies resembling Democratic ideas, hinted at criticism of Republican figures like John McCain, and invoked his personal recovery from alcoholism to propose a 12‑step “addiction” to big government and spending. Beck urged the party to reject handouts, health‑care entitlement, and expansive fiscal policies, championing a return to limited government exemplified by figures like Calvin Coolidge. The speech emphasized that the GOP must reject compromise, adopt a “big‑tent” of true conservatism, and present a unified, principled vision to regain public trust. END OF SUMMARY.
