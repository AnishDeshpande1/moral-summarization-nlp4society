SUMMARY:  
In his closing address at CPAC 2010, Glenn Beck warned that the Republican Party must first cleanse itself before it can govern effectively. Framing the GOP’s troubles as a moral and spiritual addiction—“addicted to spending and big government”—Beck likened the party to a recovering alcoholic who needs a 12‑step program of redemption. He condemned “progressivism” for “eating the Constitution” and chastised Republicans for failing to admit their own problems, urging a “Come‑to‑Jesus” moment and a return to pure, limited‑government conservatism. While briefly mocking Democrats, his primary target was internal laxity, accusing some GOP members of echoing Democratic policies and calling for ideological purity over the traditional “big‑tent” strategy. Beck’s personal story of struggle and redemption served as a moral illustration, urging the party to reject handouts, taxation, and expansive government in favor of a principled, constitutional vision of America as an idea that frees people.  

END OF SUMMARY.
