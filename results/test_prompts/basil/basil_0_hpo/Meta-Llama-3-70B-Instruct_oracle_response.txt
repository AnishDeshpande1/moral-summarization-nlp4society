SUMMARY: Glenn Beck's CPAC 2010 speech emphasized the need for Republicans to get their own house in order before seeking governance. He demanded purity among his allies, warning that supporting Democratic agendas would no longer be tolerated. Beck argued that "progressivism is eating the Constitution" and that the GOP must embark on a 12-step program of recovery, admitting their addiction to spending and big government. He urged lawmakers to deliver a heartfelt apology, saying "Hello, my name is the Republican Party, and I got a problem." Beck's message was clear: ideological purity was the path to redemption and recovery, not a "big tent" approach. He railed against government handouts, arguing that America is an idea that sets people free, not a circus or show. Beck's plea was made more effective by intertwining his personal story of struggle and redemption, making his demand for purity and success all the more compelling. END OF SUMMARY.
