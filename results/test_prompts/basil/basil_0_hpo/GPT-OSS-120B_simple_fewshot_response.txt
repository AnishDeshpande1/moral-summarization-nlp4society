SUMMARY:
Glenn Beck closed the 2010 CPAC with a fervent call for Republican moral renewal, warning that “progressivism is eating the Constitution” and demanding that the party cleanse itself before seeking power. Rather than merely attacking Democrats, Beck urged GOP members to confront their own “addiction to spending and big government,” likening the party’s needed transformation to a 12‑step recovery program. He chastised Republicans for lacking a “Come‑to‑Jesus” moment, warned against any policies that resemble the Democratic agenda, and mocked the idea of a “big‑tent” coalition, insisting that America is “not a circus” but an idea that sets people free. Beck interwove personal tales of alcoholism and redemption to underscore his plea for ideological purity, suggesting that only a return to strict conservative principles can rescue the party from moral and fiscal decay. The speech framed the GOP’s future not as a matter of electoral strategy but as a moral imperative to reject progressive influence and restore constitutional fidelity.

END OF SUMMARY.
