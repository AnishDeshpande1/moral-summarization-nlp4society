SUMMARY:
All Republican members of the House Intelligence Committee publicly demanded that Rep. Adam Schiff resign as chairman, accusing him of perpetuating a false “collusion” narrative despite the Mueller report’s finding of no evidence. In a letter obtained by Fox News and reiterated during a heated hearing, the GOP lawmakers said Schiff’s claims “undermine the faith of the American people” and are “incompatible with his constitutional duty.” President Trump and Sen. Lindsey Graham joined the rebuke, labeling Schiff’s statements as “knowingly lying” and “conspiracy‑laden.” Schiff defended his stance, pointing to Russian hacking, offers of Clinton‑damaging material and meetings between Trump associates and Russian officials, and insisted that “undoubtedly, there is collusion.” Democrats, led by Speaker Nancy Pelosi, dismissed the removal calls as “ridiculous” and framed the attack as an attempt to intimidate legitimate oversight. The moral framing positions Republicans as defenders of truth and constitutional responsibility, while casting Schiff as a partisan agitator spreading unverified accusations that threaten governmental integrity.

END OF SUMMARY.
