SUMMARY: All Republican members of the House Intelligence Committee signed a letter demanding Rep. Adam Schiff resign as chairman, accusing him of repeatedly claiming “more than circumstantial evidence” of Trump‑Russia collusion despite the Mueller report finding no such collusion. The Republicans reiterated their demand during a heated hearing, with Rep. Michael Conaway calling Schiff’s narrative false and harmful to public trust. President Trump and Sen. Lindsey Graham also criticized Schiff, with Trump urging his resignation and Graham likening Schiff to “Oliver Stone.” Schiff defended his stance, citing Russian hacking, offers of damaging information on Hillary Clinton, and meetings between Trump associates and Russian officials, and insisted collusion was “undoubtedly” present. Democrats, including Speaker Nancy Pelosi, dismissed the calls for his removal as “ridiculous,” affirming support for Schiff and the push for the full Mueller report. The dispute highlights deep partisan divisions over the interpretation of the Mueller investigation and oversight of the Russia probe. END OF SUMMARY.
