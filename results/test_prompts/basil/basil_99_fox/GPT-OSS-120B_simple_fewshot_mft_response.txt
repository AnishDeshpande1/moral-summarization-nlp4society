SUMMARY:
All Republican members of the House Intelligence Committee publicly demanded that Chairman Adam Schiff resign, accusing him of abusing his authority by promoting a “false narrative” of Trump‑Russia collusion despite Mueller’s finding of no evidence. The bipartisan letter denounced Schiff’s claims as a subversive attack on constitutional duty and an unfair cheating of the American public, saying they “undermine the faith of the American people.” President Trump and Sen. Lindsey Graham echoed the charge, labeling Schiff’s statements as knowingly dishonest and likening his rhetoric to conspiracy‑laden propaganda, a moral degradation of public discourse. Schiff defended his stance, pointing to Russian hacking and contacts as evidence of a moral breach by the Trump campaign, but Democrats, including Speaker Nancy Pelosi, dismissed the calls for his removal as “ridiculous” and framed the Republicans’ push as an attempt to silence legitimate oversight. The clash highlights competing moral judgments: Republicans frame Schiff’s actions as a subversive abuse of power and unfair deception, while Democrats portray the Republican assault as a degradation of democratic accountability.

END OF SUMMARY.
