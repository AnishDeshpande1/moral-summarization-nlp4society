SUMMARY:  
Republican members of the House Intelligence Committee denounced Rep. Adam Schiff, accusing him of abusing his chairmanship by repeatedly asserting “more than circumstantial evidence” of Trump‑Russia collusion despite the Mueller report’s finding of no such proof. In a jointly signed letter and a heated hearing, they demanded his immediate resignation, claiming they have “no faith” in his constitutional duty and labeling his statements as a “false narrative” that undermines public trust. President Trump and Sen. Lindsey Graham echoed the condemnation, calling Schiff’s claims “knowing, unlawful lies” and likening him to a conspiracy‑peddling filmmaker. Schiff, however, insisted that undisclosed Russian hacking, offers of dirt on Hillary Clinton, and contacts between Trump allies and Russian operatives constitute undeniable collusion, and he defended his stance as necessary oversight. Democratic leaders, including Speaker Nancy Pelosi, rebuked the Republican attacks as “absolutely ridiculous,” asserting that intimidation will not deter the pursuit of the full Mueller report and that constitutional oversight must continue. The clash highlights a stark moral divide: Republicans frame Schiff’s actions as reckless deceit, while Democrats portray his persistence as a principled fight for transparency.  
END OF SUMMARY.
