SUMMARY:  
Peter Strzok, a senior FBI counter‑intelligence agent, was fired after an inspector‑general report found he had **violated** bureau policy by **sending** a highly sensitive search‑warrant draft to his personal email and by exchanging **inflammatory** texts that **implied** a bias against President Trump. The report **accused** Strzok of **interfering** with the **investigations** into Hillary Clinton’s emails and the Russia probe, noting a **slow‑walked** review of new Clinton‑related evidence that could have **impacted** the 2016 election. Although the inspector‑general said there was no proof Strzok **imposed** his political views on the **investigative** work, he deemed the texts a **departure** from the impartial standard and **contradicted** FBI leadership’s assurances. Strzok’s **decision** to use personal email and his **use** of official materials raised concerns of **bias** and **bureaucratic** mishandling. Trump and allies labeled the FBI’s actions a **hoax**, **unfounded** **witch hunt**, calling Strzok **treasonous** and a **disgrace**. Strzok’s **dismissal** ends his long **service** to the agency, though his lawyer argues the **firing**—instead of a demotion—was politically **motivated** and **misleading**.  

END OF SUMMARY.
