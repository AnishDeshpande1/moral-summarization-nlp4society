SUMMARY:
Peter Strzok, a veteran FBI counter‑intelligence agent, was dismissed after an inspector‑general report found he violated bureau policy by exchanging anti‑Trump texts with colleague Lisa Page, using personal email for a search warrant, and delaying action on newly discovered Clinton‑related emails.  Strzok’s lawyer argues the firing overrode the FBI’s normal disciplinary process, portraying it as a subversion of institutional authority and an unfair, politically motivated punishment that betrays the agency’s loyalty to its own rules.  President Trump and his allies seized on the texts to claim Strzok’s bias represented a betrayal of the nation’s leader and a cheating of the political system, celebrating the termination as a restoration of proper authority and fairness.  The FBI maintains the dismissal followed standard review procedures, but critics contend the decision reflects partisan retaliation that undermines loyalty to the rule of law and the agency’s own standards.  The episode highlights a clash between claims of authority, accusations of cheating, and competing notions of loyalty to the president versus loyalty to institutional norms.  
END OF SUMMARY.
