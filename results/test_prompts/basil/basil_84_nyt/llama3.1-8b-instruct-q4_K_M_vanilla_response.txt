SUMMARY:

Peter Strzok, a senior FBI counterintelligence agent, has been fired for violating bureau policies. He was accused of sending inflammatory text messages disparaging President Trump and using his personal email account to handle sensitive information. Strzok was a key figure in the early months of the Russia investigation and was removed from the special counsel's team last summer. The inspector general's report criticized Strzok's conduct, including his decision not to move swiftly to examine new emails related to the Clinton investigation just weeks before the 2016 election.

Strzok's lawyer argued that the deputy director of the FBI had overruled the Office of Professional Responsibility and fired Strzok instead of demoting him. The firing removes a favorite target of President Trump from the ranks of the FBI, giving Director Wray and Deputy Director Bowdich a chance to move beyond the president's ire.

END OF SUMMARY.
