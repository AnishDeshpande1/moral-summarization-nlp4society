SUMMARY:

FBI senior counterintelligence agent Peter Strzok has been fired for violating bureau policies, including sending inflammatory text messages disparaging President Trump and mishandling sensitive information. The texts, exchanged with former FBI lawyer Lisa Page, were discovered by the inspector general's report, which criticized Strzok's conduct but found no evidence that his personal views affected his work on investigations. Strzok was accused of slow-walking an examination of new emails related to the Clinton investigation in 2016 and forwarding a proposed search warrant to his personal email account. The dismissal is seen as a victory for President Trump, who had repeatedly denounced Strzok and accused him of being "treasonous" and a "disgrace". Strzok's lawyer argues that his client was unfairly targeted and that the decision contradicts FBI Director Wray's testimony to Congress.

END OF SUMMARY.
