SUMMARY:  
Peter Strzok, a veteran FBI counter‑intelligence agent who exchanged disparaging texts about Donald Trump with colleague Lisa Page and was accused of mishandling a search‑warrant draft, was dismissed for “violating bureau policies.”  The firing came after a scathing Inspector General report that, while finding no direct proof that his bias altered investigative outcomes, condemned the texts as evidence of a “biased state of mind” and flagged his use of a personal email for sensitive material and a delayed response to new Clinton‑related emails.  Strzok’s lawyer argued the termination broke normal FBI practice and contradicted Director Wray’s assurances, warning that it should alarm all Americans.  Trump and his allies seized on the texts to brand the Russia probe a “witch hunt” and celebrated Strzok’s ouster as a triumph over a supposed “deep‑state” adversary.  The episode underscores a moral clash: on one side, the FBI’s effort to uphold internal standards and protect the integrity of investigations; on the other, a president’s relentless campaign to delegitimize any perceived dissent within the bureaucracy, raising concerns about politicized personnel actions and the erosion of institutional trust.  
END OF SUMMARY.
