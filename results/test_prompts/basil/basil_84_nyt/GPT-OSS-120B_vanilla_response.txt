SUMMARY: FBI senior counterintelligence agent Peter Strzok was fired after an inspector general’s report found he violated bureau policies by sending a sensitive search‑warrant draft to his personal email and exchanging hundreds of partisan texts with former FBI lawyer Lisa Page that disparaged President Trump. The report criticized Strzok’s conduct during the 2016 Clinton email and Russia investigations, noting potential bias and a delayed response to new evidence from the “Weiner laptop.” Although the IG found no proof that his political views affected investigative decisions, the FBI’s deputy director David Bowdich overruled a recommendation for a 60‑day suspension and demotion, opting for termination. Strzok’s lawyer called the firing a departure from standard practice and contradictory to FBI Director Christopher Wray’s assurances. The dismissal removes a focal point of President Trump’s “deep‑state” accusations and follows the earlier firing of former deputy director Andrew McCabe. Strzok expressed sadness over the decision, while Trump praised the outcome. END OF SUMMARY.
