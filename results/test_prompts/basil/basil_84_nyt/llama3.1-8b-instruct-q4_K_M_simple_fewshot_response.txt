SUMMARY:
FBI official Peter Strzok, who led investigations into Russian meddling and Hillary Clinton's emails, has been fired after 21 years of service. Strzok's attorney claims the decision was politically motivated and a departure from standard practice. The FBI responded that Strzok was subject to a standard review process and the Deputy Director had the authority to review and modify the disciplinary findings. Strzok's firing comes after President Trump repeatedly attacked him, citing anti-Trump bias within the bureau. Strzok's text messages, including one vowing to "stop" Trump from becoming president, were seen as evidence of bias. The inspector general report found no evidence that Strzok's personal views affected his work, but criticized his conduct in sending the texts and using personal email accounts to handle sensitive information. The firing raises concerns about the politicization of the FBI and the impact on public trust in institutions.

END OF SUMMARY.
