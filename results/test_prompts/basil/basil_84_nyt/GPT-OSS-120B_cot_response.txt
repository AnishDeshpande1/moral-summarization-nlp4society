**SUMMARY:**  
Peter Strzok, a senior FBI counter‑intelligence agent who sent disparaging, inflammatory texts about President Trump, was fired after an inspector‑general report labeled his conduct “critical” and “biased.” The report described the messages as evidence of an “illegitimate witch hunt” and suggested a willingness to take official action against the president, calling the behavior “treasonous” and a “disgrace.” Strzok also forwarded a sensitive search‑warrant draft to his personal email and delayed examining new Clinton‑related emails, actions the inspector general deemed “unpersuasive” and possibly motivated by bias. President Trump celebrated the sacking, claiming the FBI was part of a “deep state” undermining his administration. Strzok’s lawyer decried the dismissal as a departure from normal practice and warned it should trouble all Americans. The firing, like that of former deputy director Andrew McCabe, removed a favored target of Trump and allowed the FBI leadership to move beyond the president’s ire.
