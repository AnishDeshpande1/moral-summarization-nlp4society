SUMMARY:

Peter Strzok, a senior F.B.I. counterintelligence agent, was fired for violating bureau policies after an inspector general's report found him to have sent inflammatory text messages disparaging President Trump during the 2016 campaign. The investigation revealed that Strzok had used his personal email account to handle sensitive information and had slow-walked the examination of new emails related to the Clinton investigation, which could have impacted the presidential election. The inspector general's report criticized Strzok's decision to prioritize the Russia investigation over following up on the new leads, stating that it was "not free from bias." Strzok's lawyer argued that the firing was a departure from typical bureau practice and contradicted Director Wray's assurances that the F.B.I. would follow its regular process in personnel matters. The dismissal of Strzok, who served his country for over 20 years, has been seen as a move to discredit him as a witness in the special counsel investigation.

END OF SUMMARY.
