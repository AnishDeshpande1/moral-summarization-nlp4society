SUMMARY: A Republican staffer, Elizabeth Lauten, communications director for Rep. Stephen Fincher, posted a scathing Facebook rant about Malia and Sasha Obama’s demeanor at the White House turkey‑pardon ceremony, urging the teens to “show a little class,” “dress like you deserve respect,” and stop “making faces” on television. She criticized the Obama family’s respect for their positions and labeled the girls’ behavior as lacking in good role‑model qualities. After the post was widely circulated, Lauten issued an apology, saying she had reacted with harsh judgment and recognized how hurtful and offensive her words were. She said she prayed, consulted her parents, and re‑read her comments, now seeing that her remarks were hurtful and that she had judged the teenagers unfairly. Lauten pledged to learn from the experience, asking for forgiveness from those she had hurt and offended and promising to show more respect in the future. END OF SUMMARY.
