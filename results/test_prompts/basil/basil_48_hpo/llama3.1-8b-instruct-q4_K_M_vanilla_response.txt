SUMMARY:

Elizabeth Lauten, communications director for Rep. Stephen Fincher (R-Tenn.), apologized for a Facebook post criticizing Malia and Sasha Obama's behavior at the White House turkey pardon ceremony. In the post, she suggested that the two teenagers should "try showing a little class" and "dress like you deserve respect." The criticism was sparked by photos of the sisters making faces during the event. Lauten later apologized for her comments, stating that she had judged them too quickly and hurtfully. She claimed to have spent hours in prayer and reflection before realizing the error of her words.

Lauten's apology came after widespread criticism on social media, with many accusing her of being out of line. The Obama daughters are often present at the White House turkey pardon ceremony, a lighthearted annual event. Lauten acknowledged that her comments were hurtful and pledged to learn from the experience.

END OF SUMMARY.
