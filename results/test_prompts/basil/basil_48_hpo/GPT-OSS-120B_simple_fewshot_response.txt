SUMMARY:
A Republican congressional staffer, Elizabeth Lauten, resigned after posting a scathing Facebook rant about First Daughters Malia and Sasha Obama’s attire and demeanor at the White House turkey‑pardon ceremony, telling them to “show a little class” and “dress like you deserve respect, not a spot at a bar.” The comments sparked widespread condemnation as an unfair attack on teenagers who were merely fulfilling a ceremonial role. Lauten later issued a public apology, saying she had judged the girls harshly, prayed, consulted her parents, and recognized how hurtful her words were. She expressed remorse to anyone she offended and pledged to learn from the episode. The moral of the story underscores that children of public figures deserve respect and kindness rather than public shaming, and that impulsive social‑media criticism can cause unnecessary harm.

END OF SUMMARY.
