SUMMARY: A Republican communications director, Elizabeth Lauten, publicly berated the Obama teenage daughters for their casual demeanor at the White House turkey‑pardon ceremony, urging them to “show a little class” and “dress like you deserve respect.” Her harsh commentary—suggesting the girls were failing as role models and mocking the president’s jokes—was framed as a moral judgment on their behavior. After backlash, Lauten issued an apology, admitting she had judged the teens harshly and that such judgment “has no place in my heart.” She said she prayed, consulted her parents, and reflected on her words, expressing remorse for the hurt caused and pledging to learn from the episode. The incident highlights a moral lesson about the dangers of quick, judgmental criticism, especially toward young people, and the importance of humility and repentance when one’s words cause harm. END OF SUMMARY.
