SUMMARY:
In his first major foreign‑policy address, Sen. Marco Rubio portrayed the United States as a fading moral and military beacon, blaming President Obama’s “soft‑hearted” diplomacy for weakening America’s resolve. Rubio pledged to rebuild American strength by fully funding the armed forces, confronting aggressors such as China, Iran and Russia, and refusing to compromise on principles like the Cuban embargo. He warned that a weakened U.S. invites hostile regimes to undermine global commerce and threaten human rights, arguing that “the world is safest when America is strongest.” Casting Obama and former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton as architects of a disastrous, appeasing foreign policy, Rubio framed his hawkish platform as a moral duty to restore U.S. leadership and protect freedom worldwide. The Democratic National Committee dismissed his stance as a “neoconservative Cold‑War doctrine,” but Rubio insisted that decisive, force‑backed action—not diplomatic softness—will bring lasting peace.

END OF SUMMARY.
