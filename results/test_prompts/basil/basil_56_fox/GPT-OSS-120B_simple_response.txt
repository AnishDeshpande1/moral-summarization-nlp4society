SUMMARY:
In a force‑filled foreign‑policy address, Sen. Marco Rubio framed his presidential platform as a moral crusade to restore America’s “rightful” role as the world’s strongest and most virtuous power. He argued that the Obama administration’s cautious diplomacy betrayed a higher moral purpose, “flirting with disdain” for U.S. values by appeasing Russia, Iran and Cuba. Rubio pledged to fund a robust military even in peacetime, insisting that “the world is safest when America is at its strongest” and that decisive force will protect global commerce from aggressors such as China, Iran and Russia. He cast former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton as the architect of a disastrous, morally weak foreign policy and rejected the Cuban embargo‑relief championed by Pope Francis, maintaining that the embargo defends Cuban property rights and U.S. moral integrity. Rubio’s moral framing positions American strength and a hard‑line stance as the ethical antidote to “regimes that systematically oppose our principles,” promising that a powerful U.S. will bring more peace, not conflict.  
END OF SUMMARY.
