SUMMARY: U.S. Sen. Marco Rubio delivered his first major foreign‑policy speech at the Council on Foreign Relations, outlining a hawkish platform for his presidential campaign. He pledged to boost defense funding, maintain a robust global military presence, and confront nations he says undermine commerce, specifically naming Russia, China and Iran. Rubio criticized the Obama administration for being overly conciliatory, accusing it of moral weakness, “reset” policies with Russia, openness to Iran and Cuba, and of weakening America’s strategic edge. He reiterated a hard line on Cuba, defending the embargo, and dismissed diplomatic talks with Iran as unreliable. Rubio positioned himself as a seasoned foreign‑policy insider, citing his Senate Foreign Relations and Intelligence committee roles, and dismissed former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton’s record. The Democratic National Committee responded with a brief rebuttal labeling his stance as “neoconservative” and outdated. Rubio concluded that American strength ensures global peace and warned that any nation attempting to block global commerce will face U.S. response. END OF SUMMARY.
