SUMMARY:

US Senator Marco Rubio delivered his first major foreign policy speech since launching his presidential campaign, outlining his vision for a strong military and aggressive stance against nations that undermine global commerce. Rubio criticized the Obama administration's handling of foreign policy, accusing it of being overly cautious and appeasing hostile regimes. He pledged to prioritize funding for the military and take decisive action against countries like China and Iran that threaten US interests.

Rubio also defended his hawkish approach, which has been a hallmark of his Senate service, and criticized former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton as ineffective in her tenure. He emphasized the importance of American strength and moral leadership on the world stage. The Democratic National Committee responded to Rubio's speech with a statement dismissing it as "Neoconservative meets Cold War" and accusing him of promoting a failed foreign policy agenda.

END OF SUMMARY.
