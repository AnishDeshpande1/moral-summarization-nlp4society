SUMMARY:

In his first major foreign policy speech, US Senator Marco Rubio emphasized the need for a strong military and aggressive steps to combat nations that undermine global commerce. He criticized the Obama administration's cautious responses to overseas crises, saying it has led to the US losing its place as a world military and moral power. Rubio vowed to prioritize funding the military and take decisive action against aggressive nations like China and Iran. He also reiterated his opposition to normalizing relations with Cuba and Iran, citing their human rights abuses and lack of trustworthiness. Rubio's speech was seen as a hawkish approach to foreign policy, with the Democratic National Committee labeling it as "Neoconservative meets Cold War." Rubio's stance on foreign policy has been a hallmark of his service in the Senate and his presidential campaign.

END OF SUMMARY.
