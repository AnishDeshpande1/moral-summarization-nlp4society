SUMMARY:  
In his first major foreign‑policy speech, Sen. Marco Rubio warned that the United States is losing its place as a world military and moral power because of what he called the Obama administration’s **disregard** and **flirted**‑with‑**disdain** approach to **crises**. He argued that **threats** have changed since the Cold War, but the need for American **strength** has not, and that **forethought**, **design**, and years of **build**‑ing are required to **restore** that strength. Rubio pledged to **fund** the military even in times of peace, saying the world is neither **peaceful** nor **stable**. He condemned Obama for **thumbed**‑away **reset**es with Russia, an **open**‑hand **hand** to Iran, and an **opening** to Cuba, claiming the president **embraced** regimes that **undermine** global commerce and **block** U.S. interests. Rubio vowed that any nation that **tries** to **block** trade—**Russia**, **China**, **Iran**—will **receive** a **response**. He called the Obama‑Clinton foreign **policy** a **disaster**, **ineffective**, **negligent**, and **hostile**, and warned that electing such leadership would be **ineffective** and **negligent**. Rubio maintained that the **mightiest** armed forces bring **peace**, not conflict, and defended the Cuban embargo as a stand against **stolen** property and a **failed** economy, insisting sanctions cannot be **lifted** without risk.  
END OF SUMMARY.
