SUMMARY:

In his first major foreign policy speech, Sen. Marco Rubio emphasized the need for a strong military and aggressive steps to fight nations that undermine global commerce. He criticized President Obama's cautious responses to crises overseas, depicting him as overly concerned with softening the perception of the US as a bully. Rubio vowed to prioritize funding the military and take decisive action against aggressive nations like China and Iran. He also reiterated his opposition to normalizing relations with Cuba and his skepticism towards Iran's nuclear program. Rubio's foreign policy agenda is often hawkish in approach, and he has taken tougher stances than some of his Republican colleagues. He believes that the world is safest when America is strongest, and that a strong military leads to more peace, not more conflict.

END OF SUMMARY.
