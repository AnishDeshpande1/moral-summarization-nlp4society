SUMMARY:

In a major foreign policy speech, Senator Marco Rubio emphasized the need for a strong military and a strong moral leader in the US. He criticized the Obama administration for its "disregard for our moral purpose" and "flirtation with disdain" towards foreign governments that undermine global commerce. Rubio argued that the US is losing its place as a world military and moral power due to Obama's "overly cautious responses to crises overseas". He vowed to prioritize funding the military and take aggressive steps to fight nations that undermine global commerce. Rubio also slammed Obama's policies towards Russia, Iran, and Cuba, accusing him of "wasting no time stripping parts from the engine of American strength". He warned that the US must move decisively against aggressive nations like China and Iran, and that his administration would respond firmly to any attempts to block global commerce. Rubio's speech was met with criticism from the Democratic National Committee, which labeled his foreign policy agenda as "neoconservative" and "stuck in the Cold War".

END OF SUMMARY.
