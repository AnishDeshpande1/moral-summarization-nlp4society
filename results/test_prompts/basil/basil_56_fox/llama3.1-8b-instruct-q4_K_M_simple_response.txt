SUMMARY:

In his first major foreign policy speech as a presidential candidate, Marco Rubio emphasized the need for a strong military and aggressive action against nations that undermine global commerce. He criticized the Obama administration's approach to foreign policy, arguing that it has compromised American strength and moral leadership. Rubio stated that the US must prioritize funding its military and take decisive action against countries like China and Iran that threaten economic interests. He also defended keeping Guantanamo Bay open and maintained a hardline stance on Cuba and Iran.

Rubio claimed that Obama's approach to foreign policy was overly cautious and allowed for regimes hostile to American values to flourish. He accused the administration of "wasting no time stripping parts from the engine of American strength" and criticized former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton as a chief architect of this failed policy. Rubio argued that a strong America is necessary for global peace, stating that "the world is at its safest when America is at its strongest."

END OF SUMMARY
