U.S. Sen. Marco Rubio delivered a foreign policy speech in New York, outlining his vision for a strong America as a world leader. He criticized President Obama's policies as overly cautious and morally weak, arguing that the U.S. needs to take aggressive steps to protect its interests and values. Rubio emphasized the importance of a robust military and economic power, saying "the world is at its safest when America is at its strongest." He also took aim at Obama's diplomatic efforts with Cuba and Iran, calling them naive and ineffective. The Democratic National Committee responded by labeling Rubio's foreign policy as "Neoconservative meets Cold War," suggesting that his approach is hawkish and out of touch with the times. Rubio defended his stance, saying he would prioritize American strength and values over dialogue and diplomacy. His speech marked a clear departure from Obama's policies and a return to a more assertive and interventionist foreign policy.
