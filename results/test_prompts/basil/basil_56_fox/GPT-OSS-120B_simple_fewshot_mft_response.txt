SUMMARY:
In his first major foreign‑policy address, Sen. Marco Rubio framed his candidacy around restoring decisive authority and moral order to a United States he says has been subverted by the Obama administration’s “soft‑hearted” diplomacy. He warned that a timid response to crises harms American security and global commerce, arguing that only a strong, well‑funded military can protect both the nation and the innocent peoples threatened by aggressive regimes such as China, Iran and Russia. Rubio portrayed Obama’s outreach to hostile governments as a betrayal of sacred national principles, portraying the Cuban embargo and a hard line on Iran as matters of moral purity rather than mere policy. By casting himself as the guardian of America’s “moral purpose” and the defender of a sanctified, secure world, Rubio positioned his hawkish agenda as a necessary antidote to the perceived decay and subversion of U.S. authority.  
END OF SUMMARY.
