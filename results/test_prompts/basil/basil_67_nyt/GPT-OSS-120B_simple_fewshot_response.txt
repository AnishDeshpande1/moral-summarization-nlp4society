SUMMARY:
Former Mexican President Vicente Fox denounced Donald J. Trump’s upcoming visit to Mexico, declaring the Republican nominee “not welcome” and warning that President Enrique Peña Nieto’s invitation was a grave mistake. Fox condemned Trump’s demonization of Mexicans, his false promises about a border wall, and his shifting, self‑serving policies, labeling him a “false prophet” who exploits his followers. He argued that trusting Trump would betray both Americans and Mexicans, accusing the candidate of desperate poll‑boosting and lacking any capacity to lead a nation or even a business. In response, Trump tweeted that Fox had once invited him to apologize, prompting Fox to fire back on Twitter, demanding respect and reminding Trump that Mexico is not his to manipulate. The exchange frames Trump as disrespectful and untrustworthy, while casting Fox as a moral guardian defending Mexican dignity and urging voters to reject the candidate. END OF SUMMARY.
