SUMMARY:
Former Mexican President Vicente Fox denounced Donald J. Trump’s upcoming visit to Mexico, declaring the Republican nominee “not welcome” and labeling the invitation from President Enrique Peña Nieto a grave mistake. Fox framed Trump’s rhetoric and wall‑building plans as a betrayal of fairness, accusing him of cheating both Americans and Mexicans by exploiting them for political gain. He warned that trusting a leader who constantly shifts ideas and demeans Mexicans violates moral authority and subverts legitimate governance. Fox also cast Trump as a “false prophet” whose discourse degrades the sanctity of the nation, urging citizens to reject him and condemning his disrespect for Mexican dignity. In response, Trump tweeted that Fox had once invited him to apologize, prompting Fox to retort on Twitter, demanding respect and reminding Trump that Mexico “is not yours to play with.” The exchange underscores a clash over legitimate authority, perceived cheating, and the degradation of national honor.

END OF SUMMARY.
