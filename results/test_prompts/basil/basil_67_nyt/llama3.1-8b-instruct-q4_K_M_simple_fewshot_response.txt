SUMMARY:

Former Mexican President Vicente Fox publicly rejected Donald Trump's visit to Mexico, calling him "not welcome" in the country. Fox criticized current President Enrique Peña Nieto for inviting Trump, saying it was a "big mistake" and a "political risk." He accused Trump of demonizing Mexicans and vowed that Mexico would never pay for his proposed border wall. Fox also questioned Trump's ability to lead a nation, calling him a "false prophet" who is misleading his followers. Trump responded by referencing a past incident where Fox apologized for using profanity to refuse to pay for the wall, but Fox fired back with a Twitter post accusing Trump of lying and demanding respect.

Fox's harsh criticism highlights the deep-seated tensions between Trump and Mexican leaders, with Fox framing Trump's visit as an affront to Mexican dignity. The exchange underscores concerns about Trump's divisive rhetoric and his inability to build trust with foreign leaders. As Trump prepares to meet with Peña Nieto, Fox's words serve as a warning that Mexicans will not stand idly by while their country is treated disrespectfully.
