SUMMARY:

The White House released over 100 pages of emails regarding the development of talking points about the Benghazi attack in 2012. Contrary to Republican claims, the emails show that multiple government agencies contributed to changes in the talking points, not just the State Department or the White House for political gain. The CIA was involved in removing references to al Qaeda and previous security warnings, citing concerns about accuracy and the ongoing FBI investigation. Critics argue that the administration's concerns were driven by a desire to protect its reputation on terrorism.

Senior administration officials maintain that their primary goal was to provide accurate information while protecting an ongoing investigation. The release of these emails may put some Republican theories to rest, but others see it as a political move to deflect criticism. The document dump raises questions about the motivations behind changes and who sought them.

END OF SUMMARY.
