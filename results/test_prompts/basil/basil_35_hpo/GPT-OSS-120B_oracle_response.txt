SUMMARY:  
The White House released 100 pages of emails about the Obama administration’s talking‑points on the Sept. 11, 2012 Benghazi **attack** after Republican **charges** that officials had **attempted** to **alter** the narrative. The documents show that multiple agencies, not just the State Department, **ignored** some CIA suggestions and **changed** language to avoid prejudicing an FBI investigation. State Department spokeswoman Victoria Nuland asked to remove a line about prior **attacks** because it could be **abused** by members of Congress to **beat** up the department for **ignoring** warnings. CIA deputy director Michael J. Morell also wanted that line removed, deeming it irrelevant and **inaccurately** framed. Critics claim the administration was **willing** to **accommodate** political concerns, while officials say the revisions were meant to **protect** the integrity of the investigation and provide accurate **statements**. The release is intended to end the partisan **demonstrations** over Benghazi, showing that inter‑agency **providing** of facts was based on the best information available and to keep diplomats **protected** while the government **works** on future security.  

END OF SUMMARY.
