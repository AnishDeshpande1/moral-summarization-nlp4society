SUMMARY:

The White House released emails surrounding the Benghazi attack, responding to Republican claims of a cover-up. The emails show that multiple government agencies, not just the State Department, contributed to changes in the talking points used by U.N. Ambassador Susan Rice. Senior administration officials stressed that language referencing al Qaeda was removed at the CIA's request, and that Victoria Nuland's concerns about public image were also driven by a desire to avoid hurting the FBI investigation. The documents paint a more nuanced picture of the process than an earlier leaked email suggested. While the release appears to soften some criticism, it may embolden others who see the administration accommodating demands for documents.

The CIA has also conducted an internal review of the talking points changes, which showed that many changes were made due to concerns about accuracy and bureaucratic matters. The White House's timing in releasing the emails raises questions about why they chose to do so now, after months of criticism.

END OF SUMMARY.
