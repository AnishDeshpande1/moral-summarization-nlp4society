SUMMARY:

The White House released 100 pages of emails regarding the development of talking points about the Benghazi attack, in response to Republican charges that there was a massive political cover-up. The emails show that multiple government agencies drove changes to the talking points, not just the State Department. Critics argued that the administration attempted to alter the narrative for political gain, but senior officials stressed that language was removed at the request of the CIA to avoid prejudging the outcome of an FBI investigation. However, some critics still portray the administration as willing to accommodate demands and selectively release information.

The emails also show that State Department spokeswoman Victoria Nuland requested changes to remove references to previous security warnings, concerned that it could be abused by Congress to "beat up" the State Department. The White House released the documents in an attempt to put the issue to rest, saying they can now focus on working together to protect those serving overseas.

END OF SUMMARY.
