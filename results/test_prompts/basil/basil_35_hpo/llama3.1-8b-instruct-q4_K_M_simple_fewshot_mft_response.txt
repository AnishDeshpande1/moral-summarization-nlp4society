SUMMARY:

The White House released emails related to the Benghazi attack, addressing criticism from congressional Republicans that there was a massive political "cover-up". The documents show that multiple government agencies contributed to changes in the talking points, including the CIA and State Department. Critics argue that the State Department's concerns about its public image led to removal of references to al Qaeda and previous security warnings. However, senior administration officials stated that these language changes were made at the request of the CIA to avoid prejudging the outcome of a FBI investigation.

The release raises questions about why the administration chose to make the emails public now, after months of criticism. The document dump could embolden critics who see an administration willing to accommodate demands for documents. The White House hopes that this disclosure will put to rest the Benghazi issue, but critics argue that the seemingly political nature of the State Department's concerns raises questions about motivations behind changes.

The release highlights concerns over Authority/Subversion, as it appears that multiple agencies were involved in changing the talking points, potentially undermining the original message. The document also raises questions about Care/Harm, as the removal of references to al Qaeda and previous security warnings may have obscured the severity of the attack. Furthermore, the timing of the release and the White House's motivations for making it public raise concerns about Fairness/Cheating.

END OF SUMMARY.
