SUMMARY: The White House released 100 pages of emails detailing how the Obama administration crafted talking points about the Sept. 11, 2012 Benghazi attack. The documents show that multiple agencies, not just the State Department, edited the language, often at the request of the CIA, which sought to avoid premature attribution to al‑Qaeda and to protect an ongoing FBI investigation. State Department officials, including Victoria Nuland, also pushed for changes, such as removing references to prior attacks, citing concerns about political backlash. CIA Deputy Director Michael J. Morell independently recommended similar deletions, arguing they were irrelevant or could prejudice investigations. An internal CIA review confirmed many edits were made for accuracy and investigative reasons rather than political motives. Republicans, however, argue the changes were intended to shield the State Department from criticism and contradict the White House’s claim that only a single word was altered. House Speaker John Boehner’s spokesperson said the emails confirm partisan-driven revisions. White House spokesman Eric Schultz said the release should settle the issue, emphasizing the interagency process focused on facts and security. END OF SUMMARY.
