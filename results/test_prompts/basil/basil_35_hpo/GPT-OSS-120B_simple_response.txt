SUMMARY:  
The White House released 100 pages of emails to confront Republican accusations that the Obama administration deliberately “covered up” the true nature of the Sept. 11, 2012 Benghazi attack. The documents show that revisions to the talking points were driven not by partisan spin but by multiple agencies—including the CIA and State Department—concerned with accuracy, an ongoing FBI probe, and the risk of prejudicing investigations. Both CIA officials and State Department spokeswoman Victoria Nuland sought to omit references to al Qa’ida and prior warnings, arguing they were either premature or could be weaponized by Congress against the State Department. While critics portray these edits as a self‑serving attempt to protect the administration’s anti‑terrorism image, senior officials maintain the changes were procedural and factual, not political. The timing of the release—after months of pressure—raises questions about transparency, yet the White House argues the disclosure should finally settle the Benghazi controversy and allow focus on protecting U.S. personnel abroad.  

END OF SUMMARY.
