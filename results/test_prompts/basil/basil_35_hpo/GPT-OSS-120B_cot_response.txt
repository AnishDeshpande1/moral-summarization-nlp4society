**SUMMARY:**  
The White House released 100 pages of emails showing that multiple agencies, not just the State Department, altered the Benghazi talking points. Republicans had claimed a political “cover‑up” aimed at protecting the administration’s reputation and avoiding criticism for being “tough” on terrorism. The emails reveal that the CIA, not only State officials like Victoria Nuland, requested the removal of references to al Qa’ida and earlier warnings, deeming such language premature, unprofessional, and potentially abusive if used by Congress to attack the State Department. Senior officials say changes were driven by concerns over accuracy, an ongoing FBI investigation, and the need to protect the investigation rather than to manipulate the 2012 election. Critics argue the revisions reflect a political motive to shield the administration, while the White House maintains the disclosures answer the long‑standing accusations and questions.
