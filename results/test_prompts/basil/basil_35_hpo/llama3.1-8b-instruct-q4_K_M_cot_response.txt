**SUMMARY:**
The White House released the full set of emails surrounding the Obama administration's development of talking points describing the Sept. 11, 2012, attack on the U.S. outpost in Benghazi, Libya. The release aims to put an end to the "cover-up" accusations from congressional Republicans that there was a massive political influence on the talking points to sway the 2012 election. However, nothing in the emails supports these theories. Instead, senior administration officials used the emails to show that multiple government agencies drove changes to the talking points, not just the State Department. They stressed that language referencing al Qaeda and previous security warnings was removed due to concerns about accuracy and bureaucratic matters. The White House's document release may embolden critics who see an administration willing to accommodate demands for documents. END OF SUMMARY.
