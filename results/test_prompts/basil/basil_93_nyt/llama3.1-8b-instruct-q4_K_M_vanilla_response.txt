SUMMARY:

President Trump offered Democrats a deal on Saturday to end the partial government shutdown: temporary protections for 700,000 young undocumented immigrants in exchange for $5.7 billion in funding for a wall along the southern border. However, the proposal was met with rejection from Speaker Nancy Pelosi and other Democrats, who said it did not offer permanent protections for Dreamers and was an unacceptable compromise. Trump's plan also included restoring Temporary Protected Status (TPS) protection for 300,000 people and allowing 700,000 Dreamers to keep their protections for three more years. The president's advisers have been searching for an exit strategy as the shutdown enters its fifth week, with polls showing a majority of the public blaming Trump.

The deal was seen by some as an attempt to drive a wedge within the Democratic party between those who want to hold out for a more generous solution and those who may feel enough pressure to end the shutdown. The proposal will now be put to a vote in the Senate, where Democrats will have to decide whether to block it or let it come up for a vote.

END OF SUMMARY.
