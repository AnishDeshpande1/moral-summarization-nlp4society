SUMMARY:

President Trump proposed a deal to end the partial government shutdown, offering temporary protections for 700,000 young undocumented immigrants (Dreamers) in exchange for $5.7 billion in funding for a border wall. However, the proposal was met with criticism from Democrats, who rejected it as "hostage-taking" and not a genuine compromise. Trump's plan would extend DACA protections for three more years but does not offer permanent solutions for Dreamers. The deal also includes $800 million for humanitarian assistance and $805 million for drug detection technology.

Trump's advisors see the proposal as an attempt to drive a wedge within the Democratic party, forcing some lawmakers to compromise on border security. Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell has expressed support for the plan, saying it "strikes a fair compromise." However, Democrats remain divided, with some calling the deal "fake promises" and others warning that it could be seen as amnesty.

END OF SUMMARY.
