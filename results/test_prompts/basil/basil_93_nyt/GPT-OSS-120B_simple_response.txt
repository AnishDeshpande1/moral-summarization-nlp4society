SUMMARY:
President Trump, under mounting public backlash over a five‑week partial government shutdown, tried to recast himself as a peacemaker by offering Democrats a deal: $5.7 billion for a “steel barrier” on the southern border in exchange for temporary protections for about 700,000 Dreamers and restoration of TPS for 300,000 people. He framed the proposal as “fair, reasonable and common‑sense compromise,” even softening his rhetoric and pairing the address with a naturalization ceremony to appear supportive of legal immigration.  

Democratic leaders, however, rejected the offer outright, denouncing it as “hostage‑taking” and “fake promises” that provide no permanent relief for Dreamers and reward a president who previously stripped those protections. Even some Republicans, including Heritage Foundation security expert James Carafano and Senator Susan Collins, warned the plan amounted to amnesty and could spur more illegal immigration.  

The moral narrative emerging is one of a president desperate to shift blame and extract concessions, using limited humanitarian gestures to mask a hard‑line wall agenda, while opponents view the offer as an unethical bargain that exploits vulnerable immigrants for political leverage. The stalemate persists, with Senate leader Mitch McConnell weighing whether to bring the proposal to a vote amid doubts about Trump’s willingness to sign it.  
END OF SUMMARY.
