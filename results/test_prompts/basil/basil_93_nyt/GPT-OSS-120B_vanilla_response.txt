SUMMARY: President Trump attempted to break the fifth‑week partial government shutdown by offering Democrats a deal that would restore temporary protections for about 700,000 Dreamers and 300,000 TPS recipients in exchange for $5.7 billion for a “steel barrier” along the southern border. The proposal, delivered in a 13‑minute White House address, also included $800 million for humanitarian aid, $805 million for drug‑detection technology, more border agents, and new immigration judges. Speaker Nancy Pelosi and Senate Democratic leader Chuck Schumer rejected the offer, calling it hostage‑taking and insufficient because it lacks permanent Dreamer protections. Some Republicans, including Sen. Susan Collins, welcomed the move, while others on the right denounced it as amnesty. Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell said he would consider bringing the bill to the floor only if Trump pledged to sign it. With the shutdown deadline looming, the proposal may open limited negotiation space, but Democrats remain firm in refusing any deal tied to border‑wall funding. END OF SUMMARY.
