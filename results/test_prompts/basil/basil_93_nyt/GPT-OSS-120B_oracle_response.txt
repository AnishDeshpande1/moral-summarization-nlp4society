SUMMARY:
President Trump, amid a fifth‑week shutdown that many view as a self‑inflicted crisis, offered a “compromise” to Democrats: temporary protections for roughly 700,000 Dreamers and 300,000 TPS recipients in exchange for $5.7 billion for a “barrier” on the southern border. Critics on both sides called the deal a nonstarter, labeling it “hostage‑taking” and accusing Trump of raising false promises after he previously revoked DACA and rescinded TPS protections that had shielded vulnerable immigrants. The proposal was framed as a way to break the logjam and provide a path to end the impasse, pairing border security funding with humanitarian assistance, drug‑detection technology and new immigration judges. Pelosi and Senate Democrats rejected it, saying it offers no permanent protection and undermines the rule of law, while some Republicans warned it could encourage illegal immigration and incentivize human‑trafficking. Senate Majority Leader McConnell indicated he would only put the bill on the floor if Trump gave a public reassurance he would sign it, hoping the move might lead to constructive debate and finally shut down the government. END OF SUMMARY.
