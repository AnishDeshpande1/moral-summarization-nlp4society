SUMMARY:

President Trump offered Democrats a deal to end the partial government shutdown, which is entering its fifth week. He proposed temporary protections for 700,000 young undocumented immigrants in exchange for $5.7 billion in funding for a wall along the southern border. However, this proposal was met with skepticism and criticism from both parties, with Democrats calling it "hostage taking" and a "nonstarter." The deal revokes some of Trump's previous policies, such as rescinding Temporary Protected Status (TPS) for certain countries, and offers to shield Dreamers from deportation for three more years. Despite this, the proposal is seen as an attempt to break the logjam and provide Congress with a path forward in addressing the immigration crisis. However, the deal has been deemed unacceptable by many Democrats, who argue that it raises false hopes and undermines their commitment to finding a permanent solution for Dreamers. The shutdown stalemate continues, creating increasing nervousness on Capitol Hill.

END OF SUMMARY.
