**SUMMARY:**

President Trump offered a deal to Democrats in exchange for $5.7 billion in funding for a border barrier, which would provide temporary protections for 700,000 young undocumented immigrants and restore T.P.S. protection for 300,000 people. However, the proposal was rejected by Speaker Nancy Pelosi and Senator Chuck Schumer, who deemed it "hostage taking" and not a genuine compromise. The Democrats continued to insist that they will not negotiate with Trump over border security until the government reopens. Trump's plan included funding for humanitarian assistance, drug detection technology, and additional border agents, but was criticized by both Democrats and some on the right as providing amnesty to undocumented immigrants. With the shutdown entering its fifth week, Trump's advisers are searching for an exit strategy, and Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell will put legislation incorporating Trump's proposal on the floor.
