SUMMARY:
President Trump, desperate to end the fifth‑week shutdown, tried to assert his authority by offering a “compromise”: temporary protection for roughly 700,000 Dreamers and 300,000 TPS recipients in exchange for $5.7 billion for a “steel barrier” on the southern border. Democrats, led by Nancy Pelosi and Chuck Schumer, denounced the move as subversive hostage‑taking, refusing to negotiate until the government reopens and warning that the deal cheats Dreamers by offering only short‑term relief after Trump himself revoked their protections. The proposal also raised care concerns, as the shutdown harms federal workers and families while the temporary immigration safeguards do little to alleviate their suffering. Even some Republicans labeled the plan as amnesty, arguing it rewards illegal entry and undermines the rule of law. As Senate leader Mitch McConnell weighs whether to bring the bill to a vote, the debate centers on whether Trump’s authority can be checked by a fair, lasting solution or whether the offer merely shifts blame and perpetuates harm to vulnerable people. END OF SUMMARY.
