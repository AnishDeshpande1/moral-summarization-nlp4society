SUMMARY:
President Trump, under mounting public pressure from a five‑week partial shutdown, offered a last‑minute “compromise”: restore temporary protections for roughly 700,000 Dreamers and 300,000 TPS recipients in exchange for $5.7 billion to fund a “steel barrier” on the southern border. While the president framed the deal as fair and reasonable, Democrats blasted it as hostage‑taking and empty promises, noting it provides only temporary relief and no path to citizenship for the very groups Trump previously stripped of protection. Speaker Nancy Pelosi and Senate leader Chuck Schumer rejected the offer outright, insisting negotiations cannot begin until the government reopens. Even some Republicans warned the plan amounts to amnesty. The proposal, tied to a softened rhetoric and a naturalization ceremony, aims to force Democrats to shoulder blame for the shutdown, but critics argue it merely trades short‑term concessions for a continued wall‑funding demand, leaving vulnerable immigrants in limbo and deepening the political stalemate. END OF SUMMARY.
