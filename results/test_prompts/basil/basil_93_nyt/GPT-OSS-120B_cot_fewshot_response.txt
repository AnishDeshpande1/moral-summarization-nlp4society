**SUMMARY:**  
President Trump, facing mounting public backlash over the fifth‑week partial shutdown, offered Democrats a deal that would temporarily protect roughly 700,000 Dreamers and restore TPS for 300,000 people in exchange for $5.7 billion for a “steel barrier” on the southern border. Speaker Nancy Pelosi rejected the proposal before Trump even spoke, and Senate Democratic leader Chuck Schumer denounced it as “more hostage‑taking” rather than a compromise. While Republicans hailed the offer as a “fair compromise,” many Democrats called it a “fake” and “unacceptable” promise that amounts to amnesty, incensing both parties. Critics on the right also labeled the plan as amnesty, arguing it would encourage illegal immigration. The proposal, seen as a desperate attempt to shift pressure onto Democrats, failed to secure bipartisan support, leaving the shutdown stalemate unresolved.
