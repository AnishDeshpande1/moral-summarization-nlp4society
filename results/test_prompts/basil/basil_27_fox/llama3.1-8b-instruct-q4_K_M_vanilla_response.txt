SUMMARY:

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu appears in a new ad aimed at making Iran a major campaign issue in the US presidential election, particularly in Florida. The ad, paid for by Secure America Now, features Netanyahu's comments from a recent news conference where he expressed frustration with the Obama administration's handling of Iran's nuclear program. The ad is set to air in Miami and other key areas with large Jewish populations. A separate $5 million TV advertising blitz by the Republican Jewish Coalition aims to target Jewish voters in swing states.

The ads come as President Obama leads Mitt Romney 49-44 in a recent Fox News poll in Florida, which is considered a toss-up state with 29 electoral votes. Netanyahu has been critical of the Obama administration's handling of Iran and recently appeared on NBC's "Meet the Press" to urge Obama to take a tougher line with Tehran.

END OF SUMMARY.
