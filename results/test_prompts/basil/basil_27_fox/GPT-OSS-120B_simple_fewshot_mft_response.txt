SUMMARY:
Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, while officially staying out of the U.S. presidential race, fronts a new $1 million television ad funded by the conservative nonprofit Secure America Now that targets Florida’s pivotal Jewish electorate. The ad repurposes Netanyahu’s recent criticism of the Obama administration’s “soft” stance on Iran, warning that “every day that passes, Iran gets closer and closer to a nuclear bomb” and demanding “American strength, not apologies.” By invoking a respected foreign authority to condemn what the ad frames as Obama’s subversive appeasement, the message seeks to rally voters around a more assertive, traditional leadership line. Parallel to this, the Republican Jewish Coalition launches a $5 million campaign in swing states, portraying Jewish voters as feeling “buyer’s remorse” for supporting Obama and suggesting a betrayal of their interests. Both efforts cast the incumbent president as weak and disloyal to national security, while elevating strong, decisive authority as the proper response to the Iranian threat. This framing positions the election as a choice between steadfast authority and subversive leadership. END OF SUMMARY.
