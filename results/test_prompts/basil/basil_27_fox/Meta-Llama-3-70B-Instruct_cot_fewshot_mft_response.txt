SUMMARY:
Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu stars in a new ad in Florida, aiming to make Iran a major campaign issue. Netanyahu's words, taken from a recent news conference, express frustration at the Obama administration's failure to establish "red lines" for reining in Iran's rogue nuclear weapons program. He emphasizes the need for American strength, not apologies, to prevent Iran from getting nuclear weapons. The ad, paid for by Secure America Now, could have a powerful influence in heavily-Jewish markets, including Miami. Netanyahu's message is seen as strong and powerful, and his appearance on NBC's "Meet the Press" last Sunday urged Obama to take a tougher line with Iran. The ad highlights the "buyer's remorse" felt by many in the Jewish community, who voted for Obama four years ago but are now disillusioned with his economic policies and his policies toward Israel.
