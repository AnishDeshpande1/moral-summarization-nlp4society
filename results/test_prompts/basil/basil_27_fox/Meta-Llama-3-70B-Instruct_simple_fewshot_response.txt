SUMMARY:

A new political ad featuring Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu is set to air in Florida, a key battleground state, aiming to make Iran a major campaign issue. The ad, paid for by conservative advocacy group Secure America Now, uses Netanyahu's words from a recent news conference, expressing frustration at the Obama administration's failure to establish "red lines" for reining in Iran's nuclear program. The ad's message is clear: the world needs American strength, not apologies. This ad could have a powerful influence in heavily-Jewish markets, including Miami, and could sway the election. The ad is part of a larger effort by conservative groups to target Jewish voters, who are increasingly disillusioned with President Obama's economic policies and stance on Israel. The Republican Jewish Coalition has also launched a $5 million TV advertising blitz aimed at Jewish voters in swing states.

END OF SUMMARY.
