**SUMMARY:**
Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, who says he will stay out of the U.S. presidential race, appears in a new Secure America Now ad targeting Florida’s pivotal Jewish electorate. The spot uses Netanyahu’s recent remarks about the Obama administration’s “failure” to set “red lines” on Iran’s “rogue” nuclear program, emphasizing his “frustration” that “every day … Iran gets closer and closer to a nuclear bomb.” A voice‑over concludes, “The world needs American strength, not apologies.” The nonprofit is spending $1 million on the ad, calling Netanyahu a “powerful messenger” with a “powerful message” that resonates with voters who feel “disillusioned” by Obama’s policies and sense an “erosion” of Jewish support. Secure America Now claims many believe Iran could share nuclear weapons with terrorists, and that the U.S. must be “determined” to act before that happens. The campaign joins a separate $5 million Republican Jewish Coalition effort aimed at swing‑state voters.
