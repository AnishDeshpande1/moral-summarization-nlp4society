SUMMARY:

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu is starring in a new ad in Florida, a key battleground state, to make Iran a major campaign issue in the US presidential election. The ad, paid for by a conservative advocacy group, features Netanyahu expressing frustration with the Obama administration's failure to establish "red lines" for reining in Iran's nuclear program. He warns that Iran is getting closer to a nuclear bomb every day and that the world needs American strength, not apologies. The ad aims to influence Jewish voters in Florida, who are disillusioned with Obama's economic policies and his stance on Israel. This comes as polls show an erosion in Jewish support for Obama. Netanyahu's message is clear: the US must take a tougher line with Iran to prevent it from getting nuclear weapons. The ad's powerful message could sway the election in Florida, which has 29 electoral votes.

END OF SUMMARY.
