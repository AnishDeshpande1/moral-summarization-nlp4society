SUMMARY:

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu is set to appear in a new ad aimed at making Iran a major campaign issue in the US presidential election, particularly in the key battleground state of Florida. The ad, paid for by the nonprofit conservative advocacy group Secure America Now, features Netanyahu's recent comments on the Obama administration's failure to establish "red lines" for reining in Iran's nuclear program. Netanyahu urges Americans to demand strength from their leaders rather than apologies, highlighting the risks of a nuclear-armed Iran. This ad is part of a larger effort by conservative groups to sway Jewish voters and make Iran a major campaign issue.

The Republican Jewish Coalition has also launched a separate $5 million TV advertising blitz aimed at Jewish voters in swing states. Netanyahu's involvement in these ads reflects his long-standing concerns about the US approach to Iran and his desire for tougher action against Tehran. These efforts aim to capitalize on what some polls suggest is growing disillusionment among Jewish voters with President Obama's economic policies and his stance on Israel.

END OF SUMMARY.
