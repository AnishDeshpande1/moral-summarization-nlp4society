SUMMARY: Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu says he will stay out of the U.S. presidential race but appears in a new Florida ad that warns of Iran’s nuclear “failure” to be stopped. Quoting a recent press conference, he warns that every day “Iran gets closer and closer to a nuclear bomb,” and asks, “Wait until when?” The ad, funded by the conservative nonprofit Secure America Now, ends with a plea that “the world needs American strength, not apologies.” It aims to sway heavily‑Jewish markets in Miami and other swing‑state locales, suggesting that Iran could “share” nuclear weapons with terrorists and urging the U.S. to “prevent” that outcome. Netanyahu’s message, echoed on “Meet the Press,” stresses that Obama must act now to “prevent” Iran from “getting” a bomb, rather than waiting for a “getting” of more concessions. The campaign spends $1 million in Florida, while the Republican Jewish Coalition launches a $5 million blitz to “get” back disillusioned Jewish voters. END OF SUMMARY.
