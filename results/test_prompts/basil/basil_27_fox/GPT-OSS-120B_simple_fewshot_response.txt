SUMMARY:
Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, while staying out of the U.S. presidential race, is the centerpiece of a new television ad funded by the conservative nonprofit Secure America Now that will run in Florida’s heavily‑Jewish markets. The ad re‑uses Netanyahu’s recent remarks condemning the Obama administration for not setting “red lines” on Iran’s nuclear program, warning that “every day that passes, Iran gets closer and closer to a nuclear bomb.” A voice‑over concludes, “The world needs American strength, not apologies,” positioning the message as a critique of President Obama’s approach. Secure America Now is spending about $1 million on the spots in Miami, West Palm Beach and Fort Myers, hoping to sway undecided voters in the battleground state, which polls show as a tight race (Obama 49% vs. Romney 44%). At the same time, the Republican Jewish Coalition has launched a separate $5 million TV blitz targeting Jewish voters in swing states, arguing that many feel “buyer’s remorse” after supporting Obama in 2008. Both campaigns aim to turn Iran‑related security concerns into a decisive issue for the 2012 election.  
END OF SUMMARY.
