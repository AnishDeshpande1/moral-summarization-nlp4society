**SUMMARY:**  
Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, who keeps a frosty distance from the U.S. presidential race, appears in a new Florida ad that frames Iran as a looming nuclear threat. Citing his own frustration with the Obama administration’s “failure” to draw firm “red lines,” Netanyahu warns that a rogue Iran is inching closer to a bomb. The ad’s voice‑over stresses that “the world needs American strength, not apologies,” casting strength as the rightful authority against subversive threats. Paid for by the conservative nonprofit Secure America Now, the spot portrays Netanyahu as a powerful messenger whose warning resonates with Jewish voters who feel disillusioned and experience buyer’s remorse over past support for Obama. The campaign, costing $1 million, aims to tap into perceived erosion of Jewish backing for the president and to sway Florida’s 29 electoral votes. A parallel $5 million effort by the Republican Jewish Coalition amplifies the same subversive narrative across swing states.
