A new ad featuring Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu will air in Florida, aiming to make Iran a major campaign issue in the presidential election. The ad highlights Netanyahu's frustration with the Obama administration's failure to establish "red lines" for reining in Iran's rogue nuclear weapons program. Despite not mentioning President Obama or Mitt Romney by name, the ad's message is clear: the world needs American strength, not apologies. Secure America Now, a nonprofit conservative advocacy group, paid $1 million for the ad, which will air in heavily-Jewish markets including Miami. This ad comes on the heels of Netanyahu's recent appearance on NBC's "Meet the Press," where he urged Obama to take a tougher line with Iran over its nuclear program. The ad is expected to have a powerful influence in Florida, particularly among Jewish voters who are increasingly disillusioned with President Obama's economic policies and his stance on Israel.
