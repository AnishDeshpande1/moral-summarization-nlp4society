SUMMARY:

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has appeared in a new ad in Florida, a key battleground state, aiming to make Iran a major campaign issue in the US presidential election. The ad, paid for by nonprofit conservative advocacy group Secure America Now, features Netanyahu expressing frustration over the Obama administration's failure to establish "red lines" for reining in Iran's nuclear program. The ad's message is that the world needs American strength, not apologies. The ad is set to air in Miami, West Palm Beach, and Fort Myers, and is costing $1 million. Meanwhile, the Republican Jewish Coalition has launched a separate $5 million TV advertising blitz aimed at Jewish voters in swing states, highlighting "buyer's remorse" among Jewish voters who voted for Obama four years ago but are now disillusioned with his economic policies and policies toward Israel.

END OF SUMMARY.
