SUMMARY: Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, while staying out of the U.S. presidential race, appears in a new television ad targeting Florida’s pivotal electorate. Funded by the conservative nonprofit Secure America Now, the $1 million spot uses Netanyahu’s recent criticism of the Obama administration’s “soft” stance on Iran’s nuclear ambitions, urging “American strength, not apologies.” The ad will run in Jewish‑heavy markets such as Miami, West Palm Beach and Fort Myers, aiming to make Iran a key issue in the closely contested state that holds 29 electoral votes. A Fox News poll shows Obama leading Romney 49‑44 in Florida. Concurrently, the Republican Jewish Coalition has launched a separate $5 million ad campaign aimed at disaffected Jewish voters in swing states, accusing them of “buyer’s remorse” for supporting Obama in 2008. Netanyahu also pressed Obama on Iran during a recent “Meet the Press” interview, urging a tougher pre‑emptive approach. Both advertising pushes seek to sway Jewish and broader voters by linking national security concerns with the upcoming election. END OF SUMMARY.
