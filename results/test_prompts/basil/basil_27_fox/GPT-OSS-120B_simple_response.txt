SUMMARY: Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, while officially staying out of the U.S. presidential race, becomes the moral centerpiece of a $1 million television ad bought by the conservative nonprofit Secure America Now. The spot, set for Florida’s pivotal Jewish markets, warns that Iran is inching toward a nuclear bomb and condemns the Obama administration for “waiting” and offering “apologies” instead of decisive strength. The ad’s moral message—America must project power, not hesitation—aims to sway swing‑state voters by casting Iran’s threat as an ethical imperative for U.S. leadership. Parallel to this, the Republican Jewish Coalition launches a $5 million campaign targeting disillusioned Jewish voters, portraying their previous support for Obama as “buyer’s remorse” over his economic and Israel policies. Both efforts frame the election as a moral choice: support a hard‑line stance against Iran and reaffirm loyalty to Israel, or risk complacency and danger. The tactics underscore how foreign policy fears are being weaponized to influence American voters in a tightly contested Florida, where the stakes are both political and moral. END OF SUMMARY.
