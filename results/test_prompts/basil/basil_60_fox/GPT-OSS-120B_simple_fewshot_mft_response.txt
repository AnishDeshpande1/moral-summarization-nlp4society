SUMMARY:
Former Mexican President Vicente Fox loudly rejected any Mexican contribution to Donald Trump’s proposed border wall, telling Fusion’s Jorge Ramos, “I’m not going to pay for that f***ing wall—he should pay, he’s got the money.” Fox framed Trump as a subversive, self‑serving figure who betrays both Republican and Democratic principles, calling him “egocentric” and “not a Republican or a Democrat.” Emphasizing care for Hispanics, Fox warned that the “false prophet” would lead them into a desert of false promises and that the United States will fail if it falls into the hands of such a “crazy man.” He appealed to loyalty, urging U.S. Hispanics to open their eyes and defend the nation that hosts them, not to be misled. Fox echoed former President Felipe Calderón’s claim that Mexico will not pay a cent for the “stupid, useless” wall, portraying Trump’s demand as unfair cheating of Mexico. The remarks also highlighted the broader political clash, referencing journalist Jorge Ramos’s confrontation with Trump and Vice President Joe Biden’s criticism of Trump’s dangerous rhetoric, underscoring the moral stakes of authority, care, fairness and loyalty.  
END OF SUMMARY.
