SUMMARY:

Former Mexican President Vicente Fox has denounced Donald Trump's proposal for a border wall, stating that he will not contribute to it and that Trump should pay for it himself. In an interview with Jorge Ramos, Fox expressed concern about the results of the Nevada caucus, where Trump won 44% of the Hispanic vote. Fox described those who voted for Trump as "followers of a false prophet" and urged Hispanics in the US to "open their eyes" to Trump's policies. Fox also questioned Trump's allegiance to any political party, calling him "egocentric." This is not the first time that Mexican leaders have rejected Trump's wall proposal; President Felipe Calderón previously stated that Mexico will not pay for it.

Fox's comments echo those of his successor and other critics who see Trump as a threat to American values. US Vice President Joe Biden also weighed in, calling some presidential candidates' rhetoric "dangerous" and "ill-advised." Fox's interview with Ramos comes amidst growing concern about the impact of Trump's policies on international relations.

END OF SUMMARY.
