SUMMARY: Former Mexican President Vicente Fox loudly rejected any Mexican contribution to Donald Trump’s proposed U.S.–Mexico border wall, calling it a “f***ing wall” and insisting Trump should fund it himself. Fox warned Hispanic voters in the United States that supporting Trump is a mistake, labeling the president a “false prophet” who will lead them “to the desert” and urging them to “open your eyes” to protect the nation. He stressed that his stance is not about race or creed but about safeguarding the country from a “crazy man.” Fox also dismissed Trump’s claim to Republican or Democratic principles, describing him as egocentric and unaffiliated. His remarks echo those of ex‑president Felipe Calderón, who likewise declared Mexico will not pay a cent for the wall, calling it useless. The comments come amid Trump’s continued insistence on the wall, a recent confrontation between journalist Jorge Ramos and Trump, and Vice President Joe Biden’s criticism of Trump’s “dangerous” rhetoric during a U.S.–Mexico economic summit.  

END OF SUMMARY.
