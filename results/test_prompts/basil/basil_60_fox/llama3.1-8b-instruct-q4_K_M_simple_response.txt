SUMMARY:

Former Mexican President Vincente Fox has denounced Donald Trump's proposal for a border wall, stating that he will not contribute to its construction. Fox called on Trump to pay for the wall himself, citing his wealth and responsibility as a potential president. Fox also expressed concern about the results of the Nevada caucus, where Trump won 44% of the Hispanic vote, calling them "followers of a false prophet." He urged Hispanics in the US to "open their eyes" to the dangers of a Trump presidency.

Fox's comments echo those of his successor, Felipe Calderón, who also stated that Mexico will not pay for the wall. The situation has sparked criticism from U.S. Vice President Joe Biden, who described Trump's rhetoric as "dangerous" and "incredibly ill-advised." Fox's interview comes amid a growing controversy over Trump's immigration policies and his relations with the Hispanic community.
