SUMMARY:

Former Mexican President Vicente Fox strongly criticized Donald Trump's proposal for a border wall, stating that Mexico will not pay for it and calling Trump a "false prophet" who is leading his followers astray. Fox expressed concern about Trump's popularity among Hispanic voters in the Nevada caucus, warning them to "open their eyes" and recognize the dangers of electing Trump. Fox also questioned Trump's allegiance to any political party, saying he is simply egocentric. Fox's comments echo those of his successor, Felipe Calderón, who also rejected the idea of Mexico paying for a border wall. The controversy comes as U.S. Vice President Joe Biden visited Mexico and condemned Trump's "dangerous" rhetoric. Fox's interview with Jorge Ramos highlights the deep divisions between Trump and many in the international community.
