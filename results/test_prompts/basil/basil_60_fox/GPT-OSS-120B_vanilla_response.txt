SUMMARY: Former Mexican President Vicente Fox publicly refused to fund Donald Trump’s proposed U.S.–Mexico border wall, telling journalist Jorge Ramos that Trump should pay for it himself. Fox criticized Trump’s appeal to Hispanic voters, calling them “followers of a false prophet” and warning that Trump’s leadership would harm the nation. He also disputed Trump’s affiliation with either major U.S. party, labeling him “egocentric.” Fox’s remarks echo those of his successor, former President Felipe Calderón, who also said Mexico will not pay a cent for the wall and called it useless. The comments come amid ongoing U.S. political tensions: Trump reiterated his wall stance after Calderón’s remarks, while journalist Jorge Ramos previously clashed with Trump in Iowa. Meanwhile, U.S. Vice President Joe Biden, during a visit to Mexico for an economic summit, condemned Trump’s rhetoric as dangerous and unrepresentative of most Americans. END OF SUMMARY.
