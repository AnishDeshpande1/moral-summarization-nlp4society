SUMMARY:

Former Mexican President Vicente Fox strongly condemned Donald Trump's proposal for a border wall, stating that Mexico will not pay for it. Fox called Trump a "false prophet" who is leading Hispanics astray and warned them to "open their eyes" to the dangers of his administration. He also questioned Trump's allegiance to any political party, calling him egocentric. Fox echoed the sentiments of his successor, Felipe Calderón, who had previously stated that Mexico will not pay for a border wall. The comments come as Vice President Joe Biden visited Mexico and made a thinly-veiled attack on Trump's rhetoric, labeling it "dangerous" and "ill-advised". Fox's criticism of Trump highlights the concerns about his divisive language and policies, which are perceived as threatening the unity and well-being of both the US and Mexico. By refusing to pay for the wall, Mexico is asserting its sovereignty and rejecting Trump's demand for loyalty and payment.
