Even some Republican state senators who helped pass the legislation have now called for a veto, saying it has been "mischaracterized" by opponents as promoting religious intolerance. Meanwhile, supporters of the bill continue to defend it, claiming that it is necessary to protect businesses from lawsuits. The governor is expected to make a decision soon, but her office has received over 10,000 calls and emails on the matter. By vetoing this bill, Governor Brewer can send a strong message against discrimination and promote equality for all Arizonans.
