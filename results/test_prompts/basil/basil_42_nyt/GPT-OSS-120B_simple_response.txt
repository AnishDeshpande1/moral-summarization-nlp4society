SUMMARY:
Arizona Gov. Jan Brewer faces intense pressure to veto a controversial bill that would let businesses refuse service to LGBTQ+ people and others on religious grounds. While the measure passed both legislative chambers, a broad coalition—including U.S. Senators John McCain and Jeff Flake, Apple and American Airlines executives, and several Republican state senators—warns that signing it would damage the state’s reputation and economic recovery, especially with the upcoming Super Bowl and pending corporate relocations. Pro‑bill advocates, such as Cathi Herrod of the Center for Arizona Policy, argue the law protects genuine religious liberty and accuse opponents of political smearing. Opponents, including mayor Scott Smith and treasurer‑to‑candidate Doug Ducey, frame the bill as a “carte blanche” for discrimination, urging a veto and a more balanced dialogue on religious freedom. The governor’s office has received over 10,000 calls and emails, underscoring the moral clash between protecting faith‑based rights and preventing sanctioned prejudice.  

END OF SUMMARY.
