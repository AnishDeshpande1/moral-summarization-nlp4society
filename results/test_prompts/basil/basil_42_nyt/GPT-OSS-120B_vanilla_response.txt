SUMMARY: Arizona Governor Jan Brewer received intense pressure to veto a controversial “religion bill” that would allow businesses to refuse service on religious grounds, targeting gays and others. The measure passed both legislative houses and sparked national criticism from senators John McCain and Jeff Flake, corporate leaders from Apple and American Airlines, and economic officials warning it could harm Arizona’s business climate and jeopardize the upcoming Super Bowl. Even three Republican state senators who helped pass the bill defended it as a protection of religious liberty, while its proponents, like Center for Arizona Policy head Cathi Herrod, denounced opposition as political attacks. Brewer, out of state but returning soon, has until week’s end to act; her office reports over 10,000 calls and emails. Republican gubernatorial candidates, including Mesa Mayor Scott Smith and Treasurer Doug Ducey, oppose the bill, citing discrimination concerns and potential economic fallout. The controversy revives fears of boycotts similar to those after Arizona’s 2010 immigration law. END OF SUMMARY.
