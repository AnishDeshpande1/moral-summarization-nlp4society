SUMMARY:
Arizona's Governor Jan Brewer is facing pressure from various groups, including business leaders and elected officials, to veto a bill that would allow businesses to deny service to gays and others based on religious grounds. The bill, which was passed by the state legislature, has drawn national attention and criticism, with many calling it discriminatory. Apple Inc.'s executive and American Airlines' chairman have urged Brewer to reject the measure, citing concerns about its potential impact on the state's economy. Three Republican state senators who voted for the bill have also come out against it, saying it has been mischaracterized as a law promoting intolerance. The state is already struggling with an image problem after passing a divisive immigration law in 2010. Brewer is expected to make a decision by the end of the week.

END OF SUMMARY.
