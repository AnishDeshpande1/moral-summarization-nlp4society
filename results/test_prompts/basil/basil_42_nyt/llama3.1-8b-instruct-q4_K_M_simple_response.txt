SUMMARY:

Arizona Governor Jan Brewer is facing pressure from various groups, including elected officials, business leaders, and civic leaders, to veto a bill that would allow businesses to refuse service to gay individuals and others under the guise of religious freedom. The bill has sparked widespread criticism and national attention, with many arguing it would harm Arizona's economy and reputation. Apple Inc.'s executive and American Airlines' CEO have both called on Brewer to reject the measure, citing concerns about its potential impact on business. Even some Republican lawmakers who supported the bill are now urging Brewer to veto it, saying it has been "mischaracterized" as a sword of intolerance rather than a shield for religious liberties.

The controversy comes at a time when Arizona is trying to regain economic vitality and host the Super Bowl next year. Brewer's office has received over 10,000 calls and emails on the matter, with some leaders warning that if she signs the bill, it could lead to the NFL reconsidering its plans to hold the Super Bowl in the state.

END OF SUMMARY.
