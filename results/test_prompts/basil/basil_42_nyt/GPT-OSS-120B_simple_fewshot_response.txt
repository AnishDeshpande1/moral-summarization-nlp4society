SUMMARY:
Arizona Governor Jan Brewer faces intense pressure to veto a newly passed bill that would let business owners refuse service to LGBTQ + people and others on the basis of “religious liberty.” Prominent Republicans—including Senators John McCain and Jeff Flake—along with major business leaders from Apple and American Airlines, have publicly urged Brewer to reject the measure, warning that it could damage the state’s economic recovery and jeopardize the upcoming Super Bowl. Even three Republican senators who helped pass the bill acknowledge that it has become a “sword of religious intolerance” and is harming Arizona’s reputation. Supporters of the bill claim it protects genuine religious freedom, but critics argue it disguises discrimination and threatens the state’s image after past controversies. As Brewer returns from Washington, she must decide within days amid more than 10,000 calls and emails, while Republican primary rivals also condemn the legislation. The moral core of the story is clear: using religion as a shield for discrimination undermines equality, dignity, and the economic well‑being of all Arizonans.  

END OF SUMMARY.
