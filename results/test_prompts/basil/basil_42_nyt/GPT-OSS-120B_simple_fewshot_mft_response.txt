SUMMARY:
Arizona Governor Jan Brewer faces intense pressure to veto a newly‑passed bill that would let businesses refuse service to gay people and others on the grounds of religious belief. Prominent Republicans—including Senators John McCain and Jeff Flake, Apple’s Arizona executive, and the chief of American Airlines—have publicly urged Brewer to reject the measure, warning that signing it would damage the state’s reputation, jeopardize the upcoming Super Bowl, and undermine economic recovery. Even three Republican senators who helped pass the bill acknowledge that, despite their intention to protect religious liberty, the law is being portrayed as “religious intolerance” and is causing “immeasurable harm” to Arizona. Supporters such as Cathi Herrod of the Center for Arizona Policy claim opponents are spreading lies and degrading the sanctity of true religious freedom. Meanwhile, Republican gubernatorial candidates, including Mesa Mayor Scott Smith and Treasurer Doug Ducey, condemn the bill as a carte blanche for discrimination, framing it as an unjust cheating of civil rights and a degradation of moral standards. With over 10,000 calls to the governor’s office, Brewer must decide whether to uphold authority by vetoing the bill or allow a law that many view as unfair and morally corrosive to become law.  
END OF SUMMARY.
