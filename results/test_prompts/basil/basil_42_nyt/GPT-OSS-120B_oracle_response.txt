SUMMARY:  
The Arizona Legislature sent a bill to Gov. Jan Brewer that would let business owners **refuse** service on religious grounds, sparking a wave of criticism that the measure **mischaracterized** religious liberty as a tool of **intolerance**. State leaders, civic groups and major corporations warned the bill could **jeopardize** the state’s economic **comeback** and undo what has been **accomplished** since the 2010 immigration law that already drew **boycotts**. Senators McCain and Flake, along with CEOs of Apple and American Airlines, urged Brewer to **veto** the legislation, saying it would **cause** **harm** to Arizona’s image ahead of the upcoming Super Bowl. Pro‑bill advocates, including Cathi Herrod of the Center for Arizona Policy, accused opponents of **politics**, **hijacked** debate, and spreading **lies** through **reporting**. They argued the bill simply **protects** the **exercise** of religion and does not **deny** service. Republican gubernatorial hopefuls, such as Mesa Mayor Scott Smith and Treasurer Doug Ducey, condemned the bill for **giving** a license to **discriminate** and pledged to **bring** stakeholders together to **forge** a balanced solution. Brewer’s office has received over 10,000 calls as the deadline looms.  
END OF SUMMARY.
