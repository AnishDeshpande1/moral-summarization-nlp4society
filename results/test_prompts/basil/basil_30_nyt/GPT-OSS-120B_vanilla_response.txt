SUMMARY: President Obama is set to unveil a comprehensive gun‑violence reduction plan that combines a push for major legislation—especially expanded background checks and a potential assault‑weapon ban—with 19 executive‑order actions he could take unilaterally. The administration’s proposals include tighter limits on imported firearms, improved sharing of mental‑health records, CDC research on gun violence, and stricter enforcement of existing laws. Obama will likely announce the initiative within days, using a public event to signal his intent. While Democrats see an opening after years of congressional inaction, Republicans and gun‑rights groups, led by the NRA, vow strong opposition; Rep. Steve Stockman even threatened impeachment. Legislative efforts will focus on universal background checks, with the assault‑weapon ban deemed a lower priority. Senate Democrats, though cautious, may introduce a universal‑background‑check bill, while House Republicans are expected to resist. The plan reflects both a response to the Newtown massacre and broader concerns about criminal‑justice and mental‑health systems, and will rely on sustained political pressure to advance. END OF SUMMARY.
