SUMMARY:

President Obama is set to unveil a comprehensive plan to reduce gun violence, which includes major legislation to expand background checks for gun purchases and 19 separate actions he can take by invoking his executive authority. The plan reflects the uncertainty of gun politics in America and the desire to address the Connecticut shooting while confronting broader deficiencies in the country's criminal justice and mental health systems. Obama has vowed to use his office's "whatever weight" to get the proposals passed, emphasizing that it's a moral imperative to protect the most vulnerable among us. He will also propose using executive actions to impose new limits on guns imported from overseas, compel federal agencies to improve sharing of mental health records, and direct research on gun violence causes. The administration's strategy is met with opposition from gun rights groups, but allies see a rare opening for tighter gun rules after Congress has shied away from the issue for years.

END OF SUMMARY.
