SUMMARY:
President Obama announced a comprehensive gun‑violence plan that pairs a push for major legislation—expanding background checks, banning assault weapons and high‑capacity clips—with 19 executive‑order actions he could take unilaterally. He framed the effort as a “sensible, common‑sense” response to protect vulnerable communities and improve public safety. Obama stressed his “obligation” to act, promising to use his office’s authority to limit imports, improve mental‑health record sharing and direct CDC research, while warning opponents that such steps would not “abuse” constitutional rights. Democrats on Capitol Hill, including Rep. Mike Thompson and Rep. Jackie Speier, said the plan targets the biggest holes in the current system and will pressure Congress to adopt universal background checks. Republican Rep. Steve Stockman threatened to impeach, claiming the moves would threaten gun owners’ rights. The administration hopes the combined legislative and executive strategy will close safety gaps without overstepping constitutional limits. END OF SUMMARY.
