SUMMARY:

President Obama is taking executive actions to reduce gun violence, including imposing new limits on guns imported from overseas and directing research on gun violence. He will also push for major legislation to expand background checks and ban assault weapons. This effort aims to make a difference in preventing the kind of massacre that occurred at Newtown, Conn., by focusing on what works and confronting the deficiencies in the country's mental health system. Obama's plan includes 19 separate actions he can take without Congressional approval, such as cracking down on gun trafficking and improving the sharing of mental health records.

The administration is aware of the challenges ahead but believes that putting pressure on lawmakers will make a difference. Gun control advocates are eager to seize this moment and push for stronger restrictions on guns. The plan faces opposition from gun rights groups, including the National Rifle Association, which sees Obama's actions as an overreach of executive authority.

The effort aims to close holes in the current system that allow guns to fall into the wrong hands and make communities safe. By taking action now, Obama is putting his commitment to reducing violence above politics. END OF SUMMARY.
