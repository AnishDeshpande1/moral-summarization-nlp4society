SUMMARY:

President Obama is set to unveil a comprehensive plan to reduce gun violence, which includes major legislation to expand background checks for gun purchases and 19 separate actions he can take by executive order. The plan aims to prevent future mass shootings like the one in Newtown, Connecticut. Lawmakers briefed on the plan said it will focus on expanding background checks, banning assault weapons, and improving mental health records sharing. Obama is also expected to use his executive power to limit gun imports from overseas and direct research on gun violence.

The administration's strategy reflects the uncertainty of gun politics in America and the desire to address the broader deficiencies in the country's criminal justice and mental health systems. However, lawmakers and gun rights groups are likely to oppose the plan, with some threatening impeachment or lawsuits. Despite these challenges, Obama's allies see a rare opening for tighter gun rules after Congress has shied away from the issue for years.

END OF SUMMARY.
