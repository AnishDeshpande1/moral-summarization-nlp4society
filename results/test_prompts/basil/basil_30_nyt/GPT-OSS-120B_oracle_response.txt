President Obama announced a comprehensive plan to **reduce** gun **violence** in the wake of the Newtown **massacre**. The strategy calls for expanded background **checks**, tighter limits on imported firearms, improved sharing of mental‑health records, and new CDC research, together with 19 **steps** the president could take by **executive** action. Obama pledged to **focus** on **action** that **makes sense** and **difference**‑making, rather than partisan politics, and to **clear** the table of minor issues that might **allow** larger reforms to be derailed. Lawmakers highlighted **holes** in the current system—particularly exemptions for private sales and gun shows—and urged that these be **closed** to keep communities **safe**. Critics warned the president might **lie** about his authority, with some Republicans threatening impeachment. Democrats, including Rep. Mike Thompson and Rep. Jackie Speier, described the effort as the most **comprehensive** gun‑safety push in a generation, emphasizing **cracking** down on illegal trafficking and **checks** on buyers. The administration plans to **spend** political capital on universal background **checks** and to **confront** the **deficiencies** in the criminal‑justice and mental‑health systems while the Senate prepares new legislation to **prevent** another shooting.
