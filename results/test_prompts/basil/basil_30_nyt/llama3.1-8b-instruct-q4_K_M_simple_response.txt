SUMMARY:

President Obama is set to unveil a comprehensive plan to reduce gun violence, which includes major legislation to expand background checks for gun purchases and executive orders to address the issue. The plan also calls for banning assault weapons and their high-capacity clips, as well as improving mental health records sharing among federal agencies. White House officials believe that by using executive orders, Mr. Obama can take action on his own to reduce gun violence while Congress is divided on the issue. This approach has sparked controversy, with some lawmakers threatening to file articles of impeachment if the president seeks to regulate guns with executive orders.

The legislative effort will face intense opposition from gun rights groups and lawmakers who see gun rights as a defining issue in their districts. However, allies of Mr. Obama believe that there is a rare opening for tighter gun rules after years of Congressional inaction on the issue. The administration's strategy reflects its desire to address both the Connecticut shooting and broader deficiencies in the country's criminal justice and mental health systems.

END OF SUMMARY.
