SUMMARY:

Donald J. Trump took the oath of office on Friday, becoming the 45th President of the United States. This marked the culmination of his 584-day campaign journey from New York City real estate developer and reality TV star to the nation's highest office. Trump won the Republican nomination despite facing a crowded field of experienced candidates, capitalizing on a primary voting base angry at the establishment GOP for not aggressively fighting President Barack Obama's policies.

In the general election, Trump trailed Democratic nominee Hillary Clinton nearly the entire way but was helped by the release of internal Democratic Party emails stolen by Russian hackers and FBI Director James Comey's decision to reopen the probe into Clinton's private email server. Trump won Wisconsin, Michigan, and Pennsylvania by a total of 78,000 votes, defeating Clinton in states she had counted on winning.

END OF SUMMARY.
