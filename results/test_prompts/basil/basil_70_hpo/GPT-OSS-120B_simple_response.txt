SUMMARY: The piece portrays Donald J. Trump’s ascent to the presidency as a morally dubious odyssey, highlighting how a frustrated, anti‑establishment Republican base propelled a real‑estate mogul and reality‑TV personality to the nation’s highest office. It underscores that Trump’s victory over a crowded GOP field and a long‑shot against Democrat Hillary Clinton was aided not merely by populist anger but also by ethically questionable factors: the daily leak of Democratic Party emails by Russian hackers and FBI Director James Comey’s brief reopening of the Clinton email investigation. The narrative suggests that these manipulations helped Trump clinch narrow wins in Wisconsin, Michigan and Pennsylvania—states Clinton had counted on—casting his triumph as the product of both voter discontent and external interference rather than pure democratic merit. END OF SUMMARY.
