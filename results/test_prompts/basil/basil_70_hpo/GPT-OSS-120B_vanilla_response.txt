SUMMARY: On January 20, Donald J. Trump was sworn in as the 45th U.S. president, taking the oath administered by Chief Justice John Roberts. After announcing his candidacy 584 days earlier, Trump secured the Republican nomination against 16 rivals and capitalized on voter frustration with the GOP establishment and President Obama’s policies. In the general election, he trailed Democrat Hillary Clinton for most of the race but gained momentum in the final month due to the release of Democratic Party emails hacked by Russian actors and FBI Director James Comey’s brief reopening of the Clinton email investigation. Trump ultimately won key swing states—Wisconsin, Michigan, and Pennsylvania—by a combined margin of 78,000 votes, defeating Clinton’s expectations. END OF SUMMARY.
