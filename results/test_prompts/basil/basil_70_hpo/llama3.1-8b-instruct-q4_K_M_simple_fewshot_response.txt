SUMMARY:

Donald Trump was inaugurated as the 45th President of the United States, marking the culmination of his unlikely journey from New York City real estate developer and reality TV star to the nation's highest office. Despite trailing in the polls for much of the general election, Trump won crucial swing states Wisconsin, Michigan, and Pennsylvania by a total of 78,000 votes. His victory was aided by the release of internal Democratic Party emails stolen by Russian hackers and FBI Director James Comey's decision to reopen the probe into Hillary Clinton's use of a private email server. As he took office, Trump vowed to bring about change, promising "to fight for you with every breath in my body" and to restore prosperity to America.
