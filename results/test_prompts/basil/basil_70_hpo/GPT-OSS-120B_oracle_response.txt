SUMMARY:  
On January 20, 2017, Donald J. Trump—who had announced his run 584 days earlier from a Trump Tower escalator—took the presidential oath administered by Chief Justice John Roberts, becoming the 45th U.S. president. After winning a crowded Republican primary, Trump tapped into a base angry at the GOP establishment’s tepid opposition to President Barack Obama’s agenda. In the general election he trailed Democratic nominee Hillary Clinton for most of the campaign, but a late‑stage surge was aided by the near‑daily release of internal Democratic Party emails that were **stolen** by Russian hackers and by FBI Director James Comey’s brief reopening of the Clinton email investigation. Those factors helped Trump flip Wisconsin, Michigan and Pennsylvania, securing victory by a margin of about 78,000 votes—states Clinton had expected to win.  

END OF SUMMARY.
