SUMMARY:  
Republican Representative Paul D. Ryan, chair of the House Budget Committee, has been publicly **criticized** by Roman Catholic leaders after he claimed his austerity‑driven budget plan was rooted in Catholic **moral** teaching. In a letter signed by nearly 90 Georgetown faculty and priests, the signatories denounce Ryan’s **misuse** of Catholic doctrine to **defend** cuts that **decimate** food assistance for struggling families, **weaken** protections for the elderly and sick, and **give** tax breaks to the wealthiest. They argue that true Catholic social teaching requires the government to **step** in during **crises** of **unemployment**, **poverty**, and **hunger**, providing **aid** and **help** when communities face problems beyond their means. The letter includes the Vatican’s Compendium of Social Doctrine to correct Ryan’s interpretation. This dispute reflects a broader effort by Catholic leaders—both liberal and independent—to distance the Church from the Republican Party, especially after recent **comments** from Bishop Daniel Jenky accusing President Obama of **intolerant** policies and **following** the paths of Hitler and Stalin. The controversy underscores the tension between political **assistance** policies and Catholic **values**.  
END OF SUMMARY.
