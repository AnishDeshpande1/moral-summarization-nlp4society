SUMMARY:
A coalition of nearly 90 Georgetown faculty members and Jesuit priests has publicly rebuked Rep. Paul D. Ryan for invoking Catholic moral teaching to justify a budget that slashes anti‑poverty programs, weakens aid for the elderly and sick, and hands tax breaks to the wealthy. The letter frames Ryan’s stance as a subversive misuse of religious authority, contrasting his “Ayn Rand‑style” philosophy with the Gospel’s call to protect the vulnerable. It emphasizes the care‑based harm his cuts would cause to struggling families and argues that true Catholic doctrine demands government intervention when communities cannot meet basic needs such as food, health care, and shelter. By sending the Vatican’s Compendium of Social Doctrine, the signatories assert the proper authority of Church teaching and warn that degrading the poor violates the sacred duty of solidarity. The critique also seeks to separate the Catholic Church from partisan Republican politics, underscoring that the Church’s moral authority stands against policies that endanger the needy. END OF SUMMARY.
