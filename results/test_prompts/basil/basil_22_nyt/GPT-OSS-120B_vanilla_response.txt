SUMMARY: Republican House Budget Committee Chairman Paul D. Ryan has drawn sharp criticism from Catholic leaders after claiming his austerity‑focused budget plan is rooted in Catholic moral teaching. Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and priests sent him a letter ahead of his Whittington Lecture, accusing him of misusing Church doctrine to justify cuts to food assistance, elder care and health programs while favoring the wealthy, and likening his philosophy to Ayn Rand rather than the Gospel. The letter included the Vatican’s Compendium of Social Doctrine to reinforce proper Catholic teaching. This backlash reflects a broader effort by Catholic figures, including bishops and university faculty, to distance the Church from the Republican Party. Recent tensions have also arisen over bishops’ criticism of President Obama’s health‑care mandates, exemplified by Bishop Daniel Jenky’s controversial remarks comparing Obama to Hitler and Stalin, which prompted calls for his resignation from Notre Dame’s board. The United States Conference of Catholic Bishops has urged Congress to adopt a budget that prioritizes aid to the poor, contradicting Ryan’s claim that such letters do not represent all bishops. END OF SUMMARY.
