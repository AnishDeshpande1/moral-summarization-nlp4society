SUMMARY:  
Representative Paul D. Ryan, the Republican chair of the House Budget Committee, has drawn sharp rebuke from Catholic scholars and clergy after he argued that his austerity‑focused budget—cutting food aid, elder care and health programs while expanding tax breaks for the wealthy—is rooted in Catholic moral teaching. A letter signed by nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and priests, issued ahead of Ryan’s Whittington Lecture, accuses him of “misusing” Catholic doctrine, likening his agenda to Ayn Rand’s philosophy rather than the Gospel’s “preferential option for the poor.” The signatories attached the Vatican’s Compendium of Social Doctrine to underscore that Catholic teaching calls for government intervention in crises of poverty and hunger. This clash occurs amid broader tensions as Catholic leaders seek to distance the Church from the Republican Party, while some bishops have recently condemned President Obama’s health‑care mandates and even compared his policies to totalitarian regimes. Ryan maintains his budget promotes independence for the poor, but the Georgetown faculty and the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops argue that true Catholic social teaching demands robust aid to the needy.  

END OF SUMMARY.
