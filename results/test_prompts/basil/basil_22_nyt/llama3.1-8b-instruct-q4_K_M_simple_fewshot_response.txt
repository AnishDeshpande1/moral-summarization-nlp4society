SUMMARY:

Representative Paul Ryan, the Republican chairman of the House Budget Committee, has been criticized by nearly 90 faculty members and priests at Georgetown University for misusing Catholic teaching to defend his budget plan. The letter, sent in advance of Mr. Ryan's visit to Georgetown, challenges his claim that his budget is inspired by Catholic moral teaching, saying it "decimates food programs for struggling families" and gives more tax breaks to the wealthiest few. The faculty members argue that Catholic teaching calls for government to step in and help when communities face problems beyond their means to address. This criticism is part of an effort by some Catholic leaders to distance the church from the Republican Party. The United States Conference of Catholic Bishops has also issued several letters to members of Congress calling for a budget that prioritizes aid to the poor, contradicting Mr. Ryan's claim that his budget reflects the values of Ayn Rand rather than the Gospel of Jesus Christ.
