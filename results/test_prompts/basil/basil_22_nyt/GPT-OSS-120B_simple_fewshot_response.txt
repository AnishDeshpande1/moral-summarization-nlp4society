SUMMARY:
A group of nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty members and Jesuit priests have publicly rebuked Representative Paul D. Ryan for invoking Catholic moral teaching to justify a budget that slashes anti‑poverty programs, weakens aid for the elderly and sick, and hands tax breaks to the wealthy. In a letter sent before Ryan’s Whittington Lecture, the scholars argue that his plan reflects the philosophy of Ayn Rand rather than the Gospel, and they attach the Vatican’s Compendium of Social Doctrine to correct his misunderstanding of the Church’s “preferential option for the poor.” They contend that Catholic teaching obliges the government to intervene when communities cannot meet crises such as hunger, unemployment and endemic poverty. The criticism is part of a broader effort by Catholic leaders—many of whom are politically independent or liberal—to keep the Church from being co‑opted by the Republican Party, especially amid recent heated disputes between bishops and the Obama administration over religious‑freedom issues. By challenging Ryan, the Georgetown faculty aim to uphold the Church’s moral commitment to compassion, solidarity with the poor, and the common good.  
END OF SUMMARY.
