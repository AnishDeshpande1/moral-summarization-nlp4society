SUMMARY:

Representative Paul D. Ryan, a Republican chairman of the House Budget Committee, has been criticized by Catholic leaders for claiming that his budget plan, which slashes antipoverty programs, is inspired by Catholic moral teachings. A letter signed by nearly 90 faculty members and priests at Georgetown University challenged Mr. Ryan's use of Catholic teaching to defend his budget, saying it reflects the values of philosopher Ayn Rand rather than the Gospel of Jesus Christ. The letter also criticized the budget for decimating food programs for struggling families and giving more tax breaks to the wealthy.

Catholic leaders are attempting to distance the church from the Republican Party, which has been accused of using Catholic teachings to justify its policies. Bishop Daniel Jenky's recent comments comparing President Obama to Hitler and Stalin have sparked controversy, with dozens of faculty members at Notre Dame calling for his resignation. The United States Conference of Catholic Bishops has issued letters calling for a budget that prioritizes aid to the poor.

END OF SUMMARY.
