SUMMARY:

In the vice presidential debate between Joe Biden and Paul Ryan, Biden dominated the spotlight, interrupting Ryan repeatedly and making it difficult for him to finish his answers. Biden's performance was described as "terrific" by President Barack Obama, who was impressed with the vice president's passion and concern for the middle class. The debate focused on domestic policy, foreign policy, and Medicare, with Biden attempting to plow over Ryan with broadsides. Ryan, however, remained composed and knowledgeable, delivering a strong performance on substance.

The debate was seen as a success for the Democratic base, who had been anxious after Obama's poor performance in his first debate against Mitt Romney. Biden's behavior, including interrupting and laughing, was criticized by Republicans, but Democrats saw it as a sign of the vice president's confidence and commitment to pushing back against Republican policies.

END OF SUMMARY.
