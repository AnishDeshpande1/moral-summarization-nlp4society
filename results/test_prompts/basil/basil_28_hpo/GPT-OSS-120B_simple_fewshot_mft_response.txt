SUMMARY:
In the sole vice‑presidential debate, Joe Biden went beyond simply rebutting Rep. Paul Ryan – he framed the contest as a fight for the American middle class and a stand against political unfairness. By repeatedly interrupting Ryan, rolling his eyes and calling Romney’s statements “malarkey,” Biden signaled fierce concern for ordinary voters who might be harmed by Republican policies. He highlighted the Obama administration’s stimulus, green‑energy jobs and Medicare protections, portraying them as safeguards for working families, while accusing the GOP of cheating the public with misleading tax and health‑care claims. Biden’s aggressive style was praised by the Democratic camp as passionate advocacy for those most vulnerable, and condemned by Republicans as “unhinged” and disrespectful. The debate therefore underscored a moral divide: Democrats emphasizing care for the middle class and fairness in policy, Republicans warning that Biden’s tactics harmed civil discourse and distorted facts. The clash left undecided voters to weigh whether Biden’s forceful defense of the disadvantaged outweighed the perceived rudeness and partisan spin.

END OF SUMMARY.
