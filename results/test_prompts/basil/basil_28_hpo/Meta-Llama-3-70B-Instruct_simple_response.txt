SUMMARY:

Vice President Joe Biden dominated the debate against Rep. Paul Ryan, charging at him with a flurry of eye rolls, interjections, and accusations. Biden's performance was seen as a rallying cry for the Democratic base, which was disappointed with President Obama's debate performance last week. Biden's aggressive approach was a deliberate attempt to push back against Ryan and Romney, and to appeal to working-class and blue-collar voters. While Republicans criticized Biden's behavior as condescending and immature, Democrats saw it as a passionate defense of the middle class. The debate focused on domestic and foreign policy, with Biden repeatedly interrupting Ryan and refusing to let him finish his thoughts. Biden's goal was to show that he and Obama care about the middle class, and to push back against Romney's policies. Overall, Biden's performance was seen as a win for the Democrats, and a much-needed boost after Obama's lackluster debate.
