SUMMARY:  
In the sole vice‑presidential debate, Joe Biden tried to supply the **passion** and **concern** that many felt President Obama lacked the week before. From the start he **interrupted** Rep. Paul Ryan with rapid‑fire remarks, eye rolls and accusations, aiming to rally the Democratic base and push back against Republican attacks. Biden’s style—filled with laughter, **mocking** tones and occasional **plow**‑like attacks—was labeled “unhinged” by GOP spokespeople, while the Obama campaign praised his energy as a needed contrast to Obama’s more measured performance. Ryan, meanwhile, stuck to **accurate** policy figures on the economy and **bipartisan** Medicare proposals, even as Biden warned voters about a plan that would have **added** $6,400 to seniors’ costs. The debate also touched on the **death** of the U.S. ambassador in Libya, with Biden dismissing the GOP’s framing as “malarkey.” Polls showed mixed reactions, but the night likely **created** a boost for Democrats, giving them a narrative of renewed vigor ahead of the presidential contest.
