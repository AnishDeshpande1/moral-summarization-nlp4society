SUMMARY:

In the vice presidential debate, Joe Biden displayed a lot of passion and concern for the middle class, which was lacking in President Obama's previous performance. Biden interrupted Rep. Paul Ryan repeatedly, making it difficult for him to finish his answers, but this approach seemed to resonate with some undecided voters. The debate focused on domestic policy, foreign policy, and Medicare, with Biden trying to plow over Ryan with broadsides on tax policy and Medicare costs. However, Republicans criticized Biden's behavior as condescending, mocking, and immature. On the other hand, Ryan was seen as serious, sober, and steady in his responses. The debate highlighted the difference in tone between the two candidates, with Biden trying to create a more emotional connection with voters and Ryan focusing on substance and facts.

Biden also successfully countered Romney's comments about government benefits by highlighting the importance of personal responsibility. Overall, the debate showed that both candidates have their strengths and weaknesses, but Biden's passion and concern for the middle class may have helped him to win over some undecided voters.
