SUMMARY:
In the sole vice‑presidential debate, Joe Biden seized the moment that President Obama missed, launching an aggressive, high‑energy assault on Rep. Paul Ryan. By constantly interrupting, rolling his eyes and branding Ryan’s statements “malarkey,” Biden aimed to rally the Democratic base and demonstrate the passion and concern for the middle class that many felt Obama lacked. The performance energized supporters, earned praise from President Obama and campaign aides, and framed Biden as a fierce defender of ordinary Americans against a “serious, sober, steady” but less animated opponent. Republicans condemned Biden’s style as “unhinged” and “immature,” shifting focus to his demeanor rather than policy substance. Nonetheless, the debate underscored Democrats’ strategy to reclaim the narrative on working‑class and middle‑class issues ahead of the presidential contest, using Biden’s combative vigor as a moral rallying point for voters who demand assertive advocacy.

END OF SUMMARY.
