SUMMARY:

Vice President Joe Biden dominated the debate against Rep. Paul Ryan, showcasing his passion and concern for the middle class. Biden's aggressive performance was a stark contrast to President Obama's lackluster debate last week, and it seemed to rally the Democratic base. Biden repeatedly interrupted Ryan, making it difficult for him to get his answers out, and accused him of being inaccurate. Ryan, on the other hand, remained composed and focused on winning over moderate and independent voters. The debate covered domestic and foreign policy, with Biden pushing back hard against Ryan's claims, particularly on Medicare and tax policy. While Republicans criticized Biden's behavior as condescending and immature, Democrats saw it as a necessary response to Romney's policies. The Obama campaign relished in Republicans' focus on Biden's behavior, saying it was a sign they lost the debate.

END OF SUMMARY.
