SUMMARY: In the sole vice‑presidential debate, Joe Biden adopted an aggressive style that contrasted sharply with President Obama’s subdued performance in the prior presidential debate. Biden repeatedly interrupted Republican nominee Paul Ryan, using eye‑rolls, interjections and sharp accusations, aiming to rally the Democratic base and counteract anxiety after Obama’s “woeful” showing. Post‑debate polls from CBS and CNN were mixed, and analysts note vice‑presidential debates rarely shift the overall race. Obama praised Biden, calling his performance “terrific,” while Republicans labeled Biden “unhinged” and “immature,” emphasizing Ryan’s composure and substance. The debate covered foreign policy, the Benghazi attack, and domestic issues such as Medicare and tax policy. Biden attacked Ryan’s and Romney’s records, questioning the cost of proposed Medicare changes and highlighting economic figures from the Obama stimulus. Both campaigns used the aftermath to spin the event: Democrats framed Biden’s passion as a strength, Republicans focused on his demeanor. The exchange set the tone for the upcoming presidential debate, with Biden positioning himself as the aggressive counter‑force to Obama’s earlier perceived weakness. END OF SUMMARY.
