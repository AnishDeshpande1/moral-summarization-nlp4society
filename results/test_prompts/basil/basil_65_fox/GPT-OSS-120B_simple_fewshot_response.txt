SUMMARY:
Former New York City mayor Michael Bloomberg used his platform at the Democratic National Convention to morally urge voters to choose Hillary Clinton over Donald Trump, calling the election “imperative” for the country’s well‑being. Bloomberg, a billionaire who left the Republican Party to become an independent, acknowledged past disagreements with Clinton but said they must be set aside for the national good. He denounced Trump as a “dangerous demagogue,” “bomb‑thrower,” and hypocrite, attacking the former president’s business record of bankruptcies, lawsuits, and alleged exploitation of customers. Bloomberg warned that Trump’s promise to run the nation like his business would be disastrous, labeling his economic plan a “con” and a “disaster in the making.” Although he once considered a third‑party run, Bloomberg rejected it to avoid siphoning votes from Clinton. He contrasted his self‑made business success with Trump’s inherited wealth and highlighted their divergent views on immigration, emphasizing that an open policy is essential for economic growth. Bloomberg’s speech framed the endorsement as a moral imperative to protect the country from Trump’s perceived danger.

END OF SUMMARY.
