SUMMARY:

Former New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg endorsed Hillary Clinton at the Democratic National Convention, calling it "imperative" for voters to back her over Donald Trump. Bloomberg, who has an estimated net worth of $48 billion, criticized Trump as a "dangerous demagogue" and "bomb-thrower," contrasting his own business history with Trump's. He accused Trump of having a "well-documented record of bankruptcies and thousands of lawsuits." Bloomberg also charged that Trump's economic plan is a "con" and "disaster in the making."

Bloomberg, who considered running for president as an independent before opting against it, expressed worry that he would siphon away votes from Clinton and inadvertently help elect Trump. He praised Clinton, saying that even though they disagree on some issues, she deserves support for the good of the country. Bloomberg's endorsement is significant, given his past relationship with both Clinton and Trump, as well as his wealth and influence.

END OF SUMMARY.
