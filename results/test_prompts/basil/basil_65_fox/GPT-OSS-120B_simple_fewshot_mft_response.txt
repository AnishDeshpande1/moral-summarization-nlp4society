SUMMARY:
Former New York City mayor Michael Bloomberg publicly endorsed Democratic nominee Hillary Clinton at the Democratic National Convention, stressing that voters must set aside personal disagreements and rally behind her for the nation’s safety. Bloomberg framed his support as a moral duty to protect the country from what he described as Donald Trump’s dangerous, demagogic, and hypocritical leadership—characterizing Trump’s business practices and immigration stance as harmful to ordinary Americans. He accused Trump of a record of bankruptcies, lawsuits, and deceit, portraying these actions as unfair cheating that threatens public welfare. By backing Clinton, Bloomberg positioned himself as loyal to the nation’s higher authority and collective good, rejecting the subversive threat he believes Trump represents. He also noted that a third‑party run would betray Democratic voters by splitting the anti‑Trump vote, reinforcing his commitment to loyalty and the broader public interest. END OF SUMMARY.
