SUMMARY:
Former New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg endorsed Hillary Clinton at the Democratic National Convention, calling it "imperative" for voters to back her over Donald Trump. Bloomberg, who has an estimated net worth of $48 billion, criticized Trump as a "dangerous demagogue" and a "bomb-thrower," highlighting his business history and contrasting it with Trump's record of bankruptcies and lawsuits. He also accused Trump of hypocrisy, saying that running the nation like his business would be disastrous. Bloomberg expressed concern that if he had run for president himself, he could have inadvertently helped elect Trump by siphoning votes from Clinton. Despite disagreeing with Clinton on some issues, Bloomberg emphasized that their differences are secondary to the need to stop Trump. He praised Clinton's commitment to an open immigration policy and criticized Trump's economic plan as a "con" and a "disaster in the making."
END OF SUMMARY.
