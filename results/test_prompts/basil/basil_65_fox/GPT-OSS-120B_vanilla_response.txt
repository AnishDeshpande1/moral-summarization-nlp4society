SUMMARY: Former New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg publicly endorsed Democratic nominee Hillary Clinton at the Democratic National Convention, calling it imperative to defeat Republican nominee Donald Trump. Bloomberg, a billionaire who once switched from Republican to independent, acknowledged past disagreements with Clinton but urged unity for the country's sake. He criticized Trump as a “dangerous demagogue” and “bomb‑thrower,” highlighting Trump’s bankruptcies, lawsuits, and alleged hypocrisy in claiming to run the nation like his business. Bloomberg contrasted his self‑made wealth with Trump’s financial background and warned that Trump’s immigration stance and economic plan would harm the nation. He said he considered a third‑party presidential run but declined, fearing it would siphon votes from Clinton and aid Trump. Bloomberg’s relationship with both Clinton and Trump stems from New York politics and business, but he said Trump’s hard‑line immigration policies alienated him. Trump has not responded to Bloomberg’s remarks. END OF SUMMARY.
