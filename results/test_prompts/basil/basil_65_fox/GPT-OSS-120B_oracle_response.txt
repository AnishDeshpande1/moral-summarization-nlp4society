SUMMARY:
Former New York City mayor Michael Bloomberg formally endorsed Hillary Clinton at the Democratic National Convention, calling it “imperative” to put aside personal disagreements for the good of the country. Bloomberg, a billionaire who started his own business without a family check, warned that Donald Trump is a “dangerous demagogue,” a “bomb‑thrower,” and the epitome of hypocrisy. He charged that Trump’s record is riddled with lawsuits, cheated shareholders, disillusioned contractors and customers who feel ripped off, and that his economic plan is a con and a disaster in the making. Bloomberg said Trump’s hard‑line immigration stance is contrary to what is needed for economic growth and that the nation must help Clinton avoid a Trump presidency. Though he considered a third‑party run, he decided not to make one so as not to siphon votes from Clinton. Bloomberg’s endorsement underscores a moral appeal to rebuild the nation and protect it from what he views as Trump’s reckless, hypocritical leadership. END OF SUMMARY.
