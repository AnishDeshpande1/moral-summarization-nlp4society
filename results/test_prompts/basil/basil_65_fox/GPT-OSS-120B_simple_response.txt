SUMMARY: Former New York City mayor Michael Bloomberg used his platform at the Democratic National Convention to make a morally‑charged endorsement of Hillary Clinton, arguing that the nation’s welfare outweighs personal or partisan disagreements. He framed the election as a matter of national survival, labeling Donald Trump a “dangerous demagogue” and warning that Trump’s business‑centric approach would be a “disaster” for the country. Bloomberg contrasted his self‑made wealth with Trump’s inherited fortune and highlighted Trump’s history of bankruptcies, lawsuits, and alleged hypocrisy, presenting these as ethical failings that endanger democratic stability. While acknowledging past disagreements with Clinton, Bloomberg urged voters to set aside those differences for the greater good, emphasizing a moral duty to reject Trump’s “con” economic plan and anti‑immigration stance. He also disclosed that he considered a third‑party run but rejected it to avoid unintentionally aiding Trump, reinforcing his commitment to a principled, collective choice for the nation’s future. END OF SUMMARY.
