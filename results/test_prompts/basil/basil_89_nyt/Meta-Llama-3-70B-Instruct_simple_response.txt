SUMMARY:

Defense Secretary Jim Mattis resigned in protest of President Trump's decision to withdraw American forces from Syria, citing a rejection of international alliances and a failure to recognize the importance of competing with Russia and China for global power. Mattis, a retired four-star Marine general, had repeatedly expressed his concerns to the president, but was rebuffed. In his resignation letter, Mattis condemned Trump's approach to the world as destructive to American influence and power, emphasizing the importance of alliances and the need to recognize Russia and China as "clear adversaries." The resignation marks a significant blow to the Trump administration, with many lawmakers and officials expressing concern over the decision. Mattis's departure leaves a void in the administration's national security team, with many questioning Trump's ability to make informed decisions without Mattis's guidance.

END OF SUMMARY.
