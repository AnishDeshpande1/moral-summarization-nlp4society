SUMMARY:
Defense Secretary Jim Mattis resigned in protest of President Trump’s decision to pull roughly 2,000 troops from Syria and to sideline long‑standing alliances.  Mattis, a retired four‑star Marine who had been viewed as an “island of stability” and a loyal guardian of the 1.3 million active‑duty service members, framed his departure as a matter of principle: the president’s rejection of NATO, the “Defeat ISIS” coalition, and the need for allies to share the burden was a betrayal of fairness and a dangerous subversion of legitimate authority.  In his resignation letter, Mattis warned that abandoning allies—especially Kurdish partners—and ceding ground to Russia and China harmed U.S. security and the lives of troops abroad.  Senators from both parties praised his commitment to caring for service members and to fair, reciprocal alliances, while condemning Trump’s unilateral moves as disloyal and reckless.  The resignation, the first cabinet protest over a major national‑security issue since 1980, underscored a profound split between a duty‑bound defender of collective security and a president whose actions were portrayed as cheating the nation’s allies and endangering global stability.  
END OF SUMMARY.
