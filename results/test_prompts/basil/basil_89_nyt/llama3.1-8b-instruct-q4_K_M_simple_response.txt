SUMMARY:

Defense Secretary Jim Mattis resigned in protest of President Trump's decision to withdraw American forces from Syria, marking the sharpest public rebuke of the president by a cabinet member since 1980. Mattis, a retired four-star Marine general, had repeatedly expressed his concerns about the president's approach to national security, but ultimately decided that Trump's decision was "a step too far." In his resignation letter, Mattis condemned Trump's rejection of international alliances and relationships that have underpinned American security since World War II. He praised NATO and the "Defeat ISIS" coalition, while criticizing Trump's failure to recognize the importance of competing with Russia and China for global power. The president had asked Mattis to publicly endorse the Syria decision, but he refused.

The resignation marks a significant loss for Trump's national security team, which has been criticized for its inexperience and lack of cohesion. Senators from both parties have praised Mattis as an "island of stability" amidst the chaos of the Trump administration.
