SUMMARY: Defense Secretary Jim Mattis resigned on Thursday in protest of President Trump’s decision to withdraw roughly 2,000 U.S. troops from Syria and his broader rejection of traditional alliances. Mattis, a retired four‑star Marine general, had tried to persuade Trump to reverse the pullout but was rebuffed. In his resignation letter, he condemned Trump’s “destructive” approach to the world, emphasizing the importance of NATO, the anti‑ISIS coalition, and confronting revisionist powers like Russia and China. His departure marks the exit of the last senior member of Trump’s original national‑security team, leaving the Pentagon under the influence of Secretary of State Mike Pompeo and National Security Adviser John Bolton. The resignation comes amid looming congressional shutdown, market turmoil, and an upcoming drawdown of U.S. forces in Afghanistan. Senators praised Mattis as a stabilizing force, while Trump praised his service and promised a quick replacement. Mattis’s exit is the most senior cabinet protest over a national‑security policy since 1980. END OF SUMMARY.
