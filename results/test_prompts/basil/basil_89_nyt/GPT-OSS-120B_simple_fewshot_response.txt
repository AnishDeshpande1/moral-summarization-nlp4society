SUMMARY:
Defense Secretary Jim Mattis, long regarded as the steady, experienced anchor to an erratic president, quit in protest after President Trump ordered the withdrawal of roughly 2,000 U.S. troops from Syria and dismissed long‑standing alliances. Mattis, a retired four‑star Marine general, delivered a resignation letter that condemned Trump’s “destructive” world approach, stressing the importance of respecting allies, confronting Russia and China, and maintaining the NATO partnership that underpins U.S. security. He warned that abandoning Kurdish partners and ceding territory to adversaries would damage America’s influence. The resignation, the most senior cabinet protest over a national‑security policy since 1980, left Trump’s remaining security team—Mike Pompeo and John Bolton—without the “island of stability” that Mattis provided. Senators from both parties praised Mattis as a voice of reason amid chaos, while Trump praised his help in getting allies to share costs and promised a quick replacement. The episode underscores a stark moral clash: Mattis’ principled stand for collective defense versus Trump’s unilateral, “America‑first” moves that many see as endangering global stability.

END OF SUMMARY.
