SUMMARY:  
Defense Secretary Jim Mattis quit in a rare, public act of conscience, denouncing President Trump’s decision to pull roughly 2,000 troops from Syria and his broader contempt for long‑standing alliances. Mattis, a retired four‑star Marine who had long served as the administration’s “adult in the room,” argued that respecting allies, confronting revisionist powers such as Russia and China, and maintaining a clear‑eyed strategy were moral imperatives for U.S. security. His resignation letter—distributed in fifty copies throughout the Pentagon—condemned Trump’s “destructive” world view, warned that abandoning Kurdish partners and ceding ground to hostile actors would weaken America’s influence, and reaffirmed the importance of NATO and the anti‑ISIS coalition. Senators praised Mattis as an “island of stability” and lamented the loss of a principled voice inside the White House. The move marks the first cabinet‑level protest over a major national‑security policy since 1980, underscoring a deep ethical rift between a career defender of collective security and a president willing to discard it.  

END OF SUMMARY.
