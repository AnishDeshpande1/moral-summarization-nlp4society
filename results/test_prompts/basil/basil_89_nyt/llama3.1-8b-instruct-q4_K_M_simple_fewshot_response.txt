SUMMARY:
Defense Secretary Jim Mattis resigned on Thursday, citing his disagreement with President Trump's decision to withdraw American forces from Syria. Mattis, a retired four-star Marine general and former commander of US Central Command, had repeatedly expressed concerns about Trump's national security policies, but ultimately felt that withdrawing troops from Syria was a step too far. His resignation letter criticized Trump's approach to the world as "destructive" and emphasized the importance of alliances and competition with Russia and China. Mattis's departure is seen as a loss for stability in the Trump administration, which has been marked by turmoil and controversy. The move comes as Congress appears headed towards a government shutdown, and amid growing concerns about Trump's decision-making on national security issues.
