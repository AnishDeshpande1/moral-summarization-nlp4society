SUMMARY:

Defense Secretary Jim Mattis has resigned in protest of President Trump's decision to withdraw American forces from Syria and his rejection of international alliances. Mattis, a retired four-star Marine general, had repeatedly told friends and aides that he viewed his responsibility to protect the US' 1.3 million active-duty troops as worth the concessions necessary to work with the president. However, Trump's decision to withdraw troops from Syria was a step too far, and Mattis decided to resign. In his resignation letter, Mattis condemned Trump's approach to the world as destructive to American influence and power, and criticized the president's rejection of alliances and relationships that have underpinned American security since the end of World War II. Mattis will leave his position at the end of February, and Trump has said he will name a replacement soon.

END OF SUMMARY.
