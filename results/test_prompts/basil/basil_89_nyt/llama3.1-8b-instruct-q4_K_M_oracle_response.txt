SUMMARY:

Defense Secretary Jim Mattis resigned in protest of President Trump's decision to withdraw American forces from Syria, a step that he deemed too destructive to the country's influence and leadership. Mr. Mattis, known for his stability and balance in an unpredictable administration, had repeatedly expressed concerns about the president's approach to international alliances and relationships. He viewed his responsibility to protect US troops as paramount, but felt that President Trump's rejection of these alliances was a step too far. The withdrawal from Syria is seen as abandoning Kurdish fighters and other American allies, ceding critical territory to Russia and Iran. This decision has been widely criticized, with some calling it a chaotic move that undermines US leadership in the world. Mr. Mattis's resignation letter condemned President Trump's approach as destructive and urged him to take a more cleareyed view of global power dynamics. His departure marks a significant shift in the administration's national security team.

END OF SUMMARY.
