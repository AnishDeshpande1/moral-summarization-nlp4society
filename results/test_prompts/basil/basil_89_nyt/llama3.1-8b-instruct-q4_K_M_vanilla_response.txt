SUMMARY:

Defense Secretary Jim Mattis resigned on Thursday in protest of President Trump's decision to withdraw American forces from Syria and reject international alliances. Mattis, a retired four-star Marine general, had repeatedly expressed his concerns about the president's decisions but ultimately decided that the withdrawal was "a step too far." His resignation letter criticized Trump's approach to the world as destructive to American influence and power.

Mattis's departure marks a significant shift in the administration's national security team, with only two senior officials remaining from Trump's original team. The move has been met with bipartisan criticism, with some lawmakers praising Mattis as an "island of stability" amidst the chaos of the Trump administration.

Trump announced that Mattis will leave at the end of February and promised to name a replacement soon. However, the decision to withdraw troops from Syria has sparked widespread concern about the consequences for regional security and American influence in the Middle East.

END OF SUMMARY.
