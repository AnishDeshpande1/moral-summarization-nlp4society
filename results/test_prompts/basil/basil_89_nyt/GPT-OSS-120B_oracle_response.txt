SUMMARY:  
Defense Secretary **Jim Mattis**, long‑seen as a **balance** to an unpredictable president, resigned in protest after President Trump **announced** a **withdraw** of roughly 2,000 troops from Syria and **rejection** of long‑standing **allies**. Mattis, who has spent a career **protect**ing the United States’ 1.3 million active‑duty troops, said the **step** was too far and tried to **reverse** the **decision**. He was **rebuffed**, and his resignation letter – printed in 50 copies and distributed throughout the Pentagon – warned that the president’s **treating** of allies and **cleareyed** view of **malign actors** threatened U.S. **leadership**.  

The move came amid a looming **shutdown**, market **turmoil**, and the **withdrawn** drawdown of forces in Afghanistan, an **ending** of a 17‑year **war** that critics say could **plot** to **kill** American lives if **abandoned**. Mattis condemned the president’s **destructive** **influence**, the erosion of **underpinned** alliances, and the **abandoning** of Kurdish partners, warning that Russia and China seek a world **consistent** with their authoritarian model.  

Senators called Mattis an “island of **stability**” and lamented the **chaos** of a **decision** that **ceded** strategic ground. The resignation marks the first cabinet **departure** over a major security issue since 1980, highlighting the **protector** role Mattis **commanded**, **overseeing** the world’s most powerful military, and his belief that strong **action** must **deter** conflict, not **start** it, through **working** with allies rather than **buried** unilateral moves.  

END OF SUMMARY.
