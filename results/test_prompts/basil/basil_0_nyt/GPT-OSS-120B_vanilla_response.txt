SUMMARY: Glenn Beck delivered a fiery closing address at the Conservative Political Action Conference, denouncing progressivism as a “cancer” that must be excised from American politics. He criticized both parties, accusing Democrats of over‑taxing and spending while claiming Republicans are addicted to big‑government spending despite lacking a clear platform. Beck, referencing his own recovery from alcoholism, urged the GOP to acknowledge its fiscal problem. He used humor and personal anecdotes to argue that the nation is in a rough “morning” but still hopeful, and warned against blaming President Obama alone for the nation’s woes. Emphasizing personal responsibility, he warned against demonizing financial success and insisted individuals should not be forced to cover others’ mistakes. The speech was met with strong applause from the packed hotel ballroom audience. END OF SUMMARY.
