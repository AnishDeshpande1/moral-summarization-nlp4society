SUMMARY:
Glenn Beck closed the three‑day CPAP conference with a blistering critique that placed the Republican Party under the authority of its own conscience, demanding it recognize and confess a “problem” – an addiction to big‑government spending. By invoking his personal recovery from alcoholism, Beck framed the party’s denial as subversive, urging a collective admission of guilt and a return to disciplined, principled leadership. He warned that the current bipartisan excess harms ordinary Americans, emphasizing the moral cost of “tax and spend” policies that betray fairness and cheat citizens out of responsible governance. Beck’s rhetoric linked the nation’s suffering to the disease of progressivism, portraying it as a corrosive force that endangers the public’s wellbeing. He appealed to care for the American people by highlighting the erosion of hope and the “morning in America” that feels like a hung‑over, vomiting ordeal. Throughout, he stressed that fairness requires both parties to stop penalizing individuals for others’ mistakes and to stop demonizing financial success, calling for a restoration of accountable, honest authority that protects citizens from further harm. END OF SUMMARY.
