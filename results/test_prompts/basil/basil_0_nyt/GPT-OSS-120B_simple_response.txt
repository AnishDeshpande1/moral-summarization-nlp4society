SUMMARY:
Glenn Beck used his closing slot at CPAC to deliver a moral indictment of “progressivism,” which he portrayed as a cancerous disease threatening America’s future. Framing his critique as a call to personal responsibility, Beck warned that both major parties are complicit: Democrats “tax and spend,” while Republicans are “addicted to spending and big government.” He lamented a loss of faith in improvement, likening the nation’s condition to a hung‑over, vomiting morning that is still “morning in America.” Beck invoked his own recovery from alcoholism to illustrate the need for parties to acknowledge their problems, urging the Republican Party to admit its fiscal addiction. He dismissed simplistic blame on President Obama, insisting the deeper moral ailment is progressive ideology. Throughout, he stressed that happiness and success are individual pursuits, not grounds for collective punishment, concluding with a rallying cry that individuals should not be forced to pay for others’ mistakes. END OF SUMMARY.
