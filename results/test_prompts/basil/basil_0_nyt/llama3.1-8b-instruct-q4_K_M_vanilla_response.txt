SUMMARY:
Glenn Beck delivered a scathing denunciation of progressivism at the Conservative Political Action Conference, calling it a "cancer" that must be cut out of the system. He aimed his criticism not only at Democrats but also at Republicans, saying they have failed to stand up for conservative values and are instead addicted to spending and big government. Beck noted that both parties share blame, with Democrats taxing and spending while Republicans just spend. He attributed America's current state to a loss of faith in the idea that things will get better, comparing it to a "head-pounding" morning after a night out.

Beck also emphasized personal responsibility and criticized the demonization of financial success. He used his signature chalkboard to scrawl the word "progressivism" as the disease infecting America. The audience responded with wild applause to his call for conservatives to stand up against progressivism and take control of their own lives. Beck's speech was a rallying cry for conservatives, urging them to reject both parties' failed policies and reclaim American values. END OF SUMMARY.
