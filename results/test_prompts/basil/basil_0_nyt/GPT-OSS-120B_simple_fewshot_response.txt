SUMMARY:
Glenn Beck closed CPAC with a fiery denunciation of “progressivism,” calling it a cancer that must be excised from American politics. While blaming Democrats for tax‑and‑spend policies, Beck turned the spotlight on Republicans, accusing the party of an addiction to big‑government spending and urging a collective “12‑step” acknowledgment of the problem. Drawing on his own recovery from alcoholism, he framed the GOP’s need for reform as a moral imperative, insisting that both parties have failed the public’s faith in a brighter future. Beck warned that oversimplifying the crisis by blaming President Obama missed the deeper disease of progressive ideology, which he illustrated on his trademark chalkboard. Interweaving personal anecdotes, he championed personal responsibility, the right to reap the rewards of financial success, and a refusal to subsidize others’ mistakes. The audience responded with cheers and applause, embracing his call for ideological purity, moral accountability, and a renewed belief that “it’s still morning in America” despite the current “hung‑over” political climate.

END OF SUMMARY.
