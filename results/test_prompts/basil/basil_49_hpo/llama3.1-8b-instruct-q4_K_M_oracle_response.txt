SUMMARY:

The states of Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a federal lawsuit against Colorado, claiming that the state's legalization of recreational marijuana is unconstitutional under federal law. They argue that Colorado has undermined the United States Constitution by allowing the production and sale of marijuana, which remains prohibited under federal law. The regulation of recreational marijuana in Colorado is also seen as a violation of the Controlled Substances Act. Nebraska Attorney General Jon Bruning stated that Colorado's decisions regarding marijuana are not without consequences to neighboring states, and indeed all Americans.

Colorado Attorney General John Suthers defended the state's marijuana laws, saying that the suit is "without merit." He argued that the federal government has taken a hands-off approach to state medicalizations and legalizations. Supporters of marijuana policy reform, such as Harvard economist Jeff Miron, have highlighted the precarious nature of state marijuana laws.

Representatives from both sides have emphasized their positions on the issue, with Nebraska officials acting like bullies, forcing Coloradans to defend against the suit. The outcome could impact all state marijuana laws, making it a crucial test for federal policy.

END OF SUMMARY.
