SUMMARY: Nebraska and Oklahoma sued Colorado in the U.S. Supreme Court, arguing that Colorado’s voter‑approved legalization of recreational marijuana violates the Supremacy Clause because marijuana remains illegal under the federal Controlled Substances Act. Nebraska Attorney General Jon Bruning and Oklahoma Attorney General Scott Pruitt framed the suit as a defense of constitutional order and a protection of their citizens from “illegal products” trafficked across state lines. Colorado Attorney General John Suthers, backed by the Marijuana Policy Project and industry voices, dismissed the case as meritless, portraying the plaintiffs as “bullies” who seek to overturn the democratic will of Colorado voters and waste taxpayers’ money. Anti‑legalization advocate Kevin Sabet praised the lawsuit as a necessary check on the harms of legalization, while proponents such as Rep. Jared Polis and the Marijuana Policy Project condemned it as an outrageous intrusion on state sovereignty and a step backward for drug‑policy reform. The case highlights a moral clash: one side emphasizes legal hierarchy and public safety; the other stresses voter autonomy, economic opportunity, and progressive reform. END OF SUMMARY.
