SUMMARY:
Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a lawsuit in the U.S. Supreme Court challenging Colorado’s recreational marijuana legalization, arguing it violates the federal Controlled Substances Act and the Constitution’s Supremacy Clause. Attorneys General Jon Bruning (Nebraska) and Scott Pruitt (Oklahoma) claim Colorado’s law enables illegal drug trafficking into their states. Colorado Attorney General John Suthers says the suit lacks merit and will defend the state’s voter‑approved law. The case revives the legal conflict highlighted in Gonzales v. Raich (2005), where federal law trumped state marijuana regulations. Supporters of Colorado’s law, including the Marijuana Policy Project, Smart Approaches to Marijuana, industry representatives, and Rep. Jared Polis, criticize the lawsuit as an overreach that wastes taxpayers’ money and threatens state autonomy. Legal scholars warn that a ruling for Nebraska and Oklahoma could jeopardize all state marijuana reforms, urging Congress to resolve the federal‑state clash. The outcome could shape the future of marijuana policy nationwide.

END OF SUMMARY.
