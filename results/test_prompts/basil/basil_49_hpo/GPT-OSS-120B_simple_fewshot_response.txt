SUMMARY:
Nebraska and Oklahoma have taken the extraordinary step of filing a federal lawsuit in the U.S. Supreme Court, arguing that Colorado’s voter‑approved legalization of recreational marijuana violates the Constitution’s Supremacy Clause because federal law still bans the drug. Attorneys General Jon Bruning and Scott Pruitt claim Colorado’s pot is being trafficked across state lines, harming their citizens and draining resources. Colorado Attorney General John Suthers denounced the suit as “without merit,” saying the real grievance is the neighboring states’ displeasure with Colorado’s democratic choice and the federal government’s lax enforcement. Supporters of the lawsuit, including anti‑legalization advocate Kevin Sabet, praise the action as a necessary defense of the rule of law, while Colorado’s legalization advocates—Mason Tvert of the Marijuana Policy Project, industry spokesman Mike Elliott, and Rep. Jared Polis— condemn the move as bullying, wasteful, and on the wrong side of history. The case highlights a clash between federal authority, state sovereignty, and the growing public support for marijuana reform, with the potential to jeopardize all state‑level legalization efforts if the Court sides with Nebraska and Oklahoma.  
END OF SUMMARY.
