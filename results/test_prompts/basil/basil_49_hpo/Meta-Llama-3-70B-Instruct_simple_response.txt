SUMMARY:

Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a federal lawsuit against Colorado, claiming that its legalization of recreational marijuana is unconstitutional under federal law. The states argue that the Supremacy Clause of the US Constitution gives federal law precedence over state law, and since marijuana remains illegal under federal law, Colorado's laws are invalid. Colorado's Attorney General John Suthers vows to defend the state's laws, calling the suit "without merit". Pro-marijuana advocates argue that the lawsuit is an attempt to undermine the will of Colorado's voters and that it could jeopardize all state marijuana laws. They also point out that marijuana remains widely available, even in states that have not legalized it. The outcome of the lawsuit could have significant implications for the future of marijuana legalization in the US.

END OF SUMMARY.
