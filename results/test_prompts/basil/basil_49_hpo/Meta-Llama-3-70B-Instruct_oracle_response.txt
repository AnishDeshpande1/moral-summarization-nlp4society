SUMMARY:

Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a federal lawsuit against Colorado, claiming that its legalization of recreational marijuana is unconstitutional under federal law. The states argue that Colorado's decision has "undermined" the US Constitution and is in "violation" of the federal Controlled Substances Act. Colorado Attorney General John Suthers plans to "vigorously defend" the state's marijuana laws, calling the suit "without merit". The lawsuit argues that the Supremacy Clause gives federal law precedence over state law, and that Colorado's legalization is "prohibited" under federal law. If the Supreme Court rules in favor of Nebraska and Oklahoma, all state marijuana laws could be "unraveled". Supporters of marijuana legalization argue that the lawsuit is an attempt to "dictate" Colorado's laws and "interfere" with its democratic process. They claim that Nebraska and Oklahoma are "acting like bullies" and "wasting" taxpayer dollars.

END OF SUMMARY.
