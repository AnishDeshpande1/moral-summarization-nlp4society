SUMMARY:

Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a federal lawsuit against Colorado, claiming that its legalization of recreational marijuana is unconstitutional under federal law. The states argue that the Supremacy Clause of the US Constitution gives federal law precedence over state law, and that marijuana remains illegal under federal law. Colorado Attorney General John Suthers plans to defend the state's marijuana laws, calling the suit "without merit". The lawsuit could have implications for all state marijuana laws, as a Supreme Court ruling in favor of Nebraska and Oklahoma could unravel legalization efforts. Marijuana advocates argue that the lawsuit is an attempt to undermine the will of Colorado's voters and that it's unlikely the federal government would push back against legalization. 

END OF SUMMARY.
