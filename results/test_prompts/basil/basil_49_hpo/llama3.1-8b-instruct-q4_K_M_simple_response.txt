SUMMARY:

Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a federal lawsuit against Colorado in the U.S. Supreme Court, claiming that Colorado's legalization of recreational marijuana is unconstitutional under federal law. Nebraska Attorney General Jon Bruning argues that federal law takes precedence over state law, citing the Supremacy Clause of the Constitution. Colorado Attorney General John Suthers disagrees, stating that the suit is "without merit" and that neighboring states have expressed concern about Colorado-grown marijuana coming into their states.

The lawsuit has sparked debate on the issue of federalism and states' rights. Marijuana policy reform advocates argue that the suit is an attempt to undermine the will of Colorado's voters, while opponents of legalization claim that it is a threat to public health and safety. If the Supreme Court were to rule in favor of Nebraska and Oklahoma, all state marijuana laws could be put at risk.

END OF SUMMARY.
