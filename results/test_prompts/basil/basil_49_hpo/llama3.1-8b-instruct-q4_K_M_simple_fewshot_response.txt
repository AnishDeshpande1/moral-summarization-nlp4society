Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a federal lawsuit against Colorado, claiming that its legalization of recreational marijuana is unconstitutional under federal law. The states argue that the Supremacy Clause of the U.S. Constitution takes precedence over state laws, making federal marijuana prohibition supreme. However, Colorado Attorney General John Suthers believes the suit has "no merit" and will vigorously defend the state's marijuana laws. The regulation of recreational marijuana remains illegal under the 1970 Controlled Substances Act, but states that have legalized it have been able to do so because of federal guidance urging non-enforcement. If the Supreme Court were to rule in favor of Nebraska and Oklahoma, all state marijuana laws could be in jeopardy. Colorado officials are defending the state's decision to legalize recreational marijuana, arguing that it is a matter of individual states enacting policies that benefit their citizens.
