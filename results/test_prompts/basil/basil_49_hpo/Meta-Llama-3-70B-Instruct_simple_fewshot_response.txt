SUMMARY:

Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a federal lawsuit against Colorado, claiming that its legalization of recreational marijuana is unconstitutional under federal law. The lawsuit argues that Colorado's law undermines the federal ban on marijuana and is causing the drug to flow into their states, undermining their anti-marijuana laws. However, Colorado Attorney General John Suthers believes the lawsuit is without merit and plans to defend the state's marijuana laws. The lawsuit has sparked debate, with some supporting the challenge and others calling it "political grandstanding" without merit. If the Supreme Court were to rule in favor of Nebraska and Oklahoma, all state marijuana laws could be in jeopardy. The lawsuit highlights the precarious nature of state marijuana laws and the need for Congress to act on federal marijuana policy.

END OF SUMMARY.
