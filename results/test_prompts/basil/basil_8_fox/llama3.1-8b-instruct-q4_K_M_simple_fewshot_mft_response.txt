SUMMARY:

The Obama administration's tax cut compromise is facing strong opposition from liberal Democrats, who feel betrayed by the president's deal with Republicans. Despite warnings from outgoing White House economic adviser Larry Summers that failure to pass the bill would increase the risk of a double-dip recession, many House Democrats remain strongly opposed to the deal. They argue that extending expiring tax cuts for higher earners and imposing a lower estate tax on wealthy heirs are giveaways to the rich that will do little to help the economy. Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid has asked the administration for "changes" to the bill, but the specifics of these changes are unclear. Vice President Biden traveled to the Capitol to meet with House Democrats who are resisting the deal, but his efforts have been met with resistance. The moral framing of this issue is one of loyalty and betrayal, as liberal Democrats feel that Obama has abandoned their values in pursuit of a compromise with Republicans.

END OF SUMMARY.
