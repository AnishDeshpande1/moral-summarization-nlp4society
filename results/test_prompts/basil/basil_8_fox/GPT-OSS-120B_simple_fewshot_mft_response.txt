SUMMARY:
Larry Summers warned that blocking the Obama‑Republican tax compromise would raise the danger of a double‑dip recession, urging Congress to act quickly. The administration framed the deal as sound economic policy backed by expert forecasts, positioning the President and his team as legitimate authority seeking to protect the nation’s prosperity. In contrast, liberal Democrats are portrayed as subverting that authority, demanding “changes” and threatening to block the bill, actions depicted as a betrayal of party loyalty and of the President’s effort to deliver a bipartisan solution. Senate Republican leader Jon Kyl signaled readiness to bring the measure to a vote, while Senate Democrats, led by Dick Durbin and Harry Reid, quietly seek to reshape it. House leaders, including Vice President Biden and Speaker Pelosi, are caught in the clash: Biden pushes the deal forward as an act of responsible leadership, whereas Pelosi and a coalition of progressive representatives accuse the administration of selling out to the wealthy, labeling the estate‑tax cut a “bridge too far.” The article’s moral framing casts the President’s push for the tax bill as rightful authority, while the dissenting Democrats are cast as disloyal subversives undermining collective loyalty to the administration’s agenda. END OF SUMMARY.
