SUMMARY:

Outgoing White House economic adviser Larry Summers warned that failure to pass a new tax cut compromise would significantly raise the risk of a double-dip recession, which would stall the economy. He believes Congress will pass the deal, citing positive reviews from economists, and urged lawmakers to take action to prevent this risk. However, Senate Democrats are seeking changes to the bill, and House Democrats are resisting the deal, saying it gives away too much to the wealthy. They are trying to prevent the bill from coming to the floor, with some even trying to block outgoing House Speaker Nancy Pelosi from bringing it up. President Obama rejected claims that he betrayed Democrats by cutting a deal with Republicans, urging lawmakers to examine the details and "get this done." But so far, the response from House Democrats has not been very good, with Pelosi calling the estate tax provision "a bridge too far."

END OF SUMMARY.
