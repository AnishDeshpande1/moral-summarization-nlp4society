SUMMARY: Outgoing White House economic adviser Larry Summers warned that a failure to pass the new tax‑cut compromise would raise the risk of a double‑dip recession, potentially causing the economy to stall. He said positive reviews from economists show the deal is the right thing to do and that Congress should not take steps that increase the chance the economy will stall out. Senate Republicans hope to move the bill quickly, while Senate Democrats have asked for changes and are trying to prevent the deal from reaching the floor. Vice President Biden met with House Democrats who argue the agreement gives too much to the rich, and liberal lawmakers are drafting a letter to force a vote, insisting they will not just get the bill passed without a majority. President Obama rejected accusations of betrayal and urged lawmakers to listen to the economists’ forecasts and get the deal done. House Speaker Pelosi, however, said the response has not been good and that the estate‑tax provision is a bridge too far. END OF SUMMARY.
