Outgoing White House economic adviser Larry Summers warned that failure to pass a new tax cut compromise would significantly raise the risk of a double-dip recession. He believes that Congress will pass the deal President Obama struck with Republicans, despite withering criticism from liberal Democrats over the two-year extension of tax cuts for the rich as well as the middle class. Summers thinks that Congress wouldn't take a step that "materially increases the risk that this economy will stall out." However, Senate Democrats have asked for "changes" to the bill, and House Democrats are resisting the deal, saying it gives away too much to the wealthy.
