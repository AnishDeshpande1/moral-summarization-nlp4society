SUMMARY:

Larry Summers, outgoing White House economic adviser, warned that failure to pass the new tax cut compromise would significantly raise the risk of a double-dip recession. He believes Congress will pass the deal, despite criticism from liberal Democrats who argue it extends tax cuts for the rich and middle class at the expense of jobless benefits. Economists have given positive reviews of the deal, which includes extended jobless benefits and reduced Social Security payroll taxes. However, many House Democrats are resisting the bill, with some calling for a record vote to block its passage.

Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid has requested changes to the bill, and Vice President Biden met with House Democrats who oppose the deal. The bill's fate remains uncertain, despite Obama's urging lawmakers to pass it and his rejection of claims that he betrayed Democrats by cutting a deal with Republicans.

END OF SUMMARY
