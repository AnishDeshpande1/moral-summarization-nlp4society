SUMMARY:

The Obama administration is facing strong opposition from liberal Democrats over the tax cut compromise with Republicans, with many feeling betrayed by the president's decision to extend tax cuts for the wealthy. Outgoing White House economic adviser Larry Summers warned that failure to pass the deal would significantly raise the risk of a double-dip recession, while economists have given positive reviews to the agreement. However, Senate Democrats have asked for changes to the bill, and House Democrats are resisting the deal, saying it gives away too much to the wealthy. Vice President Biden met with House Democrats to try to sell the compromise, but many are unhappy with the provisions that benefit the rich. The moral framing of this issue is clear: the administration is prioritizing economic growth and job creation over the concerns of its liberal base, and failure to pass the deal would have serious consequences for the economy. The loyalty of Democrats to their party's values is being tested, and the authority of the administration is being challenged by those who feel betrayed by the compromise.

END OF SUMMARY.
