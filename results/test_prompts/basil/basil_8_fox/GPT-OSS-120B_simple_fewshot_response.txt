SUMMARY:
White House economic adviser Larry Summers warned that failing to approve the Obama‑Republican tax‑cut compromise could raise the chance of a double‑dip recession, arguing that economists see the deal as a boost to growth and jobs. President Obama and Vice‑President Biden are pushing the bill, which extends tax cuts for both middle‑class and high‑income earners and lowers the estate tax, in exchange for extended unemployment benefits and a cut to Social Security payroll taxes to spur spending. Liberal Democrats, however, view the concessions to the wealthy as a betrayal of their base and are mobilizing to block the legislation—Sen. Dick Durbin’s caucus is demanding changes, House leaders like Nancy Pelosi and members such as Peter DeFazio are drafting a letter to force a caucus vote, and some Republicans are ready to bring the bill to the Senate floor soon. The moral framing highlights a clash between the administration’s claim that passing the deal is essential for economic stability and the progressive side’s insistence that it sacrifices fairness and the interests of ordinary Americans for the rich. END OF SUMMARY.
