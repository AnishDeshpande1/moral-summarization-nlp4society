SUMMARY:

Larry Summers, outgoing White House economic adviser, warned that failing to pass a new tax cut compromise would increase the risk of a double-dip recession. He expressed confidence that Congress will pass the deal negotiated by President Obama and Republicans, despite criticism from liberal Democrats. The agreement extends tax cuts for the rich and middle class, as well as provides extended jobless benefits and a reduced Social Security payroll tax.

However, some House Democrats are resisting the deal, citing concerns that it gives away too much to the wealthy. Reps. DeFazio, McDermott, and Inslee are trying to block the bill from coming to the floor, while Senate Democrats have asked for "changes" to the bill. Vice President Biden met with House Democrats to address their concerns, but it remains unclear whether the deal will pass.

Obama urged lawmakers to examine the details of the agreement and "get this done," citing positive reviews from economists who predict higher job growth in 2011 and 2012 if Congress passes the agreement.

END OF SUMMARY.
