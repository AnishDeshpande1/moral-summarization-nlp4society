SUMMARY:

Larry Summers, outgoing White House economic adviser, warned that failure to pass the new tax cut compromise would significantly raise the risk of a double-dip recession. He believes Congress will pass the deal President Obama struck with Republicans, but thinks it's unlikely they would intentionally increase the risk of economic stall. Economists have given positive reviews of the deal, which includes extending tax cuts for the rich and middle class, as well as jobless benefits and reduced Social Security payroll taxes.

However, many Democrats are resisting the deal, arguing that it gives too much to the wealthy. Some are trying to prevent outgoing House Speaker Nancy Pelosi from bringing the compromise to the floor, while others hope to get a record vote in the caucus on a resolution against the bill. Obama has urged lawmakers to "get this done" and examine the details of the deal, which he believes will lead to higher job growth if passed. Despite his efforts, House Democrats are not listening and continue to resist the compromise.

END OF SUMMARY.
