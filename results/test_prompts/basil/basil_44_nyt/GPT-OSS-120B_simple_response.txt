SUMMARY: Former FBI agent and Marine Congressman Michael G. Grimm, touted for his “straight‑arrow” image, is poised to be indicted on federal mail and wire fraud and obstruction of justice charges tied to his post‑FBI health‑food restaurant, Healthalicious. The two‑year FBI and Brooklyn U.S. Attorney investigation, which began with scrutiny of his 2010 campaign fundraising, has already led to charges against two of Grimm’s fund‑raisers. Although the pending indictment appears to focus on business fraud rather than direct campaign‑finance violations, the case underscores a pattern of alleged deceit: underpaying workers, evading insurance requirements, and possibly lying under oath. Grimm’s lawyer denounces the probe as a politically motivated vendetta, insisting on his innocence and pledging his continued service. Meanwhile, Grimm’s recent on‑air tirade—threatening a reporter with violence—has further tarnished his reputation for temper and decorum. With a re‑election battle looming, the looming charges cast a shadow over the congressman’s claim to public‑service integrity.  

END OF SUMMARY.
