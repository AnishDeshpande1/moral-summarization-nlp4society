SUMMARY:
Representative Michael G. Grimm, a former FBI agent and Marine celebrated for his “straight‑arrow” image, is expected to be indicted on federal mail and wire fraud charges tied to his Upper East Side health‑food restaurant, as well as obstruction of justice for allegedly lying under oath. The investigation, which began with a probe of his 2010 campaign fundraising, has already led to charges against two of his fund‑raisers for illegal contributions and visa fraud. Critics note that the case reveals Grimm’s abuse of authority—leveraging his public‑service credentials while threatening a reporter with physical violence—and his disregard for the welfare of workers who were underpaid, denied overtime and forced to work cash‑only to evade labor laws. The alleged scheme also underscores serious fairness violations, suggesting cheating of both donors and constituents. Grimm’s lawyer dismisses the probe as a “politically driven vendetta,” but the mounting evidence points to a pattern of subverting lawful authority, harming vulnerable employees, and cheating the public trust.  
END OF SUMMARY.
