Representative Michael Grimm, a former F.B.I. agent and Marine, is expected to be indicted on federal fraud charges related to his conduct at a health food restaurant he owned in Manhattan after leaving the F.B.I. in 2006. The charges include mail fraud, wire fraud, and obstruction of justice for allegedly lying under oath in a federal lawsuit. Grimm's lawyer claims that the investigation was "a politically driven vendetta" and that Grimm asserts his innocence. However, evidence suggests otherwise, including allegations of campaign finance violations and improper donations from followers of an Israeli rabbi. Two of his fund-raisers have already been charged with illegal activities, one for funneling over $10,000 into his campaign and the other for visa fraud charges. The investigation began more than two years ago, and Grimm's hot-tempered behavior, including a bizarre threat to throw a reporter off a balcony, has raised concerns about his integrity and suitability for public office.
