SUMMARY:

Representative Michael G. Grimm, a former F.B.I. agent and Marine, is expected to be indicted on federal fraud charges for allegedly engaging in mail and wire fraud, as well as obstruction of justice by lying under oath in a federal lawsuit. The investigation, which began over two years ago, focused on his conduct with a health food restaurant he owned after leaving the F.B.I. in 2006. Two of his fund-raisers have already been charged for illegally funneling money into his campaign and visa fraud. Grimm's lawyer claims that the charges are part of a "politically driven vendetta" and that the congressman will be vindicated once the investigation settles. However, evidence suggests that Grimm may have misused his office by not carrying workers' compensation insurance at one of his restaurants and paying employees less than minimum wage. The congressman's past behavior has also raised concerns, including a bizarre threat to throw a reporter off a balcony when asked about the federal campaign financing investigation.

END OF SUMMARY.
