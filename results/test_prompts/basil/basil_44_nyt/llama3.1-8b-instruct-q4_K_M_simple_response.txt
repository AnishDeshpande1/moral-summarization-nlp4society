SUMMARY:

Representative Michael G. Grimm, a former F.B.I. agent and Marine, is expected to be indicted on federal fraud charges, including mail and wire fraud, obstruction of justice, and allegedly lying under oath in a federal lawsuit. The investigation, led by the F.B.I. and the U.S. attorney's office in Brooklyn, began over two years ago and examined Grimm's fund-raising for his 2010 election campaign. Two of his fund-raisers have already been charged with illegally funneling money into his campaign.

Grimm's lawyer claims that the investigation is a "politically driven vendetta" and that Grimm will be vindicated when the dust settles. However, documents show that Healthalicious, a health food restaurant owned by Grimm after he left the F.B.I., was ordered to pay $88,000 for not carrying workers' compensation insurance. Additionally, former deliverymen claimed that they were underpaid and threatened with retaliation if they spoke out.

Grimm's behavior has raised questions about his character, including a recent incident where he threatened a TV reporter on-air. The indictment could have significant implications for Grimm's re-election bid in November.
