SUMMARY:

Representative Michael G. Grimm, a Republican congressman from Staten Island and Brooklyn, is expected to be indicted on federal fraud charges. The investigation, led by the FBI and the US Attorney's office in Brooklyn, has been ongoing for over two years and focuses on his conduct at a health food restaurant he owned on Manhattan's Upper East Side. Charges are expected to include mail and wire fraud, as well as obstruction of justice for allegedly lying under oath. Two of Grimm's fund-raisers have already been charged with campaign finance violations. A lawyer for Grimm claims the investigation is a "politically driven vendetta" and that his client will be vindicated.

Grimm's reputation has taken a hit in recent months, including a bizarre incident where he threatened to throw a reporter off a balcony during an interview. The congressman faces re-election in November, with a potentially formidable Democratic challenger waiting in the wings. The indictment is expected to have significant implications for Grimm's campaign and his future in Congress.

END OF SUMMARY.
