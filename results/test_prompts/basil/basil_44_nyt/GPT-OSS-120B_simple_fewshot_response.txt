SUMMARY:
Representative Michael G. Grimm, a former FBI agent and Marine who has long marketed himself as a straight‑arrow public servant, is expected to be indicted on federal mail‑ and wire‑fraud charges tied to his Upper East Side health‑food restaurant, as well as obstruction of justice for allegedly lying under oath.  While two of his fund‑raisers have already faced criminal charges, the pending indictment appears to avoid direct campaign‑finance accusations, though the investigation remains ongoing.  Grimm’s lawyer blasted the case as a “politically driven vendetta,” insisting on his client’s innocence and pledging that he will continue to serve his constituents with the same “dedication and tenacity” that defined his military and law‑enforcement career.  The narrative, however, underscores a pattern of questionable conduct: unpaid wages and insurance violations at his restaurant, a threatening outburst toward a reporter, and a history of heated behavior that erodes public trust.  The looming charges highlight the essential need for accountability, ethical leadership, and respect for the rule of law in public office.  Voters will weigh these serious allegations against Grimm’s self‑portrayed record when they head to the polls in November.  
END OF SUMMARY.
