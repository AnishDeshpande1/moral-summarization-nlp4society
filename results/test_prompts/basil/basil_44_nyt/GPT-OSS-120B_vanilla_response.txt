SUMMARY: Former FBI agent and Marine Rep. Michael G. Grimm, a Republican from Staten Island and Brooklyn, is expected to be indicted on federal mail and wire fraud charges, plus obstruction of justice, stemming from misconduct related to his Upper East Side health‑food restaurant, Healthalicious, after leaving the FBI in 2006. The FBI and Brooklyn U.S. Attorney’s Office have been investigating his 2010 campaign fundraising for over two years; two fund‑raisers have already been charged, but the pending indictment likely will not include campaign‑finance or office‑misuse allegations. Grimm’s lawyer, William McGinley, says the charges are politically motivated and affirms Grimm’s innocence. The restaurant’s operator, Granny Sayz, LLC, faced workers‑compensation violations and wage‑law lawsuits, and was dissolved after complaints. Grimm, the sole New York City Republican in Congress, is up for re‑election and faces a strong Democratic challenger. He recently made headlines for an on‑air outburst, threatening a reporter after the State of the Union. END OF SUMMARY.
