SUMMARY:

President Trump has announced that he is considering an executive order to remove birthright citizenship for babies born in the US to non-citizen parents, a right enshrined in the 14th Amendment of the Constitution. Trump claims that this can be done through an executive order, despite experts saying it would require a constitutional amendment. This move has been met with criticism from both Democrats and some Republicans, who argue that it is unconstitutional and would punish the children of undocumented immigrants. Senator Lindsey Graham has announced plans to introduce legislation to end birthright citizenship, but even he has previously stated that this would require a constitutional amendment. Experts have warned that such an executive order would be challenged in court and likely fail, as the Supreme Court has ruled in 1898 that a child born in the US to immigrant parents is a citizen under the 14th Amendment.
