SUMMARY: President Donald Trump's vow to consider an executive order to end birthright citizenship for babies born in the U.S. to non-citizen parents has sparked controversy. Trump stated that he could use an executive order to change the policy, which is enshrined in the 14th Amendment and cannot be changed by presidential order. The president claimed that the system "has to end" and that it's a "magnet for illegal immigration." However, experts say this would be an unconstitutional attempt to erase the rights of children born in the U.S., sowing division and fanning anti-immigrant hatred ahead of the midterm elections.

Trump's statement aligns with his administration's attempts to stoke fear about immigration, including falsely claiming that migrants are criminals and threatening to cut off aid to countries where most of them hail from. Rep. Carlos Curbelo criticized Trump's idea, calling for immigration reform that reaffirms the country's tradition as a nation of immigrants. The move has been met with opposition from many Republicans and experts who say it's an attempt to end birthright citizenship, which is enshrined in the Constitution.

END OF SUMMARY.
