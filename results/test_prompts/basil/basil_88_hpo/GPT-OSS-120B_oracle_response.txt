SUMMARY: President Donald Trump told reporters he is considering an executive order to end birthright citizenship, a right guaranteed by the 14th Amendment. He stated the policy is “ridiculous” and that the system “has to end,” falsely claiming the U.S. is the only country offering citizenship to those born on its soil. Legal experts ruled that only a constitutional amendment could change the amendment, and that an executive order would be unconstitutional. Critics called the proposal an attempt to sow division and fan anti‑immigrant hatred ahead of the midterms. House Speaker Paul Ryan and other Republicans made clear the president cannot erase the Constitution with an executive order. ACLU director Omar Jadwat said the move is a transparent, blatantly unconstitutional effort to punish the children of undocumented immigrants. Senator Lindsey Graham announced he will introduce legislation “along the same lines” to end birthright citizenship, arguing the policy “makes” illegal immigration a magnet and must come to an end. Trump’s rhetoric continues stoking fear, claiming the migrant caravan is full of criminals and threatening to cut aid to Central American nations while deploying troops to the border. END OF SUMMARY.
