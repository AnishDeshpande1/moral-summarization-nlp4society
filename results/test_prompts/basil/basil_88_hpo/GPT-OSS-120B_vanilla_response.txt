SUMMARY: President Donald Trump announced on “Axios on HBO” that he is considering an executive order to eliminate birthright citizenship for children born in the United States to non‑citizen parents, a right guaranteed by the 14th Amendment. Trump claimed the change could be done without a constitutional amendment, contrary to legal consensus. He also falsely asserted the U.S. is the only country offering such citizenship. Legal experts note the 14th Amendment cannot be overridden by an executive order and cite the Supreme Court’s 1898 decision in *U.S. v. Wong Kim Ark* upholding birthright citizenship. Republicans, including House Speaker Paul Ryan, Rep. Carlos Curbelo, Rep. Justin Amash, and Sen. Lindsey Graham, criticized or dismissed the proposal, though Graham said he would introduce legislation with a similar aim, despite acknowledging a constitutional amendment is likely required. Constitutional scholar John Eastman argued the amendment’s original intent was misapplied. The proposal is seen as political rhetoric ahead of the midterm elections, part of Trump’s broader anti‑immigrant messaging, and would almost certainly face legal challenges. END OF SUMMARY.
