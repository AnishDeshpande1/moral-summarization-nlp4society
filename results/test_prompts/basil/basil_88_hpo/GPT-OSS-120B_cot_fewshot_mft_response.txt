**SUMMARY:**  
President Donald Trump announced on “Axios on HBO” that he is considering an executive order to strip birthright citizenship from babies born in the United States to non‑citizen parents—a right guaranteed by the 14th Amendment. Trump called the policy “ridiculous” and claimed it can be ended without a constitutional amendment, despite legal experts warning that such an order would be “blatantly unconstitutional” and outside the bounds of presidential authority. Critics, including House Speaker Paul Ryan and Rep. Justin Amash, said the move would “punish” children of undocumented immigrants and fuel anti‑immigrant hatred, division, and fear. The ACLU called it a transparent attempt to sow division and fan hatred ahead of the midterms. While some Republicans, like Sen. Lindsey Graham, voiced support and promised legislation, most legal scholars argue the amendment cannot be altered by executive action, underscoring a clash between authority/subversion and concerns for care, fairness, and loyalty to the nation’s constitutional tradition.
