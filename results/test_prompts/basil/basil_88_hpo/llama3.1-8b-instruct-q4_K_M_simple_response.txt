SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump is considering an executive order that would strip babies born in the U.S. to non-citizen parents of their birthright citizenship, a right enshrined in the 14th Amendment. This move has been widely criticized by experts and some Republicans as unconstitutional and a blatant attempt to fan anti-immigrant hatred ahead of the midterm elections. Trump claims he can do this via executive order, but most experts believe it would be challenged in court and likely fail. The policy change would disproportionately affect children of undocumented immigrants, whom Trump has vowed to remove from the country.

Many Republicans, including House Speaker Paul Ryan and Rep. Carlos Curbelo, have shot down Trump's idea, calling for immigration reform that makes the country more secure while reaffirming its tradition as a nation of immigrants. Meanwhile, Sen. Lindsey Graham announced plans to introduce legislation along similar lines, which would require a constitutional amendment.

END OF SUMMARY
