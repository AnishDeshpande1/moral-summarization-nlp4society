SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump has stated that he is considering an executive order to remove the right to citizenship for babies born in the U.S. to parents who aren't citizens, citing the 14th Amendment as a possible way to change this policy without a constitutional amendment. However, experts and many Republicans have disputed this claim, saying it would be unconstitutional and outside the president's authority. The 14th Amendment guarantees birthright citizenship, and changing it would require a constitutional amendment. Trump has long supported ending birthright citizenship, but his latest statement comes as he stokes fear about immigration ahead of next week's midterm elections. Some Republicans have criticized his plan, while others, including Sen. Lindsey Graham, are introducing legislation to end the policy.

END OF SUMMARY.
