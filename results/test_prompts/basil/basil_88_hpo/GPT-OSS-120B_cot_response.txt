**SUMMARY:**  
President Donald Trump announced on “Axios on HBO” that he is considering an executive order to strip birthright citizenship from babies born in the United States to non‑citizen parents—a right the 14th Amendment declares “ridiculous” and “blatantly unconstitutional.” He claimed the policy can be changed without a constitutional amendment, despite legal scholars and the Supreme Court’s 1898 ruling that such citizenship is guaranteed. Critics, including House Speaker Paul Ryan and several Republicans, warned the move is “outside the bounds” of presidential authority and would “punish” children of undocumented immigrants, fanning anti‑immigrant hatred and division ahead of the midterms. Trump’s rhetoric also called the migrant caravan “criminals, gang members and terrorists,” stoking fear and portraying the nation as heading “to hell.” While some legal experts like John Eastman argue the amendment is misapplied, most experts say an executive order would fail and trigger a major legal battle.
