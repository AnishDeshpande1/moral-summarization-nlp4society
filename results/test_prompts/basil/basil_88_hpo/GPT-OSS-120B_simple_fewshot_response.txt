SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump announced he is weighing an executive order to strip birth‑right citizenship from children born in the United States to non‑citizen parents—a right guaranteed by the 14th Amendment and historically upheld by the Supreme Court. Trump falsely claimed the U.S. is unique in granting such citizenship and suggested the change could be made without a constitutional amendment. Legal experts and the ACLU say the move would be unconstitutional and would spark a costly court battle, while Republican leaders including Speaker Paul Ryan and Rep. Justin Amash dismissed the idea as beyond presidential authority. A few allies, such as Sen. Lindsey Graham and constitutional scholar John Eastman, voiced support for revisiting the amendment, but their proposals also face constitutional hurdles. Trump’s rhetoric fits a broader anti‑immigrant campaign ahead of the midterm elections, where he has exaggerated threats from migrant caravans and deployed troops to the border. The proposed order appears aimed at punishing children of undocumented immigrants and stoking division, but most scholars expect it to fail legally.

END OF SUMMARY.
