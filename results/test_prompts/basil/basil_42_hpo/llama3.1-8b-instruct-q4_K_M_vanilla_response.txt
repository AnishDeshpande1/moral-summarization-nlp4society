SUMMARY:

Arizona Governor Jan Brewer (R) vetoed Senate Bill 1062 on Wednesday, which would have allowed businesses to refuse service to individuals based on "religious freedom" grounds. The bill was criticized by activists, lawmakers, and business interests for allowing discrimination against same-sex couples. The state's two Republican senators, Jeff Flake and John McCain, called for the governor to veto the bill, as did major corporations like Marriott, Apple, and American Airlines. The Arizona Super Bowl Host Committee also opposed the legislation, citing concerns about its potential impact on the state's economy.

The bill had been passed by the state legislature last week but faced intense criticism in recent days. Governor Brewer stated that she hadn't heard any examples of business owners' religious liberty being violated, and that the bill would create more problems than it solves.

Similar bills have been proposed in other states, including Tennessee, Kansas, South Dakota, and Maine. With the veto, Arizona joins a growing list of states that have rejected such legislation.

END OF SUMMARY.
