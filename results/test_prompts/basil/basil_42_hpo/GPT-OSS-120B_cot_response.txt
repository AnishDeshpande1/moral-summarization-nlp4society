**SUMMARY:**  
Arizona Governor Jan Brewer vetoed Senate Bill 1062, a measure that would have let businesses legally *discriminate* against same‑sex couples on *religious* grounds. Brewer argued the bill had no real *religious* justification and “the *potential* to create more problems than it purports to solve,” noting she had never heard a case where a business owner’s *religious* liberty was *violated*. The legislation faced intense *criticism* and *opposition* from activists, Republican senators Jeff Flake and John McCain, and major corporations such as Marriott, Apple, and American Airlines. The Arizona Super Bowl Host Committee warned the bill would deal a significant *blow* to the state’s *economic* growth and urged against its passage, especially with the 2015 Super Bowl slated for Glendale. Similar *discriminate*‑focused bills have emerged in other states, but Brewer’s *veto* halted Arizona’s effort.
