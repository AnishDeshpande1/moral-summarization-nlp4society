SUMMARY:
Arizona Governor Jan Brewer has vetoed legislation that would have allowed businesses to discriminate against same-sex couples on "religious freedom" grounds. The bill, SB 1062, was passed by the state legislature last week but faced intense criticism from activists, lawmakers, and business interests. Major corporations like Marriott, Apple, and American Airlines opposed the bill, citing concerns about its impact on Arizona's economy. Brewer stated that she had not heard of any instances where business owners' religious liberty had been violated, and that the bill was unnecessary. The veto is seen as a victory for LGBTQ rights advocates, who argued that such legislation would allow businesses to discriminate against same-sex couples.

The move comes amidst a broader national trend of states introducing similar bills allowing open discrimination against same-sex couples. Arizona's decision not to pass SB 1062 has been hailed by many as a positive step towards equality and inclusivity. Brewer's veto is seen as a moral stand for fairness and equal treatment, rejecting discriminatory legislation that could have caused harm to marginalized communities.

END OF SUMMARY.
