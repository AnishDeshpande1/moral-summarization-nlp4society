SUMMARY: Arizona Governor Jan Brewer vetoed Senate Bill 1062, which would have permitted businesses to refuse service on "religious freedom" grounds, effectively allowing discrimination against same-sex couples. Brewer argued the bill lacked a real religious liberty issue and could cause more problems than it solved. The Republican‑controlled legislature had passed the bill, but it faced strong opposition from activists, national and local business leaders, and the state’s own Republican senators, Jeff Flake and John McCain. Companies such as Marriott, Apple, and American Airlines wrote against it, and the Arizona Super Bowl Host Committee warned the measure would harm the state’s economic prospects, especially with the 2015 Super Bowl scheduled for Glendale. Similar “religious freedom” bills have been introduced in other states, including Tennessee, Kansas, South Dakota, and Maine. The article corrects an earlier error, noting the Super Bowl will be in Glendale, not Tempe. END OF SUMMARY.
