SUMMARY:
Arizona Governor Jan Brewer vetoed Senate Bill 1062, a measure that would have let businesses refuse service on “religious freedom” grounds and effectively legalize discrimination against same‑sex couples. Brewer argued the bill offered no real solution to any documented religious‑liberty problem and could create far more trouble than it solved. Her decision came after intense backlash from activists, business leaders and even Arizona’s own Republican senators, Jeff Flake and John McCain, who warned the law would harm the state’s reputation and economy—especially with the 2015 Super Bowl slated for Glendale. Major corporations such as Marriott, Apple and American Airlines, as well as the Arizona Super Bowl Host Committee, publicly opposed the bill, citing concerns over economic growth and moral responsibility. Brewer’s veto underscores the principle that protecting religious claims should not be used as a pretext for denying basic civil rights to LGBTQ individuals. It also highlights the broader national debate, as similar anti‑discrimination bills have surfaced in other states.

END OF SUMMARY.
