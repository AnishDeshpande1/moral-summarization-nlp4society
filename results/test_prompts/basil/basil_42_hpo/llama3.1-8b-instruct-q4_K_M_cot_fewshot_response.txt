Arizona Gov. Jan Brewer (R) announced her decision to veto legislation that would have allowed businesses to discriminate against same-sex couples on "religious freedom" grounds, effectively allowing them to violate the liberty of others. The bill had the potential to create more problems than it purported to solve and did not address a specific or present concern related to religious liberty in Arizona. Despite intense criticism from activists, lawmakers, and business interests, both in Arizona and nationally, the state legislature passed the bill, known as SB 1062. However, major corporations like Marriott, Apple, and American Airlines had opposed it, and businesses in the Phoenix area were worried about its passage, fearing it would deal a significant blow to the state's economic growth potential.
