**SUMMARY:**
Arizona Gov. Jan Brewer (R) vetoed a bill that would have allowed businesses to refuse service on "religious freedom" grounds, effectively allowing them to discriminate against same-sex couples. The governor said the bill had no basis and was not addressing a specific concern related to religious liberty in Arizona. In fact, she stated that no one has come forward with an example of business owners' religious liberty being violated. This move comes after intense criticism from activists, lawmakers, and businesses both within and outside Arizona. Major corporations like Marriott, Apple, and American Airlines expressed their opposition to the bill. The veto also addresses concerns about economic growth potential in the state, particularly as it prepares to host the Super Bowl in 2015.
