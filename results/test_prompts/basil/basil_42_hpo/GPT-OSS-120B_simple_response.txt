SUMMARY: Arizona Governor Jan Brewer vetoed Senate Bill 1062, a measure that would have let businesses refuse service on “religious freedom” grounds, effectively legalizing discrimination against same‑sex couples. Brewer argued the bill created unnecessary problems, noting she had never heard of a genuine religious‑liberty violation by business owners. The veto came after intense backlash from activists, bipartisan Republican senators, and major corporations such as Marriott, Apple and American Airlines, all warning that the law would harm Arizona’s reputation and economy—particularly with the 2015 Super Bowl slated for Glendale. The state’s Super Bowl Host Committee echoed these concerns, stating the legislation would undermine economic growth and the event’s inclusive image. Brewer’s decision highlights a moral stance that protecting civil rights for LGBTQ+ individuals outweighs unfounded claims of religious persecution, and it signals resistance to a wave of similar anti‑LGBTQ bills emerging in other states. END OF SUMMARY.
