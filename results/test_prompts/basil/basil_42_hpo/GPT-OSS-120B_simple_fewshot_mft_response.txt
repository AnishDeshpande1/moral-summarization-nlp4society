SUMMARY:
Arizona Governor Jan Brewer exercised her executive authority to veto Senate Bill 1062, a measure that would have permitted businesses to refuse service on “religious‑freedom” grounds, effectively sanctioning discrimination against same‑sex couples. Brewer argued the bill lacked any real threat to religious liberty and warned it would create more problems than it solved, positioning her decision as a subversive act against a misguided legislative push. By blocking the bill, she upheld fairness, preventing a legal loophole that would have allowed businesses to cheat the principle of equal treatment. The veto also defended a sense of societal sanctity, rejecting a law that threatened to degrade the dignity of LGBTQ Arizonans. Her action was reinforced by bipartisan criticism—from Republican senators Jeff Flake and John McCain to major corporations such as Marriott, Apple and American Airlines—who warned the legislation would harm the state’s reputation and economic prospects, especially with the 2015 Super Bowl slated for Glendale. Brewer’s refusal thus reaffirmed respect for legitimate authority, protected fairness, and preserved the moral integrity of the community.

END OF SUMMARY.
