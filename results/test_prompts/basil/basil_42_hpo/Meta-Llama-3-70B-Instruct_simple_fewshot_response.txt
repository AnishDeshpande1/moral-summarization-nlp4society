SUMMARY:

Arizona Gov. Jan Brewer vetoed a controversial bill that would have allowed businesses to legally refuse service to anyone on "religious freedom" grounds, effectively permitting discrimination against same-sex couples. Brewer cited the bill's potential to create more problems than it solves, and noted that she hadn't heard of any instances where business owners' religious liberty had been violated. The bill had faced intense criticism from activists, lawmakers, and businesses, including major corporations like Marriott, Apple, and American Airlines. The Arizona Super Bowl Host Committee also opposed the bill, warning that it would harm the state's economic growth potential. The veto is a significant victory for advocates of LGBTQ+ rights, who argued that the bill would have legalized discrimination. The moral framing of this issue is clear: is it right to prioritize religious beliefs over the rights and dignity of marginalized groups?

END OF SUMMARY.
