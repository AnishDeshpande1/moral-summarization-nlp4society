SUMMARY:

Arizona Gov. Jan Brewer (R) vetoed legislation that would have allowed businesses to discriminate against same-sex couples on "religious freedom" grounds. Despite passing in the state legislature, the bill faced intense criticism from activists, lawmakers, and major corporations. Senators Jeff Flake and John McCain, as well as companies like Marriott, Apple, and American Airlines, opposed the bill, citing concerns about its impact on Arizona's economy and reputation. The veto is a significant victory for LGBTQ rights advocates, who argued that the bill would have enabled businesses to discriminate against marginalized communities. Brewer cited the lack of evidence supporting the need for such legislation, stating it "does not address a specific or present concern related to religious liberty in Arizona." Her decision is a testament to the power of public pressure and the importance of standing up against discriminatory policies.
