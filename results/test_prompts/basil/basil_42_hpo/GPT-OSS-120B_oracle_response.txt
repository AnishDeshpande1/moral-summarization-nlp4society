SUMMARY: Arizona Governor Jan Brewer vetoed Senate Bill 1062, a measure that would have let businesses refuse service on “religious freedom” grounds and thereby discriminate against same‑sex couples. Brewer argued the bill would create more problems than it solves and noted she has not heard any instance where a business owner’s liberty was violated. The legislation faced strong opposition from both sides of the aisle, including Republican senators Jeff Flake and John McCain, as well as major corporations such as Marriott, Apple and American Airlines. Local businesses and the Arizona Super Bowl Host Committee warned that adopting the bill would be contrary to the state’s economic growth and could deal a significant blow to the upcoming 2015 Super Bowl in Glendale. Similar anti‑LGBTQ bills are pushing forward in other states, popping up in Tennessee, Kansas, South Dakota and Maine, prompting nationwide criticism. END OF SUMMARY.
