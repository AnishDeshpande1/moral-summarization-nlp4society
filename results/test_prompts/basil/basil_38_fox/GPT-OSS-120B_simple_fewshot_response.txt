SUMMARY:
The IRS and Treasury Department announced that legally married same‑sex couples can now file federal joint tax returns, granting them the same tax benefits as opposite‑sex couples. The rule, issued after the Supreme Court struck down part of the Defense of Marriage Act, applies nationwide regardless of a couple’s state of residence, but does not extend to domestic partnerships or civil unions. Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew called the decision “certainty and clear, coherent tax‑filing guidance” that gives all Americans the benefits, responsibilities and protections they deserve. Human Rights Campaign leader Chad Griffin praised the move as ending discrimination and ensuring that “committed and loving” gay and lesbian families receive equal federal tax treatment. Opponents, such as National Organization of Marriage president Brian Brown, condemned the ruling as part of a broader “lawlessness” and an attempt by the Obama administration to force same‑sex marriage on the public, arguing that only Congress should set federal tax policy.  
END OF SUMMARY.
