SUMMARY: The IRS and Treasury Department announced that legally married same‑sex couples can now file federal joint tax returns, granting them the same tax benefits as opposite‑sex couples. This follows the Supreme Court’s ruling that struck down part of the Defense of Marriage Act, clarifying that federal tax treatment applies to all legally married gay couples regardless of state recognition, but excludes domestic partnerships and civil unions. Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew called the ruling a provision of clear, nationwide tax guidance, while Human Rights Campaign leader Chad Griffin hailed it as equal treatment under federal law. Opposition came from the National Organization of Marriage, whose president Brian Brown condemned the move as overreach by the Obama administration, arguing that only Congress should set tax policy. END OF SUMMARY.
