SUMMARY:
The IRS and Treasury Department have announced that legally married same-sex couples will now be allowed to file federal joint tax returns, providing them with the same tax benefits as heterosexual couples. This decision comes after the US Supreme Court's ruling on the Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA) earlier this year. The new rules apply to all legally married gay couples nationwide, regardless of their home state's recognition of gay marriage. Domestic partnerships and civil unions are not affected by these changes. Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew stated that the decision provides "certainty and clear, coherent tax filing guidance" for same-sex couples.

Human Rights Campaign President Chad Griffin praised the ruling as a step towards equality, while Brian Brown, president of the National Organization of Marriage, criticized it as an attempt to force same-sex marriage on the public. The change is seen as a major victory for the LGBTQ+ community and a significant step forward in the fight for equal rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
