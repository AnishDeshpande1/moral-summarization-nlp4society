SUMMARY:

In a major breakthrough, the IRS and Treasury Department announced that legally married same-sex couples will now be allowed to file federal joint tax returns. This ruling provides certainty and clear guidance for all legally married same-sex couples nationwide, ensuring they can access crucial tax benefits and protections previously denied under the discriminatory Defense of Marriage Act. Human Rights Campaign President Chad Griffin hailed the decision as a major victory for equality, stating that committed and loving gay and lesbian married couples will now be treated equally under federal tax laws. However, not everyone was pleased with the decision. Brian Brown, president of the National Organization of Marriage, criticized the ruling, claiming it continues a pattern of "lawlessness" and forces same-sex "marriage" on an unwilling public. The move is seen as a significant step towards fairness and equality for same-sex couples, who will now have access to the same tax benefits as heterosexual couples.
