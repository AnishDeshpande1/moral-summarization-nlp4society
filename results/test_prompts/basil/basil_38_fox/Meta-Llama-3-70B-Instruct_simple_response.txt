SUMMARY:

In a landmark decision, the IRS and Treasury Department have announced that legally married same-sex couples can now file federal joint tax returns, granting them the same benefits as heterosexual couples. This ruling comes on the heels of the U.S. Supreme Court's historic decision to overturn part of the Defense of Marriage Act. The new policy applies to all legally married gay couples, regardless of their state of residence, but excludes domestic partnerships and civil unions. Human rights advocates have hailed the decision, saying it provides equal treatment under federal tax law and access to crucial benefits and protections. However, opponents, such as the National Organization of Marriage, have criticized the move, calling it an overreach of executive power and an attempt to force same-sex marriage on the public. This decision marks a significant step towards equality and justice for LGBTQ+ individuals and families.

END OF SUMMARY.
