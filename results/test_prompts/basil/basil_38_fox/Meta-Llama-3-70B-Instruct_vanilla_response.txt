SUMMARY:

The IRS and Treasury Department announced that legally married same-sex couples can now file federal joint tax returns, granting them the same tax benefits as heterosexual couples. This decision comes after the U.S. Supreme Court's historic ruling that overturned part of the Defense of Marriage Act earlier this year. The new rules apply to all legally married gay couples, regardless of their state of residence, but do not extend to domestic partnerships and civil unions. Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew stated that the ruling provides "certainty and clear, coherent tax filing guidance" for same-sex couples nationwide. Human Rights Campaign President Chad Griffin hailed the decision, saying it treats gay and lesbian married couples equally under federal tax laws. However, opponents, such as National Organization of Marriage President Brian Brown, criticized the decision, calling it an example of "lawlessness" and arguing that Congress alone should determine federal tax law.

END OF SUMMARY.
