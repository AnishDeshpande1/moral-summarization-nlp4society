SUMMARY:
The IRS and Treasury Department announced that legally married same‑sex couples can now file federal joint tax returns, giving them the same tax benefits as opposite‑sex couples. Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew said the rule provides “certainty and clear, coherent tax‑filing guidance” and ensures that all Americans receive the benefits, responsibilities and protections they deserve under federal tax law. Human Rights Campaign President Chad Griffin hailed the decision as a correction of the “discriminatory Defense of Marriage Act,” stressing that committed gay and lesbian families will finally be treated equally and receive the tax benefits they have been denied.  

Opposition came from the National Organization of Marriage, whose president Brian Brown called the change a continuation of “lawlessness” and accused the Obama administration of forcing same‑sex “marriage” on the public, arguing that only Congress should set federal tax policy. The debate frames the ruling as a matter of fairness—extending equal tax rights to all married couples—versus a perceived cheating of democratic process by bypassing legislative authority.  

END OF SUMMARY.
