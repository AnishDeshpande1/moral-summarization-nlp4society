SUMMARY:

In a significant move, the IRS and Treasury Department announced that legally married same-sex couples will now be allowed to file federal joint tax returns. This ruling provides equal access to tax benefits for homosexual couples, treating them the same as heterosexual couples when filing jointly. The decision comes in response to the US Supreme Court's historic ruling on the Defense of Marriage Act earlier this year. Human Rights Campaign President Chad Griffin hailed the move, stating that committed and loving gay and lesbian married couples will now be treated equally under federal tax laws. However, not everyone agrees with the decision, as Brian Brown, president of the National Organization for Marriage, believes it continues a pattern of lawlessness and forces same-sex "marriage" on an unwilling public. The ruling brings certainty and clear guidance to all legally married same-sex couples nationwide.
