SUMMARY:
The IRS and Treasury Department have announced that legally married same-sex couples will now be allowed to file federal joint tax returns, providing them with the same tax benefits as heterosexual couples. This ruling comes after a Supreme Court decision overturned part of the Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA). The new rules apply to all legally married gay couples nationwide, regardless of where they live, but do not extend to domestic partnerships and civil unions. Advocates for LGBTQ+ rights hail this decision as a step towards equality, while opponents argue it is an overreach of executive power.

Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew stated that the ruling provides certainty and access to benefits, responsibilities, and protections under federal tax law. Human Rights Campaign President Chad Griffin added that committed gay and lesbian couples will now be treated equally under federal tax laws, regardless of their state of residence. The move has been met with criticism from groups opposed to same-sex marriage, who argue it is a forced imposition on the public.

END OF SUMMARY.
