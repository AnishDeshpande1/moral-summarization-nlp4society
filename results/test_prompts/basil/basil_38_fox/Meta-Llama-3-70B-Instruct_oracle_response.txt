SUMMARY:

The IRS and Treasury Department announced that legally married same-sex couples can now file federal joint tax returns, allowing them to receive the same tax benefits as heterosexual couples. This ruling comes after the U.S. Supreme Court overturned a part of the Defense of Marriage Act, which previously denied same-sex couples equal treatment under federal tax law. The new rules provide certainty and clear guidance for all legally married same-sex couples, regardless of where they live, and give them access to benefits, responsibilities, and protections under federal tax law that all Americans deserve. This decision is a step towards treating committed and loving gay and lesbian married couples equally under federal tax laws, regardless of their state of residence. However, opponents like Brian Brown of the National Organization of Marriage argue that this decision continues a pattern of lawlessness and forces same-sex "marriage" on an unwilling public.

END OF SUMMARY.
