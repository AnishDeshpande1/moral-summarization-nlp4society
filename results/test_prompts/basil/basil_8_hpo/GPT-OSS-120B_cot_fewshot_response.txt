While Summers **downplayed** the urgency of extending cuts for the wealthy, former Clinton official Rob Shapiro offered a **sober‑minded** view, saying the **danger** lay in a contractionary fiscal stance and urging housing‑market stabilization. The administration’s pitch was stark: accept the current package now or face **consequences** and **blame** for an economic downturn. The White House framed the vote as a choice between a modest stimulus and a **dangerous** economic slide. END OF SUMMARY.
