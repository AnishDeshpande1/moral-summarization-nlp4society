SUMMARY:

Senior White House officials, including Larry Summers and David Axelrod, warned congressional Democrats that inaction on the Bush tax cuts would significantly increase the risk of a double dip recession, which could stall the economy. Rob Shapiro, a former commerce official, offered a more nuanced view, stating that a delay in passing the tax cut package would not cause a double dip, but rather highlight the need for a stimulative policy to boost growth. The White House began selling the deal to Democrats by emphasizing the consequences of failing to pass the bill, including higher taxes and lost unemployment benefits. They argue that stabilizing the housing market is crucial to averting a recession, but this issue is not currently on the congressional docket. The administration's pitch to the party is: take the package now or risk facing difficult economic consequences.

END OF SUMMARY.
