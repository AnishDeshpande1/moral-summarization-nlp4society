SUMMARY:

Senior White House officials have escalated the pressure on congressional Democrats to pass the Bush tax cuts deal, warning that inaction would significantly increase the risk of a double dip recession. Outgoing senior economic adviser Larry Summers stated that failure to pass the bill within the next couple of weeks would "materially increase the risk" of an economic downturn. The White House is selling the deal as a necessary measure to boost growth and prevent a recession, while also emphasizing the consequences of inaction, such as higher taxes and unemployment benefits expiring.

The moral framing of this issue relies heavily on the dimensions of Authority/Subversion and Care/Harm. The White House is implying that congressional Democrats are subverting their authority by not supporting the deal, which they frame as a necessary measure to prevent economic harm. They emphasize the consequences of inaction, highlighting the potential for higher taxes and job losses, and positioning themselves as guardians of economic stability.

The pitch being offered from the administration to the rest of the party is essentially: take the package now or risk being blamed for an economic downturn. The White House is framing their position as a responsible and caring approach to economic policy, while portraying Democratic opposition as irresponsible and harmful.

END OF SUMMARY.
