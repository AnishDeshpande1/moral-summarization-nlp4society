SUMMARY:

Senior White House officials, including Larry Summers and David Axelrod, warned congressional Democrats that failing to pass the Bush tax cut bill would significantly increase the risk of a double dip recession. They framed inaction as having real consequences, including higher taxes for individuals and businesses on January 1, and 2 million people losing unemployment insurance by the end of the month. The White House pitched the package as a necessary compromise, acknowledging that some provisions are unpopular but arguing they will help the economy. Rob Shapiro, a former Clinton administration official, offered a more nuanced view, suggesting that while the deal is stimulative, it may not be enough to lift the economy out of its current slow growth.

The White House is pressuring Democrats to pass the bill quickly, warning that waiting will only lead to negative consequences. They argue that trying to negotiate further will only result in a worse agreement, and that the best option is to take the package as it stands. The stakes are being framed as high, with significant economic risks if the bill does not pass.

END OF SUMMARY.
