**SUMMARY:**
The White House officials, including outgoing senior economic adviser Larry Summers, have significantly raised the stakes on congressional Democrats to pass the Bush tax cuts bill. They warn that inaction will "significantly increase the risk" of a double dip recession and stall the economy. A delay would not cause a double dip, but it could lead to a contractionary fiscal policy, which is still undesirable. The current deal is stimulative and ought to boost growth, but it's not enough to lift the economy out of its current state. If Democrats don't pass the bill, they will be blamed for an economic downturn that would be really difficult to recover from. The White House is pitching the package as a necessary measure to stabilize the economy, urging Democrats to take it now or face the consequences.
