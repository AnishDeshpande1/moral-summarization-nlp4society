SUMMARY: Senior White House officials warned that congressional **inaction** on a Bush tax‑cut extension would **raise** the risk of a double‑dip recession, urging Democrats to **pass** the bill quickly. Outgoing economic adviser Larry Summers warned that delaying the deal would **significantly increase** recession risk, while former Clinton official Rob Shapiro cautioned that a brief wait would not cause a double dip, but a contractionary fiscal stance could. The administration’s **selling** point to skeptical Democrats is that the package, though generous to the wealthy, contains measures that **help** lower‑income workers, such as a 13‑month unemployment‑insurance extension. Press Secretary Robert Gibbs said tweaks are acceptable **on** condition they don’t reduce support, and adviser David Axelrod stressed that failing to act will **lose** 2 million people their unemployment benefits and push tax burdens **up** on January 1. The White House frames the choice as a stark moral trade‑off: **pass** the deal now or face economic decline and real **losses** for ordinary Americans. END OF SUMMARY.
