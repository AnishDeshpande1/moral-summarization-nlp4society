SUMMARY:

Senior White House officials warned congressional Democrats that inaction on the Bush tax cuts would significantly increase the risk of a double dip recession, which would have devastating consequences for the economy. Larry Summers stated that not passing the bill within the next couple weeks would "materially increase the risk" of economic stagnation and a double dip recession. The White House is selling the deal to Democrats by emphasizing the need to help the economy and the people affected by the tax cuts, but the package has been met with skepticism.

The administration emphasized that passing the bill now will prevent significant consequences, such as increased taxes for individuals on January 1 and unemployment benefits being lost for 2 million people at the end of this month. The White House is cautioning Democrats against delaying or rejecting the deal, arguing that it would be fundamentally worse to wait and try to negotiate a better agreement later.

The stakes are high, and the administration is framing inaction as a choice between two undesirable options: pass the bill now with its limitations or risk facing a difficult situation without any relief. The White House is urging Democrats to pass the bill to help the economy and prevent further harm.

END OF SUMMARY.
