SUMMARY:

Senior White House officials, led by Larry Summers, warned congressional Democrats that inaction on the Bush tax cuts would significantly increase the risk of a double dip recession. Summers stated that if the bill is not passed within the next few weeks, it will "materially increase the risk" of an economic downturn. However, some experts, such as Rob Shapiro, downplayed the significance of continuing the Bush tax cuts and suggested that a delay would not cause a double dip.

The White House began selling the deal to skeptical Democrats, offering few concessions. Press Secretary Robert Gibbs stated that any changes to the package would be acceptable, but only if they don't result in decreased support. Senior adviser David Axelrod emphasized the consequences of delaying or rejecting the tax cut deal, including increased taxes and lost unemployment insurance for 2 million people.

The administration is urging Democrats to accept the current package rather than risking a potential economic downturn. Gibbs questioned whether delaying the vote would lead to a better agreement, stating that no one has proposed an alternative plan that would be fundamentally better than the current deal.
