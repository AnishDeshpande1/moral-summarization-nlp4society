SUMMARY:
Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio endorsed Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination, citing Perry's record on border security as a key factor in his decision. Arpaio is known for his hardline stance on immigration and has been critical of politicians who fail to secure the border. He praised Perry's efforts to enforce border laws and his plan to add more personnel, fencing, and surveillance along the US-Mexico border. Perry also received support from Arpaio, who claimed that no candidate has done more to secure the border than Perry. In response, Perry vowed to "secure the border within a year" if elected president.

Arpaio's endorsement is seen as a boost for Perry's campaign, which has faced criticism over his immigration platform and opposition to a full border fence. The endorsement comes after Arpaio endorsed Mitt Romney in 2008 and held a joint press conference with Herman Cain in October to discuss immigration enforcement.

END OF SUMMARY.
