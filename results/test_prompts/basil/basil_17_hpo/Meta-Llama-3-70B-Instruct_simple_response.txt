SUMMARY:

Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio, a notorious hardliner on immigration, has endorsed Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination. Arpaio's endorsement is seen as a credibility boost for Perry on immigration and border issues, despite Perry's criticized immigration platform, which deems deportation of all 11 million undocumented immigrants impractical. Perry opposes a full border fence, instead calling for increased enforcement along the southwest border. Arpaio praises Perry's border security record, citing his "tough-on-crime" stance and commitment to funding full-time border security operations. Perry vows to "secure the border within a year" if elected president, promising to increase federal personnel, fencing, and aerial resources to combat border crime. This endorsement raises concerns about the moral implications of prioritizing border security over human rights and dignity, particularly for vulnerable immigrant communities.

END OF SUMMARY.
