SUMMARY:

Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio has endorsed Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination, citing Perry's tough stance on immigration and border issues. Arpaio, known for his hardline approach to immigration, praises Perry's border security record and plan, which includes increasing personnel, fencing, and surveillance. Perry has been criticized for his immigration platform, which opposes the deportation of all 11 million undocumented immigrants and a full border fence. However, he promises to "secure the border within a year" if elected president. Arpaio's endorsement could give Perry a credibility boost on immigration and border issues. This is not Arpaio's first presidential endorsement, as he backed Mitt Romney in 2008 and held a press conference with Herman Cain last year.

END OF SUMMARY.
