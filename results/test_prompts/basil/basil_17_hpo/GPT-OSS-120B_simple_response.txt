SUMMARY:  
Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio, a staunch anti‑immigration hardliner famed for aggressive raids and his “Tent City” jail, has thrown his support behind Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination. Arpaio praises Perry as the most effective candidate on border security, citing the governor’s “border‑surge” operations that, in his view, have curbed illegal trafficking of weapons, drugs and people. Both men frame the issue as a moral imperative: a “tough‑on‑crime” presidency is needed to protect America’s national security, public safety and jobs by deploying full‑time troops, fences and surveillance from Brownsville to San Diego. Perry echoes this stance, promising to “secure the border within a year” with thousands of federal boots on the ground, strategic fencing and aerial resources. The endorsement positions Perry as a credible voice on immigration, countering criticism that mass deportation is impractical. Arpaio’s history of backing Republican candidates—Romney in 2008 and a joint press event with Herman Cain—underscores his ongoing moral crusade for stricter border enforcement.  
END OF SUMMARY.
