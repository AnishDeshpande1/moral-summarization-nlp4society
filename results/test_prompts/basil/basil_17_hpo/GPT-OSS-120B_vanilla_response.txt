SUMMARY:  
Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio has officially endorsed Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination, citing Perry’s “tough‑on‑crime” record on border security. Arpaio, a hard‑line immigration enforcer known for worksite raids and the long‑running “Tent City” jail, praised Perry’s “border surge” operations and pledged support for a full‑time security effort from Brownsville to San Diego. Perry, who has faced criticism for deeming mass deportation impractical, opposes a complete border fence but promises to increase enforcement, deploy more federal agents, and add fencing and aerial surveillance to “secure the border within a year.” Both men framed the endorsement as a response to what they view as federal failures on southern border security. Arpaio previously backed Mitt Romney in 2008 and has held joint appearances with other GOP figures such as Herman Cain on immigration issues.  

END OF SUMMARY.
