Arizona Sheriff Joe Arpaio, a hardliner on immigration, officially endorsed Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination, giving Perry a boost on border‑security authority. Both men framed immigration enforcement as a matter of authority versus subversion, emphasizing “fighting” border crime with more personnel, fencing and surveillance. Perry, who has been criticized for opposing a full fence, nonetheless “opposes” amnesty and argues that his enforcement plan will curb illegal trafficking of weapons, drugs and people. The endorsement underscores their shared view that strong, centralized authority is needed to prevent cheating the system and to restore order along the U.S.–Mexico border.
