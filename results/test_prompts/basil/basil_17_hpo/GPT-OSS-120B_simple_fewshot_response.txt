SUMMARY:
Arizona’s hard‑line sheriff, Joe Arpaio, officially endorsed Texas Governor Rick Perry for the Republican presidential nomination, giving Perry a boost on immigration and border‑security credibility. Arpaio, notorious for aggressive work‑site raids and the long‑running “Tent City” jail, praised Perry’s “border surge” operations that, he says, have helped shut down illegal trafficking of weapons, drugs and people. While Perry has been criticized for saying mass deportation of the 11 million undocumented residents is unrealistic and for opposing a full border fence, he still vows to “secure the border within a year” with more personnel, strategic fencing and aerial surveillance. Both men argue that a strong, fully funded border is essential to protecting America’s national security, public safety and jobs. Perry highlighted his past requests for National Guard troops and state‑funded enforcement, and thanked Arpaio for supporting their shared “tough‑on‑crime” agenda. The endorsement follows Arpaio’s past support for Mitt Romney in 2008 and a recent meeting with Herman Cain on immigration enforcement.

END OF SUMMARY.
