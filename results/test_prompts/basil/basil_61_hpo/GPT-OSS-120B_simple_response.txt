SUMMARY: Senator Ted Cruz (R‑TX) blasted former President Donald Trump, accusing him of mobilizing “his friends at the National Enquirer and his political henchmen” to launch a smear campaign. The Enquirer published a story alleging Cruz had affairs with five women—a claim he flatly denied, calling the attacks “garbage” and insisting his innocence. The controversy spilled onto CNN, where Cruz’s former communications director, Amanda Carpenter, was herself accused of an affair by Boston Herald columnist Adriana Cohen, who cited the same tabloid piece. Trump issued a vague response, claiming no knowledge of the Enquirer story and denying any involvement, while mocking Cruz as “Lyin’ Ted Cruz” and suggesting he surrounds himself with “political hacks.” The National Enquirer, notorious for sensationalism yet credited with exposing past scandals like those of John Edwards and Tiger Woods, defended its independence, stating no external influence shaped the story. The episode underscores a moral clash over truth, personal integrity, and the weaponization of tabloid journalism in political battles. END OF SUMMARY.
