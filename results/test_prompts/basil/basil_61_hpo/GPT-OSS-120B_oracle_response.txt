SUMMARY:  
Sen. Ted Cruz (R‑TX) blasted Donald Trump on Friday, accusing the former president of enlisting “his friends at the National Enquirer and his political henchmen” to attack him. The tabloid had just run a story alleging Cruz had multiple extramarital affairs with five women, a claim Cruz called “garbage” in a Facebook statement. The accusations sparked a heated CNN exchange in which Boston Herald columnist Adriana Cohen suggested Cruz’s former communications director Amanda Carpenter was also named in the Enquirer piece. Trump denied any involvement, saying he had “nothing to do with the National Enquirer” and dismissed Cruz as “Lyin’ Ted” while noting the paper’s history of exposing scandals such as O.J. Simpson and John Edwards. The Enquirer’s parent company, American Media Inc., rejected claims that the story was influenced by Trump backers, insisting its reporting is independent.  

END OF SUMMARY.
