**SUMMARY:**  
At its Kansas City convention, the NAACP adopted a resolution condemning **bigotry** and **racism** within the Tea Party, urging the movement to become **responsible** and reject **white supremacists** and other **extremist** elements. NAACP President Ben Jealous warned that tolerating **bigots** makes the movement **dangerous** and undermines democracy. Tea Party leaders, including Sarah Palin and activist Alex Poulter, rejected the accusations as **unfounded** and **appalling**, insisting the group is **diverse**, **patriotic**, and not **racist**. The debate, largely closed to the public, called on the Tea Party to repudiate the **racist** element and educate members that the movement is not merely about taxes but also about **responsible** civic participation. The resolution awaits final approval by the NAACP board in October.
