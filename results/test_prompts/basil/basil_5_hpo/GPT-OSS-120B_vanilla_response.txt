SUMMARY:  
At the NAACP’s annual convention in Kansas City, the organization adopted a resolution condemning racism within the Tea Party movement and urging it to repudiate any bigoted elements. NAACP leaders, including President Ben Jealous, said the resolution calls on the Tea Party to become responsible members of democracy and reject white supremacists. Tea Party representatives, such as Sarah Palin, activist Alex Poulter, and the St. Louis Tea Party, rejected the accusations, calling them unfounded and demanding the NAACP withdraw the resolution. The debate, largely closed to the public, highlighted past incidents where Tea Party demonstrators were alleged to have shouted racial slurs at Black congressmen, a claim disputed by conservatives. The final wording of the resolution will be approved by the NAACP board in October.  

END OF SUMMARY.
