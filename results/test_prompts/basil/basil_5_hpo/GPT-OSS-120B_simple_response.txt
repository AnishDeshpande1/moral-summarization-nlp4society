SUMMARY:  
At its Kansas City convention, the NAACP adopted a resolution denouncing racism and bigotry within the Tea Party movement, urging the group and all “people of good will” to repudiate any racist elements. NAACP leaders framed the issue as a moral imperative: the movement must become “responsible members of democracy” and stop welcoming white supremacists, even though the organization does not oppose the Tea Party’s policy goals. The resolution, pending final approval in October, calls for education about the movement’s broader implications and warns that unchecked bigotry could become “dangerous.” Tea Party representatives, including Sarah Palin and activist Alex Poulter, rejected the accusations, labeling them false and insisting the movement is diverse and focused on limited government, not race. A local Tea Party group also demanded the NAACP withdraw the proposal. The dispute echoes earlier incidents in which Tea Party demonstrators were accused of shouting racial slurs at Black congressmen, a claim the Tea Party denies. The NAACP’s moral framing positions the fight against racism as essential to preserving a healthy democracy, while the Tea Party frames the criticism as an unjust attack on patriotic Americans.  

END OF SUMMARY.
