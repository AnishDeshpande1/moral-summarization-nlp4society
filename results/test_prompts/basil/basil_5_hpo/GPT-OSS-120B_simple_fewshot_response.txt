SUMMARY:
The NAACP’s national convention adopted a resolution condemning racism and bigotry within the Tea Party movement, urging the group to repudiate any extremist elements. NAACP leaders said the resolution is meant to prompt self‑examination and to protect democracy from the danger of white‑supremacist infiltration. Tea Party supporters, including Sarah Palin and activist Alex Poulter, rejected the accusations, calling them unfounded and defending the movement’s diversity and focus on limited government. The debate over the resolution was largely closed to the public, and the final wording awaits board approval in October. Recent incidents—such as alleged racial slurs shouted at Black congressmen during a health‑care protest—have heightened tensions, with NAACP officials accusing the Tea Party of enabling extremist behavior, while Tea Party advocates argue they are being unfairly maligned. The episode underscores a broader moral question: whether political movements must actively police their ranks to prevent the spread of hate, or whether accusations of racism are being weaponized to discredit legitimate dissent.

END OF SUMMARY.
