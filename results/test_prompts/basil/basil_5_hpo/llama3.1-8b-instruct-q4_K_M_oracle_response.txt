SUMMARY:

Leaders of the NAACP accused tea party activists of tolerating bigotry and racism, prompting a resolution condemning such behavior within the movement. The resolution calls on tea party leaders to repudiate racist elements and activities, and to make themselves responsible members of democracy by not accepting or welcoming bigots into their organizations. Tea party supporters disputed these claims, with Sarah Palin calling them "appalling" and "false." However, the NAACP pointed to instances where racial epithets were yelled at black lawmakers, including Reps. John Lewis and Emanuel Cleaver, who were spat on during a health care debate in March.

The NAACP's resolution aims to educate its membership and the public about the dangers of racism within the tea party movement, warning that if left unchecked, such behavior could become more entrenched and lead to further division. The organization hopes that by standing up against bigotry, they can empower the tea party to take responsibility for their actions and create a more inclusive environment.

END OF SUMMARY.
