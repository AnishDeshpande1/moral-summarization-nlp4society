SUMMARY:
The NAACP condemned what it described as tolerated bigotry within the Tea Party, adopting a resolution that urges the movement to repudiate any racist elements and to act as responsible participants in democracy. NAACP leaders framed the issue as a matter of moral authority, calling on the Tea Party to subvert its own tolerance of white‑supremacist attitudes and to uphold fair, non‑discriminatory standards. They emphasized care for those harmed by hate, noting incidents in which Black congressmen were shouted at and spat upon by demonstrators linked to the group, highlighting the personal injury and societal damage such bigotry causes. The resolution also stresses fairness, accusing the Tea Party of cheating the public’s trust by allowing extremist voices to infiltrate a movement that claims to champion limited government and individual rights. Tea Party figures, including Sarah Palin and activist Alex Poulter, rejected the accusations and demanded the NAACP withdraw the resolution, insisting the movement is diverse and not racist. The NAACP’s pending board approval will finalize language that seeks to hold the Tea Party accountable, promote protection for marginalized communities, and restore equitable political discourse.  
END OF SUMMARY.
