SUMMARY:

Leaders of the NAACP accused tea party activists of tolerating bigotry and racism, prompting a resolution condemning such behavior within the movement. The resolution calls on tea party members to repudiate racist elements and activities. Tea party organizers disputed claims of racism, with some calling for the NAACP to withdraw the resolution. However, the NAACP's director of the Washington bureau stated that the resolution aims to empower the tea party to acknowledge and address instances of racism within their movement.

The controversy stems from past incidents where tea party activists were accused of using racial slurs against black lawmakers during a health care debate in March. The NAACP President said the issue is not with the tea party's existence, but rather their acceptance of white supremacists. Tea party activist Alex Poulter disputed allegations of racism, saying he has seen no evidence within the movement.

The resolution was adopted at the NAACP's annual convention in Kansas City and will be reviewed by the national board of directors in October.

END OF SUMMARY.
