SUMMARY:

Leaders of the NAACP accused tea party activists of tolerating bigotry and racism within their movement, adopting a resolution condemning such behavior. Tea party organizers disputed these claims, calling them unfounded accusations that are being used to pull people together and focus on the negative. The NAACP's resolution calls for the tea party to repudiate racist elements and activities, and to educate its membership about the dangers of tolerating bigotry. Tea party activist Alex Poulter said he has seen no evidence of racism within the movement, but the NAACP claims that extremist groups are latching on to the movement's negative aspects.

The controversy stems from past incidents where tea party activists allegedly spat at and hurled racial epithets at African American lawmakers. The NAACP is standing up against what it sees as a culture of racism and bigotry within the tea party movement, urging its members to take responsibility for their actions and not tolerate bigots among them. The resolution will be finalized in October after approval by the NAACP's national board.

END OF SUMMARY
