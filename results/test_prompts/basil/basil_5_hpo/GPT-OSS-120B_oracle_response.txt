SUMMARY:
At the NAACP’s Kansas City convention, leaders condemned bigotry and racism they say have been tolerated within the Tea Party, adopting a resolution that urges the movement to repudiate racist elements and to **stand** as **responsible** citizens of democracy. NAACP President Ben Jealous warned that the Tea Party must **make** a clear break from the **acceptance** and **welcoming** of white supremacists, and called for an **educate** campaign to show the public that the group is not merely about lower taxes. Tea Party activists, including Sarah Palin and Alex Poulter, called the accusations **false** and **unfounded**, saying the movement is diverse and that the claims are **running** like a rumor. They denounced the **spit** and racial epithets reported by Rep. John Lewis and others as **false**, insisting the Tea Party is **going** to **become** a force for liberty, not hate. NAACP director Hilary Shelton said the resolution will **pull** attention to the problem and stop groups from **looking** for excuses and **latching** onto bigotry. The final wording awaits NAACP board approval.  
END OF SUMMARY.
