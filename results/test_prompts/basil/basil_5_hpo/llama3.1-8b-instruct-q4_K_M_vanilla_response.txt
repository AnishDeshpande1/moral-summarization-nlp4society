SUMMARY:
The National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP) accused tea party activists of tolerating bigotry and racism at their annual convention in Kansas City, Missouri. A resolution was adopted condemning racism within the movement, calling on tea partiers to repudiate racist elements and activities. Tea party organizers disputed the claims, with some saying they are unfounded and that the NAACP is trying to silence them. Former Alaska Governor Sarah Palin also spoke out against the allegations, calling them false and appalling. The resolution's wording will not be released until October, but it calls on tea partiers to be responsible members of democracy and not tolerate bigots or bigotry among their members. This comes after previous incidents where some tea party activists were accused of using racial epithets during protests.

END OF SUMMARY.
