SUMMARY:

The White House released 100 pages of e-mails revealing internal jostling over the government's official talking points in the aftermath of last September's attack in Benghazi, Libya. The e-mails showed a disagreement between David H. Petraeus and his deputy Michael J. Morell over how much to disclose in the talking points, which were used by Susan E. Rice in television appearances days after the attack that killed four Americans. The State Department officials objected to references to C.I.A. warnings of extremist threats in Libya, fearing it would reflect badly on them. Petraeus dismissed the final draft as "certainly not what Vice Chairman Ruppersberger was hoping to get." The White House released the e-mails to show that intelligence officials drove the debate over the talking points and not political advisers, but critics said they confirmed that the administration had altered its account of the attack during an election campaign. The final version of the talking points is stripped of material that would have raised questions about the State Department's security posture.

END OF SUMMARY.
