**SUMMARY:**
The White House released e-mails that revealed a fierce internal jostling over the government's official talking points in the aftermath of last September's attack in Benghazi, Libya. The e-mails showed a disagreement between David H. Petraeus and his deputy, Michael J. Morell, over how much to disclose in the talking points, which were used by Susan E. Rice in television appearances days after the attack. The White House officials mostly mediated a bureaucratic tug of war between the State Department and the C.I.A. over how much to disclose, but critics say the administration airbrushed its account of the attack during an election campaign. The e-mails suggest that President Obama's aides were complicit in trying to alter the talking points for political reasons, but the White House claims it was focused on providing the facts as they knew them and protecting an ongoing investigation.
