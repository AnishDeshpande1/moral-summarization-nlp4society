SUMMARY:

Newly released emails from the White House reveal a contentious process in formulating talking points on the Benghazi attack, which killed four Americans. The emails show that intelligence officials, not political advisers, drove the debate over the talking points. However, they also suggest that the State Department's concerns about references to CIA warnings of extremist threats were paramount. While the administration has acknowledged altering the talking points to preserve classified information and avoid impacting the FBI investigation, the released emails paint a picture of bureaucratic tussling between the State Department and the CIA. The process raises questions about transparency and accountability in government, particularly with regards to how sensitive information is handled. The White House's attempt to show that intelligence officials were at the forefront of the debate over the talking points has been met with skepticism by Republicans, who argue that the administration had a "political" motive for altering the talking points.
