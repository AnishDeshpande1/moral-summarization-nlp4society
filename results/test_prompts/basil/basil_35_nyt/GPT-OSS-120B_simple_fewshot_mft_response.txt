SUMMARY:
Newly released White House emails expose a heated power struggle over the Benghazi talking points, pitting senior CIA officials against the State Department and revealing how senior authority was contested. CIA Director David Petraeus and deputy Michael Morell clashed over how much intelligence—particularly warnings about extremist threats—should be disclosed, with Morell unilaterally deleting references that could implicate the State Department. Petraeus rejected the heavily edited draft, noting it would not satisfy the House Intelligence Committee’s demand for truth. The correspondence shows officials scrambling to balance the need for accurate information about the deadly September 11 attack, which killed four Americans, against political pressures and the desire to protect the agency’s reputation. Republicans seized the emails to argue the administration subverted proper oversight and attempted to shield the State Department from blame, while the White House contended the edits were driven by bureaucratic coordination, not partisan motives. The episode highlights concerns of authority being undermined, the potential harm of obscuring facts about a lethal attack, and the fairness issue of possibly cheating the public and Congress out of a full account.

END OF SUMMARY.
