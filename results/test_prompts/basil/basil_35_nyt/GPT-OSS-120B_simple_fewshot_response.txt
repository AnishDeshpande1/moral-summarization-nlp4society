SUMMARY:
Internal White House emails expose a fierce bureaucratic battle over the Benghazi talking points, pitting the CIA against the State Department and even sparking a clash between CIA Director David Petraeus and his deputy Michael Morell. Morell removed references to CIA warnings about extremist threats, a move State officials feared would embarrass them, while Petraeus dismissed the heavily edited draft as unsatisfactory for the House Intelligence Committee. The released emails show that senior aides were chiefly mediating a tug‑of‑war between agencies under intense congressional pressure, not orchestrating a political cover‑up. Nonetheless, the final talking points were stripped of key details—such as Libya’s dangerous weapons‑filled environment—that could have cast the State Department’s security judgment in a negative light. Republicans seized on the correspondence to allege deliberate “air‑brushing” of the administration’s account during an election cycle, while the White House argues the edits were driven by intelligence concerns and the need to protect an ongoing investigation. The episode reveals the precarious balance between transparency, inter‑agency rivalry, and political optics in the aftermath of the September 11 attack.

END OF SUMMARY.
