SUMMARY:  
White House emails released on Wednesday expose a heated dispute among senior officials over the wording of the government’s talking points following the September 11, 2012 Benghazi attack. CIA Director David Petraeus and deputy Michael Morell clashed with State Department officials about how much detail to include, particularly references to CIA warnings about extremist threats and Libya’s dangerous security environment. Morell independently removed references to CIA alerts, prompting State Department objections that the edits would shield the department from criticism. Petraeus rejected the heavily edited draft, noting it would not satisfy House Intelligence Committee Chairman Rep. Dutch Ruppersberger. The emails show a rapid, late‑night drafting process involving the CIA, State Department, National Security Council, and FBI, with multiple revisions aimed at balancing factual accuracy, investigative protection, and inter‑agency concerns. Republicans seized on the releases to allege political manipulation of the narrative, while the White House argued the changes were driven by intelligence officials and necessary to protect an ongoing investigation. The final talking points, used by UN Ambassador Susan Rice, omitted references that critics say would have highlighted State Department lapses.  

END OF SUMMARY.
