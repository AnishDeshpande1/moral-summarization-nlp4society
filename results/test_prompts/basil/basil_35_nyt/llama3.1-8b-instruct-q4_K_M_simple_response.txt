SUMMARY:

The White House released emails revealing internal jostling over the government's talking points on the Benghazi attack in September. The emails showed disagreements between the State Department, Central Intelligence Agency (CIA), and administration officials over how much to disclose. CIA Deputy Director Michael J. Morell deleted a reference to CIA warnings of extremist threats in Libya, which State Department officials had objected to. However, Morell's actions were portrayed as an effort to mediate a bureaucratic tug-of-war between the two agencies.

The emails revealed no new details about the administration's evolving account of the attack, but showed that White House officials mostly mediated the disagreements under heavy time constraints from Capitol Hill. The final version of the talking points was stripped of material that would have raised questions about the State Department's security posture. The release of the emails aimed to show that intelligence officials drove the debate over the talking points.

END OF SUMMARY
