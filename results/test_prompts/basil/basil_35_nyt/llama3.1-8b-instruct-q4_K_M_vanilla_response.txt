SUMMARY:

Internal White House emails released by the Obama administration reveal a contentious debate over talking points used by Susan Rice, US Ambassador to the United Nations, in the aftermath of the Benghazi attack last September. Emails show that David Petraeus, then CIA Director, and his deputy Michael Morell disagreed on how much to disclose about C.I.A. warnings of extremist threats in Libya. The final version of the talking points was heavily scrubbed and omitted references to C.I.A. warnings.

The emails suggest that White House officials meditated a bureaucratic tug-of-war between the State Department and CIA, rather than trying to alter the talking points for political reasons as Republicans have claimed. However, some critics argue that the final version of the talking points was sanitized to protect the State Department's security posture.

The release of the emails aims to show that intelligence officials drove the debate over the talking points, rather than political advisers. The controversy surrounding the Benghazi attack and its aftermath continues, with Republicans accusing the administration of trying to cover up the truth.
