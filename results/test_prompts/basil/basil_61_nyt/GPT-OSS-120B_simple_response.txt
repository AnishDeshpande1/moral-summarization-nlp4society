SUMMARY:  
From Mar‑a‑Lago, Donald J. Trump escalated a personal attack on Senate candidate Ted Cruz, using a super‑PAC ad that displayed a nude photo of his wife Melania and then retweeting a side‑by‑side comparison that mocked Cruz’s wife, Heidi, who has a history of depression. Cruz condemned the stunt, called Trump a “sniveling coward,” and defended his spouse, but Trump persisted, threatening to “spill the beans” about Heidi. Party leaders fear such misogynistic tactics will alienate women voters and damage down‑ballot Republican candidates. The episode echoes the 2012 backlash against Todd Akin’s “legitimate rape” comment, which the GOP quickly isolated to contain the damage. Unlike the Akin controversy, media criticism of Trump’s attacks on women has been comparatively muted, raising concerns about the party’s tolerance of gender‑based harassment.  

END OF SUMMARY.
