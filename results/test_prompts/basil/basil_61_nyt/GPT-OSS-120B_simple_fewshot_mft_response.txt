SUMMARY:
Donald Trump escalated a personal attack on Senate candidate Ted Cruz by repeatedly targeting his wife, Heidi Cruz, on Twitter. After a super‑PAC released an ad using a nude photo of Melania Trump, Trump threatened to “spill the beans” about Heidi’s past depression and retweeted a side‑by‑side comparison that mocked both women. Cruz condemned the behavior, calling Trump a “sniveling coward” and demanding he leave his wife alone. The episode revives past GOP scandals—such as Todd Akin’s offensive “legitimate rape” comment—showing how personal assaults on women can cause real harm and alienate female voters. Party leaders warn that Trump’s willingness to weaponize private, vulnerable details about a candidate’s spouse is not only cruel (Care/Harm) but also unfair and dishonest (Fairness/Cheating), undermining the integrity of the primary contest and threatening broader support for Republican candidates down‑ballot. The controversy underscores the moral cost of using sexist attacks as a political strategy.
