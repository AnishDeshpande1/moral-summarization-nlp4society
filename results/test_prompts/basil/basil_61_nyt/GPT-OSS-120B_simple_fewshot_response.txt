SUMMARY:
Donald Trump escalated a personal attack on Senate candidate Ted Cruz by posting a side‑by‑side comparison of his wife, Heidi Cruz, with Melania Trump after a super‑PAC released a nude photo of Melania. Trump threatened to “spill the beans” about Heidi’s past depression and retweeted the humiliating images, prompting Cruz to call him a “sniveling coward” and demand he leave his wife alone. Party leaders warned that such misogynistic tactics by the front‑runner could alienate women voters and damage down‑ballot Republican candidates. The episode echoes past GOP scandals, such as Todd Akin’s 2012 “legitimate rape” comment, which forced the party to isolate the offender. While media criticism of Trump’s behavior has been relatively muted, the moral outrage centers on the unacceptable targeting of a candidate’s spouse, the exploitation of personal mental‑health struggles, and the broader threat these attacks pose to women’s participation in politics. END OF SUMMARY.
