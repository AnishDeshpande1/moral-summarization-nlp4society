SUMMARY: Two former presidents of Mexico, Vicente Fox and Felipe Calderón, have expressed strong opposition to Donald Trump's plan to build a wall along the US-Mexico border. They both reject the idea that Mexico will pay for the wall, with Fox calling it "a stupid wall" and Calderón saying it would be "completely useless." The former presidents also criticized Trump's understanding of immigration issues and his impact on the economy. Fox labeled Trump as "egocentric" and lacking in political values, while Calderón said closing the border would harm America's economy.

Trump has insisted that he can compel Mexico to pay for the wall through leverage, but appears to be more angered by Fox's tone than his opposition to the plan. The Mexican government has been largely silent on the issue, but Fox and Calderón have spoken out against Trump's proposal. Their statements highlight a significant disagreement between the two countries' leaders on one of Trump's signature campaign promises.
