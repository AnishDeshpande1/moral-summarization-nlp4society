SUMMARY:

Two former presidents of Mexico, Vicente Fox and Felipe Calderón, have spoken out against Donald Trump's plan to build a wall along the US-Mexico border. Both men have stated that Mexico will not pay for the wall, with Mr. Fox calling it "stupid" and Mr. Calderón saying it would be "completely useless". The former presidents expressed concern about the economic impact of closing the border and the misinformation about immigration from Mexico to the US. Mr. Trump has insisted that he can compel Mexico to pay for the wall, but has appeared more angered by Mr. Fox's tone than his message.

Mr. Trump has been critical of Mr. Fox's use of profanity in an interview with Fusion, calling on him to apologize. However, this response seems to be a shift from Mr. Trump's usual demeanor, as he is known for using profanity himself in campaign speeches. The exchange highlights the tensions between the US and Mexico over border security and immigration.

END OF SUMMARY.
