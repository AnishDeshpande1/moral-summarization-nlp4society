SUMMARY:
Former Mexican presidents Vicente Fox and Felipe Calderón publicly denounced Donald Trump’s border‑wall scheme, rejecting any notion that Mexico should foot the $8 billion bill. Fox, using profanity, called the wall “f***ing” and labeled Trump “egocentric,” a subversive critique of the president’s self‑appointed authority. He warned Hispanic voters that they were “followers of a false prophet,” framing Trump’s demand as morally corrupt and unfair—a cheating of both nations. Calderón echoed this, calling the wall “stupid” and “completely useless,” arguing that the policy would damage America’s economy and betray fair economic principles. Trump, in turn, attacked Fox’s language as indecent, demanding an apology for the profane remarks, thereby framing Fox’s criticism as a breach of sanctity and respect for authority. The exchange spotlights a clash over legitimate authority, the fairness of imposing costs on another country, and the degradation of discourse through profanity.  

END OF SUMMARY.
