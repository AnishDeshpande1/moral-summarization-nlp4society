SUMMARY:  
In his rallies, Donald J. Trump repeatedly asks, “Who’s gonna pay?” and receives the chant “Mexico!” from supporters. Former Mexican presidents have bluntly rejected this demand, framing the wall as a selfish, morally bankrupt idea. Vicente Fox, using profanity, declared “I’m not going to pay… He should pay for it. He’s got the money,” and condemned Trump as “egocentric” and devoid of political values, even labeling pro‑Trump Hispanic voters as “followers of a false prophet.” His successor, Felipe Calderón, echoed the rebuke, calling the wall “stupid,” “useless,” and insisting Mexico will not fund a single cent. Calderón also warned that Trump’s misunderstanding of immigration flows would damage the U.S. economy. Trump, meanwhile, insists he can force Mexico to foot the roughly $8 billion cost, but grew angry at Fox’s profanity and demanded an apology. The moral framing presented by the ex‑presidents portrays the wall as an irresponsible, self‑servicing project that lacks ethical justification and threatens both nations’ welfare.  

END OF SUMMARY.
