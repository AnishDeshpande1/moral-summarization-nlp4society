SUMMARY:
The NAACP adopted a resolution urging Tea Party organizations to expel “racist elements” from their ranks, asserting that with greater public influence comes a duty to act responsibly. President Benjamin Todd Jealous framed the call as a moral imperative to protect vulnerable communities, warning that silence can lead to violence and that bigotry must not be tolerated. He positioned the NAACP’s demand as a rightful challenge to the Tea Party’s authority, insisting that the movement must be held accountable for any ties to hate groups. In response, Tea Party supporters, including Sarah Palin, condemned the resolution as a partisan attack and urged the Obamas to rebuke the NAACP, while the St. Louis Tea Party Coalition threatened to have the civil‑rights group’s tax‑exempt status examined. The clash underscores a fairness issue: the NAACP accuses the Tea Party of cheating democratic norms by harboring racism, whereas Tea Party advocates claim they are being unfairly targeted and that the resolution subverts their legitimate political authority. Both sides appeal to care for their constituencies and invoke notions of justice and proper authority in a heated dispute over racial inclusion and political legitimacy.  
END OF SUMMARY.
