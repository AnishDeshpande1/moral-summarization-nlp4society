SUMMARY:
The NAACP adopted a resolution urging Tea Party organizations to expel “racist elements” from their ranks, warning that silence in the face of bigotry can lead to violence. President Benjamin Todd Jealous clarified the move was not a blanket accusation against the entire Tea Party, but a call for the movement to distance itself from known hate groups that recruit its members. He argued that greater public visibility brings a responsibility to act responsibly and reject bigotry. The resolution sparked backlash from Tea Party leaders; Sarah Palin defended the movement on Fox News, demanding that the Obamas denounce the NAACP’s action, while the St. Louis Tea Party Coalition issued its own resolution branding the civil‑rights group a partisan “political attack dog” and urged the IRS to examine its tax‑exempt status. The episode highlights ongoing accusations of racism within the Tea Party, underscoring a moral clash over whether the movement should confront and purge extremist influences or deny the existence of such prejudice.

END OF SUMMARY.
