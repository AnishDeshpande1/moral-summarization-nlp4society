SUMMARY:

The National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (N.A.A.C.P.) passed a resolution calling on Tea Party groups to remove "racist elements" from their ranks. N.A.A.C.P. President Benjamin Todd Jealous stated that the resolution is not an attempt to label the entire Tea Party movement as racist, but rather a call for them to distance themselves from known hate groups. He emphasized that with increased power comes responsibility and that silence can lead to violence.

The resolution was met with opposition from Tea Party groups and their supporters, including Sarah Palin, who defended the movement on Fox News. The St. Louis Tea Party Coalition also issued its own resolution, accusing the N.A.A.C.P. of being a "partisan political attack dog" and calling for the Internal Revenue Service to review the group's tax-exempt status.

The controversy stems from allegations of racism within the Tea Party movement, including incidents where protesters hurled racial epithets at black members of Congress.

END OF SUMMARY.
