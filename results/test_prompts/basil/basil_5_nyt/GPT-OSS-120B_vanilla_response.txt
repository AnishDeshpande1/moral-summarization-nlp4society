SUMMARY: The NAACP adopted a resolution urging Tea Party organizations to expel “racist elements” from their ranks. President Benjamin Todd Jealous clarified the call isn’t to label the whole Tea Party as racist, but to pressure groups to distance themselves from hate groups that recruit members. He warned that silence can fuel violence and demanded accountability. The resolution sparked backlash from Tea Party supporters. Sarah Palin defended the movement on Fox News, urging the Obamas to criticize the NAACP. The St. Louis Tea Party Coalition responded with its own resolution, accusing the NAACP of partisan attacks and asking the IRS to examine its tax‑exempt status. The Tea Party has faced repeated accusations of racism, including incidents where protesters allegedly used racial slurs and even spat on a Black congressman during health‑care debates, though the movement denies any racism within its ranks. END OF SUMMARY.
