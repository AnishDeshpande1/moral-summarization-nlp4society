SUMMARY:  
At its annual convention in Kansas City, the NAACP adopted a resolution urging Tea Party organizations to expel “racist elements” from their ranks. President Benjamin Todd Jealous clarified that the call is not a blanket accusation against the entire Tea Party, but a demand that the movement distance itself from known hate groups that recruit its members. He warned that silence in the face of bigotry can lead to violence, and insisted that “there is no place for the bigots in the Tea Party movement.”  

Tea Party leaders rebuffed the resolution. Sarah Palin defended the movement on Fox News, urging the Obamas to condemn the NAACP’s stance, while the St. Louis Tea Party Coalition issued its own resolution branding the civil‑rights group a “partisan political attack dog” and called for an IRS review of the NAACP’s tax‑exempt status. The controversy revives long‑standing accusations that the Tea Party harbors racism, highlighted by incidents of racial slurs and even a spitting episode directed at a Black congressman during health‑care protests. Tea Party representatives maintain that the movement is not racist.  

END OF SUMMARY.
