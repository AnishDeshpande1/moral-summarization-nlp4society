SUMMARY:
The National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (N.A.A.C.P.) passed a resolution calling on Tea Party groups to remove racist elements from their ranks. N.A.A.C.P. President Benjamin Todd Jealous emphasized that this is not an attempt to label the entire Tea Party movement as racist, but rather a demand for accountability and responsibility in light of increased public attention and power. The resolution was prompted by allegations of racism within the Tea Party movement, including instances of racial epithets and physical violence against black members of Congress.

Tea Party groups and their supporters have pushed back against the resolution, with Sarah Palin defending the movement on Fox News and accusing the N.A.A.C.P. of being a "partisan political attack dog." However, Jealous maintained that the N.A.A.C.P.'s goal is to promote accountability and ensure that there is no place for bigotry in the Tea Party movement.

END OF SUMMARY.
