SUMMARY:  
The National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP) adopted a resolution urging Tea Party groups to **weed “racist elements”** from their ranks and to **act responsibly**. NAACP President Benjamin Todd Jealous warned that **silence** in the face of **violence** invites further harm, calling on the movement to **stand** up and make it **clear** that **bigots** have no place in the Tea Party. He stressed that increased public attention brings a duty to **act responsibly** and to **review** affiliations with known hate groups. Tea Party leaders, including Sarah Palin, rejected the resolution, accusing the NAACP of a partisan attack and urging the IRS to **review** its tax‑exempt status. The controversy follows accusations that Tea Party protesters have engaged in **hurling** racial slurs and even **spitting** on a Black congressman during health‑care debates. Both sides claim the other is **acting** in bad faith, highlighting the deep‑seated dispute over **racism** within the movement.  

END OF SUMMARY.
