SUMMARY: National Security Adviser Susan Rice warned that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s planned Capitol Hill speech, arranged by House Speaker John Boehner without White House coordination, will damage the traditionally bipartisan U.S.–Israel relationship. The invitation, intended to highlight Iran’s nuclear threat amid ongoing U.S.–Iran negotiations, has heightened tensions between President Obama and Netanyahu, who opposes the Iran nuclear deal. Rice called the move “destructive” on PBS’s Charlie Rose, reflecting the administration’s strongest criticism yet. Netanyahu declined a private meeting with Senate Democrats, citing concerns about perceived partisanship, and sent a letter to Senators Durbin and Feinstein emphasizing Israel’s security worries. Vice President Biden and several Democratic lawmakers will skip the March 3 address, viewing it as an affront to the administration, while Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham condemned Biden’s absence. The episode underscores the rift over Iran policy and the politicization of the U.S.–Israel alliance.  

END OF SUMMARY.
