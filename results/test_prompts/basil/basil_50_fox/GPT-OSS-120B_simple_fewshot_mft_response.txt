SUMMARY:
National Security Adviser Susan Rice warned that Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s invitation to speak on the House floor – arranged by Republican Speaker John Boehner without consulting the White House or State Department – threatens the long‑standing, bipartisan “fabric” of U.S.–Israel relations.  By bypassing the proper chain of authority, Boehner’s move is portrayed as a subversive politicisation of a diplomatic visit, while Netanyahu’s refusal to meet privately with Senate Democrats is framed as a betrayal of loyalty to the broader American‑Israeli partnership.  Rice and other Obama officials argue the episode is unfair, turning a traditionally neutral alliance into a partisan battleground that cheats the U.S. public of a balanced foreign‑policy discussion.  Democrats, including Sens. Durbin and Feinstein, lament the perceived partisan snub, whereas Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham condemns Vice President Biden’s absence as disrespectful to an “ally” and to the shared threat of a nuclear‑armed Iran.  The article’s moral framing thus condemns the subversion of diplomatic authority, the unfair partisan manipulation of an alliance, and the betrayal of bipartisan loyalty that once underpinned U.S.–Israel ties.  
END OF SUMMARY.
