SUMMARY:  
National Security Adviser Susan Rice warned that Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s Capitol Hill address, arranged by House Speaker John Boehner without conferring with the White House or State Department, will be a destructive force on the traditionally bipartisan U.S.–Israel relationship. Rice called the invitation an affront to the diplomatic fabric and warned that the speech could threaten the alliance by derailing the Obama administration’s effort to clamp down on Iran’s nuclear program. Netanyahu, who declined a private meeting with Senate Democrats to avoid the perception of partisanship, said his sole purpose was to highlight the grave threat Iran poses and to prevent Iran from becoming a nuclear‑threshold state. Republicans such as Sen. Lindsey Graham condemned Vice President Biden’s absence as unacceptable, while several Democratic senators also skipped the event, citing concerns that the invitation was politically motivated. The episode underscores how competing influences and partisan calculations risk undermining a long‑standing, bipartisan partnership.  

END OF SUMMARY.
