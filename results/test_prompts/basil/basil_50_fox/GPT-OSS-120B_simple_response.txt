SUMMARY: National Security Adviser Susan Rice warned that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s Capitol Hill address, arranged by Republican Speaker John Boehner without White House or State Department input, will be “destructive” to the long‑standing bipartisan U.S.–Israel relationship. Rice stressed that the alliance has always been bipartisan and must stay that way, casting the invitation as a diplomatic breach that fuels political acrimony amid already‑tense Obama‑Netanyahu ties. Netanyahu, seeking to rally opposition to the Obama‑led Iran nuclear‑deal negotiations, declined a private meeting with Senate Democrats, claiming he did not want to “compound the misperception of partisanship.” Democrats, including Sens. Dick Durbin and Dianne Feinstein, expressed disappointment, while Vice President Biden and several Democrats will skip the speech, labeling it an affront to the administration’s diplomatic efforts. Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham condemned Biden’s absence as “appalling,” arguing that hearing Israel’s warning about a nuclear‑armed Iran outweighs partisan concerns. The episode underscores a moral narrative: partisan maneuvering threatens the fabric of a historic alliance and distracts from the shared security challenge posed by Iran’s nuclear ambitions. END OF SUMMARY.
