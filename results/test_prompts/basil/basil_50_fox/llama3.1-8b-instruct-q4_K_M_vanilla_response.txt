SUMMARY:

National Security Adviser Susan Rice warned that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's upcoming Capitol Hill address will be "destructive" to US-Israel relations. Rice stated that the visit, which was invited by House Speaker John Boehner without consulting the White House or State Department, is a diplomatic "no-no". The visit has further strained the relationship between President Obama and Netanyahu. Netanyahu has rejected an invitation to meet with leading Senate Democrats, citing concerns about partisanship. US officials believe Netanyahu's trip is aimed at derailing a nuclear deal with Iran, which he sees as allowing Tehran to become a nuclear threshold state. Vice President Biden will not attend the speech, and several House and Senate Democrats have also decided to skip it in protest. Republican Senator Lindsey Graham criticized Biden's absence, calling it "disrespectful" and an attempt to avoid politics surrounding Israel.

END OF SUMMARY.
