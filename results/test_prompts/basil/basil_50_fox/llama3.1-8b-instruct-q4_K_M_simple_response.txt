SUMMARY:

US National Security Adviser Susan Rice has criticized Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's upcoming Capitol Hill address, calling it "destructive" to the US-Israel relationship. Rice said that Netanyahu was invited by House Speaker John Boehner without consulting the White House or State Department, which is considered a diplomatic breach. The visit has further strained relations between President Obama and Netanyahu. Administration officials have expressed concerns about the visit, with some Democrats deciding not to attend the speech in protest. Republican Senator Lindsey Graham criticized Vice President Biden's decision not to attend, calling it "appalling" and showing disrespect towards Israel.

Netanyahu has defended his visit, saying he wants to discuss Iran's nuclear threat, which he believes poses a risk to Israel's survival. He turned down an invitation to meet with Senate Democrats privately, citing concerns about being perceived as partisan. The visit is seen by some as Netanyahu's attempt to derail the US-Iran nuclear deal, a key Obama foreign policy objective.

END OF SUMMARY.
