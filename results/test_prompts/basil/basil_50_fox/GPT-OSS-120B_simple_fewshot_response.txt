SUMMARY:
National Security Adviser Susan Rice warned that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s upcoming Capitol Hill address, arranged by Republican Speaker John Boehner without White House or State Department input, will be “destructive” to the traditionally bipartisan U.S.–Israel relationship. Rice argued that the invitation politicizes a crucial diplomatic forum and risks undermining President Obama’s efforts on a nuclear‑deal with Iran. Netanyahu declined a private meeting with Senate Democrats, claiming he did not want to appear partisan, yet his visit is widely seen as an attempt to derail the Obama‑led Iran negotiations. Vice President Biden and several Democrats will skip the speech, calling it an affront to the administration, while Republican Senator Lindsey Graham condemned Biden’s absence as disrespectful to Israel’s security concerns. The moral framing underscores that partisan maneuvering—whether by Boehner’s unilateral invite or by Republicans praising the speech and Democrats boycotting it—threatens the long‑standing bipartisan foundation of U.S.–Israel ties and endangers constructive dialogue on a shared threat. END OF SUMMARY.
