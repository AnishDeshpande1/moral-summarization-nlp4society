SUMMARY:

National Security Adviser Susan Rice warned that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's Capitol Hill address will be a destructive force in U.S.-Israel relations, threatening the bipartisan nature of their relationship. Netanyahu was invited by House Speaker John Boehner without conferring with the White House or State Department, which some consider a diplomatic no-no. The visit has been met with criticism from both sides, with Rice calling it "destructive to the fabric of the relationship." Netanyahu's opposition to the nuclear deal with Iran is seen as an attempt to derail the agreement, which would allow Iran to build a nuclear weapon. U.S. officials view his trip as partisan and aimed at influencing Israeli elections. Vice President Biden's decision not to attend the speech has been called unacceptable by some Republicans, who see it as an affront to Netanyahu and Israel. The situation is further complicated by the fact that more than a half-dozen Democrats have also decided to skip the event.

END OF SUMMARY.
