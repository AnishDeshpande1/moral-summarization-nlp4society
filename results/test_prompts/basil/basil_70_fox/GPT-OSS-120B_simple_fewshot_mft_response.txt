SUMMARY:
Donald Trump was sworn in as the 45th president, delivering an inaugural address that framed his new authority as a corrective to a corrupt Washington elite that had “reaped the rewards” while ordinary Americans “did not share” the nation’s wealth. He pledged to protect the American people, portraying the country’s divisions and the recent protests as a threat that must be subdued, and promising to “fight for you with every breath” against crime, foreign competition and Islamic terrorism. By invoking “new national pride” and “America first,” Trump appealed to loyalty, urging citizens to unite behind the flag and reject the “small group” that has betrayed the nation’s interests. His rhetoric cast the election as a restoration of fairness, promising to bring back jobs, borders, wealth and dreams that he said were stolen from the people. The ceremony also highlighted the tension between authority and subversion: House Democrats boycotted the event and protesters clashed with police, while former President Obama and his wife attended to honor democratic continuity. Trump’s team vowed a swift, decisive start, positioning the administration as a loyal champion of the populace against a cheating, self‑servicing establishment.  
END OF SUMMARY.
