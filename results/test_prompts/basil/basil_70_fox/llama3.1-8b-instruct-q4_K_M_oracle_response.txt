SUMMARY:

Donald Trump assumed the nation's highest office, vowing to restore prosperity and fight for America with every breath. In his inaugural address, he promised concrete results and an end to empty talk, ushering in a new era of action. He appealed for a "new national pride" and patriotism, saying this would eradicate divisions and prejudice. Trump rebuked those who have held power in Washington, accusing them of ignoring the people and reaping rewards while they struggled. He lamented the jobs lost to foreign rivals, describing factories as scattered tombstones, and vowed to stop American carnage. Trump cast his presidency as a return to power for the people, promising to bring back their dreams, wealth, and borders. He pledged to honor America's enduring values and make it great again.

As he begins his presidency, Trump will face challenges in governing and applying his CEO experience to running the nation. His team has vowed a "robust" start, which could include rescinding Obama actions and renegotiating trade deals. The new administration must also address the controversy surrounding Trump's Cabinet nominees and the opposition from Democrats.

END OF SUMMARY.
