SUMMARY:  
Donald Trump was sworn in as the 45th President of the United States, delivering an inaugural address that framed his ascension as a moral crusade for ordinary Americans. He pledged to restore prosperity, “fight… with every breath,” and end the “empty talk” of Washington’s elite who have hoarded wealth while the people suffered. Emphasizing a rebirth of “new national pride” and patriotism, Trump argued that love of country leaves no room for prejudice and promises to heal the nation’s divisions. He condemned the loss of manufacturing jobs, urban crime, and Islamic terrorism, vowing that “American carnage stops right here and now.” The speech cast his presidency as a historic moral reversal—an outsider businessman answering a divine‑like call to serve the people, unbound by prior political or military experience. While supporters hailed the moment as a triumph of the common citizen, protesters and many Democrats decried the transition, leading to clashes and arrests. The inauguration closed with Trump’s familiar promise: “We will make America great again,” positioning his administration as a moral mission to put “America first” and fulfill the nation’s destiny.  

END OF SUMMARY.
