SUMMARY:

Donald Trump was sworn in as the 45th President of the United States, vowing to restore prosperity and fight for America's interests. In his inaugural address, he emphasized populist themes, promising concrete results and a new national pride. He rebuked those who have held power in Washington, stating that they have enriched themselves while leaving the people behind. Trump also highlighted the need to bring back American jobs, borders, wealth, and dreams, and to eradicate Islamic terrorism.

Trump's speech was met with protests and disruptions on the edges of the security zone in Washington, with some demonstrators resorting to violence and vandalism. Despite this, prominent Democrats and past presidents attended the ceremony, including Hillary Clinton, who expressed her commitment to honoring democracy and its values.

The new president's address was characterized by his promise to put America first, stating that from now on, it will be "only America first." He closed his speech with the iconic phrase "We will make America great again."

END OF SUMMARY.
