SUMMARY:
Donald Trump was inaugurated as the 45th president, delivering a populist inaugural address that promised to restore prosperity, put “America first,” and fight for the people “with every breath.” He framed his victory as a rebuke of Washington’s entrenched elite, pledging to end job loss, crime, and “Islamic terrorism,” and to revive national pride and patriotism as a cure for division. The speech echoed 2016 campaign themes on trade, immigration and security, while stressing that the era of “empty talk” was over and action had begun. Trump’s rise from political outsider—dismissed by Beltway pundits—to the nation’s most powerful office was highlighted as historic, with his team touting his private‑sector success and vow to owe “nobody anything.” Despite protests, arrests and a Democratic boycott, former President Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama met with the Trumps, and Hillary Clinton attended, affirming respect for democracy. The new administration faces immediate challenges, including confirming Cabinet nominees and rolling back Obama policies, but Trump closed by reiterating his commitment to “make America great again.” END OF SUMMARY.
