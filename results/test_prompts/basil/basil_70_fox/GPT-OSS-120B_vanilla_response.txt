SUMMARY: Donald Trump was inaugurated as the 45th President of the United States, delivering a populist inaugural address that promised to restore prosperity, prioritize “America first,” and fight for the nation “with every breath.” He emphasized a new wave of patriotism, criticized Washington elites, and reiterated campaign themes on trade, immigration, national security, and job creation, while pledging to curb crime, terrorism, and “American carnage.” The speech lacked specific policy details but stressed action over rhetoric. Trump’s inauguration was marked by protests, arrests, and a boycott by some House Democrats, though former President Obama and other dignitaries attended. Hillary Clinton tweeted support for democracy after her loss. The new administration faces immediate challenges, including confirming Cabinet nominees amid Democratic resistance and potentially reversing Obama-era policies. Trump and Melania met the Obamas before heading to the inaugural parade and evening celebrations. END OF SUMMARY.
