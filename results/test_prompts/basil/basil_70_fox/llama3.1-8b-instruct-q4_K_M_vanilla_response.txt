SUMMARY:
Donald Trump was inaugurated as the 45th President of the United States, vowing to restore prosperity and "fight" for the country. In his inaugural address, he promised concrete results and an end to "empty talk," appealing for a "new national pride" and a rediscovery of American patriotism. He criticized those in power who have benefited from government while the people have not, and pledged to bring back jobs, borders, wealth, and dreams. Trump also vowed to eradicate Islamic terrorism and make America great again. The speech was met with protests and disruptions on the National Mall, but also drew attendance from prominent Democrats and past presidents.

Trump's team has vowed a "robust" start to his administration, which may include rescinding Obama-era actions and renegotiating trade deals. His Cabinet nominees face confirmation challenges, with Democrats slowing the process. The new president is expected to apply his CEO experience to governing, but faces significant challenges in implementing his policies.

END OF SUMMARY.
