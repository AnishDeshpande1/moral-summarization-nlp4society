SUMMARY:

Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg made comments about the prospect of a Donald Trump presidency, joking that she might move to New Zealand if it happens. However, some legal academics and Supreme Court observers are questioning her candor and its implications for the court's integrity. They argue that her comments could raise questions about her impartiality in future cases related to Trump. Under federal law, justices must recuse themselves from cases where their impartiality might be questioned.

Some experts suggest that Ginsburg may have to recuse herself from considering any Trump-related case, while others argue that she has crossed a line and created the appearance of bias. The controversy highlights the challenge of balancing judicial integrity with the desire for judges to be candid about their positions and biases.

The debate also touches on the broader issue of whether the high court is truly apolitical, given its 5-4 decision in Bush v. Gore and other partisan decisions. Some argue that the pretense of nonpartisanship does more harm than good, while others believe it's essential to maintain.
