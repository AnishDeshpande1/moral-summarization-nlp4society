SUMMARY:

Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg's recent comments about Donald Trump presidency have raised questions about her candor and the propriety of judges speaking out on politics. Legal academics and Supreme Court observers argue that she may have to recuse herself from considering any Trump-related case, as her comments could lead a "reasonable person" to question her impartiality. Ginsburg's statements are seen as crossing a line, creating an appearance of bias if she participates in future cases involving Trump or Clinton. Some experts suggest she might need to step down or be quiet about her opinions. The issue highlights the tension between judges' personal views and their duty to remain apolitical. While some argue that Ginsburg's comments are indiscretions, others see it as a reflection of the court's increasingly politicized nature, where appearances of bias are unavoidable.

END OF SUMMARY.
