SUMMARY:

Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg's candid comments about the prospect of a Donald Trump presidency have raised questions about her impartiality and the integrity of the court. Some legal academics and observers argue that her statements, which include joking about moving to New Zealand if Trump is elected, could lead to her recusal from considering any Trump-related cases. They suggest that her comments are "beyond the pale" and create an appearance of bias.

However, others argue that Ginsburg's comments are a reflection of the court's increasingly politicized nature, and that judges should be candid about their positions and biases. They point to examples such as the 5-4 decision in Bush v. Gore and the timing of judges' retirements as evidence of the court's political influence.

The debate highlights the tension between the ideal of an apolitical judiciary and the reality of a highly politicized court, with some arguing that the pretense of nonpartisanship does more harm than good.

END OF SUMMARY.
