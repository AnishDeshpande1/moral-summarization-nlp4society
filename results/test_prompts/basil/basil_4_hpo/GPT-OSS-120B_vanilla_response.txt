SUMMARY:  
Republican National Committee Chairman Michael Steele faced harsh criticism after a video showed him stating that the Afghanistan war is “largely unwinnable” and a “war of President Obama’s choosing.” Prominent conservatives, including Bill Kristol and neoconservative Dan Senor, demanded Steele’s resignation, arguing his remarks contradict the party’s long‑standing support for the conflict and embarrass Republicans during a pivotal moment with Gen. Petraeus assuming command and Congress pushing a war‑funding resolution. Steele’s spokesperson attempted to clarify that he meant Obama lacked a winning strategy, but critics called the comment “utterly irresponsible” and inconsistent with Steele’s earlier statements that the war must be won. While his remarks resonated with anti‑war Democrats and some conservative skeptics, they further destabilized Steele’s position as the party’s figurehead.  

END OF SUMMARY.
