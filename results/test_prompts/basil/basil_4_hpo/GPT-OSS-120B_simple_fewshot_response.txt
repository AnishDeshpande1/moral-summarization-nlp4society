SUMMARY:
Prominent conservatives blasted RNC chair Michael Steele after he said Afghanistan is “a largely unwinnable war of President Obama’s choosing.” Bill Kristol, a leading Republican voice, wrote a scathing letter demanding Steele’s resignation, calling the remark an “affront” to the party and to the troops. He noted that the war has been fought under both Bush and Obama and that Republicans are rallying support for the mission, making Steele’s comment not only embarrassing but a betrayal of Republican values and the soldiers’ sacrifice. Neoconservative Dan Senor echoed the condemnation, labeling the statement “utterly irresponsible” and pointing out Steele’s own past praise of Obama’s surge. Although some anti‑war Democrats and a few conservative critics share doubts about the war’s winnability, Steele is not one of them; as the party’s figurehead, his inconsistent remarks undermine his credibility and threaten his tenure. The moral of the story is clear: Steele’s careless comment betrays the party, the military, and the nation, and he must be held accountable, up to and including resignation.

END OF SUMMARY.
