SUMMARY:

Conservative leaders swiftly condemned RNC Chairman Michael Steele's statement that Afghanistan is a "war of Obama's choosing" and "largely unwinnable". Bill Kristol, a prominent conservative voice, called for Steele's resignation, labeling his comment an "affront" to the Republican Party and the soldiers "fighting" to accomplish the mission. Kristol argued that Steele's statement is "inconsistent" with the party's consistent support for the war effort. Dan Senor, a neoconservative figure, deemed Steele's comments "utterly irresponsible" and noted that Steele's RNC had previously sponsored a conference call to praise Obama's Afghan surge. Steele's statement has resonated with anti-war Democrats, but as the figurehead of the Republican Party, his tenure is now shaky. Kristol's call for resignation has sparked a push to oust Steele, with a GOP operative pitching the story to reporters.

END OF SUMMARY.
