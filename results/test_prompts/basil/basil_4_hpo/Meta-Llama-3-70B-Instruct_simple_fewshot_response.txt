SUMMARY:

Prominent conservatives, including Bill Kristol and Dan Senor, are calling for RNC Chairman Michael Steele's resignation after he was caught on camera saying that Afghanistan is a largely unwinnable war of President Obama's choosing. Steele's comments were deemed "utterly irresponsible" and "an affront" to the Republican Party and the soldiers fighting in Afghanistan. Despite Steele's attempt to clarify his remarks, the backlash continues, with many arguing that his comments are inconsistent with the party's stance on the war. The moral framing of this issue is clear: Steele's comments were a betrayal of the troops and the country, and he must take responsibility for his words. As the figurehead of the Republican Party, Steele's tenure is now shaky, and his comments have resonated with anti-war Democrats, but not with his own party. The moral of the story is that Steele's lack of accountability and inconsistency have led to a crisis of trust within the party.

END OF SUMMARY.
