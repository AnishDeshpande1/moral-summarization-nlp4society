SUMMARY:  
Prominent conservatives swiftly rebuked RNC chairman Michael Steele after he was caught on camera **saying** Afghanistan is “a largely unwinnable war of President Obama’s choosing.” Bill Kristol wrote a scathing letter demanding Steele’s resignation, arguing the war was **prosecuted** under both Presidents Bush and Obama and that Republicans have consistently **supported** it. Kristol warned that Steele’s **comment** “puts” him at odds with the entire Republican Party and called the remark an embarrassment and an affront to the party’s honor and to the soldiers who have **engaged** in the conflict, noting that countless attempts have **failed** over a thousand years. Other neoconservatives, including Dan Senor, labeled the remarks “utterly irresponsible,” pointing out Steele’s earlier praise of the Afghan surge and his December claim that Afghanistan is “a war we have to win.” While the remarks echo some anti‑war Democrats and a few conservative critics, Steele remains the Republican Party’s figurehead, and his position appears increasingly precarious.  

END OF SUMMARY.
