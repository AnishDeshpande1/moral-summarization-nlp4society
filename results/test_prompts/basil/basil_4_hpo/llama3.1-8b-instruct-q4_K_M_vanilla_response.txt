SUMMARY:

Conservative figures have criticized RNC Chairman Michael Steele for his comments on the war in Afghanistan, which they see as inconsistent and unpatriotic. Bill Kristol wrote a scathing letter to Steele calling for his resignation, stating that Steele's comment "puts [him] at odds with about 100 percent of the Republican Party." Other prominent conservatives, including Dan Senor, have also condemned Steele's comments, labeling them "utterly irresponsible." Steele initially tried to clarify his statement by saying he meant that President Obama has not provided a winning strategy for the war. However, this clarification was met with skepticism and criticism from many in the conservative community.

Steele's comments have resonated with anti-war Democrats who share similar skepticism about the war's winnability, but Steele is facing backlash within his own party. The controversy has put further pressure on Steele's tenure as RNC Chairman, which had already been shaky.
