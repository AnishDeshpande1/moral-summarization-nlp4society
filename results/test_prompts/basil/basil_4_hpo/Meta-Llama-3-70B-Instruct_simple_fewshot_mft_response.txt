SUMMARY:

Conservative leaders are calling for RNC Chairman Michael Steele's resignation after he claimed that Afghanistan is a "war of Obama's choosing" and may not be winnable. Bill Kristol, a prominent conservative voice, wrote a scathing letter demanding Steele's resignation, calling his comments "an affront" to the Republican Party and the soldiers fighting in Afghanistan. Kristol argued that Steele's statement betrays the party's commitment to the war effort and undermines the troops' morale. Other conservatives, including Dan Senor, echoed Kristol's sentiments, labeling Steele's comments as "utterly irresponsible" and "inconsistent" with his previous statements. The backlash highlights Steele's subversion of authority and betrayal of the party's values, as well as his disloyalty to the troops and the war effort. The moral of the story is clear: Steele's comments are a grave offense that warrants his removal as party chairman.

END OF SUMMARY.
