SUMMARY:

RNC Chairman Michael Steele faced backlash from prominent conservatives, including Bill Kristol and Dan Senor, after stating that Afghanistan is a largely unwinnable war of President Obama's choosing. Kristol called for Steele's resignation, labeling his comment an "affront" to the Republican Party and the soldiers fighting in Afghanistan. Senor deemed Steele's statement "utterly irresponsible," pointing out the inconsistency with Steele's previous declaration that Afghanistan is "a war we have to win." The criticism highlights Steele's failure to uphold the Republican Party's commitment to the war effort, and his apparent shift in stance. The controversy has sparked questions about Steele's leadership and tenure as RNC chairman. 

END OF SUMMARY.
