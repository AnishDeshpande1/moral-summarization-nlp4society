SUMMARY:

RNC Chairman Michael Steele faced backlash from prominent conservatives after stating that Afghanistan is a largely unwinnable war of President Obama's choosing. Bill Kristol, an influential voice among Republican lawmakers, called for Steele's resignation, citing that Steele's comment was an "affront" to the Republican Party and the soldiers fighting in Afghanistan. Other conservatives, such as Dan Senor, also criticized Steele's comments as "utterly irresponsible." Steele's spokesman attempted to clarify the comment, saying Steele meant that Obama has not been forthcoming with a winning strategy. However, Steele's previous statements, including one where he said Afghanistan is "a war we have to win," contradict his recent comment. The criticism has put Steele's tenure as RNC chairman in jeopardy.

END OF SUMMARY.
