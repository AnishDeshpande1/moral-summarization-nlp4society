SUMMARY:
Prominent conservatives quickly condemned RNC Chairman Michael Steele after his on‑camera remark that Afghanistan is an “unwinnable war of President Obama’s choosing.”  Influential voice Bill Kristol wrote that Steele’s statement betrays the Republican Party’s loyalty to the troops and to the long‑standing bipartisan commitment to win the war, demanding his resignation as a matter of restoring proper authority.  Other GOP figures, including neoconservative Dan Senor, labeled the comment “utterly irresponsible” and a subversive attack on the party’s united front, noting Steele’s prior support for the Afghan surge and his earlier declaration that the war must be won.  Critics argued that Steele’s words place him at odds with virtually the entire Republican establishment, undermining party honor and the chain of command that guides military policy.  While some anti‑war Democrats and a few conservative dissenters share his skepticism, the moral charge focuses on Steele’s perceived betrayal of party loyalty and his challenge to established authority, prompting calls for his removal.  
END OF SUMMARY.
