SUMMARY:

Conservative leaders have strongly criticized RNC Chairman Michael Steele for his comments on the Afghanistan war, calling them "utterly irresponsible" and an "affront to the honor of the Republican party". Bill Kristol wrote a letter to Steele calling for his resignation, stating that the war was not Obama's choosing but rather a bipartisan effort. Dan Senor, a neoconservative figure, also criticized Steele's comments, pointing out that they contradict his previous statements on Afghanistan. Steele had previously declared that Afghanistan is "a war we have to win", but later stated that it's an unwinnable conflict. The controversy has resonated among anti-war Democrats and some conservative critics of the Afghan deployment, but Steele is still facing backlash from within his own party. GOP operatives are now pitching stories about Steele's troubles, making his tenure as RNC Chairman seem increasingly shaky.

END OF SUMMARY.
