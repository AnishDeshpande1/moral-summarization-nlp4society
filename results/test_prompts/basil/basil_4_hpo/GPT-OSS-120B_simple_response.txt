SUMMARY:  
Prominent conservatives erupted in condemnation after RNC chair Michael Steele was caught on camera calling the Afghanistan war “largely unwinnable” and a “war of President Obama’s choosing.”  Bill Kristol led the outrage, demanding Steele’s resignation and accusing him of betraying the Republican Party, its soldiers, and the nation’s honor at a moment when Gen. Petraeus had just taken command and Republicans were pushing a clean‑war funding bill.  Kristol warned that even those who favor withdrawal should not hold the party’s top post.  Neoconservative Dan Senor echoed the charge, labeling Steele’s remarks “utterly irresponsible” and pointing out Steele’s previous support for Obama’s surge and his own December statement that Afghanistan “is a war we have to win.”  While Steele’s comments found some sympathy among anti‑war Democrats and a few conservative skeptics, critics stressed that he is neither a dissenting pundit nor a Democrat, but the figurehead of the GOP whose credibility is now in jeopardy.  The backlash quickly turned into a media push to amplify the “Steele trouble” narrative.  

END OF SUMMARY.
