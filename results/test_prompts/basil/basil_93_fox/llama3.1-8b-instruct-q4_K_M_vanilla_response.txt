SUMMARY:

President Trump proposed a compromise package to end the partial government shutdown, offering a three-year extension of protections for 700,000 DACA recipients in exchange for $5.7 billion for a border wall. However, his proposal was met with sharp criticism from Democratic lawmakers, who accused him of holding federal workers "hostage." House Speaker Nancy Pelosi called it a "non-starter," while Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer said it was "one-sided and ineffective."

Republican lawmakers, on the other hand, praised Trump's willingness to negotiate. Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell commended the president for taking bipartisan steps toward addressing current immigration issues. Other Republicans, such as Sen. Marco Rubio and House Republican Leader Kevin McCarthy, also expressed support for the proposal.

The shutdown has left 800,000 federal workers without paychecks, and both parties are seeking a solution to reopen the government. The fate of Trump's compromise package remains uncertain as negotiations continue between Congress and the White House.

END OF SUMMARY.
