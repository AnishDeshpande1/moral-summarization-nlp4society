SUMMARY:

President Trump's immigration compromise package was met with sharp reactions from Democratic and Republican lawmakers, who framed the proposal as holding federal workers hostage in exchange for a border wall. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi called it a "non-starter" and accused Trump of forcing a pointless shutdown to pay for an expensive, ineffective wall. Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer said the proposal was one-sided and ineffective, and that Trump must stop holding government hostage and end the shutdown immediately.

The Democratic senators slammed the proposal as unacceptable and accused Trump of taking away DACA protections and eliminating the TPS program for thousands of immigrants, creating a humanitarian crisis at the border. Republican lawmakers praised the president's willingness to negotiate, with Senate Majority Leader McConnell calling it a fair compromise that pairs border security investment with additional immigration measures.

The country is watching, and the shutdown continues to hurt millions of Americans. The time has come for both sides to make mutual concessions in order to reach an agreement and end the shutdown.

END OF SUMMARY.
