**SUMMARY:**  
President Trump broke the partial shutdown with a proposal he framed as a chance to restore “pride” to the immigration system, offering a three‑year extension of DACA protections for 700,000 Dreamers in exchange for $5.7 billion for a southern border wall. Democrats blasted the deal as “hostage‑taking,” calling it a “bad deal,” “pointless,” “ineffective,” and “senseless,” accusing the president of holding federal workers in “chaos” and “shame” while demanding a permanent wall. Senate leaders Schumer and Pelosi labeled the offer “untenable” and “unacceptable,” insisting the government must reopen before any “good‑faith” negotiations can begin. Republican leaders, including McConnell, Scalise, Collins, Romney, Rubio and McCarthy, praised the plan as a reasonable, bipartisan compromise and urged Democrats to end obstruction. The clash centers on whether the temporary protections constitute a moral concession or a coercive bargain, with both sides claiming the nation’s values and security are at stake.
