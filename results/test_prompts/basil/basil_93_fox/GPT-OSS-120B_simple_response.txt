SUMMARY: President Trump broke the partial shutdown by offering a three‑year extension of DACA and TPS protections for 700,000 “Dreamers” in exchange for $5.7 billion for a southern border wall. Democrats seized on the deal as moral blackmail, accusing Trump of holding federal workers hostage, reversing his own attacks on DACA, and using vulnerable immigrants as bargaining chips. Leaders such as Nancy Pelosi, Chuck Schumer, Steny Hoyer, Kirsten Gillibrand, Bob Menendez and Chris Coons denounced the proposal as a “one‑sided, ineffective” hostage‑taking that harms millions of American families and should be rejected until the government reopens.  

Republicans framed the offer as a sincere act of compromise and moral responsibility, praising Trump for pairing border security with immigration relief. Mitch McConnell, Steve Scalise, Susan Collins, Mitt Romney, Marco Rubio and Kevin McCarthy called the deal a fair, bipartisan step that can end the shutdown and protect both national security and families. The partisan clash centers on whether the president’s conditional relief is a genuine concession or an unethical leverage of a shutdown to force a wall.  

END OF SUMMARY.
