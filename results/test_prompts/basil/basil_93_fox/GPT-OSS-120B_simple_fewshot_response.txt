SUMMARY:
President Trump presented a “compromise” to end the partial government shutdown: a three‑year extension of DACA protections for 700,000 Dreamers and a temporary TPS renewal in exchange for $5.7 billion for a border wall. Democrats blasted the offer as hostage‑taking, accusing Trump of using federal workers and immigrant protections as bargaining chips after he first dismantled DACA and TPS. Leaders such as Nancy Pelosi, Chuck Schumer, Steny Hoyer, Kirsten Gillibrand and Bob Menendez called the deal a “non‑starter,” “one‑sided” and “ineffective,” insisting the government must be reopened before any negotiation. Even some Republicans, like Chris Coons, urged a broader discussion after the shutdown ends. In contrast, GOP leaders praised the proposal as a good‑faith, bipartisan step: Mitch McConnell, Steve Scalise, Susan Collins, Mitt Romney, Marco Rubio and Kevin McCarthy framed it as a reasonable concession that balances border security with immigrant relief and urged Democrats to stop obstruction. The clash highlights a moral divide—Democrats view the plan as exploitative hostage‑taking, while Republicans cast it as a necessary, strength‑based compromise.  
END OF SUMMARY.
