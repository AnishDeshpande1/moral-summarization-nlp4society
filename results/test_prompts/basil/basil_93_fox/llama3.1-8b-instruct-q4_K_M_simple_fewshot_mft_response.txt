SUMMARY:

President Trump's immigration compromise package was met with sharp reactions from Democratic and Republican lawmakers. Democrats accused Trump of holding the government hostage, using federal workers as bargaining chips, and offering temporary solutions in exchange for a permanent border wall. They criticized his proposal as "a bad deal" and called for an end to the shutdown. In contrast, many Republicans praised Trump's willingness to negotiate, calling it a "fair compromise" that incorporates priorities from both sides of the aisle.

The Democratic critique centered on Trump's use of authority to subvert the rights of undocumented immigrants, including the Dreamers and TPS recipients. They argued that his proposal would further harm these groups by making them temporary solutions rather than permanent residents. Republicans, on the other hand, saw Trump's offer as a reasonable attempt to address immigration issues while also securing the border.

The moral framing highlights concerns about care and harm, with Democrats arguing that Trump's actions are causing unnecessary suffering for federal workers and immigrants. The debate also touches on fairness and cheating, with some accusing Trump of making unreasonable demands and others seeing his proposal as a fair compromise.
