SUMMARY:
President Trump proposed a three‑year extension of DACA protections for 700,000 recipients in exchange for $5.7 billion to fund a southern border wall, aiming to end the partial government shutdown. Democrats overwhelmingly rejected the offer, calling it “hostage‑taking” and insufficient because it lacks permanent solutions for Dreamers and TPS recipients. Leaders such as Nancy Pelosi, Chuck Schumer, Steny Hoyer, Kirsten Gillibrand, Bob Menendez, and Chris Coons demanded an immediate reopening of the government before negotiations and criticized the wall as ineffective. Some Republicans praised the deal as a bipartisan compromise, with Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell, Steve Scalise, Susan Collins, Mitt Romney, Marco Rubio, and Kevin McCarthy urging Democrats to accept the proposal and end the shutdown. The debate centers on whether the temporary immigration relief is a genuine compromise or a political bargaining chip tied to the contested wall funding.

END OF SUMMARY.
