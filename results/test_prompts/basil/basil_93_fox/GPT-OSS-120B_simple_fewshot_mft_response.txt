SUMMARY:
President Trump’s “immigration compromise” – a three‑year extension of DACA and TPS for 700,000 Dreamers in exchange for $5.7 billion for a border wall – sparked fierce moral condemnation from Democrats who portrayed the deal as a betrayal of federal workers and a subversive abuse of presidential authority. Leaders such as Nancy Pelosi and Chuck Schumer accused Trump of holding the government hostage, calling the offer “one‑sided, ineffective” and a “bad deal” that sacrifices the safety and pay of millions of federal employees (care/harm). They argued the proposal cheats the nation by swapping temporary protections for a costly, ineffective wall, while ignoring a permanent solution for Dreamers and TPS recipients (fairness/cheating). Republicans, by contrast, praised the president for exercising authority to seek a balanced compromise, framing the plan as a fair, strength‑based negotiation that respects loyalty to the nation’s security and to workers who depend on a functioning government (authority/subversion, loyalty/betrayal). The clash highlights a moral battle over who is protecting American families, upholding fair deals, and respecting the legitimate power of elected officials.  

END OF SUMMARY.
