SUMMARY: President Trump’s immigration compromise to end the partial government shutdown offered a three‑year extension of DACA protections in exchange for $5.7 billion for a southern border wall. Democrats blasted the deal as a form of hostage‑taking, accusing the president of keeping federal workers in chaos and forcing a pointless shutdown while “hurting” millions of Americans. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi called the proposal a “non‑starter,” saying it showed no sympathy for workers and was a one‑sided, ineffective trade‑off that continued the crisis and damaged the nation. Senate leaders Chuck Schumer and Kirsten Gillibrand labeled the offer unacceptable, arguing that Trump’s earlier removal of DACA and TPS meant the current exchange was merely more hostage‑taking, not a real solution. Republican leaders, including Mitch McConnell, Steve Scalise and Mitt Romney, praised the move as a reasonable, good‑faith step toward reopening the government and securing the border, urging Democrats to end obstruction, reopen the government and engage in a civil discussion. The partisan impasse remains, with both sides accusing the other of refusing to address the broken immigration system while federal employees miss paychecks and the economy suffers. END OF SUMMARY.
