SUMMARY:

A National Rifle Association task force released a report calling for armed police officers, security guards or staff members in every American school. The study's central conclusion is that the presence of armed security personnel adds a layer of security and diminishes response time in a shooting. However, gun control advocates and many Democrats have criticized the recommendations as a response to the last tragedy rather than a solution to the underlying issue of gun violence. President Obama has called for tough new controls on gun ownership, but the prospects for such legislation are murky. The report drew support from at least one Newtown parent, Mark Mattioli, who hopes that the recommendations will lead to real solutions that will make children safer. The timing of the announcement was not a coincidence, as Congress is expected to consider gun legislation after lawmakers return from spring recess.

END OF SUMMARY.
