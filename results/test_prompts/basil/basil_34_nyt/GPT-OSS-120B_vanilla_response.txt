SUMMARY:
A National Rifle Association (NRA) task force, led by former Arkansas Congressman Asa Hutchinson, released a 225‑page report urging every U.S. school to have armed police, security guards, or staff, and to allow trained teachers and administrators to carry weapons. The report argues that armed personnel reduce response time in shootings, citing a 1997 Mississippi incident where an armed assistant principal stopped a gunman. It also calls for expanded police presence, coordinated federal school‑safety efforts, grant funding, and a 40‑ to 60‑hour training program developed by the NRA. Critics, including Democrats and gun‑control advocates, denounce the plan as a reactionary measure that would turn schools into “armed fortresses.” Some opposition voices, such as Rep. Mike Thompson and Marian Wright Edelman, argue against arming teachers, while a Newtown shooting victim’s parent, Mark Mattioli, supports the recommendations. Indiana’s legislature is already considering a bill requiring armed “protection officers” in schools. The report’s release coincides with upcoming Senate debates on gun legislation, where President Obama and Senate leaders aim to push broader background checks and revive assault‑weapon restrictions, though NRA lobbying makes passage uncertain.

END OF SUMMARY.
