SUMMARY:  
A NRA‑backed task force released a 225‑page report urging every U.S. school to staff armed police, security guards or teachers, and to loosen gun laws so educators can carry weapons. Led by former Rep. Asa Hutchinson, the report claims armed personnel cut response times, citing a 1997 Mississippi case where an armed assistant principal stopped a shooter. Critics—gun‑control advocates, Democrats and child‑advocacy groups—denounce the plan as a reactionary, fear‑driven strategy that turns schools into “armed fortresses.” Representative Mike Thompson warned that arming teachers merely mirrors past tragedies, while Marian Wright Edelman called the study a continuation of the NRA’s exploitation of public fear. Even so, a Newtown parent, Mark Mattioli, supported the recommendations as “real solutions” for safety. Indiana’s legislature is already moving toward mandatory armed “protection officers.” The timing aligns with upcoming Senate debates on gun control, as President Obama pushes for stricter background checks and an assault‑weapon ban, both facing strong NRA opposition.  

END OF SUMMARY.
