SUMMARY:

A National Rifle Association task force released a 225-page report recommending that every American school have armed police officers, security guards or staff members to enhance safety. The study's lead author, Asa Hutchinson, cited a 1997 case in which an assistant principal subdued a gunman with a pistol. Gun control advocates and Democrats criticized the proposal as a response to the last tragedy rather than addressing the root causes of gun violence. Representative Mike Thompson said arming teachers would not prevent future shootings. The report also recommends expanding police presence in schools, requiring states to loosen gun restrictions for trained teachers and administrators to carry weapons, and developing security plans.

The NRA's executive director, Wayne LaPierre, initially announced a plan to put armed guards in every school after the Newtown shooting. Critics see this as an attempt to prey on America's fears and turn schools into armed fortresses. Mark Mattioli, a parent who lost his 6-year-old son in the Sandy Hook shooting, expressed support for the report, saying it offers real solutions to make kids safer.

The report's recommendations come ahead of Senate debate on gun control this month, with President Obama pushing for tough new controls on gun ownership and background checks. The prospects for such legislation are murky due to the NRA's lobbying prowess.

END OF SUMMARY.
