SUMMARY:

A National Rifle Association task force released a report calling for armed police officers, security guards, or staff members in every American school to increase safety. The study's lead author, Asa Hutchinson, cited a 1997 Mississippi case where an assistant principal subdued a gunman with a pistol. Critics argue that arming teachers and administrators is a response to the latest tragedy rather than addressing the root cause of gun violence. Gun control advocates, including Representative Mike Thompson and Marian Wright Edelman, criticized the report as "preying on America's fears" and turning schools into "armed fortresses." However, one Newtown parent, Mark Mattioli, expressed support for the idea of armed officers in schools to make children safer. The task force recommends training courses for armed officers or employees and requires states to develop security plans.

END OF SUMMARY.
