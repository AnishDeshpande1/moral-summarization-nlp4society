SUMMARY:

A National Rifle Association (NRA) task force released a report calling for armed police officers, security guards, or staff members in every American school. The report also recommends loosening gun restrictions to allow trained teachers and administrators to carry weapons. Asa Hutchinson, the former Republican congressman who led the task force, cited a 1997 case where an assistant principal subdued a gunman with a pistol. Gun control advocates and Democrats criticized the recommendations, saying they are a response to the latest tragedy rather than a solution.

The report also suggests expanding police presence in schools, training officers or employees to carry guns, and requiring schools to develop security plans. One Newtown parent supported the plan, while others criticized it as "preying on America's fears" and turning schools into "armed fortresses." The recommendations are likely to be considered by Congress this month as part of a broader debate on gun control.

END OF SUMMARY.
