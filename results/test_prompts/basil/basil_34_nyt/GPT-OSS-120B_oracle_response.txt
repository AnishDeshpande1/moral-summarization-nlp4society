SUMMARY:  
A National Rifle Association‑backed task force released a 225‑page report urging that every American school have armed police, security guards or staff, and that states loosen gun restrictions so teachers and administrators can carry weapons. The report argues that armed personnel add a layer of security and diminish response time in a shooting, citing a 1997 Mississippi case where an armed assistant principal stopped a gunman who had already killed two students. Critics call the plan a continuation of the NRA’s effort to prey on fear, saturate schools with guns and turn them into armed fortresses, and warn that such measures merely respond to past tragedies and massacres without preventing future attacks. Supporters, including a Newtown parent, claim the recommendations will make schools safer. Indiana lawmakers are already considering a proposal for armed protection officers, and the task force urges federal departments to coordinate security efforts, provide grants, and develop training. The report arrives as Congress prepares to debate gun‑control legislation, with President Obama pushing broader background‑check reforms while the NRA’s influence threatens to put its own safety plan into effect.  

END OF SUMMARY.
