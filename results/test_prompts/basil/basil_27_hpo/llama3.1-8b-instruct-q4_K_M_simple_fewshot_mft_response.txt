SUMMARY:

Despite Netanyahu's previous statement that he wouldn't get involved in the US presidential election, a new ad by Secure America Now features his message on Iran's nuclear program as a major campaign issue. The ad highlights Netanyahu's frustration with the Obama administration's failure to establish "red lines" for reining in Iran and emphasizes the need for American strength over apologies. This ad is part of a larger effort by Republicans to criticize the Obama administration's relationship with Israel, which has been portrayed as dismissive of Israeli concerns. However, Netanyahu himself rejected these criticisms, saying that he knows people are trying to draw him into the election and that there is "no bus" for throwing Israel under. The ad aims to capitalize on Jewish voters' concerns about Iran and Obama's stance on Israel, but Netanyahu's own words undermine the narrative being pushed by Secure America Now.
