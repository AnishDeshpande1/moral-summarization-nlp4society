SUMMARY:  
The neoconservative nonprofit Secure America Now has produced a costly television ad that foregrounds Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s warnings about Iran’s nuclear program, targeting swing‑state Florida’s large Jewish communities. While Netanyahu publicly rebuked attempts to drag him into U.S. election politics—insisting he will not be used as a campaign pawn and downplaying reports of a snub by President Obama—the ad serves a clear partisan purpose: it reinforces the Republican narrative that the Obama administration is soft on Iran and indifferent to Israel’s security. The piece echoes Mitt Romney’s repeated accusations that Obama “threw Israel under a bus,” and follows earlier Secure America Now spots that attacked the president for an alleged “apology tour” on national security. By framing the ad as a matter of Israel’s existential concerns, the organization sidesteps direct criticism of Obama while still bolstering GOP attacks, illustrating how foreign leaders can be instrumentalized for domestic political gain.  

END OF SUMMARY.
