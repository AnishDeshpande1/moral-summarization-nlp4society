SUMMARY:
Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, despite saying he will not be used in U.S. election politics, is the centerpiece of a $1 million television ad bought by the neoconservative nonprofit Secure America Now. The spot, airing in Miami, West Palm Beach and Fort Myers—areas with large Jewish populations—features Netanyahu warning about Iran’s nuclear threat, echoing Republican attacks on President Obama for being soft on Israel. The GOP’s narrative, championed by nominee Mitt Romney, accuses Obama of “throwing Israel under a bus” and of refusing a UN meeting with Netanyahu. Netanyahu pushed back, telling CNN and NBC’s Meet the Press that he will not be drawn into the American campaign and that scheduling, not hostility, explains the missed UN encounter. Secure America Now, which also ran a similar anti‑Obama ad in July, uses the spot to reinforce the GOP’s claim that the Obama administration endangers U.S. security and Israel’s interests, even though the president is not named directly.  
END OF SUMMARY.
