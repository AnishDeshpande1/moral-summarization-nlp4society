SUMMARY: A neoconservative nonprofit, Secure America Now, has produced a new television ad featuring Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu discussing Iran’s nuclear threat. The ad is being aired in Florida markets with large Jewish populations and is part of a $1 million campaign, $400,000 of which has already been spent. The organization, whose pollster John McLaughlin has ties to Netanyahu’s Likud Party, aims to bolster Republican criticism of President Barack Obama’s stance toward Israel and Iran. GOP figures, including presidential nominee Mitt Romney, have accused Obama of “throwing Israel under a bus” and of being soft on Iran, especially after the president declined a UN General Assembly meeting with Netanyahu. Netanyahu, however, told CNN and NBC’s “Meet the Press” that he will not be drawn into U.S. election politics and praised Obama’s commitment to prevent Iran’s nuclear weapons, dismissing the “bus” claim. Secure America Now has previously run ads attacking Obama, despite its claim of representing all ideologies. The latest spot avoids naming Obama directly but reinforces the GOP narrative of a weak U.S.–Israel relationship.  
END OF SUMMARY.
