SUMMARY:  
Secure America Now, a neoconservative nonprofit, has produced a $1 million ad campaign featuring Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu that airs in Florida’s large Jewish markets. The spot highlights Israel’s security concerns over Iran’s nuclear program and echoes Republican attacks that President Barack Obama has been “dismissive” of Israel, even accusing him of having “thrown Israel under a bus.” GOP figures such as Mitt Romney have used the same narrative to claim the Obama administration is “determined” to weaken U.S. security at Israel’s “expense.” Netanyahu, however, rebuffed attempts to drag him into the American election, telling CNN he will not be used as a political pawn and characterizing reports of an Obama snub as merely a scheduling conflict. He praised Obama’s determination to stop Iran’s nuclear ambitions, saying there is “no bus” to be thrown. The ad continues Secure America Now’s pattern of targeting the president, despite its claim of bipartisan representation, and reinforces the GOP’s portrayal of the White House as indifferent to Israel’s security needs.  

END OF SUMMARY.
