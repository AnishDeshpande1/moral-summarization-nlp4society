**SUMMARY:**  
Secure America Now, a neoconservative nonprofit that prides itself on influencing American security debates, has produced a costly ad featuring Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. The spot, aired in Florida’s large Jewish markets, frames Iran’s nuclear program as an existential threat and subtly attacks President Barack Obama’s “apology tour” and perceived dismissive stance toward Israel. While Republicans have repeatedly decried Obama for “throwing Israel under a bus,” Netanyahu pushed back, saying he will not be drawn into American politicking and that there is “no bus.” He praised Obama’s determination to prevent Iran’s nuclear ambitions, showing a rare moment of respect amid the partisan attack. The ad follows a previous Secure America Now campaign that similarly criticized the Obama administration, underscoring the GOP’s narrative that the president is back‑sliding on Israel’s security.
