SUMMARY:  
Prominent Republicans are urging Arizona Governor Jan Brewer to veto SB 1062, a bill that would let businesses refuse service to gay people on the grounds of “sincerely held” religious beliefs. The measure, championed by the socially conservative Center for Arizona Policy, is framed by its supporters as a necessary shield of religious liberty against activist federal courts. Yet the bill has sparked a national outcry, with business leaders, the state’s Super Bowl committee, both Republican U.S. senators and former GOP nominee Mitt Romney condemning it as “toxic” discrimination. Even some Republican senators who helped pass the law now call for a veto, claiming the public has been misled. Critics argue the legislation weaponizes faith to legitimize bigotry, while its proponents decry the backlash as fear‑mongering and media distortion that threatens religious freedom. Governor Brewer, known for deliberate decision‑making, is expected to receive detailed legal briefings before announcing whether she will sign or veto the controversial law.  

END OF SUMMARY.
