SUMMARY:

The Arizona Legislature passed a bill, SB1062, allowing businesses to deny service based on sincerely held religious beliefs, sparking widespread opposition and calls for Gov. Jan Brewer to veto it. Prominent Republicans, including former presidential nominee Mitt Romney, are urging Brewer to give in to the demands of business leaders, civil rights groups, and politicians from both parties who see the bill as discriminatory towards gays. The legislation has caused a national uproar, with many accusing proponents of engaging in fear-mongering tactics by claiming that opponents are employing "attacks" and "characterizations" to sway public opinion. However, Democrats argue that SB1062 is toxic legislation that allows businesses to divide communities by discriminating against individuals based on their sexual orientation. Brewer will likely spend the next day or more pondering her decision before deciding whether to sign or veto the bill.

END OF SUMMARY.
