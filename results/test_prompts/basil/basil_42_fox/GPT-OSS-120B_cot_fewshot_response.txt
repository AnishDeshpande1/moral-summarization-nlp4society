**SUMMARY:**
Prominent Republicans are urging Arizona Gov. Jan Brewer to veto the controversial Senate Bill 1062, which would let businesses deny service on religious grounds. The legislation has sparked a national uproar and intense opposition from the business community, the state’s Super Bowl Committee, both Republican U.S. senators and civil‑rights groups, who label the measure “toxic” and a form of discrimination. Even some Republican senators who helped pass the bill now call for a veto, citing inaccurate information and fear‑mongering that has polarized the state. Democrats warned the bill would be polarizing and warned of intimidation tactics such as boycotts intended to persuade public opinion. Brewer, known for being deliberate and “no rookie” on high‑profile deals, will receive detailed briefings from her legal team before deciding. The Center for Arizona Policy, which promoted the bill as protecting religious liberty, rebuked critics for “false attacks” and expressed disappointment at the current climate. The outcome remains uncertain as Brewer weighs both sides.
