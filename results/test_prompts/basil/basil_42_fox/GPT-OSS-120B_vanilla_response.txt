SUMMARY:  
Prominent Republicans, business leaders, and both Arizona U.S. senators are urging Governor Jan Brewer to veto Senate Bill 1062, a controversial measure that would let businesses, churches and individuals refuse service to gay people based on “sincerely held” religious beliefs and use that belief as a defense in discrimination lawsuits. The bill, championed by the socially conservative Center for Arizona Policy, has sparked national backlash, with the state’s Super Bowl Committee, civil‑rights groups, and even former GOP presidential nominee Mitt Romney calling for a veto. Some Republican senators who helped pass the bill now argue it was mischaracterized and intended only to shield owners from lawsuits seen in other states. Democrats label the legislation “toxic” and warn it will polarize Arizona. Governor Brewer, known for deliberating on high‑profile issues, is expected to receive detailed legal briefings before deciding, but speculation leans toward a veto.  
END OF SUMMARY.
