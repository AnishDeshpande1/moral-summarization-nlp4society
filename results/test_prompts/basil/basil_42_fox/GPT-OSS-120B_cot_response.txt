Prominent Republicans are urging Arizona Gov. Jan Brewer to veto the **controversial** Senate Bill 1062, a measure that would let businesses deny service based on **religious** beliefs. The bill has sparked a national outcry, with business leaders, the Super Bowl Committee, and both Republican U.S. senators labeling it **toxic** and **discriminatory**. Former GOP nominee Mitt Romney joined the chorus, urging a veto to prevent further **discrimination**. Brewer, known for deliberate decision‑making, will receive detailed legal briefings before acting. Some Republican sponsors now claim the bill was **mischaracterized**, arguing it merely **protects** owners from frivolous lawsuits and that the media **blown** the issue out of proportion. Democrats counter that the legislation is **toxic** and **discriminates** against LGBTQ Arizonans. The Center for Arizona Policy, which **advocates** for **religious liberty**, defends the bill as a clarification of existing law and accuses opponents of **fear‑mongering**, **intimidating** the public with false attacks. The governor’s next move will determine whether the state embraces **religious** freedom or curtails **discrimination** protections.
