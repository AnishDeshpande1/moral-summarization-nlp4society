SUMMARY:  
Prominent Republicans are urging Arizona Gov. Jan Brewer to veto Senate Bill 1062, a controversial measure that would let businesses deny service based on sincerely held religious beliefs. The bill has sparked a national uproar, with business groups, the state’s Super Bowl Committee, both Republican U.S. senators and former GOP nominee Mitt Romney calling for a veto, labeling the proposal “toxic” discrimination. Some Republican senators who helped pass the bill now claim inaccurate information fueled the firestorm and argue the legislation merely protects owners from lawsuits. Democrats warn the bill will divide the state and intensify political attacks. The Center for Arizona Policy, which pushed the bill, accuses opponents of fear‑mongering and false characterizations, saying boycott threats are a new tactic to intimidate supporters of religious liberty. Governor Brewer is expected to receive detailed briefings from both sides before deciding whether to give the bill a signature or a veto.  

END OF SUMMARY.
