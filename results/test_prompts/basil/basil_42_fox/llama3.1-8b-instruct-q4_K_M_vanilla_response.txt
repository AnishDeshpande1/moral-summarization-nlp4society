SUMMARY:
Arizona Governor Jan Brewer is facing pressure from prominent Republicans, including former presidential nominee Mitt Romney, to veto a bill that allows businesses to deny service based on religious beliefs. The legislation, known as Senate Bill 1062, has sparked national outrage and criticism from the business community, politicians, and civil rights groups. While some Republican senators who pushed the bill through the Legislature are now calling for a veto, others argue that the media has blown the law out of proportion and that it's designed to protect business owners from discrimination lawsuits.

The Center for Arizona Policy, which pushed the bill, claims it simply clarifies existing state law and is needed to protect against activist federal courts. However, Democrats have dubbed the bill "toxic" legislation that allows discrimination. With widespread speculation that Brewer will veto the bill, she is expected to spend the next day or more pondering her decision before making a final determination.

END OF SUMMARY.
