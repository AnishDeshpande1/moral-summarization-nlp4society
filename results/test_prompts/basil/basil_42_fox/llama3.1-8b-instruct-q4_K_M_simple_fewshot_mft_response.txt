SUMMARY:

Prominent Republicans are urging Arizona Gov. Jan Brewer to veto a bill that would allow businesses to deny service based on religious beliefs. The bill, SB1062, has sparked national outrage and criticism from business leaders, politicians, and civil rights groups. Critics argue that the legislation allows for discrimination against marginalized communities, while proponents claim it is necessary to protect business owners with strong religious convictions from lawsuits. However, some Republican senators who supported the bill are now calling for a veto, citing "inaccurate" information about the measure's impact. The moral framing of this issue revolves around questions of authority and fairness: does allowing businesses to discriminate based on religious beliefs undermine the principle of equal treatment under the law? Does the legislation prioritize the interests of business owners over those of marginalized communities? Brewer is expected to make a decision soon, amidst growing pressure from both sides.
