SUMMARY:
Arizona's Governor Jan Brewer is facing pressure from prominent Republicans, including former presidential nominee Mitt Romney, to veto a bill that allows businesses to deny service based on religious beliefs. The legislation, which has sparked national outrage and criticism from the business community, civil rights groups, and politicians of all stripes, would permit any business or individual to cite "sincerely held" religious beliefs as a defense against claims of discrimination. Republican senators who pushed for the bill now claim it was misinterpreted by the media, while Democrats call it "toxic" legislation that will only lead to division and polarization.

The Center for Arizona Policy, which advocates for conservative social policies, argues that the bill clarifies existing state law and protects against activist federal courts. However, opponents claim that the measure would legitimize discrimination against marginalized groups, including the LGBTQ community. Brewer is expected to carefully consider the legislation before making a decision, but widespread speculation suggests she may veto it.

END OF SUMMARY.
