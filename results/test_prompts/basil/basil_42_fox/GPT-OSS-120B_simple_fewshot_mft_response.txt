SUMMARY:
Prominent Republicans and business leaders are urging Arizona Gov. Jan Brewer to exercise her authority and veto Senate Bill 1062, a controversial measure that would let businesses deny service on the basis of sincerely held religious beliefs. The bill, championed by the socially conservative Center for Arizona Policy, has ignited a national backlash for its perceived unfairness—allowing discrimination against gay customers while shielding owners from lawsuits. Even some Republican senators who helped pass the legislation now claim they were misled and warn that the law threatens fairness by privileging a minority’s religious convictions over equal treatment for all. Former GOP nominee Mitt Romney, the state’s Super Bowl Committee, and civil‑rights groups have joined Democrats in labeling the proposal “toxic” and urging Brewer to subvert the bill’s passage. Observers note Brewer’s deliberate decision‑making style; she will receive detailed briefings from both supporters and opponents before deciding whether to uphold the governor’s authority by signing the bill or to protect fairness by vetoing it. The dispute highlights a clash between deference to traditional authority and the demand for equitable, nondiscriminatory treatment.

END OF SUMMARY.
