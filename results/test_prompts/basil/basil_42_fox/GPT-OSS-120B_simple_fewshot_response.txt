SUMMARY:
Arizona Gov. Jan Brewer faces intense pressure to veto Senate Bill 1062, a controversial measure that would let businesses, churches and individuals refuse service to gay people on the basis of sincerely held religious beliefs and use that claim as a legal defense against discrimination lawsuits. The bill, championed by the socially conservative Center for Arizona Policy, has sparked a national backlash from the business community, the state’s Super Bowl committee, both Republican U.S. senators and former GOP nominee Mitt Romney, all urging a veto. Even some Republican senators who helped pass the bill now argue it was misrepresented and warn it could divide the state. Democrats label the proposal “toxic” and a direct sanction of discrimination. As Brewer returns from a governors’ conference, she is expected to receive detailed briefings from both sides before making a decision. The moral core of the dispute is clear: supporters see the bill as a protection of religious liberty, while opponents view it as an unjust sanction of prejudice that harms LGBTQ Arizonans. Whether Brewer chooses to sign or veto will signal how the state balances freedom of conscience with the right to equal treatment under the law.  
END OF SUMMARY.
