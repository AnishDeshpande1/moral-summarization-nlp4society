SUMMARY:

Rep. Bruce Braley (D-Iowa) faced criticism for comments he made about Sen. Chuck Grassley (R-Iowa) at a January fundraiser in Texas. Braley suggested that if he won the Senate seat, he may have someone with his background and experience on the Senate Judiciary Committee, implying that Grassley's lack of law school education would be a liability. The comments were released by America Rising, a conservative PAC, and sparked backlash from both Republicans and some of Braley's potential opponents.

Braley apologized to Grassley in person and posted a statement on Tuesday, emphasizing his own family's farm background and expressing appreciation for farmers. Grassley, who owns a farm himself, has often noted that he is the only non-lawyer on the Senate Judiciary Committee. The controversy comes as Braley runs to succeed retiring Sen. Tom Harkin (D-Iowa) in a key Senate seat.

END OF SUMMARY.
