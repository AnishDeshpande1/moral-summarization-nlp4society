SUMMARY:  
Rep. Bruce Braley (D‑Iowa), a candidate to replace retiring Sen. Tom Harkin, attracted criticism after a leaked January fundraiser speech in Texas where he disparaged Sen. Chuck Grassley (R‑Iowa) for being a non‑lawyer farmer who could become chair of the Senate Judiciary Committee. The comments, released by the conservative group America Rising, sparked backlash in Iowa media and from Republican leaders. Braley apologized in person to Grassley and issued a public statement emphasizing his own farming family background. Businessman Mark Jacobs, a GOP primary contender, quickly aired a radio ad to exploit the controversy. Grassley, who frequently highlights his farming roots as the only non‑lawyer on the Judiciary Committee, was directly insulted by Braley’s remarks. The incident adds pressure to Braley’s Senate race, one of several pivotal contests that will decide Senate control.  
END OF SUMMARY.
