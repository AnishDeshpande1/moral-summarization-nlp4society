**SUMMARY:**  
During a historic reading of the U.S. Constitution on the House floor, Republicans omitted sections superseded by amendments, prompting Democratic members to decry the “sacred” exercise as a “propaganda” stunt that ignored the nation’s “struggle” and the “emotional” weight of its “imperfect” past. Rep. Jesse Jackson Jr. warned that leaving out amended text diminishes the “struggle” of African Americans, women, and others to perfect the document, while Rep. Elijah Cummings called the partial reading a failure to acknowledge the country’s “imperfect” history and a missed “blessing” for unity rather than “divisiveness.” Rep. Jerrold Nadler dismissed the ceremony as “nonsense,” rejecting the notion of the Constitution as a worship‑worthy “demigods” text, insisting it is a living guide for governance. Supporters argued the event reminded legislators that the nation is a “government of laws, not of men,” and that the Founders intended a limited, not fluid, constitutional order.
