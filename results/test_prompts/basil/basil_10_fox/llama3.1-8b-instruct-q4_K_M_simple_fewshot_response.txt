In a symbolic gesture, the House of Representatives read the U.S. Constitution aloud on its first day in session. However, the event was marred by controversy as Democrats expressed concerns that parts of the document were being skipped due to amendments. Rep. Republicans defended the decision, citing the Constitution's ability to evolve through amendment. The reading sparked debate over the document's relevance and interpretation, with some lawmakers questioning its authority in modern society. Historians and experts weighed in on the significance of the event, with some viewing it as an opportunity for Congress to reconnect with the nation's founding principles.
