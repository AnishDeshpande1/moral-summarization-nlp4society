SUMMARY:

The U.S. Constitution was read aloud on the House floor, but Democrats complained that Republicans exempted parts of the document from reading, citing amendments as justification. This decision was seen as undermining the objective of the exercise and failing to acknowledge the nation's ongoing struggle for a more perfect union. Rep. Jesse Jackson Jr. emphasized the importance of reading the full document, including its imperfections, to show Americans that while we seek improvement, our government is imperfect. Historian Alan Brinkley noted that the reading was "a bit of theater," but significant given Republicans' claims about the Constitution's role in reducing government size. Roger Pilon argued that Congress needs to re-read the Constitution to be reminded of its original intention: limited government and individual freedom. The controversy highlights the ongoing struggle between those who seek to improve the document through amendment and those who believe it should be followed more closely.

END OF SUMMARY.
