SUMMARY:

A recent reading of the US Constitution on the House floor sparked controversy among lawmakers, with Democrats arguing that Republicans' decision to skip certain "obsolete" parts undermines the exercise's purpose. Rep. Jesse Jackson Jr. expressed concern that omitting sections related to slavery and Native Americans would erase their struggles for equality. Democrats also questioned the interpretation of which amendments to include, with some arguing that reading only the current constitution is a failure to acknowledge the document's imperfections.

Supporters saw the exercise as an important moment in American history, emphasizing the Constitution as the foundation of laws. However, critics like Rep. Jerrold Nadler deemed it "propaganda" and argued that the Founders' work should not be worshipped but rather used as a guide for governing. The controversy highlights differing views on the Constitution's role in shaping America's government and society.

END OF SUMMARY
