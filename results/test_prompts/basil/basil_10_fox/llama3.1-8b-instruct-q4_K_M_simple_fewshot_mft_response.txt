SUMMARY:

The reading of the U.S. Constitution on the House floor was marred by controversy over which parts of the document to include or exclude. Republicans decided to skip certain sections that have been superseded by amendments, such as the 18th Amendment's prohibition on liquor and the part about counting slaves and Native Americans as three-fifths of a person. Democratic lawmakers argued that this undermined the exercise's objective and ignored the imperfections in the original document. They pointed out that even though the Constitution has undergone 27 amendments, its original decisions remain unchanged, including those that perpetuated injustices like slavery. The controversy highlights the ongoing struggle to perfect the country and improve the Constitution, with some lawmakers viewing it as a sacred text and others seeing it as a living document in need of reinterpretation. The debate raises questions about loyalty to the document's ideals versus the need for revision and amendment.

END OF SUMMARY.
