SUMMARY:
Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez defended fellow freshman Rep. Rashida Tlaib on Twitter, accusing the GOP of hypocrisy for criticizing Tlaib's use of profanity while excusing similar language from President Trump. Tlaib had made headlines with her expletive-laced comments about impeaching Trump, which were captured on video at a bar in Detroit. House Republicans condemned the remarks, but Speaker Nancy Pelosi downplayed them, saying she didn't want to set a precedent for censoring colleagues and that Trump's own language was partly to blame. Ocasio-Cortez tweeted her support for Tlaib, saying "the Bronx and Detroit ride together." Tlaib has doubled down on her comments, tweeting that she will continue to speak truth to power.

Trump had previously made lewd comments about women in a 2005 video, which he apologized for at the time. Ocasio-Cortez accused Republicans of hypocrisy for condemning Tlaib's language while excusing Trump's.

END OF SUMMARY.
