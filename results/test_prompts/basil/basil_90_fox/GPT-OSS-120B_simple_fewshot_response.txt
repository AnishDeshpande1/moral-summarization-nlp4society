SUMMARY:

Democratic socialist Rep. Alexandria Ocasio‑Cortez defended fellow freshman Rep. Rashida Tlaib after Tlaib’s profanity‑filled call to impeach President Trump, accusing Republicans of a double standard. AOC highlighted that GOP leaders dismissed Trump’s 2005 “locker‑room talk” about sexual assault while feigning outrage over a curse word from a woman of color, saying the party has no right to police women’s behavior. She pledged support for Tlaib, noting the Bronx and Detroit stand together. Tlaib’s remarks, captured on video, reiterated her pledge to impeach the “motherf***er” and were condemned by House Republicans and Trump, who called them “disgraceful.” Speaker Nancy Pelosi expressed personal distaste for the language but refused to censor colleagues, pointing out that the president’s own rhetoric set a harsher tone. The clash was framed as a moral issue of hypocrisy, gendered double standards, and the right to speak truth to power in a constitutional crisis.  

END OF SUMMARY.
