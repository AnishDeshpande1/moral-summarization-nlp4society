SUMMARY:  
Rep. Alexandria Ocasio‑Cortez publicly defended fellow freshman Rep. Rashida Tlaib after Tlaib’s profanity‑filled call to impeach President Trump. Ocasio‑Cortez condemned the GOP’s double standard, pointing out that Republicans dismissed Trump’s own lewd “locker‑room” remarks while scandalizing Tlaib’s language. She tweeted solidarity, “I got your back…the Bronx and Detroit ride together.” Tlaib’s video, recorded after she announced an impeachment op‑ed, featured explicit profanity and a promise to “impeach the motherf***er,” prompting outrage from House Republicans and a rebuke from Trump, who called the remarks “disgraceful.” House Speaker Nancy Pelosi, while disapproving of the language, refused to police it, noting Trump’s worse conduct. Tlaib doubled down, asserting she will “always speak truth to power” and framing the incident as part of a broader constitutional crisis. The episode highlights a moral clash: progressive lawmakers argue for holding leaders accountable without censoring dissent, while Republicans accuse them of hypocrisy and moral failing.
