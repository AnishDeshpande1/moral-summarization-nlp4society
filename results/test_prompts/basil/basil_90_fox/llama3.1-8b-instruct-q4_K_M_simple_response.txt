SUMMARY: 

Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez defended her fellow House freshman Rep. Rashida Tlaib against criticism for using profanity while vowing to impeach President Trump. Ocasio-Cortez accused Republicans of hypocrisy, pointing out that they had previously dismissed Trump's own lewd comments as "locker room talk." She argued that the GOP lost its right to police women's behavior long ago and vowed to support Tlaib. House Minority Leader Kevin McCarthy called for action against Tlaib, but Speaker Nancy Pelosi downplayed her remarks, suggesting that Trump's language had started the heated rhetoric in Washington.

Pelosi stated that she is not in the business of censorship, and Ocasio-Cortez echoed this sentiment, accusing Republicans of trying to silence women. Tlaib doubled down on her comments, saying she would always speak truth to power and refusing to apologize for using profanity.

The controversy highlights the double standard applied by some politicians when it comes to language used by men versus women.

END OF SUMMARY
