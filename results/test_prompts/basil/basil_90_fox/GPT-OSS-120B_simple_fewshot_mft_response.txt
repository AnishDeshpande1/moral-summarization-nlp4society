SUMMARY:
Rep. Alexandria Ocasio‑Cortez defended fellow freshman Rep. Rashida Tlaib after Tlaib’s profanity‑filled call to impeach President Trump, framing the episode as a clash over authority and moral standards. Ocasio‑Cortez denounced Republican double standards, noting that GOP leaders dismissed Trump’s own “locker‑room talk” about sexual assault while feigning outrage over a curse word spoken by a woman, thereby accusing the party of subverting legitimate critique and degrading women’s dignity. She pledged solidarity, saying “the Bronx and Detroit ride together,” and invoked care for victims of harassment. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi, while disapproving of Tlaib’s language, refused to censor her, arguing that the president’s vulgar remarks set a lower bar for discourse and that imposing speech rules would abuse authority. Trump condemned Tlaib’s remarks as “disgraceful,” casting her as a moral transgressor. Tlaib doubled down, asserting she will “always speak truth to power” and framing the issue as a broader fight against a constitutional crisis. The narrative emphasizes fairness—highlighting GOP hypocrisy—and sanctity, contrasting the perceived degradation of women’s speech with the need to uphold respectful, equitable political dialogue.

END OF SUMMARY.
