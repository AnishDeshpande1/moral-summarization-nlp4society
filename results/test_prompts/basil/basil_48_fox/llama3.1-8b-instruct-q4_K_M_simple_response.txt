SUMMARY:

Elizabeth Lauten, communications director for Rep. Stephen Fincher (R-Tenn.), apologized for a Facebook post criticizing the Obama daughters' outfits during President Barack Obama's Thanksgiving address. In her now-deleted post, Lauten suggested that Malia and Sasha should "try showing a little class" and implied they were poor role models. She also took a shot at President Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama, suggesting they didn't respect their positions. However, after receiving backlash and prayerful reflection, Lauten apologized for her hurtful words, stating she had quickly judged the girls without considering how others would perceive her own behavior as a teenager.

Lauten's apology came after she confirmed that both Facebook postings attributed to her were genuine. The White House and Rep. Fincher's office did not publicly comment on the issue. Lauten's mistake serves as a reminder of the importance of being mindful of one's words, especially when commenting on others, particularly children.

END OF SUMMARY.
