SUMMARY:
Republican communications director Elizabeth Lauten apologized after a Facebook post harshly criticized President Obama’s daughters, Malia and Sasha, during the Thanksgiving turkey‑pardon ceremony. Lauten wrote that the teens should “try showing a little class,” that their outfits were fit for “a spot at the bar,” and blamed their parents for not respecting their positions, suggesting the girls were falling short as role models. After the backlash, she deleted the post, admitted she had judged the girls in a way she would never want to be judged, and pledged to learn from the experience. The Washington Post confirmed Lauten authored the comments; neither the White House nor Rep. Stephen Fincher, whom she works for, issued a statement. The incident serves as a reminder that public figures, especially children, deserve respect and kindness rather than scorn, and that social‑media missteps can quickly become cautionary tales about the need for empathy and humility.  
END OF SUMMARY.
