SUMMARY:

A Republican communications director, Elizabeth Lauten, faced backlash for her Facebook post criticizing President Obama's daughters, Malia and Sasha, for their outfits during the White House turkey pardon ceremony. Lauten wrote that the girls should "try showing a little class" and that their attire was more suitable for a bar than a public event. She also took a shot at President Obama and the first lady, suggesting they don't respect their positions or the nation. Lauten later apologized for her comments, stating that she had judged the girls harshly and would learn from the experience. The incident highlights the dangers of criticizing children of public figures, as noted by Patti Davis, daughter of Ronald Reagan, who wrote that "as First Daughters, you can't win." Lauten's apology was seen as a sincere effort to make amends for her hurtful words.
