SUMMARY:
A Republican communications director, Elizabeth Lauten, posted a scathing Facebook comment about First Daughters Malia and Sasha Obama during President Obama’s Thanksgiving turkey‑pardon, telling the teens to “show a little class,” dress “like you deserve respect … not a spot at the bar,” and accusing their parents of failing to respect their positions. The post attacked the authority of the First Family, degrading the dignity of the presidential office (Authority/Subversion, Sanctity/Degradation) and inflicted emotional harm on the young girls (Care/Harm). By implying the Obama family was cheating the nation of proper role‑model behavior, Lauten framed the incident as an unfair betrayal of public trust (Fairness/Cheating). After the backlash, Lauten issued a public apology, acknowledging her hurtful words, invoking prayer and parental counsel, and promising to learn from the experience. No comment was made by the White House or Rep. Stephen Fincher. The episode underscores how disrespecting legitimate authority and degrading public figures can cause personal harm and be judged as unfair and morally degrading.  
END OF SUMMARY.
