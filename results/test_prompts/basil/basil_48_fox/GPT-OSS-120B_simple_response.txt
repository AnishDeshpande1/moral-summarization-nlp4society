SUMMARY: A Republican congressional communications director, Elizabeth Lauten, posted a scathing Facebook comment about First Daughters Malia and Sasha Obama during President Obama’s Thanksgiving address, criticizing their attire and accusing their parents of lacking respect. After the post was deleted and widely condemned, Lauten issued a public apology, acknowledging that she had judged the teenagers harshly—exactly the way she would not want to be judged herself as a teen—and describing the episode as a painful lesson that prompted prayer, family counsel, and a commitment to personal growth. The Washington Post verified her authorship of the posts, while neither the White House nor Rep. Stephen Fincher, whom she serves, responded. The incident underscores the moral imperative to treat public figures, especially young people, with compassion rather than contempt, and highlights the possibility of redemption through sincere remorse and a pledge to change. END OF SUMMARY.
