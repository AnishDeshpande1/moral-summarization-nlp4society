SUMMARY: President Trump claimed that social media is discriminating against Republican and conservative voices, calling such censorship dangerous and warning he will intervene so all viewpoints can let everyone participate, “good & bad.” He urged platforms to point out that the focus on Russia should shift toward China, which he says has penetrated and infiltrated the election system. The companies have stepped up enforcement to curb hateful and violent speech that could disrupt the midterm elections, removing content from Alex Jones and Infowars for violating rules against inciting violence. Jones appealed to Trump to make censorship the big issue before the midterms. Trump’s tweets also blasted “fake” news networks while continuing his long‑standing attacks and hostility toward the press, even as he promises to be smart, tough and prepared to defend conservative speech. The tension highlights the growing division over how platforms police hate, violence and misinformation. END OF SUMMARY.
