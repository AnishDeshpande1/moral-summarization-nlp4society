SUMMARY: President Trump condemned social‑media platforms for what he called “discriminating against Republican/Conservative voices,” warning that such censorship is dangerous and vowing the administration would intervene if allies’ accounts keep being shut down. He echoed conspiracy‑laden claims, urging attention to Chinese interference alongside Russia and urging platforms to let “everybody participate, good & bad.” The companies, under pressure to curb misinformation and election disruption, have recently banned or restricted far‑right figures like Alex Jones and removed hateful content, actions Jones appealed to Trump to block. While Trump decries alleged bias and shadow‑banning, Twitter denies such practices and struggles to clarify its hate‑speech rules. Critics note Trump’s own hostility toward the press, his attempts to “open up” libel laws, and his pattern of attacking unfavorable news coverage, raising ethical concerns about his use of free‑speech rhetoric to shield partisan allies while undermining journalistic freedom. END OF SUMMARY.
