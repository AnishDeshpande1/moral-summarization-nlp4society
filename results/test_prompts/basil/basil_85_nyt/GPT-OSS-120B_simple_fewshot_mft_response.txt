SUMMARY:
President Trump warned that conservative voices are being unfairly censored on social media, accusing the platforms of discriminatory “shadow‑banning” and threatening to intervene on behalf of his allies. He framed the issue as a matter of fairness, claiming Republicans are being cheated by tech companies that silence their speech while allowing other content to remain. By invoking the danger of censorship, Trump presents himself as defending the public’s right to speak freely, appealing to the moral value of care for those harmed by silencing. He also challenges the authority of the platforms, urging them to “let everybody participate, good & bad,” and casts the media’s focus on Russian interference as a subversive distraction, redirecting attention to China. The article notes that social‑media firms have recently removed extremist content, including Alex Jones’s posts, and that Trump’s rhetoric continues to attack traditional press and propose changes to libel laws, further highlighting his stance against perceived authoritarian control and his claim to protect fairness and prevent harm to conservative expression. END OF SUMMARY.
