SUMMARY:
President Trump warned that social‑media platforms are “totally discriminating against Republican/Conservative voices” and warned that censorship is “a very dangerous thing.” He pledged that the administration would not allow conservative voices to be silenced and urged the companies to let “everybody participate, good & bad.” The warning came as major platforms have tightened enforcement ahead of the midterms, banning accounts linked to misinformation and removing Alex Jones’s Infowars content for hateful, violent speech. Jones appealed to Trump to make “censorship” a central election issue and to expose alleged Chinese interference. Trump echoed the plea, shifting criticism from Russian to Chinese influence while maintaining his broader claim that the tech giants are biasing public discourse. Although Twitter denies shadow‑banning, it has struggled to define its hate‑speech rules and recently suspended Jones’s accounts for inciting violence. Trump’s statements frame the issue as a moral fight for free expression and a warning that the government will intervene to protect conservative speech from partisan tech censorship.  
END OF SUMMARY.
