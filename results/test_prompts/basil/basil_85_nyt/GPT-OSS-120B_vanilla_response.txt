SUMMARY: President Donald Trump claimed that social‑media platforms are censoring conservative voices and warned he might intervene if allies’ accounts keep being shut down. He accused the companies of “discriminating against Republican/Conservative voices” and urged them to allow “everybody participate, good & bad.” The remarks came as major platforms have tightened enforcement ahead of the midterm elections, banning accounts linked to election‑disruption and removing content from far‑right conspiracy figure Alex Jones for hateful or violent speech. Jones, whose posts have been restricted or removed by Apple, Facebook, Google, Spotify and later suspended by Twitter, appealed to Trump to make censorship a central election issue and to highlight alleged Chinese interference. Trump echoed this, suggesting focus should shift from Russia to China. The president’s comments continue a pattern of accusing tech firms of bias, echoing earlier conservative claims of “shadow banning.” While Trump attacks mainstream news as “fake,” he has previously threatened press freedoms and pledged to “open up” libel laws, which are state‑level statutes. END OF SUMMARY.
