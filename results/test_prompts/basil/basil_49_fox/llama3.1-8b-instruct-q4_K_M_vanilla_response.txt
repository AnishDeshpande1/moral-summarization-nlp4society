SUMMARY:

Colorado's top law enforcement official, Attorney General John Suthers, is defending the state's marijuana law against a lawsuit filed by Nebraska and Oklahoma. The two states claim that Colorado's legalization of recreational marijuana is allowing the drug to flow freely into their territories, undermining their anti-marijuana laws. They argue that this violates federal law and the Constitution's supremacy clause. However, Suthers says the lawsuit lacks merit, as it focuses on non-enforcement of federal laws rather than choices made by Colorado voters.

The suit claims that Colorado's marijuana has strained Nebraska and Oklahoma's finances and legal systems, but there is no concrete evidence to support this claim. The two states have been critical of US Attorney General Eric Holder for not enforcing federal law in Colorado. If the Supreme Court accepts the case, it could set a precedent for other states with similar laws.

END OF SUMMARY.
