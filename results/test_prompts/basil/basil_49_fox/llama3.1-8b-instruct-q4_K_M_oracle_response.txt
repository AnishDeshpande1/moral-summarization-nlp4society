SUMMARY:

Colorado's Attorney General John Suthers defends the state's law legalizing marijuana, saying it has merit despite neighboring states' concerns about its flow into their territories, which they claim undermines their efforts to enforce anti-marijuana laws. Nebraska and Oklahoma filed a lawsuit seeking a court order to prevent Colorado from enforcing Amendment 64, approved by voters in 2012, arguing that the measure violates federal law and therefore the Constitution's supremacy clause. The states claim that Colorado's nonenforcement of federal marijuana ban has allowed the drug to flow freely into their territories, straining their finances and legal systems. Nebraska Attorney General Jon Bruning blamed U.S. Attorney General Eric Holder for failing to enforce federal laws in Colorado. The lawsuit asserts that Colorado's marijuana flows undermine the efforts of neighboring states to enforce their own anti-marijuana laws, and it is "overdue" for other states to take similar action. 

END OF SUMMARY.
