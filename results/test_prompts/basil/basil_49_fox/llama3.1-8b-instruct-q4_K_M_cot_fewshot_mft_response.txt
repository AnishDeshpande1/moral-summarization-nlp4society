SUMMARY:
Colorado's top law enforcement official, Attorney General John Suthers, has vowed to "vigorously defend" the state's historic law legalizing marijuana after Nebraska and Oklahoma asked the U.S. Supreme Court to declare it unconstitutional. The two states claim that Colorado's legalization is "undermining" their efforts to enforce anti-marijuana laws and "injuring" neighboring states by allowing the free flow of pot into their territories. However, Suthers argues that the lawsuit is without merit, stating that it stems from non-enforcement of federal laws regarding marijuana rather than choices made by Colorado voters. The lawsuit claims that Colorado's sales have strained Nebraska and Oklahoma's finances and legal systems, but experts argue that it's too early to know how the Supreme Court might handle the case. Colorado Attorney Brian Vicente calls the challenge "political grandstanding" without merit, pointing out that 23 states have enacted medical marijuana laws without being overturned by federal law.
