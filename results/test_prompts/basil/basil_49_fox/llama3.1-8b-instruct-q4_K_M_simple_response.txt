SUMMARY:

Nebraska and Oklahoma have filed a lawsuit against Colorado in the US Supreme Court, challenging the state's law legalizing recreational marijuana. They claim that Colorado-grown pot is flowing into their states, undermining their anti-marijuana laws and straining their finances and legal systems. Colorado Attorney General John Suthers defends the law, saying it was approved by voters and that neighboring states' concerns are unfounded. The lawsuit argues that federal law supersedes state law, making Colorado's measure unconstitutional.

The issue has sparked debate among law enforcement officials, with some supporting the lawsuit while others see it as "political grandstanding." Colorado has raised over $60 million in taxes from marijuana sales, but neighboring states claim they are shouldering the costs of enforcing anti-marijuana laws. The case highlights the tension between state and federal laws on marijuana use.

END OF SUMMARY.
