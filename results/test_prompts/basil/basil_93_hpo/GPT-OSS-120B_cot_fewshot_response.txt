**SUMMARY:**  
President Trump’s weekend “compromise” offering a three‑year DACA extension and a three‑year TPS extension for 300,000 Central Americans was hailed by some as a “wish” toward the center. However, the Senate‑Republican bill that carries the offer adds a series of “hard‑liners” provisions: it would **gut** existing asylum protections for children and trafficking victims, **ban** asylum for Central American minors, **cap** annual asylum applications for those minors at 50,000 and overall at 15,000, and **limit** future TPS cases while expanding detention. Critics called the added measures “poison” **pill**‑filled, accusing Trump of **shifting blame** and labeling the plan **obvious**, **predictable**, and **pathetic**. Immigration advocates described the changes as **extreme** and said they left them **alarmed**. With the Supreme Court’s inaction on DACA leaving Dreamer protections intact, Democrats see little incentive to back a proposal riddled with such moral compromises.
