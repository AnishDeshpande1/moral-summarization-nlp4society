SUMMARY: President Trump’s weekend “compromise” – extending DACA and temporary protected status for a few hundred thousand immigrants in exchange for border‑wall money – was hailed by some as a centrist move. In reality, the Republican‑drafted bill that carries his offer is a hard‑line wish list: it gutts asylum protections for children and trafficking victims, bans asylum for minors at ports of entry, caps Central‑American minor asylum claims at 50,000 and overall grants at 15,000, eliminates future TPS, and expands detention. Democrats dismissed the deal as insufficient and demanded a government reopening before any border talks. Critics argue the “offer” is less about compromise and more about shifting blame and punishing vulnerable migrants, especially unaccompanied children. Even some conservatives warn the asylum cuts are “the most extreme changes I’ve ever seen.” With the Supreme Court’s inaction on DACA leaving Dreamer protections intact, Democratic incentive to trade votes for Trump’s limited extensions wanes, making the bill’s chances in the Senate bleak. The moral charge of the article portrays the proposal as a betrayal of humanitarian values in favor of immigration hard‑liners. END OF SUMMARY.
