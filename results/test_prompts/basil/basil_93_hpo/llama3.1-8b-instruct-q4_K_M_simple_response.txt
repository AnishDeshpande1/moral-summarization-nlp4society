SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump's proposal for temporary protections for immigrants in exchange for border wall funding has been met with skepticism by Democrats, who consider it a non-compromise effort to shift blame rather than find a solution. The proposal, which includes a three-year extension of the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program and temporary protected status for 300,000 people from El Salvador, Haiti, Nicaragua, and Honduras, also contains provisions that gut existing asylum laws, ban asylum for minors who present themselves at ports of entry, and expand immigration detention. These measures have been criticized by immigration activists and even some conservatives as an attempt to restrict access to asylum and limit the rights of immigrants. The proposal's passage is unlikely in the Senate, where it requires 60 votes, and the Supreme Court's decision not to act on the administration's effort to end DACA has further reduced Democrats' incentive to agree to Trump's offer.

END OF SUMMARY.
