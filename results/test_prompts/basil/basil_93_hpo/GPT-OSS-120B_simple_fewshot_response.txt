SUMMARY:
President Trump’s weekend “compromise” – a three‑year extension for DACA recipients and a similar stretch for 300,000 TPS holders – was hailed by some as a centrist move. In reality, the Senate Republican bill that carries the offer is a hard‑line wish list that gut‑s the very protections it pretends to trade. It would slash asylum rights for Central American children, capping annual applications at 50,000 and approvals at 15,000, ban minors who present at ports of entry from seeking asylum, and impose permanent bans for frivolous claims. The legislation also dismantles future Temporary Protected Status, expands detention, and adds “poison pills” that make the deal unpalatable to Democrats. Critics argue the proposal is less about compromise and more about shifting blame and appeasing immigration hard‑liners like Stephen Miller, especially as the Supreme Court’s inaction leaves DACA protections intact, removing any incentive for Democrats to negotiate. The bill faces near‑certain defeat in the Senate.  
END OF SUMMARY.
