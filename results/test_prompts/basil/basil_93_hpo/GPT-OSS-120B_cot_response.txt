**SUMMARY:**  
President Donald Trump offered Democrats a “*compromise*” – a three‑year extension of DACA and temporary protected status – in exchange for funding a border wall, a move portrayed by some as a shift “*toward the center*.” However, the Senate Republican bill that incorporates the offer adds a string of “*hard‑liners*” provisions: it would *gut* existing asylum protections for children and trafficking victims, *ban* asylum for Central American minors, cap yearly asylum grants, and impose harsh penalties for *frivolous* claims. Critics called the added measures “*poison pills*,” saying the proposal is less about a deal and more about shifting *blame*, describing it as “*predictable*” and “*pathetic*.” Even conservative voices expressed alarm at the *extreme* changes. With the Supreme Court’s inaction on DACA leaving Dreamers’ protections intact, Democrats have little incentive to accept a deal riddled with these morally charged provisions.
