SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump's latest immigration proposal, which was initially seen as a compromise, has been met with criticism from Democrats and immigration advocates. The proposal would grant temporary protections to certain immigrants in exchange for funding for a border wall, but it also includes provisions that gut asylum laws for children and trafficking victims, ban asylum for minors at ports of entry, and expand immigration detention. These measures have been described as "poison pills" by some critics. The bill was introduced by Senate Republicans on Monday and is expected to face opposition from Democrats, who argue that it does not go far enough in addressing the needs of immigrants. The Supreme Court's decision not to act on Trump's effort to end DACA also reduces the incentive for Democrats to agree to his proposal.

END OF SUMMARY.
