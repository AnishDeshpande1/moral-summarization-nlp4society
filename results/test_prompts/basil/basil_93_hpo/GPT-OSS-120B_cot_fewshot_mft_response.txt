**SUMMARY:**  
President Trump’s weekend “compromise” offering a three‑year extension of DACA and TPS was hailed by some as a move toward the center, but the Senate GOP bill that follows adds a string of “hard‑liners” provisions that turn the offer into a “wish‑list.” The legislation would **gut** existing asylum protections for children, **ban** asylum for Central‑American minors, **cap** annual asylum grants at 15,000 and limit Central‑American minor applications to 50,000, and impose **stiffer**, even **permanent** **ineligibility** for “frivolous” claims. It also expands **detention** and **limits** future **temporary‑protected‑status** cases. Critics call the added measures **poison‑pills**, **obvious**, **predictable**, and **pathetic**, accusing the administration of **shifting blame** rather than seeking a genuine deal. Immigration advocates stress that these changes **harm** vulnerable children and **undermine** the **protective** framework that keeps them safe from **deportation**. With the Supreme Court’s inaction on DACA leaving Dreamer **protections** in place, Democrats see little incentive to support a bill that adds such **harmful** constraints.
