SUMMARY:

President Trump's immigration compromise package, touted as a "compromise" by some outlets, has been revealed to contain provisions that are actually far more extreme and restrictive than initially reported. The bill would gut existing asylum laws for children and trafficking victims from Central America, ban asylum for minors who present themselves at ports of entry, and cap the number of Central American minors allowed to apply for asylum each year. These measures are likely to be dead on arrival in the Senate, given their extreme nature. Immigration activists have condemned the bill as a "poison pill" designed to shift blame rather than negotiate a genuine deal. The move has further strained Trump's efforts to win over Democratic support, and is seen by many as an attempt to impose his hard-line immigration agenda without compromise.
