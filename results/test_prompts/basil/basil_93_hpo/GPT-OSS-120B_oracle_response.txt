SUMMARY: President Donald Trump’s weekend “compromise” offering a three‑year extension of DACA and a temporary protected status for 300,000 Central Americans was framed as a protective proposal, but the accompanying Senate Republican bill adds a series of “gut‑ting” measures. The legislation would ban asylum for minors, cap Central American child applicants at 50,000 and overall asylum grants at 15,000, and expand detention while removing future TPS protections. It also stuffs “poison pills” that shift blame to Democrats and threaten to permanently in‑eligible those who file frivolous claims. Critics say the changes protect no one and merely expand hard‑line immigration policy, while supporters argue the offer protects Dreamers. The bill now faces a likely dead‑on‑arrival vote in the Senate. END OF SUMMARY.
