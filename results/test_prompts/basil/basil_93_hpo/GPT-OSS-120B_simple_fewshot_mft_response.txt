SUMMARY:
President Trump’s weekend “deal” with Democrats—offering three‑year extensions for DACA recipients and for 300,000 Central‑American TPS holders—was hailed by some as a centrist compromise. In reality, the Senate Republican bill that carries the offer adds a string of hard‑line measures that betray the stated goodwill and betray vulnerable people. It gut‑s the asylum safeguards for children and trafficking victims, bans asylum for minors at ports of entry, caps Central‑American child asylum seekers at 50,000 and overall approvals at 15,000, and makes frivolous filings permanently disqualify applicants. The proposal also weakens future TPS authority and expands immigration detention. By loading the deal with “poison pills,” the administration subverts established protections and threatens serious harm to children and families seeking safety, turning a promised concession into a punitive power grab. Critics argue the move is less about negotiation and more about shifting blame while endangering the most vulnerable, exposing the stark clash between authoritarian policy‑making and the moral imperative to care for those fleeing danger.  

END OF SUMMARY.
