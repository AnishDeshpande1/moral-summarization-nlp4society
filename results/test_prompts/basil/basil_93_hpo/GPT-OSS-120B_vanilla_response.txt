SUMMARY: President Donald Trump proposed a deal to Democrats that would extend DACA and Temporary Protected Status (TPS) for three years in exchange for funding a border wall. While some media framed it as a compromise, the accompanying Senate Republican bill adds hard‑line measures: it would dismantle existing asylum protections for children and trafficking victims, ban asylum for Central American minors at ports of entry, cap annual asylum applications for those minors at 50,000 and overall grants at 15,000, eliminate future TPS, and expand immigration detention. The bill also imposes severe penalties for frivolous asylum claims, including permanent ineligibility for future relief. Democrats have rejected the offer, saying it falls short and that the government must be reopened before border talks. The proposal faces steep odds in the Senate (requiring 60 votes) and is further weakened by the Supreme Court’s inaction on ending DACA, which keeps Dreamer protections in place for now. Critics argue the “offer” is less a compromise and more a partisan wish list.  

END OF SUMMARY.
