SUMMARY:

Representative Paul D. Ryan's budget plan, endorsed by Mitt Romney, assumes the same Medicare savings as President Obama's health care law. However, Romney and Ryan have previously criticized these cuts as devastating to older Americans on Medicare. The Obama campaign argues that Romney-Ryan's plan would "end Medicare as we know it" and shift costs to beneficiaries. A Republican TV commercial claims that Obama cut $716 billion from Medicare to pay for Obamacare.

The Congressional Budget Office estimates that Ryan's budget plan includes similar savings, roughly $700 billion, which would be used to shore up Medicare and reduce budget deficits. Democrats argue that Romney-Ryan's plan is hypocritical since they had previously criticized the same cuts in the Affordable Care Act.

Both parties agree on limiting Medicare spending growth to the rate of inflation plus one-half of a percentage point. However, they differ on how to achieve this goal: Romney-Ryan would limit government contributions and allow beneficiaries to buy private insurance, while Obama's plan reduces payments to health care providers and creates a cost control board.

END OF SUMMARY.
