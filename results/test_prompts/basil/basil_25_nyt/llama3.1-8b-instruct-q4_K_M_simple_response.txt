SUMMARY:

A budget plan proposed by Representative Paul D. Ryan assumes the same amount of Medicare savings as President Obama's health care law, despite claims from Romney and Ryan that these cuts would harm millions of older Americans on Medicare. The Obama campaign argues that Ryan's plan "would end Medicare as we know it" and shift costs to beneficiaries. Republicans have countered with a TV commercial accusing Obama of cutting $716 billion from Medicare to pay for Obamacare. However, the Congressional Budget Office reports that Ryan's budget plan includes similar savings, approximately $700 billion from 2013 to 2022.

The debate centers on how to achieve fiscal solvency and reduce deficit spending. Romney and Ryan propose limiting government contributions to a fixed amount per beneficiary, allowing them to buy private insurance or contribute to traditional Medicare. In contrast, the Affordable Care Act reduces projected payments to healthcare providers and creates a cost control board to ensure savings.

END OF SUMMARY.
