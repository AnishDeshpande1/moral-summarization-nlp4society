SUMMARY:  
Representative Paul D. Ryan’s budget blueprint assumes the same $716 billion in Medicare savings that the Obama health law claims will **cut** and **shift** money away from beneficiaries, a move Republicans say will **save**, **protect** and **strengthen** Medicare while Democrats warn it could **devastate** older Americans and **end** the program as they know it. The Obama campaign calls the Romney‑Ryan ad that blames Obama for the **raid** on Medicare **dishonest** and **hypocritical**, insisting the **squeezed** savings do not **cut** any guaranteed benefit and would instead **offset** the cost of covering the uninsured and **improve** the trust‑fund **solvency**. Ryan argues his plan will **shore** up Medicare and **reduce** deficits, while Romney pledges to **restore** the funding **raided** by the Affordable Care Act and to **repeal** its expansions, which Democrats say would **wipe** out new prescription‑drug and preventive‑service benefits. Both parties claim their approach will **tackle** fiscal challenges without **rationing** care, but they differ on whether to **repeal** the law’s cost‑control board or keep it as a safeguard.  

END OF SUMMARY.
