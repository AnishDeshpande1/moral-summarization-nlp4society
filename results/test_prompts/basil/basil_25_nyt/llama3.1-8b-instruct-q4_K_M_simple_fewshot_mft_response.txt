SUMMARY:

The partisan battle over Medicare continues as Representative Paul Ryan's budget plan assumes the same amount of Medicare savings as President Obama's health care law, despite Republican claims that these cuts would devastate millions on Medicare. The Obama campaign argues that Mr. Ryan's budget plan "would end Medicare as we know it" and shift costs to beneficiaries. Republicans counter with a TV ad asserting that the Romney-Ryan Medicare plan is better for older Americans. However, Democrats point out that the Romney campaign's proposal is similar to Mr. Ryan's and would achieve savings through similar means. The debate centers on how to preserve Medicare while addressing budget deficits. The Obama administration views its health care law as essential to preserving Medicare, while Republicans argue that it raids Medicare funds to pay for new benefits.

The moral framing of this debate pits the Republican promise to "save" and "protect" Medicare against the Democratic accusation of "raiding" and "ending" the program. Authority/Subversion is at play as both sides claim their plan is the best way to preserve Medicare, while Care/Harm is a concern as seniors' access to healthcare is on the line. Fairness/Cheating is also relevant as both parties accuse each other of prioritizing partisan interests over the well-being of Medicare beneficiaries.

END OF SUMMARY.
