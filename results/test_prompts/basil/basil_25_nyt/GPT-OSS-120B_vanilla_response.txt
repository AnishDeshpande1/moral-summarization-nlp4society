SUMMARY: Representative Paul Ryan’s budget plan, endorsed by Mitt Romney, assumes Medicare savings comparable to those projected from the Affordable Care Act (ACA), despite claims from the GOP that such cuts would harm seniors. The Obama campaign countered that the Ryan‑Romney plan would “end Medicare as we know it” and shift costs to beneficiaries, accusing the Republican ad of dishonesty. Ryan argues his proposal protects and strengthens Medicare while reducing deficits, using the same $700 billion in savings the ACA claims to generate. The plan, approved by the House Budget Committee, assumes repeal of ACA expansions, which Democrats say would also eliminate new Medicare benefits like prescription‑drug coverage. Both parties agree on limiting Medicare spending growth to the rate of per‑capita economic growth plus 0.5%, but differ on methods: Republicans favor fixed contributions and private‑insurance options, while the ACA relies on a cost‑control board and cuts to providers. The debate centers on whether the savings truly preserve Medicare or constitute cuts to benefits.  
END OF SUMMARY.
