**SUMMARY:**  
Representative Paul D. Ryan’s budget blueprint assumes Medicare savings equal to those in President Obama’s health‑care law, even though Ryan and Mitt Romney have warned those cuts would be **devastating** for millions of older Americans. The Obama campaign called the Romney‑Ryan plan a **dishonest** and **hypocritical** attack, saying it would **cut** a **guaranteed** benefit and constitute a **raid** on Medicare to fund “Obamacare.” Republicans replied with a TV ad claiming the plan would **protect** and **strengthen** Medicare, restoring money taken by the Affordable Care Act. Both sides argue the same overall **savings**—about $700 billion—are needed to address **deficits** and keep the Medicare trust fund solvent. The debate centers on whether to **restore** funding by repealing the ACA’s expansions or to rely on new cost‑control measures.
