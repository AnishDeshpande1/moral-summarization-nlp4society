SUMMARY:
The article frames the Medicare fight as a moral showdown over the health of older Americans. Republicans, led by Rep. Paul Ryan and backed by Mitt Romney, claim their budget will “protect” Medicare, accusing President Obama of stealing $716 billion to fund Obamacare and warning that the GOP plan would “end Medicare as we know it.” The Obama campaign rebuts, calling the Republican ads “dishonest and hypocritical” and insisting the savings do not cut any guaranteed benefits. Both sides agree on the need to limit Medicare spending growth, but they differ on methods: Republicans propose a fixed‑payment system and want to dismantle the ACA’s cost‑control board, while Democrats argue the ACA’s savings come from cuts to new benefits like prescription‑drug coverage. The piece underscores the moral charge each side levies—Republicans painting Obama as a thief of seniors’ contributions, Democrats portraying GOP cuts as a threat to seniors’ health security. END OF SUMMARY.
