**SUMMARY:**  
The partisan fight over Medicare intensified as Rep. Paul Ryan’s budget, which assumes the same $700‑billion savings as Obama’s health law, was attacked by the Obama campaign as “dishonest” and “hypocritical.”  The campaign warned that the Ryan‑Romney plan would “end Medicare as we know it,” shifting costs to beneficiaries and constituting a “raid” on a “guaranteed” entitlement.  In response, Republicans aired a TV ad claiming Obama has turned Medicare into a “piggy bank” for Obamacare, and pledged to “protect” and “strengthen” Medicare while “restoring” funding.  Both sides argue their approach will “save” Medicare, but critics label the Ryan plan “devastating” to older Americans, accusing it of “blame” and “attack” tactics and calling the claim that Obama’s law “cuts” Medicare “wrong.”  The debate centers on how to achieve comparable savings without harming beneficiaries. END OF SUMMARY.
