SUMMARY:

Representative Paul D. Ryan's budget plan assumes the same amount of Medicare savings as President Obama's health care law, despite claims that these cuts would be devastating to millions of older Americans on Medicare. The Obama campaign argues that Mr. Ryan's plan would shift costs to beneficiaries and end Medicare as we know it, while the Republicans claim their plan is better for older Americans. However, the Congressional Budget Office reports that both plans rely on similar savings, with Mr. Ryan's plan using the money to shore up Medicare and reduce budget deficits, while Mr. Obama's plan uses it to offset the cost of covering the uninsured and improve the financial condition of the Medicare trust fund. Democrats accuse Republicans of hypocrisy for criticizing Mr. Obama's cuts while including similar ones in their own budget plan. The Romney campaign claims they would restore the funding being squeezed out of Medicare by the 2010 health law.

END OF SUMMARY.
