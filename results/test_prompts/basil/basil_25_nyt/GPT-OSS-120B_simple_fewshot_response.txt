SUMMARY:
The latest Medicare showdown underscores the Republican moral narrative that the Obama administration is stealing seniors’ hard‑earned benefits to fund “Obamacare.”  A new Ryan‑Romney TV spot warns that “Obama has cut $716 billion from Medicare” and promises to “restore the funding” for retirees.  While the Obama campaign brands the ad “dishonest and hypocritical,” pointing out that Ryan’s own budget assumes the same $700 billion in savings and would shift costs to beneficiaries, Republicans argue they are the party protecting Medicare.  Ryan says his plan will “save, protect and strengthen” the program by using the savings to shore up its solvency and reduce deficits, whereas Obama says the cuts are needed to cover the uninsured and improve the Medicare trust fund.  Both sides agree on limiting per‑beneficiary spending growth, but differ on methods: Republicans favor a fixed‑contribution model and the abolition of the ACA’s cost‑control board, while Democrats rely on the board’s automatic cuts.  The moral framing pits GOP’s claim of defending seniors against Democrats’ accusation that the Republican plan would “end Medicare as we know it.”
END OF SUMMARY.
