SUMMARY:
Donald Trump announced he is withdrawing as moderator of the Newsmax‑sponsored Republican debate scheduled for Dec. 27, stating he will not sacrifice his “right to run as an independent” should the party pressure him to do so. He framed the decision as a principled move to keep his options open and protect the integrity of the Republican contest, while still vowing to endorse the candidate he deems best. Trump noted that the debate would have been the most watched and substantive of the cycle, but most GOP hopefuls have already declined to attend, leaving only Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum willing to appear. Despite earlier hints that the forum might be cancelled, Trump maintained that it would proceed until his recent withdrawal. By stepping down, he positions himself as a guardian of voter choice and the party’s future, refusing to be constrained by party demands.

END OF SUMMARY.
