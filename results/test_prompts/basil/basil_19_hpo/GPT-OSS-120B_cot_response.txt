Donald Trump announced Tuesday that he is pulling out of his own Dec. 27 debate, saying he will not serve as moderator of the Newsmax forum. He claimed the decision protects his “right” to run as an “independent” candidate, rejecting Republican Party pressure to abandon that status. Trump had earlier promoted the event as the “most watched” and “most substantive” debate, but most Republican hopefuls declined to attend, leaving only Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum. The move drew the usual share of public “ridicule,” especially after Trump hinted he might cancel the debate altogether. Despite mixed signals, he continued to say the forum was proceeding until his final withdrawal.
