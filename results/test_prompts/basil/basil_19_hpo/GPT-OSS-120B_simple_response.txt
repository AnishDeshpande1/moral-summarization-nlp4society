SUMMARY: Donald Trump announced on Tuesday that he is withdrawing from the Dec. 27 Newsmax debate he had originally agreed to moderate. In a statement he claimed the debate would have been “the most watched…most substantive and interesting,” but said he must step back to preserve his “right to run as an independent,” a right he says the Republican Party is trying to strip from him. The move follows a chaotic week in which Trump first partnered with the conservative outlet to host a forum for the remaining GOP presidential hopefuls, only to see most candidates—except Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum—refuse to attend. His vacillation, public ridicule, and promise to endorse whichever candidate he liked best have drawn criticism for turning a political process into a personal publicity stunt and for undermining party unity. By canceling his own debate, Trump appears to prioritize his own political ambitions over the democratic need for a substantive, inclusive discussion among candidates. END OF SUMMARY.
