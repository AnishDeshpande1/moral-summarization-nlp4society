SUMMARY:
Donald Trump announced he is withdrawing as moderator of the Dec. 27 Newsmax Republican debate, claiming he must preserve his “right to run as an independent” after the party allegedly pressured him to give it up. By pulling out, Trump frames his exit as a stand against a party that would force him to sacrifice his political freedom, suggesting the GOP is trying to cheat the fair competition among candidates. The move comes after most hopefuls refused to attend, leaving only Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum, and after Trump had earlier boasted about possibly running as a Republican. Critics mocked his moderation plan, but Trump insists he will still endorse the “right” candidate, positioning his withdrawal as a defense of fairness in the nomination process rather than a personal retreat. The article highlights how Trump portrays the party’s demands as an unfair attempt to limit his options and manipulate the primary’s integrity.  
END OF SUMMARY.
