SUMMARY: Former President Donald Trump announced he will not moderate the Newsmax-hosted debate scheduled for Dec. 27, citing a desire to preserve his right to run as an independent candidate—a right he says the Republican Party is demanding he relinquish. Initially, Trump had partnered with Newsmax to hold a forum for remaining Republican presidential hopefuls, but most candidates declined to attend, leaving only Newt Gingrich and Rick Santorum willing to appear. Trump faced ridicule over his proposed role as moderator and his promise to endorse his preferred candidate during the event. After mixed signals about possibly canceling the debate, he confirmed on Tuesday that he is withdrawing from the moderator position. END OF SUMMARY.
