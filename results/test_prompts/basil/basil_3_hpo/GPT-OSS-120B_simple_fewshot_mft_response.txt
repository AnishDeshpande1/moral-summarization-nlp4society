SUMMARY:
Rep. Bob Etheridge (D‑N.C.) reacted aggressively when an anonymous “student” asked on a Washington street whether he “fully supported the Obama agenda.” In the posted video Etheridge knocks the camera down, grabs the reporter’s wrist and neck, and repeatedly demands identification, prompting criticism that he subverted the authority expected of a congressman. Commentators at Big Government framed the incident as evidence of a progressive majority abusing power, while Mediaite noted the potential harm caused by the physical assault and the ambiguous motives behind the ambush. A DNC spokesman accused Republicans of orchestrating a “tracking operation,” suggesting a cheating of fair political discourse by hiding the interviewers’ identities. Etheridge later issued an apology, expressing regret for the harmful response and pledging respect for civil dialogue, thereby acknowledging the breach of both authority and care responsibilities and the need for fairness in public interactions. END OF SUMMARY.
