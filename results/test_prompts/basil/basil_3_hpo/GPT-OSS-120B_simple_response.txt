SUMMARY:
Rep. Bob Etheridge (D‑N.C.) was filmed violently rebuffing an anonymous “student” reporter who asked if he “fully supported the Obama agenda.” Etheridge knocked the camera to the ground, seized the interviewee’s wrist and demanded identification, prompting accusations that he assaulted a young citizen. Conservative blog Big Government used the clip to denounce what it called a “hard, progressive left” that has forced Democrats to enact an unpopular “fantasy grab‑bag” of legislation, warning of recession, rising taxes and a maxed‑out national credit card. The post framed Etheridge’s outburst as evidence of a corrupt, elitist government losing touch with ordinary Americans and urged activists to target such officials. Mediaite offered a more measured view, noting the provocation of the “inflammatory” interview technique and the anonymity of the interviewers, while suggesting the incident reflected the new reality of ambush journalism on Capitol Hill. The DNC labeled the video a Republican “tracking operation,” citing the blurred faces as proof of a partisan “gotcha” campaign. Etheridge later issued an apology, expressing regret and pledging to uphold civil discourse.  
END OF SUMMARY.
