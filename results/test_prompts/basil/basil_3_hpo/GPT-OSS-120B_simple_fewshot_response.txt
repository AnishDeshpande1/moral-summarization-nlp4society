SUMMARY:
In a viral video, Rep. Bob Etheridge (D‑N.C.) reacted violently when an anonymous “student” asked on a public sidewalk whether he “fully supported the Obama agenda.” Etheridge knocked the camera to the ground, seized the reporter’s wrist and repeatedly demanded identification, eventually grabbing the young man’s neck. Conservative outlets framed the incident as evidence of a “hard, progressive left” that has driven the Democratic majority to enact unpopular, “fantasy” legislation while the nation teeters toward recession. A post on the right‑leaning blog Big Government called the congressman a “living extension of James Madison” turned “assault‑prone” and warned of a “long, hot summer” of political retribution. Mediaite noted the interview’s “ambush” style and suggested Etheridge’s response, though unseemly, was understandable given the provocative tactics. The DNC’s Brad Woodhouse labeled the video a Republican “tracking operation,” pointing to the blurred faces as proof of a partisan hatchet job. Etheridge later issued an apology, saying he “deeply and profoundly regret[ed]” his reaction and pledged to uphold civil discourse. The episode underscores the growing hostility and media‑driven confrontations between Democrats and their opponents.  
END OF SUMMARY.
