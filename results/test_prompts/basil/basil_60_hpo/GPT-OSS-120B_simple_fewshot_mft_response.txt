SUMMARY:
Former Mexican President Vicente Fox bluntly rejected Donald Trump’s demand that Mexico foot the bill for a U.S. border wall, calling it a “fucking wall” that the United States should finance itself. Fox framed Trump’s proposal as an abuse of authority, accusing the GOP hopeful of subverting democratic norms and exploiting his power to extort a foreign nation. By refusing to “pay for” a project that Fox says would harm migrants and waste billions, he appeals to the moral imperative of caring for vulnerable people and protecting them from needless suffering. Fox also labels Trump’s tactics as fundamentally unfair, portraying the wall as a scheme that cheats both Mexican taxpayers and the broader public by shifting costs onto a partner nation while promising false security. Echoing former President Felipe Calderón’s dismissal of the wall as “stupid” and “completely useless,” Fox warns that Trump’s rhetoric endangers both nations and urges Hispanic voters to see him as a “false prophet” whose promises are deceptive and harmful. The interview underscores a moral critique of Trump’s authoritarian posture, his disregard for human welfare, and his unfair exploitation of Mexico’s resources.

END OF SUMMARY.
