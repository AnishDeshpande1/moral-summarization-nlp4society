SUMMARY:
Former Mexican President Vicente Fox rejected Donald Trump's claim that Mexico will pay for a wall along the US border, saying he won't fund it as a taxpayer. Fox called Trump's idea "outrageous" and said the real estate mogul should pay for it himself. He also expressed skepticism about Trump winning the presidency, calling him a "crazy person" who doesn't understand the world today.

Fox was joined in his criticism by fellow former Mexican President Felipe Calderon, who described the wall as "stupid" and "completely useless." Both men dismissed the idea that Mexico would agree to pay for the wall. Trump has estimated the cost of the wall at $8 billion and claims he will coerce Mexico into paying for it if he's elected.

The comments from Fox and Calderon come after Trump won among Hispanics in the Nevada caucus, although Fox noted that he'd like to know more about who those Hispanic voters were and what they support. Overall, the two former presidents seem confident that Mexico will not pay for a wall under any circumstances.
