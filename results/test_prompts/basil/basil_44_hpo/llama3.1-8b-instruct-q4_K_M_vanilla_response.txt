SUMMARY:

Rep. Michael Grimm (R-N.Y.) is expected to be indicted by the U.S. attorney for the Eastern District of New York on charges including mail fraud, wire fraud, and campaign finance irregularities. Grimm's lawyer claims that the government has pursued a "politically driven vendetta" against his client and asserts Grimm's innocence. The investigation began two years ago over allegations of improper donations during Grimm's first congressional campaign. Recently, Grimm was in the news for physically threatening a reporter in the Capitol. The indictment is expected to focus on Grimm's conduct at a health food restaurant he owned after leaving the FBI in 2006. Grimm's lawyer says his client will continue to serve his constituents despite the expected charges and predicts that "when the dust settles, he will be vindicated."
