SUMMARY: Rep. Michael Grimm (R‑NY) is expected to be indicted by the U.S. Attorney for the Eastern District of New York, according to his lawyer, William McGinley. The Justice Department plans to file criminal charges, likely including mail and wire fraud related to a health‑food restaurant Grimm owned after leaving the FBI in 2006. The investigation, which began two years ago over alleged campaign‑finance violations and improper donations, has expanded amid other controversies, such as a January incident where Grimm threatened a reporter. Grimm, a Marine veteran and former FBI special agent representing New York’s 11th District, maintains his innocence and vows to continue serving his constituents. Reports indicate he may turn himself in once the indictment is unsealed, and a Washington Post update suggests a secret grand‑jury indictment has already been issued and will be made public soon. END OF SUMMARY.
