SUMMARY: Rep. Michael Grimm (R‑N.Y.) is expected to be indicted on mail and wire fraud charges tied to a health‑food restaurant he owned after leaving the FBI. His lawyer, William McGinley, blasted the Justice Department’s move as a politically motivated vendetta rather than an impartial search for truth, insisting Grimm’s innocence and promising he will be vindicated. Despite the looming indictment, McGinley said Grimm will continue to serve his constituents with the dedication that marked his career as a Marine Corps combat veteran and decorated FBI Special Agent. The investigation, which began two years ago over allegedly erroneous campaign‑finance filings and improper donations, also follows a January incident in which Grimm threatened a reporter in the Capitol. Reports indicate Grimm may turn himself in once the secret grand‑jury indictment is unsealed. END OF SUMMARY.
