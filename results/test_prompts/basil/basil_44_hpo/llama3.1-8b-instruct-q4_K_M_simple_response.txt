SUMMARY: 
Rep. Michael Grimm (R-N.Y.) is expected to be indicted on charges of mail fraud, wire fraud, and campaign finance irregularities related to his ownership of a health food restaurant in Manhattan after leaving the FBI. His lawyer claims that the government's decision is "politically driven" and not an independent search for truth. The investigation began two years ago over allegations of improper donations during Grimm's first congressional campaign. The congressman has been accused of physical intimidation towards a reporter in January, but his lawyer maintains that he will continue to serve his constituents with dedication.

The indictment is expected to be unsealed soon, and Grimm may turn himself in once it is. His lawyer asserts that Grimm's innocence will be vindicated when the dust settles. The congressman's actions have been under scrutiny, raising questions about the integrity of public officials and their accountability to the law.
