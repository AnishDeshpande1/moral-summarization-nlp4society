SUMMARY:
The NAACP adopted a resolution condemning “racist elements” and bigotry within the Tea Party movement, urging its leaders to repudiate racism, antisemitism, homophobia and other bigoted statements. NAACP President Benjamin Todd Jealous stressed that the organization supports freedom of assembly but cannot tolerate the Tea Party’s tolerance of bigotry or its attempt to push the country back toward a pre‑civil‑rights era. The resolution references a March incident in which Tea Party protesters allegedly hurled racial epithets at black lawmakers, an episode the Tea Party denies, claiming no proof and accusing the NAACP of playing the race card. In response, St. Louis Tea Party activists denounced the NAACP as a partisan, bigoted attack dog, alleging the civil‑rights group has become a harmful political organization that fails to address crime, poverty and the destruction wrought by segregation and oppression in African‑American communities. Both sides exchanged accusations of false, inflammatory rhetoric, with the Tea Party demanding the NAACP withdraw its resolution and urging the IRS to reconsider its tax‑exempt status. The NAACP’s final wording, pending board approval in October, calls on all people of good will to repudiate the dangerous racist element within the movement.  
END OF SUMMARY.
