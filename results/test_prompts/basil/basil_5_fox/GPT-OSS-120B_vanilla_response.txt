SUMMARY: The NAACP adopted a resolution at its Kansas City convention condemning “racist elements” within the Tea Party and urging its leaders to repudiate bigotry. The original draft harshly criticized the movement, but was softened to call on Tea Party members to reject racism, anti‑Semitism, homophobia and other forms of hate while affirming the right to free assembly. NAACP President Benjamin Todd Jealous said the issue is the Tea Party’s tolerance of bigotry, citing a March incident where protesters allegedly shouted racial slurs at Black lawmakers on Capitol Hill—though no visual evidence has been presented. Tea Party representatives, including Dallas founder Phillip Dennis and St. Louis organizers, dismissed the accusations, called the NAACP’s move a political “race card,” and threatened to challenge its tax‑exempt status. The St. Louis Tea Party drafted its own resolution condemning the NAACP as a partisan “bigoted” group. The NAACP’s final wording, pending board approval in October, will ask the Tea Party and others to repudiate any racist elements. The exchange highlights escalating tensions between the civil‑rights organization and the Tea Party over allegations of racism and political motives. END OF SUMMARY.
