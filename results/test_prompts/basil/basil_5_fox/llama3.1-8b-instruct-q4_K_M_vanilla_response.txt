SUMMARY:
The NAACP adopted a resolution condemning the Tea Party movement for tolerating bigotry, despite claims from Tea Partiers that it's a political ploy. The resolution was passed unanimously at the NAACP's annual convention in Kansas City, Mo., and asks Tea Party leaders to repudiate bigots within their ranks. NAACP President Benjamin Todd Jealous stated that they take no issue with the Tea Party movement itself, but rather its tolerance for racist and bigoted statements. This move comes after an incident in March where black lawmakers were allegedly hurled racial epithets at by Tea Party protesters on Capitol Hill.

Tea Party members have challenged these claims, with some accusing the NAACP of playing the "race card" and being irrelevant. The St. Louis Tea Party has drafted a resolution condemning the NAACP for its actions, calling them bigoted and partisan. The debate between the two groups highlights the controversy surrounding the Tea Party movement's handling of racist incidents.

END OF SUMMARY.
