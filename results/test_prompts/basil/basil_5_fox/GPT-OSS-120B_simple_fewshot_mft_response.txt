SUMMARY:
The NAACP voted unanimously to adopt a resolution condemning “racist elements” within the Tea Party, urging its leaders to repudiate bigotry and accept the responsibility that comes with their growing influence. President Benjamin Todd Jealous framed the call as a matter of care for marginalized groups and fairness toward all citizens, insisting the movement must ban racism, anti‑Semitaism, homophobia and other forms of hate. He also warned that tolerating such attitudes subverts the moral authority of a democratic society. Tea Party representatives, including Dallas founder Phillip Dennis and St. Louis activists, rejected the accusation, labeling the NAACP’s move a partisan “race‑card” tactic and an unjust attack that undermines the Party’s authority. They argued the Tea Party upholds freedom of assembly and fiscal responsibility, and they demanded the civil‑rights group withdraw the resolution, even threatening an IRS review of its tax‑exempt status. Both sides claim moral high ground: the NAACP stresses protecting vulnerable communities and equitable treatment, while the Tea Party stresses defending legitimate political authority and rejecting what it sees as unfair, baseless allegations. END OF SUMMARY.
