SUMMARY:

The NAACP passed a resolution condemning the Tea Party movement's tolerance for bigotry, racism, antisemitism, homophobia, and other forms of intolerance. The organization called on the Tea Party leaders to repudiate these elements within their ranks. This move comes after an incident in March where black lawmakers were allegedly subjected to racist attacks by Tea Party protesters. NAACP President Benjamin Todd Jealous stated that they take issue with the Tea Party's continued tolerance for bigotry and bigoted statements, which he believes is unacceptable.

The St. Louis Tea Party responded by drafting a resolution condemning the NAACP for its partisan attack on the movement. The resolution accused the NAACP of resorting to political tactics and urged them to withdraw their "bigoted, false and inflammatory" resolution. However, the NAACP's focus remains on highlighting the dangers of racism and intolerance within the Tea Party movement.

The incident has sparked a heated debate between the two groups, with each side accusing the other of pushing an agenda that is harmful to civil rights and the country as a whole. The outcome of this controversy will have far-reaching implications for both organizations.
