**SUMMARY:**  
The NAACP adopted a resolution condemning “racist elements” and “bigotry” within the Tea Party movement, urging its leaders to repudiate racism, anti‑Semitism, homophobia and other bigoted attitudes. NAACP President Benjamin Todd Jealous emphasized support for free assembly but denounced the Tea Party’s tolerance of bigotry and called for a clear stance against racist, anti‑Semitic and homophobic rhetoric. The resolution referenced a March incident in which Tea Party protesters were alleged to have hurled racial epithets at black lawmakers, though Tea Party members dispute the claim and say no visual proof exists. Dallas Tea Party founder Phillip Dennis dismissed the accusations as a “race card” and labeled the NAACP “irrelevant.” The St. Louis Tea Party responded with its own resolution condemning the NAACP as a “bigoted, partisan attack dog” and even urged the IRS to review the NAACP’s tax‑exempt status. Tea Party organizers defended their movement as focused on smaller government and fiscal responsibility, accusing the NAACP of abandoning its civil‑rights mission and becoming a political, bigoted organization.
