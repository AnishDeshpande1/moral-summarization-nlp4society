SUMMARY:
The NAACP’s annual convention adopted a resolution condemning “racist elements” within the Tea Party, urging its leaders to repudiate bigotry—including racism, anti‑Semitism and homophobia—and to acknowledge the responsibility that comes with political influence. While the NAACP president stressed support for free assembly, he warned that tolerance of hate threatens the civil‑rights gains of the past. The resolution references a March Capitol Hill incident in which Tea Party protesters were accused of hurling racial slurs at Black lawmakers, though no visual proof has emerged. Tea Party representatives—such as Dallas founder Phillip Dennis and St. Louis organizers Bill Hennessy and Dana Loesch—denied the accusations, called the NAACP’s move a “race‑card” tactic, and defended the movement as inclusive and focused on limited government. They accused the NAACP of abandoning its civil‑rights mission, becoming partisan, and even threatened to challenge its tax‑exempt status. The dispute highlights a moral clash: the NAACP frames its stance as a defense of equality and a call for the Tea Party to reject hate, while Tea Party leaders portray the resolution as a politically motivated attack that undermines their claimed commitment to freedom and inclusivity.  
END OF SUMMARY.
