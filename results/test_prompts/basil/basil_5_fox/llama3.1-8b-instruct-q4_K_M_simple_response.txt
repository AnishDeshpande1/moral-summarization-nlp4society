SUMMARY:

The NAACP passed a resolution condemning racist elements in the Tea Party movement, calling on its leaders to repudiate bigotry. The organization cited an incident where Tea Party protesters allegedly hurled racial epithets at black lawmakers on Capitol Hill. Despite claims of no evidence, NAACP President Benjamin Todd Jealous stated that the time has come for the Tea Party to accept responsibility and reject racism, anti-Semitism, homophobia, and other forms of bigotry.

The St. Louis Tea Party responded with a resolution condemning the NAACP for being "bigoted" and "partisan." The group accused the NAACP of playing the "race card" and called it "irrelevant." Both sides traded accusations, with the NAACP maintaining that its goal is to promote civil rights and equality, while the St. Louis Tea Party claimed that the organization has abandoned its mission.

The debate highlights the ongoing controversy surrounding racism in the Tea Party movement, with both sides refusing to back down from their claims.

END OF SUMMARY.
