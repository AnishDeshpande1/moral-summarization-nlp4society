SUMMARY:

The NAACP adopted a resolution condemning the racist elements within the Tea Party movement, calling on its leaders to repudiate bigotry and racism. The organization's president, Benjamin Todd Jealous, stated that while they support freedom of assembly and people raising their voices in democracy, they take issue with the movement's tolerance for bigoted statements and actions. The resolution was sparked by an incident where Tea Party protesters allegedly hurled racial epithets at black lawmakers on Capitol Hill.

Tea Party members have pushed back against these claims, accusing the NAACP of playing the "race card" and being irrelevant. However, the NAACP maintains that it is necessary to educate people about the movement's racist element and activities, which could potentially lead to harm for certain groups. The organization is calling on Tea Partiers to take responsibility for their words and actions, and to repudiate anti-Semitism, homophobia, and other forms of bigotry.

The debate surrounding this issue highlights the ongoing struggle against racism and oppression in American society.
