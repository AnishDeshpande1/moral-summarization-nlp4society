SUMMARY:

The NAACP passed a resolution condemning "racist elements" in the Tea Party movement, calling on its leaders to repudiate bigotry. Despite claims from Tea Partiers that the measure is a political ploy, the NAACP maintains that it aims to hold the movement accountable for tolerating racist and bigoted statements. The resolution comes after an incident where Tea Party protesters allegedly hurled racial epithets at black lawmakers on Capitol Hill. While some Tea Party members have denied any wrongdoing, others have defended themselves against charges of racism. The NAACP's move highlights the importance of accountability in political movements, particularly when they gain influence and power. As President Benjamin Todd Jealous stated, "The time has come for them to accept the responsibility that comes with influence and make clear there is no place for racism and anti-Semitism, homophobia and other forms of bigotry in their movement."
