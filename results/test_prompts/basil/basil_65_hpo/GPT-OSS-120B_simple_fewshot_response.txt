SUMMARY:
At the Democratic National Convention, former Republican mayor and billionaire Michael Bloomberg urged Americans to vote for Hillary Clinton, branding her a “sane, competent person” in stark contrast to Donald Trump, whom he called a “dangerous demagogue” and an incompetent businessman. Bloomberg warned that Trump’s record of bankruptcies, lawsuits and deceptive practices would devastate the economy, small businesses, retirement savings, national security and America’s global standing. He highlighted his own self‑made success and experience running New York City as proof that business acumen can translate into effective leadership, while accusing Trump of hypocrisy, secrecy about his wealth and an alarming ego. Bloomberg’s unscripted remarks framed the election as a moral choice between responsible, experienced governance under Clinton and the reckless, self‑serving chaos of a Trump presidency. END OF SUMMARY.
