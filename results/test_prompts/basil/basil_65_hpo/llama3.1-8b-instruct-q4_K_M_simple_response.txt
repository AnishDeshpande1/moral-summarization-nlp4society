SUMMARY:

Michael Bloomberg, former New York mayor and founder of the Bloomberg financial news empire, spoke at the Democratic National Convention, criticizing Republican nominee Donald Trump as a "dangerous demagogue" and a bad businessman who would make a disastrous president. Bloomberg argued that Trump's career is marked by bankruptcies, lawsuits, and missteps, and that his boasts don't match reality. He mocked Trump's business prowess, inheritance from his father, ego, and intellect, calling him out for hypocrisy in claiming great wealth while refusing to disclose his real net worth or release his tax returns.

Bloomberg contrasted Trump with himself, highlighting his own achievements as a successful businessman and politician who ran New York City for 12 years. He made dire predictions about the damage a Trump presidency would cause to the economy, small businesses, retirement savings, and national security. Bloomberg's speech was designed to chafe Trump's nerves, emphasizing that Americans should vote for Hillary Clinton because she is "sane" and "competent." END OF SUMMARY.
