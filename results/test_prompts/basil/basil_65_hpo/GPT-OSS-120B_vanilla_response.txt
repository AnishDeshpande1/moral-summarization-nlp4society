SUMMARY: Former New York mayor and Bloomberg founder Michael Bloomberg urged Americans at the Democratic National Convention to vote for Hillary Clinton, labeling Donald Trump a “dangerous demagogue” and an incompetent businessman. Bloomberg, a billionaire ex‑Republican who endorsed Clinton, criticized Trump’s record of bankruptcies, lawsuits, and alleged hypocrisy over his wealth, warning that Trump’s business‑focused agenda would harm the economy, small businesses, retirement savings, national debt, employment, global influence, and public safety. He contrasted his own self‑made success and experience running New York City with Trump’s inherited wealth and questioned Trump’s immigration, security, and trade policies. Bloomberg, who considered an independent presidential run, dismissed both Trump and Bernie Sanders, positioning Clinton as the sane, competent choice. END OF SUMMARY.
