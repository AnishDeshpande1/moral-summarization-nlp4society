SUMMARY:

Michael Bloomberg, former New York mayor and founder of Bloomberg news empire, spoke at the Democratic National Convention, criticizing Republican nominee Donald Trump as a "dangerous demagogue" and bad businessman who would be a disastrous president. Bloomberg, a billionaire and ex-Republican who has endorsed Hillary Clinton, argued that Trump's career is marked by bankruptcies, lawsuits, and failed business ventures. He mocked Trump's boasts about his wealth, saying the real-estate developer refuses to disclose his true net worth and won't release his tax returns.

Bloomberg contrasted himself with Trump, highlighting his own success in business and politics, which allowed him to run New York City for 12 years. He warned that a Trump presidency would harm small businesses, erode American influence worldwide, and make communities less safe. Bloomberg's speech was seen as an attempt to chafe Trump's rawest nerves and sway undecided voters to support Clinton.
