SUMMARY:  
At the Democratic National Convention, former New York mayor Michael Bloomberg urged Americans to vote for Hillary Clinton, casting the choice as a moral imperative. He portrayed Clinton as a “sane, competent person” contrasted with Donald Trump, whom he labeled a “dangerous demagogue” and an incompetent businessman. Bloomberg warned that Trump’s record of bankruptcies, lawsuits, and alleged fraud reveals a pattern of selfishness and hypocrisy, arguing that a president who runs the nation like a shady private‑enterprise would endanger the economy, retirees, small businesses, and national security. By highlighting Trump’s refusal to disclose his wealth and his ego‑driven rhetoric, Bloomberg framed the election as a test of integrity versus deceit, urging voters to reject the immoral, self‑serving candidate and support the responsible, experienced leadership of Clinton.  

END OF SUMMARY.
