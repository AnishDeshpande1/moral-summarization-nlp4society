SUMMARY:  
At the Democratic National Convention Michael Bloomberg urged Americans to vote for Hillary Clinton, arguing that Donald Trump is a “dangerous demagogue” and a disastrous choice for president. Bloomberg portrayed Trump’s career as a litany of bankruptcies, lawsuits and angry stockholders, saying the businessman “cheated” contractors and “ripped off” customers. He accused Trump of empty boasts and hypocrisy, noting that the candidate refuses to disclose his true net worth or tax returns despite bold claims about his wealth. Bloomberg warned that a Trump administration would cause massive damage: it would make it harder for small businesses to compete, threaten retirement savings, lead to greater debt and unemployment, erode America’s global influence and make communities less safe. By contrast, Bloomberg presented himself as a sane, competent alternative who built a business without a million‑dollar check from his father and used that experience to run New York City for 12 years. He concluded that voters should reject Trump’s false promises and choose Clinton for a stable, responsible future.  
END OF SUMMARY.
