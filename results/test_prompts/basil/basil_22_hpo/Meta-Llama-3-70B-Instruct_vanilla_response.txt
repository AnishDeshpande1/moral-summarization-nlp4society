SUMMARY:

Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and administrators have criticized Representative Paul Ryan's (R-Wisc.) budget, claiming it misuses Catholic social teaching to justify cuts that harm the poor. In a letter, they argue that Ryan's budget contradicts Catholic values, citing the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops' statements that the budget fails to meet moral criteria. The scholars point to the devastating impact of cuts in food programs and Pell Grants, which will affect low-income students. They correct Ryan's use of the Catholic concept of "subsidiarity," emphasizing that it demands higher levels of government provide help when local communities face problems beyond their means. The letter is signed by over a dozen Georgetown Jesuit priests and numerous faculty members from various departments. The scholars urge Ryan to reconsider his budget in light of Catholic social teaching, even assigning him a reading assignment: "The Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church."

END OF SUMMARY.
