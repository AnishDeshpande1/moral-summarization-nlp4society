SUMMARY:
Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty, administrators, and Jesuit priests joined a chorus of Catholic bishops, theologians, and social‑justice leaders in condemning Rep. Paul Ryan’s (R‑Wis.) misuse of Catholic social teaching to defend a budget that they say has a devastating impact on the poor. In a letter sent before Ryan’s Whittington Lecture, the scholars called his claims that the budget reflects Catholic moral principles “nonsense,” arguing that the cuts to food programs and Pell Grants threaten starvation, poverty and hunger. Father Thomas J. Reese warned that Ryan’s budget mirrors the values of “social Darwinists” and “survival of the fittest” rather than the gospel of compassion and love. The letter cited the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops’ “just” criteria, noting that the House‑passed resolution fails to meet them, and clarified that true “subsidiarity” requires higher‑level “subsidium” when communities face economic crises and endemic poverty. The scholars recommended Ryan read the Vatican’s Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church. END OF SUMMARY.
