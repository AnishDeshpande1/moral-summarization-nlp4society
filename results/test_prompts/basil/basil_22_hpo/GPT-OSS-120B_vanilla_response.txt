SUMMARY: Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and administrators, including Jesuit priests and scholars from various departments, sent a letter to Rep. Paul Ryan criticizing his use of Catholic social teaching to defend his budget proposal, which they say harms the poor. Citing the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops, they argue the budget’s cuts to programs such as food assistance and Pell Grants violate Catholic principles of solidarity and subsidiarity, which require higher‑level government support for those in need. The scholars contend Ryan’s references to “subsidiarity” misrepresent the doctrine and accuse him of favoring Ayn Rand’s philosophy over Gospel values. They offered Ryan a reading of the Vatican’s *Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church* and affirmed they do not oppose his speaking at Georgetown’s Whittington Lecture, only his moral justification of the budget. END OF SUMMARY.
