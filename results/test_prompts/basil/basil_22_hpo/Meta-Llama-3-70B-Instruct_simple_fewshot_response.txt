SUMMARY:

Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and administrators have criticized Representative Paul Ryan's (R-Wisc.) misuse of Catholic social teaching to defend his budget, which they argue hurts the poor. In a letter to Ryan, the scholars contend that his budget contradicts Catholic moral teaching, citing the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops' letters to Congress. They argue that Ryan's plan decimates food programs for struggling families, radically weakens protections for the elderly and sick, and gives more tax breaks to the wealthiest few. The scholars correct Ryan's interpretation of the Catholic concept of "subsidiarity," emphasizing that it demands government help when communities face problems beyond their means to address. They also accuse Ryan of reflecting the values of Ayn Rand rather than the Gospel of Jesus Christ. The letter is part of an effort by Catholic leaders to distance the church from the Republican Party and to emphasize that the Catholic faith prioritizes aid to the poor. END OF SUMMARY.
