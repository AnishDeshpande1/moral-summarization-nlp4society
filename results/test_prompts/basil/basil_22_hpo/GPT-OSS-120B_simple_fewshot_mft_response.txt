SUMMARY:
A coalition of nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty, administrators, and Jesuit priests—alongside Catholic bishops and social‑justice leaders—has publicly rebuked Rep. Paul Ryan for invoking Catholic social teaching to defend a federal budget that slashes aid to the poor. In a letter sent before Ryan’s Whittington Lecture, the scholars assert that his claim of “subsidiarity” is a distortion that justifies harmful cuts to food programs, Pell Grants and other essential services, violating the Church’s moral authority. Citing the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops, they argue the budget fails the Church’s criteria for a just society, harming vulnerable populations and degrading the sanctity of the gospel’s call to compassion. Father Thomas J. Reese warns that Ryan’s approach mirrors Ayn Rand’s philosophy rather than Christian doctrine, subverting the Church’s authoritative teachings on care for the needy. The letter urges Ryan to study the Vatican’s Compendium of Social Doctrine, emphasizing that true subsidiarity requires higher‑level government support when local communities cannot meet basic human needs.  

END OF SUMMARY.
