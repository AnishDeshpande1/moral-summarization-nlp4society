SUMMARY:

Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and administrators have criticized Representative Paul Ryan for misusing Catholic social teaching to defend his budget, which hurts the poor. The group, including Jesuit priests and theologians, sent a letter to Ryan, objecting to his claims that his budget is based on Catholic social teaching, calling it "nonsense." They argue that Ryan's budget has a devastating impact on programs for the poor, relying on disproportionate cuts in essential services. The letter quotes the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops, which stated that a just framework for future budgets cannot rely on such cuts. The scholars correct Ryan's use of the Catholic concept of "subsidiarity," pointing out that it demands higher levels of government provide help when local communities face problems beyond their means. The letter concludes that Ryan's budget reflects the values of Ayn Rand rather than the gospel of Jesus Christ, which emphasizes compassion and love. END OF SUMMARY.
