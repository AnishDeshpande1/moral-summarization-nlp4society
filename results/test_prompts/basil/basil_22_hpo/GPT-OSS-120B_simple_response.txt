SUMMARY:  
A coalition of nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty, administrators, and Jesuit priests wrote to Rep. Paul Ryan to condemn his claim that his proposed budget reflects Catholic social teaching. Citing the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops, the scholars argue that Ryan’s cuts to food assistance, Pell Grants, and other anti‑poverty programs betray the Church’s moral mandate to protect the poor and vulnerable. They accuse Ryan of misusing the principle of subsidiarity to justify “gutting” government aid, reminding him that true subsidiarity requires higher‑level support when local communities cannot meet basic needs. Father Thomas J. Reese likens Ryan’s budget to Ayn Rand’s philosophy rather than the gospel of compassion, warning that such policies harm starving families and low‑income students. The letter urges Ryan to read the Vatican’s *Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church* and to reconsider his stance before speaking at Georgetown’s Whittington Lecture.  

END OF SUMMARY.
