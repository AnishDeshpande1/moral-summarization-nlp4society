SUMMARY:  
Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and administrators wrote to Rep. Paul Ryan, accusing him of **misuse** of Catholic social teaching to defend a budget that **hurts** the poor.  In a letter sent before Ryan’s Whittington Lecture, Jesuit Father Thomas J. Reese and other scholars said Ryan’s **comments** that his budget is **based** on Catholic doctrine are **nonsense**.  They cite the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops, which warns that the House‑passed budget **fails** moral criteria because it relies on **cuts** to essential **services** for the poor, producing **devastating** effects on food aid and Pell Grants.  The scholars correct Ryan’s **use** of the principle of “subsidiarity,” noting it calls for higher‑level government **subsidium** when local communities cannot meet crises such as poverty and hunger.  They urge Ryan to read the Vatican’s *Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church* and remind him that the **gospel** calls for **compassion** and **love**, not the “survival of the fittest” ethos of Social Darwinism.  
END OF SUMMARY.
