SUMMARY:
Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty, administrators, and Jesuit priests publicly challenged Rep. Paul Ryan (R‑Wis.) for invoking Catholic social teaching to defend his budget, which they say harms the poor. In a letter sent before Ryan’s Whittington Lecture, the scholars echoed U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops’ warnings that the House‑passed budget fails moral criteria by slashing essential services for low‑income families, cutting food assistance, and reducing Pell Grants. Jesuit Father Thomas J. Reese called Ryan’s claim that his plan reflects Catholic doctrine “nonsense,” likening it to the philosophy of Ayn Rand rather than the gospel of compassion. The Georgetown group clarified the true meaning of “subsidiarity,” emphasizing that higher‑level government must step in when local communities cannot meet crises such as poverty and hunger. They urged Ryan to read the Vatican’s *Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church* to better understand Catholic teachings on the common good. The letter affirms that while the university welcomes Ryan’s speech, his budget proposals contradict the Church’s moral mandate to protect the vulnerable.  

END OF SUMMARY.
