SUMMARY:

Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and administrators have criticized Representative Paul Ryan's misuse of Catholic social teaching to defend his budget, which hurts the poor. The scholars argue that Ryan's budget contradicts Catholic values, citing the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops' letters to Congress, which state that a just budget cannot rely on disproportionate cuts to essential services for the poor. The scholars accuse Ryan of misusing the Catholic concept of "subsidiarity" to justify gutting government programs, when in fact it demands higher levels of government provide help when local communities face problems beyond their means. They also criticize Ryan's claim that his budget is consistent with Catholic moral teaching, saying it reflects the values of Ayn Rand rather than the Gospel of Jesus Christ. The scholars emphasize the devastating impact of Ryan's budget on programs for the poor, including food programs and Pell Grants. By correcting Ryan's misuse of Catholic social teaching, the scholars aim to uphold the authority of Catholic bishops and theologians, prioritize care for the poor, and promote a sanctified approach to budgeting that reflects the values of compassion and love.

END OF SUMMARY.
