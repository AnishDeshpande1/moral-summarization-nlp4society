SUMMARY: 
Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and administrators have called out Representative Paul Ryan for his misuse of Catholic social teaching in defending his budget, which hurts the poor. They argue that his comments misrepresent the true teachings of Catholicism, making claims that are based on a flawed understanding of subsidiarity. The scholars point out that cuts to essential services will devastate programs for the poor and fails to meet moral criteria outlined by the USCCB. Father Thomas J. Reese, one of the organizers, notes that Chairman Ryan's budget reflects Ayn Rand's values rather than the gospel of compassion and love. The Georgetown scholars also correct Mr. Ryan on his use of subsidiarity, stating that it demands higher levels of government provide help when local communities face problems beyond their means. They urge Representative Ryan to study the Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church, which outlines Catholic social teaching, and acknowledge its principles in his decision-making.

END OF SUMMARY.
