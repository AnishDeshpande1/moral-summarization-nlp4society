SUMMARY:

Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and administrators have criticized Representative Paul Ryan's misuse of Catholic social teaching to defend his budget, which they argue hurts the poor. The group, including Jesuit priests and scholars from various departments, sent a letter to Ryan ahead of his lecture on campus, stating that his budget has a "devastating impact" on programs for the poor. They quote the U.S. Conference of Catholic Bishops, which has repeatedly expressed concerns that Ryan's budget fails to meet moral criteria. The scholars argue that Ryan's philosophy is more in line with Ayn Rand's "survival of the fittest" than the gospel of Jesus Christ, which emphasizes compassion and love. They correct Ryan's misunderstanding of the Catholic concept of "subsidiarity," emphasizing that it demands higher levels of government provide help to local communities in need. The scholars urge Ryan to revisit Catholic social teaching, recommending a reading assignment from the Vatican.

END OF SUMMARY.
