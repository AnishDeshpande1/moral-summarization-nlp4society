**SUMMARY:**
Nearly 90 Georgetown faculty, priests, and social‑justice scholars joined a chorus of Catholic bishops to condemn Rep. Paul Ryan’s misuse of Catholic social teaching in defending a budget that they say has a devastating impact on the poor. In a letter sent before Ryan’s Whittington Lecture, the scholars called his claim that the budget reflects Catholic moral criteria “nonsense,” noting that the House‑passed plan fails to protect essential services and instead guts programs that prevent starvation and hunger. They warned that cuts to food aid, Pell Grants, and other safety nets threaten low‑income families, exacerbate poverty, unemployment and economic crisis, and betray the gospel’s call for compassion and love. Citing the Church’s doctrine of subsidiarity, the letter reminded Ryan that higher levels of government must provide “subsidium” when local communities cannot meet basic needs. The scholars urged Ryan to read the Vatican’s Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church and to align his policies with genuine Catholic values rather than the “survival of the fittest” ethos of social‑Darwinist philosophy.
