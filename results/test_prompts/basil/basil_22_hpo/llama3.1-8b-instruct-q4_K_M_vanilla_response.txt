SUMMARY:

A group of nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and administrators have written a letter to Representative Paul Ryan (R-Wisc.) criticizing his misuse of Catholic social teaching in defending his budget, which they say hurts the poor. The scholars argue that Ryan's budget contradicts the principles of Catholic social teaching, particularly with regards to helping the poor. They point out that cuts in programs such as food assistance and Pell Grants will disproportionately affect low-income individuals and communities.

The letter, signed by faculty from various departments, including theology, history, and government, quotes the US Conference of Catholic Bishops' criticism of Ryan's budget. The scholars also correct Ryan on his use of the concept of "subsidiarity," which they say he has misapplied to justify cutting government programs. They suggest that Ryan read the Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church, commissioned by John Paul II and published by the Vatican.

The letter is not objecting to Ryan's appearance on campus but rather his misuse of Catholic teaching to justify his budget.

END OF SUMMARY.
