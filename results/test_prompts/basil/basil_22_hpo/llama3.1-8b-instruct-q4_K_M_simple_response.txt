SUMMARY:

Nearly 90 Georgetown University faculty and administrators have signed a letter criticizing Representative Paul Ryan's use of Catholic social teaching to defend his budget, which they say hurts the poor. They argue that Ryan's budget contradicts the principles of compassion and love outlined in the Gospel of Jesus Christ. The scholars point out that Ryan's budget would cut essential services for the poor, including food programs and Pell Grants, making it difficult for low-income students to pursue their education.

The letter quotes the US Conference of Catholic Bishops, which wrote several letters to Congress criticizing Ryan's budget for its disproportionate cuts in essential services to the poor. The scholars also correct Ryan on his misuse of the Catholic concept of "subsidiarity," noting that it demands higher levels of government provide help when communities face problems beyond their means.

The letter is signed by over a dozen Georgetown Jesuit priests and numerous members from various departments, who urge Ryan to read the Vatican's "Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church" to understand Catholic social teaching correctly.

END OF SUMMARY.
