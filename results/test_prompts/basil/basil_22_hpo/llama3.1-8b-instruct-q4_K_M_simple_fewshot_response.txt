SUMMARY:

Catholic scholars at Georgetown University have criticized Representative Paul Ryan's budget plan, saying it contradicts Catholic social teaching. Nearly 90 faculty members signed a letter to Ryan, citing the devastating impact of his budget on programs for the poor and vulnerable. They argue that Ryan's plan reflects the values of Ayn Rand rather than the Gospel of Jesus Christ. The scholars point out that Ryan misuses Catholic concepts such as "subsidiarity" to justify cutting government programs. In fact, they say, subsidiarity demands that higher levels of government provide help when local communities face problems beyond their means. The letter also references the Vatican's Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church, which was commissioned by John Paul II and published by the Vatican. The scholars call on Ryan to revisit his budget plan and prioritize aid to the poor in line with Catholic moral teaching.

END OF SUMMARY.
