SUMMARY:
In the vice‑presidential debate, Joe Biden revived Lloyd Bentsen’s 1988 playbook, using sharp sarcasm and relentless interruptions to paint Rep. Paul Ryan as a modern‑day Dan Quayle and a political lightweight. Biden’s “Cheshire Cat” grin and frequent laughs turned the stage into a comedy roast, while he dismissed Ryan’s arguments as “malarkey” and mocked his tax‑cut logic by dubbing him “Jack Kennedy.” The former vice president seized the moment to defend President Obama’s agenda, attacking Ryan’s Medicare stance and highlighting perceived hypocrisy over stimulus grants. Ryan, though polite, appeared flustered and unable to match Biden’s ferocious barroom‑brawler style. Moderator Martha Raddatz forced a more aggressive tone than previous debates, keeping the candidates on the defensive. Overall, Biden’s performance was a decisive, withering riposte that underscored his experience and out‑matched Ryan’s tentative responses, reinforcing the moral message that Biden’s vigor and wit better serve the nation than Ryan’s tentative, inconsistent positions.  
END OF SUMMARY.
