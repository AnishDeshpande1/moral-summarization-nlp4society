SUMMARY:
Joe Biden used his debate experience from 1988 to take on Representative Paul D. Ryan in the vice presidential debate. He employed a strategy of shaking his head, scoffing, and using withering retorts to paint Ryan as a latter-day Dan Quayle. When Ryan mentioned John F. Kennedy, Biden responded by saying "So, now you're Jack Kennedy?" The debate was seen as a chance for Biden to relive past debates and let loose, which he did. He frequently interrupted Ryan and used patronizing grins and mocking tone to attack his policy on Medicare. Ryan held his own but appeared abashed at times.

Biden also went after Mitt Romney's infamous remark about the "47 percent," with Ryan reminding him of his own propensity for gaffes. Moderator Martha Raddatz was seen as more forceful than Jim Lehrer, pushing the candidates to be specific and changing subjects abruptly. Overall, Biden dominated the debate with his quick wit and sharp retorts.

END OF SUMMARY.
