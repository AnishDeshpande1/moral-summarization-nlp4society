SUMMARY: The vice‑presidential debate on Oct. 2, 2012, saw Joe Biden channel his former Senate colleague Lloyd Bentsen’s 1988 debate tactics, delivering sharp, humor‑laden attacks on Rep. Paul Ryan. Biden repeatedly mocked Ryan’s remarks, notably when Ryan invoked John F. Kennedy, prompting Biden’s “So, now you’re Jack Kennedy?” retort. While Biden’s performance was energetic and combative—often interrupting and using patronizing grins—Ryan tried to stay respectful but appeared flustered. Biden also seized the moment to criticize Ryan’s support for the Obama stimulus and highlighted his own personal tragedy. Moderator Martha Raddatz took a more assertive role than previous debate moderators, pushing candidates for specifics. Overall, the debate was a spirited, confrontational exchange, with Biden dominating the repartee and aiming to redeem the Obama campaign after a poor showing in the presidential debate. END OF SUMMARY.
