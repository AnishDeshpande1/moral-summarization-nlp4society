SUMMARY: In the vice‑presidential debate, Joe Biden invoked his old Senate colleague Lloyd Bentsen’s 1988 playbook, using sharp sarcasm and a “Cheshire Cat” grin to lampoon Rep. Paul Ryan’s remarks, even mocking Ryan for invoking John F. Kennedy. Biden’s relentless interruptions and patronizing grins turned the night into a theatrical roast, while Ryan tried to stay respectful, his “tilted‑head, choirboy smile” slipping into irritation as Biden attacked his Medicare stance and highlighted perceived hypocrisy over stimulus grants. The debate also resurfaced personal tragedy when Biden recalled the car crash that **killed** his wife and young daughter, a moment that contrasted with Ryan’s anecdote about Mitt Romney’s charitable aid to a family whose children were **injured** in an accident. Moderator Martha Raddatz pushed the candidates hard, refusing the passive style of previous debates. The exchange was a “night of withering ripostes,” dominated by Biden’s aggressive barroom‑brawler style, leaving Ryan looking abashed amid the verbal sparring. END OF SUMMARY.
