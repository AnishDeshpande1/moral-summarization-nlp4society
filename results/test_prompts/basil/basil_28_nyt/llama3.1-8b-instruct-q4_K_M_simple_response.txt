SUMMARY:

The vice presidential debate between Joe Biden and Paul Ryan was marked by a sharp and spirited exchange, with both candidates delivering lacerating blows. However, it was Biden who dominated the night, using his "inner barroom brawler" to unleash a barrage of withering retorts against Ryan's policies. Biden repeatedly interrupted Ryan, mocking him for opposing the Obama stimulus while asking for government funds for his own district. He also poked fun at Ryan's attempts to soften Mitt Romney's image and reminded viewers of Romney's infamous "47 percent" remark.

Biden's performance was a clear attempt to relive past debates and restore the Obama campaign's momentum after President Obama's dismal performance in his debate with Mitt Romney. The moderator, Martha Raddatz, pushed the candidates to be specific and kept the debate on track, but Biden's aggressive style left Ryan looking disconcerted at times.

END OF SUMMARY.
