SUMMARY:
President Trump praised the special counsel’s rare statement that disputed a BuzzFeed report alleging he ordered Michael Cohen to lie to Congress about the timing of a Trump Tower Moscow deal. Trump called the story a “total phony,” condemned the mainstream media for “lost tremendous credibility,” and claimed the episode was a “sad day for journalism” but a “great day for our country.” BuzzFeed’s report, based on two law‑enforcement sources, said Cohen told investigators he was instructed by Trump to conceal the president’s involvement in the Russian negotiations, marking what the outlet called the first known instance of Trump directing a subordinate to lie about Russia. Mueller’s office issued a brief denial, saying the article’s description of statements and documents was “not accurate,” without providing specifics. Trump’s allies, including Rudy Giuliani, hailed the correction as a victory over a “hysterical” press. BuzzFeed stood by its reporting, insisting no part of the story appears inaccurate and promising further details when possible. The clash underscores deep concerns about presidential accountability, media integrity, and the rule of law.  
END OF SUMMARY.
