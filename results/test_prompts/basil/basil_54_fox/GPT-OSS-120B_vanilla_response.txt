SUMMARY: Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson urged lawmakers to amend a controversial religious‑freedom bill that critics say could permit discrimination based on sexual orientation. Initially set to sign the measure, Hutchinson reversed course amid a national backlash similar to that faced by Indiana over its own religious‑freedom law. He emphasized preserving the bill’s intent—to prevent government infringement on religious beliefs without a compelling interest—while aligning it more closely with the 1993 federal law. Hutchinson called for “recalling” the bill or passing additional legislation to correct discrepancies, noting generational divides and even his son’s petition to veto it. Business leaders, including Walmart and local chambers, have opposed the bill, fearing discrimination and economic harm. The Human Rights Campaign has targeted tech firms with ads, and the White House condemned Indiana’s analogous law as a significant expansion beyond the federal standard. Hutchinson also plans an executive order on tolerance while seeking a balanced solution.

END OF SUMMARY.
