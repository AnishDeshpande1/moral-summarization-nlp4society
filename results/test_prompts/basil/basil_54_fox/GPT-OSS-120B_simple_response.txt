SUMMARY: Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson, after initially signaling he would sign a controversial religious‑freedom bill, has asked lawmakers to revise it amid a national backlash that the measure could sanction discrimination against LGBTQ people. While he reaffirms the bill’s purpose—to prevent government infringement on sincere religious beliefs without a compelling interest—he stresses the need for “the right balance” and for the text to more closely follow the 1993 federal law. Hutchinson cites a “generational gap” and even his son’s petition urging a veto, underscoring the moral tension between protecting religious tradition and upholding equal‑rights protections. Business leaders, including Wal‑Mart and local chambers, warn the bill threatens tolerance, economic growth, and basic fairness. The governor also plans an executive order to promote tolerance. The episode mirrors similar disputes in Indiana, where lawmakers faced pressure to clarify language after critics warned the law could enable discrimination. The White House and LGBTQ advocacy groups have condemned such expansions as contrary to widely‑held American values of inclusion and non‑discrimination. Hutchinson’s call for amendment reflects an attempt to reconcile religious liberty with the moral imperative to protect marginalized communities. END OF SUMMARY.
