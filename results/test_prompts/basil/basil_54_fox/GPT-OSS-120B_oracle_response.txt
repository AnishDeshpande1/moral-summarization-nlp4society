SUMMARY: Arkansas Republican Gov. Asa Hutchinson urged lawmakers to make changes to a controversial religious‑freedom bill after a wave of criticism that it could permit discrimination against LGBTQ people. Although he still supports the bill’s intent to protect religious belief, Hutchinson said the legislation must more precisely mirror the 1993 federal law and therefore should be recalled or amended to remedy the perceived problems. He faced pressure to reject the measure from major employers such as Wal‑Mart, the Little Rock mayor, the Chamber of Commerce and Acxiom, who warned the bill would be discriminatory and stifle economic development. The governor also noted a generational gap, citing his own son’s petition urging a veto. Critics, including the Human Rights Campaign and the White House, labeled the bill’s language as discriminatory and suggested it expands beyond the original law. Hutchinson said he would also issue an executive order to promote tolerance while seeking true balance between religious tradition and non‑discrimination. END OF SUMMARY.
