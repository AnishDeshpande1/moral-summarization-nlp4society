SUMMARY:
Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson asked lawmakers to amend a controversial religious‑freedom bill he had slated to sign after a wave of national criticism that the measure could permit discrimination against LGBTQ people. While maintaining support for the bill’s core purpose—preventing government infringement on sincere religious beliefs without a compelling interest—Hutchinson said changes are needed to make the law match the 1993 federal statute and to address a “generational gap” in views on tolerance. He cited pressure from major employers such as Walmart, the Little Rock mayor, the Chamber of Commerce and the Human Rights Campaign, as well as his own son’s petition urging a veto. Hutchinson promised to issue an executive order on tolerance and urged the legislature to “recall” or revise the bill to balance religious tradition with non‑discrimination. The episode reflects a broader national clash, echoed in Indiana’s similar legislation, over how to protect religious liberty while upholding equal rights for all citizens.

END OF SUMMARY.
