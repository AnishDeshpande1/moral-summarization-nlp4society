SUMMARY:
Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson, a Republican, urged lawmakers to revise a controversial religious‑freedom bill that critics say could let businesses discriminate against LGBTQ people. After initially pledging to sign it, Hutchinson backed off amid a national backlash that framed the bill as an abuse of governmental authority and a cheat on basic fairness principles. He argued the measure should simply mirror the 1993 federal law that protects religious belief without a compelling interest, and he asked legislators to “recall” or amend the bill to fix provisions that stray from that standard. Hutchinson highlighted a “generational gap” and noted even his son signed a petition urging a veto, underscoring internal challenges to authority. Business leaders—including Wal‑Mart, the Little Rock Chamber of Commerce and Acxiom—joined the fairness critique, warning the bill would sanction discrimination and hurt the state’s economy. The White House and LGBTQ advocates similarly condemned the legislation as an unfair expansion of power that subverts equal rights. Hutchinson said he is working on an executive order to promote tolerance while seeking a balanced, fair compromise.

END OF SUMMARY.
