SUMMARY:
Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson has asked state lawmakers to change a controversial religious freedom bill that critics say could allow businesses to discriminate based on sexual orientation. Initially, Hutchinson planned to sign the bill but reversed his decision after facing backlash similar to what Indiana faced over its own religious freedom law. The governor wants to strike a "right balance" between protecting religious beliefs and preventing discrimination. He supports the intent of the legislation, which mirrors a 1993 federal law, but argues that some provisions do not accurately replicate it. Hutchinson is working on an executive order to address tolerance issues and has urged lawmakers to recall the bill or pass additional legislation to remedy the issue. The governor's decision comes after pressure from major employers, including Walmart, and the White House, which has opposed similar laws in other states.

END OF SUMMARY.
