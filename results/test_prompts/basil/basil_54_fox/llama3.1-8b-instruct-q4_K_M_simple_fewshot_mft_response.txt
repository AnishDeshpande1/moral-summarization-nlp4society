SUMMARY:

Arkansas Republican Gov. Asa Hutchinson has urged state lawmakers to change a controversial religious freedom bill, amid criticism that it could allow businesses and others to discriminate based on sexual orientation. Hutchinson initially planned to sign the bill but backed off as his state faced intense backlash. The governor wants to strike a "right balance" between protecting religious freedoms and preventing discrimination. He supports the intent of the legislation, which prohibits state and local governments from infringing upon someone's religious beliefs without a compelling interest. However, he believes that some provisions do not "precisely mirror" a 1993 federal law, which is his goal for the bill to reflect. Hutchinson has also faced pressure from top employers, including retail giant Wal-Mart, which complained that the measure was discriminatory and would stifle economic development.

The governor's move reflects the intense debate in state capitals across the country where similar legislation is being considered. Critics argue that such laws could allow businesses to discriminate based on sexual orientation, while supporters claim they are simply replicating existing federal law.
