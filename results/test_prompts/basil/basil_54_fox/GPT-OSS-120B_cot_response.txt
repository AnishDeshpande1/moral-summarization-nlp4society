Arkansas Republican Gov. Asa Hutchinson urged lawmakers to amend a controversial religious‑freedom bill after a swift national backlash and sharp criticism that the measure could permit discrimination based on sexual orientation. Although he still backs the bill’s intent to protect religious belief without a compelling state interest, Hutchinson says the language must more precisely mirror the 1993 federal law and avoid discriminatory effects. He cited a “generational gap” and even his son’s petition urging a veto, while noting pressure from major employers such as Wal‑Mart and the state’s Chamber of Commerce, who argue the bill is discriminatory and could stifle economic development. The governor also promised an executive order to promote tolerance. The episode reflects a broader cultural clash over values, with the White House and LGBTQ advocacy groups joining the outcry against similar legislation in Indiana. Hutchinson’s call for “the right balance” seeks to reconcile religious tradition with non‑discrimination while preserving Arkansas’s appeal to new businesses.
