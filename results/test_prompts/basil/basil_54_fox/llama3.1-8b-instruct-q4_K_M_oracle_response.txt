SUMMARY: 
Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson is urging lawmakers to make changes to a controversial religious freedom bill that critics say could allow businesses to discriminate based on sexual orientation. Initially, the governor planned to sign the bill but backed off as his state faced intense backlash. He wants to strike a balance between protecting religious beliefs and preventing discrimination. Despite supporting the intent of the legislation, which prohibits state and local governments from infringing upon someone's religious beliefs without a compelling interest, Hutchinson believes that some provisions do not mirror federal law precisely. To address this issue, he is working on an executive order to promote tolerance. The governor has also faced pressure from top employers, including Wal-Mart, to reject the bill due to concerns it would be discriminatory and stifle economic development in the state. Hutchinson hopes to attract technology firms by showing that his state values diversity and inclusivity.

END OF SUMMARY.
