**SUMMARY:**

Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson has urged state lawmakers to revise a controversial religious freedom bill that critics argue could allow businesses and individuals to discriminate based on sexual orientation. The bill, which was sent to his desk earlier this week, has sparked intense criticism nationwide, with many calling for its veto. Hutchinson, while supporting the intent of the legislation, wants changes made so it aligns more closely with a 1993 federal law that prohibits state and local governments from infringing on someone's religious beliefs without a compelling interest. He aims to strike the right balance between religious traditions and non-discrimination values, which he acknowledges are at the heart of the debate. Hutchinson has also announced plans for an executive order addressing tolerance in the state.
