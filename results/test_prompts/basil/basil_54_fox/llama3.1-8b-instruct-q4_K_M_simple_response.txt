SUMMARY:

Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson has called for changes to a controversial religious freedom bill, citing concerns that it could allow businesses to discriminate based on sexual orientation. Despite initially planning to sign the bill, Hutchinson backed off in the face of intense national criticism and pressure from major employers like Walmart. He wants to strike a "right balance" between protecting religious beliefs and preventing discrimination. The governor has asked lawmakers to recall the bill or pass additional legislation to remedy the issue, which he believes does not mirror a 1993 federal law as intended. Hutchinson's chief complaint is that some provisions do not precisely reflect the federal law, and he is working on an executive order to promote tolerance. The move reflects growing debate across the country over how to balance religious traditions with non-discrimination laws.

END OF SUMMARY.
