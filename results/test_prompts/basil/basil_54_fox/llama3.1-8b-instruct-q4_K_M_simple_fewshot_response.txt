SUMMARY:

Arkansas Governor Asa Hutchinson has urged state lawmakers to revise a controversial religious freedom bill, citing concerns that it could allow businesses to discriminate based on sexual orientation. The bill, similar to one passed in Indiana, was met with criticism from opponents who argue it would permit discrimination against LGBTQ individuals. Hutchinson initially planned to sign the bill but backed off as his state faced intense backlash. He wants changes made to more closely mirror a 1993 federal law and is working on an executive order to address tolerance issues. The governor's about-face reflects the ongoing debate over balancing religious freedoms with non-discrimination protections. Major employers, including Wal-Mart, have urged Hutchinson to reject the bill, citing concerns it would stifle economic development.
