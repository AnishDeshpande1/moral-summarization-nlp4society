SUMMARY: Michael Bloomberg’s recent harsh criticism of Donald Trump at the Democratic National Convention may suggest a long‑standing feud, but their relationship has actually been limited and mostly cordial. The two New York billionaires moved in different ultra‑rich circles—Bloomberg as a philanthropist and arts patron, Trump as a flamboyant real‑estate and media figure. They rarely interacted, meeting only on a golf course during a 2007 charity event and Bloomberg’s brief appearances on “The Apprentice.” Their families have a minor connection, with their daughters being friends, and Trump once tweeted praise for Bloomberg’s mayoral work. The amicable tone ended when Trump entered politics; Bloomberg considered an independent presidential run to block Trump, then withdrew to avoid splitting the vote. Trump later dismissed Bloomberg’s wealth and business, acknowledging they were no longer friends. Their limited past encounters contrast sharply with the current political animosity. END OF SUMMARY.
