SUMMARY:

The article highlights the contrast between Michael R. Bloomberg and Donald J. Trump, two billionaires who have interacted cordially in the past but now seem to have a rift. According to people who know both of them, their interactions were limited yet polite, with Mr. Bloomberg being known as a generous philanthropist and supporter of the arts, while Mr. Trump has been described as flinty in his giving and relishes in being crude. The men have met on golf courses, including a charity event at Trump National Golf Club, where Mr. Trump showed deference to Mr. Bloomberg by suggesting he start first. However, their cordiality dissipated when Mr. Trump declared his candidacy, prompting Mr. Bloomberg to explore an independent presidential bid to block him from being elected, citing concerns that "you can't fix crazy" and that Trump is a public menace.
