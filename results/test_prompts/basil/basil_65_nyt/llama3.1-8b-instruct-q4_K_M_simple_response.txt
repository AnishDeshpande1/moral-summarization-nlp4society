SUMMARY:

Former New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg and President Donald Trump have a complex history, despite their differences in wealth and politics. While they existed in different social circles, they had limited but cordial interactions until Trump's presidential candidacy. Bloomberg has described Trump as a "public menace" who is not sane, while Trump has criticized Bloomberg's worth and business dealings. However, the two billionaires did meet on golf courses, including a charity event at Trump National Golf Club in 2007, where they were set to play together but couldn't agree on who would start. They also appeared together on Trump's reality TV show "The Apprentice." Their relationship turned sour when Bloomberg began exploring his own presidential bid as an independent, partly to block Trump from being elected.

Their interactions highlight the contrast between their personalities and values: Bloomberg is known for his philanthropy and cultural support, while Trump is often criticized for his crass behavior and business dealings. The two billionaires' complicated history underscores the deep-seated differences that have driven their public feud.

END OF SUMMARY.
