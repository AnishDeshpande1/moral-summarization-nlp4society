SUMMARY:
Michael Bloomberg’s fiery DNC speech condemning Donald Trump as a “public menace” and “not quite sane” suggests a long‑standing rivalry, but insiders say the two ultra‑rich New Yorkers have had only limited, mostly cordial interactions. Bloomberg is portrayed as a low‑key philanthropist and arts patron who made his fortune in finance and technology, while Trump is depicted as a brash real‑estate mogul and reality‑TV star who flaunts his wealth. Their few meetings – a 2007 charity golf round, Bloomberg’s cameo on “The Apprentice,” and occasional friendly tweets – were brief and polite. Even family ties exist, with their daughters reportedly friends. The goodwill faded once Trump entered politics; Bloomberg explored an independent presidential run to block Trump, then withdrew to avoid siphoning votes for a candidate he called “crazy.” Trump, in turn, dismissed Bloomberg’s wealth and attacked his business. The moral framing underscores that Bloomberg’s harsh rhetoric stems from genuine concern over Trump’s temperament and policies, while highlighting Trump’s opportunistic shift from occasional friend to adversary.  
END OF SUMMARY.
