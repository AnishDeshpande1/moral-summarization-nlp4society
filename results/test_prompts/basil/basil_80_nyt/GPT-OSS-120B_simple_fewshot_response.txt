SUMMARY:
President Trump opened the new year by denouncing former Clinton aide Huma Abedin for “disregarding basic security protocols” after a Daily Caller report revealed she had sent State Department passwords to her private Yahoo account in 2009 – a lapse that later fed a massive 2013 Yahoo hack. Trump framed the incident as evidence of Clinton’s reckless handling of classified information and called his own Justice Department a “deep state” that shields such misconduct. He urged his administration to investigate Abedin, portraying the effort as a moral fight against the corruption and negligence of the 2016 Democratic opponent. By highlighting the release of thousands of Abedin’s work emails from her estranged husband’s laptop and recalling James Comey’s last‑minute disclosure that hurt Clinton’s campaign, Trump casts the investigation as a necessary check on a corrupt establishment. He also invoked the case of a Navy sailor jailed for photographing a submarine, suggesting the president’s willingness to grant clemency. The post reinforces Trump’s narrative that the “deep state” protects Democratic wrongdoing while he seeks accountability and transparency.

END OF SUMMARY.
