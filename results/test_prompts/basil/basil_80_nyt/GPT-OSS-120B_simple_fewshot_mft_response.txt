SUMMARY:
President Trump opened the new year by using his “Crooked Hillary” Twitter account to attack former Clinton aide Huma Abedin, accusing her of “disregarding basic security protocols” after she allegedly forwarded government passwords to a private Yahoo account in 2009. By labeling his own Justice Department a “deep state,” Trump seeks to delegitimize the authority of established institutions and portray them as subversive actors. The post also frames the alleged mishandling of passwords as a serious breach that could have harmed national security, invoking the Care/Harm foundation. Moreover, Trump’s call for an investigation into Abedin and his reference to a jailed Navy sailor seeking a pardon suggest a narrative of selective justice, implying that the political elite are unfairly protected while his opponents are punished—highlighting the Fairness/Cheating dimension. The article notes that the State Department released thousands of Abedin’s work emails from her estranged husband’s laptop, a source of ongoing controversy that Trump leverages to further question the integrity of his political rivals and the institutions meant to oversee them.
