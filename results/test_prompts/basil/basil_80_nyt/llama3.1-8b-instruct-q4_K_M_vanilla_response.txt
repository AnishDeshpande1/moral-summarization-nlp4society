SUMMARY:
President Trump made his first Twitter post of the year on Tuesday, accusing former Clinton aide Huma Abedin of disregarding basic security protocols by forwarding government passwords to her private email account in 2009. Trump also called the Justice Department a "deep state" and implored his administration to investigate Abedin. The allegations were based on an article in The Daily Caller, which reported that Yahoo was hacked in 2013, affecting all three billion of its accounts. This is not the first time Trump has attacked Clinton or the Justice Department, as he has criticized them repeatedly since the 2016 election. The tweet also referenced the case of a Navy sailor who was jailed for taking pictures inside a military submarine and had hoped to receive a pardon from the president.

END OF SUMMARY.
