SUMMARY: President Donald Trump posted a Twitter message within 48 hours of the new year, accusing former Clinton aide Huma Abedin of ignoring security protocols by allegedly forwarding government passwords to her private Yahoo account in 2009. He also labeled his own Justice Department a “deep state” and urged an investigation into Abedin. The claim stems from a Daily Caller article; Yahoo was later hacked in 2013, and the data was offered for sale by an Eastern European hacking group. The State Department recently released about 3,000 work‑related emails found on the laptop of Abedin’s estranged husband, Anthony Weiner, a matter linked to the 2016 Clinton email controversy that Trump says cost Clinton the election. Trump also referenced a separate case of a Navy sailor jailed for photographing a submarine, who had sought a presidential pardon. The post reflects Trump’s ongoing attacks on Hillary Clinton and criticism of the Justice Department.

END OF SUMMARY.
