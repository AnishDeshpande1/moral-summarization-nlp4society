SUMMARY: President Trump opened the new year with a sharply moralized tweet, denouncing former Clinton aide Huma Abedin for “disregarding basic security protocols” and labeling his own Justice Department a “deep state” that undermines democratic accountability. Citing a Daily Caller report that Abedin allegedly forwarded government passwords to her private Yahoo account in 2009—an account later compromised in a massive 2013 hack—Trump urged his administration to investigate her conduct, framing the issue as a betrayal of public trust. He linked this accusation to his broader, ongoing moral crusade against Hillary Clinton, whom he repeatedly casts as corrupt, and to his criticism of the Justice Department and Attorney General Jeff Sessions for failing to pursue opponents. The State Department’s recent release of about 3,000 work‑related emails from Abedin’s estranged husband’s laptop, part of a public‑records request, further fuels the narrative of hidden wrongdoing. Trump also invoked the case of a Navy sailor jailed for photographing a submarine, suggesting the need for presidential clemency. Throughout, Trump presents himself as a defender of national security and moral integrity against a purported “deep state” and former Democratic elites. END OF SUMMARY.
