SUMMARY:
President Obama expressed concerns about Russian hacking into the US election system, stating that he hopes President-elect Trump will take it seriously. He accused many Republicans of being more focused on defeating Hillary Clinton than on addressing foreign influence in the election. Obama said he has "great confidence" in intelligence reports that Russia carried out the hacking and warned that unless there is a change in attitude, the US will continue to be vulnerable to foreign influence.

Obama also criticized Trump's praise for Russian President Putin, saying it was unwarranted and likening it to Ronald Reagan's views. He said he told Putin to "cut it out" when they met in China in September and promised that there will be consequences for the cyberattacks. Obama emphasized the need for a thoughtful and methodical US response to Russia's actions.

The president-elect has been accused of being dismissive of the hacking allegations, but Obama insisted that cooperation between his administration and Trump's team would continue during the transition period.
