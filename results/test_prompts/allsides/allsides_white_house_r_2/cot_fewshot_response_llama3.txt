SUMMARY:
President Obama expressed concern about Russian hacking into the US election system, hoping that President-elect Trump will take action to prevent foreign influence in future elections. He criticized Republicans for being more focused on defeating Hillary Clinton than addressing Russia's alleged meddling in the election. Obama also blasted Republican officials and voters for expressing positive views of Vladimir Putin, calling it a "vulnerability" to foreign influence. He warned that unless this changes, the US will continue to be vulnerable to foreign interference. The President emphasized the need for a thoughtful and methodical response to Russia's actions, stating that there will be "consequences" for the cyberattacks.
