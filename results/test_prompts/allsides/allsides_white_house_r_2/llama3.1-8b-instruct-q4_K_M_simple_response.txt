SUMMARY:

President Obama expressed concern about the allegations of Russian hacking into the US election system, stating that many Republicans were more focused on defeating Hillary Clinton than preventing foreign influence in the election. He criticized Republican officials and voters for their positive views of Vladimir Putin, saying it was "troubling" and that Ronald Reagan would be appalled. Obama blamed partisan divides in Washington for the lack of action against Russia's alleged meddling.

He confirmed that intelligence reports give him confidence in the assessment that Russia carried out the hacking, and warned that unless there is a change, the US will continue to be vulnerable to foreign influence. Obama also chided journalists for publishing news stories based on stolen Democratic documents, saying it was not a winning strategy.

Obama emphasized that he hopes President-elect Trump will take action against Russian hacking and expressed his hope for a smooth transition to ensure a peaceful transfer of power.

END OF SUMMARY.
