SUMMARY:

President Obama expressed concern about Russian hacking into the US election system, urging President-elect Trump to take action against foreign influence in the electoral process. He criticized Republicans for being more interested in defeating Hillary Clinton than addressing Moscow's alleged meddling. The president believes Russia is responsible for hacking and leaking Democratic officials' emails, and he warned that unless the GOP takes a stand against Putin, the US will remain vulnerable to foreign interference. Obama also blasted Republican voters who approve of Putin, saying Ronald Reagan would be disappointed. He emphasized the need for a thoughtful response to Russian cyberattacks, warning that there will be consequences for Moscow's actions. The president urged cooperation with Trump's team and called for a smooth transition.
