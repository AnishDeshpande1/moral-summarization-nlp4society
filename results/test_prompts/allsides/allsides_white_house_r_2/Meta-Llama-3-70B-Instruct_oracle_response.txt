SUMMARY:

President Obama expressed concern that President-elect Trump is not showing sufficient concern about allegations of Russian hacking into the US election system. He criticized Republicans for being more interested in defeating Hillary Clinton than in combating Moscow's alleged meddling in the election. Obama believes that Russian President Vladimir Putin was involved in the hacking, and that the intelligence reports give him "great confidence" in the assessment. He blasted Republican officials and voters for expressing positive views of Putin, noting that 37% of Republican voters approve of the former KGB head. Obama warned that partisan divides in Washington make the US vulnerable to foreign influence, and that the country needs a more robust response to Russia's cyberattacks. He promised that there will be "consequences" for Russia's actions, and that the US response will increase the "costs" to Russia of future cyberattacks.

END OF SUMMARY.
