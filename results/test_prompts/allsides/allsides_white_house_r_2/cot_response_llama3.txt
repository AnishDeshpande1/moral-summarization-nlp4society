SUMMARY:
President Obama expressed concern about the alleged Russian hacking into the U.S. election system, urging President-elect Trump to take appropriate measures to prevent foreign influence in the electoral process. He criticized Republicans for prioritizing defeating Hillary Clinton over addressing Moscow's alleged meddling, saying that Ronald Reagan would be appalled by their support for Russian leader Vladimir Putin. Obama emphasized that Russia is responsible for the hacking and warned of consequences for future cyberattacks. He also cautioned journalists against publishing stolen documents and called for a thoughtful response to Russia's actions. The President-elect has rejected the administration's argument that Russia hacked into Democratic officials' emails, prompting White House frustration. As the transition period begins, Obama reiterated his commitment to ensuring a smooth handover of power.
