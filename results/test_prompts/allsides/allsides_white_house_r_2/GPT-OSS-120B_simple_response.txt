SUMMARY:
President Obama used his year‑end press conference to warn that the incoming Trump administration must take the Russian hacking allegations seriously, condemning Republicans for prioritizing partisan victory over defending American elections. He reiterated confidence in CIA/FBI intelligence that Russia, under Vladimir Putin, interfered to help Trump, and rebuked GOP voters who admire the former KGB chief. Obama lamented that “Ronald Reagan would roll over in his grave,” and accused the GOP of preferring to damage Democrats rather than confront an autocratic adversary, warning this attitude leaves the nation vulnerable to foreign influence. He affirmed that Russia will face “thoughtful, methodical” consequences, increasing the cost of future cyberattacks, and recalled telling Putin to “cut it out” in a September meeting. While refusing to state outright that Clinton lost because of the hack, Obama criticized the media’s coverage of her campaign and pledged a smooth transition. Senator Ben Sasse called for a robust, multi‑pronged response, echoing Obama’s call for decisive action. The briefing was briefly disrupted when a journalist fainted, prompting Obama to summon medical help.  
END OF SUMMARY.
