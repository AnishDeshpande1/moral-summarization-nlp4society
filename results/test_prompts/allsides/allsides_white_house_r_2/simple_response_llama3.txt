SUMMARY:
President Obama expressed concern over Russian hacking into the US election system, urging President-elect Trump to take action against foreign influence in the process. He criticized Republicans for prioritizing defeating Hillary Clinton over addressing Moscow's alleged meddling. Mr. Obama stated that he has "great confidence" in the assessment of the CIA and FBI that Russia carried out the hacking, which he believes was intended to help Donald Trump win the election. The President also chided Republican officials and voters who express positive views of Vladimir Putin. He emphasized that the US response to Russian cyberattacks must be thoughtful and methodical, with consequences for future attacks.

END OF SUMMARY.
