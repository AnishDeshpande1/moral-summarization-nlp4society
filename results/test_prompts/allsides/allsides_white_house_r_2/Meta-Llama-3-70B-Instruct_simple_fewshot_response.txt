SUMMARY:

President Obama expressed hope that President-elect Trump will take concerns about Russian hacking of the US election system seriously, criticizing Republicans for being more focused on defeating Hillary Clinton than combating Moscow's alleged meddling. Obama stated that he has "great confidence" in the assessment of the CIA and FBI that Russia carried out the hacking, and blamed partisan divides in Washington for the GOP's lack of action against Russian President Vladimir Putin. The President also chided journalists for publishing news stories based on stolen Democratic documents and announced that there will be "consequences" for Russia's cyberattacks. Obama's comments were seen as a rebuke to Trump and his team, who have rejected the administration's argument that Russia hacked into Democratic officials' emails to help defeat Clinton. The President's stance was marked by a sense of urgency and frustration, highlighting the need for a united response to foreign interference in the US election process.

END OF SUMMARY.
