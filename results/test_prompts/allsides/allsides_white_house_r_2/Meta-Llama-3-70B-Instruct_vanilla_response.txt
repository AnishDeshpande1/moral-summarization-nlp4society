SUMMARY:

President Obama expressed hope that President-elect Donald Trump will take concerns about Russian hacking of the US election system seriously. He criticized Republicans for being more focused on defeating Hillary Clinton than addressing Moscow's alleged meddling. Obama stated that he has "great confidence" in the CIA and FBI's assessment that Russia carried out the hacking, and believes that Russian President Vladimir Putin was involved. He also blasted Republican officials and voters for expressing positive views of Putin, citing a poll that found 37% of Republican voters approve of the former KGB head. Obama warned that unless partisan divides change, the US will continue to be vulnerable to foreign influence. He promised that there will be "consequences" for Russia's cyberattacks and that the US response will increase the "costs" to Russia of future cyberattacks.

END OF SUMMARY.
