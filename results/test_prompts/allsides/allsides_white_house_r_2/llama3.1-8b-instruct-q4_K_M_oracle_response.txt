SUMMARY:

President Obama expressed concern that President-elect Trump is not adequately addressing allegations of Russian hacking into the U.S. election system, which he believes had a significant influence on the outcome. He emphasized that it's not just about defeating Hillary Clinton, but rather about ensuring the integrity of the democratic process and preventing foreign interference. Obama stated that he has great confidence in the CIA and FBI assessments that Russia carried out the hacking and tried to help Trump win the election.

Obama also criticized Republican officials and voters for expressing positive views of Putin, who he said is an autocratic leader whose actions are a threat to U.S. national security. He noted that partisan divides in Washington are contributing to the problem, as some politicians are more interested in hurting Democrats than taking a stand against Russian aggression.

The President-elect has called Putin a strong leader and Trump's team has rejected the administration's argument that Russia hacked into Democratic officials' emails. Obama warned that unless there is a change in attitude, the U.S. will continue to be vulnerable to foreign influence.

END OF SUMMARY.
