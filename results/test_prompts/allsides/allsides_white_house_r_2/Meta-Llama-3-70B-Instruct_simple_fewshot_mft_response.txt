SUMMARY:

President Obama expressed his hope that President-elect Donald Trump will take concerns about Russian hacking of the US election system seriously, unlike many Republicans who were more focused on defeating Hillary Clinton. Obama criticized Republicans for being more interested in hurting Democrats than standing against Russian President Vladimir Putin, who he believes was involved in the hacking. The president also blasted Trump for calling Putin a strong leader, saying it would have made Ronald Reagan "roll over in his grave." Obama emphasized the need for a unified response to Russian interference, warning that partisan divides will leave the US vulnerable to foreign influence. He vowed to take action against Russia, but in a "thoughtful, methodical way." The president's comments were marked by a sense of moral urgency, emphasizing the importance of protecting the integrity of the electoral process and standing up to authoritarian leaders.

END OF SUMMARY.
