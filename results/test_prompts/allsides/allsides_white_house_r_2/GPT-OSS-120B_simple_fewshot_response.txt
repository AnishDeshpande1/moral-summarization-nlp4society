SUMMARY:
President Obama warned that President‑elect Donald Trump must take the Russian hacking allegations seriously, condemning many Republicans for prioritizing partisan attacks on Hillary Clinton over defending the election from foreign interference. Obama reiterated his confidence in CIA and FBI assessments that Putin’s Russia was behind the cyberattacks and noted that a sizable share of GOP voters approve of the former KGB chief. He criticized Trump’s flattering remarks about Putin, saying even Ronald Reagan would disapprove, and blamed partisan divisions for leaving the United States vulnerable to external influence. Obama pledged a “thoughtful, methodical” U.S. response that will raise the cost of future Russian cyber‑attacks, while insisting on a smooth transition and continued cooperation with the incoming administration. He also rebuked journalists for amplifying stolen Democratic emails and highlighted Senator Ben Sasse’s call for a stronger, multi‑dimensional response to Russian aggression. The remarks underscored Obama’s view that the election’s fairness was compromised and that defending democratic institutions must rise above partisan politics.

END OF SUMMARY.
