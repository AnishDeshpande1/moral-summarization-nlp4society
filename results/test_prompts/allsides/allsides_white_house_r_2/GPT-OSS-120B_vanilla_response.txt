SUMMARY: President Obama urged President‑elect Donald Trump to take the alleged Russian hacking of the 2016 election seriously, criticizing many Republicans for prioritizing partisan goals over addressing foreign interference. Obama expressed confidence in intelligence assessments that Russia, under Vladimir Putin, conducted the cyberattacks and said he had warned Putin to stop. He condemned GOP officials and voters who expressed favorable views of Putin and warned that partisan divisions leave the U.S. vulnerable to foreign influence. Obama called for a thoughtful, measured U.S. response to increase the cost of future Russian cyberattacks and pledged a smooth transition despite ongoing tensions with the incoming administration. He also rebuked journalists for reporting on stolen Democratic emails and noted that the media’s coverage of Hillary Clinton was problematic. Senator Ben Sasse called for a stronger, comprehensive response to Russia. The press briefing was briefly interrupted when a journalist fainted, prompting Obama to request medical assistance. END OF SUMMARY.
