SUMMARY:
President Obama expressed his hope that President-elect Trump would take appropriate measures to address allegations of Russian hacking into the U.S. election system, emphasizing the importance of ensuring a fair and secure electoral process. The president criticized Republican officials for downplaying the significance of Russia's alleged meddling in the election, noting that 37% of Republican voters approve of Russian President Putin. Obama also blasted Trump for calling Putin a strong leader, saying that Ronald Reagan would not approve. He warned that unless partisan divides are addressed, the U.S. will remain vulnerable to foreign influence. The president emphasized the need for a thoughtful and methodical response to Russia's cyberattacks, threatening consequences if necessary.
