SUMMARY:
President Obama expressed concern over Russian hacking allegations, urging President-elect Trump to address the issue. He criticized Republicans for prioritizing defeating Hillary Clinton over addressing foreign influence in elections. Obama stated that he has "great confidence" in intelligence reports indicating Russia's involvement and warned of vulnerabilities if partisan divides persist. He also condemned Republican officials and voters who express positive views of Russian President Vladimir Putin, calling it a threat to national security. The president emphasized the need for thoughtful and methodical US retaliation against Russia's cyberattacks. Obama declined to directly link Hillary Clinton's election loss to Russian meddling but criticized media coverage of her during the campaign.

END OF SUMMARY.
