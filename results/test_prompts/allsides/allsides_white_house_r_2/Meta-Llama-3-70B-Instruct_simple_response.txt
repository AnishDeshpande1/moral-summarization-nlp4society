SUMMARY:

President Obama expressed his hope that President-elect Trump will take concerns about Russian hacking in the US election system seriously, unlike many Republicans who prioritized defeating Hillary Clinton over addressing Moscow's alleged meddling. Obama emphasized that foreign influence in elections should not be a partisan issue. He criticized Republican officials and voters for their positive views of Vladimir Putin, citing a poll where 37% of Republican voters approved of the former KGB head. Obama blamed partisan divides for the GOP's lack of action against Russia's autocratic leader, making the US vulnerable to foreign influence. He reiterated that Russia was responsible for the hacking and promised consequences, while also chiding journalists for publishing news stories based on stolen documents. Obama's comments highlighted the need for a non-partisan approach to addressing foreign interference in elections.

END OF SUMMARY.
