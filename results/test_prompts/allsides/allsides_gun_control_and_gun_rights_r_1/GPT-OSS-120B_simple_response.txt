SUMMARY:  
Baltimore Mayor Jack Young, a Democrat, begged residents to stop “shooting each other” so that hospitals wouldn’t be overwhelmed with gun‑victim patients during the coronavirus surge. On the same day, Maryland Democrats in the General Assembly voted down Governor Larry Hogan’s Violent Firearms Offenders Act—a bill that would have made gun theft a felony and increased penalties for repeat illegal gun carriers and gun‑related drug dealers. Democrats stripped the bill of mandatory‑minimum sentences, arguing the punishments were too harsh, and the Senate ultimately rejected it. Republicans, including House Minority Leader Nic Kipke and senators Michael Hough and Bob Cassilly, condemned the move as a betrayal of public safety, saying Democrats “undermined” the legislation and gave leniency to violent offenders. Hogan’s proposal enjoyed broad public support—over 85 % of Baltimore residents and 84 % of African‑American voters backed it. The legislative session adjourned amid the pandemic, leaving both sides dissatisfied: the mayor decried ongoing “senseless” shootings that threaten health resources, while lawmakers argued over how best to curb the city’s rising gun violence.  

END OF SUMMARY.
