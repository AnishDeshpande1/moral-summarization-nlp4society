SUMMARY:

In a shocking display of subversion of authority, Maryland Democrats rejected a bill aimed at combating gun violence, despite Baltimore mayor Jack Young's public plea to residents to stop shooting each other so hospitals could be used to treat coronavirus patients. The Violent Firearms Offenders Act, pushed by Republican governor Larry Hogan, would have strengthened penalties for violent criminals, but Democrats removed the mandatory minimum sentencing increase, arguing it would punish violent criminals too severely. This decision prioritizes the interests of criminals over the safety and well-being of law-abiding citizens, undermining public safety and fairness. The bill's rejection is particularly egregious given the overwhelming support among Baltimore residents and African Americans statewide. By failing to address the issue of crime, Democrats have demonstrated a callous disregard for the harm caused by gun violence and a lack of fairness in their approach to justice. END OF SUMMARY.
