SUMMARY:
In Maryland, the Democratic-controlled state legislature rejected a bill aimed at combating gun violence, despite widespread support among residents and lawmakers of both parties. The bill, proposed by Republican Governor Larry Hogan, would have strengthened penalties for violent criminals and made theft of a firearm a felony. Democrats removed mandatory minimum sentencing from the bill, arguing it was too severe. Meanwhile, Baltimore Mayor Jack Young publicly begged residents to stop shooting each other so hospitals could be used to treat coronavirus patients. The city has seen a spike in violence, with seven people shot on Tuesday night. Hogan's bill had 85% support among Baltimore residents and 84% among African Americans statewide. GOP lawmakers accused Democrats of undermining public safety by gutting the legislation. The rejection of the bill is seen as a disappointment for those who advocate for stronger measures to combat gun violence.

END OF SUMMARY.
