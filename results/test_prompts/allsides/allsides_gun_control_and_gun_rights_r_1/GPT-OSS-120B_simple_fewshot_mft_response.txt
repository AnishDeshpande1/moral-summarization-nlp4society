SUMMARY:
Maryland Democrats voted down Governor Larry Hogan’s Violent Firearms Offenders Act, a bill that would have made gun theft a felony and raised penalties for repeat illegal gun users and drug‑dealer shooters. While the Senate passed a version of the measure, Democrats stripped out mandatory‑minimum sentencing, arguing the harsher punishments would be unfairly severe. Senate President Bill Ferguson praised the bipartisan compromise, but House Speaker Adrienne Jones declined comment. Republicans, including House Minority Leader Nic Kipke and Senators Michael Hough and Bob Cassilly, condemned the cuts as undermining public safety and accused Democrats of subverting authority to protect criminals. On the same day, Baltimore Mayor Jack Young, a Democrat, appealed to residents to cease “senseless” shootings so that hospitals would not be overwhelmed with gun‑victim patients during the coronavirus crisis—a plea grounded in the care/harm foundation. Polls show overwhelming public support for the original bill, especially among Baltimore and African‑American voters, highlighting the tension between authority‑driven enforcement, concern for community health, and perceptions of fairness in criminal sentencing. END OF SUMMARY.
