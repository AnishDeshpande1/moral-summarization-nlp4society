SUMMARY: Maryland Democrats voted down Governor Larry Hogan’s Violent Firearms Offenders Act, which would have made gun theft a felony and increased penalties for repeat illegal gun carriers and drug‑related gun use. While the Senate passed the bill on March 10, Democrats stripped mandatory minimum sentencing enhancements, arguing they were too harsh. Senate President Bill Ferguson praised the bipartisan effort, but House Speaker Adrienne Jones did not comment. Republican leaders, including House Minority Leader Nic Kipke and senators Michael Hough and Bob Cassilly, criticized the Democrats for weakening the bill and called its failure a major disappointment. Meanwhile, Baltimore Mayor Jack Young appealed to residents to curb shootings, warning that gun violence was filling hospitals needed for COVID‑19 patients. The mayor’s plea came as violent crime surged in Baltimore, and an internal poll showed strong public support for the legislation. The legislature adjourned amid the pandemic. END OF SUMMARY.
