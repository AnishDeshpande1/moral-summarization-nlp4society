SUMMARY:

Maryland Democrats rejected a bill aimed at combating gun violence, despite Baltimore Mayor Jack Young's plea to residents to stop shooting each other so hospitals could focus on treating coronavirus patients. The bill, pushed by Republican Governor Larry Hogan, would have strengthened penalties for violent criminals, including making the theft of a firearm a felony and increasing penalties for those who repeatedly carry illegal guns or use a gun while drug dealing. Democrats removed the mandatory minimum sentencing increase, arguing it would punish violent criminals too severely. The bill's failure to pass is a moral failure, as it prioritizes the interests of criminals over the safety of citizens and the needs of the healthcare system during a pandemic. The people of Baltimore, who overwhelmingly supported the bill, deserve better from their elected officials. END OF SUMMARY.
