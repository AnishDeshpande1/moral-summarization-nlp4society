SUMMARY:  
Maryland Democrats rejected Governor Larry Hogan’s Violent Firearms Offenders Act, a bill intended to strengthen penalties for violent crimes involving guns. The legislation would have made the theft of a firearm a felony, increase sentences for repeat offenders, and add harsher punishments for those who carry illegal guns, use them while dealing drugs, or employ assault‑style weapons. Democrats removed mandatory‑minimum enhancements, arguing the increase was too severe, and the Senate passed the trimmed version. Republican leaders called the defeat a failure to address the state’s crime problem, accusing Democrats of undermining public safety by pushing breaks for gun‑related dealing and selling. Meanwhile, Baltimore Mayor Jack Young pleaded with residents to stop the shootings and shooting spree that fill hospitals with people shot senselessly, urging the community to help keep beds available for COVID‑19 patients. The bill’s rejection left both parties claiming they had not done enough to curb violence.  
END OF SUMMARY.
