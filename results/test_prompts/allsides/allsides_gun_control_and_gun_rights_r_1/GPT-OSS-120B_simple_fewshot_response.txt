SUMMARY:
Maryland Democrats voted down Governor Larry Hogan’s Violent Firearms Offenders Act, stripping it of mandatory‑minimum sentences that would have stiffened penalties for gun thieves, repeat illegal carriers and drug‑dealers who use firearms. While Senate President Bill Ferguson praised the bill’s bipartisan elements, Democrats argued the harsher punishments would be unjustly severe, and House Speaker Adrienne Jones declined to comment. Republicans, including House Minority Leader Nic Kipke and Senators Michael Hough and Bob Cassilly, condemned the cuts as endangering public safety. On the same day, Baltimore Mayor Jack Young, a Democrat, pleaded with residents to stop the surge of shootings that could “clog up our hospitals” needed for COVID‑19 patients, underscoring the moral urgency of protecting community health amid a pandemic. Despite strong public support for the legislation—over 80 % of Baltimore and African‑American voters favored it—the bill failed, leaving both sides frustrated about the state’s ability to curb violent crime while safeguarding hospitals for the coronavirus crisis.  
END OF SUMMARY.
