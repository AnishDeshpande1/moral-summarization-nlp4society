SUMMARY:
Maryland Democrats rejected a bill aimed at combating gun violence, despite the overwhelming support of Baltimore residents (85%) and African Americans statewide (84%). The bill, pushed by Republican Governor Larry Hogan, would have strengthened penalties for violent criminals. However, Democrats voted down the legislation after removing its mandatory minimum sentencing increase, citing concerns that it would punish violent criminals too severely. Despite this, Mayor Jack Young publicly begged residents to stop shooting each other so hospitals could be used to treat coronavirus patients. The failure to pass the bill was met with disappointment from Republicans and some Democrats, who felt that crime in the state had not been adequately addressed. Violent crime has spiked in Baltimore in recent days, with seven people shot on Tuesday night. The rejection of the bill comes as the city struggles to cope with the pandemic.

END OF SUMMARY.
