SUMMARY:

Maryland Democrats rejected a bill aimed at combating gun violence, despite Baltimore Mayor Jack Young's public plea to residents to stop shooting each other to free up hospital beds for coronavirus patients. The bill, pushed by Republican Governor Larry Hogan, would have strengthened penalties for violent criminals, including making the theft of a firearm a felony and increasing penalties for repeated illegal gun carriers and drug dealers who use guns. Democrats removed the mandatory minimum sentencing increase, arguing it was too harsh, and the bill ultimately failed to pass. The move was criticized by Republicans, who accused Democrats of undermining public safety. The rejection of the bill comes as violent crime has spiked in Baltimore, with seven people shot on Tuesday night. Young urged residents to stop the violence, saying hospitals need to focus on the pandemic.

END OF SUMMARY.
