SUMMARY:
The House Judiciary Committee subpoenaed former White House staff secretary Rob Porter to testify about alleged Trump attempts to obstruct the Mueller Russia probe. Porter, along with ex‑campaign manager Corey Lewandowski and former deputy chief of staff Rick Dearborn, is scheduled to appear before the Democratic‑led panel, which says the testimony will help decide whether to recommend impeachment. Committee chair Jerrold Nadler argues that the evidence shows Trump tried to force White House counsel Don McGahn to fire the special counsel and then lie about it, and also pressured Attorney General Jeff Sessions to steer the investigation away from the 2016 campaign. The Mueller report quotes Trump threatening Porter, calling McGahn a “lying bastard,” and directing Porter to contact potential replacement Attorney General Rachel Brand to end the probe. Democrats stress that “no one is above the law,” warning that any other American would have faced prosecution for the same conduct. The White House has not yet indicated whether it will block Porter’s testimony, as it did with earlier aides. Porter left the White House in February 2018 after domestic‑abuse allegations.

END OF SUMMARY.
