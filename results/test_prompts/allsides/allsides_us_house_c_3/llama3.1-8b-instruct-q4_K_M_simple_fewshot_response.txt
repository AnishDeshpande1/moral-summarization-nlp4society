SUMMARY:
The House Judiciary Committee has subpoenaed former White House aide Rob Porter to testify about alleged efforts by President Trump to impede a federal probe into Russian interference in the 2016 presidential election. This is the third subpoena issued to a former Trump adviser within two weeks, following similar subpoenas to Corey Lewandowski and Rick Dearborn. The committee's chairman, Jerrold Nadler, stated that the proceedings will help determine whether to recommend Trump's impeachment to the full House of Representatives. Porter was a key witness in the Mueller report, which documented Trump's efforts to obstruct justice by directing McGahn to fire Mueller and then ordering him to lie about it. The Democrats view Porter's testimony as crucial to their investigation into the Trump presidency and are seeking to uncover evidence of Trump's alleged attempts to pressure Sessions to direct Mueller's investigation away from his campaign.
