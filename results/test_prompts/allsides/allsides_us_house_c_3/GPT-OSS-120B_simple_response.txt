SUMMARY:  
The House Judiciary Committee subpoenaed former White House staff secretary Rob Porter to testify about alleged Trump attempts to obstruct the Mueller Russia investigation. Porter, along with ex‑adviser Corey Lewandowski and former Deputy Chief of Staff Rick Dearborn, will appear before the Democratic‑led panel, which says the hearings will help decide whether to recommend impeachment. Committee Chair Jerrold Nadler stressed the moral principle that “no one is above the law,” arguing that any other American would have faced prosecution for the conduct detailed in the Mueller report. The subpoena follows revelations that Trump allegedly ordered White House counsel Don McGahn to fire the special counsel, then instructed McGahn to lie about it, and pressured Attorney General Jeff Sessions to steer the probe away from the campaign. Porter is said to have been asked by Trump to pressure McGahn and to contact potential replacement officials, actions the report characterizes as attempts to end the investigation. Porter, who left the White House amid domestic‑abuse allegations, has not yet commented.  

END OF SUMMARY.
