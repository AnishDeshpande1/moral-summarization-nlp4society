SUMMARY:
The US House Judiciary Committee has subpoenaed three former Trump aides, including Rob Porter, to testify about alleged efforts by President Donald Trump to impede the investigation into Russian interference in the 2016 presidential election. Chairman Jerrold Nadler stated that the proceedings will help determine whether to recommend Trump's impeachment to the full House of Representatives. The Democrats view these witnesses as crucial to their investigation, as they have firsthand knowledge of key episodes described in Special Counsel Robert Mueller's report. Porter, a former White House staff secretary, witnessed several incidents involving Trump's attempts to obstruct justice, including directing McGahn to fire Mueller and then ordering him to lie about it. Nadler emphasized that "no one is above the law," and that Trump would have been prosecuted based on the evidence uncovered by Mueller if he were any other American.

END OF SUMMARY.
