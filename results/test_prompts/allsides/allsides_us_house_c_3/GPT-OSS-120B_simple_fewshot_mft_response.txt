SUMMARY:
The House Judiciary Committee subpoenaed former White House staff secretary Rob Porter to testify about alleged attempts by President Donald Trump to subvert legal authority and obstruct the Mueller investigation into Russian election interference. Democrats say the testimony will expose Trump’s misuse of power—pressuring former counsel Don McGahn to fire the special counsel, demanding false denials, and trying to replace Attorney General Jeff Sessions—to shield himself from accountability. The committee’s chair, Jerrold Nadler, framed the probe as a matter of fairness, insisting that no one is above the law and that Trump’s conduct amounts to cheating the justice system and harming the nation’s democratic institutions. Porter’s alleged recollections—Trump calling McGahn a “lying bastard” and seeking a replacement to end the probe—illustrate a pattern of authority abuse that endangers the rule of law and the public’s trust. The subpoena follows two other recent subpoenas of Trump advisers, underscoring a broader effort to hold the president accountable for actions viewed as both harmful and unjust.

END OF SUMMARY.
