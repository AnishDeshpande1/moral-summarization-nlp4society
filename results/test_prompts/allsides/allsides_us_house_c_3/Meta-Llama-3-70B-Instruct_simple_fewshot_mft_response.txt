SUMMARY:

The House Judiciary Committee has subpoenaed former White House aide Rob Porter to testify about President Trump's alleged efforts to impede the federal probe into Russian interference in the 2016 election. Porter, a key witness in the Mueller report, is expected to provide insight into Trump's attempts to obstruct justice, including directing Don McGahn to fire the special counsel and then ordering him to lie about it. Democrats argue that Trump's actions constitute a clear case of obstruction of justice, and that Porter's testimony is crucial in determining whether to recommend Trump's impeachment. The White House has previously blocked the testimony of McGahn and other former aides, but it remains unclear whether they will attempt to do so with Porter and Dearborn. The committee's investigation is a critical step in holding the President accountable for his actions and ensuring that no one is above the law.

END OF SUMMARY.
