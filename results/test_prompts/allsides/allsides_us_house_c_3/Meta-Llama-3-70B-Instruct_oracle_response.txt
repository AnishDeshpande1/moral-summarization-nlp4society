SUMMARY: The U.S. House Judiciary Committee has subpoenaed former White House aide Rob Porter to testify about President Donald Trump's alleged efforts to impede a federal probe into Russian interference in the 2016 presidential election. Porter, who witnessed several episodes of Trump's attempts to obstruct justice, is set to appear before the committee on September 17. Democrats want to determine if Trump's actions, including directing White House Counsel Don McGahn to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller and then ordering him to lie about it, warrant impeachment. Porter's testimony is crucial, as he was involved in Trump's alleged attempts to pressure then-Attorney General Jeff Sessions to direct the Russia investigation away from the 2016 Trump campaign. The committee's chairman, Jerrold Nadler, emphasized that "no one is above the law" and that Trump's actions would have led to prosecution for any other American. Porter's testimony may shed light on Trump's alleged lying, abuse of power, and obstruction of justice. END OF SUMMARY.
