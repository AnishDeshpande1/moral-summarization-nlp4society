SUMMARY: The U.S. House Judiciary Committee subpoenaed former White House staff secretary Rob Porter to testify about alleged Trump efforts to obstruct the Mueller Russia investigation. Porter joins former Trump campaign manager Corey Lewandowski and ex‑Deputy Chief of Staff Rick Dearborn, all scheduled to appear on Sept. 17. Chairman Jerrold Nadler said their testimony will help decide whether to recommend impeachment. Mueller’s report cites Porter as a key witness to Trump’s attempts to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller, pressure White House Counsel Don McGahn to lie about it, and seek a replacement for Attorney General Jeff Sessions to halt the probe. Porter recalled Trump calling McGahn a “lying bastard” and asking Porter to contact former Associate Attorney General Rachel Brand as a potential replacement for Sessions. Porter left the White House in Feb. 2018 after domestic‑abuse allegations. It remains unclear if the White House will try to block his or Dearborn’s testimony, as it did with McGahn. END OF SUMMARY.
