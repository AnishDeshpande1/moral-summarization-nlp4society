**SUMMARY:**  
The U.S. House Judiciary Committee subpoenaed former White House aide Rob Porter to testify about alleged attempts by President Donald Trump to impede the Mueller Russia probe. Porter, along with former campaign manager Corey Lewandowski and ex‑Deputy Chief of Staff Rick Dearborn, is scheduled to appear before the committee, which Chairman Jerrold Nadler says will help decide whether to recommend impeachment. Democrats describe Trump’s conduct as a key, damaging effort to obstruct justice—pressuring White House Counsel Don McGahn to fire the special counsel, then ordering a false denial, and urging Porter to contact potential replacement Attorney General Rachel Brand. Nadler stressed that “no one is above the law” and that any other American would have been prosecuted based on Mueller’s evidence. Porter, who left the White House after accusations of domestic abuse, could not be reached for comment. The White House’s response to the subpoenas remains uncertain.
