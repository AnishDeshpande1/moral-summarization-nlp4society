SUMMARY:

The House Judiciary Committee has subpoenaed former White House aide Rob Porter to testify about President Trump's alleged efforts to impede the federal probe into Russian interference in the 2016 presidential election. Porter, a former White House staff secretary, witnessed several episodes laid out in the Mueller report, including Trump's attempts to obstruct justice by directing Don McGahn to fire the special counsel and then ordering him to lie about it. Democrats want to determine whether to recommend Trump's impeachment to the full House of Representatives. Porter's testimony is crucial, as he took contemporaneous notes during several incidents that interest Democrats. The move comes after the committee subpoenaed two other former Trump advisers, Corey Lewandowski and Rick Dearborn, to testify about Trump's alleged efforts to pressure then-Attorney General Jeff Sessions to direct the Russia investigation away from the 2016 Trump campaign. The subpoenas are part of the committee's investigation into Trump's presidency and potential abuses of power.

END OF SUMMARY.
