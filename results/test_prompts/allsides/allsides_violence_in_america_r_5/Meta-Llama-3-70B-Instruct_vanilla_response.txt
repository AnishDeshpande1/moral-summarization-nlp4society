The attack has reignited debates over gun control and terrorism, with some calling for a change in policy to allow troops to be armed at military facilities. The incident has also become a part of the 2016 presidential campaign, with Obama's policies on terrorism being scrutinized. The FBI is conducting a thorough investigation, but authorities are treating the shooting as an "act of domestic terrorism." The attack is a reminder of the growing threat from the Islamic State, with federal authorities having charged 62 people with planning attacks on the homeland.
