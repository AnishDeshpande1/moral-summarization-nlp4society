SUMMARY:

President Obama called the murder of four Marines in Tennessee "heartbreaking" and urged Americans not to jump to conclusions, but Republicans were quick to blame him for the domestic terrorism. Gov. Bobby Jindal termed the killing as terrorism and said Obama needs to confront the dangers posed by "evil" jihadists. The shooting reignited the debate over guns at military facilities, with an advocacy group calling for a change in policy. The FBI identified the gunman as Mohammad Youssuf Abdulazeez, a naturalized U.S. citizen, who shot at a military recruiting center before heading to a Navy operations support center, killing four Marines before being killed himself. Authorities are treating the shooting as an "act of domestic terrorism." The threat from the Islamic State is growing, with federal authorities charging 62 people with planning attacks on the homeland. The shooting bore a resemblance to other gun attacks, highlighting the dangers and threats posed by radical Islamic terrorism.
