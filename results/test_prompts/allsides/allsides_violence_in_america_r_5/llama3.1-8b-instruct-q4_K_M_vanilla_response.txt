SUMMARY:

President Obama called the murder of four Marines in Tennessee "heartbreaking" and urged Americans not to jump to conclusions. However, Republicans quickly blamed him for the shooting, which investigators termed domestic terrorism. Louisiana Governor Bobby Jindal said the killing was an example of radical Islamic terrorism and accused the White House of being dishonest about the threat.

The FBI identified the gunman as 24-year-old Mohammad Youssuf Abdulazeez, a naturalized US citizen who shot at a military recruiting center before killing the four Marines. The shooting reignited debates over guns in military facilities and terrorism policies.

Lawmakers are now debating how to confront violent extremists, with Democrats saying white supremacists and anti-government extremists pose more of a danger than radicalized Muslims. The incident has become part of the 2016 presidential campaign, with some candidates using it to criticize Obama's policies.

END OF SUMMARY.
