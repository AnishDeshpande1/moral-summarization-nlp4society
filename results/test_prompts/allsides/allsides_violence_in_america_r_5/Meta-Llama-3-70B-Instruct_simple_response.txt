SUMMARY:

President Obama called the murder of four Marines in Tennessee "heartbreaking" and urged Americans not to jump to conclusions, but Republicans were quick to blame him for the domestic terrorism. Gov. Bobby Jindal accused Obama of not confronting the dangers posed by "evil" jihadists, claiming that radical Islam is at war with the US. The shooting reignited debates over guns at military facilities and terrorism, with Obama's policies under scrutiny. The FBI identified the gunman as Mohammad Youssuf Abdulazeez, a naturalized US citizen, and authorities are treating the shooting as an "act of domestic terrorism." While the motives are still unclear, the attack highlights the growing threat of Islamic State-inspired terrorism in the US. Republicans argue that Obama's administration has been too slow to acknowledge the threat, while Democrats point out that white supremacists and anti-government extremists also pose a danger.
