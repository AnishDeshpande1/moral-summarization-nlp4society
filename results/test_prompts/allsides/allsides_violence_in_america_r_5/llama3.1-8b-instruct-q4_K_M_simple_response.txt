SUMMARY:

President Obama called the murder of four Marines in Tennessee "heartbreaking" and urged Americans not to jump to conclusions, but Republicans quickly blamed him for what investigators termed domestic terrorism. Louisiana Governor Bobby Jindal accused the White House of being out of touch with the threat posed by radical Islamic terrorism. The shooting reignited debates over guns at military facilities and terrorism policies. The FBI identified the gunman as Mohammad Youssuf Abdulazeez, a naturalized US citizen born in Kuwait, who shot at a military recruiting center before killing four Marines.

The incident is being treated as an act of domestic terrorism by authorities, with some lawmakers linking it to online propaganda or extremist influence. Rep. Adam Schiff noted that it's too early to say if the gunman had ties to al Qaeda or ISIS. The shooting highlights growing concerns over lone-wolf incidents and the threat posed by the Islamic State.

The debate is likely to be a major part of the 2016 presidential campaign, with Republicans accusing Obama of not taking terrorism seriously enough.

END OF SUMMARY.
