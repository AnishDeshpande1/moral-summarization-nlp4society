SUMMARY: President Obama condemned the killing of four Marines in Tennessee as “heartbreaking,” urging restraint while investigators treated the incident as domestic terrorism. Republican candidates, including GOP hopeful Gov. Bobby Jindal, quickly labeled the attack as jihadist terrorism and criticized the Obama administration for not confronting “radical Islam.” The shooter, 24‑year‑old Mohammad Youssuf Abdulazeez, a naturalized U.S. citizen born in Kuwait, opened fire at a military recruiting center and a Navy support facility before being killed. The FBI and U.S. Attorney’s Office are investigating motives, with early indications pointing to extremist influences. The event reignited debates over gun policies at military sites and the broader focus of U.S. counter‑terrorism, as Democrats argue that white‑supremacist and anti‑government threats may be more pressing. Congressional leaders from both parties called for vigilance against all forms of extremism. The incident adds to a rising tally of alleged Islamic State‑linked plots, highlighting terrorism’s role in the 2016 presidential campaign. END OF SUMMARY.
