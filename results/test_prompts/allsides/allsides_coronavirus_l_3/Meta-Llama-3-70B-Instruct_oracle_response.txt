SUMMARY:

The COVID-19 pandemic has unleashed a unique threat to global stability, with disinformation and misinformation spreading rapidly online. As governments implement social distancing measures to slow the virus's spread, a parallel outbreak of false claims and conspiracy theories has emerged. These nefarious lies, often perpetuated by social media and even the White House, pose a dangerous hindrance to public safety. Repeatedly, Donald Trump has lied and misled the public about the virus, imperiling countless lives. Disinformation not only misinforms but also exhausts critical thinking, destroys trust in objective truth, and sows seeds of uncertainty. It's essential to reject falsehoods and instead seek accurate information to inform and protect the public. We cannot afford to perpetuate fallacies that contribute to public confusion and ignorance. Every day counts, and it's our responsibility to hold ourselves accountable for not spreading disinformation, whether it comes from the web or the president himself. By doing so, we can educate the public on the facts and ways to protect themselves, ultimately saving lives.

END OF SUMMARY.
