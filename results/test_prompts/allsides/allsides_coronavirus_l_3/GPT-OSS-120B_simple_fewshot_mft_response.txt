SUMMARY:
The article warns that COVID‑19 disinformation—ranging from bogus health tips to conspiracy theories about 5G and governmental plots—poses a grave threat to public safety. By spreading falsehoods, both online actors and the White House undermine trustworthy authority, erode citizens’ ability to protect one another, and cause real harm through confused or dangerous behaviors. The piece highlights numerous false statements made by President Donald Trump, such as claims that warm weather will end the virus, that a vaccine is imminent, and that the FDA has approved chloroquine, all of which subvert expert guidance and jeopardize lives. This manipulation of information is framed as an unfair cheating of the public: it distorts facts, sows doubt, and weakens the collective consensus needed for effective pandemic response. The author urges individuals to reject and not amplify these lies, stressing that caring for the community requires demanding truthful, evidence‑based communication from authorities and rejecting any rhetoric that cheats the public out of accurate knowledge. Only by upholding honest authority, protecting health, and insisting on fairness can society overcome the deadly “infodemic” accompanying COVID‑19.
