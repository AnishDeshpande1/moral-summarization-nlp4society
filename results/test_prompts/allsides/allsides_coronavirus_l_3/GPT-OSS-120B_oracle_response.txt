SUMMARY:  
Disinformation has become a grave threat that compounds the COVID‑19 crisis, turning an already dangerous pandemic into a missteps‑laden response fraught with confusion. As the virus spreads, nefarious claims—ranging from garlic cures to 5G conspiracy theories—originate online and even from the White House, where repeated lies and disingenuous statements have muddied public understanding. This mismanagement and the absence of clear leadership have filled the information void with falsehoods that obscure facts, exhaust critical thinking, and destroy consensus. The resulting fallout—doubt, panic, and squandered resources—imperils lives, as thousands have already died and many more face harm from misguided health decisions. To protect the public, we must halt the proliferation of misinformation, reject the push of bankrupt theories, and cease abetting the spread of lies. Only through rigorous, fact‑based education can we restore trust, prevent further consequences, and ensure a coordinated, effective response to the virus.  

END OF SUMMARY.
