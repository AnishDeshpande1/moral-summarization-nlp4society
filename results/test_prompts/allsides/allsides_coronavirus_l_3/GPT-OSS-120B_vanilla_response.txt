SUMMARY: The article warns that alongside the COVID‑19 health crisis, a parallel “infodemic” of misinformation and disinformation is threatening public safety. False claims—from garlic cures to 5G conspiracy theories and lab‑origin rumors—have spread rapidly online, amplified by social‑media virality and even statements from President Donald Trump, who has repeatedly downplayed the virus, promised unrealistic vaccine timelines, and promoted unproven treatments. This misinformation erodes trust in facts, hampers coherent public response, and endangers lives by confusing people about prevention and treatment. The piece urges individuals to reject unverified claims, rely on credible sources, and help stop the spread of false information to support effective pandemic management. END OF SUMMARY.
