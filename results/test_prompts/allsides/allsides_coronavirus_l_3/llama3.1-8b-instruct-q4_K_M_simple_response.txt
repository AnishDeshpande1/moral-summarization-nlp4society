SUMMARY:

In the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic, a growing threat is emerging: disinformation. Misleading information about the virus is spreading rapidly online, often fueled by social media platforms and misinformation from places of authority, such as the White House. President Trump's repeated lies and misleading statements have contributed to this problem, imperiling countless lives with each passing day.

Disinformation not only misinforms but also exhausts the public's critical thinking on a mass level, destroying trust in objective truth. In a time when accurate information is crucial for public health and safety, disinformation is a deadly crisis that can have serious consequences.

It is essential to distinguish between cautious skepticism and blindly parroting falsehoods. We must hold ourselves responsible for not abetting the proliferation of disinformation, whether online or from places of authority. By promoting factual information and critical analysis, we can combat this threat and support public health efforts.

END OF SUMMARY.
