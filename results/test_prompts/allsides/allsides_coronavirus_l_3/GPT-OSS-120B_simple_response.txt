SUMMARY: The article warns that COVID‑19 is not only a health crisis but also an “information crisis” in which rampant misinformation and disinformation threaten public safety and democratic trust. It describes false claims—from home remedies to 5G conspiracies—and highlights how pandemic‑related lies from political leaders, especially President Donald Trump, have amplified confusion, eroded confidence in objective truth, and hampered effective public‑health responses. The author argues that spreading unverified or deliberately false information undermines collective reasoning, fuels panic, and wastes critical resources, urging individuals to reject both baseless online theories and official falsehoods. Moral responsibility, the piece insists, lies with every citizen to stop amplifying falsehoods, support factual communication, and thereby protect lives and preserve social cohesion during the pandemic. END OF SUMMARY.
