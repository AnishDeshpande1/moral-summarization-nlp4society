SUMMARY:
The article warns that a parallel “infodemic” of misinformation and disinformation is threatening public health as COVID‑19 spreads. False claims—from garlic cures to 5G conspiracies and the notion that social‑distancing is a pretext for martial law—are proliferating online, amplified by social‑media virality. The piece argues that this falsehood flood is especially dangerous when it originates from high‑level officials; it cites numerous false statements made by President Donald Trump about the virus’s seasonality, vaccine timelines, and unproven treatments, describing them as harmful lies that erode trust in facts. Disinformation, the author contends, not only misleads but also exhausts collective critical thinking, undermines confidence in objective truth, and sabotages the consensus needed for effective public‑health action. The moral imperative presented is clear: individuals must refuse to spread unverified claims, demand accurate information, and hold leaders accountable, because every hour of confusion can cost lives. By curbing the spread of falsehoods—whether from the internet or the White House—we can protect public health, preserve trust, and support the coordinated response needed to combat the pandemic.
END OF SUMMARY.
