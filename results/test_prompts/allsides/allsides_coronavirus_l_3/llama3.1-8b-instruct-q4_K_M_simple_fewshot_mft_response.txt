SUMMARY:

The COVID-19 pandemic has given rise to a new threat: disinformation. As the virus spreads, misinformation about its causes, symptoms, and treatments has exploded online, threatening public health and safety. Social media platforms have enabled this spread, with false claims and conspiracy theories being shared rapidly across the globe. The author argues that disinformation is not just a nuisance, but a deadly threat to public health, as it exhausts critical thinking and obscures truth. This is particularly concerning given the lack of clear facts and leadership from the federal government during the crisis. Donald Trump's repeated lies about the virus have contributed to this problem, imperiling countless lives with each passing day. The author emphasizes that disinformation does not just misinform, but destroys public consensus, critical thinking, and ultimately, public health efforts. In a time where every hour counts, it is essential to hold ourselves responsible for not abetting the proliferation of disinformation.

END OF SUMMARY.
