SUMMARY:
President Trump unveiled an executive order to create a "National Garden of American Heroes" featuring statues of prominent Americans, including Martin Luther King Jr., Abraham Lincoln, and Harriet Tubman. The garden will be established as part of a broader effort to preserve America's history and heritage in the face of growing calls to tear down Confederate statues and other racist iconography. Trump framed his initiative as a response to the "far-left fascism" that seeks to "overthrow the American Revolution" by erasing national symbols and memories. The executive order establishes a task force to select locations for the garden, which will be open prior to July 4, 2026. Trump's move is seen as an attempt to counter efforts to dismantle Confederate monuments and promote a more inclusive understanding of American history.

END OF SUMMARY.
