SUMMARY:
The White House issued an executive order creating a “National Garden of American Heroes,” a monument‑building project meant to reinforce respect for national authority and heritage. By installing statues of celebrated figures—from founding fathers and civil‑rights leaders to frontier icons—the order frames these monuments as sacred symbols that teach responsibility and inspire loyalty to the American story, warning that vandalism or removal of such statues desecrates a shared, sanctified inheritance. The order condemns the recent wave of statue‑toppling as a harmful, unfair attack on the nation’s moral fabric, portraying activists as subversive actors who betray the country’s values and cheat future generations out of their historical legacy. A task force will use Interior Department funds to locate and construct the garden, with a temporary traveling collection to broaden public access, all slated for opening before the nation’s 250th‑anniversary in 2026. The administration’s language links the defense of these monuments to protecting the nation’s unity, moral order, and the sanctity of its collective memory against a “far‑left cultural revolution” that seeks to rewrite history.

END OF SUMMARY.
