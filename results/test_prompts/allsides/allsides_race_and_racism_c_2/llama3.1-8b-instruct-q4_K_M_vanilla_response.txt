SUMMARY:

President Trump announced an executive order to create the "National Garden of American Heroes", which will feature statues of prominent Americans, including John Adams, Susan B. Anthony, and Martin Luther King Jr. The garden is intended to promote national unity and patriotism, particularly in response to calls to remove Confederate statues across the country. The executive order establishes a task force to develop options for the creation of the garden, with 60 days to submit a report to the White House.

The order also addresses concerns about the destruction of monuments, including those honoring former presidents George Washington and Thomas Jefferson who owned slaves but were not part of the Confederacy. Trump criticized protesters who are tearing down Confederate statues, accusing them of wanting to "overthrow the American Revolution" and fundamentally change the country. The National Garden is set to open prior to July 4, 2026, the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence.

END OF SUMMARY.
