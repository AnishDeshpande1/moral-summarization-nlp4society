SUMMARY:

President Trump has announced an executive order to create a "National Garden of American Heroes" featuring statues of prominent Americans, amidst calls to tear down Confederate statues across the country. The garden will include statues of notable figures such as John Adams, Susan B. Anthony, and Martin Luther King Jr. The executive order emphasizes the importance of preserving the memory of our American story and stirring a spirit of responsibility for future generations. It also addresses current protests and vandalism, underscoring that destroying monuments is a desecration of our common inheritance. Trump has railed against those tearing down statues, accusing them of wanting to overthrow the American Revolution and fundamentally change the country. He warned that the left-wing cultural revolution is designed to destroy our national heritage, perpetuating poverty, violence, and hunger. The task force has 60 days to submit a report detailing options for the creation of the National Garden, which will be open prior to July 4, 2026.

END OF SUMMARY.
