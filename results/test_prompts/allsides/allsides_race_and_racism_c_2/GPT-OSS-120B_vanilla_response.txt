SUMMARY: The White House issued an executive order on Friday to create a “National Garden of American Heroes,” a new monument featuring statues of notable Americans such as John Adams, Susan B. Anthony, Frederick Douglass, Martin Luther King Jr., Harriet Tubman, and others. The order establishes a Task Force for Building and Rebuilding Monuments to American Heroes, which must submit a location and design plan within 60 days and use Interior Department funds. The garden is slated to open before July 4, 2026, the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence, and will also maintain a traveling collection of statues for public display nationwide. The order condemns recent vandalism and removal of Confederate and other historic statues, arguing that monuments belong to all generations and should not be destroyed due to “fashionable political passions.” President Trump, speaking at Mount Rushmore, warned that left‑wing cultural forces aim to erase America’s heritage by tearing down statues. END OF SUMMARY.
