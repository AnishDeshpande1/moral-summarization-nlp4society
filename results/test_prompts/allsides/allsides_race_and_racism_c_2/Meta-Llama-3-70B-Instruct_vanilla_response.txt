SUMMARY:

The White House has unveiled an executive order to create a "National Garden of American Heroes" featuring statues of prominent Americans. The garden will include statues of figures such as John Adams, Susan B. Anthony, and Martin Luther King Jr. The executive order establishes a task force to submit a report on potential locations and options for the garden's creation within 60 days. The garden is set to open before July 4, 2026. The order also addresses the current debate over Confederate statues, with President Trump accusing those who want to tear them down of wanting to "overthrow the American Revolution" and fundamentally change the country. Trump emphasized that the statues belong to generations past and future, and should not be destroyed at the whim of current political passions.

END OF SUMMARY.
