SUMMARY:

The White House has unveiled an executive order to create a "National Garden of American Heroes" featuring statues of prominent Americans. The move comes as the nation grapples with calls to tear down Confederate statues and address racist iconography. The executive order establishes a task force to create the garden, which will include statues of notable figures such as John Adams, Frederick Douglass, and Martin Luther King Jr. The order also condemns the destruction of monuments, stating that they belong to future generations and are part of our common inheritance. In a speech at Mount Rushmore, President Trump railed against those tearing down statues, accusing them of wanting to "overthrow the American Revolution" and fundamentally change the country. He warned against a "left-wing cultural revolution" that seeks to destroy our national heritage.

END OF SUMMARY.
