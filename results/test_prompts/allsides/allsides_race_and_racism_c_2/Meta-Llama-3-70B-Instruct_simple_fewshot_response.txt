SUMMARY:

President Trump has announced an executive order to create a "National Garden of American Heroes" featuring statues of prominent Americans. The garden will include statues of figures such as John Adams, Susan B. Anthony, and Martin Luther King Jr. The executive order also addresses the current controversy over Confederate statues, with Trump condemning those who seek to tear them down as "far-left fascists" who want to "overthrow the American Revolution." Trump's move is seen as a response to the growing calls to remove Confederate statues and other racist iconography across the country. The task force established by the executive order has 60 days to submit a report on options for the creation of the National Garden, which is set to be open by July 4, 2026. Trump's speech at Mount Rushmore, where he announced the executive order, was marked by a strong defense of American heritage and a rejection of what he sees as a "left-wing cultural revolution."

END OF SUMMARY.
