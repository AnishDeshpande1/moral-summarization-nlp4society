SUMMARY:
The White House issued an executive order creating a “National Garden of American Heroes,” a permanent site of statues that will **preserve** the memory of figures who made great **sacrifices** and **placed** their lives in **service** to the nation. The order forms a Task Force to use Interior Department funds to design the garden, with a report due in 60 days and an opening before July 4, 2026, the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence. Statues will include John Adams, Susan B. Anthony, Frederick Douglass, Martin Luther King Jr., Harriet Tubman, and others, while a traveling collection will be **displayed** at sites across the country. The order denounces recent **protests** that have **vandalized** and **destroyed** monuments, calling such acts a **desecrate** of the nation’s common inheritance. It warns that activists who **destroy** statues of Confederate leaders, or even those who **owned** slaves such as George Washington and Thomas Jefferson, seek to **overthrow** the American Revolution. President Trump condemned a “far‑left fascism” that would **censor**, **banish**, **blacklist**, **persecute**, and **punish** anyone who does not **recite** its **allegiance** or **follow** its **rituals**, arguing this cultural **revolution** would undo the progress that lifted humanity out of **poverty**, **violence**, and **hunger**. END OF SUMMARY.
