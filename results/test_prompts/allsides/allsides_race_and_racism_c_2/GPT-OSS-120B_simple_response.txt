SUMMARY: The White House issued an executive order creating a “National Garden of American Heroes,” a new monument complex meant to honor a broad spectrum of historic Americans—from founding fathers and pioneers to civil‑rights leaders and innovators. The order frames statues as “silent teachers” and moral beacons, arguing that preserving them safeguards a shared inheritance and instills responsibility in future generations. It condemns recent vandalism and calls to remove Confederate or other controversial monuments, labeling such acts as desecration driven by “far‑left fascism” that seeks to erase the nation’s heritage. A task force will, within 60 days, propose sites and plans, with the garden slated to open before July 4, 2026, the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence, and will also maintain a traveling collection for public access. By juxtaposing revered figures like Frederick Douglass, Martin Luther King Jr., and Harriet Tubman with founders who owned slaves, the order asserts that even flawed heroes deserve remembrance, warning that discarding monuments threatens the continuity of American civilization. END OF SUMMARY.
