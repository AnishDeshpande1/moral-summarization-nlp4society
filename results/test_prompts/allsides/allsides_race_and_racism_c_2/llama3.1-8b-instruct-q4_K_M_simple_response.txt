SUMMARY:

President Trump announced an executive order to create the "National Garden of American Heroes", featuring statues of prominent Americans, in response to calls to remove Confederate statues across the country. The garden will include statues of notable figures such as Martin Luther King Jr., Abraham Lincoln, and Harriet Tubman, among others. According to the executive order, these statues are a way to preserve the memory of America's story and inspire future generations.

The move is framed by Trump as a counter to what he sees as a "left-wing cultural revolution" aimed at overthrowing the American Revolution and destroying national heritage. He argues that tearing down statues is a form of desecration, saying that these monuments belong to all Americans, past, present, and future. The task force established by the executive order has 60 days to submit a report detailing options for creating the National Garden.

END OF SUMMARY.
