SUMMARY:
President Trump unveiled an executive order creating a “National Garden of American Heroes,” a new monument park featuring statues of celebrated figures such as John Adams, Susan B. Anthony, Frederick Douglass, Martin Luther King Jr., Abraham Lincoln, Ronald Reagan and Harriet Tubman. The order establishes a task force, funded by the Interior Department, to plan the garden’s location and to maintain a traveling collection of statues for public display, with a goal to open before July 4, 2026—the nation’s 250th Independence anniversary. By emphasizing that statues are “silent teachers” and “our common inheritance,” the administration frames the garden as a moral duty to honor the virtues and sacrifices of America’s heroes, despite their flaws. The order also condemns recent vandalism and removal of monuments, arguing that tearing down statues—whether Confederate or of other historical figures—desecrates shared heritage and succumbs to a “far‑left cultural revolution” seeking to erase the nation’s foundational story. Trump’s rhetoric casts the preservation of monuments as a defense of American values against a radical, censorious agenda. END OF SUMMARY.
