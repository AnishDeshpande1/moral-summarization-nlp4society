SUMMARY:

Following the Orlando nightclub shooting, the US is grappling with its problem of mass shootings. Australia's experience in banning rapid-fire rifles and tightening gun regulations in 1996 serves as an example to consider stricter measures. Four steps are proposed: prohibiting those on the "no-fly" list from buying guns, requiring background checks for all sales, strengthening the background check system, and encouraging victims and others to report prohibited purchasers. Additionally, a return to the federal assault weapons ban is suggested, with loopholes tightened. The author argues that restricting access to high-capacity magazines and assault-style rifles is necessary due to their lethal potential.

The gun industry's reliance on sales of more powerful guns to existing owners is also highlighted as a factor in opposing stricter regulations. Critics like John Lott argue that most self-defense situations require only a revolver, but the author counters that this ignores the reality of mass shootings and the need for more restrictive measures.

END OF SUMMARY.
