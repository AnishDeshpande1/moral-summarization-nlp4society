SUMMARY:

In the wake of the Orlando shooting, House Democrats staged a sit-in in Congress to urge action on mass shootings. America isn't alone in suffering from this problem, but it's well past time to examine steps to diminish the chances of more mayhem. The US should take four key steps: prohibit individuals on the "no-fly" list from buying guns, require background checks for all gun sales, strengthen the background check system by including prohibited purchasers and allowing sufficient evaluation time, and encourage victims to report misconduct to add individuals to the prohibited purchaser list. These measures are long overdue, especially in light of videos urging extremists to take advantage of America's lax gun laws. Additionally, a return to the federal assault weapons ban is necessary, as these deadly guns have no place in civilian hands. The National Rifle Association's claim that assault weapons are "protection weapons" permitted by the Second Amendment is not credible. By taking action and tightening gun regulations, we can prevent more shootings like Orlando and keep our communities safe from killing sprees.

END OF SUMMARY.
