SUMMARY:

After the Orlando nightclub shooting, there is renewed debate about addressing mass shootings in the US. Australia's experience shows that dramatic action can lead to results: no mass public shootings in 20 years after a ban on rapid-fire rifles and tightening of gun laws. The US should consider four steps: prohibiting those on the "no-fly" list from buying guns, requiring background checks for all sales, strengthening the background check system, and ensuring prohibited purchasers are added to the rolls. Additionally, returning to the federal assault weapons ban is necessary, as modern assault weapons are as deadly as machine guns used in the 1920s.

The article argues that these measures are not extreme, but rather common-sense steps to prevent mass shootings. Gun sellers object to tighter laws because they need to sell more guns to existing customers, which can be done by promoting more powerful and faster-shooting weapons. The author concludes that assault weapons should be banned due to their deadliness and the fact that a revolver is sufficient for self-defense in most cases.

END OF SUMMARY.
