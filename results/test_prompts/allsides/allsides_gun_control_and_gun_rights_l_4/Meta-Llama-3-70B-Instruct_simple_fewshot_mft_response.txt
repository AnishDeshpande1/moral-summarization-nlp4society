SUMMARY:

In the wake of the Orlando nightclub shooting, the US is once again forced to confront its problem with mass shootings. While a complete ban on rapid-fire rifles like Australia's may not be politically feasible, there are four crucial steps that can be taken to diminish the chances of more mayhem. Firstly, anyone on the "no-fly" list should be a prohibited purchaser. Secondly, federal law must require background checks for all gun sales. Thirdly, the background check system needs to be strengthened by including all prohibited purchasers and giving the FBI sufficient time to evaluate purchasers. Lastly, the police, prosecutors, and the public must ensure that all prohibited purchasers are properly added to the rolls. By taking these steps, the US can prevent guns from falling into the wrong hands and reduce the harm caused by mass shootings. The gun lobby's opposition to these measures is a clear example of subverting the authority of the government to prioritize profits over people's lives.

END OF SUMMARY.
