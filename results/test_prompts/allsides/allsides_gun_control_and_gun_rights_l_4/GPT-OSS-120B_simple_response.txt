SUMMARY: The article argues that America’s moral duty to prevent mass shootings demands swift, commonsense gun reforms, echoing Australia’s successful post‑1996 crackdown. It frames the issue as a matter of public safety versus profit‑driven gun interests, urging four specific actions: (1) bar anyone on the terrorist “no‑fly” list from buying guns; (2) require universal background checks for every sale; (3) strengthen the check system by ensuring all prohibited persons are entered promptly and giving investigators adequate time; and (4) mobilize police, prosecutors, and citizens to report violent or abusive behavior so offenders are added to the prohibited list. The piece also calls for reinstating a federal assault‑weapons ban, targeting high‑capacity, rapid‑fire firearms like the AR‑15, which it deems morally indefensible for civilian use. By highlighting the stark contrast between a declining hunting market and the gun industry’s push for more lethal weapons, the author frames resistance as driven by profit, not public protection. The moral imperative is clear: protect innocent lives by denying dangerous weapons to those who pose a threat. END OF SUMMARY.
