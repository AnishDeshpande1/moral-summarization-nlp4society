SUMMARY:

In the wake of the Orlando nightclub shooting, the US is again faced with the urgent need to address its mass shooting problem. Australia's 1996 ban on rapid-fire rifles and tightened gun regulations have led to 20 years without a mass public shooting, a stark contrast to the US. It's well past time to take action to diminish the chances of more mayhem. Four steps can be taken: prohibit suspected terrorists from purchasing guns, require federal background checks for all gun sales, strengthen the background check system, and encourage the public to report prohibited purchasers. Additionally, reinstating the federal assault weapons ban with tightened loopholes can help keep deadly weapons out of the wrong hands. The gun industry's profit-driven motives must not be allowed to prioritize over people's lives. It's time to take action to prevent further suffering and mayhem.

END OF SUMMARY.
