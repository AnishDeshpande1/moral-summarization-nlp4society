SUMMARY:
The article argues that the United States must restore authoritative government action to protect citizens from the harm of mass shootings. Citing Australia’s successful post‑1996 gun bans, it urges Congress to re‑establish strong, enforceable regulations that safeguard the public. The author proposes four concrete steps: (1) add anyone on the “no‑fly” terrorist list to the prohibited‑purchaser roster; (2) require universal background checks for every gun sale; (3) strengthen the background‑check system so all prohibited individuals are entered promptly and given adequate review time; and (4) mobilize police, prosecutors and the public to ensure these prohibitions are consistently applied. The piece also calls for a return to a federal assault‑weapons ban, arguing that such weapons are excessively lethal and serve no legitimate defensive purpose, thus posing an unacceptable risk to community safety. By reinforcing authority through comprehensive legislation and enforcement, the author frames these measures as essential to caring for victims and preventing further tragedy. END OF SUMMARY.
