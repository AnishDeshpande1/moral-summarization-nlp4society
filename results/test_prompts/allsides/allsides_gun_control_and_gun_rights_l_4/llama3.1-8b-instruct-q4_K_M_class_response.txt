SUMMARY:

The recent shooting in Orlando has left the public wondering if there is any feasible path to dealing with mass shootings in America. Australia, which had a string of mass shootings before 1996, responded by banning rapid-fire rifles and tightening gun regulation, resulting in no mass public shootings for 20 years. The US should take similar steps to diminish the chances of mayhem. Four proposals are necessary: anyone on the "no-fly" list should be prohibited from purchasing guns; federal law must require background checks for all gun sales; the background check system needs to be strengthened by adding all prohibited purchasers and giving the FBI sufficient time to evaluate legitimacy; and the police, prosecutors, and public must take initiative to add prohibited purchasers to the rolls. Additionally, a return to the federal assault weapons ban is necessary, as these weapons are deadly and should be banned. Gun sellers object to tighter laws because they profit from selling more powerful and faster-shooting weapons.

END OF SUMMARY.
