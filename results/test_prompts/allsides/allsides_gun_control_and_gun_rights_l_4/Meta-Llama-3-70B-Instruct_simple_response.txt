SUMMARY: The recent mass shooting in Orlando has sparked a renewed debate on gun control in the US. In contrast to Australia, which implemented a complete ban on rapid-fire rifles and tightened gun regulations in 1996, resulting in no mass public shootings in 20 years, the US has failed to take significant action. The author proposes four steps to diminish the chances of more mayhem: prohibiting suspected terrorists from purchasing guns, requiring federal background checks for all gun sales, strengthening the background check system, and taking initiative to add prohibited purchasers to the rolls. Additionally, the author advocates for a return to the federal assault weapons ban, citing the deadly nature of modern assault weapons. The gun industry's profit-driven motives are seen as a major obstacle to reform, as they seek to promote the sale of more powerful weapons. By implementing these measures, the US can take a crucial step towards reducing the risk of mass shootings. END OF SUMMARY.
