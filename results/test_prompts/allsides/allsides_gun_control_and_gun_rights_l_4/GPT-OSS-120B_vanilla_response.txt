SUMMARY: The article discusses the ongoing debate over gun control in the United States following the Orlando nightclub shooting and a House Democrats sit‑in. It cites Australia’s 1996 gun reforms, which eliminated mass public shootings for two decades, as a model. The author proposes four immediate steps: (1) bar anyone on the “no‑fly” list from purchasing guns; (2) require universal background checks for all sales; (3) strengthen the background‑check system by ensuring all prohibited persons are entered and allowing sufficient time for FBI review; and (4) actively add violent abusers and other disqualifiers to the prohibited purchaser list. The piece also calls for reinstating a federal assault‑weapons ban and closing loopholes, arguing that modern assault rifles like the AR‑15 are excessively lethal and unnecessary for self‑defense. It critiques the gun industry’s push for more powerful weapons and dismisses claims that such arms are needed for protection. END OF SUMMARY.
