SUMMARY:

In the wake of the Orlando nightclub shooting, House Democrats staged a sit-in to push for stricter gun control laws. The US can learn from Australia, which banned rapid-fire rifles and tightened gun regulations in 1996, resulting in no mass public shootings in 20 years. The article proposes four steps to reduce the chances of mass shootings: 1) prohibit suspected terrorists from buying guns, 2) require federal background checks for all gun sales, 3) strengthen the background check system, and 4) ensure all prohibited purchasers are added to the rolls. Additionally, the article suggests reinstating the federal assault weapons ban, which was previously in place from 1994 to 2004. The ban would prohibit weapons like the AR-15, designed to be as lethal as possible. The article argues that stricter gun laws are necessary to prevent further mayhem.

END OF SUMMARY.
