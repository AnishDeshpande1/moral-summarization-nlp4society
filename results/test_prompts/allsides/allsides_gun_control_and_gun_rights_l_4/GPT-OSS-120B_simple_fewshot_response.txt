SUMMARY:
The article argues that the United States has a moral imperative to act on gun violence after the Orlando nightclub shooting, pointing to Australia’s success in ending mass public shootings through strict gun bans. It proposes four practical steps: bar anyone on the “no‑fly” list from purchasing firearms; require universal background checks for all sales; improve and expedite the background‑check system so all prohibited individuals are captured; and ensure police, prosecutors and the public actively add violent abusers and other disqualifiers to the prohibited‑purchaser list. The piece also calls for reinstating a federal assault‑weapons ban and closing loopholes, noting that the gun industry profits from encouraging more powerful weapons despite declining hunting. By framing gun control as a necessary, compassionate response to prevent further carnage, the author presents stricter regulations as a moral duty to protect communities and stop profiteers from exploiting lax laws. END OF SUMMARY.
