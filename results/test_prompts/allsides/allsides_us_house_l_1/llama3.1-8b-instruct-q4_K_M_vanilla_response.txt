SUMMARY:
Former National Security Council official Fiona Hill testified before impeachment investigators, criticizing Republicans for promoting unsubstantiated conspiracy theories about Ukrainian interference in the 2016 presidential election. Hill stated that these theories are a "fictional narrative" perpetuated by Russian security services and contradict the US intelligence community's consensus conclusion that Russia interfered on behalf of President Trump.

Hill also recounted her account of a July 10 meeting where she claimed US Ambassador to the EU Gordon Sondland mentioned a quid pro quo, conditioning Ukraine's White House visit on investigations into Joe Biden's son and Ukrainian election interference. Hill emphasized that promoting these false narratives advances Russian interests and undermines national security.

She urged investigators not to promote politically driven falsehoods and highlighted the ongoing concern of Russian election interference, which has had lasting effects on the US, including undermining its foreign service and support for Ukraine. Hill's testimony aims to counter President Trump's efforts to downplay Russia's role in 2016 and shift blame onto Ukraine.

END OF SUMMARY.
