SUMMARY: Former National Security Council official Fiona Hill opened impeachment testimony by denouncing Republican conspiracy theories that Ukraine, not Russia, interfered in the 2016 U.S. election. She reiterated the intelligence community’s consensus that Russia conducted a campaign to aid Donald Trump, a finding supported by Special Counsel Robert Mueller. Hill criticized Trump’s attempts to recast the hacks as an “inside job” and warned against legitimizing narratives that portray Ukraine as a U.S. adversary. She recounted a July 10 meeting where Ambassador Gordon Sondland allegedly disclosed a quid‑pro‑quo linking a White House visit for President Volodymyr Zelensky to investigations of a company tied to Hunter Biden and alleged Ukrainian interference. National Security Advisor John Bolton reportedly told Hill he was not part of any “drug deal” being arranged. Hill urged investigators to avoid politically driven falsehoods that serve Russian interests and to scrutinize any actions that subvert U.S. national security for personal or political gain. She also emphasized that Russian election interference remains an ongoing threat, undermining truth, the foreign service, and U.S. support for Ukraine. END OF SUMMARY.
