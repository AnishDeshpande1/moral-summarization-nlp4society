SUMMARY:

Fiona Hill, a former National Security Council official, has excoriated Republicans for indulging in unsubstantiated conspiracy theories about Ukrainian interference in the 2016 presidential election. She refused to legitimize an alternative narrative that Ukraine, not Russia, attacked the US in 2016, which is a fictional narrative perpetrated by Russian security services. Hill emphasized that the US intelligence community's consensus conclusion is that Russia interfered in the 2016 election on Trump's behalf, a conclusion bolstered by Special Counsel Robert Mueller's work. However, Trump has repeatedly tried to undercut this notion, embracing a conspiracy theory that the hacks were an "inside job." Hill condemned this effort, urging investigators not to promote politically driven falsehoods that advance Russian interests. She warned that if the President or anyone else impedes or subverts national security to further domestic political or personal interests, it is worthy of attention. Hill also highlighted the ongoing concern of Russian election interference, which has torn the nation apart and undermined US support for Ukraine.

END OF SUMMARY.
