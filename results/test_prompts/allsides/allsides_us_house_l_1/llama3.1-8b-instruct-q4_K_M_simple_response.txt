SUMMARY:

Former National Security Council official Fiona Hill testified before impeachment investigators, criticizing Republicans for promoting unsubstantiated conspiracy theories about Ukrainian interference in the 2016 presidential election. Hill emphasized that these claims are a "fictional narrative" perpetuated by Russian security services to undermine trust in Ukraine and shift blame away from Russia's own election interference. She highlighted the US intelligence community's consensus conclusion that Russia interfered on Trump's behalf, as well as Special Counsel Robert Mueller's findings.

Hill also testified about a key meeting where Ambassador Gordon Sondland "blurted out" a quid pro quo deal with Ukraine, which was then denied by John Bolton. She urged investigators not to promote "politically driven falsehoods" that advance Russian interests and warned of the ongoing threat of Russian election interference. Hill's testimony underscored the danger of using baseless claims to undermine national security for personal or political gain.

END OF SUMMARY.
