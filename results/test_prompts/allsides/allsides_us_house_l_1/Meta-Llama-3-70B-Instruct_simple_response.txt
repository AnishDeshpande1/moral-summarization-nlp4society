SUMMARY:

Fiona Hill, a former National Security Council official, strongly criticized Republicans for promoting unsubstantiated conspiracy theories about Ukrainian interference in the 2016 presidential election. She emphasized that these theories are a "fictional narrative" perpetuated by Russian security services to distract from Russia's actual interference. Hill refused to legitimize an alternative narrative that Ukraine, not Russia, attacked the US in 2016, which contradicts the US intelligence community's consensus conclusion. She highlighted the absurdity of the Ukrainian election interference narrative pushed by Trump and Giuliani, which advances Russian interests. Hill urged the impeachment investigators not to promote "politically driven falsehoods" and to focus on the ongoing concern of Russian election interference, which is tearing the nation apart and undermining the career foreign service. She emphasized that the President's actions to further domestic political or personal interests at the expense of national security are worthy of attention.

END OF SUMMARY.
