SUMMARY: Former NSC official Fiona Hill blasted Republicans for peddling baseless conspiracy theories that blame Ukraine—not Russia—for the 2016 election interference, calling the narrative a “fictional” story spread by Russian security services. She refused to legitimize the false claim that Ukraine was a U.S. adversary and warned that such politically‑driven falsehoods serve Russian interests and endanger national security. Hill recounted a July 10 meeting where Ambassador Gordon Sondland allegedly linked a White House visit for President Zelensky to investigations of a company tied to Joe Biden’s son and to alleged Ukrainian meddling in 2016, a quid‑pro‑quo that National Security Advisor John Bolton dismissed. Emphasizing that the intelligence community and Mueller’s report confirm Russian interference, Hill urged investigators not to promote lies that aid a foreign adversary. She warned that ongoing Russian election meddling continues to fracture America, erode truth, and politicize U.S. support for Ukraine, and that any attempt by the President or others to subvert national security for personal or political gain deserves serious scrutiny. END OF SUMMARY.
