SUMMARY:
Former National Security Council official Fiona Hill opened her impeachment testimony by sharply rebuking Republicans for peddling baseless conspiracy theories that blame Ukraine for the 2016 election interference—a narrative Trump has repeatedly promoted to deflect Russian culpability. Hill emphasized the intelligence community’s unanimous finding that Russia, not Ukraine, orchestrated the hack and election‑meddling, a conclusion reinforced by the Mueller report. She warned that endorsing the false Ukrainian‑interference story not only distorts the truth but serves Russian interests and undermines U.S. national security. Hill recounted a July 10 meeting where Ambassador Gordon Sondland allegedly disclosed a quid‑pro‑quo linking a White House visit for President Zelensky to investigations of a company tied to Joe Biden’s son, and described National Security Advisor John Bolton’s warning that he was not part of any “drug deal.” Calling the Trump‑Giuliani Ukraine narrative “absurd,” Hill urged investigators to reject politically driven falsehoods, noting that ongoing Russian interference continues to fracture the nation, erode confidence in truth, and politicize U.S. support for Ukraine. END OF SUMMARY.
