SUMMARY:

Fiona Hill, a former National Security Council official, criticized Republicans for promoting conspiracy theories about Ukrainian interference in the 2016 presidential election. She stated that this narrative is fictional and perpetuated by Russian security services to distract from Russia's own interference. Hill emphasized that the US intelligence community has concluded that Russia interfered in the 2016 election on Trump's behalf. She also recounted a July 10 meeting where US Ambassador to the EU Gordon Sondland implied a quid pro quo between the US and Ukraine, with Ukraine investigating Joe Biden's son and purported Ukrainian election interference in exchange for a White House visit. Hill urged investigators not to promote politically driven falsehoods that advance Russian interests and highlighted the ongoing concern of Russian election interference, which has undermined US support for Ukraine and eroded trust in the country's institutions.

END OF SUMMARY.
