SUMMARY:
Former National Security Council official Fiona Hill opened impeachment testimony by denouncing Republican officials who promote baseless conspiracy theories that shift blame for the 2016 election interference from Russia to Ukraine. She warned that these false narratives, advanced by President Trump and his allies, constitute a subversive attack on legitimate authority—the U.S. intelligence community’s consensus that Russia meddled on Trump’s behalf, a finding reinforced by the Mueller report. Hill described the effort to recast Ukraine as the adversary as a dishonest manipulation that cheats the American public and undermines national security. Citing her closed‑door testimony, she recounted Gordon Sondland’s admission of a quid‑pro‑quo linking a White House visit for President Zelensky to investigations of a company tied to Joe Biden’s son, and John Bolton’s refusal to be complicit in the scheme. Hill urged investigators not to legitimize politically driven falsehoods that serve Russian interests, emphasizing that any attempt by the President or others to subvert U.S. security for personal or partisan gain is a grave breach of fairness and an abuse of authority. She highlighted that Russian interference remains a continuing threat, and that politicizing U.S. support for Ukraine further erodes trust in government institutions.  
END OF SUMMARY.
