SUMMARY:

The First Step Act, a landmark criminal justice reform bill, has passed the Senate with overwhelming bipartisan support. The bill addresses concerns that the war on drugs has led to the imprisonment of too many Americans for non-violent crimes without adequately preparing them for their return to society. It reduces mandatory minimum sentences for drug-related crimes, allows judges to circumvent federal mandatory minimum sentences for non-violent offenders, and expands rehabilitative opportunities for federal prisoners. The act also bans inhumane correctional practices, such as the shackling of pregnant women, and provides a path home for elderly and terminally ill inmates. The bill's passage is a victory for both progressives and law-and-order conservatives, who have come together to address the catastrophic harm of the incarceration industry. The act represents a step towards healing for communities disproportionately affected by the criminal justice system, and its passage is a testament to the power of bipartisan cooperation in addressing a pressing moral issue.

END OF SUMMARY.
