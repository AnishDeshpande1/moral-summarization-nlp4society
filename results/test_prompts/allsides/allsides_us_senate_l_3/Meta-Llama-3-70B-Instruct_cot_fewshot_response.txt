SUMMARY:
The First Step Act, a landmark bill, has cleared the Senate with a sweeping 87-12 vote, marking a historic bi-partisan victory. The bill seeks to reform the criminal justice system, addressing concerns that the nation's war on drugs has led to the imprisonment of too many Americans for non-violent crimes without adequately preparing them for their return to society. The act would reduce mandatory minimum sentences for drug-related crimes, allow judges to circumvent federal mandatory minimum sentences, and expand rehabilitative opportunities for federal prisoners. It would also ban inhumane correctional practices and provide hope and a second chance to those who earn it. The bill represents a step toward healing for communities disproportionately affected by the criminal justice system.
