SUMMARY: The First Step Act, a landmark bill aimed at reforming the criminal justice system, has cleared the Senate with overwhelming bipartisan support. The bill addresses concerns that the war on drugs has led to the imprisonment of too many Americans for non-violent crimes without adequately preparing them for their return to society. It gives judges more discretion when sentencing some drug offenders, allows them to circumvent federal mandatory minimum sentences for non-violent offenders, and expands rehabilitative opportunities for federal prisoners. The act also bans inhumane correctional practices, such as the shackling of pregnant women, and provides a path home for elderly and terminally ill inmates. By reducing mandatory minimum sentences and ending "three strikes" mandatory life sentences, the bill offers hope and a second chance to those who earn it, while also saving billions of dollars. This historic reform is a crucial step towards healing and unwinding the catastrophic harm of the incarceration industry. END OF SUMMARY.
