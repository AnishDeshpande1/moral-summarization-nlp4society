SUMMARY:

The First Step Act, a landmark bill aimed at reforming the US criminal justice system, has passed the Senate in a bipartisan vote of 87-12. The bill, which reduces mandatory minimum sentences for non-violent drug crimes and boosts prisoner rehabilitation efforts, has attracted support from both progressive advocates and law-and-order conservatives. President Trump has expressed his support for the bill, tweeting that it will "keep our communities safer" and provide hope to those who have made mistakes.

The bill would give judges more discretion when sentencing some drug offenders, expand rehabilitative opportunities for federal prisoners, and ban inhumane correctional practices such as shackling pregnant women. It would also end "three strikes" mandatory life sentences and retroactively extend the 2010 Fair Sentencing Act, which could affect thousands of drug offenders serving disproportionately long sentences.

The bill is a significant step towards addressing mass incarceration and has been praised by advocacy groups such as #cut50 and the ACLU.

END OF SUMMARY.
