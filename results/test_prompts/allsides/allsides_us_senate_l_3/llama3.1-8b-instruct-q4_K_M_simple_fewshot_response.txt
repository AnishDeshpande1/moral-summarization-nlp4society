SUMMARY:

The First Step Act, a landmark bill aimed at reforming the US criminal justice system, has cleared the Senate in a bipartisan vote. The bill would reduce mandatory minimum sentences for non-violent crimes, boost prisoner rehabilitation efforts, and provide a path to release for elderly and terminally ill inmates. It also ends "three strikes" mandatory life sentences and retroactively extends the 2010 Fair Sentencing Act, which could affect thousands of drug offenders serving disproportionately long sentences. Supporters from both parties, including President Trump, hail the bill as a historic step towards reducing mass incarceration and providing second chances to those who earn it. Critics acknowledge that the bill is not perfect but welcome its efforts to address the nation's prison crisis. With the Senate vote, the bill now heads back to the House for final approval before reaching the president's desk.

END OF SUMMARY.
