SUMMARY:  
The First Step Act, a landmark bipartisan bill, cleared the Senate 87‑12, giving judges greater **discretion** in **sentencing** and **circumventing** harsh mandatory minimums for non‑violent, **drug‑related** offenses. The legislation **addresses** the crisis of mass **imprisonment** by **ending** “three‑strikes” **convictions** and limiting **felony**‑related **firearm** statutes, while expanding **rehabilitative** programs, **invest**ing in re‑entry services, and **banning** inhumane practices such as the **shackling** of pregnant women. It also creates a path **home** for elderly or terminally ill inmates. Supporters from both sides of the aisle praised the **fight** to give second chances to those who have **mistakes**, calling the vote a **passing** of **safer**, **healing** reform. President Trump lauded the **signing** of the bill, noting it will save billions and **lead** the nation toward **consensus** and **unwinding** the harms of the incarceration industry. While the act impacts only federal prisoners (under 10% of the total), advocates argue it is a crucial first step toward broader **reform**.  

END OF SUMMARY.
