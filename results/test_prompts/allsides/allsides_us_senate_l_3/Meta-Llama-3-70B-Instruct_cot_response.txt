SUMMARY:
The First Step Act, a landmark bill, has cleared the Senate with bipartisan support, marking a historic victory for criminal justice reform. The bill addresses concerns that the war on drugs has led to the imprisonment of too many Americans for non-violent crimes without adequately preparing them for their return to society. The act would reduce mandatory minimum sentences, allow judges to circumvent federal mandatory minimum sentences for non-violent offenders, and expand rehabilitative opportunities for federal prisoners. It would also ban inhumane correctional practices and provide hope and a second chance to those who earn it, while saving billions of dollars. The bill is a step towards healing for communities disproportionately affected by the criminal justice system. While not perfect, the act is a common sense solution to the mass incarceration crisis, and its passage is a big bipartisan victory. END OF SUMMARY.
