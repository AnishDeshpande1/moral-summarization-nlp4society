**SUMMARY:**  
The Senate’s 87‑12 vote on the First Step Act marks a **historic**, **bipartisan** triumph that bridges **progressives** and **law‑and‑order conservatives**. The bill **reduces** mandatory minimums for **non‑violent** drug offenses, gives judges greater **discretion**, and expands **rehabilitation** programs, aiming to make communities **safer** and offer **hope** and a **second‑chance** to those who earn it. It bans **inhumane** practices such as the **shackling** of **pregnant** inmates, provides pathways home for **elderly** and **terminal** prisoners, and ends “three‑strikes” **life** sentences except for **serious‑violent** felons. By **retroactively** applying the 2010 **Fair Sentencing** Act, it seeks a more **fair**, less **disproportionate** system, making thousands **eligible** for **release**. Leaders from both parties praised the **consensus**, calling the measure a step toward **healing** communities scarred by **mass‑incarceration** **crisis**. While the ACLU notes the bill is not perfect, it welcomes the **common‑sense**, **justice**‑oriented **reform** now moving toward the President’s signature.
