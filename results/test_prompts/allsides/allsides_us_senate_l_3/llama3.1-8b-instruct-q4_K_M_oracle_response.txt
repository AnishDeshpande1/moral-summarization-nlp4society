SUMMARY:

The First Step Act, a landmark bill aimed at reforming the criminal justice system, has cleared the Senate in a bipartisan victory. This legislation addresses concerns that the war on drugs had led to the imprisonment of too many Americans for non-violent crimes without adequately preparing them for their return to society. The act would give judges more discretion when sentencing some drug offenders and boost prisoner rehabilitation efforts. It also aims to reduce mandatory minimum sentences, ban inhumane correctional practices such as shackling pregnant women, and provide a path home for elderly and terminally ill inmates. The bill would end "three strikes" mandatory life sentences and invest in re-entry programming, potentially affecting thousands of people serving long sentences. This historic reform represents a step towards healing communities that have been disproportionately affected by the criminal justice system. As President Trump tweeted, this legislation will keep our communities safer and provide hope for those who earn it.

END OF SUMMARY.
