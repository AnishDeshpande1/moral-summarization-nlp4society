**SUMMARY:**  
The First Step Act, a *historic* criminal‑justice reform bill, cleared the Senate with a bipartisan 87‑12 vote, earning praise from progressives, law‑and‑order conservatives, and public figures. The legislation aims to give judges *rehabilitative* discretion, reduce mandatory minimums for non‑violent drug offenses, and end *inhumane* practices such as shackling pregnant inmates. It also provides *second chances* for elderly, terminally ill, and wrongly sentenced prisoners, extending the 2010 Fair Sentencing Act to address *catastrophic* racial disparities. President Trump hailed the bill as a step toward a *safer* America, promising *hope* and *common‑sense* savings. Advocates like Jessica Jackson Sloan and Senator Cory Booker called the act a move toward *healing* communities harmed by mass incarceration, while the ACLU noted it is not perfect but a necessary stride in the *justice* reform effort. The bill affects only federal inmates—less than 10 % of the nation’s prison population—yet it signals a *great* bipartisan commitment to a more *human* system.
