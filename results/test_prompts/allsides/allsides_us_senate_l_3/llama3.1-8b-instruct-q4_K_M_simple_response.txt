SUMMARY:
A landmark bill, the First Step Act, has cleared the Senate in a bipartisan effort to reform the US criminal justice system. The bill aims to reduce mandatory minimum sentences for non-violent drug crimes and increase rehabilitation opportunities for federal prisoners. It also addresses concerns about mass incarceration, particularly affecting low-income communities and those struggling with mental illness and addiction. The legislation would give judges more discretion when sentencing and provide a pathway for release for thousands of inmates serving long sentences.

Public figures such as Patricia Arquette and Kim Kardashian West have supported the bill, which has been praised by both progressives and law-and-order conservatives. President Trump has expressed support for the bill, calling it a "historic criminal justice reform" that will keep communities safer and provide hope to those who earn it. The bill now returns to the House for another vote before reaching the president's desk.

END OF SUMMARY.
