SUMMARY:

The US Senate has passed the First Step Act, a landmark bill that aims to reform the criminal justice system. The bill, which received bipartisan support, would reduce drug crime sentences, give judges more discretion when sentencing non-violent offenders, and boost prisoner rehabilitation efforts. The bill would also ban inhumane correctional practices, provide a path for elderly and terminally ill inmates to return home, and invest in re-entry programming. Additionally, it would retroactively extend the 2010 Fair Sentencing Act, potentially affecting thousands of drug offenders serving disproportionately long sentences. The bill has received support from both progressive advocates and law-and-order conservatives, including President Trump, who is expected to sign it into law. The bill's passage is seen as a significant step towards addressing the nation's mass incarceration crisis.

END OF SUMMARY.
