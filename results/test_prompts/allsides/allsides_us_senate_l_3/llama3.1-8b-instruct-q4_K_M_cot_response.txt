**SUMMARY:**

The First Step Act, a landmark bill aimed at reforming the criminal justice system, has cleared the Senate with a significant bipartisan victory. This historic legislation seeks to address concerns that the nation's war on drugs had led to the imprisonment of too many Americans for non-violent crimes without adequately preparing them for their return to society. The bill would reduce mandatory minimum sentences for certain drug-related crimes, allow judges to have more discretion when sentencing offenders, and expand rehabilitative opportunities for federal prisoners. This bipartisan effort is seen as a step towards healing the communities that have been disproportionately affected by mass incarceration. As one advocate noted, "Tonight, we stand as neither Republican or Democrats but as people seeking to take the first step of many to criminal justice reform." The bill would provide hope and a second chance to those who earn it, making America's communities safer.
