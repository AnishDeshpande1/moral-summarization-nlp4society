SUMMARY:
Harvard Law School professor Alan Dershowitz criticized congressional Democrats for their efforts to impeach President Trump, comparing them to the Russian secret police under Josef Stalin. Dershowitz argued that Democrats are "making crimes up" and trying to create a false narrative to justify impeachment. He pointed out that there is no federal crime of collusion with a foreign country and that obstruction of Congress is not a crime, but rather part of the system of checks and balances.

Dershowitz also criticized CNN and MSNBC for their biased reporting, saying they are "parroting the party line" and misleading their viewers. He claimed that he has been banned from appearing on these networks because they don't want to allow a liberal Democrat to tell the truth about the Constitution. Dershowitz's comments have been seen as a rare instance of a liberal Democrat criticizing his own party's impeachment efforts.

Dershowitz concluded that Democrats' actions are "a threat to all Americans" and should be condemned by organizations like the ACLU, which is usually vocal on issues related to civil liberties.

END OF SUMMARY.
