SUMMARY:
Harvard Law professor Alan Dershowitz, a self‑identified liberal Democrat, blasted congressional Democrats for their impeachment push against President Trump, likening their tactics to the fabrications of Soviet secret police under Stalin. On John Catsimatidis’ radio show he warned that Democrats are “creating crimes out of nothing,” citing their alleged invention of “collusion” and “obstruction of Congress” as legal offenses that do not exist in federal statutes. Dershowitz argued that demanding a whistle‑blower’s identity would be a crime only if one could “make up” the crime, echoing a Stalin‑era KGB official’s boast. He contended that genuine checks and balances, such as invoking executive privilege, are being mischaracterized as criminal conduct, endangering the nation’s constitutional order. Dershowitz also condemned the media, saying CNN and MSNBC have silenced his dissenting view and are parroting a partisan line, while the ACLU remains silent. The moral framing presented portrays the Democrats’ impeachment effort as a dangerous, law‑less overreach that threatens constitutional norms and democratic liberty.

END OF SUMMARY.
