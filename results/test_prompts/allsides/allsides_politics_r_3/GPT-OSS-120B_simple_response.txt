SUMMARY: Harvard Law professor Alan Dershowitz, a self‑identified liberal Democrat, condemned congressional Democrats as morally comparable to Stalin’s secret police, accusing them of fabricating crimes to impeach President Trump. On John Catsimatidis’ radio show he warned that Democrats are “making crimes out of nothing,” citing invented offenses such as “collusion” and “obstruction of Congress,” which he says lack statutory basis and betray constitutional checks and balances. Dershowitz invoked the Soviet KGB chief Lavrentiy Beria’s line “show me the man and I’ll find you the crime,” to illustrate the danger of creating legal fictions. He argued that this reckless pursuit threatens all Americans and even the ACLU remains silent. Though traditionally liberal, Dershowitz has been shunned by left‑leaning media for his anti‑impeachment stance, claiming CNN has banned him for refusing to repeat the party line. The moral framing presented positions the Democrats’ impeachment drive as a dangerous abuse of power that undermines the rule of law and constitutional integrity. END OF SUMMARY.
