SUMMARY:

Harvard Law School professor Alan Dershowitz has compared congressional Democrats' efforts to impeach President Trump to the actions of the Russian secret police under Josef Stalin. Dershowitz argued that Democrats are "making crimes up" and creating a precedent for the executive branch to be intimidated by Congress. He pointed out that there is no crime of collusion with a foreign country, and obstruction of Congress is part of the system of checks and balances. Dershowitz warned that this behavior puts all Americans at risk, as it sets a dangerous precedent where Congress can create crimes out of nothing. He criticized CNN for banning him from their airwaves and accused them of presenting biased information to their viewers.

Dershowitz's comments highlight his concern about the erosion of constitutional protections and the potential for abuse of power by Congress. His moral framing emphasizes the importance of upholding the rule of law and protecting individual rights, while also cautioning against the dangers of unchecked Congressional authority.

END OF SUMMARY.
