SUMMARY:
Harvard law professor Alan Dershowitz, a self‑identified liberal Democrat, denounced congressional Democrats on a radio show, likening their impeachment drive to the fabricated crimes of Stalin’s secret police. He warned that the effort to “create crimes out of nothing” threatens the nation’s safety, invoking a moral appeal to protect citizens from harmful, unjust persecution (Care/Harm). Dershowitz argued that the Democrats are subverting constitutional authority by inventing illegal “collusion” and “obstruction of Congress” charges that do not exist in federal law, thereby undermining legitimate governmental authority and the rule of law (Authority/Subversion). He framed this as a profound fairness issue, accusing the party of cheating the public by manufacturing offenses and silencing watchdog groups like the ACLU, which should speak out against such deceit (Fairness/Cheating). Dershowitz also criticized mainstream media, especially CNN, for censoring his dissenting view, suggesting a broader pattern of partisan bias that further harms democratic fairness and accountability.  

END OF SUMMARY.
