SUMMARY:  
Former President Barack Obama condemned the language of leaders who “feed a climate of fear and hatred” after the deadly mass shootings in El Paso, Texas, and Dayton, Ohio, urging Americans to hold officials accountable for gun‑law reform. Donald Trump retaliated by citing a Fox & Friends segment that blamed Obama for “32 mass shootings” during his tenure, deflecting criticism from his own rhetoric on immigration and race. The article underscores the moral stakes: Obama links normalized racist and xenophobic discourse—exemplified by Trump’s remarks about immigrants and people of colour—to the root causes of violence, while Trump’s focus on video games and mental illness sidesteps the responsibility of political leaders. Critics, including Beto O’Rourke, argue that Trump’s own hateful rhetoric has emboldened white supremacists, as seen in the El Paso shooter’s manifesto. The piece frames the debate as a moral contest over whether leaders choose to denounce hatred and protect vulnerable communities or to perpetuate division that fuels tragedy.
