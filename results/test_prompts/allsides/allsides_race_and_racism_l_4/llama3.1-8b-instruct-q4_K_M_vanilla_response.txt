SUMMARY:
US President Donald Trump has responded to former President Barack Obama's statement on mass shootings, citing a Fox & Friends segment that claimed Obama had 32 mass shootings during his presidency. This is in response to Obama's call for Americans to "soundly reject language" from leaders who feed fear and hatred or normalize racist sentiments. Trump has faced criticism for not addressing gun laws as a cause of the shootings, instead blaming video games, the internet, and mental illness.

Obama's statement noted that mass shootings are becoming more frequent and that until public officials hold others accountable for changing gun laws, tragedies will continue to happen. Trump's response has been met with opposition from Democrats, including 2020 presidential candidate Beto O'Rourke, who said Trump should not visit El Paso due to his role in creating the hatred that led to the tragedy.

The shootings in Texas and Ohio have left at least 31 people dead and 53 injured. Obama's statement also condemned the language of leaders who demonize others based on their appearance or nationality, saying it has "no place in our politics and public life".

END OF SUMMARY.
