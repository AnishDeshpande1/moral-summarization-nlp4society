SUMMARY:

President Trump responded to former President Obama's call to reject language that feeds a climate of fear and hatred by citing a Fox & Friends segment that downplayed Obama's own handling of mass shootings during his presidency. Trump's tweet was seen as a deflection of responsibility, as he has been criticized for identifying video games, the internet, and mental illness as the cause of recent mass shootings, rather than guns. This approach raises concerns about Trump's willingness to subvert the truth and prioritize his own political interests over the well-being of the American people. Furthermore, Trump's rhetoric has been accused of emboldening white nationalists, and his visit to El Paso and Dayton has been met with opposition from Democrats who argue that he has no place in these cities. Obama's statement, on the other hand, emphasized the importance of unity and accountability in the face of mass shootings, urging Americans to stand up and demand change from their public officials. END OF SUMMARY.
