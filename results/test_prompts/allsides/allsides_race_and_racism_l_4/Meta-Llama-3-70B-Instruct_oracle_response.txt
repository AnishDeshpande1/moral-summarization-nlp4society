SUMMARY:

Donald Trump responded to former President Barack Obama's statement on mass shootings, citing a Fox & Friends segment that claimed Obama had 32 mass shootings during his presidency. Obama's statement urged Americans to "soundly reject language" that feeds a climate of fear and hatred, and to hold public officials accountable for changing gun laws. Trump has faced criticism for identifying video games, the internet, and mental illness as the cause of the shootings, but not guns. He has also been accused of emboldening white nationalists through his racist statements, which have been linked to the El Paso attack. Obama's comments implied that Trump's language has contributed to the normalisation of racism, which has been at the root of most human tragedy throughout history. Trump's visit to El Paso and Dayton, Ohio has been met with opposition, with critics arguing that he has no place in these cities after helping to create the hatred that made the tragedies possible.

END OF SUMMARY.
