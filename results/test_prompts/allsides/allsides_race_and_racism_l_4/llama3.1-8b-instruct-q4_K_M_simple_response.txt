SUMMARY:

Donald Trump responded to former President Barack Obama's statement on mass shootings by citing a Fox & Friends segment that claimed Obama had 32 mass shootings during his presidency. This response was met with criticism, as Trump has been accused of emboldening white nationalists through his racist statements and not addressing the root cause of gun violence. Obama's statement called for Americans to reject language that feeds fear and hatred, and normalize racist sentiments, suggesting that Trump is responsible for such rhetoric.

Trump's visit to El Paso and Dayton was opposed by Beto O'Rourke, who argued that Trump should not come to El Paso given his role in creating the hatred that led to the tragedy. Obama noted that the El Paso shooting followed a trend of individuals embracing white supremacy ideologies, and advised Americans to denounce language that demonizes others based on their appearance or nationality.

END OF SUMMARY.
