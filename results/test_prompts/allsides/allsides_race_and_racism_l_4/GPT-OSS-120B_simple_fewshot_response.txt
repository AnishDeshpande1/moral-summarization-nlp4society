SUMMARY:
Barack Obama warned Americans to reject the language of leaders who “feed a climate of fear and hatred” after two mass shootings killed 31 people in Texas and Ohio. In response, Donald Trump cited a Fox & Friends segment that claimed Obama presided over 32 mass shootings, attempting to deflect blame and shift the focus to previous administrations. Trump continued to point to video games, the internet and mental illness rather than guns, while critics say his own rhetoric has emboldened white‑nationalist violence – the El Paso shooter quoted Trump’s anti‑immigrant language. Trump's planned visits to El Paso and Dayton have drawn condemnation from Democrats, including Beto O’Ridge, who argues the president’s own hateful remarks have helped create the hatred behind such attacks. Obama’s plea emphasized holding officials accountable for gun‑law reform and denouncing racist, dehumanizing speech, noting that such language has historically fueled tragedy. The exchange highlights a moral clash: Obama urges responsibility and unity, while Trump resorts to historical blame‑shifting and rhetoric that many see as contributing to the very violence Obama condemns. END OF SUMMARY.
