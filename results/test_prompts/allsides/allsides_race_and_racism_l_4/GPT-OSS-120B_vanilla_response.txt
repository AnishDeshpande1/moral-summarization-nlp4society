SUMMARY: Former President Barack Obama condemned rhetoric that normalizes racism and warned that such language fuels mass shootings, referencing the recent attacks in El Paso, Texas, and Dayton, Ohio, which left 31 dead. Obama’s remarks, though not naming Trump, were widely interpreted as a rebuke of the former president’s anti‑immigrant and white‑supremacy‑friendly comments. In response, Donald Trump cited a Fox & Friends segment, claiming Obama presided over “32 mass shootings” and suggesting previous presidents never condemned him after tragedies like Sandy Hook. Trump has faced criticism for attributing gun violence to video games, the internet, and mental illness rather than firearms, and for rhetoric that critics say emboldens white nationalists. The El Paso shooter’s manifesto reportedly echoed Trump’s language. Trump plans to visit the shooting sites, prompting opposition from Democrats such as Beto O’Rourke, who argue his rhetoric contributed to the violence. Obama emphasized the need to reject leaders who demonize immigrants and minorities, linking such discourse to historical tragedies. END OF SUMMARY.
