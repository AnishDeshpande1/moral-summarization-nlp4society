SUMMARY: A gunman entered West Freeway Church of Christ in White Settlement, Texas, and opened fire, killing three men—two during the incident and one later in transit. The attack was abruptly stopped when two volunteer security members drew their firearms and killed the shooter within six seconds, preventing further casualties among the 240‑plus congregants present. Authorities have not disclosed motives or the identities of the victims or shooter, who was described as a transient local. Texas officials praised the volunteers as heroes and highlighted the state’s permissive gun‑carry laws. The incident follows heightened concern over church security after the 2017 Sutherland Springs massacre and occurred shortly after a separate anti‑Semitic knife attack in New York. Governor Greg Abbott and other officials condemned the violence and called for prayers for the community. END OF SUMMARY.
