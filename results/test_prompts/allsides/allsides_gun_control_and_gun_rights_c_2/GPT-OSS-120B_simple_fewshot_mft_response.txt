SUMMARY:
A gunman entered West Freeway Church of Christ in White Settlement, Texas, and opened fire, killing two men and wounding others. Within six seconds, two volunteer members of the church’s security team—acting as legitimate authority figures—drew their firearms and stopped the shooter, an act described by state officials as “heroic” and credited with saving countless lives. Texas Lt. Gov. Dan Patrick and Gov. Greg Abbott praised the volunteers’ swift, lawful response and highlighted the state’s permissive gun‑carry laws that enable such protective authority. The tragedy underscores the stark harm caused by the attacker, while the courageous intervention of the security volunteers exemplifies a moral commitment to protect the community. Officials condemned the violent subversion of a place of worship and called for prayers for the victims and their families. The incident follows heightened concerns about church security after the 2017 Sutherland Springs massacre and coincides with a separate anti‑Semitic stabbing in New York, reinforcing the urgency of safeguarding sacred spaces from violent subversion.

END OF SUMMARY.
