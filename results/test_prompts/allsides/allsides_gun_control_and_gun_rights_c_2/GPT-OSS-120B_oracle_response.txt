SUMMARY: A man pulled a shotgun into West Freeway Church of Christ in White Settlement and opened fire, killing two congregants and wounding others. Within six seconds two volunteer security members drew their weapons, confronted the gunman and, in a heroic action, took him down, saving an untold number of lives. Their quick response stopped the shooting and prevented a far worse tragedy. Authorities called the incident “terrible” and “tragic,” noting the loss and death of three men and the fatalities that could have been higher. Texas officials praised the security team for acting, helping prevent further loss and protecting the congregation. The attack, investigated as possible terrorism, came hours after a knifewielding assailant stabbed five people at a Hanukkah celebration, prompting New York officials to direct a hate‑crimes investigation. The incident has revived the issue of church security and the right of licensed handgun owners to carry weapons in Texas churches. Community leaders and the governor urged prayers for those affected and condemned the violence. END OF SUMMARY.
