SUMMARY:

A gunman opened fire at West Freeway Church of Christ in North Texas on Sunday, killing two people before being fatally shot by two volunteer security team members. The incident occurred during a church service attended by over 240 congregants, and the shooter's motive remains unclear. However, authorities praised the quick response of the security team, who drew their weapons within six seconds to stop the attack and save "untold number of lives." Texas Lt. Gov. Dan Patrick hailed the state's gun laws, saying that they allowed parishioners to defend themselves. The shooting is being investigated, but officials have noted that it could have been far worse if not for the heroic actions of the security team. The incident has left a community in shock and mourning, with Governor Greg Abbott condemning the "evil act of violence" and calling on Texans to pray for the victims.

END OF SUMMARY.
