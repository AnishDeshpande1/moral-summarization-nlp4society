SUMMARY: A gunman entered the West Freeway Church of Christ in White Settlement, Texas, and opened fire, killing three men. Within seconds, two volunteer members of the church’s security team drew their legally‑carried handguns and fatally shot the attacker, an act described by officials as “heroic” and credited with saving “untold” lives among the 240 worshippers present. Texas Lt. Gov. Dan Patrick and Gov. Greg Abbott praised the volunteers, linking their swift response to the state’s permissive gun‑carry laws. The tragedy, which occurred during a livestreamed service, follows the 2017 Sutherland Springs massacre and underscores ongoing concerns about church security. Authorities have not disclosed a motive, and the shooter’s background remains unclear. Community leaders, including the church’s senior minister, mourned the two security volunteers who died while stopping the shooter and called for prayers for the victims and the broader community. END OF SUMMARY.
