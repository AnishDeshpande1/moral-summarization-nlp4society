SUMMARY:
A shotgun attack at West Freeway Church of Christ in White Settlement, Texas, was swiftly stopped by two volunteer security members who drew their legally‑owned handguns and killed the gunman within six seconds, preventing far greater loss of life. Texas officials, including Lt. Gov. Dan Patrick and Gov. Greg Abbott, praised the parishioners’ “heroic” response and highlighted the state’s permissive gun‑carry laws that allow concealed weapons in churches unless posted otherwise. The incident, occurring during a livestreamed service with over 240 attendees, underscores the importance of armed church security—a lesson reinforced after the 2017 Sutherland Springs massacre. While the motive remains unknown and the victims’ names have not been released, authorities and community leaders lauded the defenders for protecting the congregation and averting a larger tragedy. The episode contrasts with a recent knife attack at a New York rabbi’s home, which was labeled domestic terrorism by Gov. Andrew Cuomo.

END OF SUMMARY.
