SUMMARY:

A gunman opened fire inside West Freeway Church of Christ in White Settlement, Texas, killing two people on Sunday. However, the attack was quickly brought to an end by two volunteer security team members who drew their weapons and fatally shot the gunman, saving an "untold number of lives." The Texas Department of Public Safety described their actions as "heroic." There were over 240 parishioners in the church at the time of the shooting, which was being livestreamed. The victims and the gunman have not been named, and authorities have not provided a possible motive. The incident highlights the importance of church security, which became a major issue in Texas after a similar attack in 2017. Governor Greg Abbott condemned the "evil act of violence" and praised the church members who took down the shooter.

END OF SUMMARY.
