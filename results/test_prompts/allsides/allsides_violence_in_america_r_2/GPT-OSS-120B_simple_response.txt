SUMMARY: The author, a lifelong anti‑Semitism activist and scholar, argues that American Jews have been uniquely fortunate, yet the recent Pittsburgh synagogue massacre marks the first large‑scale killing of Jews on American soil—a tragedy that underscores the lethal, “exterminationist” nature of anti‑Semitism. Citing the Passover Haggadah’s warning that each generation faces those who seek Jewish annihilation, he stresses that anti‑Jew hatred is not merely persecution but a drive to destroy Jews entirely, a force that has historically devastated societies, exemplified by Nazi Germany. He claims contemporary anti‑Semitism in the U.S. is being misrepresented by left‑wing media and organizations, which he accuses of inflating threats for political ends, while the real surge in hostility comes from left‑leaning Jews and from Israel’s regional enemies. As a practicing Jew and supporter of concealed‑carry rights, he urges vigilance and armed protection for synagogues, warning that the safety of Jews is inseparable from America’s own fate. He concludes that defending Jews is a moral imperative for all who love America. END OF SUMMARY.
