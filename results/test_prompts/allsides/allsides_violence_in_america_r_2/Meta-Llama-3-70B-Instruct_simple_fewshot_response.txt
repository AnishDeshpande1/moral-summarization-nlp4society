SUMMARY:

The author, a Jewish American, reflects on the Pittsburgh synagogue massacre, which he calls a unique American tragedy. He notes that anti-Semitism is exterminationist, aiming to kill Jews, and that it destroys every society in which it grows. The author criticizes left-wing Jewish organizations and media for spreading false information about an increase in anti-Semitism in America, citing the example of bomb threats to Jewish Community Centers that were later found to be made by a mentally disturbed teenager and a black radical. He argues that the biggest increase in anti-Semitism comes from the left and that Jews who wear yarmulkes or are vocally pro-Israel on college campuses face threats. The author prays that armed guards will not be needed at American synagogues and urges Americans to fight anti-Semitism, as the fate of the nation depends on it.

END OF SUMMARY.
