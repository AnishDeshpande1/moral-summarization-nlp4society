SUMMARY:

The author reflects on the Pittsburgh synagogue massacre as a unique American tragedy, marking the first time in US history that Jews were murdered solely for being Jewish. Anti-Semitism is described as exterminationist, aiming to annihilate all Jews, rather than just persecute or enslave them. The author notes that anti-Semitism has devastating consequences for society, citing the example of Nazi Germany's destruction.

The author also critiques Jewish organizations and media outlets for inflating anti-Semitic incidents, such as bomb threats, and blaming President Trump for increasing anti-Semitism. Instead, they argue that the biggest increase in anti-Semitism comes from the left, particularly on college campuses.

The author concludes by emphasizing the importance of fighting anti-Semites not just for Jews' sake but also for the nation's. As a Jew who attends synagogue regularly, they pray that armed guards will never be needed at American synagogues.
