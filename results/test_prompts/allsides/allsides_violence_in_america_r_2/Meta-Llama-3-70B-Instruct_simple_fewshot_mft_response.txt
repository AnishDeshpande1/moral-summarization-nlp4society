SUMMARY:

The author, a Jewish American, reflects on the Pittsburgh synagogue massacre, emphasizing that it is a unique American tragedy. He argues that anti-Semitism is exterminationist, aiming to kill Jews, and that it destroys every society in which it grows. The author criticizes Jewish leftists for blaming President Trump for the increase in anti-Semitism, citing the Pittsburgh murderer's criticism of Trump for his close connections to Jews and Israel. Instead, the author asserts that the biggest increase in anti-Semitism comes from the left, threatening Jewish annihilation. He urges Americans to fight anti-Semitism, not just for the sake of Jews, but for the nation's fate. The author's message is clear: America's uniqueness is exemplified by the fact that Jews do not need armed guards in their synagogues, and it is essential to preserve this aspect of American society.

END OF SUMMARY.
