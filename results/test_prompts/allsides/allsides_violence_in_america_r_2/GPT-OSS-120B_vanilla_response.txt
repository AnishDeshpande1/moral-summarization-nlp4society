SUMMARY: The author, a longtime anti‑Semitism activist and scholar, argues that while American Jews have historically enjoyed relative safety, the recent Pittsburgh synagogue shooting represents a tragic first mass murder of Jews in the United States. He stresses that anti‑Semitism is uniquely exterminationist, seeking total annihilation rather than mere persecution, and that it harms the societies that nurture it, citing Nazi Germany as an example. The piece criticizes left‑leaning media and Jewish organizations for exaggerating anti‑Jewish threats after the 2016 election, claiming most bomb threats were hoaxes, and contends that the current rise in anti‑Semitism stems from left‑wing college activists rather than the political right. The author also blames external enemies of Israel for broader threats to Jews, while advocating for armed self‑defense at synagogues and urging all Americans to oppose anti‑Semitism, asserting that a nation’s fate is tied to its treatment of Jews. END OF SUMMARY.
