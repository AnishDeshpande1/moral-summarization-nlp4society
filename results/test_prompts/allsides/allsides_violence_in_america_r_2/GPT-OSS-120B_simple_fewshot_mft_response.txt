SUMMARY:
The author, a lifelong Jewish activist and scholar, argues that American Jews remain “the luckiest” yet face a uniquely American tragedy: the Pittsburgh synagogue massacre, the first mass killing of Jews in the United States. He stresses the **sanctity** of Jewish life, warning that anti‑Semitism is not merely persecution but an **exterminationist** ideology that seeks total annihilation, as echoed in the Passover Haggadah. The piece invokes **care** for victims and their families, condemning the murder as a profound harm to both Jews and the nation. It calls for **loyalty** to America, asserting that the safety of Jews reflects the health of the whole country and urging all citizens, even those indifferent to Jews, to oppose anti‑Semites. The author critiques the **authority** of left‑leaning media and Jewish organizations, accusing them of subverting truth by exaggerating anti‑Semitic threats and falsely blaming President Trump, while claiming that the true rise in anti‑Semitism stems from the political left and hostile foreign enemies of Israel. He appeals to **fairness**, demanding an honest assessment of the sources of hate and urging support for self‑defense measures, such as concealed‑carry, to protect synagogues without compromising America’s unique tradition of safety. END OF SUMMARY.
