**SUMMARY:**  
A federal judge struck down a Trump‑administration rule that would let health‑care workers refuse abortions and other services on moral or religious grounds. Judge Paul A. Engelmayer said the HHS overstepped its authority, calling the rule “a classic solution in search of a problem” built on a false claim and a lie about rising conscience complaints. He warned the measure would be burdensome, damaging, and costly, forcing hospitals to double staff and potentially denying care to vulnerable populations, including women, LGBT people, and those with HIV/AIDS. The judge also said the rule could lead to discriminatory practices, allowing even receptionists or elevator operators to refuse work, and that cutting federal funding for non‑compliance should be left to Congress. Planned Parenthood hailed the decision as a block on legal cover for discrimination, while some Republicans dismissed it as absurd.
