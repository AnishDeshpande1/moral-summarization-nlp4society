SUMMARY:
A federal judge in New York struck down a Trump administration rule that allowed health care workers to refuse participation in abortions or other procedures on moral or religious grounds. U.S. District Judge Paul A. Engelmayer ruled that the Department of Health and Human Services overstepped its authority, creating a "solution in search of a problem." He also found that the measure could be costly, burdensome, and damaging to emergency care, and that the rationale for the rule was based on false claims.

The judge warned that the rule could allow even low-level staff members, such as hospital receptionists or elevator operators, to refuse to do their jobs if it conflicted with their moral or religious beliefs. The 19 states, District of Columbia, and health organizations that sued to block the rule argued that it would be discriminatory and interfere with people's access to healthcare.

The ruling was seen as a victory for those advocating for reproductive rights and against discrimination in healthcare. As Planned Parenthood's acting president stated, "Today, the Trump administration has been blocked from providing legal cover for discrimination."
