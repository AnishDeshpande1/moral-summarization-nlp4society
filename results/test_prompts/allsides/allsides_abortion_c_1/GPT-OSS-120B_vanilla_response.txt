SUMMARY: A federal judge, Paul A. Engelmayer, struck down a Trump administration rule that would allow health‑care workers to refuse participation in abortions and other procedures on moral or religious grounds. The judge ruled that the Health and Human Services (HHS) department exceeded its authority, that the claim of rising conscience‑based complaints was false, and that the rule would be costly, burdensome, and harmful to emergency care. The rule would have expanded “conscience” protections to a broad range of staff—receptionists, pharmacists, lab workers, even elevator operators—allowing them to refuse tasks such as scheduling, counseling, or referrals, and threatened to cut federal funding for non‑compliance. Nineteen states, D.C., several local governments and health groups sued, arguing the rule would discriminate and restrict access to care. Planned Parenthood and the American Psychological Association praised the decision, while Republican Senator Ben Sasse called it “absurd” and urged an appeal. The judge emphasized that any changes to employer obligations for religious objections should come from Congress, not HHS. END OF SUMMARY.
