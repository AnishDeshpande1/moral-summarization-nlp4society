SUMMARY:

A federal judge has struck down a Trump administration rule that would have allowed health care workers to refuse to participate in abortions or other procedures on moral or religious grounds. The judge ruled that the rule was unlawful, as it went beyond existing law and was based on a false claim that there was a significant increase in complaints about workers being forced to violate their conscience. The rule would have broadened the list of health care personnel who can refuse to participate, including receptionists, elevator operators, and ambulance drivers, and would have restricted employers' ability to inquire about employees' objections. Critics argued that the rule would have been discriminatory, interfered with people's access to health care, and would have been costly and burdensome. The ruling is a victory for those who believe that health care workers should not be allowed to discriminate against patients based on their religious or moral beliefs. 

END OF SUMMARY.
