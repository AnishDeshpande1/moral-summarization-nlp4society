SUMMARY:

A federal judge in New York has struck down a Trump administration rule that would have allowed health care workers to refuse to participate in abortions and other procedures on moral or religious grounds. The judge ruled that the Department of Health and Human Services overstepped its authority and that the measure could be costly and damaging to emergency care. The rule would have expanded the list of health care personnel who can refuse to participate and restricted employers' ability to inquire about employees' objections. Critics argued that the rule would have interfered with people's access to health care and been discriminatory. The judge's decision was praised by health organizations and advocacy groups, while a Republican senator called the ruling "absurd mush" and urged the Trump administration to appeal.

END OF SUMMARY.
