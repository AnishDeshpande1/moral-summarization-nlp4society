SUMMARY:
A federal judge in New York struck down a Trump administration rule that allowed health care workers to refuse participation in abortions or other procedures on moral or religious grounds. U.S. District Judge Paul A. Engelmayer ruled that the Health and Human Services Department overstepped its authority and went beyond existing law. He also said that the measure could be costly, burdensome, and damaging to emergency care. Nineteen states, the District of Columbia, and health organizations had sued to block the rule, arguing it would be discriminatory and interfere with people's access to healthcare. The judge found that the department made false claims about a significant increase in complaints from workers being forced to violate their conscience. The ruling blocks the rule from taking effect on November 22. Health care institutions have expressed concerns that the rule could force some employers to double or triple staff, particularly during emergencies.

END OF SUMMARY.
