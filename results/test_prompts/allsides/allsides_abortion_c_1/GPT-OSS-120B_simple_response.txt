SUMMARY: A federal judge blocked a Trump administration rule that would have let a broader range of health‑care workers refuse to provide abortions or related services on moral or religious grounds. Judge Paul Engelmayer said the HHS overstepped its authority, relied on false claims of widespread conscience complaints, and created a “solution in search of a problem” that would harm emergency care and increase costs. Plaintiffs—including 19 states, D.C., local governments and health groups—argued the rule would discriminate and restrict patients’ access to care. Planned Parenthood hailed the decision as a defeat of “legal cover for discrimination,” while the American Psychological Association warned it would endanger vulnerable populations such as women, LGBT people, and those with HIV/AIDS. Critics like Sen. Ben Sasse called the ruling “absurd” and urged an appeal. The judge emphasized that Congress, not an agency, should decide any changes to conscience‑protection laws and warned that the rule could force hospitals to dramatically increase staffing, even for non‑clinical staff such as receptionists or elevator operators. END OF SUMMARY.
