SUMMARY: A federal judge blocked the Trump administration's rule that would have allowed health care workers to refuse participation in abortions or other procedures on moral or religious grounds, deeming it discriminatory and outside of their authority. Judge Paul Engelmayer stated that the Health and Human Services Department overstepped its bounds by issuing a rule that was based on false claims about an increase in complaints from workers being forced to violate their conscience. The new rule could have been costly and damaging to emergency care, as well as inhibiting employers' ability to organize workplace arrangements efficiently. Engelmayer emphasized that the HHS lacked authority to create major portions of the rule, including a provision that threatened to cut off federal funding for institutions violating it. He argued that this measure was a classic "solution in search of a problem" and should be left to Congress to decide whether to change laws regarding employers' duty to accommodate religious objections. The ruling is seen as a victory for those who argue against discrimination and interference with people's access to healthcare.

END OF SUMMARY.
