SUMMARY: A federal judge has struck down a Trump administration rule that would have allowed healthcare workers to refuse to participate in abortions and other procedures on moral or religious grounds. The judge ruled that the U.S. Health and Human Services Department overstepped its authority and went beyond existing law in issuing the rule, which was based on a lie about a significant increase in complaints about workers being forced to violate their conscience. The measure was deemed costly, burdensome, and damaging to emergency care, and could have jeopardized the health of vulnerable populations. The rule was also seen as discriminatory and would have interfered with people's access to healthcare. The judge's ruling blocked the Trump administration from providing legal cover for discrimination, and instead left it to Congress to decide whether to change the laws regarding employers' duty to accommodate religious objections. The ruling was hailed as a victory by healthcare organizations and advocates, while a Republican senator called it "absurd mush" and urged the administration to appeal. END OF SUMMARY.
