SUMMARY: A federal judge struck down a Trump administration HHS rule that would let health‑care workers refuse abortions and other services on moral or religious grounds, finding the agency had **overstepped** its authority and gone **beyond** existing law. The judge said the rule was a **solution in search of a problem**, that the department’s claim of a surge in complaints was **untrue**, and that the measure would be **costly**, **damaging**, and could **inhibit** emergency care. Plaintiffs—including 19 states, D.C., local governments and health groups—argued the rule would be **discriminatory** and **interfere** with access to care; the court **blocked** it, denying the administration legal **cover** for **discrimination**. The ruling noted the HHS action was **outside** its authority and based on false **claims**, warning that it could **jeopardize** vulnerable populations and force employers to **decide** staffing changes. The judge said Congress, not HHS, should **decide** any changes to the **conscience** provisions and warned against **violating** existing legal standards. END OF SUMMARY.
