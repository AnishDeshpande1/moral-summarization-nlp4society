SUMMARY:

A federal judge has struck down a Trump administration rule that would have allowed health care workers to refuse to participate in abortions and other procedures on moral or religious grounds. The judge ruled that the Department of Health and Human Services overstepped its authority and that the measure could be costly, burdensome, and damaging to emergency care. The rule was based on a false claim that there was a significant increase in complaints about workers being forced to violate their conscience. The judge warned that the rule could force some health care employers to double or triple staff, particularly during emergencies, and could jeopardize the health of vulnerable populations, including women, LGBT people, and people with HIV or AIDS. The ruling was hailed as a victory for access to healthcare and a blow to discrimination, while opponents called it "absurd mush" and urged an appeal. 

END OF SUMMARY.
