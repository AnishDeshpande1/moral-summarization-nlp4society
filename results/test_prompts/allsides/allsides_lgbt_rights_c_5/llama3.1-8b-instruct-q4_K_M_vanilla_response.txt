SUMMARY:

The Obama administration is set to file two briefs in same-sex marriage cases at the US Supreme Court by the end of this month. In the Proposition 8 case from California, the administration faces difficult questions on whether same-sex couples have a constitutional right to marry. President Obama's position on gay marriage has shifted over the past eight months, and his administration must decide how to argue in court. The brief can take various routes, including defending a constitutional right to marry or arguing that California cannot revoke the right it previously granted. The decision will be made by President Obama himself.

The outcome of this case is crucial for both sides, as a Supreme Court decision could invalidate laws in some 30 states. Same-sex marriage advocates are holding their breath for now, hoping to avoid appearing to pressure the administration. Experts believe that while the brief may not directly influence the justices' decision, it will still be an important statement on what is morally right and wrong.

END OF SUMMARY.
