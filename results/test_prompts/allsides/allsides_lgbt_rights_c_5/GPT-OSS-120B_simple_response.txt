SUMMARY:  
The Obama administration faces a pivotal, symbolic decision on gay marriage as it prepares to file a brief in the Supreme Court’s Proposition 8 case. While not required, the brief will signal the government’s moral stance on whether same‑sex couples possess a constitutional right to marry—a question that could affect laws in about 30 states. President Obama’s rhetoric has shifted from a cautious, states‑rights view to a more expansive claim of equality, echoing the 1967 ruling that ended bans on interracial marriage. The administration can choose a bold defense of a universal marriage right, a narrower argument that California cannot retract a right it briefly granted, or simply contest the standing of the initiative’s sponsors. Both marriage‑equality advocates and traditional‑marriage opponents view the brief as a moral symbol, even if its legal impact may be limited. Lawyers note that briefs often speak to the nation’s conscience as much as to the justices, underscoring the case’s moral significance beyond its legal technicalities.  

END OF SUMMARY.
