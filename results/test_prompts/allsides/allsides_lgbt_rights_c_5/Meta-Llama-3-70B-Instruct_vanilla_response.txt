SUMMARY:

The Obama administration is expected to file two briefs in same-sex marriage cases at the US Supreme Court by the end of the month. The Proposition 8 case from California poses tricky questions for the administration, with President Obama expected to make the final decision on the arguments to be made. The case involves a California ballot initiative banning same-sex marriage, which was struck down by a federal district court and appeals court. The administration's brief could take various legal routes, including arguing for a constitutional right to marry, which would invalidate laws in 30 states. However, Obama's changing position on the issue adds complexity, with his inaugural address suggesting a more expansive view of marriage equality. The administration's stance will be symbolic and important for both the gay community and traditional marriage advocates.

END OF SUMMARY.
