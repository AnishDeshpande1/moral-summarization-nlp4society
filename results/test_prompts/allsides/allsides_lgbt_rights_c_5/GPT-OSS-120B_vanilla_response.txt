SUMMARY: The Obama administration is poised to file a brief in the Supreme Court’s Proposition 8 case, which challenges California’s ban on same‑sex marriage. While the government isn’t required to intervene, it will, and President Obama must decide which legal arguments to advance. Options range from a sweeping claim that the Constitution guarantees a fundamental right to marry—potentially overturning bans in about 30 states—to narrower approaches, such as arguing that California cannot rescind marriage rights it previously granted or that the initiative’s sponsors lack standing. Obama’s public stance has evolved from supporting state‑level decisions to endorsing broader equality, hinting at a more robust constitutional argument. Both marriage‑equality advocates and traditional‑marriage opponents view the administration’s position as symbolically crucial, though some legal experts doubt its impact on the justices. The brief will be paired with a case on the Defense of Marriage Act, where the administration already argues DOMA is unconstitutional. The outcome could shape the national legal landscape for same‑sex marriage.

END OF SUMMARY.
