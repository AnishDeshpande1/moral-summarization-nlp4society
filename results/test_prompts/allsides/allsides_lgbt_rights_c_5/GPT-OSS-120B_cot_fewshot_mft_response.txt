**SUMMARY:**
The Obama administration is poised to make a symbolic yet potentially far‑reaching choice on gay marriage as it prepares two briefs for the Supreme Court, including the contentious California Proposition 8 case. The core legal question hinges on whether same‑sex couples possess a constitutional **right** to marry, a decision that could invalidate bans in about 30 states and address past **discrimination**. President Obama’s evolving stance—from endorsing marriage equality while leaving the issue to the **states**, to declaring that the nation’s “journey is not complete until our gay brothers and sisters are treated like anyone else under the law”—signals a move toward broader **fairness** and **equal** treatment. The administration could argue that California’s revocation of a previously granted marriage **right** is **unjust**, that the state’s distinction between civil benefits and marriage constitutes **discriminatory** treatment, or simply challenge the standing of Proposition 8’s sponsors. Advocates on both sides recognize the brief’s symbolic weight as a statement about what is **morally** right and **just** in America. While legal experts debate the brief’s impact on the justices, its language may shape public perception of **fairness** and **justice** in the fight against **discrimination**.
