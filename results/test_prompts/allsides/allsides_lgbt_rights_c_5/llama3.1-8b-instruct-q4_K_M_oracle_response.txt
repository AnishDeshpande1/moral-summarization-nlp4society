SUMMARY:

The Obama administration is expected to file briefs in two same-sex marriage cases at the U.S. Supreme Court, including Proposition 8 from California. This case poses difficult questions for the administration, particularly since President Obama's position on gay marriage has seemingly changed over the past eight months. The president initially said that the decision on legalizing gay marriage should be left to the states, but later stated that same-sex couples have a constitutional right to marry. The government could argue in favor of this right, or take a lesser stance, such as saying California's ban on gay marriage was unconstitutional discrimination because it revoked a previously granted fundamental right. The decision will be a symbolic one for both sides, with the government's brief potentially making a statement about what is morally right and wrong. Ultimately, the Supreme Court justices may not rely heavily on the administration's position in their decision.

END OF SUMMARY.
