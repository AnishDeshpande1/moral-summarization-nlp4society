SUMMARY:

The Obama administration faces a crucial decision on gay marriage as it prepares to file two briefs in same-sex marriage cases at the US Supreme Court. The Proposition 8 case from California poses thorny questions, and President Obama is expected to make the final decision on the Justice Department's arguments. The case revolves around the California ballot initiative banning same-sex marriage, which was struck down by a federal district court as unconstitutional discrimination. The administration's brief will determine whether same-sex couples have a constitutional right to marry, which could invalidate laws in 30 states. Obama's stance has evolved, from initially supporting same-sex marriage but leaving legalization to the states, to seemingly advocating for a more basic constitutional right to marry. The administration could take a bold, full-throated defense of the right to marry, arguing that California's ban is discriminatory, or take a more nuanced approach. The decision will have significant symbolic importance, with both sides urging the administration to take their side.

END OF SUMMARY.
