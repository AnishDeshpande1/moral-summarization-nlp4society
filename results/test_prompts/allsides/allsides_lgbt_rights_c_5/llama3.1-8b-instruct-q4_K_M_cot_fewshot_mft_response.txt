SUMMARY:
The Obama administration faces a difficult decision on same-sex marriage, with two possible briefs in a pair of Supreme Court cases. The Proposition 8 case from California poses the thornier questions, as it challenges the constitutional right to marry for same-sex couples. President Obama has changed his position on this issue over the past eight months, seemingly taking a more expansive view at his inauguration. The administration could make a bold defense of the right to marry or argue lesser positions, such as that California's ban on gay marriage is discriminatory and unconstitutional. For the gay community, the position the government takes in this case is hugely important – if nothing else, as a symbol. Same-sex marriage advocates are holding their collective breath, hoping to avoid the impression that the gay community is pressuring the administration. The decision will have far-reaching implications for laws in some 30 states.
