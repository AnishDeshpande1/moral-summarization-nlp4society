SUMMARY:

The Obama administration is expected to file a brief in the Proposition 8 case, which challenges California's ban on same-sex marriage. The case poses difficult questions for the administration, and President Obama's position on gay marriage has seemingly changed over time. In May, he endorsed same-sex marriage from a personal point of view but said it should be left to the states. However, at his inauguration in January, he seemed to take a more expansive view, suggesting that same-sex couples have a constitutional right to marry. The administration's brief will be crucial, as it could either defend the right to marry or argue for lesser positions, such as the state's ability to revoke previously granted rights. Same-sex marriage advocates are holding their breath, hoping the administration will take their side and uphold fairness and equality. The brief is seen as a symbol of what is morally right and wrong, with implications extending beyond the case itself.

END OF SUMMARY.
