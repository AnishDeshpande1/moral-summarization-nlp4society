**SUMMARY:**  
The Obama administration faces a pivotal, symbolic decision on gay marriage as it prepares briefs for two Supreme Court cases, including California’s Proposition 8. The core moral question is whether same‑sex couples possess a constitutional **right** to marry, a **right** that would overturn bans in about 30 states. President Obama’s rhetoric has shifted from viewing marriage as a state issue to asserting that the nation’s **journey** is incomplete until gay citizens are treated **equal** under the law, echoing the **historic** 1967 ruling on interracial marriage. The administration can adopt a **bold** stance—defending the **right** to marry on **fair** and **moral** grounds—or pursue narrower arguments, such as protecting the brief period when California allowed gay marriage or challenging the standing of Proposition 8’s sponsors. Both advocates of gay marriage and supporters of **traditional** marriage view the government’s brief as a powerful **symbol** of what is **morally** **right** and **wrong** in America. Legal experts doubt the brief will sway the justices, but its moral message may resonate widely with the public.
