SUMMARY:

The Obama administration faces a crucial decision on gay marriage as it prepares to file two briefs in same-sex marriage cases at the U.S. Supreme Court. The Proposition 8 case from California poses tricky questions, and President Obama is expected to make the final decision on the Justice Department's arguments. The administration's brief could take various legal routes, including a bold defense of the right to marry, arguing that California cannot revoke the right, or that the state's law amounts to discrimination. The gay community and traditional marriage advocates are eagerly waiting for the administration's stance, which will carry significant symbolic weight. While some lawyers believe the administration's brief may not greatly influence the justices, others argue that it will make a statement about what is morally right and wrong, resonating with Americans beyond the Supreme Court.

END OF SUMMARY.
