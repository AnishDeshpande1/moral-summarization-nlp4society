SUMMARY:
The Obama administration is wrestling with a pivotal, highly symbolic decision about gay marriage as it prepares to file briefs in two Supreme Court cases, including the contentious California Proposition 8 case. While the government is not required to intervene, officials recognize that the arguments it chooses will signal the administration’s moral stance on marriage equality. President Obama’s own rhetoric has shifted—from endorsing same‑sex marriage but deferring to states, to asserting that true equality demands equal legal treatment for gay couples. The Justice Department can adopt a bold, sweeping defense of a constitutional right to marry, echo a narrower “cannot revoke a right once granted” argument, or simply challenge the standing of Proposition 8’s private sponsors. Advocates on both sides view the brief as a powerful symbolic gesture about what is morally right, even if its legal impact may be limited. The administration’s ultimate choice will convey whether it believes the nation’s journey toward equality is complete only when gay couples enjoy the same marriage rights as everyone else.  
END OF SUMMARY.
