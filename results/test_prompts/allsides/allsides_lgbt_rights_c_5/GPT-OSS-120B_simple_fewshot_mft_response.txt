SUMMARY:
The Obama administration is poised to file a brief in the Supreme Court’s Proposition 8 case, a decision that carries heavy symbolic weight for the fight over marriage equality. By choosing how to argue the case—whether to assert a broad constitutional right to marry, to emphasize that California cannot rescind a right it once granted, or to challenge the standing of the initiative’s sponsors—the government will signal whether it views the denial of same‑sex marriage as a fundamental injustice. Advocates see a strong, fairness‑driven brief as a necessary stand against the cheating of gay couples out of equal legal recognition, while opponents hope for a narrower approach that leaves other states’ bans untouched. The administration’s ultimate stance will thus reflect its commitment to treating same‑sex couples fairly under the law, rather than allowing a discriminatory ban to persist.

END OF SUMMARY.
