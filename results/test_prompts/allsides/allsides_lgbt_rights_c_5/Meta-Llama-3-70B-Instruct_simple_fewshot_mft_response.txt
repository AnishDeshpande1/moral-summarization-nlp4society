SUMMARY:

The Obama administration is set to file two briefs in same-sex marriage cases at the U.S. Supreme Court, with the Proposition 8 case from California posing tricky political and legal questions. The administration's stance on gay marriage has evolved, with President Obama recently expressing support for same-sex couples' right to marry. However, the administration must now decide whether to argue for a constitutional right to marry, or take a more limited approach. The decision is crucial, as it will have significant implications for the gay community and the institution of marriage. The moral framing of this issue revolves around the dimension of Fairness/Cheating, as the administration's stance will be seen as either upholding the principle of equal treatment under the law or perpetuating discrimination against same-sex couples. The administration's brief will be closely watched, not just for its legal arguments, but also as a statement about what is morally right and wrong.
