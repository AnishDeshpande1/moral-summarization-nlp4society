SUMMARY:

The Obama administration is facing a crucial decision on whether to file a brief in support of same-sex marriage in the Proposition 8 case, which challenges California's ban on gay marriage. President Obama has changed his position on the issue, initially suggesting that states should decide on marriage laws but later indicating a more expansive view of constitutional rights. The administration is weighing various legal arguments, including whether California's initial recognition of same-sex couples' right to marry gives them a fundamental right to do so. The decision will have significant implications for the country, with some 30 states potentially affected by a Supreme Court ruling in favor of gay marriage. Both sides are lobbying hard, and the outcome is seen as symbolic of the nation's values on equality and justice.

END OF SUMMARY.
