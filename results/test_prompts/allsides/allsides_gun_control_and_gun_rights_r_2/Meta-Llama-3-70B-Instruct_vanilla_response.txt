SUMMARY:

A gunman opened fire at the West Freeway Church of Christ in Texas, but a 71-year-old security volunteer, Jack Wilson, quickly stopped the shooter, saving lives. This incident highlights the effectiveness of allowing law-abiding citizens with concealed-carry licenses to bring their weapons into places of worship. Texas Governor Greg Abbott had signed a bill making it legal for Texans with concealed-carry licenses to bring their weapons into churches, a move that has generated hostility among gun-control advocates. The article argues that allowing trained and licensed individuals to carry guns can deter mass shootings and mitigate damage. It also criticizes draconian gun laws that make it impossible for normal people to practice their right to self-defense, citing the example of Orthodox Jews in New York who are increasingly becoming targets of violence.

END OF SUMMARY.
