SUMMARY:
A 71‑year‑old former deputy sheriff, Jack Wilson, used his legally‑carried concealed weapon to stop a gunman inside West Freeway Church of Christ in Texas, demonstrating how “good guys with guns” can save lives. The incident underscores the importance of Texas Gov. Greg Abbott’s law that lets licensed, background‑checked citizens carry firearms in places of worship— a right the author argues Democrats label “irrational” while actually protecting congregations from violent attackers. The piece argues that gun‑free zones invite shooters, whereas armed, law‑abiding volunteers can deter or mitigate mass shootings, citing similar heroic actions at First Baptist Church (Texas) and New Life Church (Colorado). It condemns restrictive gun laws in states like New York that leave ordinary people defenseless, especially minority communities targeted by hate‑motivated violence. Framed as a moral imperative, the narrative asserts that the Second Amendment and the natural right of self‑defense— championed historically by figures such as John Adams and Ze’ev Jabotinsky— must be upheld to allow citizens to protect themselves, their families, and their faith communities. END OF SUMMARY.
