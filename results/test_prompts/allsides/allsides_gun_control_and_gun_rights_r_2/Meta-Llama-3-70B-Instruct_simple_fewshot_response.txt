SUMMARY:

A recent incident at the West Freeway Church of Christ in Texas highlights the importance of allowing law-abiding citizens to carry guns in places of worship. A 71-year-old security volunteer, Jack Wilson, quickly stopped a potential mass murderer, saving countless lives. This incident underscores the effectiveness of good guys with guns in preventing tragedies. Despite this, gun control advocates continue to push for stricter laws, ignoring the fact that mass shooters are often undeterred by laws. The Texas law allowing concealed carry in churches is seen as a positive step towards self-defense, but is met with hostility by those who prioritize gun control over individual rights. The moral imperative is clear: allowing trained and licensed citizens to carry guns can save lives and prevent tragedies. By prioritizing self-defense, we can create safer communities and protect our most vulnerable citizens. END OF SUMMARY.
