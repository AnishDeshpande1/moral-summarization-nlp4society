SUMMARY:

In a recent incident at West Freeway Church of Christ in Texas, a gunman was stopped by a 71-year-old security volunteer, Jack Wilson, who was legally carrying a firearm. This heroic act of self-defense saved lives and highlights the importance of allowing trained and licensed individuals to protect themselves and their communities in places of worship. The Texas law, signed by Governor Greg Abbott, explicitly permits concealed-carry license holders to bring their weapons into churches, a move that has been met with hostility by gun-control advocates. However, this incident demonstrates that "good guys with guns" can indeed stop mass shooters and mitigate damage. The presence of armed citizens can deter murderers, who often prefer soft targets. In contrast, draconian laws in states like New York limit individuals' right to self-defense, forcing them to sit defenseless. The author argues that allowing congregations to govern their own security can be a lifesaving measure, and that the right to self-protection is a fundamental aspect of human nature. END OF SUMMARY.
