SUMMARY:

A gunman opened fire on parishioners at West Freeway Church of Christ in Texas, but a 71-year-old security volunteer, Jack Wilson, quickly stopped the potential mass murderer. This was possible due to a Texas law allowing concealed-carry license holders to bring weapons into places of worship. The author argues that "good guys with guns" can save lives, and this incident proves it. In contrast, gun-control advocates often prioritize laws over self-defense, even though most mass shootings occur in gun-free zones. The author cites examples of concealed carriers stopping or mitigating mass shootings, and notes that allowing citizens to defend themselves in places of worship has no downside. The article concludes by highlighting the importance of self-defense, particularly for Jews, who have historically been targeted by violence.

END OF SUMMARY.
