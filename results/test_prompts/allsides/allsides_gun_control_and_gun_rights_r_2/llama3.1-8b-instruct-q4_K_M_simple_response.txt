This incident highlights the effectiveness of allowing law-abiding citizens to protect themselves and their communities in places of worship. The author argues that gun control advocates often use euphemisms like "doing something" about gun violence to justify restricting the rights of law-abiding citizens. The article references several instances where concealed carriers have stopped mass shooters, and concludes that there is no downside to allowing congregations to govern their own security.

The author also draws a historical parallel between the Jewish people's fight for self-defense in Israel and the current debate over gun laws in the US. They argue that Jews should be free to practice their right to self-defense, as enshrined in the Second Amendment. The article concludes by praising Texas' approach to gun laws and criticizing draconian regulations in other states.
