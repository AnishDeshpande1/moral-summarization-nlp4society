SUMMARY:

A recent incident at the West Freeway Church of Christ in Texas highlights the importance of allowing law-abiding citizens to carry firearms for self-defense. Jack Wilson, a congregant and security volunteer, stopped a potential mass murderer within seconds of the attack. This event demonstrates that good guys with guns can indeed save lives. The author argues that gun control advocates often prioritize restricting law-abiding citizens' right to self-defense over actual solutions to preventing mass shootings. They suggest that laws allowing concealed carry in places of worship have been unfairly criticized by media and politicians. The author draws parallels between the Israeli experience, where Jews took up arms for self-defense, and the current situation in the United States, arguing that it is essential to preserve the right to bear arms for personal protection. This stance emphasizes the moral imperative of protecting individual rights and freedoms, particularly when it comes to self-defense.

END OF SUMMARY.
