SUMMARY: A 71‑year‑old former deputy sheriff, Jack Wilson, used his concealed‑carry permit to stop a gunman inside West Freeway Church of Christ in White Settlement, Texas, shortly after the state passed a law allowing licensed gun owners to carry in places of worship. The article argues that such laws enable “good guys with guns” to protect congregants, citing similar incidents in Texas and Colorado where armed civilians intervened in mass shootings. It criticizes Democratic opposition to the Texas law, claiming it unfairly restricts law‑abiding citizens while doing little to deter determined attackers. The piece also links the right to bear arms to historical Jewish self‑defense and Zionist ideology, suggesting that restrictions in states like New York leave vulnerable communities defenseless. The author concludes that allowing churches and other venues to decide their own security measures, including permitting armed volunteers, is a practical, constitutionally protected means of enhancing public safety. END OF SUMMARY.
