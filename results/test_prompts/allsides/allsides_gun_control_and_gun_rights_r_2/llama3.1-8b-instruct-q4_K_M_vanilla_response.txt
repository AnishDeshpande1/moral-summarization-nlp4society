A mass shooting at the West Freeway Church of Christ in Texas was stopped by Jack Wilson, a congregant and security volunteer, who drew his gun and killed the shooter within seconds. This incident highlights the effectiveness of allowing law-abiding citizens to carry concealed firearms in places of worship. The article argues that such laws are often misunderstood or misrepresented by gun-control advocates, who frame them as making it easier for mass shooters to target churches and schools.

The author notes that most high-profile mass shootings occur in gun-free zones, and that the presence of good guys with guns can sometimes mitigate the damage inflicted by murderers. The article also references two previous incidents where civilians with guns stopped mass shootings at a Texas church and a Colorado Springs church.

The article concludes that allowing congregations to govern their own security is a reasonable approach, as it respects the Second Amendment right to self-defense and allows individuals to protect themselves, their community, and their family in places of worship.
