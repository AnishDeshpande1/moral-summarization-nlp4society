SUMMARY:

The recent shooting at West Freeway Church of Christ in Texas highlights the importance of allowing good guys with guns to protect themselves and their communities. A 71-year-old congregant and security volunteer, Jack Wilson, stopped a potential mass murderer in mere seconds, thanks to a Texas law that permits concealed-carry license holders to bring their weapons into places of worship. This incident underscores the moral imperative to prioritize self-defense and protect innocent lives. Gun control advocates, however, continue to push for laws that inhibit law-abiding citizens from owning guns, ignoring the reality that trained and licensed individuals can mitigate harm and save lives. The argument that guns rarely stop mass shootings is misleading, as most high-profile incidents occur in gun-free zones. By allowing congregations to govern their own security, we can empower citizens to defend themselves and their communities, rather than leaving them defenseless. This moral framing emphasizes the importance of Authority/Subversion, Care/Harm, and Fairness/Cheating, highlighting the need to prioritize self-defense, protect innocent lives, and ensure that law-abiding citizens are not unfairly restricted.

END OF SUMMARY.
