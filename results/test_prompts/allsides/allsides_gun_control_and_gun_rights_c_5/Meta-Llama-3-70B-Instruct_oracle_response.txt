SUMMARY:

A former U.S. serviceman, Devin Kelley, who committed the deadliest mass shooting on record in Texas, had a history of violence and threatening behavior. In 2012, he escaped from a mental hospital while facing court-martial on domestic violence charges, and was later convicted. Kelley had been "sneaking firearms" onto a U.S. Air Force base and "attempting to carry out death threats" against his military commanders. He was apprehended without incident, but later killed himself after opening fire on worshipers at a church, killing 26 people, including an unborn child, and wounding 20 others. The Air Force failed to enter his conviction into a government database, allowing him to purchase guns despite his criminal record. This "appalling" lapse has sparked calls for legislation to block those convicted of domestic violence from purchasing guns. Kelley's troubled past, including behavior problems as a student and a history of suspensions, drugs, and insubordination, has raised questions about how he was able to carry out such a lethal rampage.

END OF SUMMARY.
