SUMMARY:

A police report revealed that Devin Kelley, the gunman who committed the deadliest mass shooting in Texas history, escaped from a mental hospital in 2012 while facing court-martial on domestic violence charges. He was later convicted and discharged from the Air Force in 2014. Despite having a criminal record, Kelley passed background checks to buy guns at a San Antonio sporting goods store twice. The Air Force acknowledged it failed to enter his conviction into a government database used for screening gun buyers. This lapse has led to calls for legislation to ensure domestic violence convictions are reported correctly and prevent gun sales to those with such records. The incident highlights weaknesses in the background check system, sparking debate over gun ownership and control.

Kelley's troubled past includes numerous behavior problems as a student, including suspensions, and a history of domestic disputes. His phone was sent to the FBI lab for analysis, but access has been delayed. The shooting has reignited the gun control debate, with President Trump suggesting stricter reviews may not have prevented the attack.
