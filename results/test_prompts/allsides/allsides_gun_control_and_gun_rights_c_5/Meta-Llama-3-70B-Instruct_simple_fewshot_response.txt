SUMMARY:

A former U.S. serviceman, Devin Kelley, committed the deadliest mass shooting in Texas history, killing 26 people, including an unborn child, and wounding 20 others. Kelley had a troubled past, having escaped from a mental hospital in 2012 while facing court-martial for domestic violence charges. He was later convicted and sentenced to a year in military detention. Despite his criminal record, Kelley was able to purchase guns twice in the past year due to the Air Force's failure to enter his conviction into a government database. This lapse has been called "appalling" by a Republican congressman. Kelley's behavior problems began in school, and his adult life was marked by turbulence, including a domestic dispute with his in-laws. The massacre has reignited the debate over gun ownership, with some calling for stricter reviews of gun purchases, while others, like President Trump, believe it would not have made a difference.

END OF SUMMARY.
