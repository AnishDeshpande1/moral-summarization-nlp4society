SUMMARY:

Devin Kelley, the perpetrator of the deadliest mass shooting in Texas history, escaped from a mental hospital in 2012 while facing court-martial on domestic violence charges. Despite being convicted and having a record that should have prevented him from buying guns, Kelley purchased firearms twice at a San Antonio sporting goods store using required background checks. The Air Force failed to enter his conviction into the National Criminal Information Center (NCIC) system, allowing him to purchase guns despite federal law prohibiting it for those with domestic violence convictions. Kelley's troubled history includes numerous behavior problems as a student and a history of domestic disputes. His actions have rekindled debate over gun ownership and Second Amendment protections. President Trump believes stricter reviews would not have stopped the shooting, but others argue that tighter laws could have prevented such tragedies.

END OF SUMMARY.
