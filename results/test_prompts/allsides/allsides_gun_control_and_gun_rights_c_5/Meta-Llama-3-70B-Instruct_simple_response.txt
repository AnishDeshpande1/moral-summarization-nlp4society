SUMMARY:

The former US serviceman, Devin Kelley, who committed the deadliest mass shooting in Texas, had a troubled past, including escaping from a mental hospital in 2012 while facing court-martial for domestic violence charges. Despite being convicted, Kelley was able to purchase guns twice in the past year due to the Air Force's failure to enter his conviction into a government database. This lapse allowed him to pass criminal background checks. Kelley's history of violence and mental disorders was well-documented, including behavior problems as a student and a bad-conduct discharge from the Air Force. The massacre, which killed 26 people, including an unborn child, has reignited the debate over gun ownership and the need for stricter reviews of gun purchases. Two senators have planned to co-sponsor legislation to block those convicted of domestic violence from legally purchasing guns.

END OF SUMMARY.
