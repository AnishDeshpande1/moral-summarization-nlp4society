SUMMARY:

A former U.S. serviceman, Devin Kelley, who committed the deadliest mass shooting on record in Texas, had a history of violence and behavior problems. He escaped from a mental hospital in 2012 while facing court-martial on domestic violence charges for which he was later convicted. The Air Force failed to enter his conviction into a government database that screens prospective gun buyers, allowing him to pass background checks twice at a sporting goods store. Kelley's troubled past included numerous suspensions and behavior issues as a student, as well as a history of assaulting his first wife and stepson. He also had a domestic dispute with his in-laws, sending threatening text messages to his mother-in-law. The shooting, which killed 26 people and wounded 20, rekindled the debate over gun ownership and stricter reviews of gun purchases. Federal law prohibits selling guns to those convicted of domestic violence crimes, highlighting the failure to prevent Kelley's actions.

END OF SUMMARY.
