SUMMARY:

A mass shooting at the First Baptist Church in Sutherland Springs, Texas, left 26 people dead and 20 wounded. Devin Kelley, the perpetrator, had a troubled past, including domestic violence convictions and behavior problems as a student. He was convicted by court-martial of assaulting his first wife and stepson while serving in the Air Force, but the conviction was not entered into the National Criminal Information Center (NCIC) system, allowing him to pass background checks and purchase guns. Kelley's record included nine suspensions for issues such as drugs, insubordination, and dishonesty. He was apprehended after escaping from a mental hospital in 2012 and later killed himself during a failed getaway attempt. The failure of the Air Force to report his conviction has sparked calls for legislation aimed at blocking gun purchases by those with domestic violence records. Experts say this case exposes a weak link in the background check system, allowing violent individuals to obtain guns. The shooting has reignited debate over gun ownership and stricter reviews of gun purchases.

END OF SUMMARY.
