SUMMARY:

A police report revealed that Devin Kelley, the former US serviceman who committed the deadliest mass shooting in Texas, escaped from a mental hospital in 2012 while facing court-martial on domestic violence charges. He was later convicted and had a history of behavior problems as a student and in his adult life. Kelley stormed into a church in Sutherland Springs, Texas, on Sunday, killing 26 people, including an unborn child, and wounding 20 others before killing himself. Despite having a criminal record, Kelley was able to purchase guns twice in the past year due to an Air Force failure to enter his conviction into a government database. The incident has sparked debate over gun ownership and background checks, with lawmakers planning to introduce legislation to block those convicted of domestic violence from purchasing guns.

END OF SUMMARY.
