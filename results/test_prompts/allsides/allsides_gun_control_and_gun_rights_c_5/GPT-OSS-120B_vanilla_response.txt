SUMMARY: A police report revealed that Devin Kelley, the former Air Force serviceman responsible for the deadliest mass shooting in Texas history at Sutherland Springs, escaped a mental hospital in 2012 while awaiting a court‑martial on domestic‑violence charges. Despite warnings that he was a danger to himself and others and had made death threats, he was later recaptured at an El Paso bus station. In 2012 Kelley was convicted by court‑martial of assaulting his wife and stepson, but the Air Force failed to enter his conviction into the National Criminal Information Center database, allowing him to pass background checks and purchase firearms legally. On November 5, 2017, Kelley entered a church with a semi‑automatic rifle, killing 26 people (including an unborn child) and wounding 20 before killing himself after being injured by a civilian. The incident exposed a gap in the background‑check system, prompting congressional proposals to block gun purchases by anyone convicted of domestic violence, and an Air Force inquiry and Defense Department review of reporting procedures. END OF SUMMARY.
