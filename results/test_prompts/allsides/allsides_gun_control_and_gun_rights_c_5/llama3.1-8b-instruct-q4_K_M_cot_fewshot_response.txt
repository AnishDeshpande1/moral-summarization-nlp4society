SUMMARY:
The deadly mass shooting in Sutherland Springs, Texas, has exposed a previously unnoticed weak link in the system of background checks. Devin Kelley, 26, had escaped from a mental hospital in 2012 after facing court-martial on domestic violence charges for which he was later convicted. The Air Force had failed to enter his conviction into a government database that all licensed firearms dealers are required to use to screen prospective gun buyers for their criminal history. This lapse allowed Kelley to pass background checks and purchase guns, despite having a record that should have prevented those purchases. The failure to report Kelley's record is an appalling lapse, and the case has rekindled the ongoing debate over gun ownership. Authorities say Kelley was motivated by some unspecified domestic dispute with his in-laws, but it is not clear what role this played in Sunday's violence.
