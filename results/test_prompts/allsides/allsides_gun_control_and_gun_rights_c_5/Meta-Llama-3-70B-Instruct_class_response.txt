SUMMARY:

The deadliest mass shooting in Texas history was committed by Devin Kelley, a former U.S. serviceman with a troubled past. Kelley escaped from a mental hospital in 2012 while facing court-martial on domestic violence charges, and was later convicted. He had a history of "sneaking firearms" onto a U.S. Air Force base and making "death threats" against his military commanders. Despite being found guilty of assaulting his first wife and stepson, Kelley was able to purchase guns due to the Air Force's "appalling" failure to enter his conviction into a government database. On Sunday, Kelley stormed into a church in Sutherland Springs, Texas, and opened fire on worshipers, killing 26 people, including an unborn child, and wounding 20 others. Kelley killed himself after being wounded by an armed civilian. The massacre has sparked a debate over gun ownership and raised questions about the effectiveness of background checks. The Air Force has opened an inquiry into the matter, and two U.S. senators plan to co-sponsor legislation to ensure that those convicted of domestic violence are blocked from legally purchasing a gun. 

END OF SUMMARY.
