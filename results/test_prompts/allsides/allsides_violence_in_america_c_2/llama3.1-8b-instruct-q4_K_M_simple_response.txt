SUMMARY:
Patrick Crusius, the 21-year-old suspect in the El Paso mass shooting that left 22 dead, was indicted for capital murder on September 12, 2019. Prosecutors are seeking the death penalty and federal prosecutors are considering hate-crime charges. The indictment comes after a series of mass shootings in August, including one in Dayton, Ohio, and another in West Texas cities of Midland and Odessa. Crusius allegedly targeted Mexicans during the El Paso shooting, stating "I'm the shooter" and that he was responding to the "Hispanic invasion of Texas." His defense team has vowed to prevent his execution.

The incident has sparked debate over gun control and immigration policies. Texas Governor Greg Abbott acknowledged mistakes were made in his handling of the situation, but resisted calls for an emergency special session on new gun-control measures. Democrats criticized Abbott's proposals as insufficient, while Lieutenant Governor Dan Patrick drew criticism from the National Rifle Association for supporting background checks.

END OF SUMMARY.
