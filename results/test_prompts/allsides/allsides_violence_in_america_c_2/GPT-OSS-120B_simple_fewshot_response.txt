SUMMARY:
A Texas grand jury indicted 21‑year‑old Patrick Crusius on a capital‑murder charge for the Aug. 3 Walmart shooting in El Paso that killed 22 people, most of them Hispanic, and left the community reeling. Prosecutors, seeking the death penalty, say Crusius drove over ten hours to the border city, surrendered to police, and posted a manifesto decrying a “Hispanic invasion,” echoing divisive immigration rhetoric that critics blame on President Trump. The indictment, still pending public release, also opens the door to federal hate‑crime charges that could carry the death penalty. Governor Greg Abbott’s terse “good” tweet and his reluctance to pursue sweeping gun‑control reforms—despite recent spate of mass shootings in Texas and Ohio—have drawn sharp criticism from Democrats, gun‑control advocates, and the NRA‑aligned Lt. Gov. Dan Patrick. Survivors continue to hold vigils while Crusius remains jailed without bond on suicide watch. The case underscores the moral urgency of confronting hate‑fueled violence and the political battle over firearm regulation and immigration rhetoric in America.

END OF SUMMARY.
