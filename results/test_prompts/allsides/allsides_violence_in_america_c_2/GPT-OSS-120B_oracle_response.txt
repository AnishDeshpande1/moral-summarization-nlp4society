SUMMARY:  
A Texas man, Patrick Crusius, was indicted on a capital‑murder count for the Aug. 3 **shooting** at a Walmart in El Paso that left 22 people **dead**. He remains **jailed** without bond while prosecutors seek the death penalty and consider **hate‑crime** charges that could also bring capital punishment. Crusius confessed to the **attack**, saying he was **targeting** Mexicans and had posted a hateful manifesto echoing divisive political rhetoric. The **massacre** sparked national debate over **violence**, **division**, and gun policy, with Texas officials offering tepid responses and resisting comprehensive **background‑check** reforms. In the wake of this **shooting**, similar **attacks** occurred in Dayton, Ohio, and in Midland‑Odessa, Texas, resulting in further **killing**, **injuring**, and **wounding** of victims, though those gunmen were later **killed** by police. Critics accused leaders of **mistakes** that **inflamed** tensions and failed to curb the rising tide of **shootings** across the country.
