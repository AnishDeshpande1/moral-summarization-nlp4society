A Texas grand jury indicted Patrick Crusius on a capital murder count for the Aug. 3 El Paso Walmart shooting that left 22 victims dead, and prosecutors are seeking the death penalty. The attack, motivated by hate and targeting Mexicans, was linked to a divisive anti‑immigration screed that many blamed for inflaming violence. Crusius remains jailed without bond, isolated on suicide watch, while survivors hold vigils and protest the perceived injustice. The indictment fuels anger over gun‑control and immigration debates; Governor Abbott’s “good” tweet and his “defend” messaging were condemned as insufficient, especially after his earlier “take matters into our own hands” fundraiser was called a mistake. Federal officials are weighing additional hate‑crime charges that could also carry the death penalty.
