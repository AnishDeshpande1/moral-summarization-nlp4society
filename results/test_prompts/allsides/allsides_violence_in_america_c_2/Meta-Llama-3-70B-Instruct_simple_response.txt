SUMMARY: 

Patrick Crusius, a 21-year-old man, has been indicted for capital murder in connection with the mass shooting at a Walmart in El Paso, Texas, that left 22 people dead. Prosecutors are seeking the death penalty for Crusius, who remains jailed without bond. The shooting was motivated by a racist ideology, with Crusius targeting Mexicans and publishing a screed that echoed President Donald Trump's divisive rhetoric on immigration. The massacre was followed by other mass shootings in Ohio and West Texas, fueling anger among gun control and immigration advocates. Texas Governor Greg Abbott has faced criticism for not taking stronger action on gun control, including resisting calls for an emergency special session to vote on new measures. Abbott's recommendations for firearm safety have been deemed insufficient by Democrats and gun-control groups. The indictment of Crusius is a step towards justice, but the larger issue of gun violence and racism remains a pressing concern.

END OF SUMMARY.
