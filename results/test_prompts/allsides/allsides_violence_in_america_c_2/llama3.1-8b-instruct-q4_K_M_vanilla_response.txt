SUMMARY:
A 21-year-old man, Patrick Crusius, has been indicted for capital murder in connection with the El Paso mass shooting that left 22 dead on August 3. Crusius is accused of gunning down people at a busy Walmart and targeting Mexicans, allegedly motivated by a "Hispanic invasion" of Texas. Prosecutors are seeking the death penalty, and federal prosecutors are considering hate-crime charges that could also carry the death penalty. Crusius' defense lawyers have vowed to prevent him from being executed.

The El Paso shooting was one of several mass shootings in the US last month, including attacks in Dayton, Ohio, and Midland/Odessa, Texas. The recent violence has sparked anger among gun control and immigration advocates and caused political blowback for Texas Governor Greg Abbott, who has resisted calls to convene an emergency special session on new gun-control measures. Abbott's recommendations for firearm safety do not include mandatory background checks of private gun sales. Democrats and gun-control groups have criticized his proposals as insufficient.

END OF SUMMARY.
