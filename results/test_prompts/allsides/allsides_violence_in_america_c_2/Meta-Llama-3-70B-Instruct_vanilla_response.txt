SUMMARY: 

Patrick Crusius, 21, has been indicted for capital murder in connection with the August 3 mass shooting at a Walmart in El Paso, Texas, that left 22 people dead. Prosecutors are seeking the death penalty for Crusius, who remains jailed without bond. Crusius surrendered to police after the attack, saying "I'm the shooter," and allegedly targeted Mexicans. A screed published shortly before the shooting echoed President Donald Trump's divisive rhetoric on immigration. The massacre was followed by other mass shootings in Ohio and West Texas, fueling anger among gun control and immigration advocates. Texas Governor Greg Abbott has resisted calls for new gun-control measures, instead releasing a list of firearm safety recommendations that didn't include mandatory background checks. Democrats and gun-control groups have criticized Abbott's recommendations as insufficient.

END OF SUMMARY.
