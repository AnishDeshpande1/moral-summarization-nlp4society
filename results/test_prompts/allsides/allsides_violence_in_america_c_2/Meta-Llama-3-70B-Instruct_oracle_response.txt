SUMMARY: 

Patrick Crusius, a 21-year-old from Texas, was indicted for capital murder in connection with the August 3 massacre at a Walmart in El Paso, Texas, that left 22 people dead. Crusius, who is jailed without bond, surrendered to police after the attack, saying "I'm the shooter," and was targeting Mexicans. Prosecutors alleged that Crusius was the author of a screed that parroted President Trump's divisive rhetoric about immigration, leading to accusations that Trump's words inflamed political and racial tensions. The massacre was the first in a series of mass shootings that left dozens dead and reignited the debate over guns in American politics. Federal prosecutors are weighing hate-crime charges against Crusius that could also carry the death penalty. The recent mass violence has fueled anger among gun control and immigration advocates, and caused political blowback, with some criticizing Texas Gov. Greg Abbott's response as insufficient.

END OF SUMMARY.
