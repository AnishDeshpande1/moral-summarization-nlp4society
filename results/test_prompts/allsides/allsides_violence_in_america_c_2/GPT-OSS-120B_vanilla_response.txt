SUMMARY: A grand jury indicted 21‑year‑old Patrick Crusius of Allen, Texas, on a single count of capital murder for the Aug. 3 mass shooting at a Walmart in El Paso that killed 22 people, most of them Hispanic. Prosecutors are seeking the death penalty and are also considering federal hate‑crime charges that could carry capital punishment. Crusius, who surrendered and claimed responsibility, had posted a manifesto denouncing a “Hispanic invasion of Texas,” echoing anti‑immigrant rhetoric that critics say was fueled by President Trump’s statements. He remains jailed without bond on suicide watch. The indictment, not yet public, follows a spate of recent mass shootings—including attacks in Dayton, Ohio, and Midland‑Odessa, Texas—that have intensified national debates over gun control and immigration. Texas Governor Greg Abbott simply tweeted “good” in response and has resisted calls for an emergency session on gun‑law reforms, offering only voluntary background‑check measures, which Democrats and gun‑control advocates deem insufficient. END OF SUMMARY.
