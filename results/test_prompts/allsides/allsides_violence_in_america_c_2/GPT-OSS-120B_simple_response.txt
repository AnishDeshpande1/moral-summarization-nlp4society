SUMMARY:  
A Texas grand jury indicted 21‑year‑old Patrick Crusius for capital murder in the Aug. 3 El Paso Walmart massacre that killed 22, most of them Hispanic, and left the shooter on suicide watch. Prosecutors, seeking the death penalty, say Crusius drove ten hours to target “Mexicans” after posting a hate‑filled manifesto echoing President Trump’s anti‑immigrant rhetoric, prompting many to blame the president for inflaming racial hatred. Crusius’ lawyers vowed to fight any execution. Governor Greg Abbott’s curt “good” tweet and his later “mistakes were made” admission about a “DEFEND Texas” fundraiser highlight the political moral calculus surrounding the tragedy. While federal officials consider hate‑crime charges that could also bring the death penalty, Texas leaders have resisted sweeping gun‑control reforms, offering only modest voluntary‑background‑check recommendations that anger gun‑control advocates and Democrats. The El Paso shooting, part of a spate of recent mass murders, underscores a moral crisis linking hate‑motivated violence, political rhetoric, and the nation’s struggle over gun policy.  

END OF SUMMARY.
