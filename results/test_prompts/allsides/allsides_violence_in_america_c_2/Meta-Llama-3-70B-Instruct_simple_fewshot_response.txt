SUMMARY:

A 21-year-old man, Patrick Crusius, has been indicted for capital murder in connection with the mass shooting at a Walmart in El Paso, Texas, that left 22 people dead. The shooting was motivated by a desire to target Mexicans, and Crusius' actions were influenced by divisive rhetoric about immigration, echoing President Donald Trump's sentiments. The indictment comes after a series of mass shootings last month that left dozens dead and reignited the debate over gun control in the US. While Texas Governor Greg Abbott has resisted calls for stricter gun-control measures, Democrats and gun-control advocates have criticized his recommendations as insufficient. The case has sparked outrage and fueled anger among those calling for greater action on gun control and immigration reform. Crusius faces the death penalty if convicted.

END OF SUMMARY.
