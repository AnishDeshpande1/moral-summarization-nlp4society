SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump has come under fire for his comments suggesting that four minority female House members, known as "the Squad", should "go back" to their countries of origin. The comments were made in a series of tweets, which also accused the congresswomen of using "foul language" and being critical of Israel. However, three of the four targets were born in the United States, and Trump's comments have been widely criticized as racist and xenophobic.

House Speaker Nancy Pelosi called on lawmakers to pass a resolution condemning Trump's remarks, while several Republicans, including Reps. Will Hurd and Justin Amash, also spoke out against the president's attacks. British Prime Minister Theresa May also weighed in, saying that Trump's language was "completely unacceptable".

The controversy has united Democrats in condemnation of Trump's racial tactics, with several members of "the Squad" responding to his comments on social media. Trump has denied that his comments were racist and claimed that the congresswomen "hate" the United States and Israel.
