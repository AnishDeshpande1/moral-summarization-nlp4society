SUMMARY: President Donald Trump faced bipartisan condemnation after tweeting that four minority Democratic congresswomen—Alexandria Ocasio‑Cortez, Ayanna Pressley, Rashida Tlaib, and Ilhan Omar—should “go back” to the countries they supposedly came from. Trump denied the remarks were racist, claiming the lawmakers “hate” the United States and Israel and should leave if dissatisfied. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi called for a resolution condemning the president’s xenophobic comments, urging Republican support. The targeted members, three U.S.‑born and one Somali‑born refugee, denounced the tweets as racism. International leaders, including UK Prime Minister Theresa May, also criticized Trump’s language. A few Republicans, such as Rep. Will Hurd, Sen. Pat Toomey, and former Rep. Justin Amash, joined the rebuke, labeling the remarks racist and unbecoming. The incident united Democrats in condemnation and highlighted ongoing tensions over immigration, race, and the president’s rhetoric ahead of the 2020 election. END OF SUMMARY.
