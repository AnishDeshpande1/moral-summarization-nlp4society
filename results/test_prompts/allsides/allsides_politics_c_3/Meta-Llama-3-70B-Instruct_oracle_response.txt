SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump sparked outrage with racist tweets telling four minority Democratic congresswomen to "go back" to other countries, despite most of them being born in the United States. Trump denied his comments were racist, claiming that "if you hate our country, if you're not happy here, you can leave." House Speaker Nancy Pelosi called for a resolution condemning Trump's xenophobic remarks, saying "the House cannot allow the President's characterization of immigrants to our country to stand." The congresswomen, known as "the Squad," reacted strongly, with Rep. Ayanna Pressley tweeting "THIS is what racism looks like." International leaders, including British Prime Minister Theresa May, also condemned Trump's language as "completely unacceptable." Even some Republicans, such as Rep. Will Hurd, spoke out against Trump's attacks, calling them "racist, and xenophobic" and "unbecoming of the leader of the free world." Trump's comments were widely criticized for promoting hate, division, and xenophobia, and for degrading the offices of the congresswomen.

END OF SUMMARY.
