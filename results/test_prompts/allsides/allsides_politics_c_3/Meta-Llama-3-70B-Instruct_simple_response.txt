SUMMARY:

President Trump sparked outrage by telling four minority Democratic congresswomen to "go back" to their countries, despite most of them being born in the US. He denied his comments were racist, claiming they "hate" the US and Israel. House Democrats condemned his remarks as xenophobic and racist, with Speaker Nancy Pelosi calling for a resolution to condemn Trump's words. The targeted congresswomen, known as "the Squad," reacted strongly, with Rep. Ayanna Pressley tweeting "THIS is what racism looks like." International leaders, including British Prime Minister Theresa May, also criticized Trump's language. Some Republicans, such as Rep. Will Hurd and Sen. Pat Toomey, spoke out against Trump's attacks, calling them "racist" and "unbecoming of the leader of the free world." The incident highlights Trump's divisive rhetoric and the moral imperative for leaders to promote unity and respect for all citizens.

END OF SUMMARY.
