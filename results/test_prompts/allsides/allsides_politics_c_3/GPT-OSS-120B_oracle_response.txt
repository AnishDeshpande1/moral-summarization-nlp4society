SUMMARY:  
President Donald Trump sparked a fresh wave of condemnation after he told four minority Democratic congresswomen – Alexandria Ocasio‑Cortez, Ayanna Pressley, Rashida Tlaib and Ilhan Omar – to “go back” to the places they supposedly came from.  Trump denied that his remarks were racist, calling his language “white‑nationalist”‑style rhetoric that simply tells critics they can leave if they hate the country.  House Speaker Nancy Pelosi demanded a resolution to pass that condemns the president’s xenophobic tweets and his characterization of immigrants as “crime‑infested.”  The congresswomen labeled the president’s comments as “racist” and “disgusting,” saying they illustrate exactly what racism looks like.  Even some Republicans, including Rep. Will Hurd and Sen. Pat Toomey, called the tweets hateful, divisive and unbecoming of a leader.  International leaders, such as Britain’s Theresa May, denounced the remarks as unacceptable.  The episode has united Democrats in fighting the president’s tactics, accusing him of degrading and attacking the nation’s diversity while demanding accountability for corruption and hate.  
END OF SUMMARY.
