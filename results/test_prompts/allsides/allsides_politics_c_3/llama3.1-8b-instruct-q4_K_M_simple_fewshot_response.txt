SUMMARY:

President Trump's comments about four minority female House members, suggesting they should "go back" to their countries of origin, have been widely condemned as racist and xenophobic. Trump's tweets targeted Reps. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, Ayanna Pressley, Rashida Tlaib, and Ilhan Omar, all of whom are U.S. citizens. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi called on lawmakers to pass a resolution condemning Trump's remarks, saying they reflect his plan to "Make America White Again." The congresswomen themselves responded with force, with Ocasio-Cortez calling Trump's comments "what racism looks like" and Tlaib accusing him of degrading American communities. Even some Republicans have spoken out against Trump's attacks, with Rep. Will Hurd calling them "racist and xenophobic" and Sen. Pat Toomey saying they were "wrong." International leaders, including British Prime Minister Theresa May, have also criticized Trump's language.
