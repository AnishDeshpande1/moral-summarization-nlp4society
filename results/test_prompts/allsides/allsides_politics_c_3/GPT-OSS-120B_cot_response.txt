**SUMMARY:**  
President Donald Trump sparked a fresh wave of condemnation after tweeting that four minority Democratic congresswomen – Alexandria Ocasio‑Cortez, Ayanna Pressley, Rashida Tlaib and Ilhan Omar – should “go back” to the “crime‑infested places” they supposedly came from. Trump denied the remarks were racist, claiming they were merely criticism of “hate” toward the United States and Israel. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi called for a resolution condemning the president’s xenophobic language, asserting that his “Make America Great Again” slogan is really about making America white again. The targeted members, three U.S.‑born and one refugee, denounced the tweets as “what racism looks like.” International leaders, including Britain’s Theresa May, labeled the comments unacceptable. Even some Republicans, such as Rep. Will Hurd and Sen. Pat Toomey, called the statements racist and unbecoming of a world leader. The incident unified Democrats in condemning Trump’s xenophobic attacks.
