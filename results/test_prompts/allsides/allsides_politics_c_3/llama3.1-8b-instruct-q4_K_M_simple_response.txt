SUMMARY:

US President Donald Trump is facing criticism for his comments on four minority female House members, known as "the Squad". He told them to "go back" to their countries of origin and claimed they "hate" the US and Israel. However, three out of the four were born in the US, and one came as a refugee. Trump denied his comments were racist, but many have condemned them as xenophobic and discriminatory.

House Speaker Nancy Pelosi called for a resolution condemning Trump's remarks, saying they reflect his plan to "Make America Great Again" by making it white again. The Squad members, including Reps. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, Ayanna Pressley, Rashida Tlaib, and Ilhan Omar, have responded with strong words, calling out Trump's racism and criticizing his policies.

International leaders, including British Prime Minister Theresa May, have also criticized Trump's comments. Some Republicans, such as Reps. Will Hurd and Pat Toomey, have spoken out against Trump's attacks, saying they are racist and unbecoming of the leader of the free world.

END OF SUMMARY.
