SUMMARY:  
President Donald Trump ignited a fresh controversy by tweeting that four minority Democratic congresswomen—Alexandria Ocasio‑Cortez, Ayanna Pressley, Rashida Tlaib and Ilhan Omar—should “go back” to the countries they supposedly came from, despite three being U.S.‑born and Omar arriving as a refugee as a child. Trump denied racism, claiming the women “hate” America and Israel and suggesting they could leave if dissatisfied. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi promptly called for a resolution condemning the president’s xenophobic remarks, labeling them an attempt to make America “white again.” Democrats uniformly denounced the tweets as blatant racism, with Pressley declaring “THIS is what racism looks like” and Tlaib retorting that Trump will soon be out of the White House. International leaders, including Britain’s Theresa May, also condemned the language. A handful of Republicans—Rep. Will Hurd, Sen. Pat Toomey, and former GOP Rep. Justin Amash—joined the criticism, calling the remarks racist, unbecoming, and divisive. The episode unified Democrats in condemning Trump’s racial tactics and underscored the moral imperative to reject xenophobia in public discourse.  

END OF SUMMARY.
