Trump denied his comments were racist, saying they were simply suggesting that those who hate the US and Israel should leave. However, most of the targeted lawmakers were born in the US. The tweets drew international criticism, with British Prime Minister Theresa May calling the language used "completely unacceptable". Some Republicans spoke out against Trump's attacks, labeling them xenophobic and racist. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi said Trump's comments reflect his desire to make America white again, highlighting the importance of diversity and unity in the country. The episode has united Democrats in condemnation of Trump's tactics and exposed deep divisions within the Republican Party. As one member of Congress put it, Trump's remarks are "racist and disgusting". The incident highlights the need for leaders to promote unity and inclusivity, rather than division and hate.
