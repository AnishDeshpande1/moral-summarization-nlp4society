SUMMARY:
Kentucky county clerk Kim Davis appeared in federal court after repeatedly refusing to issue marriage licenses, citing “God’s authority,” even though the Supreme Court’s Obergefell decision guarantees same‑sex marriage. The article condemns Davis as a public official who willfully violated her oath, defied both the governor and a federal judge, and now faces contempt charges that are “undeniably” justified. It also exposes how several 2016 Republican presidential hopefuls—Mike Huckabee, Rand Paul and Bobby Jindal—have lauded Davis, portraying her as a heroic defender of “godly virtue” against “judicial tyranny.” The piece warns that celebrating her illegal stance fuels a broader conservative movement that seeks to restore state‑level discrimination and could encourage future candidates to champion outright illegality in the name of anti‑gay bias. While some Republicans like Carly Fiorina have urged her resignation, the article argues that protecting constitutional rights must trump religious objections, and that any lenient punishment could turn Davis into a martyr for anti‑LGBTQ + causes.  
END OF SUMMARY.
