SUMMARY: Kentucky County Clerk Kim Davis will appear in federal court for contempt after repeatedly refusing to issue marriage licenses, citing “God’s authority” despite the Supreme Court’s ruling in *Obergefell v. Hodges* that guarantees same‑sex marriage. The author condemns Davis as a government official who has broken her oath, defied the Constitution, and should be punished. The piece highlights how her defiance has been embraced by a fringe of the conservative movement and by certain Republican presidential hopefuls—most notably Mike Huckabee, who praised her as a hero against “judicial tyranny,” Rand Paul, who framed her stance as a commendable stand for conscience, and Bobby Jindal, who argued she should not have to choose between belief and job. While some Republicans like Carly Fiorina have urged her resignation, the author warns that harsh penalties could turn Davis into a martyr, encouraging further illegal, anti‑gay activism among future Republican leaders. END OF SUMMARY.
