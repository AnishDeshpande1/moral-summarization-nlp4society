SUMMARY: Kentucky County Clerk Kim Davis appeared in federal court for contempt after repeatedly refusing to issue marriage licenses since the Supreme Court’s 2015 Obergefell decision legalizing same‑sex marriage. Citing her religious beliefs, Davis defied orders from the governor, a federal judge, and the Supreme Court, which denied her appeal. Her stance has drawn praise from several Republican presidential hopefuls, including Mike Huckabee, Rand Paul, and Bobby Jindal, who frame her actions as resistance to “judicial tyranny.” Others, like Carly Fiorina, have urged her to resign. Critics argue Davis is violating her oath, the Constitution, and the law, and warn that harsh penalties could cast her as a martyr and encourage further anti‑gay legal defiance. END OF SUMMARY.
