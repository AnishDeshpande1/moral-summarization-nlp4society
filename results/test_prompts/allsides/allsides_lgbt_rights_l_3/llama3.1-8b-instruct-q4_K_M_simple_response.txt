SUMMARY:

Kentucky county clerk Kim Davis is facing contempt charges for refusing to issue marriage licenses to gay couples, citing her religious beliefs. Despite being ordered by the governor and a federal judge to do her job, she has continued to defy the law. Her actions are seen as an abuse of authority and a defiance of the Constitution. Several Republican presidential candidates, including Mike Huckabee, Rand Paul, and Bobby Jindal, have publicly supported Davis' stance, with some even calling her a hero for defying the Supreme Court.

This development is concerning because it may embolden others to defy the law in the name of anti-gay discrimination. The situation highlights a disturbing trend among some Republican candidates who are willing to champion brazen illegality in pursuit of their ideological goals. In contrast, other Republicans like Carly Fiorina have urged Davis to either resign or give up her job.

END OF SUMMARY.
