SUMMARY:

Reddit has removed over 2,000 communities that have a history of hate speech from its platform in an effort to curb online harassment. The move comes as several brands have pledged to ban ads on Facebook due to the spread of hate speech. Reddit's purge includes subreddits such as "The_Donald," a pro-Trump forum, and "Gender Critical," a TERF (Trans-Exclusionary Radical Feminist) forum, which has promoted anti-trans rhetoric. The company stated that these communities consistently broke its rules and failed to meet expectations for moderation.

The removal of these communities is part of Reddit's efforts to create more rules around hate speech and promote a safer online environment. While the company did not name every community being removed, it did provide a list of at least 10 subreddits that were shut down. Reddit administrators have the power to step in when users break site rules, and this move is seen as an effort to hold communities accountable for their actions.

END OF SUMMARY.
