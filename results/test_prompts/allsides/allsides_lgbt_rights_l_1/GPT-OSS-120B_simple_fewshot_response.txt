SUMMARY:
Reddit announced it will remove roughly 2,000 subreddits that repeatedly violated its content policy, citing a duty to curb hate speech amid broader protests for gender, racial and LGBTQ+ equality. Among the targeted communities are “The_Donald,” a pro‑Trump forum known for extremist political content and alleged white‑genocide rhetoric; “Gender Critical,” a feminist‑terf subreddit that denies trans women’s identities; and “CapoTrapHouse,” a radical‑left forum. Reddit says these groups consistently hosted rule‑breaking material, antagonized other users and ignored moderator‑level warnings, quarantines and other corrective steps. The platform, which hosts over 100,000 active communities and sees 430 million monthly visitors, argues that allowing such hate‑filled spaces harms broader online discourse and that stronger enforcement is a moral responsibility. While the company did not list every subreddit, it provided a sample of the bans and emphasized that this purge is only the beginning of a larger effort to tighten rules on hate speech. The move reflects growing pressure from civil‑rights groups and advertisers demanding safer, more inclusive online environments.  
END OF SUMMARY.
