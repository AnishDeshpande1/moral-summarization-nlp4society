SUMMARY:  
Reddit, confronting a surge of hate speech amid broader protests for gender, racial and LGBTQ + equality, has taken a moral stand by banning roughly 2,000 sub‑communities that repeatedly violated its content policies. The platform—home to 430 million monthly users—targeted notorious hubs such as the pro‑Trump “The_Donald,” the TERF‑aligned “Gender Critical,” and the radical left “ChapoTrapHouse,” citing persistent rule‑breaking, harassment and the promotion of extremist ideologies. Reddit’s officials stressed that allowing these spaces to flourish would weaponize the site against vulnerable groups, undermining the company’s responsibility to protect its users. By removing these toxic forums, Reddit signals a commitment to curb hate, support civil‑rights movements, and set a precedent for stricter enforcement of hate‑speech rules. The purge is presented as the first step in a broader ethical mission to make the platform safer and more inclusive.  

END OF SUMMARY.
