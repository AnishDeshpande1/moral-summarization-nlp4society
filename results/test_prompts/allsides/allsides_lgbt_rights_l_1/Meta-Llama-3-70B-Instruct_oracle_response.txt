SUMMARY: Reddit has taken a significant step towards curbing hate speech on its platform by shutting down over 2,000 communities that have a history of promoting hate speech. These communities, including "The_Donald" and "Gender Critical", have consistently broken the site's rules by hosting and upvoting rule-breaking content, antagonizing other communities, and refusing to meet basic expectations. Reddit administrators have worked in good faith to help these communities preserve their spaces, but ultimately had to take stronger action against those who try to weaponize parts of the platform against others. The banned communities have promoted hate speech, including anti-trans rhetoric and support for ethnic cleansing. By taking responsibility to support its communities, Reddit is dealing squarely with the hate it can mitigate today through its policies and enforcement. This purge is just the start of the company's mission to craft more rules around hate speech. END OF SUMMARY.
