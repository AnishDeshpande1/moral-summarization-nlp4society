SUMMARY:

Reddit has taken a significant step to curb hate speech on its platform by shutting down over 2,000 communities that have a history of promoting hate speech. The banned subreddits include "The_Donald," a pro-Trump forum, and "Gender Critical," a TERF forum that consistently spews anti-trans rhetoric. Reddit's decision comes after several brands vowed to ban advertisements on Facebook in protest of hate speech. The company argued that these communities broke several rules, including hosting and upvoting rule-breaking content, antagonizing other communities, and refusing to meet basic expectations. Reddit reiterated its commitment to supporting its communities by taking stronger action against hate speech, with the purge of 2,000 communities being just the start of its mission to craft more rules around hate speech. This move is a crucial step towards creating a safer and more inclusive online environment.

END OF SUMMARY.
