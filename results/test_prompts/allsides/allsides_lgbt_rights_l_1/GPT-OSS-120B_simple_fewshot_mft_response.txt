SUMMARY:
Reddit, exercising its authority to enforce community standards, announced a sweeping purge of roughly 2,000 subreddits that repeatedly violated its content policy and propagated hateful, degrading speech. By targeting forums such as the pro‑Trump “The_Donald,” the TERF‑aligned “Gender Critical,” and the radical left “CapoTrapHouse,” the platform aims to curb subversive groups that weaponize Reddit against marginalized people and undermine the safety of gender‑, racial‑, and LGBTQ‑plus minorities. The company argues that these communities have consistently broken rules, harassed others, and refused cooperation, constituting unfair cheating of the system and a threat to the collective well‑being. Reddit frames the action as a moral duty of care, protecting vulnerable users from harm and preserving the sanctity of public discourse from degradation by hate. Administrators stress that this is only the beginning of a broader effort to tighten rules against hate speech, reinforcing fair, respectful engagement while asserting platform authority over subversive, harmful content.

END OF SUMMARY.
