SUMMARY:

Reddit has shut down over 2,000 communities that have a history of hate speech, including subreddits "The_Donald", "Gender Critical", and "CapoTrapHouse". The move comes amid protests for gender, racial, and LGBTQ+ equality, and follows several brands vowing to ban advertisements on Facebook in protest of hate speech. Reddit administrators stepped in after the communities broke several rules, including hosting and upvoting rule-breaking content, antagonizing other communities, and refusing to meet basic expectations. The banned communities have promoted hate speech, including anti-trans rhetoric and support for ethnic cleansing. Reddit reiterated that the purge is just the start of its mission to craft more rules around hate speech, and will work to support its communities by taking stronger action against those who try to weaponize parts of the platform.

END OF SUMMARY.
