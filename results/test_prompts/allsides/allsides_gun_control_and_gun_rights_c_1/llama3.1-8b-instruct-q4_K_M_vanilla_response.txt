SUMMARY:

A shooting incident occurred in Springfield, Missouri on Sunday night, March 15, 2020, resulting in the deaths of five people, including a police officer and the gunman. The shooter, identified as 31-year-old Joaquin S. Roman, was driving randomly through the city and firing multiple times from his vehicle before crashing into a convenience store. He then walked inside and opened fire on customers and employees, killing three people. Two police officers were injured, with one being shot in the line of duty while trying to rescue a victim. The motive behind the shooting remains unclear. Officer Christopher Walsh was hailed as a hero for rushing to protect his community despite knowing the risks. Police identified the other victims as store employee Troy Rapp and customers Shannon Perkins and Matthew Hicks-Morris.

END OF SUMMARY.
