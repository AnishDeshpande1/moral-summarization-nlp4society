SUMMARY: A random, violent rampage in Springfield, Missouri on March 15 2020 left five dead—including police Officer Christopher Walsh, a veteran who rushed into a gunfire‑filled convenience store to protect civilians—and the 31‑year‑old gunman, who later took his own life. The shooter fired from his vehicle as he drove through the city, crashed into a Kum & Go, entered the store and opened fire, killing two employees and a customer. Officers responding to earlier shooting reports were ambushed; the first two officers arrived were shot, and Officer Josiah Overton was wounded. Chief Paul Williams praised Walsh’s selfless bravery, calling him a hero whose sacrifice exemplifies the highest duty to community. The tragedy also claimed Troy Rapp, Shannon Perkins and Matthew Hicks‑Morris, leaving families in profound grief. Witnesses described the surreal, unprovoked attack, and investigators continue to piece together motives. The community mourns the loss of its protectors and victims, while expressing gratitude for the courage of first responders. END OF SUMMARY.
