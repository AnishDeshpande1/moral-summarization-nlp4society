SUMMARY: A random shooting spree occurred in Springfield, Missouri, on the night of March 15, 2020. A 31‑year‑old male driver, identified as Joaquin S. Roman, fired from his vehicle as he drove north through the city, then crashed into a Kum & Go gas station and convenience store. He entered the store, opened fire on customers and employees, and later died by self‑inflicted gunshot. The attack left five dead: police Officer Christopher Walsh (32), store employee Troy D. Rapp (57), waste‑company employee Shannon R. Perkins (46), customer Matthew J. Hicks‑Morris (22), and the gunman. Two officers were injured, including Officer Josiah Overton, whose injuries are not life‑threatening. Witnesses reported hearing gunfire from the suspect’s car and later from inside the store. Police are still investigating motives and multiple crime scenes. The community expressed shock and gratitude for the officers’ bravery. END OF SUMMARY.
