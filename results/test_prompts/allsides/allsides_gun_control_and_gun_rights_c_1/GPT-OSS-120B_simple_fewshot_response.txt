SUMMARY:
A random, mobile shooter drove through Springfield, Missouri, firing from his vehicle before crashing into a Kum & Go convenience store, where he entered and opened fire. The rampage left five dead—including police Officer Christopher Walsh, a veteran hailed as a heroic protector, three civilians (store employee Troy Rapp, waste‑company worker Shannon Perkins, and customer Matthew Hicks‑Morris)—and the gunman, who died by self‑inflicted wound. Two other officers, including Officer Josiah Overton, were wounded but survived. Police Chief Paul Williams described the incident as a “roving active shooter” and expressed profound sorrow for the loss of an officer who “rushed in without regard to his own safety.” The shooter’s motive remains unknown, and investigators are still processing multiple crime scenes while the community grapples with grief over the senseless violence and the sacrifice of those who gave their lives to protect others. END OF SUMMARY.
