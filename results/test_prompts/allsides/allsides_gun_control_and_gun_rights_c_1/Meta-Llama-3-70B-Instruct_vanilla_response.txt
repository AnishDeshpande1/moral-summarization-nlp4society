SUMMARY:

A gunman fired randomly from his vehicle in Springfield, Missouri, on Sunday, March 15, 2020, before crashing into a convenience store and killing five people, including a police officer. The gunman, identified as 31-year-old Joaquin S. Roman, also died from a self-inflicted gunshot wound. The victims included Officer Christopher Walsh, 32, who was shot and killed while trying to rescue a victim, as well as three citizens: Troy D. Rapp, 57, Shannon R. Perkins, 46, and Matthew J. Hicks-Morris, 22. Another officer, Josiah Overton, was injured but is expected to recover. The gunman's motives remain unclear. The shooting spree began with reports of multiple shooting calls throughout the city, with the gunman firing randomly from his vehicle before crashing into the store. The police chief praised the bravery of the officers who responded to the scene.

END OF SUMMARY.
