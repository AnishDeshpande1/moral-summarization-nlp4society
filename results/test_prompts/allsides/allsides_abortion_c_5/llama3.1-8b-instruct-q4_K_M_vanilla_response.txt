SUMMARY:
The US Supreme Court has agreed to decide whether California's law requiring crisis pregnancy centers to post signs about state-funded abortions and contraceptives violates their free speech rights. The law, passed in 2015, requires licensed healthcare facilities to post a notice stating that the state provides comprehensive family planning services, while non-licensed medical facilities must indicate they have no licensed medical provider on staff. The crisis pregnancy centers argue that this requirement forces them to advertise for abortions, violating their free speech rights under the First Amendment. California Attorney General Xavier Becerra defends the law as necessary to ensure women are informed about different healthcare options. The Supreme Court's decision could impact abortion regulations in Democratic-leaning states and potentially threaten laws passed in Republican-leaning states.

END OF SUMMARY.
