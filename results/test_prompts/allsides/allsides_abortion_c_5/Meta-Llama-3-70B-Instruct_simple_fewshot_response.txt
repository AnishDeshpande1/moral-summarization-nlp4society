SUMMARY:

The US Supreme Court has agreed to hear a case involving a California law that requires private facilities, including Christian-based "crisis pregnancy centers," to post signs informing clients about state-funded abortions and contraceptives. The facilities argue that the law violates their free speech rights by forcing them to promote a message they disagree with. California defends the law, saying it ensures women are informed about the nature of different facilities and have access to information about state programs providing abortions and birth control. The court's decision could have implications for laws regulating anti-abortion pregnancy centers and abortion clinics in both Democratic- and Republican-leaning states. The case is one of several the court has taken involving free speech arguments, including a challenge to a Minnesota law prohibiting political messages in polling stations and a case involving a Christian baker's refusal to make a wedding cake for a gay couple.
