**SUMMARY:**  
The U.S. Supreme Court agreed to hear a challenge to California’s Reproductive FACT Act, which forces crisis pregnancy centers—often Christian‑aligned, anti‑abortion facilities—to post state‑provided information about abortions and contraceptives. Plaintiffs, represented by the conservative Alliance Defending Freedom, argue the law compels “coerced” speech and violates their First‑Amendment freedom, claiming the government should not “punish” pro‑life centers for declining to advertise abortions. California defends the statute as a responsible effort to ensure women have “power” through accurate, “informed” and “personal” health information, asserting the state must protect both freedom of speech and freedom from misleading messages. The case could reshape how Democratic‑led states regulate anti‑abortion centers and affect similar laws in Republican states, while the Court also plans to consider other free‑speech disputes involving conservative groups.
