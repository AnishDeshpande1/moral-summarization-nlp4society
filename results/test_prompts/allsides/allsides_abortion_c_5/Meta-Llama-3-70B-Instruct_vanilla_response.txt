SUMMARY:

The US Supreme Court has agreed to hear a case involving a California law that requires private facilities that counsel pregnant women against abortion to post signs providing information on how to access state-funded abortions and contraceptives. The law, known as the Reproductive FACT Act, was passed in 2015 and upheld by a lower court. The challengers, including Christian-based non-profit facilities, argue that the law violates their free speech rights by forcing them to post information that contradicts their beliefs. California argues that the law is necessary to ensure women are fully informed about the nature of different facilities and to regulate the healthcare industry. The Supreme Court's decision could have implications for laws regulating abortion clinics in both Democratic and Republican-leaning states.

END OF SUMMARY.
