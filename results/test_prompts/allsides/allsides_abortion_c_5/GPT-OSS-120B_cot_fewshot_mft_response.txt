**SUMMARY:**  
The U.S. Supreme Court agreed to hear a challenge to California’s Reproductive FACT Act, a law that forces privately run, Christian‑based “crisis pregnancy centers” to post state‑funded abortion and contraceptive information. The centers argue the requirement **violates** their **free speech** rights and forces **coerced** messaging, while Alliance Defending Freedom says the **state** should **protect** both **free** expression and people from compelled speech. California defends the law as a matter of **fairness** and **justice**, insisting that **information** is **power** that enables women to make informed **care** decisions about their health, thereby preventing **harm**. The case pits **authority** of a Democratic‑led legislature against claims of **subversion** by anti‑abortion groups, raising broader questions about the balance between **rights**, **care**, and **fairness** in reproductive health regulation.
