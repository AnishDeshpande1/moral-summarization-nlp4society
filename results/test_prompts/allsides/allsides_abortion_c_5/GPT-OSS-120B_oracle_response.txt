SUMMARY:
The U.S. Supreme Court agreed to hear a challenge by Christian‑based “crisis pregnancy centers” to California’s 2015 Reproductive FACT Act, which forces private facilities that counsel against abortion to post state‑funded abortion and contraceptive information. The centers claim the posting requirement **punishes** their free‑speech rights, while the state argues the law is needed to protect **healthcare** **care** consumers and ensure women are fully informed. The law, **backed** by a Democratic legislature and Governor Jerry Brown, mandates notices at licensed facilities and additional disclosures at non‑licensed ones. The 9th Circuit had previously **upheld** the statute, and the Supreme Court has a history of **striking** down restrictive abortion regulations, though it also hears cases involving **refusal** to provide services based on religious beliefs. The outcome could affect both Democratic‑led attempts to regulate anti‑abortion centers and Republican‑driven requirements on abortion clinics.

END OF SUMMARY.
