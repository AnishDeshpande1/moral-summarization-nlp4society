SUMMARY: The U.S. Supreme Court has agreed to review a challenge to California’s 2015 Reproductive FACT Act, which requires crisis pregnancy centers—private facilities that counsel against abortion—to post notices about state-funded abortions and contraceptives. The centers, represented by the Alliance Defending Freedom, claim the mandate violates their First Amendment free‑speech rights. California, defended by Attorney General Xavier Becerra, argues the law ensures women receive complete information about reproductive health options. The 9th Circuit upheld the law last year; the Supreme Court previously declined a similar challenge in New York City. The case could affect how Democratic states regulate anti‑abortion centers and may influence similar statutes in Republican‑leaning states. The Court also agreed to hear other free‑speech cases, including a Minnesota voting‑apparel ban and a baker’s refusal to make a gay wedding cake, highlighting its current focus on speech‑related disputes.  

END OF SUMMARY.
