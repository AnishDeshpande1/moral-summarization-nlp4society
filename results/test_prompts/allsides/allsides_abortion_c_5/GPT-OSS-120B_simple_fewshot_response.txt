SUMMARY:
The U.S. Supreme Court agreed to hear a challenge by Christian‑based “crisis pregnancy centers” to California’s 2015 Reproductive FACT Act, which forces facilities that counsel women against abortion to post state‑provided information about publicly funded abortions and contraception. The centers, represented by the conservative Alliance Defending Freedom, argue the law compels them to speak against their beliefs and violates First‑Amendment free‑speech rights. California, defended by Attorney General Xavier Becerra, says the law simply ensures women have accurate information to make personal health decisions and fulfills the state’s duty to regulate health services. The case pits anti‑abortion groups’ claim of protection from coerced speech against the state’s interest in informing women about all reproductive options. A ruling against the law could limit Democratic states’ ability to regulate anti‑abortion centers while also affecting similar requirements on abortion clinics in Republican states. The Court’s decision will reflect its ongoing willingness to entertain free‑speech challenges brought by conservative groups.

END OF SUMMARY.
