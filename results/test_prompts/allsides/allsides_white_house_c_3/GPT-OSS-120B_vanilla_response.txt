SUMMARY: The United States and Russia have reached a deal to secure and destroy Syria’s chemical weapons, ending weeks of U.S. threats of military strikes. The agreement, negotiated by Secretary of State John Kerry and Russian counterpart Sergey Lavrov, requires Syria to disclose its arsenal within a week, begin international inspections by November, and eliminate its stockpile by 2014. Any violations will be referred to the UN Security Council, where Russia holds a veto. President Obama emphasized verification and cautioned against simply trusting Russia and Assad, while acknowledging the diplomatic win despite domestic criticism. Critics, including Senate Foreign Relations Committee’s top Republican, question Syria’s commitment, but experts view the pact as a substantive success for Obama, albeit with poor optics, and a diplomatic victory for Russia. The deal removes the immediate need for U.S. military action while aiming to prevent future chemical attacks by the Assad regime.  
END OF SUMMARY.
