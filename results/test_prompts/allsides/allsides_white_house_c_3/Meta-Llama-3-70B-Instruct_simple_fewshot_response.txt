SUMMARY:

The US and Russia have reached an agreement to secure Syria's chemical weapons stockpile, effectively taking the threat of US military action off the table. The deal, announced on Saturday, was led by the Russians and will be scrutinized by a distrustful Congress. The Obama administration is approaching the deal with caution, emphasizing the need for verification and concrete actions to demonstrate Assad's commitment to giving up his chemical weapons. The agreement requires Syria's chemical weapons stockpile to be removed or destroyed by 2014, with international inspections beginning by November and full disclosure of its arsenal within a week. While the deal meets Obama's goal of taking away Assad's ability to conduct another chemical attack, it requires a leap of faith in trusting the Syrians and Russians to keep their part of the deal. Despite its imperfections, the agreement offers the best way out of Obama's unpopular call for military action. END OF SUMMARY.
