SUMMARY:

The U.S. and Russia have reached an agreement on securing Syria's chemical weapons stockpile, effectively removing the threat of military action against Syria. This push for a diplomatic solution was led by the Russians, with President Obama announcing that the international community expects the Assad regime to live up to its public commitments. The deal requires Syria to secure and destroy its chemical weapons stockpile by 2014, with international inspections beginning in November. However, this agreement is not without its challenges, as the U.S. must take a leap of faith in trusting the Syrians and Russians to keep their part of the deal.

The administration will need to see concrete actions from Assad to demonstrate his commitment to giving up his chemical weapons. If successful, this deal would meet Obama's goal of taking away the Assad regime's ability to conduct another chemical attack, which killed 1,400 civilians in August. However, the agreement has been criticized for being imperfect and allowing Syria to potentially continue its attacks.

END OF SUMMARY.
