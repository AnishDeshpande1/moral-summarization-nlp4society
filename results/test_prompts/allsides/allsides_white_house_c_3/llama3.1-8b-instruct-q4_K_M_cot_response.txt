**SUMMARY:**
The U.S. and Russia have reached an agreement to secure Syria's chemical weapons stockpile, effectively taking the threat of military action off the table. The Obama administration is cautious in trusting the Russians and Syrians to keep their part of the deal, calling for "concrete actions" from Assad to demonstrate his commitment to giving up his chemical weapons. The deal requires international inspections by November and the removal or destruction of Syria's chemical arsenal by 2014. While some critics argue that Obama's administration is taking a leap of faith in trusting the Syrians and Russians, they acknowledge that it offers the best way out of an unpopular call for military action. However, others point to the "dubious" claims made by Putin about the rebels being responsible for chemical attacks and question whether Assad will live up to his commitments.
