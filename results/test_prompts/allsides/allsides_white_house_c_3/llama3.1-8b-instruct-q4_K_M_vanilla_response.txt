SUMMARY:

The US and Russia have reached an agreement on securing Syria's chemical weapons stockpile, effectively removing the threat of US military action against Syria. The deal was brokered by Secretary of State John Kerry and Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov over three days in Geneva. Under the terms of the agreement, Syria must remove or destroy its chemical weapons stockpile by 2014, with international inspections beginning in November. The Syrians will also provide full disclosure of their arsenal within a week.

The Obama administration is cautious about trusting Russia and Syria to keep their part of the deal, but sees it as the best way out of an unpopular call for military action. President Obama has said that the US expects Assad's regime to live up to its commitments, and that any potential violations will be taken to the UN Security Council.

The deal is seen as a win for Russia and Syria, with some experts calling it a "diplomatic solution" to the crisis. However, the White House may face criticism from lawmakers who are skeptical of Assad's commitment to the agreement.

END OF SUMMARY.
