SUMMARY:
The article frames the U.S.–Russia deal to secure Syria’s chemical weapons as a morally responsible, albeit uneasy, compromise that averts further bloodshed. After President Obama’s failed push for limited strikes and intense saber‑rattling, Russian diplomacy stepped in, allowing the United States to avoid a war while still demanding accountability from Assad’s regime. Obama and Secretary of State John Kerry stress a “verify‑and‑verify” approach, underscoring the moral imperative to ensure Syria truly relinquishes its chemical arsenal by 2014 and to monitor compliance through UN inspections. The piece acknowledges the distrust between Washington, Moscow, and Damascus, noting that the agreement hinges on a “leap of faith” in both Russian and Syrian good faith. Critics warn that the deal’s imperfect safeguards leave open the question of Assad’s sincerity, yet experts argue that, morally, preventing another chemical attack and sparing civilian lives constitutes a substantive win for Obama, even if the political optics are mixed. The narrative presents the diplomatic resolution as the ethically preferable path over further military aggression.
