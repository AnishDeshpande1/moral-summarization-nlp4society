SUMMARY:

The US and Russia have reached an agreement to secure Syria's chemical weapons stockpile, effectively taking military action off the table. The Obama administration is cautiously optimistic, emphasizing the need for verification and concrete actions to demonstrate Assad's commitment to giving up his chemical weapons. The deal requires Syria to remove or destroy its chemical weapons by 2014, with international inspections beginning in November. The administration is wary of trusting the Russians and Assad, given their history of deception and Putin's recent op-ed criticizing American exceptionalism. Critics argue that the deal allows Assad to escape accountability for the brutal chemical attack that killed 1,400 Syrian civilians. Despite the imperfections, the agreement offers the best way out of Obama's unpopular call for military action, but the administration's cautious tone reflects concerns about the deal's implementation and the need to hold Assad and Russia accountable.

END OF SUMMARY.
