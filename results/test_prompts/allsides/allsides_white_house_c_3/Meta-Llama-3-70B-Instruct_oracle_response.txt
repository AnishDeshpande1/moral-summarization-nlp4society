SUMMARY:

The US and Russia have reached an agreement to secure Syria's chemical weapons stockpile, effectively taking the threat of US military action off the table. This diplomatic push, led by the Russians, saves the day after more than two weeks of saber-rattling. However, the Obama administration is proceeding with caution, as Congress is distrustful of Russian President Vladimir Putin and Syrian President Bashar Assad. The agreement requires Syria to remove or destroy its chemical weapons by 2014, with international inspections beginning in November and full disclosure of its arsenal within a week. Any violations will be taken to the UN Security Council for response. While the deal is far from perfect, it meets Obama's goal of taking away the Assad regime's ability to conduct another chemical attack. However, it requires a leap of faith in trusting the Syrians and Russians to keep their part of the deal. Despite the optics being bad, the deal is a win for Obama, as it prevents another war in the Middle East.

END OF SUMMARY.
