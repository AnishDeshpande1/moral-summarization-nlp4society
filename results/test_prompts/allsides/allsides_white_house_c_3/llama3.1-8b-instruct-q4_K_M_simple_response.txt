SUMMARY:

The US and Russia have reached an agreement on securing Syria's chemical weapons stockpile, effectively removing the threat of US military action. The deal was brokered by Secretary of State John Kerry and his Russian counterpart Sergey Lavrov after a last-minute diplomatic push. While the Obama administration is cautious in trusting the Syrians and Russians to keep their commitments, they are willing to take a leap of faith to avoid military action.

The agreement requires Syria's chemical weapons stockpile to be removed or destroyed by 2014, with international inspections starting by November. Any potential violations will be taken to the UN Security Council for response. The deal is seen as a diplomatic solution to the crisis, but critics argue that it allows Syria and Russia to avoid accountability.

President Obama has expressed skepticism about trusting the Russians and Syrians, but sees the agreement as a necessary step to prevent further chemical attacks. While the optics of the deal may be poor, experts consider it a win on substance, as it avoids another war in the Middle East.

END OF SUMMARY.
