SUMMARY:
At least 54,000 U.S. nursing‑home residents and workers have died of COVID‑19, accounting for over 40 % of the nation’s coronavirus deaths despite representing less than 1 % of the population. The crisis has exposed long‑standing flaws in the nursing‑home system—limited resources, poor government support, underpaid staff, weak oversight, and financial incentives that produce substandard care. Experts argue that the country must **change** how it **supports** its aging population. One proposal calls for a massive **fund**‑backed shift to **at‑home care**, using new technology and community **support** to improve health **outcomes** and allow facilities to focus on intensive cases. Critics warn that a rapid **change** could **leave** behind low‑income and minority seniors unless **action** includes more funding, higher staff pay, equitable health policies, and stronger **oversight** to **fix** systemic inequities. The pandemic, while tragic, may finally force the nation to **ignore** past complacency and pursue a more compassionate, sustainable model of elder **care**.  

END OF SUMMARY.
