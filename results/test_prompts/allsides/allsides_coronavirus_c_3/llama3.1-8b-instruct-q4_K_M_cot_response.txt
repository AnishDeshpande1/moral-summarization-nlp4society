**SUMMARY:**
The COVID-19 pandemic has highlighted the devastating impact of the virus on nursing homes, with over 54,000 residents and workers dying in the United States. This crisis has exposed long-standing flaws in the US nursing home system, including limited resources, poor government support, underpaid staff, lack of oversight, and financial structures that incentivize substandard care. The situation is especially dire for people over 65, who represent more than three-quarters of America's coronavirus deaths. Critics argue that a radical change is needed to improve the way the country cares for the elderly, such as increasing funding for at-home care supported by government funds, new technology, and community members. However, others caution that this approach could leave behind poor people and people of color who lack stable housing and financial resources.
