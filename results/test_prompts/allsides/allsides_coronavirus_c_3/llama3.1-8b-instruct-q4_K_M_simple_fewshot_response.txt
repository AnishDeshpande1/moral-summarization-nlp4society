At least 54,000 residents and workers at nursing homes have died from COVID-19 in the United States, highlighting the devastating impact of the virus on the elderly population. People over 65 account for more than three-quarters of America's nearly 131,000 coronavirus deaths, with a fatality rate for nursing home residents who contract the virus more than three times the national average. Experts argue that the US nursing home system is flawed and needs radical change, citing long-standing problems such as limited resources, poor government support, underpaid staff, and lack of oversight. Some propose shifting elder care to at-home care with government funding, new technology, and community support, while others argue that this could exacerbate existing inequalities. As the country prepares for a major spike in the elderly population by 2050, it is essential to address these issues to ensure better health outcomes and more equitable care for seniors.
