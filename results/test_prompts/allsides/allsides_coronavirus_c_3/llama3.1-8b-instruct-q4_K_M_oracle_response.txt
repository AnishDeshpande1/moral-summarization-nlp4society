SUMMARY: The COVID-19 pandemic has highlighted the need for radical change in the US nursing home system, which is plagued by long-standing problems such as limited resources, poor government support, and underpaid staff. Critics argue that these issues have been exacerbated by the virus, leading to devastating outbreaks and a fatality rate more than three times the national average. To address this crisis, experts propose shifting the majority of elder care to at-home settings, supported by government funds, new technology, and community members. This approach would allow nursing homes to focus on supporting those with intensive health needs. However, others argue that such a restructuring could leave behind vulnerable populations, highlighting the need for more funding and equitable healthcare systems. Ultimately, action is needed to change the current system and prioritize care and oversight of eldercare facilities.

END OF SUMMARY.
