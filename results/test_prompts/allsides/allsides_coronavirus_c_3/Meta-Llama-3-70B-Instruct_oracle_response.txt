SUMMARY:

The COVID-19 pandemic has exposed the flaws in the US nursing home system, resulting in at least 54,000 deaths. The elderly are disproportionately affected, with those over 65 accounting for more than three-quarters of coronavirus deaths. Critics argue that the system's vulnerabilities stem from limited resources, poor government support, underpaid staff, and lack of oversight, leading to substandard care. To address this, some experts propose a radical change in the way the country cares for the elderly, shifting the focus to at-home care supported by government funds, new technology, and community members. This approach could lead to better health outcomes and be more cost-effective. However, others warn that a massive restructuring of elder care could leave behind poor people and people of color, who may not have access to stable housing and resources for at-home care. Instead, they advocate for increased funding for care and staff pay, a more equitable healthcare system, and greater government accountability to fix the existing system.

END OF SUMMARY.
