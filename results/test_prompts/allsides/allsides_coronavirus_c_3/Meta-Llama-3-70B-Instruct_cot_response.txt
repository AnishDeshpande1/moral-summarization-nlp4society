The COVID-19 pandemic has highlighted the devastating flaws in the US nursing home system, where at least 54,000 residents and workers have died. The vulnerable elderly population, combined with the ease of virus spread in communal spaces, has led to devastating outbreaks. Critics argue that the system's problems, including limited resources, poor government support, underpaid staff, and lack of oversight, have contributed to the high fatality rate. To address these issues, some experts propose a radical change in the way the country cares for the elderly, prioritizing at-home care supported by government funds, new technology, and community members.
