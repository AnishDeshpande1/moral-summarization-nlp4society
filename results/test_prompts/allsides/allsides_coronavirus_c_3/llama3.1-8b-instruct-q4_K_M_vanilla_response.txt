SUMMARY:
At least 54,000 residents and workers at nursing homes in the US have died from COVID-19, making up over 40% of the country's total deaths. The elderly population is disproportionately affected by the virus, with those over 65 representing more than three-quarters of America's coronavirus deaths. Experts argue that the pandemic has highlighted long-standing problems with the US nursing home system, including limited resources, poor government support, and underpaid staff.

Some experts propose shifting elder care to at-home care supported by government funds, new technology, and community members, which could be cheaper and lead to better health outcomes. However, others argue that this approach could leave behind poor people and people of color who lack stable housing and financial resources. The article presents various perspectives on how to address the issue, including increasing funding for care and staff pay, improving government accountability, and exploring new technologies and family support systems.

END OF SUMMARY.
