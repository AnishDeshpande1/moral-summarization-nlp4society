SUMMARY:

The COVID-19 pandemic has exposed the vulnerabilities of the US nursing home system, with at least 54,000 residents and workers having died from the virus. The elderly are disproportionately affected, with those over 65 accounting for more than three-quarters of the country's coronavirus deaths. Experts argue that the crisis highlights long-standing problems in the system, including limited resources, poor government support, underpaid staff, and financial structures that incentivize substandard care. To address these issues, some propose a shift towards at-home care for seniors, supported by government funds, new technology, and community members. Others argue that this approach could leave behind poor people and people of color, and instead advocate for increased funding for care and staff pay, a more equitable healthcare system, and greater government accountability. The moral imperative is clear: the US must fundamentally reform its elder care system to protect the lives and dignity of its most vulnerable citizens.

END OF SUMMARY.
