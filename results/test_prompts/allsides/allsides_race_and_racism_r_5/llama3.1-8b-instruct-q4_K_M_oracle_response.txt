SUMMARY: 
President Obama addressed the nation on Saturday, urging Americans to remain optimistic about race relations amidst recent violent attacks. He acknowledged the loss of life, including two black males fatally shot by police officers and five Dallas law-enforcement officials killed in a sniper attack. However, he emphasized that these actions do not define the entire nation. The President revived a task force formed after the Michael Brown shooting to discuss next steps in addressing racial tensions. Obama noted that violent crime has decreased over the past 15 years and expressed his commitment to stopping gun violence. He also renewed his call for stricter gun laws, stating that they can make it harder for people to carry out their anger with weapons. The President acknowledged that confronting non-state actors like the Islamic State group will be an ongoing challenge but emphasized that he has made progress in this area.

END OF SUMMARY.
