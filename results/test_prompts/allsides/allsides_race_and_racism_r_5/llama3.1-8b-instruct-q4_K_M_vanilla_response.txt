SUMMARY:

President Obama urged Americans to remain optimistic about race relations following the fatal shootings of two black males by police officers and five Dallas law-enforcement officials. He stated that "we cannot let the actions of a few define all of us" and that violent crime in the U.S. has decreased over the past 15 years. Obama also announced the revival of a task force formed after the Ferguson shooting to discuss next steps on improving police-community relations.

The president renewed his commitment to stopping gun violence, saying that making it harder for people to carry out their anger with weapons can help reduce racial tension. He declined to label the Dallas sniper attack as a hate crime, citing the complexity of mass shootings.

Obama also addressed issues such as Brexit, ISIS, and NATO, reiterating the U.S.'s commitment to stopping gun violence and partnering with countries to eliminate terrorist threats. He emphasized the importance of a sensible and orderly process for Britain's exit from the EU.

END OF SUMMARY.
