SUMMARY:

In the aftermath of two black males being fatally shot by police officers, followed by a gunman killing five Dallas law-enforcement officials, President Obama urged Americans to remain optimistic about race relations. He emphasized that the actions of a few individuals do not define an entire community or nation. The president also revived the task force formed after the Michael Brown shooting in Ferguson, Mo., and announced plans to bring members to the White House to discuss next steps. Obama acknowledged that violent crime has decreased over the past decade but expressed concern about the ease with which people can carry out their anger with weapons. He called on Americans to work together to address racial tension and gun violence, saying that it is essential for police officers in gun-filled communities to have a "margin of error" when deciding how to engage with individuals who may be armed.
