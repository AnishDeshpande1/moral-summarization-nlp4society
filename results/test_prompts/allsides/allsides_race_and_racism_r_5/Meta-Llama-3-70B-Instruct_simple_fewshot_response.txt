SUMMARY:

President Obama urged Americans to remain optimistic about race relations despite the recent fatal shootings of black males by police officers and the retaliatory killing of five Dallas law-enforcement officials. He insisted that race relations have not regressed to the point of 1960's rioting, citing a decrease in violent crime over the past 15 years. Obama also renewed his commitment to stopping gun violence, arguing that it's harder for people to carry out their anger with weapons. He sympathized with police officers in gun-filled communities, emphasizing the need to address the gun issue. The president tried to calm public anxiety about personal safety, saying violent crime is actually down in the U.S. He also reflected on his presidency, acknowledging the challenges of confronting non-state actors like the Islamic State group and the importance of partnering with countries to eliminate terrorist threats.

END OF SUMMARY.
