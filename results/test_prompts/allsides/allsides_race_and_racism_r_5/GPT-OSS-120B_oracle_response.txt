SUMMARY: President Obama urged Americans to stay optimistic about race relations after two black men were shot by police and a gunman later killed five Dallas officers. He warned that the actions of a few should not define all, and insisted that the nation has not slipped into the rioting of the 1960s. Obama said the recent loss of life, while hard and depressing, gives a foundation to build on, noting that violent crime has actually declined over the past decade. He revived the task force created after the Michael Brown shooting, bringing police, community and civil‑rights leaders together to discuss next steps and to stop further violence. The president condemned the Dallas sniper as a “demented individual,” stressing that such attackers do not speak for any group and that we must make it harder for angry people to obtain weapons. He highlighted the safety challenges police face in gun‑filled communities and called for addressing the gun issue rather than setting it aside. Obama also addressed broader topics—Brexit, ISIS, NATO—and reaffirmed U.S. commitment to partners while warning that globalization can increase inequality. END OF SUMMARY.
