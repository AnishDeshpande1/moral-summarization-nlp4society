SUMMARY:

In the aftermath of two black males being fatally shot by police officers and a gunman killing five Dallas law-enforcement officials, President Obama urged Americans to remain optimistic about race relations. He emphasized that the actions of a few individuals should not define the entire nation, and that race relations have not regressed to the point of 1960's rioting. The president also renewed his commitment to stopping gun violence, arguing that making it harder for people to carry out their anger with weapons is crucial. He sympathized with police officers who have to make difficult decisions in gun-filled communities, and stressed that addressing the gun issue is essential for their safety. Obama also tried to calm public anxiety about personal safety, highlighting that violent crime is actually down in the U.S. Throughout his speech, the president appealed to the moral foundations of authority, care, and fairness, emphasizing the need for collective action to build on the progress made so far.

END OF SUMMARY.
