SUMMARY:

President Obama addressed the recent fatal shootings of two black men by police officers and the subsequent killing of five Dallas law-enforcement officials by a gunman. He urged Americans to remain optimistic about race relations, saying that the actions of a few individuals should not define the entire nation. Obama argued that race relations in the US have not regressed to the point of 1960s rioting and that violent crime has decreased over the past 15 years. He also renewed his commitment to stopping gun violence and announced plans to revive a task force formed after the Ferguson shooting to discuss next steps. Obama called the Dallas sniper a "demented individual" and emphasized that police officers have a difficult time in communities where guns are prevalent. He also addressed the Brexit issue, saying that the UK and EU must ensure a sensible and orderly exit process.

END OF SUMMARY.
