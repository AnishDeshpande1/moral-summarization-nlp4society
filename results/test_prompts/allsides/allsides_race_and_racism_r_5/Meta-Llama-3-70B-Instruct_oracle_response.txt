SUMMARY:

President Obama urged Americans to remain optimistic about race relations despite the recent fatal shootings of two black males by police officers and the retaliatory killing of five Dallas law-enforcement officials. He emphasized that "we cannot let the actions of a few define all of us." The president acknowledged the "loss of life" and "violence" but insisted that race relations have not regressed to the 1960s. He revived a task force to address the issue, which will include police officers, community and civil rights activists. Obama also renewed his commitment to stopping gun violence, saying it's essential to make it "harder" for people to carry out their anger with weapons. He sympathized with police officers who have "very little margin of error" in gun-filled communities and cited the need to address the gun issue to ensure their safety. The president also tried to calm public anxiety about personal safety, saying violent crime is actually down in the U.S.

END OF SUMMARY.
