SUMMARY:

President Obama addressed the nation on Saturday, urging Americans to remain optimistic about race relations despite recent tragedies. He emphasized that "we cannot let the actions of a few define all of us" and pointed out that violent crime has decreased in the US over the past decade. The President also revived a task force formed after the Michael Brown shooting in Ferguson, Mo., which will include police officers, community activists, and others to discuss next steps.

Obama condemned the Dallas sniper attack as an act by "a demented individual" who did not speak for all Americans. He also spoke sympathetically of police officers working in gun-filled communities and called for a reduction in racial tension through increased access to mental health services and job training programs.

The President highlighted his commitment to stopping gun violence, saying it is essential to making progress on racial tensions. He acknowledged the difficulty of interpreting motives behind mass shootings but emphasized that these events do not define American society.

END OF SUMMARY.
