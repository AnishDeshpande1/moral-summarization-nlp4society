SUMMARY:

Former Defense Secretary James Mattis publicly condemned President Donald Trump's leadership, stating that Trump "tries to divide us" instead of uniting the American people. Mattis argued that Trump's deliberate effort to divide has led to a crisis, and it's time for Americans to come together without him. He drew a contrast between the American ethos of unity and Nazi ideology, emphasizing that "In Union there is Strength." Mattis criticized Trump's response to the police killing of George Floyd, including the use of troops to violate Constitutional rights and provide a "bizarre photo op" for the President. He also implicitly criticized current Defense Secretary Mark Esper and other senior officials for militarizing the response to protests, which erodes the moral ground between the military and civilian society. Mattis urged Americans to reject this approach and instead rely on civilian leaders to keep public order, emphasizing the importance of protecting the country and defending its promise. END OF SUMMARY.
