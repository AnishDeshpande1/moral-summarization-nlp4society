SUMMARY:
Retired General James Mattis condemns President Trump for abandoning the unifying purpose of American governance and for subverting legitimate authority. He argues that Trump’s divisive rhetoric and the militarized response to the George Floyd protests betray the nation’s core values of care for citizens and protection of constitutional rights, causing real harm to the social fabric. Mattis frames the president’s actions as unfair, equating the “divide and conquer” tactics of Nazi ideology with today’s politics and accusing Trump of cheating the public out of honest, collective leadership. By highlighting the duty of loyalty to the Constitution, past generations, and future children, Mattis calls for a return to true loyalty to the republic rather than to a partisan leader. He urges civilian authorities to reclaim public‑order responsibilities, warning that using the military as a “battlespace” erodes moral trust and betrays the bond between the armed forces and the society they serve. Mattis concludes that the period of silence is over and that unity, not political subversion, must guide America forward.  
END OF SUMMARY.
