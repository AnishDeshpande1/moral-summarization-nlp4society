SUMMARY:

Former Defense Secretary James Mattis has broken his silence to criticize President Donald Trump, stating that Trump is the first president in his lifetime who does not try to unite the American people. Mattis argues that Trump's actions have deliberately divided the country, and that the president's response to the George Floyd protests has threatened the American experiment. Mattis specifically criticizes Trump's decision to use troops to violate the Constitutional rights of citizens, and the "bizarre photo op" that followed. He also implicitly criticizes current Secretary of Defense Mark Esper and other senior officials for militarizing the response to protests. Mattis emphasizes the importance of unity and the need for civilian leaders to maintain public order, rather than relying on the military. He concludes that Americans can unite without Trump, drawing on the strengths of civil society.

END OF SUMMARY.
