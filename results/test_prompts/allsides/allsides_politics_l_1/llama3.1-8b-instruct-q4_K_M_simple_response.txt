SUMMARY:

Former US Secretary of Defense James Mattis has publicly condemned President Donald Trump's actions, stating that he is the first president in his lifetime who does not try to unite Americans. Mattis argues that Trump's deliberate effort to divide the country has led to a crisis, and that it is up to citizens to unite without him. He contrasts the American ethos of unity with Nazi ideology, emphasizing the importance of "In Union there is Strength." Mattis criticizes Trump for ordering troops to violate the constitutional rights of their fellow citizens during the George Floyd protests and for using military force against civilians in Washington D.C. He also implies criticism towards current Secretary of Defense Mark Esper and other senior officials.

Mattis argues that militarizing the response to civil unrest erodes the moral ground between the military and civilian society, and that keeping public order is a responsibility of civilian leaders. His statement marks a significant departure from his previous stance as a former Cabinet official, where he believed it was inappropriate to criticize a sitting president.
