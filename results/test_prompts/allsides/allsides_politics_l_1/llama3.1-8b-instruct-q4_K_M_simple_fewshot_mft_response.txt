SUMMARY:

In his latest statement, former Defense Secretary Jim Mattis condemned President Trump's actions as a threat to the American experiment and the Constitution. Mattis emphasized that Trump's decision to deploy troops against peaceful protesters in Washington D.C. was a betrayal of the oath sworn by military personnel to protect citizens' rights. He criticized the use of militarized force against civilians, stating that it erodes the trust between the military and society. Mattis also implicitly rebuked current Secretary of Defense Mark Esper for his role in this decision. The former defense secretary highlighted the importance of civilian leadership in maintaining public order, emphasizing that the military should only be used on rare occasions at the request of state governors.

Mattis's statement marked a turning point in his silence since resigning from the Trump administration. He drew parallels between the Nazi ideology of "Divide and Conquer" and Trump's actions, underscoring the need for unity and respect for civilian authority. The former defense secretary urged Americans to reject the militarization of domestic responses and to prioritize the principles of democracy.

END OF SUMMARY.
