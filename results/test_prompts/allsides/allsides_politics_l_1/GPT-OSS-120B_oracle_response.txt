SUMMARY:
Former Secretary of Defense James Mattis argues that Donald Trump “does not try to unite the American people—he does not even pretend to try,” and instead actively tries to divide the nation. Mattis says three years of this deliberate effort have left the country without mature leadership, and that the American experiment is now threatened by the president’s actions and response to the police killing of George Floyd and the ensuing protests. He calls for citizens to draw on the strengths of civil society to surmount the crisis, confident that “in union there is strength.” Mattis condemns the idea of treating cities as a battlespace to be dominated by the military, warning that militarizing our response erodes the moral ground that protects the trusted bond between those who have sworn to support and defend the Constitution and the society they protect. He urges that keeping public order rest with state and local leaders who understand their communities, and that the military be called only in rare, lawful circumstances. END OF SUMMARY.
