SUMMARY:
Retired General James Mattis condemns President Trump for abandoning the moral duty of national unity, likening his divisive tactics to the “divide and conquer” strategy of the Nazis and urging a return to the American principle that “in union there is strength.” Mattis argues that Trump’s response to the George Floyd protests—using the military for a political photo‑op and treating cities as battlefields—violates the Constitution and threatens the nation’s democratic experiment. He stresses that the armed forces must remain apolitical, intervening only at the request of state governors in rare, lawful circumstances, and that civil order is the responsibility of civilian leaders who are accountable to their communities. While acknowledging a period of necessary silence after his resignation, Mattis now declares that the president’s actions endanger the country’s core values and must be publicly rejected. He calls on citizens to unite, drawing on the strength of civil society, to protect the promise fought for by past generations and to safeguard the future of America’s children.

END OF SUMMARY.
