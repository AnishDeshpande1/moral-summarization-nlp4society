SUMMARY:

Former Secretary of Defense James Mattis has publicly criticized President Donald Trump, stating that he is the first president in his lifetime who does not try to unite the American people. Mattis argues that Trump's actions have led to division and a crisis in the country. He contrasts the American ethos of unity with Nazi ideology, emphasizing the importance of "In Union there is Strength." Mattis's criticism comes after he resigned from the administration over policy differences, but now feels that the situation has become dire enough to speak out.

Mattis specifically criticizes Trump's response to the police killing of George Floyd and the ensuing protests, saying that it was a "bizarre photo op" and a violation of citizens' constitutional rights. He also implicitly critiques current Secretary of Defense Mark Esper and other senior officials for militarizing the response to the protests. Mattis emphasizes the importance of maintaining a trusted bond between the military and civilian society.

END OF SUMMARY.
