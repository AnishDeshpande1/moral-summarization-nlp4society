SUMMARY: Retired General James Mattis publicly condemns President Donald Trump, accusing him of deliberately dividing the nation and endangering the American experiment. Mattis argues that Trump’s handling of the George Floyd protests, including the use of the military for a “photo‑op” and treating cities as “battlespaces,” violates constitutional rights and undermines civilian‑military trust. He contrasts U.S. unity with Nazi “divide and conquer” tactics, urging Americans to unite without Trump’s leadership. Mattis also criticizes Defense Secretary Mark Esper and other officials for supporting militarized domestic responses, asserting that public order should be managed by state and local authorities, not the armed forces. He states his earlier silence was temporary and now, after three years of what he calls “immature leadership,” he feels compelled to speak out. END OF SUMMARY.
