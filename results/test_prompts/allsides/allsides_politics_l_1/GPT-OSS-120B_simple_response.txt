SUMMARY:  
Retired General James Mattis condemns President Donald Trump as the first leader of his lifetime who actively seeks to divide America rather than unite it. Mattis argues that three years of Trump’s “deliberate effort” have left the nation without mature leadership, but he urges citizens to draw on civil‑society strengths to restore unity—an ethic he likens to the Allied resolve against Nazi “divide and conquer” tactics, recalling the “In Union there is Strength” rallying cry before Normandy. While Mattis previously restrained criticism to protect the military’s apolitical stance, he now declares his silence period over, asserting that Trump’s handling of the George Floyd protests—using the military for a “photo op” and treating cities as “battlespace”—threatens the constitutional experiment. He warns that militarizing domestic unrest erodes the moral bond between soldiers and the public, and that law‑and‑order should remain the purview of civilian state and local leaders. Mattis calls for a return to constitutional fidelity and civic unity, emphasizing duty to fellow citizens, past generations, and future children.  
END OF SUMMARY.
