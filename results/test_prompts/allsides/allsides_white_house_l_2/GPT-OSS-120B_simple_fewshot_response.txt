SUMMARY:
The article argues that the Trump administration’s reaction to the Michael Flynn leak repeats the moral failure of past presidents who tried to crush whistle‑blowers instead of confronting the underlying problem. By likening today’s “weaponized leaks” to the Watergate‑era “Plumbers” and noting that former presidents—Nixon and Obama—found that intimidation of leakers neither stopped the flow of information nor earned public trust, the piece frames the current crackdown as misguided and potentially harmful to democratic accountability. President Trump’s promises to “find” and criminalize leakers, his criticism of the FBI, and the leaked memo from Secretary Tillerson warning that leaks chill candid advice all illustrate a pattern of blaming insiders rather than addressing systemic leakage. The moral message is clear: attempts to silence leaks through legal threats and secret investigations are ineffective, erode morale, and threaten transparency, while a free press and honest whistle‑blowing remain essential to a healthy government.

END OF SUMMARY.
