SUMMARY:
The article argues that the Trump administration’s reaction to the Flynn‑related leaks reveals a dangerous mix of authoritarian overreach, unfair scapegoating, and misplaced loyalty. By branding the leaks “criminal” and threatening a new “Plumbers” operation, President Trump and Chief of Staff Reince Priebus treat whistle‑blowers as subversive enemies rather than addressing systemic problems, echoing Nixon’s abuse of authority. This punitive stance betrays the principle of fairness: leaks are not inherently criminal, and past attempts to punish leakers have rarely succeeded or been just. Moreover, the administration’s loyalty appears misdirected—protecting its own image and allies while vilifying those who expose misconduct, thereby undermining true loyalty to the nation’s democratic norms. The piece warns that such authoritarian, unfair, and disloyal tactics risk repeating historic scandals like Watergate, rather than restoring responsible governance. END OF SUMMARY.
