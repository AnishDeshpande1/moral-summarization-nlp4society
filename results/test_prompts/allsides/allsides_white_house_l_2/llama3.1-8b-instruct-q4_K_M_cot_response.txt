**SUMMARY:**
The resignation of National Security Advisor Michael Flynn has led to a renewed focus on the Washington rules of engagement, where leaks are a norm. President Trump's warning that "the spotlight has finally been put on the low-life leakers" and his promise to catch them highlights the administration's misapprehension of the struggle ahead. History shows that trying to close off leaks leads to blame-shifting, low morale, resignations, and eventual abandonment by Congress. The White House has chosen to treat the leakers as the problem, rather than acknowledging their role in keeping the government accountable. President Trump's invocation of the power of the Attorney General is unlikely to stop the leaks, which are a "criminal act" only if pursued relentlessly. The administration's reaction to a leak about Chief of Staff Reince Priebus's irregular contact with the FBI has been a chastisement of law enforcement, highlighting the president's alarm at classified information being given to the media.
