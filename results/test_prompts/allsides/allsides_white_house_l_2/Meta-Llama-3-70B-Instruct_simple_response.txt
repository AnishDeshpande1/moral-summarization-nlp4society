SUMMARY:

The Trump Administration's handling of leaks has taken a worrying turn, reminiscent of the Nixon Administration's disastrous approach. After National Security Advisor Michael Flynn's resignation, the White House has chosen to blame leakers instead of addressing the real issues. President Trump has threatened to catch leakers, but history shows that such efforts often lead to blame-shifting, low morale, and political abandonment. The Administration's approach has already led to the creation of a "New Plumbers" unit, tasked with stopping leaks, and the Attorney General has been invoked to investigate. However, the record shows that leaks are not always criminal, and pursuing leakers can be counterproductive. The White House's reaction to leaks has been heavy-handed, with the President chastising law enforcement and demanding that they "FIND NOW." This approach will likely lead to a culture of fear and intimidation, and may ultimately backfire.

END OF SUMMARY.
