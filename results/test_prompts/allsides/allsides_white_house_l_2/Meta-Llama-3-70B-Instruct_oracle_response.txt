SUMMARY:

The Trump Administration's handling of leaks has taken a familiar, yet ominous turn. Despite President Trump's claims that leakers will be "caught," history suggests otherwise. The White House's focus on stopping leaks will likely lead to "abandonment" by Congress, low morale, and eventually, political downfall. The administration's approach is reminiscent of the Nixon era, where attempts to plug leaks led to the infamous Watergate scandal. By treating leakers as the problem, the White House is misapprehending the struggle ahead. The president's warnings of criminal leaks and invocation of the Attorney General's power will likely lead to a new era of "Plumbers" - a group tasked with stopping leaks, but ultimately doomed to fail. The recent leak about Chief of Staff Reince Priebus's contact with the FBI has only accelerated this downward spiral. As the White House continues its "quest" to stop leaks, it will likely find itself mired in a cycle of blame-shifting and political damage. END OF SUMMARY.
