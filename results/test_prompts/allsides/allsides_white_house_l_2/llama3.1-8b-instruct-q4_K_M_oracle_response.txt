SUMMARY:

The Washington leaker phenomenon continues, with the recent Flynn scandal leading to a new administration shakeup. President Trump's reaction to the leaks has been one of outrage, tweeting that the leakers will be "caught" and vowing to look into the matter seriously. However, history shows that this approach often leads to blame-shifting, low morale, resignations, and eventually abandonment by Congress.

The White House's treatment of the leakers as a problem has led to concerns about the creation of a new era of surveillance and suppression of information. The president invoked the power of the Attorney General to stop the leaks, but this approach has proven futile in the past. Leaks have become an integral part of Washington's culture, with many considering them a necessary check on government power.

The administration's quest to silence leakers may ultimately lead to more harm than good, as it chills the willingness of officials to seek candid advice. The recent leak of Secretary of State Tillerson's memo warning against leaks is a prime example of this phenomenon. As the White House continues down this path, it risks creating an environment of fear and intimidation that could have devastating consequences.

END OF SUMMARY.
