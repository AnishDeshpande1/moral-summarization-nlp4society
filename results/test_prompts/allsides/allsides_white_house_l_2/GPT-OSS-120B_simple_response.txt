SUMMARY:  
The recent Flynn‑Kislyak leak sparked the first Trump‑era resignation and exposed the enduring moral hazard of Washington’s “weaponized leaks.” President Trump’s vow to hunt down leakers echoes Nixon’s futile crackdown that birthed the Plumbers and led to Watergate, suggesting a repeat of history’s lesson: suppressing leaks fuels blame‑shifting, low morale, and political ruin. The administration’s rhetoric—branding leaks as “criminal” and mobilizing the Justice Department and FBI—ignores the fact that truthful disclosures have never been prosecuted, while leakers are rarely caught. Past attempts by both Obama and Nixon to contain leaks only produced more scandal. The White House’s reaction to a new leak involving Chief of Staff Reince Priebus illustrates the same mis‑calculation, prompting Trump to publicly chastise the FBI and issue an all‑caps “FIND NOW” order, effectively reviving a modern “Plumbers” effort. Even Secretary of State Rex Tillerson’s memo warning against leaks was itself leaked, underscoring the futility of trying to silence the leaker’s power. The moral of the piece warns that the Trump administration’s leak‑busting crusade repeats a historic pattern that ultimately undermines governance.  
END OF SUMMARY.
