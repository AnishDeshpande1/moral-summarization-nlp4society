SUMMARY:

The White House, under President Trump's leadership, is escalating its efforts to combat leaks in the government. After National Security Advisor Michael Flynn resigned due to leaked information, the president vowed to catch the leakers and even called for the Justice Department to investigate. However, history suggests that this approach will only lead to blame-shifting, low morale, and eventual congressional abandonment.

The administration's response to a leak about Chief of Staff Reince Priebus contacting the FBI about Russia has sparked concerns that they are launching a "Leak Apocalypse" reminiscent of the Nixon era. The president accused the FBI of being unable to stop leaks and even questioned their effectiveness in finding leakers within their own ranks.

The situation has led some experts to warn that the administration is setting a dangerous precedent by targeting whistleblowers and journalists who report on government information. The leak of a memo warning against leaking itself has only added to the concerns about the White House's approach to transparency and accountability.

END OF SUMMARY.
