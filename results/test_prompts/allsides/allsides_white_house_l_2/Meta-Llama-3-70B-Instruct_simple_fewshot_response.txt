SUMMARY:

The article highlights the Trump Administration's misguided efforts to stop leaks in the White House, drawing parallels with the Nixon Administration's failed attempts to plug leaks during the Watergate scandal. Despite the president's vows to catch leakers, history suggests that such efforts will only lead to blame-shifting, low morale, and political abandonment by Congress. The article argues that leaks are a natural part of Washington politics and that the real concern should be the manipulation of information, not the leaks themselves. The president's reaction to the Flynn resignation and subsequent leaks has been to invoke the power of the Justice Department, threatening to pursue leakers criminally, which the article suggests is unlikely to be effective. The article concludes that the Trump Administration's approach will only lead to further leaks and a breakdown in trust between the government and the media.
