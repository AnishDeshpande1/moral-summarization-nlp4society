SUMMARY:

The tragic death of Kate Steinle, a 31-year-old woman, at the hands of an undocumented immigrant with a criminal record, has sparked a heated debate about immigration. However, it is essential to separate the actions of this individual from the broader issue of undocumented immigration. Research has shown that crime rates have decreased as the immigrant population has grown, and most undocumented immigrants come to the US to work and provide a better life for themselves and their families. The author argues that deporting as many undocumented immigrants as possible is not the solution, as it wastes taxpayer money and fails to target serious criminals. Instead, the focus should be on smarter and better immigration enforcement, prioritizing recent arrivals and serious criminals. The government's new deportation priorities and President Obama's proposed executive action on immigration could have made a difference in preventing such tragedies. It is crucial to avoid vilifying all undocumented immigrants by false association and to address the issue with a nuanced and compassionate approach.

END OF SUMMARY.
