SUMMARY: Kate Steinle was fatally shot on a San Francisco pier by Juan Francisco Lopez‑Sanchez, an undocumented Mexican immigrant with multiple felony convictions who had been deported five times. The article argues that while Lopez‑Sanchez is a dangerous individual, he should not be used to stereotype the 11 million undocumented immigrants in the U.S., most of whom are law‑abiding and contribute positively. It cites research showing no link between illegal immigration and higher crime rates and highlights that many mass shootings are committed by native‑born white men. The piece criticizes past immigration enforcement for wasting resources on low‑risk workers while missing repeat offenders, and praises recent DHS policy shifts to prioritize recent border crossers, serious criminals, and terrorism threats. It also notes that ICE failed to obtain a proper warrant before releasing Lopez‑Sanchez in March, and suggests that Obama’s proposed immigration executive actions might have helped allocate resources to capture such criminals. The article concludes that the tragedy should not lead to vilifying all undocumented immigrants. END OF SUMMARY.
