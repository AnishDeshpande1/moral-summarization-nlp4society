SUMMARY:

A Mexican immigrant, Juan Francisco Lopez-Sanchez, has been arrested for the fatal shooting of Kate Steinle, 31, on a San Francisco pier. Lopez-Sanchez had seven felony convictions and was in the country without authorization after being deported five times. However, the author argues that this incident should not be used to vilify all undocumented immigrants, who are often productive members of society. The author notes that crime rates have fallen as the immigrant population has grown, and that research shows no correlation between immigration and crime. The article also highlights the failure of the current immigration enforcement system, which has wasted resources on deporting non-criminals while allowing serious offenders like Lopez-Sanchez to remain at large. The author suggests that the government should focus on smarter and better immigration enforcement, prioritizing convicted criminals, recent border crossers, and terrorism threats.
