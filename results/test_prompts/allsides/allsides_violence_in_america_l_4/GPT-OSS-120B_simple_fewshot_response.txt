SUMMARY:
Kate Steinle was murdered on a San Francisco pier by Juan Francisco Lopez‑Sanchez, an undocumented Mexican man with a lengthy criminal record who had re‑entered the U.S. after multiple deportations. The article stresses that Lopez‑Sanchez is an individual offender, not a representative of the 11 million undocumented immigrants who, research shows, do not increase crime rates. It argues that illegal entry itself is a civil violation, not a crime, and that most undocumented people are law‑abiding workers. The piece condemns the tragedy while warning against sweeping vilification of all immigrants, pointing out that many undocumented individuals are heroes and that the real problem is the failure of immigration enforcement to prioritize dangerous repeat offenders. It calls for smarter, targeted deportations—focusing on recent border crossers, convicted felons, and terrorism threats—rather than broad sweeps, and notes recent DHS policy shifts toward such priorities. The moral takeaway is that while Lopez‑Sanchez should have been detained, the broader immigrant community should not be scapegoated for his act.  
END OF SUMMARY.
