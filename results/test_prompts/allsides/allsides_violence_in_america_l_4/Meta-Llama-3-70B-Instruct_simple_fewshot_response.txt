SUMMARY:

A fatal shooting in San Francisco has sparked debate over illegal immigration. Kate Steinle, 31, was killed by Juan Francisco Lopez-Sanchez, a Mexican immigrant who was in the country without documentation and had seven felony convictions. While Lopez-Sanchez's actions are inexcusable, the author argues that he does not represent the majority of undocumented immigrants, who are productive members of society. Research shows that crime rates have fallen as the immigrant population has grown, and being in the country without authorization is a civil infraction, not a crime. The author criticizes the focus on deporting undocumented immigrants, citing the high cost and inefficiency of current enforcement efforts. Instead, they advocate for smarter and more targeted enforcement, focusing on serious criminals and recent arrivals. The Department of Homeland Security's recent shift in deportation priorities is seen as a step in the right direction.

END OF SUMMARY.
