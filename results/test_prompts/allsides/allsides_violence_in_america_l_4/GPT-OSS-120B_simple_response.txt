SUMMARY: The tragic killing of Kate Steinle by Juan Francisco Lopez‑Sanchez, an undocumented immigrant with a long criminal record, should not be used to demonize all undocumented immigrants. The author argues that while Lopez‑Sanchez is a dangerous individual who illegally re‑entered the U.S. and should have been detained, the vast majority of undocumented people are law‑abiding, productive residents. Research shows immigration does not increase crime, and most undocumented migrants come seeking work for their families. The piece condemns wasteful enforcement that targets harmless workers while letting repeat offenders slip through, urging smarter, targeted deportations focused on recent border crossers, serious felons, and terrorism threats. It also notes that past policy failures—such as costly, ineffective deportations and the lack of an executive action that would have freed resources for criminals—contributed to the tragedy. The moral takeaway is that immigration policy must distinguish between individual criminals and the broader immigrant community, avoiding blanket vilification while improving enforcement priorities. END OF SUMMARY.
