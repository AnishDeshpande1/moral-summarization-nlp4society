SUMMARY:

A 31-year-old woman, Kate Steinle, was fatally shot on a San Francisco pier by an undocumented immigrant who had been deported five times and had seven felony convictions. While the incident has sparked debate about immigration policies, the author argues that it is not representative of the estimated 11 million undocumented immigrants in the US, who are mostly productive members of society. Research shows that crime rates have actually decreased as the size of the immigrant population grew from 1990 to 2010. The author suggests that deporting all undocumented immigrants is not a solution and that smarter immigration enforcement is needed, focusing on serious criminals rather than innocent people working in communities.

The Department of Homeland Security has announced plans to rethink its deportation priorities, focusing on convicted criminals, recent border crossers, and terrorism threats. This move is seen as a step in the right direction. The author concludes that Kate Steinle's death was a tragedy, but it should not be used to vilify all undocumented immigrants.
