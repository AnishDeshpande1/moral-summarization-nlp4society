SUMMARY:  
Kate Steinle was shot and killed on a San Francisco pier by Juan Francisco Lopez‑Sanchez, an undocumented immigrant who had been deported and re‑entering the United States illegally, a federal felony. Lopez‑Sanchez has multiple convictions and offenses, including drug felonies, but he does not represent the immigration population at large. Research shows the myth that illegal immigration increases crime is wrong; crime rates fell as the immigrant population grew. The article warns against overlooking or ignoring the many undocumented immigrants who provide societal benefits and even rescued people during emergencies. Deporting as many undocumented immigrants as possible is wasted effort; the government needs smarter enforcement that focuses on threats such as convicted criminals, recent border crossers, and terrorism. The DHS move to prioritize these categories is a step forward, though ICE erred by releasing Lopez‑Sanchez without a warrant. President Obama’s executive action could have freed resources to go after criminals. Steinle’s death was a tragedy, but the failed system should not vilify all undocumented immigrants by association.  
END OF SUMMARY.
