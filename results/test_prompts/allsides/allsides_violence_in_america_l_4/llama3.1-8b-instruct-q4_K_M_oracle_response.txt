SUMMARY:

A tragic incident occurred on San Francisco's pier, where Kate Steinle was fatally shot by Juan Francisco Lopez-Sanchez, an undocumented immigrant who had been deported five times and reentered the country without authorization. This event has sparked a heated debate about immigration policies, with some using it as proof that increased illegal immigration leads to more crime. However, research from the Immigration Policy Center shows that crime rates have actually fallen in the US as the size of our immigrant population grew. The death of Kate Steinle was not representative of the 11 million undocumented immigrants in the country, who are largely productive members of society. Lopez-Sanchez's actions were a felony and a horrific crime, but they do not warrant vilifying all undocumented immigrants. Instead, immigration authorities should focus on targeting serious criminals, recent border crossers, and terrorism threats, as the Department of Homeland Security has announced it will do. This move is a step in the right direction towards smarter and better immigration enforcement.

END OF SUMMARY.
