SUMMARY:

The recent fatal shooting of Kate Steinle in San Francisco by an undocumented Mexican immigrant, Juan Francisco Lopez-Sanchez, has sparked controversy. Lopez-Sanchez, a repeat felon, should not have been free, but his case does not represent the majority of undocumented immigrants, who are productive members of society. Research shows that crime rates have fallen as the immigrant population has grown. The article argues that deporting all undocumented immigrants is not the solution, as Lopez-Sanchez had been deported five times before. Instead, the focus should be on smarter immigration enforcement, targeting serious criminals and recent arrivals. The Department of Homeland Security's new deportation priorities are a step in the right direction. The article also highlights the importance of President Obama's proposed executive action on immigration, which would have freed up resources to target criminals like Lopez-Sanchez.

END OF SUMMARY.
