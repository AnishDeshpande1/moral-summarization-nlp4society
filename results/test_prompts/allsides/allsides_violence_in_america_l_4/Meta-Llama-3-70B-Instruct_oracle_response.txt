SUMMARY:

A tragic incident in San Francisco has sparked controversy over illegal immigration. Kate Steinle, 31, was fatally shot while walking with her father on a busy pier. The alleged perpetrator, Juan Francisco Lopez-Sanchez, a Mexican immigrant without documentation, has seven felony convictions, including four for drug offenses. However, this individual's horrific crime does not represent the overwhelming majority of immigrants, who are productive members of society. Research shows that crime rates have fallen as the immigrant population has grown. It is a myth that increased illegal immigration leads to more crime. In fact, being in the country without authorization is a civil infraction, not a crime. Most undocumented immigrants come to work and provide a better life for themselves and their families. The government's focus should be on targeting serious criminals, not deporting as many undocumented immigrants as possible. The Department of Homeland Security's recent move to rethink deportation priorities is a step in the right direction. Lopez-Sanchez's case highlights the need for smarter and better immigration enforcement, rather than vilifying all undocumented immigrants by false association.

END OF SUMMARY.
