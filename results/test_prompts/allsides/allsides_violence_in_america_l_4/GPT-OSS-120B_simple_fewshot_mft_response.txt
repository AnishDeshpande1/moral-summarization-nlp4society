SUMMARY:
The fatal shooting of Kate Steinle in San Francisco by Juan Francisco Lopez‑Sanchez, an undocumented immigrant with multiple felony convictions, underscores a failure of authority and a need for stronger, smarter immigration enforcement. While the perpetrator’s illegal re‑entry and violent history make him a clear threat, the author warns against subverting justice by blaming all undocumented immigrants for his crime, emphasizing that most are law‑abiding workers and that criminality is not inherent to undocumented status. The piece calls for care toward victims and public safety by prioritizing the removal of convicted criminals, recent border crossers, and terrorism threats, rather than expending resources on productive, non‑criminal immigrants. It also highlights fairness, noting that the system has wasted taxpayer money on repeated deportations of Lopez‑Sanchez while neglecting serious offenders, and argues that targeted enforcement—potentially aided by the stalled Obama executive action—would better protect communities without unjustly vilifying an entire population.  

END OF SUMMARY.
