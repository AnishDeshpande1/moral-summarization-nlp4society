SUMMARY:
Former national‑security adviser John Bolton is set to publish a bombshell memoir that accuses President Trump of putting personal and partisan gain above the nation’s welfare. Bolton alleges Trump sought electoral help from China, endorsed Chinese prison camps, threatened journalists, and mishandled Ukraine—claims that paint a portrait of a leader unfit for office. Trump dismisses the book as lies from a disgruntled ex‑staffer and tried to block its release on national‑security grounds, but a federal judge allowed it to proceed. The timing fuels a moral debate: critics argue Bolton is exploiting a crisis for profit, while others contend the revelations expose grave abuses that could sway voters already angry over the pandemic and racial‑justice protests. Democrats weigh whether to summon Bolton again, noting that Republican senators who ignored his testimony may now face political retribution. Commentators acknowledge Bolton’s self‑interest but stress the book’s unique, detailed evidence of misconduct, suggesting it could still shape public judgment even if it cannot retroactively alter the impeachment outcome.

END OF SUMMARY.
