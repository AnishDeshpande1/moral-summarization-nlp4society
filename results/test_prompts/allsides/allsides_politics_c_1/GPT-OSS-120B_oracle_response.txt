SUMMARY: Former national security adviser John Bolton’s forthcoming book unleashes a torrent of accusations that paint President Trump’s decisions as driven by personal political gain rather than the nation’s best interests. Bolton alleges Trump was speaking about executing journalists, seeking election help from China’s Xi Jinping, endorsing China’s prison camps for Muslims, and engaging in wrongdoing with Ukraine that fueled the impeachment saga. Trump dismissed the book as a compilation of lies and claimed Bolton is merely trying to get even after being fired. The administration sued to block the release, citing classified material, but a federal judge ruled in Bolton’s favor. While Trump’s allies decry the claims as fabrications, liberals criticize Bolton for profit‑motivated timing, arguing the revelations arrive amid a health crisis and a national movement for racial justice, making them seem irrelevant. Yet some warn the book could still sway voters dissatisfied with the president’s handling of the pandemic and protests, and could undercut his anti‑China stance. Democrats now debate whether to summon Bolton for testimony, noting that Republican senators who refused to hear him and voted to acquit may now face electoral backlash. The book is set for release on June 23. END OF SUMMARY.
