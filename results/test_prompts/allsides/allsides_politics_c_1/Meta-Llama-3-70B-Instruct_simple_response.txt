SUMMARY:

Former national security adviser John Bolton's upcoming book accuses President Trump of being unfit for the presidency, making decisions based on reelection chances rather than the country's best interest. The book alleges Trump asked for election help from Chinese President Xi Jinping, endorsed China's use of prison camps for Muslims, and spoke about executing journalists. Trump has dismissed the book as "a compilation of lies and made up stories" and attempted to block its release over claims of classified information. The book's revelations have sparked debate, with some arguing that Bolton's decision to share his claims now, rather than during the impeachment trial, prioritizes personal profit over the country's interest. Others believe the book could still be impactful, highlighting Trump's wrongdoing and potentially resonating with voters dissatisfied with his handling of the pandemic and protests. The book's release is scheduled for June 23, and Democrats are considering seeking Bolton's testimony in light of the new allegations.

END OF SUMMARY.
