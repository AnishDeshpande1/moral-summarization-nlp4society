SUMMARY: Former national security adviser John Bolton's upcoming book contains scathing accusations against President Trump, portraying him as unfit for the presidency who made decisions based on personal gain rather than the country's best interest. Accusations include Trump asking for election help from Chinese President Xi Jinping, endorsing China's use of prison camps for Muslims, and speaking about executing journalists. The president called the book "a compilation of lies" from a former staffer trying to get even. A federal judge ruled in Bolton's favor, allowing the book's release despite claims it contains classified information. Democrats argue that the accusations could have been impactful during the impeachment trial, but now seem irrelevant given the health crisis and national movement for racial justice. The revelations may still resonate with voters discontent with Trump's handling of the pandemic and protests, potentially undercutting his attempts to present himself as a strong adversary to China.

END OF SUMMARY.
