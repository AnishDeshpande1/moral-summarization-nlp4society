**SUMMARY:**  
Former national security adviser John Bolton’s forthcoming book levels a barrage of accusations against President Trump, portraying him as unfit for office and alleging lies, made‑up stories, and classified‑information breaches. Bolton claims Trump prioritized reelection over the country’s best interests, seeking help from China, endorsing prison camps, and discussing the execution of journalists, all of which he says constitute serious wrongdoing and harm to the nation. Trump dismissed the book as a compilation of lies from a former staffer “just trying to get even” after being fired, while his allies echo this criticism and attempt to undercut Bolton’s claims. Democrats argue the revelations could have damaged Trump’s impeachment prospects and expose Republican senators who refused Bolton’s testimony to political damage. Liberals criticize Bolton for personal profit motives, suggesting the timing—amid a health crisis and racial‑justice protests—makes the book’s allegations largely irrelevant. Nonetheless, some observers contend the book’s specific allegations of abuse of authority and unfair conduct may still resonate with voters dissatisfied with Trump’s handling of the pandemic and protests.
