SUMMARY:

Former national security adviser John Bolton's upcoming book contains scathing accusations against President Trump, portraying him as unfit for the presidency who made decisions based on personal gain rather than the country's best interest. Bolton alleges that Trump asked Chinese President Xi Jinping for election help and endorsed China's use of prison camps for Muslims. The president responded by calling the book "a compilation of lies" from a former staffer seeking revenge. A federal judge ruled in favor of Bolton, allowing the book's release despite claims it contains classified information. Democrats argue that Bolton's testimony could have been impactful during the impeachment trial and that his revelations may still resonate with voters discontent with Trump's handling of the pandemic and protests. Republicans, on the other hand, suggest that Bolton's motives are self-serving and that his accusations would not have changed the outcome of the impeachment saga.

END OF SUMMARY.
