SUMMARY:

Former national security adviser John Bolton's upcoming book accuses President Trump of consistently making decisions based on his reelection chances rather than what's in the best interest of the country. Bolton portrays Trump as unfit for the presidency, citing instances of wrongdoing, including asking for election help from Chinese President Xi Jinping and endorsing China's use of prison camps for Muslims. Trump has dismissed the book as "a compilation of lies and made up stories" from a former staffer seeking revenge. However, a federal judge has ruled in Bolton's favor, allowing the book's release. Democrats argue that Bolton's claims could have been especially impactful during the impeachment trial, but instead, he chose to share them in a book, putting personal profit over the country's interest. The accusations could still resonate with voters discontent with Trump's handling of the pandemic and protests, potentially hurting Republican senators seeking reelection. 

END OF SUMMARY.
