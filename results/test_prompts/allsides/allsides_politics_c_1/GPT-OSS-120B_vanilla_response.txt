SUMMARY: Former national security adviser John Bolton’s forthcoming book, set for release on June 23, alleges that President Trump was unfit for office, prioritizing reelection over national interest. Bolton claims Trump sought election help from China’s Xi Jinping, endorsed Chinese prison camps for Muslims, discussed executing journalists, and engaged in dubious Ukraine dealings central to the impeachment. Trump denounced the book as lies from a disgruntled former staffer, while the administration sued to block its publication over alleged classified material; a federal judge rejected the request. Bolton, who left the White House in September 2019 after clashes with Trump, was not subpoenaed by House Democrats or Senate Republicans during the impeachment inquiry. The revelations have sparked partisan debate: Trump allies dismiss Bolton, while liberals criticize his timing and profit motives. Some argue the book’s details could still sway voters amid the COVID‑19 crisis and racial‑justice protests, potentially harming Republican senators who previously refused his testimony. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi indicated Democrats may reconsider calling Bolton. Experts note the memoir’s vivid accusations are politically damaging despite Bolton’s self‑interest, though some contend they would not have altered the impeachment outcome.

END OF SUMMARY.
