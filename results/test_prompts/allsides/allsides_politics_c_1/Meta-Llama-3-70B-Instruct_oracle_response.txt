SUMMARY:

Former national security adviser John Bolton's upcoming book accuses President Trump of making decisions based on his reelection chances rather than the country's best interest. The book alleges Trump asked for election help from Chinese President Xi Jinping, endorsed China's use of prison camps for Muslims, and spoke about executing journalists. Bolton portrays Trump as unfit for the presidency, citing his consistent wrongdoing. Trump has dismissed the book as "a compilation of lies and made up stories" from a former staffer seeking revenge. A federal judge ruled in Bolton's favor, allowing the book's release. Democrats argue that Bolton's claims could have been impactful during the impeachment trial, but instead, he chose to profit from the book. The revelations may still resonate with voters discontent with Trump's handling of the coronavirus crisis and protests. The book's release could hurt Republican senators seeking reelection who refused to hear Bolton's testimony and voted to acquit Trump. END OF SUMMARY.
