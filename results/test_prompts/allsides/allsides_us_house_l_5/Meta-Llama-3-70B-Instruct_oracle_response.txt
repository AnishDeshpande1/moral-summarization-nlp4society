SUMMARY:

House Republicans have introduced a sweeping immigration bill that claims to prevent the separation of children from their parents at the US-Mexico border. However, this is a lie. The bill does not outlaw family separation, nor does it stop the Trump administration from prosecuting asylum seekers who enter the US illegally, resulting in parents being sent into criminal custody without their children. Instead, the bill gets rid of legal protections for children and families in immigration detention, allowing ICE to detain them indefinitely. This means that if families are kept together, parents must be released with them, but the bill does not force the government to keep families together. In fact, it allows ICE to detain children and parents in the same way as adults, with no limitations on the conditions of their detention. The bill is a way for the Trump administration to maximize the detention of asylum seekers, including parents, and prevent them from disappearing into the US as unauthorized immigrants. Ultimately, the bill would lead to children being held in facilities that are essentially jails with their parents for months or even years.

END OF SUMMARY.
