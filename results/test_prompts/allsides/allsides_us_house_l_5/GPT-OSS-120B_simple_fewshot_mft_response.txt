SUMMARY:
Republicans in the House are poised to vote on a sweeping immigration compromise that bundles $25 billion for the border wall, a pathway for DACA‑eligible youth, and cuts to family‑based visas while expanding employer‑based immigration. House Speaker Paul Ryan and GOP leaders claim the bill will stop the Trump administration’s practice of separating children from their parents at the border. In reality, the legislation does not ban family separation; instead it eliminates the “least‑restrictive‑conditions” requirement that currently limits how long children may be detained and obliges ICE to treat accompanied minors the same as adults. By removing these protections, the bill would give the administration broader authority to keep families in detention indefinitely, effectively enabling the very practice it purports to end. The author frames this as a subversion of legitimate authority that prioritizes political goals over the care and safety of vulnerable children, inflicting harm on families and violating basic fairness by stripping legal safeguards that prevent indefinite, punitive detention. If passed, the measure would empower the government to detain parents and children together in adult facilities for months or years, undermining compassionate treatment and equitable immigration enforcement.  
END OF SUMMARY.
