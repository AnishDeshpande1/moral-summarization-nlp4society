SUMMARY:
A new “compromise” immigration bill introduced by House Republicans claims to stop family separation at the border, but the text tells a very different story. While the legislation bundles $25 billion for the wall, DACA pathways and changes to legal immigration, it actually **removes the legal safeguards that protect children in detention**. By eliminating the Flores‑agreement requirement that kids be held in the “least restrictive” setting and released without delay, the bill would let ICE detain families indefinitely, effectively giving the Trump administration the green light to keep parents and children together in adult‑type detention centers for months or years. The Republican press release’s promise that “accompanied alien minors must not be separated from their parent or legal guardian” has no counterpart in the bill’s language. Consequently, the measure does not outlaw family separation; instead it erodes the limited protections that have prevented the worst abuses, allowing the administration to continue its policy of prosecuting parents while holding children in prolonged, harsh detention. In short, the bill threatens to legitimize the very family‑detention practices critics have condemned as inhumane and contrary to American values of compassion and fairness.  
END OF SUMMARY.
