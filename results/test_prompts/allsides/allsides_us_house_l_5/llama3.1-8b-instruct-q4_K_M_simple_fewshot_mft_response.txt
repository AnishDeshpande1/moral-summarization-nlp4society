SUMMARY:

The House of Representatives is set to vote on a compromise immigration bill that claims to end family separation at the US-Mexico border. However, a closer look reveals that the bill doesn't actually outlaw family separation, but rather removes extra legal protections for children and families in immigration detention. This means that if parents are detained, their children must be released with them, effectively allowing the Trump administration to keep families together in ICE detention indefinitely. The bill overrules a 20-year court agreement, known as the Flores agreement, which requires the government to release children without unnecessary delay and keep them in the "least restrictive" conditions possible. By removing these protections, the bill allows the Trump administration to continue separating families while keeping children detained with their parents in ICE facilities. This move is a blatant attempt by the Trump administration to maximize detention of asylum seekers and undermine the Flores agreement, which prioritizes the care and well-being of migrant children.

END OF SUMMARY.
