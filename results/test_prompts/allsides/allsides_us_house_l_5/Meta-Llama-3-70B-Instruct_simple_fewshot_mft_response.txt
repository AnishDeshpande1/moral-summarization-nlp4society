SUMMARY:

The House Republican "compromise" immigration bill claims to prevent family separation at the US-Mexico border, but it's a lie. The bill doesn't outlaw family separation, nor does it stop the Trump administration from prosecuting asylum seekers who enter the US between ports of entry. Instead, it gets rid of legal protections for children and families in immigration detention, allowing the Trump administration to detain them indefinitely. This means that if the family is kept together, their parents must be released with them, but the administration can still keep children and parents in ICE detention until their cases are resolved, which could take months or even years. The bill prioritizes the authority of the Trump administration over the welfare of immigrant children and families, perpetuating harm and unfair treatment. It's a thinly veiled attempt to justify the administration's cruel policies, rather than a genuine effort to protect vulnerable individuals.

END OF SUMMARY.
