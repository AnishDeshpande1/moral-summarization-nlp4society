SUMMARY:

A new immigration bill proposed by Republicans in the House of Representatives claims to end family separation at the US-Mexico border, but it actually allows for indefinite detention of children and families. The bill removes extra protections for immigrant children, including a requirement that they be kept in "least restrictive" conditions and not detained longer than necessary. This means that if parents are detained with their children, they must be released together. However, the Trump administration could still separate families by prosecuting asylum seekers who enter between official border crossings. The bill does not outlaw family separation or require ICE to keep parents and children together in detention.

In reality, the House bill would allow the Trump administration to hold families in immigration detention indefinitely, which is what it has wanted all along. This means that children could be held in facilities designed for adults alongside their parents for months or even years until they receive legal status or are deported. The bill's language is misleading, and its true intention is to enable indefinite family detention. END OF SUMMARY.
