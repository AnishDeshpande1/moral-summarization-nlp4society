SUMMARY:

A new immigration bill proposed by Republicans in the House of Representatives aims to address border security, DACA protections, and family-based immigration. However, critics argue that the bill's focus on preventing family separation is misleading. According to experts, the bill would actually allow for indefinite detention of children and families at the US-Mexico border, effectively keeping them in custody until their cases are resolved. This contradicts claims by House Speaker Paul Ryan and others that the bill would prevent family separation. The bill would also repeal a 20-year-old court agreement known as the Flores agreement, which limits when children can be detained. Critics argue that this would enable the Trump administration to detain families indefinitely, rather than releasing them with their children.

END OF SUMMARY.
