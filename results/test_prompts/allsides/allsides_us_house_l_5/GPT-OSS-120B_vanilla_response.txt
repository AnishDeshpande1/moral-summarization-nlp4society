SUMMARY: A Republican “compromise” immigration bill slated for a House vote includes $25 billion for a border wall, a pathway to legal status for DACA recipients, and changes to family‑based immigration. While GOP leaders claim the bill will stop the Trump administration’s practice of separating children from parents at the border, the text does not outlaw family separation. Instead, it eliminates existing legal safeguards that require children to be held in the least restrictive conditions and released promptly. The bill permits ICE to detain immigrant children together with their parents at its discretion, effectively overruling the 20‑year Flores agreement that limits child detention to about 20 days. Consequently, the administration could keep families in detention indefinitely, rather than being forced to release children. The bill contains no provision mandating that parents stay with their children, nor does it address the prosecution‑based separations currently used. Thus, rather than ending family separations, the legislation could allow prolonged detention of families in adult facilities. END OF SUMMARY.
