SUMMARY:

The House Republican "compromise" immigration bill claims to prevent family separation at the US-Mexico border, but it's a lie. The bill doesn't outlaw family separation, nor does it stop the Trump administration from prosecuting asylum seekers who enter the US between ports of entry, resulting in family separation. Instead, the bill gets rid of legal protections for children and families in immigration detention, allowing ICE to detain them indefinitely. This means that if the family is kept together, their parents must be released with them, but the Trump administration can still detain children and parents in ICE detention centers for months or even years. The bill is a thinly veiled attempt to further marginalize and dehumanize immigrant communities, and to undermine the principles of equality and justice that are fundamental to American democracy.

END OF SUMMARY.
