SUMMARY:

A new immigration bill released by Republicans in the House of Representatives claims to end family separation at the US-Mexico border, but it actually does the opposite. The bill removes extra legal protections for children and families in immigration detention, allowing the Trump administration to keep them in custody indefinitely. This means that if a family is kept together, their parents must be released with them, effectively ending family separation only by forcing families into ICE detention together. The bill also allows ICE to detain immigrant children who come to the US with their parents or guardians in the same way it would detain adults, overturning a 20-year court agreement that restricts immigration detention of children. This move is a blatant attempt to maximize the detention of asylum seekers and prevent people from disappearing into the US as unauthorized immigrants.
