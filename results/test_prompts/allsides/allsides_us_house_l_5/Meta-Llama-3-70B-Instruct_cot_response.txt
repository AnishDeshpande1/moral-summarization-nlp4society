SUMMARY:
The House Republicans' new immigration bill claims to end family separation, but it's a lie. The bill doesn't outlaw family separation, instead, it allows the Trump administration to detain children and parents indefinitely. The bill gets rid of the Flores agreement, which requires children to be released "without unnecessary delay" and kept in the "least restrictive" conditions possible. This means that the administration can detain families for months or even years, effectively turning detention centers into jails. The bill's "clarification" of family detention allows ICE to detain immigrant children with their parents or guardians, giving the Secretary of Homeland Security discretion to detain them indefinitely. This is a blatant attempt to maximize detention and punish asylum seekers. The bill doesn't even specify that children will be kept with their parents in detention, leaving them vulnerable to being separated again. END OF SUMMARY.
