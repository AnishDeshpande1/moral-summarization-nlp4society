SUMMARY:
House Speaker Paul Ryan and other Republicans are misleadingly claiming that their new immigration bill would prevent the Trump administration from separating children from their parents at the US-Mexico border. However, the bill does not outlaw family separation, nor does it stop the administration from prosecuting asylum seekers who enter the US between ports of entry for illegal entry, resulting in parents being separated from their children. Instead, the bill gets rid of the extra legal protections that children and families have in immigration detention, allowing ICE to detain children and parents indefinitely. This means that if the family is kept together, their parents must be released with them, but the Trump administration can still keep children and parents in ICE detention until their cases are resolved, which could take months or even years. The bill allows for the indefinite detention of families, which is what Trump has wanted all along. END OF SUMMARY.
