SUMMARY:  
Republicans unveiled a “compromise” immigration bill that they tout as ending family separation at the border. In reality, the legislation does **not** ban the practice. It strips away the legal safeguards that require children to be held in the least restrictive setting and released without unnecessary delay, effectively allowing ICE to detain families—including children—indefinitely. The bill merely removes the “Flores” protections, giving the Trump administration the discretion to keep parents and kids together in adult detention facilities or continue separating them for prosecution. While GOP leaders claim the bill will stop the administration’s policy of separating children from their parents, the text contains no language guaranteeing that families stay together, nor does it prevent the prosecution‑based separations already in use. If passed, the law would likely enable longer, harsher detention of families rather than protect vulnerable children, fulfilling the administration’s goal of eliminating “catch‑and‑release” by keeping asylum seekers locked up indefinitely.  

END OF SUMMARY.
