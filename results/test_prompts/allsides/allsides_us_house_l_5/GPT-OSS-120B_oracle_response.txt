SUMMARY:  
Republicans released a “compromise” immigration bill that House leaders claim will **prevent** the Trump administration from **separating** children from their parents at the border. In fact, the bill does **not** **outlaw** family **separation** or **force** the government to **release** families together. Instead, it **gets rid of** existing legal **limitations** that require children to be kept in the **least restrictive** conditions and **released** without unnecessary delay. The new “Clarification of Standards for Family Detention” gives the Secretary of Homeland Security **discretion** to **detain** immigrant children alongside adults, **overruling** the Flores **agreement** that has **prevented** indefinite **detention** for 20 years. By **removing** the “loopholes” that **force** ICE to **release** children, the bill would allow the administration to **keep** parents and children **detained** indefinitely, **maximizing** detention and **preventing** the **release** of families. The Republican summary **lies** about **ending** family **separation**; the bill **does not** require parents and children to be **kept** together, nor does it **address** the practice of **sending** parents to criminal custody. The only **way** families might stay together is if the administration **chooses** to **stop** prosecuting parents, which is unlikely.  

END OF SUMMARY.
