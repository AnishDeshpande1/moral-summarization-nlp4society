SUMMARY:

The House Republicans' "compromise" immigration bill claims to prevent family separation at the US-Mexico border, but it does not outlaw the practice. Instead, it would allow the Trump administration to detain children and parents indefinitely in ICE detention centers. The bill eliminates legal protections for children and families in immigration detention, allowing ICE to detain them in the same way as adults. This would overturn a 20-year-old court agreement that limits the detention of children. The bill does not specify conditions for detaining children and does not require ICE to keep parents and children together. It would essentially allow the Trump administration to jail families together for months or years until their cases are resolved. The bill's passage would not end family separation, but rather, would allow the administration to detain families indefinitely.

END OF SUMMARY.
