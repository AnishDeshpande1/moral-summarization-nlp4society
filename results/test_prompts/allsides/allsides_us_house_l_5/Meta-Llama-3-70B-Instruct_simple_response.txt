SUMMARY:

The Republican "compromise" immigration bill in the House of Representatives claims to prevent family separation at the US-Mexico border, but it's a lie. The bill doesn't outlaw family separation, and instead, it would allow the Trump administration to detain children and parents indefinitely in ICE detention centers. The bill gets rid of legal protections for children and families in immigration detention, allowing the administration to keep families in custody for months or even years. This is what Trump has wanted all along. The bill's provision would overrule a 20-year-old court agreement that limits the detention of children, and it doesn't specify any conditions for detaining children. The only way the bill could possibly end family separation is if the Trump administration decides to stop prosecuting parents and keep families together in detention, but this would still mean holding children in facilities that are essentially jails with their parents. The bill is a thinly veiled attempt to justify the administration's cruel and inhumane treatment of immigrant families.

END OF SUMMARY.
