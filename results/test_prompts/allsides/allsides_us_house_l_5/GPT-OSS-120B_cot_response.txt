**SUMMARY:**  
The Republican “compromise” immigration bill, touted as a “sweeping” solution, promises $25 billion for a border wall and a path to legal status for DACA recipients, while cutting family‑based immigration. House leaders claim it will stop the Trump administration’s family **separation** policy, but the bill actually eliminates existing **protect**ions that require children to be kept in the “least restrictive” **detention** conditions and released without **indefinite** delay. By removing these **loopholes**, the legislation would allow ICE to hold **children** and **families** indefinitely, effectively legalizing the administration’s practice of keeping parents in **criminal** custody while their kids are placed with sponsors. The bill contains no language banning **separation** or requiring parents and **children** to stay together; instead it gives the Secretary of Homeland Security discretion to detain them as adults. If passed, the law would likely enable the administration to continue **prosecution** and **detention** of asylum seekers, extending the “catch and release” controversy rather than ending the humanitarian crisis.
