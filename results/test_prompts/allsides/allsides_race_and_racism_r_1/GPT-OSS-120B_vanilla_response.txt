SUMMARY: Fox announced it will keep Nick Cannon on “The Masked Singer” despite his recent anti‑Semitic and anti‑white remarks made on his “Cannon’s Class” podcast with Professor Griff. Cannon and Griff promoted the false claim that Black people cannot be anti‑Semitic and called white and Jewish people “the true savages.” After initially refusing to apologize, Cannon issued a public apology, expressing remorse and pledging education and dialogue. Fox said Cannon’s remorse and steps toward learning justified continuing his role and that the network condemns all hate. In contrast, ViacomCBS terminated its relationship with Cannon, citing his failure to initially acknowledge the bigotry. Cannon also announced a temporary leave from his morning radio show to focus on reflection and education, receiving support from figures like Charlamagne tha God and Sean “Diddy” Combs. END OF SUMMARY.
