SUMMARY:
Fox announced it will keep Nick Cannon on “The Masked Singer” after he made anti‑Semitic and anti‑white remarks on his podcast with Professor Griff, who is known for hateful commentary. Cannon claimed Black people cannot be anti‑Semitic and labeled white people and Jews as “the true savages.” After initially refusing to apologize, Cannon issued a public apology, saying he was “remorseful,” “ashamed,” and committed to education and dialogue with Jewish leaders. Fox said Cannon’s remorse and steps toward learning justified continuing his role and that the network condemns all hate. In contrast, ViacomCBS cut ties with Cannon, stating it was “deeply troubled” by his failure to initially acknowledge the bigotry. Cannon also stepped back from his morning radio show to focus on reflection and education, receiving support from figures like Charlamagne tha God and Sean “Diddy” Combs. The situation highlights the tension between holding public figures accountable for hateful speech and offering paths to redemption through sincere apology and learning. END OF SUMMARY.
