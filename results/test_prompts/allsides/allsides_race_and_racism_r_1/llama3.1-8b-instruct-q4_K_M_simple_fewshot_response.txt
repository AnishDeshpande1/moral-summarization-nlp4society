SUMMARY:

Fox has decided to allow Nick Cannon to remain on its show "The Masked Singer" despite his anti-Semitic and anti-white comments, while ViacomCBS terminated its relationship with him. The controversy arose from a podcast episode where Cannon pushed anti-Semitic theories and claimed that white people and Jewish people are the "true savages." Fox stated that it will help Cannon advance an important conversation about his mistakes, but also condemned all forms of hate directed towards any community. In contrast, ViacomCBS deemed Cannon's apology insufficient and took action to distance itself from him. The incident highlights the importance of accountability and education in addressing hurtful speech, as well as the need for companies to take a strong stance against hate and bigotry.
