SUMMARY:
Fox announced it will keep Nick Cannon on “The Masked Singer” despite his anti‑Semitic and anti‑white remarks made on his “Cannon’s Class” podcast with Professor Griff. The comments, which labeled white people and Jews as “the true savages,” were condemned as hateful, bigotry‑driven and hurtful, reinforcing harmful stereotypes. Cannon later issued a remorseful apology, expressing shame for the divisive and hurtful language and pledging deeper education and accountability. Fox said Cannon’s apology was “clear and remorseful” and that the network will move forward by supporting dialogue and combating hate. In contrast, ViacomCBS terminated its relationship with Cannon, condemning the bigotry and stating he had failed to adequately acknowledge the anti‑Semitic prejudice. Cannon’s earlier refusal to apologize, calling apologies “empty,” sparked criticism over fairness and responsibility. Prominent figures, including Charlamagne tha God and Sean “Diddy” Combs, defended him, while the network emphasized a commitment to combat hate and protect harmed communities. END OF SUMMARY.
