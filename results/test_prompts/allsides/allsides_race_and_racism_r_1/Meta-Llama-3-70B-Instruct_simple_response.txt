SUMMARY:

Fox has decided to keep Nick Cannon as the host of "The Masked Singer" despite his recent anti-Semitic and anti-white comments on his podcast "Cannon's Class". Cannon had made derogatory remarks about Jewish people and claimed that black people cannot be anti-Semitic. After initially refusing to apologize, Cannon eventually issued a statement expressing remorse for his words and committed to educating himself and making amends. Fox has accepted his apology and will continue to work with him, stating that they condemn all forms of hate and will combat bigotry of any kind. In contrast, ViacomCBS has terminated its relationship with Cannon, citing his failure to acknowledge or apologize for perpetuating anti-Semitism. The incident highlights the importance of accountability and education in combating hate speech and promoting understanding between different communities.

END OF SUMMARY.
