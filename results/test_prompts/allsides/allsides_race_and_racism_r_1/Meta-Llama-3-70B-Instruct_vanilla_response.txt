SUMMARY:

Fox has decided to keep Nick Cannon as the host of "The Masked Singer" despite his recent anti-Semitic and anti-white comments on his podcast "Cannon's Class". Cannon had made derogatory remarks about Jewish people and white people, claiming that black people cannot be anti-Semitic. He later issued an apology and expressed remorse for his words. Fox stated that they will move forward with Cannon and help him advance the conversation on combating bigotry. However, ViacomCBS terminated its relationship with Cannon, condemning his comments as bigotry. Cannon had initially refused to apologize, but later issued a statement expressing his deepest apologies to the Jewish community. He also announced that he would be taking time away from his morning radio show to educate himself further. Despite the backlash, Cannon has received support from prominent figures such as Charlamagne tha God and Sean 'Diddy' Combs.

END OF SUMMARY.
