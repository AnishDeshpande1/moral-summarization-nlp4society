SUMMARY: Fox said it will keep Nick Cannon on “The Masked Singer” after his anti‑Semitic and anti‑white comments on a podcast with Professor Griff, where they pushed hateful theories and claimed black people cannot be anti‑Semitic. Cannon said “white people” and Jews are “the true savages,” and his words lacked context and inadvertently promoted hate. After initially refusing to apologize, Cannon issued a statement apologizing, saying his rhetoric was misguided and that he wants healing and discussion rather than forced apologies. Fox noted Cannon is remorseful, has taken steps to educate himself, make amends and help advance a broader conversation, condemning all forms of hate and bigotry. ViacomCBS, however, terminated its relationship, condemning bigotry and saying Cannon failed to acknowledge his antisemitism. Cannon demanded ownership of “Wild ‘N Out,” ordered ViacomCBS to back down, and refused further demands, arguing that apologies are empty. He announced a break from his radio show to commit to deeper reflection and education, thanking rabbis and leaders who chose to help rather than chastise. END OF SUMMARY.
