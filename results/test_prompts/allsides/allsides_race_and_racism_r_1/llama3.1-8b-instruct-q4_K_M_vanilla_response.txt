SUMMARY:

Nick Cannon will remain on Fox's "The Masked Singer" despite his anti-Semitic and anti-white comments made during an interview with Professor Griff on his podcast. Fox stated that Cannon is remorseful for his words, which promoted hate and lack understanding and context. Cannon apologized after initially refusing to do so, saying he was uninformed and naive about the Jewish experience. He also expressed gratitude to Rabbis and community leaders who helped enlighten him.

In contrast, ViacomCBS terminated its relationship with Cannon, condemning bigotry of any kind. Fox, on the other hand, will continue to work with Cannon, hoping to advance an important conversation about hate and understanding. Multiple prominent figures have backed Cannon, including radio host Charlamagne tha God and rapper Sean "Diddy" Combs. Cannon has announced he will take time away from his morning radio show to commit to deeper reflection and education.

END OF SUMMARY.
