SUMMARY:

Fox has decided to keep Nick Cannon as the host of "The Masked Singer" despite his anti-Semitic and anti-white comments on his podcast "Cannon's Class". Cannon had made inflammatory statements, claiming that black people cannot be anti-Semitic and that white people and Jewish people are "the true savages". After initially refusing to apologize, Cannon eventually issued a statement expressing remorse for his words and a desire to educate himself and make amends. Fox has accepted his apology and will continue to work with him, stating that they "will move forward with Nick and help him advance this important conversation, broadly". In contrast, ViacomCBS has terminated its relationship with Cannon, condemning his bigotry and refusal to acknowledge or apologize for perpetuating anti-Semitism. The incident highlights the importance of accountability and self-reflection, as well as the need for education and dialogue to combat hate and bigotry. END OF SUMMARY.
