SUMMARY:  
Fox has chosen to keep Nick Cannon on “The Masked Singer” after he made overtly anti‑Semitic and anti‑white remarks in a June 30 podcast with Professor Griff, who is notorious for hate‑filled rhetoric. Cannon and Griff promoted the false claim that “the Semitic people are black people” and called white and Jewish people “the true savages.” After a public outcry, Cannon issued a belated apology, claiming remorse and a desire for “healing” rather than a conventional “I’m sorry.” Fox accepted his apology, emphasizing Cannon’s “remorse,” “education” and the network’s commitment to “combat bigotry of any kind,” while framing the incident as an opportunity for dialogue. In contrast, ViacomCBS terminated Cannon’s relationship, condemning his failure to promptly acknowledge anti‑Semitism. Cannon’s subsequent statements—first a vague Facebook note, then a refusal to apologize as “empty,” and finally a more detailed contrition—have been supported by figures like Charlamagne tha God and Sean “Diddy” Combs. The moral framing presented by Fox portrays forgiveness and education as the path forward, whereas ViacomCBS’s stance underscores zero tolerance for hate speech.  

END OF SUMMARY.
