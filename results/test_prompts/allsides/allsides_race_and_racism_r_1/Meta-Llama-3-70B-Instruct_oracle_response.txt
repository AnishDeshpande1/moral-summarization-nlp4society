SUMMARY:

Fox has decided to keep Nick Cannon as the host of "The Masked Singer" despite his anti-Semitic and anti-white comments on his podcast "Cannon's Class". Cannon's remarks, which promoted hate and lacked understanding and context, were widely condemned. He initially refused to apologize, instead demanding an apology from ViacomCBS and seeking full ownership of the comedy show "Wild 'N Out". However, after backlash, Cannon issued a sincere apology, acknowledging that his words were wrong and hurtful. Fox accepted his apology, stating that they will "move forward with Nick and help him advance this important conversation, broadly". The network condemned all forms of hate and bigotry, and believes that this moment calls for dialogue and education. In contrast, ViacomCBS terminated its relationship with Cannon, citing his failure to acknowledge or apologize for perpetuating anti-Semitism. Cannon has since taken steps to educate himself and make amends, and has announced that he will be taking time away from his morning radio show to commit to deeper reflection and education.

END OF SUMMARY.
