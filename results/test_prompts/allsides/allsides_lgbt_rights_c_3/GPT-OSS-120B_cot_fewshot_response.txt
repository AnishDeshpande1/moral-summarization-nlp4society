**SUMMARY:**  
A three‑judge panel of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Midwest struck down Wisconsin’s and Indiana’s bans on same‑sex marriage, finding the states offered “no reasonable basis” and that their arguments were riddled with holes. Writing for the unanimous court, Judge Richard Posner criticized the bans as rooted in discriminatory “tradition” and mere “distress,” not in genuine welfare or protection of children. He highlighted that many children are adopted by same‑sex couples and would be better off emotionally and economically, noting that the states’ claim that marriage prevents out‑of‑wedlock births is contradicted by empirical evidence showing rising birth‑outside‑marriage rates. Posner called the states’ logic a flawed “reward” system that penalizes homosexual couples while favoring heterosexuals, and he rejected the notion that historical tradition can justify discrimination. Judges Ann Claire Williams and David Hamilton joined the opinion. All three cases head to the Supreme Court.
