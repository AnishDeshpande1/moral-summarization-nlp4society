SUMMARY:

In a significant victory for the LGBTQ+ community, the U.S. Court of Appeals has struck down gay-marriage bans in Wisconsin and Indiana. Judge Richard Posner's scathing opinion rejected the states' arguments, calling them "full of holes" and "untenable." He argued that the ban on same-sex marriage is not about protecting children, but rather about perpetuating discrimination against LGBTQ+ individuals. The court noted that many children are adopted by homosexual couples, and would be better off if their adoptive parents were married. Posner also rejected the states' claim that conventional marriage is the solution to the problem of accidental pregnancies, citing empirical data that shows out-of-wedlock births have increased despite the ban on gay marriage. This decision follows two other federal appeals courts striking down gay-marriage bans, and all three cases are being appealed to the U.S. Supreme Court.
