SUMMARY: A three‑judge panel of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Seventh Circuit unanimously struck down Wisconsin’s and Indiana’s bans on same‑sex marriage, joining the 10th and 4th Circuits in declaring such bans unconstitutional. Writing for the panel, Judge Richard Posner criticized the states’ justifications as “full of holes,” noting that the bans offer no reasonable basis and that tradition cannot justify discrimination. He highlighted the states’ flawed argument that marriage prevents out‑of‑wedlock births, citing data showing rising illegitimacy rates despite the bans. Posner also pointed out that children adopted by same‑sex couples would be better off with married parents, undermining the claim that bans protect children. The decision, joined by Judges Ann Claire Williams and David Hamilton, will be appealed to the Supreme Court, which is expected to address the issue before its June term ends. END OF SUMMARY.
