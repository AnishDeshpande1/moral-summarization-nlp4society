SUMMARY:

A federal appeals court has struck down same-sex marriage bans in Wisconsin and Indiana, citing the states' lack of evidence for their argument against gay marriage. Judge Richard Posner wrote that the only rationale presented by the states was "so full of holes it cannot be taken seriously." He argued that the states' claim that traditional marriage is necessary to protect children from accidental pregnancies is flawed, as many children are adopted by same-sex couples who would benefit emotionally and economically from being married. The court also rejected the argument that tradition should dictate laws regarding same-sex marriage, citing the Supreme Court's 1967 decision striking down a ban on interracial marriage. Posner concluded that mere distress at the idea of same-sex marriage is not enough to justify the ban.

END OF SUMMARY.
