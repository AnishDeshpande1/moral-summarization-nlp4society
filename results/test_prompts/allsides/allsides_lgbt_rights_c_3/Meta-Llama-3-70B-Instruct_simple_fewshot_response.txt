SUMMARY:

In a landmark decision, the U.S. Court of Appeals has struck down same-sex-marriage bans in Wisconsin and Indiana, becoming the third federal appeals court to do so. The unanimous three-judge panel, led by Judge Richard Posner, ruled that the states had no reasonable basis for forbidding same-sex marriage, citing that their arguments were "full of holes" and "cannot be taken seriously." The court's decision highlights the importance of equality and justice, particularly for the welfare of American children. The ruling is a significant step forward for the LGBTQ+ community, providing a strong rebuke to discriminatory laws and policies. The moral framing of this story emphasizes the need to stand up against unjust laws and to promote equality and fairness for all individuals, regardless of their sexual orientation.
