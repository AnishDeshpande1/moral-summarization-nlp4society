SUMMARY:  
A three‑judge panel of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Midwest struck down Wisconsin’s and Indiana’s bans on same‑sex marriage, joining the 10th and 4th Circuits in deeming such prohibitions unjustified. Writing for the unanimous court, Judge Richard Posner – a Reagan appointee – condemned the states’ rationales as “full of holes” and morally unsound. He argued that the bans masquerade as child‑welfare policies but actually punish gay couples while rewarding heterosexual couples who, paradoxically, are more likely to produce unwanted pregnancies. Posner highlighted that children adopted by same‑sex couples would be better off emotionally and financially if those couples could marry. He also dismantled the claim that traditional marriage protects children, noting that out‑of‑wedlock birth rates rose after the bans were enacted and that “tradition” cannot justify discrimination, citing the Supreme Court’s overturning of interracial‑marriage bans. While acknowledging public discomfort with gay marriage, Posner stressed that distress alone does not legitimize denial of rights. Judges Ann Claire Williams and David Hamilton joined the opinion. The rulings are appealed to the Supreme Court, which is expected to address the issue soon.  
END OF SUMMARY.
