SUMMARY:

A federal appeals court has struck down gay-marriage bans in Wisconsin and Indiana, citing that the states provided no reasonable basis for forbidding same-sex marriage. Judge Richard Posner wrote that the states' arguments were "full of holes" and that their positions were inconsistent with empirical data on out-of-wedlock births. The court also rejected the argument that the ban helped to foster a centuries-old tradition of marriage as between a man and a woman, citing the Supreme Court's 1967 decision striking down a ban on interracial marriage.

Posner noted that many children are adopted by homosexual couples and would be better off if their adoptive parents were married. He criticized the states' position for rewarding heterosexual couples who produce unwanted children with the right to marry, while denying same-sex couples the right to do so. The decision is likely to be appealed to the U.S. Supreme Court.

END OF SUMMARY.
