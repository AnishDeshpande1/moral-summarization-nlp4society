SUMMARY: A three‑judge panel of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Midwest struck Wisconsin’s and Indiana’s bans on same‑sex marriage, joining the 10th and 4th Circuits that have already struck similar prohibitions. Writing for the unanimous court, Judge Richard Posner—appointed by Reagan—said the states offered no reasonable basis for the bans and that their arguments were full of holes. He framed the issue as one of child welfare, noting that denying gay couples the right to marry leaves many children “abandoned” to single‑parent or foster‑care situations, while same‑sex couples adopting would be better off emotionally and economically if they could marry. Posner dismissed the claim that traditional marriage prevents accidental pregnancies, pointing to rising out‑of‑wedlock birth rates despite the bans. He also rejected the reliance on “tradition” as a justification, calling it a form of discrimination comparable to the invalidated ban on interracial marriage. The opinion, joined by Judges Ann Claire Williams and David Hamilton, calls the bans unjustified and signals that the Supreme Court will likely review the matter soon. END OF SUMMARY.
