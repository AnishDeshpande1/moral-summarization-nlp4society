SUMMARY:

The U.S. Court of Appeals for the Midwest has struck down same-sex marriage bans in Wisconsin and Indiana, becoming the third federal appeals court to do so. Judge Richard Posner, writing for a unanimous three-judge panel, stated that the states provided "no reasonable basis" for forbidding same-sex marriage. The court rejected the states' arguments, including the claim that banning same-sex marriage encourages heterosexual couples to marry and reduce accidental pregnancies. Posner noted that many children of accidental pregnancies are adopted by same-sex couples, who would benefit from marriage. The court also dismissed the argument that the ban on same-sex marriage helps preserve a centuries-old tradition of marriage between a man and a woman, citing the Supreme Court's 1967 decision striking down a ban on interracial marriage. The cases are expected to be appealed to the U.S. Supreme Court, which may take on the issue before the end of its current term in June.

END OF SUMMARY.
