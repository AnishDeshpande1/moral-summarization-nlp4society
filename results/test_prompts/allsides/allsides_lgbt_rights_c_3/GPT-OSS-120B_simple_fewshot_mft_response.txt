SUMMARY:
A three‑judge panel of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Midwest struck down Wisconsin’s and Indiana’s bans on same‑sex marriage, declaring the states offered “no reasonable basis” for the prohibitions. Writing for the unanimous court, Judge Richard Posner—appointed by Reagan—focused on the harm the bans inflict on children and families, noting that denying marriage to gay couples harms children who could be adopted by loving, married same‑sex parents, while the states’ own policies fail to protect children from out‑of‑wedlock births. He highlighted the unfairness of a system that rewards heterosexual couples who may create unwanted pregnancies with marriage rights, yet punishes homosexual couples—who are “model parents” and do not produce such pregnancies—by denying them marriage. Posner also rejected the states’ appeal to “tradition,” calling it an unjust, discriminatory excuse that conflicts with past Supreme Court rulings against racial discrimination. The opinion, joined by Judges Ann Claire Williams and David Hamilton, frames the bans as both harmful to vulnerable children and fundamentally unfair, violating principles of care and equality. The decisions are now headed to the Supreme Court. 
END OF SUMMARY.
