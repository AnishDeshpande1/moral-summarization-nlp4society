SUMMARY:

A federal appeals court has struck down same-sex marriage bans in Wisconsin and Indiana, citing no reasonable basis for the prohibitions. Judge Richard Posner's scathing opinion argued that the states' claims were "full of holes" and ignored the welfare of American children. The states' argument that marriage between a man and a woman encourages responsible parenting and prevents child abandonment was debunked by Posner, who pointed out that many children are adopted by same-sex couples who would benefit from marriage. The opinion also rejected the states' claims that traditional marriage should be preserved, citing the Supreme Court's 1967 decision striking down interracial marriage bans. The ruling is the third of its kind, following similar decisions in Utah, Oklahoma, and Virginia, and is expected to be appealed to the U.S. Supreme Court. The moral imperative is clear: discrimination against same-sex couples is unjustified and harmful to children.

END OF SUMMARY.
