SUMMARY:

A federal appeals court has struck down same-sex marriage bans in Wisconsin and Indiana, joining two other courts that have reached similar conclusions. The unanimous three-judge panel, led by Judge Richard Posner, found that the states' arguments for forbidding gay marriage were "full of holes" and lacked a "reasonable basis." At its core, the issue is about the welfare of American children, with the states arguing that same-sex couples are often irresponsible and produce unwanted children. However, Posner countered that many of these children are adopted by model homosexual parents who would benefit from being married. The court also rejected the idea that tradition can be a lawful ground for discrimination, citing the Supreme Court's 1967 decision on interracial marriage. With this ruling, all three cases are now being appealed to the U.S. Supreme Court.
