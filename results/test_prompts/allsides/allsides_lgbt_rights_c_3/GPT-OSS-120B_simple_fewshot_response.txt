SUMMARY:
A three‑judge panel of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Seventh Circuit has struck down Wisconsin’s and Indiana’s bans on same‑sex marriage, joining two other appellate courts that have already invalidated similar bans. Writing for the unanimous court, Judge Richard Posner – a Reagan appointee – declared that the states offered “no reasonable basis” for prohibiting marriage equality and mocked the flimsy, contradictory arguments that the bans protect children or preserve tradition. He highlighted that many children adopted by gay couples thrive emotionally and economically, and that the data on out‑of‑wedlock births actually undermine the states’ claim that marriage promotion reduces unwanted pregnancies. Posner also noted that “tradition” cannot justify discrimination, citing the Supreme Court’s precedent overturning bans on interracial marriage. The decision, joined by Judges Ann Claire Williams and David Hamilton, will be appealed to the Supreme Court, which is expected to address the issue soon. The ruling frames the bans as unfounded, discriminatory policies that harm families and deny equal rights to LGBTQ Americans.  
END OF SUMMARY.
