SUMMARY:
A male student entered Noblesville West Middle School in Indiana with two handguns and opened fire, critically injuring a teacher and a fellow student. The suspect was quickly detained after a science teacher, Jason Seaman, tackled him, preventing further casualties. The incident occurred at 9:06 a.m., and the victims were taken to a hospital while families were notified. Law enforcement, including the FBI and ATF, secured the scene and launched an investigation; no additional threats were found, though a possible threat to the nearby Noblesville High School was reported. State and local officials, including Governor Eric Holcomb, Vice President Mike Pence, Rep. Andre Carson, Sen. Todd Young, and the Indianapolis Colts, expressed prayers and gratitude for the rapid response of police and first responders. Parents and students described the shock and fear, while the Indiana State Teachers Association called for decisive action to end school violence. This shooting follows a recent Texas school attack, underscoring ongoing concerns about safety in American schools.

END OF SUMMARY.
