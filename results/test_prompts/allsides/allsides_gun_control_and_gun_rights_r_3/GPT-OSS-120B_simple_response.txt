SUMMARY:
A male student was taken into custody after opening fire at Noblesville West Middle School in Indiana, wounding a teacher and a fellow student. The suspect entered the classroom armed with two handguns, but a science teacher, Jason Seaman, tackled him, disarming the gun and likely preventing further casualties, though Seaman himself was shot multiple times and required surgery. Authorities, including local police, the FBI and ATF, secured the scene and opened an investigation, noting no additional threats were found despite a separate warning to nearby Noblesville High School. Community leaders—Vice President Mike Pence, Rep. Andre Carson, Sen. Todd Young, Gov. Eric Holcomb, the Indianapolis Colts, and the Indiana State Teachers Association—issued statements of prayer, gratitude to first responders, and a moral outcry demanding decisive action to end school violence, asking “When is enough, enough?” Parents and students expressed fear and relief, highlighting the heroism that averted a greater tragedy. The incident follows a recent mass shooting in Texas, underscoring a national crisis of school safety.

END OF SUMMARY.
