SUMMARY: A male student was arrested after opening fire at Noblesville West Middle School in Indiana on Friday morning, injuring a teacher and a student who were taken to hospital. The shooter entered the classroom with two handguns after briefly leaving, but a science teacher, Jason Seaman, tackled him, sustaining multiple gunshot wounds. No further threat was found at the school, though a separate threat to nearby Noblesville High School was investigated. Federal agencies, including the FBI and ATF, are on the scene, and an investigation is underway. The incident prompted statements of support and prayers from Indiana officials, including Gov. Eric Holcomb, Vice President Mike Pence, Rep. Andre Carson, Sen. Todd Young, as well as the Indianapolis Colts and the Indiana State Teachers Association, which called for action on school safety. Parents and students described the chaos, with reports of 16 shots fired. The school, which lacks metal detectors but has a full‑time resource officer, has about 1,300 middle‑school students. This shooting follows a deadly attack at a Texas high school the previous week. END OF SUMMARY.
