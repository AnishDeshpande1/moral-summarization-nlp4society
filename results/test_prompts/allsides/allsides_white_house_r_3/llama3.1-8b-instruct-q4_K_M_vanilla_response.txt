SUMMARY:

The terrorist attacks in Paris, carried out by the Islamic State (IS), occurred just 12 hours after President Obama stated that IS had been "contained". At least 127 people were killed and many more wounded in the shootings and bombings. French President Francois Hollande blamed IS for an "act of war", while Obama said the US-led coalition has made progress in containing the group. However, this statement contradicts his previous assessment of IS as a "jayvee team" among terrorist groups.

The attacks are the second major attack carried out by IS in two weeks, following the downing of a Russian passenger plane in the Sinai peninsula on October 31. Obama has spoken with Hollande to offer condolences and reaffirmed US support for France. The two leaders pledged to work together to defeat terrorism.

Obama is set to depart Washington for the G-20 summit in Turkey, where he will meet with world leaders to discuss the fight against IS, the Syrian civil war, and the European refugee crisis. He will convene his National Security Council before leaving to review the latest intelligence on the Paris attacks.

END OF SUMMARY.
