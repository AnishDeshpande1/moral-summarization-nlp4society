SUMMARY: Terrorist attacks in Paris, claimed by the Islamic State, killed at least 127 people and injured many more, occurring just 12 hours after President Obama declared the group “contained.” French President François Hollande labeled the attacks an “act of war” and cancelled his trip to the G‑20 summit in Turkey. Obama, speaking to ABC News, reiterated that ISIL had not gained ground in Iraq or Syria and emphasized U.S. and coalition progress in curbing foreign fighters, despite earlier criticism for downplaying the threat. He expressed condolences to France and pledged continued U.S. support, offering assistance to French investigators. Obama will meet Hollande and other leaders at the G‑20 summit to discuss counter‑terrorism, the Syrian conflict, and the European refugee crisis, and will convene the National Security Council before departing. The attacks follow a recent ISIL‑claimed bombing in Paris and the downing of a Russian plane in the Sinai. END OF SUMMARY.
