SUMMARY:

President Obama's assertion that the Islamic State (IS) had been "contained" was contradicted by the terrorist attacks in Paris, which left at least 127 people dead and scores wounded. IS claimed responsibility for the attacks, just 12 hours after Obama's statement. French President Francois Hollande blamed IS for "an act of war." The incident raises questions about the effectiveness of the US-led coalition fighting IS in Syria and Iraq. Security officials had warned that French citizens who joined IS in Syria could return to France to carry out attacks. Obama was criticized earlier this year for downplaying IS as a "jayvee team," suggesting it was not a major concern at the time. The Paris attack is the second major strike by IS in two weeks, following the downing of a Russian passenger plane on October 31. The incident has led to increased cooperation between the US and France to combat terrorism.

END OF SUMMARY.
