SUMMARY:  
Paris was struck by a wave of **attacks** – **shootings**, **bombings**, and **killings** – that left at least 127 people **dead** and scores **wounded**. French President François Hollande called the assault an **act of war** and blamed the Islamic State, which also claimed the **downing** of a Russian passenger plane. The attacks came just 12 hours after President Obama declared the extremist group “**contained**” and said it was not **gaining** ground or launching a systematic **march** across Iraq and Syria. Obama reiterated that the U.S.–led coalition is **fighting** ISIS and working to **reduce** the flow of foreign fighters, while offering “steadfast, unwavering” support to France and promising assistance with the **investigation**. Hollande cancelled his trip to the G‑20 summit in Turkey; Obama will attend the summit after convening the National Security Council to review the latest intelligence. Both leaders pledged to work with allies worldwide to **defeat** the scourge of **terrorism**.  
END OF SUMMARY.
