SUMMARY:

In a stark contradiction, President Obama declared that the Islamic State had been "contained" just 12 hours before the group carried out devastating terrorist attacks in Paris, killing at least 127 people. The President's statement has been widely criticized, as it appears to downplay the severity of the threat posed by the Islamic State. French President Francois Hollande, on the other hand, was quick to condemn the attacks as "an act of war." The Obama administration's handling of the Islamic State has been called into question, with critics pointing to the group's recent successes, including the downing of a Russian passenger plane. The President's response to the attacks has been to offer condolences and reiterate the US's commitment to fighting terrorism, but his initial assessment of the Islamic State's strength has been widely seen as a grave mistake.

END OF SUMMARY.
