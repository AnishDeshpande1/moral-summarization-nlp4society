SUMMARY:

Just hours after President Obama claimed that the Islamic State had been "contained", the extremist group carried out a devastating terrorist attack in Paris, killing at least 127 people and injuring many more. French President Francois Hollande condemned the attack as "an act of war". In an interview with ABC News, Obama had rejected the notion that the Islamic State was gaining strength, claiming that "we have contained them". However, the Paris attack and the downing of a Russian passenger plane in the Sinai peninsula on October 31, which also killed all 224 aboard, suggest that the group's threat is far from contained. Obama spoke with Hollande to offer condolences and pledged to work together to defeat terrorism. The attack has led to the cancellation of Hollande's trip to the G-20 summit in Turkey, where he was to meet with Obama and other world leaders to discuss the fight against the Islamic State. END OF SUMMARY.
