SUMMARY:
President Obama’s claim that the Islamic State had been “contained” was starkly contradicted by the devastating attacks in Paris, which killed at least 127 people and wounded many more. French President François Hollande labeled the assault an “act of war” by ISIS, while Obama, in a recent ABC interview, insisted the group was not gaining strength and that the U.S.-led coalition had succeeded in limiting its expansion in Iraq and Syria. Critics noted Obama’s earlier dismissal of ISIS as a “jayvee team,” underscoring concerns that the threat had been underestimated. The Paris bombings, possibly the second major ISIS strike in two weeks, followed the group’s claim of responsibility for downing a Russian plane over Sinai. In response, Obama expressed condolences to France, reaffirmed U.S. support, and promised assistance with the French investigation. Hollande canceled his G‑20 trip to Turkey, where he was to meet Obama and other leaders on counter‑terrorism, the Syrian civil war, and the European refugee crisis. Obama will convene the National Security Council before departing for the summit to review intelligence on the Paris attacks.

END OF SUMMARY.
