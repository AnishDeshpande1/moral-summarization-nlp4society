SUMMARY: The Paris terrorist attacks, claimed by the Islamic State and killing at least 127 people, occurred just 12 hours after President Obama declared the extremist group “contained.” Obama’s repeated assurances that ISIS was not gaining strength and his earlier dismissive comment calling it a “jayvee team” have been sharply criticized as naïve and morally irresponsible, especially given warnings that returning French fighters could strike at home. French President Hollande labeled the bombings an “act of war” and condemned the attacks, while Obama offered condolences and pledged continued support, promising to work with France and the international community to defeat terrorism. The attacks underscore the moral failure of underestimating ISIS, highlighting the urgent need for honest assessment and decisive action. Hollande cancelled his G‑20 trip to Turkey, and Obama will convene his National Security Council before attending the summit, reflecting the gravity of the situation and the moral imperative to confront the terror threat. END OF SUMMARY.
