**SUMMARY:**  
The Islamic State’s terrorist attacks in Paris, which killed at least 127 people, occurred just 12 hours after President Obama declared the extremist group “contained.” French President Hollande labeled the assault an “act of war” and expressed horror at the violence, while Obama reiterated U.S. support for France and vowed to continue the fight against the scourge of terrorism. The attacks, the second major Islamic State strike in two weeks, underscore concerns that hundreds of French citizens who joined the group abroad could return to carry out further attacks. Obama and Hollande spoke by phone to offer condolences and coordinate a joint response, and both leaders will meet with other world leaders at the upcoming G‑20 summit to discuss defeating the terrorist threat. The White House announced that the president will convene his National Security Council before traveling to Turkey for the summit.
