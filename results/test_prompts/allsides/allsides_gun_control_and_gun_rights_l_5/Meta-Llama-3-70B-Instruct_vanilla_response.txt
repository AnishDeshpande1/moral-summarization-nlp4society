SUMMARY:

Advocates for stricter gun control claim momentum in the political environment, despite the NRA's dominance. Since the 2012 Sandy Hook massacre, 18 states have enacted background check laws, and gun-control advocates have improved their record in fighting NRA-backed attempts to expand gun rights. The NRA had significant victories in Kansas and Maine, passing laws allowing gun owners to carry concealed firearms without a permit. However, gun-control advocates won a major victory in California with a protective-order law, allowing police or family members to take action if a person makes threats or displays disturbing behavior. Both sides are pulling harder, with gun-control advocates restricting access in blue and purple states, while the NRA argues that arming more people in more places is the key to preventing mass shootings. Despite defeats, gun-control advocates have stopped NRA-backed efforts to enact campus-carry laws in 14 of 16 states.

END OF SUMMARY.
