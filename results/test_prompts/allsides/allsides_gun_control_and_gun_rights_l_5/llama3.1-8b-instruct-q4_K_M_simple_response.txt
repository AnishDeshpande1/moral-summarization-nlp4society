SUMMARY:

Despite years of pushing for stricter gun control, advocates have made significant gains at the state level. Since the Sandy Hook massacre in 2012, six additional states have enacted background check laws, bringing the total to 18 nationwide. Gun-control groups are celebrating their improved record in fighting NRA-backed efforts to expand gun rights and protecting legislators targeted by the NRA in elections. The California protective-order law is seen as a major win, allowing police or family members to take action if someone makes threats or displays disturbing behavior. This model is being touted as a promising approach for other states to follow.

Gun-control advocates are confident that they have momentum on their side, while the NRA and its allies argue that arming more people in more places is key to preventing mass shootings. Despite some defeats, gun-control groups claim to have stopped 14 of the 16 campus-carry bills introduced in statehouses, a significant shift from past years.

END OF SUMMARY.
