SUMMARY:  
Tim Daly of the Center for American Progress says the gun‑policy fight is now a “draw.” Since the 2012 Sandy Hook massacre, gun‑control advocates have gained momentum, adding half‑a‑dozen new state background‑check laws for a total of 18, and improving their record against NRA‑backed expansions of gun rights. They claim the “wind at our backs” and that gun control is no longer a political “third rail.” The NRA’s biggest wins were “permit‑less concealed carry” laws in Kansas and Maine, and “open‑carry” and “campus‑carry” statutes in Texas, prompting businesses to post gun‑free signs and city officials to advise when to call police. California passed a broad protective‑order law allowing police or family members to intervene when someone shows threatening behavior, a response to the 2014 Isla Vista shooting. Both sides are intensifying their efforts: gun‑control groups, funded by Bloomberg, say they stopped NRA‑backed campus‑carry bills in 14 of 16 states, while the NRA continues to push for more armed presence in schools and campuses. The battle has become a more evenly matched tug‑of‑war across the nation.  
END OF SUMMARY.
