SUMMARY:
Tim Daly of the Center for American Progress says the gun‑policy fight is “a draw,” but advocates see a shift after the 2012 Sandy Hook massacre. Since then, 18 states have enacted background‑check laws, and gun‑control supporters claim they are now better at fighting NRA‑backed attempts to expand gun rights and protecting legislators who have been targeted by the gun lobby. Shannon Watts of Moms Demand Action says the issue is no longer a third rail, noting successes in stopping proposed campus‑carry bills in 14 of 16 states. Meanwhile, the NRA celebrates victories in Kansas and Maine, where “open carry” laws allow concealed guns without a permit, and Texas has passed both open‑carry and campus‑carry measures, prompting businesses to post signs and officials to warn against calling 311 or 911 for merely acting with a holstered weapon. California’s new protective‑order law aims to address threats and rampage‑style behavior without infringing constitutional rights, allowing police or family members to intervene before a violation escalates to a shooting. Both sides continue to pull harder, with gun‑control advocates seeking to restrict access while the NRA argues more guns will prevent future shootings.  
END OF SUMMARY.
