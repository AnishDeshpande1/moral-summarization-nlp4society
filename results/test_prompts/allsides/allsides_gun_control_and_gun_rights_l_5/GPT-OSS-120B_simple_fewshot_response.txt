SUMMARY:
Gun‑control advocates say the tide is finally turning after years of NRA dominance.  Since Sandy Hook, a half‑dozen more states have adopted background‑check laws, bringing the total to 18, and recent victories—including California’s expansive protective‑order law and defeats of NRA‑backed campus‑carry bills in 14 of 16 states—are hailed as evidence that “the wind is at our backs.”  Leaders such as Tim Daly of the Center for American Progress and Shannon Watts of Moms Demand Action stress that gun‑rights measures are no longer a “third rail” and that the movement is better equipped to protect legislators from NRA retaliation.  While the NRA celebrates new open‑carry and permit‑less concealed‑carry laws in Kansas, Maine and Texas, opponents warn that these policies force businesses and communities to confront a normalised presence of guns.  Overall, the ongoing state‑level tug‑of‑war reflects a growing moral resolve to curb gun violence through stricter regulations, even as gun‑rights groups continue to push for broader firearm access.  
END OF SUMMARY.
