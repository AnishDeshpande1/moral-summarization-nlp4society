SUMMARY: In the ongoing battle over gun control, advocates for stricter laws claim momentum despite the NRA's continued influence. Since the 2012 Sandy Hook Elementary School massacre, 18 states have enacted background check laws, and gun-control supporters have improved their record in fighting NRA-backed attempts to expand gun rights. The NRA's victories, such as allowing concealed carry without a permit in Kansas and Maine, have been met with resistance from local businesses and governments. In contrast, California's protective-order law, designed to prevent shootings like the 2014 Isla Vista rampage, allows police to take action against individuals making threats or displaying disturbing behavior. Gun-control advocates boast about stopping NRA-backed efforts to enact campus-carry laws in 14 states, protecting legislators targeted by the NRA. As the tug of war continues, both sides are pulling harder, with gun-control supporters seeking to restrict access to guns and the NRA arguing that arming more people is the key to preventing mass shootings. END OF SUMMARY.
