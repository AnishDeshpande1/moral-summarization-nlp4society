These single words have moral connotations, indicating a shift in the balance of power between gun control advocates and opponents.

**SUMMARY:**
Gun control advocates claim to be gaining momentum after years of fighting against the NRA's dominance. Despite defeats in some states, they point to victories such as California's protective-order law, which allows police or family members to take action if a person makes threats or displays disturbing behavior. This model is seen as promising for other states to follow. In contrast, the NRA and its allies argue that arming more people is the key to preventing mass shootings. However, gun control advocates have successfully stopped campus-carry laws in 14 out of 16 states where bills were introduced. They attribute this success to their efforts to restrict access in blue and purple states. The battle over gun laws has become a more evenly matched tug of war, with both sides pulling harder.
