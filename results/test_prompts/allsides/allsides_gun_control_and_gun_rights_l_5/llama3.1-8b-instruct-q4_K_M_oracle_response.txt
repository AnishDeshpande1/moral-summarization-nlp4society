SUMMARY: The gun control debate has become a more evenly matched tug of war, with both sides pulling harder. Gun-control advocates have made progress in restricting access to guns, enacting background check laws in half a dozen states and protecting legislators targeted by the NRA in elections. Despite the NRA's victories in Kansas and Maine, where concealed carry laws were expanded, gun control supporters claim momentum since the 2012 Sandy Hook Elementary School massacre. The California protective-order law is seen as a major win for advocates, allowing police or family members to take action if someone makes threats or displays disturbing behavior. Gun-control proponents are stopping NRA-backed efforts to enact campus-carry laws in many states and restricting access to guns in blue and purple states. However, the NRA argues that arming more people can prevent mass shootings. As the battle over gun laws intensifies, both sides are pushing hard for their agendas, with some business owners taking advantage of provisions allowing them to restrict guns from their stores.

END OF SUMMARY.
