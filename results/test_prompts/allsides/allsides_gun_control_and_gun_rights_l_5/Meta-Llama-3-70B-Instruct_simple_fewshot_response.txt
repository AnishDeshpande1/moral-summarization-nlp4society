SUMMARY:

Despite the NRA's continued influence, gun control advocates claim momentum in the battle over gun laws. Since the 2012 Sandy Hook massacre, an additional six states have enacted background check laws, bringing the total to 18 nationwide. While the NRA has scored victories in Kansas and Maine, allowing gun owners to carry concealed firearms without a permit, gun control advocates have improved their record in fighting NRA-backed attempts to expand gun rights. California's new protective-order law, allowing police or family members to take action if a person makes threats or displays disturbing behavior, is seen as a promising model for other states to follow. The tug of war over gun laws has become more evenly matched, with both sides pulling harder. Gun-control advocates, backed by millions from former New York Mayor Michael Bloomberg, have stopped NRA-backed efforts to enact campus-carry laws in 14 of 16 states where bills were introduced. The moral imperative to act on gun violence remains, with advocates pushing for stricter gun control measures.

END OF SUMMARY.
