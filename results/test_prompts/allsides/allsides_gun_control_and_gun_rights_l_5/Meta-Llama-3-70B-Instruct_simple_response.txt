SUMMARY:

Despite the NRA's continued influence, advocates for stricter gun control have made significant progress at the state level. Since the Sandy Hook massacre, an additional six states have enacted background check laws, bringing the total to 18 nationwide. While the NRA has scored victories in Kansas and Maine, allowing gun owners to carry concealed firearms without a permit, gun-control advocates have successfully fought off NRA-backed attempts to expand gun rights in other states. A notable win for gun-control advocates is the California protective-order law, which allows police or family members to take action if someone makes threats or displays disturbing behavior. This law was designed in response to the 2014 Isla Vista shooting rampage. As the battle over gun laws continues, both sides are digging in, with gun-control advocates pushing to restrict access to guns and the NRA arguing that arming more people is the key to preventing mass shootings.

END OF SUMMARY.
