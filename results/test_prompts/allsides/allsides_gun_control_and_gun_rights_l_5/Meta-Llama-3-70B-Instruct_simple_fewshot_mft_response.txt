SUMMARY:

In the ongoing battle over gun control, advocates for stricter laws claim momentum despite the NRA's continued influence. Since the Sandy Hook massacre, 18 states have enacted background check laws, and gun-control advocates have improved their record in fighting NRA-backed attempts to expand gun rights. However, the NRA has scored victories in Kansas and Maine, allowing gun owners to carry concealed firearms without a permit. The California protective-order law, which allows police or family members to take action if a person makes threats or displays disturbing behavior, is seen as a promising model for other states. The debate has become a more evenly matched tug of war, with gun-control advocates pushing to restrict access and the NRA arguing that arming more people is the key to preventing mass shootings. Despite setbacks, gun-control advocates boast about stopping NRA-backed efforts to enact campus-carry laws in 14 states. The moral imperative to prioritize care and harm, fairness and cheating, and authority and subversion remains a driving force in the battle over gun control.

END OF SUMMARY.
