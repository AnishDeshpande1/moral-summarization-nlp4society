SUMMARY:

Gun control advocates claim momentum despite failed federal efforts, citing passage of additional state laws and improved record in fighting NRA-backed attempts to expand gun rights. Since the Sandy Hook massacre in 2012, six more states have enacted background check laws, bringing the total to 18 nationwide. California's protective-order law is seen as a big win for gun-control advocates, allowing police or family members to take action if someone makes threats or displays disturbing behavior. The NRA has scored some victories, including passing "open carry" and "campus carry" laws in Texas and Kansas. However, gun-control advocates have successfully blocked campus-carry laws in 14 of the 16 states where bills were introduced.

Gun-control advocates say they have gained ground, with some even calling it a "tug of war" between the two sides. The NRA has pushed for arming more people in more places to prevent mass shootings, but gun-control advocates argue that this approach is misguided and will only lead to more violence. With millions from former New York Mayor Michael Bloomberg backing their efforts, gun-control advocates are optimistic about their chances of passing stricter laws.

END OF SUMMARY.
