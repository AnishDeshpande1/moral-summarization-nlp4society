SUMMARY:  
The article argues that keeping the economy shut creates serious health consequences that rival those of the virus itself. Suicide rates are spiking, cardiac procedures are not being received, cancer screenings are declining, and childhood vaccinations have ground to a halt, all of which could devastate public health. Data show missed and delayed cancer diagnoses, thousands of deaths from drug abuse and suicide, and a surge in domestic‑violence calls. Economic devastation and starvation predictions warn that the ultra‑poor could die in greater numbers than the virus, while widening inequality worsens health outcomes. Since most counties have few or no COVID‑19 deaths, the author says restrictions should be eased and the virus managed with available hospital beds and testing. State officials must make choices based on scientific data, not judgment from a privileged ruling class. The working‑class deserves sympathy and respect, and should be allowed to support their families by reopening safely.  

END OF SUMMARY.
