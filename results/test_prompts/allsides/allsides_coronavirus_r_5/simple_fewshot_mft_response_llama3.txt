SUMMARY:

The article argues that reopening the economy is necessary to address the serious health consequences of prolonged lockdowns, including increased suicide rates, declining cancer screenings, and delayed childhood vaccinations. The author highlights the widening economic disparity between the rich and the poor, which has its own health implications. They emphasize that state and local officials need to make responsible decisions on how and when to reopen based on scientific data. The article also notes that many average Americans are struggling to support their families amid the pandemic, with little at stake for scientists, doctors, and political figures who are calling the shots.

The author's moral framing relies heavily on the dimensions of Care/Harm (emphasizing the harm caused by prolonged lockdowns) and Fairness/Cheating (arguing that average Americans are unfairly burdened while those in power have less at stake). The article also touches on Authority/Subversion, as it challenges the dominant narrative that reopening the economy is a health-versus-the-economy issue.

END OF SUMMARY.
