SUMMARY:

The article argues that the lockdown due to COVID-19 is not only a health-versus-economy issue, but also a health-versus-health issue. It highlights the serious health consequences of keeping the economy shut down, including increased suicide rates, delayed medical procedures, and declined cancer screenings. The author emphasizes that reopening the economy is crucial to mitigate these risks and ensure localities have the necessary resources. Statistics show that since the lockdown began, there has been a significant increase in substance abuse, missed cancer diagnoses, and calls to domestic violence hotlines. The article concludes that it's time to ease up restrictions and manage the virus prudently, allowing state and local officials to make responsible decisions based on scientific data. The moral imperative is to prioritize the well-being of average Americans who have everything at stake, rather than judgment from the ruling class.

END OF SUMMARY.
