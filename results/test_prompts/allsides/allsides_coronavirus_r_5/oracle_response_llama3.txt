SUMMARY:

We must reopen our economy, not just for economic reasons but also to mitigate the serious health consequences of keeping it shut down. The lockdown has led to spiking suicide rates, cardiac procedures not being received, cancer screenings declining, and children going without their routine vaccinations. Moreover, there are widening economic disparities between the rich and the poor, which have devastating health implications. We must ease restrictions and manage this virus prudently, allowing local officials to make responsible decisions based on scientific data. Many counties have no coronavirus deaths, and most cases come from just 2% of reported counties, making it safe to reopen in many areas. Americans are not asking for a one-size-fits-all approach but the choice to take risks safely and responsibly. Instead of judgment, we should show sympathy and respect for those who have everything at stake, from their personal health to their economic livelihoods. It's time to stop devastating our economy and nation's health and start reopening prudently.

END OF SUMMARY.
