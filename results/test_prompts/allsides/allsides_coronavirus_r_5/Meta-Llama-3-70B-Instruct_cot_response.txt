SUMMARY:
The lockdown is not just a health-versus-economy issue, but a health-versus-health issue. While there are risks associated with reopening the economy, the consequences of keeping it shut down are devastating. The lockdown has led to a spike in suicide rates, a decline in cancer screenings, and a widening economic disparity between the rich and the poor, which has its own health implications. The goal of "flattening the curve" was to ensure localities had the necessary resources, which they now do. It's time to ease up restrictions and manage the virus prudently. State and local officials need to make responsible decisions on how and when to reopen based on scientific data. Many average Americans are not getting paid and must continue to put food on their table and support their families amid the backdrop of this pandemic. They deserve respect and sympathy, not judgment, as they ask for the choice to take the risk of reopening safely and responsibly. END OF SUMMARY.
