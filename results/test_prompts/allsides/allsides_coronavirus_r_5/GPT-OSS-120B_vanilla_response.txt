SUMMARY: The article argues that COVID‑19 lockdowns create a “health‑versus‑health” dilemma, harming public health through increased mental‑health crises, delayed cancer screenings, missed vaccinations, domestic violence, and economic hardship that disproportionately affects low‑income Americans. It cites statistics on rising substance‑abuse hotline traffic, a 86‑94% drop in cancer screenings, projected missed cancer diagnoses, potential deaths from drug abuse and suicide, and a surge in domestic‑violence calls. The piece also highlights global poverty risks, noting UN forecasts of 130 million people facing starvation and 30 million children at risk of death. In the U.S., one‑fifth of workers lost jobs, with the poorest hardest hit, and research links economic inequality to poorer health outcomes. The author contends that hospitals now have sufficient capacity and testing to allow a cautious reopening, emphasizing that many counties have few or no COVID‑19 deaths and that a uniform lockdown is unnecessary. The article calls for state and local leaders to base reopening decisions on data, granting working‑class Americans the choice to resume work safely, and urges respect for those bearing the economic and health risks. Kelly Sadler, communications director for America First Policies, authored the piece. END OF SUMMARY.
