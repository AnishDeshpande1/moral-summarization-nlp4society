SUMMARY:

The lockdown is not just a health-versus-economy issue, but a health-versus-health issue. The consequences of keeping the economy shut down include spiking suicide rates, delayed cardiac procedures, declining cancer screenings, and widening economic disparity between the rich and poor. The goal of "flattening the curve" was to ensure localities had hospital beds and testing available, which is now the case. Continuing the lockdown would devastate the economy and nation's health. Statistics show a 1,000% increase in mental health hotline texts, 86-94% decrease in cancer screenings, and projected 80,000 missed cancer diagnoses. The lockdown has also led to economic devastation, with the ultra-poor being disproportionately affected. It's time to ease up restrictions and manage the virus prudently, with state and local officials making responsible decisions based on scientific data. The working class, who have the most at stake, deserve sympathy and respect for their desire to take calculated risks to support their families.

END OF SUMMARY.
