SUMMARY:

The COVID-19 lockdown is not only a health-versus-economy issue, but also a health-versus-health issue. The prolonged shutdown has severe health consequences, including spiking suicide rates, delayed cardiac procedures, and declining cancer screenings. Moreover, the economic disparity between the rich and poor has its own health implications. While there are risks associated with reopening the economy, the availability of hospital beds and testing can mitigate these risks. The goal of "flattening the curve" was to ensure localities had these resources, and now that they do, it's time to ease up restrictions and manage the virus prudently. State and local officials must make responsible decisions on how and when to reopen based on scientific data. The moral imperative is clear: prioritize the health and economic well-being of all Americans, particularly the working class, who have the most at stake.

END OF SUMMARY.
