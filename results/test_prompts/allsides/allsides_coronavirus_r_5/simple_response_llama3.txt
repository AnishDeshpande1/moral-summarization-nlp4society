SUMMARY:
The article argues that keeping the economy shut down due to COVID-19 is having serious health consequences, including increased suicide rates, delayed medical procedures, and declined cancer screenings. It also highlights the widening economic disparity between the rich and the poor, which has its own health implications. The author suggests that reopening the economy is necessary, as hospital beds and testing capabilities are now available to mitigate risks. They point out that the original goal of "flattening the curve" was to ensure localities had these resources, but new goals have been set without considering the economic toll. The article concludes that it's time to ease restrictions and reopen safely, with state and local officials making responsible decisions based on scientific data.
