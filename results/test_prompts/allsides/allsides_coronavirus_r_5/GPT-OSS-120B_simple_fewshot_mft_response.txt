SUMMARY:
The article argues that the COVID‑19 lockdown has become a “health‑versus‑health” dilemma, not merely a trade‑off between health and the economy. It condemns mainstream media and political elites for abusing authority and subverting ordinary Americans’ needs, while the real harms of shutdowns—rising suicides, missed cancer screenings, halted vaccinations, domestic‑violence spikes, and widening poverty‑driven health risks—are ignored. Citing statistics on mental‑health crises, delayed cancer diagnoses, drug‑related deaths and child‑starvation threats, the piece frames these outcomes as severe care violations that demand urgent redress. It also highlights stark fairness concerns: low‑income workers have suffered disproportionate job loss and health threats, whereas policymakers and many professionals remain insulated and can work from home. The author calls for responsible, data‑driven reopening policies that respect the dignity and safety of working‑class Americans, urging officials to stop imposing blanket restrictions and to treat the public with sympathy rather than judgment. The moral imperative is to balance care for all citizens while correcting the unfair, authoritarian handling of the crisis.  
END OF SUMMARY.
