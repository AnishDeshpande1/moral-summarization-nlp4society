SUMMARY:

The lockdown is not just a health-versus-economy issue, but a health-versus-health issue. The consequences of keeping the economy shut down are severe, with spiking suicide rates, declining cancer screenings, and missed vaccinations. The economic disparity between the rich and poor is also devastating, with the ultra-poor being pushed to the brink of starvation. The lockdown has led to a 1,000% increase in mental health hotline texts, and nearly 75,000 Americans could die from drug or alcohol abuse and suicide. It's time to ease up restrictions and manage the virus prudently, making responsible decisions based on scientific data. Many areas of the country are safe to reopen, and it's essential to respect the choices of working-class Americans who have everything at stake, from their personal health to their economic livelihoods. Instead of judgment, they deserve sympathy and respect.

END OF SUMMARY.
