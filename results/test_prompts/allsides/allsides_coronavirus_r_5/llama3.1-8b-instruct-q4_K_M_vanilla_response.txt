SUMMARY:

The author argues that the COVID-19 lockdown is not just a health-versus-economy issue, but also a health-versus-health issue due to the consequences of prolonged economic shutdowns. Citing statistics, they highlight the devastating effects on mental and physical health, including increased suicide rates, delayed cancer screenings, and missed vaccinations. The author also notes that the widening economic disparity between the rich and poor has its own health implications.

They argue that with hospital beds and testing available, it's time to ease up restrictions and reopen the economy prudently. They point out that many parts of the country are safe to reopen, and that state and local officials should make decisions based on scientific data rather than following one-size-fits-all policies.

The author emphasizes that reopening is not just about economic recovery, but also about preserving people's health and well-being, particularly for those who have lost their jobs or livelihoods. They call for sympathy and respect for workers who are taking risks to support their families during the pandemic.

END OF SUMMARY.
