SUMMARY:
The article argues that the COVID-19 lockdown is not just a health-versus-economy issue, but also a health-versus-health issue due to the serious consequences of keeping the economy shut down. It highlights statistics such as increased suicide rates, delayed cancer screenings, and reduced childhood vaccinations since the lockdown began. The article also points out that the economic devastation caused by the pandemic disproportionately affects low-income workers and can lead to worse health outcomes. It argues that it's time to reopen the economy prudently, taking into account scientific data and local conditions. Many areas have not reported a single COVID-19 death, and 62% of cases come from just 2% of counties. The article calls for state and local officials to make responsible decisions on reopening and for Americans to be given the choice to take risks safely and responsibly.

END OF SUMMARY.
