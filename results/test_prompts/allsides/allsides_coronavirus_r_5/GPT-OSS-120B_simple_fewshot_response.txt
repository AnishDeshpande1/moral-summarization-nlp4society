SUMMARY:
The article argues that keeping the U.S. economy shut is not merely an “economy‑versus‑health” dilemma but a “health‑versus‑health” crisis, because lockdowns are causing serious medical harms—rising suicide and substance‑abuse deaths, missed cancer screenings, delayed vaccinations, and increased domestic‑violence calls. It cites statistics showing dramatic drops in cancer screenings, projected missed cancer diagnoses, thousands of potential drug‑related deaths, and a surge in mental‑health crises. The piece also highlights the pandemic’s disproportionate economic devastation for low‑income Americans, linking poverty to poorer health outcomes and warning that hunger and starvation could kill more people than the virus itself. With hospital capacity and testing now available, the author contends that “flattening the curve” has succeeded and that continued stay‑at‑home orders are an unjustified extension of elite preferences. Since many counties have few or no COVID‑19 deaths, the author calls for data‑driven, localized reopening, urging respect and sympathy for working‑class Americans who must risk their health to support their families. The moral imperative presented is to balance the twin health threats by ending blanket lockdowns and reopening the economy responsibly.  
END OF SUMMARY.
