SUMMARY:

The lockdown has been framed as a health-versus-economy issue, but it's actually a health-versus-health issue. The consequences of keeping the economy shut down are severe, including spiking suicide rates, delayed cancer screenings, and widening economic disparity. The goal of "flattening the curve" was to ensure localities had hospital beds and testing available, which they now do. It's time to ease up restrictions and manage the virus prudently, prioritizing the health and economic well-being of Americans. The ruling class, who have less at stake, are calling the shots for the working class, while average Americans are asking for the choice to take calculated risks safely and responsibly. The moral imperative is clear: we must reopen the economy, respecting the autonomy of individuals to make decisions about their own health and livelihoods, while also addressing the health implications of economic inequality.

END OF SUMMARY.
