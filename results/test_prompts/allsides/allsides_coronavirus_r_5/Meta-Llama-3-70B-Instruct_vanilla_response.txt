SUMMARY:

The article argues that the coronavirus lockdown is not just a health-vs-economy issue, but also a health-vs-health issue. The lockdown has led to serious health consequences, including increased suicide rates, delayed cardiac procedures, decreased cancer screenings, and missed vaccinations. The economic disparity between the rich and poor has also worsened, with low-income workers being disproportionately affected. The article cites statistics showing the devastating impact of the lockdown on mental health, cancer diagnoses, and domestic violence. It also highlights the economic devastation, with the UN predicting that 130 million people could be pushed to the brink of starvation by the end of 2020. The author argues that it's time to ease up restrictions and manage the virus prudently, with state and local officials making responsible decisions based on scientific data. The article concludes that Americans want the choice to take calculated risks and reopen the economy safely and responsibly.
