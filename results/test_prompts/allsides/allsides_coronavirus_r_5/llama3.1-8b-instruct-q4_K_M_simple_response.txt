SUMMARY:

The author argues that the ongoing lockdown due to the coronavirus pandemic is having serious health consequences, particularly for vulnerable populations such as low-income workers. The closure of businesses and loss of income has led to increased rates of suicide, delayed medical procedures, and a decline in cancer screenings. Furthermore, the economic disparity between the rich and the poor is widening, which can have long-term health implications. The author suggests that reopening the economy with caution and based on scientific data can help mitigate these risks.

The author emphasizes that Americans want to take responsibility for their own risk-taking, rather than being dictated by politicians and medical experts who are largely unaffected by the economic consequences of lockdowns. The article highlights statistics showing the devastating impact of lockdowns on vulnerable populations, including increased substance abuse, domestic violence, and child vaccination rates.

END OF SUMMARY.
