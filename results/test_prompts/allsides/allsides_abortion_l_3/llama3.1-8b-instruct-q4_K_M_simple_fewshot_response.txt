SUMMARY:

The Trump administration's "domestic gag rule" bars clinics that provide or refer patients for abortions from getting federal family planning funds under Title X. This move, which was finalized in March and blocked by a court injunction on July 3, would leave low-income patients without access to vital health services. The rule requires providers to be physically and financially separate from any entity that provides or refers for abortions, effectively barring Planned Parenthood affiliates from getting Title X funding. Abortion opponents have applauded the move, while reproductive rights groups argue it will harm vulnerable populations. Critics also claim the rule violates laws requiring unbiased counseling about all options, including abortion. The Trump administration's actions on Title X are part of a broader effort to restrict access to contraception and abortion. The rule is seen as a strategy to boost support among conservatives ahead of the 2020 elections.
