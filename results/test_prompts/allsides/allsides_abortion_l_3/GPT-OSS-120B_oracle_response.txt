SUMMARY:  
In March the Trump administration **issued** a final **rule** that **bars** any clinic that performs or **refers** abortions from receiving federal Title X family‑planning funds. The “domestic gag **rule**” **restricts** funding to providers that are **separated** from abortion services, effectively **blocking** Planned Parenthood and similar **providers** from the program. Reproductive‑rights groups and several states quickly **challenged** the **rule**, arguing it would **leave** low‑income patients without essential **care** and **violates** legal requirements for unbiased **counseling**. On July 3 the Ninth Circuit **issued** a preliminary **injunction**, **ensuring** the **rule** stays **blocked** while the lawsuits proceed. Advocates hail the decision as a **victory** in the **fight** to **upend** the administration’s attempt to **disentangle** taxpayers from the “abortion industry” and to **stop** the **restriction** of contraceptive services. Critics warn the **rule** will **shrink** the network of Title X **providers**, creating serious **repercussions** for millions who **receive** care, and will **limit** access to contraception. The case reflects the broader **action** and **rhetoric** of the Trump administration to **allow** exemptions and **requirements** that shape abortion policy.  

END OF SUMMARY.
