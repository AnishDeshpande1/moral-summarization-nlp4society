SUMMARY:

The Trump administration's "domestic gag rule" has been blocked by the US Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit, preventing clinics that provide or refer patients for abortions from receiving federal family planning funds. The rule, which was finalized in March, would have barred Planned Parenthood and other providers from receiving funding under Title X, which pays for birth control and other family planning services for low-income patients. Anti-abortion groups have applauded the rule, arguing that organizations that provide abortion should not receive federal support. However, reproductive rights advocates argue that the rule would leave low-income patients without access to health care they need. The rule is part of a broader effort by the Trump administration to limit access to contraception and abortion, and comes as the president ramps up his rhetoric on abortion, which he may see as a winning issue for him and the Republican Party in 2020.

END OF SUMMARY.
