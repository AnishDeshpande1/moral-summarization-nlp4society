SUMMARY:
The Trump administration's "domestic gag rule" bars clinics that provide or refer patients for abortions from getting federal family planning funds, a move that critics say will limit access to contraception and abortion. The rule is considered unconscionable and unethical by Planned Parenthood, as it compromises the oath to serve patients and help them make the best decision for their own health. The administration's decisive action to disentangle taxpayers from the abortion industry has been applauded by anti-abortion groups, but opponents argue it will leave low-income patients without access to vital health services. The rule is also considered illegal, as it violates laws requiring unbiased counseling about all options, including abortion. The move is seen as a winning strategy for the president going into 2020, but opponents will continue to fight against the rule. END OF SUMMARY.
