SUMMARY:

The Trump administration's "domestic gag rule" bars clinics that provide or refer patients for abortions from receiving federal family planning funds under Title X. Planned Parenthood, which sees about 41% of patients who get family planning services under Title X, is among those affected. The rule requires providers to be physically and financially separate from any entity that provides or refers for abortions. This would bar all Planned Parenthood centers from getting Title X funding.

The rule has been challenged in court by state officials and reproductive rights groups, with a recent preliminary injunction blocking its implementation while legal challenges proceed. Critics argue the rule will leave low-income patients without access to health care they need. Abortion opponents have praised the rule as disentangling taxpayers from the "big abortion industry." The Trump administration's actions on Title X are part of a broader effort to limit access to contraception and abortion.

END OF SUMMARY.
