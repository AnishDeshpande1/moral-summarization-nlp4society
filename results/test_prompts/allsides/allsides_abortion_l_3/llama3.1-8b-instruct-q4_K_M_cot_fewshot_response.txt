SUMMARY:
The Trump administration has released a final rule barring clinics that provide or refer patients for abortions from getting federal family planning funds. The "domestic gag rule" would require providers to be both physically and financially separate from any entity that provides or refers for abortions, effectively locking out Planned Parenthood and other organizations that perform abortions. Critics argue that the rule is unconscionable and unethical, as it will leave many low-income patients without access to vital health services. The rule also violates laws requiring unbiased counseling about all options, including abortion. If implemented, the proposed changes would shrink the network of participating providers and have major repercussions for low-income women across the country who rely on them for family planning care. The Trump administration's move is seen as a winning strategy in its opposition to abortion going into 2020. END OF SUMMARY.
