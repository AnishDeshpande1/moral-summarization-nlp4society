The Trump administration’s final “domestic gag rule” bars any clinic that performs abortions or refers patients for abortions—most notably Planned Parenthood—from receiving Title X federal family‑planning funds. Opponents argue the rule is **unconscionable** and **unethical**, claiming it will strip **vital** health services from **low‑income** patients who rely on the nation’s only family‑planning program. Legal challenges from Planned Parenthood, the ACLU, the AMA and several states have kept the rule from taking effect; the Ninth Circuit’s July 3 injunction was hailed as a **victory** by reproductive‑rights attorneys. Supporters, including anti‑abortion groups, praised the **decisive** action as a **winning** step to keep taxpayer money from the “big abortion industry.” The dispute underscores a broader Trump strategy to limit contraception and abortion access ahead of the 2020 election, pitting **unbiased** counseling rights against politically driven restrictions.
