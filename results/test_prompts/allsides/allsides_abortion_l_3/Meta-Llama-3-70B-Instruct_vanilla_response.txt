SUMMARY:

The Trump administration's "domestic gag rule" has been blocked by a preliminary injunction issued by the US Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit. The rule, which was set to take effect, would have barred clinics that provide or refer patients for abortions from receiving federal family planning funds under Title X. The rule was challenged in court by state officials and reproductive rights groups, who argue that it would leave low-income patients without access to healthcare services. Planned Parenthood, which sees 41% of patients who receive family planning services under Title X, would be heavily impacted by the rule. Anti-abortion groups have backed the rule, arguing that organizations that provide abortion should not receive federal support. The ruling is seen as a victory for reproductive rights advocates, who will continue to fight to block the rule permanently.

END OF SUMMARY.
