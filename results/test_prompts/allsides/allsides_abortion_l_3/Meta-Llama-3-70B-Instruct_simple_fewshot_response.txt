SUMMARY:

The Trump administration's "domestic gag rule" bars clinics that provide or refer patients for abortions from receiving federal family planning funds under Title X. The rule, which has been challenged in court, would require providers to be physically and financially separate from any entity that provides or refers for abortions. This means that Planned Parenthood and other providers that perform abortions would be barred from receiving Title X funding, leaving low-income patients without access to health care they need. Anti-abortion groups have applauded the rule, while reproductive rights advocates argue that it is illegal and will harm patients. The rule is part of a broader effort by the Trump administration to limit access to contraception and abortion, and is seen as a key issue for the president and the Republican Party in 2020. This morally reprehensible move would essentially deny vulnerable populations access to essential health services, and is a blatant attack on reproductive rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
