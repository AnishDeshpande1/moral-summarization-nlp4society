SUMMARY:
The Trump administration's "domestic gag rule" has been blocked by a preliminary injunction, allowing low-income patients to continue receiving vital health services from providers like Planned Parenthood. The rule, which bars clinics that provide or refer patients for abortions from getting federal family planning funds, has been met with legal challenges from reproductive rights groups and state officials. Anti-abortion groups have applauded the rule, arguing that organizations that provide abortion should not receive federal support. However, critics argue that the rule will leave many low-income patients without access to contraceptive care and is an "unconscionable and unethical" attempt to limit access to abortion. The move is part of a larger effort by the Trump administration to restrict access to contraception and abortion, which the president may see as a "winning" issue for him and the Republican Party in 2020.
