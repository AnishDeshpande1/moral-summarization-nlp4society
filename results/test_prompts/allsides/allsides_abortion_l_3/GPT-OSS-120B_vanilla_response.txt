SUMMARY: The Trump administration’s final rule, dubbed the “domestic gag rule,” bars any clinic that performs or refers abortions—including Planned Parenthood—from receiving federal Title X family‑planning funds. The rule, intended to separate Title X providers from abortion services, would eliminate funding for many low‑income patients who rely on these clinics for contraception and other care. Planned Parenthood, the AMA, NFPRHA, ACLU and several states sued, securing temporary injunctions; the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals issued a new preliminary injunction on July 3, halting the rule while litigation proceeds. Advocates argue the rule would cripple access to reproductive health services, noting Planned Parenthood serves about 41% of Title X patients and is often the sole provider in many counties. Anti‑abortion groups praise the rule as cutting taxpayer support for “the abortion industry.” The controversy reflects broader Trump‑era actions limiting contraception and abortion access, amid rising political focus on the issue ahead of the 2020 election. END OF SUMMARY.
