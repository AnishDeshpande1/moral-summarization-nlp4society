SUMMARY:
The Trump administration’s final “domestic gag rule” on Title X seeks to cut off federal family‑planning funds from any clinic that performs or refers abortions, effectively barring Planned Parenthood – which serves about 41 % of low‑income patients – from the nation’s only program that provides birth‑control and other essential health services.  Critics argue the rule is a morally indefensible power grab that will leave millions of poor women without affordable contraception, forcing them to rely on an overstretched network of providers.  The Ninth Circuit’s July 3 preliminary injunction is hailed as a vital victory for reproductive‑rights advocates, who say the rule violates patients’ right to unbiased counseling and jeopardizes public health.  Pro‑life groups applaud the move as a necessary step to keep taxpayer money from “the abortion industry,” but opponents warn it will cripple access to care for the most vulnerable, undermining the oath of medical professionals to serve patients’ health needs.  The legal battle continues, reflecting a broader Trump strategy to weaponize abortion policy for political gain.

END OF SUMMARY.
