SUMMARY:

Rex Tillerson, the Secretary of State nominee, faced a contentious confirmation hearing before the Senate Foreign Relations Committee. Sen. Marco Rubio clashed with Tillerson over his views on Russian President Vladimir Putin, with Rubio pressing for him to brand Putin a "war criminal." However, Tillerson refused to do so, instead describing Russia as an "unfriendly adversary" and stating that it poses a danger but is not unpredictable in advancing its own interests.

Tillerson also addressed other areas of concern, including China's building of man-made islands in the South China Sea and radical Islam. He stated that the US currently has too many competing goals in Syria and needs to focus on defeating ISIS before discussing who should lead the country.

The hearing was marked by tension and levity, with Rubio accusing Tillerson of being "cool" under fire and Tillerson joking about his lack of knowledge on ExxonMobil's climate change policies.

END OF SUMMARY.
