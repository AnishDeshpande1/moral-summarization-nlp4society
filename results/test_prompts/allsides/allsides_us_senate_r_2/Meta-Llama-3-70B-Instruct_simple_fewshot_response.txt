SUMMARY:

Secretary of State nominee Rex Tillerson clashed with Sen. Marco Rubio during his confirmation hearing, refusing to brand Russian President Vladimir Putin a "war criminal" and instead taking a more deliberative approach. Rubio pressed Tillerson on policy regarding Russia, Cuba, Saudi Arabia, and the Philippines, but Tillerson maintained a cool demeanor. While Tillerson wouldn't categorize Putin as a "war criminal," he did take a harder line towards Russia than President-elect Donald Trump has, calling Russia an "unfriendly adversary." Tillerson also addressed other areas, including China's actions in the South China Sea, radical Islam, and Syria. The hearing was marked by tense exchanges and protests from the audience. The moral framing of this issue is clear: Tillerson's refusal to condemn Putin and his more measured approach to international relations are a departure from Rubio's more hawkish stance, and raise questions about Tillerson's ability to lead the State Department.

END OF SUMMARY.
