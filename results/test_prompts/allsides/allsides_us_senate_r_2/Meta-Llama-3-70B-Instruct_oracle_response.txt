SUMMARY:

Secretary of State nominee Rex Tillerson faced a contentious confirmation hearing, where he clashed with Sen. Marco Rubio over policy towards Russia, Cuba, Saudi Arabia, and the Philippines. Rubio pressed Tillerson to label Russian President Vladimir Putin a "war criminal," but Tillerson refused, citing a more deliberative approach. Tillerson acknowledged that Russia "poses a danger" and is an "unfriendly adversary," but stopped short of calling it an enemy. He criticized the Obama administration's response to Russia's annexation of Crimea, calling it a "taking of territory that was not theirs" and a "weak response." Tillerson also addressed China's "illegal" building of man-made islands and the threat of radical Islam, which he said "poses a grave risk to the stability of nations." He emphasized the need to "serve" American interests and warned that the US risks "plunging the world deeper into confusion and danger" if it doesn't lead. Tillerson's testimony was marked by a sense of realism and caution, as he sought to allay concerns about his approach to international relations.

END OF SUMMARY.
