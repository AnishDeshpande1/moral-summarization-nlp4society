SUMMARY:  
In a sharply moralized hearing, Senator Marco Rubio pressed Secretary‑of‑State‑designate Rex Tillerson to brand Russian President Vladimir Putin a “war criminal” and to condemn Saudi Arabia and the Philippines as human‑rights violators. Tillerson, measured but defiant, refused to use the demanded labels, instead emphasizing a realistic, “clear‑eyed” view of foreign cultures and warning that Russia, while dangerous, is predictable in pursuing its own interests. He called Russia an “unfriendly adversary,” condemned its annexation of Crimea, and criticized the Obama administration’s weak response. Tillerson also denounced China’s illegal island building, radical Islam, and highlighted cyber‑intrusion as the greatest U.S. threat, while urging decisive American leadership to avoid global chaos. The hearing mixed serious moral questioning with moments of levity, such as a climate‑policy quip with Sen. Tim Kaine, and was punctuated by colorful protests—including Code Pink activists and a T‑rex costume mocking Tillerson’s name. Throughout, Tillerson framed his answers in terms of patriotism and the duty to serve American interests above all.  
END OF SUMMARY.
