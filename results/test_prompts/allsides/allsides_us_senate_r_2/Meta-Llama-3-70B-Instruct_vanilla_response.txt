SUMMARY:

Rex Tillerson, Secretary of State nominee, clashed with Sen. Marco Rubio during his confirmation hearing, refusing to label Russian President Vladimir Putin a "war criminal". Tillerson took a harder line towards Russia than President-elect Donald Trump, calling it an "unfriendly adversary" and stating that it poses a danger to the US. Rubio pressed Tillerson on policy regarding Russia, Cuba, Saudi Arabia, and the Philippines, but Tillerson maintained a more deliberative approach. Tillerson also addressed other global issues, including China's actions in the South China Sea, radical Islam, and the need to focus on defeating ISIS in Syria. The hearing was tense, with protesters in attendance, but Tillerson remained calm and composed throughout.

END OF SUMMARY.
