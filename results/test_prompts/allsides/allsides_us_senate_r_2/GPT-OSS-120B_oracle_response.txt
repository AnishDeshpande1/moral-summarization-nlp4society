SUMMARY:  
At a tense Senate Foreign Relations Committee hearing, Secretary‑of‑State nominee Rex Tillerson and Sen. Marco Rubio clashed over Russia, Cuba, Saudi Arabia and the Philippines. Rubio pressed Tillerson to label President Vladimir Putin a “war criminal” and to brand Saudi Arabia a “human‑rights violator,” but Tillerson stopped short, describing Russia as an “unfriendly adversary” that “poses a danger” while noting it is “advancing its own interests.” He warned that the “absence of a firm response” to Russia’s annexation of Crimea encouraged further aggression and that a weak U.S. “response” would risk “plunging” the world into confusion. Tillerson also called China’s island “building,” the rise of radical Islam a “risk,” and ISIS an “imminent threat” that must be “defeated,” while labeling cyber‑intrusions the “greatest and most complex threat.” He admitted the Obama administration “stumbled” and urged America to “serve” its people, lest it “take” a back‑seat and “advance” danger. Protesters were ejected for disrupting the hearing.  
END OF SUMMARY.
