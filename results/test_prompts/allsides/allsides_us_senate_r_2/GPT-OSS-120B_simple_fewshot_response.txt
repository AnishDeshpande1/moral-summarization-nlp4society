SUMMARY:
During the Senate Foreign Relations Committee hearing, Secretary‑of‑State nominee Rex Tillerson faced an aggressive line of questioning from Sen. Marco Rubio, who pressed him to label Russian President Vladimir Putin a “war criminal” and to brand Saudi Arabia and the Philippines as human‑rights violators. Tillerson calmly deflected, refusing to use the demanded labels while emphasizing a realistic, “clear‑eyed” approach to foreign policy. He warned that Russia is a “dangerous, unfriendly adversary” and criticized the Obama administration’s weak response to Crimea, yet stopped short of calling Moscow an enemy. Tillerson also condemned China’s illegal island building, highlighted cyber threats and ISIS, and acknowledged U.S. missteps, urging decisive leadership. The hearing featured moments of levity and protest, underscoring the tension between Rubio’s confrontational stance and Tillerson’s measured, principled diplomacy. The moral framing suggests that responsible statesmanship requires sober judgment over partisan grandstanding.  
END OF SUMMARY.
