SUMMARY:

Rex Tillerson, Secretary of State nominee, faced tough questioning from Sen. Marco Rubio during his confirmation hearing before the Senate Foreign Relations Committee. Rubio pressed Tillerson to label Russian President Vladimir Putin as a "war criminal," but Tillerson refused, saying he was "clear-eyed and realistic" about dealing with cultures. Despite this, Tillerson took a harder line towards Russia than President-elect Donald Trump has done, calling it an "unfriendly adversary." He also addressed other areas, including China's building of man-made islands in the South China Sea and radical Islam, which he said posed a significant threat to global stability.

Tillerson defended his business ties to Russia, saying that his love for country would guide him to serve only American interests. The hearing was marked by tense exchanges between Tillerson and Rubio, but also included moments of levity when Sen. Tim Kaine asked about ExxonMobil's climate change policies and Tillerson responded with humor.

END OF SUMMARY.
