SUMMARY:

Rex Tillerson, Secretary of State nominee, faced a contentious exchange with Sen. Marco Rubio during his confirmation hearing. Rubio pressed Tillerson to brand Russian President Vladimir Putin a "war criminal," but Tillerson refused, stating that Russia poses a danger and is not unpredictable in advancing its own interests. He also acknowledged that the Obama administration's response to Crimea was weak, allowing Russia to take territory without consequence.

Tillerson's testimony highlighted his more deliberative approach to diplomacy compared to President-elect Donald Trump's statements on Russia. He called for defeating ISIS as the primary goal in Syria and named cyber intrusions as a grave threat facing the US. Tillerson also acknowledged that the US government had "stumbled" under the Obama administration, and emphasized the need for a strong response to prevent plunging the world into danger.

The hearing was marked by tense exchanges between Rubio and Tillerson, but ultimately ended with Tillerson's assertion of his commitment to serving the interests of the American people. END OF SUMMARY
