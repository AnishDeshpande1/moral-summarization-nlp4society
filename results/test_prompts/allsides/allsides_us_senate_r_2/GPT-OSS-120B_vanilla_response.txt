SUMMARY:  
At a Senate Foreign Relations Committee hearing, Secretary‑of‑State nominee Rex Tillerson faced aggressive questioning from Sen. Marco Rubio, who pressed him to label Russian President Vladimir Putin a “war criminal,” to condemn Saudi Arabia as a “human‑rights violator,” and to denounce Philippine leader Rodrigo Duterte. Tillerson declined to use those specific terms, instead describing Russia as an “unfriendly adversary” and warning that NATO allies are justified in being alarmed by a resurgent Russia. He asserted that Crimea’s annexation was illegal and criticized the Obama administration’s weak response. Tillerson also highlighted threats from China’s island building, radical Islam, ISIS, and cyber intrusions, and called for clearer U.S. leadership. The hearing touched on his ties to Russia, ExxonMobil’s climate policies, and Trump’s Twitter use, with moments of levity and protests outside, including Code Pink and T‑rex costumed demonstrators.  

END OF SUMMARY.
