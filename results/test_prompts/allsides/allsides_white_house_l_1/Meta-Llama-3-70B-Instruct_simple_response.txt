SUMMARY:

Retired Marine Gen. John Kelly, outgoing chief of staff to President Donald Trump, embodies the ideology of the Trump era, including casual cruelty and tolerance for bigotry. Despite his more disciplined approach, Kelly shares Trump's worldview, as seen in his defense of Confederate Gen. Robert E. Lee, his disdain for black female critics, and his support for Rob Porter, a senior aide accused of domestic abuse. Kelly's indifference to the allegations against Porter and his role in architecting the administration's "zero tolerance" policy towards immigrants, which led to child separation, demonstrate his commitment to Trump's ideology. The article argues that Kelly's tenure shows that the appearance of moderation is not moderation, and that the ethos of the administration flows from the top. The lesson from Kelly's tenure is that a more disciplined Trump would be a more effective one, and that the administration's cruelty and contempt for common decency are inherent to its ideology.

END OF SUMMARY.
