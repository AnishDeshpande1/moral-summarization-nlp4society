SUMMARY:

Retired Marine Gen. John Kelly, outgoing chief of staff to President Donald Trump, has served for nearly two years, leaving the White House as chaotic and disordered as when he took the job. Kelly's commitment to Trump's ideology, including casual cruelty and tolerance for bigotry, has been on full display. He demonstrated disdain for black female critics, refused to apologize, and praised Confederate Gen. Robert E. Lee as an "honorable man." Kelly's response to allegations of domestic abuse against Rob Porter was indifferent, calling him a "man of true integrity and honor." He also defended the administration's "zero tolerance" policy, which led to child separation, showing a blatant disregard for human life. Kelly's tenure has been marked by chaos, impulse, and a display of contempt for common decency. His departure serves as a reminder that the appearance of moderation is not moderation, and that the ethos of the administration flows inexorably from the man at the top.

END OF SUMMARY.
