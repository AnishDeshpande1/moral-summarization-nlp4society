SUMMARY:
Retired Marine Gen. John Kelly is leaving his post as President Trump’s chief of staff, but his exit does not erase the way he reinforced the administration’s abusive ethos. Rather than subverting Trump’s authoritarian impulses, Kelly amplified them—defending the “zero‑tolerance” family‑separation policy, praising Confederate General Lee, and protecting a domestic‑abuse accused aide. His attacks on Black Congresswoman Frederica Wilson and his refusal to apologize reveal a pattern of disrespect toward marginalized voices, betraying a fairness principle and harming those who speak out. By endorsing policies that caused children’s trauma and by excusing bigotry, Kelly helped legitimize cruelty and unequal treatment, showing that his “moderate” façade concealed complicity in the administration’s harmful, unfair agenda. The article argues that Kelly’s failure to truly curb Trump’s authoritarianism demonstrates that an appearance of order cannot substitute for genuine moral authority; the administration’s core values—authoritarian subversion, neglect of care, and systemic unfairness—remained intact under his watch. END OF SUMMARY.
