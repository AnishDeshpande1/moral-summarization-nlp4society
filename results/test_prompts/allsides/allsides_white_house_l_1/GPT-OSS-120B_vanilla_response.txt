SUMMARY: Retired Marine Gen. John Kelly, who served as President Trump’s chief of staff for nearly two years, announced he will leave the White House by year‑end. While Kelly presented himself as a stabilizing “adult in the room,” the article argues he embodied and reinforced core Trumpist ideologies—casual cruelty, tolerance of bigotry, and indifference to misconduct. Examples include his hostile exchange with Black Congresswoman Frederica Wilson, historically revisionist remarks praising Confederate leaders, unwavering defense of aide Rob Porter amid domestic‑abuse allegations, and his role in designing the “zero‑tolerance” family‑separation policy, dismissing its moral impact. The piece contends Kelly’s disciplined demeanor did not curb Trump’s agenda; instead, it lent credibility to it, suggesting that a more orderly administration could have been even more effective at implementing ethnonationalist policies. The key lesson is that superficial moderation cannot mask an administration’s underlying ethos, which is set by the president himself. END OF SUMMARY.
