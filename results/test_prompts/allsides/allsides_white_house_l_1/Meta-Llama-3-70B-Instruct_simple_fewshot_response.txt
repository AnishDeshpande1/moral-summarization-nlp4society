SUMMARY:

Retired Marine Gen. John Kelly, President Trump's chief of staff, announced his departure from the White House by the end of the year. While Kelly may have brought a sense of normalcy and temperance to the administration, he shares Trump's ideology, including a tolerance for bigotry and cruelty. This is evident in his past comments, such as blaming the Civil War on a "lack of ability to compromise" and praising Confederate Gen. Robert E. Lee. Kelly also showed disdain for black female critics of the administration and backed a senior aide accused of domestic abuse. Most notably, he was an architect of the administration's "zero tolerance" policy towards immigrants, which led to child separation. Kelly's actions demonstrate that even a more disciplined Trump administration would still be driven by a cruel and bigoted ideology. The lesson from Kelly's tenure is that the appearance of moderation is not moderation, and what matters is the administration's ethos, which flows from the top.
