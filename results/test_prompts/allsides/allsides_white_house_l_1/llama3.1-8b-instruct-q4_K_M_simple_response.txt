SUMMARY:

John Kelly's tenure as White House chief of staff is coming to an end, but his legacy will not be one of moderation or change. Despite his attempt to bring normalcy and temperance to the administration, Kelly shares President Trump's basic worldview, including a commitment to casual cruelty and tolerance for bigotry. He has shown disdain for black female critics, praised Confederate Gen. Robert E. Lee, and backed a senior aide accused of domestic abuse. Kelly was also an architect of the "zero tolerance" policy that led to child separation at the border, demonstrating his willingness to dehumanize an entire class of people. The author argues that Kelly's failure to control Trump is actually beneficial, as it has exposed the administration's true ethos and shown that the appearance of moderation is not truly moderate. Ultimately, what matters is the man at the top and the ethos he sets for the administration.

END OF SUMMARY.
