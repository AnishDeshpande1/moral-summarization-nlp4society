SUMMARY:
Retired Marine Gen. John Kelly announced he will leave his post as President Trump’s chief of staff by year‑end, exiting a White House that remains chaotic and morally compromised. While Kelly may have seemed a temper‑moderate “adult in the room,” the article argues he was in fact a faithful conduit of Trump’s ideology—tolerating cruelty, bigotry and bad behavior. It cites his hostile exchange with Black Rep. Frederica Wilson, his nostalgic praise of Confederate General Robert E. Lee soon after the Charlottesville “Unite the Right” rally, his defense of abusive aide Rob Porter, and his role in designing the “zero‑tolerance” family‑separation policy, shrugging off its humanitarian horror. The piece concludes that Kelly’s apparent moderation was a façade; his alignment with Trump’s ethnonationalist worldview meant the administration’s cruelty persisted, and a disciplined Trump could have been even more dangerous. The lesson offered is that the ethos of an administration derives from its leader, not from a superficially restrained chief of staff.  
END OF SUMMARY.
