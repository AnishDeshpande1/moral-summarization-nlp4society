SUMMARY:

John Kelly, Chief of Staff to President Donald Trump, announced his resignation on November 10. Despite being seen as a stabilizing force in the White House, Kelly's tenure was marked by controversy and alignment with Trump's ideology. He showed a commitment to "Trumpism" through his casual cruelty and tolerance for bigotry, evident in his spat with Rep. Frederica Wilson and his praise of Confederate Gen. Robert E. Lee. Kelly also defended Trump's policies, including the separation of children from their mothers at the border, calling it a necessary measure that would result in the children being "taken care of." The article argues that Kelly's moderation was merely an appearance, and that he shared the same worldview as Trump. In fact, the author suggests that Kelly's inability to control Trump may have been beneficial, as it prevented a more effective and ruthless implementation of Trump's policies.

END OF SUMMARY.
