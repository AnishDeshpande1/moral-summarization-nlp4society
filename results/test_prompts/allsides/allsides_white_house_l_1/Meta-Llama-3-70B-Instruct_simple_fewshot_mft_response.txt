SUMMARY:

Retired Marine Gen. John Kelly, President Trump's chief of staff for nearly two years, has announced his departure from the White House by the end of the year. While Kelly may have brought a sense of normalcy and temperance to the administration, he shares Trump's basic worldview, which is marked by casual cruelty, tolerance for bigotry, and disrespect for critics. Kelly's tenure has been marred by controversies, including his spat with Rep. Frederica Wilson, his praise for Confederate Gen. Robert E. Lee, and his defense of senior aide Rob Porter, who was accused of domestic abuse. Most notably, Kelly was an architect of the administration's "zero tolerance" policy toward immigrants, which led to the separation of children from their parents. Kelly's actions and words have revealed a commitment to the president's ideology, which prioritizes harm and cruelty over care and compassion. The lesson from Kelly's tenure is that the appearance of moderation is not moderation, and that the ethos of the administration flows from the top down.

END OF SUMMARY.
