SUMMARY:

Retired Marine Gen. John Kelly, President Donald Trump's chief of staff, has announced his departure from the White House by the end of the year. Despite his efforts to bring normalcy and temperance to the administration, Kelly shares Trump's basic worldview, which includes casual cruelty, tolerance for bigotry, and disrespect for critics. Examples of this include his spat with Rep. Frederica Wilson, where he accused her of taking false credit for an FBI field office, and his comments on the Civil War, where he blamed the lack of compromise and praised Confederate Gen. Robert E. Lee. Kelly also defended senior aide Rob Porter against domestic abuse allegations and was an architect of the administration's "zero tolerance" policy towards immigrants, which led to child separation. The article argues that Kelly's tenure shows that the appearance of moderation is not moderation, and what matters is the ethos of the administration, which flows from the president.

END OF SUMMARY.
