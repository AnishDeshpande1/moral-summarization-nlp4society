SUMMARY:

A bipartisan resolution has been introduced in the House of Representatives, condemning "blasphemy" laws that punish religious dissent, including blasphemy, heresy, and apostasy. The resolution, sponsored by Rep. Jamie Raskin (D-MD) and Rep. Mark Meadows (R-NC), calls on over 70 governments to repeal such laws, which often target marginalized religious communities. The resolution cites alarming reports of religious freedom restrictions, including Russia's ban on Jehovah's Witnesses, China's detention of Uighur Muslims, and Pakistan's use of blasphemy laws. The U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom has cheered the resolution, urging the release of prisoners held on blasphemy charges. The resolution also notes countries that have repealed blasphemy laws, such as Ireland, Greece, and Canada. If passed, the resolution would add blasphemy, apostasy, and heresy as reasons for nations to be designated as "countries of particular concern" for religious freedom.

END OF SUMMARY.
