SUMMARY:
A bipartisan resolution has been introduced in the US House of Representatives, condemning "blasphemy" laws that punish religious dissent, including heresy and apostasy. The resolution, backed by religious liberty advocates, urges over 70 governments to repeal such laws, which "inappropriately position governments as arbiters of religious truth" and empower officials to impose religious dogma on individuals or minorities through violence. The resolution cites alarming examples of religious freedom restrictions, including Russia's ban on Jehovah's Witnesses, China's detention of Uighur Muslims, and Pakistan's use of wide-ranging blasphemy laws. The US Commission on International Religious Freedom has cheered the resolution, saying such laws often target marginalized religious communities and legitimize egregious acts of persecution and violence. The resolution calls for the release of all prisoners held on blasphemy, heresy, or apostasy charges. END OF SUMMARY.
