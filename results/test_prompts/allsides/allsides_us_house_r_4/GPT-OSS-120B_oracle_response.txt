SUMMARY:  
A bipartisan House resolution introduced by Rep. Jamie Raskin (D‑MD) and Rep. Mark Meadows (R‑NC) condemns laws that **punish** religious dissent and **empower** governments to **impose** religious dogma through state‑backed **violence**. The measure calls on more than 70 nations to repeal **blasphemy**, **heresy**, and **apostasy** statutes that **ban** or **detain** believers, noting how such laws are **used** and **weaponized** to **target** minorities. It cites Russia’s ban on Jehovah’s Witnesses, China’s mass **detention** of Uighur Muslims, and Pakistan’s extensive **blasphemy** provisions that have left dozens on death row. The resolution urges the release of all individuals **held** on these charges and urges the U.S. to add blasphemy‑related persecution to the “countries of particular concern” list. It also highlights nations that have **repealed** such statutes, including Ireland, Greece, and Canada, praising their steps to **repeal** criminalized **blasphemous** speech. Religious‑freedom advocates welcome the effort, arguing that these laws **legitimize** persecution and must be **repealed**.  

END OF SUMMARY.
