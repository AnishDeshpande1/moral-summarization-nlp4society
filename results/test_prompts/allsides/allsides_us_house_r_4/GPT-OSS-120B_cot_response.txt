**SUMMARY:**  
In House Resolution 512, Democrat Rep. Jamie Raskin and Republican Rep. Mark Meadows jointly **condemn** “blasphemy,” **heresy**, and **apostasy** laws that allow governments to act as arbiters of religious **truth** and to **weaponize** state power against **marginalized** believers. The bipartisan measure urges more than 70 nations to repeal statutes that criminalize religious dissent, citing **extremist** bans on Jehovah’s Witnesses in Russia, the mass **persecution** of Uighur Muslims in China, and Pakistan’s **death‑row** sentences for **blasphemy**. Supporters, including the U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom, argue these laws foster **violence**, **oppression**, and **egregious** human‑rights abuses. The resolution aligns with UN covenants protecting **religious‑rights** and would expand the list of “countries of particular concern” to include nations that maintain such statutes. It also highlights positive steps by Ireland, Greece, and Canada in repealing similar laws, framing the effort as a defense of **freedom** and **liberty** worldwide.
