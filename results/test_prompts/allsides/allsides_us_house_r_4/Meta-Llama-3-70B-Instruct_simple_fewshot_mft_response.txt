SUMMARY:

Two congressmen, Rep. Jamie Raskin (D-MD) and Rep. Mark Meadows (R-NC), have introduced a bipartisan resolution condemning "blasphemy" laws, which punish religious dissent and empower governments to impose religious dogma on individuals or minorities. The resolution recognizes that such laws inappropriately position governments as arbiters of religious truth and lead to violence and persecution. The move is cheered by religious liberty advocates, who highlight the alarming rise of religious freedom restrictions globally. The resolution cites examples of Russia's ban on Jehovah's Witnesses, China's detention of Uighur Muslims, and Pakistan's use of blasphemy laws, which have led to the imprisonment of nearly a million individuals. The U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom has endorsed the resolution, calling for the release of prisoners held on blasphemy, heresy, or apostasy charges. The resolution aims to raise awareness of the egregious acts of persecution and violence legitimized by these laws, and to promote religious freedom and expression.

END OF SUMMARY.
