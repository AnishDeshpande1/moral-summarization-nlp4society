SUMMARY:

Two US congressman have introduced a bipartisan resolution condemning "blasphemy" laws, which punish individuals for expressing dissenting religious views. Rep. Jamie Raskin and Mark Meadows call on over 70 governments to repeal these laws, citing the use of violence sanctioned by the government to target marginalized communities. The resolution notes that blasphemy laws empower officials to impose their own dogma on individuals or minorities through the power of the government. It also highlights the detention of nearly a million Uighur Muslims in China and Pakistan's wide-ranging blasphemy laws. The US Commission on International Religious Freedom praises the resolution, stating that such laws are often weaponized to legitimize persecution and violence against those who hold different beliefs.

The resolution would add blasphemy, apostasy, and heresy as reasons for nations to be designated as "countries of particular concern" for religious freedom under the 1998 federal international religious freedom act. The US has already designated some countries for their use of such laws, but more needs to be done to support those who are persecuted.

END OF SUMMARY.
