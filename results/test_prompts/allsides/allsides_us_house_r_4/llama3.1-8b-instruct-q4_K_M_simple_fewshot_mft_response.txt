SUMMARY:

Two US congressmen, Rep. Jamie Raskin (D-MD) and Rep. Mark Meadows (R-NC), have introduced a bipartisan resolution condemning "blasphemy" laws that punish religious dissent. The resolution, HR 512, calls on over 70 governments to repeal such laws, which are often used to target marginalized communities. The lawmakers argue that these laws position governments as arbiters of religious truth and empower officials to impose dogma through violence or coercion. The resolution cites alarming reports of persecution and violence perpetrated by countries with blasphemy laws, including Russia's ban on Jehovah's Witnesses and Pakistan's use of wide-ranging blasphemy laws. Religious freedom advocates have cheered the resolution, which notes that nations like Ireland, Greece, and Canada have taken steps to repeal such laws. The US Commission on International Religious Freedom has also endorsed the resolution, calling for awareness and action to address these egregious acts of persecution.
