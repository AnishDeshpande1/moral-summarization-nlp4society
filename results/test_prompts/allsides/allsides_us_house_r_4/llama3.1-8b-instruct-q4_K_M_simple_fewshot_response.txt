SUMMARY:
Two US congressmen, Rep. Jamie Raskin (D-MD) and Rep. Mark Meadows (R-NC), have introduced a bipartisan resolution condemning "blasphemy" laws that punish religious dissent. The resolution calls on over 70 governments to repeal such laws, which are often used to target marginalized communities. The lawmakers argue that these laws position governments as arbiters of religious truth and empower officials to impose dogma on individuals or minorities through violence sanctioned by the government. The resolution cites examples of countries using blasphemy laws to silence dissent, including Russia's ban on Jehovah's Witnesses and Pakistan's use of wide-ranging blasphemy laws. The US Commission on International Religious Freedom has praised the resolution, saying it will raise awareness about egregious acts of persecution and violence legitimized by these laws.

END OF SUMMARY.
