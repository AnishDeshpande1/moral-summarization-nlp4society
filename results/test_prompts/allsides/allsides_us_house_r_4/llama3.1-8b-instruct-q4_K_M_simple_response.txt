SUMMARY:
Two US Congressmen have introduced a bipartisan resolution (HR 512) condemning "blasphemy" laws that punish religious dissent, heresy, and apostasy. The resolution calls on over 70 governments to repeal such laws, which are often used to target marginalized communities. It highlights egregious examples of persecution, including Pakistan's use of wide-ranging blasphemy laws, Russia's ban on Jehovah's Witnesses, and China's detention of nearly a million Uighur Muslims. The resolution also notes countries that have taken steps to repeal similar laws, such as Ireland and Canada. The US Commission on International Religious Freedom welcomed the resolution, stating that it will help raise awareness about persecution and violence legitimized by these laws. The legislation would add blasphemy, apostasy, and heresy as reasons for nations to be designated as "countries of particular concern" for religious freedom.

END OF SUMMARY.
