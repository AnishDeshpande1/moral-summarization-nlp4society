SUMMARY:
Two congressman, a Maryland Democrat and the Republican Chair of the Freedom Caucus, have introduced a bipartisan resolution condemning "blasphemy" laws that position governments as arbiters of religious truth and empower officials to impose religious dogma on individuals or minorities through violence sanctioned by the government. The resolution, HR 512, calls on more than 70 governments to repeal such laws, citing alarming reports of restrictions on religious freedom, including Russia's ban on Jehovah's Witnesses as an "extremist organization" and Pakistan's use of wide-ranging blasphemy laws that result in persecution and violence against marginalized communities. The U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom has cheered the resolution, urging widespread bipartisan support to raise awareness of these egregious acts and advocate for the release of prisoners held on such charges.
