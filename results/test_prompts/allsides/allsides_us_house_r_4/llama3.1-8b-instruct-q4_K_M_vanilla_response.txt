SUMMARY:

Two US Congressmen, Jamie Raskin (D-MD) and Mark Meadows (R-NC), have introduced a bipartisan resolution (HR 512) condemning "blasphemy" laws that punish religious dissent. The resolution calls on over 70 governments to repeal such laws, which are often used to target marginalized communities. The lawmakers recognize that these laws position governments as arbiters of religious truth and empower officials to impose dogma through violence or coercion. The resolution cites examples of countries with restrictive blasphemy laws, including Pakistan, Russia, and China.

The U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom (USCIRF) has endorsed the resolution, urging the release of prisoners held on blasphemy charges. The resolution also notes countries that have repealed such laws, such as Ireland and Canada. If passed, HR 512 would add blasphemy, apostasy, and heresy to the list of reasons for nations to be designated as "countries of particular concern" for religious freedom.

END OF SUMMARY.
