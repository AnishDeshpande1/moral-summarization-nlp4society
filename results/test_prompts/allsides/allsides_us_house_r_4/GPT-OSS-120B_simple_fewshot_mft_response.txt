SUMMARY:
A bipartisan House resolution introduced by Democrat Rep. Jamie Raskin and Freedom Caucus Chair Rep. Mark Meadows condemns “blasphemy,” heresy and apostasy laws as unlawful subversions of individual conscience. The measure argues that such statutes illegitimately empower governments to act as arbiters of religious truth, allowing officials to impose dogma or even sanction violence against dissenters. By calling on more than 70 nations to repeal these laws, the resolution frames the issue as a defense of vulnerable minorities from state‑sanctioned persecution—a clear appeal to the moral foundation of Care/Harm. It also challenges the authority of regimes that use religious law to control citizens, highlighting abuses in Russia, China, Pakistan and others, while praising reforms in Ireland, Greece and Canada. The U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom and UN human‑rights covenants back the effort, proposing that blasphemy‑related offenses become grounds for designating “countries of particular concern.” The bipartisan push signals a moral stance that subverting oppressive religious authority protects the harmed and upholds universal freedom of belief.

END OF SUMMARY.
