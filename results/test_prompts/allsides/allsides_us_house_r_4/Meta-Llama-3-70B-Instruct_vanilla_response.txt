SUMMARY:

Two US congressmen, Jamie Raskin (D-MD) and Mark Meadows (R-NC), have introduced a bipartisan resolution condemning "blasphemy" laws, which punish religious dissent. The resolution, HR 512, urges over 70 governments to repeal laws criminalizing blasphemy, heresy, and apostasy, citing examples of religious freedom restrictions in Russia, China, and Pakistan. The resolution recognizes that such laws empower governments to impose religious dogma on individuals or minorities, leading to persecution and violence. The US Commission on International Religious Freedom has cheered the resolution, calling for the release of prisoners held on blasphemy charges. The resolution also notes countries that have repealed blasphemy laws, including Ireland, Greece, and Canada. If passed, the resolution would add blasphemy, apostasy, and heresy as reasons for nations to be designated as "countries of particular concern" for religious freedom.

END OF SUMMARY.
