SUMMARY:  
Congressional leaders from opposite parties—Democrat Rep. Jamie Raskin and Republican Freedom Caucus Chair Rep. Mark Meadows—have introduced a bipartisan resolution (H.R. 512) that condemns “blasphemy” laws as immoral tools that let governments dictate religious truth and sanction violence against dissenters. The resolution argues that such statutes—targeting blasphemy, heresy, and apostasy—violate fundamental human dignity and religious liberty, a principle echoed by the U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom and the United Nations’ covenant on civil and political rights. Citing egregious examples from Russia, China, Pakistan and others, the bill urges the repeal of these oppressive laws and calls for the release of prisoners held on such charges. It also proposes expanding the “countries of particular concern” list to include nations that maintain blasphemy statutes, while highlighting positive steps taken by Ireland, Greece and Canada in abolishing similar bans. Supporters present the measure as a moral stand for the protection of conscience and the right to question or leave any faith without fear of state‑sanctioned persecution.  

END OF SUMMARY.
