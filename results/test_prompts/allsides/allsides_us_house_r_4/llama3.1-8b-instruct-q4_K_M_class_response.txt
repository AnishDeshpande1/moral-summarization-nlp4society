SUMMARY:

Two congressman, Rep. Jamie Raskin (D-MD) and Rep. Mark Meadows (R-NC), have introduced a bipartisan resolution condemning the use of "blasphemy" laws that restrict religious freedom. The resolution, HR 512, calls on over 70 governments to repeal laws punishing blasphemy, heresy, and apostasy, or the renunciation of a religious belief. These laws position governments as arbiters of religious truth and empower officials to impose their own dogma through violence sanctioned by the government. The resolution highlights egregious cases of persecution and violence legitimized by these laws, including Pakistan's use of wide-ranging blasphemy laws, China's detention of nearly 1 million Uighur Muslims, and Russia's ban on Jehovah's Witnesses as an "extremist organization." The resolution also notes nations that have taken steps to repeal or criminalize such laws. Religious freedom advocates hail the resolution as a crucial step towards upholding civil rights for religious practice and expression.

END OF SUMMARY
