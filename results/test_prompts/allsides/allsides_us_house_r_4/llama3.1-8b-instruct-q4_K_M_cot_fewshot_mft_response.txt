SUMMARY:
Two US congressmen have introduced a bipartisan resolution condemning "blasphemy" laws that punish individuals for expressing dissenting religious views. The resolution, HR 512, calls on over 70 governments to repeal such laws, which position governments as arbiters of religious truth and empower officials to impose religious dogma through violence sanctioned by the government. According to reports from international organizations, blasphemy laws are often used to target marginalized religious communities, with nearly a million Uighur Muslims detained in northwest China and over 40 individuals on death row for blasphemy in Pakistan. The resolution notes that such laws legitimize egregious acts of persecution and violence, and calls for the release of all prisoners held on blasphemy charges. By passing this resolution, Congress aims to raise awareness about the rise of freedom restrictions and promote greater protection for religious freedom worldwide. END OF SUMMARY.
