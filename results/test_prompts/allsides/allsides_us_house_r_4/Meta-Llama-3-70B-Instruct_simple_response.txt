SUMMARY:

In a rare display of bipartisanship, two congressmen, Rep. Jamie Raskin (D-MD) and Rep. Mark Meadows (R-NC), have introduced a resolution condemning "blasphemy" laws that punish religious dissent. The resolution urges over 70 governments to repeal laws that restrict religious freedom, citing alarming reports of rising restrictions. The lawmakers argue that such laws inappropriately position governments as arbiters of religious truth and empower officials to impose religious dogma on individuals or minorities. The resolution highlights egregious cases of persecution, including Russia's ban on Jehovah's Witnesses, China's detention of Uighur Muslims, and Pakistan's use of blasphemy laws. The U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom has cheered the resolution, calling for the release of prisoners held on blasphemy charges. The resolution also notes nations that have taken steps to repeal blasphemy laws, such as Ireland, Greece, and Canada.

END OF SUMMARY.
