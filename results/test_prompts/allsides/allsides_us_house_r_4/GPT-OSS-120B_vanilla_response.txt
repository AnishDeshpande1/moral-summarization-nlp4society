SUMMARY:  
Congressmen Rep. Jamie Raskin (D‑MD) and Rep. Mark Meadows (R‑NC) have introduced House Resolution 512, a bipartisan effort to condemn and urge the repeal of “blasphemy,” heresy, and apostasy laws worldwide. The resolution calls on more than 70 governments to eliminate statutes that punish religious dissent and empower officials to enforce religious dogma, often through violence. It highlights cases such as Russia’s ban on Jehovah’s Witnesses, China’s detention of Uighur Muslims, and Pakistan’s death‑penalty blasphemy prosecutions. The U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom (USCIRF) praised the measure, noting that such laws target marginalized faith communities and calling for the release of prisoners held on these charges. The resolution aligns with UN covenants protecting religious expression and would expand the criteria for designating “countries of particular concern” under the 1998 International Religious Freedom Act to include blasphemy‑related statutes. While nations like Ireland, Greece, and Canada have recently repealed similar laws, many remain on the list of offenders. The proposal aims to raise awareness and pressure governments to uphold religious liberty.  

END OF SUMMARY.
