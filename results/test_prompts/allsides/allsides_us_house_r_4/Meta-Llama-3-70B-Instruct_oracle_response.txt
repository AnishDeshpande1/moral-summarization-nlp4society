SUMMARY:

A bipartisan resolution introduced by Rep. Jamie Raskin and Rep. Mark Meadows condemns "blasphemy" laws, which punish religious dissent and empower governments to impose religious dogma on individuals or minorities through violence sanctioned by the state. The resolution calls on over 70 governments to repeal laws criminalizing blasphemy, heresy, and apostasy, citing alarming reports of religious freedom restrictions on the rise. The resolution highlights egregious examples, including Russia's ban on Jehovah's Witnesses, China's detention of nearly a million Uighur Muslims, and Pakistan's use of blasphemy laws to target marginalized communities. The U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom cheered the resolution, urging the release of prisoners held on blasphemy charges. The resolution also notes nations that have taken steps to repeal blasphemy laws, such as Ireland, Greece, and Canada. By condemning these laws, the resolution aims to raise awareness of the persecution and violence they legitimize.

END OF SUMMARY.
