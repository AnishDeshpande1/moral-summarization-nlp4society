SUMMARY:
Congressmen Jamie Raskin (D‑MD) and Mark Meadows (R‑NC) have introduced bipartisan House Resolution 512 condemning “blasphemy,” heresy and apostasy laws, which they argue let governments enforce religious dogma and legitimize persecution. The resolution urges more than 70 countries to repeal such statutes and calls for the release of prisoners held on these charges. It cites examples such as Russia’s ban on Jehovah’s Witnesses, China’s detention of Uighur Muslims, and Pakistan’s death‑penalty blasphemy sentences. The U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom praised the measure, noting that blasphemy laws are often weaponized against marginalized faith communities. The resolution would expand the list of “countries of particular concern” under the 1998 International Religious Freedom Act to include nations with blasphemy statutes, adding to existing designations like Burma, Iran and Saudi Arabia. It also highlights recent positive steps by Ireland, Greece and Canada in repealing similar laws. Religious‑liberty advocates welcome the effort as a moral stand against state‑sanctioned religious oppression.

END OF SUMMARY.
