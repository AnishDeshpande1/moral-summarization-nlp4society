SUMMARY: Over 180 CEOs have publicly opposed recent U.S. laws that ban abortion and restrict women’s reproductive healthcare, branding such measures as antithetical to equality, human rights, and sound business practice. The CEOs—ranging from Jack Dorsey (Twitter, Square) to leaders of H&M, The Body Shop, Glossier, Yelp, and fashion designers—signed a full‑page New York Times ad titled “Don’t Ban Equality.” They argue that restricting reproductive care harms women’s autonomy, economic success, mental and physical health, and undermines diverse, inclusive workforces that drive corporate success. Executives from The Body Shop and Seventh Generation stress that access to reproductive health is a human right essential for empowerment and that businesses have a moral duty to champion social change. Surveys show nearly half of millennials expect CEOs to speak on societal issues. Earlier, female CEOs also ran a similar ad, and entertainment giants warned they might halt production in states like Georgia with strict bans. The campaign, coordinated by the ACLU, Planned Parenthood, NARAL, and the Center for Reproductive Rights, urges more leaders to join the cause via DontBanEquality.com. The moral framing positions defending reproductive rights as a fundamental component of gender equality and corporate responsibility. END OF SUMMARY.
