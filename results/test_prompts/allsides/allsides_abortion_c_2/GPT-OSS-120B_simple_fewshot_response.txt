SUMMARY:
More than 180 CEOs, including Jack Dorsey, Rebecca Minkoff and leaders from Yelp, H&M, The Body Shop and Glossier, placed a full‑page New York Times ad titled “Don’t Ban Equality” to condemn state laws that ban abortion and restrict women’s reproductive health care. They argue that such restrictions violate human‑rights principles, undermine women’s autonomy, economic security and workplace empowerment, and damage companies’ ability to build diverse, inclusive workforces and attract talent. Executives from The Body Shop and Seventh Generation stress that protecting reproductive rights is essential to gender equality and corporate social responsibility. The campaign, coordinated with the ACLU, Planned Parenthood, NARAL and the Center for Reproductive Rights, also launches DontBanEquality.com for additional sign‑ups. The CEOs frame opposition to the bans as a moral imperative: defending women’s health, freedom and equality is both the right thing to do and good for business. END OF SUMMARY.
