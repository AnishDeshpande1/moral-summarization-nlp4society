SUMMARY: Over 180 CEOs have signed a letter opposing laws that restrict women's reproductive healthcare, including abortion, which they believe is against their values and bad for business. They argue that restricting access to reproductive care inhibits the ability to build diverse workforces and recruit top talent. The CEOs, who include Jack Dorsey and Diane Von Furstenburg, say that such legislation is punishable by damaging businesses and perpetuating inequality. They emphasize the importance of protecting the human rights of women, including their right to access reproductive healthcare. Andrea Blieden from The Body Shop notes that access to reproductive health care is a human right, essential for women's autonomy, economic success, physical and mental health, and empowerment in the workplace. The CEOs are urging other business leaders to stand with them and join this cause, using their voices to protect women's equality and reproductive rights. END OF SUMMARY.
