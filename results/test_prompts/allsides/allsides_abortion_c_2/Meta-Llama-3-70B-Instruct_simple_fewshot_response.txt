SUMMARY:

Over 180 CEOs have signed a letter opposing laws that restrict women's reproductive healthcare, including abortion. The letter, published as a full-page ad in The New York Times, argues that such laws are "against our values, and is bad for business." The executives believe that restricting access to reproductive care inhibits their ability to build diverse and inclusive workforce pipelines, recruit top talent, and protect the well-being of their employees. They emphasize that access to reproductive healthcare is a human right, critical to women's autonomy, economic success, physical and mental health, and general empowerment in the workplace. The letter is a response to recent restrictive abortion laws passed in several states, including Alabama, Georgia, Arkansas, Indiana, and Missouri. The CEOs, including those from Twitter, Square, Yelp, H&M, and Glossier, are taking a stand to protect women's reproductive rights and promote equality.

END OF SUMMARY.
