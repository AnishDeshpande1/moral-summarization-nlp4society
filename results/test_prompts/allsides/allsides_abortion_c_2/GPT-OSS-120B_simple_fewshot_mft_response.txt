SUMMARY:
More than 180 CEOs placed a full‑page “Don’t Ban Equality” ad in The New York Times to denounce state laws that curtail women’s reproductive health care, including the new Alabama ban that criminalizes abortion with up to 99 years in prison and offers no rape or incest exceptions. By publicly opposing these authoritarian restrictions, the business leaders subvert governmental overreach and assert that such policies betray fundamental fairness by denying women equal rights and economic opportunity. They argue that limiting reproductive care harms women’s autonomy, physical and mental health, and undermines inclusive workplaces, which in turn damages companies’ ability to recruit and retain talent. Executives from firms such as The Body Shop, H&M, Yelp, and Disney cite a moral duty to protect human rights and promote gender equality, echoing public‑opinion data showing that many workers expect CEOs to speak out on social issues. The campaign, coordinated with the ACLU, Planned Parenthood, NARAL and the Center for Reproductive Rights, invites additional CEOs to join the call for fair, humane policies that respect women’s bodily autonomy and prevent unjust state authority.  
END OF SUMMARY.
