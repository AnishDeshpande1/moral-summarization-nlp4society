SUMMARY:

Over 180 CEOs have signed a letter opposing laws that restrict women's reproductive healthcare, including abortion. The letter, published as a full-page ad in The New York Times, sends a clear message that such restrictions are "against our values, and is bad for business." The executives argue that these laws inhibit their ability to build diverse workforces, recruit top talent, and protect the well-being of their employees. They emphasize that access to reproductive healthcare is a human right, essential for women's autonomy, economic success, and empowerment in the workplace. The CEOs believe that companies must be a force for good and speak up on pivotal issues like reproductive rights and women's equality. This move is seen as a moral imperative, with many CEOs recognizing their responsibility to stand up for what is right and just.

END OF SUMMARY.
