SUMMARY:

Over 180 CEOs have signed a letter opposing laws that restrict and ban women's access to reproductive healthcare, including abortion. The letter, published as a full-page ad in The New York Times, sends a clear message that such legislation is "against our values, and is bad for business." The executives argue that restricting access to reproductive care inhibits their ability to build diverse and inclusive workforce pipelines and protect the well-being of their employees. They emphasize that access to reproductive healthcare is a human right, critical to women's autonomy, economic success, and empowerment in the workplace. By speaking out, the CEOs aim to fulfill their responsibility to protect and respect women's human rights. The letter is a stand against restrictive legislation, including Alabama's ban on abortion, which includes no exceptions for cases of rape or incest. The CEOs believe that companies must be a force for good and speak up on pivotal issues like reproductive rights and women's equality.

END OF SUMMARY.
