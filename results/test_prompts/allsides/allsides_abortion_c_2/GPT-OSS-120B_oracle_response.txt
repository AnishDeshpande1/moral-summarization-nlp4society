SUMMARY:
More than 180 CEOs signed a full‑page New York Times letter titled “Don’t Ban Equality,” condemning recent state legislation that bans women’s reproductive health care, including abortion. The CEOs—including Jack Dorsey, Rebecca Minkoff, and leaders from Yelp, H&M, The Body Shop, Glossier and Seventh Generation—argue that restricting access is against their values, harms equality, and undermines workplace empowerment. They warn that such bans, punishable by decades in prison and lacking exceptions for rape or incest, will restrict their ability to build inclusive workforces, recruit top talent, and protect the well‑being of employees who keep businesses thriving. Andrea Blieden of The Body Shop called reproductive health care a human right and said companies must protect and fulfill women’s rights. Joey Bergstein of Seventh Generation stressed that CEOs must stand and join the fight, using their platforms for social change. A 2017 survey showed nearly half of millennials expect CEOs to speak on societal issues. The letter, coordinated by the ACLU, Planned Parenthood, NARAL and the Center for Reproductive Rights, also launched DontBanEquality.com to use business influence to defend rights and equality.  
END OF SUMMARY.
