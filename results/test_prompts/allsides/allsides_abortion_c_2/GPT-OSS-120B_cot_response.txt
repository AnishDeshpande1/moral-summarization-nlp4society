**SUMMARY:**  
More than 180 CEOs placed a full‑page “Don’t Ban Equality” ad in The New York Times to denounce state laws that restrict women’s reproductive healthcare, including abortion. They argue such bans violate fundamental human rights and undermine women’s autonomy, economic success, physical and mental health, and overall empowerment. Executives from companies like Twitter, Square, H&M, The Body Shop, and Seventh Generation say restricting access harms their ability to build diverse, inclusive workforces, recruit top talent, and protect employee well‑being. The letter, organized with the ACLU, Planned Parenthood, NARAL and the Center for Reproductive Rights, calls on business leaders to use their values to champion equality and social change, warning that restrictive laws threaten the inclusive culture and moral responsibility businesses owe to their workers.
