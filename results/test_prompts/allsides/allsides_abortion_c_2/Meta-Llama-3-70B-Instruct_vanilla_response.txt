SUMMARY:

Over 180 CEOs have signed a letter opposing laws that restrict women's reproductive healthcare, including abortion. The letter, published as a full-page ad in The New York Times, argues that such laws are "against our values, and is bad for business." The executives claim that restricting access to reproductive care inhibits their ability to build diverse workforces, recruit top talent, and protect employee well-being. The letter emphasizes the importance of respecting and protecting women's human rights, including access to reproductive healthcare. CEOs from companies like Twitter, Yelp, and H&M have signed the letter, which was spearheaded by organizations like the ACLU and Planned Parenthood. The move comes after several states, including Alabama and Georgia, have adopted restrictive abortion laws. The CEOs believe it's essential for business leaders to speak up on important social issues, including reproductive rights and women's equality.

END OF SUMMARY.
