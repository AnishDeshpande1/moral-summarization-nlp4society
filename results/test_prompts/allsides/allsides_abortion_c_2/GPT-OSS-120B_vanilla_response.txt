SUMMARY: Over 180 CEOs have signed a full‑page New York Times ad titled “Don’t Ban Equality,” opposing state laws that restrict women’s reproductive health care, including recent ultra‑restrictive abortion bans in Alabama, Georgia, Arkansas, Indiana and Missouri. Signatories such as Jack Dorsey, Rebecca Minkoff, and CEOs of Yelp, H&M, The Body Shop, Glossier, and Seventh Generation argue that limiting abortion access harms diversity, talent recruitment, and employee well‑being, and contravenes corporate values and business interests. Executives highlighted reproductive care as a human right essential for women’s autonomy, economic success, and workplace empowerment. The campaign, organized by the ACLU, Planned Parenthood, NARAL, and the Center for Reproductive Rights, also launched DontBanEquality.com for additional sign‑ups. Recent related actions include a prior ad by seven female CEOs affirming abortion as healthcare, and entertainment firms like Disney, Netflix, and WarnerMedia considering halting production in Georgia if its law takes effect. A 2017 Weber Shandwick survey found nearly half of millennials expect CEOs to speak on societal issues. END OF SUMMARY.
