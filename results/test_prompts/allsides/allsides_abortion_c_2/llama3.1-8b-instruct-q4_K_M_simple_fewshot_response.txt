SUMMARY:

More than 180 CEOs have signed a letter opposing laws that restrict women's reproductive healthcare, including abortion. The letter, titled "Don't Ban Equality," was published in The New York Times and comes after several states adopted restrictive abortion legislation. The signatories, which include Twitter and Square CEO Jack Dorsey, fashion designer Rebecca Minkoff, and CEOs from companies like Yelp and H&M, argue that restricting access to reproductive care is "against our values" and "bad for business." They claim that such laws inhibit their ability to build diverse workforces and protect the well-being of employees. The letter emphasizes that women's access to reproductive healthcare is a human right essential for autonomy, economic success, physical and mental health, and empowerment in the workplace. The signatories call on other business leaders to join their cause and stand up against restrictive abortion laws.

END OF SUMMARY.
