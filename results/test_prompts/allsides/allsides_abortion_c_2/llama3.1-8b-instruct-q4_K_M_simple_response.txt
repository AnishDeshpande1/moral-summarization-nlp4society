SUMMARY:

Over 180 CEOs have signed a letter opposing restrictive abortion laws, calling them "against our values" and "bad for business". The letter, titled "Don't Ban Equality", was published in The New York Times and comes after several states adopted laws banning doctors from performing abortions at any stage of pregnancy. The executives argue that restricting access to reproductive care inhibits their ability to build diverse workforces and recruit top talent. They emphasize that access to reproductive health care is a human right, essential for women's autonomy, economic success, physical and mental health, and empowerment in the workplace.

The letter was spearheaded by organizations including the ACLU, Planned Parenthood, and NARAL Pro-Choice America. CEOs from companies such as Yelp, H&M, and Glossier signed the letter, joining a growing number of business leaders speaking out on reproductive rights and women's equality. As one CEO noted, "reproductive rights and women's equality is one of the most important issues of our time" and it's essential for business leaders to use their voices to advocate for these rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
