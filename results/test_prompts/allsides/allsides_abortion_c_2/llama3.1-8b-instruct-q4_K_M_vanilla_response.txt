SUMMARY:

More than 180 CEOs have signed a letter opposing restrictive abortion laws, including Alabama's law that bans doctors from performing abortions at any stage of pregnancy, punishable by 99 years in prison. The letter, published as a full-page ad in The New York Times, argues that restricting reproductive healthcare is "against our values" and "bad for business." CEOs from companies such as Yelp, H&M, and Glossier claim that such legislation inhibits their ability to build diverse workforces and recruit top talent. The letter was spearheaded by organizations including the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU), Planned Parenthood, and NARAL Pro-Choice America. Several major entertainment companies, including Disney and Netflix, have also announced they will consider ceasing production in states with restrictive abortion laws. The CEOs aim to send a clear message that reproductive rights are essential for women's autonomy, economic success, and overall well-being.

END OF SUMMARY.
