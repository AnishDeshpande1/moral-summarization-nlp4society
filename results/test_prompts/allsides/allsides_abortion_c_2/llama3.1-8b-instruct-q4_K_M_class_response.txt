SUMMARY: Over 180 CEOs have signed a letter opposing laws that restrict women's reproductive healthcare, including abortion, which they believe is against their values and bad for business. They argue that such legislation inhibits their ability to build diverse workforces, recruit top talent, and protect the well-being of employees. The CEOs, who include Jack Dorsey and Rebecca Minkoff, emphasize that access to reproductive care is a human right and essential for women's autonomy, economic success, physical and mental health, and empowerment in the workplace. They believe it's crucial for business leaders to speak out on issues like this, which they consider one of the most important challenges of our time. The letter was spearheaded by organizations like the ACLU, Planned Parenthood, and NARAL Pro-Choice America, who are fighting against laws that punish doctors with 99 years in prison for performing abortions, even in cases of rape or incest. By standing together on this issue, the CEOs aim to protect women's rights and promote equality.

END OF SUMMARY.
