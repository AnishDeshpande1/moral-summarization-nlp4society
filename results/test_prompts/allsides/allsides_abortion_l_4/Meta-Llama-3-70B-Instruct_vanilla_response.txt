SUMMARY:

Texas politician Wendy Davis recently stated that the goal should be to have "zero abortions," suggesting that a world without abortion is desirable. However, this stance is criticized as denying reality and stigmatizing women who need abortion care. Doctors and reproductive rights advocates argue that women will always need abortion care, and that it's a necessary medical procedure that saves lives and protects autonomy. They point out that even with access to contraception, women may still face challenges and circumstances that make abortion necessary. Framing abortion as a necessary evil or a problem to be solved erases the complexity of women's experiences and decisions. Instead, policymakers should focus on empowering women to make the best medical decisions for themselves, based on their unique circumstances. By prioritizing women's health and autonomy, the number of abortions becomes less relevant. END OF SUMMARY.
