SUMMARY:

Wendy Davis's statement on the campaign trail, suggesting that a world without abortion is a desirable goal, is a flawed and harmful stance. This position denies the reality that women will always need abortion care and perpetuates stigma around the procedure. Dr. Anne Davis, a second-trimester abortion provider, emphasizes that abortion is sometimes necessary to save a woman's life, and basing policy on the false claim that it's not necessary sacrifices the health of patients. Dr. Lin-Fan Wang, a doctor of family medicine, notes that even with increased access to contraception, women will still face challenges and need abortion care. The focus should be on supporting women's autonomy and health, rather than reducing the number of abortions. By framing abortion as a necessary evil, politicians erase the fact that women choose abortion in various circumstances. Instead, policymakers should prioritize ensuring that women are empowered to make the best medical decisions for themselves, free from coercion and violence. END OF SUMMARY.
