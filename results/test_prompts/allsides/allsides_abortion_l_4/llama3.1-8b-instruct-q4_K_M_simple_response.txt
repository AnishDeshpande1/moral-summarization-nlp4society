SUMMARY:

A recent interview by Wendy Davis sparked criticism for her statement that a world without abortion is something everyone wants, suggesting that zero abortions are the goal. However, experts argue that this position denies reality and erases the complexity of women's experiences with abortion. Dr. Anne Davis and Dr. Lin-Fan Wang emphasize that abortion care is necessary to save women's lives and protect their autonomy. They point out that even with access to contraception, unintended pregnancies can still occur due to various circumstances such as human error, bad luck, or reproductive coercion.

The authors argue that framing zero abortions as a goal is not only unrealistic but also shaming, as it reduces the necessity of abortion care to a simple binary issue. Instead, they advocate for policies and advocacy that focus on women's unique situations and empower them to make informed medical decisions. By shifting the emphasis from numbers to individual experiences, we can have nuanced conversations about abortion and prioritize women's health and autonomy.

END OF SUMMARY.
