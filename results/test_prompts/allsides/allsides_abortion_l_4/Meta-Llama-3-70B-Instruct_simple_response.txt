SUMMARY:

The article critiques politicians, including Wendy Davis, who suggest that the goal of reproductive rights should be to have "zero abortions." This stance is seen as a denial of reality, as women will always need abortion care. The article argues that framing abortion as a necessary evil or a problem to be solved erases the fact that women choose abortion in various circumstances, and that it is a medical procedure that saves lives and protects autonomy. Instead of focusing on reducing the number of abortions, policymakers should prioritize ensuring that women have access to healthcare and are empowered to make the best medical decisions for themselves. The article quotes medical professionals who emphasize that women's circumstances are unique and that there will always be a need for abortion care, even with increased access to contraception and healthcare.
