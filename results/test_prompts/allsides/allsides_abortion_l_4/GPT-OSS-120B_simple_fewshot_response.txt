SUMMARY:
In a campaign interview, Wendy Davis echoed a familiar “zero‑abortion” talking point, claiming the goal should be no abortions while urging better healthcare and planning for women. The article argues this stance is both unrealistic and harmful, noting that abortions will always be needed to protect women’s health, autonomy, and circumstances that contraception cannot fully prevent. Medical experts Dr. Anne Davis and Dr. Lin‑Fan Wang stress that abortions save lives, that stigma and false claims about “never necessary” policies endanger patients, and that each woman’s situation is unique. They warn that framing abortion as a problem to be solved mirrors anti‑abortion rhetoric and distracts from genuine support—removing cost barriers, improving contraceptive access, and respecting individual decisions. The piece calls for nuanced, women‑centered discourse rather than the impossible and shaming goal of “zero abortions.”  
END OF SUMMARY.
