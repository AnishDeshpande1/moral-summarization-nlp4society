SUMMARY:

Texas gubernatorial candidate Wendy Davis has sparked controversy by suggesting that the goal should be to see "zero abortions." However, this stance is misguided and denies the reality that women will always need access to abortion care. Abortion is a necessary medical procedure that saves women's lives and protects their autonomy. By framing it as a necessary evil, Davis and other politicians erase the fact that women choose abortion in various circumstances, not just to save their lives. Moreover, even with access to contraception, women will still face challenges in getting the care they need. The focus should be on empowering women to make the best medical decisions for themselves, rather than striving for a unrealistic goal of "zero abortions." This approach is not only unrealistic but also shames women who need abortion care. Instead, policymakers should prioritize ensuring access to safe and legal abortion, while respecting the autonomy and dignity of women.

END OF SUMMARY.
