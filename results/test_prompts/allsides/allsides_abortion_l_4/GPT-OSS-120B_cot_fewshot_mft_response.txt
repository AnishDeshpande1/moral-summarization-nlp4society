**SUMMARY:**  
In a campaign interview, Wendy Davis echoed the familiar political line that the goal should be “zero abortions,” a slogan she and many moderates use to avoid the “extreme” label and the political death that can follow. Critics argue this framing is both false and shaming, creating stigma and ignoring the real, necessary role of abortion in protecting women’s health and autonomy. Doctors like Dr. Anne Davis and Dr. Lin‑Fan Wang stress that abortion saves lives, that it is a safe medical procedure, and that denying it sacrifices patient health. They highlight that barriers to contraception, reproductive coercion, and inevitable human error mean abortions will always be necessary. Policies should therefore focus on caring for each woman’s unique circumstances, ensuring fairness and empowering choice rather than pursuing an unattainable, misleading “zero abortions” goal that subverts realistic care.
