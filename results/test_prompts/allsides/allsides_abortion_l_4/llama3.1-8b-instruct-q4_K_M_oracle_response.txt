SUMMARY:

The article critiques Wendy Davis' statement that the goal is to achieve zero abortions, arguing that it denies reality and erases the complexity of women's experiences. Dr. Anne Davis and Dr. Lin-Fan Wang emphasize that abortion care is a necessary medical procedure that saves women's lives and protects their autonomy. They highlight the importance of removing barriers to healthcare and planning, rather than shaming or denying access to reproductive care. The authors argue that framing abortion as a problem to be solved or a social ill to be eradicated perpetuates stigma and misconceptions about the necessity of abortion care.

The article emphasizes the need for nuanced conversations about abortion, focusing on women's health and circumstances, rather than advocating for policies that deny basic medical care. Dr. Wang notes that each woman's situation is unique, and policymakers should prioritize supporting women in making informed decisions based on their individual needs. By doing so, we can reduce the stigma surrounding abortion and ensure that women receive the necessary care to make choices about their bodies.
