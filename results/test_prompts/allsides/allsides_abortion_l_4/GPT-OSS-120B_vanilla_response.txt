SUMMARY: In a recent campaign interview, Wendy Davis echoed a common political line that the goal should be “zero abortions,” arguing that better healthcare and planning would achieve this. The article criticizes this stance, asserting that a world without abortions is unrealistic and that such rhetoric stigmatizes women. Experts Dr. Anne Davis and Dr. Lin‑Fan Wang emphasize that abortions are sometimes necessary to save lives, protect health, and address complex personal circumstances—including contraceptive failure, reproductive coercion, and changing financial situations. They argue that policy should focus on removing barriers to contraception and abortion care, respecting individual autonomy, rather than pursuing an unattainable “zero abortions” target. The piece calls for nuanced, women‑centered discussions that recognize abortion as a safe, essential medical procedure, not a problem to be eradicated. END OF SUMMARY.
