SUMMARY:
In a campaign interview, Texas legislator Wendy Davis echoed a familiar political line—“the goal should be zero abortions”—a framing used by leaders like Bill Clinton to appear moderate and avoid the stigma of being “extreme.” The author argues that this rhetoric subverts reality and undermines women’s health, presenting an unrealistic authority‑driven objective that ignores the essential care women need. Leading physicians, Dr. Anne Davis and Dr. Lin‑Fan Wang, stress that abortions are sometimes life‑saving and that denying this fact harms patients and betrays fairness by restricting essential medical care. They point out that even with perfect contraception access, barriers, contraceptive failure, economic shifts, and reproductive coercion ensure that abortions will always be necessary. Policies should therefore reject the false “zero‑abortion” goal, which mirrors anti‑abortion lawmakers’ deceptive narratives, and instead uphold a fair, compassionate approach that respects each woman’s unique circumstances and safeguards her health. By shifting focus from abstract numbers to genuine care and equitable access, the debate can honor both the authority of medical expertise and the moral imperative to protect women from harm. END OF SUMMARY.
