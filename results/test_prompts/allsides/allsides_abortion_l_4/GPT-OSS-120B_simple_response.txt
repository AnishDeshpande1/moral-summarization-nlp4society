SUMMARY: In a recent campaign interview, Wendy Davis echoed a common political line—advocating for “zero abortions” while promising better health care and planning for women. The article condemns this stance as a misleading, shame‑based framing that denies the reality that abortion will always be needed. Citing physicians Dr. Anne Davis and Dr. Lin‑Fan Wang, it stresses that abortions save lives, protect autonomy, and arise from diverse, often unavoidable circumstances such as medical emergencies, reproductive coercion, contraceptive failure, or sudden financial hardship. The piece argues that policies should focus on removing barriers to contraception and comprehensive care, not on an unrealistic goal of eliminating abortions, which mirrors anti‑abortion rhetoric that endangers women’s health. It calls for nuanced, compassionate discourse that respects each woman’s unique situation and prioritizes her health over abstract statistics. The moral message is clear: the reproductive‑rights movement must reject the false ideal of “zero abortions” and instead champion informed, patient‑centered decision‑making. END OF SUMMARY.
