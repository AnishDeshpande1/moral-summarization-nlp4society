SUMMARY:

Wendy Davis, a politician, has fallen into the trap of framing abortion as a necessary evil, suggesting that a world without abortions is a desirable goal. However, this stance denies the reality that women will always need abortion care. By framing abortion as a problem to be solved, Davis and others like her are perpetuating the stigma surrounding this essential medical procedure. Dr. Anne Davis and Dr. Lin-Fan Wang, both medical professionals, argue that abortion is a lifesaving procedure that protects women's autonomy and health. They emphasize that women will always face challenges in accessing contraception and other care, and that circumstances can change, making wanted pregnancies unwanted. The focus should be on empowering women to make the best medical decisions for themselves, rather than striving for a unrealistic goal of zero abortions. This approach is not only false but also shaming and harmful, echoing the rhetoric of anti-abortion lawmakers who seek to deny women basic medical care.

END OF SUMMARY.
