SUMMARY:

A new report reveals that the Priority Enforcement Program (PEP), intended to deport "criminal aliens," is failing to target its intended group. Instead, half of the "holds" placed on immigrants suspected of violating US immigration law were individuals with past arrests but no convictions. Many were picked up for minor offenses like traffic violations, and their arrest triggered a process leading to deportation proceedings. Only one quarter of those targeted had committed serious offenses like murder or sexual assault. The program's focus on "criminal aliens" is misleading, as ICE now targets people with no criminal record at a slightly higher rate than before. This has sparked concerns about the program's effectiveness and its impact on community policing, with critics arguing that it creates a humanitarian crisis and undermines trust between immigrants and law enforcement. END OF SUMMARY.
