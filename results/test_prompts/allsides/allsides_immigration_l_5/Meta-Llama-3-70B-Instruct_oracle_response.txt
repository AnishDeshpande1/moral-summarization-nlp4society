SUMMARY:

A new report reveals that the Priority Enforcement Program, intended to expedite the deportation of "criminal aliens," is missing its target. Half of the "holds" placed on immigrants suspected of violating US immigration law were individuals with past arrests but no convictions. Many were picked up during traffic violations or had their charges dropped, yet were still detained and faced deportation proceedings. Only one quarter of those targeted had committed serious offenses like murder or sexual assault, with the most common conviction being for drunk driving. This raises concerns about the program's focus on "criminal aliens" and its impact on community policing needs. Immigration advocates argue that the program is not keeping anyone secure, and instead, is creating a humanitarian crisis. The report's findings suggest that the program is falling short of its intended goals, and instead, is targeting people with no criminal record at a higher rate than before. END OF SUMMARY.
