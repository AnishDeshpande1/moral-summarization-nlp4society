SUMMARY: A new report by the Transactional Records Access Clearinghouse shows that the Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) Priority Enforcement Program (PEP) is missing its target of deporting only “criminal aliens.” Half of the  “holds” placed on immigrants in local jails were for individuals who had been arrested but never convicted, many for minor traffic violations or dropped charges. Only 25 % faced serious offenses such as murder or sexual assault; the most common convictions were DUI, simple assaults and traffic offenses. Although ICE claims PEP narrows focus to convicted felons and serious misdemeanors, recent data indicate the agency is still detaining people with no criminal record at rates equal to or higher than before. Critics argue the program is essentially a rebranded Secure Communities, with new detainer forms that target recent illegal entrants lacking convictions. Resistance from sanctuary cities remains, and advocates warn the policy may deter crime victims from contacting police. END OF SUMMARY.
