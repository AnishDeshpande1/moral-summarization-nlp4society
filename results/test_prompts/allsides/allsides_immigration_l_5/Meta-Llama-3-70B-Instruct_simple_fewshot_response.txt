SUMMARY:

A new report reveals that the Priority Enforcement Program (PEP), intended to deport "criminal aliens," is missing its target. Half of the "holds" placed on immigrants were for those with past arrests but no convictions, including traffic violations and dropped charges. Only a quarter had committed serious offenses like murder or sexual assault. The most common conviction was for drunk driving. This contradicts Immigration and Customs Enforcement's (ICE) claims that it has narrowed its criteria to focus on "criminal aliens." In reality, ICE now targets people with no criminal record at a slightly higher rate than before. The program's flaws have led to a humanitarian crisis, with many immigrants, including victims of crimes, fearing to report incidents to the police. The report raises questions about the effectiveness and morality of the PEP program.

END OF SUMMARY.
