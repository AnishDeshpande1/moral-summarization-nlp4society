SUMMARY:

A new report reveals that the Priority Enforcement Program (PEP), intended to expedite the deportation of "criminal aliens," is missing its target. An analysis of requests by federal officials for local jails to hold immigrants suspected of violating US immigration law found that half of the "holds" were placed on people who had been arrested but had no criminal conviction. Many had been picked up during traffic violations or had their charges dropped. Only one quarter had committed serious offenses like murder or sexual assault. The most common conviction was for drunk driving. Despite claims of narrowing the criteria, ICE now targets people with no criminal record at a slightly higher rate than before. Immigration advocates suspect that the PEP program is just a name change from its predecessor, Secure Communities, which contributed to a record number of deportations under Barack Obama.

END OF SUMMARY.
