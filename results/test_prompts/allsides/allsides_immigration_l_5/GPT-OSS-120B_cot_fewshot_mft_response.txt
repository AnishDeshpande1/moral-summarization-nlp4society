**SUMMARY:**  
A new report shows that the Priority Enforcement Program (PEP), which claims to target “criminal” aliens who threaten public safety, is missing its target. Half of the immigration “holds” placed on detainees involve people with no convictions—often arrested for minor traffic violations or whose charges were dropped. The program’s “common‑sense” approach, promoted by ICE as a fairness‑driven effort to focus on convicted offenders, instead detains many unconvicted, illegal immigrants, raising concerns about subversion of due‑process authority and potential harm to victims who may now fear reporting crimes. Critics argue the policy is a “joke,” unfairly cheating justice by treating unconvicted individuals like dangerous criminals, while sanctuary‑city advocates warn it undermines community policing and public security. ICE maintains the program protects security, but the evidence suggests it fails to uphold the fairness and care it purports to prioritize.
