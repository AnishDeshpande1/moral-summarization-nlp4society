SUMMARY:

A new report reveals that the Priority Enforcement Program (PEP), aimed at deporting immigrants who officials call “the worst of the worst,” is missing its target. Analysis of requests for local jails to hold suspected undocumented immigrants found that half of the so-called "holds" were placed on individuals with past arrests but no convictions. This has led to many being detained and deported, despite not posing a significant threat to public safety or national security. Immigration advocates have criticized ICE's PEP program, saying it prioritizes enforcement over community policing needs and perpetuates a cycle of fear among immigrant communities. By targeting individuals without criminal records, the program undermines trust between law enforcement and the communities they serve. The report highlights the need for more nuanced and effective immigration policies that prioritize fairness, compassion, and due process.

END OF SUMMARY.
