SUMMARY:

A recent report reveals that the Priority Enforcement Program, intended to expedite the deportation of "criminal aliens," is failing to target its intended audience. Instead, half of the "holds" placed on immigrants were for individuals with no criminal convictions, with some having been arrested for minor offenses like traffic violations. This raises concerns about the program's fairness and the harm it may cause to innocent individuals. The report also highlights the program's subversion of authority, as it appears to be targeting people without criminal records at a higher rate than before. Furthermore, the program's focus on deportation rather than community policing needs may be exacerbating the humanitarian crisis at the US-Mexico border. The report's findings suggest that the program is not prioritizing public safety and national security, but rather perpetuating a system that cheats immigrants out of their rights and dignity. 

END OF SUMMARY.
