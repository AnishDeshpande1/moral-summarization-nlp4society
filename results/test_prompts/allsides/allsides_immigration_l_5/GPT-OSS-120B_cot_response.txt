**SUMMARY:**  
A new report shows the Priority Enforcement Program (PEP), meant to target “the worst of the worst” *criminal* *aliens*, is missing its mark. Half of the ICE “holds” were placed on people who had only been *arrested* and lacked a *conviction*, many for minor traffic violations. While ICE claims the program now focuses on *felony* and serious *misdemeanor* offenders, data reveal that more individuals with no *criminal* record are being *detained* and faced *deportation* than before. Critics say the policy fuels a *humanitarian* *crisis* and erodes trust, as *victims* of crime may avoid calling police for fear of being *detained* themselves. Despite ICE’s assertions of a “common‑sense” *enforcement* approach that protects *public safety* and *national security*, undocumented communities and advocates argue the program merely re‑brands the earlier Secure Communities scheme, using new *detainer* forms to hold *illegal* entrants who pose no *security* risk. The controversy highlights the tension between *policy* aimed at *protect*ing citizens and the rights and *justice* owed to immigrant residents.
