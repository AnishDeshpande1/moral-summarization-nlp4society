**SUMMARY:**
The Priority Enforcement Program (PEP), intended to target "the worst of the worst" criminal aliens, has been criticized for prioritizing people with past arrests but no convictions. According to a new report, half of the so-called “holds” were placed on individuals who had not committed serious crimes, such as murder or sexual assault. Many of these individuals were arrested for minor offenses like drunk driving or traffic violations. This "common-sense approach" by Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) has been met with criticism from immigration advocates, who claim it is a "joke" that targets people without criminal records. The program's focus on convicted criminals and individuals who threaten public safety and national security has been compromised, as ICE now targets people with no criminal record at a higher rate than before. This has led to concerns about the erosion of trust between law enforcement and immigrant communities.
