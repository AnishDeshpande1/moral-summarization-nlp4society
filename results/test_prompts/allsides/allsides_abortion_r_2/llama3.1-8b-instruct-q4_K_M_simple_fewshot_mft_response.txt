SUMMARY:

President Trump made history by becoming the first president to speak at the anti-abortion March for Life rally, where he vowed to defend unborn children as their strongest defender in the White House. He criticized Democrats for embracing "radical and extreme" positions on abortion and called for an end to late-term abortions. The move comes amid a new Gallup poll showing record-high dissatisfaction with U.S. abortion laws, with 58% of Americans expressing discontent. The survey found that since Trump took office in 2017, the share of respondents wanting less-strict abortion policies has risen, fueled by Democrats and independents expressing such sentiments. Trump's actions have been seen as a clear signal of his commitment to anti-abortion policies, which aligns with an increase in abortion restrictions across states. The Supreme Court will also hear arguments this year on a Louisiana law requiring abortion providers to have admitting privileges at a local hospital.

END OF SUMMARY.
