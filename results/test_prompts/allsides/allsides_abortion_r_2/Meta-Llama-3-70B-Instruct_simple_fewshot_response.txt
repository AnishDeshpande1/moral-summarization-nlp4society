SUMMARY:

President Trump became the first president to speak in person at the anti-abortion March for Life rally, vowing to defend unborn children and slamming Democrats for embracing "radical and extreme positions" on abortion. Trump's administration has been a champion for life, appointing anti-abortion judges and federal workers, cutting taxpayer funding for abortions, and calling for an end to late-term abortions. A new Gallup poll shows that discontent over U.S. abortion policy is at an all-time high, with 58% of respondents dissatisfied and 32% satisfied. The poll also found that support for easing abortion policy has risen, particularly among Democrats and independents. Meanwhile, many states have enacted restrictive anti-abortion laws, and the Supreme Court will hear arguments on a Louisiana law requiring abortion providers to have admitting privileges at a local hospital. The rally and poll results highlight the ongoing debate over abortion policy in the United States.

END OF SUMMARY.
