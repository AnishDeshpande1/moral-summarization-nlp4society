SUMMARY: President Trump made history on Friday by speaking in person at the anti‑abortion March for Life rally, declaring that “unborn children have never had a stronger defender in the White House” and invoking the Bible’s view that every person is “wonderfully made.” He condemned Democrats for supporting “taxpayer‑funded abortion… up until the moment of birth,” while March for Life leaders praised the administration’s anti‑abortion judges, funding cuts, and push to end late‑term abortions. A new Gallup poll shows record‑high dissatisfaction with U.S. abortion laws (58% dissatisfied) and a split among those dissatisfied—roughly equal numbers now want stricter versus less‑strict rules, reflecting growing Democratic and independent support for easing restrictions since 2017. Meanwhile, a Kaiser Family Foundation poll finds 59% of Americans favor legal abortion in most or all cases, yet a majority still endorse specific restrictions such as doctor‑admitting‑privilege requirements and mandatory waiting periods. The Supreme Court is set to hear another abortion‑restriction case this year. The data illustrate a nation deeply divided, with pro‑life advocates framing the issue as a moral defense of the unborn against increasingly permissive policies. END OF SUMMARY.
