SUMMARY:
President Trump made history by speaking in person at the anti‑abortion March for Life rally, positioning himself as the “strongest defender” of unborn children and invoking the Bible to stress the sanctity of life. He castigated Democrats for “radical, extreme” policies that allegedly fund abortions up to birth, portraying their stance as a betrayal of moral authority and a cheating of the nation’s most vulnerable. The rally’s leaders praised the administration’s appointment of anti‑abortion judges, cuts to federal abortion funding, and efforts to end late‑term procedures, framing these actions as just and proper enforcement of a higher moral order. A new Gallup poll revealed record‑high dissatisfaction with U.S. abortion laws, with public opinion split between those demanding stricter rules and those seeking looser restrictions, reflecting a growing perception that current policies are unfairly lenient. Parallel polls showed broad support for certain restrictions (e.g., doctor admitting‑privilege, waiting periods, ultrasound disclosure) while opposition to criminal penalties, underscoring a complex balance between reverence for life and concerns about governmental overreach. The article frames Trump’s rally appearance as a rightful assertion of authority against subversive, unfair, and morally degrading liberal policies.  
END OF SUMMARY.
