President Trump became the first president to address the March for Life rally in person, declaring that “unborn children have never had a stronger defender in the White House” and invoking the Bible’s notion that each person is “wonderfully made.” He condemned Democrats for embracing “radical and extreme positions” and supporting “taxpayer‑funded abortion, all the way up until the moment of birth.” March for Life president Jeanne Mancini praised the administration as a “consistent, unwavering champion for life,” citing anti‑abortion judges, cuts to federal abortion funding, and a push to end late‑term procedures. A new Gallup poll shows record‑high dissatisfaction with U.S. abortion laws, with Americans split between wanting stricter versus less‑strict rules. While a Kaiser Family Foundation poll finds 59 % support legal abortion in most or all cases, many still endorse specific restrictions such as doctor admitting‑privilege requirements and mandatory ultrasounds, though criminalizing abortion is unpopular. The Supreme Court is set to hear another challenge to state restrictions this year.
