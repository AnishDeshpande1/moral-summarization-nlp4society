SUMMARY:
President Trump made history by becoming the first sitting president to address the anti‑abortion March for Life rally in person, declaring that “unborn children have never had a stronger defender in the White House.” He praised the administration’s record of appointing anti‑abortion judges, cutting federal funding for abortions and seeking to end late‑term procedures, while condemning Democrats for supporting “taxpayer‑funded abortion” to the moment of birth. The March for Life praised the administration as steadfast “champions for life.” A new Gallup poll showed unprecedented dissatisfaction with U.S. abortion laws (58% dissatisfied) and a split among those dissatisfied—roughly equal numbers now favor stricter versus less‑strict regulations, a shift that began after Trump took office. Other polls reveal a majority still support legal abortion in most or all cases and want Roe v. Wade upheld, though many also back specific restrictions such as doctor‑admitting‑privilege rules and waiting periods. The data highlight a growing public debate over abortion policy amid ongoing court battles and state‑level restrictions.  

END OF SUMMARY.
