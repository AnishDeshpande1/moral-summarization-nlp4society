SUMMARY: President Trump became the first president to speak in person at the anti‑abortion March for Life rally, declaring that “unborn children have never had a stronger defender in the White House.” Citing the Bible, he said each person is “wonderfully made” and slammed Democrats for backing “taxpayer‑funded abortion…up until the moment of birth.” March for Life president Jeanne Mancini praised the administration’s “champions for life” record, noting appointments of anti‑abortion judges, cuts to federal abortion funding and calls to end late‑term procedures. A new Gallup poll shows record‑high dissatisfaction with U.S. abortion laws (58%) and a split among those dissatisfied—22% want less‑strict laws, 24% want stricter. The Kaiser Family Foundation found 59% favor legal abortion in most or all cases, while many still support certain restrictions such as provider admitting‑privilege rules and waiting periods. The Supreme Court will hear a case on Louisiana’s admitting‑privilege law, echoing earlier rulings. Overall, the article frames the debate as a moral struggle, emphasizing the Trump administration’s “support” for life and its role as a “defender” against what it calls radical abortion policies. END OF SUMMARY.
