SUMMARY: President Donald Trump made history on Friday by speaking in person at the anti‑abortion March for Life rally, emphasizing his administration’s support for “unborn children” and condemning Democrats for backing “taxpayer‑funded abortion.” The March for Life praised Trump’s record of appointing anti‑abortion judges, cutting federal abortion funding, and opposing late‑term abortions. A new Gallup poll shows unprecedented dissatisfaction with U.S. abortion laws (58% dissatisfied, 32% satisfied) and a shift toward favoring less‑restrictive policies, especially among Democrats and independents. While 22% of adults want less‑strict laws and 24% want stricter laws, overall support for easing restrictions has grown since 2017. A Kaiser Family Foundation poll found 59% favor legal abortion in most or all cases, yet many also back specific restrictions such as provider admitting‑privilege rules, waiting periods, and mandatory ultrasounds. Most Americans (≈77% in a 2019 NPR/PBS poll) want Roe v. Wade upheld, though a sizable portion seeks additional limits or expansions. The Supreme Court is set to hear a case challenging a Louisiana admitting‑privilege law, continuing legal battles over state abortion restrictions. END OF SUMMARY.
