SUMMARY:
A coalition of 14 Wisconsin parents has sued the Madison Metropolitan School District, alleging that the district’s transgender policy unlawfully subverts parental authority. Filed by the conservative law firm Wisconsin Institute for Law and Liberty, the lawsuit claims the policy lets teachers address students by their self‑identified gender and pronouns without notifying parents, thereby denying parents the constitutional right to direct their children’s upbringing and religious beliefs. The complaint argues that the district’s 2018 guidance— which encourages “disrupting the gender binary” and permits students of any age to socially transition at school without parental consent— violates the state constitution’s due‑process and religious‑freedom guarantees. By keeping parents in the dark about a child’s gender identity, the district is portrayed as usurping the traditional parental role and overriding families’ moral authority. The district says it has not yet been served and will defend its guidance, but the suit frames the school’s actions as a clear subversion of parental control over crucial life‑changing decisions.  

END OF SUMMARY.
