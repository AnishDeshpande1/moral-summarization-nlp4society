SUMMARY:
Fourteen Wisconsin parents, represented by the conservative law firm Wisconsin Institute for Law and Liberty, have sued the Madison Metropolitan School District for a policy that lets teachers address students by their self‑identified gender without first notifying parents. The lawsuit claims the policy violates the state constitution’s due‑process and religious‑freedom guarantees by stripping parents of their fundamental right to guide their children’s upbringing and to raise them according to their faith, which holds that God created only male and female. It argues that allowing children to “socially transition” at school without parental consent endangers their health and moral development, and that the district has no compelling interest in keeping parents in the dark about possible gender dysphoria. The parents contend that schools should not usurp decisions that belong to families, and they seek a court order to restore parental control over any discussion of a child’s gender identity. The district says it will defend its guidance for transgender and non‑binary students but has not yet responded to the filing.  
END OF SUMMARY.
