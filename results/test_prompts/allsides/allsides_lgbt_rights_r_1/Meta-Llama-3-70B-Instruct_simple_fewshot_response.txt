SUMMARY:

A group of 14 Wisconsin parents has filed a lawsuit against the Madison Metropolitan School District, alleging that its policy allowing teachers to address students by their preferred "gender identity" without notifying parents is unconstitutional. The policy, implemented in 2018, enables students to socially transition to a different gender identity at school without parental notice or consent. The parents argue that this violates their fundamental right to direct the upbringing of their children, as well as their religious freedom guarantee. They claim that the policy interferes with their ability to guide their children through life-altering decisions and provide professional help they may urgently need. The lawsuit highlights the importance of parental involvement in their children's education and the need for schools to respect parents' rights and beliefs. By prioritizing the school district's ideology over parental rights, the policy undermines the values of family and community.

END OF SUMMARY.
