SUMMARY:  
A group of 14 Wisconsin parents has filed a lawsuit against the Madison Metropolitan School District, alleging that the district’s 2018 policy **allows** teachers to address students by their **chosen** names and pronouns **without notifying** parents. The parents claim the policy **prohibits** their constitutional right to direct their children’s upbringing and **circumvents** parental involvement in any **transition** related to gender identity. The complaint **asserts** that the district’s **adopting** of a **policy** that **enables** children of any age to **pick** new names and pronouns **without parental consent** **violates** the state constitution’s due‑process and religious‑freedom guarantees. The lawsuit describes the district’s actions as an attempt to **hide** students’ gender struggles, **interfering** with parents’ ability to provide guidance and professional help. WILL, the conservative law firm representing the parents, says the suit seeks to **vindicate** parental rights and that the district’s **committed** stance to **affirming** self‑identified gender and to **disrupt** the gender binary is unconstitutional. The district has not yet responded.  
END OF SUMMARY.
