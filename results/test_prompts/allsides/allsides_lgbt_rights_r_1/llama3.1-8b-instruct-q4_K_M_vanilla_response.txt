SUMMARY:

A group of 14 Wisconsin parents have filed a lawsuit against the Madison Metropolitan School District (MMSD) over its policy allowing teachers to address students by their preferred gender identity without notifying parents first. The policy, implemented in 2018, allows students to pick new names and pronouns at school without parental permission. The parents argue that this policy violates the state constitution's due process clause, which protects parents' right to raise their children according to their beliefs.

The lawsuit, filed by the conservative law firm Wisconsin Institute for Law and Liberty (WILL), claims that keeping parents in the dark about their children's sexuality interferes with their ability to guide them through life-altering decisions. The parents also argue that the policy violates the state constitution's religious freedom guarantee, as they believe God intended humans to be male and female.

The district has not commented on the lawsuit yet, but stands by its guidance on transgender and nonbinary students.
