SUMMARY: A coalition of 14 Wisconsin parents, represented by the conservative law firm Wisconsin Institute for Law and Liberty (WILL), has sued the Madison Metropolitan School District (MMSD). The lawsuit challenges MMSD’s 2018 policy that permits teachers and staff to use students’ self‑identified names and gender pronouns without notifying parents. Plaintiffs argue the policy infringes on constitutional parental rights under the state’s due‑process clause and religious‑freedom guarantee, claiming it allows children to socially transition at school without parental consent or knowledge. While legal name changes still require parental approval, the district’s guidance encourages affirming each student’s gender identity and “disrupting the gender binary.” WILL contends there is no compelling governmental interest in keeping such information from parents and that the policy unlawfully circumvents parental involvement in decisions affecting a child’s health and future. MMSD has not yet been served and declined comment pending review, but affirmed its support for transgender and nonbinary student guidance.  
END OF SUMMARY.
