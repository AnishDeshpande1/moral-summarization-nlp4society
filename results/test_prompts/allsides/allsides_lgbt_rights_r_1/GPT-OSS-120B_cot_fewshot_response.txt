**SUMMARY:**  
A group of 14 Wisconsin **parents** has sued the Madison Metropolitan School District, arguing that its policy allowing teachers to use a child’s self‑identified name and pronouns **violates** the **constitutional** **right** of parents to direct their children’s upbringing. The lawsuit, filed by the conservative firm WILL, claims the district’s rules breach the state **due‑process** clause that **protects** parental **consent** and **beliefs**, and infringe on **religious** **freedom** because the parents hold that **God** created only **male** and **female** humans. They contend the policy circumvents parental involvement in decisions that affect a child’s health, identity and future, and that keeping parents “in the dark” about gender‑related issues undermines their ability to provide guidance and professional help. The district says it will stand by its guidance for transgender and nonbinary students, but has not yet responded to the complaint.
