SUMMARY:  
Fourteen Wisconsin parents, represented by the conservative firm Wisconsin Institute for Law and Liberty, have sued the Madison Metropolitan School District, claiming its policy that lets teachers use students’ self‑identified names and pronouns without parental notice violates constitutional due‑process and religious‑freedom rights. The lawsuit argues that parents have a fundamental, God‑ordained right to direct their children’s upbringing and to be informed of any gender‑related decisions that could affect a child’s health and future. By allowing children of any age to “socially transition” at school without parental consent, the district is said to usurp parental authority and conceal gender‑dysphoria issues that parents should address. The plaintiffs contend there is no compelling state interest in keeping such matters secret from parents. The district has not yet responded but maintains its support for transgender and nonbinary students.  

END OF SUMMARY.
