SUMMARY:

The Trump administration has blocked former White House counsel Don McGahn from testifying before Congress about the special counsel report on Russian election interference. The Justice Department released a legal opinion stating that McGahn cannot be forced to testify, citing "long-standing, bipartisan, and constitutional precedent." This move has been met with sharp criticism from Democrats, with House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerry Nadler accusing Trump of trying to protect himself from federal law enforcement. Nadler's committee still expects McGahn to appear on Tuesday, and some Democrats have threatened impeachment if he does not comply with the subpoena. This is the latest example of the Trump administration's "disdain for the law," according to Nadler. The move has sparked outrage among Democrats, with some calling for impeachment proceedings to begin if McGahn does not testify.

END OF SUMMARY.
