SUMMARY:

The Trump administration's move to block former White House counsel Don McGahn from testifying before Congress about the special counsel report on Russian election interference has sparked sharp criticism and even threats of impeachment. The Department of Justice issued a legal opinion stating that lawmakers cannot compel McGahn to answer questions under oath, claiming it is necessary to protect future presidents' ability to execute their responsibilities. However, this move has been condemned as an attempt to obstruct justice and interfere with the investigation into Trump's alleged misconduct.

Democratic leaders have called out the administration's disdain for the law, with one suggesting that impeachment would be warranted if McGahn did not testify. The committee will still meet on Tuesday morning, despite the White House's intervention, and expects McGahn to appear. The move is seen as an effort to impede the truth from being revealed and to obstruct the committee's work in collecting evidence.

The administration has repeatedly shown a disregard for oversight, with officials refusing to comply with congressional subpoenas and instructed others not to testify. This has led some to view Trump's actions as a cancer on the presidency, warranting an impeachment inquiry.

END OF SUMMARY.
