SUMMARY:
Donald Trump has used a Justice Department legal opinion to block former White House counsel Don McGahn from testifying before the House Judiciary Committee about the Mueller report, igniting fierce Democratic condemnation and calls for impeachment. The opinion cites long‑standing precedent that a former presidential aide cannot be compelled to appear under oath, a move the White House says protects the “office of the presidency” for future presidents. Democrats argue this is a blatant attempt to hide evidence of obstruction of justice, noting McGahn’s firsthand knowledge of Trump’s efforts to fire Mueller and pressure Jeff Sessions. Chairman Jerry Nadler called the action “disdain for the law” and warned that continued defiance could trigger an impeachment inquiry, a sentiment echoed by member David Cicilline. The administration’s pattern of blocking oversight—refusing testimony from former security director Carl Kline and Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin’s tax returns—underscores concerns that Trump is placing personal immunity above constitutional accountability. The committee plans to proceed with its hearing and will consider contempt sanctions if McGahn does not appear, reinforcing the principle that no one, not even the president, is above the law.

END OF SUMMARY.
