SUMMARY: President Donald Trump, via a Justice Department legal opinion, has blocked former White House counsel Don McGahn from complying with a House Judiciary Committee subpoena to testify about the Mueller report on Russian election interference. The opinion cites longstanding precedent that a former presidential counsel cannot be compelled to testify. Trump defended the move as protecting the “office of the presidency” for future presidents. Democrats, led by Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerry Nadler and member David Cicilline, condemned the action, calling it obstruction and warning it could trigger an impeachment inquiry if McGahn does not appear. The committee plans to proceed with its hearing and may hold McGahn in contempt. This follows a pattern of the Trump administration resisting congressional oversight, including refusals by former officials to testify and the Treasury Secretary’s refusal to provide Trump’s tax returns. The dispute heightens tensions between the executive and legislative branches over accountability for alleged obstruction of justice. END OF SUMMARY.
