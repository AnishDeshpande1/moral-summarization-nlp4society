SUMMARY:

President Trump has blocked former White House counsel Don McGahn from testifying before Congress about the special counsel report on Russian election interference. This move has been condemned by Democratic lawmakers, who argue that it is an attempt to impede their ability to gather evidence and uphold the rule of law. The Trump administration's actions are seen as a clear example of subverting authority and undermining the integrity of the legislative branch. By blocking McGahn's testimony, Trump is attempting to conceal his own alleged misconduct and obstruct justice. This behavior is a betrayal of the public trust and a disregard for the principle of fairness. If McGahn does not testify, Democratic lawmakers have threatened to open an impeachment inquiry, citing the president's repeated attempts to impede their investigations and block oversight. The move has been criticized as a blatant attempt by Trump to use his power to protect himself from accountability.

END OF SUMMARY.
