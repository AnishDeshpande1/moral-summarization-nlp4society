SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump has blocked former White House counsel Don McGahn from testifying before Congress about the special counsel report on Russian election interference. The Justice Department released a legal opinion stating that lawmakers cannot compel McGahn to answer questions under oath, citing long-standing precedent and constitutional protection for the office of the presidency.

The move was met with sharp criticism from Democratic leaders, who accused Trump of attempting to impede their ability to gather evidence and obstruct justice. House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerry Nadler said the committee will still expect McGahn to appear on Tuesday morning despite the White House's intervention. Some Democrats even suggested that impeachment proceedings could be warranted if McGahn does not comply with the subpoena.

The move is part of a broader pattern of Trump's administration blocking attempts at oversight by the Democratic-controlled House, including instructing former personnel security director Carl Kline not to testify and refusing to hand over Trump's tax returns.
