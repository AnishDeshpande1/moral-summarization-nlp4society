SUMMARY:
Donald Trump has used the Justice Department’s legal opinion to block former White House counsel Don McGahn from complying with a House Judiciary subpoena, a move portrayed as a blatant subversion of congressional authority. Democrats on the committee, led by Chairman Jerry Nadler, condemn the action as an unfair attempt to shield the president from accountability and as a cheating of the rule‑of‑law that obliges officials to testify about the Mueller report’s findings on obstruction of justice. They argue that Trump’s insistence that the decision serves “the office of the presidency” is a betrayal of the public’s trust and a violation of loyalty to the Constitution, putting the president above the law. Rep. David Cicilline warned that continued refusal to testify could trigger an impeachment inquiry, emphasizing that no one—including the commander‑in‑chief—should be exempt from legal scrutiny. The administration’s pattern of blocking oversight, from McGahn to Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin, is depicted as a systematic effort to undermine democratic institutions, cheat the American people out of truth, and betray the loyalty owed to the nation’s governing framework.  
END OF SUMMARY.
