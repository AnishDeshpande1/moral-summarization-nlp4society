SUMMARY:

The Trump administration has blocked former White House counsel Don McGahn from testifying before Congress about the special counsel report on Russian election interference, sparking sharp criticism and even threats of impeachment. The Justice Department claims that lawmakers cannot compel McGahn to answer questions under oath, citing "long-standing, bipartisan, and constitutional precedent." However, this move is seen as an effort to obstruct justice and protect Trump from accountability. McGahn is a central figure in the Mueller report, having threatened to resign when Trump ordered him to have Mueller fired. Democrats condemn the White House's intervention as a "disdain for the law" and an attempt to impede the truth. If McGahn does not respond to the subpoena, some Democrats argue that it would be grounds for impeachment. The Trump administration's repeated attempts to block oversight and conceal evidence have led to accusations of criminal misconduct and a "cancer on the presidency."

END OF SUMMARY.
