SUMMARY:  
The Justice Department, citing long‑standing bipartisan precedent, has issued an opinion that former White House counsel Don McGahn cannot be forced to testify before the House Judiciary Committee about the Mueller report. President Trump’s administration used the opinion to block McGahn’s appearance, arguing the move protects the “office of the presidency” for future presidents. Democrats, led by Judiciary Chairman Jerry Nadler and Rep. David Cicilline, denounced the action as a blatant disregard for the law and an attempt to hide alleged obstruction of justice documented in the Mueller report. They warned that continued refusal could trigger contempt citations and even an impeachment inquiry, emphasizing that no one—including the president—is above the law. The episode fits a pattern of the Trump administration obstructing congressional oversight, as seen in previous refusals by senior officials to comply with subpoenas on security clearances and tax returns. The moral stakes are clear: the administration’s choice to shield the president at the expense of transparency and accountability threatens the fundamental principle that the rule of law supersedes political loyalty.  

END OF SUMMARY.
