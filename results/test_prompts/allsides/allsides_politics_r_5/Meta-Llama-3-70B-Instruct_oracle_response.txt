SUMMARY:

A former Trump campaign adviser, George Papadopoulos, has been cooperating with federal investigators as a "proactive cooperator" after accepting a plea deal for lying to the FBI about his contacts with Russians. This term suggests that Papadopoulos may have worn a wire to help prosecutors in the Office of Special Counsel Robert Mueller, who is investigating Russian interference in the presidential campaign. Papadopoulos lied to FBI agents about his interactions with three Russian nationals, including discussing arranging meetings between Russian government officials and the Trump campaign. He also claimed to have known the Russians prior to joining the Trump campaign, but investigators obtained evidence showing he was lying. The White House downplayed the revelations, saying Papadopoulos played a small part on the campaign and that the Trump team did not collude with the Russian government. However, legal analysts believe that Papadopoulos may have worn a wire, recording conversations with Trump associates, which could be a significant development in the investigation into Russian interference and possible collusion.

END OF SUMMARY.
