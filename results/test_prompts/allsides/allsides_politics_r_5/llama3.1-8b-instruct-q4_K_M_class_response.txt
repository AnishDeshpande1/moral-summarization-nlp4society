SUMMARY:

George Papadopoulos, the former Trump campaign adviser who accepted a plea deal for lying to the FBI about his contacts with Russians, is now considered a proactive cooperator with federal investigators. Court documents reveal that Papadopoulos' status as a "proactive cooperator" means he engaged in undercover work on behalf of the government, possibly wearing a wire and recording conversations with individuals suspected of involvement in criminal activity. This cooperation has been disclosed to the public, which could undermine Papadopoulos' ability to serve as an informant if his role is revealed prematurely. Prosecutors made note of Papadopoulos' efforts to cooperate with the government during his sentencing hearing. His plea deal acknowledged that he lied to agents about arranging meetings between Russian officials and the Trump campaign. The revelation has sparked widespread speculation about the extent of his cooperation, with some experts suggesting it could be a major development in the investigation into Russian interference in the presidential campaign.

END OF SUMMARY.
