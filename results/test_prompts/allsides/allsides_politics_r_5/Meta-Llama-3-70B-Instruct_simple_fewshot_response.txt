SUMMARY:

A former Trump campaign adviser, George Papadopoulos, has been revealed to be a "proactive cooperator" with federal investigators, sparking speculation that he may have worn a wire to help prosecutors in the Office of Special Counsel Robert Mueller's investigation into Russian interference in the presidential campaign. Papadopoulos pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI about his contacts with Russians and has been cooperating with investigators since his arrest in July. The term "proactive cooperator" suggests that Papadopoulos may have engaged in undercover work, such as wearing a wire and meeting with persons suspected of involvement in criminal activity. This revelation has raised questions about the extent of Papadopoulos' cooperation and whether he may have recorded conversations with Trump associates. Legal analysts believe that Papadopoulos may have worn a wire, potentially uncovering new evidence in the investigation. The White House has downplayed the revelations, but the development has significant implications for the ongoing investigation into Russian interference and potential collusion with the Trump campaign.

END OF SUMMARY.
