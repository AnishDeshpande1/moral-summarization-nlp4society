SUMMARY:
George Papadopoulos, a former Trump campaign adviser, has been revealed to be a "proactive cooperator" with federal investigators, sparking speculation that he wore a wire to help prosecutors in the Office of Special Counsel Robert Mueller's investigation into Russian interference in the presidential campaign. Papadopoulos pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI about his contacts with Russians and has been cooperating with the government since his arrest in July. His cooperation has been described as "efforts to cooperate with the Government" and he has been meeting with and providing information to the Government on request. The White House has downplayed the revelations, claiming that Papadopoulos played a small part on the campaign and that the Trump team did not engage in collusion with the Russian government. However, legal analysts suggest that Papadopoulos may have worn a wire, recording conversations secretly with people who are subjects and targets of this investigation, which could be a "very, very big deal".
