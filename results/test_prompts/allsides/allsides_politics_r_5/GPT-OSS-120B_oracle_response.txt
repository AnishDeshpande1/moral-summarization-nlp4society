SUMMARY:  
A court filing revealed that former Trump campaign adviser George Papadopoulos is being labeled a “proactive cooperator” with federal investigators, implying he may have worn a wire to aid the Mueller investigation into Russian interference and possible collusion. Prosecutors asked a judge to seal filings, arguing that premature disclosure of his arrest would undermine his ability to cooperate. The plea deal, filed after Papadopoulos was arrested for lying to FBI agents about contacts with three Russian nationals, requires him to continue meeting with and providing information to the government. Evidence showed he had claimed prior knowledge of the Russians and discussed arranging meetings between Russian officials and the Trump campaign, while a London‑based professor warned that the Russian government had obtained “thousands” of Hillary Clinton’s emails. The White House downplayed his role, saying he played a small part and that there was no collusion. The revelation came as Mueller’s office unsealed an indictment charging Paul Manafort and Rick Gates with money laundering and conspiracy, though the indictment contains no collusion evidence. Legal analysts speculated that Papadopoulos may have been recording conversations, a development that could dramatically expand the scope of the investigation.  

END OF SUMMARY.
