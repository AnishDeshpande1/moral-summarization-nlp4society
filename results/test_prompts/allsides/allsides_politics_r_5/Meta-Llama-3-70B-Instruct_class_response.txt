SUMMARY:

George Papadopoulos, a former Trump campaign adviser, has been revealed as a "proactive cooperator" with federal investigators, sparking speculation that he wore a wire to help prosecutors in the Office of Special Counsel Robert Mueller's investigation into Russian interference in the presidential campaign. Papadopoulos lied to the FBI about his contacts with Russians and acknowledged his deception as part of his plea deal. His cooperation efforts have been significant, with prosecutors noting that premature disclosure of his arrest would "significantly undermine" his ability to serve as a proactive cooperator. This has led to speculation that Papadopoulos engaged in undercover work, such as wearing a wire and meeting with persons suspected of involvement in criminal activity. The White House has downplayed the revelations, but the development has touched off widespread speculation about the extent of Papadopoulos' cooperation and its potential implications for the investigation into collusion, money laundering, and conspiracy.

END OF SUMMARY.
