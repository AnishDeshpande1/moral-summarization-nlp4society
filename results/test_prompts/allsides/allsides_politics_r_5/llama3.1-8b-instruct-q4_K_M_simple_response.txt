SUMMARY:

Former Trump campaign adviser George Papadopoulos has been cooperating with federal investigators as a "proactive cooperator", according to court documents. This term suggests that he may have worn a wire or engaged in undercover work to help prosecutors, specifically Robert Mueller's team investigating Russian interference in the presidential campaign. Papadopoulos pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI about his contacts with Russians and has been cooperating with investigators since July. Prosecutors have noted that they will consider his cooperation as part of his sentencing. Experts speculate that Papadopoulos' cooperation may involve wearing a wire or recording conversations with Trump associates, which could potentially reveal new information about Russian interference.

The White House downplayed the revelations, stating that Papadopoulos played a small role in the campaign and there was no collusion with Russia.
