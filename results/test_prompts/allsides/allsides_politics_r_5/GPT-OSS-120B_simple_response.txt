SUMMARY:  
The court documents reveal that George Papadopoulos, a former Trump campaign adviser who pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI about his contacts with Russian nationals, has been labeled a “proactive cooperator.”  This morally charged term implies that Papadopoulos may have been working undercover for the government—potentially wearing a wire and feeding recordings of his conversations with Trump associates to Special Counsel Robert Mueller’s team.  Prosecutors argue that keeping his cooperation secret is essential to preserve its effectiveness, and they promise to note his “efforts to cooperate” at sentencing if he continues to provide information.  Papadopoulos admitted he deceived agents about his meetings with three Russians and about a London professor’s claim that Russia possessed “thousands” of Hillary Clinton emails.  While the White House downplays his role, legal experts speculate that his covert recordings could dramatically expand the investigation’s reach.  The revelation comes as Mueller’s office unsealed indictments against Paul Manafort and Rick Gates, though those charges still lack direct collusion evidence.  

END OF SUMMARY.
