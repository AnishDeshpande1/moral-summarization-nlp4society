SUMMARY:

Former Trump campaign adviser George Papadopoulos, who accepted a plea deal for lying to the FBI about his contacts with Russians, is now considered a "proactive cooperator" in the investigation into Russian interference in the presidential campaign. This means that Papadopoulos has been engaging in undercover work on behalf of the government, possibly wearing a wire and meeting with individuals suspected of involvement in criminal activity. The government's disclosure suggests that Papadopoulos was recording conversations secretly with Trump associates as part of his cooperation agreement. This revelation touches off widespread speculation about the extent of Papadopoulos' cooperation and its potential impact on the investigation. Prosecutors have indicated that they will note Papadopoulos' efforts to cooperate at his sentencing, provided he continues to meet with and provide information to the government on request. The Trump team has downplayed the significance of Papadopoulos' actions, but experts believe that his cooperation could be a significant development in the investigation into collusion between the Trump campaign and Russian government.

END OF SUMMARY.
