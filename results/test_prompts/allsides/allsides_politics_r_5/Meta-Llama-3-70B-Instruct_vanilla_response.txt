SUMMARY:

George Papadopoulos, a former Trump campaign adviser, has been cooperating with federal investigators as a "proactive cooperator" since his arrest in July. This term suggests that Papadopoulos may have worn a wire to record conversations with Trump associates, helping prosecutors in the Office of Special Counsel Robert Mueller's investigation into Russian interference in the presidential campaign. Court documents reveal that Papadopoulos lied to FBI agents about his contacts with Russians, and he has since acknowledged his wrongdoing and agreed to cooperate with the government. Prosecutors have asked a federal judge to seal court filings in the case, citing that "premature disclosure" of his arrest would undermine his ability to serve as a cooperative witness. Legal analysts speculate that Papadopoulos may have recorded conversations with Trump associates, potentially uncovering new evidence in the investigation.
