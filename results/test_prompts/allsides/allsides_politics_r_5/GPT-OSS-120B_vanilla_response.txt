SUMMARY: A court filing revealed that George Papadopoulos, the former Trump campaign adviser who pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI about contacts with Russians, is designated a “proactive cooperator.” This term suggests he may be working undercover for Special Counsel Robert Mueller, potentially wearing a wire or recording conversations with Trump associates. Prosecutors asked a judge to seal related filings, arguing that premature disclosure would jeopardize his cooperation. Papadopoulos admitted to falsifying statements about his interactions with three Russian nationals and discussing possible meetings between the campaign and Russian officials. While the White House downplayed his role, legal analysts speculated his cooperation could involve covert recordings used to further the investigation. The revelation coincided with Mueller’s indictment of former campaign chairman Paul Manafort and associate Rick Gates on money‑laundering and conspiracy charges, though the indictment contains no collusion evidence. END OF SUMMARY.
