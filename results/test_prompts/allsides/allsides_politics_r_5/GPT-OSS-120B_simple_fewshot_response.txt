SUMMARY:
Federal prosecutors have labeled former Trump campaign adviser George Papadopoulos a “proactive cooperator,” a term that implies he may have worn a wire and fed investigators secret recordings to aid the Mueller probe into Russian interference. Court filings seeking to seal the case argue that disclosing his arrest would jeopardize his ability to continue cooperating. Papadopoulos, who pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI about contacts with Russian nationals, allegedly arranged meetings between the campaign and Moscow officials and was warned by a London professor that Russia possessed “thousands” of Hillary Clinton’s emails. While the White House downplays his role, the revelation fuels concerns that the Trump team concealed wrongdoing and that the investigation may have been bolstered by insider recordings. Legal analysts argue that Papadopoulos’ covert cooperation could dramatically expand the scope of the probe, underscoring the administration’s pattern of deception and the importance of rigorous accountability to protect the integrity of U.S. elections.

END OF SUMMARY.
