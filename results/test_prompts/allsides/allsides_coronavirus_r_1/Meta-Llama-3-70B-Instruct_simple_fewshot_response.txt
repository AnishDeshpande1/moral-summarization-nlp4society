SUMMARY:

In Rockland County, New York, officials have taken drastic measures to combat the spread of COVID-19 by issuing subpoenas to eight individuals who refused to cooperate with contact tracers. The individuals, allegedly infected with the coronavirus, had declined interview requests to identify others they came into contact with at a June 17 party. County Executive Ed Day and Health Commissioner Patricia Rupert emphasized that the subpoenas are necessary to protect public health, with non-compliance resulting in a $2,000 daily fine. They stressed that their goal is not to punish, but to ensure that infected individuals do the right thing for their neighbors by voluntarily speaking with health officials. The officials' frustrations echo those of health officials nationwide, who face resistance from infected individuals in their efforts to contain the virus through contact tracing. By resorting to subpoenas, Rockland County officials are taking a firm stance in prioritizing public health over individual obstinacy.

END OF SUMMARY.
