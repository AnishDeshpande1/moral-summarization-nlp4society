SUMMARY:
Rockland County officials issued subpoenas to eight individuals who tested positive for COVID‑19 after a June 17 party, after those people repeatedly refused to cooperate with contact tracers. County Executive Ed Day warned that “ignorance, stupidity, or obstinance” must not endanger public health, and health commissioner Patricia Rupert said non‑compliance would cost $2,000 per day. The subpoenas are intended to compel interviews that can identify further exposures and prevent additional spread. Officials stressed that they are not seeking punishment but merely asking residents to do the right thing for their neighbors by providing information that enables timely quarantine of contacts. The county’s frustration mirrors a broader national challenge: many infected individuals ignore calls, deny attendance, or have others answer falsely, undermining the essential public‑health tool of contact tracing needed to contain the virus. By enforcing cooperation, Rockland aims to protect the health of the community and stop the outbreak from growing. END OF SUMMARY.
