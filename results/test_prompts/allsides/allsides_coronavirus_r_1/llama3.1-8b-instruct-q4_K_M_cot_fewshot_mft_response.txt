SUMMARY:
Officials in New York's Rockland County have issued subpoenas to eight people who allegedly contracted COVID-19 for refusing to speak with contact tracers voluntarily. The county executive, Ed Day, stated that the health of their community will not be "compromised because of ignorance, stupidity, or obstinance." Patricia Rupert, the county's health commissioner, added that failure to comply with the subpoenas would result in a $2,000 per day fine. The officials aim to use the contact tracers to determine who else may have been infected and prevent further spread of the virus. Day emphasized that they only want to speak with those who are positive for COVID-19 to keep their neighbors safe, but the individuals' refusal to cooperate has forced them to resort to subpoenas. The goal is to contain a coronavirus infection cluster that began at a June 17 party. By issuing these subpoenas, the officials hope to ensure the necessary cooperation from those who have contracted the disease and prevent further harm to the community.
