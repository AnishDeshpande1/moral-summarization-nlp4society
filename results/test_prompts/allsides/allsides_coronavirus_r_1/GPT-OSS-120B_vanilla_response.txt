SUMMARY: Rockland County officials in New York have issued subpoenas to eight individuals who tested positive for COVID‑19 after refusing to cooperate with contact tracers about a June 17 party in Clarkstown. County Executive Ed Day warned that non‑compliance threatens public health, while Health Commissioner Patricia Rupert said the subpoenas carry a $2,000‑per‑day penalty and are intended to compel interviews to identify further contacts and halt virus spread. Rupert described the party host as symptomatic yet still holding the event, and noted that many infected attendees either ignore calls, deny attendance, or provide false information, hindering tracing efforts. The county emphasizes that contact tracing is critical for notifying exposures and enforcing 14‑day quarantines, but officials say they are struggling to contain the outbreak cluster linked to the party. END OF SUMMARY.
