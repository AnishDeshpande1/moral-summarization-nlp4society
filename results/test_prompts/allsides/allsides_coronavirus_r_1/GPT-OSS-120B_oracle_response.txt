SUMMARY:  
Rockland County officials announced they have issued subpoenas to eight people who tested positive for COVID‑19 after a June 17 party, because those individuals have refused to speak with contact tracers voluntarily. County Executive Ed Day warned that the health of the county will not be compromised by ignorance, stupidity, or obstinance, and Health Commissioner Patricia Rupert said non‑cooperation will be costly, imposing a $2,000‑per‑day penalty. Rupert explained that the subpoenas are meant to force the infected partygoers to answer questions, prevent further spread, and keep the virus from stemming unchecked. She described how many have been refusing to answer phones, denying attendance, or having parents answer falsely, which hampers the essential contact‑tracing effort needed to stem the outbreak. The county says the host held the party despite showing symptoms, and officials stress that cooperation is required to protect neighbors and stop the spread.  
END OF SUMMARY.
