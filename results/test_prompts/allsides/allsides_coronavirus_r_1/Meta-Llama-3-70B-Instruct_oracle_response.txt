SUMMARY:

In Rockland County, New York, officials have issued subpoenas to eight individuals who have refused to cooperate with contact tracers, citing "ignorance, stupidity, or obstinance" as the reason. The county executive, Ed Day, emphasized that the "health of our county" will not be "compromised" due to such behavior. The individuals, who attended a party on June 17, have declined interview requests, refusing to answer questions about who they came into contact with, thereby hindering efforts to prevent the further spread of COVID-19. Health officials have been met with refusal, with some individuals hanging up or denying attendance at the party, despite evidence to the contrary. The county's health commissioner, Patricia Rupert, stressed that contact tracing is essential to stemming the spread of the disease, and that the refusal to cooperate is "costly" - with fines of $2,000 per day. Officials are urging individuals to "do the right thing" and voluntarily speak with health officials to keep the disease from spreading.

END OF SUMMARY.
