**SUMMARY:**

Health officials in Rockland County, New York have issued subpoenas to eight people who allegedly contracted the coronavirus after attending a party in June. The individuals are being forced to comply with contact tracers due to their ignorance and obstinance in refusing to voluntarily speak with them. According to Patricia Rupert, the county's health commissioner, "the health of our county" is at stake because of these people's stupidity in putting others at risk by not cooperating with efforts to contain the spread of the virus. The officials are using the subpoenas to compel the individuals to talk about who they were in contact with and prevent further spread of the disease. As Ed Day, the county executive, said, "we want people to do the right thing for their neighbors" and voluntarily speak to health officials.
