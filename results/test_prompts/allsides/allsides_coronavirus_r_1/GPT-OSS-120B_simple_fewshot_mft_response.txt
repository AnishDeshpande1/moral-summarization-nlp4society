SUMMARY:
Rockland County officials announced that they have issued subpoenas to eight individuals who tested positive for COVID‑19 after a June 17 party and have refused to cooperate with contact tracers. County Executive Ed Day warned that “the health of our county will not be compromised because of ignorance, stupidity, or obstinance,” emphasizing the authority of public‑health mandates. Health Commissioner Patricia Rupert said non‑cooperation forces the county to compel testimony at a cost of $2,000 per day, stressing that the subpoenas are a necessary exercise of governmental authority to protect residents. Both officials framed the action as a duty to “do the right thing for our neighbors,” linking the enforcement of tracing rules to the moral imperative of caring for the community and preventing further harm. They highlighted that many infected partygoers are evading calls, denying attendance, or providing false information, which endangers public health. By using legal tools to ensure compliance, the county aims to safeguard the community and stop the spread of the virus.  
END OF SUMMARY.
