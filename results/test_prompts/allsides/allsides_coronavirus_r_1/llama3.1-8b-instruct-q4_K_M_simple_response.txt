SUMMARY:

Officials in New York's Rockland County have issued subpoenas to eight people who allegedly contracted COVID-19 at a June 17 party, but refused to speak with contact tracers voluntarily. The individuals are being fined $2,000 per day for their refusal, which health officials say is putting the community at risk. According to Patricia Rupert, the county's health commissioner, many of those infected or in contact with them have been ignoring phone calls and refusing to cooperate with investigators. This has forced officials to take more drastic measures to contain the spread of the virus.

Health officials emphasize that their goal is not to punish individuals but to prevent further transmission by identifying and isolating those who may be carrying the virus. The county executive, Ed Day, stated that officials want people to "do the right thing" and voluntarily cooperate with contact tracers, but some individuals are putting their neighbors at risk through their refusal.

END OF SUMMARY.
