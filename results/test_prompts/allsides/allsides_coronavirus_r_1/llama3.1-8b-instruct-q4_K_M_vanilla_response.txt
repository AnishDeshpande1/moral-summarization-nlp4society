SUMMARY:

 Officials in Rockland County, New York have issued subpoenas to eight people who allegedly contracted COVID-19 at a June 17 party but refused to speak with contact tracers voluntarily. The county executive and health commissioner stated that the health of the community will not be compromised due to ignorance or obstinance. Those who fail to comply with the subpoenas will face fines of $2,000 per day. The officials are trying to compel the individuals to talk about their contacts to prevent further spread of the virus. According to public health officials, contact tracing is essential in stemming the COVID-19 spread. The infected partygoers have allegedly refused to answer phone calls or speak with contact tracers, forcing officials to resort to subpoenas. The county is struggling to contain a coronavirus infection cluster that began at the June 17 party.

END OF SUMMARY.
