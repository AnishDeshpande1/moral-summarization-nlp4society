SUMMARY: Rockland County officials, frustrated by eight COVID‑19 positive partygoers who refused to cooperate with contact tracers, have issued subpoenas to compel interviews about a June 17 gathering in Clarkstown. County Executive Ed Day warned that “ignorance, stupidity, or obstinance” will not be allowed to jeopardize public health, while Health Commissioner Patricia Rupert announced a $2,000‑per‑day penalty for non‑compliance. Authorities argue the subpoenas are a last resort to protect neighbors and prevent further spread, emphasizing that the party host held symptoms yet proceeded with the event. Officials stress that voluntary cooperation is a moral duty to the community; the lack of answers, false denials, and parental cover‑ups are portrayed as reckless behavior that endangers others. By invoking legal force, the county seeks to uphold collective responsibility and enforce the ethical imperative that individuals act for the common good during the pandemic. END OF SUMMARY.
