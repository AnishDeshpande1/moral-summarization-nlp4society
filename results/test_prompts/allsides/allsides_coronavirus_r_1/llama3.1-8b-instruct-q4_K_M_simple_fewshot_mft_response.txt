SUMMARY:

In Rockland County, New York, officials have issued subpoenas to eight people allegedly infected by the coronavirus for refusing to speak with contact tracers voluntarily. The county executive, Ed Day, and health commissioner, Patricia Rupert, emphasized that they are not trying to get anyone in trouble but rather want to identify others who may have contracted the disease to prevent further spread. However, these individuals have been ignoring or evading contact from health officials, forcing the county to resort to subpoenas. The officials stressed the importance of cooperation in containing the outbreak and preventing harm to others. Day said that failure to comply with the subpoenas would result in a $2,000 per day fine. Rupert lamented that some individuals are refusing to answer their phones or provide information about who they have been in contact with, hindering efforts to control the spread of the virus.

END OF SUMMARY.
