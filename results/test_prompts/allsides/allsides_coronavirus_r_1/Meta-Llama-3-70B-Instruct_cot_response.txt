SUMMARY:
In Rockland County, New York, officials have issued subpoenas to eight individuals who refused to cooperate with contact tracers, putting the "health of our county" at risk. Ed Day, the county executive, emphasized that the county will not compromise due to "ignorance, stupidity, or obstinance." Patricia Rupert, the health commissioner, warned that failure to comply will be "costly," with a daily fine of $2,000. The individuals, who allegedly contracted COVID-19 at a June 17 party, have refused to answer phones or speak with contact tracers, causing "frustration" among health officials. Day urged people to do the "right thing" for their neighbors and cooperate voluntarily. However, the county was forced to resort to subpoenas due to the individuals' "refusal" to talk. Rupert stressed the importance of "necessary cooperation" to contain the outbreak, which has been hindered by "promises" from parents that their adult children have been home consistently, when they have not.
