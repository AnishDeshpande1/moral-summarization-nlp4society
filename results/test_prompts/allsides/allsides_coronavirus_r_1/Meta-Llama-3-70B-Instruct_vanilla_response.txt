SUMMARY:

Officials in Rockland County, New York, have issued subpoenas to eight individuals who allegedly contracted COVID-19 at a party on June 17. The individuals have refused to cooperate with contact tracers, ignoring interview requests and failing to provide information about their contacts. County Executive Ed Day and Health Commissioner Patricia Rupert warned that failure to comply with the subpoenas would result in a $2,000 daily fine. The officials emphasized that their goal is to prevent the spread of the virus, not to penalize individuals. They expressed frustration with the lack of cooperation, citing instances of people hanging up, denying attendance at the party, or having parents answer for them. Contact tracing is crucial to stemming the spread of COVID-19, and the officials are determined to take action to protect public health.

END OF SUMMARY.
