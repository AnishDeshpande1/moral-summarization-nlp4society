SUMMARY:
Severe COVID‑19 illness in children is rare, and the risk of death is far lower than for adults, yet serious cases can occur, just as they do with influenza, which regularly kills many children. Therefore, we should not close schools simply because of COVID‑19; instead, the key to keeping schools safe lies in community‑wide control of the virus. This requires the three W’s—wear a mask, wash your hands, watch your distance—plus robust testing, effective isolation, thorough tracing, and supportive quarantine that provides services such as alternative housing when needed. The CDC’s guidelines give eight safety steps for reopening schools. First, shield the most vulnerable by allowing remote participation for high‑risk children, staff, and those with underlying conditions when community spread is high. Second, reduce risk by canceling large assemblies, moving meals to classrooms, using pre‑packaged food, and limiting shared surfaces, while maintaining essential services like in‑school meals. Activities such as choir, large band rehearsals, and many team sports should be out, though outdoor PE and small‑group activities can continue with masking and distancing. Localities must tailor these measures to their current transmission levels.

END OF SUMMARY.
