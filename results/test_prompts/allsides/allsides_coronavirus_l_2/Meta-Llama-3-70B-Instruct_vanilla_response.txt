SUMMARY:

The risk of severe illness and death from COVID-19 in children is low, but serious illness can occur. Closing schools is not necessary, as long as communities control the spread of the virus. The CDC has released guidelines for safely reopening schools, which require adhering to the three W's (wear a mask, wash your hands, watch your distance) and implementing strategic testing, isolation, and contact tracing. To reopen safely, schools should shield the most vulnerable, reduce risk wherever possible, and modify essential services. This includes enabling remote participation for high-risk students and staff, reducing large assemblies, and modifying services like meal preparation and library capacity. In areas with good control, some activities like band and orchestra practice may continue, while others like team sports may be too risky. Recess and physical education classes can continue with outdoor play, masks, and physical distancing.

END OF SUMMARY.
