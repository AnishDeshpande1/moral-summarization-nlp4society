SUMMARY:

While severe illness from COVID-19 in children is rare, it's crucial to keep our schools safe. The key to achieving this lies not within the school walls, but in how well communities control the coronavirus throughout the community. Adhering to the three W's - wear a mask, wash your hands, and watch your distance - and implementing strategic testing, effective isolation, complete contact tracing, and supportive quarantine are essential. By doing so, we can keep our schools safe and prevent the spread of the disease. The CDC has released guidelines for safely reopening schools, which must be tailored to each locality's unique needs. To reduce risk, schools can take measures such as shielding the most vulnerable, reducing large in-person assemblies, and modifying essential services. By taking these steps, we can control the spread of COVID-19 and keep our schools safe. By keeping our communities safe, we can ensure a safe reopening of schools.

END OF SUMMARY.
