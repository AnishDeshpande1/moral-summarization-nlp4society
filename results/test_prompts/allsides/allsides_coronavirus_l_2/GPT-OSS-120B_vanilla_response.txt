SUMMARY: The article argues that severe COVID‑19 illness in children is rare and far less lethal than for adults, comparable to the risk from seasonal flu, which does not justify school closures. The key to safe schools is strong community control of the virus through mask‑wearing, hand‑washing, distancing, testing, isolation, contact tracing, and supportive quarantine measures. The CDC provides reopening guidelines that districts must adapt locally; in areas with high transmission, in‑person schooling may be infeasible. When reopening, schools should follow eight safety steps, notably protecting vulnerable students and staff, limiting large gatherings, modifying cafeterias and libraries, eliminating high‑risk activities like group singing, and restricting team sports. Outdoor recess and PE can continue with masks and distancing, and essential services such as meals should remain. Tailored, evidence‑based strategies are essential for a safer fall semester. END OF SUMMARY.
