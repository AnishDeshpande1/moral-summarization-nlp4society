SUMMARY:
COVID‑19 rarely causes severe illness or death in children, and its risk is far lower than that of seasonal flu, which still claims many young lives each year. Therefore, school closures are not justified by the virus itself; the key to keeping schools safe lies in controlling community transmission. This requires universal adherence to the three W’s—wear masks, wash hands, watch distance—plus robust testing, isolation, contact tracing, and supportive quarantine measures. The CDC’s reopening guidelines, adapted locally, outline eight safety steps, foremost among them protecting the most vulnerable (children with health risks, older staff) by allowing remote participation when community spread is high. Additional measures include eliminating large assemblies, modifying cafeterias and libraries, suspending choir, limiting sports, and conducting outdoor, masked, distanced recess. In regions where the virus is well‑controlled, in‑person schooling can proceed safely under these protocols. The moral lesson is clear: safeguarding schools depends not on shutting them down, but on collective community action to suppress the virus and protect the most at‑risk members of society.  
END OF SUMMARY.
