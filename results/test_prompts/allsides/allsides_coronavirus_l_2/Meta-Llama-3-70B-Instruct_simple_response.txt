SUMMARY:

While severe illness from COVID-19 in children is rare, it's crucial to take measures to ensure schools remain safe. Closing schools isn't necessary, but rather, controlling the coronavirus in the community is key. Adhering to the three W's (wear a mask, wash your hands, watch your distance) and implementing strategic testing, isolation, and contact tracing can help box in the virus. The CDC has released guidelines for safely reopening schools, which must be tailored to each locality's unique needs. To reopen safely, schools should shield the most vulnerable, reduce risk wherever possible, and modify essential services. This includes enabling remote participation for high-risk individuals, reducing large gatherings, and modifying activities like choir rehearsals, team sports, and recess. By following these measures, schools can reopen safely and provide a better fall for students.

END OF SUMMARY.
