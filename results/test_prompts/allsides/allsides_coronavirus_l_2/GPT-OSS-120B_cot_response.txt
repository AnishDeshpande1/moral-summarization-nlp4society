**SUMMARY:**  
Severe COVID‑19 illness in children is rare, and the risk of death is far lower than for adults, yet serious cases—like the rare inflammatory syndrome—do occur, just as they do with influenza, which routinely kills hundreds of children. Schools should not be closed solely because of COVID‑19; the most important protective measure is community‑wide control of the virus. This requires adhering to the three W’s—*wear* a mask, *wash* hands, and *watch* physical *distance*—and employing strategic *testing*, effective *isolation*, thorough *tracing*, and *supportive* *quarantine* (including services and alternative housing). The CDC’s *guidelines* call for districts to *shield* the *vulnerable*—children, older staff, and those with underlying conditions—by offering remote participation when community *risk* is high. Schools must *reduce* *risk* by canceling large assemblies, modifying cafeterias, limiting shared surfaces, and adjusting activities such as choir and team sports. Essential services like meals must continue, while other programs may be *modified* or limited. In areas with good *control*, in‑person schooling can be *possible* if these eight safety measures are followed.
