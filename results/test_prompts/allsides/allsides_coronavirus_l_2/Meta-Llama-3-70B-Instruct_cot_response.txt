SUMMARY:
Keeping schools safe during the COVID-19 pandemic requires a community-wide effort to control the spread of the virus. While severe illness from COVID-19 in children is rare, it's essential to take precautions to shield the most vulnerable, such as children, older staff, and those with underlying health conditions. Schools can reduce risk by modifying certain activities, such as canceling large in-person student assemblies and choir rehearsals, and implementing safety measures like wearing masks, washing hands, and watching physical distance. In areas where the coronavirus is under good control, some activities like band and orchestra practice may be able to continue. By following these measures, schools can reopen safely and provide essential services, such as in-school meals, while supporting the well-being of students and staff. END OF SUMMARY.
