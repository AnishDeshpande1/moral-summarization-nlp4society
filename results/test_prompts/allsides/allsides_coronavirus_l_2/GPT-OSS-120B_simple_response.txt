SUMMARY:
The author argues that while COVID‑19 can rarely cause severe illness in children, its risk to them is far lower than that of influenza, and schools should not be closed solely because of the virus. The moral responsibility lies with the broader community to control transmission through the “three W’s”—wear masks, wash hands, watch distance—plus robust testing, isolation, contact tracing, and supportive quarantine. By following CDC guidance and tailoring plans to local conditions, schools can safely reopen in most districts. Key moral steps include protecting the most vulnerable (allowing high‑risk students and staff to learn remotely unless community spread is minimal) and reducing unnecessary risks (cancelling large assemblies, modifying meals, limiting shared surfaces, suspending choir, and restricting team sports). Essential services like school meals must continue, while activities such as recess can proceed outdoors with masks and distancing. The overarching ethical message is that safeguarding schools depends not on shutting them down but on collective community action to suppress the virus.  
END OF SUMMARY.
