SUMMARY:

The risk of severe illness and death from COVID-19 in children is low, but serious illness can occur. Closing schools is not necessarily the solution, as it is the community's control of the coronavirus that matters most. The CDC has released guidelines for safely reopening schools, which must be tailored to each locality's unique needs. To reopen safely, schools should shield the most vulnerable, reduce risk wherever possible, and modify essential services. This includes enabling remote participation for high-risk individuals, reducing in-person assemblies, and modifying services like meal provision and extracurricular activities. By following these measures, schools can prioritize the care and safety of their students and staff. It is the responsibility of authorities to ensure that schools reopen in a way that minimizes harm and maximizes the well-being of all individuals involved. By doing so, we can create a safer and more caring environment for our children to learn and thrive.

END OF SUMMARY.
