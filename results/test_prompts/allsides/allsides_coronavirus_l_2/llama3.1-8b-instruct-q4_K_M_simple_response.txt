SUMMARY:

Despite the low risk of severe illness from COVID-19 in children, schools can still take measures to ensure safety. Closing schools due to the virus is not necessary, but rather depends on how well communities control the spread of the disease. The key to keeping schools safe lies in controlling the virus throughout the community through measures such as wearing masks, washing hands, and social distancing. This requires strategic testing, effective isolation, contact tracing, and quarantine support.

Schools should prioritize the vulnerable, including children with underlying health conditions, by allowing them to participate remotely when possible. To reduce risk, large gatherings, in-person student assemblies, and shared food preparation can be avoided or modified. Essential services, such as meals for students who depend on them, may need to continue with modifications.

Schools should develop solutions tailored to their unique needs and follow the CDC's guidelines for safely reopening schools. By taking these measures, a safe return to in-person schooling is possible, even in areas where the virus is under control.

END OF SUMMARY.
