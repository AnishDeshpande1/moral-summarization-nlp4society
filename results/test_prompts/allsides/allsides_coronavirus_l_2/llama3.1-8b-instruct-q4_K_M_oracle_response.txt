SUMMARY:

To ensure the safety of children in schools, it is crucial to control the spread of COVID-19 within communities through measures such as adhering to the three W's (wear a mask, wash your hands, watch your distance) and strategic testing, isolation, tracing, and quarantine—providing services for those affected. By shielding the most vulnerable individuals, including children, older staff, and those with underlying health conditions, schools can reduce risk and reopen safely. This requires reducing large in-person gatherings, such as student assemblies and choir rehearsals, and implementing modifications to essential services like libraries and cafeterias. Physical-education classes can continue if students adhere to physical-distancing guidelines while playing outdoors in small groups.

Schools should aim to reopen by following these basic safety measures: shield the most vulnerable, reduce risk wherever possible, and provide testing, isolation, tracing, and quarantine—providing services as necessary. By taking control of COVID-19 within their communities, schools can create a safe environment for students to learn and thrive.

END OF SUMMARY.
