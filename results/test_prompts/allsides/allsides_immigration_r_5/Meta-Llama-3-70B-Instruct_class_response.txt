SUMMARY:

Former President Obama strongly condemned President Trump's decision to end the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program, calling it "cruel" and lacking "basic decency". Obama argued that DACA recipients "pose no threat" and "are not taking away anything from the rest of us", and that targeting them is "wrong" because they have done nothing wrong. He emphasized that these young people want to contribute to the country, starting new businesses, staffing labs, serving in the military, and otherwise contributing to the nation. Obama questioned the morality of deporting individuals who are an integral part of American communities, asking "What if our kid's science teacher, or our friendly neighbor turns out to be a Dreamer? Where are we supposed to send her?" He stressed that the decision was a "moral question" and not legally required, and that kicking out DACA recipients won't improve the economy or benefit Americans.

END OF SUMMARY.
