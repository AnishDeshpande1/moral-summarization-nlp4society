SUMMARY:

Former President Obama strongly criticized President Trump's decision to end the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) amnesty program, calling it "cruel" and "contrary to our spirit, and to common sense." Obama argued that DACA recipients pose no threat and are not taking away anything from others, and that targeting them is morally wrong. He claimed that these young people are contributing members of society who want to start new businesses, staff labs, serve in the military, and otherwise contribute to the country they love. Obama's criticism was framed as a moral imperative, with him stating that Trump's decision is not required legally, but rather a "political decision, and a moral question." By using language like "cruel" and emphasizing the harm caused to these young people, Obama highlighted his concern for their well-being and emphasized the importance of showing compassion and empathy.

END OF SUMMARY.
