SUMMARY:

Former President Obama strongly criticized President Trump's decision to end the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program, calling it "cruel" and lacking "basic decency". Obama argued that DACA recipients pose no threat and are not taking anything away from others, but rather want to contribute to the country. He questioned the morality of targeting young people who are in the US through no fault of their own, and asked where they would be sent if deported. Obama claimed that the decision was a political choice, not a legal requirement, and that it would not lower unemployment, lighten taxes, or raise wages. He had previously hinted that he would speak out if Trump took action on DACA, despite precedent against former presidents criticizing the current president.

END OF SUMMARY.
