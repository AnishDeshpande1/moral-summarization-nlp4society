SUMMARY:

Former President Obama strongly criticized President Trump for ending the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program, calling the decision "cruel" and lacking "basic decency." Obama argued that DACA recipients "pose no threat" and "are not taking away anything from the rest of us," and that targeting them is "wrong" and "self-defeating." He claimed that the decision was a "political" and "moral" choice, rather than a legal necessity. Obama emphasized that DACA recipients are contributing members of society, including teachers, neighbors, and first responders, and that deporting them would not improve the economy or benefit Americans. The former president's comments were a rare instance of a former president publicly criticizing the current president.

END OF SUMMARY.
