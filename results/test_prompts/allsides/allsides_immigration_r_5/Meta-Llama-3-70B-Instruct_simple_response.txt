SUMMARY:

Former President Obama strongly condemned President Trump's decision to end the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program, calling it "cruel" and lacking "basic decency". Obama argued that DACA recipients, often referred to as "Dreamers", pose no threat and are not taking anything away from others. He emphasized that they want to contribute to the country by starting businesses, working in labs, serving in the military, and more. Obama questioned the moral grounds of targeting these young people, who are in the US through no fault of their own, and asked what would happen if a Dreamer was a science teacher or friendly neighbor. He stressed that the decision was political, not legally required, and would not improve unemployment rates, taxes, or wages. Obama's passionate response underscored the moral imperative to protect these young people and allow them to continue contributing to the country they love.

END OF SUMMARY.
