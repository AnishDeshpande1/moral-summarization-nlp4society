SUMMARY:
Former President Barack Obama condemned President Trump’s decision to dismantle the DACA program, calling the move “cruel,” lacking “basic decency,” and “contrary to our spirit and common sense.” Obama argued that Dreamers pose no threat, contribute to the economy, serve in the military, and enrich communities, so targeting them is morally wrong and self‑defeating. He framed the rollback as a political choice, not a legal necessity, and warned that deporting these young people would harm ordinary Americans—teachers, neighbors, softball teammates, first responders, and ROTC cadets—without any benefit to employment or taxes. By invoking empathy and the promise of the American Dream, Obama positioned the issue as a clear moral failing on Trump’s part, urging the nation to protect a vulnerable group that has done nothing wrong. END OF SUMMARY.
