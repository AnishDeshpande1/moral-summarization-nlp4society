SUMMARY: Former President Barack Obama condemned President Trump’s decision to dismantle the DACA program, labeling the move “cruel,” lacking “basic decency,” and contrary to American spirit and common sense. Obama argued that DACA recipients—often called “Dreamers”—pose no threat, contribute to the economy, serve in the military, and enrich communities as teachers, neighbors, and first responders. He emphasized that targeting these young people, who are here through no fault of their own, is morally wrong and self‑defeating, offering no economic benefit such as lower unemployment or reduced taxes. Obama framed the rollback as a political, not legal, choice and urged compassion, asking what would happen to a child’s science teacher or a friendly neighbor if they were forced to return to a country they barely know. He warned that expelling Dreamers would betray the nation’s values and undermine the future contributions of a generation eager to serve the country that gave them opportunity. END OF SUMMARY.
