SUMMARY: Former President Barack Obama sharply criticized President Trump for ending the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program, calling the move “cruel,” lacking basic decency, and contrary to common sense. In a lengthy Facebook post, Obama argued that DACA recipients—often called “Dreamers”—pose no threat, contribute to the economy, serve in the military, and enrich communities. He stressed that the decision to roll back DACA is a political and moral choice, not a legal necessity, and warned that removing these young people would not improve employment or tax rates. Obama highlighted personal examples, such as a science teacher, a softball teammate, a first responder, and an ROTC cadet, to illustrate their integration and value. The criticism follows Attorney General Jeff Sessions’ announcement of a gradual dismantling of the program, and marks a rare public rebuke from a former president despite traditional norms against such commentary. END OF SUMMARY.
