SUMMARY:

Former President Obama strongly condemned President Trump's decision to end the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program, calling it "cruel" and lacking "basic decency." Obama argued that DACA recipients pose no threat and are not taking anything away from others, but rather want to contribute to the country they love. He emphasized that targeting these young people is wrong and self-defeating, as they want to start businesses, staff labs, serve in the military, and contribute to the country. Obama framed Trump's decision as a political and moral choice, rather than a legal necessity, and urged Americans to consider the human impact of deporting individuals who are here through no fault of their own. By ending DACA, Obama argued that Trump is betraying the trust of these young people and going against the values of compassion and fairness that America represents.

END OF SUMMARY.
