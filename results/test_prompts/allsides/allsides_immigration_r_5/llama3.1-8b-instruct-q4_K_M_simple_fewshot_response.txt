Former President Obama criticized President Trump for ending the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) amnesty program, calling it "cruel" and "contrary to our spirit". Obama argued that DACA recipients pose no threat and are contributing members of society. He claimed that ending the program is a "political decision" and a "moral question", and urged Americans not to threaten the future of these young people who are here through no fault of their own. Obama's criticism comes after Trump announced his intention to gradually roll back the amnesty program, which was thought to be on shaky legal ground.

Obama framed his criticism in moral terms, emphasizing that ending DACA would harm innocent individuals and damage the country's values. He urged Americans to consider the human impact of this decision and to treat DACA recipients with compassion and respect. The controversy highlights the ongoing debate over immigration policy and the competing moral visions of Obama and Trump.
