SUMMARY:

Former President Obama strongly condemned President Trump's decision to end the DACA amnesty program, calling it "cruel" and "lacking basic decency". Obama argued that DACA recipients "pose no threat" and "are not taking away anything from the rest of us", emphasizing that they want to "serve" and "contribute" to the country. He questioned the moral implications of targeting these young people, asking "What if our kid's science teacher, or our friendly neighbor turns out to be a Dreamer? Where are we supposed to send her?" Obama framed the decision as a "moral question", stating that it's a "self-defeating" move that won't benefit the country. He highlighted the positive contributions of DACA recipients, such as starting businesses, staffing labs, and serving in the military. Obama urged that we shouldn't "threaten" the future of these young people who are here through no fault of their own.

END OF SUMMARY.
