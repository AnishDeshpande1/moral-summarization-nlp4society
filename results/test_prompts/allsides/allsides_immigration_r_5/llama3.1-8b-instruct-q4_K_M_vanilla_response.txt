SUMMARY:

Former President Obama criticized President Trump for ending the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program, calling it "cruel" and "contrary to our spirit". Obama argued that DACA recipients pose no threat and are not taking away anything from others. He claimed that they want to contribute to the country by starting businesses, staffing labs, serving in the military, and other ways. Obama also emphasized that the decision to end DACA was a "political" one, rather than a legal requirement, and questioned where DACA recipients would be sent if they were deported.

Obama's comments came after Attorney General Jeff Sessions announced that the administration would gradually roll back the program. Obama hinted in his post that he had previously considered weighing in on the issue, despite precedent against former presidents criticizing the current one. He concluded by emphasizing that ending DACA would not have any positive economic impact and would only hurt the young people who are here through no fault of their own.

END OF SUMMARY.
