SUMMARY:
Former President Obama sharply condemned President Trump’s decision to dismantle the DACA program, branding the move “cruel,” lacking “basic decency,” and a betrayal of American values. Obama argued that the policy’s removal is a subversive abuse of presidential authority, driven by politics rather than legal necessity, and it harms innocent young people who contribute to the nation as students, workers, soldiers, and community responders. He emphasized the moral duty of care toward “Dreamers,” who pose no threat and owe their loyalty to the United States, asking where they would be sent if expelled. By framing the rollback as a betrayal of the country’s spirit of inclusion and a violation of the social contract, Obama called for empathy and solidarity, urging the public to reject a decision that sacrifices vulnerable individuals for political point‑scoring. The post positions Trump’s action as an unjust, authority‑driven subversion that endangers the well‑being of loyal, productive members of society. END OF SUMMARY.
