SUMMARY:

President Trump faced intense backlash after reportedly using a vulgar term to describe Haiti, El Salvador, and African countries during a meeting with lawmakers on immigration. Trump denied using the term, instead blaming Democrats for proposing "outlandish" immigration plans. The president's comments sparked widespread outrage, with many lawmakers and human rights officials denouncing them as racist. The governments of Haiti and El Salvador also demanded explanations and lodged protests. Trump's remarks have thrown upcoming negotiations on immigration into turmoil, with Democrats threatening to block a new government spending plan unless the DACA program is addressed. The incident highlights Trump's divisive rhetoric and the moral imperative for leaders to condemn racism and xenophobia. Trump's attempt to redirect the focus on Democratic proposals and his own stance on immigration only served to further inflame the situation.
