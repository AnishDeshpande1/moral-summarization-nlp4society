SUMMARY:

President Trump faced backlash from Congress and globally after reportedly using a vulgar term to describe Haiti, El Salvador, and African countries during a meeting with lawmakers. Trump denied using the term, instead blaming Democrats for proposing "unacceptable" immigration ideas. He tweeted that the language used was "tough" but not derogatory, and suggested recording future meetings. The reported comments sparked outrage, with lawmakers and human rights officials calling them "racist" and "shameful." The governments of Haiti and El Salvador demanded explanations, and a UN human rights spokesman condemned the remarks. Trump also rejected a bipartisan DACA proposal, saying it lacked funding for a US-Mexico wall and did not end "chain migration." The controversy has cast a shadow over upcoming immigration negotiations between the White House and Congress.

END OF SUMMARY.
