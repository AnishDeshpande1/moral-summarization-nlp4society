SUMMARY:

President Trump is facing backlash for reportedly using a derogatory term to describe Haiti, El Salvador, and African countries during a meeting with lawmakers on Thursday. He denied the allegations, saying he was referring to the economic situation in those countries, but his comments have been widely condemned as racist by lawmakers, human rights officials, and foreign governments. Trump's remarks were made during negotiations over the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program, which allows young people brought to the US illegally by their parents to remain in the country without fear of deportation.

Trump has threatened to end DACA unless Democrats agree to new security measures, including a border wall. The controversy has sparked calls for Trump to apologize and is likely to overshadow upcoming negotiations on immigration. Some Republicans have implicitly rebuked Trump's comments, while others have stayed silent or defended him. The incident has also raised questions about the president's views on immigration and his commitment to promoting diversity and equality.

END OF SUMMARY.
