SUMMARY:
President Trump tried to downplay a reported “shithole countries” remark about Haiti, El Salvador and African nations, claiming he never used a vulgar term and shifting blame to Democrats’ “outlandish” DACA proposals. While denouncing the alleged comment as “tough” language, he used a White House event honoring Martin Luther King Jr. to preach equality, a stark contrast to the accusations of racism that have ignited a global and congressional backlash. Lawmakers from both parties, as well as the United Nations and the governments of Haiti and El Salvador, condemned the statements as shameful, racist and unpresidential, demanding an apology. Trump also threatened to kill DACA unless Democrats fund a border wall and end “chain migration,” accusing the bipartisan plan of inviting “high‑crime” immigrants from non‑white countries. Even some Republicans, including Sen. Jeff Flake and Sen. Marco Rubio, rebuked the president’s language as abhorrent. The controversy now overshadows immigration negotiations and fuels fears that prejudice is guiding U.S. policy.  
END OF SUMMARY.
