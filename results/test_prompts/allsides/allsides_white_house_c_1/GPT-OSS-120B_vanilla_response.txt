SUMMARY: President Trump faced intense criticism after a Washington Post report alleged he referred to Haiti, El Salvador and African nations as “shithole countries” during a DACA meeting. Trump denied using the vulgar term, tweeting that his language was “tough” but not derogatory, and claimed he only described Haiti as “very poor and troubled.” He shifted focus to denounce a bipartisan DACA proposal, calling it a “big setback” and demanding stricter immigration measures, including a border wall. The remarks sparked condemnation from Democrats, Haitian and Salvadoran governments, and UN human‑rights officials, who labeled the comments racist. Republican reactions were mixed: some, like Sen. Marco Rubio, highlighted Haitian contributions, while others, such as Sen. Jeff Flake, called the language “abhorrent.” The controversy threatens upcoming immigration negotiations and could affect DACA legislation and broader funding talks, with Democrats threatening to block a spending plan that could lead to a government shutdown. END OF SUMMARY.
