SUMMARY:

US President Trump faced intense criticism for reportedly using derogatory language to describe Haiti, El Salvador, and African countries during a meeting with lawmakers on Thursday. Trump denied the allegations, stating that his comments were "tough" but not derogatory, and instead blamed Democrats for proposing an "unacceptable" immigration plan. The controversy has sparked outrage from human rights officials, lawmakers, and even some Republican colleagues, who denounced the comments as racist.

The government of Haiti and El Salvador have lodged official protests over Trump's remarks, with the UN human rights spokesman calling them "shocking and shameful." Meanwhile, some congressional Democrats have threatened to block a new government spending plan unless the DACA program is addressed, risking a government shutdown. The incident has also highlighted the deep divide between Trump and lawmakers on immigration policy, with Trump pushing for a "merit-based" system that prioritizes immigrants from certain countries.

END OF SUMMARY.
