SUMMARY:

President Trump is facing backlash from lawmakers and human rights officials after reportedly using a vulgar term to describe Haiti, El Salvador, and African countries during a meeting on immigration. Trump denied using the term, instead redirecting focus to what he called "unacceptable" Democratic proposals on immigration. However, his comments have been widely denounced as racist and shameful. Lawmakers and officials from Haiti, El Salvador, and the United Nations have condemned Trump's remarks, with some calling for an apology. Trump's goal, according to Democrats, is to allow immigration from predominantly white countries and block it from predominantly black and brown countries. The controversy has cast a shadow over upcoming negotiations on immigration and has sparked concerns about racism and prejudice in decision-making.

END OF SUMMARY.
