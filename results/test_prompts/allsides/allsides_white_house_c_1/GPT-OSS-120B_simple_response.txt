SUMMARY:  
President Trump faced a worldwide and congressional backlash after The Washington Post reported he called Haiti, El Salvador and African nations “shithole countries” during a DACA meeting. Trump tweeted that he never used that vulgar term, claiming his language was “tough” but the real issue was a “outlandish” Democratic DACA proposal. He shifted attention to immigration security demands, including a border wall, while denying any derogatory remarks about Haitians.  

Lawmakers and human‑rights officials denounced the alleged comments as racist, shameful, and unpresidential. Rep. Mia Love (R‑UT), whose parents immigrated from Haiti, called for an apology, and Rep. Luis Gutierrez (D‑IL) accused Trump of opposing legal immigration based on skin color or country of origin. Haiti and El Salvador governments demanded explanations, and the UN human‑rights office labeled the remarks “shocking and shameful.”  

Within Congress, the controversy clouds pending immigration negotiations and threatens a government shutdown over DACA funding. Some Republicans, like Sen. Marco Rubio, highlighted Haitian contributions and urged peace, while others, such as Sen. Jeff Flake, condemned the president’s language as “abhorrent and repulsive.”  

The episode underscores a moral clash over racism, dignity, and the nation’s immigration values.  

END OF SUMMARY.
