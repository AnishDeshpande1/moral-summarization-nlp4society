SUMMARY:
President Trump faced a global and congressional firestorm after The Washington Post reported he called Haiti, El Salvador and African nations “shithole countries.” He **ignored** questions about the remarks at a White House event honoring Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., instead **rejecting** a bipartisan DACA **proposal** and **threatening** to **kill** the program unless Democrats fund a border wall and end “chain migration.” Trump’s tweets claimed his language was “tough,” not **shameful** or **abhorrent**, and he **used** the occasion to stress “all created equal.” Lawmakers denounced the **comments** as **unpresidential**, **ignorance**, **prejudice**, and **racism**. Rep. Mia Love called them **divisive** and said they **fly** in the face of national values. Haiti and El Salvador demanded explanations, and a UN human‑rights spokesman called the alleged remarks **shocking** and **repulsive**. Democrats argued Trump **opposes** legal immigration when migrants look a certain way, seeking to **block** entry from “high‑crime” countries while **allow** those from privileged nations. Republicans were split; Sen. Marco Rubio highlighted Haitian contributions, while Sen. Jeff Flake called the President’s words **repulsive**. The controversy now **takes** center stage in upcoming immigration talks, with both parties warning that a government shutdown could **stop** funding for essential services unless DACA is addressed.  

END OF SUMMARY.
