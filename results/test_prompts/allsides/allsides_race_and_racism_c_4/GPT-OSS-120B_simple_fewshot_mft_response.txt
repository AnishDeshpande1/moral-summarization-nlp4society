SUMMARY:
Scientists using ground‑penetrating radar in Tulsa have uncovered evidence of mass graves from the 1921 Tulsa race massacre, exposing the hidden scale of a horrific act of white‑perpetrated violence that devastated the prosperous Black‑owned Greenwood District, known as “Black Wall Street.” An unverified accusation against a Black man sparked white mobs to loot, burn 35 city blocks and kill dozens—officially 36, but likely up to 300—leaving thousands homeless. For decades the tragedy was deliberately concealed: authorities ignored victims, insurance firms refused claims, newspapers sanitized the story as a “riot,” and schools omitted it from curricula, effectively cheating the public of the truth and denying justice to the victims and their descendants. The recent discovery confronts this historic injustice, offering a chance for care and reparative action—identifying the buried bodies, acknowledging the harm inflicted, and correcting the false narrative that erased Black achievement and suffering. Ongoing investigations, planned excavations, and upcoming film and TV projects aim to restore the full history before the centennial, reinforcing a moral demand for honesty, accountability, and compassion toward those still wounded by the massacre. END OF SUMMARY.
