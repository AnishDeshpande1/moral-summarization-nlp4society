SUMMARY:

Scientists in Tulsa, Oklahoma have discovered evidence of mass graves connected to the 1921 race massacre using ground-penetrating radar. The event, known as the Tulsa race riot or massacre, was a violent incident where white mobs attacked and killed residents of the Greenwood District, also known as "Black Wall Street". Historians believe that the true number of deaths could be as high as 300, but the event has been largely forgotten in American history.

Local leaders hope to uncover more evidence before the 100th anniversary of the event in 2021. A feature film and two television series are being produced about the massacre. The incident highlights a systematic effort to erase it from history, including labeling it as a riot rather than a targeted attack on black success.

Historians and commentators argue that the massacre contradicts the popular narrative of black progress in America, emphasizing the need for a more accurate understanding of this dark period in U.S. history. END OF SUMMARY.
