SUMMARY:
Using ground‑penetrating radar, scientists in Tulsa uncovered evidence of mass graves tied to the 1921 **massacre**, a **violent** **event** long **omitted** from textbooks. The thriving Greenwood District—celebrated as “Black Wall Street”—was shattered on May 31, 1921 when an unproven **assault** accusation sparked white **resentment** that erupted into a **rampage** of **looting**, **burning**, and **killing**. By June 1, 35 city blocks were **burned**, thousands were left **homeless**, and the official **death** toll of 36 is now believed to be as high as 300. Law‑enforcement and insurers **declined** to act, allowing the **attack** to be **strip**ped from the record and **minimized** by calling it a “riot,” a **mislabeling** that **flipped** victim and perpetrator, **blaming** Black residents and **absolving** the white mob. Newspapers **cut** references, bodies were **tossed** into the Arkansas River, and the story was **whitewashed**, **sanitized**, and effectively **erased** from curricula until the 2000s. Today, scholars urge careful excavation of the possible **mass graves**, DNA testing to ID the **victims**, and broader public awareness through upcoming films and series, hoping to confront this **bombing** of Black prosperity that challenges the dominant narrative of **slavery**‑to‑civil‑rights progress.  
END OF SUMMARY.
