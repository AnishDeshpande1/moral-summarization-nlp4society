SUMMARY:

A mass grave connected to the 1921 Tulsa race massacre, one of the deadliest incidents of racial violence in American history, has been discovered using ground-penetrating radar. The event, which occurred on May 31, 1921, saw white mobs flood into Greenwood District, home to most of Tulsa's black population, killing residents and destroying businesses. Despite its scale, the massacre was largely forgotten until recent years, with many Americans only becoming aware of it through a fictionalized reenactment in the HBO drama "Watchmen". The discovery of the mass grave is seen as an opportunity for the city to confront its past and provide justice to the victims' families. The event's erasure from history is attributed to a systematic effort by authorities and insurance companies to downplay its severity, with many accounts being cut or destroyed. Local leaders hope to complete an excavation project in time for the 100th anniversary of the event in 2021.

The discovery highlights the need for accountability and acknowledgment of past injustices, particularly those that have been deliberately erased from history. It also underscores the importance of confronting and learning from America's dark past to build a more equitable future.
