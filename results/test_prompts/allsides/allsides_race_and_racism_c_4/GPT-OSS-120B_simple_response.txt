SUMMARY:  
Recent ground‑penetrating radar work in Tulsa uncovered possible mass‑grave sites tied to the 1921 Tulsa race massacre, a violent white‑on‑black attack that razed the thriving “Black Wall Street” district and left up to 300 dead. The atrocity has been systematically hidden: officials suppressed records, the event was euphemistically called a “riot,” and schools omitted it for decades, creating a sanitized national narrative that downplays black prosperity and white terror. Voices from journalists and historians stress that this erasure was intentional, framing the white mob as victims and the black community as aggressors. The recent discoveries, upcoming films and TV series, and scholarly work aim to restore the truth before the centennial, confronting the moral failure of historical amnesia and demanding recognition of the victims and accountability for the past.  

END OF SUMMARY.
