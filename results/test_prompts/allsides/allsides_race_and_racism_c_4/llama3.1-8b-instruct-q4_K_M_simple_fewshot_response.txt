SUMMARY:

A recent discovery in Tulsa, Oklahoma, using ground-penetrating radar has uncovered evidence of mass graves connected to the 1921 race massacre that took place in the city's Greenwood District, also known as "Black Wall Street." The event was a violent attack by white mobs on the black community, resulting in an estimated 300 deaths and widespread destruction. Despite its significance, the Tulsa race massacre has been largely forgotten in American history. Historians attribute this to the efforts of those who sought to erase the event from record, including law enforcement and insurance companies. Local leaders hope to have further excavation completed by the 100th anniversary of the event in 2021. The discovery highlights the need for a more inclusive understanding of American history, one that acknowledges the complexities and traumas faced by black communities.
