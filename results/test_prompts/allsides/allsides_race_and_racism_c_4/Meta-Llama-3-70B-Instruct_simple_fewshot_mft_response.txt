SUMMARY:

The recent discovery of mass graves in Tulsa, Oklahoma, has brought to light the horrific 1921 race massacre, a dark chapter in American history. The massacre, which resulted in the destruction of the thriving Greenwood District, known as "Black Wall Street," was sparked by an unproven accusation of a black man assaulting a white woman. White mobs looted homes, burned businesses, and killed residents, leaving thousands homeless and an estimated 36 to 300 dead. The event was subsequently erased from history, with law enforcement and insurance companies declining to act, and a widespread effort to strip the historical record of evidence. This brutal act of racial violence, which contradicts the popular narrative of black progress in America, is a stark reminder of the harm inflicted upon black communities and the need for accountability and fairness. The discovery of the mass graves is a crucial step towards acknowledging and rectifying the past, and it is essential that we confront this painful history to ensure that such atrocities are never repeated.

END OF SUMMARY.
