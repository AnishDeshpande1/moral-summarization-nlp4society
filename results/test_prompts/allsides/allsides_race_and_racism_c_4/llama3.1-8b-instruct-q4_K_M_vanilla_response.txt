SUMMARY:
Scientists in Tulsa, Oklahoma have used ground-penetrating radar to discover evidence of mass graves connected to the 1921 Tulsa race massacre, which is considered one of the worst incidents of racial violence in American history. The event occurred when a white mob attacked the predominantly black Greenwood District, also known as "Black Wall Street," after an unproven accusation of assault by a black man on a white woman. The official death toll was 36, but historians believe it could be as high as 300. Despite its significance, the massacre has been largely forgotten until recent years, with some attributing this to a lack of discussion and documentation in the past.

Local leaders are working to excavate the possible mass grave sites, with hopes to complete the project by the 100th anniversary of the event in 2021. Several film and television projects are also in development to raise awareness about the massacre. Historians have noted that the event contradicts the popular narrative of black progress in America, highlighting the need for a more accurate representation of history.

END OF SUMMARY.
