SUMMARY:

The 1921 Tulsa race massacre, considered one of the worst incidents of racial violence in American history, has long been buried and forgotten. Recently, scientists discovered evidence of mass graves connected to the event, which saw a white mob destroy the thriving Greenwood District, known as "Black Wall Street," killing hundreds and leaving thousands homeless. The massacre was largely unknown due to a widespread effort to erase it from history, with law enforcement and insurance companies declining to act, and a lack of paper trail. Historians believe the incident was part of a period of widespread racial violence after the Civil War, often omitted from the narrative of black progress in America. The discovery of the mass graves and upcoming projects, including a feature film and TV series, aim to bring attention to this dark chapter in American history. It's essential to acknowledge and learn from this painful past to move towards a more honest and inclusive understanding of the country's history.

END OF SUMMARY.
