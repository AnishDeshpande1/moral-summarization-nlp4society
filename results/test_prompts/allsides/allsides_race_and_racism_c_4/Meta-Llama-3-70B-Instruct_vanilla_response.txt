SUMMARY:

Scientists in Tulsa, Oklahoma, have discovered evidence of mass graves connected to the 1921 race massacre, a largely forgotten event in American history. The massacre, which occurred on May 31, 1921, was sparked by an unproven accusation of assault by a black man on a white woman, leading to white mobs looting homes, burning businesses, and killing residents in the Greenwood District, known as "Black Wall Street." The official death toll was 36, but historians believe it could be as high as 300. The event was largely unknown due to a widespread effort to erase it from history, with many Americans only becoming aware of it this year through an HBO drama. Further examination of the possible mass grave sites is necessary before excavation, with local leaders hoping to complete the project by the 100th anniversary of the event in 2021.

END OF SUMMARY.
