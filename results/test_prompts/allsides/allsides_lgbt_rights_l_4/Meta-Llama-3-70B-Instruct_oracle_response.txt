SUMMARY:

The recent hanging of two men in Iran, allegedly for consensual sodomy, raises concerns about human rights and LGBT equality. However, the Obama administration's efforts to engage with Iran diplomatically may be hindered by this incident. The administration has been walking a tightrope between promoting LGBT human rights and pursuing international politics. Iran's cooperation is seen as crucial in managing the chaos in Iraq and the Islamic State. Conservatives, who have historically opposed LGBT equality, are now using Iran's human rights record to criticize the administration's policy of engagement. This phenomenon, known as "homonationalism," involves invoking LGBT equality as a justification for American foreign policies, while perpetuating the myth that sexual diversity is a Western invention. The tragedy of these executions may not spark widespread outrage, as it may be seen as a strategic impediment to diplomatic efforts.

END OF SUMMARY.
