SUMMARY:  
The hanging of two men in Iran – Abdullah Ghavami Chahzanjiru and Salman Ghanbari Chahzanjiri – resulted in their deaths by execution, possibly for consensual sodomy. Their execution joins a wave of Iranian executions that total over 400 in the first half of 2014, a record of criminalization and persecution of LGBT people that human‑rights groups decry as a travesty. The Obama administration, meanwhile, is trying to halt Iran’s nuclear program through constructive engagement, managing strategic cooperation that is deemed essential to contain chaos in Iraq and the rise of the Islamic State, even as U.S. airstrikes continue and the region faces possible disintegration. Conservatives seize on Iran’s oppression and antisemitism to bash the administration, claiming that the U.S. must champion LGBT rights abroad while allowing repression at home. The phenomenon of “homonationalism,” described by Jasbir Puar, shows how the U.S. imposes its own notions of sexuality, perpetuating the myth that sexual diversity is a Western invention and, paradoxically, demanding tolerance that can inhibit indigenous gender expressions. Thus, despite the tragedy, the administration and the LGBT movement are unlikely to raise a hue‑and‑cry, for fear of making the situation worse.  
END OF SUMMARY.
