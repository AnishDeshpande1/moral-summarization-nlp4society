The recent hanging of two men in Iran, allegedly for consensual sodomy, highlights the complexities of international LGBT politics. While the Obama administration has been advocating for LGBT human rights, it is walking a tightrope between promoting these rights and pursuing diplomacy with Iran to halt its nuclear program. The administration's critics, including conservatives, will likely use this incident to argue against engagement with Iran, despite their own records of opposing LGBT equality at home. This phenomenon, dubbed "homonationalism," involves invoking LGBT equality to justify American foreign policies, while ignoring the nuances of non-Western cultures and perpetuating the myth that sexual diversity is a Western invention. Given these complexities, it is unlikely that the Obama administration or the LGBT movement will strongly condemn this incident, lest they inadvertently make the situation worse. END OF SUMMARY.
