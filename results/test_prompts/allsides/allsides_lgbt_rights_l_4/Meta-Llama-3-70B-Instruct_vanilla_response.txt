SUMMARY:

The recent execution of two men in Iran, allegedly for consensual sodomy, has sparked concerns about human rights and LGBT issues. However, the Obama administration may not take strong action due to its efforts to engage with the Iranian regime and address other pressing issues, such as Iran's nuclear program and the crisis in Iraq. The administration's approach has been criticized by conservatives, who argue that the US should take a tougher stance on human rights. Ironically, some conservatives who oppose LGBT equality at home have used Iran's human rights record to argue against the administration's policy of engagement. The phenomenon of invoking LGBT equality to justify American foreign policies has been criticized as "homonationalism" or "imperialism," as it can perpetuate Western conceptions of sexuality and gender identity and inhibit tolerance of non-Western forms of sexuality and gender identity.

END OF SUMMARY.
