SUMMARY:

The tragic hanging of two men in Iran, allegedly for consensual sodomy, raises questions about the Obama administration's approach to LGBT human rights and international politics. While the execution is a horrific human rights abuse, the administration's pursuit of constructive engagement with Iran's regime and cooperation on managing the chaos in Iraq and the Islamic State may take precedence. Conservatives who favor a hawkish foreign policy may use this incident to criticize the administration's engagement policy, while some may hypocritically invoke LGBT equality to justify their stance. The author argues that this "homonationalism" can be harmful, as it imposes Western conceptions of sexual and gender identity onto other cultures and perpetuates the myth that sexual diversity is a Western invention. The administration and LGBT movement may not prioritize this incident, as it may be seen as a strategic liability in the pursuit of broader diplomatic goals. The moral framing of this article highlights the tensions between authority and subversion, care and harm, and fairness and cheating in international politics and human rights advocacy.

END OF SUMMARY.
