SUMMARY:

Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell called for the Senate Intelligence Committee to investigate recent CIA findings that the Russian government tried to influence the U.S. presidential election, labeling the allegations "disturbing". He dismissed calls for a special select committee, citing the Intelligence Committee's capability to conduct a complete review of the matter on a bipartisan basis. McConnell noted that he and incoming Senate Democratic Leader Charles Schumer will join the panel as ex-officio members. He emphasized that the Russians are not friends, having invaded Crimea, and that the U.S. should approach issues assuming Russia does not wish the country well. McConnell cautioned against leaks about U.S. intelligence findings, suggesting they may be motivated by partisan gain. He declined to comment on the possible appointment of ExxonMobile CEO Rex Tillerson, who has close ties with Putin, to serve as Secretary of State. Trump's team dismissed efforts to investigate Russian interference as an attempt to delegitimize his win.

END OF SUMMARY.
