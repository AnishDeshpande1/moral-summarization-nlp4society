SUMMARY:

Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell has called for the Senate Intelligence Committee to investigate recent CIA findings that Russia tried to influence the U.S. presidential election. He described the allegations as "disturbing" and stated that the committee is "more than capable of conducting a complete review" of the matter. McConnell emphasized the importance of following the regular order and reviewing the issue on a bipartisan basis, noting that he sits on the panel as an ex-officio member and that incoming Senate Democratic Leader Charles Schumer will soon join it in the same capacity.

The majority leader also stressed that the Russians are not friends to the U.S., having invaded Crimea, and that lawmakers should approach issues with the assumption that Russia does not wish the country well. He cautioned against leaked characterizations of the motivation behind Russian hacking, suggesting a possible partisan lens, and warned that any leaks about U.S. intelligence findings could be "irresponsible" and potentially "illegally" done for "partisan political gain".

END OF SUMMARY.
