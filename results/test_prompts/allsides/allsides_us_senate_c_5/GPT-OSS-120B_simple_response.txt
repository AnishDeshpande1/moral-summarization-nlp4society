SUMMARY:  
In a sharply moralized debate, Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell framed the Russian interference issue as a matter of national honor and security, insisting that the Senate Intelligence Committee—rather than a special select panel—should conduct a thorough, bipartisan review of the CIA’s findings that Moscow tried to sway the 2016 election. He stressed that “the Russians are not our friends” and warned against treating leaked intelligence as partisan “political weapons.” McConnell praised the bipartisan effort of Senators John McCain, Charles Schumer and Richard Burr, urging them to expose the truth without succumbing to partisan spin. While condemning any unauthorized leaks as “irresponsible, likely illegal,” he also cautioned against rushing to appoint figures with close ties to Putin, such as Rex Tillerson. The Trump camp dismissed the investigation as a bitter attempt to delegitimize the election outcome, calling the CIA report “ridiculous.” Throughout, the moral tone underscored a duty to protect American democracy from foreign adversaries while resisting partisan exploitation of intelligence.  

END OF SUMMARY.
