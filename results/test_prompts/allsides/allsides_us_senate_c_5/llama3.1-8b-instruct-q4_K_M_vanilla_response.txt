SUMMARY:

Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell has called for the Senate Intelligence Committee to investigate recent CIA findings that Russia tried to influence the US presidential election. McConnell, who sits on the panel as an ex-officio member, dismissed calls for a special select committee and said the regular order should be followed. He noted that Senators John McCain and Richard Burr will also review the issue on a bipartisan basis.

McConnell made it clear that he views Russia as an adversary, not a friend, and condemned allegations of Russian meddling in US cybersecurity measures. However, he suggested that leaked characterizations of the motivation behind Russian hacking should be weighed carefully, implying a possible partisan lens.

The investigation comes after reports emerged that American intelligence agencies have concluded that Russia acted to help President-elect Donald Trump and harm Hillary Clinton during the election. Trump has dismissed the CIA report as "ridiculous" and accused Democrats of trying to delegitimize his win.

END OF SUMMARY.
