SUMMARY:

Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell expressed concerns about Russian interference in the US presidential election, calling it "disturbing" and urging the Senate Intelligence Committee to investigate. He dismissed calls for a special select committee, saying the intelligence panel is "more than capable of conducting a complete review." McConnell emphasized that any investigation should be bipartisan and follow the regular order.

McConnell also made clear his views on Russia, stating that they are not friends of the US and have invaded Crimea. He noted that some senators are nervous about Russian influence and suggested approaching issues with the assumption that Russia does not wish the US well.

The Trump administration has dismissed efforts to investigate Russian interference, with a spokesman saying it's an attempt to delegitimize Trump's win. President-elect Donald Trump himself called the CIA report "ridiculous" on Sunday and again on Monday, suggesting that if the election results were reversed, it would be called a conspiracy theory.

END OF SUMMARY.
