SUMMARY: Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell defended the Senate Intelligence Committee’s role in reviewing recent CIA findings that Russia attempted to influence the 2016 U.S. presidential election, rejecting calls for a special bipartisan commission. He emphasized a bipartisan, “regular order” approach and noted his ex‑officio membership on the panel, while Democratic leader Chuck Schumer and Sen. John McCain also urged a broader congressional investigation. McConnell warned against treating leaked intelligence as partisan weaponry and stressed that Russia is an adversary, citing its actions in Crimea and cyber‑hacking. He declined to comment on potential Secretary‑of‑State nominee Rex Tillerson. Meanwhile, Trump’s spokesperson dismissed the investigation as a partisan effort to delegitimize the election outcome, and President‑elect Trump himself called the CIA report “ridiculous.” The debate highlights partisan tensions over how to address alleged Russian interference and the appropriate congressional response.  
END OF SUMMARY.
