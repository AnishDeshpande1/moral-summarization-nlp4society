SUMMARY:
Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell defended a measured, bipartisan response to the CIA’s claim that Russia tried to influence the 2016 election, urging the Senate Intelligence Committee—on which he sits as an ex‑officio member—to lead a thorough review rather than creating a special select commission. He warned that partisan leaks of intelligence could be “irresponsible, likely illegal and potentially for political gain,” and stressed that the United States must treat Russia as a hostile adversary, not a friend. McConnell also rebuked calls for an extended impeachment trial, arguing that dragging out the process would serve only partisan interests and distract from pressing national security concerns. While acknowledging the seriousness of Russian cyber‑threats, he cautioned against politicizing the issue, noting that the intelligence community’s findings should be examined carefully and on a bipartisan basis. The moral framing presents McConnell’s stance as a defense of responsible, non‑partisan oversight that protects national security and upholds the integrity of the democratic process, contrasting it with opponents who are portrayed as seeking to weaponize the Russia narrative to delegitimize President‑elect Trump’s victory.

END OF SUMMARY.
