SUMMARY:

Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell has called for the Senate Intelligence Committee to investigate the CIA's findings that Russia attempted to influence the US presidential election. McConnell dismissed calls for a special select committee, saying the Intelligence Committee is capable of conducting a complete review of the matter. He noted that he and incoming Senate Democratic Leader Charles Schumer will be part of the committee. McConnell views Russian President Vladimir Putin as an adversary and believes the US should approach Russia with caution. He condemned allegations of Russian hacking, but cautioned against leaks about US intelligence findings. McConnell also declined to comment on the possible appointment of ExxonMobile CEO Rex Tillerson as Secretary of State. Meanwhile, President-elect Donald Trump has dismissed the CIA report, calling it "ridiculous" and suggesting that the investigation is an attempt to delegitimize his win.

END OF SUMMARY.
