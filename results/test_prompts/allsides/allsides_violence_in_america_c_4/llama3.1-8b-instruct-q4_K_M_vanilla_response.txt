SUMMARY:
Protests in St. Louis over the acquittal of white police officer Jason Stockley, who shot black man Anthony Lamar Smith in 2011, turned violent for a third night running on Sunday. Over 80 people were arrested as police used pepper spray and riot gear to disperse demonstrators. The protests began after Stockley's acquittal on Friday, with 33 arrests and 10 injured officers reported. On Saturday, about 100 protesters clashed with police, resulting in nine arrests. The violence has evoked memories of the 2014 Ferguson riots following the shooting of Michael Brown by a white officer. Police have blamed "a group of agitators" for the trouble, while organizers say it's a response to institutional racism and the acquittal of Stockley. Many protesters fear for their safety and the safety of their loved ones, with one mother saying "Windows can be replaced. Lives can't." The protests are being led by an informal group known as the Ferguson frontline.

END OF SUMMARY.
