SUMMARY:  
More than 80 people were arrested Sunday night in St. Louis after protests over the acquittal of former officer Jason Stockley turned violent for the third consecutive night. While earlier demonstrations were largely peaceful, a small group that remained after dark smashed windows, threw ceramic pots, and tried to block an interstate ramp. Police in riot gear responded with pepper spray, tackled protesters, and seized weapons, including handguns and spray‑bottle chemicals. Mayor Lyda Krewson condemned the “agitators,” and acting police commissioner Lawrence O’Toole asserted the city was “in control.” The unrest follows Stockley’s Friday not‑guilty verdict for the 2011 shooting death of Anthony Lamar Smith, a case that revived memories of the 2014 Ferguson riots after Michael Brown’s killing. Protesters, organized in part by the Ferguson Frontline group, emphasized that “windows can be replaced, lives can’t.” No injuries were reported, and a few detained journalists were released after about 30 minutes. This marks the largest of three nights of protests, with roughly 1,000 demonstrators on Sunday.  

END OF SUMMARY.
