SUMMARY:

Protests in St. Louis over the acquittal of white police officer Jason Stockley, who shot black man Anthony Lamar Smith, turned violent for the third night running. Over 80 people were arrested as police used pepper spray and riot gear to disperse demonstrators. The protests began after Stockley's acquittal on Friday, with 33 arrests and 10 officers injured on the first night. Violence flared again on Saturday and Sunday nights, with protesters smashing windows and attempting to block highways.

Mayor Lyda Krewson blamed "a group of agitators" for the violence, while Acting Police Commissioner Lawrence O'Toole stated that the police were in control. The protests are part of a larger movement against institutional racism, which has allowed police to be cleared of wrongdoing in several shootings of unarmed black men. Many protesters, including mothers and family members of victims, fear for their loved ones' safety every night.

The violence evokes memories of the 2014 Ferguson riots following the shooting of Michael Brown by a white officer. The protests are organized by the Ferguson frontline group, which aims to address systemic racism in law enforcement.

END OF SUMMARY.
