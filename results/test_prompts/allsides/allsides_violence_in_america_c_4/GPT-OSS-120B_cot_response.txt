**SUMMARY:**  
More than 80 people were arrested after a third night of protests in St. Louis turned violent following the acquittal of former officer Jason Stockley for the 2011 shooting of Anthony Lamar Smith. While the majority of demonstrators remained non‑violent, a small group of agitators sparked disorder, smashing windows and blocking a highway ramp. Police in riot gear responded with pepper spray, asserting they were in control and would protect the city. Mayor Lyda Krewson blamed the trouble on a handful of aggravators, emphasizing that most protesters were peaceful. The clashes echoed the 2014 Ferguson riots, reviving concerns about institutional racism and the repeated clearing of police officers in shootings of unarmed Black men. No injuries were reported, though several officers were previously injured and many protesters were arrested. “Windows can be replaced. Lives can’t,” warned Missy Gunn of the Ferguson Frontline, reflecting the fear and moral urgency driving the protests.
