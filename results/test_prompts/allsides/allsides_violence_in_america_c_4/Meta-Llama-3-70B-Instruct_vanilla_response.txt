SUMMARY:

Protests in St. Louis turned violent for a third night, with over 80 people arrested on Sunday. The protests were sparked by the acquittal of a white police officer, Jason Stockley, who shot and killed a black man, Anthony Lamar Smith, in 2011. Despite a larger, peaceful protest earlier in the day, a small group of demonstrators remained and clashed with police, smashing windows and attempting to block a highway ramp. Police used pepper spray and made mass arrests, with some protesters defying orders to disperse. The mayor and police commissioner blamed a small group of "agitators" for the violence. The protests have drawn comparisons to the 2014 Ferguson riots, with some demonstrators citing institutional racism and police brutality.

END OF SUMMARY.
