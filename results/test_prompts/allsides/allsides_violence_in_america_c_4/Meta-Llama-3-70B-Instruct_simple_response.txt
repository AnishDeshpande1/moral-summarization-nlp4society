SUMMARY:

Protests in St. Louis turned violent for the third night in a row, with over 80 people arrested, as demonstrators continued to express outrage over the acquittal of a white police officer who shot and killed a black man in 2011. Despite a larger, peaceful protest earlier in the day, a small group of agitators sparked violence, smashing windows and attempting to block a highway ramp. Police in riot gear responded with pepper spray and mass arrests. The mayor and police commissioner blamed the trouble on a small group of agitators, while protesters argued that the violence was a result of systemic racism and a lack of accountability for police officers. The protests, which have drawn parallels to the 2014 Ferguson riots, have been organized by an informal group known as the Ferguson frontline, which is focused on highlighting institutional racism and police brutality.

END OF SUMMARY.
