SUMMARY:  
The St. Louis protests that erupted after a white police officer, Jason Stockley, was acquitted of murdering Black motorist Anthony Lamar Smith turned violent for the third night, leading to more than 80 arrests. While city officials, including Mayor Lyda Krewson and Acting Police Commissioner Lawrence O’Toole, emphasized that “the vast majority of protesters are non‑violent” and blamed the unrest on a “group of agitators,” demonstrators—many of them part of the Ferguson Frontline—expressed deep frustration that the justice system repeatedly shields officers from accountability. The clashes, marked by smashed windows, thrown ceramic pots, and confrontations with riot‑gear‑clad police, evoked painful memories of the 2014 Ferguson riots that sparked the Black Lives Matter movement. Protesters warned that “windows can be replaced, lives can’t,” underscoring the moral urgency behind their demand for systemic change and genuine safety for Black communities.  

END OF SUMMARY.
