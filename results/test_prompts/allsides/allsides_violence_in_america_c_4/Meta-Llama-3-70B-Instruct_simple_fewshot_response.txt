SUMMARY:

Protests in St. Louis turned violent for a third night, with over 80 people arrested, as demonstrators clashed with police over the acquittal of a white policeman who shot a black man. Despite a larger, peaceful protest, a small group of agitators sparked violence, smashing windows and attempting to block a highway ramp. Police used pepper spray and made mass arrests, with officers tackling protesters who defied orders. The mayor and police commissioner blamed the trouble on a small group of agitators, while protesters claimed the police response was heavy-handed. The protests followed the acquittal of former police officer Jason Stockley, who was charged with the 2011 shooting death of Anthony Lamar Smith. The violence evoked memories of the 2014 Ferguson riots, with protesters decrying institutional racism and police brutality. The protests have been organized by an informal group known as the Ferguson frontline, which has focused on police accountability and racial justice.

END OF SUMMARY.
