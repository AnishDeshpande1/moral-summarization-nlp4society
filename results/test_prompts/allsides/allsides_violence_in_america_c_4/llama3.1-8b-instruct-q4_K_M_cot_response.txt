**SUMMARY:**

Protests in St. Louis turned violent for a third night, as more than 80 people were arrested following the acquittal of white police officer Jason Stockley in the murder of black man Anthony Lamar Smith. The peaceful protests that took place earlier were marred by disorder, with protesters smashing windows and attempting to block an interstate highway. Mayor Lyda Krewson noted that "the vast majority of protesters are non-violent," but Acting police commissioner Lawrence O'Toole struck a hard stance, saying: "We're in control, this is our city and we're going to protect it." The protests evoked memories of the riots following the 2014 shooting of Michael Brown in nearby Ferguson, Missouri. The violence highlights the ongoing issue of institutional racism that has allowed police to be cleared of wrongdoing in several shootings of unarmed black men.
