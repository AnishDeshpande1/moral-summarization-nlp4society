SUMMARY:

Protests in St. Louis over the acquittal of former police officer Jason Stockley turned violent for a third night running, with more than 80 people arrested. Despite earlier peaceful protests, a small group of demonstrators smashed windows and attempted to block an interstate highway, prompting police to use pepper spray and make mass arrests. The violence evoked memories of the 2014 Ferguson riots, which gave rise to the Black Lives Matter movement. Protesters argue that institutional racism has allowed police to be cleared of wrongdoing in several shootings of unarmed black men. The acquittal of Stockley, who shot Anthony Lamar Smith in 2011, was particularly galling for many, as prosecutors argued that he planted a weapon in Smith's car. The protests have been organized by the Ferguson frontline group, which focuses on addressing institutional racism and police brutality. As one protester noted, "Windows can be replaced, but lives can't." The situation remains tense, with authorities vowing to protect the city from further violence.
