SUMMARY:

Protests in St. Louis over the acquittal of a white policeman who shot a black man turned violent for a third night, with over 80 people arrested. The protests, sparked by the acquittal of former police officer Jason Stockley, highlight the systemic racism and lack of accountability in the justice system. The authorities' response, marked by the use of pepper spray and mass arrests, raises concerns about the subversion of authority and the harm inflicted on peaceful protesters. The mayor's attempt to blame "agitators" for the violence rings hollow, as the vast majority of protesters are non-violent. The police commissioner's hard stance, claiming control over the city, is a thinly veiled attempt to justify the excessive use of force. The protests are a cry for justice and accountability, with the Ferguson frontline group organizing the demonstrations to highlight institutional racism. The authorities' failure to address these concerns only perpetuates a cycle of violence and mistrust. 

END OF SUMMARY.
