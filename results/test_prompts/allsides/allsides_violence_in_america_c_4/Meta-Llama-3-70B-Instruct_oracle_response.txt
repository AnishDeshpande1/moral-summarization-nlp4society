SUMMARY: Over 80 people were arrested in St. Louis as protests turned violent for a third night, following the acquittal of a white policeman who shot and killed a black man in 2011. The protests, which began peacefully, escalated into violence as demonstrators defied orders to disperse and smashed windows, attempting to block a highway ramp. Police in riot gear used pepper spray and made mass arrests, tackling protesters who refused to comply. The mayor blamed a "group of agitators" for the trouble, while the acting police commissioner vowed to "protect" the city. The protests were sparked by the acquittal of former police officer Jason Stockley, who was charged with first-degree murder in the shooting death of Anthony Lamar Smith. The violence evoked memories of the 2014 riots in nearby Ferguson, Missouri, following the shooting of a black teenager by a white officer. The protests have been marked by clashes, injuries, and arrests, with demonstrators decrying institutional racism and fearing for their lives. END OF SUMMARY.
