SUMMARY:
The article argues that U.S. foreign policy since 9/11 has been marked by a series of flawed, often harmful decisions that have strained alliances and endangered lives. It condemns the lack of responsible authority in both the Bush and Obama administrations, highlighting how unilateral excess and later disengagement led to unnecessary harm. Yet it stresses that true leadership requires the courage to recognize and admit mistakes—a moral duty of authority rather than subversion. The author points to George W. Bush’s second term as a rare example of a leader who faced his own failures, re‑organized his cabinet, and pursued fair, effective policies, thereby restoring competence and protecting national interests. By contrast, Obama’s aloof, White‑House‑centric style leaves senior officials sidelined and perpetuates unfair blame‑shifting. The piece urges Obama to follow Bush’s model: admit errors, enact systemic change, and act with care for the American people and global partners, thereby restoring fairness and responsible authority in U.S. foreign policy. END OF SUMMARY.
