SUMMARY:

The article critiques the US foreign policy since 9/11, highlighting the flaws of both the Bush and Obama administrations. While Bush's unilateralism led to the Iraq invasion, Obama's reactive approach has resulted in disengagement and dithering. The article argues that Obama's errors in judgment and execution have contributed to damaged relations and crises in various regions. However, it suggests that Obama can still learn from his mistakes and institute change, citing George W. Bush as a model. Bush recognized his errors in his second term and made sweeping changes to his administration, policies, and priorities, leading to better results in various regions. The article concludes that Obama should emulate Bush's willingness to admit errors and embrace change, rather than being aloof from his Cabinet and playing the blame game.
