SUMMARY:

The article critiques the US foreign policy since 9/11, highlighting the errors of both the Bush and Obama administrations. The author argues that Obama's reactive approach has led to disengagement and dithering, contributing to damaged relations and crises worldwide. However, the author also suggests that Obama can learn from Bush's example of recognizing mistakes and instituting change. Bush's willingness to admit errors and bring in new perspectives led to improved results in various regions and issues. In contrast, Obama's approach has been marked by a lack of accountability and a White House-centric approach that sidelines his Cabinet. The article urges Obama to emulate Bush's ability to lead administration-wide change from within, acknowledging his mistakes and taking corrective action. This requires a willingness to subvert one's own authority and prioritize the care and well-being of others over personal interests. By doing so, Obama can restore fairness and effectiveness to US foreign policy.

END OF SUMMARY.
