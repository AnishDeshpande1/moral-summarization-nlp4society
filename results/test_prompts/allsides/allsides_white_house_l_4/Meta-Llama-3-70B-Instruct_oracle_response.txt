SUMMARY:

The period since 9/11 has been marked by flawed US foreign policies, swinging from unilateralist excess to reactive disengagement and dithering. The invasion of Iraq, eavesdropping, and crises in Syria, Libya, Egypt, Israel, Ukraine, Afghanistan, and Africa are testaments to these mistakes. However, the US system is built to change, and Obama can still correct his errors. A rare and vital quality in a leader is the ability to recognize mistakes, as exemplified by George W. Bush, who instituted sweeping changes in his second term. Bush replaced key officials, modified policies, and engaged more actively on key issues, leading to better results in various regions. Obama, on the other hand, is seen as aloof from his Cabinet, which has contributed to strains and dysfunctions. To grow in office, Obama should learn from Bush's willingness to admit mistakes and lead administration-wide change from within. By doing so, he can stand up to himself and demand change, rather than giving up, punting to the future, or playing the blame game. END OF SUMMARY.
