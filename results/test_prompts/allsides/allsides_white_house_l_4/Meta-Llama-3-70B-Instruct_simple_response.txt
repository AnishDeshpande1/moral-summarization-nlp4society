SUMMARY:

The article reflects on the US foreign policy since 9/11, criticizing the unilateralist approach of the Bush administration and the reactive disengagement of the Obama administration. The author argues that the nation's problems stem from a heightened sense of vulnerability and human errors that can be fixed. The article praises George W. Bush's ability to recognize and correct his mistakes in his second term, citing his sweeping reforms and willingness to bring in new perspectives. In contrast, Obama is seen as aloof from his Cabinet and resistant to change. The author suggests that Obama could learn from Bush's example, acknowledging his own errors and leading administration-wide change. This ability to self-reflect and adapt is crucial for effective leadership, and Obama would do well to emulate Bush's willingness to stand up to himself and demand change.

END OF SUMMARY.
