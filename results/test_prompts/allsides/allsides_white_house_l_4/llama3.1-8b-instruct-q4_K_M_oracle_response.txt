SUMMARY:

The US foreign policy since 9/11 has been marred by flawed policies, including the invasion of Iraq under Bush's administration, which was followed by Obama's disengagement and dithering. This reactive approach led to crises in various regions, straining relations with allies and exacerbating existing dysfunctions. However, the author suggests that Obama can learn from Bush's ability to recognize mistakes and lead change.

In his second term, Bush reordering his administration, replacing key officials, and modifying policies led to constructive change. He instituted a sweeping overhaul of his team, launching initiatives such as the surge in Iraq and the "light footprint" approach to fighting terror. Bush's willingness to admit errors and bring new perspectives into his team was a hallmark of his leadership.

To grow in office, Obama needs to emulate Bush's example by standing up to himself and demanding change from within. This requires recognizing mistakes and leading administration-wide reform. By doing so, Obama can restore confidence and buck the trend of disengagement that has characterized his presidency thus far.

END OF SUMMARY.
