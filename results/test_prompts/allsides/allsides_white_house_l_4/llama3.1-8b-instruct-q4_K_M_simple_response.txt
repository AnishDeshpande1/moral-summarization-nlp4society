SUMMARY:

A recent history of US foreign policy since 9/11 reveals a series of flawed policies, including the invasion of Iraq under Bush and disengagement under Obama. These mistakes have led to strained relations with allies and damaged national interests. However, the author argues that leaders can learn from their predecessors' mistakes and adapt to changing circumstances. George W. Bush is cited as an example of a leader who recognized his errors in office and initiated significant changes in his second term, including replacing key officials and reforming policies.

The author suggests that Obama could benefit from emulating Bush's willingness to admit mistakes and lead administration-wide change. This would require recognizing the need for constructive change and taking steps to address flaws in policy and leadership. The article concludes that by acknowledging their own shortcomings and embracing change, leaders can overcome even the most significant challenges and achieve better results.

END OF SUMMARY.
