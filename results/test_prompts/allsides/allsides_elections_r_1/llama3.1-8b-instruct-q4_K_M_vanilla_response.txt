SUMMARY:

Joe Biden, the likely Democratic presidential nominee, has unveiled his plan to address the COVID-19 pandemic, which includes a $1 trillion infrastructure program and aid for state and local governments. However, he has been criticized for not addressing allegations of sexual assault made by Tara Reade, a former staffer who claims Biden assaulted her in 1993. Reade alleges that Biden pushed her against a wall, groped her, and touched her genitals without consent.

Reade's account is supported by her former neighbor Lynda LaCasse, who says Reade detailed the alleged incident to her in the mid-90s. A 1993 video clip also surfaced showing a woman believed to be Reade's mother discussing her daughter's problems working for Biden on CNN's "Larry King Live." Major media outlets have not asked Biden about the allegations directly.

The Biden campaign has denied the claim, but the lack of direct questioning from media outlets has sparked controversy and raised questions about how seriously the allegations are being taken.
