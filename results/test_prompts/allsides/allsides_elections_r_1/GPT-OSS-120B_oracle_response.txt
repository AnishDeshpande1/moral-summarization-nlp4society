SUMMARY:  
Democratic presidential hopeful Joe Biden outlined a $1 trillion infrastructure plan to create jobs and fund state and local aid, while criticizing the Trump administration’s COVID‑19 response. At the same time, Biden has been quiet about accusations from former staffer Tara Reade, who said she was assaulted in 1993. Reade said Biden pushed her against a wall, groped her, and touched her genitals, then told her supervisors that she had been harassed and was later told to find another job. Two former staffers denied recalling the incident, and a third denied the claim outright. Reade’s former neighbor said Reade had told her about the alleged assault, and a 1993 CNN clip showed a woman claiming her daughter left Washington because she “could not get through with her problems.” In 2019, Reade said Biden rubbed her shoulders, played with her hair, and was asked to dress more conservatively, being told “don’t be so sexy.” Biden has said he will be more mindful of personal space, while his campaign denied the assault allegations.  

END OF SUMMARY.
