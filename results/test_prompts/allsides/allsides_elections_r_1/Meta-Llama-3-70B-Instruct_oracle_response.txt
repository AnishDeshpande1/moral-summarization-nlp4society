SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential nominee Joe Biden has unveiled a plan to tackle the Coronavirus, criticizing the Trump administration's response. His plan includes a $1 trillion infrastructure program to create jobs, environmental initiatives, and massive aid for state and local governments. However, Biden has been quiet about allegations of sexual assault made by a former staffer, Tara Reade. Reade accuses Biden of pushing her against a wall, groping her, and touching her genitals in 1993 while she worked on his Senate staff. She claims her concerns were not taken seriously, and she was eventually told to find another job. Two of Biden's former staffers deny recalling Reade or the incident, while a third issued a statement denying the claim. However, Reade's former neighbor has come forward to support her account, saying Reade felt "assaulted" and didn't know what to do. Biden has previously acknowledged making women feel uncomfortable with inappropriate displays of affection, but has denied Reade's allegation, saying it "absolutely did not happen."

END OF SUMMARY.
