SUMMARY: The piece presents a moral dilemma surrounding Democratic frontrunner Joe Biden. On one hand, Biden is lauded for proposing a bold, compassionate response to the COVID‑19 crisis—advocating a $1 trillion infrastructure plan, green initiatives, and substantial aid to state and local governments to revive the economy and create jobs. On the other hand, the article foregrounds serious sexual‑assault allegations made by former staffer Tara Reada, describing a 1993 incident in which Biden allegedly forced himself on her and ignored her complaints. While some former colleagues deny the story, Reada’s former neighbor corroborates her account, and a 1993 interview hints at a pattern of silencing victims. The report notes that Biden has previously acknowledged uncomfortable behavior toward women and pledged to respect personal space, yet major outlets have not pressed him on the assault claim, and his campaign dismisses it. The moral framing underscores the tension between Biden’s policy ambitions to help the nation and the ethical imperative to address and investigate accusations of personal misconduct. END OF SUMMARY.
