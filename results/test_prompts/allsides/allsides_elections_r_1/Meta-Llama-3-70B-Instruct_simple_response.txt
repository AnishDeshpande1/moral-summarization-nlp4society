SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential nominee Joe Biden has unveiled a plan to tackle the Coronavirus pandemic, calling for increased spending and a $1 trillion infrastructure program to create jobs and aid state and local governments. However, Biden's campaign has been marred by allegations of sexual assault from a former staffer, Tara Reade, who claims he groped and touched her genitals in 1993. Despite Reade's detailed account, which has been corroborated by her former neighbor, Biden's campaign has denied the allegations, saying they "absolutely did not happen." The lack of scrutiny from major media outlets and the campaign's dismissal of Reade's claims raise questions about accountability and the importance of taking sexual assault allegations seriously.

END OF SUMMARY.
