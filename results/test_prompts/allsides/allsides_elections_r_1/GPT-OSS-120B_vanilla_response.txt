SUMMARY:  
Likely Democratic nominee Joe Biden outlined a new COVID‑19 response, calling for substantially increased federal spending, a $1 trillion infrastructure plan, expanded environmental projects and significant aid for state and local governments. While promoting these policies, Biden has remained largely silent on renewed allegations from former Senate staffer Tara Reade, who claims she was sexually assaulted by Biden in 1993. Reade says Biden pushed, groped, and touched her inappropriately; she reports that her complaints were dismissed and she was urged to find another job. Two former staffers said they do not recall the incident, and a third publicly denied it; Reada’s former neighbor corroborated Reade’s story. A 1993 CNN “Larry King Live” clip was later found in which a woman—identified as Reade’s mother—alluded to her daughter’s difficulties after working for a prominent senator. Earlier, eight women, including Reade, accused Biden of inappropriate physical behavior, which he acknowledged and said he would be more mindful of personal space. The Biden campaign has rejected Reade’s assault claim, citing a prior statement that such allegations should be independently investigated and asserting the incident did not occur.  

END OF SUMMARY.
