SUMMARY:  
In a campaign‑plane interview, Hillary Clinton contrasted her and Sen. Tim Kaine’s forward‑looking, service‑oriented agenda with Donald Trump’s apparent focus on personal vengeance. Clinton condemned Trump’s Gettysburg speech, where he threatened to sue the women accusing him of sexual misconduct, framing it as a misuse of presidential power and a sign that he cares more about settling scores than governing. She warned that such behavior threatens America’s democratic traditions, citing his refusal to concede an election and his calls to jail her over a private‑email controversy as evidence of a “dictatorship‑like” disregard for constitutional safeguards. By contrast, Clinton pledged to begin work immediately after the election, preparing to serve the American people on Jan. 20. The pair also highlighted the strategic importance of Pennsylvania, urging support for Democratic Senate candidate Katie McGinty to break congressional gridlock. Throughout, the moral narrative positioned Clinton as a guardian of law, institutions, and women’s rights, while casting Trump as a self‑interested threat to democracy.
