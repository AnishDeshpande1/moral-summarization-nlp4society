SUMMARY:

Hillary Clinton spoke to reporters on her campaign plane, drawing a contrast between her and Donald Trump's plans for their first 100 days in office. She criticized Trump for threatening to sue women who have accused him of making unwanted sexual advances, saying he would spend his time "suing women" rather than governing. By contrast, she said she and running mate Tim Kaine would focus on working for the American people.

Clinton also spoke at a rally in Pittsburgh, where she emphasized that Trump is a danger to America's democratic traditions. She criticized him for suggesting that he might not concede the election if he loses, saying this shows a "troubling disregard" for constitutional freedoms and protections. Clinton also spent time making a case for Democratic Senate candidate Katie McGinty, who is challenging incumbent Republican Pat Toomey.

Clinton and Kaine are trying to make inroads among college-educated women who normally vote Republican. The pair believe that winning Pennsylvania would cut off one possible path by which Trump could reach the needed 270 electoral votes.

END OF SUMMARY.
