SUMMARY:

Hillary Clinton contrasted her approach to the first 100 days in office with Donald Trump's, criticizing his plan to sue women who accused him of sexual misconduct. Speaking on her campaign plane, Clinton said Trump seemed more focused on settling personal scores than governing. She emphasized her own commitment to working for the American people, starting on January 20. Clinton also reiterated her concerns about Trump's disregard for democratic traditions, citing his refusal to concede the election and his suggestion that she should be jailed. At a rally in Pittsburgh, Clinton campaigned for Democratic Senate candidate Katie McGinty, highlighting the importance of down-ballot races in achieving her policy agenda. Clinton's focus on Pennsylvania, a crucial state in the election, underscores her efforts to win over college-educated women who typically vote Republican.

END OF SUMMARY.
