SUMMARY:
Hillary Clinton and running‑mate Tim Kaine used their campaign plane to contrast Trump’s focus on personal vendettas with their own agenda of responsible governance. They warned that Trump’s promise to sue the women accusing him of sexual misconduct shows a willingness to weaponize the legal system against vulnerable victims, underscoring a disregard for the care and safety of women. By invoking Gettysburg—a symbol of American authority and democratic tradition—Clinton framed Trump’s rhetoric as a subversive attack on constitutional norms and the rule of law. In contrast, she pledged that, if elected, she and Kaine would begin work immediately, respecting institutions and protecting citizens rather than pursuing scores. The remarks also highlighted the broader stakes in Pennsylvania, linking the fight against Trump’s authoritarian posture to the need to support Democratic candidates like Katie McGinty to break legislative gridlock and improve people’s lives.  
END OF SUMMARY.
