SUMMARY:
Hillary Clinton used a campaign‑plane appearance to contrast her governing style with Donald Trump’s focus on personal retribution. She highlighted Trump’s Gettysburg speech, where he promised to sue the women accusing him of sexual misconduct, portraying him as more interested in “settling scores” than in leading the nation. Clinton and running‑mate Tim Kaine argued they would begin working immediately after the election, preparing to serve the American people from day one, while Trump’s threats signaled a disregard for democratic norms and constitutional protections. Clinton warned that Trump’s refusal to accept a potential election loss blurs the line between leadership and dictatorship, and she condemned his calls to imprison her over a private‑email investigation as antithetical to the rule of law. By stressing the importance of an independent judiciary and highlighting Democratic Senate candidate Katie McGinty’s fight against Republican Pat Toomey, Clinton framed the race as a defense of America’s democratic traditions against a candidate who weaponizes the legal system for personal vengeance. The moral message is clear: Trump’s vindictive tactics threaten the country’s democratic foundations, whereas Clinton pledges responsible, law‑abiding governance.  
END OF SUMMARY.
