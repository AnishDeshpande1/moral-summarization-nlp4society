SUMMARY:

Hillary Clinton drew a stark contrast between her approach to governance and Donald Trump's, highlighting his intention to sue women who have accused him of sexual misconduct. Clinton emphasized that Trump's priority is settling personal scores, whereas she and her running mate, Tim Kaine, are focused on serving the American people. Clinton warned that Trump's behavior threatens America's democratic traditions, citing his refusal to concede the election and his desire to jail his opponent. She argued that these actions demonstrate a disregard for constitutional freedoms and protections. In contrast, Clinton vowed to work tirelessly to prepare for office and make a positive impact on people's lives. The Clinton campaign's emphasis on Pennsylvania, a crucial state, underscores the importance of this election in shaping the country's future.
