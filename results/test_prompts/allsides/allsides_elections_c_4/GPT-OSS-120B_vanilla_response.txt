SUMMARY: Hillary Clinton and running mate Tim Kaine used a campaign plane stop in Pittsburgh to contrast their approach to governance with that of Republican nominee Donald Trump. Clinton criticized Trump’s Gettysburg speech, saying he intends to spend his first 100 days suing women who have accused him of sexual misconduct, indicating a focus on personal vendettas rather than governing. She emphasized that she and Kaine will begin preparing to serve immediately after the election, highlighting their commitment to democratic norms and the rule of law, and warning that Trump’s refusal to concede could threaten constitutional freedoms. Clinton also defended herself against Trump’s calls to jail her over her private email use, underscoring the independence of the judiciary. At the rally, she promoted Democratic Senate candidate Katie McGinty, whose victory over incumbent Republican Pat Toomey is seen as crucial for breaking Senate gridlock and supporting a potential Clinton administration. Both campaigns are targeting Pennsylvania as a pivotal battleground to secure the necessary Electoral College votes. END OF SUMMARY.
