SUMMARY:

Hillary Clinton and her running mate, Sen. Tim Kaine, criticized Donald Trump's plan for his first 100 days in office, focusing on his threat to sue women who have accused him of sexual misconduct. In contrast, Clinton emphasized the importance of governing and working with others, saying she and Kaine will "keep talking about what we want to do" if elected. The Clinton campaign sees this as an opportunity to appeal to college-educated women who normally vote Republican.

Clinton also denounced Trump's comments on refusing to concede the election and his suggestion that she should be jailed for using a private email system while secretary of state. She argued that these comments demonstrate a disregard for constitutional freedoms and protections, and that he shows a "troubling" desire for personal revenge rather than governing.

In her speech, Clinton also emphasized the importance of down-ballot races, highlighting Democratic Senate candidate Katie McGinty's bid to defeat incumbent Republican Pat Toomey in Pennsylvania. END OF SUMMARY.
