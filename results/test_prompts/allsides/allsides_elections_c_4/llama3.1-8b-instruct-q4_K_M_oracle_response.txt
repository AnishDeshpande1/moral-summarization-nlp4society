SUMMARY: Hillary Clinton and her running mate, Sen. Tim Kaine, criticized Donald Trump's intent to sue women who have accused him of making unwanted sexual advances, calling it an example of his personal vendetta and avenging behavior. They argued that Trump is more interested in settling scores than governing the country. In contrast, they promised to focus on leading the nation with dignity and respect for democratic traditions. Clinton warned that Trump's refusal to concede defeat and his call to jail her are signs of a disregard for constitutional freedoms and protections, which could lead to dictatorship. She emphasized the importance of breaking through gridlock and making a difference in people's lives. The Clinton campaign sees Pennsylvania as a crucial state, where winning would cut off one possible path to victory for Trump. END OF SUMMARY.
