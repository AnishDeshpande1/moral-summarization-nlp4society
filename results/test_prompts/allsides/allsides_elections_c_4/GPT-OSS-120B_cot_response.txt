**SUMMARY:**  
On a campaign flight, Hillary Clinton contrasted her and Tim Kaine’s focus on governing with Donald Trump’s pre‑election threats to sue women who accused him of sexual misconduct. Clinton portrayed Trump as more interested in personal vengeance than public service, calling his stance a “danger” to America’s democratic traditions and warning that refusing to accept election results blurs the line between “leadership and dictatorship.” She emphasized Trump’s “disregard” for constitutional “freedoms” and “protections,” and mocked his calls to jail her over a private‑email controversy, reminding listeners that the United States relies on “laws,” “courts,” and an “independent judiciary.” Clinton also highlighted the strategic importance of Pennsylvania, urging support for Democratic Senate candidate Katie McGinty to break gridlock and deliver real change. The campaign framed the election as a moral choice between upholding democratic values and succumbing to authoritarian impulses.
