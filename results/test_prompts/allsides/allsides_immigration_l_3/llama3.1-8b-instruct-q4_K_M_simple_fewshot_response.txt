SUMMARY:

The Trump administration is planning to dispatch at least 800 active duty troops to the southern border, in an effort to deter migrants from crossing into the US. Homeland Security Secretary Kirstjen Nielsen stated that American soldiers have "no intention" of shooting migrants, but will apprehend them instead. The move comes as a caravan of thousands of migrants makes its way through Mexico, fleeing violence and poverty. Trump has been using the issue to bolster his election-season warnings about illegal immigration, claiming without evidence that Democrats organized the caravan and portraying migrants in a sinister light. Nielsen emphasized the importance of officer safety, but it is unclear what protocols will be in place for handling the large number of migrants expected to arrive at the border. The administration is also weighing an order to deny asylum applications, which would likely face challenges in court.

END OF SUMMARY.
