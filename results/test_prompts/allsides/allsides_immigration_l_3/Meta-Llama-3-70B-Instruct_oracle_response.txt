SUMMARY:

The Trump administration plans to dispatch 800 active duty troops to the southern border to apprehend migrants trying to cross into the US from Mexico. Homeland Security Secretary Kirstjen Nielsen stated that American soldiers have "no intention" of shooting migrants, but they will be stopped and apprehended. The government is considering an order to deny asylum applications, citing emergency powers that may face legal challenges. The move comes as a caravan of thousands of migrants, many fleeing violence, extortion, and poverty, makes its way through Mexico. Trump has been using the caravan to stoke fears about immigration, claiming without evidence that "Middle Easterners" are among the group and portraying Democrats as pro-illegal immigration. The migrants, including families with children, are seeking a better future, but Trump has tweeted that they should return home and apply for citizenship legally. The situation has sparked a political firestorm in the US, with the government planning drastic measures to stop the migrants.

END OF SUMMARY.
