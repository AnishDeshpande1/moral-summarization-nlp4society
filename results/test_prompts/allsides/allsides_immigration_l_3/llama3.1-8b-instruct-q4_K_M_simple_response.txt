SUMMARY:
The US government is planning to deploy at least 800 active duty troops to the southern border, amidst a caravan of thousands of migrants from Central America attempting to cross into the US. Homeland Security Secretary Kirstjen Nielsen stated that while there is "no intention" to shoot at people, they will be apprehended and forced to apply for asylum. The deployment comes as President Trump seeks to make immigration a top issue in the upcoming midterm elections. Critics argue that Trump's rhetoric has created fear and misinformation about the migrant caravan, which is primarily composed of Hondurans fleeing poverty and violence.

Trump has claimed without evidence that Middle Easterners are among the group and has used Twitter to urge migrants to return home. The migrants have stated they will continue their journey northward despite Trump's warnings. The situation highlights concerns over the humanitarian treatment of migrants and the potential for human rights abuses at the border. The deployment of troops raises questions about the militarization of immigration enforcement.

END OF SUMMARY.
