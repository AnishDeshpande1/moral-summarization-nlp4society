SUMMARY:

The Trump administration is planning to dispatch at least 800 active duty troops to the southern border to apprehend migrants attempting to cross into the US from Mexico. Homeland Security Secretary Kirstjen Nielsen stated that American soldiers have "no intention" of shooting migrants, but they will be apprehended. The government is also weighing an order to deny asylum applications at the border, citing emergency powers that would likely face legal challenges. President Trump has been using the migrant caravan to stoke fear and rally his base ahead of the midterm elections, claiming without evidence that Democrats organized the caravan and that "Middle Easterners" are among the group. The migrants, mostly Hondurans fleeing poverty and violence, are determined to reach the US and claim asylum, despite Trump's warnings to turn back. The administration's actions are seen as a cynical ploy to exploit fears about immigration for political gain. 

END OF SUMMARY.
