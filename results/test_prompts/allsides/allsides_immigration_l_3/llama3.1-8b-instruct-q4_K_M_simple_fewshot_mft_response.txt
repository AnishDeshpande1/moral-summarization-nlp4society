SUMMARY:

Homeland Security Secretary Kirstjen Nielsen clarified that US soldiers have "no intention" of shooting migrants trying to cross the border, but they will be apprehended. This statement comes as President Trump moves towards a plan to close the border, dispatching at least 800 active duty troops to the southern border. The administration is weighing an order to deny asylum applications at a fortified border, asserting emergency powers that would likely face challenges in US courts. Nielsen emphasized the importance of officer and agent safety, but her statement has sparked concerns about the treatment of migrants. Many in the caravan are fleeing violence or poverty in Central America, including families with children. Trump has used the caravan to bolster his election-season warnings about immigration, claiming without evidence that "Middle Easterners" were among the group and that Democrats organized the caravan. The migrants' response to Trump's tweets is largely indifference, with many stating they will continue heading north.

END OF SUMMARY.
