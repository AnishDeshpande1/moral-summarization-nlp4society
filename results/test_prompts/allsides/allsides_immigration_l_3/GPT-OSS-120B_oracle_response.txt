SUMMARY: Homeland Security Secretary Kirstjen Nielsen told Fox News that U.S. soldiers have “no intention” to shoot migrants who try to cross from Mexico, but they will be apprehended and border agents will have the ability to use force to protect themselves. President Trump, seeking to make immigration a central issue for the November midterms, announced plans to send at least 800 active‑duty troops to the southern border, bolstering National Guard units already there. The administration is weighing an emergency order that could stop asylum applications, a move likely to be claimed unconstitutional. Trump has repeatedly portrayed the Central American caravan—largely Honduran families fleeing poverty, violence and extortion—as a threat, falsely claiming that “Middle Easterners” are among them and accusing Democrats of portraying the migrants as welcome. He urged the caravan to “turnaround” and said those who wish to apply for citizenship must do so like “millions of others.” Migrants, many with children, say they only want to give their kids a better future and ask for a more humanitarian response. END OF SUMMARY.
