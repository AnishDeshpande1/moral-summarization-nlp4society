SUMMARY:

Homeland Security Secretary Kirstjen Nielsen stated that American soldiers have "no intention" of shooting migrants trying to cross into the US from Mexico, but they will be apprehended. The Trump administration is planning to dispatch at least 800 active-duty troops to the southern border in response to a caravan of thousands of migrants making its way through Mexico. The troops will provide logistical support to the border patrol and help with apprehending migrants. Nielsen emphasized that the safety of US officers and agents is a priority, but they have the authority to use force if necessary.

The move comes as President Trump has been using the caravan as an issue to boost his election-season warnings about immigration. He has claimed that Democrats are pro-illegal immigration and organized the caravan, although there is no evidence to support this claim. The migrants in the group are mostly Hondurans seeking to escape poverty and violence, and they plan to continue heading north despite Trump's tweets urging them to return home.

END OF SUMMARY.
