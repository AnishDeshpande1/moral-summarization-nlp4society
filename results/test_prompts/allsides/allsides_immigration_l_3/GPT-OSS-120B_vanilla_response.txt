SUMMARY: Homeland Security Secretary Kirstjen Nielsen said U.S. troops deployed to the southern border will not be ordered to shoot migrants, but will apprehend them. The Trump administration plans to send at least 800 active‑duty soldiers to assist the National Guard as a large Central American caravan moves through Mexico toward the U.S. The president is considering an executive action that could deny asylum claims at the border, invoking emergency powers that are likely to face legal challenges. Trump has framed the caravan as a national‑security threat to energize his base ahead of the November midterm elections, falsely claiming involvement of “Middle Easterners” and accusing Democrats of supporting illegal immigration. The caravan, mainly Honduran families fleeing violence and poverty, has dwindled from about 7,000 members after entering Mexico. Trump has urged the migrants to turn back via Twitter. Migrants who reach the border can still seek asylum under U.S. and international law, though cases often take years to resolve. END OF SUMMARY.
