SUMMARY:

Democrats introduced a bill aimed at closing loopholes in the 1963 Equal Pay Act, which would require employers to prove that pay differences are related to job performance, not gender, and allow women who believe they were discriminated against to sue for damages. Republicans blocked the motion, arguing that it was politically inspired and would reward trial lawyers at the expense of employers. President Obama criticized Republicans for putting "partisan politics ahead of American women and families." Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid accused Republicans of wanting to stick their heads in the sand about the issue of pay discrimination. Democrats acknowledged privately that they expected to make political gains with women by pressing to close the pay gap, which they said has women earning just 77 cents on the dollar compared with men.

The moral framing of this issue is clear: fairness and equality are at stake. Republicans' opposition to the bill suggests a disregard for the well-being of women and a prioritization of partisan politics over people's needs. By blocking the bill, Republicans are undermining efforts to address the longstanding issue of pay discrimination, which harms not only women but also the economy as a whole.
