SUMMARY:

The Senate blocked a Democratic bill aimed at closing the pay gap between men and women, which Democrats claimed would ensure that women don't face discrimination in the workplace. However, Republicans countered that the bill would reward trial lawyers at employers' expense by allowing for huge class-action lawsuits with little defense. President Obama criticized Republicans for putting "partisan politics ahead of American women and families." Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid accused Republicans of wanting to stick their heads in the sand about the longstanding issue of pay discrimination, while Democrats argued that they stand for equal rights. The bill would have required employers to prove that differences in pay were related to job performance, not gender, but was seen as an overreach by some Republicans. Sen. Barbara Mikulski vowed to return to the bill until it passes, saying "we are not giving up for equal pay." Democrats framed the issue as one of wanting to protect women from being discriminated against and ensure they receive fair compensation.

END OF SUMMARY.
