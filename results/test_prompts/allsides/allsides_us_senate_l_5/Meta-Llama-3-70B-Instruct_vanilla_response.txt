SUMMARY:

The US Senate has blocked a Democratic measure aimed at ensuring equal pay for women, with Republicans arguing that the bill would reward trial lawyers at the expense of employers. The bill, which needed 60 votes to pass, received only 52 votes, with no Republican support. The measure would have required employers to prove that pay differences were not based on gender, allowed employees to share salary information, and enabled women to sue for damages if they believed they were discriminated against. Democrats accused Republicans of putting "partisan politics ahead of American women and families," while Republicans claimed the bill was politically motivated and would lead to costly lawsuits. The bill's sponsor, Sen. Barbara Mikulski, vowed to bring the measure back until it passes. The outcome is seen as a political win for Democrats, who hope to capitalize on the issue with women voters in the upcoming election.

END OF SUMMARY.
