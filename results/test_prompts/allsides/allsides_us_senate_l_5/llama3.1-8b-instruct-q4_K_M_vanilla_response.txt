SUMMARY:

A bill aimed at closing the pay gap between men and women was blocked by Republicans in the Senate, despite President Obama's support. Democrats argued that the bill would ensure equal pay for women and close loopholes in the 1963 Equal Pay Act. Republicans countered that the bill would reward trial lawyers at the expense of employers and accused Democrats of using it as a political tool to gain votes from women.

The bill would require employers to prove that pay differences are based on job performance, not gender, and allow employees to share salary information with each other. President Obama criticized Republicans for prioritizing partisan politics over women's rights. Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid vowed to bring the measure back until it passes. The vote fell short of the 60 votes needed, with only 52 senators supporting the bill.

The issue is likely to be revisited in the future, with Democrats vowing not to give up on equal pay for women.
