SUMMARY:  
Democrats pushed a pay‑equity bill that would force employers to prove wage differences are based on performance, not gender, and would let women sue for discrimination. Republicans blocked the measure, calling it a political overreach that “allows” costly class‑action suits and burdens employers, while accusing Democrats of putting partisan politics ahead of real solutions. Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid *stood* with Lilly Ledbetter, saying Republicans want to “stick their heads in the sand” about women being paid less. GOP leaders, including Mitch McConnell and Mike Enzi, labeled the proposal the “trial‑lawyers bonanza” and argued it is the wrong way of dealing with the issue. Even moderate Republican Olympia Snowe called the bill an overreach and said it goes too far. Despite the defeat, sponsor Barbara Mikulski vowed to bring the bill back, saying she is not giving up the fight for equal pay and equal rights.  

END OF SUMMARY.
