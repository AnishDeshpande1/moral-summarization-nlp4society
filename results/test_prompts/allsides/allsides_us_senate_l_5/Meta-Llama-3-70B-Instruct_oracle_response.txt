SUMMARY:

Democrats pushed for a bill to ensure women don't face pay discrimination, but Republicans blocked it, citing concerns that it would reward trial lawyers at employers' expense. President Obama criticized Republicans for putting "partisan politics ahead of American women and families." Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid accused Republicans of "wanting to stick their heads in the sand" about the longstanding issue of women being paid less than men. The bill aimed to close loopholes in the 1963 Equal Pay Act, requiring employers to prove pay differences were related to job performance, not gender. Republicans argued they oppose pay discrimination but disagreed with the Democrats' bill, which they saw as a "reward for pro-Democratic trial lawyers." Democrats vowed to bring the measure back until it passes, with Sen. Barbara Mikulski stating, "We will be back... We are not giving up for equal pay, for equal rights."

END OF SUMMARY.
