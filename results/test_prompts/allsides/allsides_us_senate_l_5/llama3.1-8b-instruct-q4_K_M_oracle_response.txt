SUMMARY:

A bill aimed at closing the pay gap between men and women was blocked by Republicans, who claimed it would reward trial lawyers at employers' expense. Democrats argue that the measure is necessary to ensure that women don't face discrimination in the workplace. The bill would require employers to prove that differences in pay are related to job performance, not gender, and allow women to sue for damages if they believe they were discriminated against.

President Obama criticized Republicans for putting "partisan politics ahead of American women and families." Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid accused them of wanting to "stick their heads in the sand" about the issue. Democrats vowed to return to the bill until it passes, saying that it's essential for ensuring equal pay and rights for women.

Republicans argued that the bill was a "reward for pro-Democratic trial lawyers" and would place a "tremendous burden on employers through lawsuits." They claimed that Democrats were using the issue as politics rather than genuinely addressing the problem of pay discrimination. The debate highlights the moral imperative to ensure equal pay and rights for women.

END OF SUMMARY.
