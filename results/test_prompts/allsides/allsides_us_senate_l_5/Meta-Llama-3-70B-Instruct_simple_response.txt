SUMMARY:

In a move widely seen as politically motivated, Republicans blocked a Democratic bill aimed at ensuring equal pay for women, claiming it would reward trial lawyers at the expense of employers. The bill, which needed 60 votes to pass, received only 52, with not a single Republican supporting it. President Obama and Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid criticized Republicans for putting "partisan politics ahead of American women and families" and "sticking their heads in the sand" on the issue of pay discrimination. Democrats argued that the bill would close loopholes in the 1963 Equal Pay Act, while Republicans claimed it was an "overreach" that would lead to costly lawsuits. The bill's sponsor, Sen. Barbara Mikulski, vowed to bring the measure back until it passes, emphasizing the importance of equal pay and equal rights. The move is seen as a bid to appeal to women voters in an election year.

END OF SUMMARY.
