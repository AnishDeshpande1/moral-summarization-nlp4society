SUMMARY:
A Democratic bill aimed at closing loopholes in the 1963 Equal Pay Act, which would ensure that women don't face pay discrimination, was blocked by Republicans on Tuesday. Despite criticism from President Obama and Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid, who accused Republicans of putting "partisan politics ahead of American women and families," the motion failed to pass with only 52 votes. Republicans argued that the bill would reward trial lawyers at the expense of employers and put a burden on businesses through lawsuits. The bill's supporters claimed it was necessary to address the longstanding issue of pay inequality, which they said has women earning just 77 cents on the dollar compared with men. Senate Democrats have vowed to bring the measure back until it passes. The moral framing of this issue is clear: is it right to prioritize fairness and equality for women in the workplace, or are concerns about employer liability and litigation a more pressing concern?
