SUMMARY:

A bill aimed at closing the pay gap between men and women was blocked by Republicans in the Senate, who claimed it would reward trial lawyers at employers' expense. Democrats argued that the bill, which updates the 1963 Equal Pay Act, is necessary to ensure women are not discriminated against when it comes to pay. The bill requires employers to prove differences in pay are related to job performance, not gender, and allows women who believe they were discriminated against to sue for damages. President Obama criticized Republicans for prioritizing "partisan politics" over the interests of American women and families.

Despite the setback, Democrats vowed to continue pushing for the legislation. Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid accused Republicans of trying to hide from the issue, while Senator Barbara Mikulski pledged to return to the bill until it passes.

END OF SUMMARY.
