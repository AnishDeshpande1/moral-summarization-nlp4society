SUMMARY:

Attorney General William Barr announced on Thursday that 51 arrests have been made for federal crimes related to violent rioting during protests over the death of George Floyd. Barr stated that authorities are working to restore order and have deployed resources to quell outbreaks of violence in several places. He also noted that "three different sets of actors" are present at demonstrations: peaceful protesters, groups engaging in looting, and extremist agitators who are hijacking protests for their own agendas.

Barr attributed the violent activity to Antifa and other extremist groups, as well as foreign actors playing both sides to exacerbate violence. He also acknowledged concerns about police misconduct and promised to meet with law enforcement officials and community leaders to find solutions. Barr defended President Trump's visit to St. John's Church near the White House, stating that it was an "entirely appropriate" decision by the president as head of the executive branch.

END OF SUMMARY.
