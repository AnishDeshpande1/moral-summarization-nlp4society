SUMMARY: Attorney General William Barr announced that federal authorities have made 51 arrests for crimes tied to violent rioting during the protests sparked by George Floyd’s death. He said the Justice Department is working around the clock to restore order and quell outbreaks of violence, noting that while many peacefully expressed grief, others hijacked the demonstrations to engage in arson, looting, assaults on law‑enforcement officers and innocent people, and even the murder of a federal agent. Barr described these acts as senseless anarchy intended to terrify and intimidate citizens. He identified three sets of actors: peaceful protesters exercising First Amendment rights, groups exploiting the unrest for looting, and extremist agitators—including Antifa and foreign actors—who have instigated and participated in the violent activity to pursue their own agendas and exacerbate the chaos. Acknowledging misconduct and injustices within policing, Barr pledged to meet community leaders to seek constructive solutions and uphold equal protection under the Constitution. He also stated the President’s walk to St. John’s Church was not a political act and was unrelated to perimeter changes. END OF SUMMARY.
