Attorney General William Barr announced that 51 federal arrests have been made in connection with “violent” rioting, “lawlessness,” “arson,” “looting,” “assaults,” and even the “murder” of a federal agent during protests over George Floyd’s death. While acknowledging widespread “injustice” and a lack of “confidence” in the criminal‑justice system among African Americans, he pledged to meet community leaders to seek “equal” “protection” under the law.
