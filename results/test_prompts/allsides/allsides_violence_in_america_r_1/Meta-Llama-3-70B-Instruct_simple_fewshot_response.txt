SUMMARY:

Attorney General William Barr announced that 51 arrests have been made for federal crimes related to violent rioting amid protests over the death of George Floyd. Barr condemned the violence, saying it was not an exercise of First Amendment rights, but rather crimes designed to terrorize citizens and intimidate communities. He identified three groups present at the demonstrations: peaceful protesters, looters, and extremist agitators who have hijacked the protests. Barr cited evidence that Antifa and other extremist groups, as well as foreign actors, have been involved in instigating and participating in the violent activity. While acknowledging concerns about police misconduct and injustices in the criminal justice system, Barr emphasized that the vast majority of police officers do their job bravely and righteously. He also defended President Trump's visit to St. John's Church, saying it was not a political act and that the President should be able to walk outside the White House.

END OF SUMMARY.
