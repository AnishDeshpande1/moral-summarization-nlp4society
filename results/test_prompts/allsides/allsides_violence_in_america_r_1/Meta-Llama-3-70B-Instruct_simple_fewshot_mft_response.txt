SUMMARY:

Attorney General William Barr announced that 51 arrests have been made for federal crimes related to violent rioting amidst protests over George Floyd's death. Barr condemned the "senseless acts of anarchy" and emphasized that authorities are working to restore order. He distinguished between peaceful protesters, looters, and extremist agitators, citing evidence that the latter group is responsible for the violent activity. Barr acknowledged concerns about police misconduct and injustices in the criminal justice system, pledging to find "constructive solutions." The Attorney General's remarks underscore the importance of maintaining authority and preventing subversion, while also addressing the harm and unfairness experienced by African Americans. By condemning violent rioting and acknowledging systemic injustices, Barr's statement seeks to strike a balance between upholding authority and promoting care and fairness.

END OF SUMMARY.
