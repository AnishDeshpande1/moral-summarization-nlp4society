SUMMARY:

Attorney General William Barr announced that 51 arrests have been made for federal crimes related to violent rioting during protests over the death of George Floyd. Barr condemned the "senseless acts of anarchy" and "lawlessness" that have hijacked peaceful protests, resulting in violent rioting, arson, looting, and assaults on law enforcement officers and innocent people. He emphasized that these acts are crimes, not exercises of First Amendment rights. Barr identified three groups at the demonstrations: peaceful protesters, looters, and extremist agitators who have hijacked the protests. He stated that evidence points to Antifa and other extremist groups, as well as foreign actors, as instigators of the violent activity. Barr also acknowledged concerns about police misconduct and injustices in the criminal justice system, promising to work towards constructive solutions.

END OF SUMMARY.
