SUMMARY:

Attorney General William Barr announced that 51 arrests have been made for federal crimes related to violent rioting amid protests over the death of George Floyd. Barr condemned the "senseless acts of anarchy" and "crimes designed to terrify fellow citizens and intimidate communities" that have hijacked peaceful protests. He emphasized that authorities are working to restore order and quell outbreaks of violence, which have resulted in arson, looting, assaults, and even murder. Barr identified three sets of actors at the demonstrations: peaceful protesters, groups exploiting the opportunity to engage in looting, and extremist agitators who have hijacked the protests to pursue their own agendas. He acknowledged that there is evidence that Antifa and other extremist groups, as well as foreign actors, have been involved in instigating and participating in the violent activity. Barr also addressed concerns about police misconduct and injustices in the criminal justice system, emphasizing the need for equal protection of the laws.

END OF SUMMARY.
