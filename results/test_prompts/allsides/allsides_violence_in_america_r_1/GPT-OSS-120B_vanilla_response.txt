SUMMARY: Attorney General William Barr announced that 51 arrests have been made for federal crimes linked to violent rioting during the nationwide protests sparked by George Floyd’s death. He emphasized that while many demonstrators are peacefully exercising First Amendment rights, others have turned protests into lawlessness, including arson, looting, assaults, and the murder of a federal agent. Barr identified three groups at the protests: peaceful protesters, opportunistic looters, and extremist agitators—citing Antifa, other extremist factions, and foreign actors as contributors to the violence. He pledged continued federal effort to restore order and said he will meet with law‑enforcement and community leaders to address concerns about police misconduct and systemic injustice. Regarding President Donald Trump’s walk to St. John’s Church after protesters were cleared from Lafayette Park, Barr said the decision to expand the security perimeter was unrelated to the President’s actions and considered the walk “appropriate,” not a political act. END OF SUMMARY.
