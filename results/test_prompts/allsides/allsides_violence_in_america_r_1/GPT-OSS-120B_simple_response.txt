SUMMARY: Attorney General William Barr announced that federal authorities have made 51 arrests for crimes tied to violent rioting during the George Floyd protests. He emphasized that while many demonstrators peacefully exercised their First‑Amendment rights, a minority “hijacked” the movement to commit arson, looting, assaults on police and civilians, and even the murder of a federal agent—acts he called “senseless anarchy” rather than lawful protest. Barr identified three groups at the scenes: peaceful protesters, opportunistic looters, and extremist agitators (including Antifa and other political factions) he said bear responsibility for the worst violence, with foreign actors also seeking to inflame tensions. He pledged continued federal effort to restore order and to meet with law‑enforcement and community leaders to address African‑American mistrust of the justice system, affirming the Constitution’s guarantee of equal protection. Regarding President Trump’s walk to St. John’s Church after Lafayette Park was cleared, Barr claimed no coordination and deemed the president’s actions appropriate, not political. END OF SUMMARY.
