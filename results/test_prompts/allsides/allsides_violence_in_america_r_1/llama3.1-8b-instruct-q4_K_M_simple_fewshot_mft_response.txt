SUMMARY:

Attorney General William Barr condemned the violent rioting that has erupted across the country following the death of George Floyd, stating that such acts are not exercises of First Amendment rights but rather crimes designed to terrify citizens and intimidate communities. He noted that authorities have made 51 arrests for federal crimes related to violent rioting and that resources have been deployed to quell outbreaks of violence in several places. Barr emphasized that while many protesters have peacefully expressed their anger and grief, others have hijacked the protests to engage in lawlessness, including looting, arson, and assaults on law enforcement officers and innocent people. He also acknowledged concerns about police misconduct and injustices within the criminal justice system, stating that he will meet with the department's commission on law enforcement and community leaders to find constructive solutions.

Barr's statement highlights the importance of maintaining authority and order in the face of chaos and violence, while also acknowledging the need for reform and accountability within the justice system. He emphasized the importance of respecting the rule of law and protecting citizens from harm, while also addressing legitimate concerns about police misconduct and systemic injustices.
