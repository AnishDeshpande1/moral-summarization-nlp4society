SUMMARY: Democratic presidential candidates are intensifying efforts to protect abortion rights after restrictive state laws in Georgia and Alabama. Senator Cory Booker proposes creating a White House Office of Reproductive Freedom and pledges executive and legislative actions, including codifying Roe v. Wade and funding overseas abortions. Senator Kirsten Gillibrand argues that abortion restrictions conflict with Christian faith and the Constitution, promising to repeal the Hyde Amendment and support federal protection of abortion. Front‑runner Joe Biden also backs congressional legislation to enshrine abortion rights if needed. Meanwhile, House and Senate Democrats are pushing the Women’s Health Protection Act again, a bill introduced annually since 2013 to safeguard Roe v. Wade. Senator Richard Blumenthal warns of a “five‑alarm fire” over threats to women’s reproductive rights. END OF SUMMARY.
