SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential hopefuls are doubling down on their efforts to protect abortion access and reproductive rights after numerous states passed laws to restrict abortion. Sen. Cory Booker proposes creating a "White House Office of Reproductive Freedom" to advance reproductive rights and take executive action to respond to the "coordinated attack" on Americans' rights to control their own bodies. He vows to pursue legislation to codify Roe v. Wade into federal law and fund abortions overseas. Sen. Kristen Gillibrand claims that laws restricting abortion access are "against Christian faith" and violate the separation of church and state. She wants to enshrine abortion access into US federal law and eliminate the Hyde Amendment, which bars federal funding for abortions. Democratic lawmakers are taking steps to protect abortion access, citing the "danger to women's reproductive rights" and the need to "sound the alarm" and "command the urgency and immediacy" of the situation.

END OF SUMMARY.
