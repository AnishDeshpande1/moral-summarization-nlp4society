SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential hopefuls are intensifying their efforts to protect abortion access, condemning recent state laws restricting abortion as an attack on human rights. Sen. Cory Booker vows to create a "White House Office of Reproductive Freedom" and take executive action to protect abortion on his first day in office. He also plans to codify Roe v. Wade into federal law and fund abortions overseas. Sen. Kristen Gillibrand argues that laws restricting abortion access are "against Christian faith" and violate the separation of church and state. She and other candidates aim to enshrine abortion access into federal law and eliminate the Hyde Amendment, which bars federal funding for abortions. Democratic lawmakers are also pushing the "Women's Health Protection Act" to protect abortion and Roe v. Wade. The moral imperative is clear: restricting abortion access is an attack on human dignity, freedom, and democracy.

END OF SUMMARY.
