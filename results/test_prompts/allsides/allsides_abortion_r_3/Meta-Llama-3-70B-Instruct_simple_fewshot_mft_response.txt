SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential hopefuls are intensifying their efforts to protect abortion access, condemning recent state laws that restrict abortion as an attack on women's rights and autonomy. Cory Booker proposes a "White House Office of Reproductive Freedom" and vows to take executive action to protect abortion on his first day as president. Kristen Gillibrand argues that laws restricting abortion access are "against Christian faith" and violate the separation of church and state. Joe Biden's campaign also supports enshrining abortion rights into federal law if necessary. The candidates' stance is rooted in the moral foundations of Authority/Subversion, as they seek to counter the oppressive laws that undermine women's autonomy and reproductive freedom. They also appeal to the Care/Harm dimension, emphasizing the harm caused by restrictive laws to women's health and well-being. The Fairness/Cheating dimension is also invoked, as the candidates argue that restricting abortion access is unfair and cheats women of their fundamental rights. Finally, the Sanctity/Degradation dimension is implicit, as the candidates seek to protect the dignity and sanctity of women's bodies and lives.

END OF SUMMARY.
