SUMMARY:  
Democratic presidential hopefuls are intensifying a moral campaign to safeguard abortion access after restrictive state laws in Georgia, Alabama and elsewhere. Cory Booker proposes a White House Office of Reproductive Freedom and promises immediate executive action and legislation to codify Roe v. Wade, framing the fight as a “coordinated response” to attacks on bodily autonomy and even pledging funding for abortions abroad. Senator Kirsten Gillibrand argues that abortion restrictions violate both the Constitution’s separation of church and state and “Christian faith,” invoking free will as a shared moral principle. She also vows to repeal the Hyde Amendment, which bars federal funding for abortions. Front‑runner Joe Biden signals support for federal constitutional protection of abortion if needed. Meanwhile, Democrats are pushing the Women’s Health Protection Act again, describing the threat to reproductive rights as a “five‑alarm fire” demanding urgent action. The overall moral framing presents abortion rights as essential to personal liberty, religious conscience, and democratic values, demanding decisive federal leadership.  
END OF SUMMARY.
