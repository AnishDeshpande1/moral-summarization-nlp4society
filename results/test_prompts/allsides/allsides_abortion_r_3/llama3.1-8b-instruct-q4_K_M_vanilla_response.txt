SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential hopefuls, including Sen. Cory Booker, Sen. Kristen Gillibrand, and Joe Biden, are emphasizing their commitment to protecting abortion access in the face of restrictive state laws. Booker has announced plans for a "White House Office of Reproductive Freedom" and proposed legislative actions to protect abortion rights. Gillibrand claims that restricting abortion is "against Christian faith" and vows to eliminate the Hyde Amendment, which bars federal funding for abortions. Biden's campaign has also expressed support for enshrining abortion rights into federal law.

In Congress, lawmakers are pushing forward the "Women's Health Protection Act," a measure that aims to protect Roe v. Wade, the landmark Supreme Court decision legalizing abortion. Sen. Richard Blumenthal warned of a "five-alarm fire" threatening women's reproductive rights and urged swift action to safeguard access to abortion.

The Democratic candidates' efforts aim to counter restrictive state laws and ensure continued access to abortion services nationwide. Their proposals focus on protecting Roe v. Wade, eliminating funding restrictions, and promoting reproductive freedom.

END OF SUMMARY.
