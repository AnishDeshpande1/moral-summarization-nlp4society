SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential hopefuls are intensifying their efforts to protect abortion access in response to recent state laws restricting abortion. Sen. Cory Booker proposes creating a "White House Office of Reproductive Freedom" and taking executive and legislative actions to protect abortion, including codifying Roe v. Wade into federal law. Sen. Kristen Gillibrand claims that laws restricting abortion access are "against Christian faith" and wants to enshrine abortion access into federal law, eliminating the Hyde Amendment. Joe Biden's campaign also supports enshrining abortion rights into federal law if necessary. Democratic lawmakers are pushing the "Women's Health Protection Act" to protect abortion and Roe v. Wade. Sen. Richard Blumenthal warns of a "five-alarm fire" threatening women's reproductive rights. The Democrats aim to make abortion access a central issue in the 2020 presidential election.

END OF SUMMARY.
