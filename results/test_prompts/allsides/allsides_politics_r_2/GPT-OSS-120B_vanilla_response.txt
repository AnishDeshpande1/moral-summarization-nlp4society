SUMMARY: Acting Homeland Security Deputy Secretary Ken Cuccinelli claimed he was verbally harassed by Maryland Governor Martin O’Malley at an Irish pub in Washington, D.C., during a Gonzaga College High School alumni event on November 27, 2019. Cuccinelli described O’Malley’s outburst as a “passion‑laced, shame‑invoking tirade” over the Trump administration’s immigration policies, alleging that O’Malley shouted curses, threatened him, and was joined by associates. Cuccinelli said he tried to de‑escalate by moving to another bar area, but O’Malley followed and continued the confrontation. The incident was reported to Fox News, and O’Malley later texted the Washington Post expressing no regret, criticizing Cuccinelli for “caging refugee children.” The episode sparked commentary on political harassment, with some Democrats reportedly praising the confrontation. END OF SUMMARY.
