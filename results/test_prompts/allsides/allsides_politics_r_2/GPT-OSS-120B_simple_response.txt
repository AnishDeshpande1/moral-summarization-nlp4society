SUMMARY:  
In a night meant for a quiet alumni gathering at Washington’s Dubliner Irish pub, Acting Homeland Security Deputy Secretary **Ken Cuccinelli** was violently confronted by Maryland Governor **Martin O’Malley**. The author portrays O’Malley’s outburst—filled with “passion‑laced, shame‑invoking tirade” and vulgar curses about immigration policy, the President, and Cuccinelli’s Italian heritage—as a morally reprehensible act of public harassment. Cuccinelli described O’Malley’s neck veins bulging with anger, his followers joining the verbal assault, and the governor even pressing him to “take a swing.” The incident escalated until a Democratic friend intervened, allowing Cuccinelli to retreat. The piece emphasizes that O’Malley’s behavior, while shocking, is allegedly consistent with his past conduct, and underscores the author’s view that such aggression—especially when celebrated by left‑wing figures—represents a disgraceful, ethically wrong use of political power. The moral framing condemns O’Malley’s intimidation of a public servant and denounces the left’s apparent endorsement of the harassment.
