Acting Homeland Security Deputy Secretary Ken Cuccinelli was publicly harassed by Maryland Gov. Martin O'Malley at an Irish pub in Washington, D.C. O'Malley unleashed a "passion-laced and shame-invoking tirade" over the Trump administration's immigration policies, particularly the treatment of immigrant refugee children. Cuccinelli described O'Malley's behavior as "sad" and "shocking," with veins "bulging" on his neck as he cursed and screamed. O'Malley also inspired others to join in the "cussing assault." When Cuccinelli tried to deescalate the situation, O'Malley followed him, bumping into him and inviting him to take a swing. The incident was widely condemned, with many calling O'Malley's behavior "disgusting" and "harassment." Even a senior adviser to a Democrat presidential candidate seemed "happy" about the incident, promoting a culture of harassment against Trump administration officials. END OF SUMMARY.
