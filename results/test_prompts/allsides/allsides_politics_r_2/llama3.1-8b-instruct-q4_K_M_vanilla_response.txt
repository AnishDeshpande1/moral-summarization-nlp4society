SUMMARY:
Maryland Gov. Martin O'Malley publicly harassed Acting Homeland Security Deputy Secretary Ken Cuccinelli at an Irish pub in Washington, D.C. on Wednesday. O'Malley was reportedly screaming and cursing at Cuccinelli over the Trump administration's immigration policies, with veins bulging on his neck. Cuccinelli attempted to deescalate the situation by leaving, but O'Malley followed him and confronted him face-to-face. A friend intervened, forcing a brief conversation between the two men. O'Malley defended his behavior in a text to The Washington Post, saying he was expressing outrage over the treatment of refugee immigrant kids. This incident has sparked controversy, with some on the left celebrating the harassment and criticizing Cuccinelli's policies. Senior Adviser to Pete Buttigieg's presidential campaign appeared to condone O'Malley's actions, prompting criticism from others who deemed it "disgusting" for a presidential campaign to promote harassment of officials.
