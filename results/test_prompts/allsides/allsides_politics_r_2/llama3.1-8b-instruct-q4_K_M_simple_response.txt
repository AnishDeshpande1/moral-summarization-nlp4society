SUMMARY:

Maryland Governor Martin O'Malley publicly harassed Acting Homeland Security Deputy Secretary Ken Cuccinelli at an Irish pub in Washington, D.C., over the Trump administration's immigration policies. According to Cuccinelli, O'Malley engaged in a "passion-laced and shame-invoking tirade" that left him feeling uncomfortable and forced him to leave the area. O'Malley was reportedly so enraged that his neck veins were bulging, and he even invited Cuccinelli to take a swing at him. The incident has raised concerns about the escalating rhetoric and behavior of some Democrats towards Trump administration officials.

The incident is all the more disturbing given that senior adviser to Democratic presidential candidate Pete Buttigieg appeared to celebrate O'Malley's actions on Twitter. This kind of harassment and celebration of it are morally reprehensible, as they promote a culture of intolerance and aggression. As Cuccinelli noted, his behavior was "sad" and "shocking," but also not surprising given O'Malley's history of such outbursts.

END OF SUMMARY.
