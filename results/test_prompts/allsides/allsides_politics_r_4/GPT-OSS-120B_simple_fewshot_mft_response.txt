SUMMARY:
The article argues that the president is shielded from indictment not because he is innocent but because the Justice Department treats a sitting president as untouchable, leaving the nation to live under the “specter of prison” while an alleged co‑conspirator runs the country. This exemption subverts the principle of equal authority before the law and threatens the public’s welfare, exposing citizens to harm as they are governed by someone implicated in felonies. The author frames Trump’s campaign‑finance violations and the convictions of close aides as clear cases of cheating that violate fairness, noting that his defenders cling to a cult‑like loyalty, refusing to acknowledge the corrupt “fruit” of his tree. By praising figures who resisted Trump—Comey, Mueller, Rosenstein, and honest journalists—the piece highlights loyalty to truth versus betrayal of democratic norms. The piece warns that the current tolerance of this subversion of authority, together with the moral decay of loyalty to a corrupt leader, endangers the nation’s care for its people and its commitment to fairness.
