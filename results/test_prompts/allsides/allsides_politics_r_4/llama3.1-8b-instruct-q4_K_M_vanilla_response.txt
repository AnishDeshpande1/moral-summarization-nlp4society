SUMMARY:

Prosecutors have declined to indict President Donald Trump in connection with campaign finance violations, citing the Justice Department's policy that a sitting president is exempt from indictment. This means Trump will likely serve out his term under the cloud of potential guilt, despite being implicated in a crime by his longtime personal lawyer Michael Cohen. Trump's defenders downplay the significance of the charges and claim there's no connection to Russia, but critics argue this is a cultlike behavior to shield the president from accountability. Five of Trump's associates have been convicted or pleaded guilty, and he has shown a pattern of employing people with questionable character. The article suggests that Trump's actions are characteristic of a corrupt tree, producing evil fruit, and that the worst may be yet to come.

END OF SUMMARY.
