SUMMARY:  
The article frames President Donald Trump as the “corrupt tree” whose “evil fruit” is evident in the criminal convictions of his close associates. It argues that prosecutors have not indicted him not because of innocence but because a long‑standing Justice Department policy shields a sitting president, leaving the nation under the “specter of prison” while an “unindicted co‑conspirator” governs. Trump’s defenders are portrayed as cult‑like believers who would “rather eat the foul fruit than recognize the nature of the tree.” The piece highlights Trump’s comfort with felons—five of his aides have been convicted or pleaded guilty—contrasting this with the integrity of figures like James Comey, Robert Mueller, and Rod Rosenstein, as well as former President Barack Obama, whose administrations lacked credible corruption allegations. It notes Trump’s repeated lies about hush‑money payments and his willingness to employ financially desperate, potentially compromised operatives such as Paul Manafort. The author warns that, while Tuesday’s convictions are a “bad day” for the president, the moral decay suggested by his circle predicts even worse consequences ahead.  

END OF SUMMARY.
