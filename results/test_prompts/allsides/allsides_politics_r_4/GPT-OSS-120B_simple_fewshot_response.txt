SUMMARY:
The article argues that President Donald Trump avoided indictment not because he is innocent but because the Justice Department’s long‑standing rule shields a sitting president from criminal charges. Consequently, the nation is being governed by an “unindicted co‑conspirator” while Trump’s close associates face convictions or guilty pleas for campaign‑finance crimes, tax fraud and other felonies. The author likens Trump’s supporters to a cult that prefers “foul fruit” over truth, and condemns Trump’s habit of surrounding himself with criminals—citing Michael Cohen, Paul Manafort, Michael Flynn and others—while disparaging his animus toward ethical figures like James Comey, Robert Mueller and Barack Obama. Although Trump has denied knowledge of hush‑money payments, he later admitted they occurred and that he reimbursed Cohen. The piece suggests that Trump’s pattern of lying and his willingness to employ felons reveal a corrupt “tree” whose poisonous fruit the country is forced to swallow, warning that the worst may still lie ahead.
