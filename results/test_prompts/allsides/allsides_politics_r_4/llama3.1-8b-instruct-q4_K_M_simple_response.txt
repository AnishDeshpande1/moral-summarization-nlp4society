SUMMARY:

A sitting US president, Donald Trump, may not face indictment despite being implicated in a crime by his close associate Michael Cohen. This is due to the Justice Department's policy of exempting presidents from prosecution until they leave office. Trump's defenders downplay the significance of campaign finance violations and ignore connections to Russia. The author argues that Trump has a pattern of surrounding himself with criminals, including five associates convicted or pleading guilty. Trump's animus towards those with integrity, such as Robert Mueller and James Comey, is also highlighted. The author concludes that Trump's actions suggest a corrupt tree bearing evil fruit, and the nation is being governed by an unindicted co-conspirator.
