SUMMARY: The article argues that President Donald Trump cannot be indicted while in office because the Justice Department’s longstanding policy shields a sitting president from criminal charges. Consequently, Trump may finish his term under the “specter of prison” despite multiple close associates—Michael Cohen, Paul Manafort, Michael Flynn, and others—being convicted or pleading guilty to felonies. The piece contends Trump’s defenders downplay these crimes and liken their loyalty to a cult, emphasizing his tolerance for criminal companions and hostility toward principled figures like James Comey, Robert Mueller, and Rod Rosenstein. It notes Trump’s repeated falsehoods, including admissions about hush‑money payments to Stormy Daniels and reimbursing Cohen. The author warns that, although prosecutors may delay indictment and Congress may avoid impeachment, the nation is being governed by an “unindicted co‑conspirator,” and the worst may still lie ahead. END OF SUMMARY.
