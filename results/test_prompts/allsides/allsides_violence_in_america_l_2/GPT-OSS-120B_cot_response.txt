**SUMMARY:**  
Lynette Lederman, former president of Pittsburgh’s Tree of Life Synagogue, condemned President Trump as a “purveyor of hate speech” and said she does not welcome him in Pittsburgh after the antisemitic massacre that killed 11 worshippers. She urged local leaders to uphold “values” that reject the president’s “polarizing” rhetoric and demanded a full denouncement of white nationalism before any visit. A coalition of Jewish leaders echoed this, stating Trump is not welcome until he repudiates white supremacist ideology. While Trump labeled the media “the true enemy of the people” and accused it of fake, inaccurate reporting, Tree of Life Rabbi Jeffrey Myers took a contrasting stance, saying the president, as the nation’s leader, is always welcome. The shooter, charged with 29 federal crimes, highlighted a surge in antisemitic incidents, which the ADL attributes to mainstreaming of hate. In response, interfaith fundraising efforts raised over $600,000, showing solidarity and support for the grieving community.
