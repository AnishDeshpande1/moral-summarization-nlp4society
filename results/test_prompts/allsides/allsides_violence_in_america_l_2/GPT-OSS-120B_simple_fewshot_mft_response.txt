SUMMARY:
Lynette Lederman, former president of Pittsburgh’s Tree of Life Synagogue, said the city will rely on its own leaders after the anti‑Jewish massacre and warned that President Trump is “a purveyor of hate speech” who is not welcome until he denounces white nationalism. Her statement, echoed in an open letter from local Jewish leaders, frames the president’s rhetoric as harmful and unjust, calling for fairness and a repudiation of the hateful ideology that threatens the community’s safety. While Rabbi Jeffrey Myers welcomed the president, emphasizing that hate transcends religion or politics, many mourners and advocacy groups highlighted the deep betrayal felt by the victims’ families and the need to protect sacred communal values. The incident, the deadliest anti‑Jewish attack in U.S. history, underscores the profound harm inflicted on innocent worshippers and the urgent demand for moral accountability, solidarity, and a reaffirmation of the sanctity of life against degrading hate. The contrasting responses reveal a community torn between loyalty to national leadership and the imperative to uphold care, fairness, and the dignity of all. END OF SUMMARY.
