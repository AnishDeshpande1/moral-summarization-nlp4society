SUMMARY:

A former president of the Tree of Life Synagogue in Pittsburgh, Lynette Lederman, has condemned President Trump's rhetoric, labelling him a "purveyor of hate speech" and stating that he is not welcome in the city. Lederman instead relies on local political leadership, who "stand by us" and "believe in values" that are not reflected in Trump's presidency. A coalition of local Jewish leaders has also called on Trump to "fully denounce white nationalism" before visiting Pittsburgh. The president's polarizing rhetoric has been criticized in the aftermath of the mass shooting, which is the deadliest attack against Jewish people in America. The Anti-Defamation League reports a 57% rise in antisemitic incidents in 2017, with hate speech and violence becoming increasingly mainstream. As the community mourns the loss of 11 lives, Lederman urges leaders to "stop the words of hate" and promote "safety in solidarity".

END OF SUMMARY.
