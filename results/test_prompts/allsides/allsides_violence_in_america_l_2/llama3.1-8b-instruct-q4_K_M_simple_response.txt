SUMMARY:

Following the Pittsburgh synagogue shooting that killed 11 people, Rabbi Lynette Lederman expressed her opposition to President Trump's visit to the city, labeling him a "purveyor of hate speech". She stated that she and other local leaders would not welcome Trump until he denounces white nationalism. A coalition of Jewish leaders also signed an open letter calling for Trump to avoid Pittsburgh. Meanwhile, Rabbi Jeffrey Myers welcomed the president to the city, saying hate is not a political issue and exists in all people. The shooting has been condemned as the most deadly attack against Jewish people in America, with many attributing it to a rise in antisemitic attacks fueled by Trump's polarizing rhetoric. As the community mourns the victims, fundraising efforts have driven financial support, with over $90,000 raised so far.

END OF SUMMARY.
