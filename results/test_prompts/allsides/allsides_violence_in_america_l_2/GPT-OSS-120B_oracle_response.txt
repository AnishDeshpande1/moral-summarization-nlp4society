SUMMARY:  
Lynette Lederman, former president of Pittsburgh’s Tree of Life Synagogue, said President Trump is a “purveyor of hate speech” and is not welcome in the city until he denounces white nationalism. She urged leaders to “stop the words of hate” and to stand by values that reject the president’s rhetoric. A coalition of local Jewish leaders echoed this, demanding Trump denounce antisemitic hate and warning that his polarizing rhetoric fuels a rise in antisemitic incidents, harassment and violence. The deadly shooting that killed 11 worshippers—an attack marked by gunfire, tearing through the sanctuary and hateful rants—has spurred nationwide solidarity, safety efforts and fundraising campaigns that have raised hundreds of thousands of dollars for the grieving community. While Rabbi Jeffrey Myers welcomed the president, emphasizing that hate knows no religion, race or political party, the ADL warned that antisemitism is moving from the margins to the mainstream as supremacists repeat hostile rhetoric. Trump, meanwhile, labeled the media “the true enemy of the people,” accusing it of inaccurate, fraudulent reporting and demanding it report fairly. The shooter, charged with multiple murders, was released from hospital and faces federal prosecution.  
END OF SUMMARY.
