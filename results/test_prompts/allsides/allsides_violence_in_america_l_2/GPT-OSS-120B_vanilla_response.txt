SUMMARY:  
Lynette Lederman, former president of Pittsburgh’s Tree of Life Synagogue, told CNN she will rely on local leaders after the mass shooting and does not want President Donald Trump to visit the city, calling him a “purveyor of hate speech.” A coalition of local Jewish leaders echoed this, demanding Trump denounce white nationalism before being welcome. Despite the backlash, the synagogue’s current rabbi, Jeffrey Myers, said the president is always welcome as the nation’s leader. Trump, who labeled the attack an “evil antisemitic attack,” is scheduled to visit Pittsburgh on Tuesday; he later attacked the media on Twitter, calling it “the true Enemy of the People.” The shooter, Robert Bowers, has been charged with 29 federal crimes, including 11 counts of murder, and was released from the hospital pending court appearance. The ADL noted a 57% rise in antisemitic incidents, warning that hate rhetoric is moving into the mainstream. Community fundraising efforts have raised over $600,000 to support victims’ families.  

END OF SUMMARY.
