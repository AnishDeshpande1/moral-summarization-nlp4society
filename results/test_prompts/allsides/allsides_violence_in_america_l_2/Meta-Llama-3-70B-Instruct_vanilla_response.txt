SUMMARY:

Lynette Lederman, a former president of the Tree of Life Synagogue in Pittsburgh, has stated that President Donald Trump is not welcome in the city following the mass shooting that killed 11 people. Lederman believes Trump's rhetoric has contributed to the rise of hate speech and white nationalism. A coalition of local Jewish leaders has also called on Trump to fully denounce white nationalism before visiting the city. Trump has labeled the shooting an "evil antisemitic attack" and is set to visit Pittsburgh on Tuesday. The shooting is the deadliest attack against Jewish people in America, and the Anti-Defamation League has reported a 57% rise in antisemitic incidents in 2017. Fundraising efforts have raised over $600,000 for the grieving community. The suspected gunman, Robert Bowers, has been charged with 29 federal crimes, including 11 counts of murder.

END OF SUMMARY.
