SUMMARY:

A former president of the Tree of Life Synagogue in Pittsburgh, Lynette Lederman, has spoken out against President Donald Trump's visit to the city, labeling him a "purveyor of hate speech". Lederman stated that she would rely on local political leadership and does not want the president to visit, as his values do not align with those of the community. This comes after a mass shooting at the synagogue, which resulted in the deaths of 11 people. The shooting has been condemned as an "evil antisemitic attack" by Trump, but many have criticized his polarizing rhetoric and failure to fully denounce white nationalism. The Anti-Defamation League has reported a 57% rise in antisemitic incidents in 2017, and many see Trump's words as contributing to an environment of hate. In contrast, the current rabbi of the synagogue has welcomed Trump's visit, stating that hate is not a political issue.

END OF SUMMARY.
