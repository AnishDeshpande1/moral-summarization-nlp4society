SUMMARY:
Lynette Lederman, former president of Pittsburgh’s Tree of Life Synagogue, said President Trump is “a purveyor of hate speech” and will not be welcome in the city after the antisemitic mass shooting that killed 11 worshippers. She urged reliance on local leaders and joined a letter from Jewish groups demanding Trump denounce white nationalism before any visit. While the White House confirmed Trump’s planned trip, the synagogue’s current rabbi, Jeffrey Myers, said the president is always welcome as the nation’s leader, emphasizing that hate transcends politics. The tragedy sparked a surge of solidarity fundraising, including a Muslim‑led campaign that raised over $90,000 and a student‑run effort that collected more than $545,000. The shooter, Robert Bowers, was charged with 29 federal crimes and is awaiting trial. The incident has intensified criticism of Trump’s polarizing rhetoric; the president responded by attacking the media as “the true Enemy of the People.” The moral framing highlights the community’s call for accountability and denouncement of hate, contrasting local leaders’ pleas for unity with the president’s confrontational stance. END OF SUMMARY.
