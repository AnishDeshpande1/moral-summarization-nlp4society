SUMMARY:

In the aftermath of the Pittsburgh synagogue shooting that left 11 people dead, Rabbi Lynette Lederman stated that she does not want President Trump to visit the city, labeling him a "purveyor of hate speech". A coalition of local Jewish leaders also signed an open letter calling for Trump to avoid Pittsburgh until he denounces white nationalism. Trump is set to visit the city on Tuesday despite criticism over his polarizing rhetoric. The shooting has been described as the most deadly attack against Jewish people in America, with a 57% rise in reported incidents of antisemitic harassment and violence in 2017.

The suspect, Robert Bowers, was charged with 29 federal crimes, including 11 counts of murdering victims exercising their religious beliefs. Bowers had purchased his firearms legally and had no previous criminal record. The Tree of Life rabbi, Jeffrey Myers, welcomed Trump to the city, saying that hate does not know any particular group or party.

END OF SUMMARY.
