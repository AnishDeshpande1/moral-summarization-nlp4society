SUMMARY: The author argues that former President Trump’s push to hold the Republican National Convention amid the COVID‑19 pandemic is driven by selfish political calculus rather than public health concern. Whether Trump hoped to blame a Democratic governor or to create a partisan showdown, the result is a reckless plan that forces thousands of supporters—and potentially millions of Americans—to risk infection for “optics.” The piece condemns Trump’s apparent indifference to the lives of his followers, labeling his behavior as morally reprehensible, self‑absorbed, and reminiscent of a schoolyard bully seeking attention. In contrast, former Vice President Joe Biden is portrayed as a responsible leader who criticizes Trump’s mask‑less bravado and “macho” conduct, emphasizing that true leadership protects the vulnerable rather than endangers them. The author warns that such reckless, selfish actions will further erode Trump’s political legitimacy and could be a decisive factor in his removal from office. END OF SUMMARY.
