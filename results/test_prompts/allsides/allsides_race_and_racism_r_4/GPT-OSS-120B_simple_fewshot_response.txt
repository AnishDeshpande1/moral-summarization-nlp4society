SUMMARY:
A Fort Worth police officer fatally shot 28‑year‑old Atatiana Jefferson, a Black woman watching her 8‑year‑old nephew, after a neighbor called about an open front door. Body‑cam video shows officers approaching the home, one shouting “Put your hands up,” then firing through a window without announcing his presence. Jefferson’s family and attorney Lee Merritt called the killing “murder,” emphasizing that the officer “didn’t have time to perceive a threat” and that the police, who are supposed to protect, failed to do so. The officer, on administrative leave, has not been named, and investigators are probing why he did not identify himself or warn the occupants. The incident reignites outrage over the pattern of white officers killing unarmed Black people, echoing the recent Amber Guyger case. Community members expressed grief, confusion, and fear of calling police, while Senator Bernie Sanders urged a Justice Department investigation, demanding an end to killings of unarmed Black Americans. The family demands a swift, thorough investigation and accountability.  
END OF SUMMARY.
