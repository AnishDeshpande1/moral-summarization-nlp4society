**SUMMARY:**
A white Fort Worth police officer fatally shot 28‑year‑old Atatiana Jefferson, an unarmed Black woman, while she was watching her 8‑year‑old nephew inside her home. The officer, who did not announce his presence before firing, claimed he perceived a threat, but family attorney Lee Merritt called the killing “murder,” accusing the officer of subverting his authority to protect. Jefferson’s sister said the police, who are supposed to protect, instead caused an unspeakable loss and denied her family justice. The family expressed confusion and grief, demanding a thorough, transparent investigation and accountability for the apparent cheating of legal fairness. Police placed the officer on administrative leave while the department, under Chief Ed Kraus, promises a deeper news conference. The incident reignited calls from figures like Sen. Bernie Sanders for the Justice Department to end the killing of unarmed Black Americans, highlighting systemic harm and the need for fair authority.
