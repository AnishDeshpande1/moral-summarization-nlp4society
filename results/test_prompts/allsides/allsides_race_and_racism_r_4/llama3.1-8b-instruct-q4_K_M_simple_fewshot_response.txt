SUMMARY:
A white police officer in Fort Worth, Texas, shot and killed Atatiana Jefferson, a 28-year-old black woman, inside her home while responding to a neighbor's call about an open front door. The officer claimed he did not have time to perceive a threat before firing, but an attorney for the family disputed this, saying "You didn't hear the officer shout, 'Gun, gun, gun.'" The video released by police shows two officers searching the home from outside with flashlights before one shouts and then fires through a window. The shooting has sparked outrage, with Democratic presidential candidate Sen. Bernie Sanders calling on the Justice Department to investigate. The family of Botham Jean, who was also killed by a white officer in Dallas, is also represented by the same attorney. The incident highlights ongoing concerns about police brutality and racial bias in law enforcement. The Fort Worth Police Department has promised a thorough investigation, but many are questioning why Jefferson's life was taken unnecessarily.
