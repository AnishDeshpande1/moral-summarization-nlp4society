SUMMARY:
A white Fort Worth police officer fatally shot 28‑year‑old Atatiana Jefferson, a Black woman watching her 8‑year‑old nephew, after a neighbor called about an open front door. The officer, who did not announce his presence or identify himself as police before firing through a window, claimed he “perceived a threat,” yet his family’s attorney argues he had no time to assess danger and labels the act murder. The department placed the officer on administrative leave and opened an investigation, but withheld interior footage and the officer’s name, raising concerns of authority being misused and subverted. Jefferson’s sister and aunt voiced anguish, calling out the failure of those meant to protect and demanding justice and fairness for a life taken without clear cause. Community member James Smith, who made the call, expressed guilt and fear of calling police again. The incident echoes the recent conviction of former Dallas officer Amber Guyger for killing a Black neighbor, prompting calls from Senator Bernie Sanders for a Justice Department probe, underscoring demands for equitable treatment and accountability.  
END OF SUMMARY.
