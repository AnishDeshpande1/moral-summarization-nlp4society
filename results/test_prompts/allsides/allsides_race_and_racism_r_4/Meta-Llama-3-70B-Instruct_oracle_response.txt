SUMMARY:

A white police officer in Fort Worth, Texas, killed a 28-year-old black woman, Atatiana Jefferson, inside her home while responding to a neighbor's call about an open front door. The officer didn't announce himself before firing the fatal shot, and the family's attorney, Lee Merritt, called it "murder." The police department released bodycam footage, which showed the officer shouting "Put your hands up, show me your hands" before firing through a window. Jefferson's family is seeking "justice" and demanding to know why she was killed. The officer is on administrative leave pending an investigation. The family is left with a sense of "loss" and "concern," and the neighbor who called the police is "shaken" and "upset," feeling partly responsible for her death. The incident has sparked outrage, with Democratic presidential candidate Sen. Bernie Sanders calling for a Justice Department investigation, saying "the killings of unarmed Black Americans have got to end." END OF SUMMARY.
