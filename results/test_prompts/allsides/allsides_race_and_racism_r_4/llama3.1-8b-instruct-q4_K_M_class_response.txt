SUMMARY: A white police officer's fatal shooting of Atatiana Jefferson, a 28-year-old black woman, inside her Texas home has sparked outrage. According to the family's attorney, Lee Merritt, "He didn't have time to perceive a threat. That's murder." The officer responded to a neighbor's call about an open front door and shot through a window without announcing himself as police. Jefferson was watching her 8-year-old nephew at the time of the shooting. The Fort Worth Police Department released bodycam footage, showing officers searching from outside before one shouted "Put your hands up" and fired. The family demands justice for this unspeakable loss and expects a thorough investigation. Democratic presidential candidate Sen. Bernie Sanders called on the Justice Department to investigate, stating that "The killings of unarmed Black Americans have got to end." Jefferson's family is still trying to understand why she was killed, describing it as "inconceivable" and "confusing." The officer involved has been placed on administrative leave pending further investigation. END OF SUMMARY.
