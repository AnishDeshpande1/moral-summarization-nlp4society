**SUMMARY:**  
A white Fort Worth police officer fatally shot 28‑year‑old Atatiana Jefferson, a Black woman, inside her home after responding to a neighbor’s call about an open front door. Family attorney Lee Merritt called the shooting “murder,” noting the officer never shouted “Gun, gun, gun” and had no time to perceive a threat. Body‑cam video shows officers searching the house, ordering “Put your hands up,” then firing through a window, leaving Jefferson’s family in profound loss and confusion. Jefferson’s sister said, “the people that are supposed to protect us are actually not here to protect us,” underscoring the injustice of an unarmed Black woman killed by a white officer. The officer, on administrative leave, has not been named, and an investigation is pending. Calls for justice echo recent cases, including the murder conviction of former Dallas officer Amber Guyger for killing her Black neighbor. Senator Bernie Sanders demanded a Justice Department probe, insisting the killings of unarmed Black Americans must end.
