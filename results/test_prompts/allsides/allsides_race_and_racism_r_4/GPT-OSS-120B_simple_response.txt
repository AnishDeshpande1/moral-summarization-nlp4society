SUMMARY: A Fort Worth white police officer fatally shot 28‑year‑old Black woman Atatiana Jefferson inside her home after a neighbor reported an open front door. Family attorney Lee Merritt called the killing “murder,” saying the officer “didn’t have time to perceive a threat” and never announced himself, violating the duty to protect. Body‑cam footage shows officers outside, one shouting “Put your hands up,” then a shot through a window. Jefferson’s sister lamented that those meant to protect “are actually not here to protect us,” and the family demands a swift, thorough investigation. The officer, on duty since 2018, is on administrative leave; his name remains withheld. Police confirmed the nephew was present and that a gun was later found, but it is unclear if Jefferson possessed it. The incident reignites outrage over the lethal force used against unarmed Black people, echoing the recent conviction of former Dallas officer Amber Guyger for a similar murder. Calls for federal scrutiny grew, with Sen. Bernie Sanders urging Justice Department action. END OF SUMMARY.
