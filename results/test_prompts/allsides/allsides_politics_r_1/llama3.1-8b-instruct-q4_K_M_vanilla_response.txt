SUMMARY:

US President Donald Trump is set to sign an executive order targeting social media companies, including Twitter, Facebook, and Google, following his threat to shut down websites he accused of stifling conservative voices. This move comes after Twitter tagged Trump's tweets about unsubstantiated claims of mail-in voting fraud with a warning label. The dispute has sparked a broader conservative backlash against Big Tech, with Republican lawmakers considering changes to Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act, which shields online platforms from legal liability.

Trump's threat is his strongest yet in a series of attacks on social media companies, accusing them of censorship and bias against conservatives. Shares of Twitter and Facebook fell on Wednesday following Trump's tweets. Mark Zuckerberg, Facebook's CEO, said censoring platforms would not be the "right reflex" for a government worried about censorship.

The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) has stated that any action taken by Trump would be limited by the First Amendment of the US Constitution.

END OF SUMMARY.
