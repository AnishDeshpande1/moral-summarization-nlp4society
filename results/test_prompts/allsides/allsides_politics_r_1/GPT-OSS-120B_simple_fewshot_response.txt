SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump announced he will sign an executive order targeting social‑media companies after threatening to shut down platforms he says silence conservative voices. The move follows Twitter’s first‑time labeling of Trump’s false claims about mail‑in‑vote fraud with a fact‑check warning, a step the president condemned as censorship. A federal appeals panel also dismissed a lawsuit by a conservative group accusing Google, Facebook, Twitter and Apple of colluding to suppress right‑wing views. Trump warned that if “Big Tech” continues to censor ahead of the 2020 election, Americans will lose their freedom, and he demanded the companies “clean up your act, NOW.” Tech firms and the ACLU argue the First Amendment limits any government shutdown of private platforms, and companies like Facebook and Google declined comment. Lawmakers from both parties are debating reforms to Section 230, which shields platforms from liability for user content. The controversy underscores a growing clash between conservative leaders who view the tech industry as a partisan threat and the platforms’ claim they are merely policing misinformation.

END OF SUMMARY.
