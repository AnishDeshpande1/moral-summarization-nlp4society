SUMMARY: President Donald Trump announced he will sign an executive order targeting social‑media companies after accusing them of silencing conservative voices. The threat follows Twitter’s first‑time labeling of Trump’s tweets about alleged mail‑in voting fraud with a fact‑check warning. A federal appeals court also dismissed a lawsuit by a conservative group alleging that Google, Facebook, Twitter and Apple conspired to suppress right‑wing views. Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg warned that government‑mandated censorship would be a poor response. The ACLU noted the First Amendment would limit Trump’s actions. Trump vowed to “regulate or close” platforms that he says censor conservatives, citing concerns ahead of the 2020 election. Shares of Twitter and Facebook fell. Earlier White House drafts on anti‑conservative bias had stalled. Tech firms argue they are not politically biased and cite internal committees for misinformation decisions. Concurrently, major tech companies face antitrust investigations and possible reforms to Section 230, with lawmakers like Sen. Josh Hawley questioning their legal immunity. END OF SUMMARY.
