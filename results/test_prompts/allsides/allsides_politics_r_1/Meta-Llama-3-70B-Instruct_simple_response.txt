SUMMARY:

President Trump is set to sign an executive order targeting social media companies, claiming they stifle conservative voices. This comes after Twitter fact-checked his tweets about mail-in voting fraud, sparking a dispute. Trump threatened to shut down privately owned companies, but it's unclear how he can follow through on this threat. Meanwhile, a court upheld the dismissal of a lawsuit accusing tech companies of suppressing conservative views. Facebook's CEO Mark Zuckerberg cautioned against government censorship, while the ACLU emphasized that the First Amendment limits Trump's actions. Trump's threat is part of a broader conservative backlash against Big Tech, with shares of Twitter and Facebook falling as a result. The move raises concerns about government overreach and censorship, with some arguing that tech companies should not be regulated or shut down.

END OF SUMMARY.
