SUMMARY: President Donald Trump announced he will sign an executive order targeting social‑media giants, warning he will shut down platforms he says are silencing conservative voices. The move follows Twitter’s first‑time labeling of Trump’s false claims about mail‑in‑vote fraud, a step the president decried as censorship. A federal appeals panel recently dismissed a lawsuit accusing Google, Facebook, Twitter and Apple of a conservative‑bias conspiracy, and Facebook’s CEO warned against government‑imposed censorship. The ACLU reminded that the First Amendment limits presidential power, while tech firms argue they are not politically biased and merely combat misinformation. Trump’s rhetoric frames the battle as a moral fight for free speech and election‑year freedom, casting “Big Tech” as an authoritarian force that must be regulated or shut down. Meanwhile, lawmakers on both sides debate reforms to Section 230, and Republicans like Sen. Josh Hawley are pressing platforms to abandon legal immunity for what they see as editorializing on presidential statements. The controversy highlights a deepening moral clash over who decides what speech is permissible online. END OF SUMMARY.
