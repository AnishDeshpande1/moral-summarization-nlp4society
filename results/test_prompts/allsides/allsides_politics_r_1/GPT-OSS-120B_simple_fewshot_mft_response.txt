SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump announced that he will sign an executive order aimed at regulating—or even shutting down—social‑media platforms he says are unfairly silencing conservative voices. Framing the tech giants as subversive authorities that cheat the public by biasing political speech, Trump threatened to act after Twitter labeled his unfounded mail‑in‑ballot fraud claims with a fact‑check warning. A federal appeals panel dismissed a lawsuit by a conservative group accusing Google, Facebook, Twitter and Apple of a coordinated conspiracy to suppress right‑wing views, reinforcing the notion that the alleged bias may be overstated. Critics, including the ACLU, warned that any presidential move would clash with First‑Amendment protections and abuse governmental authority. Meanwhile, tech firms defended their policies as neutral, citing internal committees that decide misinformation labels. The episode highlights a clash between perceived authoritarian overreach by the president and claims of unfair, partisan censorship by powerful online platforms.  
END OF SUMMARY.
