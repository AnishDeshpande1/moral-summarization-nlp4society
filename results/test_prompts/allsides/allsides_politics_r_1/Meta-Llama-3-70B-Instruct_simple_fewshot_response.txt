SUMMARY:

President Trump is set to sign an executive order on social media companies, threatening to shut them down if they don't comply with his demands. This comes after Twitter tagged Trump's tweets about unsubstantiated claims of fraud in mail-in voting with a warning, prompting readers to fact-check the posts. Trump accused social media companies of stifling conservative voices and vowed to "strongly regulate" or "close them down" if they don't change their ways. The move has sparked concerns about censorship and the limits of government power. The American Civil Liberties Union warned that the First Amendment of the U.S. Constitution limits any action Trump could take. Tech companies have been accused of anti-competitive practices and violating user privacy, and are facing antitrust probes by federal and state authorities. Trump's threat is his strongest yet, and shares of Twitter and Facebook fell in response. The move raises concerns about the spread of misinformation and the responsibility of social media companies to regulate harmful content.

END OF SUMMARY.
