SUMMARY: President Donald Trump is set to sign an executive order targeting social media companies, claiming they are "silencing" conservative voices. The move comes after Twitter tagged Trump's tweets about mail-in voting with a warning, prompting readers to fact-check the posts. Trump threatened to "shut down" or "strongly regulate" the companies, accusing them of "censoring" conservative views. Facebook's CEO Mark Zuckerberg countered that government censorship would be the "wrong reflex." The American Civil Liberties Union warned that the First Amendment limits Trump's actions. Trump's threat is his strongest yet, sparking a conservative backlash against "Big Tech." Shares of Twitter and Facebook fell, and the Internet Association denied any political bias. The dispute raises concerns about "freedom" and the role of tech companies in regulating online speech. END OF SUMMARY.
