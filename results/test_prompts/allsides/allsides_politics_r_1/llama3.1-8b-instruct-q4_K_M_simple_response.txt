SUMMARY:
US President Donald Trump plans to sign an executive order targeting social media companies, including Twitter, Facebook, and Google, after accusing them of stifling conservative voices. This move comes after Twitter tagged Trump's tweets about unsubstantiated claims of mail-in voting fraud with a warning label. The president threatened to shut down these privately-owned companies if they continue to suppress conservative views. However, experts point out that Trump's actions may be unconstitutional and could have far-reaching consequences for free speech online. The American Civil Liberties Union notes that the First Amendment limits any action Trump can take against social media platforms.

Facebook's CEO Mark Zuckerberg cautioned that censoring a platform would not be the right reflex for a government worried about censorship. Meanwhile, a US appeals court upheld the dismissal of a lawsuit against these tech companies accusing them of conspiring to suppress conservative views. The tech industry is facing growing scrutiny over alleged anti-competitive practices and user privacy concerns.

END OF SUMMARY.
