SUMMARY: President Donald Trump announced he will sign an executive order aimed at **regulating** or even **closing** social‑media companies he claims are **censoring** and **silencing** conservative voices. The threat follows Twitter’s first‑time decision to tag Trump’s false mail‑in‑ballot claims with a fact‑check warning, a move Trump decries as a violation of **freedom**. A federal appeals panel upheld the dismissal of a lawsuit accusing Google, Facebook, Twitter and Apple of a conspiracy to **censor** conservatives. Facebook’s CEO warned that government‑mandated **censoring** would be the wrong reflex, while the ACLU reminded that the First Amendment limits any presidential action. Tech giants argue they are politically neutral and point to ongoing antitrust probes and Section 230 debates. Republicans, including Sen. Josh Hawley, accuse the platforms of **violating** free speech and demand they lose legal immunity. Trump’s strongest threat yet underscores a broader conservative backlash against perceived **silencing** by “Big Tech.”  

END OF SUMMARY.
