SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump is set to sign an executive order on social media companies, threatening to shut down websites he accuses of stifling conservative voices. The move comes after Twitter tagged Trump's tweets about mail-in voting with a warning, prompting readers to fact-check the posts. Trump has claimed that social media platforms are censoring conservative voices and has threatened to regulate or close them down. However, it is unclear how he can follow through on this threat, as the companies are privately owned. Facebook's CEO Mark Zuckerberg has stated that censoring a platform would not be the right reflex for a government worried about censorship. The American Civil Liberties Union has also noted that the First Amendment of the US Constitution limits any action Trump could take. Shares of Twitter and Facebook fell on Wednesday following Trump's threats.

END OF SUMMARY.
