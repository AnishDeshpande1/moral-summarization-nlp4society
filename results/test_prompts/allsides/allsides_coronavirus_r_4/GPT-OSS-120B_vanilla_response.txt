SUMMARY: Protestors affiliated with the “Take Back Kentucky” group hung an effigy of Democratic Governor Andy Beshear outside the state capitol on Sunday, protesting COVID‑19 shutdown measures. About 100 participants began by celebrating the Second Amendment, then marched to the governor’s residence chanting “Come out Andy,” before returning to the capitol where a few hung the effigy—a likeness of Beshear with the Latin motto “sic temper tyrannies” (“thus always to tyrants”). The figure was removed shortly after. The act drew swift bipartisan condemnation. Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell denounced it as hateful despite supporting free speech, and Kentucky Secretary of State Michael G. Adams called it “disgusting.” Louisville Mayor Greg Fischer and House Democrats also condemned the stunt, labeling it “vile” and “beyond reprehensible.” END OF SUMMARY.
