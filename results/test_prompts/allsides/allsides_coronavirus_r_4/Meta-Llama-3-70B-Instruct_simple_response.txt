SUMMARY:

A protest in Kentucky turned ugly when demonstrators hung an effigy of Democratic Gov. Andy Beshear outside the state capitol, sparking widespread condemnation from both parties. The protest, organized by Take Back Kentucky, aimed to "celebrate freedom and fight back against the unconstitutional shutdown over the coronavirus." However, the event took a dark turn when around 100 people marched to the governor's house, shouting "Come out Andy," and later hung the effigy with a message that read "sic temper tyrannies," a Latin phrase that translates to "thus always to tyrants." The effigy was swiftly taken down, but not before it drew sharp criticism from lawmakers, including Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell, who called the action "unacceptable" and said "there is no place for hate in Kentucky." Other officials, including Democratic Louisville Mayor Greg Fischer and Kentucky House Democrats, also denounced the effigy, calling it "vile" and "beyond reprehensible."

END OF SUMMARY.
