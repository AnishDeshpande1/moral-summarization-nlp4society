SUMMARY:
Protestors from the “Take Back Kentucky” movement hung an effigy of Democratic Governor Andy Beshear outside the state Capitol in a demonstration against COVID‑19 restrictions. After a rally that began with Second‑Amendment chants, participants marched to the governor’s home, shouted “Come out Andy,” and later displayed a likeness of Beshear on a hanging dummy bearing the Latin warning “sic temper tyrannis” (“thus always to tyrants”). The effigy was removed shortly thereafter. The stunt provoked swift bipartisan condemnation. Republican Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell denounced the act as hateful despite supporting peaceful protest, and Kentucky Secretary of State Michael G. Adams called it “disgusting.” Democratic Mayor Greg Fischer and Kentucky House Democrats also blasted the display as “vile” and “beyond reprehensible,” urging respect for public health measures and honoring those who have sacrificed during the pandemic. The incident illustrates a broad consensus that political dissent must not devolve into intimidation or hate symbols. END OF SUMMARY.
