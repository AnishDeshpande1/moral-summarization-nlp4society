SUMMARY:

Protestors in Kentucky hung an effigy of Democratic Gov. Andy Beshear outside the state capitol during a demonstration on Sunday, organized by Take Back Kentucky. The protest aimed to "celebrate freedom" and fight against coronavirus restrictions, but quickly turned into a call against restrictions. Protestors marched to the governor's house, shouting for him to come out, before returning to the capitol where they hung the effigy with a message reading "sic temper tyrannies". The effigy was later taken down.

The protestor's actions sparked swift backlash from both parties, with Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell and Secretary of State Michael G. Adams condemning the effigy hanging as unacceptable and vile. Democratic Louisville Mayor Greg Fischer also condemned the act, saying it is beyond reprehensible. Kentucky House Democrats issued a lengthy statement against the effigy, calling it "beyond reprehensible".

The incident has sparked controversy on Memorial Day weekend, with many calling for respect for those who have made sacrifices for their country.

END OF SUMMARY.
