SUMMARY:
Protestors in Kentucky hung an effigy of Democratic Governor Andy Beshear outside the state capitol to protest COVID‑19 shutdowns, branding the governor a tyrant. While the rally began with Second‑Amendment chants, it quickly turned into a hostile display that mocked the governor’s authority and degraded his person with a “sic temper tyrannis” banner. The act drew swift bipartisan condemnation as an unacceptable breach of respect for legitimate authority and a harmful, hateful gesture that desecrated the symbolic dignity of public office. Republican Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell affirmed support for peaceful protest but denounced the effigy as “unacceptable” and “no place for hate.” GOP Secretary of State Michael G. Adams likened the act to the words of John Wilkes Booth, while Democratic leaders, including Louisville Mayor Greg Fischer and Kentucky House Democrats, called the stunt “vile,” “beyond reprehensible,” and contrary to the care owed to the community during the pandemic. The effigy was removed shortly after the outcry, underscoring broad consensus that subversive, degrading actions that threaten public officials and public health are morally indefensible.  
END OF SUMMARY.
