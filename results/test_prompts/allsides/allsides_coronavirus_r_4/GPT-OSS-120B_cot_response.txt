**SUMMARY:**  
Protestors in Kentucky hung an effigy of Democratic Gov. Andy Beshear outside the state capitol to “celebrate freedom” and protest what they called an “unconstitutional” COVID‑19 shutdown. The demonstration, organized by Take Back Kentucky, began with Second‑Amendment chants and later turned into a vocal call against the governor’s restrictions, with participants shouting “Come out Andy.” The effigy bore Beshear’s face and the Latin phrase “sic temper tyrannis” (“thus always to tyrants”). The act was swiftly deemed “unacceptable” and “vile” by leaders from both parties. Republican Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell, while defending the First Amendment, condemned the “hate” and called the action “disgusting.” Kentucky’s Republican Secretary of State and Democratic officials, including Louisville Mayor Greg Fischer, also “condemn” the “reprehensible” effigy. The effigy was removed shortly after the protest.
