SUMMARY:

A protest in Kentucky turned ugly when demonstrators hung an effigy of Democratic Gov. Andy Beshear outside the state capitol, sparking widespread condemnation from both parties. The rally, organized by Take Back Kentucky, aimed to "celebrate freedom and fight back" against coronavirus restrictions, but it descended into a call to action against the governor's measures. Protesters marched to the governor's house, shouting "Come out Andy," before returning to the capitol to hang the effigy, which featured a picture of Beshear's face and the Latin phrase "sic temper tyrannies," meaning "thus always to tyrants." The effigy was later taken down. The actions were swiftly denounced by lawmakers, including Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell, who called the display "unacceptable" and said "there is no place for hate in Kentucky." Others, including Democratic Louisville Mayor Greg Fischer, condemned the "vile act" and emphasized the importance of protecting the community from the pandemic.

END OF SUMMARY.
