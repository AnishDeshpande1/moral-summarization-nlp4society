SUMMARY:

A protest in Kentucky turned unacceptable when demonstrators hung an effigy of Democratic Gov. Andy Beshear outside the state capitol, sparking widespread condemnation from both parties. The protest, meant to "celebrate freedom and fight back" against coronavirus restrictions, began with a celebration of the Second Amendment but escalated into a call to action against the governor's shutdown measures. Protesters marched to the governor's house, shouting "Come out Andy," before returning to the capitol to hang the effigy, which featured a picture of Beshear's face and a message that read "sic temper tyrannies," or "thus always to tyrants." The act was swiftly denounced by leaders, including Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell, who called it "unacceptable" and said "there is no place for hate in Kentucky." Others, including Democratic Louisville Mayor Greg Fischer, condemned the "vile act," emphasizing the importance of protecting the community from the pandemic and promoting freedom through safe and healthy practices. END OF SUMMARY.
