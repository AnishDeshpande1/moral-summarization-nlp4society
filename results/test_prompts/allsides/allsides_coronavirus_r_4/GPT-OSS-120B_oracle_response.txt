SUMMARY:
Protestors in Kentucky hung an effigy of Democratic Gov. Andy Beshear outside the state capitol during a rally organized by Take Back Kentucky. The demonstrators claimed they were fighting “unconstitutional shutdown” measures imposed over the coronavirus. After celebrating the Second Amendment, the crowd marched to the governor’s house shouting “Come out Andy,” then returned to the capitol where several participants hung the effigy from a tree, displaying Beshear’s face and the Latin motto “sic temper tyrannis” (“thus always to tyrants”). The act was quickly taken down, but the hanging sparked swift condemnation from both parties. Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell denounced the action as unacceptable hate, while Republican Secretary of State Michael G. Adams called it “disgusting.” Democratic leaders, including Louisville Mayor Greg Fischer and Kentucky House Democrats, urged the public to honor those who protect the community, to stay healthy and safe, and to reject such vile acts. The widespread backlash highlighted the moral divide over pandemic restrictions and the limits of protest.

END OF SUMMARY.
