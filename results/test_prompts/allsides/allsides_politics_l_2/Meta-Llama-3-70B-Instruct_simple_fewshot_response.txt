SUMMARY:

The Trump White House has released a list of 39 Obama administration officials who may have had access to NSA reports identifying Michael Flynn, Trump's national security adviser, in conversations with Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak. The list includes high-ranking officials such as FBI Director James Comey, CIA Director John Brennan, and Vice President Joe Biden. However, the release of this list has been seized upon by Trump supporters to promote the false "Obamagate" claim that Obama unfairly targeted Flynn. In reality, the officials on the list were authorized recipients of the information, and the unmasking was approved through normal protocol. Flynn had multiple red flags, including secretly agreeing to lobby for Turkey and having compromising ties to foreign states. The Obama administration's actions were likely motivated by a legitimate concern for national security, rather than a political vendetta. The Trump administration's attempts to spin this as a scandal are a blatant example of deflection and dishonesty.
