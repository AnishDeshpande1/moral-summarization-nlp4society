SUMMARY:

The Trump White House has released a list of 39 Obama administration officials who may have had access to NSA reports that identified Michael Flynn, Trump's national security adviser, in conversations with Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak. The list includes FBI Director James Comey, Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew, and Vice President Joe Biden, among others. The reports were part of NSA electronic surveillance of Kislyak, which picked up Flynn's attempts to influence Russia's reaction to sanctions imposed by Obama. The list does not indicate who specifically requested the "unmasking" of Flynn's identity, but notes that each individual was an authorized recipient of the original report.

Promoters of President Trump's "Obamagate" claim have seized on the list, falsely suggesting that Obama was involved in an effort to unfairly smear Flynn. However, there is no evidence to support this claim, and the list simply shows that officials with regular access to classified information were authorized to hear about Flynn's suspect activities. END OF SUMMARY.
