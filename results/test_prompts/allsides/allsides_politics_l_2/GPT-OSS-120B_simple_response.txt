SUMMARY:  
A de‑classified list released by the Trump White House shows that 39 senior Obama‑era officials—including James Comey, Jacob Lew, James Clapper, John Brennan, Denis McDonough and Vice President Joe Biden—were authorized recipients of an NSA report that named Michael Flynn. The report stemmed from surveillance of Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak, which captured Flynn’s attempts to influence Russia after Obama imposed sanctions for election interference. The memo clarifies that the “unmasking” of Flynn’s identity followed normal protocol; only 16 officials formally requested it, and the list does not prove who actually saw the unmasked name.  

Trump allies have seized on the list to push the debunked “Obamagate” narrative, falsely alleging that Obama officials, especially Biden, abused power to smear Flynn. While Biden was on the authorized list, there is no evidence he personally ordered any unmasking, and such requests would be routine for officials overseeing a U.S. citizen engaged in covert foreign contacts. Flynn, who later pleaded guilty to lying to the FBI about his dealings with Kislyak and other foreign actors, posed genuine national‑security concerns. The Justice Department, under Attorney General William Barr, is now moving to dismiss the case, but political spin continues to distort the facts.  

The moral takeaway: the release of the list does not reveal a conspiratorial cover‑up by Obama officials; it merely shows standard intelligence sharing about a suspect who had compromised ties to Russia and Turkey, while partisan actors exploit the information for partisan attacks.  

END OF SUMMARY.
