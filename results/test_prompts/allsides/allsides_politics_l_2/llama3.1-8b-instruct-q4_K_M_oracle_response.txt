SUMMARY:

The Trump White House released a list of Obama officials who may have received access to NSA reports on Michael Flynn's conversations with Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak. Flynn attempted to influence Russia's reaction to sanctions, but his actions were seen as interfering in foreign policy. The list includes 39 officials, including FBI Director James Comey and Vice President Joe Biden, who were authorized recipients of the information. However, it is not clear who actually saw the "unmasked" report.

Promoters of Trump's "Obamagate" claim seized on the release to bolster their false claim that Obama was involved in unfairly smearing Flynn. Sen. Rand Paul misrepresented the list to smear Biden, claiming he ordered the unmasking of Flynn's private conversation. However, there is no evidence to support this claim.

The Justice Department's decision to drop charges against Flynn has been criticized as an abuse of power. Flynn's involvement with foreign states, including Turkey and Russia, raises concerns about his influence over US policy. The list highlights the potential for officials to misrepresent or manipulate information to further their own interests. END OF SUMMARY.
