SUMMARY:

A list of officials in President Barack Obama's administration who may have received access to National Security Agency reports about Michael Flynn, Trump's national security adviser, has been released. The reports revealed secret conversations between Flynn and Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak, which Flynn attempted to influence Russia's reaction to sanctions imposed by Obama. The list includes 39 officials, including FBI Director James Comey, Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew, and Vice President Joe Biden, who may have seen the report that included Flynn's identity. However, there is no evidence that Biden personally ordered Flynn's "unmasking." Critics of Obama are misrepresenting the information to bolster their claims of wrongdoing.

It's worth noting that Flynn had potentially compromising ties to foreign states, including Turkey and Russia, which may explain why Obama officials were given access to the information. The Justice Department has previously noted that Flynn was investigated in 2016 due to suspicions that he was a Russian asset. His guilty plea included lying about his conversations with Kislyak and other foreign officials.

END OF SUMMARY.
