SUMMARY: The Trump White House released a declassified list of 39 Obama‑era officials who may have received NSA reports that “unmasked” Michael Flynn’s name in surveillance of Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak. The list, provided at Acting DNI Richard Grenell’s request and publicized by Sen. Charles Grassley, notes that 16 officials had formally requested unmaskings, but does not identify them. Named recipients include James Comey, Jacob Lew, James Clapper, John Brennan, Denis McDonough and Vice President Joe Biden, all of whom were authorized to see classified material. The release is being used by Trump allies to push the debunked “Obamagate” narrative that Obama officials improperly targeted Flynn. Flynn pleaded guilty in 2017 to lying to the FBI about his contacts with Kislyak; the Justice Department, under AG William Barr, is now seeking to drop the case. While some, like Sen. Rand Paul, falsely claimed Biden ordered the unmasking, there is no evidence of that. The documents simply show routine access by senior officials to intelligence concerning Flynn’s questionable foreign contacts. END OF SUMMARY.
