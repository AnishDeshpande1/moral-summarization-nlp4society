SUMMARY:
A declassified list released by the Trump White House shows that dozens of senior Obama‑era officials—including FBI Director James Comey, former CIA chief John Brennan, former Vice President Joe Biden and others—were authorized to receive an NSA “unmasking” that identified Michael Flynn by name in surveillance of Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak. The memo stresses that the recipients were regular, authorized holders of classified intelligence and that the unmasking followed standard protocol, not a secret plot. Nonetheless, Trump allies have seized on the disclosure to fuel the “Obamagate” narrative, portraying the Obama officials as abusing their authority to sabotage Flynn and to unfairly weaponize classified information against a political opponent. Critics note that the claim is misleading—there is no evidence Biden or any other official personally ordered the unmasking, and the list merely reflects routine access to intelligence concerning Flynn’s questionable contacts with foreign governments. The framing casts the Obama officials as subversive actors who cheated the political process, while portraying Trump’s challenge as a fight for fairness against a corrupt establishment.

END OF SUMMARY.
