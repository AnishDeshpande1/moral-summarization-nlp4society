SUMMARY:

A Mexican man, Jose Ines Garcia Zarate, was acquitted of killing a woman on a popular pier in San Francisco, sparking outrage from President Donald Trump and other conservatives. Trump called the verdict a "complete travesty of justice" and demanded cities like San Francisco scrap immigration policies that bar cooperation with federal deportation efforts. City officials vowed to stand behind their "sanctuary city" policy, which they argue improves public safety by allowing immigrants to cooperate with police without fear. Garcia Zarate had been deported five times and was wanted for a sixth, but was released from San Francisco's jail despite a federal request to detain him for deportation. The verdict has reignited the debate over immigration policies, with Trump and others calling for stricter laws and the construction of a border wall. San Francisco officials, on the other hand, are standing firm in their commitment to being a sanctuary city.

END OF SUMMARY.
