SUMMARY:
The acquittal of Mexican national Jose Inés Garcia Zarate for the murder of Kate Steinle ignited a firestorm among conservatives who blame San Francisco’s “sanctuary city” policies for the tragedy. President Trump denounced the verdict as a “complete travesty of justice” and urged the construction of a U.S.–Mexico border wall, while Attorney General Jeff Sessions demanded that cities stop shielding illegal immigrants from federal deportation. Hashtags such as #BoycottSanFrancisco and #kateswall trended as politicians and celebrities—including Sarah Palin and James Woods—condemned the city as unsafe. City officials defended the sanctuary policy, arguing it encourages cooperation with police and protects vulnerable residents. The jury did not find Garcia Zarate guilty of murder, only of illegal firearm possession, a charge carrying up to three years. Immigration advocates pointed out that he had no violent record and that the case was mishandled by prosecutors. The episode has revived the national debate over sanctuary cities, immigration enforcement, and the need for stronger border security.  
END OF SUMMARY.
