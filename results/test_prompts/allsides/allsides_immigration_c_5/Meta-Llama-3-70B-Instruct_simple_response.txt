SUMMARY:

A jury acquitted Jose Ines Garcia Zarate, a Mexican man, of killing Kate Steinle on a San Francisco pier in 2015. The verdict sparked outrage from President Donald Trump, U.S. Attorney General Jeff Sessions, and conservative politicians, who demanded that cities like San Francisco scrap their "sanctuary city" policies that bar cooperation with federal deportation efforts. Trump called the verdict a "complete travesty of justice" and reiterated his call for a border wall. City officials, however, vowed to stand behind their policy, arguing that it improves public safety by allowing immigrants to cooperate with police without fear. The case has reignited the debate over immigration policies, with critics accusing San Francisco of being a haven for criminals and proponents defending the city's commitment to being a safe haven for all.
