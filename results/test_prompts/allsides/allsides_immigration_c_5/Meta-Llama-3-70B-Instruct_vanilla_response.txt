SUMMARY:

A jury in San Francisco acquitted Jose Ines Garcia Zarate, a Mexican man, of killing Kate Steinle on a popular pier in 2015. The verdict sparked outrage, with President Donald Trump calling it a "complete travesty of justice" and demanding cities like San Francisco scrap immigration policies that bar cooperation with federal deportation efforts. The city's "sanctuary city" policy allowed Garcia Zarate to be released from jail despite a federal request to detain him for deportation. City officials vowed to stand behind their policy, which they believe improves public safety by allowing immigrants to cooperate with police without fear. Garcia Zarate had been deported five times and was wanted for a sixth. He was convicted of being a felon in possession of a firearm and will likely be deported again.

END OF SUMMARY.
