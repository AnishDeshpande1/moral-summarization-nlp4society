The acquittal of Jose Ines Garcia Zarate, a Mexican man who fatally shot Kate Steinle on a San Francisco pier in 2015, has sparked outrage and calls for a boycott of the city. President Donald Trump and U.S. Attorney General Jeff Sessions have criticized San Francisco's "sanctuary city" policy, which bars cooperation with federal deportation efforts. Trump tweeted that the verdict was a "complete travesty of justice" and demanded the construction of a U.S.-Mexico border wall. City officials, however, have vowed to stand behind their policy, arguing that it improves public safety by allowing immigrants to cooperate with police without fear. The case has reignited debates over immigration policies, with Trump and others using Steinle's death to justify stricter laws. In contrast, city officials and advocates argue that demonizing immigrants is unfair and harmful. The verdict has also raised questions about the prosecution's handling of the case, with some arguing that a better case could have been made to convict Garcia Zarate of second-degree murder.
