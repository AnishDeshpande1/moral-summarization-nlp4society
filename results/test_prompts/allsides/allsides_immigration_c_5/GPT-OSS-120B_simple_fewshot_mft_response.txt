SUMMARY:
The jury’s acquittal of Mexican immigrant Jose Inés García Zarate for the murder of Kate Steinle sparked a wave of outrage that framed the case in stark moral terms. President Trump and Attorney General Sessions portrayed the verdict as a “complete travesty of justice,” invoking Authority by condemning San Francisco’s sanctuary policy as subversive to federal law and demanding that cities abandon any refusal to cooperate with deportations. Conservative figures and hashtags such as #BoycottSanFrancisco and #kateswall called for punitive action, emphasizing Care by depicting immigrants as a threat to public safety and arguing that the city’s stance harms American citizens. At the same time, city officials and Democratic leaders defended the sanctuary approach as a Fairness issue, asserting that protecting undocumented residents from fear of deportation enables cooperation with police and upholds justice for all, including victims’ families. They highlighted the lack of evidence that García Zarate intended murder, stressing that the legal process was followed and that labeling all immigrants as criminals is a cheating of moral equity. The debate thus pivots on authority versus local autonomy, the care of citizens versus immigrant rights, and the fairness of holding a city accountable for a single trial outcome.  
END OF SUMMARY.
