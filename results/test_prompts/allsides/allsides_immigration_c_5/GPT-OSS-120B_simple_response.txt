SUMMARY: The article frames the acquittal of Mexican immigrant Jose Inés García Zarate for the murder of Kate Steinle as a moral flashpoint in the national debate over sanctuary cities. Conservative leaders, including President Trump and Attorney General Sessions, portray the verdict as a “travesty of justice” and call for a boycott of San Francisco, urging the construction of a border wall and the repeal of local policies that shield undocumented immigrants from federal deportation. In contrast, city officials and Democratic lawmakers defend San Francisco’s long‑standing sanctuary stance as a moral commitment to public safety and the protection of vulnerable communities, arguing that such policies encourage cooperation with police and do not make immigrants criminals. The jury’s decision—convicting García Zarate only of illegal firearm possession—feeds both narratives: critics see it as evidence of a broken system that endangers citizens, while supporters view it as a vindication of a humane approach that resists demonizing immigrants. The piece underscores the ethical clash between a perceived duty to protect the nation’s borders and a moral obligation to shelter those fleeing hardship. END OF SUMMARY.
