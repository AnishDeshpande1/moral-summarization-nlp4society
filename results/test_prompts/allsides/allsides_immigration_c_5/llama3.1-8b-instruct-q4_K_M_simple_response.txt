SUMMARY:

A jury acquitted Jose Ines Garcia Zarate, a Mexican man, of killing Kate Steinle on San Francisco's pier, despite his history of deportations. President Donald Trump and U.S. Attorney General Jeff Sessions condemned the verdict, calling it a "travesty of justice" and demanding cities like San Francisco scrap their immigration policies. The city stands by its "sanctuary city" policy, which allows immigrants to cooperate with police without fear. City officials argue that sanctuary policies improve public safety.

Trump used Steinle's death as an example of why the country needs tougher immigration laws, but the judge in the case did not allow immigration politics into the courtroom. Garcia Zarate's defense team argued that he found a gun on the pier and it fired when he picked it up. The city is facing backlash from conservative politicians and celebrities, with some calling for a boycott of San Francisco.

The Steinle family expressed disappointment with the verdict, but San Francisco officials remain committed to their sanctuary policy. Hundreds of other cities have similar immigration policies, and many are standing in solidarity with San Francisco.

END OF SUMMARY.
