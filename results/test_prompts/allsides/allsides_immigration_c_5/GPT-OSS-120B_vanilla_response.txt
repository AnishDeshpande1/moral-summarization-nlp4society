SUMMARY: A jury acquitted Mexican national Jose Ines Garcia Zarate of first‑degree murder and involuntary manslaughter in the 2015 killing of Kate Steinle on San Francisco’s Pier 39, convicting him only of felon‑in‑possession of a firearm. The verdict sparked a backlash from President Donald Trump, Attorney General Jeff Sessions, and conservative figures, who called for a boycott of San Francisco and demanded the city abandon its “sanctuary” policies that limit cooperation with federal immigration enforcement. Hashtags such as #BoycottSanFrancisco and #kateswall trended, while city officials defended the sanctuary stance, arguing it promotes public safety by encouraging immigrant cooperation with police. Garcia Zarate, a repeat illegal‑entry offender who had been released from jail despite a federal request for detention, will likely be deported after serving his firearm sentence. The case revived national debate over sanctuary cities, immigration enforcement, and the political use of high‑profile crimes. The Steinle family expressed disappointment, saying justice was rendered but not served. END OF SUMMARY.
