SUMMARY: The Trump administration has published new visa rules aimed at combating "birth tourism," where women travel to the US to give birth and obtain US citizenship for their children. The rules will protect the US from national security risks and defend American taxpayers from having their hard-earned dollars siphoned away to finance the direct and downstream costs associated with birth tourism. The practice is considered an abuse of the system, and the new rules will help eliminate the criminal activity associated with the industry. Applicants will be denied tourist visas if they are determined to be coming to the US primarily to give birth, and those with medical needs will be treated like other foreigners coming to the US for medical treatment. The rules will help prevent fraud and evasion, and protect the integrity of American citizenship. The move is part of a broader immigration crackdown, and critics argue it could put pregnant women at risk. END OF SUMMARY.
