SUMMARY:
The Trump administration has issued new visa regulations that bar tourist visas for anyone the consular officer believes is traveling primarily to give birth in the United States—a practice known as “birth tourism.”  Applicants must now prove a genuine medical need and demonstrate they can cover all related expenses, aligning the process with other medical‑treatment visas.  White House press secretary Stephanie Grisham framed the rule as a necessary step to close an “immigration loophole,” protect national security, and shield American taxpayers from funds diverted to support foreign children’s citizenship.  While birth tourism is technically legal, the administration argues that the industry exploits the 14th Amendment’s birthright citizenship and fuels fraudulent visa activity.  Critics warn the policy could endanger pregnant women and raise practical concerns about how officers will assess intent without violating interview protocols.  Nonetheless, supporters, including immigration‑restriction groups, view the rule as a vital measure to dismantle a lucrative “birth tourism” market that they say undermines the integrity of U.S. citizenship.

END OF SUMMARY.
