SUMMARY:
The Trump administration has introduced new visa rules aimed at restricting "birth tourism," where women travel to the US to give birth and obtain citizenship for their children. Applicants will now face a higher hurdle in obtaining tourist visas, requiring them to prove they are traveling for medical reasons rather than giving birth. The administration claims this move is necessary to protect American taxpayers from the costs associated with birth tourism and to prevent national security risks. Critics argue that these new rules could put pregnant women at risk by making it harder for them to obtain medical care in the US. While the practice of birth tourism itself is not illegal, the Trump administration has been cracking down on related activities, including arresting operators of birth tourism agencies and denying visas to pregnant women attempting to cross the US-Mexico border.
