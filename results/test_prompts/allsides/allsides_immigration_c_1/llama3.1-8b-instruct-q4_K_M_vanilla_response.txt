SUMMARY:
The Trump administration has introduced new visa rules aimed at restricting "birth tourism," where women travel to the US to give birth and obtain citizenship for their children. Applicants will be denied tourist visas if they are determined to be coming primarily for childbirth, unless they have a medical need. Those with medical needs must prove they can pay for treatment, transportation, and living expenses. The new rules take effect on Friday and are part of the administration's broader immigration crackdown. Birth tourism is a lucrative business, with companies charging up to $80,000 to facilitate it. While the practice is technically legal, authorities have arrested operators for visa fraud or tax evasion. Critics argue that the policy could put pregnant women at risk by making them prove their medical needs. The US has been cracking down on birth tourism since before Trump's presidency.

END OF SUMMARY.
