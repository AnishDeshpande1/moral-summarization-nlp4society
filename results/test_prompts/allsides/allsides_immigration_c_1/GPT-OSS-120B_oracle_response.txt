SUMMARY:  
The Trump administration issued new visa rules to **close** a perceived loophole that **combat** “birth tourism,” where foreign women travel to the United States to give birth and **obtain** citizenship for their children. Consular officers must now determine whether an applicant’s primary purpose is to give birth, and those deemed to be **lying** about their intent will be denied tourist visas. Applicants with genuine medical needs must prove they can cover all costs, mirroring treatment of other foreign patients. The White House says the policy will **protect** American taxpayers from money **siphoned** away by the lucrative industry and **defend** the **integrity** of U.S. citizenship. Officials argue the rule will **eliminate** **fraud** and **evasion** by birth‑tourism operators and reduce **risk** to the nation. Critics warn the measures could endanger pregnant women, as officers cannot directly ask about pregnancy yet must assess intent. The State Department notes the practice is legal but **abuses** the system, and estimates tens of thousands of foreign‑born women have given birth in the U.S. since 2012.  

END OF SUMMARY.
