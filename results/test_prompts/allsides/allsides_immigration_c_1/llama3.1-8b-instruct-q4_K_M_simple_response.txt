SUMMARY:

The Trump administration has introduced new visa rules aimed at preventing "birth tourism," where women travel to the US to give birth and obtain citizenship for their children. Under the new rules, applicants will be denied tourist visas if they are determined to be coming primarily to give birth, rather than for a medical need. Those with medical needs will be treated like other foreigners seeking medical treatment and must prove they can afford it. The administration claims this will protect national security, taxpayers, and the integrity of American citizenship.

Critics argue that the policy could put pregnant women at risk by denying them entry based on suspicion alone. The practice is not illegal but has been a lucrative business for companies that facilitate it, with some charging up to $80,000 per person. The administration estimates that in 2012, around 36,000 foreign-born women gave birth in the US and left the country.

END OF SUMMARY.
