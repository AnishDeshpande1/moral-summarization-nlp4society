SUMMARY:

The Trump administration has introduced new visa rules aimed at restricting "birth tourism," where women travel to the US to give birth and obtain citizenship for their children. The rules, which take effect Friday, will deny tourist visas to applicants determined by consular officers to be coming primarily to give birth. Those with medical needs will still be allowed, but must prove they have sufficient funds to cover expenses. Critics argue that this policy could put pregnant women at risk and raise questions about how officers would determine pregnancy status. The administration claims the policy is necessary to combat "national security risks" and protect American taxpayers from financial burdens associated with birth tourism. However, critics point out that regulating tourist visas for pregnant women may not address the root issue of birthright citizenship, which President Trump has threatened to end but scholars say is difficult to do.

END OF SUMMARY.
