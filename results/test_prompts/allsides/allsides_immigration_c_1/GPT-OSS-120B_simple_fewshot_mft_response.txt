SUMMARY:
The Trump administration issued new visa regulations that bar tourist visas for applicants judged to be traveling primarily for “birth tourism,” a practice the White House says subverts U.S. immigration law and exploits the country’s birth‑right citizenship. By tightening authority over consular decisions, the rule seeks to protect national security and prevent the misuse of public resources, arguing that the policy safeguards American taxpayers from having their money diverted to cover birth‑related costs. Officials frame the change as a fight against cheating the system, describing birth‑tourism operators as engaging in criminal fraud that undermines the integrity of U.S. citizenship. At the same time, critics warn the rule could harm vulnerable pregnant women, who may be denied entry or subjected to invasive scrutiny, raising concerns about the humanitarian impact of the policy. The administration contends that the measure is a necessary, lawful response to an “endemic abuse” that threatens both the nation’s security and the fairness of its immigration system. END OF SUMMARY.
