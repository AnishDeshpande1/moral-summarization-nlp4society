SUMMARY: 
The Trump administration has introduced new visa rules aimed at restricting "birth tourism," where women travel to the US to give birth and obtain citizenship for their children. The new rules, which take effect on Friday, will deny tourist visas to applicants who are determined by consular officers to be coming primarily to give birth. Those with medical needs will be treated like other foreigners seeking medical treatment and must prove they have sufficient funds to cover expenses. According to the State Department, this practice is a "glaring immigration loophole" that has led to abuses, including visa fraud and tax evasion. The Trump administration claims that closing this loophole will combat these abuses and protect American taxpayers from financial burdens associated with birth tourism. Critics, however, argue that the new policy could put pregnant women at risk by denying them medical care and protection under the law. END OF SUMMARY.
