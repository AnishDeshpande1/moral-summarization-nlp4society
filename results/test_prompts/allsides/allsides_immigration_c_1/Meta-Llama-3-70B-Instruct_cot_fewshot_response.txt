The Trump administration has published new visa rules aimed at restricting "birth tourism," where women travel to the United States to give birth so their children can have U.S. citizenship. The rules will deny tourist visas to applicants determined to be coming to the U.S. primarily to give birth. This move aims to "combat these endemic abuses" and "protect the United States from the national security risks created by this practice." The practice of traveling to the U.S. to give birth is fundamentally legal, but authorities have arrested operators of birth tourism agencies for visa fraud or tax evasion. The new rules will "defend American taxpayers" and "protect the integrity of American citizenship." Critics argue that the policy could put pregnant women at risk.
