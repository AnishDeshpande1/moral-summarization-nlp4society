SUMMARY:

The Trump administration has introduced new visa rules aimed at restricting "birth tourism," where women travel to the US to give birth and obtain US citizenship for their children. Applicants will be denied tourist visas if consular officers determine they are coming to the US primarily to give birth. The rules, which take effect Friday, are intended to combat "endemic abuses" and protect American taxpayers from financing the costs associated with birth tourism. The practice is fundamentally legal, but the administration claims it is not a legitimate activity for pleasure or recreation. Critics argue that the policy could put pregnant women at risk and raise questions about how officers will determine whether a woman is pregnant. The move is part of a broader immigration crackdown, with the administration also turning away pregnant women at the US-Mexico border.

END OF SUMMARY.
