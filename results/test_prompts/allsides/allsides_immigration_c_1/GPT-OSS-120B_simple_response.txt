SUMMARY: The Trump administration has issued new visa regulations to block “birth tourism,” declaring that tourists who intend primarily to give birth in the United States will be denied visas. Applicants must now prove a genuine medical need and demonstrate sufficient funds for treatment, travel and living expenses. The White House frames the rule as a moral imperative to safeguard national security, protect American taxpayers from subsidizing foreign children’s citizenship, and preserve the integrity of U.S. citizenship. While the practice of giving birth on U.S. soil is legal, officials argue that the commercial industry exploiting it constitutes “endemic abuse” and criminal activity. Critics warn the policy may endanger pregnant women and raise practical concerns about how consular officers can assess pregnancy intentions without violating interview protocols. Nonetheless, the administration presents the crackdown as a necessary step to close a “glaring immigration loophole” and curb a lucrative, foreign‑run industry that allegedly costs U.S. resources.  

END OF SUMMARY.
