SUMMARY:

Law enforcement is crucial in maintaining civilization, as it reminds society of the importance of legality. Deterrence, or the fear of punishment, plays a significant role in encouraging citizens to obey laws. The lack of law enforcement can lead to chaos and undermine other laws. The author argues that if one law is left unenforced, others will follow suit. This is evident in "sanctuary cities" where immigration laws are ignored, leading to the current caravan of Central Americans attempting to enter the US illegally.

The same principle applies to campus protests, where administrators often turn a blind eye to violent disruptions. If students were held accountable for their actions, there would likely be fewer illegal and violent protests. The author also highlights the importance of accountability in celebrity culture, where threats against the president are taken lightly because of their fame and wealth.

END OF SUMMARY.
