SUMMARY:
The article argues that lax law‑enforcement erodes authority and invites widespread subversion, harming both public safety and the moral order. By allowing “sanctuary cities,” unchecked illegal immigration, and violent campus protests to go unpunished, society sends the message that laws can be ignored without consequence, which the author says degrades the sanctity of the legal system. This permissiveness, the piece claims, creates a cascade of unfairness: law‑breakers receive special immunity while law‑abiding citizens—especially conservatives and ordinary workers—suffer intimidation and danger. The author cites the migrant caravan, celebrity threats against the president, and disruptive protesters as examples of how fear of punishment, rather than personal virtue, is needed to deter harmful behavior. Restoring firm, uniform deterrence would reaffirm legitimate authority, protect citizens from harm, re‑establish fairness by treating all violators equally, and preserve the sacred respect owed to law and order. Without such enforcement, the author warns, anarchy spreads, and the degradation of legal norms threatens the very civilization that depends on them.  
END OF SUMMARY.
