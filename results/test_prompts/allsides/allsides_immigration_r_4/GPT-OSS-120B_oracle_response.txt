SUMMARY:
The piece argues that law enforcement is essential not merely to **deal** with individual violators but to remind society that there can be no **civilization** without **legality**. Citizens **obey** and **comply** with rules largely out of fear of **consequences**—the shame of arrest, fines or imprisonment—rather than pure virtue. When violations go **unpunished**, they **greenlight** further breaches; an **unenforced** law **weakens** the whole system. The author warns that **sanctuary** policies **forbid** full immigration **enforcement**, **ignore** the presence of millions of undocumented residents, and **invite** more illegal **enter**‑ings such as the Central American **caravan**. Likewise, campus **protests** that become **violent**, with agitators **shouting**, **trying** to **intimidate**, and **ignoring** peaceful‑assembly rules would be curbed if participants were **charged** with **misdemeanors** or **felonies** and faced **arrests**. Recent celebrity **fantasized** **harm**, **wishes** to **beat**, **blow**, **cut**, or **shoot** the president, showing a belief they are above **consequences**. The author cautions that such **extralegal**, **unlawful** **laxity**—whether **helping** the **undocumented**, **evading** law, or **stopping** judicial nominations—**nullifies** legal authority and risks an **anarchy** where **force** is absent. END OF SUMMARY.
