SUMMARY: The article argues that consistent law enforcement is essential for societal order, emphasizing deterrence over moral character. It claims that lax enforcement—exemplified by sanctuary cities, the large population of undocumented immigrants, and permissive responses to campus protests—undermines all laws and encourages further violations. The piece cites examples such as Central American migrant caravans, violent campus disruptions by affluent students, and celebrities threatening President Trump, suggesting that without real consequences, these behaviors proliferate. It warns that political polarization and the belief that moral ends justify illegal means further erode legal norms, potentially leading to reciprocal abuses across the political spectrum. The author concludes that without visible, uniform policing and prosecution, the rule of law collapses, echoing Kant’s notion that freedom without force becomes anarchy. END OF SUMMARY.
