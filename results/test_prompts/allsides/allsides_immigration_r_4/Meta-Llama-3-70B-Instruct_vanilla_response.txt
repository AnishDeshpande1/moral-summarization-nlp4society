The article argues that law enforcement is essential to maintain civilization and deter individuals from breaking the law. Without consequences, individuals are more likely to disobey laws, leading to a breakdown in society. The author cites examples of how a lack of law enforcement has led to chaos, such as the migrant caravan heading to the US border, violent campus protests, and celebrities making threats against the President without facing consequences. The article suggests that if laws were enforced, and individuals were held accountable, such incidents would decrease. The author also notes that ignoring laws can be contagious and lead to a slippery slope, where other groups may also feel justified in breaking the law.
