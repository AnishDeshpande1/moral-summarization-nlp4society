SUMMARY:
The article argues that a strong, uniform system of law‑enforcement is the moral backbone of civilization. It contends that most people obey not out of innate virtue but out of fear of punishment, and that visible, prompt deterrence prevents one violator from inspiring countless others. By allowing “sanctuary cities,” tolerating millions of undocumented residents, and ignoring campus‑protest violence, society signals that laws can be ignored without consequence, which in turn fuels larger threats such as the Central‑American migrant caravan and the erosion of respect for immigration and other statutes. The piece also points to celebrities and public figures who threaten President Trump without repercussions, suggesting that applying real penalties—such as arrests or travel bans—would curb the “assassination chic.” It warns that if left unchecked, the current legal laxity could spread to other issues, enabling future “sanctuary zones” for any political cause, from abortion to gun control. Citing Kant’s warning that “anarchy is law and freedom without force,” the author concludes that without consistent policing and accountability, the moral order collapses and every high‑minded claim to liberty becomes hollow. END OF SUMMARY.
