SUMMARY:

Law enforcement is essential for maintaining civilization, as it deters individuals from breaking laws by instilling fear of punishment. Ignoring laws can have far-reaching consequences, including the erosion of societal norms and the emboldening of lawbreakers. The presence of "sanctuary cities" and millions of undocumented immigrants in the US sends a message that immigration laws can be ignored, leading to a caravan of thousands of Central Americans attempting to enter the country illegally.

The same principle applies to campus protests, where students often assume they can ignore laws about peaceful assembly without consequence. Celebrities have also been emboldened by their fame and influence to make threats against public officials. If these individuals were held accountable for their actions, it would likely stop such behavior.

Ignoring laws can boomerang, leading to a breakdown in social order and the erosion of respect for the law. As German philosopher Immanuel Kant noted, "anarchy is law and freedom without force." Without effective law enforcement, our high-minded talk about rights means nothing.

END OF SUMMARY.
