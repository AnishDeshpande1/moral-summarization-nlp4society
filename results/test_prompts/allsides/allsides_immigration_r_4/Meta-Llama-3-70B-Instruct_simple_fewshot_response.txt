SUMMARY:

The article argues that law enforcement is essential to maintain social order and deter individuals from breaking the law. The author contends that the lack of consequences for illegal actions, such as ignoring immigration laws or disrupting public events, has led to a culture of lawlessness. The presence of "sanctuary cities" and the estimated 12 million undocumented immigrants in the US sends a message that laws can be ignored. The author suggests that if laws were enforced, and individuals faced consequences for their actions, there would be no need for caravans of migrants or violent protests. The article also criticizes the double standard where celebrities and politicians are not held accountable for their actions, and the erosion of the rule of law has led to a breakdown in social norms. The author concludes that without law enforcement, society descends into anarchy, and the Bill of Rights becomes meaningless.
