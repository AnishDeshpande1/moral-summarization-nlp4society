**SUMMARY:**  
The article argues that law and legality are the foundations of civilization, sustained not by innate virtue but by the fear of punishment—shame, arrest, fines, or imprisonment—that deters illegal behavior. It warns that lax enforcement, exemplified by sanctuary cities and the unchecked presence of millions of undocumented immigrants, signals that laws can be ignored, encouraging further violations such as illegal border crossings and campus protests that turn violent. The author cites celebrity threats toward the president and attacks on conservative officials as evidence that without consistent deterrence, even high‑profile individuals feel immune from consequences. By allowing any group—whether migrants, protesters, or political opponents—to act above the law, society risks spreading anarchy, where rights become meaningless without police presence and district‑attorney enforcement. Restoring uniform, prompt law enforcement would re‑establish deterrence, protect public safety, and preserve the rule of law.
