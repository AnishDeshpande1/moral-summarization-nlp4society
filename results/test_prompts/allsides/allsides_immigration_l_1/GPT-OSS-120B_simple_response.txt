SUMMARY:  
The author argues that immigration is the true engine of America’s past, present, and future greatness, countering anti‑immigrant rhetoric like that of Donald Trump. Immigrants—legal and undocumented—have built and sustained the nation, founding or inspiring a large share of iconic companies and about 40 % of Fortune 500 firms, generating $857 billion in sales and nearly 4 million jobs. Contrary to critics, undocumented immigrants contribute more to Medicare than they receive, while native‑born citizens create a larger deficit. Crime data show native‑born males are far more likely to be incarcerated than foreign‑born residents. The real problem, the author claims, is the overly complex U.S. citizenship process, which pushes people toward illegal status and fuels dangerous smuggling networks. Mass deportations and a border wall are portrayed as impractical, costly, and morally wrong. Instead, the piece urges lawmakers and citizens to welcome immigrants—who seek freedom and opportunity—as the essential contributors who made America a “shining city upon a hill” and who will ensure its continued greatness.  

END OF SUMMARY.
