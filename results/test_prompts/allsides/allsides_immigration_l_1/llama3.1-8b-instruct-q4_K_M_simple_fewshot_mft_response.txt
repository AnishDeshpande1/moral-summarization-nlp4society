SUMMARY:

The author argues that immigrants are the backbone of America's greatness, introducing specialized skills and creating jobs. They point out that many successful companies were founded by immigrants or their offspring, and that immigrant-founded businesses produce millions of jobs and services. Critics often ignore this aspect of immigration, instead focusing on alleged abuses of domestic social programs and correlations between crime and immigration. However, studies show that undocumented immigrants contribute more to Medicare than they receive, and that native-born residents are more likely to be incarcerated than foreign-born individuals. The author advocates for a more welcoming approach to immigration, rather than rounding up 11 million undocumented immigrants or building a wall. They argue that this would only encourage human smuggling and prolong the crisis.

The author's moral framing relies on Authority/Subversion, as they challenge Trump's anti-immigrant rhetoric and advocate for a more inclusive approach. Care/Harm is also present, as they highlight the benefits of immigration and the harm caused by restrictive policies. Fairness/Cheating is relevant, as they point out that undocumented immigrants contribute to Medicare and are not a drain on society. Loyalty/Betrayal is also important, as they argue that native-born citizens and lawmakers should welcome immigrants rather than disparage them.
