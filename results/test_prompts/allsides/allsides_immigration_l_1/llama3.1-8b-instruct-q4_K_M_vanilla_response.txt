SUMMARY:

Immigrants have been essential to America's growth and prosperity, contributing specialized skills and founding successful businesses. In fact, 40% of Fortune 500 companies can trace their origins to immigrant founders or offspring. Immigrant-founded businesses generate $857 billion in annual sales and employ nearly 4 million workers. Contrary to critics' claims, undocumented immigrants actually contribute more to Medicare than they receive, resulting in a surplus.

Critics often correlate crime with immigration, but studies show that native-born males are 10 times more likely to be incarcerated than foreign-born counterparts. The real issue is the current U.S. citizenship process, which can lead to lengthy paperwork and administrative delays, incentivizing unlawful alternatives.

Building a wall or rounding up undocumented immigrants is impractical and costly. Most immigrants come to the U.S. for freedom and opportunity, not to harm society. Native-born citizens and lawmakers should welcome them instead of disparaging them.

Immigrants have made America great in the past, and they will continue to do so in the future.

END OF SUMMARY.
