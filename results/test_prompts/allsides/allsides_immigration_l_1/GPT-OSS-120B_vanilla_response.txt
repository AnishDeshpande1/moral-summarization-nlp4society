SUMMARY: The article argues that immigration is essential to America's past, present, and future greatness, countering anti‑immigrant rhetoric. It highlights that immigrants founded or helped build major companies—about 40% of Fortune 500 firms trace roots to immigrants—generating $857 billion in sales and nearly 4 million jobs. Studies show undocumented immigrants contribute more to Medicare than they receive, while native‑born citizens receive a larger net benefit. Crime data indicate native‑born males are far more likely to be incarcerated than foreign‑born individuals. The piece criticizes the overly complex U.S. citizenship process, which pushes some to illegal entry, and warns that mass deportations or a border wall are impractical and costly. Most immigrants, documented or not, seek freedom and opportunity, and the author calls for welcoming policies, asserting that immigrants made America great and will continue to do so. END OF SUMMARY.
