SUMMARY:

Contrary to anti-immigrant rhetoric, immigrants have sustained America's greatness, introducing specialized skills and founding profitable companies like Budweiser and McDonald's. In fact, 40% of Fortune 500 businesses can trace their beginnings to immigrants. These businesses produce millions of jobs and services, selling $857 billion in goods annually and employing almost 4 million workers. Critics often ignore these benefits, instead claiming that undocumented immigrants abuse social programs like Medicare. However, a study found that they pay $13.8 billion more into Medicare than they receive, resulting in a surplus. Moreover, native-born residents are more likely to engage in wrongdoing, and the current U.S. citizenship process is unnecessarily bureaucratic and convoluted, incentivizing unlawful alternatives. Rather than disparaging immigrants, native-born citizens and lawmakers should welcome them, recognizing that they come to the U.S. for freedom and opportunity. Immigrants are what made America great in the past and will make it great again in the future.

END OF SUMMARY.
