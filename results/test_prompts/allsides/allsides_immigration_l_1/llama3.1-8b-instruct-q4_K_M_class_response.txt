SUMMARY:

The notion that immigrants are a burden on America is a misconception. In reality, they have sustained the country's growth and prosperity by introducing specialized skills and entrepreneurship. Immigrants, regardless of status, contribute significantly to the economy, with many founding successful businesses that create jobs and services. A study found that immigrant-founded companies sell $857 billion in goods annually and employ nearly 4 million workers.

Critics often ignore the positive impact of immigration and instead focus on perceived abuses such as undocumented immigrants using domestic social programs. However, research shows that they actually pay more into Medicare than they receive, resulting in a surplus. Conversely, native-born residents contribute less to the program, creating a deficit. The true danger to public safety lies not with immigrants but with an unnecessarily bureaucratic citizenship process that encourages unlawful alternatives.

Rounding up undocumented immigrants is impractical and irresponsible, given the country's debt burden. A more constructive approach would be to welcome and integrate newcomers, recognizing them as key contributors to America's greatness past, present, and future.

END OF SUMMARY.
