SUMMARY:

The article argues that immigrants have played a crucial role in shaping America's greatness, contrary to Donald Trump's anti-immigrant rhetoric. Immigrants have introduced specialized skills, founded profitable companies, and created millions of jobs. In fact, 40% of Fortune 500 businesses can trace their beginnings to immigrants. Critics who argue that undocumented immigrants abuse social programs or commit crimes are misinformed, as studies have shown that they contribute more to Medicare than they receive and are less likely to be incarcerated than native-born citizens. The article concludes that the current U.S. citizenship process is bureaucratic and convoluted, encouraging unlawful alternatives and creating public safety and health risks. Instead of building a wall or rounding up undocumented immigrants, lawmakers should welcome them and recognize their contributions to America's greatness. Immigrants have made America great in the past and will continue to do so in the future.

END OF SUMMARY.
