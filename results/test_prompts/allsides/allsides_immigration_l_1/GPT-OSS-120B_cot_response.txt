**SUMMARY:**  
Immigrants are the common denominator of America’s **greatness**, contradicting Donald Trump’s **incessant** anti‑immigrant **bluster**. The **truth** is that immigrants—legal or **undocumented**—have supplied **specialized** skills that built and sustain the nation. Iconic, **profitable** and **celebrated** firms such as Budweiser and McDonald’s were founded by immigrants, and roughly 40 % of Fortune 500 companies trace roots to them, generating $857 billion in sales and nearly 4 million jobs. Critics claim **abuse** of social programs, yet studies show undocumented workers contribute a **surplus** to Medicare, while native‑born residents create a **deficit**. Crime fears are unfounded: native‑born males are ten times more likely to be incarcerated than foreign‑born peers. The real **danger** lies in the **unnecessarily** **bureaucratic**, **convoluted** citizenship process, which pushes some to **unlawful** or **desperate** paths and fuels human smuggling. Rounding up 11 million people or building a wall is **illegal**, costly, and ineffective. Most immigrants seek **freedom** and **opportunity**; they should be **welcomed**, not **disparaged**. Immigrants made America great before and will make it great again.
