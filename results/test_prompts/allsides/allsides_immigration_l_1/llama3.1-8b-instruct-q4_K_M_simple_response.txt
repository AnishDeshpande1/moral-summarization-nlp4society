SUMMARY:
Contrary to anti-immigrant rhetoric, immigrants have been instrumental in building and sustaining America's greatness. They introduce specialized skills, found successful businesses, and contribute significantly to the economy. In fact, 40% of Fortune 500 companies have roots in immigrant-founded businesses, creating millions of jobs and services. Critics often focus on undocumented immigrants' supposed abuse of social programs, but studies show they actually pay more into Medicare than they receive. Moreover, native-born citizens are more likely to be incarcerated than foreign-born individuals. The current U.S. citizenship process is the true danger, causing applicants to fall through cracks or languish in paperwork. History shows that underground economies can lead to unintended social consequences, making it unreasonable to round up undocumented immigrants and impractical to build a wall.

Immigrants come to America for freedom and opportunity, fleeing hardship, and should be welcomed by native-born citizens and lawmakers. Rather than disparaging them, we should recognize their value in shaping America's greatness past and future. By streamlining the citizenship process and addressing underlying issues, we can create a more inclusive and prosperous society.

END OF SUMMARY.
