SUMMARY:
The article argues that immigrants are essential to America’s past, present, and future greatness, countering Donald Trump’s anti‑immigrant rhetoric. It highlights how immigrant founders and their descendants built iconic companies like Budweiser and McDonald’s, and notes that about 40 % of Fortune 500 firms trace roots to immigrants, generating $857 billion in sales and nearly 4 million jobs. The piece refutes common criticisms: a Health Affairs study shows undocumented immigrants contribute $13.8 billion more to Medicare than they receive, while native‑born citizens create a $30.9 billion deficit; a Public Policy Institute of California study finds native‑born men are ten times more likely to be incarcerated than foreign‑born men. The real problem, the author claims, is the overly bureaucratic citizenship process, which pushes people toward illegal entry and fuels human smuggling. Mass deportations and a border wall are portrayed as impractical, wasteful, and harmful to a nation already in debt. The moral conclusion urges lawmakers and citizens to welcome immigrants—both documented and undocumented—as the true drivers of American opportunity and prosperity.

END OF SUMMARY.
