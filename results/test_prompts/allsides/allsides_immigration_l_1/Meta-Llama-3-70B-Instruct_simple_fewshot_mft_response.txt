SUMMARY:

The article argues that immigrants are the backbone of America's greatness, contrary to Donald Trump's anti-immigrant rhetoric. Immigrants bring specialized skills, found profitable companies, and create jobs. In fact, 40% of Fortune 500 businesses were founded by immigrants or their offspring. Critics who claim undocumented immigrants abuse social programs ignore the fact that they pay more into Medicare than they receive. Moreover, native-born residents receive more than they contribute, resulting in a deficit. The article also debunks the correlation between crime and immigration, citing a study that found native-born males are 10 times more likely to be incarcerated than their foreign-born counterparts. The current U.S. citizenship process is bureaucratic and convoluted, incentivizing unlawful alternatives. The article concludes that rounding up 11 million undocumented immigrants is impractical, and building a wall is a pointless, taxpayer-subsidized project. Instead, native-born citizens and lawmakers should welcome immigrants, who come to the U.S. for freedom and opportunity.

END OF SUMMARY.
