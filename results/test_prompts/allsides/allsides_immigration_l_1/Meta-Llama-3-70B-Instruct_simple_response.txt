SUMMARY:

The article argues that immigrants are the backbone of America's greatness, contrary to anti-immigrant rhetoric. Immigrants have built and sustained the country, introducing specialized skills and founding profitable companies like Budweiser and McDonald's. In fact, 40% of Fortune 500 businesses can trace their beginnings to immigrants. These businesses generate millions of jobs and billions of dollars in goods annually. Critics who claim undocumented immigrants abuse social programs are misinformed, as they actually contribute more to Medicare than they receive. The article also debunks the correlation between crime and immigration, citing studies that show native-born males are more likely to be incarcerated. The current citizenship process is bureaucratic and convoluted, encouraging unlawful alternatives and risking public safety and health. Instead of building walls and deporting immigrants, the article urges welcoming them as they come to the US seeking freedom and opportunity.
