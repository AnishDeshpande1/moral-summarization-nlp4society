SUMMARY:
A covert Project Veritas video shows Kyle Jurek, identified as a paid field organizer for Senator Bernie Sanders’s campaign, openly advocating violent rebellion if President Trump wins reelection. Jurek declares “cities burn” and predicts police will be “beaten” at the 2020 Democratic National Convention, invoking the 1968 Chicago riots. He calls Trump supporters “Nazis,” urges their “re‑education” in Soviet‑style gulags, and praises antifa tactics, even suggesting dragging MSNBC staff “out by their hair, and light[ing] them on fire.” The footage is framed as evidence of subversive, extremist rhetoric within a mainstream campaign, portraying a threat to public safety (Care/Harm) and a breach of democratic fairness (Fairness/Cheating) by threatening violence against opponents and law‑enforcement. Project Veritas highlights Jurek’s short‑term $11,000 contract with the Sanders campaign and a brief stint at the Democratic Senatorial Campaign Committee, questioning whether the campaign will distance itself or deny the incident as an isolated case. The report underscores concerns that such radical, authority‑challenging statements undermine lawful authority and endanger civic order.
