SUMMARY:

A Project Veritas video released Tuesday showed Kyle Jurek, a campaign organizer for Democratic presidential hopeful Sen. Bernard Sanders, making disturbing comments about violence and reeducation of Trump supporters. Jurek stated that "cities burn" if President Trump is reelected and predicted riots at the 2020 Democratic National Convention in Milwaukee, where police would be beaten and the National Guard would be called in. He also expressed support for ideological reeducation of Trump voters, comparing them to Nazis and praising Soviet-style gulags as a means of breaking their "privilege." Jurek's comments raise concerns about the potential for violence and subversion of authority if Sanders does not win the nomination. The video has sparked criticism, with some accusing Jurek of being the "true but hidden face" of the Sanders campaign.

The moral framing of this article relies heavily on the dimensions of Authority/Subversion (Jurek's comments about breaking Trump supporters' "privilege" and reeducation) and Care/Harm (the potential for violence and harm to individuals if Sanders does not win). The Fairness/Cheating dimension is also relevant, as Jurek's comments raise questions about fairness and the potential for cheating in the democratic process.

END OF SUMMARY.
