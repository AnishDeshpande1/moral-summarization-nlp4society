SUMMARY:  
A covert Project Veritas video captures Kyle Jurek, a paid field organizer for Senator Bernie Sanders’ 2020 campaign, openly advocating violence and extremist rhetoric. Jurek boasts that “cities burn” if President Trump is re‑elected and predicts police will be “beaten” at the Democratic National Convention in Milwaukee, invoking the chaotic 1968 Chicago protests. He calls Trump supporters “Nazis,” urges their “re‑education” in Soviet‑style gulags, and praises antifa violence, even suggesting the burning of MSNBC staff. The footage also reveals Jurek’s past six‑month, $11,000 contract with the Sanders campaign and a brief stint with the Democratic Senatorial Campaign Committee. Project Veritas’ James O’Keefe poses questions about whether Jurek represents a broader campaign mindset, while the Sanders campaign declined comment. Critics note Project Veritas’ history of disputed editing, a claim O’Keefe denies. The video raises serious moral concerns about extremist language, incitement to violence, and the endorsement of totalitarian re‑education tactics within a mainstream political campaign.  

END OF SUMMARY.
