SUMMARY:
Seattle authorities dismantled the "autonomous zone" known as CHOP, which police chief Carmen Best described as "lawless and brutal". The zone, established after the killing of George Floyd, was marked by four shootings, robberies, assaults, and property crimes. President Trump had called for action against the demonstrators, labeling them "domestic terrorists". On Wednesday, police arrested 31 people for failure to disperse, assault, and other alleged crimes. Protester Harry "Rick" Hearns supported the police crackdown, blaming outsiders for the violence. The zone had become less crowded and active in recent days, with businesses in the area pushing for a tougher stance by authorities. Despite the crackdown, protester Lencho Williams vowed to regroup, saying "You can't stop a revolution. Black lives matter now and forever."
