SUMMARY: Seattle authorities dismantled the "autonomous zone" on Wednesday, arresting 31 people for failure to disperse, assault, and other alleged crimes. The zone, deemed "lawless and brutal" by Police Chief Carmen Best, had been occupied by protesters for weeks, with reports of shootings, deaths, and violence. The area had become an "unlawful assembly" with robberies, assaults, and property crimes, prompting U.S. Attorney General William Barr to commend Best for distinguishing between the right to protest and violent crime. The police crackdown was supported by some protesters, who blamed outsiders for bringing violence to the otherwise peaceful occupation. The zone, which was established in response to the killing of George Floyd, had become a symbol of racial injustice and police brutality. With the police re-establishing control, the area is no longer a hub for violent crime and unrest. END OF SUMMARY.
