SUMMARY: Seattle police dismantled the Capitol Hill Occupied Protest (CHOP) “autonomous zone” on Wednesday, arresting 31 people for failing to disperse, assault and other charges. Mayor Jenny Durkan declared the gathering an “unlawful assembly,” and Police Chief Carmen Best cited recent shootings, two teen deaths, robberies and assaults as evidence the zone had become “lawless and brutal.” U.S. Attorney General William Barr praised Best for distinguishing protest rights from violent crime, while President Trump had called protesters “domestic terrorists.” Some former CHOP participants, like Harry “Rick” Hearns, welcomed the crackdown, blaming outsiders for the violence. Police re‑entered the East Precinct and Cal Anderson Park, using armored vehicles, bike barricades and protective gear, while city crews removed tents. The area, once crowded with speeches, art and free‑food stations, had thinned in recent days. Local businesses have urged tougher action, and two class‑action lawsuits aim to bar future “lawless autonomous zones.” Protesters, including Lencho Williams, say they will regroup and continue demands for police defunding, Black community funding, and amnesty. END OF SUMMARY.
