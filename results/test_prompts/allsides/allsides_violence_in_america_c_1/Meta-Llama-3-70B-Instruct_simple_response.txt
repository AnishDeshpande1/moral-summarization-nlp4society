SUMMARY:

Seattle authorities dismantled the "autonomous zone" in the Capitol Hill neighborhood, arresting 31 people for various crimes. The police chief, Carmen Best, described the zone as "lawless and brutal" due to recent shootings and the deaths of two teenagers. The zone was established in response to the killing of George Floyd and had become a symbol of the nationwide protests against racial injustice and police brutality. However, the area had seen a rise in violence and property crimes, prompting criticism from President Donald Trump and conservative pundits. The police operation to retake the zone was largely peaceful, with officers facing little resistance. While some protesters condemned the violence and blamed outsiders, others vowed to regroup and continue the movement, emphasizing that "Black lives matter now and forever." The incident highlights the tension between the right to protest and the need to maintain law and order.

END OF SUMMARY.
