SUMMARY:

Seattle authorities have dismantled the "autonomous zone" in the Capitol Hill neighborhood, arresting 31 people for various crimes, including failure to disperse and assault. The police chief, Carmen Best, described the zone as "lawless and brutal" due to recent shootings and the deaths of two teenagers. The move was commended by U.S. Attorney General William Barr, who praised Best for distinguishing between the right to protest and violent crime. The zone, which was established in response to the killing of George Floyd, had become increasingly violent, with reports of robberies, assaults, and property crimes. While some protesters, like Harry "Rick" Hearns, supported the police crackdown, others, like Lencho Williams, vowed to regroup and continue the movement. The incident highlights the tension between the need to maintain order and the right to protest, as well as the importance of distinguishing between peaceful demonstration and violent crime.

END OF SUMMARY.
