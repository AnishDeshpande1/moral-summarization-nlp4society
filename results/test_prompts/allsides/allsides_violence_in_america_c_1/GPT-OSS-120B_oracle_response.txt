SUMMARY: Seattle police dismantled the Capitol Hill “autonomous zone,” which the police chief called “lawless and brutal.” Officers in helmets and protective gear entered the area, arrested 31 people for failure to disperse, assault and other alleged crimes, and began re‑establishing control of the East Precinct and Cal Anderson Park after Mayor Jenny Durkan declared the gathering an unlawful assembly. The police chief cited a recent spate of shootings, two fatal deaths, robberies, assaults and other violence that had plagued the zone. U.S. Attorney General William Barr praised the effort as “restoring the rule of law,” while President Trump labeled the demonstrators domestic terrorists. Protester Harry “Rick” Hearns, who volunteered armed security, said the violence was “brought” by outsiders and supported the crackdown. Although the CHOP had once hosted speeches on police injustice and brutality, medic stations and free‑food tents have vanished. Activist Lencho Williams warned that the movement will regroup, insisting “Black lives matter now and forever.” END OF SUMMARY.
