**SUMMARY:**  
Seattle police, wearing helmets and extra protective gear, moved in on Wednesday to dismantle the Capitol Hill Occupied Protest (CHOP), which the police chief called “lawless and brutal.” The city declared the gathering an “unlawful assembly” after a spate of shootings, robberies, assaults and other violent crimes, including two teen deaths. Officers arrested 31 people for failure to disperse and other offenses, while U.S. Attorney General William Barr praised the effort to distinguish the right to protest from terror and restore the rule of law. Protester Harry “Rick” Hearns, who had volunteered armed security, supported the crackdown, blaming outsiders for the violence. The zone, once a hub for peaceful speeches, street art and Black Lives Matter messages, had dwindled in crowds and activity. Local businesses demanded a tougher stance, and class‑action lawsuits aim to prevent future autonomous zones. Yet some demonstrators, like Lencho Williams, vowed to regroup, saying the movement’s demands—defund the police, fund the Black community, amnesty—remain a revolution that cannot be stopped.
