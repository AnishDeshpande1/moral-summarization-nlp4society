SUMMARY:

Seattle authorities dismantled the "Capitol Hill Occupied Protest" (CHOP) zone on Wednesday, arresting 31 people for various crimes. The police chief, Carmen Best, described the zone as "lawless and brutal" due to recent shootings and the deaths of two teenagers. The zone was established after the police abandoned the East Precinct building following clashes with protesters in the wake of George Floyd's killing. The area had become a hub for protests against racial injustice and police brutality, but had also seen violence and property crimes. U.S. President Donald Trump had called for action against the demonstrators, labeling them "domestic terrorists." While some protesters condemned the violence, others vowed to regroup and continue the movement. The police operation met little resistance, and authorities are working to re-establish control over the area.

END OF SUMMARY.
