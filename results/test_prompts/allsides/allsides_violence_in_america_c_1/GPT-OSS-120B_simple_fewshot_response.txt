SUMMARY:
Seattle police dismantled the Capitol Hill Occupied Protest (CHOP) after the city’s chief called the autonomous zone “lawless and brutal,” citing four shootings, two deaths, robberies and assaults. Mayor Jenny Durkan declared the gathering an “unlawful assembly,” and 31 people were arrested for failing to disperse and other crimes. U.S. Attorney General William Barr praised the action as restoring the rule of law, while President Trump labeled the demonstrators “domestic terrorists.” Some former CHOP participants, such as Harry “Rick” Hearns, supported the crackdown, blaming outside agitators for the violence. The police re‑entered the East Precinct, cleared barricades and removed tents, while the once‑crowded area of protests, speeches and free‑food stations has largely emptied. Business owners and conservative commentators have used the episode to argue that the protests were far from peaceful. Nevertheless, activists like Lencho Williams vowed to regroup and continue demands for police reform, insisting “you can’t stop a revolution.” The episode underscores the tension between protecting civil‑rights demonstrations and enforcing public safety.  
END OF SUMMARY.
