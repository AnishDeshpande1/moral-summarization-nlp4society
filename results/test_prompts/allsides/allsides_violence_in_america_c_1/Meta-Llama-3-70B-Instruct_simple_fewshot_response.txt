SUMMARY:

Seattle authorities dismantled a protest zone, dubbed "lawless and brutal" by the city's police chief, after weeks of occupation. The zone, known as CHOP, was established in response to the killing of George Floyd and had become a symbol of the Black Lives Matter movement. However, the area had seen a recent spate of shootings and violence, prompting the police to move in. 31 people were arrested for failure to disperse, assault, and other alleged crimes. The police chief and U.S. Attorney General William Barr commended the action, while the Seattle Mayor, a Democrat, was criticized by President Trump. Some protesters, like Harry "Rick" Hearns, supported the police crackdown, blaming outsiders for the violence. Others, like Lencho Williams, vowed to regroup and continue the movement, saying "you can't stop a revolution." The incident highlights the tensions between law enforcement and protesters, as well as the challenges of balancing free speech with public safety.

END OF SUMMARY.
