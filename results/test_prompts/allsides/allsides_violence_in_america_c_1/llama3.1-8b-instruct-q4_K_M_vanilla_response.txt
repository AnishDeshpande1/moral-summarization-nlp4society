SUMMARY:
Seattle authorities dismantled the Capitol Hill Occupied Protest (CHOP) zone on Wednesday, arresting 31 people for alleged crimes. Police Chief Carmen Best called the area "lawless and brutal" due to recent shootings, robberies, and assaults. US President Donald Trump had previously condemned the protesters as "domestic terrorists." The police operation was met with little resistance, with some officers sipping Starbucks coffee while clearing out the zone. Business owners in the trendy neighborhood had been pushing for a tougher stance by authorities. Protesters vowed to regroup, citing disorganization and disagreements over demands.

Meanwhile, US Attorney General William Barr commended Best for restoring order, highlighting her distinction between peaceful protest and violent crime. A protester who volunteered to provide armed security at CHOP expressed support for the police crackdown, blaming outsiders for the violence.

The CHOP zone was established in Seattle after George Floyd's killing in Minneapolis sparked nationwide protests against racial injustice and police brutality.
