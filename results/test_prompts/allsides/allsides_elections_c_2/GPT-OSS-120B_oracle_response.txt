SUMMARY:  
Former Rep. Justin Amash announced the formation of an exploratory committee to seek the Libertarian Party’s presidential nomination, positioning his bid as a chance to **restore** constitutional respect and **bring** together Americans of every background. Amash, a longtime libertarian‑focused lawmaker, argues that the nation needs a **serving** president who will curb the **exploding** federal deficit and reject the **misbehavior** and **excusing** of wrongdoing he sees in the current administration. His criticism of President Trump’s **defenses** of “misbehavior” has drawn harsh rebukes from GOP leaders, who accuse him of undermining the party. By entering the race, Amash hopes to offer a **third‑party** alternative that could attract disaffected Republicans and voters wary of both Trump’s **assault** on democratic norms and Biden’s policies. Libertarians view him as a potential game‑changer who might shift the election narrative and challenge the two‑party dominance.
