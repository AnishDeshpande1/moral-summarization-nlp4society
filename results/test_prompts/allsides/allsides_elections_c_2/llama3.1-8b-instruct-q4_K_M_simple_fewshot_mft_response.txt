SUMMARY:

Representative Justin Amash (I-Mich.) has announced his exploratory committee for the Libertarian Party's presidential nomination, signaling his potential third-party White House bid. Amash has been under pressure from Libertarians to run and has built a national profile with his vocal attacks against President Trump. As a former Republican congressman, Amash has centered his career around libertarian values, advocating for less government intervention and sharp cuts in federal spending. His decision to leave the GOP last summer was due to his criticism of Republicans' fiscal conservatism and their failure to address what he sees as Trump's "misbehavior." By running as a third-party candidate, Amash may potentially draw support away from both Trump and former Vice President Joe Biden. The move raises questions about the integrity of the Republican Party and its willingness to hold Trump accountable for his actions. Amash's candidacy could also challenge the notion that voters are limited to choosing between two major party candidates.
