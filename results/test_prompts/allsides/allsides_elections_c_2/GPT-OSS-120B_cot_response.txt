**SUMMARY:**  
Rep. Justin Amash announced an exploratory committee to seek the Libertarian Party’s presidential nomination, framing his bid as a call for humility, trust and respect for the Constitution. He said he is honored to take “first steps toward serving Americans of every background.” Amash’s libertarian values—less government, sharp spending cuts, and a refusal to tolerate misbehavior—have drawn both pressure from Libertarians and an avalanche of criticism from the GOP, which accuses him of frivolous and false attacks on President Trump. After leaving the Republican Party over what he called the party’s dangerous weaponization of politics and the President’s assault on democratic norms, Amash positions himself as a third‑party alternative that could attract voters tired of the current partisan noise. Whether he will pull support from Trump or Biden remains uncertain.
