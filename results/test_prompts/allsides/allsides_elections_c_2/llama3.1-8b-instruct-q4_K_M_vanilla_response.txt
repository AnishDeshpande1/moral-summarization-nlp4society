SUMMARY:
Rep. Justin Amash (I-Mich.) announced the launch of an exploratory committee to seek the Libertarian Party's presidential nomination, indicating he may run as a third-party candidate in the 2020 election. Amash, a former Republican congressman, has been considering a White House bid for months and has been encouraged by Libertarians to throw his hat into the ring. He has centered his career around libertarian values, advocating for less government intervention and sharp cuts in federal spending.

Amash's criticism of President Trump has earned him rebukes from the GOP, but he could potentially draw support away from Trump or Democratic nominee Joe Biden. A prominent third-party contender, Amash would face off against other Libertarians to win the party's nomination.

The move has sparked interest among Libertarians and "Never Trump" Republicans who see Amash as a viable alternative. It remains unclear whether Amash's candidacy would draw more support away from Trump or Biden, but his addition to the race could shake up the 2020 election landscape.

END OF SUMMARY.
