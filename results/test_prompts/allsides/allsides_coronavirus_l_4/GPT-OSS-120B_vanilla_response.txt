SUMMARY: The article criticizes Democratic leaders, especially New York Governor Andrew Cuomo, for policies that worsened COVID‑19 outcomes in nursing homes, leading to over 6,000 deaths—about 6% of the state’s residents—while Florida, which prohibited similar transfers, saw a lower rate of 1.6%. It acknowledges Cuomo’s later imposition of a lockdown that helped curb the surge. In contrast, Republican‑led states such as Arizona, Florida, and Texas have failed to mandate masks or enforce basic precautions, with governors even blocking local mask mandates. The piece links conservative backlash against pandemic measures to broader cultural paranoia and notes threats faced by public health workers. It argues that without competent leadership, the U.S. will continue to suffer, contrasting the situation with other nations that are cautiously reopening. The author cites a comment by Senator Lindsey Graham that the war is “limited” if deaths stay near 120,000, but declares that President Trump has already lost the battle against the virus. END OF SUMMARY.
