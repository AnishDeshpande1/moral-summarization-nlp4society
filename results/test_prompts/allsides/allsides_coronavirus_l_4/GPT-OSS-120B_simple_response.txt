SUMMARY: The article condemns Republican‑led states for reckless pandemic policies while casting Democratic leaders, especially New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo, as morally culpable yet ultimately responsible for averting an even worse disaster. It blames Cuomo’s early decision to force nursing‑home residents back and block testing for a catastrophic death toll in New York, contrasting it with Florida’s lower nursing‑home mortality. The piece praises Cuomo’s later lockdown as a necessary correction, but denounces Arizona, Florida and Texas for refusing mask mandates and blocking local COVID‑19 rules, linking these failures to a broader “paranoia and resentment” of the modern right. It highlights threats against public‑health workers and portrays the GOP’s denial as a hypocritical tool of social control, suggesting that without competent leadership the nation will remain “pummeled” while other countries recover. The author ends by noting that even a modest death ceiling (≈120,000) is hailed as a “limited” casualty count by Trump allies, implying that Trump has already lost the war against the virus. END OF SUMMARY.
