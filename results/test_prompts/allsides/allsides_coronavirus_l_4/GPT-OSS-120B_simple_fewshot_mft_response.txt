SUMMARY:
The article condemns the disastrous abuse of authority by Democratic leaders, especially New York Governor Andrew Cuomo, whose orders forcing nursing‑home residents back into facilities and banning testing allowed Covid‑19 to ravage those homes, killing over 6 percent of residents. By contrast, states that resisted such top‑down mandates—Florida, Arizona and Texas—kept nursing‑home death rates far lower and have refused even basic mask requirements, despite pressure from liberal officials. The piece argues that liberal enthusiasm for protests and strict social‑distancing rules reveals a hypocritical use of authority to control the population, while the right’s growing paranoia and resentment are a natural response to officials who endanger public health. It highlights the real human cost—threats, harassment and even armed attacks on public‑health workers—underscoring the moral imperative to protect the vulnerable. The author concludes that, unlike countries with competent leadership that are beginning to recover, the United States’ failure to enforce responsible authority will continue to cause needless harm, and even Trump’s limited “victory” is already lost.

END OF SUMMARY.
