SUMMARY:

The article criticizes the handling of the COVID-19 pandemic by Democratic leaders, particularly New York Governor Andrew Cuomo, who forced nursing homes to take back infected residents, leading to a high death toll. In contrast, Florida's ban on such transfers resulted in a much lower death rate. The article also condemns the lack of action by Republican governors in Arizona, Florida, and Texas, who failed to implement simple measures like mask mandates, and even prevented cities from doing so. This inaction is attributed to a culture of paranoia and resentment on the right, which has led to public health workers facing death threats and harassment. The article concludes that the US will continue to struggle with the pandemic due to incompetent leadership, while other countries with competent leaders are slowly returning to normal.
