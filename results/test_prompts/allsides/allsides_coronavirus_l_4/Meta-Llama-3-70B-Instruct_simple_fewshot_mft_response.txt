SUMMARY:

The COVID-19 pandemic has exposed devastating failures of leadership, particularly among Democratic leaders. New York Governor Andrew Cuomo's decision to force nursing homes to take back infected residents without testing them led to catastrophic consequences, with over 6,000 deaths. In contrast, Florida's prohibition on such transfers resulted in a significantly lower death rate. Meanwhile, Arizona, Florida, and Texas have failed to implement basic measures like mask mandates, and their governors have even prevented cities from instituting their own requirements. The lack of leadership and accountability has led to a culture of paranoia and resentment, with public health workers facing death threats and harassment. The author argues that the political will to contain the virus is lacking, and as a result, the US will continue to suffer while other countries with competent leadership begin to return to normal.

END OF SUMMARY.
