SUMMARY:

The article criticizes Democratic leaders, particularly New York Governor Andrew Cuomo, for their handling of the coronavirus pandemic. Cuomo's decision to force nursing homes to take back infected residents and prohibiting testing led to a devastating 6% death rate among nursing home residents. In contrast, Florida's ban on such transfers resulted in a much lower 1.6% death rate. The article also condemns Republican leaders in Arizona, Florida, and Texas for not implementing basic measures like mask mandates, and for preventing cities from doing so. The author argues that this lack of action is fueled by a paranoid and resentful political culture that views public health measures as social control. As a result, public health workers face harassment and death threats, and the US will continue to struggle with the pandemic.

END OF SUMMARY.
