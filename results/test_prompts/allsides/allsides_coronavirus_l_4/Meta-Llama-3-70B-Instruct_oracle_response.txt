SUMMARY:

The article highlights the devastating failures of Democratic leaders, particularly New York Governor Andrew Cuomo, in handling the coronavirus pandemic. Cuomo's errors, such as forcing nursing homes to take back infected residents and barring them from testing, contributed to the catastrophic loss of over 6,000 lives. In contrast, Florida's prohibition on such transfers resulted in a significantly lower death rate. The article also criticizes the lack of action by governors in Arizona, Florida, and Texas, who failed to impose simple measures like mask-wearing, and even prevented cities from doing so. This inaction is attributed to a culture of paranoia and resentment on the right, which has led to public health workers facing death threats and harassment. The article concludes that the US will continue to struggle with the pandemic due to a lack of competent leadership, unlike other countries that are slowly returning to normal. The war against the virus has been lost, with over 120,000 fatalities.
