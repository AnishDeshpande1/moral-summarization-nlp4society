SUMMARY:

The article criticizes Democratic leaders, particularly New York's Governor Andrew Cuomo, for their handling of the coronavirus pandemic in March. Cuomo's decision to force nursing homes to take back residents who had been hospitalized with COVID-19 led to a devastating outbreak that killed over 6,000 people. In contrast, Florida's ban on such transfers resulted in significantly fewer deaths among nursing home residents.

The article also notes that Republican governors in Arizona and Texas have failed to take adequate measures to contain the pandemic, including refusing to mandate mask-wearing until last week. The author argues that this lack of leadership is contributing to ongoing dangers from the pandemic, despite some countries returning to normal with competent governance.

The article concludes by criticizing President Trump's handling of the pandemic, noting that the country has already surpassed 120,000 coronavirus-related deaths, a benchmark set by Lindsey Graham in mid-May. The author argues that Trump has lost the "war" against COVID-19 and that the country will continue to suffer as a result.

END OF SUMMARY.
