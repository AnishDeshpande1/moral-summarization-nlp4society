SUMMARY:
President Obama announced that, during his visit to Russia for the G‑20 summit, he will meet with LGBT‑rights activists and representatives of groups defending human rights, the environment and free media. The meeting follows Russia’s controversial June law banning the “promotion” of gay relationships to minors and a ban on adoptions by countries that recognize same‑sex marriage, which have sparked international condemnation and an increase in violence against Russia’s LGBT community. Obama has repeatedly criticized these anti‑gay statutes, saying they offend him deeply, while refusing to call for a boycott of the Sochi Winter Olympics so as not to punish American athletes. The planned meeting, arranged after Obama canceled a scheduled summit with President Vladimir Putin, is praised by Human Rights First as a clear signal of U.S. solidarity with those targeted by Russia’s crackdown on civil society and LGBT rights. Putin, for his part, downplays the criticism, claiming normal relations with activists and awarding them state honors.

END OF SUMMARY.
