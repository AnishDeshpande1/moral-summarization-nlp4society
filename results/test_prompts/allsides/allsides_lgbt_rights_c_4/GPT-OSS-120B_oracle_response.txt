SUMMARY: President Obama will meet LGBT‑rights activists during his Russia visit for the G‑20 summit, joining groups that champion free media, the environment and human‑rights causes. The meeting follows Russia’s recent legislation that bans promotion of gay relationships to minors and bans adoptions by countries allowing same‑sex marriage, laws that have sparked an uptick in violence against the country’s LGBT community. Human‑rights advocates argue the Russian crackdown targets civil society and call for stronger U.S. condemnation of these abuses. Obama, who previously criticized the anti‑gay legislation as deeply offensive, cancelled a planned encounter with President Putin after a series of disputes—including Russia’s decision to grant asylum to Edward Snowden and its blocking of UN action on Syria. While refusing to penalize American athletes by boycotting the Sochi Winter Olympics, Obama’s upcoming meeting is intended to signal that the United States stands in solidarity with those targeted by Russia’s oppressive laws. END OF SUMMARY.
