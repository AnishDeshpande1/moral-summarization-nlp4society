**SUMMARY:**  
President Obama will meet LGBT‑rights activists during his visit to Russia for the G‑20 summit, joining groups that champion human rights, free media and the environment. The meeting follows intense criticism of Russia’s June law banning the promotion of gay relationships to minors and a related ban on adoptions by countries that allow same‑sex marriage. Human‑rights organizations report an uptick in violence against Russia’s LGBT community and have called for a boycott of the Sochi Winter Olympics. Obama has repeatedly said he is deeply offended by Russia’s anti‑gay legislation and has been sharply critical of the crackdown, while still refusing to endorse a boycott that would penalize U.S. athletes. The decision to meet civil‑society activists, praised by Human Rights First, signals U.S. solidarity with those targeted by Russia’s policies and reaffirms a commitment to LGBT equality and broader human rights. Putin responded that Russia maintains normal relations and even honors some activists with state prizes.
