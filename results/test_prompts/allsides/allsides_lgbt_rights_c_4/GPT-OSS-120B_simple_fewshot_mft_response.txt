SUMMARY:
President Obama will meet LGBT‑rights activists, human‑rights groups, environmentalists and free‑media advocates during his visit to Russia for the G‑20 summit, signaling a direct challenge to Moscow’s authoritarian stance. The White House official confirmed the Friday gathering will give civil‑society voices a platform after Russia’s recent “gay‑propaganda” law and a ban on adoptions by countries recognizing same‑sex marriage, both of which have spurred a rise in violence against LGBT Russians. By confronting these repressive policies, Obama underscores U.S. commitment to care for the vulnerable and to fairness for a community denied equal treatment, while subverting Russian authority that seeks to silence dissent. The meeting follows Obama’s cancellation of a planned summit with President Putin, a move prompted by disputes over Snowden’s asylum and Russia’s blocking of UN action on Syria. Human‑rights advocates praised the decision, urging Obama to denounce Russia’s abuses and reaffirm American support for LGBT equality. Putin, meanwhile, downplayed the criticism, claiming normal relations with the targeted groups.

END OF SUMMARY.
