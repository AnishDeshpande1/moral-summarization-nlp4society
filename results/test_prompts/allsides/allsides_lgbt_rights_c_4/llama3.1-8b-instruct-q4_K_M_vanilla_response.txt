SUMMARY:

President Obama will meet with lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) rights activists during his visit to Russia for the G-20 summit. This meeting comes after international criticism of Russia's laws banning the promotion of gay relationships to minors and adoptions by countries allowing same-sex marriages. Human rights groups have reported increased violence against the LGBT community in Russia since the laws were passed.

The meeting will also include representatives from groups supporting human rights, the environment, free media, and others. Obama has been sharply critical of the Russian laws, saying he is "offended" by them. However, he had previously considered boycotting the 2014 Winter Olympics in Sochi, but decided against it to avoid penalizing American athletes.

The meeting is seen as a way for Obama to show support for the LGBT community and civil society in Russia, which has been targeted by the government's crackdown on human rights. END OF SUMMARY.
