SUMMARY:

President Obama will meet with LGBT rights activists in Russia during his visit for the G-20 summit, despite criticism from human rights groups over the country's anti-LGBT laws. Russia has faced international backlash since passing a law banning promotion of gay relationships to minors and another law blocking adoptions by countries allowing same-sex marriages. Human rights groups have reported increased violence against LGBT individuals in Russia since the laws were passed. Obama has been sharply critical of the Russian laws, saying he is "offended" by them. The meeting with activists is seen as a sign that the US stands in solidarity with those targeted by Russia's crackdown on human rights and LGBT community.

END OF SUMMARY.
