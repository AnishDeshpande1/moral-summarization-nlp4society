SUMMARY: President Obama will meet with LGBT rights activists and other civil‑society representatives during his visit to Russia for the G‑20 summit, according to an unnamed White House official. The meeting, scheduled for Friday, follows international criticism of Russia’s June law banning the promotion of gay relationships to minors and a law prohibiting adoptions by countries that recognize same‑sex marriage. Human‑rights groups say violence against Russia’s LGBT community has risen since the legislation, prompting calls to boycott the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics. Obama, while rejecting a boycott of the Games, has condemned the anti‑gay laws, calling them “offensive.” The summit also comes after Obama cancelled a planned meeting with President Vladimir Putin amid disputes over the Snowden asylum grant and Russia’s UN Security Council veto on Syria. Human Rights First praised Obama’s decision to meet activists, urging him to publicly denounce Russia’s human‑rights abuses. Putin responded to criticism by saying he regularly honors civil‑society figures and maintains “normal relations.” END OF SUMMARY.
