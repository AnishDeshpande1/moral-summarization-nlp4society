SUMMARY:

President Obama will meet with LGBT rights activists and other human rights groups during the G-20 summit in Russia, despite the country's recent laws banning the promotion of gay relationships to minors and increased violence against the LGBT community. The meeting is a clear signal that the US stands in solidarity with those targeted by Russia's crackdown on civil society and LGBT rights. Russia has faced international criticism for its laws, which have led to calls for a boycott of the 2014 Winter Olympics in Sochi. Obama has been sharply critical of the laws, saying they are offensive and that he doesn't want to penalize American athletes. The meeting comes after Obama canceled a scheduled meeting with Russian President Vladimir Putin, following a string of disputes including Russia's decision to grant asylum to Edward Snowden and its blocking of action against Syria. Putin has downplayed the criticism, saying he has normal relations with LGBT individuals and doesn't see anything out of the ordinary. END OF SUMMARY.
