SUMMARY:

President Obama will meet with lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) rights activists during the G-20 summit in Russia, amid international criticism of Russia's laws banning promotion of gay relationships to minors and adoptions by countries allowing same-sex marriages. The meeting is a bold move, as Obama has been sharply critical of the Russian laws, calling them "anti-gay and lesbian legislation." Human rights groups have reported an increase in violence against LGBT Russians since the laws passed. The meeting sends a clear signal that the United States stands in solidarity with those targeted by Russia's civil society and LGBT crackdown. It is a moral imperative to speak out against Russia's human rights abuses and reaffirm the United States' commitment to LGBT equality and human rights. By meeting with activists, Obama is exercising his authority to promote care and fairness, and to counter Russia's harmful and discriminatory laws. The move is a significant step towards promoting human rights and dignity for all, regardless of sexual orientation or gender identity.

END OF SUMMARY.
