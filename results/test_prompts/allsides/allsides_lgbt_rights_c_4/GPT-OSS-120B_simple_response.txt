SUMMARY: President Obama will meet LGBT‑rights activists and other civil‑society groups during his visit to Russia for the G‑20 summit, a move praised by human‑rights advocates as a moral stand against Russia’s anti‑gay legislation. The meeting follows Russia’s June law banning the “promotion” of gay relationships to minors and a ban on adoptions by countries recognizing same‑sex marriage, which have spurred increased violence toward LGBT Russians and calls to boycott the Sochi Winter Olympics. Obama has condemned these laws, calling them deeply offensive, while refusing to support an Olympic boycott to protect American athletes. The decision comes after Obama canceled a planned summit with President Vladimir Putin amid disputes over Edward Snowden’s asylum and Russia’s veto of UN action on Syria. Human Rights First lauded the activist meeting as a clear signal of U.S. solidarity with those targeted by Russia’s crackdown, urging Obama also to publicly denounce the abuses. Putin, when pressed, downplayed the criticism, claiming normal relations and occasional state honors for activists. END OF SUMMARY.
