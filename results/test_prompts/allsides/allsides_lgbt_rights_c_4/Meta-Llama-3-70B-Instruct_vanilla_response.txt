SUMMARY:

President Obama will meet with LGBT rights activists and other human rights groups during the G-20 summit in Russia this week. The meeting comes amidst international criticism of Russia's laws banning the promotion of gay relationships to minors and adoptions by same-sex marriage countries. Human rights groups have reported an increase in violence against LGBT individuals in Russia since the laws passed. Obama has been critical of the laws, but has not supported a boycott of the 2014 Winter Olympics in Sochi. The meeting is seen as a signal of US solidarity with those targeted by Russia's crackdown on civil society and LGBT individuals. Human Rights First has applauded the decision, calling on Obama to speak out against Russia's human rights abuses and reaffirm US support for LGBT equality and human rights. Russian President Vladimir Putin has downplayed the criticism, saying he has normal relations with LGBT individuals and awards them state prizes for their achievements.

END OF SUMMARY.
