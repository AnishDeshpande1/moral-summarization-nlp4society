SUMMARY:

President Obama will meet with lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) rights activists during the G-20 summit in Russia, amidst international criticism of Russia's anti-LGBT laws. The meeting, scheduled for Friday, will include representatives from groups supporting human rights, the environment, free media, and LGBT rights. Russia has faced criticism for its laws banning the promotion of gay relationships to minors and banning adoptions by countries allowing same-sex marriages, leading to reports of increased violence against LGBT individuals. Obama has been sharply critical of these laws, stating that "nobody is more offended" than he is by such legislation. The meeting is seen as a significant gesture of solidarity with Russia's LGBT community and a reaffirmation of the US stance on LGBT equality and human rights. Human rights groups have applauded the decision, urging Obama to speak out against Russia's human rights abuses during the summit.

END OF SUMMARY.
