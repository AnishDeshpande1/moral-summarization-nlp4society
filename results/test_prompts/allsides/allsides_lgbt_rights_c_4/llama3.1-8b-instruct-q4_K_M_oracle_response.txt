SUMMARY: President Obama will meet with lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender rights activists during his visit to Russia for the G-20 summit. This decision comes after international criticism of Russia's laws banning the promotion of gay relationships to minors and blocking adoptions by countries allowing same-sex marriages. Human rights groups have reported an uptick in violence against the LGBT community since these laws were passed, leading to calls for a boycott of the 2014 Winter Olympics in Sochi. Obama has been sharply critical of Russia's legislation, stating that he is "offended" by it. The meeting with activists will demonstrate the United States' solidarity with those targeted by Russia's crackdown on civil society and human rights. This move is seen as a stand against Russia's abuses and a reaffirmation of the US commitment to LGBT equality and human rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
