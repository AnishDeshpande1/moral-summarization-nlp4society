SUMMARY:

President Obama will meet with lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) rights activists during his visit to Russia for the G-20 summit. This meeting comes amidst international criticism of Russia's laws banning promotion of gay relationships to minors and adoptions by countries allowing same-sex marriages. Human rights groups have reported increased violence against LGBT individuals in Russia since the laws passed. Obama has been sharply critical of these laws, stating that he is "offended" by them. The meeting with activists sends a signal that the US stands in solidarity with those targeted by Russia's civil society and LGBT crackdown. Human Rights First applauded the decision, urging Obama to also speak out against Russia's human rights abuses and reaffirm the US commitment to LGBT equality and human rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
