SUMMARY:
Chicago’s mayor announced the new Accountability on Communication and Transparency Ordinance, cementing the city’s long‑standing Welcoming City policy and formally barring ICE from accessing police databases or receiving routine assistance. By codifying earlier executive actions, the ordinance protects immigrant and refugee families from federal enforcement, emphasizing that cooperation with ICE would betray the city’s moral duty to safeguard vulnerable people. The mayor frames the refusal to aid ICE as an act of moral authority that subverts a federal agenda he characterizes as “cynical” and harmful, arguing that true authority lies in defending the community’s health, safety, and dignity. The measure is presented as a matter of fairness—ensuring immigrants are not unfairly targeted or “cheated” of their rights—while upholding the sanctity of Chicago’s diverse heritage and the sacred value of inclusion. The mayor links the policy to public safety, claiming that trust between police and immigrant residents is essential, and condemns the national debate that demonizes immigrants. By protecting families from fear of arrest, the ordinance seeks to preserve the city’s unity, equity, and the moral integrity of its historic role as a beacon for newcomers.  
END OF SUMMARY.
