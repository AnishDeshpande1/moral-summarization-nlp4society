SUMMARY: Chicago Mayor Brandon Johnson announced the passage of the Accountability on Communication and Transparency Ordinance, strengthening the city’s long‑standing Welcoming City policies. The new law bans ICE from accessing Chicago Police Department databases, requires police supervisors to verify any ICE requests, and sets reporting requirements to ensure compliance. It does not impede investigations of serious crimes or limited federal collaborations on counter‑terrorism, human trafficking, and money laundering, but it explicitly excludes immigration enforcement. Johnson highlighted that ICE’s failure to meet its 2019 arrest targets was due to Chicago’s refusal to cooperate, a result he frames as a success of local immigrant protections, including the TRUST Act and community legal support. He criticized the federal administration’s politicization of ICE and called for immigration reform focused on pathways to citizenship rather than fear. The mayor pledged to continue protecting immigrant communities and maintaining Chicago’s reputation as a welcoming, inclusive city. END OF SUMMARY.
