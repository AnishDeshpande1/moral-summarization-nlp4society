SUMMARY:  
The Chicago mayor announced the new Accountability on Communication and Transparency Ordinance, which **bolsters** the city’s long‑standing Welcoming City policy and **codifies** extensive **protections** for immigrant and refugee families. The ordinance **prohibits** ICE from accessing Chicago Police Department databases and requires any ICE request to be **confirmed** as **truthful** and not aimed at **immigration enforcement**. It also **establishes** reporting requirements, **provides** legal guidance to protect patrons from **targeted** **raids**, and ensures law‑enforcement can still pursue serious **crimes** and **enterprises** such as **counter‑terrorism**, **human trafficking**, and **money laundering**, with cooperation **terminated** if actions become **inconsistent** with city policy.  

Mayor Lopez praised the **support** of local officials, the **services** of immigration attorneys, and community **educate**‑and‑**empower** programs that keep neighborhoods **safe** and **whole**. She condemned the current administration’s **politized** **campaign** that **scares** undocumented families, calling it **demonization** that fuels **fear**, **arrests**, and makes children **afraid** of parents **missing**. Emphasizing that immigrants are a **sacred** part of Chicago’s heritage, she urged a national shift toward **welcoming**, **inclusion**, **equity**, and an end to **division** and **demonization**. The city will continue to **protect** its diverse residents and uphold the values that make Chicago a model of **unity** and opportunity.  

END OF SUMMARY.
