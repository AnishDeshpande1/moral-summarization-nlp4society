SUMMARY:

Chicago Mayor Lori Lightfoot has announced the passage of the Accountability on Communication and Transparency Ordinance, which strengthens the city's Welcoming City Ordinance and prohibits ICE from accessing Chicago Police Department databases. The ordinance also prohibits CPD from assisting ICE with immigration enforcement operations and establishes reporting requirements to ensure compliance. This move is a major expansion of immigrant protections in Chicago, and Lightfoot attributes the decline in ICE arrests in 2019 to the city's refusal to cooperate with ICE. The mayor argues that the current administration has politicized ICE into a campaign against immigrants and refugees, and that cities like Chicago should not have to work against federal agencies to keep families safe. The ordinance is a step towards creating a more inclusive and equitable society, where immigrants are welcomed and valued. By standing up for its neighbors and rejecting division and demonization, Chicago is reaffirming its values of unity, equity, and opportunity.

END OF SUMMARY.
