SUMMARY:

The mayor of Chicago announced that the city's arrests by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) have dramatically fallen in 2019 due to local officials refusing to cooperate. The city has passed the Accountability on Communication and Transparency Ordinance, which prohibits ICE from accessing Chicago Police Department databases and requires CPD supervisors to confirm ICE requests are truthful and not related to immigration enforcement. The ordinance also establishes reporting requirements and provides legal guidance to protect immigrant patrons from ICE raids. The mayor attributes the decline in ICE arrests to the city's "unequivocal support" of immigrant and refugee communities, citing the importance of diversity and protecting values that have made the U.S. a beacon of hope. The mayor criticizes the current administration's politicization of ICE and calls for a national immigration policy that welcomes new residents with clear pathways to citizenship.

END OF SUMMARY.
