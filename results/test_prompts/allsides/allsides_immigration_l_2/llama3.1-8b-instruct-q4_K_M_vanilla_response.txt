SUMMARY:

Chicago Mayor announced a major victory for immigrant communities, as ICE arrests in the area dropped dramatically in 2019 due to local officials' refusal to cooperate. The mayor attributed this success to the city's Welcoming City Ordinance, which was passed by the City Council and codifies previous executive actions. The ordinance prohibits ICE from accessing Chicago Police Department databases and requires supervisors to confirm that requests for assistance will not support immigration enforcement.

The mayor praised the efforts of local advocates, state leaders, and community groups in protecting immigrant communities. However, he noted that this should not be necessary and that cities like Chicago should not have to "butt heads" with federal agencies to keep families safe. The mayor emphasized the importance of crafting immigration policy based on welcoming principles rather than fear and demonization.

The ordinance aims to protect immigrant patrons in public facilities from ICE raids and will provide legal guidance to those facilities. The mayor vowed to continue protecting communities, strengthening Chicago's status as a Welcoming City, and fighting for unity, inclusion, and opportunity.

END OF SUMMARY.
