SUMMARY:

Chicago's mayor proudly announces that the city's arrests by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) have dramatically fallen in 2019 due to local officials' refusal to cooperate. This is a result of the city's unwavering support for immigrant and refugee communities, rooted in the belief that diversity is the nation's strength. The city has passed the Accountability on Communication and Transparency Ordinance, which prohibits ICE from accessing police databases and requesting assistance with immigration enforcement operations. The ordinance aims to protect immigrant communities and ensure that law enforcement agencies focus on serious and violent crimes, not immigration enforcement. The mayor emphasizes that cities should not have to "butt heads" with federal agencies to keep families safe and communities whole, and that immigration policy should be based on welcoming newcomers with clear pathways to citizenship, not fear of arrests. The city will continue to protect its communities and fight for unity, inclusion, and opportunity.

END OF SUMMARY.
