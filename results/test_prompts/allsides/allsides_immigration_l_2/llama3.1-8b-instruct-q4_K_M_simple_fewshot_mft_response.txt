SUMMARY:

Chicago's mayor announced the codification of measures to protect immigrant communities, making it one of the largest expansions of immigrant protections in the city since the Welcoming City Ordinance was adopted over 30 years ago. The ordinance prohibits ICE from accessing Chicago Police Department databases and bars them from turning to CPD for assistance with immigration enforcement operations. It also establishes reporting requirements to ensure compliance with the Welcoming City Ordinance and provides legal guidance to public facilities on how to protect immigrant patrons in case of ICE raids. The mayor emphasized that this move is necessary because the current administration has politicized ICE into a campaign against immigrants and refugees, highlighting the importance of community trust for effective local policing. By prioritizing the safety and well-being of immigrants, Chicago aims to promote unity, inclusion, equity, and opportunity.

The mayor's actions are seen as a direct response to the Trump administration's immigration policies, which have led to increased fear among immigrant communities. The ordinance is designed to prevent ICE from targeting families and individuals without cause, ensuring that they can continue to contribute to the city's economy and society without fear of deportation. By standing up for its immigrant neighbors, Chicago is reaffirming its values of unity, inclusion, equity, and opportunity.

END OF SUMMARY.
