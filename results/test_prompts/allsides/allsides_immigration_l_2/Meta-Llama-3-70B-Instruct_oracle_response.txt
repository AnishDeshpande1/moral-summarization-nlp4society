SUMMARY:

The City of Chicago has taken a strong stance in supporting and protecting its immigrant and refugee communities. The Accountability on Communication and Transparency Ordinance, passed by the City Council, bolsters the Welcoming City Ordinance, prohibiting ICE from accessing Chicago Police Department databases and requiring CPD supervisors to confirm ICE requests are truthful and not related to immigration enforcement. The ordinance establishes reporting requirements and provides legal guidance to public facilities on how to protect immigrant patrons from ICE raids. This move is a direct response to the current administration's politized campaign against immigrants and refugees, which has created fear and division. By taking these measures, Chicago is ensuring the safety and unity of its communities, standing up for its neighbors, and promoting a welcoming and inclusive environment. The city's actions are a beacon of hope, demonstrating that no one should be demonized for who they are. END OF SUMMARY.
