SUMMARY:

Chicago Mayor announced that ICE arrests fell dramatically in 2019 due to local officials' refusal to cooperate, citing the city's commitment to supporting immigrant and refugee communities. The city passed an ordinance to bolster its Welcoming City Ordinance, which prohibits ICE from accessing police databases and requires confirmation of truthful requests for assistance. The measure also establishes reporting requirements and provides legal guidance to protect immigrants.

The mayor credits the decrease in arrests to the efforts of local leaders, advocates, and community navigators who have educated residents about their rights. However, they lament that cities like Chicago must work diligently to keep families safe due to the current administration's "cynical campaign against immigrants and refugees."

The mayor emphasizes the importance of welcoming newcomers with clear pathways to citizenship, rather than fearing arrests. They vow to continue protecting communities, strengthening Chicago's status as a Welcoming City, and fighting for unity, inclusion, equity, and opportunity.

END OF SUMMARY.
