SUMMARY:  
Chicago Mayor Lori Lightfoot used a City Council meeting to cement the city’s “Welcoming City” ethos into law, passing the Accountability on Communication and Transparency Ordinance. The measure bars ICE from accessing Chicago Police Department databases, requires police supervisors to verify any ICE request, and sets reporting and legal‑guidance requirements to protect immigrant residents. Lightfoot stresses that the ordinance does not impede investigations of serious crimes or rare joint operations on terrorism, human trafficking, or money‑laundering, but it firmly excludes immigration enforcement. She frames the policy as a moral duty to safeguard the city’s diverse immigrant and refugee families, arguing that protecting them builds community trust, strengthens the economy, and upholds America’s historic values of inclusion and opportunity. The mayor cites the recent ICE deputy director’s admission that arrests fell because Chicago refused cooperation, calling this a positive outcome of the city’s commitment to dignity and safety. Lightfoot condemns the federal administration’s politicization of ICE, urging a national shift toward welcoming policies and clear pathways to citizenship, and pledges continued work for unity, equity, and the city’s reputation as a world‑class home.  
END OF SUMMARY.
