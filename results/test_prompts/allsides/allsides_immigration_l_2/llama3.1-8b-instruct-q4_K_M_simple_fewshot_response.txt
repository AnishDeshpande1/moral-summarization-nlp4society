SUMMARY:

Chicago Mayor Lori Lightfoot announced that her city's law enforcement will not cooperate with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) in immigration enforcement operations. This decision is a result of the city's commitment to protecting its immigrant and refugee communities, which are essential to the city's diversity and economic growth. The mayor's office passed an ordinance that prohibits ICE from accessing Chicago Police Department databases and ensures that any requests for assistance must be reviewed by a supervisor. This move comes after a drop in ICE arrests in the Chicago area due to local officials' refusal to cooperate with federal agencies. The mayor emphasized that immigration policy should prioritize welcoming newcomers with clear pathways to citizenship, rather than fear of arrest. The city's actions are seen as a necessary step towards protecting its communities and promoting unity, inclusion, and equity.
