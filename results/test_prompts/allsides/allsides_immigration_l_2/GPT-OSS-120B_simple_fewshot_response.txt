SUMMARY:
Chicago’s mayor announced the passage of the Accountability on Communication and Transparency Ordinance, strengthening the city’s long‑standing Welcoming City policies. The new law bars ICE from accessing Chicago Police Department databases, requires supervisory verification of any ICE requests, and establishes reporting to the mayor’s office and the Inspector General. It affirms that the ordinance does not impede investigations of serious crimes or limited cooperation on counter‑terrorism, human‑trafficking and money‑laundering cases, but it expressly excludes immigration enforcement. The mayor framed the ordinance as a moral duty to protect immigrant and refugee families, emphasizing that Chicago’s diversity fuels its economic strength and social fabric. He praised local advocates, the TRUST Act, immigration attorneys and community navigators for helping undocumented residents understand their rights, and condemned the federal administration’s politicization of ICE. By refusing to cooperate with ICE, Chicago helped the agency miss its 2019 arrest targets, a result the mayor presented as a triumph of inclusion, community trust and the city’s commitment to “unity, equity and opportunity.”
END OF SUMMARY.
