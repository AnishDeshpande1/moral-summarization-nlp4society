SUMMARY:
Chicago's mayor has announced the codification of measures to protect immigrant and refugee communities. The Accountability on Communication and Transparency Ordinance prohibits ICE from accessing Chicago Police Department databases, prevents them from turning to CPD for assistance with immigration enforcement operations, and requires reporting requirements. This move is seen as a pushback against the Trump administration's efforts to "scare undocumented families into the shadows." The mayor emphasizes that immigration policy should be about welcoming newcomers with clear pathways to citizenship, not fear of arrests. Chicago's immigrants are described as essential to building the city, driving the economy, and creating a competitive edge. The move is hailed as one of the largest expansions of immigrant protections in 30 years.
