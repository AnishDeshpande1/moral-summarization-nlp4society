SUMMARY:

Alabama's near-total abortion ban, which would only allow abortions in cases where a pregnancy poses a "serious health risk" to the mother, has been sued by Planned Parenthood and the ACLU. The lawsuit argues that the ban is unconstitutional and disproportionately harms black and low-income people. Alabama Republicans intentionally crafted the law to be extreme, knowing it would lead to a court challenge that could ultimately overturn Roe v. Wade. The plaintiffs argue that banning abortion will have devastating consequences for their patients' lives. This is just one of many attempts by state legislatures to restrict access to abortion, with more than 350 restrictions introduced this year alone.

The lawsuit highlights the disproportionate impact on black and low-income people, who already face significant barriers to accessing healthcare. By suing over the ban, Planned Parenthood and the ACLU are fighting to protect their patients' rights and ensure that women have access to safe and legal abortion services. The outcome of this case has far-reaching implications for reproductive rights nationwide.
