SUMMARY: Planned Parenthood and the ACLU filed a federal lawsuit challenging Alabama’s near‑total abortion ban, which allows abortions only when a pregnancy threatens the mother’s health and provides no exceptions for rape, incest, or socioeconomic circumstances. The law imposes severe penalties, up to 99 years in prison for providers, and was deliberately crafted by Republican lawmakers to provoke legal challenges that could overturn Roe v. Wade. The suit, filed on behalf of Planned Parenthood Southeast, three state clinics, and Dr. Yashica Robinson, argues the “serious health risk” exception is unworkably narrow, violates the Constitution, and disproportionately harms Black and low‑income women, who represent a majority of the state’s abortion patients. ACLU attorney Alexa Kolbi‑Molinas and Planned Parenthood’s Leana Wen emphasized the necessity of litigation to protect patients’ lives. The case adds to a wave of restrictive abortion measures across the U.S., with over 350 new state restrictions this year and recent bans in Missouri and Georgia, though none are yet in effect. END OF SUMMARY.
