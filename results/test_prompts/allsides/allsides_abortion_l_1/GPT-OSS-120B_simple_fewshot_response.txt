SUMMARY:
Planned Parenthood and the ACLU have sued Alabama over the state’s near‑total abortion ban, a law engineered by Republican lawmakers to provoke a court challenge that could be used to overturn Roe v. Wade. The ban allows abortions only when a pregnancy threatens a “serious health risk” to the mother, provides no exceptions for rape or incest, and threatens providers with up to 99 years in prison. The plaintiffs argue the health‑risk exception is impossibly narrow, the ban is flagrantly unconstitutional, and it will hit Black and low‑income women hardest—who, despite being under one‑third of the state’s population, account for more than 60 % of abortions. The lawsuit frames the legislation as a politically‑motivated assault on women’s health and autonomy, designed to exploit the anti‑abortion agenda of Alabama’s GOP leaders and President Trump’s supporters. Plaintiffs say they have no choice but to fight in court to protect patients’ lives, even as similar restrictive bills sweep through other states.

END OF SUMMARY.
