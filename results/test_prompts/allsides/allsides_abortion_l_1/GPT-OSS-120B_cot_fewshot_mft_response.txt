**SUMMARY:**
Planned Parenthood and the ACLU sued Alabama over its near‑total abortion ban, a law engineered by Republican legislators to be “blatantly unconstitutional” and to force a legal challenge that could overturn Roe v. Wade. The ban allows abortions only for a “serious health risk,” a narrowly‑tailored exception that the plaintiffs argue is impossible to meet and therefore **restrictive** and **politically‑motivated**. They contend the law **exploits** women’s **health** and **lives** for a right‑wing **agenda**, disproportionately **harm**ing **black** and **low‑income** patients who already bear the brunt of abortion restrictions. Alexa Kolbi‑Molinas of the ACLU called the measure an **anti‑abortion** attack that **pushes** reproductive rights further out of reach. Planned Parenthood’s Leana Wen said the organization has **no choice** but to **fight** through lawsuits, legislatures and the ballot box, asserting that the ban is **unconstitutional**, **severe**, and a clear violation of **fairness**. The case marks the first step in a broader battle against a wave of state‑level abortion restrictions.
