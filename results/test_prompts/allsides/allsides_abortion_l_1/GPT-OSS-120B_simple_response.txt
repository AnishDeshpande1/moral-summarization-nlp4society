SUMMARY: Planned Parenthood and the ACLU have sued Alabama over its near‑total abortion ban, a law deliberately crafted by Republican lawmakers to provoke a court challenge that could overturn Roe v. Wade. The statute permits abortions only when a pregnancy threatens a “serious health risk,” offers no exceptions for rape or incest, and threatens up to 99 years in prison for providers—measures the plaintiffs argue are intentionally unconstitutional and politically motivated. The lawsuit contends the narrow health‑risk exception is impossible to meet and that the ban will disproportionately harm Black and low‑income women, who constitute a majority of the state’s abortion patients. Representatives of the ACLU and Planned Parenthood frame the fight as a moral imperative to protect vulnerable lives from a draconian agenda fueled by anti‑abortion politics and the Trump administration. They assert that legal action is the only way to defend patients’ health and dignity, even as similar restrictive bills sweep across other states, prompting nationwide protests.  

END OF SUMMARY.
