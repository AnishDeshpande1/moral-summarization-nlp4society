SUMMARY:

Planned Parenthood and the ACLU have sued Alabama over its near-total abortion ban, which only allows abortions if the pregnancy poses a "serious health risk" to the mother, with no exceptions for rape or incest. The law, engineered by Republicans, aims to challenge Roe v. Wade, the 1973 Supreme Court decision that legalized abortion nationwide. The lawsuit argues that the law is unconstitutional and will disproportionately harm black and low-income people, who make up a significant portion of those seeking abortions in Alabama. This is the first step in a long legal battle over the ban. The ACLU and Planned Parenthood claim that Alabama has been pushing abortion care out of reach with medically unnecessary restrictions. The lawsuit was filed on behalf of Planned Parenthood Southeast, Alabama's three abortion clinics, and a doctor who performs abortions in the state.

END OF SUMMARY.
