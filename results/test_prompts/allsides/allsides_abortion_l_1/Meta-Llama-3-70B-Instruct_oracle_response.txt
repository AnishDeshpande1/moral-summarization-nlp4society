SUMMARY:

Alabama's near-total abortion ban, which allows abortions only if a pregnancy poses a "serious health risk" to the mother, has sparked a lawsuit from Planned Parenthood and the ACLU. The law, engineered by Republicans to overturn Roe v. Wade, has no exceptions for rape or incest and could imprison abortion providers for up to 99 years. Critics argue that the law is "blatantly unconstitutional" and "politically motivated," designed to "push" abortion care out of reach and "exploit" people's health and lives for political gain. The lawsuit contends that the "serious health risk" exception is too narrow and will disproportionately "harm" black and low-income people, who make up a significant portion of those seeking abortions in the state. With over 350 abortion restrictions introduced this year, abortion rights advocates are fighting to protect access to safe and legal abortions. As Planned Parenthood's president Leana Wen stated, "We have no choice" but to fight through every channel to ensure patients' lives are protected.

END OF SUMMARY.
