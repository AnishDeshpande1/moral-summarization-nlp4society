SUMMARY:

Planned Parenthood and the ACLU have sued Alabama over its near-total abortion ban, which only allows abortions if the pregnancy poses a "serious health risk" to the mother, with no exceptions for rape or incest. The law is intentionally unconstitutional, designed to force abortion rights advocates to sue and potentially overturn Roe v. Wade. The lawsuit argues that the ban is medically unnecessary, politically motivated, and will disproportionately harm black and low-income people, who make up a significant portion of those seeking abortions in the state. This is part of a larger trend, with over 350 abortion restrictions introduced in state legislatures this year. Abortion remains legal in every state, but these laws aim to restrict access and ultimately overturn Roe v. Wade. The lawsuit is a necessary step to protect women's lives and reproductive rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
