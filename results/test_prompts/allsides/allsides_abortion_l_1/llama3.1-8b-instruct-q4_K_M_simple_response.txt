SUMMARY: Alabama Republicans have passed a near-total abortion ban that would only allow abortions if the pregnancy poses a "serious health risk" to the mother, with no exceptions for rape or incest. Abortion providers could face up to 99 years in prison for performing the procedure. The law is designed to be unconstitutionally severe, forcing abortion rights advocates to sue and potentially use as a vehicle to overturn Roe v. Wade. Planned Parenthood and the ACLU have filed a lawsuit against Alabama, arguing that the ban is unconstitutional and would disproportionately harm black and low-income people. According to the lawsuit, 60% of people who get abortions in Alabama are black, despite making up less than a third of the state's population. The lawsuit aims to block the ban from going into effect, highlighting the need for access to safe and legal abortion services.

END OF SUMMARY.
