**SUMMARY:**  
Planned Parenthood and the ACLU sued Alabama Friday over the state’s near‑total abortion ban, a law engineered by Republicans to force a court challenge and potentially overturn Roe v. Wade. The ban allows abortions only for a “serious health risk,” with no exceptions for rape or incest, and imposes up to 99‑year prison terms—an approach described as “blatantly unconstitutional” and “politically‑motivated.” ACLU attorney Alexa Kolbi‑Molinas said the extreme ban shows how far anti‑abortion legislators will go to push their agenda, exploiting women’s health for political gain. The lawsuit argues the “serious health risk” exception is so narrow it is impossible to meet and that the ban will disproportionately harm black and low‑income people, who already bear the brunt of abortion restrictions. Planned Parenthood’s Leana Wen insisted lawsuits are the only way to protect patients’ lives, fighting through “every channel…the courts, the legislatures, the ballot boxes.” Across the nation, similar restrictive bills are advancing, sparking protests.
