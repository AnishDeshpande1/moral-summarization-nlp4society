SUMMARY:

Planned Parenthood and the ACLU have sued Alabama over its near-total abortion ban, which would only allow abortions if a pregnancy poses a "serious health risk" to the mother. There are no exceptions for rape or incest, and providers could face up to 99 years in prison for performing the procedure. The lawsuit argues that the law is unconstitutionally severe and was designed by Republicans to force abortion rights advocates to sue, with the goal of overturning Roe v. Wade. The ACLU and Planned Parenthood claim that the law would disproportionately harm black and low-income people, who account for a majority of those seeking abortions in Alabama. The lawsuit was filed on behalf of Planned Parenthood Southeast, three abortion clinics in Alabama, and a doctor who performs abortions in the state.

END OF SUMMARY.
