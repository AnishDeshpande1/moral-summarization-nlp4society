SUMMARY:

Alabama's near-total abortion ban, which allows abortions only if the pregnancy poses a "serious health risk" to the mother, has been sued by Planned Parenthood and the ACLU. The law, which has no exceptions for rape or incest, could lead to 99 years in prison for abortion providers. Republicans engineered the law to be unconstitutionally severe, aiming to overturn Roe v. Wade. The ACLU argues that the law is "blatantly unconstitutional" and will disproportionately harm black and low-income people, who make up a majority of those seeking abortions in the state. The lawsuit is a necessary step to protect patients' lives and fight against the anti-abortion agenda. With over 350 abortion restrictions introduced in state legislatures this year, this legal battle is just the beginning.

END OF SUMMARY.
