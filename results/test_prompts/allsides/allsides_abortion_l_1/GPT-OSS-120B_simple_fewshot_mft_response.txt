SUMMARY:
Planned Parenthood and the ACLU filed a federal suit challenging Alabama’s near‑total abortion ban, a law deliberately crafted by Republican legislators to provoke a court fight that could overturn Roe v. Wade. The plaintiffs argue the ban subverts constitutional authority by letting politicians, emboldened by a Trump‑era anti‑abortion agenda, replace established legal protections with draconian penalties—up to 99 years in prison for providers. By allowing abortions only when a pregnancy poses an “extremely narrow” serious‑health risk, the statute ignores any exceptions for rape or incest, weaponizing state power against women’s autonomy. The lawsuit stresses the severe care‑related harm the ban would inflict, especially on Black and low‑income patients who already comprise the majority of those seeking abortions in Alabama; it would disproportionately deny them essential health care. Framed as a profound fairness issue, the challengers claim the law cheats the public by cloaking political motives in the guise of health policy, violating equal protection and due‑process rights. The case marks the first step in a broader battle against a wave of restrictive abortion measures sweeping the nation.  

END OF SUMMARY.
