SUMMARY:  
U.S. COVID‑19 cases surged by more than 47,000 in one day, the largest spike since the pandemic began, prompting Dr. Anthony Fauci to warn that daily increases could soon reach 100,000 unless the nation tamp down the virus. He stressed that the risk is national, not confined to California, Texas, Arizona or other emerging hot spots, and that the country is “not in total control.” The surge has strained ICU beds, especially in Texas and Arizona, and threatens to cost  lives and further inflict  economic damage, adding to the 126,000 deaths already recorded. Critics argue that President Donald Trump failed  to manage the crisis, citing “historic mismanagement” that cost  lives and inflicted  unnecessary hardship on the economy. In response, Democratic rival Joe Biden condemned the administration’s handling, while states like California, Texas and Florida have begun to close  bars and other venues to curb spread. The EU has also removed the U.S. from its “safe list,” tightening travel restrictions as the pandemic’s toll continues to rise.  

END OF SUMMARY.
