SUMMARY: The United States recorded a record daily increase of over 47,000 COVID‑19 cases on Tuesday, the largest one‑day spike since the pandemic began, according to Reuters. Dr. Anthony Fauci warned the surge could soon double to 100,000 new cases per day without a nationwide response, noting that California, Texas, Arizona, Florida and South Carolina are now epicenters and that ICU capacity is tightening, especially in Texas and Arizona. Over 126,000 Americans have died and the economy, already in recession, faces further decline. The EU removed the U.S. from its “safe list” for non‑essential travel. Politically, Democrats criticize President Trump’s handling of the crisis, while former Vice President Biden blames “historic mismanagement.” States such as California, Texas and Florida have begun re‑closing bars, and the Northeast added travelers from several hot‑spot states to quarantine requirements. Fauci cautioned that a vaccine may not be available until early next year. END OF SUMMARY.
