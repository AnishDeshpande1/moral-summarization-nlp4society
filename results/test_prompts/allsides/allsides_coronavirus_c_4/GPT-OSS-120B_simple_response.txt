SUMMARY:  
A Reuters tally shows a record surge of +47,000 U.S. COVID‑19 cases in one day, the steepest since the pandemic began, prompting Dr. Anthony Fauci to warn that daily infections could soon hit 100,000 without a national response. California, Texas, Arizona, Florida and South Carolina are now hotspots, with hospitals reporting ICU shortages and fears that Independence Day gatherings will worsen the spread. Fauci stressed that “we are not in total control” and urged collective action, warning that neglecting any region endangers the whole country.  

The spike has revived criticism of President Donald Trump’s pandemic handling; Democrats label it “historic mismanagement” that cost lives and deepened the economic crisis, while former Vice President Joe Biden blames Trump for unnecessary death and hardship. In contrast, the EU has removed the United States from its “safe list,” restricting travel. States are re‑imposing restrictions, closing bars and mandating 14‑day quarantines for travelers from high‑case states.  

The moral takeaway is clear: leadership that downplays the virus and fails to coordinate a unified, science‑based response bears responsibility for the renewed suffering, and urgent, collective action is required to protect public health and prevent further loss.  

END OF SUMMARY.
