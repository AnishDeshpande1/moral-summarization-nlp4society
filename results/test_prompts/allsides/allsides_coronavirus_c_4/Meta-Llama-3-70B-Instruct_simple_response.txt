SUMMARY:

The US has seen a record one-day spike in COVID-19 cases, with over 47,000 new cases reported on Tuesday. Dr. Anthony Fauci, the government's top infectious disease expert, warned that the number could soon double unless a nationwide effort is made to control the virus. He expressed concern that the country is not in control of the pandemic, and that the surge in cases puts the entire country at risk. The rise in cases has been particularly sharp in California, Texas, and Arizona, with hospital intensive care beds in short supply in some areas. The European Union has excluded Americans from its "safe list" of countries, and critics have blamed President Trump's "historic mismanagement" of the pandemic for the crisis. Trump's rival, Joe Biden, has unveiled an updated plan to tackle the pandemic, calling for more testing and contact tracing.

END OF SUMMARY.
