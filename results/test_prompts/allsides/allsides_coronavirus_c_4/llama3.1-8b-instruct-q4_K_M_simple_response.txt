SUMMARY:

A significant spike in new COVID-19 cases has been reported in the US, with over 47,000 cases recorded on Tuesday, the highest one-day total since the start of the pandemic. Dr. Anthony Fauci warned that this number could soon double unless a nationwide effort is made to control the virus. California, Texas, and Arizona have emerged as new hotspots, with record increases in cases reported. The rise in cases has put a strain on hospitals, with some areas experiencing shortages of intensive care beds. The European Union has excluded Americans from its "safe list" due to concerns over the spread of the virus.

Criticism of President Trump's handling of the pandemic is growing, with his rival Joe Biden accusing him of "historic mismanagement." Fauci expressed concern that the daily increase in new cases could reach 100,000 unless swift action is taken. The pandemic has already resulted in over 126,000 American deaths and millions of lost jobs.

END OF SUMMARY.
