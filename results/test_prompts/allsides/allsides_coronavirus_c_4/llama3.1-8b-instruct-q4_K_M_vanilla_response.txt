SUMMARY:

The US has seen its largest one-day spike in COVID-19 cases since the start of the pandemic, with over 47,000 new cases reported on Tuesday. Dr. Anthony Fauci warned that this number could soon double unless a nationwide push is made to control the virus. California, Texas, and Arizona have emerged as new hotspots, with record increases in cases. Fauci expressed concern about the situation, saying "we can't just focus on those areas that are having the surge. It puts the entire country at risk." The EU has also excluded Americans from its "safe list" of countries for non-essential travel due to the rising cases.

Criticism is mounting against President Donald Trump's handling of the pandemic, with his rival Joe Biden saying he "failed us". California, Texas, and Florida have closed bars recently reopened, while other states have added travelers from these areas to their self-quarantine list. Hospitals in Texas are reporting a significant increase in COVID-19 patients, with one hospital CEO expressing concern about Independence Day celebrations.

END OF SUMMARY.
