SUMMARY:

The COVID-19 pandemic continues to surge in the US, with over 47,000 new cases reported on Tuesday, the largest one-day spike since the start of the pandemic. Dr. Anthony Fauci warned that this number could soon double unless a nationwide effort is made to contain the virus. California, Texas, and Arizona have emerged as hotspots, with hospital intensive care beds in short supply. The European Union has excluded Americans from its "safe list" due to the rising cases and hospitalizations. President Trump's handling of the pandemic has been criticized by his rival Joe Biden, who stated that Trump's "historic mismanagement" cost lives and inflicted unnecessary economic damage.

Fauci emphasized the need for a nationwide push to control the virus, as the current spike puts the entire country at risk. The lack of control is evident in the record increases in cases in several states, including Texas and Florida, where hospital beds are quickly filling up with COVID-19 patients. Fauci's concerns about the potential spread of the virus during Independence Day celebrations highlight the urgent need for action to contain the pandemic.

END OF SUMMARY.
