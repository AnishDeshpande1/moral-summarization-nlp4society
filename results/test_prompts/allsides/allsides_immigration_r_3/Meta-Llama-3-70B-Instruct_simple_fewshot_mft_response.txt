SUMMARY:

The New York City Commission on Human Rights has declared that using the term "illegal alien" to describe an undocumented person can violate laws protecting employees and tenants from discrimination, with fines up to $250,000. However, this broad interpretation may be unconstitutional under the First Amendment. The guidance notes that even a single comment can constitute harassment, which raises concerns about the limits of free speech. Critics argue that the government cannot simply prohibit politically incorrect statements about undocumented people and must limit the scope of anti-discrimination mandates to satisfy broad free speech guarantees. This overreach undermines authority, causes harm to individuals, and is unfair to those who may hold differing opinions. The city's interpretation must be balanced with the need to protect individuals from discrimination while also upholding the principles of free speech.

END OF SUMMARY.
