SUMMARY:
New York City's Commission on Human Rights has declared that using the term "illegal alien" pejoratively to describe an undocumented person violates anti-discrimination laws, potentially resulting in fines of up to $250,000. However, this interpretation may be unconstitutional under the First Amendment. The guidance notes that even a single comment can constitute harassment, which could push the limits of free speech. To survive a First Amendment test, government decrees must come with limiting conditions. The Supreme Court requires conduct to be severe or pervasive enough to make the work environment hostile before it is considered harassment. Eliminating this standard may not adequately protect speech. The government cannot simply prohibit people from making politically incorrect statements about undocumented individuals; instead, it must limit the scope of anti-discrimination mandates.

END OF SUMMARY.
