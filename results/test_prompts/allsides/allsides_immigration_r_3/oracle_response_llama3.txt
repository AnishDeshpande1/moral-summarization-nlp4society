SUMMARY:

New York City's Commission on Human Rights declared that using the term "illegal alien" pejoratively violates laws designed to protect employees and tenants from discrimination, potentially resulting in fines of up to $250,000. However, this interpretation may be unconstitutional under the First Amendment. The guidance notes that even a single comment made with intent to demean, humiliate, or harass can constitute harassment. This approach eliminates the "severe or pervasive" standard found in federal law, which is alarming. The government cannot simply prohibit people from making certain statements about undocumented individuals; it must limit the scope of anti-discrimination mandates to satisfy the broad free speech guarantees enjoyed by all people.

END OF SUMMARY.
