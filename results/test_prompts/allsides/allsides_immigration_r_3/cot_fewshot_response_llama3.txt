SUMMARY:
The New York City Commission on Human Rights has declared that using the term "illegal alien" pejoratively to describe an undocumented person violates laws designed to protect employees and tenants from discrimination. The commission's guidance states that using such language with intent to demean, humiliate, or harass a person is illegal under the law. However, some argue that this interpretation may be unconstitutional under the First Amendment, as it could limit free speech rights of employers and landlords. The guidance notes that even a single comment made in circumstances where it signals discriminatory views about one's immigration status or national origin can constitute harassment. Critics argue that government decrees to prohibit free speech must come with certain limiting conditions to survive a First Amendment test.
