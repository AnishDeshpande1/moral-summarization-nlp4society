SUMMARY:

New York City's Commission on Human Rights has issued guidance that using the term "illegal alien" pejoratively to describe an undocumented person may result in fines of up to $250,000. The city's interpretation of the law is broad and some argue it could be unconstitutional under the First Amendment. The guidance applies to workplace harassment, tenants' rights, and public accommodation, but not to casual conversations or threats made on the street. Critics argue that the guidance eliminates a crucial limit on liability by removing the requirement for "severe or pervasive" behavior before it is considered harassment. This could allow for lawsuits over single comments made in certain circumstances. Some experts warn that this may push the guidance into unconstitutional territory, as government decrees prohibiting free speech must come with limiting conditions to survive a First Amendment test.

END OF SUMMARY.
