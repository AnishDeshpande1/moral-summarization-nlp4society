New York City's Commission on Human Rights has declared that using the term "illegal alien" pejoratively to describe an undocumented person could result in fines of up to $250,000. The city's interpretation of the law may be too broad and unconstitutional under the First Amendment. The guidance notes that even a single comment made with discriminatory views about immigration status or national origin can constitute harassment. Critics argue that this is a problem because government decrees must come with limiting conditions to survive a First Amendment test. Attorney Hans Bader writes that the Supreme Court requires conduct to be severe or pervasive enough to make the work environment hostile before it's considered harassment under federal law. New York City has eliminated this limit, raising concerns about free speech guarantees enjoyed by all people.
