SUMMARY:

New York City's Commission on Human Rights has declared that using the term "illegal alien" pejoratively to describe an undocumented person violates anti-discrimination laws, potentially resulting in fines of up to $250,000. The guidance notes that even a single comment signaling discriminatory views about immigration status or national origin can constitute harassment. This move raises concerns about the limits on free speech and whether it may be unconstitutional under the First Amendment. The government must balance anti-discrimination with free speech protections by imposing certain limiting conditions for laws to survive a First Amendment test.

END OF SUMMARY.
