SUMMARY:

The New York City Commission on Human Rights has declared that using the term "illegal alien" to describe an undocumented person can violate laws protecting employees and tenants from discrimination, with fines of up to $250,000. However, the city's interpretation of the law may be unconstitutional under the First Amendment. The guidance notes that even a single comment made by an employer can constitute harassment, which may push the guidance into unconstitutional territory. Critics argue that the government cannot simply prohibit people from making politically incorrect statements about undocumented people and must limit the scope of anti-discrimination mandates to satisfy broad free speech guarantees. The courts have held that anti-discrimination ordinances can survive scrutiny, but NYC's guidance may go too far. The issue raises concerns about the balance between protecting individuals from discrimination and preserving free speech.

END OF SUMMARY.
