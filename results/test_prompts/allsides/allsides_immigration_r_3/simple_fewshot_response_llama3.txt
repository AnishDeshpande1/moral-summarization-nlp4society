SUMMARY:

New York City's Commission on Human Rights has declared that using the term "illegal alien" pejoratively to describe an undocumented person violates anti-discrimination laws, potentially resulting in fines of up to $250,000. However, this interpretation may be unconstitutional under the First Amendment. The guidance notes that even a single comment signaling discriminatory views about one's immigration status or national origin can constitute harassment. This raises concerns about limiting free speech and could push the guidance into unconstitutional territory. Prohibiting certain political statements without sufficient limits on liability is alarming. The government cannot simply ban making politically incorrect statements about undocumented people; instead, it must limit the scope of anti-discrimination mandates to satisfy broad free speech guarantees.

END OF SUMMARY.
