SUMMARY:  
New York City’s Commission on Human Rights has ruled that using the phrase “illegal alien” in a demeaning way toward undocumented individuals violates the city’s Human Rights Law and can carry fines up to $250,000. The guidance targets harassment in workplaces, housing and public accommodations, but does not criminalize casual street remarks or threats to call ICE. Critics argue the commission’s expansive definition—punishing even a single disparaging comment—oversteps constitutional limits, eliminating the “severe or pervasive” threshold that the Supreme Court requires to protect speech. Attorney Hans Bader warns that stripping this safeguard threatens free‑speech rights, asserting that anti‑discrimination rules must be narrowly drawn to survive First‑Amendment scrutiny. The moral tension highlighted is between protecting vulnerable groups from harassment and preserving the fundamental right to free expression, with the city’s approach seen as potentially overreaching.  

END OF SUMMARY.
