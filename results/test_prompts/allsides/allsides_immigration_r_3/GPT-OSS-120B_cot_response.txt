**SUMMARY:**  
Last week New York City’s Commission on Human Rights ruled that using the term “illegal alien” **pejoratively** to describe an undocumented person—when intended to **demean**, **humiliate**, or **harass**—violates the city’s Human Rights Law and can trigger fines up to $250,000. While the guidance limits its reach to workplace, housing, and public‑accommodation contexts, it expands liability so that even a single disparaging comment may constitute **harassment**. Critics argue this **problem** pushes the rule into **unconstitutional** territory because it discards the federal “**severe** or **pervasive**” standard that protects **free speech**. Attorney Hans Bader warns that the city’s approach is **alarming**, removing the modest limit that ensures conduct is truly **hostile** before penalties apply. The government cannot simply ban **politically incorrect** statements about undocumented people without narrowly tailoring anti‑**discrimination** mandates to satisfy the First Amendment.
