SUMMARY:

The New York City Commission on Human Rights has declared that using the term "illegal alien" to describe an undocumented person in a derogatory manner is illegal and can result in fines of up to $250,000. While the law aims to protect employees and tenants from discrimination, its broad interpretation may be unconstitutional under the First Amendment. The guidance notes that even a single comment made by an employer can be considered harassment, which raises concerns about the limitation of free speech. Critics argue that the government cannot simply prohibit people from making politically incorrect statements and must limit the scope of anti-discrimination mandates to satisfy broad free speech guarantees.

The moral framing of this issue emphasizes the importance of protecting individuals from discrimination and harassment, while also upholding the fundamental right to free speech. The government must strike a balance between these competing values to ensure that its anti-discrimination efforts do not infringe upon the rights of others.
