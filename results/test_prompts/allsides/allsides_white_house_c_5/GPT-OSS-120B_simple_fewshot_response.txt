SUMMARY:
Attorney General Eric Holder announced that two U.S. attorneys—Ronald C. Machen Jr. (D.C.) and Rod J. Rosenstein (Maryland)—will lead criminal investigations into recent unauthorized disclosures of classified material, including a secret terrorist “kill list” and details of an al‑Qaida infiltration operation. Holder warned that such leaks jeopardize national security and will not be tolerated, and he empowered the prosecutors to work with intelligence officials and pursue any violations uncovered. President Obama rebuked accusations that his administration deliberately released classified information, calling the suggestion “offensive” and “wrong.” The leaks have spurred bipartisan concern on the Senate Intelligence Committee: Republican Vice‑Chair Saxby Chambliss called for a special counsel, while Democrat Chair Dianne Feinstein hesitated, noting the time‑consuming nature of a special prosecutor and suggesting legislative reforms to expand inspectors‑general powers. Senator John McCain also urged the appointment of a special counsel, alleging the leaks may have been politically motivated to aid the President’s election prospects. The episode underscores a moral imperative for rigorous accountability to protect the country’s security while ensuring transparent, lawful oversight of any breaches.  
END OF SUMMARY.
