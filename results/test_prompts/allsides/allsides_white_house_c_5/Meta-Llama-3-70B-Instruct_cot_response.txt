Attorney General Eric Holder has assigned two US attorneys to investigate possible leaks of classified information, which can "compromise the security of this country and all Americans" and will not be "tolerated". President Obama struck back at critics who accused the White House of leaking classified material for political gain, calling the notion "offensive" and "wrong". The Congressional Intelligence Committee has united in their concern over the leaks, with some members calling for a special counsel to investigate. Senator John McCain suggested that some of the leaks may have been calculated to boost the election prospects of President Barack Obama, which the White House denies. The investigation aims to prosecute criminal violations and follow all appropriate leads to determine the source of the leaks, which are considered "gravely serious breaches of our national security".
