SUMMARY:

Attorney General Eric Holder has assigned two US attorneys to investigate possible leaks of classified information, including details about a secret terrorist kill list and an intelligence operation that infiltrated al-Qaida in the Arabian Peninsula. Holder emphasized that leaking such information "can compromise the security of this country and all Americans" and will not be tolerated. President Obama also denied accusations that his administration leaked classified material for political gain, calling it "offensive" and "wrong".

The investigation comes amid calls from lawmakers to investigate the leaks, with some suggesting a special counsel or legislation to give inspectors general more investigatory authority. Senator John McCain has called on Obama to appoint a special counsel to fully investigate the breaches of national security.

Holder's statement reflects a commitment to upholding national security and protecting Americans from potential harm caused by such leaks. The investigation aims to uncover those responsible for leaking sensitive information and hold them accountable.

END OF SUMMARY.
