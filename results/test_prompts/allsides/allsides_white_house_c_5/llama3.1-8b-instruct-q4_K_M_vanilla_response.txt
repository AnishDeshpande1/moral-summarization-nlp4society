SUMMARY:
Attorney General Eric Holder assigned two US attorneys to investigate possible leaks of classified information, including details about a secret terrorist kill list and an intelligence operation that infiltrated al-Qaida in the Arabian Peninsula. President Obama denied allegations that his White House intentionally leaked classified material for political gain, calling it "offensive" and "wrong". The leak investigation is part of a growing controversy surrounding national security breaches, with some lawmakers calling for a special counsel to lead the probe.

Senator John McCain has suggested that some leaks may have been calculated to boost Obama's election prospects, but White House officials deny this. Senator Dianne Feinstein proposed legislation to give inspectors general more investigatory authority, citing the need for swift action in light of the allegations. Holder emphasized that leaking classified information can compromise national security and will not be tolerated.

END OF SUMMARY.
