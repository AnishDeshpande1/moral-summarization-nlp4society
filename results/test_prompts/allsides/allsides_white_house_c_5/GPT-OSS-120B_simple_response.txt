SUMMARY: Attorney General Eric Holder announced the appointment of two U.S. attorneys—Ron C. Machen Jr. (D.C.) and Rod J. Rosenstein (Maryland)—to lead criminal investigations into recent unauthorized disclosures of classified national‑security material, including a secret terrorist “kill list” and details of a covert al‑Qaida operation. Holder stressed that leaking such information endangers the country and “will not be tolerated.” President Obama rebuked accusations that the White House deliberately released secrets, calling the notion “offensive” and “wrong.” Congressional leaders expressed alarm: Republican Sen. Saxby Chambliss urged a special counsel, while Democratic Sen. Dianne Feinstein hesitated, noting the urgency and proposing stronger inspector‑general powers. Sen. John McCain accused the White House of possibly using leaks to aid President Obama’s election prospects and demanded an immediate special counsel to investigate and prosecute these “gravely serious breaches” of national security. The moral framing underscores a collective duty to protect classified information and hold accountable any who compromise it. END OF SUMMARY.
