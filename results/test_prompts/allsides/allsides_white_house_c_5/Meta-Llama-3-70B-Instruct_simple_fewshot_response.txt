SUMMARY:

Attorney General Eric Holder has assigned two prosecutors to investigate possible leaks of classified information, including details about a secret terrorist kill list and an intelligence operation that infiltrated al-Qaida. The move comes after intense criticism of the White House, with some accusing it of leaking sensitive information for political gain. President Obama has denied the allegations, calling them "offensive" and "wrong." The Congressional Intelligence Committee has also expressed concern over the leaks, with some members calling for a special counsel to investigate. The investigation is crucial, as the leaking of classified information can compromise national security and put Americans at risk. The White House has denied any involvement in the leaks, but the investigation will aim to uncover the source of the disclosures and hold those responsible accountable.

END OF SUMMARY.
