SUMMARY: Attorney General Eric Holder announced that U.S. Attorneys Ronald C. Machen Jr. (District of Columbia) and Rod J. Rosenstein (District of Maryland) will lead criminal investigations into recent possible leaks of classified national‑security information, including a secret terrorist kill list and details of an al‑Qaida infiltration operation. Holder emphasized that the prosecutors are authorized to pursue criminal violations, coordinate with the intelligence community, and follow leads across government branches. President Obama denied White House involvement in the leaks, calling the accusations offensive. Congressional leaders expressed concern: Republican Sen. Saxby Chambliss called for a special counsel, while Democratic Sen. Dianne Feinstein hesitated, noting the time‑consuming nature of a special prosecutor and suggesting legislative reforms to empower inspectors general. Sen. John McCain also urged the appointment of a special counsel, alleging the leaks may have been politically motivated to aid President Obama’s election prospects. Updates noted ongoing debate over investigative approaches and the urgency of addressing the breaches. END OF SUMMARY.
