SUMMARY:

Attorney General Eric Holder launched a probe into the leaking of classified information, assigning two U.S. attorneys to investigate possible instances of unauthorized disclosures. The investigation aims to uncover the sources behind leaks that compromised national security and put Americans at risk. President Obama denied any involvement in releasing sensitive information for political gain, calling it "offensive" and "wrong." Holder emphasized that leaking classified information can compromise the security of the country and will not be tolerated.

The move comes as lawmakers called for a special counsel to investigate the matter, with Senator John McCain suggesting that some leaks may have been calculated to boost Obama's election prospects. Senator Dianne Feinstein proposed legislation to give inspectors general more authority to investigate breaches of national security. Holder authorized U.S. Attorneys Machen and Rosenstein to prosecute any violations discovered during their investigation.

The White House has denied leaking classified material, but the controversy highlights concerns over the unauthorized release of sensitive information. The investigation will aim to uncover the truth behind these leaks and ensure that those responsible are held accountable for their actions. END OF SUMMARY.
