SUMMARY: Attorney General Eric Holder has assigned two prosecutors to investigate possible leaks of classified information, a move that comes after intensified calls for a probe into how sensitive national security details were leaked to the media. The leaking of classified information "can compromise the security of this country and all Americans, and it will not be tolerated," Holder said. President Obama also struck back at critics who accused the White House of leaking classified material for political gain, calling the notion "offensive" and "wrong." The Congressional Intelligence Committee has also expressed concern over the leaks, with some members calling for a special counsel to investigate. The investigation will aim to prosecute criminal violations and follow all appropriate leads within the Executive and Legislative branches of government. The leaking of classified information is a serious breach of national security, and the government is taking steps to prevent further unauthorized disclosures. END OF SUMMARY.
