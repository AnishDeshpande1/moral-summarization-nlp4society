SUMMARY:
Attorney General Eric Holder announced that two senior U.S. attorneys—Ronald C. Machen Jr. and Rod J. Rosenstein—will lead criminal probes into recent unauthorized disclosures of classified material, including a secret terrorist “kill list” and an al‑Qaida infiltration operation. Holder stressed that such leaks endanger national security and will not be tolerated, invoking the authority of the Justice Department to pursue violations and to coordinate with intelligence agencies. President Obama rebuked accusations that the White House deliberately released the information, defending the administration’s authority and condemning the notion as “offensive.” Meanwhile, bipartisan pressure mounted in Congress: Republican Sen. Saxby Chambliss called for a special counsel, while Democratic Sen. Dianne Feinstein hesitated, citing the need for swift action, and Sen. John McCain warned that the leaks could have been used to aid the President’s election prospects, demanding accountability and suggesting that those responsible be prosecuted. The moral framing emphasizes respect for legitimate authority, the need to protect citizens from harm caused by leaks, and the demand for fair, impartial justice against those who betray national security for partisan gain.  
END OF SUMMARY.
