SUMMARY: A teenage boy, Dimitrios Pagourtzis, 17, went on a rampage at a Houston-area high school, fatally shooting eight students and two teachers. The attack was allegedly driven by his rejection by one of the victims, Shana Fisher, 16, who had embarrassed him in class after he made aggressive advances towards her. Fisher was the first to be killed in the massacre, which is the fourth-deadliest mass shooting at a U.S. public school in modern history. Pagourtzis confessed to the killings after being taken into custody, but authorities have offered no motive yet. The community is grieving, with many attending vigils and emotional services to mourn the victims. The shooting has reignited the national debate over gun ownership, with some calling for stricter gun control laws and others advocating for increased security measures in schools. Pagourtzis' family is cooperating with authorities and has expressed sadness and dismay over the events. END OF SUMMARY.
