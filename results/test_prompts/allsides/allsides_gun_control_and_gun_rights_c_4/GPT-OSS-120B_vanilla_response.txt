SUMMARY: A 17‑year‑old student, Dimitrios Pagourtzis, opened fire at Santa Fe High School in Texas, killing eight students and two teachers. According to a Los Angeles Times report quoting the mother of victim Shana Fisher, Pagourtzis had made persistent romantic advances toward Fisher for four months, which she rejected; she later embarrassed him in class, after which he allegedly shot her and others. Police say Pagourtzis confessed but have not confirmed a motive. The school district denied claims that Pagourtzis was bullied. He used a pistol and shotgun, reportedly obtained from his father. Authorities have not released further details, and his attorney cited his emotional state as a barrier to information. Governor Greg Abbott noted the weapons were likely legally acquired and suggested Pagourtzis considered suicide but lacked courage. The shooting, the fourth‑deadliest at a U.S. public school, reignited national gun‑control debates, prompting responses from the NRA and community vigils. The suspect’s family expressed shock and cooperation with investigators. END OF SUMMARY.
