SUMMARY: A teenage boy opened fire in a Texas high‑school shooting, killing eight students and two teachers in a rampage that left the community grieving. The mother of Shana Fisher, one of the victims, said her daughter had rejected the shooter’s aggressive advances and embarrassed him in class before he began the massacre a week later. Authorities have not confirmed a motive, but the suspect confessed to the killings and is now in jail. The school district denied claims that the attacker had been bullied, describing him as a quiet loner. In the aftermath, churches and mosques held vigils, with mourners holding candles, embracing families, and attending services to honor those who died. State officials noted the guns were likely obtained legally from the suspect’s father and mentioned the shooter’s journals hinted at a desire for suicide, though he lacked the courage to act. The governor, the NRA, and community leaders called for ways to keep kids protected without limiting gun rights. The shooter’s family expressed that they were saddened and dismayed by the attack. END OF SUMMARY.
