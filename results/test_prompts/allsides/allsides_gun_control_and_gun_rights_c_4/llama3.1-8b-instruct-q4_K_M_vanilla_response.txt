SUMMARY:

A high school shooting in Santa Fe, Texas, left 10 people dead, including eight students and two teachers. Dimitrios Pagourtzis, 17, is charged with the murders after confessing to authorities. According to reports, Pagourtzis had made aggressive advances towards one of the victims, Shana Fisher, who rejected him four months prior to the shooting. Her mother stated that her daughter's rejection may have been a motive for the attack.

The incident is the fourth-deadliest mass shooting at a US public school in modern history. The suspect's family has denied any involvement and cooperated with authorities. Texas Governor Greg Abbott said Pagourtzis obtained firearms from his father, who likely acquired them legally.

The shooting has sparked debate over gun ownership and safety measures in schools. The National Rifle Association (NRA) has proposed installing metal detectors in schools and providing free security assessments to schools. The tragedy has also led to a show of support for the community, with many businesses displaying "Santa Fe strong" signs and vigils being held for the victims.

END OF SUMMARY.
