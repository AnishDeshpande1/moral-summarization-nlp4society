A tragic shooting incident occurred at Santa Fe High School in Texas, leaving 10 people dead, including eight students and two teachers. According to his victim's mother, Sadie Rodriguez, Pagourtzis had been spurned by one of his victims after making aggressive advances, which ultimately led to the massacre. This incident highlights the dangers of unchecked aggression and the importance of addressing mental health issues.

The National Rifle Association (NRA) has responded to the shooting, with its President Oliver North advocating for metal detectors in schools and more security assessments, rather than limiting Second Amendment rights. However, critics argue that this approach is inadequate, given the ease with which Pagourtzis obtained his firearms from his father. The incident raises questions about the role of parents and authorities in preventing such tragedies.

The shooting has sparked a renewed debate on gun control and school safety, with many calling for stricter measures to prevent similar incidents in the future. The tragedy is a stark reminder of the importance of prioritizing care and harm prevention over authority and power dynamics.
