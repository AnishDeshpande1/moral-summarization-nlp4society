SUMMARY:  
The Reuters report links the Santa Fe, Texas, school massacre to a personal grievance: the shooter, 17‑year‑old Dimitrios Pagourtzis, had allegedly pursued 16‑year‑old Shana Fisher with aggressive advances for four months. Fisher’s mother told the Los Angeles Times that her daughter finally rebuffed him in class, humiliating him, and a week later he opened fire on “everyone he didn’t like,” starting with Fisher. While officials have not confirmed a motive, the story frames the tragedy as a violent retaliation to romantic rejection, echoing a recent Maryland shooting where a former boyfriend killed his ex‑girlfriend. The school denied claims that Pagourtzis was bullied, describing him as a quiet football player. The community responded with vigils, church and mosque services, and calls for safety measures. Politicians and the NRA debated gun‑rights versus school security, while the governor noted the weapons came from the suspect’s father and that the shooter may have contemplated suicide. The moral framing presented by the mother suggests that personal rejection can precipitate horrific, indiscriminate violence.  

END OF SUMMARY.
