SUMMARY:
The White House is preparing an emergency funding request of $50‑$60 billion to address the massive damage caused by Superstorm Sandy, underscoring the federal government’s responsibility to act decisively for the suffering citizens. Officials highlighted the urgent need for aid—over 300,000 New York homes damaged, 265,000 businesses affected, and thousands still homeless—and warned that a modest “down payment” would only prolong hardship for families and communities. HUD Secretary Shaun Donovan stressed that the aid must not only rebuild what was lost but also strengthen the region’s defenses, calling for smarter, safer reconstruction and resilient infrastructure such as underground power lines where feasible. FEMA chief Craig Fugate noted the limited $4.8 billion left in the disaster relief fund and the importance of immediate, comprehensive assistance rather than budget cuts elsewhere. While Democrats broadly support the full request, it remains uncertain whether Republicans will endorse the authority‑driven, care‑focused plan to fully fund recovery and future protection.  
END OF SUMMARY.
