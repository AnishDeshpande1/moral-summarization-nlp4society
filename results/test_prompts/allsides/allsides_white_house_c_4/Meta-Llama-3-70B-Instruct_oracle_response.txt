SUMMARY:

The White House is seeking emergency funding of $50-60 billion to address the devastating aftermath of Hurricane Sandy, which caused enormous damage and widespread devastation. The damage tally in New York alone is estimated to be $32.8 billion, with over 300,000 homes seriously damaged, 265,000 businesses impacted, and thousands of people still homeless. The Obama administration wants to ensure that homes are rebuilt, businesses restored, and communities made whole, but also wants to build back stronger, smarter, and more resiliently to prevent future devastation. The funding will also go towards preventing similar damage in the future by making decisions on design, materials, and infrastructure. Providing only a small amount of aid now would cause hardships for families and communities, and the administration believes that disaster aid should not be offset by cuts elsewhere in the budget.

END OF SUMMARY.
