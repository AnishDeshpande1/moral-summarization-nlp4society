SUMMARY:
The White House will ask Congress for an emergency Sandy funding package of $50‑$60 billion, aiming to provide immediate aid and long‑term assistance to the devastated Northeast. Senators report that New York alone faces over $32.8 billion in damages, with 300,000 homes damaged, 265,000 businesses impacted and thousands still homeless. New Jersey and Connecticut add to a regional request of about $80 billion. HUD Secretary Shaun Donovan says the money must both rebuild homes and businesses and ensure future resilience—building stronger, smarter, safer infrastructure, from boardwalk design to generator‑capable gas stations and possibly burying power lines. FEMA chief Craig Fugate warns that a small down payment from the $4.8 billion disaster fund would create hardships for families awaiting assistance, delaying restoration and reconstruction. Democrats argue the aid should not be offset by cuts elsewhere, while Republicans remain undecided. The administration stresses that timely, sufficient assistance is essential to prevent further devastation and to help communities fully recover.  
END OF SUMMARY.
