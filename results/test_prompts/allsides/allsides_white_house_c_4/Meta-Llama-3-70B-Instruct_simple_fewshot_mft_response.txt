SUMMARY:

The White House is set to request $50-60 billion in emergency funding to address the devastating aftermath of Hurricane Sandy, with a focus on not only rebuilding but also preventing future devastation. The damage is staggering, with over 300,000 homes seriously damaged and 265,000 businesses impacted in New York alone. Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan emphasized the need to "build back stronger, smarter, safer, and more resiliently" to ensure that communities are protected from future superstorms. The administration is committed to providing adequate resources to support affected families and businesses, rather than offering a "down payment" that would only prolong their suffering. This approach prioritizes the care and well-being of those affected, rather than subverting their needs to budgetary concerns. By taking a proactive and responsible approach, the government can demonstrate its authority and commitment to protecting its citizens.

END OF SUMMARY.
