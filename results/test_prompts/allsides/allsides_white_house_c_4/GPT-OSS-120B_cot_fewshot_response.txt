**SUMMARY:**
The White House will request an **emergency** funding package of $50‑$60 billion to cover the massive **damages** from Superstorm Sandy, which left hundreds of thousands of homes **devastated**, thousands **homeless**, and countless **families**, **businesses**, and **communities** waiting for relief. Senators from the affected Northeastern states estimate a **conservative** need of about $80 billion. HUD Secretary Shaun Donovan says a portion of the aid must be used to rebuild **stronger**, **smarter**, and **safer**, creating a **resiliently** designed region— from upgraded boardwalks to underground **power** lines. FEMA chief Craig Fugate warns that a simple **down payment** would create **hardships**, forcing those affected to wait months for assistance. Donovan also argues the aid should not be **offset** by **cuts** elsewhere in the budget. The administration seeks swift, comprehensive help rather than piecemeal relief. **END OF SUMMARY.**
