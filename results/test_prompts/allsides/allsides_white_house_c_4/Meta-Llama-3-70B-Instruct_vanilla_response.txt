SUMMARY:

The White House is set to request $50-60 billion in emergency funding from Congress to aid in the recovery from Hurricane Sandy. The storm caused widespread devastation, with New York alone estimating damages of $32.8 billion and over 300,000 homes seriously damaged. The administration wants to use some of the funds to prevent similar devastation in the future, by building up the region's defenses against superstorms. Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan emphasized the need to rebuild "stronger, smarter, safer and more resiliently" and make decisions now on how to rebuild. The administration opposes providing a small amount of funding now and more later, saying it would cause hardships for families and communities waiting to rebuild. Democrats agree, but it's unclear if Republicans will support the request without offsetting cuts elsewhere in the budget.

END OF SUMMARY.
