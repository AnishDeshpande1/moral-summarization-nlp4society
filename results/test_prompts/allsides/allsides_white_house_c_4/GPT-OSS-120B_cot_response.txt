**SUMMARY:**  
The White House will ask Congress for an emergency funding request of $50‑$60 billion after Hurricane Sandy left massive **damage** and **devastation** across the Northeast. Senators reported over 300,000 **damaged** homes, thousands of **homeless** families, and billions in losses, with New York alone estimating a **conservative** $32.8 billion in damages. HUD Secretary Shaun Donovan said the aid must not only rebuild homes and businesses but also make communities **smarter**, **safer**, and more **resilient** for future storms. He warned that a small **down‑payment** now would create **hardship**, forcing families and businesses to wait months for full recovery. While the administration has $4.8 billion left in its disaster relief fund, lawmakers debate how much to allocate now versus later, and whether to offset the aid with other **cuts**. The debate centers on balancing immediate relief with long‑term protection against another superstorm.
