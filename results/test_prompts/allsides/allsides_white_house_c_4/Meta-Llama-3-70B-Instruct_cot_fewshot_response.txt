SUMMARY:
The White House is seeking emergency funding of $50 billion to $60 billion to address the devastating damages caused by Hurricane Sandy. The storm has left over 300,000 homes seriously damaged, 265,000 businesses impacted, and thousands of people homeless in New York alone, with estimated damages of $32.8 billion. Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan wants to ensure that homes are rebuilt, businesses restored, and communities made whole, but also built back stronger, smarter, and safer to prevent future devastation. The administration plans to use the funds to build resilience, including decisions on design, materials, and infrastructure. However, FEMA head Craig Fugate warns that a down payment would cause hardships for families and communities waiting to rebuild. Most Democrats agree that disaster aid should not be offset by cuts elsewhere in the budget, but it's unclear whether Republicans will support this approach. END OF SUMMARY.
