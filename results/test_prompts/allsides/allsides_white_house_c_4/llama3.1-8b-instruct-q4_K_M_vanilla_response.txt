SUMMARY:

The White House plans to request $50-60 billion in emergency funding from Congress to aid in the recovery from Hurricane Sandy. The storm caused widespread damage, with estimates suggesting that New York alone has suffered $32.8 billion in damages. The Northeastern states are seeking a total of $80 billion in federal aid. Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan stated that at least part of the funding will be used to build up defenses against future superstorms.

Donovan emphasized the importance of making decisions now about rebuilding, including design and materials for boardwalks and power lines. FEMA Director Craig Fugate warned that burying power lines underground may not be the best solution in all areas, citing problems it caused in New York City. Some lawmakers propose providing a "down payment" on rebuilding funds, but Donovan argued that this would cause undue hardship to families and communities.

The funding request is expected to be sent to Congress this week, amidst other pressing issues such as year-end tax increases and spending cuts.

END OF SUMMARY.
