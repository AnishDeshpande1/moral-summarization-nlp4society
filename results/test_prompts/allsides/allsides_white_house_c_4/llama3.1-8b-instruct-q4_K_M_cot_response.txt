The White House is set to request an emergency funding bill of $50 billion to $60 billion in the aftermath of Hurricane Sandy. The damage was widespread and caused devastating effects, with over 300,000 homes seriously damaged and thousands of New Yorkers still homeless. The Northeastern states are asking for about $80 billion in federal aid, which will be used not only to rebuild but also to build back stronger and more resiliently. Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan emphasized the need for a 21st-century response, making decisions now that will ensure communities are made whole again. However, some lawmakers want to provide just a down payment on the rebuilding money, which would cause hardships for families and small businesses waiting for a decision about what resources are available to help them rebuild their lives.
