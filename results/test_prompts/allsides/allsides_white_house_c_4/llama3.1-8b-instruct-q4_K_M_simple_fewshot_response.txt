SUMMARY:

In the aftermath of Hurricane Sandy, the White House is seeking emergency funding from Congress to help repair damages in the Northeastern states. Top administrators have requested $50 billion to $60 billion in aid, with some of that money going towards preventing similar devastation in the future. The damage from Superstorm Sandy was extensive, with over 300,000 homes seriously damaged and thousands of people still homeless. Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan emphasized the need for long-term rebuilding efforts, including designing and building communities to be more resilient against future storms. Some lawmakers want to provide a smaller amount of funding now and more later, but Donovan argues that this would cause hardships for families and small businesses waiting to rebuild. Democrats generally agree with this stance, but it is unclear whether Republicans will support the request.

END OF SUMMARY.
