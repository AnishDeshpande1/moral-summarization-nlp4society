SUMMARY:

The White House is set to request $50-60 billion in emergency funding to address the devastating impact of Hurricane Sandy. The storm caused widespread damage, with New York, New Jersey, and Connecticut seeking a combined $80 billion in federal aid. The administration plans to allocate at least some of the funds towards preventing future devastation by building up the region's defenses against superstorms. Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan emphasized the need to rebuild "stronger, smarter, safer, and more resiliently" with a 21st-century approach. Lawmakers are considering providing a down payment on the funding, but Donovan warned that this would cause hardships for families and communities waiting to rebuild. Democrats generally agree that disaster aid should not be offset by cuts elsewhere in the budget, but Republican support is uncertain.

END OF SUMMARY.
