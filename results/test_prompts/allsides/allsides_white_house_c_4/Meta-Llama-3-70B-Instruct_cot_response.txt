SUMMARY:
The White House is seeking emergency funding of $50-60 billion to address the enormous damage caused by Hurricane Sandy, with a focus on preventing future devastation. The storm left over 300,000 homes seriously damaged, 265,000 businesses impacted, and thousands of people homeless, with estimated damages of $32.8 billion in New York alone. The administration wants to ensure that homes are rebuilt, businesses restored, and communities made whole, but also "build back stronger, smarter, safer and more resiliently" to prepare for future superstorms. Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan emphasized the need for a comprehensive approach, including decisions on design, materials, and infrastructure. Providing a "down payment" of funds would cause hardships for families and communities, and the administration believes that disaster aid should not be offset by cuts elsewhere in the budget.
