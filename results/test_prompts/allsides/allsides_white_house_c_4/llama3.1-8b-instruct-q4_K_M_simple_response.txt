SUMMARY:

In the aftermath of Hurricane Sandy, the White House is requesting $50-60 billion in emergency funding from Congress. The damage from the storm was widespread, with over 300,000 homes damaged and thousands still homeless in New York alone. The Northeastern states are asking for a total of $80 billion in federal aid to repair and rebuild affected areas.

Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan emphasized that rebuilding efforts should prioritize resilience and safety, using this opportunity to build back stronger and smarter. He highlighted the importance of making decisions now, such as designing buildings with storm resistance in mind and using generator-capable gas stations.

The administration opposes paying for disaster aid by cutting funds elsewhere in the budget, as it would cause hardships for families and communities waiting to rebuild. While some lawmakers propose providing a smaller amount of funding upfront, Donovan argues that this would only prolong the recovery process. The fate of the emergency funding request remains uncertain, but most Democrats agree with the administration's stance.

END OF SUMMARY
