SUMMARY:

In the aftermath of Hurricane Sandy, federal officials are seeking emergency funding from Congress to support rebuilding efforts in the Northeastern states. The White House plans to request $50 billion to $60 billion in aid, with a focus on preventing similar devastation caused by future storms. Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan emphasized the need for resilience and smart rebuilding, citing examples of design and materials that can withstand extreme weather conditions. However, some lawmakers want to provide only a small amount of funding now, arguing that this will cause hardships for families and communities waiting for assistance. Donovan strongly opposed this approach, stating that it would delay the rebuilding process and leave people "waiting for months or longer to be able to get on with their lives." Democrats seem to agree with Donovan's assessment, but it is unclear whether Republicans will support providing full funding without offsetting cuts elsewhere in the budget.
