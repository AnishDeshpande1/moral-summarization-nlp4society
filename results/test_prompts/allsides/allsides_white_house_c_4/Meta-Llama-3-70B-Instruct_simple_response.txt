SUMMARY:

The White House is set to request $50-60 billion in emergency funding from Congress to aid in the recovery from Hurricane Sandy, with a focus on not only rebuilding but also building back stronger and more resiliently. The devastating storm left over 300,000 homes seriously damaged, 265,000 businesses impacted, and thousands of people homeless in New York alone, with estimated damages of $32.8 billion. The Obama administration's point man on Sandy, Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan, emphasized the importance of making decisions now to build back smarter, including decisions on design, materials, and infrastructure. He warned against providing only a small amount of the rebuilding money now, saying it would cause hardships for families and communities waiting to rebuild. The administration also opposes offsetting disaster aid with cuts elsewhere in the budget. The moral imperative is clear: the federal government has a responsibility to provide adequate aid to those affected by the storm, and to invest in building back stronger to prevent similar devastation in the future.

END OF SUMMARY.
