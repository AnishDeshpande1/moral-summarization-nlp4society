SUMMARY: The White House will request an emergency funding package of $50‑$60 billion from Congress to address damage from Hurricane Sandy, with additional $80 billion sought by Northeastern states. Senators highlighted the scale of destruction: over 300,000 homes and 265,000 businesses in New York alone, $32.8 billion in state damages, and thousands still homeless. HUD Secretary Shaun Donovan said the aid should not only rebuild homes and businesses but also fund resilient, 21st‑century infrastructure—e.g., stronger designs, generator‑capable gas stations, and possibly underground power lines. FEMA Administrator Craig Fugate noted that underground utilities can create new issues, as seen in New York City. The administration has $4.8 billion left in its disaster relief fund for immediate assistance, but officials warn a “down payment” approach—providing only a small initial sum—would delay recovery for families and communities. Democrats favor full funding without offsetting cuts elsewhere; Republican support remains uncertain. END OF SUMMARY.
