SUMMARY:

The White House will request $50-60 billion in emergency funding from Congress to aid the Northeastern states affected by Hurricane Sandy, which caused immense devastation and damage. Sen. Kirsten Gillibrand reported that her state alone suffered over $32.8 billion in damages, with thousands of New Yorkers still homeless. Housing and Urban Development Secretary Shaun Donovan emphasized the need for a 21st-century response to rebuild stronger, safer, and more resiliently. He proposed using some of the funds to build up the region's defenses against future superstorms, including upgrading power grids and building boardwalks that can withstand extreme weather conditions. The administration has $4.8 billion left in its disaster relief fund but fears a "down payment" approach would cause hardships for families, small businesses, and communities waiting for assistance. Donovan argued that aid should not be offset by cuts elsewhere in the budget, emphasizing the need to ensure those affected can rebuild and restore their lives.

END OF SUMMARY.
