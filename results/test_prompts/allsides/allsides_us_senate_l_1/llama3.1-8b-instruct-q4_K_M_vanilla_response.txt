SUMMARY:

Senate Homeland Security Committee Chairman Ron Johnson is investigating corruption allegations involving Hunter Biden, son of Democratic presidential nominee Joe Biden, and the Ukrainian gas company Burisma. Johnson claims his investigations are nonpartisan, but Democrats accuse him of abusing his power to boost Trump's political prospects and jeopardizing US election security. Republicans are concerned that relitigating the Russia investigation could backfire, especially as the country deals with the coronavirus pandemic and social unrest.

Johnson defends his probes, saying he is "concerned about this democracy" and wants to uncover the truth about the transition process. He claims the incoming Trump administration was "sabotaged" by the outgoing Obama team. Democrats counter that Johnson's efforts are aimed at hurting Biden's chances in the election. Johnson plans to release reports on his investigations before Election Day, which would thrust the issues back into the spotlight.

Johnson insists he is not doing anyone's bidding and is simply seeking the truth. However, his critics argue that he is misusing his position for partisan gain.

END OF SUMMARY.
