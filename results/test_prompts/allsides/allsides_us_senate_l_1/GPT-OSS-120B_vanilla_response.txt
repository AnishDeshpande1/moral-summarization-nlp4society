SUMMARY:  
Senator Ron Johnson is leading Senate Homeland Security investigations into two partisan issues: alleged corruption by Hunter Biden’s role on Ukrainian gas firm Burisma and claims that the Obama administration sabotaged the Trump transition. Republicans argue Johnson is exposing a “Russian disinformation” effort and protecting democracy, while Democrats accuse him of partisan abuse, misusing oversight to aid Trump and diverting attention from the pandemic, unemployment and protests over police violence. Johnson insists he is non‑partisan, saying his probe aims to reveal truth about the 2016 election, the transition, and the impeachment trial, and to reform future transfers of power. He plans to release reports this summer and fall, which could impact the upcoming election. Democrats, including Sen. Brian Schatz, criticize the timing and motives, labeling the investigations as Republican‑party errands. Johnson rebuffs the criticism, claiming Democrats fear the findings and urging them to confront the “truth.”  

END OF SUMMARY.
