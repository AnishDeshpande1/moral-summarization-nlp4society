SUMMARY:

Senate Homeland Security Committee Chairman Ron Johnson is leading an investigation into corruption allegations involving Hunter Biden, son of Democratic presidential nominee Joe Biden. Johnson claims his efforts are nonpartisan, but Democrats see it as a misuse of power to boost Trump's political prospects and undermine the integrity of the election. Republicans are concerned that their priorities are not in order, especially as the country faces the coronavirus pandemic, high unemployment, and unrest over police killings. Johnson portrays his investigation as an honest effort to find the truth, but Democrats argue it is a partisan attack on Biden.

Johnson's actions have been seen by some as an abuse of power, as he intends to release reports on his investigations before the election, which could impact the outcome. His comments and actions have been criticized by Democrats, who see him as running errands for the Republican National Committee. Johnson claims that Democrats are "afraid of the truth" and dismisses their criticism as representative of a "collegial" Senate.

END OF SUMMARY.
