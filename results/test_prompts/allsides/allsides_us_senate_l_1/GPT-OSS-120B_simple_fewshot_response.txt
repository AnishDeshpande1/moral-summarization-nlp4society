SUMMARY:
Sen. Ron Johnson has intensified two partisan investigations—one into Hunter Biden’s Burisma board seat and another into alleged “sabotage” of the Trump transition—while insisting he is “non‑partisan” and merely protecting democracy. Democrats charge that Johnson is weaponising Senate oversight to aid Trump, spread Russian‑linked disinformation, and distract from the pandemic, soaring unemployment and protests over police violence. They warn his summer and fall reports will re‑ignite election‑year controversy and accuse him of “running errands for the Republican National Committee.” Johnson rebuffs the criticism, claiming he is a “straight shooter” exposing truths the Obama‑Biden team tried to hide, and challenges Democrats to name what they fear he will reveal. Behind the public sparring, Republican senators privately fear the probes could backfire by appearing out‑of‑step with the nation’s urgent crises. Johnson remains undeterred, framing his relentless inquiries as a duty to expose a corrupted transition and restore a peaceful transfer of power.  

END OF SUMMARY.
