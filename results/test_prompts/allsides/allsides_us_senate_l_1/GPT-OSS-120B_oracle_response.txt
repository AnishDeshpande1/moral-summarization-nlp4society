SUMMARY:  
Sen. Ron Johnson is spearheading Senate investigations into alleged corruption surrounding Hunter Biden’s Burisma board seat and claims that former Vice President Joe Biden tried to shield his son from a Ukrainian probe. Democrats accuse Johnson of abusing and misusing Senate oversight to serve Republican political aims, even operating a Russian‑disinformation front that jeopardizes U.S. election security. Johnson, however, insists he is “nonpartisan,” saying he is merely addressing the truth about a transition he believes was corrupted and sabotaged by the outgoing Obama team. He argues his Homeland Security panel’s inquiry will ensure a peaceful transfer of power and expose how officials may have unmasked Michael Flynn. Republicans worry the renewed focus on the 2016 Russia investigation and the Biden‑Burisma issue could backfire amid the pandemic, soaring unemployment and nationwide uprisings against police brutality and killings of unarmed Black Americans. Democrats denounce Johnson’s work as a misuse of his position, noting his plan to release reports this summer and fall will keep the controversy gripping the Senate as Election Day approaches. Johnson dismisses the criticism, claiming Democrats are afraid of the truth and urging them to stop ignoring what he calls a corrupted transition.  
END OF SUMMARY.
