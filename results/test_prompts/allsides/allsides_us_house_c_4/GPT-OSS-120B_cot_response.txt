**SUMMARY:**  
U.S. Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin missed the House Ways and Means Committee’s final 5 p.m. deadline to turn over President Donald Trump’s six years of tax returns, prompting Chairman Richard Neal to warn of a “crusade” for oversight and to consider a subpoena. Mnuchin’s letter said the request was “unprecedented” and that the Justice Department must first determine its legality, a stance Democrats denounce as a “violation” of the law and an “unparalleled contempt” for the rule of law. Republicans condemn the effort as a political “fishing expedition.” With the deadline passed, Neal may hold Mnuchin or IRS Commissioner Charles Rettig in contempt, risking legal penalties and possibly a Supreme Court showdown. Democrats assert they will defend the right of oversight and expose a “corrupt” administration that hides Trump’s tax records.
