SUMMARY:

US Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin missed a congressional deadline to turn over President Donald Trump's tax returns, setting the stage for a possible court battle between Congress and the administration. House Ways and Means Committee Chairman Richard Neal had requested six years of Trump's individual and business tax records by 5 p.m. on Tuesday, but the deadline passed without the documents being provided. Mnuchin released a letter to Neal, pledging to make a final decision on whether to provide the tax records by May 6.

Democrats are confident that they will succeed in any legal fight over Trump's tax returns and have threatened to hold Mnuchin or IRS Commissioner Charles Rettig in contempt of Congress if they ignore a subpoena. The issue is expected to ultimately end up in the US Supreme Court, with Democrats seeking to impose oversight on the administration.

House Democrats are motivated by concerns about possible conflicts of interest posed by Trump's continued ownership of extensive business interests while serving as president.
