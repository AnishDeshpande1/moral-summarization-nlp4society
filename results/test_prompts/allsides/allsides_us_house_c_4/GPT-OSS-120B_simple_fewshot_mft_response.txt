SUMMARY:
U.S. Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin missed the House Ways and Means Committee’s 5 p.m. deadline to turn over President Donald Trump’s six years of personal and business tax returns, prompting Chairman Richard Neal to warn of a subpoena and a possible court fight that could reach the Supreme Court. Mnuchin’s letter said the Treasury is still consulting the Justice Department because the request is “unprecedented” and must be “consistent with the law,” a stance Democrats denounce as a blatant subversion of congressional authority. They argue the refusal harms the public’s right to oversight and threatens the nation’s democratic health, while Republicans label the demand a partisan fishing expedition. The administration’s repeated missed deadlines and its claim that the president “is not inclined” to release the returns are portrayed as cheating the clear legal requirement that the Treasury “shall furnish” taxpayer data to an authorized lawmaker. Lawmakers warn that continued defiance could lead to contempt charges, financial penalties, or even jail time, underscoring the clash between lawful authority, the need to protect the public from unchecked power, and the fairness of enforcing the rules equally.  

END OF SUMMARY.
