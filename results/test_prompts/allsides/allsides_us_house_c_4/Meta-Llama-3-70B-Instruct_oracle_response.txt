SUMMARY:

U.S. Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin has failed to meet a final congressional deadline for turning over President Donald Trump's tax returns to lawmakers, setting the stage for a possible court battle between Congress and the administration. Mnuchin's decision to miss the deadline has sparked controversy, with Democrats accusing him of choosing to violate the law to keep Trump's records hidden away. Despite being notified of the deadline, Mnuchin released a letter pledging to make a final decision on whether to provide Trump's tax records by May 6, sparking concerns that he is defying the law. House Ways and Means Committee Chairman Richard Neal has threatened to subpoena Trump's tax records, which could lead to a legal battle that may ultimately have to be settled by the U.S. Supreme Court. Democrats argue that Mnuchin's failure to comply with the deadline is a clear example of the administration's contempt for the rule of law. END OF SUMMARY.
