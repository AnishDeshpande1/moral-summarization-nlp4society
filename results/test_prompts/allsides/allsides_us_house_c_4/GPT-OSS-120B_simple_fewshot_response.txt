SUMMARY:
U.S. Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin missed the House’s final 5 p.m. deadline to turn over President Donald Trump’s six years of personal and business tax returns, prompting Ways and Means Chairman Richard Neal to warn of a subpoena and a looming court showdown that could reach the Supreme Court. Mnuchin’s letter to Neal said the Treasury is still consulting the Justice Department because the request is “unprecedented,” leaving the IRS to miss the deadline a second time. Democrats argue the records are essential to expose possible conflicts of interest and to enforce congressional oversight, accusing the administration of flouting the law and showing contempt for the rule of law. Republicans denounce the effort as a partisan “fishing expedition.” The White House maintains Trump will not release the returns while under audit, a claim his former lawyer Michael Cohen disputes. Legal scholars say Congress could hold Mnuchin or IRS Commissioner Charles Rettig in contempt, risking fines or jail if they defy a subpoena. The dispute sets the stage for a high‑stakes legal battle over congressional authority and presidential transparency.  
END OF SUMMARY.
