SUMMARY:

US Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin missed a congressional deadline to turn over President Donald Trump's tax returns, setting the stage for a possible court battle between Congress and the administration. House Ways and Means Committee Chairman Richard Neal had requested six years of Trump's individual and business tax records, but Mnuchin released a letter saying he would make a final decision by May 6. This is the second time the administration has missed a deadline for the tax returns.

Democrats say they are confident of succeeding in any legal fight over Trump's tax returns and have threatened to hold Mnuchin or IRS Commissioner Charles Rettig in contempt of Congress if they ignore a subpoena. The law is clear that the Treasury secretary "shall furnish" taxpayer data upon request from an authorized lawmaker, making Mnuchin's delay a potential violation of the law.

The standoff highlights the Trump administration's disregard for transparency and oversight, with Democrats accusing them of hiding behind a claim that releasing tax returns could lead to an audit. A legal battle is likely to ensue, with the Supreme Court potentially involved in the final decision.

END OF SUMMARY.
