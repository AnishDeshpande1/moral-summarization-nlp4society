SUMMARY:

U.S. Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin's decision to miss the final congressional deadline for turning over President Donald Trump's tax returns has set the stage for a possible court battle between Congress and the administration. This failure to comply with the law is a clear indication of the administration's contempt for rule of law, as Representative Bill Pascrell stated. The Ways and Means Committee Chairman Richard Neal had set a deadline for the Internal Revenue Service and Treasury to provide six years of Trump's individual and business tax records, which lapsed without receiving the documents.

Mnuchin's decision to miss the deadline is not surprising, given the administration's history of ignoring House deadlines for the tax returns. The committee may now have to subpoena Trump's tax records, risking a legal battle that could end up with the Supreme Court. This battle would be a crusade to impose plain oversight on this corrupt administration and ensure that the law is complied with.

END OF SUMMARY.
