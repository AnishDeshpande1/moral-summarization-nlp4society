SUMMARY:
U.S. Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin missed the House Ways and Means Committee’s final 5 p.m. deadline to turn over President Donald Trump’s six years of personal and business tax returns, setting up a likely legal showdown. Chairman Richard Neal had warned that failure to comply could lead to a subpoena and possible contempt proceedings. Mnuchin, citing ongoing Justice Department consultation, said a final decision on releasing the records would come by May 6, labeling the request “unprecedented.” Democrats argue the records are needed to investigate potential conflicts of interest, while Republicans denounce the effort as a partisan “fishing expedition.” The White House indicated Trump is unlikely to voluntarily provide the returns, noting he may consider it after any audit concludes. Legal experts say the dispute could proceed to federal court and potentially the Supreme Court, with officials facing possible penalties for defying a congressional subpoena. END OF SUMMARY.
