**SUMMARY:**

The U.S. Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin failed to meet the deadline set by House Ways and Means Committee Chairman Richard Neal for turning over President Donald Trump's tax returns, setting the stage for a possible court battle between Congress and the administration. Democrats want Trump's returns as part of their investigations into possible conflicts of interest posed by his continued ownership of extensive business interests while serving as president. Republicans have condemned the request as a political "fishing expedition" by Democrats. The White House said Trump is unlikely to hand over his tax returns, citing an ongoing audit. Neal informed IRS Commissioner Charles Rettig that failure to comply with the deadline would be viewed as a denial and has threatened to hold Mnuchin or Rettig in contempt of Congress if they ignored a subpoena. Democrats are confident they will succeed in any legal fight over Trump's tax returns.
