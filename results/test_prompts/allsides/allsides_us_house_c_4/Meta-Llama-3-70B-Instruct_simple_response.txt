SUMMARY:

US Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin has failed to meet a congressional deadline to turn over President Donald Trump's tax returns, setting the stage for a possible court battle. This move is seen as a violation of the law and a contempt for the rule of law. Democrats, led by House Ways and Means Committee Chairman Richard Neal, are seeking Trump's tax records as part of their investigations into possible conflicts of interest posed by his continued ownership of business interests while serving as president. Mnuchin has pledged to make a final decision on whether to provide the tax records by May 6, but Democrats are likely to subpoena the records and take the administration to court if they do not comply. This battle could ultimately end up in the Supreme Court. The administration's refusal to release the tax returns is seen as an attempt to hide the truth and undermine congressional oversight.

END OF SUMMARY.
