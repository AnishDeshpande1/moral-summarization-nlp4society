SUMMARY:  
Rev. Franklin Graham, son of Billy Graham, had a UK tour stop scheduled at ACC Liverpool, but the venue cancelled the event, saying his biblical view of marriage “as one man and one woman” is “incompatible with our values.” Protesters, including the Liverpool Labour LGBT Network, labeled Graham a “homophobic hate preacher.” In response, Graham issued an open letter to the LGBTQ community, stating he is coming “not to condemn” but to present the Gospel, which he describes as “inclusive” and “love‑filled.” He reiterated that he sees homosexuality as a sin, calling it a “guilty” condition that carries the “penalty” of spiritual death and separation from God, yet he emphasized that all people are sinners in need of forgiveness. Graham defended free speech and religious liberty, insisting he is “speaking for everybody” and that the LGBTQ community is “absolutely welcome” at his events.  

END OF SUMMARY.
