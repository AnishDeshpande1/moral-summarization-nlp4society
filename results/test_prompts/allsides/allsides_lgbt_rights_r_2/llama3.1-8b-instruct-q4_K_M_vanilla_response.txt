SUMMARY:
Rev. Franklin Graham, son of Billy Graham, was scheduled to speak at ACC Liverpool in the UK as part of his tour. However, officials at the venue announced that they would cancel his event due to his biblical interpretation of marriage as a union between one man and one woman being "incompatible with our values." Protesters had been rebuking the venue for allowing Graham to share the Gospel, calling him a "homophobic hate preacher."

Graham responded to the decision in an open letter to the LGBTQ community in the UK, stating that he is coming to present the Gospel, not to condemn them. He admitted that he sees homosexuality as a sin, but emphasized that God defines humanity as guilty of sin and in need of forgiveness. Graham defended his right to free speech and religious liberty, saying he comes from love, not hate, and invited the LGBTQ community to attend one of his events.

The cancellation of Graham's event has sparked controversy, with some supporting the venue's decision while others argue it is an infringement on freedom of speech.
