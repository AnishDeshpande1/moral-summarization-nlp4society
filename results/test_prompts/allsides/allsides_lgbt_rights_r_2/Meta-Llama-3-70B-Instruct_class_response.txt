SUMMARY:

The Rev. Franklin Graham's event at ACC Liverpool has been stopped due to his views on marriage being "incompatible with our values." Graham's biblical interpretation of marriage as a union between one man and one woman sparked criticism from groups like the Liverpool Labour LGBT Network, who rebuked the venue for allowing him to share the Gospel. Graham replied to the decision, penning an open letter to the LGBTQ community, stating he is coming to present the Gospel, not to condemn them. He acknowledged that he sees homosexuality as a sin, but emphasized that God views all humans as sinners in need of forgiveness and cleansing. Graham defended the right to free speech and religious liberty, saying the Gospel "is inclusive" and he is coming out of love, not hate. He extended an invitation to the LGBTQ community to attend one of his events, saying they are "absolutely welcome."

END OF SUMMARY.
