SUMMARY:

The Rev. Franklin Graham's event at ACC Liverpool was canceled due to his views on marriage, which are deemed "incompatible with our values." Graham's biblical interpretation of marriage as a union between one man and one woman sparked protests, with critics labeling him a "homophobic hate preacher." In response, Graham penned an open letter to the LGBTQ community, emphasizing that he is coming to present the Gospel, not to condemn them. While he acknowledges that the Bible defines homosexuality as a sin, he stresses that all humans are sinners in need of forgiveness and cleansing. Graham defends his right to free speech and religious liberty, asserting that the Gospel is inclusive and that he is coming out of love, not hate. The cancellation raises concerns about the suppression of religious views and the erosion of free speech, highlighting the tension between protecting marginalized groups and upholding the values of tolerance and inclusivity.
