SUMMARY:

Rev. Franklin Graham, son of Billy Graham, has been banned from speaking at ACC Liverpool due to his biblical views on marriage, which are deemed "incompatible with our values." Graham's event was part of a larger UK tour, but protesters, including the Liverpool Labour LGBT Network, labeled him a "homophobic hate preacher." In response, Graham penned an open letter to the LGBTQ community, stating he is coming to present the Gospel, not to condemn them. While he acknowledges that the Bible defines homosexuality as a sin, he emphasizes that all humans are sinners in need of forgiveness. Graham defends free speech and religious liberty, asserting that his message is inclusive and comes from a place of love. He invites the LGBTQ community to attend his events, saying they are "absolutely welcome." This incident highlights the tension between religious beliefs and progressive values, raising questions about the limits of free speech and the role of religious leaders in public discourse.

END OF SUMMARY.
