SUMMARY:

Rev. Franklin Graham, son of Billy Graham, was scheduled to speak at ACC Liverpool as part of his UK tour. However, the venue canceled the event due to Graham's views on marriage, which are "incompatible with our values." Graham's biblical interpretation of marriage as a union between one man and one woman was deemed homophobic by some, including the Liverpool Labour LGBT Network. In response, Graham wrote an open letter to the LGBTQ community, stating that he is not coming to condemn them, but to present the Gospel. He acknowledged that the Bible defines homosexuality as a sin, but emphasized that all humans are sinners in need of forgiveness. Graham defended free speech and religious liberty, saying the Gospel is inclusive and he is coming out of love, not hate. He invited the LGBTQ community to attend his events, saying they are "absolutely welcome."

END OF SUMMARY.
