SUMMARY:
The Rev. Franklin Graham, a prominent evangelical preacher and son of Billy Graham, had his June 2020 UK tour stop at ACC Liverpool cancelled after venue officials said his biblical definition of marriage—“one man and one woman”—conflicted with their values. Local activists, including the Liverpool Labour LGBT Network, condemned Graham as a “homophobic hate preacher” and pressured the arena to reject his appearance. In response, Graham issued an open letter to the UK LGBTQ community, asserting he is coming to share the Gospel, not to condemn, while reaffirming his belief that homosexuality is sinful according to the Bible. He framed his stance as a matter of religious liberty and free speech, emphasizing that the Gospel is “inclusive” and that all, including LGBTQ individuals, are “absolutely welcome” at his events. The cancellation highlights the clash between Graham’s traditionalist, faith‑based moral teachings and contemporary LGBTQ‑affirming values, raising questions about the limits of religious expression when it conflicts with inclusive community standards.  
END OF SUMMARY.
