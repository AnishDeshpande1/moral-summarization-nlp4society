SUMMARY:

Rev. Franklin Graham, son of Billy Graham, was scheduled to speak at ACC Liverpool as part of his UK tour. However, the venue cancelled the event due to Graham's views on marriage, which they deemed "incompatible with our values." Graham's biblical interpretation of marriage as a union between one man and one woman sparked criticism from groups like the Liverpool Labour LGBT Network, who called him a "homophobic hate preacher." In response, Graham wrote an open letter to the LGBTQ community, stating that he is coming to the UK to present the Gospel, not to condemn them. He acknowledged that he believes homosexuality is a sin, but emphasized that God's message is one of love and forgiveness, and that everyone is welcome to attend his events. Graham also defended the right to free speech and religious liberty. Despite the cancellation, Graham's UK tour will continue.

END OF SUMMARY.
