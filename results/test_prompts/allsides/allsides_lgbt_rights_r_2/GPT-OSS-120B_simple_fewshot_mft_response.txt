SUMMARY:
The Liverpool venue ACC cancelled the scheduled appearance of evangelist Franklin Graham after protests that his biblical definition of marriage – “one man and one woman” – conflicts with the venue’s inclusive values. Critics, including the Liverpool Labour LGBT Network, labeled Graham a “homophobic hate preacher” and argued that giving him a platform would cause emotional harm to LGBTQ people and perpetuate unfair discrimination. In response, Graham issued an open letter asserting that he does not intend to condemn the UK LGBTQ community, emphasizing that all humans are sinners in need of God’s love and forgiveness. He framed his message as an exercise of religious liberty and free speech, claiming the Gospel is “inclusive” and that LGBTQ individuals are “absolutely welcome” at his events. The cancellation thus reflects concerns over protecting vulnerable groups from harmful rhetoric (Care/Harm) and upholding equitable treatment by refusing to endorse speech that many view as unjust and discriminatory (Fairness/Cheating).
