SUMMARY:

The New Georgia Project, founded by Rep. Stacey Abrams, is at the center of a voter registration drive controversy in Georgia. Rev. Raphael Warnock accused Secretary of State Brian Kemp of engaging in voter suppression, implying that Kemp's investigation into potential fraud was motivated by racism. However, Kemp's office claims to have identified 28 cases of fraud and 26 suspicious cases, with some applications being forged or falsified, which is a felony offense in Georgia. Kemp has issued a subpoena for the New Georgia Project to produce documents related to their voter registration drive, but Abrams calls this an "abuse of power" and a "witch hunt." The issue has become highly politicized, with both sides accusing each other of attempting to suppress votes or commit fraud. As the November election approaches, Kemp insists that his office is simply doing its duty in investigating reports of potential wrongdoing.

END OF SUMMARY.
