SUMMARY:

Georgia's Republican Secretary of State Brian Kemp is investigating the New Georgia Project, a minority voter registration drive led by Rep. Stacey Abrams, for potential fraud. The investigation was sparked by allegations of 28 cases of forged signatures and false information on voter registration forms in 14 counties. Kemp claims his office is simply doing its duty to investigate reports of forgery and fraud, while Abrams accuses him of a "witch hunt" and voter suppression.

The investigation has taken on a partisan tone, with the New Georgia Project accusing Kemp of trying to intimidate minority voters. Rep. Raphael Warnock, pastor of Ebenezer Baptist Church, denounced the probe as an attempt to suppress minority votes. Kemp maintains that his office is committed to ensuring the integrity of the electoral process and will investigate any allegations of voter fraud.

The issue has become highly politicized ahead of November's election, with Democratic candidate Michelle Nunn relying on registering minority voters to potentially take the seat from Republican David Perdue.
