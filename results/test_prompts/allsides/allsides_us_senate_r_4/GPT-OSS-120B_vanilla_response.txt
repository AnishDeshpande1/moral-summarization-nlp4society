SUMMARY:
Rev. Raphael Warnock, pastor of Ebenezer Baptist Church, condemned a Georgia fraud investigation into the New Georgia Project’s minority voter registration drive, calling it voter suppression. Secretary of State Brian Kemp’s office is probing alleged fraud in registering over 100,000 minority voters, citing 28 confirmed cases of forged signatures and false information across 14 counties, plus 26 suspicious cases. The New Georgia Project, founded by Rep. Stacey Abrams, denies wrongdoing, labeling the subpoena and investigation a “witch hunt” and an abuse of power that distracts from voter outreach. The issue is politically charged ahead of the close U.S. Senate race between Democrat Michelle Nunn and Republican David Perdue, with both sides eyeing the large unregistered minority electorate. Kemp maintains the probe is a legal duty and that valid registrations will be processed, while Warnock and the project accuse him of intimidation and voter suppression tactics.

END OF SUMMARY.
