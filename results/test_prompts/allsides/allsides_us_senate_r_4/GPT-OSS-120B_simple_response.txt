SUMMARY:  
Rev. Raphael Warnock, pastor of Atlanta’s historic Ebenezer Baptist Church, condemned Georgia’s new fraud probe into the New Georgia Project’s minority voter‑registration drive as a modern form of voter suppression, likening it to the legacy of the Ku Klux Klan. Republican Secretary of State Brian Kemp, who ordered the investigation, claimed the probe uncovered 28 confirmed fraud cases—25 forged registrations and three false canvassing sheets—plus 26 suspicious ones, stressing that forgery is a felony and that his office must protect election integrity.  

The controversy erupts amid a tight U.S. Senate race, with Democrats hoping to mobilize the state’s 800,000 unregistered minority voters. Stacey Abrams, founder of the New Georgia Project and adviser to Democratic candidate Michelle Nunn, denounced the subpoena as an “overly burdensome” witch hunt that diverts resources from legitimate outreach. She and Warnock accused Kemp of a pattern of intimidation, arguing that the language of “fraud,” “forgery,” and “felony” chills civic participation.  

Kemp retorted that he is merely fulfilling his duty, insisting any valid registrations will be processed and denying racist intent. Both sides frame the dispute as a moral battle over the right of minorities to vote versus the state’s responsibility to guard against electoral fraud.  

END OF SUMMARY.
