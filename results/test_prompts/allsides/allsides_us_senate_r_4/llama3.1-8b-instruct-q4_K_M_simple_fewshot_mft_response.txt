SUMMARY:

Georgia's Republican Secretary of State Brian Kemp launched an investigation into allegations of voter registration fraud by the New Georgia Project, a group led by Democratic House Minority Leader Rep. Stacey Abrams and advising Michelle Nunn's Senate campaign. The investigation follows reports of 28 confirmed cases of forgery and 26 suspicious cases in 14 counties. Kemp's office claims that falsifying a voter registration form is a felony, but the New Georgia Project denies any wrongdoing and accuses Kemp of a "witch hunt" and "voter suppression." Rev. Raphael Warnock, a pastor at Atlanta's Ebenezer Baptist Church, condemned the investigation as "voter suppression," comparing it to the Ku Klux Klan's efforts to intimidate minority voters. The situation has become highly politicized, with Kemp insisting that his duty is to investigate allegations of voter registration fraud and the New Georgia Project accusing him of abuse of power.

END OF SUMMARY.
