SUMMARY:

Rev. Raphael Warnock, pastor of Atlanta's Ebenezer Baptist Church, criticized a fraud probe into a minority voter registration drive, accusing Georgia's Republican Secretary of State Brian Kemp of voter suppression. Kemp's office is investigating the New Georgia Project, which aims to register over 100,000 minority voters before the October 6 deadline. The probe has found 28 cases of fraud in 14 counties, including forged signatures and false information. Warnock and the project's founder, Rep. Stacey Abrams, claim the investigation is a "witch hunt" and an abuse of power, aimed at suppressing minority votes. Kemp denies the allegations, saying his office is simply investigating reports of fraud brought to his attention by several counties. The issue is highly politicized, with the outcome potentially affecting the tight Senate race between Democrat Michelle Nunn and Republican David Perdue.

END OF SUMMARY.
