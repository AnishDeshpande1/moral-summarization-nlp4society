SUMMARY:

Georgia's Republican Secretary of State Brian Kemp launched an investigation into allegations of voter registration fraud by the New Georgia Project, a group led by Democratic House Minority Leader Stacey Abrams and associated with Senatorial candidate Michelle Nunn. The project has registered over 100,000 minority voters, but Kemp's office claims to have found evidence of forgery and false information on 28 applications. Abrams denies any wrongdoing, calling the investigation a "witch hunt" and accusing Kemp of voter suppression. Rev. Raphael Warnock, pastor of Ebenezer Baptist Church, spoke out against the probe, equating it with the Ku Klux Klan's efforts to suppress minority votes. However, Kemp maintains that his office is simply doing its duty in investigating reports of fraud and forgery. The incident highlights the tension between election officials' efforts to ensure the integrity of elections and accusations of voter suppression by those who seek to register more voters.

END OF SUMMARY.
