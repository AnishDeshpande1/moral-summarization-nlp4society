SUMMARY:
Sen. Raphael Warnock condemned Georgia Secretary of State Brian Kemp’s fraud investigation into the New Georgia Project’s minority voter‑registration drive, likening the probe to “Ku Klux Klan” voter‑suppression tactics. Kemp’s office says it has found 28 alleged fraud cases— forged signatures and false information— and issued a subpoena demanding all related documents, calling the matter a serious felony issue. The New Georgia Project, founded by Stacey Abrams, argues the subpoena is an “overly burdensome” witch‑hunt that intimidates groups seeking to register minority voters ahead of a tight Senate race between Democrat Michelle Nunn and Republican David Perdue. Abrams and Warnock accuse Kemp of a pattern of voter suppression, while Kemp insists he is merely enforcing the law and processing legitimate registrations. The clash highlights the moral stakes of protecting voting rights versus safeguarding election integrity, underscoring how politicized accusations can chill civic participation and erode public trust in democratic processes.  
END OF SUMMARY.
