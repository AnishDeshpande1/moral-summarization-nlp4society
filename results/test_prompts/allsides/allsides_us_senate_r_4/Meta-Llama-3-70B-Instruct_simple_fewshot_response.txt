SUMMARY:

Rev. Raphael Warnock, pastor of Atlanta's Ebenezer Baptist Church, accused Georgia's Republican Secretary of State Brian Kemp of voter suppression, likening him to the Ku Klux Klan, over a fraud probe into a minority voter registration drive. Kemp's office is investigating the New Georgia Project, which Warnock is associated with, for allegedly committing fraud in its drive to register over 100,000 minority voters. The probe has found 28 cases of fraud, including forged signatures and false information. Warnock and the NGP claim the investigation is a "witch hunt" and an "abuse of power," while Kemp insists it's his duty to investigate reports of fraud. The issue is highly politicized, with the outcome potentially affecting the Senate race between Democratic candidate Michelle Nunn and Republican David Perdue. The moral framing of this issue is clear: Warnock and the NGP are fighting for the rights of minority voters, while Kemp's actions are seen as an attempt to suppress their voices. By accusing Kemp of voter suppression, Warnock is highlighting the importance of protecting democratic values and ensuring equal access to the ballot.

END OF SUMMARY.
