**SUMMARY:**
Rev. Raphael Warnock denounced Georgia Secretary of State Brian Kemp’s fraud probe of the New Georgia Project as voter suppression, saying “you don’t have to wear a hood or be a member of the Klan to be engaged in voter suppression.” Kemp’s office claims 28 cases of fraud, including forged signatures and false information, and has issued a subpoena, calling the alleged wrongdoing a felony. The investigation, which Kemp says is a duty to stop abuse and intimidation, has been labeled a “witch hunt” and “dog and pony show” by the project’s leaders, who argue the subpoena is overly burdensome and an abuse of power that chills voter‑registration efforts. Stacey Abrams, founder of the project, insists the drive is nonpartisan and accuses Kemp of a pattern of racist, smear tactics. Both sides trade accusations of false claims and political intimidation as the November Senate race tightens in Georgia.
