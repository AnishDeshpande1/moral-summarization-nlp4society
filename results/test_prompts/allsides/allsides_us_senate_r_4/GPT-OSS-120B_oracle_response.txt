SUMMARY:  
Rev. Raphael Warnock condemned a Georgia fraud probe as voter suppression, saying “you don’t have to wear a hood” to recognize wrongdoing. Secretary of State Brian Kemp’s office is investigating the New Georgia Project’s minority voter‑registration drive, alleging 25 fraudulent applications, forged signatures and falsifying information – a felony under Georgia law. Kemp says his investigators have “figured” out 28 cases of fraud and will stop any illegal activity, while accusing the group of abuse and a witch‑hunt. The project, founded by Stacey Abrams, calls the subpoenas burdensome and an abuse of power, arguing the accusations chill future registration efforts. Abrams maintains the work is nonpartisan, despite its impact on the tight Senate race between Michelle Nunn and David Perdue. Both sides trade charges: Kemp insists he is merely enforcing the law, while Warnock and the New Georgia Project decry the probe as a smear campaign and a hunt to suppress minority voters.  

END OF SUMMARY.
