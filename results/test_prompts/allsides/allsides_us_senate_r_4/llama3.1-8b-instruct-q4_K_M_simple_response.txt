SUMMARY:

A controversy is unfolding in Georgia over voter registration drives by the New Georgia Project, led by Rep. Stacey Abrams. Georgia's Republican Secretary of State Brian Kemp has launched an investigation into allegations of voter registration fraud and forgery. Kemp claims that his office has confirmed 28 cases of fraud and 26 suspicious ones, with some involving forged signatures or false information. The New Georgia Project denies any wrongdoing, accusing Kemp of a "witch hunt" and a pattern of voter suppression.

The controversy is taking on racial undertones, with Rev. Raphael Warnock accusing Kemp's investigation of being motivated by a desire to suppress minority votes. Kemp denies the charge, insisting that his office is simply doing its job in investigating reports of fraud. Abrams claims that the subpoena issued to her organization is an "abuse of power" and is distracting from their voter registration efforts.

The issue has become highly politicized ahead of the November elections, with both sides accusing each other of playing dirty tricks.

END OF SUMMARY.
