Raphael Warnock denounced Georgia Secretary of State Brian Kemp’s fraud probe as a modern form of voter suppression, invoking the legacy of the Klan and warning that “you don’t have to wear a hood” to engage in it. Kemp’s office claims to have uncovered 28 cases of fraudulent, forged, and felony‑level voter registrations by the New Georgia Project, a group founded by Stacey Abrams to register minority voters. Abrams called the subpoena a “witch hunt,” an abuse of power, and a burdensome, chilling “fishing expedition” that threatens voter registration efforts. The investigation, she says, is an intimidation tactic and a false smear campaign aimed at undermining minority participation ahead of a tight Senate race. Kemp insists he is merely doing his job, investigating genuine fraud reports, and not blocking legitimate registrations. The clash highlights the moral battle over voting rights, with both sides accusing the other of racism, suppression, and illegal conduct.
