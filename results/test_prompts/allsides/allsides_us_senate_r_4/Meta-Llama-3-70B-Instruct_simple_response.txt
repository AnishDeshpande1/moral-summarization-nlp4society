SUMMARY:

Rev. Raphael Warnock, pastor of Atlanta's Ebenezer Baptist Church, accused Georgia's Republican Secretary of State Brian Kemp of voter suppression, likening his actions to those of the Ku Klux Klan. Kemp's office is investigating the New Georgia Project, a minority voter registration drive, for fraud. Warnock claimed that Kemp's actions are aimed at disenfranchising minority voters, while Kemp insisted that his office is simply investigating reports of fraud and forgery. The New Georgia Project has registered over 85,000 new voters, but only 51,000 have been processed. Warnock and others argue that Kemp's investigation is a "witch hunt" and an "abuse of power" designed to intimidate and suppress minority voters. The issue has become highly politicized, with the outcome potentially impacting the tight Senate race in Georgia.

END OF SUMMARY.
