SUMMARY:
Georgia's Republican Secretary of State Brian Kemp launched a probe into a minority voter registration drive, accusing the New Georgia Project of committing fraud. The group, founded by Rep. Stacey Abrams, is trying to register 100,000 minority voters before the Oct. 6 deadline. Kemp's investigator confirmed 28 cases of fraud in 14 counties and another 26 were labeled suspicious. Falsifying a voter registration form is a felony in Georgia. Abrams denies any wrongdoing, accusing Kemp of a "witch hunt" and a "fishing expedition." She claims Kemp's actions are an attempt to suppress minority voters and intimidate the group. Kemp insists that his office is simply doing its duty to investigate reports of fraud and forgery. The probe has become highly politicized ahead of the November elections. Kemp's investigation has been criticized by Rev. Raphael Warnock, who accused him of voter suppression and racism.
