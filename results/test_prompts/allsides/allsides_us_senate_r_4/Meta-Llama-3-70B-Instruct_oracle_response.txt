SUMMARY:

Rev. Raphael Warnock, pastor of Atlanta's Ebenezer Baptist Church, denounced a fraud probe into a minority voter registration drive, accusing Georgia's Republican Secretary of State Brian Kemp of voter suppression. Warnock claimed that Kemp's investigation was a form of voter suppression, reminiscent of the Ku Klux Klan's tactics. Kemp's office is investigating the New Georgia Project, which aims to register over 100,000 minority voters, for fraud and forgery. The probe has uncovered 28 cases of fraud, including forged signatures and false information. Warnock and the project's founder, Rep. Stacey Abrams, claim that the investigation is a "witch hunt" and an "abuse of power," aimed at intimidating minority voters. Kemp insists that his office is simply doing its job, investigating reports of fraud and forgery. The issue is particularly contentious due to the narrow margins in the race to replace retiring U.S. Sen. Saxby Chambliss. The probe has sparked accusations of voter suppression, with Warnock demanding that Kemp "stop this dog and pony show" and focus on registering valid voters.

END OF SUMMARY.
