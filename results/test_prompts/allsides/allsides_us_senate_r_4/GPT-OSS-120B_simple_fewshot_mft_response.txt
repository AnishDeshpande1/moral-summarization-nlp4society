SUMMARY:
Rev. Raphael Warnock condemned Georgia Secretary of State Brian Kemp’s fraud investigation into the New Georgia Project’s minority voter‑registration drive as a politically motivated “witch hunt” that subverts democratic authority and cheats voters of their rights. Kemp’s office, asserting its authority, subpoenaed the group after alleging 28 confirmed fraud cases and 26 suspicious filings, labeling forged signatures a felony. Warnock and project founder Stacey Abrams argued the probe abuses state power, intimidates organizers, and unfairly targets efforts to register over 100,000 minority voters—an act they say undermines electoral fairness. Kemp defended the investigation as a lawful duty, insisting any legitimate registrations will be processed and accusing opponents of false accusations and “dog‑and‑pony” smear tactics. The clash unfolds amid a tight Senate race, with Democrats hoping minority registration will tip the balance, while Republicans claim the inquiry protects election integrity. Both sides frame the dispute as a struggle between legitimate authority and subversive overreach, and between fair democratic participation and cheating through either voter‑suppression or fraudulent registration.

END OF SUMMARY.
