SUMMARY:

The article reflects on the forgiveness expressed by relatives of the Charleston church shooting victims towards the alleged perpetrator, Dylann Roof. While acknowledging the nobility of this act, the author also expresses frustration and irritation towards those who praise the victims for not seeking revenge. The author, a Christian convert from Judaism, contrasts the African-American community's Christian approach to suffering with the Jewish approach of nationalism and self-defense. The article notes that African-Americans have historically responded to racism with non-violence, but also acknowledges a lesser-known dimension of black nationalism that advocates for self-defense and political violence. The author wonders why African-Americans don't respond to racial injustices with more outward violence, given the enormity of their historical suffering. The article concludes that White America is fortunate that the victims of its gravest injustices have chosen a less confrontational response.

END OF SUMMARY.
