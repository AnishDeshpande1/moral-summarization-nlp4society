SUMMARY:

A columnist reflects on the expression of forgiveness by relatives of the victims in the Charleston church shooting, calling it a demonstration of Christian love but also expressing frustration and irritation at the response. The author argues that this approach to suffering and oppression is distinctively Christian and has been an enormous benefit for African-Americans throughout their history in the United States. However, the author also notes that there is another dimension of the black struggle in America, dominated by nationalism and a willingness to countenance political violence in self-defense.

The author contrasts this with Jewish responses to persecution, suggesting that if Jews were treated similarly to African-Americans, they would likely respond with more nationalist and violent measures. The columnist concludes that white America is fortunate that African-American victims have consistently chosen a less confrontational response.

END OF SUMMARY.
