SUMMARY:

The author reflects on the forgiveness expressed by the relatives of the Charleston church shooting victims towards the alleged perpetrator, Dylann Roof. While acknowledging the nobility of this act, the author also expresses frustration and irritation at the expectation that victims of racial violence should respond with forgiveness rather than righteous rage. The author contrasts this Christian approach with a nationalist approach, citing examples of African-American leaders who have advocated for self-defense and political violence in response to racial injustice. The author argues that it is remarkable that African-Americans have not responded with more violence towards white America, given the history of abuses they have suffered. The article raises questions about the morality of forgiveness in the face of unrepentant evil and the expectations placed on victims of racial violence.

END OF SUMMARY.
