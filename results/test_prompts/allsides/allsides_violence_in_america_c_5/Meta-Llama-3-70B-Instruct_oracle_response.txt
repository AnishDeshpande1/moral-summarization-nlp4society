SUMMARY:

The author reflects on the remarkable display of Christian forgiveness by the relatives of the Charleston church shooting victims towards the alleged white supremacist perpetrator, Dylann Roof. While acknowledging the nobility of this act, the author expresses frustration and irritation towards those who praise the victims for refraining from righteous rage. The author questions whether responding to merciless violence with passivity and acceptance is truly commendable. In contrast, the author admires the unwavering insistence of post-Holocaust Jews to protect themselves against mortal enemies, exemplifying nationalism as a powerful way to safeguard against oppression. The author highlights the distinctively Christian approach of African-Americans to their suffering, which has been both beneficial and limiting. The article concludes that white America is fortunate that the victims of its gravest injustices have chosen a less confrontational response, rather than resorting to nationalist violence and political terrorism.

END OF SUMMARY.
