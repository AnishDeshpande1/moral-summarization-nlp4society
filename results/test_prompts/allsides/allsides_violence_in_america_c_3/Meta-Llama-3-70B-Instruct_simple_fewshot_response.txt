SUMMARY:

President Trump visited the Pittsburgh synagogue where 11 worshipers were killed in an anti-Semitic attack, lighting candles for each of the victims. Thousands of protesters, including members of the Jewish community, gathered nearby, criticizing Trump's inflammatory rhetoric, which they believe may have contributed to the attack. Trump's visit was seen as ill-timed by Pittsburgh's mayor, and congressional leaders of both parties declined to join him. The president made no public remarks, but his press secretary said he wanted to show respect for the victims and their families. Trump also visited wounded police officers and met with the wife of one of the slain congregants. The visit came just a week before national elections, and critics argue that Trump's rhetoric has fueled white nationalist and neo-Nazi activity. Protesters cited Trump's tweets about the migrant caravans, which they believe may have provoked the attack.

END OF SUMMARY.
