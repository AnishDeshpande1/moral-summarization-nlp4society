SUMMARY: President Donald Trump visited Pittsburgh’s Tree of Life synagogue on Tuesday, lighting candles for each of the 11 victims of the anti‑Semitic mass shooting and placing stones at a memorial, while thousands protested his presence. Accompanied by First Lady Melania Trump, Ivanka Trump, Jared Kushner, and Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin, he spent about 30 minutes at the site without speaking, describing the visit as “humbling and sad.” The trip, not joined by congressional leaders, occurred a week before the 2020 election and sparked criticism that Trump’s inflammatory rhetoric on immigration and alleged support for extremist narratives may have helped incite the attack. Protesters invoked local icon Fred Rogers and called for bridges, not walls. The shooter, Robert Bowers, faces 29 federal hate‑crime charges and could face the death penalty. Local Jewish leaders have demanded Trump denounce white nationalism before being welcome in Pittsburgh, a sentiment signed by over 78,000 people. END OF SUMMARY.
