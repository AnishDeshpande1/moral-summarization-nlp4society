SUMMARY:

US President Donald Trump visited the Pittsburgh synagogue attacked by an anti-Semitic gunman, where he lit candles for each of the 11 slain worshipers. However, his visit was met with widespread disapproval and protests from thousands of people who criticized his inflammatory rhetoric as potentially having contributed to the attack. Critics argue that Trump's words have meaning and can incite violence against minority groups. The accused gunman, Robert Bowers, was charged with 29 federal felony counts, including hate crimes, and could face the death penalty if convicted.

The visit came just seven days before national elections, where Trump's Republican Party is seeking to maintain control of both houses of Congress. Protest organizers noted that the gunman believed anti-Semitic lies about Jews funding Central American migrant caravans, which they claim was fueled by Trump's rhetoric. A group of local Jewish leaders also issued an open letter to Trump, stating that he is not welcome in Pittsburgh until he fully denounces white nationalism.

END OF SUMMARY.
