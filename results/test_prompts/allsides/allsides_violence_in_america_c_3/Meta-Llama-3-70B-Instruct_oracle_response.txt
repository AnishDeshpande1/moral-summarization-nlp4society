SUMMARY: U.S. President Donald Trump visited the Pittsburgh synagogue where an anti-Semitic gunman attacked and killed 11 worshipers, sparking widespread criticism that his inflammatory rhetoric may have provoked the deadliest attack on American Jewry. Trump lit candles for each of the slain victims and placed stones on a memorial, but made no public remarks. Thousands of protesters, including members of the Jewish community, held an anti-Trump rally, accusing him of contributing to a surge in white nationalist and neo-Nazi activity. The protesters cited Trump's tweets about the Central American migrant caravans, which they said may have fueled the gunman's beliefs. The visit came just seven days before national elections, with many criticizing Trump's rhetoric and calling for him to denounce white nationalism. The first funerals for the victims were held earlier, with more than 1,800 people paying respects. The accused gunman faces 29 federal felony counts, including hate crimes, and could face the death penalty if convicted. END OF SUMMARY.
