However, his trip was met with widespread disapproval and protests, with many critics arguing that his inflammatory rhetoric may have contributed to the deadliest attack on American Jewry. The president's visit was seen as an attempt to exploit the tragedy for political gain, rather than a genuine expression of condolences. The protesters, including members of the Jewish community, held an anti-Trump rally, singing Old Testament psalms and carrying signs with slogans such as "We build bridges not walls." The visit has heightened the national debate over Trump's rhetoric, which critics say has encouraged far-right extremists. The moral framing of this article suggests that Trump's actions are an affront to authority, care, and fairness, and that his rhetoric has contributed to a culture of harm and subversion.
