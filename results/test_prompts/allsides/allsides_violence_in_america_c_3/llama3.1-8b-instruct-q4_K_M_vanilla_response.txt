SUMMARY:
U.S. President Donald Trump visited the Pittsburgh synagogue attacked by an anti-Semitic gunman, where he lit candles for each of the 11 slain worshipers. Thousands protested his presence in the city, criticizing his inflammatory rhetoric that may have helped provoke the attack. Trump's visit came as he faced widespread disapproval over his handling of the incident and its aftermath. The president made no public remarks during his 30-minute visit to the synagogue, but was accompanied by first lady Melania Trump and other White House officials.

The visit took place just seven days before national elections that will determine whether the Republican Party maintains control of Congress or if Democrats seize a majority in one chamber or both. Protesters held an anti-Trump rally near the synagogue, singing Old Testament psalms and carrying signs invoking lessons on friendship and kindness from the late Fred Rogers.

Critics have accused Trump's rhetoric of contributing to a surge in white nationalist and neo-Nazi activity. The attack has heightened a national debate over Trump's language, with some arguing that his words may have helped provoke the bloodshed.

END OF SUMMARY.
