**SUMMARY:**  
President Donald Trump visited the Tree of Life synagogue in Pittsburgh, the site of an anti‑Semitic mass shooting that left 11 worshippers slain and many more wounded. Accompanied by First Lady Melania Trump, he entered the temple to light yahrzeit candles, placed a stone on a memorial marker, and spent a brief, “humbling and sad” moment with families, police officers and the wife of a victim. Thousands of protesters gathered nearby, chanting for bridges, not walls, and invoking the legacy of Fred Rogers. Critics condemned Trump’s inflammatory rhetoric and anti‑immigrant lies, arguing they may have provoked the hate‑filled attack and fueled white nationalist, extremist activity. Local Jewish leaders demanded that Trump denounce white nationalism before he would be welcome in Pittsburgh. The visit occurred a week before the national elections that will decide control of Congress.
