SUMMARY:

In a major victory for religious liberty, Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker has withdrawn restrictions on religious services amid the coronavirus pandemic, following lawsuits from the Thomas More Society. The governor's decision marks the end of a shameful chapter of discrimination by the government against houses of worship and religious leaders. The Thomas More Society hailed the move as a total and complete victory for people of faith, who can now practice their religion without abusive limits and restrictions. The governor's administration had previously imposed mandatory restrictions on church activities, but has now replaced them with recommendations from the Illinois Department of Health. This guidance does not obligate or encourage places of worship to resume in-person activity, and instead strongly recommends continuing remote services, particularly for vulnerable individuals. The Thomas More Society's lawsuits had previously led to the loosening of restrictions on "drive-in" and outdoor religious services. Today, people of faith across Illinois can breathe a little freer, knowing that their fundamental freedom of religion is critical to a self-governing democracy.

END OF SUMMARY.
