SUMMARY:
Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker has rescinded his COVID‑19 mandates that limited worship services to ten or fewer attendees, replacing them with non‑binding health department recommendations. The change follows three lawsuits filed by the Thomas More Society on behalf of churches, which the firm hailed as a “total and complete victory for people of faith.” The society argued that the governor had abused pandemic authority to subvert religious liberty, discriminating against worshippers and violating fairness by imposing unequal restrictions. While health officials stress the continued need to protect vulnerable congregants—emphasizing care for the elderly and those with co‑morbidities—their guidance stops short of obligating in‑person services, reflecting a balance between public‑health authority and respect for sacred worship practices. The lawsuits prompted Pritzker to lift limits on drive‑in, outdoor, and other forms of worship, ending what the society described as a “shameful chapter of discrimination.” The episode underscores tensions between governmental authority, the care of public health, fairness toward religious communities, and the sanctity of religious freedom in a democratic society.  
END OF SUMMARY.
