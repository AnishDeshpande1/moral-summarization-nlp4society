SUMMARY:

Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker has withdrawn restrictions on religious services amid the coronavirus pandemic, following lawsuits from the Thomas More Society. The nonprofit law firm hailed the decision as a victory after Pritzker said he would remove mandates requiring no more than 10 people to be present at services. Instead, the Illinois Department of Health will provide recommendations for places of worship. The Thomas More Society declared total victory for its church clients, stating that fundamental freedom of religion is critical to a self-governing democracy. The firm had filed three lawsuits against Pritzker's order on behalf of several churches and their pastors. Pritzker's decision comes after he lifted restrictions on "drive-in" services and outdoor religious services following complaints from the firm. The updated guidance aims to balance the desire for more worship options with keeping people safe during the pandemic.
