SUMMARY:

Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker has withdrawn restrictions on religious services amid the coronavirus pandemic, following lawsuits from the Thomas More Society. The governor will replace mandates on Illinois churches with recommendations from the Illinois Department of Health. The Thomas More Society hailed the decision as a "total and complete victory" for people of faith, after filing three lawsuits against Pritzker's order. The lawsuits argued that the restrictions violated religious liberty and forced churches to choose between obeying the law and exercising their faith. The governor's decision marks a significant shift, as he had previously imposed limits on church activities as part of his coronavirus stay-at-home orders. The Thomas More Society's Vice President and Senior Counsel, Peter Breen, emphasized that the fundamental freedom of religion is "critical to a self-governing democracy." The decision is seen as a victory for religious freedom and a rebuke to government overreach.

END OF SUMMARY.
