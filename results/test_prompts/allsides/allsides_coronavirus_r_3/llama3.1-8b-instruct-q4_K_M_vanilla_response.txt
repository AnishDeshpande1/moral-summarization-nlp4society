SUMMARY:

The governor of Illinois, J.B. Pritzker, has lifted restrictions on religious services amid the coronavirus pandemic following lawsuits from the Thomas More Society. The nonprofit law firm had filed three lawsuits against Pritzker's order, which required no more than 10 people to be present at church services. In response to the lawsuits, Pritzker announced that he would replace mandates with recommendations from the Illinois Department of Health. According to the department, places of worship are still strongly recommended to continue remote services for vulnerable individuals.

The Thomas More Society hailed the decision as a "total and complete victory" for people of faith in Illinois. The firm's vice president, Peter Breen, stated that Pritzker's administration had abused the pandemic to stomp on religious liberty. With the lifting of restrictions, churches in Illinois can now choose how and where to practice their religion.

END OF SUMMARY.
