SUMMARY:  
Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker withdrew the COVID‑19 limits on religious activities, replacing mandatory restrictions with health department recommendations. The Thomas More Society hailed the move as a total victory for religious liberty, declaring an end to what it called governmental abuse and discrimination against churches. Vice President Peter Breen said the governor’s decision to lift and loosen all mandates allows people of faith to practice worship more freely and to breathe a little freer, while still trying to keep congregants safe. The nonprofit law firm cited three lawsuits that secured the removal of limits on drive‑in, outdoor, and indoor services, prompting the governor to remove the earlier restrictions. Health officials stressed that the guidance does not obligate in‑person worship and that remote services remain strongly recommended for vulnerable individuals.  

END OF SUMMARY.
