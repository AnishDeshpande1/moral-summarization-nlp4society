SUMMARY:

Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker has withdrawn restrictions on religious services amid the coronavirus pandemic following lawsuits from the Thomas More Society. The governor will replace mandates on Illinois churches with recommendations from the Illinois Department of Health. The new guidance does not obligate or encourage in-person worship, instead recommending remote services, especially for vulnerable individuals. The Thomas More Society hailed the decision as a "total and complete victory" for people of faith, citing the protection of religious liberty. The firm had filed three lawsuits against Pritzker's order, which led to the loosening of restrictions on "drive-in" and outdoor religious services. The governor's decision marks the end of a "shameful chapter of discrimination" against houses of worship and religious leaders.

END OF SUMMARY.
