SUMMARY: Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker announced the removal of strict COVID‑19 limits on church gatherings after being sued by the Thomas More Society. The nonprofit law firm celebrated the move as a “total and complete victory for people of faith,” arguing that the governor had abused pandemic powers to suppress religious liberty. While the state health department now offers only advisory guidance—still urging remote worship for vulnerable populations—the legal challenges forced Pritzker to drop restrictions on indoor, drive‑in and outdoor services. Thomas More Society’s Vice President Peter Breen hailed the decision as the end of “a shameful chapter of discrimination” and emphasized that free religion is essential to a self‑governing democracy. The society filed three suits on behalf of churches, prompting the governor to ease all mandates. Health officials note that worship remains “essential” and that the new guidance balances desire for in‑person worship with safety. END OF SUMMARY.
