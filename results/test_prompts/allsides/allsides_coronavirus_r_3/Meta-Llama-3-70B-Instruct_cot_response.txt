SUMMARY:
The governor of Illinois, J.B. Pritzker, has withdrawn restrictions on religious services amid the coronavirus pandemic, following lawsuits from the Thomas More Society. This decision is hailed as a "total and complete victory" for people of faith, as it removes mandates on Illinois churches and replaces them with recommendations from the Illinois Department of Health. The governor's administration was accused of "abused" and "stomping" on the religious liberty of the people of Illinois. The Thomas More Society, a nonprofit law firm, filed three lawsuits against Pritzker's order, arguing that it was a "shameful chapter of discrimination" against houses of worship and religious leaders. The firm's Vice President, Peter Breen, emphasized that fundamental freedom of religion is "critical to a self-governing democracy." The updated guidance is designed to take into account the desire for more worship options, while also trying to keep people safe as possible. END OF SUMMARY.
