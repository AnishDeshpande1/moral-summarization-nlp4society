SUMMARY:

In a major victory for religious freedom, Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker has withdrawn restrictions on religious services amid the coronavirus pandemic, following lawsuits from the Thomas More Society. The governor's decision marks a complete reversal of his previous mandates, which limited church services to 10 people or less. The new guidelines from the Illinois Department of Health are now merely recommendations, rather than obligations. The Thomas More Society hailed the decision as a "total and complete victory" for people of faith, who can now exercise their religious liberty without government overreach. The lawsuits filed by the Society exposed the government's abuse of power and discrimination against houses of worship. The victory is a testament to the critical importance of protecting fundamental freedoms, including the freedom of religion, in a self-governing democracy. By backing down, Pritzker has finally acknowledged the essential nature of religious activities during the pandemic.

END OF SUMMARY.
