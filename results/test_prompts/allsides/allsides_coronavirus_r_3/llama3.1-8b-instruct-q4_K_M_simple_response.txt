SUMMARY:
Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker has withdrawn restrictions on religious services amid the coronavirus pandemic, following lawsuits from the Thomas More Society. The governor's decision comes after multiple lawsuits were filed against him for imposing limits on church activities as part of his stay-at-home orders. According to Peter Breen, Vice President and Senior Counsel at the Thomas More Society, this is a "total and complete victory" for people of faith in Illinois. Breen argued that the governor had abused the pandemic to stomp on religious liberty, but has now handed a complete victory to churches by issuing guidelines rather than mandatory restrictions. The updated guidance allows churches to resume in-person activities while still recommending remote services for vulnerable individuals.

The Thomas More Society filed three lawsuits against Pritzker's order, leading to the loosening of other restrictions as well. Breen emphasized that fundamental freedom of religion is critical to a self-governing democracy and that people of faith should not have had to file repeated lawsuits to secure their basic rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
