SUMMARY: Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker announced the removal of mandatory limits on church gatherings that had capped attendance at ten people, replacing them with non‑binding health department recommendations. The change follows lawsuits filed by the conservative legal group Thomas More Society on behalf of several churches, which the group hailed as a “total and complete victory” for religious liberty. The new guidance advises worship places to continue offering remote services, especially for vulnerable individuals, but does not require in‑person worship. The Thomas More Society cited three suits—representing The Beloved Church, Jesus House Restoration Ministries, and a group of Lake County churches—that led to the lifting of earlier restrictions on drive‑in and outdoor services. The Illinois Department of Health emphasized that religious activities remain essential and that the guidance aims to balance worship options with safety. Pritzker did not comment. END OF SUMMARY.
