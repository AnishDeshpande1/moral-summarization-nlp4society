SUMMARY:

In a major victory for religious freedom, Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker has withdrawn restrictions on religious services amid the coronavirus pandemic following lawsuits from the Thomas More Society. The governor's decision is a complete victory for people of faith, as it removes mandatory limits on church activities and replaces them with recommendations from the Illinois Department of Health. The Thomas More Society, a nonprofit law firm, hailed the decision as a triumph, stating that the governor's administration had "abused the COVID-19 pandemic to stomp on the religious liberty of the people of Illinois." The firm argued that the fundamental freedom of religion is critical to a self-governing democracy, and that people of faith should not have had to file lawsuits to secure their basic rights. The decision marks the end of a "shameful chapter of discrimination" against houses of worship and religious leaders.

END OF SUMMARY.
