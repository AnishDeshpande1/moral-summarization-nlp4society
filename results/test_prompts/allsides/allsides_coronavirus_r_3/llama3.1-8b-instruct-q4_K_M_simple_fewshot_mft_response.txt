SUMMARY:

Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker has withdrawn restrictions on religious services amid the coronavirus pandemic, following lawsuits from the Thomas More Society. The nonprofit law firm hailed the decision as a victory for people of faith, stating that it marked "the end of a shameful chapter of discrimination" by the government against houses of worship and religious leaders. The governor's office had previously mandated that no more than 10 or more people could be present at services, but will now only provide recommendations to places of worship on how to proceed safely. The Thomas More Society filed three lawsuits against Pritzker's order, citing violations of fundamental freedom of religion. Peter Breen, Vice President and Senior Counsel for the Thomas More Society, stated that "people of faith across Illinois should breathe a little freer" as a result of the decision. The move is seen as a victory for those who fought against what they saw as an abuse of power by the government.
