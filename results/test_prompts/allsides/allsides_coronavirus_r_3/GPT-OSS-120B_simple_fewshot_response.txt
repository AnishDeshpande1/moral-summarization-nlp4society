SUMMARY:
Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker announced the removal of his COVID‑19 mandates that limited worship services to ten or fewer attendees, replacing them with non‑binding health recommendations. The change follows three lawsuits filed by the conservative, pro‑religion nonprofit Thomas More Society on behalf of churches in Illinois. The society hailed the move as “a total and complete victory for people of faith,” saying the governor had abused pandemic powers to suppress religious liberty and that the new guidance respects the essential nature of worship while still urging caution for vulnerable congregants. The lawsuits prompted Pritzker to first ease rules on drive‑in services, then on outdoor gatherings, and now to drop all location‑specific mandates. Thomas More Society’s Vice President Peter Breen declared the decision the end of a “shameful chapter of discrimination” and praised the restored freedom for Illinois churches. State health officials reiterated that remote worship remains strongly recommended for safety.  

END OF SUMMARY.
