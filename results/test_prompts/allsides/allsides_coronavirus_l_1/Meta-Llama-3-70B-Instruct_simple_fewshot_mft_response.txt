SUMMARY:

The article argues that Donald Trump's mishandling of the COVID-19 pandemic has made the US more vulnerable to another pandemic. By attempting to withdraw from the World Health Organization, Trump has further damaged international bonds and diminished America's global standing. This recklessness will harm not only the WHO but also the US, which may not receive warnings or support if a new crisis emerges. The article emphasizes the importance of vigilance and caution in preventing the spread of a new respiratory virus. It highlights the challenges faced by countries like the US, Brazil, Russia, and India, which are struggling to control COVID-19 and may not be able to detect a new virus in time. The author condemns Trump's subversion of authority and lack of care for human life, which has led to a compromised global response to the pandemic.
