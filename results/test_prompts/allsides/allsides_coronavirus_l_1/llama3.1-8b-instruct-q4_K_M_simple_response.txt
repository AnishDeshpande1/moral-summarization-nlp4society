SUMMARY:

The article criticizes Donald Trump's handling of the US response to the pandemic, arguing that his actions have made the country more vulnerable to another outbreak. By trying to withdraw from the World Health Organization (WHO), Trump is further fraying international bonds and diminishing America's global standing. This could lead to a lack of support or warnings if a new crisis emerges.

However, the article also notes an optimistic scenario where people remain vigilant against COVID-19, which could theoretically protect against a second respiratory virus. Tests used to diagnose COVID-19 can be tweaked to cover other viruses, and existing vaccines could be adapted to protect against new flu strains.

The article contrasts countries like South Korea and Germany, which have successfully controlled COVID-19, with nations like the US and Brazil, which are struggling. In these latter countries, a new virus would be harder to spot due to redirected resources and overwhelmed healthcare systems.
