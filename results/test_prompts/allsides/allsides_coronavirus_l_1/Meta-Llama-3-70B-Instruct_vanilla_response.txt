SUMMARY:

The article argues that the US, under Donald Trump's leadership, is more vulnerable to another pandemic due to its mishandling of the current COVID-19 outbreak. The country's international standing has been damaged, and its withdrawal from global alliances may discourage other nations from cooperating. This could lead to a lack of warnings and support if a new crisis emerges. While a second respiratory virus may be harder to spread if people remain vigilant, the US's struggling healthcare system may not be able to detect it quickly. Resources such as equipment, healthcare workers, and public-health experts are already stretched thin or burning out, making it difficult to respond to a new virus. In contrast, countries like South Korea, New Zealand, and Germany, which have successfully controlled COVID-19, may be better equipped to handle a new pandemic.

END OF SUMMARY.
