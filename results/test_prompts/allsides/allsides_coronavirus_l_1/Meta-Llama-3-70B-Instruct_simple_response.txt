SUMMARY:

Donald Trump's mishandling of the COVID-19 pandemic has made the US more vulnerable to another pandemic. His attempts to pull out of the World Health Organization have frayed international bonds, which could encourage other nations to follow suit, ultimately harming global health efforts. The US's damaged international standing and selfish actions, such as buying up the world's stock of important drugs, will make it less likely to receive warnings or support in the event of a new crisis. While a second respiratory virus could be detected and controlled if people remain vigilant, the US's struggling healthcare system and redirected resources make it less likely to spot and respond to a new outbreak. In contrast, countries like South Korea, New Zealand, and Germany, which have successfully controlled COVID-19, are better prepared to handle a new pandemic.

END OF SUMMARY.
