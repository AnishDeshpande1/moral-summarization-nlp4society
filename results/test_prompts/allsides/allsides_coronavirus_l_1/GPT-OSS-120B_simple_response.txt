SUMMARY:  
The article condemns former President Donald Trump for weakening America’s pandemic defenses, arguing his mishandling of COVID‑19 and attempts to withdraw from the World Health Organization have eroded international trust and left the U.S. more exposed to a future outbreak. The moral warning is that disengagement from global health institutions not only harms the WHO when it is most needed, but also diminishes America’s ability to receive warnings or aid in a new crisis. While an optimistic scenario envisions vigilant citizens, mask‑wearing, and multiplex testing that could curb a second respiratory virus, the piece stresses that such safeguards are unlikely in overstretched nations like the U.S., Brazil, Russia, and India. Resources already depleted by COVID‑19—tests, personnel, and data analysts—would be further strained, allowing a new pathogen to spread unnoticed. The author frames these risks as a consequence of selfish, short‑sighted leadership that prioritizes political petulance over global cooperation and public health.  

END OF SUMMARY.
