SUMMARY: Donald Trump's mishandling of the COVID-19 pandemic has made the US more vulnerable to another pandemic. His attempts to pull the US out of the World Health Organization have frayed international bonds, which could harm the global response to a new crisis. The US's failure to adequately prepare for COVID-19 has diminished its international standing, making it less likely to receive warnings or support if a new crisis emerges. A new respiratory virus could spread unchecked in countries like the US, Brazil, Russia, and India, which are stretched and struggling to control COVID-19. The resources normally used to detect potential viruses have been redirected for COVID-19, making it harder to spot a new virus. In contrast, countries like South Korea, New Zealand, and Germany, which have successfully controlled COVID-19, are better prepared to respond to a new pandemic. END OF SUMMARY.
