SUMMARY:
The article condemns President Trump’s misuse of authority, arguing that his attempts to withdraw the United States from the World Health Organization undermine global cooperation and weaken America’s capacity to respond to future pandemics. By fraying international bonds and abandoning multilateral health institutions, the administration endangers not only the WHO but also U.S. citizens, who may receive fewer warnings and less support when a new pathogen emerges. The piece stresses the care‑oriented imperative to keep people safe: continued vigilance—mask‑wearing, distancing, and avoiding crowds—can protect the public from a second respiratory virus, and existing testing and vaccine infrastructure could be adapted to detect and combat it. However, the current strain on health‑care resources, already stretched by COVID‑19, means a new virus would further harm an already vulnerable population. The author warns that Trump’s subversive actions jeopardize both national and global health, calling for responsible leadership that prioritizes public welfare over political maneuvering.  
END OF SUMMARY.
