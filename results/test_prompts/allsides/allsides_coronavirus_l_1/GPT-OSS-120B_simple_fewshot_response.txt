SUMMARY:
The article warns that President Donald Trump’s mishandling of the COVID‑19 crisis has left the United States vulnerable to a second pandemic and has eroded its global leadership. By attempting to withdraw from the World Health Organization, Trump risks prompting other countries to disengage from vital international health cooperation, further weakening the WHO at a critical moment and diminishing America’s ability to receive early warnings or assistance. The piece argues that continued vigilance—mask‑wearing, distancing and avoiding indoor crowds—could help shield the nation from a new respiratory pathogen, and that existing testing platforms and flu‑vaccine technology provide a modest defensive edge. However, the United States, unlike countries that have contained COVID‑19 (South Korea, New Zealand, Germany), faces strained testing supplies, exhausted health‑care workers and overburdened public‑health infrastructure, making it easier for a novel virus to spread unnoticed. The moral implication is clear: Trump’s failure to lead responsibly and to maintain international health partnerships endangers both domestic and global safety, and urgent, coordinated action is needed to prevent a compounded health disaster.  
END OF SUMMARY.
