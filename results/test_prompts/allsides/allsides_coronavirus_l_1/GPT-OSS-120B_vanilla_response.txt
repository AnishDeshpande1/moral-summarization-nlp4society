SUMMARY: The article warns that the United States, under Donald Trump’s leadership, has weakened its pandemic preparedness by attempting to withdraw from the World Health Organization, damaging international cooperation and its own reputation. This disengagement could prompt other nations to follow suit, reducing global warning systems and aid during future health crises. While vigilant public behavior (social distancing, masking) may help guard against a second respiratory virus, the U.S. faces significant challenges: strained testing resources, exhausted healthcare workers, and redirected surveillance capacity. Advances such as multiplex tests and adaptable flu vaccines could aid detection and response, but their impact depends on a country’s overall pandemic control. Nations that have contained COVID‑19 (e.g., South Korea, New Zealand, Germany) are better positioned for a new outbreak, whereas countries like the U.S., Brazil, Russia, and India risk having a new virus go unnoticed and spread rapidly. The article underscores that without restored international collaboration and robust domestic resources, a second pandemic could overwhelm an already faltering U.S. system. END OF SUMMARY.
