SUMMARY:

House Republicans confronted Rep. Adam Schiff, Chairman of the House Intelligence Committee, demanding his resignation due to his two-year investigation into President Trump's alleged conspiracy with Russia. Schiff defended himself by listing Trump associates' contacts with foreigners, but failed to provide evidence of coordination with Russia in the 2016 election. Republicans cited Special Counsel Robert Mueller's findings, which cleared Trump's campaign of conspiring with Russia. Schiff reacted angrily, calling Trump's actions "immoral," "unethical," and "unpatriotic." House Speaker Nancy Pelosi defended Schiff, calling Republicans "scaredy-cats." Republicans accused Schiff of promoting a false narrative and abusing his power, likening him to Sen. Joseph McCarthy. They also criticized Schiff's hiring of a prosecutor, Daniel Goldman, who endorsed the Christopher Steele dossier, which Mueller rejected.

END OF SUMMARY.
