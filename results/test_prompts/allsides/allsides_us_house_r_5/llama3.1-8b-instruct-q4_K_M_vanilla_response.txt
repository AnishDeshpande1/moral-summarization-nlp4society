SUMMARY:

A group of Republican lawmakers have called for House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff's resignation, citing his two-year investigation into President Trump's alleged collusion with Russia. They argue that the Mueller report found no evidence of such collusion and that Schiff has continued to promote a "demonstrably false narrative." Schiff defended himself by listing instances of Trump associates interacting with Russians, but did not provide evidence of coordinated election interference.

The Republicans signed a letter accusing Schiff of abusing his power and damaging the integrity of the committee. Rep. Devin Nunes and others echoed these sentiments, comparing Schiff to Sen. Joseph McCarthy's tactics during the 1950s Red Scare. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi defended Schiff, saying he is "proud" of his work and that Republicans are just "scaredy-cats."

The dispute highlights the ongoing partisan divide over Russia's role in the 2016 election and the Trump campaign's potential involvement.

END OF SUMMARY.
