SUMMARY:

Republicans on the House Intelligence Committee confronted Chairman Adam Schiff, demanding his resignation and accusing him of abusing his post to push a false “Russia‑collusion” narrative despite Mueller’s conclusion that no conspiracy was found. In a signed letter, GOP members—including former chair Devin Nunes and Rep. Michael Turner, who likened Schiff to Senator McCarthy—asserted they have “no faith” in his ability to fulfill constitutional duties and called his continued investigations “immoral,” “unethical,” and “unpatriotic.” Schiff defended the hearing, citing contacts between Trump associates and Russian actors, and warned that such contacts are corrupt. Democrats, led by Speaker Nancy Pelosi, praised Schiff’s work and dismissed Republican attacks as fear‑driven. The moral framing presented by the article paints Schiff as a partisan agitator endangering governmental integrity, while Republicans are cast as defenders of truth and constitutional order. END OF SUMMARY.
