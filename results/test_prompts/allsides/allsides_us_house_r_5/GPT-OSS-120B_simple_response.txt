SUMMARY:  
Republicans denounced House Intelligence Chairman Adam Schiff, demanding his resignation and claiming they “have no faith” in his ability to fulfill his constitutional duties after two years of what they label “immoral” and “unpatriotic” conspiracy attacks on President Trump. Schiff defended his actions by citing questionable contacts between Trump allies and foreign actors, but he offered no proof of direct Russian election‑interference coordination, a point the GOP highlights to argue his claims are false and ethically corrupt. The GOP letter cites Mueller’s closure of the Russia probe without finding collusion and accuses Schiff of abusing his position, spreading a “demonstrably false narrative,” and damaging institutional integrity. Schiff retorted, calling the Trump‑Russia contacts “immoral, unethical, unpatriotic, and corrupt,” and likened Republican criticism to McCarthyism, accusing them of reviving a discredited witch‑hunt playbook. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi defended Schiff, calling Republicans “scaredy‑cats.” The dispute centers on competing moral judgments: Republicans portray Schiff’s pursuit as a baseless, damaging moral panic, while Schiff frames the Trump‑Russia contacts as a profound ethical breach.  

END OF SUMMARY.
