SUMMARY:

House Republicans have called for Rep. Adam Schiff to resign as Chairman of the House Intelligence Committee, citing his two-year crusade against President Trump and his refusal to accept the findings of Special Counsel Robert Mueller's investigation. Schiff has been accused of promoting a "demonstrably false narrative" of collusion between Trump associates and Russia, despite Mueller's conclusion that there was no such conspiracy. Republicans argue that Schiff's actions have damaged the integrity of the Committee and undermined faith in U.S. government institutions. Schiff, however, remains defiant, labeling Trump's interactions with Russians as "immoral," "unethical," and "unpatriotic." House Speaker Nancy Pelosi has defended Schiff, dismissing Republican criticism as a sign of fear. The moral framing of this story is clear: Schiff's pursuit of truth and accountability is pitted against Republican efforts to obscure and deny the truth. Schiff's refusal to back down is a testament to his commitment to upholding the integrity of the Committee and the Constitution.

END OF SUMMARY.
