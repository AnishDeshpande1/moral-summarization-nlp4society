SUMMARY:

Republicans are calling for House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff's resignation, citing his "incompatible" actions as chairman and accusing him of "interfering" in the investigation. They claim he has "abused" his position by promoting a "demonstrably false narrative" about Russian collusion with Trump's campaign. Schiff defended himself by listing instances where Trump associates interacted with Russians, but Republicans argue that these interactions were not evidence of collusion.

The Republicans' letter to Schiff states that they have "no faith in your ability to discharge your duties" as chairman and urge his resignation due to his "actions both past and present." They accuse him of promoting a false narrative despite the findings of Special Counsel Robert Mueller, which refuted claims of significant evidence of collusion. Schiff responded by saying that he thinks it's "immoral," "unethical," and "unpatriotic" for Trump associates to interact with Russians during the campaign.

House Speaker Nancy Pelosi defended Schiff, calling Republicans "scaredy-cats." The dispute highlights ongoing tensions between Democrats and Republicans on the committee over how to investigate Russian interference in the 2016 election.

END OF SUMMARY.
