SUMMARY:  
Republicans confronted House Intelligence Committee Chairman Rep. Adam B. Schiff, urging his resignation because they claim his “actions” are “incompatible” with his duty to “discharge” oversight of the intelligence community. They cite the Mueller report’s finding that there was no proven Russian “interference” or “collusion,” accusing Schiff of continuing to “proclaim” a false “narrative” and to “investigate” a phantom threat. Schiff defended himself by listing contacts in which Trump associates “interacted” with Russian‑linked individuals, calling those contacts “immoral,” “unethical,” “unpatriotic,” and “corrupt.” He rebuffed the GOP letter, saying the Republican accusations are “misrepresented” and that their push to “add” prosecutors rather than intelligence analysts only “damages” the Committee’s credibility. Rep. Michael R. Turner likened Schiff to Senator Joseph McCarthy, accusing him of “abusing” his power and “hurling” conspiracy charges. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi praised Schiff, calling the GOP critics “scaredy‑cats.” The clash centers on whether Schiff’s pursuit of the Russia story is a legitimate “oversight” function or a politically driven “narrative” that has “undermined” faith in U.S. institutions.  
END OF SUMMARY.
