SUMMARY:  
Republicans confronted House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff, demanding his resignation and accusing him of persisting in a false “collusion” narrative despite Special Counsel Robert Mueller’s conclusion that there was no criminal conspiracy between the Trump campaign and Russia. The GOP‑led letter, signed by Rep. Devin Nunes and other Republicans, criticized Schiff for reopening the Russia probe and for allegedly abusing his position to promote misinformation. During a hearing on Russian influence operations, Schiff cited contacts between Trump associates and Russian-linked individuals, though he did not present evidence of coordinated election interference. He defended his actions as exposing unethical behavior, while House Speaker Nancy Pelosi backed him. Republicans countered that any alleged collusion involved the Clinton campaign and dismissed the Steele dossier as a Kremlin hoax, likening Schiff’s tactics to McCarthyism. The dispute highlights deep partisan divisions over the investigation into Russian interference and the credibility of the committee’s oversight.  

END OF SUMMARY.
