SUMMARY:

House Republicans have accused Rep. Adam Schiff of misusing his authority as chairman of the Intelligence Committee, citing his continued claims of Trump-Russia collusion despite the conclusion of Special Counsel Robert Mueller's probe. The Republicans claim that Schiff has damaged the integrity of the committee and undermined faith in U.S. government institutions by promoting a "demonstrably false narrative." They also accuse him of abusing his power by hiring prosecutors instead of intelligence analysts, and liken him to Sen. Joseph McCarthy for his alleged abuse of authority. Schiff has defended himself, stating that he believes Trump's interactions with Russians during the campaign were immoral, unethical, and unpatriotic. The Republicans have called for Schiff's resignation as chairman, citing their lack of faith in his ability to discharge his duties. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi has supported Schiff, saying that the Republicans are "just scaredy-cats" who don't know what to do.

The moral framing of this issue is clear: Schiff's pursuit of truth and accountability is being attacked by those who seek to undermine the legitimacy of the investigation and protect the president at all costs. The Republicans' accusations of authority subversion, unfair treatment, and betrayal of trust highlight their concerns about Schiff's actions as chairman. END OF SUMMARY.
