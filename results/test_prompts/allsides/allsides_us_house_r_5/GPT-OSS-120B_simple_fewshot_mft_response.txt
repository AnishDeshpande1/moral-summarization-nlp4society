SUMMARY:

Republicans confronted House Intelligence Committee Chairman Rep. Adam Schiff, accusing him of subverting the Committee’s constitutional authority by persisting in a “false narrative” of Trump‑Russia collusion despite Mueller’s finding of no criminal conspiracy. They argue Schiff is cheating the American public and the rule of law, abusing his chairmanship to promote unsubstantiated claims and thereby betraying the loyalty owed to the institution and the nation. The GOP letter, signed by Rep. Devin Nunes and others, declares they have “no faith” in Schiff’s ability to discharge his duties and demand his immediate resignation as a matter of restoring proper authority and institutional integrity. Schiff defended his actions, listing dubious contacts between Trump associates and Russian actors and labeling the president’s behavior as “immoral, unethical, unpatriotic and corrupt,” thereby framing the president’s conduct as a breach of national sanctity. Democrats, led by Speaker Nancy Pelosi, rallied behind Schiff, portraying the Republicans as fearful “scared‑cats” trying to silence legitimate oversight. The clash is cast as a battle over who upholds true authority, fairness, loyalty to the country, and the moral sanctity of American governance.

END OF SUMMARY.
