SUMMARY:

A heated confrontation took place between House Intelligence Committee Chairman Rep. Adam B. Schiff (D-CA) and Republican members who accused him of promoting conspiracy charges against President Trump without evidence. Republicans cited the Mueller investigation's conclusion that there was no collusion between Trump associates and Russia, urging Schiff to resign due to his "inability to discharge your duties." Schiff defended himself by listing instances of inappropriate contacts between Trump associates and foreign nationals. He called such interactions "immoral," "unethical," and "unpatriotic."

House Republicans compared Schiff's actions to McCarthyism, claiming he has abused his power by promoting a false narrative about Russian collusion. They accused him of hiring prosecutors instead of intelligence analysts, pointing to the Christopher Steele dossier as an example of promoted conspiracy charges. The confrontation highlights the ongoing partisan divisions in Congress over the handling of the Russia investigation and the credibility of Schiff's leadership.

END OF SUMMARY.
