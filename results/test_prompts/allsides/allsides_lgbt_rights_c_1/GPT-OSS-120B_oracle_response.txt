SUMMARY:  
Julie Berman, a Toronto trans rights activist, was **murdered** on Dec. 22, 2019 after previously speaking out about **transphobia** at a 2017 Trans Day of Remembrance. While **vocal** about a friend’s earlier **murder**, Berman herself became the latest **victim** of **assault** and **violence**; police found her with severe **head injuries** and she was later pronounced **dead**. Her friend Davina Hader, a member of The 519, described the loss as **heartbreaking** and noted Berman’s **activism** and long‑term **involvement** in community **events** and **workshops** aimed at making trans people feel **safe**. The suspect, Colin Harnack, is not known to be part of the LGBTQ community. Studies show trans people in Ontario are **targets** of **threatened**, **harassed**, and physical **assaults**, with many remaining **vulnerable** and under‑reported. Recent **deaths** and **fatalities**, including Alloura Wells, have highlighted systemic failures in how cases are **handled**. Pride Toronto and The 519 have responded with increased **support**, **programs**, and education, yet leaders say the efforts are still **insufficient**. The **incident** underscores the urgent need for broader **awareness** and a truly **welcoming** society where trans individuals can **participate** without fear.  

END OF SUMMARY.
