SUMMARY: Julie Berman, a Toronto trans‑rights activist and longtime volunteer at The 519, was found dead with head injuries on Dec. 22, 2019 and has been charged with second‑degree murder. Berman had spoken at a 2017 Trans Day of Remembrance event about transphobia and the murder of a trans friend, urging vigilance in the community. Her friend Davina Hader, also of The 519, recalled Berman’s plea not to release the event video and now considers releasing it. Police identified the suspect, 29‑year‑old Colin Harnack, as not being part of the LGBTQ community. The murder underscores persistent violence against trans people in Ontario; a 2015 Trans PULSE study found 20 % of trans Ontarians experienced physical or sexual assault and 34 % faced verbal threats, with many not reporting crimes. Pride Toronto’s executive director Olivia Nuamah noted three recent trans deaths in Toronto, including Alloura Wells, and criticized past police and community‑center handling of her case. While The 519 and Pride Toronto have increased education and support programs, officials say they remain insufficient. Berman’s death is seen as a tragic reminder of the ongoing danger faced by trans individuals in the city. END OF SUMMARY.
