SUMMARY:

Julie Berman, a Toronto trans rights activist, was murdered on December 22, 2019. Berman had spoken out against transphobia and violence against the trans community, and her death is a tragic irony. She was a vocal advocate for her community, and her murder has sparked outrage and sadness among her friends and allies. The suspect, Colin Harnack, 29, has been charged with second-degree murder. Berman's death is part of a larger pattern of violence against trans people, who are disproportionately targeted for physical and sexual assault, harassment, and murder. According to a 2015 study, 20% of Ontario's trans people had been physically or sexually assaulted, and 34% had been verbally threatened or harassed. The incident highlights the need for greater awareness, education, and support for the trans community, as well as accountability from law enforcement and social services. Berman's legacy will be remembered as a call to action against transphobia and violence.
