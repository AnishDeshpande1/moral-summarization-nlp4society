SUMMARY:  
Julie Berman, a longtime Toronto trans‑rights activist, was found dead with head injuries on Dec. 22, 2019, after being assaulted in her Brunswick‑Harbord neighbourhood. The suspect, Colin Harnack, is not known to be part of the LGBTQ community and faces a second‑degree murder charge. Berman had famously spoken at a 2017 Trans Day of Remembrance event about transphobia and the murder of a trans friend, urging vigilance within the community. Her death, described by friends and advocates as a heartbreaking echo of the very violence she warned against, underscores the persistent threat faced by trans people in Ontario. A 2015 Trans PULSE study found that 20 % of Ontario’s trans residents had been physically or sexually assaulted and 34 % harassed, with many incidents under‑reported and 24 % experiencing police harassment. Toronto’s trans community has suffered multiple recent violent deaths, including Alloura Wells, highlighting systemic failures in protection and response. While organizations like The 519, Pride Toronto, and other nonprofits have expanded education, support, and anti‑violence programs, leaders say these efforts remain insufficient, leaving many trans individuals vulnerable. Berman’s murder serves as a stark moral reminder of the urgent need to combat transphobic violence and ensure safety for all LGBTQ2+ members.  

END OF SUMMARY.
