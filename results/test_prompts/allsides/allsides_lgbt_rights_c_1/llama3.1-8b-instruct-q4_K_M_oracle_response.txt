SUMMARY: A Toronto trans rights activist, Julie Berman, was murdered on December 22, 2019, in an incident that highlights the high rates of violence against the trans community. Two years earlier, she had given a powerful speech about transphobia and violence at a trans day of remembrance event, where she talked about a close friend who had been murdered. Berman's activism was aimed at raising awareness and promoting vigilance among her community members. Her death is a heartbreaking reminder that many trans people do not feel safe and are vulnerable to violence. In fact, a study found that 20% of Ontario's trans people have been physically or sexually assaulted for being trans, while 34% have been verbally threatened or harassed. The community is struggling to cope with the loss and is calling for more support programs and events to address this issue.

END OF SUMMARY.
