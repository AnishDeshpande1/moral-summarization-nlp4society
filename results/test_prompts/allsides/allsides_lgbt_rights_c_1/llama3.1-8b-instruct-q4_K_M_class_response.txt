SUMMARY: 

A Toronto trans rights activist, Julie Berman, was murdered on December 22, 2019. Two years earlier, she delivered a speech about the high rates of violence and transphobia in Toronto at a trans day of remembrance event. Tragically, she became one of the victims of this very violence. The incident is heartbreaking for the community, as Berman had been advocating for greater vigilance to prevent such incidents. According to a study, trans people are targets of specifically directed violence, with 20% experiencing physical or sexual assault and 34% facing verbal threats or harassment. This study also found that many trans individuals do not report these assaults due to fear of further violence, including from police. The community is mourning the loss of Berman and calling for greater support and resources to prevent such tragedies in the future. The incident highlights the need for increased awareness and action to address the high rates of violence faced by the trans community.

END OF SUMMARY.
