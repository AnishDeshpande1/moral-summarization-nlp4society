SUMMARY:

Julie Berman, a Toronto trans rights activist, was murdered on December 22, 2019. She had spoken out against transphobia in the city at a 2017 event, sharing her own experiences with violence and loss. Two years later, she was assaulted and killed in her home. The police have charged Colin Harnack, 29, with second-degree murder.

The incident is part of a larger pattern of violence against Toronto's trans community. According to a 2015 study, 20% of Ontario's trans people have been physically or sexually assaulted for being trans. Many trans individuals do not report these incidents due to fear and mistrust of the police.

Local organizations, including The 519 and Pride Toronto, are working to provide support and education for the trans community. However, more needs to be done to address the high rates of violence experienced by trans people. Julie Berman's murder is a tragic reminder of this issue.

END OF SUMMARY.
