SUMMARY:

Toronto trans rights activist Julie Berman was murdered on December 22, 2019, after being assaulted and suffering from head injuries. This heartbreaking incident is a stark reminder of the high rates of violence the trans community experiences. Berman had spoken out against transphobia at a trans day of remembrance event in 2017, where she shared the story of a trans friend who had been murdered. Her own murder is a tragic irony, highlighting the very issue she was trying to raise awareness about.

The trans community is often targeted by specifically directed violence, with studies showing that 20% of trans people in Ontario have been physically or sexually assaulted for being trans. Many trans people do not report these assaults to the police, and some even experience harassment by police. The incident is a sad reminder of the struggles and vulnerabilities faced by the trans community, and the need for more support and education.

Berman's death is the third violent death in Toronto's trans community in recent years, and her activism will be deeply missed. The incident should serve as a wake-up call to provide more support and services to the trans community, and to work towards a more welcoming and inclusive society. END OF SUMMARY.
