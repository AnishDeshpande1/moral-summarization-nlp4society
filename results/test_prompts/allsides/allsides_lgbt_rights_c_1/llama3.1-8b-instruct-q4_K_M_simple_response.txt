SUMMARY:

Toronto trans rights activist Julie Berman was murdered on December 22, 2019, just over two years after delivering a powerful speech about transphobia at the Trans Day of Remembrance event in 2017. Davina Hader, a friend and member of The 519 organization, described the incident as "heartbreaking" and said that Berman's activism was focused on making her community more vigilant against violence. A study by the Trans PULSE project found that 20% of Ontario's trans people have been physically or sexually assaulted for being trans, while another 34% have experienced verbal threats or harassment.

Olivia Nuamah, executive director of Pride Toronto, noted that violence is a "part of life" for many in the trans community and that there have been three violent deaths in Toronto's trans community in the past couple of years. She emphasized the need for more support programs and education to address the high rates of violence faced by trans people.

END OF SUMMARY.
