SUMMARY:

Julie Berman, a Toronto trans rights activist, was murdered on December 22, 2019. Two years prior, she spoke at a Trans Day of Remembrance event, discussing transphobia and the murder of a trans friend. Tragically, she became a victim of the same violence she was fighting against. Police found her with head injuries in a Toronto residence and pronounced her dead at the hospital. A 29-year-old man, Colin Harnack, has been charged with second-degree murder. Berman was a dedicated activist and hairdresser who worked with The 519, an organization advocating for LGBTQ inclusion. Her death highlights the alarming rates of violence against the trans community, with studies showing 20% of Ontario's trans people have been physically or sexually assaulted due to their gender identity. The incident serves as a reminder of the need for increased support and awareness for the trans community.

END OF SUMMARY.
