SUMMARY:
The brutal murder of Julie Berman, a longtime Toronto trans‑rights activist, underscores a grave breach of community care and fairness. Berman had publicly warned about transphobic violence, recalling a murdered friend at a 2017 Trans Day of Remembrance, only to become a victim herself two years later. Police identified Colin Harnack, a non‑LGBTQ individual, as the suspect and charged him with second‑degree murder, highlighting a subversive act that attacks both the authority of activists protecting vulnerable members and the trust that binds the community. Friends and leaders from The 519 and Pride Toronto stressed the collective betrayal—how systemic neglect and inadequate police response have left trans people exposed to hate‑driven harm. They called for stronger institutional accountability and solidarity, urging the city to honor its duty of care, uphold fairness, and reinforce loyalty to the most marginalized. Berman’s death is presented as a stark reminder that violence against trans individuals is not isolated but a symptom of broader injustice that must be confronted through unified, compassionate action. END OF SUMMARY.
