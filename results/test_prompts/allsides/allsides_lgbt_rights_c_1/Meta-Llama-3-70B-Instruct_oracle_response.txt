SUMMARY:

Toronto trans rights activist Julie Berman was brutally murdered on December 22, 2019, after being assaulted and suffering from head injuries. This heartbreaking incident is a stark reminder of the high rates of violence the trans community experiences. Berman was a vocal advocate against transphobia, having spoken at a trans day of remembrance event in 2017 about the murder of a trans friend. Her own death is a tragic irony, highlighting the vulnerability of trans individuals who are often targets of specifically directed violence. According to a 2015 study, 20% of Ontario's trans people have been physically or sexually assaulted, and 34% have been verbally threatened or harassed. The incident is an alarming reminder of the need for increased vigilance and support for the trans community. Berman's activism and commitment to her community will be deeply missed. Her death is a devastating loss, and a stark reminder of the need for greater awareness and action to address the violence and transphobia that plagues the community.
