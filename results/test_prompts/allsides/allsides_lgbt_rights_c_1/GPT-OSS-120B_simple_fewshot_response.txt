SUMMARY:
Julie Berman, a longtime Toronto trans‑rights activist and hairdresser, was found dead on Dec. 22, 2019 after suffering fatal head injuries. Two years earlier she had spoken at a Trans Day of Remembrance event about transphobia and the murder of a trans friend, urging vigilance in her community. Her friend Davina Hader, a member of The 519, recalled Berman’s heartfelt warning and noted that the violence she warned about ultimately claimed her life. Police have charged Colin Harnack with second‑degree murder; he is not known to be part of the LGBTQ community. The tragedy underscores a disturbing pattern of targeted violence against trans people in Ontario—studies show 20 % have been physically or sexually assaulted and many feel unsafe with police. Recent deaths of trans Ontarians, including Alloura Wells, have drawn criticism of institutional responses and highlighted gaps in support services. Community leaders, such as Pride Toronto’s Olivia Nuamah, stress that while Toronto strives to be inclusive, the trans community still faces lethal hostility and needs stronger protection, education, and resources. Berman’s loss is mourned as a reminder of the urgent moral duty to safeguard vulnerable lives. END OF SUMMARY.
