SUMMARY:

Toronto trans rights activist Julie Berman was murdered on December 22, 2019, after being assaulted and found with head injuries in a Toronto residence. This tragic event is a heartbreaking irony, as Berman had spoken out against transphobia and violence against trans people, including the murder of a close friend, at a Trans Day of Remembrance event in 2017. Her death is a stark reminder of the high rates of violence faced by the trans community, with a 2015 study showing that 20% of Ontario's trans people had been physically or sexually assaulted for being trans, and 34% had been verbally threatened or harassed. The incident highlights the need for greater vigilance and support for the trans community, which often feels vulnerable and unsafe. Berman's murder is the third violent death in Toronto's trans community in recent years, and her loss is deeply felt by those who knew her.
