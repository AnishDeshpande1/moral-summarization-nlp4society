SUMMARY:

The US House of Representatives has passed a $4.5 billion emergency border aid package to care for thousands of migrant families and unaccompanied children detained at the US-Mexico border. The bill, which passed along party lines, includes provisions to improve standards of care for migrant children, provide access to legal assistance, and protect families from being separated. The bill also blocks funding for President Trump's border wall and prevents information on sponsors of immigrant children from being used for deportation. The White House has threatened to veto the bill, citing concerns that it would hinder border security efforts. The Senate is expected to pass a different, bipartisan bill, setting up a showdown between the two chambers. The urgent need for funding to address the humanitarian crisis on the border has prompted lawmakers to act quickly, with Congress set to recess for the July 4 holiday.

END OF SUMMARY.
