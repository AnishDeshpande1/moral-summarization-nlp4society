**SUMMARY:**
The House passed a $4.5 billion emergency border‑aid package aimed at providing humanitarian assistance to thousands of migrant families and unaccompanied children detained after crossing the U.S.–Mexico border. After a last‑minute push by top Democratic leaders, the measure cleared the chamber along party lines, with progressives and Hispanic lawmakers conceding to modest changes to avoid a dire humanitarian debacle. The bill allocates more than $1 billion for shelter, food, medical care and legal aid, and nearly $3 billion to improve standards for unaccompanied children, mandating better conditions, translators and the replacement of inadequate contractors. Democrats framed the effort as a response to the cruel, terror‑inducing policies that tear families apart, insisting that “families belong together” and that innocent children deserve protection. Republicans warned the legislation was partisan, political point‑scoring that would not solve the problem, while the White House threatened a veto, arguing the bill lacks funding for border‑security and ICE detention beds. The Senate is expected to offer a competing measure, setting up a showdown before the July 4 recess.
