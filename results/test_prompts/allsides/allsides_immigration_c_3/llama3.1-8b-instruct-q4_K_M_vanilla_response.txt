SUMMARY:

The US House of Representatives passed a $4.5 billion emergency border aid package by a vote of 230-195, providing funding to care for thousands of migrant families and unaccompanied children detained at the US-Mexico border. The bill includes over $1 billion to shelter and feed migrants and almost $3 billion to care for unaccompanied children. It also sets standards for care at Department of Health and Human Services "influx shelters" and blocks funding from being used for a border wall or immigration raids.

The White House has threatened to veto the bill, saying it would hamstring border security efforts. However, Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell suggests that the House should accept the Senate's companion measure, which received only one "nay" vote in committee. The legislation is expected to face a showdown with the Republican-led Senate, which may try to force Democrats to send President Trump a different bill.

The humanitarian emergency at the border has been exacerbated by reports of gruesome conditions in detention facilities, including inadequate food, water, and sanitation.
