SUMMARY: The House passed a $4.5 billion emergency aid bill for migrant families and unaccompanied children detained at the U.S.-Mexico border, voting 230‑195 along party lines. After last‑minute concessions to progressive and Hispanic lawmakers, the legislation includes over $1 billion for shelter and food and nearly $3 billion for care of unaccompanied children, mandates higher standards for “influx shelters,” blocks funds from being diverted to a border wall, and prohibits using sponsor information for deportations. Only three Republican moderates supported the measure; the four dissenting Democrats were AOC, Ilhan Omar, Ayanna Pressley, and Rashida Tlaib. The White House threatens a veto, arguing the bill hampers border‑security funding, while Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell urges the House to adopt the Senate’s companion bill, which faces little opposition. The urgency stems from reports of dire conditions in detention facilities, including a Texas station where hundreds of children were housed in unsanitary, overcrowded conditions. Agencies are running out of money as border apprehensions near 133,000 per month. The legislation now heads to the Senate for a showdown before the July 4 recess. END OF SUMMARY.
