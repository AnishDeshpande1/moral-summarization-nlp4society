SUMMARY:

The House passed a $4.5 billion emergency border aid package to care for thousands of migrant families and unaccompanied children detained after crossing the U.S.-Mexico border. The bill, which passed along party lines, aims to prevent the humanitarian emergency from turning into a debacle by ensuring that children have food, clothing, sanitary items, shelter, and medical care. It also provides access to legal assistance and protects families, as families belong together. The legislation seeks to mandate improved standards of care at Department of Health and Human Services "influx shelters" and block information on sponsors of immigrant children from being used to deport them. President Trump has threatened to veto the bill, arguing that it would hamstring his administration's border security efforts. However, House Democrats argue that their bill is essential to providing humanitarian aid and preventing further suffering among migrant families.

END OF SUMMARY.
