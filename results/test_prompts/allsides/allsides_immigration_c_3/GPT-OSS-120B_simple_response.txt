SUMMARY:  
After frantic last‑minute negotiations, House leaders pushed through a $4.5 billion emergency aid package aimed at protecting migrant families and unaccompanied children detained at the U.S.–Mexico border. Speaker Nancy Pelosi framed the legislation as a moral imperative: “children need food, clothing, shelter, medical care, legal help, and families belong together.” The bill earmarks more than $1 billion for shelter and feeding of detained migrants and nearly $3 billion for the care of unaccompanied children, mandates higher standards for “influx shelters,” guarantees translators, and bars any diversion of funds to a border wall or ICE detention beds. It passed the House 230‑195 along party lines, with only three GOP Republicans supporting it and four progressive Democrats dissenting. Democrats argue the funding is essential to stop a humanitarian crisis highlighted by reports of overcrowded, unsanitary detention sites where infants have suffered and children have died. The White House threatens a veto, insisting the bill neglects border‑security funding, while Senate leaders push a rival measure. The showdown underscores a stark moral divide: humanitarian aid for vulnerable children versus punitive immigration enforcement.  

END OF SUMMARY.
