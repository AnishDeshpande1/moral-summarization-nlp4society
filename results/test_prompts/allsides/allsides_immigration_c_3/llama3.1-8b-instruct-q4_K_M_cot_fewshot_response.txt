The House passed a $4.5 billion emergency border aid package to care for thousands of migrant families and unaccompanied children detained after crossing the U.S.-Mexico border. The bill, which contains more than $1 billion to shelter and feed migrants, seeks to mandate improved standards of care at Department of Health and Human Services "influx shelters" that house children waiting to be placed with sponsors. The legislation is a response to recent reports of gruesome conditions in Border Patrol stations, including inadequate food, water, and sanitation. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi called the bill a necessary measure to prevent a humanitarian crisis on the border, saying it would ensure that children have food, clothing, sanitary items, shelter, and medical care. However, the White House has threatened to veto the bill, citing concerns that it would hamstring the administration's immigration enforcement efforts.
