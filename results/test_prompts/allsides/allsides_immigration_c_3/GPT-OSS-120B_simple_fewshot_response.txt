SUMMARY:
The House narrowly approved a $4.5 billion emergency aid package aimed at protecting migrant families and unaccompanied children detained at the U.S.–Mexico border. Led by Speaker Nancy Pelosi, Democrats wrestled with progressive and Hispanic lawmakers before settling on modest concessions, emphasizing that “families belong together” and that children must receive food, shelter, medical care and legal help. The bill earmarks over $1 billion for migrant detention facilities and nearly $3 billion for children in HHS shelters, bans any funds from being diverted to the border wall, and blocks the use of sponsor information for deportations. Only three Republicans supported it, while four high‑profile freshman Democrats voted against it. The White House threatens a veto, claiming the measure undermines border security, and Senate leader Mitch McConnell urges acceptance of a Senate alternative. Amid reports of horrendous conditions—overcrowded, unsanitary stations where children have died—the legislation is framed as a moral imperative to stop a humanitarian crisis and protect vulnerable children, contrasting compassionate aid with punitive immigration policies.

END OF SUMMARY.
