SUMMARY:
The House passed a $4.5 billion emergency border aid package to care for migrant families and unaccompanied children detained at the U.S.-Mexico border. The bill, which was approved along party lines, includes provisions to mandate improved standards of care at shelters housing children waiting to be placed with sponsors in the United States. It also blocks information on sponsors from being used to deport immigrant children and denies additional funding for Immigration and Customs Enforcement detention beds. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi emphasized that the bill prioritizes humanitarian needs over immigration enforcement measures, including a border wall. The Senate is expected to pass its own version of the bill, which may prompt a showdown between the two chambers. President Trump has threatened to veto the House bill, citing concerns about hamstringing his administration's border security efforts.

END OF SUMMARY.
