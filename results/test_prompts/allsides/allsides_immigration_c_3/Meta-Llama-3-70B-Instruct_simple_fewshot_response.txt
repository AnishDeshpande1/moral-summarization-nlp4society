SUMMARY:

The House of Representatives passed a $4.5 billion emergency border aid package to care for migrant families and unaccompanied children detained at the US-Mexico border. The bill, which passed along party lines, aims to provide humanitarian aid and improve standards of care for detained migrants. It blocks funding for President Trump's border wall and immigration raids, and denies additional funding for Immigration and Customs Enforcement detention beds. The White House has threatened to veto the bill, citing concerns that it would hamstring border security efforts. The Senate is expected to take up a companion measure, which may lead to a showdown between the two chambers. The urgent need for funding to prevent a humanitarian crisis on the border has driven the legislative effort, with agencies set to run out of money and lawmakers facing pressure to act before the July 4 recess.

END OF SUMMARY.
