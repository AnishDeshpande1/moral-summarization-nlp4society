SUMMARY:

The House passed a $4.5 billion emergency border aid package to care for migrant families and unaccompanied children detained at the US-Mexico border, but the Senate's top Republican suggested that the House should accept the Senate measure instead. The bill includes funding for shelter, food, and medical care for migrants, as well as improved standards of care at "influx shelters" housing children waiting to be placed with sponsors. The White House has threatened to veto the bill, arguing it would hamstring border security efforts. Pressure is intense to wrap up the legislation before Congress leaves for a weeklong July 4 recess, and agencies are about to run out of money. Lawmakers' sense of urgency was amplified by recent reports of gruesome conditions in Border Patrol stations, including one where over 300 infants and children were being housed under inadequate conditions.
