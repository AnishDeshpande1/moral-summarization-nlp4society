SUMMARY:

A news article compares Hillary Clinton's campaign tactics to those of former President Richard Nixon, dubbed "Tricky Dick". Both campaigns have been accused of using pay-for-play schemes, leveraging donations from corporations and wealthy individuals for influence. WikiLeaks has released emails showing the Clinton Foundation's involvement in profiteering and the Clinton State Department's lax attitude towards U.S. intelligence law.

The article also highlights similarities between Nixon's campaign team and Podesta's leadership in Clinton's campaign, with both being accused of ruthlessness and a lack of moral compass. Clinton's consultants have been caught lampooning certain groups as "needy" or stuck in medieval times, and aides have been accused of bartering for face time on the basis of cash donations.

The article concludes that while Nixon's misdeeds were eventually exposed, leading to his resignation, it is unclear whether Clinton will meet a similar fate due to the lack of investigative reporting and the media's bias towards her.

END OF SUMMARY.
