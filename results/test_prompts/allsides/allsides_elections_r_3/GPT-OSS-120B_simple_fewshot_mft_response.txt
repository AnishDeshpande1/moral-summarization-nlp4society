SUMMARY:
The article paints former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton as a ruthless, subversive politician who has repeatedly abused her authority. It claims her campaign mirrors Richard Nixon’s “CREEP” operation, using pay‑for‑play deals, corporate donations and quid‑pro‑quo arrangements that cheat ordinary voters and betray public trust. Leaked emails and Project Veritas footage are presented as evidence that Clinton’s aides bartered access, staged provocations at Trump rallies and plotted illegal ballot tactics, showing a willingness to harm opponents and the democratic process. The piece argues that Clinton’s handling of the private‑email scandal, the Clinton Foundation’s alleged profiteering and the Benghazi controversy exemplify a pattern of deception and cheating, while her repeated “cannot remember” statements to investigators are framed as betrayals of loyalty to the nation. By comparing her tactics to Nixon’s Watergate era, the author suggests that Clinton’s subversive use of power endangers the country’s institutions, harms ordinary citizens, violates fairness, and betrays the loyalty owed to the American public.
