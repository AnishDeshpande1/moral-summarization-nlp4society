SUMMARY: The article portrays former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton as a ruthless, Nixon‑style politician, alleging that recent leaks—including Anthony Weiner’s laptop, WikiLeaks emails, and Project Veritas footage—expose a campaign built on pay‑for‑play, quid‑pro‑quo deals and unethical tactics. It draws parallels between Clinton’s operation, led by John Podesta, and Richard Nixon’s 1972 re‑election effort (CREEP), suggesting similar fundraising excesses, manipulation of donors, and attempts to disrupt opponent rallies. The piece cites alleged instances of Clinton aides bartering access for donations, planning provocative protest tactics, and seeking foreign business deals, while accusing Clinton of repeatedly claiming she “cannot remember” key details about the email controversy. The author warns that, like Nixon, Clinton may eventually face downfall, but argues that contemporary media bias and lack of independent investigative journalism could shield her from accountability. END OF SUMMARY.
