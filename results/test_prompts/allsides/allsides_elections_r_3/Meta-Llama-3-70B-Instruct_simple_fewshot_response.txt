SUMMARY:

This article draws a stark comparison between Hillary Clinton's campaign and Richard Nixon's 1972 re-election campaign, highlighting their similarities in ruthless politics and pay-for-play profiteering. The author argues that Clinton's campaign has trumped Nixon's skullduggery, with WikiLeaks revelations exposing her consultants' lack of moral compass and her own willingness to prioritize political self-interest over policy positions. The article also notes the eerie resemblance between Clinton's campaign and Nixon's, including the use of similar tactics to disrupt opponents' rallies and the stonewalling of investigations. The author questions whether Clinton's hubris will eventually lead to a Nixonian downfall, but notes that the lack of independent investigative reporting may prevent this from happening anytime soon.

END OF SUMMARY.
