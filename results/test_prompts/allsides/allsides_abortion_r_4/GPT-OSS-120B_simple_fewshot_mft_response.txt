SUMMARY:  
The article argues that both Alabama’s near‑total abortion ban and New York’s late‑term abortion law reflect political extremes that subvert responsible authority and jeopardize public care. It notes President Trump’s exaggerated claims about “executing” babies and the media’s double standard in covering Alabama versus New York, highlighting how partisan leaders manipulate fear rather than protect vulnerable women and fetuses. Citing Gallup data, the piece stresses that most Americans care for early‑stage abortions but oppose third‑trimester procedures, suggesting current laws are unfairly cheating the public’s nuanced preferences. The author contends that the Supreme Court’s Roe v. Wade decision imposed a rigid authority that forced both sides into absolutist positions, prompting Alabama’s GOP to deliberately pass an unconstitutional ban in hopes of overturning Roe, while Democrats seek “abortion sanctuary” statutes to shield future rights. This strategic escalation ignores the broader public’s desire for compromise, undermining fair democratic deliberation and harming women’s health. Ultimately, the piece warns that exploiting authority for partisan gain, neglecting care for individuals, and disregarding the public’s fair expectations will only deepen polarization.  
END OF SUMMARY.
