SUMMARY:

President Trump's claim that Democrats support late-term abortion until birth is exaggerated, according to fact-checkers. However, it is based on comments made by Virginia governor Ralph Northam and Democratic delegate Kathy Tran about liberalizing abortion laws in their state. Their bill would have allowed abortions up to the 40th week of pregnancy, including during labor, if the mother's mental or emotional health was threatened.

The article argues that both the New York and Alabama abortion laws are extreme, as most Americans support early-stage abortions but oppose them later on. The media has reacted differently to each law, with some criticizing New York's law while furore over Alabama's near-total ban. The article suggests that Roe v. Wade's permissive abortion regime has led to polarization in the debate and that overturning it could allow states to pass laws that reflect majority opinion.

END OF SUMMARY.
