SUMMARY:

The article discusses the controversy surrounding abortion laws in the US, particularly in Alabama and New York. President Trump has been criticized for his comments on late-term abortions, which fact-checkers say are a distortion of Virginia Governor Ralph Northam's remarks. Northam had explained that in cases where a fetus is not viable, the infant would be delivered and kept comfortable, and a discussion would ensue between the physician and the mother. The article notes that both Alabama's near-total abortion ban and New York's liberalization of abortion laws are extreme and do not reflect the attitudes of the American people, who generally support early-stage abortions but oppose late-term ones. The article suggests that the polarized debate on abortion is a result of the 1973 Roe v. Wade decision, which imposed a permissive abortion regime and foreclosed state-level compromise. The article concludes that overturning Roe and allowing states to pass laws that reflect majority opinion might not defuse the political passion, but could be a step towards finding a compromise.

END OF SUMMARY.
