SUMMARY: The article examines the clash over Alabama’s near‑total abortion ban and New York’s recent law expanding late‑term abortion access, arguing that both represent political extremes that distort public opinion. It notes President Trump’s exaggerated claims about “executing” babies and clarifies that Virginia Governor Ralph Northam’s remarks were misrepresented. While New York’s legislation sought to create a “sanctuary” for later abortions, Alabama’s law was deliberately unconstitutional, aiming to provoke the Supreme Court into overturning Roe v. Wade. Polling shows most Americans support early‑term abortions but oppose third‑trimester procedures, suggesting a public desire for compromise. The piece contends that Roe’s rigid framework forced both pro‑life and pro‑choice camps into absolutist positions, prompting the pro‑life movement to adopt an incremental strategy—now challenged by “go‑for‑broke” legislators in Alabama. Ultimately, the author urges a return to moderate, consensus‑building policies, warning that partisan extremes and activist‑driven legislation undermine democratic deliberation and the nation’s capacity for balanced moral judgment. END OF SUMMARY.
