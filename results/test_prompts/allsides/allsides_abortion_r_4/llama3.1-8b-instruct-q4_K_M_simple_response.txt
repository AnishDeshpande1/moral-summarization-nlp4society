SUMMARY:

A controversy is brewing over abortion laws in the US, particularly in Alabama and New York. President Trump accused Democrats of pushing for late-term abortions, but critics say this is an exaggeration. In fact, both states' laws are extreme, with most Americans supporting early-stage abortions but opposing them later on. The media's reaction highlights their own biases on the issue. The author argues that Roe v. Wade, a landmark Supreme Court decision in 1973, has created a polarized dynamic where the debate is dominated by extremes.

The Alabama law, which bans nearly all abortions, is likely unconstitutional under current precedent, but its sponsors hope to challenge Roe through the court's new conservative majority. The New York law, on the other hand, aims to create a post-Roe "sanctuary" in case the court reverses the decision. The author believes that overturning Roe could lead to more compromise and reflection of public attitudes.

END OF SUMMARY.
