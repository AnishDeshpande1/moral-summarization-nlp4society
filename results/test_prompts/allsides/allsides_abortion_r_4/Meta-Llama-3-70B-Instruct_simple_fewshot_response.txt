SUMMARY:

The debate over abortion laws has become increasingly polarized, with both sides accusing each other of extremism. President Trump has repeatedly claimed that Democrats support "taxpayer-funded abortion, all the way up until the moment of birth," a claim disputed by critics. However, Virginia governor Ralph Northam's remarks about late-term abortions have been distorted and exaggerated by Trump and others. Meanwhile, polling shows that most Americans support early-stage abortions, but opposition grows as the fetus develops. The media's differing reactions to New York's liberal abortion law and Alabama's near-total ban reveal a bias in favor of abortion rights. The legal and political landscape has been shaped by the Supreme Court's 1973 Roe v. Wade decision, which has been criticized for being too permissive and foreclosing state-level compromise. As a result, both pro-choice and pro-life activists have taken more absolutist positions, making it difficult to find a compromise that reflects the views of the majority. END OF SUMMARY.
