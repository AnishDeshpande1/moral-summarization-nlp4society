SUMMARY: The article examines the race to develop a COVID‑19 vaccine, noting that over 100 candidates are in development and the U.S. “Operation Warp Speed” aims for millions of doses by year‑end. While Dr. Anthony Fauci is cautiously optimistic about a vaccine by early 2021, experts warn that achieving herd immunity—requiring about 70% of the population—will likely take longer. Skepticism stems from the unprecedented speed required (the current record is four years), potential manufacturing and distribution bottlenecks, and risks of an unsafe or short‑lived vaccine that could fuel anti‑vaccine sentiment. Some argue resources should also focus on testing, tracing, and treatments rather than relying on a near‑term vaccine. Opinions quoted highlight concerns about public willingness to vaccinate, supply‑chain challenges, and the danger of delaying proven mitigation measures while awaiting a “miracle cure.” The piece underscores that a vaccine, while not a magic solution, remains the most viable path out of the pandemic, but realistic expectations and parallel public‑health strategies are essential. END OF SUMMARY.
