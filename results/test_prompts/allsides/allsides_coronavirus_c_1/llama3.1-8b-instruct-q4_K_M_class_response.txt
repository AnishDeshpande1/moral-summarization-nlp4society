SUMMARY: 
The development of an effective vaccine is crucial to establishing herd immunity against the coronavirus pandemic, which could lead to millions of deaths if left unchecked. While promising progress has been made on several vaccine candidates, experts caution that rushing development may backfire by increasing the risk of an unsafe vaccine being released, potentially undermining trust in vaccines and hindering mitigation efforts. The "Operation Warp Speed" initiative aims to produce hundreds of millions of doses by the end of the year, but some worry about investing too much in a best-case scenario without preparing for potential setbacks or bottlenecks in production and supply chain distribution. This could lead to hoarding and inefficient distribution of vaccines, ultimately breaking the momentum of efforts to combat the pandemic.

The tragedy is that policymakers are banking on a future vaccine instead of implementing effective systems of testing, tracing, and therapeutic medicines to reduce the number of cases and deaths right now. By doing so, they may be contributing to unnecessary deaths and economic instability, leaving the public hobbled by uncertainty and inaction. A better approach would be to prepare for the possibility that a vaccine might not be widely available for years, focusing on mitigation measures and therapeutic interventions to reduce the spread of the virus and alleviate suffering. END OF SUMMARY.
