SUMMARY: 

Experts agree that developing an effective vaccine is crucial to ending the coronavirus pandemic, but there are concerns about rushing the development process. Despite promising progress on several candidates, some experts warn that investing too much in a best-case scenario may lead to disappointment and undermine efforts to put necessary virus mitigation measures in place now. Others argue that the idea of a vaccine being imminent may hold back these efforts, which could be crucial in reducing unnecessary deaths right now.

There are also concerns about the supply chain for vaccine materials breaking down, potentially slowing down availability after discovery. Meanwhile, experts emphasize the importance of developing effective systems of testing, tracing, and therapeutic medicines that can last as long as they're needed. They say waiting until enough people have been infected could lead to millions of deaths, and that a vaccine is the quickest and safest way to establish herd immunity.

The debate highlights the need for a more cautious approach, preparing for the possibility that it may take years before a vaccine is widely available. This perspective says we should be skeptical of relying solely on a future miracle cure, and instead focus on immediate actions to mitigate the spread of the virus. END OF SUMMARY.
