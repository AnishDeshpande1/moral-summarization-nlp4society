SUMMARY: The development of a COVID-19 vaccine is underway, with over 100 potential vaccines in various stages of development. While experts are "cautiously optimistic" about having a vaccine ready by early 2021, others are skeptical about the rapid development and distribution of millions of doses. They warn that investing too much in a best-case scenario may undermine efforts to establish effective testing, tracing, and therapeutic measures that can mitigate the spread of the virus. Rushing development may also lead to unsafe vaccines, increasing anti-vaccine sentiment and ultimately causing more deaths. Moreover, the supply chain for vaccine materials could break down, leading to bottlenecks and hoarding. Some argue that policymakers are banking on a future vaccine while people are dying now, and that there are existing measures that can reduce the number of cases and deaths right now. The tragedy is that people are suffering and dying unnecessarily while waiting for a miracle cure. END OF SUMMARY.
