SUMMARY:

The development of an effective vaccine is widely considered the only way to end the coronavirus pandemic. With over 100 potential vaccines in development, the Trump administration's "Operation Warp Speed" aims to have millions of doses available by the end of the year. Dr. Anthony Fauci is "cautiously optimistic" that a vaccine could be ready by early 2021. However, experts warn that rushing development may backfire, increasing the risk of an unsafe vaccine and undermining herd immunity. Moreover, waiting for a vaccine may lead to millions of deaths, emphasizing the need for mitigation measures. Some argue that investing too much in a best-case scenario may distract from developing effective systems of testing, tracing, and therapeutic medicines. The author suggests that a more realistic approach would be to prepare for a longer timeline, acknowledging that setbacks could increase anti-vaccine sentiment. The supply chain for vaccine materials could also break down, slowing down vaccine availability. END OF SUMMARY.
