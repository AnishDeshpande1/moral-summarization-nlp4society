SUMMARY:

Experts believe that developing an effective vaccine is crucial in ending the coronavirus pandemic, but some are skeptical about meeting the goal of having millions of doses available by the end of the year. Dr. Anthony Fauci expressed "cautious optimism" that a vaccine could be ready by early 2021, but experts warn that even if a vaccine is developed quickly, it may not be widely available for months or years to come.

There are concerns about investing too much in what may be a best-case scenario, and some argue that preparing for the possibility of a longer development process would be a more prudent approach. Others worry that rushing development could lead to an unsafe vaccine being approved, increasing anti-vaccine sentiment among the public.

Meanwhile, some experts emphasize the need for immediate action on virus mitigation measures, citing the ongoing spread of the virus and unnecessary deaths. A vaccine is seen as the quickest and safest way to establish herd immunity, but its effectiveness and availability remain uncertain.

END OF SUMMARY.
