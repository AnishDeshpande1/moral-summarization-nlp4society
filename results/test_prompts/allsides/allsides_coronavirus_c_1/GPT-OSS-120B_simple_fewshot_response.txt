SUMMARY:
The article stresses that ending the COVID‑19 pandemic ultimately depends on an effective vaccine, but warns against treating it as a near‑term miracle. While Operation Warp Speed and early trials have generated cautious optimism that a vaccine could appear by early 2021, experts caution that developing, scaling and distributing hundreds of millions of doses in months would shatter historical records and may not succeed. Relying on a rapid vaccine risks diverting resources from proven mitigation tools—testing, tracing, therapeutics—and could fuel anti‑vaccine sentiment if safety is compromised. The piece argues the moral responsibility is to prepare for a longer timeline by strengthening current public‑health measures and ensuring robust, global supply chains, rather than banking on a “golden day” that may never arrive. In short, a vaccine remains the safest path to herd immunity, but the ethical imperative is to act now with proven strategies while realistically planning for a vaccine that may take years to become widely available. END OF SUMMARY.
