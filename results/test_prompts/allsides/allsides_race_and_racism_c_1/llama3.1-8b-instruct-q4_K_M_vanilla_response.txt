SUMMARY:

Civil rights leader John Lewis passed away at the age of 80 on July 17, 2020. He was a key figure in the US civil rights movement, leading voting rights demonstrations in Selma, Alabama, in the 1960s. On March 7, 1965, known as Bloody Sunday, Lewis and hundreds of marchers were confronted by state troopers and sheriff's posse members on the Edmund Pettus Bridge. The confrontation led to a brutal attack, with Lewis being knocked to the ground and beaten with a nightstick.

The incident helped lead to the passage of the federal Voting Rights Act. Despite the violence, Lewis continued to fight for civil rights, eventually becoming a congressman. In 2015, he returned to Selma with President Barack Obama to commemorate the 50th anniversary of Bloody Sunday. Lewis was remembered as a hero and a leader who inspired others to "get in good trouble" and help redeem America's soul.

END OF SUMMARY.
