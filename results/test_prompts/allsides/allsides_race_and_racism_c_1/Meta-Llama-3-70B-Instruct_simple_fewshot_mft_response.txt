SUMMARY:

The article honors the life and legacy of John Lewis, a civil rights icon who played a crucial role in the 1960s movement. On March 7, 1965, Lewis led hundreds of marchers across the Edmund Pettus Bridge in Selma, Alabama, where they were met with brutal violence from state troopers. The event, known as Bloody Sunday, shocked the nation and propelled Lewis to the global stage as a hero of the U.S. civil rights movement. The ensuing confrontation helped lead to the passage of the federal Voting Rights Act. The article highlights Lewis's courage and conviction in the face of subversion and harm, as well as his commitment to fairness and equality. It also emphasizes the sanctity of the civil rights movement and the degradation of racial segregation. Through his actions, Lewis demonstrated the importance of standing up against authority when it perpetuates harm and injustice. His legacy serves as a powerful reminder of the ongoing struggle for racial justice and the need for continued activism to redeem the soul of America.

END OF SUMMARY.
