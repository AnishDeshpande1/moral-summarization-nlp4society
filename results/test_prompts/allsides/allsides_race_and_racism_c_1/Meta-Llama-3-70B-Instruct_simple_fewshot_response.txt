SUMMARY:

John Lewis, a civil rights icon, died on July 17, 2020, at the age of 80. Lewis was a key figure in the 1960s civil rights movement, leading voting rights demonstrations in Selma, Alabama. On March 7, 1965, Lewis led hundreds of marchers across the Edmund Pettus Bridge, where they were met with violence from Alabama state troopers. The brutal attack, known as Bloody Sunday, helped lead to the passage of the federal Voting Rights Act. Lewis's courage and determination inspired a generation of activists, and he continued to fight for racial justice throughout his life. As a congressman, Lewis remained a vocal advocate for civil rights, urging Americans to "get in good trouble, necessary trouble, and help redeem the soul of America." Lewis's legacy serves as a powerful reminder of the ongoing struggle for racial equality and justice in the United States.

END OF SUMMARY.
