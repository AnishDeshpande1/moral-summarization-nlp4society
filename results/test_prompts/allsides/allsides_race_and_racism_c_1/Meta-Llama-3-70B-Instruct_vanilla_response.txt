SUMMARY:

John Lewis, a civil rights leader, died on July 17, 2020, at the age of 80. Lewis was a key figure in the 1960s civil rights movement, leading voting rights demonstrations in Selma, Alabama. On March 7, 1965, Lewis led hundreds of marchers across the Edmund Pettus Bridge, where they were met by Alabama state troopers armed with gas canisters and nightsticks. The ensuing confrontation, known as Bloody Sunday, shocked the nation and helped lead to the passage of the federal Voting Rights Act. Lewis was brutally beaten by a state trooper, sustaining a fractured skull. Despite the violence, Lewis continued to fight for racial equality, becoming a hero of the U.S. civil rights movement. He went on to serve in Congress, where he continued to advocate for civil rights and social justice.

END OF SUMMARY.
