SUMMARY:
At a Saturday night rally in Tulsa, President Donald Trump used his first post‑COVID campaign appearance to sell a second term built on conflict, not prosperity. He downplayed the 120,000 COVID deaths, joked about “testing” fewer people, and dismissed the pandemic’s toll as a reason supporters would praise him. Rather than policy, his speech was a barrage of insults and scapegoating: he labeled journalists “the most dishonest people anywhere on earth,” attacked Democrats, athletes who knelt, and a host of ethnic groups, calling the virus “Kung Flu,” immigrants “animals,” and naturalized congresswoman Ilhan Omar a threat who would turn the U.S. into “the country from where she came.” He glorified violence, urging the crowd to use firearms against “lunatics,” praising ICE agents who “start swinging,” and urging governors to crush protesters, even offering to send troops to Seattle. The rally presented a vision of America under Trump that would amplify hatred, demagoguery, constitutional subversion, and likely more violence. END OF SUMMARY.
