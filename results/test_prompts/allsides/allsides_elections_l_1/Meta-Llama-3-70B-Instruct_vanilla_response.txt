SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump relaunched his reelection campaign in Tulsa, Oklahoma, with a rally that focused on attacking his enemies rather than promoting his accomplishments. He blamed anarchists, journalists, Democrats, and illegal aliens for the country's problems. Trump downplayed the 120,000 American deaths from the coronavirus, joking that testing fewer people would result in fewer reported infections. He insulted government employees, athletes who kneel during the national anthem, and politicians like Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez. Trump also made ethnic attacks, using slurs and insinuations against various groups, including naturalized Americans. He encouraged violence, praising ICE agents who "start swinging" at immigrants and suggesting that Americans take matters into their own hands. Trump's speech was filled with lies, hatred, and demagoguery, offering a vision of a second term marked by more division, strife, and potentially even violence.

END OF SUMMARY.
