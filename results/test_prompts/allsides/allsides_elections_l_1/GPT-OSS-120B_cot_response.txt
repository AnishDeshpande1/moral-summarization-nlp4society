**SUMMARY:**  
At a Tulsa rally, President Donald Trump relaunched his 2024 campaign by framing the election as a battle against “enemies” – anarchists, journalists, Democrats and “illegal aliens.” He dismissed the 120,000 COVID deaths as “horrible” but claimed people would still praise him. Instead of highlighting achievements, he attacked critics with words like “sadistic,” “vicious,” “hateful,” “sinister,” and called reporters “the most dishonest people anywhere on earth.” He used racial slurs such as “Kung Flu,” labeled immigrants “animals,” and denigrated Rep. Ilhan Omar as a threat from “Somalia.” Trump encouraged the crowd to boo the press, booed “thugs” and “lunatics,” and urged governors to use force, even offering to send troops to Seattle. He praised ICE agents who “start swinging” and suggested firearms are “damn nice” against protesters. The rally portrayed a vision of a second term built on hatred, strife, and the subversion of constitutional norms.
