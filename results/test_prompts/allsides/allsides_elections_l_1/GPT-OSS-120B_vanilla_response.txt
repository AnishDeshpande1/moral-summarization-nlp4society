SUMMARY: President Donald Trump launched his 2024 reelection campaign at a Tulsa rally, framing the election as a battle against “anarchists, journalists, Democrats, and millions of illegal aliens.” He downplayed the COVID‑19 death toll, joked about testing, and claimed his handling would be praised despite the fatalities. The speech focused on attacking opponents, calling them “sadistic,” “vicious,” and “sinister,” and urging the crowd to boo reporters. Trump vilified athletes kneeling for the anthem, Democratic leaders such as Alexandria Ocasio‑Cortez, and Washington Mayor Muriel Bowser, threatening financial repercussions for D.C. He used racist and xenophobic language, labeling COVID‑19 “Kung Flu,” calling immigrants “animals,” and targeting naturalized congresswoman Ilhan Omar as a threat to the nation. He praised ICE agents who “start swinging,” encouraged armed citizens to confront protesters, and offered to deploy troops to quell unrest in Seattle. The rally emphasized hatred, division, and the promise of further violence and authoritarian measures in a second term. END OF SUMMARY.
