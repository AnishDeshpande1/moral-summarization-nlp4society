SUMMARY:
At a Tulsa rally, President Donald Trump used his first post‑COVID campaign stop to launch a second‑term bid built on hatred and division rather than achievements. He downplayed the 120,000 pandemic deaths, joked about infecting children, and blamed “anarchists, journalists, Democrats and millions of illegal aliens” for the nation’s ills. The speech was a litany of insults – calling reporters “the most dishonest people,” athletes “sinister,” and a government worker a “dumb son of a bitch.” Trump weaponized ethnicity, labeling COVID “Kung Flu,” vilifying immigrants, and accusing Rep. Ilhan Omar of wanting to turn the U.S. into “Somalia.” He praised ICE agents who “start swinging,” urged citizens to use firearms against “lunatics,” and urged governors to crush protesters, even offering to send troops to Seattle. The rally showcased Trump’s strategy of inciting fear, encouraging violence, and promising more “lies, hatred, demagoguery and subversion of constitutional government” in a second term. END OF SUMMARY.
