SUMMARY:
At a Tulsa rally, President Donald Trump relaunched his 2024 campaign by attacking perceived enemies rather than outlining policy achievements, framing the election as a battle against “anarchists, journalists, Democrats and millions of illegal aliens.” He downplayed the 120,000 COVID‑19 deaths, joked about testing fewer people, and dismissed criticism as partisan scapegoating, thereby subverting the authority of public health experts and the media. Trump’s speech was saturated with hostile language that portrayed opponents as “sadistic,” “vicious,” “hateful” and “sinister,” encouraging the crowd to boo reporters and labeling them the world’s most dishonest people. He vilified immigrants, people of color, and naturalized officials, calling the virus “Kung Flu,” describing undocumented migrants as “animals,” and urging violent resistance to protests, even suggesting the use of firearms and federal troops. By presenting these groups as threats and promising harsher repression, Trump framed his platform as a punitive response to those he deemed immoral, casting the political contest as a fight for fairness against a cheating elite that has allegedly betrayed the nation. END OF SUMMARY.
