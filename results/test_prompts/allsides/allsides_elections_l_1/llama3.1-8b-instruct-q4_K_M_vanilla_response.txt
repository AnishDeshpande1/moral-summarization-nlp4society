SUMMARY:

In Tulsa, Oklahoma, President Donald Trump relaunched his reelection campaign by emphasizing enmity and strife rather than prosperity. He joked about the 120,000 Americans killed by the coronavirus and downplayed the severity of infections. Trump's speech focused on attacking people he dislikes, including journalists, athletes who kneel during the national anthem, and undocumented immigrants. He also challenged the loyalty of a naturalized American, Rep. Ilhan Omar, implying that she would want to impose Somali customs on the US.

Trump appealed to violence, praising ICE agents for "start swinging" at immigrants with criminal records and encouraging his followers to take matters into their own hands during protests. He suggested sending troops to put down domestic unrest and even joked about letting Seattle suffer as a lesson to its residents.

The speech was marked by hate-filled rhetoric, ethnic attacks, and demagoguery. Trump's message is clear: he plans to accelerate the destruction of America in a second term, promoting lies, hatred, and subversion of constitutional government.

END OF SUMMARY.
