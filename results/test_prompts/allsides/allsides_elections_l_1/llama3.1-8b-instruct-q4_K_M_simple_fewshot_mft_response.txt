SUMMARY:

At his recent rally in Tulsa, Oklahoma, President Donald Trump's campaign speech was marked by enmity, hatred, and subversion of constitutional government. Trump ran on four more years of strife, targeting various groups as enemies, including anarchists, journalists, Democrats, and "millions and millions of illegal aliens." He shrugged off the 120,000 Americans killed by the virus, joking that fewer infections would be found if he tested fewer people. Trump encouraged his followers to take matters into their own hands, praising Immigration and Customs Enforcement agents who "start swinging" at immigrants with criminal records and labeling them as "animals." He also urged the governor of Washington state to attack protesters, offering to send troops to put down domestic unrest. This speech was a call for more lies, hatred, demagoguery, and subversion of constitutional government, and perhaps even more violence. Trump's intent to accelerate the destruction of America is no longer hidden.

By promoting hate and division, Trump disregards the authority of democratic institutions and undermines the principles of fairness and equality. His words also demonstrate a lack of care for human life, as he trivialized the loss of 120,000 Americans to COVID-19. The speech highlights Trump's continued reliance on demagoguery and subversion, rather than addressing the real issues facing America.

END OF SUMMARY.
