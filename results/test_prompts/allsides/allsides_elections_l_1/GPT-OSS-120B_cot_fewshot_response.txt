**SUMMARY:**  
At a Tulsa rally, President Donald Trump relaunched his campaign by framing the 2020 election as a fight against “enmity” and “strife,” targeting anarchists, journalists, Democrats and “millions and millions of illegal aliens.” He dismissed the 120,000 COVID‑19 deaths, joking about “testing fewer people,” and called the virus “Kung Flu.” Trump labelled opponents “sadistic,” “vicious,” “sinister,” and a “dumb son of a bitch,” while branding reporters “the most dishonest people anywhere on earth.” He vilified immigrants as “animals,” protesters as “thugs” and “lunatics,” and warned that voting for Biden would bring “lies, hatred, demagoguery, subversion of constitutional government and destruction.” He praised ICE agents who “start swinging” and urged governors to use force, even suggesting he would enjoy sending troops to quell unrest. The speech was a cascade of moral condemnation, urging violence and promising a second term built on hatred and conflict.
