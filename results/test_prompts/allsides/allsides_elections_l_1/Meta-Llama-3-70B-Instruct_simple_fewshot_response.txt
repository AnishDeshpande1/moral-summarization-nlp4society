SUMMARY:

President Trump relaunched his reelection campaign in Tulsa, Oklahoma, with a divisive and inflammatory speech that pitted Americans against each other. Instead of highlighting his accomplishments, Trump devoted his speech to attacking and insulting his perceived enemies, including journalists, Democrats, and ethnic minorities. He downplayed the severity of the COVID-19 pandemic, joked about the death toll, and encouraged violence against immigrants and protesters. Trump's speech was filled with ethnic attacks, slurs, and insinuations, challenging the loyalty of naturalized Americans and pitting black Americans against Latin Americans. He appealed explicitly to violence, praising ICE agents who "start swinging" at immigrants and encouraging his followers to take matters into their own hands. Trump's speech was a stark warning of what a second term would look like: more lies, hatred, demagoguery, and subversion of constitutional government, with the potential for more violence. END OF SUMMARY.
