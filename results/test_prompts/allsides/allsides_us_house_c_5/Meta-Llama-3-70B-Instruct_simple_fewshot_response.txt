SUMMARY:

House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff has pushed back against calls for his resignation, defending his past comments about Trump-Russia collusion despite the conclusion of Special Counsel Robert Mueller's probe. Schiff listed contacts between the Trump campaign and Russia, calling them "not OK" and signaling his intent to continue investigating. Republicans, including President Trump, have attacked Schiff, claiming he has lost credibility and damaged the integrity of the committee. However, Democrats have rallied around Schiff, calling the GOP attack an "ambush" and "cheap-shot." The moral framing of this issue is clear: Schiff's pursuit of truth and accountability is being attacked by those who seek to undermine the legitimacy of the investigation and protect the president at all costs. The GOP's attempts to discredit Schiff and end his chairmanship are a blatant attempt to silence a vocal critic of the president and stifle the investigation into Trump's links to Russia. END OF SUMMARY.
