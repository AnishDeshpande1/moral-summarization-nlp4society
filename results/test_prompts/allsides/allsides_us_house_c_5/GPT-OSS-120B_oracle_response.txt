SUMMARY:  
House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff defended his “investigation” into the Trump campaign’s Russian “links” despite GOP accusations that his “promoting” of a “damaged” narrative has “abused” the panel’s integrity. Schiff warned that the president’s son being offered “dirt” to “help” Trump was “not OK,” and rebuked calls to force his resignation. Republicans, including Rep. Michael Turner, likened Schiff to Joseph McCarthy and claimed his actions have “interfered” with the committee, “prematurely” ending the GOP‑led probe and “reviving” and “expanding” the inquiry in a partisan “division.” They interrupted the hearing, expressing “dissatisfaction” and accusing him of “misrepresenting” classified information and “advancing” Putin’s work. Democrats rallied to Schiff’s “defense,” calling the GOP attack an “ambush” and “cheap‑shot.” The clash highlighted concerns that the president could be “compromised” by foreign powers and underscored the volatile politics surrounding the impeachment process.  

END OF SUMMARY.
