SUMMARY:
In a sharply partisan hearing, House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff defended his “not OK” assessment of the Trump campaign’s contacts with Russia, rejecting Republican calls for his resignation as a subversive attack on legitimate oversight. GOP members such as Rep. Mike Conaway and Rep. Michael Turner accused Schiff of betraying the committee’s integrity, likening his actions to McCarthy‑style witch hunts and claiming he is advancing Putin’s interests—an allegation framed as a moral breach of loyalty and an affront to the sanctity of the nation’s security. Democrats countered that the Republicans’ demands are a cheap‑shot ambush that harms democratic accountability and fairness, pointing to the Mueller report’s finding of no conclusive collusion and arguing that the GOP’s push to silence Schiff constitutes a cheating of the public’s right to truth. President Trump escalated the fight, tweeting that Schiff should be forced out for “knowingly and unlawfully lying and leaking,” further polarizing the debate over authority, loyalty, and the moral health of the nation’s institutions. The clash underscores a broader struggle over who commands legitimate authority and whose actions are deemed a betrayal of the country’s core values.  
END OF SUMMARY.
