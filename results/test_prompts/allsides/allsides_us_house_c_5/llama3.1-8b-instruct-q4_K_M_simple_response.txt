SUMMARY:

House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff (D-Calif.) is facing calls to step down from President Trump and other Republicans, who claim he has lost confidence in the panel by promoting a "demonstrably false" narrative about the Trump campaign's contacts with Russia. However, Schiff aggressively defended himself at an Intelligence Committee hearing on Russia, listing contacts between the Trump campaign and Russia that he described as "not OK." He also criticized the president and his family for their contacts with Russia, saying "I don’t think it is OK."

The hearing was marked by partisan divisions, with Republican Rep. Mike Conaway calling for Schiff to step down and Democratic Rep. Mike Quigley defending him against what he called an "ambush" and "cheap shot." The controversy comes after Attorney General William Barr's revelation that Mueller's investigation did not find evidence of collusion between the Trump campaign and Russia.

END OF SUMMARY.
