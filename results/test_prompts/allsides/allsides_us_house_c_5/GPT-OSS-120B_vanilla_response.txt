SUMMARY:  
House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff faced intense Republican criticism during a public impeachment hearing. Republicans demanded his resignation, accusing him of pushing a false narrative about Trump‑Russia collusion and likening his tactics to those of Joseph McCarthy. Rep. Mike Conaway and Rep. Michael Turner publicly called for Schiff to step down, claiming the committee’s integrity was compromised. Schiff defended his work, highlighting contacts between the Trump campaign and Russia and asserting that “it’s not OK” for the president’s son to be offered dirt. Democrats rallied behind him, condemning the GOP attacks as an “ambush” and “cheap‑shot.” The hearing occurred after the Justice Department announced that Mueller’s investigation found no evidence of conspiracy between the Trump campaign and Russia, a finding Trump used to claim exoneration and to demand Schiff’s resignation. Despite the partisan clash, Schiff said the committee will continue its broad probe into possible Russian influence and other foreign threats to the president.  

END OF SUMMARY.
