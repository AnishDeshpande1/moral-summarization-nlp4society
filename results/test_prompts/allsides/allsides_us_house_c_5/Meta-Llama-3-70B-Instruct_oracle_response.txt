SUMMARY:

House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff defended himself against calls to step down, pushing back against President Trump and Republicans who claim he damaged the committee's integrity by promoting a "demonstrably false" narrative about Trump's links to Russia. Schiff argued that it's "not OK" for the president's son to have been offered dirt as part of an effort to help Trump, and that the committee's investigation into Russian meddling and potential compromise of the president is necessary. Republicans, including Rep. Mike Conaway, attacked Schiff, saying he had lost confidence in the panel and was advancing Putin's work by misrepresenting information. Democrats defended Schiff, calling the GOP attack an "ambush" and "cheap-shot." The hearing was marked by partisan divisions and interruptions, with Schiff vowing to continue the investigation into Trump's financial and otherwise links to Russia.

END OF SUMMARY.
