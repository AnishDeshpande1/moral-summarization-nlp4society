SUMMARY:
House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff faced fierce Republican backlash during a hearing on Russia, with GOP members demanding his resignation and accusing him of pushing a “demonstrably false” collusion narrative that undermines the committee’s integrity. Republicans such as Rep. Mike Conaway and Rep. Michael Turner likened Schiff to Joseph McCarthy and claimed he is advancing Putin’s agenda, while President Trump tweeted that Schiff should be forced out for “knowingly and unlawfully lying and leaking.” Democrats defended Schiff, calling the attacks an “ambush” and “cheap‑shot.” The article frames Schiff’s continued probe into Trump’s Russian contacts as an abuse of power and partisan witch‑hunt, suggesting that his refusal to step down endangers proper congressional oversight and fuels political division. The moral judgment presented is that Schiff is protecting a corrupt administration by perpetuating baseless accusations, whereas Republicans are standing up for truth and constitutional responsibility.
