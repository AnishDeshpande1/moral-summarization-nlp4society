SUMMARY:

House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff defended himself against calls to step down from President Trump and other Republicans, citing contacts between the Trump campaign and Russia that he deemed "not OK." Schiff's fierce defense came after Rep. Mike Conaway called for him to step down, accusing him of promoting a "demonstrably false" narrative that has "damaged" the "integrity" of their panel. Trump and his GOP allies have seized on the revelation that Mueller's investigation did not find evidence of collusion, claiming it exonerates Trump and attacking Schiff and other Democrats who described what they viewed as evidence of collusion. Schiff has said he plans to move forward with the panel's investigation into Trump's links to Russia, alleging that the president could be potentially "compromised" by the Kremlin or another foreign power. Republicans have accused Schiff of "lying" and "leaking" and "abusing" his power, with some likening him to Joseph McCarthy.

END OF SUMMARY.
