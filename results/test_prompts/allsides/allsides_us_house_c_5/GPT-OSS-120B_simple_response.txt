SUMMARY: In a sharply partisan showdown, House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff defended his relentless probe into President Donald Trump’s Russian contacts, denouncing the alleged collusion as “not OK” and refusing calls for his resignation. Schiff’s remarks came after Republican members, including Rep. Mike Turner, accused him of abusing power and likening him to Joseph McCarthy, while GOP leaders demanded the whistleblower be exposed and called for Schiff’s removal. Democrats rallied to Schiff’s defense, branding the Republican attacks an “ambush” and a “cheap‑shot.” The hearing underscored the moral battle over truth and accountability: Trump and his allies hailed the Mueller report’s lack of criminal conspiracy as exoneration, while Schiff and fellow Democrats insisted that the investigation remains vital to uncovering possible foreign compromise of the president. The episode highlighted the deep ethical divide in Congress, with Republicans seeking to silence the inquiry and Democrats insisting that transparency and responsibility must prevail. END OF SUMMARY.
