SUMMARY:

House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff aggressively pushed back against calls for him to step down, defending his past comments on President Trump's contacts with Russia. Schiff described the president's son being offered dirt as part of an effort to help Trump as "not OK," signaling he'll fiercely defend himself from accusations of promoting a "demonstrably false" narrative that has damaged the integrity of the panel.

The controversy comes after Attorney General William Barr notified Congress that Mueller's investigation did not find evidence of collusion between the Trump campaign and Russia. However, Schiff plans to move forward with the panel's sprawling investigation into Trump's links to Russia, which he believes could potentially compromise the president.

Republicans interrupted the hearing, with Rep. Michael Turner likening Schiff to Joseph McCarthy and accusing him of advancing Putin's work by abusing his power and misrepresenting information. Democrats came to Schiff's defense, calling the GOP attack an "ambush" and a "cheap-shot."

END OF SUMMARY.
