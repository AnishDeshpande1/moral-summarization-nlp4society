SUMMARY:

House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff (D-Calif.) pushed back against calls for him to step down from leading the Trump impeachment trial, defending his past comments about the president and his family's contacts with Russia. Schiff listed contacts between the Trump campaign and Russia that he described as "not OK" and signaled he would fiercely defend himself from the calls to end his chairmanship. Republicans, including Rep. Mike Conaway (R-Texas), called for Schiff to step down, accusing him of promoting a "demonstrably false" narrative that has damaged the "integrity" of their panel. President Trump also called for Schiff's resignation from Congress, claiming he lied and leaked information. Democrats defended Schiff, calling the GOP attack an "ambush" and "cheap-shot".

END OF SUMMARY.
