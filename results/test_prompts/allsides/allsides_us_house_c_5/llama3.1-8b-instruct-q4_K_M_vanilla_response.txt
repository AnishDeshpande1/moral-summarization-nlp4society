SUMMARY:

House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam Schiff (D-Calif.) pushed back against calls for him to step down, defending his past comments on Trump's contacts with Russia. Schiff argued that it was not okay for the president's son to have been offered dirt from Russia and signaled he would fiercely defend himself from criticism. The controversy comes after Attorney General William Barr notified Congress that Mueller's investigation did not find evidence of collusion between the Trump campaign and Russia.

Rep. Mike Conaway (R-Texas) called for Schiff to step down, saying he had lost confidence in the panel due to a "demonstrably false" narrative. Other Republicans, including Rep. Michael Turner (R-Ohio), accused Schiff of advancing Putin's work and misrepresenting information. Democrats defended Schiff, calling the GOP attack an "ambush." The hearing marked another dramatic flashpoint for the House Intelligence Committee, which has been known for its partisan divisions.

END OF SUMMARY.
