SUMMARY:

House Speaker John Boehner is calling on President Obama to release emails that allegedly show how the White House wanted to change the Benghazi attack "talking points" to downplay the role of extremist groups linked to al Qaeda. Boehner claims that senior White House officials directed changes to the talking points to avoid embarrassing the State Department for missing early warnings. The Obama administration has previously acknowledged altering the talking points to preserve classified information and avoid impacting the FBI investigation. However, Boehner argues that there is no evidence that anyone within the administration raised concerns about securing classified information. The controversy highlights the administration's lack of transparency and accountability in the wake of the Benghazi attack, which resulted in the deaths of four Americans. Secretary of State John Kerry has vowed to "leave no stone unturned" in investigating the matter, but the administration's handling of the issue has sparked widespread criticism and mistrust.

END OF SUMMARY.
