SUMMARY:
House Speaker John Boehner demanded that President Obama release emails showing senior White House officials deliberately altered the Benghazi “talking points” to downplay the role of Islamist terrorists and protect the State Department from embarrassment. Boehner argued the administration lied, claiming only “stylistic” changes, while a GOP‑led report says the edits removed references to al‑Qaeda‑linked groups. He also pressed for an email from State Department Acting Assistant Secretary Beth Jones that, a day after the attack, labeled the perpetrators “Islamic terrorists” – an email the State Department allegedly blocked from congressional copies. Rep. Trey Gowdy read excerpts, highlighting that the State Department warned the Libyan ambassador of the terrorist link before Susan Rice’s televised statements portraying the incident as a protest. Secretary of State John Kerry, speaking from Rome, pledged a thorough investigation and promised accountability if misconduct is found. The moral framing underscores a perceived cover‑up by the Obama administration and a call for transparency and responsibility.

END OF SUMMARY.
