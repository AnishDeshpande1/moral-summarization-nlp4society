SUMMARY:

House Speaker John Boehner is calling on President Obama to release emails that show how the White House altered talking points about the Benghazi attack to remove references to extremist groups linked to al Qaeda. Boehner claims that senior White House officials directed changes to the talking points, which were used by Ambassador Susan Rice in her Sunday morning news shows appearance. The House GOP report found no evidence that anyone within the administration raised concerns about preserving classified information.

Boehner also wants the disclosure of an email from a top State Department official who referred to "Islamic terrorists" as carrying out the attack, contradicting Rice's claim that it was a spontaneous protest. Secretary of State John Kerry vowed to get answers and put the issue to rest, promising to handle any culpability with discipline.

The controversy surrounding the Benghazi attack has raised questions about the administration's handling of sensitive information and its willingness to alter facts to suit their narrative. The issue continues to be a contentious one for both parties.

END OF SUMMARY.
