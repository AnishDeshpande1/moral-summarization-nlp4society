SUMMARY: House Speaker John Boehner urged President Obama to release emails that, according to a GOP‑led House report, show White House officials directing changes to the talking points used by UN Ambassador Susan Rice after the September 11, 2012 Benghazi attack. The report claims the alterations removed references to extremist groups to shield the State Department from criticism over missed warnings. Boehner also demanded disclosure of a State Department email from September 12 that labeled the attackers “Islamic terrorists,” which he says was withheld from committees. Rep. Trey Gowdy read excerpts of that email, noting it was sent before Rice’s televised statements describing the incident as a protest‑turned‑violence. The administration has said changes were merely stylistic to protect classified information. Secretary of State John Kerry, speaking from Rome, pledged a thorough investigation, promising accountability if misconduct is found. END OF SUMMARY.
