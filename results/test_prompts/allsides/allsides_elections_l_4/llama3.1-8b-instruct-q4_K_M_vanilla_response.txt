SUMMARY:

Donald Trump has softened his stance on immigration, a key issue in his campaign, by suggesting that undocumented migrants will pay back taxes and work with the US government to remain in the country. This shift comes as he tries to court non-white voters, who are largely supporting Hillary Clinton according to recent polls. Trump's previous statements on immigration were often inconsistent and vague.

In another controversy, Trump claimed that inner cities are more violent than war zones, which is not supported by crime statistics. The US has seen a decline in crime over the past quarter century, with some low-income areas continuing to show high rates of criminal activity.

Trump also repeated an unsubstantiated statistic about African American unemployment, stating that 58% of young black men and women are unemployed. However, the latest figures from the Bureau of Labor Statistics put this number at 25.7%. Economists have criticized Trump's campaign for using misleading statistics to make their point.

END OF SUMMARY.
