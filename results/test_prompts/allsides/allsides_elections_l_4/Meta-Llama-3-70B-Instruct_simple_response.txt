SUMMARY:

Donald Trump, the GOP nominee, has softened his stance on immigration, a key issue in his campaign, in an attempt to court non-white voters. However, his statements on the matter have been contradictory and misleading. Trump claimed that undocumented immigrants would not get citizenship, but would have to pay back taxes and work with the government. He also met with African American and Latino activists, trying to appeal to these voter groups. Meanwhile, Trump made false and offensive claims about inner cities, stating that they are more dangerous than war zones and that crime is at record highs. In reality, crime rates have been declining for over 25 years. Trump also exaggerated the unemployment rate among young African Americans, claiming it was 58% when the actual rate is 25.7%. His campaign's methodology for calculating this statistic has been criticized by economists as misleading. Trump's attempts to appeal to non-white voters ring hollow in the face of his dishonest and divisive rhetoric.

END OF SUMMARY.
