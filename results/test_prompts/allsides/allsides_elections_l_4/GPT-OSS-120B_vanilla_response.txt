SUMMARY:  
The article critiques Donald Trump’s recent attempts to moderate his hard‑line immigration stance and to court non‑white voters. After meeting his Hispanic advisory council, Trump suggested undocumented immigrants would pay taxes and face no amnesty, hinting at a possible path to legal status, while still denying any “flip‑flopping.” Simultaneously, he delivered inflammatory remarks about inner‑city crime, claiming U.S. cities are safer in war zones and portraying African‑American communities as devastated by Democratic policies. Trump cited a claim that 58 % of black youth are unemployed—a figure derived from a misleading calculation that inflates the official Bureau of Labor Statistics rate of about 26 %. Fact‑checks reveal few Black executives in his companies and that his crime narrative contradicts long‑term declines in U.S. crime rates. Polls show Trump trailing heavily among Hispanic and African‑American voters, with only about 1 % support from Black voters. The piece highlights the gap between Trump’s rhetoric and the underlying data, labeling many of his statements as false or deceptive.  

END OF SUMMARY.
