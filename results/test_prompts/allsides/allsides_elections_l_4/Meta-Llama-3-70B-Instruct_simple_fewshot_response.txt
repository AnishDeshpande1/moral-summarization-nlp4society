SUMMARY:

Donald Trump, the GOP nominee, has softened his stance on immigration, a key issue in his campaign. After meeting with his Hispanic advisory council, Trump suggested that undocumented immigrants might not be deported, contradicting his previous promises. He also stated that they would have to pay back taxes, with no amnesty. This shift in stance comes as Trump tries to court non-white voters, particularly Hispanics and African Americans. However, his claims about the safety of inner cities and African American unemployment have been disputed. Trump claimed that war zones are safer than America's inner cities, which is not supported by crime statistics, and that 58% of African American youth are unemployed, a figure that has been disputed by economists. Trump's attempts to reach out to minority voters have been met with skepticism, with many questioning his sincerity and accuracy.

END OF SUMMARY.
