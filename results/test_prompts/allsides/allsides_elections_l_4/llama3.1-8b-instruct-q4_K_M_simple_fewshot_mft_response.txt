SUMMARY:

President Trump's immigration stance has been inconsistent, with him initially promising to deport undocumented migrants and later appearing to soften his position. In an interview with Fox News, Trump said immigrants would have to pay back taxes but would not receive amnesty, contradicting previous statements. This shift in stance comes as he tries to court Hispanic and African American voters, who are crucial for his election chances. However, polls show him trailing Hillary Clinton among non-white voters.

Trump's claims about crime in inner cities are also misleading, as crime statistics have been declining over the past quarter century. His statement that 58% of young black men and women are unemployed is also exaggerated, using a flawed calculation that includes people not actively seeking employment. This rhetoric may be intended to exploit fears and anxieties among African American voters, but it is morally reprehensible.

Trump's stance on immigration and crime highlights his disregard for accuracy and fairness, as well as his willingness to use divisive rhetoric to gain support. His actions demonstrate a lack of care for the well-being of marginalized communities and a subversion of authority, as he ignores expert evidence and statistical facts to advance his own agenda.

END OF SUMMARY.
