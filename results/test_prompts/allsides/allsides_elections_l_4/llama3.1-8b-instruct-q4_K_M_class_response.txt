SUMMARY:

Donald Trump, the GOP nominee, has been accused of telling lies about his immigration stance, contradicting previous statements on deporting undocumented migrants. He now claims to work with them and offer a path forward, but still denies amnesty. This shift in tone comes as he tries to court non-white voters, particularly African Americans and Latinos, who are largely supporting Hillary Clinton.

Trump's claims about inner city crime are also being questioned, with experts pointing out that crime rates have been declining for over 25 years. He uses language that is offensive to the very voters he's trying to reach, stating that war zones are safer than some of America's inner cities. Trump's statement about African American unemployment, claiming 58% of young black men and women are unemployed, has also been debunked by economists as statistically misleading.

Trump's campaign has raised eyebrows with its use of flawed statistics and manipulation of data to make his case. As he tries to appeal to minority voters, he is being met with skepticism and criticism for his lack of authenticity and commitment to addressing the issues that truly matter to them. END OF SUMMARY.
