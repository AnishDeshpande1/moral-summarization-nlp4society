SUMMARY:

Donald Trump, the GOP nominee, has softened his stance on immigration, a signature issue of his campaign, in an attempt to court non-white voters. However, his words are laced with lies and manipulation. He claims that undocumented immigrants will "pay back taxes" and "work with them," but his previous promises of deportation contradict this new stance. Trump's rhetoric is also offensive, as he paints a bleak picture of America's inner cities, claiming they are more dangerous than war zones. This is a blatant lie, as crime statistics show a steady decline since 1991. Furthermore, Trump's claims of employing many African Americans are misleading, as there are "few if any" black executives in his organization. His outreach to African American voters is also questionable, as he delivers speeches to largely white audiences and receives only 1% of their support. Trump's lies and manipulation are a clear attempt to deceive and exploit vulnerable communities. END OF SUMMARY.
