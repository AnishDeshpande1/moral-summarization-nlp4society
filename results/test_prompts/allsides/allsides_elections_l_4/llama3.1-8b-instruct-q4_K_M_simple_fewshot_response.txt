SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump's immigration proposal has been met with criticism from Democratic lawmakers, who accuse him of holding federal workers "hostage" by linking protections for DACA recipients to funding for a border wall. Trump's plan would extend protections for 700,000 DACA recipients in exchange for $5.7 billion for the wall, but Democrats argue it is "one-sided" and does not provide a permanent solution for Dreamers and TPS recipients. Trump has also made contradictory statements on immigration, initially suggesting that undocumented migrants would be deported, but later saying they would have to pay taxes and work with authorities. This latest proposal comes as Trump tries to court non-white voters, including African Americans and Hispanics, who are crucial in the 2020 election. However, his efforts have been marred by controversy, including claims of lying about African American unemployment rates and using language that is offensive to minority communities.
