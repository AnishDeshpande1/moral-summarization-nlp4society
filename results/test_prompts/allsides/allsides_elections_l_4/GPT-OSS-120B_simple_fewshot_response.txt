SUMMARY:
Donald Trump’s campaign is back‑tracking on its hallmark hard‑line immigration stance while desperately courting Hispanic and Black voters. After a meeting with his Hispanic advisory council, Trump told Fox’s Sean Hannity there would be “no amnesty” but that undocumented workers could “pay taxes” and “work with us,” a vague concession that contradicts his earlier promise of mass deportations. The next day he held a round‑table with African‑American and Latino activists, yet his rhetoric turned hostile, claiming inner‑city crime is “worse than war zones” and accusing Democrats of abandoning Black communities. He inflated Black youth unemployment to 58 % by misusing Bureau of Labor Statistics data, mixing non‑workers into the count to exaggerate the crisis. Fact‑checks show overall crime has declined for decades and Trump’s businesses employ few Black executives, undermining his claims of “so many” Black employees. The campaign’s pattern of falsehoods and manipulative statistics reveals a morally dubious strategy: distorting reality to win minority votes while offering no genuine policy solutions. END OF SUMMARY.
