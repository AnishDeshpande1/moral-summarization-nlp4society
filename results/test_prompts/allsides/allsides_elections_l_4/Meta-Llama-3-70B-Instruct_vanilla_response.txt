SUMMARY:

Donald Trump has softened his stance on immigration, a key issue in his campaign, and is now trying to court non-white voters. After meeting with his Hispanic advisory council, Trump suggested that undocumented immigrants could pay back taxes and work with the government, contradicting his previous promises of deportation. He also met with African American and Latino activists, attempting to appeal to these groups. However, Trump's claims about inner cities being more dangerous than war zones and African American unemployment rates have been disputed. He claimed that 58% of African American youth are unemployed, but the actual rate is 25.7%. Trump's attempts to reach out to non-white voters come as he trails Hillary Clinton in polls, with a 62-point deficit among likely non-white voters.

END OF SUMMARY.
