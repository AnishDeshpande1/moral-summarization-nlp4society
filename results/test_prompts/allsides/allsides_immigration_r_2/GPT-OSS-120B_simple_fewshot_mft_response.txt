SUMMARY:
Texas Governor Greg Abbott broke with every other GOP governor by refusing to accept any refugee resettlement, arguing that a “broken” federal immigration system and limited state resources force Texas to prioritize its own residents—including migrants and the homeless—over newcomers. The decision sparked an outcry from progressive voices, whom the author labels “woke police” and “morality police,” accusing Abbott of cruelty, xenophobia and violating American values. Critics such as Tom Steyer, Catherine Rampell, Monica Rhor, RAICES Texas and the Dallas Morning News denounce the move as “inhumane,” “shameful” and “morally wrong,” claiming it betrays the nation’s historic duty to welcome the persecuted. The author rebuts these claims, framing the anti‑refugee stance as an exercise of legitimate state authority against a subversive liberal agenda that inflates the moral significance of refugees while ignoring the real harms to native citizens, the strain on public services, and the unfair burden placed on taxpayers. By refusing mass migration, the author argues Texas upholds a sanctified national identity and protects the fairness of resource distribution, warning that unchecked immigration would degrade the country’s social fabric and overwhelm its institutions.  
END OF SUMMARY.
