SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Greg Abbott has become the first GOP governor to decline refugee resettlement, citing the disproportionate impact on the border state due to the federal government's broken immigration system. Critics, including presidential candidate Tom Steyer and columnists Monica Rhor and Catherine Rampell, have labeled the decision as "inhumane," "shameful," and "morally wrong." However, the author argues that proponents of mass migration are actually causing harm to both destination and origin countries, leading to a decline in the quality of life for all involved. The author suggests that the true moral course of action is not to encourage migration, but to find effective ways to help those in need in their own countries. The article concludes that the push for mass migration is driven by a twisted definition of virtue and a disregard for the well-being of citizens in destination countries.

END OF SUMMARY.
