SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Greg Abbott became the first Republican governor to decline refugee resettlement, citing the impact on his state's resources and infrastructure. This decision was met with criticism from left-wing groups and media outlets, who accused him of being "inhumane" and violating American values.

The author argues that proponents of mass migration are often motivated by a twisted sense of virtue and fail to consider the consequences of their actions. They claim that mass migration can lead to a decline in living standards for those left behind in migrant-sending countries, as well as strain on social services and infrastructure in destination countries.

The author also questions the motivations of liberal politicians who advocate for mass immigration while ignoring the needs of their own citizens, such as homelessness in cities like Austin. They argue that the true moral course of action is to help migrants where they are, rather than encouraging them to migrate.
