SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Greg Abbott has become the first GOP governor to decline refugee resettlement, citing the "disproportionate" impact on the border state due to the federal government's "broken" immigration system. Critics, including presidential candidate Tom Steyer and columnists, have labeled the decision as "inhumane," "shameful," and "morally wrong." However, the article argues that proponents of mass immigration are actually causing harm to both destination and origin countries, leading to a brain drain in the latter and strain on social services and infrastructure in the former. The author suggests that instead of encouraging migration, the focus should be on helping people in need in their own countries. The article also criticizes establishment conservatives and Democrats who downplay the numbers of refugees and immigrants, warning that a tipping point will be reached if their wishes are granted, leading to a fundamental change in America's character.
