SUMMARY:
The article condemns Texas Gov. Greg Abbott’s decision to stop refugee resettlement, not because it is morally right, but because it exposes a broader cultural clash over immigration. The author argues that liberal criticism—labeling the move “inhumane,” “shameful,” and “un‑American”—is a moral panic that conflates refugees with all migrants and serves a “woke” agenda. He claims that mass migration, even when limited to vetted refugees, ultimately harms both destination and origin countries: it strains social services, erodes national character, and drains the “best people” from poorer nations, leaving them worse off. The piece suggests that true moral action is to aid people in their home countries rather than encouraging their relocation. While acknowledging that current refugee numbers are small, the author warns that continued liberal support for any increase could eventually tip the demographic balance, leading to a “socialist‑leaning” America that no longer reflects “freedom‑loving” values. The moral framing presented is that anti‑refugee policies are defensible and that the moral high ground belongs to those who prioritize national resources and cultural cohesion over open‑border altruism.

END OF SUMMARY.
