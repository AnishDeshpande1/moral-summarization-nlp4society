SUMMARY: Texas Governor Greg Abbott became the first GOP governor to reject federal refugee resettlement, arguing the policy strains the state’s resources. His move sparked widespread criticism from Democrats, media outlets, and immigration advocates, who labeled the decision “inhumane,” “shameful,” and contrary to American values. The article then shifts to a broader, partisan argument against refugee and mass immigration, claiming such policies are driven by xenophobia and fear, burden social services, and harm both destination and origin countries by draining talent. The author contends that the moral response should focus on aiding people in their home nations rather than encouraging migration, warning that even modest refugee numbers could eventually lead to demographic and cultural shifts that threaten “freedom‑loving” America. The piece also references a counter‑argument from the Washington Examiner, which downplays refugee figures and defends accepting them, but the author dismisses this as insufficient to prevent a future “tipping point.”
