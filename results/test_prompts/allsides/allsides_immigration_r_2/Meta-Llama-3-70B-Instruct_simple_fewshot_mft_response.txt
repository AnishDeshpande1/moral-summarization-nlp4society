SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Greg Abbott's decision to decline refugee resettlement has sparked outrage from the "woke police," who accuse him of being "inhumane" and "morally wrong." However, the author argues that the true moral high ground lies with immigration hawks, who recognize the negative impact of mass migration on both destination and origin countries. By creating a system that encourages the strong and wealthy to leave their home countries, proponents of mass immigration have actually made the world a worse place. The author contends that the moral course of action is not to encourage migration, but to find effective ways to help people in need in their own countries. The critics' accusations of xenophobia and fear are merely a smokescreen to impose their twisted definition of "virtue" on society. The author's stance is rooted in a commitment to fairness, care, and sanctity, and a rejection of the subversion of American values and the harm caused by unchecked immigration.

END OF SUMMARY.
