SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Greg Abbott has become the first GOP governor to decline refugee resettlement, citing the "disproportionate" impact on the border state due to the Federal government's "broken" immigration system. Critics, including presidential candidate Tom Steyer, have condemned the decision as "inhumane" and "morally wrong." However, the author argues that the true moral course of action is not to encourage migration, but to find effective ways to help refugees and their countries of origin. The author contends that mass migration creates a vacuum that draws millions of people, leading to negative impacts on both destination and origin countries. By encouraging migration, proponents of mass immigration have actually caused the entire planet to become worse. The author suggests that the focus should be on helping people in their own countries rather than encouraging them to migrate.

END OF SUMMARY.
