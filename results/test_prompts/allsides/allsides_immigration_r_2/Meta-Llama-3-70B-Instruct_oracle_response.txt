SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Greg Abbott's decision to decline refugee resettlement has sparked outrage from critics who accuse him of being "inhumane" and "morally wrong." Abbott cited the "disproportionate" impact on the border state due to the Federal government's "broken" immigration system. However, proponents of mass migration argue that welcoming refugees is fundamental to America's national character. The author counters that mass migration creates a vacuum that draws millions, leading to a worse outcome for both destination and origin countries. By encouraging migration, proponents are causing the planet to become worse, negatively impacting social services, infrastructure, and social fabric. The author argues that the true moral course of action is to find effective ways to help those in need in their own countries, rather than encouraging migration. This decision is not about turning backs on those in need, but about taking responsibility for those already in the country, including refugees, migrants, and the homeless. END OF SUMMARY.
