Texas Governor Greg Abbott's decision to decline refugee resettlement in his state has sparked outrage from liberal critics who claim it is "inhumane" and violates American values. However, the author of this article argues that proponents of mass migration have caused the entire planet to become worse by creating a system where those strong enough to move are encouraged to do so, leaving behind origin countries with declining prospects for improvement. The author suggests that encouraging migration rather than finding ways to help people right where they are is not the true moral course of action. They argue that mass immigration will eventually lead to a tipping point where socialist-leaning voters outnumber freedom-loving ones, causing America to "cease to be America." This perspective challenges the conventional wisdom that open borders and mass migration are morally justifiable.
