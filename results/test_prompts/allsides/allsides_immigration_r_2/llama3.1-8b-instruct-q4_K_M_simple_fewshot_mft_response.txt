SUMMARY:

Texas Governor Greg Abbott has declined refugee resettlement in his state, citing the impact on border states due to the federal government's "broken" immigration system. Critics have condemned Abbott's decision as "inhumane" and a violation of American values, accusing him of xenophobia and fear-mongering. However, the author argues that proponents of mass migration are actually causing harm to both destination and origin countries, creating social and economic problems. The author suggests that encouraging migration rather than finding effective ways to help those in need is not the moral course of action. They argue that allowing a billion people to migrate to the West would lead to significant social, economic, and cultural changes, potentially making destination countries worse off. The author also criticizes establishment "conservatives" for downplaying the numbers of refugees and immigrants, ignoring the potential consequences of unchecked migration.
