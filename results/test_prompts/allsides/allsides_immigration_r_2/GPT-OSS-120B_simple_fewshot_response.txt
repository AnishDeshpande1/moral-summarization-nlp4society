SUMMARY:
Texas Governor Greg Abbott became the first GOP governor to block refugee resettlement, arguing the “broken” federal immigration system and limited state resources should focus on residents, migrants already here, and the homeless. The decision sparked a chorus of condemnation from progressives, whom the author derides as “woke police” imposing a twisted virtue, labeling the move “inhumane,” “shameful,” and “morally wrong.” The piece argues that critics conflate refugee aid with broader immigration, but the author deliberately treats them as the same threat: an influx of impoverished, culturally different peoples that strains social services, infrastructure, and the social fabric of destination nations while draining talent from origin countries. It claims mass migration ultimately harms both host and source societies and that the true moral path is to help people in their own lands rather than invite them in. The author warns that even modest refugee numbers, combined with broader immigration reforms, could eventually tip the political balance toward “socialist‑leaning” voters, eroding America’s character.

END OF SUMMARY.
