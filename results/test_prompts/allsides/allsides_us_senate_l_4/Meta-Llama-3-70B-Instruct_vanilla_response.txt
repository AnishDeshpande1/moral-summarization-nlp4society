SUMMARY:

Senator Al Franken's resignation speech failed to show contrition for his alleged sexual misconduct, instead focusing on his own struggles and offering advice on running for office. He did not apologize or acknowledge the harm done to the women who accused him, contradicting his earlier statement where he expressed remorse. Franken's speech was criticized for not following the five-point checklist for a helpful public apology, which includes specifically acknowledging the harm done, admitting one's role, expressing remorse, explaining which behaviors will be stopped, and sharing a plan for making amends. By not taking responsibility for his actions, Franken's speech may have harmed the cause of protecting women from men's abuses of power and made it harder to fight against injustices.

END OF SUMMARY.
