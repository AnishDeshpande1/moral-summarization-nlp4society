SUMMARY:

Al Franken's resignation speech on Thursday was criticized for lacking contrition and sincerity. Despite multiple women reporting that he had groped or harassed them, Franken did not apologize in his speech. Instead, he focused on his own struggles and offered advice to those running for office. He also seemed to take back his previous apologies, stating that some of the allegations against him were "simply not true." This approach was seen as a failure to acknowledge the harm done and admit his role in it.

Franken's speech was criticized by sociologist David Karp, who outlined a five-point checklist for helpful public apologies. Franken's statement failed on the first step, specifically acknowledging the harm done. By going back on his earlier statements, Franken essentially took down his accusers with him, making it harder to fight against injustices such as harassment and abuse of power.

END OF SUMMARY.
