SUMMARY:

Democratic Senator Al Franken's resignation speech was an opportunity for him to show contrition and take responsibility for his actions, but he squandered it by failing to apologize and instead giving advice on running for office. Franken previously apologized for his behavior, saying "there are some women... who feel that I have done something disrespectful that's hurt them, and for that I am tremendously sorry." However, in his resignation speech, he took back those apologies and offered a vague denial of the reports by his accusers. This decision has harmed the cause of protecting women from men's abuses of power and encourages voters to doubt and shame the accusers. By prioritizing his own reputation over taking responsibility for his actions, Franken has made it harder to fight against injustices like harassment and assault. His behavior is a stark reminder that accountability and taking responsibility for one's actions are essential in holding public figures accountable, especially when it comes to issues of power and abuse.
