SUMMARY:

Al Franken's resignation speech on Thursday was an opportunity for him to show contrition and take responsibility for his actions, but he squandered it by not apologizing or taking accountability. Instead, he focused on his own struggles and gave advice on running for office as if he were still a role model. This master class in what not to do when accused of sexual misconduct encouraged voters to doubt and shame the women who came forward with allegations against him. Franken's decision to retract his earlier apology and deny the allegations undermined the cause he claims to care about - protecting women from men's abuses of power. By prioritizing his own reputation, he took his accusers down with him, making it harder to fight for justice in similar cases.
