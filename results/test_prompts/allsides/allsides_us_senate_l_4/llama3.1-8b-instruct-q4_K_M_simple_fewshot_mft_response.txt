SUMMARY:

Sen. Al Franken's resignation speech was an opportunity for him to show contrition and take responsibility for his actions, but he squandered it by failing to apologize sincerely. Instead of acknowledging the harm done, admitting his role in it, expressing remorse, explaining which behaviors he'll stop in the future, and sharing a plan for making amends, Franken focused on his own feelings and offered advice on running for office as though he were still a role model. By going back on his earlier statements, Franken essentially took all of his accusers down with him, harming their credibility and making it harder to fight against harassment and abuse. This betrayal of the victims is a stark reminder of the importance of accountability in positions of power, particularly when it comes to issues of consent and respect for women's boundaries. By prioritizing his own reputation over doing what was right, Franken undermined the cause he claims to care about – protecting women from men's abuses of power.

END OF SUMMARY.
