SUMMARY:

Al Franken's resignation speech was a missed opportunity to show contrition and apologize sincerely for his sexual misconduct. Instead, he focused on his own struggles, offered advice on running for office, and seemed to take back his previous apologies. Franken's statement failed to acknowledge the harm done, admit his role, express remorse, or explain how he would make amends. He claimed some allegations were false and that his previous apology may have given a "false impression." This contrasts with his earlier statement, where he said he was "tremendously sorry" for his actions. Franken's denial of some allegations and lack of clear explanation of his wrongdoing encouraged voters to doubt and shame his accusers. By prioritizing his own reputation over helping the cause of protecting women from men's abuses of power, Franken made it harder to fight injustices. He chose to harm the women who came forward rather than help them. His speech was a master class in what not to do when accused of sexual misconduct.

END OF SUMMARY.
