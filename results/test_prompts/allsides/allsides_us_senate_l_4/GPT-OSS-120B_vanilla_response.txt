SUMMARY: Senator Al Franken (D‑MN) resigned after multiple women accused him of sexual harassment, but his resignation speech failed to apologize or acknowledge the harm. Instead of taking responsibility, Franken offered vague denials, focused on personal struggles, and gave political advice, undermining the victims and missing an opportunity to support the broader #MeToo movement. Experts note that a proper apology should acknowledge harm, admit responsibility, express remorse, outline behavior changes, and propose amends—steps Franken omitted. By retracting his earlier apology and casting doubt on the accusations, he bolstered narratives that dismiss women's reports and prioritized his own reputation over accountability. The article argues that his actions hinder progress on addressing sexual misconduct in politics, contrasting his departure with ongoing controversies involving figures like Roy Moore and Donald Trump. END OF SUMMARY.
