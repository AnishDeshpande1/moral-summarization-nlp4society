SUMMARY: 

Senator Al Franken's resignation speech fell short of apologizing for his sexual misconduct, instead focusing on his own struggles and offering vague denials of the allegations against him. This was a missed opportunity to show contrition and take responsibility for his actions. Franken's statement failed to meet the basic criteria for a helpful public apology, including acknowledging harm done, admitting his role, expressing remorse, and explaining how he would make amends. By backtracking on his earlier apologies, Franken undermined the cause of protecting women from men's abuses of power and gave ammunition to those who doubt the accounts of his accusers. His speech was a "master class in what not to do" when accused of sexual misconduct. Franken's decision to prioritize his own reputation over taking responsibility for his actions made it harder to fight against injustices like sexual harassment and assault.
