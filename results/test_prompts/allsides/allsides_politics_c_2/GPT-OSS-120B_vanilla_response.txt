SUMMARY: Retired Marine General Jim Mattis, who resigned as Defense Secretary in 2018, issued a rare public rebuke of President Trump’s handling of the nationwide protests after George Floyd’s death. In an Atlantic op‑ed, Mattis condemned the clearing of peaceful demonstrators from near the White House for a Trump photo‑op, calling it an “abuse of executive authority” and warning against “militarizing” the response. He stressed that the Constitution should never be violated by troops and that military deployment domestically should occur only at a state governor’s request and in extreme cases. Mattis also criticized Trump’s divisive rhetoric, saying the president deliberately splits the nation and lacks mature leadership. The statement coincided with Defense Secretary Mark Esper’s refusal to invoke the Insurrection Act and with former Joint Chiefs leaders also denouncing the use of the military against protesters. Trump responded by mocking Mattis on Twitter. Mattis concluded by praising frontline workers and urging unity under the Constitution’s “better angels.”  
END OF SUMMARY.
