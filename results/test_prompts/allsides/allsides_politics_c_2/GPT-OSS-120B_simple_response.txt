SUMMARY:
Retired Marine General Jim Mattis, former Defense Secretary, broke his usual silence to deliver a forceful moral rebuke of President Trump’s handling of the nationwide protests after George Floyd’s death. In an Atlantic op‑ed, Mattis invoked his oath to defend the Constitution, condemning the “abuse of executive authority” that ordered troops to clear peaceful demonstrators for a Trump photo‑op at a Washington church and warning against “militarizing” the response. He denounced Trump’s divisive rhetoric, the framing of cities as a “battlespace,” and any use of the Insurrection Act except in rare, governor‑requested cases. Mattis praised the protesters’ demand for equal justice, called the demonstrations “wholesome and unifying,” and urged the nation to follow its “better angels” rather than treat fellow citizens as enemies. The statement echoed similar moral condemnations from former Joint Chiefs leaders, emphasizing that the military’s role is to protect, not police, the populace. Mattis’ critique framed the president’s actions as a violation of constitutional rights and a threat to national unity, urging accountability and a return to core American values.  
END OF SUMMARY.
