SUMMARY:
Retired Marine Gen. Jim Mattis broke his usual silence to condemn President Trump’s handling of the nationwide protests, calling it an “abuse of executive authority” that threatens constitutional rights. Mattis warned that militarizing the response—using troops or the Insurrection Act to “dominate” cities—subverts the proper authority of civilian leadership and endangers the sacred duty of the military to protect, not oppress, fellow citizens. He framed the peaceful demonstrators’ demand for equal justice as a matter of fairness and moral care, insisting that a few lawbreakers should not tarnish a movement rooted in conscience and national values. Mattis accused Trump of betraying the nation’s loyalty by deliberately dividing Americans and degrading the nation’s core principles, while praising the self‑sacrifice of ordinary workers and service members who uphold the country’s sanctity. The rebuke echoed similar condemnations from former Joint Chiefs leaders, underscoring a shared belief that the Constitution, justice, and unity must be defended against authoritarian subversion. END OF SUMMARY.
