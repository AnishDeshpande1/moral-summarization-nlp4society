SUMMARY:

Former Defense Secretary Jim Mattis issued a scathing rebuke of President Trump's handling of nationwide unrest, saying he has sought to divide Americans and warning against "militarizing our response" to the protests. Mattis accused Trump of pursuing a divisive strategy, stating that he is "the first president in my lifetime who does not try to unite the American people—does not even pretend to try." He criticized Trump's use of the military to quash peaceful demonstrations and his photo-op at St. John's Church, saying it was an abuse of executive authority. Mattis also emphasized the importance of respecting the rights of peaceful protest and the Constitution, stating that "America is not a battleground" and "our fellow citizens are not the enemy." He concluded by calling on Americans to reject Trump's divisive tactics and work towards unity.

Mattis' criticism comes after days of nationwide protests over police brutality and systemic racism. His statement has been joined by other military leaders, including former Joint Chiefs of Staff chairmen Gen. Martin Dempsey and Adm. Mike Mullen, who have also condemned the use of the military to quash peaceful demonstrations.

END OF SUMMARY.
