SUMMARY:

Former Defense Secretary Jim Mattis issued a scathing rebuke of President Trump's handling of nationwide unrest, accusing him of seeking to divide Americans and militarizing the response to protests. Mattis warned against violating the Constitutional rights of citizens and using the military for a "bizarre photo op" for the president. He called the actions an "abuse of executive authority" and urged against treating cities as a "battlespace" to be dominated. Mattis, who resigned over policy differences, broke his silence to speak out against Trump's pursuit of a divisive strategy, saying the president "tries to divide us" and has shown "disdain for the rights of peaceful protest." Mattis emphasized the importance of respecting the rights of citizens and upholding the Constitution, and urged Americans to "reject and hold accountable those in office who would make a mockery of our Constitution." He called for unity and justice, saying the protests are a "wholesome and unifying demand" for equal justice under the law.

END OF SUMMARY.
