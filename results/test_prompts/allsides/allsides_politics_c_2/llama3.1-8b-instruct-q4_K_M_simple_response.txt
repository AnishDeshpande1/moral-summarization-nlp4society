SUMMARY:

Former Defense Secretary James Mattis has issued a scathing rebuke of President Trump's handling of nationwide unrest, calling it an "abuse of executive authority" that seeks to divide Americans. Mattis, a retired Marine Gen., said troops were ordered to violate citizens' constitutional rights and provide a "bizarre photo op" for the president. He warned against "militarizing our response" to protests and urged governors to use the military only when necessary. Mattis accused Trump of pursuing a divisive strategy and criticized his handling of the pandemic, saying Americans from various professions have made sacrifices to serve their fellow citizens.

Mattis' statement comes as other high-ranking military officials, including former Joint Chiefs of Staff chairmen Gen. Martin Dempsey and Adm. Mike Mullen, also condemned Trump's actions. The president responded with a tweet calling Mattis "the world's most overrated General." Matts' rebuke highlights concerns about the militarization of domestic politics and the need for leaders to prioritize unity and respect for constitutional rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
