SUMMARY:

Former Defense Secretary Jim Mattis has issued a scathing rebuke of President Trump's handling of nationwide protests, accusing him of seeking to divide Americans and warning against "militarizing our response" to the demonstrations. Mattis, a retired Marine general, condemned the use of troops to clear peaceful protesters from around the White House, calling it an "abuse of executive authority." He also rejected the notion of viewing American cities as a "battlespace" to be dominated by the military. Mattis's critique is extraordinary, as it is rare for military leadership to speak out against a sitting president. He urged Americans to reject divisive leadership and instead work towards unity, citing the country's response to the pandemic as an example of citizens putting their lives on the line to serve their fellow citizens. Mattis's statement is a powerful call to uphold the Constitution and reject those who would make a mockery of it.

END OF SUMMARY.
