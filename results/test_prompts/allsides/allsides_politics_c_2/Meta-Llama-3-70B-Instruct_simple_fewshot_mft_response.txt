SUMMARY:

Former Defense Secretary Jim Mattis has issued a scathing rebuke of President Trump's handling of nationwide unrest, accusing him of seeking to divide Americans and warning against "militarizing our response" to the protests. Mattis, a retired Marine general, condemned Trump's actions as an "abuse of executive authority" and said the president's goal is to "divide" rather than unite the country. Mattis argued that the protests are a "wholesome and unifying demand" for equal justice under the law, and that the country's response to the pandemic shows that Americans are willing to make sacrifices for the safety of their communities. Mattis' critique is a rare instance of a military leader speaking out against a sitting president, and his words carry significant moral weight. By speaking out, Mattis is upholding the values of the Constitution and rejecting Trump's divisive strategy, which Mattis believes is a betrayal of the country's values and a harm to its citizens.

END OF SUMMARY.
