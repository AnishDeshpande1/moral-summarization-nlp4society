SUMMARY:

Former Defense Secretary Jim Mattis has issued a scathing rebuke of President Trump's handling of nationwide unrest, accusing him of seeking to divide Americans and warning against "militarizing our response" to the protests. Mattis, a retired Marine general, said Trump's actions on Monday, including a photo op at a historic church, were an "abuse of executive authority." He also criticized the president's divisive strategy, saying Trump is the first president in his lifetime who does not try to unite the American people. Mattis' statement was extraordinary, as it is rare for military leadership to speak out against a sitting president. His critique was echoed by other former military leaders, including Gen. Martin Dempsey and Adm. Mike Mullen, who also condemned the use of the military to quash the demonstrations. Mattis' statement was a powerful call to reject divisive leadership and to unite behind the values of equal justice and respect for the Constitution.

END OF SUMMARY.
