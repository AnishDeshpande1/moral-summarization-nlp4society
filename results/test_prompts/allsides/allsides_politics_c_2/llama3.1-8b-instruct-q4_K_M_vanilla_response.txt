SUMMARY:

Former Defense Secretary James Mattis has issued a scathing rebuke of President Trump's handling of nationwide unrest, saying he has sought to divide Americans and warning against "militarizing our response" to the protests. In a statement published in The Atlantic, Mattis called Monday's events outside the White House an "abuse of executive authority," where peaceful protesters were cleared to make way for Trump's photo op. He also criticized Trump's use of language, saying he has pursued a divisive strategy and failed to unite the country.

Mattis' comments are extraordinary, as it is rare for military leadership to speak out against a sitting president. He was joined in his criticism by two former Joint Chiefs of Staff chairmen, Gen. Martin Dempsey and Adm. Mike Mullen, who condemned the use of the military to quash demonstrations on Monday.

Trump responded to Mattis' comments with a tweet, calling him "the world's most overrated General."
