SUMMARY:

Former Defense Secretary Jim Mattis issued a scathing rebuke of President Trump's handling of nationwide unrest, saying he has sought to divide Americans and warning against militarizing the response to protests. Mattis, who resigned over policy differences with Trump, stated that troops are ordered to violate the Constitutional rights of their fellow citizens, which is an abuse of executive authority. He called Monday's actions "bizarre" and referred to them as a photo op for the president.

Mattis emphasized that America's military should not be used to quash protests or dominate the crowd, but rather to protect and serve its fellow citizens. He condemned Trump's pursuit of a divisive strategy, saying it is a mockery of the Constitution and risks further politicizing the military. Mattis also praised the peaceful protesters who are demanding equal justice under the law, stating that their call for unifying demand should be supported by all.

Trump responded to the criticism with a tweet calling Mattis "the world's most overrated General." END OF SUMMARY.
