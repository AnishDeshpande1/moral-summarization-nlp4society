SUMMARY:
Retired Marine Gen. Jim Mattis, who left the Pentagon after clashing with President Trump, has broken his usual silence to condemn the administration’s handling of the nationwide protests sparked by George Floyd’s death. In an Atlantic op‑ed, Mattis warned that Trump is deliberately dividing the nation and warned against “militarizing our response,” calling the president’s photo‑op at St. John’s Church an “abuse of executive authority” that would force troops to violate citizens’ constitutional rights. He urged that the military be used only at a state’s request and on rare occasions, rejecting the president’s talk of turning cities into a “battlespace.” Mattis praised the peaceful protesters’ demand for equal justice, denounced the small number of lawbreakers, and accused Trump of being the first president in his lifetime who does not even pretend to unite Americans. The rebuke follows similar condemnations from former Joint Chiefs chairs and current Defense Secretary Mark Esper, who also opposed invoking the Insurrection Act. Trump retorted by labeling Mattis “the world’s most overrated General.” Mattis’s statement frames the president’s actions as a moral failure that threatens constitutional democracy.  
END OF SUMMARY.
