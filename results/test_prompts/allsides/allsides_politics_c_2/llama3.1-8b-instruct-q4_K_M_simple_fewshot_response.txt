SUMMARY:
Former Defense Secretary Jim Mattis issued a scathing rebuke of President Trump's handling of nationwide unrest, saying he has sought to divide Americans and warning against "militarizing our response" to the protests. Mattis called Monday's actions an "abuse of executive authority," where peaceful protesters were physically cleared from around the White House to make way for the president's appearance outside a historic church. He accused Trump of pursuing a divisive strategy, saying he is the first president in his lifetime who does not try to unite the American people. Mattis also praised the protesters, saying they are only demanding equal justice under the law and that their actions are "defined by tens of thousands of people of conscience." His statement comes as current Defense Secretary Mark Esper said he opposed invoking the Insurrection Act, which would allow the president to deploy the military within the United States.
