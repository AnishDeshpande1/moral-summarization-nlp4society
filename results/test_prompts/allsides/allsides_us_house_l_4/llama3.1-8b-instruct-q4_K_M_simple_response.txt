SUMMARY:

Michael Cohen, former attorney for President Trump, is set to testify before the House Oversight Committee on Wednesday. In his prepared opening statement, obtained by The New York Times, Cohen makes several explosive claims about Trump's involvement in various scandals. Cohen alleges that he overheard a call between Trump and Roger Stone discussing WikiLeaks' plans to release hacked emails during the presidential campaign. He also claims that Trump was aware of discussions regarding a potential setup for Donald Trump Jr.'s meeting with a Russian lawyer at Trump Tower in New York.

Cohen further accuses Trump of making racist remarks, encouraging him to make misleading claims about building a Trump Tower in Moscow, and asking him if he could name a country run by a black person that wasn't a "shithole." Cohen's testimony is expected to be challenged due to his past lies to Congress. The hearing promises to be explosive, with Cohen set to spill all he knows about Trump under oath.

END OF SUMMARY.
