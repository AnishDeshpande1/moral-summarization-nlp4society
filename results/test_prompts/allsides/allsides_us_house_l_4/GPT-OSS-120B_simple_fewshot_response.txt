SUMMARY:
Former President Donald Trump’s longtime fixer Michael Cohen is set to testify before the House Oversight Committee, and his prepared opening statement reads like a bombshell indictment of the former president’s conduct. Cohen alleges he heard Trump’s ally Roger Stone tell Trump on a July 2016 speaker‑phone call that WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange was about to dump damaging Democratic emails—a claim that, if true, would place Trump directly in the loop on a Russian‑sourced hack. He also says Trump encouraged him to lie publicly about the Trump Tower Moscow project, asked him to fabricate a “shithole” remark about a Black‑led country, and hinted at a set‑up for Donald Jr.’s meeting with a Russian lawyer. Cohen will produce a check signed by Trump in 2017 to reimburse the hush‑money payout to Stormy Daniels. Though Cohen has already admitted lying to Congress, prosecutors will press him to corroborate his accusations. The testimony promises to expose further evidence of Trump’s willingness to conceal wrongdoing, manipulate foreign actors, and indulge in racist rhetoric, reinforcing concerns that his administration repeatedly flouted legal and ethical norms.

END OF SUMMARY.
