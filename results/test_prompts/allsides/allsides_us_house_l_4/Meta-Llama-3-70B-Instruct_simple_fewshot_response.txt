SUMMARY:

Michael Cohen, former personal attorney and "fixer" for Donald Trump, has prepared a bombshell opening statement for his testimony before the House Oversight Committee. Cohen alleges that he overheard a call between Trump and Roger Stone in July 2016, in which Stone claimed to have spoken with WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange about releasing hacked emails that would damage Hillary Clinton's campaign. This is the most direct allegation yet that Trump was aware of WikiLeaks' plans. Cohen also claims that Trump encouraged him to make misleading public statements about Trump Tower Moscow discussions, and that he witnessed Donald Trump Jr. tell his father about setting up a meeting with a Russian lawyer during the campaign. Additionally, Cohen plans to present a copy of a check personally signed by Trump in August 2017 to pay him back for the hush money payoff to porn actress Stormy Daniels. While Cohen's credibility has been questioned due to his past lies to Congress, his testimony could still have significant implications for Trump and his associates.

END OF SUMMARY.
