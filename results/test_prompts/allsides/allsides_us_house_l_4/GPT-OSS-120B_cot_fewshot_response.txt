**SUMMARY:**  
Michael Cohen’s prepared opening statement for his House Oversight testimony reads like a scandal‑filled confession, packed with claims of lying, fraud and other illegal misconduct that suggest deep corruption within Donald Trump’s inner circle. Cohen alleges he heard Roger Stone tell Trump about WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange’s plan to release hacked emails, a revelation that could point to collusion. He also says Trump encouraged deceptive public statements about a Trump Tower Moscow project, asked a racist “shithole” question, and signed a hush‑money check to reimburse the Stormy Daniels payoff. While Cohen admits he has no direct evidence, his accusations of deception, falsehoods and betrayal aim to expose what he describes as systemic misconduct and obstruction of justice. The testimony, coming as Cohen begins a three‑year prison sentence for his own fraud and campaign‑finance violations, promises to be a highly charged, morally framed exposé of Trump’s alleged wrongdoing.
