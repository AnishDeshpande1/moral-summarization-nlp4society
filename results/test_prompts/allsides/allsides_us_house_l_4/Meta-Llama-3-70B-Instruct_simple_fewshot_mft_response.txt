SUMMARY:

Michael Cohen's prepared testimony before the House Oversight Committee has explosive allegations against Donald Trump, his former boss. Cohen claims that he overheard a call between Trump and Roger Stone, in which Stone told Trump about WikiLeaks' plans to release hacked emails during the presidential campaign. This is the most direct allegation yet that Trump was aware of WikiLeaks' plans, which is being investigated by Special Counsel Robert Mueller. Cohen's statement also alleges that Trump was involved in various dirty dealings, including paying hush money to porn actress Stormy Daniels and making racist remarks in private. The testimony raises serious concerns about Trump's integrity and fairness, as well as his potential subversion of the law. Cohen's allegations, if true, would be a blatant example of cheating and unfairness, undermining the rule of law and the democratic process. The testimony is a significant development in the ongoing investigation into Trump's conduct, and its implications are far-reaching.

END OF SUMMARY.
