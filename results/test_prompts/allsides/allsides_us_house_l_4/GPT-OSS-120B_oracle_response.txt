SUMMARY:  
Michael Cohen’s prepared opening statement for his House Oversight Committee testimony has been released, and it is packed with explosive accusations. Cohen claims he heard Roger Stone tell Donald Trump about WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange’s plans to release hacked emails that would damage Hillary Clinton’s campaign—an allegation that suggests Trump was at least aware of the Russian‑attributed theft. He also alleges Trump encouraged false statements about a Trump Tower Moscow project, possibly set up a meeting between Donald Jr. and a Russian lawyer, and made racist remarks, including asking Cohen to name a “shithole” country run by a Black person. Cohen says he will produce a check signed by Trump in August 2017 to reimburse the hush‑money payoff to Stormy Daniels. While Cohen admits he has no “direct evidence” of collusion, he says his suspicions are strong. The statement lists numerous crimes, fraud, campaign‑finance violations, and lying to Congress by both Cohen and Trump, framing the whole saga as a pattern of illegal and unethical behavior.  

END OF SUMMARY.
