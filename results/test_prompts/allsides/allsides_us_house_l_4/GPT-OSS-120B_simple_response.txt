SUMMARY:  
Michael Cohen’s prepared opening statement, released by The New York Times, reads like a confession‑turned‑accusation that seeks to expose the moral failures of Donald Trump and his inner circle. Cohen, Trump’s former “fixer,” claims he overheard Roger Stone tell Trump in July 2016 that Julian Assange was preparing a “massive dump” of hacked Democratic emails—a revelation that suggests Trump’s campaign was at least aware of Russian‑orchestrated sabotage. While Cohen admits he has no “direct evidence” of collusion, he frames his “suspicions” as a moral imperative to reveal truth after his own guilty pleas for financial crimes, bank fraud, campaign‑finance violations, and lying to Congress. He also alleges Trump encouraged false statements about a Moscow tower, hinted at racist remarks, and asked him to name a non‑“shithole” country run by a Black leader. Most strikingly, Cohen says he will produce a check signed by Trump in August 2017 to reimburse the Stormy Daniels hush‑money payoff, underscoring a pattern of personal gain over public duty. Though his credibility is tarnished by past lies, Cohen’s testimony aims to hold Trump accountable for deceit, corruption, and possible complicity in foreign interference.  

END OF SUMMARY.
