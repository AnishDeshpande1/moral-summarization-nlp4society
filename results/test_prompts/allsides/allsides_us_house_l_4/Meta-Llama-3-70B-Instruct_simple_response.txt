SUMMARY:

Michael Cohen, former attorney and "fixer" for Donald Trump, is set to testify before the House Oversight Committee, and his prepared opening statement is explosive. Cohen alleges that he overheard a call between Trump and Roger Stone in July 2016, where Stone claimed to have spoken with WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange about releasing hacked emails that would damage Hillary Clinton's campaign. This is the most direct allegation yet that Trump was aware of WikiLeaks' plans, which special counsel Robert Mueller has attributed to Russian intelligence officers. Cohen also claims Trump encouraged him to make misleading claims about Trump Tower Moscow, and that Trump made racist remarks in private. Additionally, Cohen will present a copy of a check personally signed by Trump to pay him back for the hush money payoff to Stormy Daniels. While Cohen has admitted to lying to Congress in the past, his testimony could be damaging to Trump. The hearing promises to be explosive, with Cohen seemingly willing to spill all he knows about Trump.

END OF SUMMARY.
