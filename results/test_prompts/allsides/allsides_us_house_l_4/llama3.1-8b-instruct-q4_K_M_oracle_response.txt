SUMMARY:

Michael Cohen, former attorney to President Trump, is set to testify before the House Oversight Committee about his involvement in various crimes committed on behalf of Trump. According to his prepared statement, Cohen overheard a call between Trump and Roger Stone discussing WikiLeaks' plans to release hacked emails that would damage Hillary Clinton's campaign. This is a direct allegation that Trump was aware of WikiLeaks' plans, which were attributed to Russian intelligence officers by special counsel Robert Mueller.

Cohen also claims that Trump encouraged him to make misleading claims about talks to build a Trump Tower in Moscow and knew about the potential setup for Donald Trump Jr.'s meeting with a Russian lawyer. Additionally, Cohen alleges that Trump made racist remarks in private and had knowledge of various illicit activities committed on his behalf.

The testimony promises to be explosive, with Cohen presenting evidence of Trump's involvement in various crimes, including financial fraud and campaign finance violations. The hearing will challenge Trump's claims of innocence and shed light on the extent of his awareness of the theft of hacked emails.

END OF SUMMARY.
