SUMMARY:

Michael Cohen's prepared opening statement for his testimony before the House Oversight Committee has been released, and it contains explosive claims about Donald Trump. Cohen alleges that he overheard a call between Trump and Roger Stone in July 2016, in which Stone told Trump that WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange planned to release hacked emails that would damage Hillary Clinton's campaign. This is the most direct allegation yet that Trump was aware of WikiLeaks' plans. Cohen also claims that Trump was aware of discussions to build a Trump Tower in Moscow, and that he lied to Congress about it. Additionally, Cohen alleges that Trump made racist remarks in private and that he was involved in setting up a meeting with a Russian lawyer at Trump Tower. Cohen plans to present a copy of a check signed by Trump to pay him back for the hush money payoff to Stormy Daniels. While Cohen's credibility has been questioned due to his past lies to Congress, his testimony promises to be explosive.

END OF SUMMARY.
