SUMMARY:
The article releases Michael Cohen’s prepared opening statement for his House Oversight testimony, in which he directly challenges the authority of former President Donald Trump by alleging that Trump was informed about WikiLeaks’ planned email releases and that he encouraged deceptive statements about the Trump Tower Moscow deal. Cohen accuses Trump of subverting democratic oversight and abusing his power, portraying the former president’s actions as a systematic cheating of the public and the political process. He claims to have overheard a July 2016 speaker‑phone call in which Roger Stone told Trump about Julian Assange’s imminent “massive dump” of Democratic‑party emails—suggesting Trump’s inner circle was complicit in a scheme that harmed electoral fairness. Cohen also alleges Trump’s private racist remarks, the set‑up of Donald Jr.’s meeting with a Russian lawyer, and a personally signed hush‑money check to reimburse the Stormy Daniels payoff, framing these as further examples of Trump’s cheating and abuse of authority. While Cohen’s credibility is questioned due to his prior false statements to Congress, the statement is positioned as a direct affront to Trump’s authority and a call for accountability for the alleged betrayals of fairness.  
END OF SUMMARY.
