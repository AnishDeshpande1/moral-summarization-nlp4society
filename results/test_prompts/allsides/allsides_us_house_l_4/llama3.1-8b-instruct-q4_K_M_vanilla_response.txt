SUMMARY:

Michael Cohen, former attorney to President Trump, is set to testify before the House Oversight Committee on Wednesday. His prepared opening statement, obtained by The New York Times, contains explosive claims about Trump's involvement in various scandals. Cohen alleges that he overheard a call between Trump and Roger Stone discussing WikiLeaks' plans to release hacked emails during the presidential campaign. He also claims that Trump was aware of discussions about building a Trump Tower in Moscow and encouraged him to make misleading public statements about it. Additionally, Cohen says that Trump asked him if he could name a country run by a black person that wasn't a "shithole." Cohen will present a check signed by Trump to pay back hush money for the Stormy Daniels affair. While some of these claims are corroborated, others rely on Cohen's word alone.

END OF SUMMARY.
