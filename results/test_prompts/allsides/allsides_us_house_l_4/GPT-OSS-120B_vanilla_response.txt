SUMMARY:
Michael Cohen’s opening statement for his House Oversight Committee testimony, obtained by The New York Times, contains several explosive allegations about former President Donald Trump. Cohen claims he overheard Roger Stone tell Trump on a July 2016 speaker‑phone call that WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange planned a “massive dump” of emails that would damage Hillary Clinton’s campaign—suggesting Trump’s inner circle knew about the forthcoming Russian‑hacked releases. He also says he will produce a Trump‑signed check from August 2017 repaying the Stormy Daniels hush‑money payment. Additional claims include: Trump urging Cohen to lie about a Trump Tower Moscow deal; Trump not directly ordering Cohen to lie to Congress; Cohen witnessing Donald Jr. discuss arranging a meeting with a Russian lawyer at Trump Tower; and a private racist remark by Trump about “a country run by a black person that wasn’t a ‘shithole.’” Cohen, who pleaded guilty to multiple crimes and is set to begin a three‑year prison term, says he has “suspicions” but no direct evidence of collusion with Russia. His testimony will be closely scrutinized given his prior admission of lying to Congress.

END OF SUMMARY.
