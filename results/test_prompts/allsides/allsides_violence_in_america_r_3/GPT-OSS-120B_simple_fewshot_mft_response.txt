SUMMARY:
President Trump denounced the Las Vegas mass shooting as an “act of pure evil,” invoking moral authority to unite the nation and condemn the violence. In a solemn, faith‑centered address, he expressed deep care for the 58 victims and 515 injured, offering prayers and invoking Scripture that comforts the “broken‑hearted” and “crushed in spirit.” He framed the tragedy as a violation of sacred values, contrasting the darkness of the attack with the sanctity of hope, family, faith and shared American values. Trump announced a visit to Las Vegas to meet law‑enforcement, first responders and victims’ families, ordered flags at half‑staff, and pledged continued presidential leadership against such subversive evil. The administration’s response, highlighted by statements from Sarah Sanders and First Lady Melania Trump, reinforced the moral hierarchy: the president as the moral authority guiding a grieving nation, caring for the wounded, and defending the sacred bond that should not be degraded by hatred or fear. The narrative stresses that unity, guided by authority and reverence for life, will resist and outshine the profane act of violence.

END OF SUMMARY.
