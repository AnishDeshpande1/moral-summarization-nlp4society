SUMMARY:

President Trump delivered a somber address from the White House, calling the Las Vegas attack an "act of pure evil" and offering his prayers to the victims' families. He announced plans to visit Las Vegas on Wednesday to meet with law enforcement and first responders. In his speech, Trump emphasized the importance of unity in the face of tragedy, saying that "our bonds cannot be shattered by evil." He also referenced Scripture, stating that God is close to those who grieve and seeking comfort in those words for the nation. The president's message focused on providing solace and support to those affected by the attack, while also acknowledging the need for hope in the face of darkness.

The Trump administration has been working closely with Nevada officials, including Governor Brian Sandoval and Las Vegas Mayor Carolyn Goodman, to respond to the tragedy. First lady Melania Trump also offered her condolences on Twitter, expressing her heart and prayers for the victims' families. The president's address aimed to provide a sense of comfort and unity in the midst of chaos, highlighting the importance of faith and shared values during this difficult time.
