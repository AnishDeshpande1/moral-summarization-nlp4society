SUMMARY: President Donald Trump condemned the Las Vegas mass shooting as an “act of pure evil,” calling for national unity rooted in faith, family, and shared values. In a somber, faith‑focused address from the White House’s Diplomatic Room, he prayed for the victims’ families, invoked Scripture that comforts the broken‑hearted, and promised that “our unity cannot be shattered by evil.” He announced a visit to Las Vegas to meet law‑enforcement, first responders, and grieving families, ordered flags at half‑staff, and urged the nation to seek hope “even in the darkest space.” The president’s remarks emphasized moral resilience, the need for collective prayer, and the belief that “a single ray of hope” can illuminate darkness. He and First Lady Melania offered condolences on Twitter, while White House Press Secretary Sarah Sanders confirmed communications with Nevada officials. The shooter, 64‑year‑old Stephen Paddock, was killed; investigators continue to seek a motive. END OF SUMMARY.
