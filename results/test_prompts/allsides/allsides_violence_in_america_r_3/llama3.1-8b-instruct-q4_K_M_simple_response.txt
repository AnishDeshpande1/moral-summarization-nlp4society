SUMMARY:
President Trump addressed the nation on Monday morning, calling the Las Vegas attack an "act of pure evil" that left 58 people dead and 515 hospitalized. He announced plans to visit Las Vegas on Wednesday to meet with law enforcement, first responders, and families of victims. The president ordered flags to be flown at half-staff in honor of those who died. In a faith-focused speech, Trump offered his prayers for the families of the victims, saying "Scripture teaches us the Lord is close to the broken hearted." He emphasized the importance of unity and shared values in times of tragedy, stating that "our unity cannot be shattered by evil."

The president expressed hope that even the darkest moments can be illuminated by a single ray of hope. First lady Melania Trump also offered her condolences on Twitter, saying "My heart and prayers go out to victims, families & loved ones." Authorities are still investigating the motive behind the attack, with retired FBI special agent John Iannarelli noting that it was likely a well-planned event.

END OF SUMMARY.
