SUMMARY:

President Trump addressed the nation after the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history, calling the Las Vegas attack an "act of pure evil" that left 58 people dead and 515 hospitalized. In a unifying speech, Trump offered prayers and condolences to the victims' families, citing scripture to comfort those grieving. He emphasized the importance of faith, family, and shared values in the face of evil, saying "Our unity cannot be shattered by evil. Our bonds cannot be broken by violence." Trump also announced a visit to Las Vegas to meet with law enforcement, first responders, and victims' families, and ordered flags to be flown at half-staff. The suspect, Stephen Paddock, was killed, and authorities are investigating his motive. Trump and the First Lady Melania Trump offered their condolences and prayers for the nation.

END OF SUMMARY.
