SUMMARY:
President Trump addressed the nation on Monday morning, calling the Las Vegas attack an "act of pure evil" that left 58 people dead and 515 hospitalized. He announced he will visit Las Vegas on Wednesday to meet with law enforcement and families of victims. The president ordered the flag be flown at half-staff and offered prayers for those affected. In a somber speech, Trump called upon the bonds that unite Americans, including faith, family, and shared values, saying "our unity cannot be shattered by evil." He also expressed hope that even in darkness, there can be illumination. The president has spoken with Nevada Gov. Brian Sandoval, Las Vegas Mayor Carolyn Goodman, and Sheriff Joe Lombardo. Authorities have identified the suspect as 64-year-old Stephen Paddock and are confident his roommate, Marilou Danley, has been located.

END OF SUMMARY.
