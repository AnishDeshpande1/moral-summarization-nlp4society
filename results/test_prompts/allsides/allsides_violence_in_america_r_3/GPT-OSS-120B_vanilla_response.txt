SUMMARY: President Donald Trump condemned the Las Vegas mass shooting, calling it an “act of pure evil,” and delivered a solemn, faith‑focused address to the nation. The attack killed 58 people and injured 515, making it the deadliest modern U.S. shooting. Trump announced a visit to Las Vegas on Wednesday to meet law‑enforcement, first responders, and victims’ families, and ordered flags at the White House and federal buildings to be flown at half‑staff. He offered prayers, invoked Scripture, and urged national unity based on faith, family, and shared values, saying hope can shine even in darkness. White House Press Secretary Sarah Sanders confirmed Trump’s calls with Nevada Governor Brian Sandoval, Las Vegas Mayor Carolyn Goodman, and Sheriff Joe Lombardo. Trump and First Lady Melania expressed condolences via Twitter. The shooter, 64‑year‑old Stephen Paddock, was killed; investigators are searching for his roommate Marilou Danley as a person of interest. Officials described the attack as well‑planned with automatic weapons, but motive remains unknown. END OF SUMMARY.
