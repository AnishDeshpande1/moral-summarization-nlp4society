SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump condemned the Las Vegas attack as an “act of pure evil,” calling the mass shooting the deadliest in modern U.S. history. The blast left 58 people dead and 515 hospitalized, with several officers wounded and two off‑duty police officers killed. Trump announced a visit to Las Vegas to meet law‑enforcement, first responders and families of the victims, and ordered the flag at half‑staff. In a faith‑focused address he prayed for the nation, asking God to help the broken‑hearted and crushed in spirit, and urged that the bonds of faith, family and shared values not be shattered by violence. He offered condolences and sympathies, blessing the victims and their families, and said the United States must seek hope amid the chaos and fear. The president and First Lady Melania Trump posted prayers and condolences on Twitter, while officials continue to locate the suspect’s roommate and investigate the motive behind the feared act.  

END OF SUMMARY.
