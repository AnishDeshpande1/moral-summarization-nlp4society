SUMMARY:

President Trump addressed the nation, calling the Las Vegas attack an "act of pure evil" that left 58 people dead and 515 hospitalized. He announced a visit to Las Vegas to meet with law enforcement, first responders, and families of victims. The president ordered flags to be flown at half-staff to honor those who died. In a unifying speech, Trump offered prayers and condolences to the families of victims, saying "our unity cannot be shattered by evil" and "our bonds cannot be broken by violence." He emphasized the importance of faith, family, and shared values in times of chaos and darkness. Trump prayed for the nation, seeking comfort in scripture and hoping for a day when "evil is banished and the innocent are safe from hatred and fear." The suspect, Stephen Paddock, was killed, and authorities are investigating his motive.

END OF SUMMARY.
