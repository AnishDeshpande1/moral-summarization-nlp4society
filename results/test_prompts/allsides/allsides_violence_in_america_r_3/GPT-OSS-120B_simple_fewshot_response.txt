SUMMARY:
President Trump condemned the Las Vegas mass shooting – the deadliest in modern U.S. history – as an “act of pure evil” and framed the tragedy in moral and faith‑based terms. In a somber White House address, he offered prayers, invoked Scripture, and called for national unity rooted in faith, family and shared values, insisting that “our bonds cannot be broken by violence.” He announced a visit to Las Vegas to meet victims’ families, first responders and law‑enforcement, and ordered flags at half‑staff on federal buildings. The administration highlighted the shooter’s identity (64‑year‑old Stephen Paddock) and the ongoing investigation, while emphasizing that the motive remains unknown. Trump’s remarks aimed to provide comfort, portray the nation’s response as compassionate and united, and suggest that even in darkness “a single ray of hope” can illuminate the path forward. The First Lady also expressed condolences via social media.

END OF SUMMARY.
