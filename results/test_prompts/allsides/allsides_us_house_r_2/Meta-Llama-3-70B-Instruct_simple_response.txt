SUMMARY:

The US House of Representatives has launched the first public hearing of Donald Trump's impeachment investigation, a historic process to determine whether the President should be removed from office. The inquiry centers on whether Trump used his office to pressure Ukraine officials for personal political gain. Rep. Adam Schiff emphasized that the answer to this question will affect not only Trump's presidency but the future of the office itself. Two seasoned diplomats, William Taylor and George Kent, testified about Trump's alleged misuse of foreign policy for personal and political gain ahead of the 2020 election. The narrative has split Americans, with Trump calling the inquiry a "witch hunt." The Constitution sets a vague bar for impeachment, and there's no consensus yet that Trump's actions meet the threshold of "high crimes and misdemeanors." The hearing marks a significant moment in Trump's chaotic presidency, as the constitutional system of checks and balances holds him accountable for his actions.

END OF SUMMARY.
