SUMMARY:

The US House has launched the first public hearing of Donald Trump's impeachment investigation, a process to determine if the president should be removed from office. The core question is whether Trump used his office to pressure Ukraine officials for personal political gain, which Rep. Adam Schiff described as "simple and terrible." The hearing marks a remarkable moment, as it's the first chance for the public to hear about Trump's actions towards Ukraine and consider if they are impeachable offenses. Two seasoned diplomats, William Taylor and George Kent, will testify about the president's alleged misuse of foreign policy for personal and political gain ahead of the 2020 election. Trump has dismissed the inquiry as a "witch hunt," but the Constitution sets a dramatic bar for impeachment, and there's no consensus yet that Trump's actions meet the threshold of "high crimes and misdemeanors." The inquiry stems from Trump's July 25 phone call with Ukraine's president, where he asked for a "favor" and used military aid as leverage. END OF SUMMARY.
