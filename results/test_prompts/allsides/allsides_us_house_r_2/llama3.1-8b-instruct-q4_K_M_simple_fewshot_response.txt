SUMMARY:
The U.S. House launched its impeachment investigation into President Trump, focusing on whether he used his office to pressure Ukraine officials for personal political gain. Rep. Adam Schiff outlined the central question: "whether the president used his office to pressure Ukraine officials for personal political gain." The inquiry stems from Trump's July 25 phone call with Ukraine's president, in which he asked for a favor - investigations into Democrats and Joe Biden - while holding military aid as leverage. Democratic leaders argue that this constitutes an impeachable offense, while Trump calls it a "witch hunt." The investigation will determine whether Trump's actions meet the threshold of "high crimes and misdemeanors" for impeachment.
