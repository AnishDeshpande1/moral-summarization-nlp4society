**SUMMARY:**  
The U.S. House opened the first public hearing of the impeachment inquiry into President Trump, asking whether the commander‑in‑chief abused his authority by pressuring Ukraine for personal political gain. Rep. Adam Schiff framed the issue as a matter of misconduct and fairness, warning that the outcome will shape the future of the presidency and the nation’s sense of justice. The hearing, broadcast live, featured diplomats William Taylor and George Kent testifying under subpoena about the July 25 call that many view as a “witch hunt”‑style subversion of constitutional norms. Trump has denounced the process as a “witch hunt,” echoing past accusations of cheating and partisan attacks. An anonymous whistleblower’s complaint sparked the investigation, which now examines whether the president’s leverage of military aid constitutes high crimes and misdemeanors. As witnesses appear, the inquiry challenges the president’s use of authority and raises profound questions of fairness versus cheating in American governance.
