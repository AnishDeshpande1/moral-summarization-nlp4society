SUMMARY:

The US House has launched the first public hearing of Donald Trump's impeachment investigation, examining whether the president used his office to pressure Ukraine officials for personal political gain. The hearing marks a significant moment in American history, with the country watching and considering whether Trump's actions constitute impeachable offenses. Two seasoned diplomats, William Taylor and George Kent, are testifying about Trump's alleged use of foreign policy for personal and political gain ahead of the 2020 election. The inquiry stems from Trump's July 25 phone call with Ukraine's president, where he asked for an investigation into Democrats and Joe Biden in exchange for military aid. The White House has instructed officials not to testify, but witnesses have appeared behind closed doors to share their knowledge. The hearing will determine whether Trump's actions meet the threshold of "high crimes and misdemeanors" and whether he should be removed from office.

END OF SUMMARY.
