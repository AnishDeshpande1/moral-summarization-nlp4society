SUMMARY:

The U.S. House launched public hearings on the impeachment investigation against President Donald Trump, focusing on whether he used his office for personal political gain in dealings with Ukraine. Rep. Adam Schiff highlighted the core question: did Trump pressure Ukraine officials to investigate Democrats and a potential 2020 rival, Joe Biden? The inquiry stems from Trump's July phone call with Ukraine's president, where he asked for "a favor" while withholding military aid to leverage an investigation. Democratic House Speaker Nancy Pelosi initially resisted impeachment but pressed ahead after concerns raised by an anonymous whistleblower.

The proceedings have sparked debate, with Americans divided along partisan lines. The Constitution sets a vague bar for impeachment, and there is no consensus on whether Trump's actions meet the threshold of "high crimes and misdemeanors." Trump has called the inquiry a "witch hunt," echoing Nixon's defense.

END OF SUMMARY
