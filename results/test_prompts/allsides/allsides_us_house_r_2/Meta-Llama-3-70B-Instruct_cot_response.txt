SUMMARY:
The U.S. House launched the first public hearing of Donald Trump's impeachment investigation, a process to determine whether the president should be removed from office for using his office to pressure Ukraine officials for personal political gain. Rep. Adam Schiff emphasized that the matter is "as simple and as terrible as that." The hearing is a remarkable moment, even for a White House full of them, and will affect not only the future of this presidency but the future of the presidency itself. Two seasoned diplomats, William Taylor and George Kent, testified about Trump's actions toward Ukraine, which are allegedly impeachable offenses. Trump calls the inquiry a "witch hunt," but the narrative is splitting Americans, mostly along the same lines as Trump's unusual presidency. The inquiry stems from Trump's July 25 phone call with Ukraine's president, when he asked for a "favor" and held military aid as leverage, which is considered "high crimes and misdemeanors."
