SUMMARY:

The U.S. House launched an impeachment investigation into President Donald Trump's actions, questioning whether he used his office to pressure Ukraine officials for personal political gain. Rep. Adam Schiff emphasized that the inquiry will determine if Trump committed misconduct and engaged in impeachable offenses. The proceedings focus on a July 25 phone call where Trump asked Ukraine's president to investigate Democrats, including Joe Biden, as leverage for holding back military aid. Two seasoned diplomats, William Taylor and George Kent, testified about Trump's actions, which they described as an attempt to use foreign policy for personal gain ahead of the 2020 election.

The inquiry stems from a whistleblower complaint, and the White House has instructed officials not to testify. The investigation aims to determine if Trump committed high crimes and misdemeanors, meeting the threshold for impeachment set by the Constitution. Trump denies any wrongdoing, calling it a "witch hunt." The outcome will affect not only his presidency but also the future of the office itself.

END OF SUMMARY.
