SUMMARY:
The House opened the first public hearing of Donald Trump’s impeachment inquiry, focusing on whether the president abused his office by pressuring Ukraine for personal political gain. Democratic Chairman Adam Schiff framed the issue as a test of what conduct the American people should tolerate from their commander‑in‑chief, warning that the outcome will shape the very nature of the presidency. The hearing, broadcast live, featured career diplomats William Taylor and George Kent testifying about the July 25 call in which Trump allegedly demanded Ukrainian investigations into the 2016 election and his 2020 rival, Joe Biden, while withholding military aid. The investigation, spurred by an anonymous whistleblower, has faced staunch resistance from the White House, which barred officials from appearing and labeled the process a “witch hunt.” Yet witnesses continue to be subpoenaed, underscoring the constitutional check on executive power. The episode highlights a stark moral dilemma: if a president can weaponize foreign policy for self‑interest, it threatens the integrity of the office and the rule of law that underpins American democracy. END OF SUMMARY.
