SUMMARY:

The US House has launched the first public hearing of Donald Trump's impeachment investigation, a process that will determine whether the president should be removed from office. The inquiry centers around whether Trump used his office to pressure Ukraine officials for personal political gain. The hearing marks a remarkable moment, as the country has only been here three times before, and never against the backdrop of real-time commentary. Two seasoned diplomats, William Taylor and George Kent, will testify about Trump's actions toward Ukraine, which allegedly used foreign policy for personal and political gain ahead of the 2020 election. The narrative has split Americans, mostly along party lines, with Trump calling the inquiry a "witch hunt." The Constitution sets a vague bar for impeachment, and there's no consensus yet that Trump's actions meet the threshold of "high crimes and misdemeanors." The inquiry raises questions about the limits of presidential authority and the fairness of Trump's actions, which may have subverted the democratic process for personal gain.

END OF SUMMARY.
