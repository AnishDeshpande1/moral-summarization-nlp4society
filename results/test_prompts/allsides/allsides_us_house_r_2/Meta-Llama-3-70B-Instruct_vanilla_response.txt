SUMMARY:

The US House of Representatives has launched the first public hearing of Donald Trump's impeachment investigation. The hearing aims to determine whether Trump used his office to pressure Ukraine officials for personal political gain. Rep. Adam Schiff outlined the core question of the inquiry, stating that the answer will affect not only Trump's presidency but the future of the office itself. Two seasoned diplomats, William Taylor and George Kent, testified about Trump's actions towards Ukraine, which allegedly involved using foreign policy for personal and political gain ahead of the 2020 election. Trump has called the inquiry a "witch hunt" and tweeted "READ THE TRANSCRIPT" of his phone call with Ukraine's president. The inquiry stems from Trump's July 25 phone call, in which he asked Ukraine to investigate Democrats and his potential 2020 rival, Joe Biden.

END OF SUMMARY.
