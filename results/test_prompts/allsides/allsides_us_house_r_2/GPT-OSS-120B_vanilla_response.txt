SUMMARY: The U.S. House held the first public hearing of the impeachment inquiry into President Donald Trump, focusing on whether he abused his office to pressure Ukraine for personal political gain. Chairman Rep. Adam Schiff framed the issue as a test of presidential conduct. Testimony came from diplomats William Taylor and George Kent, who described Trump’s July 25 call with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky, in which Trump allegedly demanded investigations into political rivals while withholding military aid. The inquiry was triggered by a whistleblower complaint and a partially released transcript. Speaker Nancy Pelosi moved forward with the investigation despite initial hesitation. The White House barred officials from testifying, but many witnesses have been subpoenaed. Trump dismissed the process as a “witch hunt,” likening it to Nixon’s defense. The hearing marks a rare, highly partisan impeachment proceeding, comparable to past cases like Andrew Johnson, but without a clear public consensus yet. The outcome will influence both Trump’s presidency and broader expectations of presidential accountability. END OF SUMMARY.
