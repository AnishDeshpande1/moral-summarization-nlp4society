SUMMARY:
The House opened the first public hearing of the impeachment inquiry into President Donald Trump, focusing on whether he abused the highest authority of the presidency for personal political gain. Chairman Adam Schiff framed the issue as a stark test of “what kind of conduct the American people may come to expect from their commander‑in‑chief,” underscoring a subversion of legitimate authority. Testimony from career diplomats William Taylor and George Kent will examine Trump’s July 25 call to President Zelensky, in which the president allegedly leveraged military aid to pressure Ukraine into investigating his political rivals—an act portrayed as cheating the public and violating the fairness owed by an elected leader to the nation. While Trump dismisses the process as a “witch hunt,” the inquiry, spurred by a whistleblower’s report, seeks to hold him accountable for using his office to further personal ambitions, a breach of both constitutional authority and the principle of fair governance. The hearing marks a rare, highly visible challenge to presidential power, aiming to determine if such conduct rises to “high crimes and misdemeanors” worthy of removal.  
END OF SUMMARY.
