SUMMARY:
The U.S. House opened the first public hearing of Donald Trump’s impeachment inquiry, asking whether the president **used** his office to **pressure** Ukraine for personal political gain. Chairman Adam Schiff warned that the outcome will shape the future of the presidency and the **misconduct** the American people may come to expect. The hearing, broadcast live, featured diplomats William Taylor and George Kent testifying about the president’s alleged **actions** that may constitute impeachable **offenses**. Central to the probe is the July 25 call in which Trump **used** aid as leverage to demand a Ukraine investigation of Joe Biden, a move critics label as **using** foreign policy for election advantage. While Trump dismisses the process as a “witch hunt,” Democrats argue the conduct meets the constitutional threshold of **crimes** and **misdemeanors**. Witnesses have been subpoenaed despite White House instructions not to testify. The inquiry marks a rare constitutional test, echoing past impeachments but unfolding under unprecedented real‑time scrutiny.  
END OF SUMMARY.
