SUMMARY:
The US House launched its first public hearing on Donald Trump's impeachment investigation, focusing on whether the president used his office for personal political gain in dealings with Ukraine. Rep. Adam Schiff outlined the core question of the inquiry: whether Trump pressured Ukrainian officials to investigate Democrats and Joe Biden in exchange for military aid. Two diplomats, William Taylor and George Kent, testified about their experiences with Trump's actions towards Ukraine. The hearing is the first chance for Americans and the world to see and hear directly about Trump's actions and consider whether they are impeachable offenses. The inquiry stems from Trump's July 25 phone call with Ukrainian President Zelenskiy, in which he asked for a favor while holding military aid as leverage.

The proceedings have been live-streamed on social media, and the country is closely watching to see if Trump's actions meet the threshold of "high crimes and misdemeanors" required for impeachment. The outcome remains uncertain, with opinions divided among Americans and no clear consensus yet.
