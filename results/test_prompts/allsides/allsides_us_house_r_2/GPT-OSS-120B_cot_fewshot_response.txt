**SUMMARY:**  
The U.S. House opened the first public hearing of the impeachment inquiry into President Donald Trump, focusing on whether he used the power of his office to pressure Ukraine for personal and political gain. Rep. Adam Schiff framed the issue as a matter of terrible misconduct that could reshape what Americans expect from their commander‑in‑chief. The hearing, broadcast live, featured diplomats William Taylor and George Kent testifying under subpoena, while the White House labeled the process a “witch hunt.” An anonymous whistleblower’s complaint sparked the investigation, which hinges on whether Trump’s July 25 call constituted high crimes and misdemeanors, leveraging military aid as leverage. Though Speaker Pelosi was initially reluctant, the constitutional system of checks and balances now forces a chaotic showdown that Trump cannot control. The outcome will determine if the president’s conduct rises to the level of impeachment.
