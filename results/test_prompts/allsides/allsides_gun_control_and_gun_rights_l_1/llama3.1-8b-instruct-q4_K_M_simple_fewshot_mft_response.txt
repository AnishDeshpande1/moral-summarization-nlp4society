SUMMARY:

Mayors from cities across the US are taking action against gun violence, recognizing that leadership from Washington is not forthcoming. They acknowledge the deadly nexus between intolerance and easy access to military-grade firepower, which drives mass shootings. These mayors are empowering young people to demand change, focusing on calling out hate speech and keeping guns out of the hands of those who shouldn't have them. The author shares their personal story, highlighting the impact of gun violence on communities and families, including their own ancestors' experiences with anti-Semitic violence and immigration. They emphasize the importance of listening to young people's concerns and ideas in addressing this "generation-defining challenge." The mayors are working together to create a nation where everyone belongs and feels safe, driven by a sense of civic responsibility and a desire to honor the sacrifices made by their ancestors.

END OF SUMMARY.
