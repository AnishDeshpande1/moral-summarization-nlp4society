SUMMARY:

Mayors from across the US are taking action to address gun violence in their communities, as they believe Washington is not providing sufficient leadership. The mayors, from both red and blue states, are focusing on empowering young people to demand change and taking steps to keep weapons out of the hands of those who shouldn't have them. The author, a mayor themselves, reflects on the personal connection they have to cities affected by mass shootings, such as Gilroy, Dayton, and El Paso, where their ancestors first found freedom and belonging in the US. They emphasize the importance of involving young people in the conversation, citing their own Youth Council to End Gun Violence, which advises on gun safety and engages young people in the issue. The ultimate goal is to create a nation where everyone feels safe and belongs, an inheritance they want to leave for future generations. 

END OF SUMMARY.
