SUMMARY:  
Mayors across the United States—from Las Vegas to Orlando, San Bernardino to Parkland—are forced to lead their communities after mass shootings, many fueled by racist ideology, anti‑immigrant hate and easy access to military‑grade weapons. The author argues that waiting for federal action is futile; instead, local leaders must unite with young people demanding an end to hate speech and stricter gun controls. Personal family histories tied to Gilroy, Dayton and El Paso illustrate how immigrant ancestors sought freedom, now threatened by gun violence. Youth councils, like the author’s Youth Council to End Gun Violence formed after the Marjory Stoneman Douglas shooting, are already shaping policy and creating campaigns against normalized gun terror. The moral imperative is clear: protect the next generation’s right to grow up safe and free, honoring the sacrifices of earlier immigrants. By empowering youth and confronting intolerance and gun obsession head‑on, mayors aim to preserve the American promise of belonging for all.  

END OF SUMMARY.
