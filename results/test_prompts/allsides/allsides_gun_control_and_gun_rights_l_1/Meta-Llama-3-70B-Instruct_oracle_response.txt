SUMMARY: The author, a mayor, reflects on the grim reality of mass shootings in various cities and towns, resulting in memorials rather than monuments. The mayors, including the author, have had to lead their communities through unthinkable tragedies, often driven by racist ideology, malice, and hate speech. The author emphasizes the agonizing responsibility of mayors to take action, as the deadly nexus between intolerance and gun obsession continues to wreak havoc and destroy lives. The author highlights the importance of empowering young people to call out hate speech and take courageous steps to keep weapons out of the wrong hands. The author shares personal connections to cities affected by gun violence, including Gilroy, Dayton, and El Paso, and notes that these cities hold a special place in their history, where their ancestors first discovered freedom and belonging. The author concludes by emphasizing the need to involve and take seriously the concerns of young people to build a nation where everyone belongs and feels safe. END OF SUMMARY.
