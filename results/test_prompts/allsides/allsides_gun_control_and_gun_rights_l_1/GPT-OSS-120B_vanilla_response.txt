SUMMARY: The article discusses how U.S. mayors across the political spectrum are confronting the surge of mass shootings, especially those fueled by hate and extremist rhetoric. With little help from Washington, these local leaders are taking direct action, focusing on youth engagement and gun‑safety initiatives. Mayor [author] shares personal ties to cities like Gilroy, Dayton and El Paso, illustrating how gun violence touches communities with deep immigrant histories. He highlights the US Conference of Mayors Youth Summit and his own Youth Council to End Gun Violence, formed after the Marjory Stoneman Douglas shooting, which advises city policies and creates campaigns against normalized gun violence.
