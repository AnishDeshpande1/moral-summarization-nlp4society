SUMMARY:

In response to mass shootings, mayors from across the US are taking action to address gun violence, despite feeling that leadership from Washington is lacking. Many of these cities have been affected by racist ideology and hate speech, which has contributed to the rise in gun violence. Mayors are working with young people who are demanding that their leaders take meaningful steps to keep guns out of the wrong hands. The mayor of Los Angeles reflects on the impact of gun violence on her own family's history, from the Mexican Revolution to the current migration crisis at the US-Mexico border. She emphasizes the importance of involving and taking seriously the concerns of young people in addressing this generation-defining challenge. As a leader, she is committed to leaving a nation where everyone belongs and feels safe, just as her ancestors did when they arrived in America seeking freedom and opportunity.

END OF SUMMARY.
