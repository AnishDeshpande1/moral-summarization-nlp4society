SUMMARY:

In the aftermath of mass shootings, mayors across the US are taking action to confront the deadly nexus between intolerance and easy access to military-grade firepower. Despite the lack of leadership from Washington, mayors are empowering young people to demand meaningful steps to keep weapons out of the wrong hands. The author, a mayor themselves, reflects on the personal connections they have to cities affected by gun violence, including Gilroy, Dayton, and El Paso, where their ancestors first discovered freedom and belonging in the US. The author emphasizes the importance of involving young people in the fight against gun violence, citing their own Youth Council to End Gun Violence, which advises on issues of gun safety. The moral imperative is clear: to protect citizens from harm, ensure fairness in the face of hate speech and intolerance, and uphold the authority of leaders who prioritize the safety and well-being of their people.

END OF SUMMARY.
