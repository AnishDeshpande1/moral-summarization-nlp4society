SUMMARY:

In the aftermath of mass shootings, mayors across the US are facing an unbearable responsibility in leading their communities forward. The author, a mayor themselves, notes that these tragedies are often driven by racist ideology and hate speech broadcast from those in power. Rather than relying on Washington for leadership, mayors are taking action to address the "deadly nexus between intolerance and a sick obsession with guns." They are working with young people who demand change, including empowering them to call out hate speech and advocating for stricter gun control measures. The author shares personal connections to cities affected by mass shootings, emphasizing that these tragedies are not just statistical abstractions but human experiences that should galvanize collective action.

The author concludes that it is essential to listen to the concerns and ideas of young people, who are "the leaders of today and tomorrow." By working together, mayors aim to create a nation where everyone belongs and feels safe. This vision is rooted in the legacy of those who risked everything to build America into what it is today.

END OF SUMMARY.
