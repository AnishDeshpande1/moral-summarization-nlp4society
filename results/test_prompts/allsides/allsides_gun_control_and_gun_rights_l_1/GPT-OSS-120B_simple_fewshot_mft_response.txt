SUMMARY:
Mayors across the United States—whether in blue or red municipalities—are confronting the tragic legacy of mass shootings that stem from hate‑filled rhetoric, racist ideology and unchecked access to military‑grade weapons. Recognizing that Washington’s response often falls short, these local leaders are asserting their authority to fight the subversive forces that normalize gun violence. They are demanding that hate speech be called out and that weapons be kept out of the hands of those who should not possess them, framing the issue as a matter of fairness: protecting all citizens from a system that cheats the vulnerable out of safety. By empowering young people, who bring righteous, life‑saving demands to city halls, mayors are caring for the most harmed—families and communities scarred by loss—while insisting on equitable, responsible policies that honor the nation’s founding promise of freedom without fear. This collective civic effort seeks to restore safety, uphold just authority, and ensure that every American, past and future, can live without the threat of gun‑induced harm.  
END OF SUMMARY.
