SUMMARY:

Mayors from various cities across the US are grappling with the aftermath of mass shootings, which have become all too common in recent years. These tragedies often involve racist ideology, hate speech, and easy access to military-grade firepower. In response, mayors are taking action to empower young people to demand change, focusing on calling out hate speech and keeping guns out of the wrong hands.

The author shares personal connections to several cities affected by mass shootings, highlighting the devastating impact on communities. They emphasize the importance of listening to the concerns and ideas of young people, who are leading efforts to address gun violence. The author's own Youth Council to End Gun Violence has been instrumental in engaging young people around this issue.

The summary concludes with a call to action, urging readers to join forces with mayors and young leaders to build a nation where everyone belongs and feels safe.
