SUMMARY:

The controversy surrounding Rep. Ilhan Omar's comments on Israel has sparked a heated debate. While Omar's defenders argue that her criticism of US-Israel policy is being unfairly smeared as anti-Semitic, others believe that her language has been careless and hurtful. The author argues that Omar's words have value on their own terms, and that using language that can be read as anti-Semitic is problematic, even if one supports a revision of US policy in Israel. The author also criticizes the Democratic Party's handling of the situation, suggesting that they should focus on more pressing issues rather than condemning Omar. The controversy comes at a time when the US's long-standing consensus on Israel is breaking down, with younger Democrats increasingly critical of US support for Israel. The author concludes that Omar's defenders are correct that her criticism was destined to be smeared, but that Democrats cannot afford to alienate Jewish voters, and that Omar's language should be more careful.

END OF SUMMARY.
