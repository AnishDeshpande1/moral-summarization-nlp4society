SUMMARY:
The article critiques Rep. Ilhan Omar’s recent remarks about “dual loyalty” to Israel, arguing that—even if intended as a critique of U.S. policy—they echo anti‑Semitic tropes and damage the progressive cause. While Democrats should defend Omar against right‑wing attacks, the party’s decision to bring a floor vote condemning “dual‑loyalty smears” (without naming her) is seen as a waste of time and a risky split that alienates pro‑Israel Jewish voters, a crucial electoral bloc. The piece stresses that genuine support for Palestinian justice requires careful language and an honest reckoning with Israel’s occupation, not deflection through “what‑about‑ism” about other global atrocities. It notes a shifting Democratic base—young voters are less staunchly pro‑Israel—and warns that mishandling Omar’s comments will hurt both the party’s moral credibility and its ability to advance a fair U.S. stance on Israel‑Palestine. Ultimately, the author calls for internal resolution, consistent condemnation of anti‑Semitism, and a broader Democratic shift away from unconditional support for right‑wing Israeli governments.

END OF SUMMARY.
