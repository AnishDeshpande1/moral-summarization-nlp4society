SUMMARY:

Democratic leadership is planning to hold a floor vote to condemn dual-loyalty smears, seemingly in response to Rep. Ilhan Omar's (D-Minn) comments about Israel. Critics argue that Omar has repeatedly tripped over language that can be read as anti-Semitic, and that this is problematic for those who support revising American policy on Israel. The controversy comes at a time when public opinion on Israel is shifting, with younger Democrats less likely to describe Israel as an ally. Reps. Omar and Rashida Tlaib (D-Mich) have been vocal critics of US policy in Israel, but their criticism has been smeared as anti-Semitic. Democratic leaders are being criticized for bringing the issue to a vote, rather than addressing more problematic actors like Steve King or Duncan Hunter.

The article argues that Omar's defenders are correct that her comments were destined to be smeared as anti-Semitic, and that Democrats should listen to allies who say her words are hurtful. The controversy highlights the need for careful language when debating Israel policy, and the importance of standing up to criticism from others.
