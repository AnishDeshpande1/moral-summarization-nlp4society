SUMMARY: Freshman Rep. Ilhan Omar sparked controversy after a panel remark about “political influence that says it is okay for people to push for allegiance to a foreign country,” followed by a tweet rejecting expectations of loyalty to Israel. Democratic leaders, breaking with past practice, scheduled a floor vote to condemn dual‑loyalty smears, though they did not name Omar. The article argues that while criticism of U.S. Israel policy is legitimate, Omar’s language can be read as anti‑Semitic and risks alienating Jewish voters, a crucial Democratic bloc. It criticizes Democrats for focusing on Omar instead of more overtly racist figures like Steve King or Duncan Hunter, and warns that internal party disputes weaken support for Palestinian justice. The piece notes a shifting public view: younger Democrats are less supportive of Israel, and the bipartisan consensus on U.S. policy is eroding, especially as Netanyahu aligns closely with the GOP. Omar and Rep. Rashida Tlaib, the first Muslim women in Congress, remain targets of right‑wing attacks, but the author calls for careful word choice and internal resolution of the issue rather than a public censure. END OF SUMMARY.
