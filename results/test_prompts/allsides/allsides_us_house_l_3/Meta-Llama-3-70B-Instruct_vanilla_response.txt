SUMMARY:

The controversy surrounding Rep. Ilhan Omar's comments about Israel has reignited, with her recent remarks sparking accusations of anti-Semitism. Democratic leadership has scheduled a floor vote to condemn dual-loyalty smears, without directly referencing Omar. While some defend Omar, others argue that her language has been problematic and has undermined efforts to change the debate around Israel in the US. The article suggests that Omar's defenders are correct that her criticism of US-Israel policy would have been smeared as anti-Semitic regardless, but also acknowledges that her language has been hurtful to some American Jews. The decision to bring this to the floor is seen as problematic, as it opens up a split in the Democratic Party and distracts from more pressing issues. Instead, leaders could focus on rejecting attempts to criminalize boycotts of Israel and listening to activists who reject the party's stance on the issue.
