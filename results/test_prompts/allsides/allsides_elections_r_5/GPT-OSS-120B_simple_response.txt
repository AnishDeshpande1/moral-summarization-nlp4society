SUMMARY: The article accuses President Obama of a deliberate moral failure surrounding the September 11, 2012 Benghazi attack. It claims internal emails and diplomatic cables prove that Obama and his team knowingly mischaracterized the assault as a spontaneous protest sparked by an anti‑Muslim video, while intelligence already identified the perpetrators as the al‑Qaeda‑linked group Ansar al‑Sharia. The author argues this deception was driven by political self‑interest—to protect Obama’s image as a champion against terrorism and to hide the consequences of his 2011 Libya intervention, which the piece says created a “failed Islamist state” and enabled jihadist expansion. The narrative portrays the administration’s refusal to request immediate military rescue as a callous abandonment of American diplomats, resulting in the deaths of Ambassador J. Christopher Stevens and three staff members. Framed as a grave ethical breach, the author concludes that Obama’s actions constitute a cover‑up and a betrayal of American lives, rendering him unfit for re‑election. END OF SUMMARY.
