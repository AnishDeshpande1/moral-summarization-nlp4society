SUMMARY:
U.S. senators clashed over how President Trump’s impeachment trial should be run, exposing a battle over authority and fairness. Republican leader Mitch McConnell warned he has not ruled out witnesses but stopped short of granting Democrats’ demand for pre‑approved testimony, invoking the 1999 Bill Clinton trial as a “fair” precedent and suggesting that extending the process could harm the Republican agenda. Democrats, led by Chuck Schumer, argued that excluding documents and witnesses would make the trial appear a sham—a betrayal of justice that harms the public’s trust. They pressed for four subpoenas, accusing the White House of a “witch hunt” and “impeachment scam” that cheats the American people out of a transparent proceeding. President Trump and his allies framed the Democrats’ push for witnesses as a partisan delay tactic, portraying the effort to limit testimony as protecting the nation from unnecessary harm. The dispute underscores competing claims of legitimate authority, concern for the nation’s wellbeing, and demands for a fair, honest process. END OF SUMMARY.
