SUMMARY:

U.S. senators are at odds over the shape of an impeachment trial for President Donald Trump, with Republican leader Mitch McConnell indicating that he has not ruled out hearing from witnesses. However, Democrats are pushing for testimony to ensure a fair trial and prevent it from being seen as a "sham." The House voted to impeach Trump on two charges: abuse of power and obstructing Congress' investigation. With the Senate controlled by Republicans, it is unlikely that Trump will be convicted and removed from office. Despite this, Democrats are urging for witnesses to be heard, including acting Chief of Staff Mick Mulvaney and former national security adviser John Bolton, who were blocked from testifying in the House proceedings. The trial's outcome remains uncertain as senators debate the rules of the proceeding.
