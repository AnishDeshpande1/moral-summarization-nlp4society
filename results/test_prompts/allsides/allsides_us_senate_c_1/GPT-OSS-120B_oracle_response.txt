SUMMARY:  
U.S. senators sparred over how to conduct the upcoming impeachment **trial** of President Donald Trump. Republican leader Mitch McConnell, citing the 1999 impeachment **trial** of President Bill Clinton, said the Senate “hasn’t ruled out witnesses” and wants to handle the case “just like we did with President Clinton” – a call for **fair** procedures. Democrats, led by Senate Majority Leader Chuck Schumer, argue that without **documents** and **witnesses**, the process will look like a **sham** and a **hunt**, demanding that the Senate **hold** a **fair** **trial** with subpoenaed testimony. The House impeached Trump on two articles – **pressuring** Ukraine to investigate Joe Biden and his son, and **abuse** of power plus **obstructing** Congress. Trump and his allies label the impeachment effort a “**Witch Hunt**” and urge a swift conclusion, while Pelosi has not yet transmitted the articles to the Senate. The White House blocked four potential **witnesses**, heightening the partisan clash over how the **trial** will proceed.  

END OF SUMMARY.
