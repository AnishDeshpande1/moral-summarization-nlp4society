SUMMARY:

The impeachment trial of President Donald Trump is set to begin in early 2020, but senators are at odds over the inclusion of witnesses. Republican leader Mitch McConnell has not ruled out hearing from witnesses, citing the 1999 impeachment trial of President Bill Clinton as a model. However, Democrats, led by Chuck Schumer, are demanding that witnesses be allowed to testify, arguing that without them, the trial would be a "sham" and lack credibility. The White House has blocked four potential witnesses, including acting Chief of Staff Mick Mulvaney and former national security adviser John Bolton, from testifying. Trump has accused Democrats of delaying tactics and urged for a quick conclusion to the impeachment process. The moral framing of this issue is clear: allowing witnesses to testify is a matter of fairness and transparency, while blocking them would be an attempt to conceal the truth and undermine the integrity of the trial. The American people deserve a legitimate and thorough investigation into the President's actions.

END OF SUMMARY.
