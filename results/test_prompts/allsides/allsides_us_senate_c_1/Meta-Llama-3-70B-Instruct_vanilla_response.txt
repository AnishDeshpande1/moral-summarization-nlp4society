SUMMARY:

US Senators are debating the format of President Donald Trump's impeachment trial, with Republican leader Mitch McConnell saying he hasn't ruled out hearing from witnesses as Democrats have demanded. McConnell cited the 1999 impeachment trial of President Bill Clinton as a model, where senators decided on witnesses after opening arguments and a written question period. Democrats, led by Chuck Schumer, are pushing for witnesses, including current and former administration officials, and the release of relevant documents. Schumer warned that a trial without witnesses and documents would seem like a "sham trial" to the American people. Trump, who was impeached by the House of Representatives on charges of abuse of power and obstructing Congress, has urged for a quick conclusion to the process, accusing Democrats of delaying tactics. The Senate cannot take action until it receives the articles of impeachment from House Speaker Nancy Pelosi.

END OF SUMMARY.
