SUMMARY: U.S. senators debated procedural rules for President Donald Trump’s upcoming impeachment trial. Republican leader Mitch McConnell said the Senate has not ruled out hearing witnesses but favored handling the case similarly to the 1999 Bill Clinton trial, which involved limited video testimony and written questions. Democrats, led by Senate Majority Leader Chuck Schumer, insist the trial must include witnesses and documents, warning that a trial without them would appear a “sham.” The House impeached Trump on two articles—abuse of power and obstruction of Congress—related to his pressure on Ukraine to investigate Joe Biden and his son. Trump and Republicans argue the trial should proceed quickly, while Democrats seek to subpoena four witnesses, all of whom the White House blocked from testifying in the House. Senate action cannot begin until the House transmits the articles of impeachment. END OF SUMMARY.
