SUMMARY:
U.S. senators sparred over the shape of the upcoming impeachment trial of President Donald Trump. Republican leader Mitch McConnell said the Senate “hasn’t ruled out witnesses” and wants to handle the case “fair”‑ly, citing the 1999 Clinton impeachment as a model. Democrats, led by Chuck Schumer, argue that a trial without documents and witnesses would be a sham and a “witch hunt”‑style scam, urging the release of blocked administration records and the subpoenaing of four witnesses. Trump and his allies denounced the Democrats’ push as a “crazy” delay, calling the impeachment process a “witch hunt” and “scam” that wastes time and threatens a negative outcome for the president. While the House has impeached Trump on abuse of power and obstructing Congress, the Republican‑controlled Senate doubts it will convict, and the dispute continues over whether to allow testimony that could expose further abuse or obstructing evidence.
