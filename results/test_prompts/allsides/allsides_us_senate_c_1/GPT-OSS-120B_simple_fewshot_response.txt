SUMMARY:
U.S. senators clashed over how the Senate impeachment trial of President Donald Trump should be conducted. Republican leader Mitch McConnell told Fox News that, while he has not ruled out hearing witnesses, he prefers to follow the precedent set by the 1999 Bill Clinton trial, which featured limited, video‑based testimony and a written question period. Democrats, led by Senate Majority Leader Chuck Schumer, argue that a trial without documents and witnesses would look like a “sham” and demand the subpoena of four former administration officials. The House, controlled by Democrats, voted along party lines to impeach Trump on abuse‑of‑power and obstruction charges related to his pressure on Ukraine to investigate the Bidens. Trump and his allies denounce the process as a partisan “witch hunt” and urge a swift conclusion, while the White House has already blocked the four proposed witnesses. The dispute highlights deep partisan divisions over procedural fairness, transparency, and the legitimacy of the impeachment effort.

END OF SUMMARY.
