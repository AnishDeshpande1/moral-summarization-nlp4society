**SUMMARY:**  
U.S. senators clashed over the shape of President Donald Trump’s impeachment trial, with Republican leader Mitch McConnell hinting that witnesses might be allowed, invoking the “fair” precedent of the 1999 Clinton trial. Democrats, led by Chuck Schumer, demanded witnesses and documents, warning that a trial without them would look like a “sham” or “show” trial. Trump and his allies denounced the process as a “Witch Hunt” and “impeachment scam,” accusing Democrats of “delaying tactics.” Senate Majority Leader McConnell said the Senate cannot act until it receives the articles of impeachment, while Trump called the proceedings a “Crazy” abuse of power. The dispute centers on whether the Senate will hear testimony from current and former administration officials, a decision that could prolong the trial and bring new evidence against Trump.
