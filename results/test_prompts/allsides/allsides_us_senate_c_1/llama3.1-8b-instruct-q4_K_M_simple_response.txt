SUMMARY:

A debate is unfolding in the US Senate over the shape of President Donald Trump's impeachment trial, with Republicans led by Mitch McConnell indicating they may allow witnesses to testify. However, Democrats, led by Chuck Schumer, are pushing for the inclusion of documents and witnesses, arguing that a trial without them would be a "sham." The House has already voted to impeach Trump on charges of abuse of power and obstruction of Congress.

McConnell cited the 1999 impeachment trial of President Bill Clinton as a model, while Schumer urged his colleagues to demand the release of relevant documents. Democrats have requested four witnesses, including former national security adviser John Bolton, who was blocked from testifying in the House proceedings.

Trump has accused Democrats of delaying tactics and called for a quick conclusion to the impeachment process. The fate of the trial remains uncertain, with little chance Trump will be convicted and removed from office by the Republican-controlled Senate.

END OF SUMMARY.
