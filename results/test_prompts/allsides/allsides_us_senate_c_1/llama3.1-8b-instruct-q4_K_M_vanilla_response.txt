SUMMARY:

U.S. senators debated the shape of President Donald Trump's impeachment trial on Monday, with Republican leader Mitch McConnell saying he has not ruled out hearing from witnesses. However, McConnell stopped short of agreeing to a Democratic request for the Senate to agree ahead of time to take testimony during the trial. The 1999 impeachment trial of President Bill Clinton was cited as a model. Democrats, led by Chuck Schumer, urged for witness testimony and the release of relevant Trump administration documents. Schumer said that without witnesses and documents, the trial would seem like a "sham trial." The House voted to impeach Trump on two charges last week, but there is little chance he will be convicted in the Republican-controlled Senate.

The standoff between Democrats and Republicans comes as House Speaker Nancy Pelosi has not yet transmitted the articles of impeachment to the Senate. McConnell said the Senate cannot take action until it receives the articles. The White House blocked four witnesses from testifying during impeachment proceedings in the House, including acting Chief of Staff Mick Mulvaney and former national security adviser John Bolton.

END OF SUMMARY.
