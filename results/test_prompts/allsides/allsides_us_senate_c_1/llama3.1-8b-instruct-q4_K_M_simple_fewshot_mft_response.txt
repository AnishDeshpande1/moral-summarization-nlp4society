SUMMARY:

The impeachment trial of President Donald Trump is underway, with senators debating the shape of the proceedings. Republican leader Mitch McConnell has not ruled out hearing from witnesses, citing the 1999 impeachment trial of President Bill Clinton as a model. However, Democrats are pushing for witnesses to be included, arguing that it is essential for a fair trial. Chuck Schumer, the top Democrat in the Senate, urged his colleagues to call for the release of relevant Trump administration documents and to subpoena key witnesses, including acting Chief of Staff Mick Mulvaney and former national security adviser John Bolton. The White House has blocked these witnesses from testifying during impeachment proceedings in the House of Representatives in December. McConnell's stance on witnesses is seen as a way to limit the trial and prevent damaging evidence from being presented against Trump. Democrats are calling for a fair and transparent trial, highlighting the importance of witnesses and documents in uncovering the truth about Trump's actions.

END OF SUMMARY.
