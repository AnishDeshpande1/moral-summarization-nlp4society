SUMMARY:  
In a sharply partisan showdown, Senate leaders debated how to conduct President Donald Trump’s impeachment trial. Republican leader Mitch McConnell invoked the “fairness” of the 1999 Bill Clinton trial as a model, saying the Senate “hasn’t ruled out witnesses” but wants to avoid a prolonged, “show‑trial” spectacle. Democrats, led by Chuck Schumer, argued that excluding documents and witnesses would make the process a “sham” and undermine public trust, urging the Senate to subpoena four key officials. The House, voting along party lines, impeached Trump on abuse‑of‑power and obstruction charges tied to a Ukraine‑Biden scandal, while Trump and his allies denounced the effort as a “Witch Hunt” and “Impeachment Scam,” demanding a swift conclusion. The clash reflects a moral framing: Republicans claim procedural fairness and caution against a politically motivated circus, whereas Democrats stress accountability and transparency to prevent a miscarriage of justice.  

END OF SUMMARY.
