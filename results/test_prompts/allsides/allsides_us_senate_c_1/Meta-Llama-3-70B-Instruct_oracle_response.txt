SUMMARY: 

US Senators are at odds over the format of President Donald Trump's impeachment trial, with Republican leader Mitch McConnell saying he hasn't ruled out hearing from witnesses as Democrats have demanded. McConnell cited the 1999 impeachment trial of President Bill Clinton as a model, where senators decided on witnesses after opening arguments and a written question period. Democrats, however, insist that the trial must include witnesses and documents, or it would seem like a "sham trial" to the American people. Trump was impeached by the House of Representatives on charges of abuse of power and obstructing Congress' investigation into his pressuring Ukraine to investigate Joe Biden. Trump has denied any wrongdoing and accused Democrats of delaying tactics. The White House blocked four witnesses, including acting Chief of Staff Mick Mulvaney and former national security adviser John Bolton, from testifying during impeachment proceedings in the House. 

END OF SUMMARY.
