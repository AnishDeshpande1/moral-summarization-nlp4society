SUMMARY:

House Democrats have introduced a bill to grant permanent protections to undocumented immigrants brought to the US as children, known as Dreamers. However, the bill's fate rests with Senate Republicans, who have increasingly opposed the DREAM Act over the years. Once supported by many Senate Republicans, the bill is now seen as "amnesty" by the party's conservative wing. Even Republicans who personally believe Dreamers deserve citizenship are hesitant to support the bill due to political concerns. The White House and most Republicans only endorse a Dreamer fix in exchange for billions of dollars in border security or wholesale changes to the legal immigration system. The bill's prospects in the Senate appear dire, with many Republicans saying it has no chance of passing without significant concessions. The partisan divide and internal polarization within the Republican Party have made it difficult to find a solution that both parties can endorse.

END OF SUMMARY.
