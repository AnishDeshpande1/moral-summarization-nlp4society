SUMMARY:

House Democrats have introduced a bill to grant permanent protections to undocumented immigrants brought to the country as children, known as Dreamers. However, the fate of these individuals rests in the hands of Senate Republicans, who have struggled to find a solution that they and their conservative base can endorse. In the past, many Senate Republicans supported the DREAM Act, viewing it as an act of "compassion" rather than "amnesty." But as the party's conservative wing gained influence, they began to see it as an act of "amnesty" and backed away. Today, most Senate Republicans are unlikely to support the bill, fearing political risks and seeing little payoff. They would rather tie a Dreamer fix to billions of dollars in border security or wholesale changes to the legal immigration system. The bill's prospects in the Senate appear dire, leaving Dreamers in legal limbo, and vulnerable to being "punished" for the actions of their parents.

END OF SUMMARY.
