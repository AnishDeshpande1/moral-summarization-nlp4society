SUMMARY:

House Democrats have introduced a bill to grant permanent protections to undocumented immigrants brought to the US as children, known as Dreamers. However, the bill's fate is uncertain in the Senate, where Republicans have increasingly opposed the DREAM Act over the years. In the past, many Senate Republicans supported the bill, but as the party's conservative wing grew, they began to view it as "amnesty" rather than "compassion." Today, most Senate Republicans are unlikely to support a stand-alone bill, instead wanting to tie it to border security or immigration system changes. Even Republican senators who support the DREAM Act, such as Lindsey Graham and Marco Rubio, believe a comprehensive solution is needed. The bill is likely to pass in the Democratic-controlled House but faces significant hurdles in the Republican-led Senate.

END OF SUMMARY.
