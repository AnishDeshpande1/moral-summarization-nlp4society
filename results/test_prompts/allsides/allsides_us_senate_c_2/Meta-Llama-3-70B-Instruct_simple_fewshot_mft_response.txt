SUMMARY:

House Democrats have introduced a bill to grant permanent protections to undocumented immigrants brought to the country as children, known as Dreamers. However, the bill's fate is uncertain in the Senate, where Republicans have increasingly opposed the DREAM Act, viewing it as "amnesty" rather than an act of compassion. The GOP's shift away from supporting the DREAM Act is attributed to the growing influence of the party's conservative wing and the intensification of polarization between the parties. While some Republicans, like Sen. Roy Blunt, claim to support Dreamers, they are unwilling to vote for a stand-alone bill, instead demanding border security or wholesale changes to the legal immigration system in exchange for a Dreamer fix. The bill's prospects in the Senate appear dire, with many Republicans prioritizing their political concerns over supporting a measure that would bring relief to hundreds of thousands of individuals. The GOP's stance is seen as a betrayal of the values of compassion and fairness, and a subversion of the authority of the legislative process.

END OF SUMMARY.
