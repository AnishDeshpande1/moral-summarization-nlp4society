SUMMARY:

House Democrats have introduced a bill to grant permanent protections to undocumented immigrants brought to the US as children, known as Dreamers. However, the fate of this legislation rests in the hands of Senate Republicans, who have struggled for years to come up with a solution that satisfies their conservative base. The Republican Party's stance on immigration has shifted over time, from supporting the DREAM Act in 2003 to opposing it today. Many Senate Republicans now require a comprehensive solution that includes border security or changes to the legal immigration system.

Senators like Lindsey Graham and Tim Scott acknowledge the need for progress but emphasize that compromise is necessary. The House bill, which creates a pathway to citizenship for Dreamers and foreigners with Temporary Protected Status, faces an uphill battle in the Senate. With few Republicans willing to support a stand-alone Dreamer bill, its prospects appear dire.

END OF SUMMARY.
