House Democrats have introduced the Dream and Promise Act, a bill that would grant permanent protection and a pathway to citizenship for Dreamers and TPS holders, framing the effort as a matter of **compassion** and fairness. Historically, many Senate Republicans supported the DREAM Act, arguing it was **unfair** to punish children for their parents’ actions. Over time, the party’s **conservative** wing has recast the legislation as **amnesty**, and internal **polarization** has turned **agony** into political risk. Prominent Republicans such as Sen. Richard Lugar describe the current climate as one where senators are unwilling to “bleed and die” on the issue, while hard‑line voices like Sen. Marco Rubio warn the stand‑alone bill will be altered and lose its purpose. Some GOP leaders, including Sen. Lindsey Graham and Sen. Tim Scott, suggest a broader, **hard‑line** immigration package that pairs Dreamer relief with border‑security funding. With the Democratic‑controlled House poised to pass the measure, its fate now rests in a Senate where **zealous** opposition and the fear of endorsing **amnesty** make passage unlikely.
