SUMMARY:

The fate of Dreamers, undocumented immigrants brought to the US as children, hangs in the balance as Senate Republicans struggle to come up with a solution that they can endorse. The DREAM Act was once seen as an act of compassion by many Republicans, but over time, the party's more conservative wing has viewed it as amnesty. This shift has led to a dwindling number of Republican co-sponsors for the bill. Even supporters like Sen. Richard Lugar acknowledge the internal battle between personal beliefs and political concerns about supporting "amnesty." Those who support the DREAM Act are now seen as willing to bleed and die on this issue, but it may not be worth the risk. The White House has endorsed a Dreamer fix only in exchange for border security or wholesale changes to the immigration system. Senate Republicans seem unwilling to bring up a stand-alone bill, fearing that it will be amended with unrelated provisions.

END OF SUMMARY.
