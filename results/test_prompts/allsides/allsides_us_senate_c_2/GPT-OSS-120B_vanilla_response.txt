SUMMARY: House Democrats introduced the Dream and Promise Act, a bill that would give permanent protection and a path to citizenship for undocumented immigrants brought to the U.S. as children (Dreamers) and for those with Temporary Protected Status. While the measure is expected to pass the Democratic‑controlled House, its fate rests with the Republican‑led Senate, where support has waned over two decades. Early Senate versions of the DREAM Act enjoyed bipartisan backing, but as the GOP’s conservative wing grew, co‑sponsorship fell from 12 Republicans in 2003 to none by 2011. Current Senate leaders view a stand‑alone Dreamer bill as politically untenable, preferring a broader immigration package that ties Dreamer relief to border‑security funding or wider immigration reform. Senators such as Lindsey Graham and Tim Scott call for a comprehensive solution, while others like Marco Rubio argue any Dreamer bill will be altered with unrelated amendments, ensuring its defeat. The bill’s prospects remain dim in the Senate despite vocal support from some GOP members and former bipartisan advocates like the late Sen. Richard Lugar. END OF SUMMARY.
