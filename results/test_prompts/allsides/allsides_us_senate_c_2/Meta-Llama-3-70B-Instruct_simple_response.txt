SUMMARY:

House Democrats introduced a bill to grant permanent protections to undocumented immigrants brought to the US as children, but its fate rests with Senate Republicans, who have struggled to find a solution that their conservative base can endorse. Despite once having widespread support, the GOP has backed away from the DREAM Act, viewing it as "amnesty" rather than "compassion." The party's shift is attributed to polarization and the influence of the tea party movement and Trump's followers. Even Republican supporters of the DREAM Act, like Sen. Roy Blunt, are hesitant to take a stand, fearing political risks. Instead, most Republicans want a comprehensive solution that includes border security or changes to the legal immigration system. The bill's prospects in the Senate appear dire, with many Republicans opposing a stand-alone Dreamer bill. The moral framing of this issue highlights the GOP's abandonment of compassion and its prioritization of political concerns over doing what is right.

END OF SUMMARY.
