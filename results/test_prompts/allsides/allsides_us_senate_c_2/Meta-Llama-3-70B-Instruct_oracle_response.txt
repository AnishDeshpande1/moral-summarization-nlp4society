SUMMARY:

House Democrats introduced a bill to grant permanent protections to undocumented immigrants brought to the US as children, known as Dreamers. However, the fate of these Dreamers rests in the hands of Senate Republicans, who have struggled to find a solution that they and their conservative base can endorse. The GOP has backed away from the idea, viewing the DREAM Act as an act of "amnesty" rather than "compassion." The party's more conservative wing has intensified polarization, leaving senators seeing little payoff to support the DREAM Act and all kinds of risks. Many Republicans, like Sen. Lindsey Graham, want a more comprehensive solution that includes billions of dollars in border security or wholesale changes to the legal immigration system. The House bill, which would create a pathway to citizenship for Dreamers and foreigners with Temporary Protected Status, stands little chance in the Republican-led Senate, where it would be blocked by amendments and fall apart.

END OF SUMMARY.
