SUMMARY:  
House Democrats introduced the Dream and Promise Act, a bill that would **protect** the roughly 800,000 undocumented immigrants brought to the U.S. as children and also extend rights to those with Temporary Protected Status, a program **allowed** after their homelands were **ravaged** by **war** and **disasters**. The measure would give a permanent pathway to citizenship, ending the temporary shelter of DACA that was **blocked** by courts. Yet its fate now rests with Senate Republicans, whose **intensified** internal battle over “**amnesty**” versus compassion has **killed** bipartisan support. Former GOP champion Richard Lugar says the party’s **beating** from the Tea Party and Trump era has turned the issue into a political **exchange**, with many senators fearing they will **use** the bill as a vehicle for other amendments, causing it to **become** something else and **fall** apart. Senators such as Lindsey Graham and Tim Scott argue a broader immigration fix is needed, while Marco Rubio warns a stand‑alone Dreamer bill has no chance. Even pro‑Dreamer Republicans like Roy Blunt remain non‑committal, leaving the **end** of the legislative effort uncertain.
