House Democrats have introduced a bill to grant permanent protections to undocumented immigrants brought to the country as children, known as Dreamers. However, the fate of these Dreamers rests in the hands of Senate Republicans, who have struggled to find a solution that they and their conservative base can endorse. In the past, many Senate Republicans supported the DREAM Act as an act of "compassion," but as the party's more conservative wing viewed it as an act of "amnesty," they backed away from the idea. Today, most Senate Republicans prioritize border security and wholesale changes to the legal immigration system over a stand-alone Dreamer bill. The bill's prospects in the Senate appear dire, with many Republicans seeing little payoff to support the DREAM Act and all kinds of risks. As Sen.
