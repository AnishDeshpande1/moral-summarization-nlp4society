House Democrats introduced the Dream and Promise Act, a bill that would grant permanent protections and a pathway to citizenship for undocumented immigrants brought to the United States as children and for TPS holders. While a few GOP members, such as Sen. Roy Blunt, say they are *pro‑Dreamers*, most argue that any *hard‑line* stance must be tied to a broader *compromise* on border security and immigration reform. Veteran Republican supporters like the late Sen. Richard Lugar recall earlier *fairness* debates, yet today the party’s base demands a trade‑off, fearing *injustice* to citizens and a loss of *liberty* for native voters. Consequently, the House bill is expected to pass the Democratic chamber easily but faces an uncertain fate in the Republican‑led Senate, where internal divisions and political calculations dominate.
