SUMMARY:
House Democrats have introduced the Dream and Promise Act, a bill that would grant permanent protection and a path to citizenship for undocumented immigrants brought to the United States as children and for TPS holders. While the legislation enjoys broad support in the Democratic‑controlled House, its fate now rests with a Senate that has grown increasingly hostile to any measure seen as “amnesty.” The article traces the erosion of bipartisan backing for the DREAM Act—from a 2003 version with twelve Republican co‑sponsors to a 2011 version with none—highlighting how the GOP’s conservative base has turned compassion for Dreamers into a political liability. Even longtime Republican supporters like the late Sen. Richard Lugar warn that senators see no payoff in backing the bill, and leaders such as Sen. Marco Rubio dismiss a stand‑alone Dreamer measure as doomed to be hijacked by unrelated amendments. With Republicans demanding border‑security funding or sweeping immigration reforms in exchange, the Dreamers’ future remains in legal limbo, underscoring a deepening partisan divide over immigration compassion versus “amnesty.”  
END OF SUMMARY.
