SUMMARY:

House Democrats have introduced a bill to grant permanent protections to undocumented immigrants brought to the country as children, known as Dreamers. However, the fate of this legislation rests in the hands of Senate Republicans, who have struggled for years to come up with a solution that aligns with their conservative base. The shift within the Republican Party from supporting the DREAM Act as an act of "compassion" to viewing it as an act of "amnesty" has made it increasingly difficult for senators to support the bill. Many Republicans, including Senator Marco Rubio, have taken a hard-line stance on immigration issues, making it unlikely that the stand-alone House bill will pass in the Senate. Instead, the White House and most Republicans are pushing for a comprehensive solution that includes border security and changes to the legal immigration system. This development raises concerns about the lack of empathy and care for Dreamers, as well as the prioritization of partisan politics over their welfare.
