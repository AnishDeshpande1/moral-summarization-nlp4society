SUMMARY:

The fate of undocumented immigrants brought to the US as children, known as Dreamers, hangs in the balance as Senate Republicans struggle to come up with a solution that aligns with their conservative base. Despite initial support from some Republicans, including Sen. Orrin Hatch, who sponsored the 2003 version of the DREAM Act, the party has increasingly viewed it as an act of "amnesty" rather than compassion. The Trump administration's decision to end the DACA program has left Dreamers in limbo, prompting a push by House Democrats for a permanent legislative fix. However, prospects in the Republican-led Senate appear dire, with many seeing little payoff in supporting the DREAM Act and facing risks of being "killed" or "blocked" from advancing any immigration-related legislation. Instead, Republicans are pushing for an "exchange" of border security measures for protection of Dreamers, which has become a contentious issue.

END OF SUMMARY.
