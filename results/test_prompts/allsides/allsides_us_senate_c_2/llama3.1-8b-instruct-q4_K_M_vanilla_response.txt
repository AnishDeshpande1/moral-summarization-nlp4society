SUMMARY:

House Democrats have introduced the Dream and Promise Act, which would grant permanent protections to undocumented immigrants brought to the US as children, also known as Dreamers. However, the bill's fate is uncertain in the Senate, where Republicans have struggled to find a solution for nearly two decades. The party has shifted from supporting the DREAM Act to opposing it, with many Republicans now viewing it as "amnesty." Senators like Roy Blunt and Lindsey Graham say that a standalone Dreamer bill has no chance of passing, and instead want to tie protections for Dreamers to border security or wholesale changes in the immigration system. The White House has also endorsed this approach. Democrats are pushing for a clean bill to protect Dreamers, but it's unclear if they'll be able to overcome Republican opposition. Many Republicans have abandoned their support for the DREAM Act due to conservative pressure and polarization within the party.

END OF SUMMARY.
