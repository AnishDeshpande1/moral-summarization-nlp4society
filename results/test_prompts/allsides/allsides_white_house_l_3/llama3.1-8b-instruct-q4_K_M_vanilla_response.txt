SUMMARY:
President Barack Obama visited the Islamic Society of Baltimore, his first visit to a mosque in the United States, to address "inexcusable political rhetoric against Muslim-Americans" from Republican presidential candidates. He sought to recast the image of Islam and encouraged members of the faith to speak out against terror. Obama emphasized that Muslims are essential to the fabric of America and should not be singled out for extra scrutiny. He also condemned GOP counterterror plans, saying they would only amplify messages coming from terrorist groups.

Obama's speech was seen as a forceful counterpoint to language used by some Republican presidential candidates, including Donald Trump's proposal to ban all Muslims from entering the country. The President acknowledged that some Muslim leaders had been targeted and killed for speaking out against terrorism, but emphasized the importance of amplifying their voices. Obama also highlighted the contributions of the Muslim-American community and called for more positive depictions of Muslims in American media.

END OF SUMMARY.
