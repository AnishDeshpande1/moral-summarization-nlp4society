SUMMARY:

President Barack Obama made a historic visit to the Islamic Society of Baltimore, seeking to rebut "inexcusable political rhetoric against Muslim-Americans" from Republican presidential candidates. He emphasized that Muslims are essential to America's fabric and encouraged them to speak out against terror. Obama condemned GOP counterterror plans that single out Muslims for extra scrutiny, arguing they would only amplify messages coming from terrorist groups. He also decried bigotry and the use of religious screens in national security measures, which he believes reinforce the ideas of terrorists. Obama urged Americans to protect all faiths and accept people without bias into the United States. He referenced conversations with young Muslim parents whose children are worried about being forced out of the country due to Islamophobia. Obama's visit aimed to counter GOP claims that he hasn't been forceful enough in demanding that Muslim leaders help root out extremism, while also highlighting the positive contributions of Muslim-Americans.

END OF SUMMARY.
