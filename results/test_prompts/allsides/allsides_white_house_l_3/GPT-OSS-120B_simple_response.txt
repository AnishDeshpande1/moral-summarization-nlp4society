SUMMARY: President Barack Obama used his historic first U.S. mosque visit to confront “inexcusable political rhetoric” targeting Muslim‑Americans and to affirm that Muslims belong fully to the American fabric. Speaking at Baltimore’s Islamic Society, he declared, “You’re Muslim and American,” and warned that GOP proposals to single out Muslims for extra scrutiny only amplify terrorist propaganda. Obama framed the issue as a moral imperative: America must reject bigotry, protect all faiths, and resist reinforcing the terrorists’ narrative. He shared the anxieties of young Muslim families fearing deportation or round‑ups, urging a nation free of such fear. While praising Muslim leaders who condemn terrorism, he called for louder amplification of their voices and for media to depict Muslims beyond security roles. The visit, prompted by Muslim advocates, was intended as a public repudiation of Islamophobia, echoing George W. Bush’s post‑9/11 mosque outreach. Though some dismissed the gesture as “too late,” Obama stressed that inclusion and respect are not optional during crises but essential to America’s moral core. END OF SUMMARY.
