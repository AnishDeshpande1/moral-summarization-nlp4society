**SUMMARY:**

President Barack Obama visited the Islamic Society of Baltimore, a 47-year-old mosque with thousands of attendees, to address "inexcusable political rhetoric against Muslim-Americans" from Republican presidential candidates. He sought to recast the warped image of Islam and encouraged members of the faith to speak out against terror. Obama described Muslims as essential to the fabric of America, saying "you fit right here," emphasizing that they are both Muslim and American. He decried GOP counterterror plans that would single out Muslims for extra scrutiny, warning that applying religious screens would only amplify messages coming from terrorist groups. The President urged people of all faiths to be accepted without bigotry into the United States.
