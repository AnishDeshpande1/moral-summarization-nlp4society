SUMMARY:

President Barack Obama visited a mosque in Baltimore, Maryland, in an effort to counter anti-Muslim rhetoric from Republican presidential candidates. He emphasized that Muslims are an essential part of American society, saying "You're Muslim and American" and that they should not be subjected to extra scrutiny. Obama criticized GOP counterterror plans that target Muslims, stating that such approaches only amplify terrorist messages. He also encouraged Muslim leaders to speak out against extremism and demanded more positive depictions of Muslims in American media. This visit marks Obama's first to a mosque in the United States, and it comes as he seeks to use his platform to advocate against bigotry and discrimination. While some Muslim-Americans have welcomed the visit, others have criticized it as too little, too late.

END OF SUMMARY.
