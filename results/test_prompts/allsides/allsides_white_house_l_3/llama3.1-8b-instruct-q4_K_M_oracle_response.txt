SUMMARY:

President Barack Obama made a historic visit to the Islamic Society of Baltimore, seeking to rebut "inexcusable political rhetoric against Muslim-Americans" from Republican presidential candidates. He emphasized that Muslims are an essential part of America's fabric and encouraged them to speak out against terrorism. Obama condemned GOP counterterror plans that would single out Muslims for extra scrutiny, arguing that applying religious screens would only amplify the messages of terrorist groups. He also highlighted the importance of protecting all faiths and preventing the reinforcement of terrorist ideas. Citing conversations with young Muslim parents whose children are worried about being removed from the country, Obama demanded that people of all faiths be accepted without bias into the United States. He called for more positive depictions of Muslims in American media and encouraged lifting up their contributions to society.

END OF SUMMARY.
