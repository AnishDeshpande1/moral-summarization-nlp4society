SUMMARY:

President Barack Obama visited a mosque in the United States for the first time, seeking to rebut "inexcusable political rhetoric" against Muslim-Americans from Republican presidential candidates. He emphasized that Muslims are essential to the fabric of America, saying "you fit right here" and "you're Muslim and American." Obama decried GOP counterterror plans that would single out Muslims for extra scrutiny, insisting that applying religious screens would only "amplify" messages from terrorist groups. He urged people of all faiths to be "accepted" without bias and demanded that Muslim leaders "speak out" against terror. Obama also called for more positive depictions of Muslims in American media, saying "we have to lift up the contributions of the Muslim-American community." He warned against "bigotry" and "reinforcing" terrorist ideologies, emphasizing that America must "truly protect" all faiths. The visit was seen as a long-overdue rejection of Islamophobia, with many Muslim groups welcoming the move.

END OF SUMMARY.
