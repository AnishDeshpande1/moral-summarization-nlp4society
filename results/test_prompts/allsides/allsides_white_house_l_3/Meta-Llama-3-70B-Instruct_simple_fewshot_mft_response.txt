SUMMARY:

President Obama visited a mosque in the United States for the first time, seeking to counter "inexcusable political rhetoric against Muslim-Americans" from Republican presidential candidates. He emphasized that Muslims are an essential part of America's fabric, saying "You fit right here... You're Muslim and American." Obama decried GOP counterterror plans that would single out Muslims for extra scrutiny, insisting that applying religious screens would amplify terrorist messages. He advocated for acceptance of people of all faiths without bias, citing conversations with young Muslim parents whose children are worried about being removed from the country. Obama's speech was a calibrated message, citing the work of Islamic centers while identifying areas for improvement. He demanded more positive depictions of Muslims in American media, emphasizing the need to "lift up the contributions of the Muslim-American community." The visit was seen as a public rejection of Islamophobia, with many Muslim groups having called for Obama to schedule a stop at a U.S. mosque.

END OF SUMMARY.
