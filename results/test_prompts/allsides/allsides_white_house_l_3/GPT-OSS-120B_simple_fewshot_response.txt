SUMMARY:
President Barack Obama used his historic first visit to a U.S. mosque to denounce the “inexcusable political rhetoric” targeting Muslim‑Americans from Republican presidential hopefuls. He affirmed that Muslims are “Muslim and American,” rejecting proposals to single out Muslims for extra security screening and warning that such religious profiling fuels terrorist propaganda. Obama called on the nation to reject bigotry, protect all faiths, and amplify Muslim voices that condemn terrorism, noting that these leaders are often threatened for speaking out. He urged the media to portray Muslims positively beyond security narratives and highlighted the longstanding contribution of Islam to American life. While acknowledging past conspiracy theories falsely labeling him a Muslim, Obama framed his visit as a moral stand against Islamophobia and a reminder that all citizens deserve acceptance without bias. The speech aimed to counter GOP anti‑Muslim rhetoric, support Muslim‑American families worried about discrimination, and reinforce the principle that America’s strength lies in its religious diversity. END OF SUMMARY.
