SUMMARY: President Barack Obama made his first-ever visit to a U.S. mosque, speaking at the Islamic Society of Baltimore to counter “inexcusable political rhetoric” from Republican presidential candidates targeting Muslim‑Americans. He affirmed that Muslims are fully American, condemned GOP proposals to single out Muslims for counter‑terrorism scrutiny, and warned that such “religious screens” would aid terrorist narratives. Obama highlighted Muslim leaders’ condemnation of terrorism, called for greater positive representation of Muslims in media, and urged the nation to protect all faiths without bias. The visit responded to calls from Muslim groups for a public rebuke of Islamophobia, echoing George W. Bush’s post‑9/11 mosque visit, and aimed to counter both Republican bigotry and accusations that Obama had not pressured Muslim communities enough on extremism. While praised by some community leaders, many Muslim‑Americans on social media deemed the gesture “too late.” Obama also referenced longstanding conspiracy theories about his own faith, noting they were unfounded. The speech sought to reinforce Muslim‑American inclusion as the nation heads into the 2016 election. END OF SUMMARY.
