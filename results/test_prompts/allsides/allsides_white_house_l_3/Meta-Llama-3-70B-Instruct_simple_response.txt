SUMMARY:

President Barack Obama visited a mosque in the United States for the first time, seeking to counter "inexcusable political rhetoric" against Muslim-Americans. He emphasized that Muslims are an essential part of America's fabric, saying "you're Muslim and American" and urging acceptance without bias. Obama decried GOP counterterror plans that single out Muslims, arguing they amplify terrorist messages. He shared heart-wrenching conversations with young Muslim parents whose children fear being removed from the country, stressing that people of all faiths should be accepted without bias. Obama also acknowledged the importance of Muslim leaders condemning terrorism and called for more positive depictions of Muslims in American media. The visit was seen as a long-overdue rejection of Islamophobia, with many Muslim-Americans welcoming the message, while others felt it was too little, too late.

END OF SUMMARY.
