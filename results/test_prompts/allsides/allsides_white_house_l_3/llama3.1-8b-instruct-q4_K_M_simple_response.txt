SUMMARY:
In his first visit to a US mosque, President Barack Obama addressed the Islamic Society of Baltimore, aiming to counter "inexcusable political rhetoric against Muslim-Americans" from Republican candidates. He emphasized that Muslims are essential to America's fabric and encouraged them to speak out against terrorism. Obama criticized GOP counterterror plans that single out Muslims for extra scrutiny, saying it would amplify terrorist messages. He also condemned Islamophobia and bigotry, stating that people of all faiths should be accepted without bias into the US. The President acknowledged concerns from young Muslim parents whose children were worried about being removed or rounded up due to their faith. Obama's visit was seen as a response to Republican presidential candidates' divisive language, particularly Donald Trump's proposal to ban Muslims from entering the country.

END OF SUMMARY.
