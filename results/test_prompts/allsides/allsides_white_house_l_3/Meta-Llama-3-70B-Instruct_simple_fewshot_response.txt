SUMMARY:

President Barack Obama visited a mosque in the United States for the first time, seeking to counter "inexcusable political rhetoric against Muslim-Americans" from Republican presidential candidates. He emphasized that Muslims are essential to America's fabric and should not be singled out for extra scrutiny. Obama decried GOP counterterror plans, saying they would only amplify terrorist messages. He encouraged Muslims to speak out against terror and advocated for the acceptance of all faiths without bias. The President also called for more positive depictions of Muslims in American media, citing a long history of Islam in America. This visit was seen as a significant step in countering Islamophobia, with many Muslim groups having called for Obama to visit a U.S. mosque. However, some Muslim-Americans felt that the visit was too late in coming, using the hashtag #TooLateObama to express their disappointment. Overall, Obama's message emphasized the importance of inclusivity and acceptance in American society.

END OF SUMMARY.
