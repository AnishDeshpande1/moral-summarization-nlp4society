SUMMARY:

Racism is a public health crisis that has been exacerbated by systemic inequalities in healthcare, education, housing, and job opportunities. Black Americans are disproportionately affected by police violence, with nearly three times the rate of death as white Americans. The COVID-19 pandemic has laid bare these inequities, with predominantly black counties seeing three times the rate of infection and six times the rate of COVID-related death as white counties. Black and brown Americans are overrepresented among essential workers who have stayed on the job during lockdowns, putting them at higher risk of contracting the disease. Furthermore, they receive worse care than white Americans when they fall ill, with a striking link between long-term air pollution exposure and coronavirus deaths. Systemic racism has had its knee on the neck of the African American community since America began, and it is time to acknowledge and address this reality by pressing for meaningful policy change.
