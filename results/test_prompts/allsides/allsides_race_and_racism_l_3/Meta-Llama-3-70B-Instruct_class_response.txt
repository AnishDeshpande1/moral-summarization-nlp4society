SUMMARY:

Racism is a public health crisis, evident in the police violence that kills black Americans at nearly three times the rate of white Americans. The legacy of slavery and discrimination persists in modern medicine, contributing to health inequities. The recent death of George Floyd, ruled a homicide, highlights the cruel irony of citing "underlying health conditions" as a contributing factor, deflecting blame from the officers responsible. Racism is deadly, causing chronic stress, adverse health outcomes, and immunodeficiency. Black Americans suffer from higher rates of diabetes, hypertension, and heart disease, and are more likely to be obese and have insufficient sleep. The covid-19 pandemic has exposed these inequities, with predominantly black counties seeing higher rates of infection and death. It is our responsibility to acknowledge and address systemic racism, which has had its knee on the neck of the African American community since America began. We must press for meaningful policy change to end this crisis.

END OF SUMMARY.
