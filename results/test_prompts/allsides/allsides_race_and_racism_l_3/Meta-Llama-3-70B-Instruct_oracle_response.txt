SUMMARY: Racism is a public health crisis that kills black Americans at nearly three times the rate of white Americans, perpetuating a legacy of slavery and discrimination in modern medicine and social determinants of health. The recent death of George Floyd, ruled a homicide, is a gut-wrenching reminder of this reality. The attempt to deflect blame by pointing to "underlying health conditions" is cruel and ironic, as racism is a key driver of these conditions. Chronic stress caused by discrimination triggers adverse health outcomes, including high blood pressure, heart disease, and immunodeficiency. The COVID-19 pandemic has laid bare these inequities, with predominantly black counties seeing three times the rate of infection and six times the rate of COVID-related death. It is time to acknowledge that systemic racism has had its knee on the neck of the African American community since America began, and we have a responsibility to address it through meaningful policy change. END OF SUMMARY.
