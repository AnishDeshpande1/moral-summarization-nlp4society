**SUMMARY:**  
The public‑health community sees the surge of police violence against Black Americans as a visceral reminder that **racism** is a **public health crisis**. The **cruel** and **ironic** attempts to cite “underlying health conditions” in George Floyd’s homicide serve to **deflect** **blame** and mask the **deadly** impact of systemic **racism**. Black Americans endure higher rates of diabetes, hypertension, asthma and heart disease—outcomes driven by **structural** **discrimination**, chronic **stress**, and other **adverse** social determinants. These **disparities** are amplified by unequal access to jobs, housing, education, healthy food, and clean air, creating profound **inequities**. The COVID‑19 pandemic exposed these faults: Black‑majority counties face three times more infections and six times more deaths, while **essential** workers and polluted neighborhoods increase exposure and mortality. As James Baldwin warned, facing this reality is the first step toward change. We all bear **responsibility** to acknowledge the **injustice**, connect the dots, and demand **meaningful** policy reform.
