SUMMARY:

The killing of George Floyd is a stark reminder that racism is a public health crisis. The legacy of slavery and discrimination persists in modern medicine and social determinants of health, leading to higher rates of chronic diseases, obesity, and insufficient sleep among black Americans. The role of racism in these underlying conditions cannot be denied, and it is cruel to deflect blame from the officers responsible for Floyd's death by citing "underlying health conditions." Racism itself is hard on a person's health, causing chronic stress that triggers adverse health outcomes. The covid-19 pandemic has laid bare these inequities, with predominantly black counties experiencing higher rates of infection and death. It is time to face the fact that systemic racism has had its knee on the neck of the African American community since America began, and we have a responsibility to acknowledge it and address it. We must press for meaningful policy change to address the many ways that decades of entrenched discrimination are hurting health and cutting lives short.

END OF SUMMARY.
