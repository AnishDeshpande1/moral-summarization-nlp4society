SUMMARY:

Racism is a public health crisis in the US, with black Americans facing disproportionate rates of police violence, poverty, and poor living conditions. These social determinants of health contribute to higher rates of diabetes, hypertension, asthma, and heart disease among black Americans. The author argues that racism itself has a direct impact on physical and mental well-being, causing chronic stress and triggering adverse health outcomes.

The COVID-19 pandemic has laid bare these inequities, with predominantly black counties seeing three times the rate of infection and six times the rate of death as white counties. Black Americans are also more likely to contract the disease due to their disproportionate presence in essential jobs and receive worse care when they fall ill.

The author concludes that systemic racism has been a "knee on the neck" of the African American community since America's inception, and it is time for all of us to acknowledge and address this reality. With growing evidence and tools available, we cannot let this moment pass without pressing for meaningful policy change to dismantle entrenched discrimination.

END OF SUMMARY.
