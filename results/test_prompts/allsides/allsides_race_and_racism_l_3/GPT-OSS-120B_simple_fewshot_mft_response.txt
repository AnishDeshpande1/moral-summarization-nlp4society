SUMMARY:
The article argues that systemic racism is a public‑health crisis that the nation’s authorities have failed to confront, subverting the duty of government and institutions to protect all citizens. It details how police violence, exemplified by George Floyd’s murder, and the disproportionate impact of COVID‑19 on Black communities reveal a pattern of harm inflicted on people of color. The piece links higher rates of diabetes, hypertension, asthma and COVID‑19 deaths among Black Americans to entrenched inequities in housing, employment, education, environmental exposure and access to quality care—clear violations of fairness and evidence of cheating the system of its own promise of equal protection. Chronic stress from discrimination further damages health, underscoring the care/harm dimension. By exposing these injustices and calling for concrete policy reforms, the author urges a subversive challenge to the status quo, demanding that authorities acknowledge and rectify the unfair, harmful legacy of racism in health outcomes.  
END OF SUMMARY.
