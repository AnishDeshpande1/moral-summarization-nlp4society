SUMMARY:

The killing of George Floyd is a stark reminder that racism is a public health crisis. Systemic racism has led to significant health disparities between black and white Americans, with black Americans suffering from higher rates of chronic diseases, obesity, and sleep deprivation. The social determinants of health, such as job opportunities, income, and access to quality education and healthcare, are key drivers of these inequities. Chronic stress caused by discrimination can trigger adverse health outcomes, and evidence suggests that racism endured by black mothers contributes to high maternal and infant mortality rates. The COVID-19 pandemic has exposed these inequities, with predominantly black counties experiencing higher rates of infection and death. It is time to acknowledge the role of systemic racism in perpetuating health disparities and to press for meaningful policy change to address these injustices.

END OF SUMMARY.
