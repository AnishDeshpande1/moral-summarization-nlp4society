SUMMARY:
Systemic racism is a public health crisis that has been laid bare by the COVID-19 pandemic. Black Americans are disproportionately affected, suffering from higher rates of diabetes, hypertension, asthma, and heart disease due to underlying conditions fueled by racism. The legacy of slavery and discrimination persists in modern medicine and social determinants of health. Chronic stress caused by discrimination can trigger adverse health outcomes, including high blood pressure and immunodeficiency. The pandemic has highlighted the inequities in healthcare access, with black and brown Americans receiving worse care than white Americans. Air pollution exposure is a significant contributor to coronavirus deaths, with black Americans 75% more likely to live near polluting facilities. It is time to acknowledge and address systemic racism, which has had its knee on the neck of the African American community since America began. We have the tools to identify solutions and press for meaningful policy change.

END OF SUMMARY.
