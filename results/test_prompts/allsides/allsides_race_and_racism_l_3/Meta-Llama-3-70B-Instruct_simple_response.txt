SUMMARY:

Racism is a public health crisis, evident in the police violence that disproportionately affects Black Americans, as well as in the legacy of slavery and discrimination that persists in modern medicine and social determinants of health. The underlying health conditions of Black Americans, such as diabetes, hypertension, and heart disease, are exacerbated by racism, which is a key driver of health inequities. Chronic stress caused by discrimination triggers adverse health outcomes, and racism itself is hard on a person's health. The COVID-19 pandemic has laid bare these inequities, with Black Americans facing higher rates of infection, death, and worse care. It is time to face the immutable fact that systemic racism has been suffocating the African American community since America began. We have a responsibility to acknowledge and address this crisis, using the tools we have to identify the ways in which discrimination hurts health and cuts lives short.

END OF SUMMARY.
