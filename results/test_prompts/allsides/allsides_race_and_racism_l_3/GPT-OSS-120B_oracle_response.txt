SUMMARY:  
The public‑health community warns that racism is a crisis that kills Black Americans through police violence, a homicide rate nearly three times that of whites, and through the lingering legacy of slavery and discrimination in medicine and social determinants of health. Attempts to offer “underlying health conditions” as an explanation for George Floyd’s death merely deflect blame from the officers whose actions ended his life, while also exposing how racism hardens health outcomes—diabetes, hypertension, asthma, heart disease, immunodeficiency and accelerated aging—through chronic stress. The COVID‑19 pandemic laid bare these inequities: Black counties experience three times the infection rate and six times the death rate, partly because Black workers are essential employees, face poorer care, and live 75 % more often near polluting facilities that worsen asthma and COVID mortality. As James Baldwin wrote, “Not everything that is faced can be changed, but nothing can be changed until it is faced.” It is time to acknowledge and address the knee‑on‑the‑neck racism that has been hurting and cutting lives short, and to press for justice and meaningful policy change.  

END OF SUMMARY.
