SUMMARY:

President Trump's grandiosity is evident in his frequent use of the word "treason" to describe political opponents, including Democrats who disagree with him. He has used the term over two dozen times on Twitter this year alone, often in reference to those who resist his agenda or criticize his behavior. This misuse of the term is disturbing, according to Carlton Larson, a law professor at UC Davis, as it confuses loyalty to the country with loyalty to Trump. Historically, treason was defined as levying war against the US or providing aid and comfort to enemies, but Trump's use of the word suggests that he views disloyalty to himself as equivalent to high treason. This echoes the monarchic practice of deploying the term to silence political opponents. Larson notes that this anachronistic use of "treason" is particularly concerning in the modern era, where it can be used to justify the execution of political enemies.

END OF SUMMARY.
