SUMMARY:

The article discusses how President Trump's grandiosity is evident in his language, particularly in his use of the word "treason". Trump has used the term to describe disloyalty to himself, rather than its actual meaning of betraying one's country. He has tweeted the word over two dozen times this year, targeting political opponents such as Democrats, prosecutors, and even House Speaker Nancy Pelosi. Experts argue that Trump's misuse of the term is disturbing and anachronistic, echoing the way monarchs used it in the past to execute political opponents. The article highlights Trump's self-conception as the indispensable protagonist in the American story, and how his language reveals a lack of understanding of the Constitution and the seriousness of the crime of treason.

END OF SUMMARY.
