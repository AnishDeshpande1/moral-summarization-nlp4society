SUMMARY:

Donald Trump's grandiose behavior is exemplified in his misuse of the term "treason", which he wields as a weapon to attack political opponents. Trump's understanding of treason is woefully inaccurate, confusing loyalty to the country with loyalty to himself, echoing the monarchic idea that treason is betrayal of the king. He has used the term two dozen times on Twitter this year alone, labeling Democrats who opposed his border measures, prosecutors working under Special Counsel Robert Mueller, and Democratic Representative Adam Schiff as "treasonous". This anachronistic use of the term is disturbing, as it has historically been used to execute political opponents. Trump's actions subvert the authority of the Constitution, cheat the public of a fair understanding of the term, and betray the loyalty owed to the country. His behavior is a stark reminder of the dangers of unchecked power and the importance of upholding the rule of law.

END OF SUMMARY.
