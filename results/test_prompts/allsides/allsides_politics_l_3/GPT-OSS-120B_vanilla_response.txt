SUMMARY: The article examines former President Donald Trump’s habit of inflating his personal importance and misusing the term “treason.” Trump frequently uses first‑person language to portray himself as the central hero of the nation and has repeatedly labeled political opponents, media critics, and officials as “treasonous,” despite the Constitution’s narrow definition of treason as levying war against the United States or aiding its enemies. His misuse began on Twitter in 2018 and escalated to dozens of instances in 2023, targeting Democrats, Special Counsel investigators, Rep. Adam Schiff, and Speaker Nancy Pelosi. Legal scholar Carlton Larson notes that Trump conflates loyalty to the president with loyalty to the country, echoing monarchical abuses of the term and distorting its legal meaning. The article warns that such casual invocation of treason is historically alarming and unprecedented among U.S. presidents. END OF SUMMARY.
