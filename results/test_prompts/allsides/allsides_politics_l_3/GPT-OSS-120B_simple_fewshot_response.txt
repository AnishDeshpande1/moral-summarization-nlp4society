SUMMARY:
The article criticizes Donald Trump’s habit of inflating his own importance and weaponizing the term “treason” to attack political opponents. By repeatedly using first‑person language (“I’m the one that did the capturing”) and grandiose claims (“the strongest economy ever”), Trump frames himself as the essential hero of America. More seriously, he distorts the constitutional definition of treason—levying war against the United States or aiding its enemies—by labeling dissenters, investigators, and Democratic leaders as “traitors.” Since 2018 he has invoked the word dozens of times on Twitter, calling border‑policy opponents “treasonous,” accusing Mueller’s team of a “Treasonous Hoax,” and demanding that Rep. Adam Schiff and Speaker Nancy Pelosi be investigated for “Fraud & Treason.” Legal scholars note that this misuse conflates loyalty to the president with loyalty to the nation, echoing monarchical tactics and undermining the serious, narrowly‑defined crime of treason. The piece warns that such casual, inaccurate deployment of the term is disturbing and unprecedented for a U.S. president. 
END OF SUMMARY.
