SUMMARY:

US President Trump's grandiosity is evident in his frequent use of the word "treason" on Twitter, often misusing it to describe disloyalty to himself or opposition to his policies. He has used the term over two dozen times this year alone, labeling Democrats who opposed his border measures as "TREASONOUS" and accusing prosecutors working under Special Counsel Robert Mueller of perpetrating a "Treasonous Hoax!" Trump's use of the word is anachronistic and echoes the way monarchs deployed it in centuries past. Carlton Larson, a professor at UC Davis School of Law, notes that Trump has misused the term to confuse loyalty to the country with loyalty to himself, which is not the original intention of the framers of the Constitution. This misuse of the word raises concerns about the potential for using treason as a way to silence political opponents.

END OF SUMMARY.
