SUMMARY: The article condemns Donald Trump’s self‑aggrandizing rhetoric and, most importantly, his reckless abuse of the word “treason.” By constantly casting himself as the nation’s savior (“I’m the one that did the capturing,” “the strongest economy ever”) and then redefining treason to mean disloyalty to him, Trump blurs the line between loyalty to the country and loyalty to a single leader—a distortion that the framers of the Constitution deliberately guarded against. His Twitter attacks label political opponents, prosecutors, and congressional investigators as “treasonous,” echoing monarchical tactics used to eliminate dissent. Legal scholar Carlton Larson warns that this misuse is historically inaccurate, morally troubling, and reminiscent of regimes that weaponize treason to crush opposition. The piece frames Trump’s behavior as a dangerous moral failing: inflating his own importance while corrupting a fundamental legal concept, thereby threatening democratic norms and the rule of law. END OF SUMMARY.
