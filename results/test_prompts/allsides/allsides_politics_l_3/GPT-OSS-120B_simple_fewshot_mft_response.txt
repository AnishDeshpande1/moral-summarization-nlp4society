SUMMARY:
The article critiques Donald Trump’s self‑aggrandizing rhetoric as a blatant subversion of legitimate authority and a distortion of fairness. By repeatedly labeling political opponents—Democrats, prosecutors, and congressional leaders—as “traitors” or “treasonous,” Trump conflates loyalty to himself with loyalty to the nation, turning a constitutional crime into a partisan weapon. This misuse of the term treason inflates his own authority while delegitimizing dissent, effectively cheating the public of an honest political discourse. Experts note that Trump’s language revives the old‑world notion of treason as betrayal of a monarch, betraying the democratic principle that loyalty should be owed to the country, not a single leader. The piece underscores how such rhetoric undermines legitimate governmental authority, weaponizes accusations of betrayal, and creates an unfair playing field where dissent is criminalized rather than debated.
