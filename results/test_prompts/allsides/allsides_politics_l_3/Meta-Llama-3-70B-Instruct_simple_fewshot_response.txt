SUMMARY:

Donald Trump's grandiosity is evident in his language, where he casts himself as the central figure in the American story. He has misused the term "treason" to describe disloyalty to him or his policies, rather than its constitutional definition as a betrayal of the country. Trump has used the term over two dozen times on Twitter this year alone, targeting political opponents such as Democrats who opposed his border measures, prosecutors working under Special Counsel Robert Mueller, and Democratic Representative Adam Schiff, who is leading the impeachment probe. Trump's use of the term is anachronistic and disturbing, confusing loyalty to the country with loyalty to himself. Experts argue that Trump's misuse of the term is inaccurate and woefully so, and that it has the potential to be used to execute political opponents, as has been the case in other countries.

END OF SUMMARY.
