SUMMARY:
Donald Trump's grandiosity has been on full display, as he frequently uses the word treason to describe his opponents' actions. He has misused the term to imply that disloyalty to him or his policies constitutes a betrayal of the country. In contrast to the framers' definition of treason in Article III of the Constitution, which involves levying war against the United States or giving aid and comfort to enemies, Trump defines it as antipathy for himself or his behavior. This anachronistic use of the term echoes the way monarchs deployed it in centuries past, confusing loyalty to the country with loyalty to the president. Trump's misuse of the word has raised concerns that he is attempting to silence criticism and intimidate his opponents, echoing authoritarian tactics used in other countries.
