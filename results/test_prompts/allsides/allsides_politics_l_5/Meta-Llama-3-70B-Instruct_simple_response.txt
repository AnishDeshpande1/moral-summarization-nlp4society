SUMMARY:

Alabama Republican Senate candidate Roy Moore is denying allegations of pursuing sexual relationships with teenagers, including a 14-year-old, when he was in his 30s. He claims the Washington Post's report is a "desperate attempt to stop my political campaign" and threatens to sue the publication. However, it's common for public figures to threaten legal action but fail to follow through. The Post's report is based on interviews with over 30 people, including four women who alleged Moore pursued them when they were between 16 and 18 years old. One woman claimed Moore had sexual contact with her when she was 14, which could have been a sexual offense due to her age. Moore has denied the allegations, calling them "completely false" and "fake news." He also claimed to have the "highest regard for the protection of young children." Despite his denials, several Republicans have withdrawn their endorsements of Moore following the report.

END OF SUMMARY.
