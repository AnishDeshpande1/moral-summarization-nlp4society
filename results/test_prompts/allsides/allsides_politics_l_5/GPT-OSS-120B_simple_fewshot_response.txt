SUMMARY:
Alabama Senate hopeful Roy Moore vowed to sue the Washington Post after it published a detailed investigation alleging he pursued sexual relationships with teenagers, including a 14‑year‑old when he was in his 30s. Moore dismissed the claims as “completely false” and “fake news,” accusing the paper of a politically motivated attack aimed at destroying his campaign. The Post’s report, based on more than 30 interviews, described how the 14‑year‑old was taken to Moore’s home, partially undressed and touched in ways that could constitute sexual offenses, while three other women said Moore pursued them as minors aged 16‑18. Although Alabama’s consent age is 16 and statutes of limitations have expired, the allegations raise serious moral questions about Moore’s conduct and his professed “highest regard for the protection of young children.” The story prompted several Republicans, including the National Republican Senatorial Committee, to withdraw support. Moore’s threats of legal action echo past instances—such as Donald Trump’s unfiled lawsuit against the New York Times and Harvey Weinstein’s aborted suit—highlighting a pattern of powerful figures attempting to silence damaging reporting rather than address the underlying accusations.

END OF SUMMARY.
