SUMMARY: In the final presidential debate, GOP nominee Donald Trump refused to commit to accepting election results, saying he would “keep you in suspense.” The article argues this reflects a longstanding pattern of Trump rejecting democratic norms and constitutional limits. It cites his past refusals to support party nominees, claims of election fraud, encouragement of violence, and disdain for free speech and the Constitution. The piece also highlights Trump’s admiration for authoritarian leaders like Vladimir Putin, Saddam Hussein, and Bashar al‑Assad, and his proposals to implement unconstitutional policies such as banning Muslims, seizing foreign assets, and targeting the media. The author concludes that Trump’s behavior and rhetoric indicate he seeks unchecked, authoritarian power rather than to uphold democratic republican principles. END OF SUMMARY.
