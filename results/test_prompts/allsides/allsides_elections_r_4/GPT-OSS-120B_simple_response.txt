SUMMARY: In the final presidential debate, GOP nominee Donald Trump refused to pledge acceptance of election results, promising only to “keep you in suspense,” a stance the author calls horrifying and unsurprising. The piece argues that Trump has consistently rejected democratic norms and the legitimacy of the electoral process, from his 2012 tweet dismissing Obama’s victory as a “sham” to his 2016 primary refusal to support the eventual Republican nominee. Throughout his campaign, Trump has disparaged constitutional principles, encouraged violence against protesters, spread baseless claims of voter fraud, and praised authoritarian dictators such as Putin, Saddam Hussein, Gaddafi, and Assad. He has floated unconstitutional policies—banning Muslims, seizing foreign assets, targeting the press—and repeatedly claimed he alone can solve America’s problems, reflecting a vanity‑driven, authoritarian outlook. The author concludes that Trump is not seeking to lead a democratic republic but to become an unchecked, unaccountable ruler who despises the very constitutional limits that protect democracy. END OF SUMMARY.
