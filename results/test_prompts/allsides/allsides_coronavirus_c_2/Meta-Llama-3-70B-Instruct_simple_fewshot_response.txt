SUMMARY:

The Trump administration's handling of immigrant detention and deportation has turned ICE into a domestic and global spreader of the coronavirus. Despite lockdowns and measures to prevent the spread of the virus, ICE has continued to detain people, move them from state to state, and deport them, often without proper testing or precautions. Over 40,000 immigrants have been deported since March, with at least 3,000 testing positive for the virus in detention centers. Many deportees have arrived in their home countries sick with COVID-19, with 11 governments confirming cases. The administration's pressure on countries to accept deportees has led to cooperation, with some countries receiving humanitarian aid in exchange. The moral failure lies in the prioritization of deportation over public health, putting both immigrants and the general public at risk. The government's actions have turned a global health crisis into a humanitarian crisis.

END OF SUMMARY.
