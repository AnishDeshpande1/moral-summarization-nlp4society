SUMMARY:  
The New York Times‑Marshall Project investigation exposes a moral crisis in U.S. immigration enforcement during the COVID‑19 pandemic. Undocumented immigrant Admild, sick with coronavirus, was denied adequate medical care by ICE and forced onto a deportation flight back to Haiti, exemplifying a pattern where detainees are left vulnerable while the agency continues to detain, transfer, and deport people despite known health risks. Interviews with over 30 detainees reveal overcrowded, unsanitary facilities lacking protective gear, turning ICE into a “domestic and global spreader” of the virus. At least 3,000 detainees have tested positive, yet testing remains limited and sick individuals are still moved on more than 750 internal flights and over 200 deportation flights. Countries such as El Salvador and Honduras have accepted thousands of deportees, some ill, after Trump’s promises of aid, further spreading COVID‑19 abroad. ICE maintains it follows CDC guidelines, but the evidence shows systemic neglect and a willingness to prioritize deportations over human health, raising profound ethical questions about the treatment of vulnerable migrants.  

END OF SUMMARY.
