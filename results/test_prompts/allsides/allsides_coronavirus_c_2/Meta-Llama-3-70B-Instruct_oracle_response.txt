SUMMARY:

The US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) has been detaining and deporting immigrants despite the COVID-19 pandemic, putting them and others at risk of infection. Undocumented immigrants like Admild, who tested positive for the virus while in detention, were given only Tylenol and sent back to their home countries, spreading the virus globally. ICE has continued to detain people in cramped and unsanitary conditions, making social distancing impossible, and has moved thousands of detainees across the country, including those showing symptoms. At least 3,000 detainees have tested positive for the virus, and ICE has confirmed that deportees have returned home with COVID-19. The Trump administration has pressured countries to accept these sick deportees, promising humanitarian aid. ICE claims to have taken precautions, but its actions have turned it into a domestic and global spreader of the virus. Deportation flights continue, putting lives at risk.

END OF SUMMARY.
