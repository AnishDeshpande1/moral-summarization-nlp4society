SUMMARY:

Despite the global efforts to prevent the spread of COVID-19, Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) has continued to detain people, move them from state to state, and deport them, often without proper testing or protective measures in place. An investigation by The New York Times revealed that ICE's actions have contributed to the domestic and global spread of the virus. Detainees described cramped and unsanitary conditions, with social distancing being impossible and protective gear scarce. At least four deportees tested positive for the virus shortly after arriving at their destination countries. ICE has confirmed over 3,000 coronavirus-positive detainees in its detention centers, despite limited testing. The Trump administration's pressure on other countries to accept deportees, often without proper health measures, has also contributed to the spread of the virus. Many governments have accepted deportees with COVID-19, and at least 11 countries have confirmed that deportees returned home with the virus.

END OF SUMMARY.
