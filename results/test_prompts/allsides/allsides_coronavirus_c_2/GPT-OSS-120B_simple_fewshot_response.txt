SUMMARY:
The New York Times and The Marshall Project expose how ICE’s continued detention, transfer, and deportation of immigrants during the COVID‑19 pandemic has turned the agency into a conduit for the virus both inside the United States and abroad. Undocumented detainees like Admild, who was sick with COVID‑19 when forced onto a deportation plane, received only minimal medical care, while cramped, unsanitary facilities made social distancing impossible. At least 3,000 detainees have tested positive, yet testing remains scarce and sick people are still moved between prisons and shipped to other countries. Over 750 domestic ICE flights and more than 200 deportation flights have carried potentially infected migrants, spreading the disease to nations that, under pressure from the Trump administration, accepted thousands of ill deportees in exchange for promised aid. ICE claims it follows CDC guidelines, but the evidence shows a reckless disregard for detainees’ health and public safety. The moral of the story is clear: policies that prioritize enforcement over humane treatment and ignore basic health safeguards endanger vulnerable people and fuel a global health crisis, demanding accountability and compassionate reform.  
END OF SUMMARY.
