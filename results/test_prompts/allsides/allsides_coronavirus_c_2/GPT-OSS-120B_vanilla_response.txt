SUMMARY: An investigation by The New York Times and The Marshall Project found that ICE’s detention and deportation practices have helped spread COVID‑19. Undocumented immigrant Admild, who tested positive for coronavirus in an ICE detention center in Louisiana, was sent to a plane despite symptoms and later deported to Haiti. Interviews with over 30 detainees described overcrowded, unsanitary facilities lacking protective gear, making social distancing impossible. ICE has confirmed at least 3,000 positive cases among detainees, though testing remains limited. Researchers tracked more than 750 domestic ICE flights and over 200 deportation flights from March to June, many carrying sick migrants. Some deportees from India, Haiti, Guatemala and El Salvador tested positive after arrival. Pressure from the Trump administration led countries such as El Salvador, Honduras and others to accept thousands of deportees, with promises of aid, including ventilators. Eleven nations have confirmed that returned migrants were infected. ICE claims it follows CDC guidelines but admits testing is only a sample. Deportations continue despite the pandemic. END OF SUMMARY.
