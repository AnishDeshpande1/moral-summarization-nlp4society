SUMMARY:  
The New York Times and The Marshall Project expose how ICE has **continued** to **detain**, **move**, and **deport** immigrants despite a raging pandemic. In May, undocumented Haitian Admild, who was **sick** and had **tested** positive for COVID‑19 while in an ICE facility, told an official at the airport and was only **given** Tylenol before being sent **back** on a deportation **flight**. Interviews with more than 30 detainees describe **cramped**, **unsanitary** centers where social‑distancing was **impossible** and protective gear was scarce, turning ICE into a **spreader** of the virus. At least four deportees from India, Haiti, Guatemala and El Salvador **tested** positive after arrival, and ICE has **confirmed** over 3,000 **positive** detainees, though **testing** remains **limited**. The investigation tracked over 750 domestic ICE **flights** and 200 **deportation** flights that **included** sick migrants, some **showing** symptoms and **panicking** about **dying** in detention. Under **pressure** from the Trump administration, countries such as El Salvador and Honduras **took** thousands of deportees, promising aid while their governments reported **returned** cases of COVID‑19. ICE claims it follows CDC **guidelines**, yet **sampling** remains scarce and **deporting** and **moving** detainees **continues**.  

END OF SUMMARY.
