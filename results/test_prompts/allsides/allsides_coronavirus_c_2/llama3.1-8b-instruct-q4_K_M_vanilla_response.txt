SUMMARY:

Despite the COVID-19 pandemic, Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) has continued to detain and deport immigrants, putting them at risk of contracting the virus. An investigation by The New York Times found that ICE's detention centers are overcrowded and unsanitary, making social distancing impossible. Testing for the virus has been limited, with only a sampling of detainees being tested before deportation.

At least 3,000 coronavirus-positive detainees have been confirmed in ICE facilities, and some deportees have reported testing positive shortly after arrival at their destination countries. The Trump administration's pressure on countries to accept deportees has led many governments to cooperate, despite the risks. El Salvador and Honduras have accepted over 6,000 deportees since March.

ICE claims it follows CDC guidelines, but its testing efforts are limited. Deportation flights continue, with some countries confirming that deportees returned home with COVID-19. Immigrant detainees describe "unsafe conditions" and a lack of protective gear in detention centers.

END OF SUMMARY.
