SUMMARY:
The COVID-19 pandemic has exposed the harsh realities of Immigration and Customs Enforcement's (ICE) detention centers, where immigrants are held in unsanitary conditions with inadequate protective gear and limited testing. At least 3,000 detainees have tested positive for the virus, and some were deported to other countries despite showing symptoms. The Trump administration's pressure on countries to accept deportees has led to the spread of the virus globally. An investigation by The New York Times found that ICE continued to detain people and move them between facilities, turning itself into a domestic and global spreader of the virus. The agency's actions have put vulnerable individuals at risk of serious illness or even death. As the world grapples with the pandemic, it is unacceptable for governments to prioritize deportation over public health, especially when they can take measures to protect both immigrants and their own citizens.

END OF SUMMARY.
