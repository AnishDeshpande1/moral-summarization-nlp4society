SUMMARY:

The COVID-19 pandemic has exposed the unsafe conditions in Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) detention facilities, where social distancing is nearly impossible and protective gear is scarce. Despite this, ICE has continued to detain people, move them from state to state, and deport them, often without adequate testing or precautions. At least 3,000 coronavirus-positive detainees have been confirmed in ICE's detention centers, with many more likely infected. The agency's practices have turned it into a domestic and global spreader of the virus, putting not only detainees but also communities around the world at risk.

The Trump administration has pressured countries to accept deportees, often without adequate safeguards or resources. At least 11 countries have confirmed that deportees returned home with COVID-19. This raises serious questions about ICE's accountability and the moral implications of its actions. The agency's prioritization of deportation over public health and safety is a stark example of the harm caused by subverting authority and ignoring the well-being of vulnerable populations.

END OF SUMMARY.
