SUMMARY:

The US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) has been deporting immigrants, including those with COVID-19, to their home countries, contributing to the global spread of the virus. Despite lockdowns and measures to prevent the spread of the coronavirus, ICE has continued to detain people, move them from state to state, and deport them, often without proper testing or precautions. An investigation revealed that ICE detention centers have unsafe conditions, making social distancing impossible, and that deportees have tested positive for the virus shortly after arriving in their home countries. The Trump administration has pressured countries to accept these deportees, with promises of humanitarian aid. This morally reprehensible practice has put countless lives at risk, both in the US and abroad. ICE's actions are a stark example of the administration's disregard for human life and well-being.

END OF SUMMARY.
