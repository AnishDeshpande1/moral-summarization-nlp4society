SUMMARY:

A New York Times investigation reveals that Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) has become a domestic and global spreader of the coronavirus. Despite lockdowns and measures to prevent the spread of the virus, ICE has continued to detain, move, and deport people, often without proper testing or precautions. Over 30 immigrant detainees described cramped and unsanitary detention centers where social distancing was impossible and protective gear was scarce. At least 3,000 detainees have tested positive for the virus, and ICE has confirmed that deportees have returned to their home countries with Covid-19. The Trump administration has pressured countries to accept deportees, with some countries receiving humanitarian aid in exchange. ICE claims to have taken precautions, but its actions have contributed to the spread of the virus both domestically and globally.

END OF SUMMARY.
