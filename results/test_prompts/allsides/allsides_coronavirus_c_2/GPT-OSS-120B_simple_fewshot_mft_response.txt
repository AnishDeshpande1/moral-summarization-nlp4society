SUMMARY:
The article exposes how ICE’s exercise of governmental authority has subverted basic human care, turning detention centers and deportation flights into vectors for COVID‑19. Undocumented immigrant Admild, sick with coronavirus, was denied medical treatment and forced onto a deportation plane, exemplifying ICE’s neglect of its duty to protect vulnerable people. Interviews with dozens of detainees describe overcrowded, unsanitary facilities where social distancing and protective gear were virtually absent—conditions the agency claims meet CDC guidelines but that in practice spread disease. ICE’s internal transfers and more than 750 domestic flights, as well as over 200 deportation flights, moved sick detainees across states and to foreign countries, even as the Trump administration pressured other nations to accept them in exchange for aid. At least four deportees arrived abroad COVID‑positive, and 11 governments have confirmed infected returnees. ICE acknowledges testing only a fraction of detainees, yet continues deportations despite the clear health risk. The piece argues that the authority exercised by ICE and the Trump administration subverts the moral imperative to care for the sick, endangering both migrants and the broader public.  
END OF SUMMARY.
