SUMMARY: The Senate will vote Thursday on two bills aimed at ending the 32‑day government shutdown. The first bill, backed by Senate leaders Mitch McConnell and Chuck Schumer, allocates $5.7 billion for a border wall and includes immigration concessions offered by President Trump. If it fails to reach the 60‑vote filibuster threshold, senators will move to a short‑term funding measure to keep the government open until Feb. 8, which also requires 60 votes and faces a possible Trump veto. Senate Republican Lindsey Graham will propose a separate plan urging Trump to sign a full‑government reopening if House Speaker Nancy Pelosi backs a bipartisan border‑related bill. Pelosi and House Democrats are pushing a spending package that funds immigration judges and port‑of‑entry construction but excludes wall funding, a proposal likely to stall in the GOP‑controlled Senate. Centrist House members have drafted a letter suggesting a wall vote next month in exchange for an immediate government reopening. Trump continues to demand wall funding, framing it as essential to national security, while immigration groups criticize the Senate bill’s restrictive asylum provisions as a “Trojan Horse.” The House’s own funding bill would reopen the government through September without wall money. END OF SUMMARY.
