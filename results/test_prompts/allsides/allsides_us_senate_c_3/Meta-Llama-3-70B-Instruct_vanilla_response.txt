SUMMARY:

The US Senate will vote on two bills to fund the federal government, aiming to end the longest government shutdown in history. The first bill would provide $5.7 billion for a border wall and make concessions to Democrats, while the second bill is a short-term measure to reopen the government until February 8. Both bills require 60 votes to pass, and President Trump has threatened to veto the second bill. Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell urged Democrats to put the country ahead of politics and vote to end the shutdown. Meanwhile, House Democrats are pushing their own spending bill with $1 billion for construction at ports of entry and hiring new immigration judges, but no money for a border wall. Centrist Democrats are also seeking a way out of the shutdown, proposing a vote on a wall in exchange for reopening the government.

END OF SUMMARY.
