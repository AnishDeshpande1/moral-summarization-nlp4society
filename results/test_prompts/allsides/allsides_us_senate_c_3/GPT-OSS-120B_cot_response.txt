**SUMMARY:**  
The Senate will vote Thursday on two bills aimed at ending the longest government shutdown in history. Senate leaders McConnell and Schumer say a bipartisan “*common‑sense*” deal—$5.7 billion for a border wall plus concessions—could finally close the “*politics*” that have kept the nation in “*chaos*.” If the wall bill fails to overcome a filibuster, a short‑term funding measure will be considered, though President Trump has threatened a *veto*. Republicans argue that Democrats are using the shutdown as “*hostage*” against immigration reforms, while Democrats accuse the GOP of “*complicity*” in “*unacceptable*” border policies that increase *suffering* at the border. House Democrats propose a separate spending plan that funds immigration judges and ports of entry but excludes wall money, a proposal likely to die in the GOP‑led Senate. Centrist Democrats are drafting a letter urging a wall vote only after the government reopens, hoping to end the standoff without further *threat* to the American people.
