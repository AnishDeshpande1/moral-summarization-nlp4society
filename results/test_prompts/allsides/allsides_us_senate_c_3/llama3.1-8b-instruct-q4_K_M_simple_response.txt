SUMMARY:
The US Senate will vote on two bills to end the longest government shutdown in history, which has lasted for 32 days. The first bill provides $5.7 billion for a border wall and other concessions offered by President Trump over the weekend. However, Democrats are opposed to this measure, calling it "unacceptable" and arguing that it would increase chaos and suffering at the border. If the first bill fails, senators will vote on a short-term measure that would reopen the government until February 8. House Democrats have also proposed their own spending bill, which includes funding for construction at ports of entry and hiring new immigration judges, but does not include money for a border wall.

President Trump has threatened to veto any measure that does not include border wall funding, while Senate Republicans are pushing for bipartisan support. The shutdown has caused widespread suffering among federal employees and contractors, and both sides are under pressure to find a solution. The outcome of the vote is uncertain, but it marks a crucial moment in the standoff between Trump and Congress.
