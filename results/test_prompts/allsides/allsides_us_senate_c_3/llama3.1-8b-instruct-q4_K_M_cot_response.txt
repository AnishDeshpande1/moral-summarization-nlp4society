**SUMMARY:**
The Senate will vote on two bills to end the longest government shutdown in history, which has lasted for 32 days. The first bill provides $5.7 billion for a border wall and other concessions that President Trump offered to Democrats over the weekend. If it fails, senators will then vote on a short-term measure that would reopen the government until Feb. 8. However, Trump has threatened to veto this bill, calling it "unacceptable" and stating that he will not be "held hostage" by Democrats. Meanwhile, House Democrats are pushing their own spending bill that offers roughly $1 billion for construction at ports of entry and hiring new immigration judges, but no money for a border wall, which they see as a "bargaining chip". The shutdown has caused chaos and suffering at the border, with many immigrants being denied asylum. END OF SUMMARY.
