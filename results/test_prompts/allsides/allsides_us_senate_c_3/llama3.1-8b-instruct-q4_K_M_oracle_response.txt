SUMMARY: 

The US Senate is set to vote on two bills to fund the federal government, aiming to end the longest government shutdown in history. Senate leaders have announced that if one bill fails to pass, they will consider a short-term measure to reopen the government until February 8. However, President Trump has threatened to veto this proposal, and House Democrats are pushing their own spending bill without funding for his border wall. The situation is seen as a standoff between Trump's demands for a border wall and Democratic opposition. Some centrist Democrats are trying to find a way out of the shutdown by offering a vote on a border wall next month if the government is reopened. Meanwhile, immigration groups have criticized a Senate bill that includes $5.7 billion for the wall, calling it a "Trojan Horse" that would harm asylum seekers. The situation has been marked by chaos and suffering at the border, with Trump accusing Democrats of playing politics.

END OF SUMMARY.
