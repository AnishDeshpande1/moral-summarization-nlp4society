SUMMARY:

The US Senate will vote on two bills to fund the federal government, marking the first movement in weeks towards ending the longest government shutdown in history. The bills include a proposal from President Trump for $5.7 billion in border wall funding and a short-term measure that would reopen the government until Feb 8. However, both bills require 60 votes to pass, which is uncertain given bipartisan opposition. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi has rejected Trump's demand for border wall funding, while centrist Democrats are pushing for a compromise proposal to offer funds for ports of entry and immigration judges in exchange for opening the government. Trump has threatened to veto any short-term measure that does not include border wall funding. Immigration groups have criticized the Senate bill as being too restrictive on asylum seekers.

END OF SUMMARY.
