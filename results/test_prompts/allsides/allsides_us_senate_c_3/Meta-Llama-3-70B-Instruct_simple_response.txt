SUMMARY:

The Senate is set to vote on two bills to end the longest government shutdown in history, with the first bill providing $5.7 billion for a border wall and concessions offered by President Trump to Democrats. If that bill fails, senators will vote on a short-term measure to reopen the government until February 8. However, Trump has threatened to veto the latter bill. Democrats are pushing their own spending bill with no money for a border wall, while centrist Democrats are seeking a compromise to end the shutdown. Trump accused Democrats of playing politics, insisting on border wall funding, while Democrats argue that the president is holding the American people hostage. The proposed bill has drawn criticism from immigration groups, who label it a "Trojan Horse" that would destroy the concept of asylum in the US.

END OF SUMMARY.
