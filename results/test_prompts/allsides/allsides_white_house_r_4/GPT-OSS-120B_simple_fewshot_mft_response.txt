SUMMARY:
The article condemns President Obama’s foreign‑policy “doctrine” as a pattern of subversion and betrayal of both American authority and global moral responsibilities. By offering only fleeting, symbolic outrage over crises—from Egypt and Iran to Ukraine, Nigeria, Syria and now Iraq—the author argues Obama harms innocent victims while pretending to care, thereby violating the Care/Harm foundation. His selective, short‑lived interventions are portrayed as cheating the principle of Fairness, delivering promises of protection without delivering real, sustained aid. The piece accuses Obama of disloyalty to allies, abandoning them when they need support, and of undermining the authority of democratic institutions by favoring appearance over action. Ultimately, the author frames the “Obama Doctrine” as a cynical strategy of popularity‑seeking posturing that sacrifices genuine care, fairness, loyalty and legitimate authority, leaving massive civilian casualties and eroding trust in U.S. leadership. END OF SUMMARY.
