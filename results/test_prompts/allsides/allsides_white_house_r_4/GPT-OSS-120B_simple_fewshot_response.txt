SUMMARY:
The article argues that the “Obama Doctrine” is little more than theatrical concern for human suffering, used to boost the president’s image while avoiding real, sustained action. It claims Obama’s foreign policy is a patchwork of brief interventions—Libya, limited airstrikes against ISIL, symbolic outrage over crises in Egypt, Iran, Ukraine, Nigeria, Syria, and Iraq—followed by swift disengagement that leaves civilians to die. The author portrays Obama as an emotional manipulator who tailors his public anger to win media praise, yet lacks the willingness to lead decisively or commit to long‑term solutions. This emphasis on appearances, the piece warns, is dangerous: popularity‑driven posturing results in ineffective, short‑term force and massive civilian casualties, ultimately breeding both unpopularity and humanitarian disaster.

END OF SUMMARY.
