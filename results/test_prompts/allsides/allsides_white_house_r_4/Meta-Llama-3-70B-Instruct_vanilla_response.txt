SUMMARY:

The article argues that the Obama Doctrine is a facade, where President Obama pretends to care about suffering innocents only when it's convenient and headlines-worthy. In reality, he doesn't truly care and is unwilling to take lasting action to effect change. The author cites examples of Obama's short-term and ineffective deployments of power in various crises, including Libya, Egypt, Iran, Ukraine, Nigeria, and Syria. In each case, Obama expresses outrage and determination, but ultimately does little to nothing to address the issues. The article concludes that Obama's focus is on appearances rather than realities, and that his "emotional manipulation" and "posturing" ultimately lead to massive casualties and unpopularity.
