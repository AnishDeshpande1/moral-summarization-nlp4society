SUMMARY:

The Obama Doctrine is a facade, where President Obama pretends to care about the suffering of innocents when it hits the headlines, but ignores it the rest of the time. He is a master emotional manipulator, capable of achieving effective posturing when it comes to human rights and internationalism, but lacks true commitment to taking lasting action that effects change. His foreign policy is marked by short-term and ineffective deployments of power, leaving innocents to die. This is evident in his handling of crises in Egypt, Iran, Ukraine, Nigeria, and Syria, where he expressed outrage but took no concrete action. In Iraq, he will drop a few bombs, bluster, and then move on, leaving erstwhile allies to die. The Obama Doctrine is rooted in popularity rather than a genuine desire to confront evil and lead. The end result is massive casualties and unpopularity, as world events require prolonged and sustained action rather than posturing.

END OF SUMMARY.
