SUMMARY:  
The article argues that the “Obama Doctrine” is not a coherent strategy but a moral failing: Obama pretends to care about humanitarian crises only when they generate headlines, then abandons them. The author claims Obama’s foreign policy is driven by image‑craft and short‑term posturing rather than genuine commitment, citing examples such as Libya, Egypt, Iran, Ukraine, Nigeria, Syria, and the recent limited airstrikes against ISIS. Each case is portrayed as an instance where Obama expressed outrage or optimism but failed to take effective, sustained action, leading to continued suffering and death. The piece concludes that Obama’s reliance on emotional performance and superficial displays of force makes him an “emotional pro and foreign‑policy dilettante,” a dangerous combination that ultimately results in massive casualties and, paradoxically, unpopularity. The moral judgment is clear: caring for appearances over realities is ethically indefensible.  
END OF SUMMARY.
