**SUMMARY:**
A federal judge ruled that New York Governor Andrew Cuomo and Mayor Bill de Blasio violated **fundamental rights** by imposing **arbitrary**, low‑occupancy limits on **religious** gatherings while giving **preferential** leeway to mass protests after George Floyd’s death. Judge Gary L. Sharpe called the officials’ “Social Distancing Protocol” a **sham** and highlighted the **hypocrisy** of allowing thousands of protesters to assemble freely yet restricting churches to 25 % capacity. The ruling described this **double standard** as **“preferential treatment”** and warned it resembled an **absolute monarchy** that grants **freedom** to some but not to others. By blocking the limits, the court protected **freedom** of worship and rejected the **compelling justification** the state offered for the unequal restrictions. The decision is hailed by the Thomas More Society as a step against the emerging trend of **absolute monarchy** under the guise of public health. END OF SUMMARY.
