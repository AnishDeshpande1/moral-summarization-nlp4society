SUMMARY:
A federal judge issued a preliminary injunction against New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo (D) and NYC Mayor Bill de Blasio (D), prohibiting them from imposing arbitrary limits on church gatherings amid the COVID-19 pandemic. The ruling notes that the officials' decision to support mass protests of racial injustice while limiting religious gatherings is hypocritical, amounting to "preferential treatment" for protesters. The judge ruled that outdoor gatherings cannot be limited and indoor gatherings must follow the same guidelines as nonessential businesses under New York's reopening plan. The Thomas More Society, a conservative national law firm, represented the group of Catholic priests and Jewish congregants who brought the complaint. This decision is seen as an important step in addressing the double standard exercised by Cuomo and de Blasio, who allowed mass protests while restricting other forms of public assembly.

END OF SUMMARY.
