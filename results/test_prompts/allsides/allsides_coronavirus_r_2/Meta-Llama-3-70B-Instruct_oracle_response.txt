SUMMARY:

A federal judge has ruled in favor of religious freedom advocates, prohibiting New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo and New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio from limiting religious gatherings while supporting mass protests of racial injustice. The judge's decision is a win against the "curtailment of fundamental rights without compelling justification." The ruling highlights the hypocrisy of the officials, who enforced limitations on other forms of public assembly while encouraging mass protests, effectively giving "preferential treatment" to the protesters. The judge noted that the officials could have discouraged protests in the name of public health, but instead chose to "exercise discretion" and "sent a clear message" that mass protests are deserving of special treatment. The decision is an important step towards "inhibiting the suddenly emerging trend of exercising absolute monarchy on pretext of public health." It exposes the double standard, where "freedom for me, but not for thee" is the reality.

END OF SUMMARY.
