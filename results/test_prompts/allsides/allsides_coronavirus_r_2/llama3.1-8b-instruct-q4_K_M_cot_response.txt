A federal judge has ruled that New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo and Mayor Bill de Blasio's restrictions on religious gatherings amid the coronavirus pandemic are unjust, highlighting a troubling double standard in their treatment of mass protests versus church gatherings. The preliminary injunction prohibits Cuomo and de Blasio from enforcing arbitrary limits on church gatherings, while noting that they had shown hypocrisy by allowing thousands to protest in the streets without imposing similar limitations. Judge Gary L. Sharpe argued that the officials' actions demonstrated a "preferential treatment" for protesters, rather than exercising freedom equally for all. The ruling is seen as an important step towards preventing absolute monarchy-like behavior under the pretext of public health. The Thomas More Society, representing Catholic priests and Jewish congregants, welcomed the decision, calling it a victory for religious freedom advocates.
