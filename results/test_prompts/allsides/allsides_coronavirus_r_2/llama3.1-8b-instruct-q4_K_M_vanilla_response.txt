SUMMARY:
A federal judge, U.S. District Judge Gary L. Sharpe, has issued a preliminary injunction against New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo and NYC Mayor Bill de Blasio, prohibiting them from imposing arbitrary limits on church gatherings during the coronavirus pandemic. The decision comes after it was noted that the officials allowed mass protests of racial injustice to occur without restrictions, while enforcing limitations on other public assemblies. Sharpe argued that this double standard amounts to "preferential treatment" for protesters and a curtailment of fundamental rights. The injunction allows outdoor gatherings without limitation and indoor gatherings in line with New York's reopening plan. The Thomas More Society, representing Catholic priests and Jewish congregants, welcomed the decision as an important step towards protecting religious freedom.

END OF SUMMARY.
