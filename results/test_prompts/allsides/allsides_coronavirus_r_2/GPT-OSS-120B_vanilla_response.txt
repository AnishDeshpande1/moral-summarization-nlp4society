SUMMARY: A federal judge, U.S. District Judge Gary L. Sharpe, issued a preliminary injunction blocking New York Governor Andrew Cuomo, Attorney General, and Mayor Bill de Blasio from imposing arbitrary limits on religious gatherings during the COVID‑19 pandemic. The ruling says officials cannot restrict outdoor worship and must align indoor limits with the state’s Phase 2‑3 reopening plan, which allows 100% occupancy for essential businesses and 50% for non‑essential ones, while religious services were capped at 25% occupancy. The decision follows a lawsuit filed by Catholic priests and Jewish congregants, represented by the conservative Thomas More Society. Judge Sharpe criticized the officials for allowing mass protests after George Floyd’s death to proceed without enforcement, labeling this a “preferential treatment” that violated religious‑assembly rights. The court emphasized that government may only limit fundamental rights with a compelling justification, and the injunction restores parity between religious services and other public gatherings. END OF SUMMARY.
