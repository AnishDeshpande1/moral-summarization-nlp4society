A federal judge has ruled that New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo and NYC Mayor Bill de Blasio are wrong to limit religious gatherings amid the pandemic while allowing mass protests of racial injustice. The judge issued a preliminary injunction prohibiting the officials from imposing arbitrary limits on church gatherings. The ruling notes that the officials' actions amount to "preferential treatment" for protesters, as they allowed thousands of people to gather in the streets without restrictions. The judge argues that this double standard is hypocritical and goes against fundamental rights without compelling justification. The Thomas More Society, which represented a group of Catholic priests and Jewish congregants, hailed the decision as an important step towards inhibiting absolute monarchy on pretext of public health. The ruling effectively states that religious gatherings cannot be limited out-of-step with phases two and three of New York's reopening plan.
