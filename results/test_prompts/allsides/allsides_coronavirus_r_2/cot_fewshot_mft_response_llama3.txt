A federal judge has ruled that New York Governor Andrew Cuomo and Mayor Bill de Blasio are wrong to limit religious gatherings amid the coronavirus pandemic while allowing mass protests of racial injustice. The ruling states that Cuomo and de Blasio cannot impose arbitrary limits on church gatherings, citing a "double standard" in their actions. The Thomas More Society, a conservative not-for-profit national law firm, represented the group of Catholic priests and Jewish congregants who brought the complaint.
