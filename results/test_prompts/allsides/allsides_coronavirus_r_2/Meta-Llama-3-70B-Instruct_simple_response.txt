SUMMARY:

A federal judge has ruled in favor of religious freedom advocates, prohibiting New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo and New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio from enforcing arbitrary limits on church gatherings during the coronavirus pandemic. The judge argued that the officials' support for mass protests against racial injustice while limiting religious gatherings was hypocritical and amounted to "preferential treatment" for the protesters. The ruling states that the officials cannot impose limitations on outdoor gatherings and must allow indoor gatherings to operate at the same capacity as "essential" and "nonessential" businesses. The judge's decision is seen as a win for religious freedom and a step towards preventing the abuse of power under the pretext of public health.

END OF SUMMARY.
