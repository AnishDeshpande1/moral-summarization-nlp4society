SUMMARY:
A federal judge ruled that New York Governor Andrew Cuomo and Mayor Bill de Blasio cannot limit religious gatherings amid the COVID-19 pandemic while allowing mass protests of racial injustice to continue. Judge Gary L. Sharpe issued a preliminary injunction prohibiting the officials from ordering or enforcing arbitrary limits on church gatherings. The decision noted the hypocrisy in limiting religious gatherings while allowing larger protests, citing "preferential treatment" for protesters. The ruling effectively states that the officials cannot impose limitations on outdoor gatherings and indoor gatherings out-of-step with New York's reopening plan. The Thomas More Society represented a group of Catholic priests and Jewish congregants who brought the complaint. The decision is seen as an important step in preventing absolute monarchy in the name of public health, allowing for freedom for all, not just some.
