SUMMARY:
A federal judge ruled that New York Governor Andrew Cuomo and Mayor Bill de Blasio cannot limit religious gatherings while allowing mass protests amid the COVID-19 pandemic. Judge Gary L. Sharpe issued a preliminary injunction, stating that the officials' decisions were arbitrary and resulted in the curtailment of fundamental rights without compelling justification. The ruling noted the hypocrisy of limiting outdoor gatherings to 25 people while allowing thousands of protesters to gather without restriction. The judge argued that the officials gave "preferential treatment" to the protesters, sending a clear message that mass protests are deserving of special treatment. This decision is seen as an important step towards protecting religious freedom and preventing the exercise of absolute monarchy in the name of public health. END OF SUMMARY.
