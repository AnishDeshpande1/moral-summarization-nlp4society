SUMMARY:

A federal judge has ruled in favor of religious freedom advocates, prohibiting New York Governor Andrew Cuomo and New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio from limiting religious gatherings during the coronavirus pandemic while supporting mass protests of racial injustice. The judge's decision notes that the officials' actions amount to "preferential treatment" for protesters, as they encouraged and supported large gatherings for protests while enforcing strict limitations on other forms of public assembly, including religious gatherings. The ruling argues that this double standard is unjust and violates fundamental rights. The judge's order effectively prohibits the officials from imposing arbitrary limits on church gatherings, allowing them to operate at the same capacity as "essential" and "nonessential" businesses. This decision is a crucial step in protecting religious freedom and preventing the abuse of power under the guise of public health. It highlights the importance of upholding the principles of equality and fairness in the application of the law.

END OF SUMMARY.
