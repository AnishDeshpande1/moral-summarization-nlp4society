SUMMARY:

A federal judge has ruled that New York Governor Andrew Cuomo and Mayor Bill de Blasio are wrong to limit religious gatherings amid the COVID-19 pandemic while allowing mass protests of racial injustice. The ruling grants a preliminary injunction prohibiting the officials from ordering or enforcing arbitrary limits on church gatherings. The decision highlights the hypocrisy of allowing thousands of protesters to gather without restrictions, while limiting religious gatherings to 25% occupancy. Judge Gary L. Sharpe noted that the officials' actions amount to "preferential treatment" for the protesters, and that it is not the judiciary's role to second guess their decisions when they result in the curtailment of fundamental rights without compelling justification. This ruling is a significant victory for religious freedom advocates and highlights the importance of treating all forms of public assembly equally under the law.

END OF SUMMARY.
