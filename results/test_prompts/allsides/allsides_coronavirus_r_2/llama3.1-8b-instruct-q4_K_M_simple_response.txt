SUMMARY:
A federal judge ruled that New York Governor Andrew Cuomo and Mayor Bill de Blasio cannot limit religious gatherings amid the coronavirus pandemic while allowing mass protests of racial injustice. U.S. District Judge Gary L. Sharpe issued a preliminary injunction, stating that the officials' actions were arbitrary and discriminatory. The decision notes that Cuomo and de Blasio showed "preferential treatment" for protesters by allowing them to gather in large numbers without restrictions, while enforcing limits on religious gatherings. This double standard is seen as a flagrant disregard for public health rules and social distancing measures. The ruling effectively lifts limitations on outdoor gatherings and aligns indoor gathering limits with the state's reopening plan. This decision is seen as an important step towards protecting freedom of assembly and preventing government overreach.

END OF SUMMARY.
