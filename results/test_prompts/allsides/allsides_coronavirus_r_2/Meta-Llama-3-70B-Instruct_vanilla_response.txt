SUMMARY:

A federal judge has ruled in favor of religious freedom advocates, stating that New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo and NYC Mayor Bill de Blasio cannot limit religious gatherings while supporting mass protests. The judge's preliminary injunction prohibits the officials from enforcing arbitrary limits on church gatherings, citing a double standard in their treatment of protests and religious assemblies. The judge noted that while "essential" businesses are allowed 100% occupancy and "nonessential" businesses are allowed 50% occupancy, religious gatherings are limited to 25% occupancy. The ruling argues that the officials' support for protests while limiting other gatherings amounts to "preferential treatment" and a curtailment of fundamental rights without compelling justification. The decision is seen as a win for religious freedom advocates and a step towards inhibiting the trend of exercising absolute authority on pretext of public health.

END OF SUMMARY.
