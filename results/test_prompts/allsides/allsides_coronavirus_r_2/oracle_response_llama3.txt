SUMMARY:

A federal judge ruled that New York officials are wrong to limit religious gatherings amid the pandemic while supporting mass protests of racial injustice. Judge Gary L. Sharpe issued a preliminary injunction prohibiting Cuomo and de Blasio from imposing arbitrary limits on church gatherings. The decision states that outdoor gatherings cannot be limited, and indoor gatherings must follow phases two and three of the state's reopening plan. This is an important step towards inhibiting the trend of exercising absolute monarchy on pretext of public health. The judge pointed out the hypocrisy, arguing that there was preferential treatment for protesters. Cuomo and de Blasio had spoken openly in support of New York residents' freedom to protest while still enforcing limitations on other forms of public assembly. The ruling is a win for religious freedom advocates who are fighting against the curtailment of fundamental rights without compelling justification.

END OF SUMMARY.
