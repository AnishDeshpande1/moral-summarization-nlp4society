SUMMARY: A federal judge sided with religious‑freedom advocates, blocking New York Governor Andrew Cuomo and Mayor Bill de Blasio from imposing arbitrary caps on church services while they tacitly allowed massive protests against racial injustice. Judge Gary L. Sharpe issued a preliminary injunction, warning that the judiciary will not second‑guess officials unless they infringe fundamental rights without a compelling health justification. The ruling highlights the double standard: New York’s reopening plan permits 100 % occupancy for “essential” businesses and 50 % for “non‑essential” ones, yet religious gatherings were limited to 25 % even as thousands gathered on the streets for George Floyd protests without enforcement. Conservative group the Thomas More Society, representing Catholic priests and Jewish congregants, hailed the decision as a blow against a “sham” public‑health pretext used to impose “absolute monarchy” on citizens. The judge echoed this moral critique, calling the officials’ selective leniency “preferential treatment” that undermines equal protection of assembly rights. The case is framed as a defense of true religious liberty and a rebuke of governmental hypocrisy that favors political protest over worship. END OF SUMMARY.
