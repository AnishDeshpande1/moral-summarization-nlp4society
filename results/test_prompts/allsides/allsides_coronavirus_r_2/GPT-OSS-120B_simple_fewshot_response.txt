SUMMARY:
A federal judge has blocked New York Governor Andrew Cuomo and Mayor Bill de Blasio from imposing stricter limits on religious services than on other public gatherings during the COVID‑19 pandemic. Judge Gary L. Sharpe ruled that the officials’ 25 % occupancy cap on churches, while allowing higher capacities for businesses and tolerating massive racial‑justice protests, violated fundamental religious‑freedom rights and showed a double standard. The decision, hailed by the Thomas More Society as a victory against “absolute monarchy” masquerading as public‑health policy, underscores that the government may not curtail constitutionally protected worship without a compelling justification. By allowing thousands to protest while restricting worshippers, the state gave “preferential treatment” to protests and unjustly burdened religious communities. The ruling affirms the moral principle that all citizens deserve equal protection of their basic liberties, especially the right to worship, regardless of political or social pressures. END OF SUMMARY.
