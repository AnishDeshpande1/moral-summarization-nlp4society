SUMMARY:
A federal judge has blocked New York Governor Andrew Cuomo and Mayor Bill de Blasio from imposing arbitrary limits on religious services while they openly encouraged mass protests over racial injustice. Judge Gary L. Sharpe ruled that the officials’ “social‑distancing protocol” unfairly singled out churches—capping indoor worship at 25 % capacity and outdoor gatherings at 25 people—while allowing thousands of protestors to gather without restriction. The decision frames the governors’ actions as a subversion of legitimate authority: they used public‑health power to suppress one form of assembly but gave preferential treatment to politically favored protests, thereby cheating the principle of equal protection. By enforcing stricter limits on worship, the officials jeopardized the care and well‑being of congregants who seek spiritual solace, while neglecting the health risks posed by unregulated protests. The ruling, brought by the Thomas More Society on behalf of Catholic priests and Jewish worshippers, underscores the need for consistent, fair application of public‑health rules and rebukes the double standard that privileged certain gatherings over others. It reaffirms that government authority must not be wielded to discriminate or harm citizens under the guise of safety.  
END OF SUMMARY.
