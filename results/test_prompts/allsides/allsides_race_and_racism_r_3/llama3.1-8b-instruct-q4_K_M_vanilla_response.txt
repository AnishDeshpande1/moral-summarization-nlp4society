SUMMARY:

Confederate monuments are being removed across the US, sparking debate over their place in American history. President Trump and conservatives argue that these statues are part of America's heritage and should be preserved, while others see them as an affront to black citizens who were enslaved by the Confederacy. The article argues that Confederate symbols deserve reevaluation and often mothballing. Many statues were erected to promote a dishonest version of the Confederacy, and their presence is unnecessary. However, the destruction of these monuments should be done lawfully, not through mob action.

The article also distinguishes between direct commemorations of the war dead, such as cemeteries, which can be preserved, and symbols like the Confederate flag, which should be shunned. Military bases named after Confederate generals are a harder case, but as their names become more widely known, it will be difficult to ask African-American soldiers to look past them.

END OF SUMMARY.
