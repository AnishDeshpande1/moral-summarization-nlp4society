SUMMARY:

The article argues that Confederate statues and symbols should be reevaluated and often removed from public spaces. These monuments were often erected to promote a distorted, pro-Confederacy narrative and serve as an affront to Black citizens who should not have to see defenders of slavery glorified. While learning about historical figures like Robert E. Lee doesn't require giant statues, commemorations of the Confederate war dead should be preserved to mark the human cost of the conflict. The article also suggests that military bases named after Confederate generals should be renamed, as it will become increasingly difficult to ask African-American soldiers to serve at bases named after those who fought to preserve slavery. The Richmond statue of Lee, set to be removed, is a prime example of a monument that should be relocated to a museum or battlefield.

END OF SUMMARY.
