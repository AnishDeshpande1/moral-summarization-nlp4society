SUMMARY:

The Confederate statues and symbols should be reevaluated and often removed, as they are an unnecessary affront to black citizens and glorify a viciously flawed cause. Many of these monuments were erected to promote a dishonest version of the Confederacy, and their context cannot be ignored. While learning about historical figures like Robert E. Lee doesn't require grand statues, direct commemorations of the Confederate war dead should be preserved to mark the human cost of the conflict. The Confederate flag should be shunned, and military bases named after Confederate generals should be reconsidered. President Donald Trump and conservatives have defended these symbols, but this impulse is a mistake. The statues should be transferred to museums or battlefields, and their removal should be done lawfully, not through mob action. It's time to acknowledge the true nature of the Confederacy and stop honoring its leaders. 

END OF SUMMARY.
