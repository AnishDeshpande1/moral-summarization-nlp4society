SUMMARY:

The removal of Confederate statues and symbols is a necessary step in reevaluating our nation's history. These monuments were often erected to promote a dishonest, romanticized version of the Confederacy, and their presence is an affront to black citizens who should not have to see defenders of chattel slavery honored. While learning about historical figures like Robert E. Lee doesn't require giant statues, direct commemorations of the Confederate war dead should be preserved to acknowledge the human cost of the conflict. However, symbols like the Confederate flag should be shunned as representations of a flawed cause. The names of military bases named after Confederate generals should also be reconsidered, as it's increasingly difficult to ask African-American soldiers to serve under names that evoke a traitorous act. Ultimately, secession was a traitorous act that threatened to destroy the American nation, and its leaders don't deserve a place of honor in our landscape.

END OF SUMMARY.
