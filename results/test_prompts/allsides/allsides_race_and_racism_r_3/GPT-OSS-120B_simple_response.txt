SUMMARY: The article argues that secession was a treasonous act rooted in slavery and that Confederate leaders do not merit public honor. While Trump and many conservatives defend Confederate monuments as heritage, the author maintains that these statues are offensive to Black citizens and were largely erected to promote a sanitized “Lost Cause” myth. The piece calls for a lawful reassessment: prominent statues glorifying the Confederacy should be removed from public spaces and placed in museums or battlefields, whereas genuine war‑dead memorials and cemeteries may remain as historical markers. Military bases named after Confederate generals present a tougher dilemma, but growing awareness should eventually lead to change. Vandalism is condemned, but the moral imperative is to discontinue public glorification of figures who fought to preserve chattel slavery. END OF SUMMARY.
