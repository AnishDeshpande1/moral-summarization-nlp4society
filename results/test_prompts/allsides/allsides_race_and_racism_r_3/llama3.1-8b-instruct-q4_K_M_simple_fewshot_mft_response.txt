SUMMARY:

Confederate statues and symbols are being reevaluated in light of their historical context, with many arguing that they are an unnecessary affront to black citizens who were enslaved by defenders of chattel slavery. The statues were often erected as part of a push to enshrine a dishonest version of the Confederacy, and their presence can be seen as a betrayal of the values of equality and justice. While it is important to learn about historical figures like Robert E. Lee, this should not require staring up at large public monuments that lionize them. Instead, these statues should be transferred to museums or battlefields where they can be contextualized as part of a complex and painful history. This process should be done lawfully, rather than through mob action. The Confederate flag and direct commemorations of the Confederate war dead should also be reevaluated, with the goal of promoting healing and reconciliation.
