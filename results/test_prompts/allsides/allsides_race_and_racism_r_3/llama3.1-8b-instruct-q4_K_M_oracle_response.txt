SUMMARY:

The author argues that Confederate statues and symbols are an affront to black citizens, who shouldn't have to see defenders of chattel slavery put on a pedestal. These monuments were erected as part of the push to enshrine a dishonest version of the Confederacy. The statues threaten to destroy the memory of the true cost of the Civil War, which was about slavery and not states' rights. President Trump's stance on preserving Confederate symbols is misguided, and it's time to reevaluate these monuments. While it's understandable that tradition and heritage are important, they shouldn't be used to justify honoring those who defended a flawed cause. The author suggests that big statues in prominent public spaces should be transferred elsewhere, while direct commemorations of the Confederate war dead should be preserved. This is not about tearing down history but about acknowledging the truth about the Confederacy's creation and legacy.

END OF SUMMARY.
