SUMMARY:

The article argues that Confederate statues and symbols in the US are an affront to black citizens, who shouldn't have to see defenders of chattel slavery honored. Many of these monuments were erected to enshrine a dishonest version of the Confederacy, and their removal is long overdue. The author believes that learning about historical figures like Robert E. Lee doesn't require grand statues in public spaces. Instead, they suggest transferring such monuments to museums or battlefields, where their context can be understood. Direct commemorations of Confederate war dead should be preserved, but symbols of the Confederacy, like the flag, should be shunned. The article also rejects President Trump's stance on preserving Confederate names and symbols, arguing that they are a reminder of a treasonous act that threatened to destroy the American nation.
