SUMMARY:

The removal of Confederate statues and symbols is a necessary step in reevaluating our nation's history. Secession was a traitorous act that threatened to destroy the American nation, and its leaders don't deserve a place of honor. Confederate statues are an unnecessary affront to black citizens, who shouldn't have to see defenders of chattel slavery put on a pedestal. Many of these statues were erected to enshrine a dishonest, prettied-up version of the Confederacy. President Trump's defense of these statues and symbols is misguided, as it fails to consider the context of their creation. Instead, we should make distinctions between symbols of the Confederacy and direct commemorations of the war dead. The Confederate flag should be shunned as a symbol of a viciously flawed cause. Ultimately, Confederate statues and symbols should be mothballed, and their removal should be done lawfully, not through mob action.

END OF SUMMARY.
