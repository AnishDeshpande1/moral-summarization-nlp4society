SUMMARY:

The author argues that Confederate statues and symbols should be reevaluated and often removed, as they are an unnecessary affront to black citizens and perpetuate a dishonest, romanticized version of the Confederacy. The statues were often erected to glorify the Confederacy and downplay its true purpose: to preserve chattel slavery. The author distinguishes between statues in prominent public spaces, which should be transferred to museums or battlefields, and direct commemorations of the Confederate war dead, which should be preserved to mark the human cost of the conflict. The author also criticizes President Trump's defense of the statues, citing the harm they cause to black citizens and the betrayal of American values they represent. Ultimately, the author concludes that Confederate leaders do not deserve a place of honor in our landscape, and their statues should be removed.

END OF SUMMARY.
