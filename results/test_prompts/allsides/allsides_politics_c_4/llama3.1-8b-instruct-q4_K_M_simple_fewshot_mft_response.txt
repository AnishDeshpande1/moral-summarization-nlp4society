SUMMARY:

Donald Trump's presidency is marked by a disregard for authority and the rule of law, as evident in his denial of collusion despite overwhelming evidence to the contrary. His former attorney, Michael Cohen, has pleaded guilty to breaking the law on Trump's orders, while his former campaign manager, Paul Manafort, was found guilty on eight felony charges. Trump's behavior since the investigations began has lent credence to his possible guilt, including his compulsion to lie about everything and undermine the veracity of the investigation. This sets a low example for a president, who should be held to the highest standards of integrity and honesty. The real scandal is not collusion, but Trump himself, whose actions demonstrate a lack of care for the harm he causes others, a disregard for fairness in his dealings, and a betrayal of the trust placed in him by the American people.
