SUMMARY:

At a recent rally, President Trump denied collusion with Russia, despite his former attorney Michael Cohen pleading guilty to crimes including tax evasion, bank fraud, and illegal campaign contributions. Cohen admitted to making six-figure payments to women to buy their silence, and Trump ordered him to break the law by circumventing campaign finance reporting. Trump's former campaign manager, Paul Manafort, was also found guilty of eight felony charges. Trump's denial of collusion is jarring, especially given his history of lying, bullying, and harassing law enforcement officials. He has also taken to Twitter to undermine the investigation and has groveled to Russian dictator Vladimir Putin, claiming the investigation is a "witch hunt." Trump's behavior has lent credence to his possible guilt, and his uncontrollable compulsion to lie has set an appallingly low example for the presidency. The real scandal isn't collusion, but Trump himself.
