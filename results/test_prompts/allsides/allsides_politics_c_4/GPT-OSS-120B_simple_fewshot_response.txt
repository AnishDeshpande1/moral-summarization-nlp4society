SUMMARY:
The article condemns President Donald Trump’s relentless denial of Russian collusion, portraying it as part of a broader pattern of dishonesty and misconduct that makes him unfit for office. It highlights recent legal blows—Michael Cohen’s guilty plea to tax evasion, fraud and hush‑money payments, and Paul Manafort’s conviction on multiple fraud charges—yet notes Trump continues to dismiss collusion allegations despite overwhelming evidence of his willingness to break the law, lie to the public, bully investigators, and praise Vladimir Putin over U.S. intelligence. The piece argues that even if collusion were hard to prove, Trump’s constant falsehoods and “truth‑is‑what‑I‑say‑it‑is” mindset constitute a scandal far worse than any conspiracy, setting a deplorable example for the nation. In short, the real scandal is Trump’s character and his erosion of democratic norms, not the existence of a Russian deal. END OF SUMMARY.
