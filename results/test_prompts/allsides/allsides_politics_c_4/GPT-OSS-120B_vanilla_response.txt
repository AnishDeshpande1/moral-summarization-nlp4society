SUMMARY: The article critiques Donald Trump’s repeated denials of collusion with Russia, highlighting that these claims have become routine rally rhetoric. It notes the irony that his denials come amid serious legal troubles for his close associates—Michael Cohen’s guilty plea for tax evasion, fraud, and hush‑money payments, and Paul Manafort’s conviction on tax and bank fraud charges. The piece argues that proving collusion would require evidence that Trump knowingly participated in a Trump Tower meeting with Russian representatives, a difficult legal hurdle. However, the author contends that Trump’s broader pattern of lying, harassment of investigators, and deference to Vladimir Putin undermines his credibility and fitness for office. While acknowledging the complexity of proving collusion, the article concludes that the real scandal is Trump’s character and conduct, not the alleged Russian deal. END OF SUMMARY.
