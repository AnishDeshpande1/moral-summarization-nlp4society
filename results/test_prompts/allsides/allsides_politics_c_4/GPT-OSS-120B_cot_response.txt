**SUMMARY:**  
At a West Virginia rally on Aug. 21, President Trump repeatedly demanded “collusion” to be found, echoing his long‑standing “There is No Collusion” refrain alongside “Fake News” and “Hillary’s Emails.” His denial came hours after former attorney Michael Cohen pleaded guilty to tax evasion, bank fraud and illegal campaign contributions, admitting Trump ordered hush‑money payments, and after campaign manager Paul Manafort was convicted of similar crimes. Despite these revelations, Trump persists in denying any collusion with Russia, a charge now ancillary to the larger picture of his alleged misconduct. Critics point to his constant lying, bullying of law‑enforcement officials, and harassing tactics, as well as his willingness to side with Russian dictator Vladimir Putin over U.S. intelligence. The article argues that Trump’s unfit character, compulsive mendacity, and low example constitute the real scandal, not the collusion allegation itself.
