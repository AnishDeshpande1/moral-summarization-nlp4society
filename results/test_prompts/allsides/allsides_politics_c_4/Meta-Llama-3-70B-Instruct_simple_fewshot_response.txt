SUMMARY:

Donald Trump's repeated denials of collusion with Russia are becoming increasingly hollow as his former associates face guilty verdicts for various crimes. Trump's former personal attorney, Michael Cohen, pleaded guilty to tax evasion, bank fraud, and illegal campaign contributions, including making six-figure payments to women to buy their silence during the 2016 election. Trump's former campaign manager, Paul Manafort, was found guilty on eight felony tax and bank fraud charges. Despite these developments, Trump continues to deny collusion, a charge that is now almost beside the point. What is clear is Trump's poor character, marked by a compulsive need to lie about everything. His behavior since the investigations began has lent more credence to his possible guilt, and his denials ring hollow. The real scandal isn't collusion; it's Donald Trump himself, who has set a appalling example for the nation.
