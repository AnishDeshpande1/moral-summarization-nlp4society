SUMMARY: The article frames Donald Trump’s repeated denial of “collusion” as a moral failing rather than a substantive political issue. It notes that even as his former lawyer Michael Cohen pleaded guilty to crimes—including hush‑money payments ordered by Trump—and former campaign manager Paul Manafort was convicted of fraud, Trump continued to shout “There is no collusion” to a cheering crowd. The piece argues that proving collusion would be legally difficult, but Trump’s broader pattern of lying, bullying investigators, and praising Vladimir Putin reveals a deeper character defect. By constantly rejecting facts, creating his own reality, and refusing to set a truthful example, Trump undermines the integrity of the presidency. The author concludes that the true scandal is not any alleged Russian deal, but Trump’s own unfitness for office and the corrosive moral example he sets. END OF SUMMARY.
