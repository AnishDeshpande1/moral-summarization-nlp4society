SUMMARY:

Donald Trump's repeated denial of collusion with Russia is becoming increasingly hollow, especially given the recent guilty pleas from his former personal attorney, Michael Cohen, and campaign manager, Paul Manafort. Cohen admitted to making six-figure payments to women to buy their silence in advance of the 2016 election, which he said was ordered by Trump to circumvent the campaign-finance reporting system. This is just one example of Trump's evasion of accountability for his actions. His behavior since the investigations began has lent credence to his possible guilt, with his lying about various issues becoming a hallmark of his presidency.

The real scandal isn't collusion, but rather Trump's own character and fitness for office. His lying, bullying, and harassing of law enforcement officials undermine the investigation and are a clear example of his poor behavior. Trump has set a low standard for honesty and integrity in the White House, and it is up to Democrats to stop imitating his incivility at the polls.

END OF SUMMARY.
