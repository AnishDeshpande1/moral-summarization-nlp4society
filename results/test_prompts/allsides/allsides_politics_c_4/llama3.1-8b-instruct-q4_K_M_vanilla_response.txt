SUMMARY:

Despite the denial of Russian collusion by President Trump, his former personal attorney Michael Cohen's guilty plea and former campaign manager Paul Manafort's conviction have raised more questions about Trump's involvement. Trump has repeatedly denied any wrongdoing, but his behavior since the investigations began has lent credence to possible guilt. His tendency to lie about everything, including his knowledge of key events, has become a hallmark of his presidency.

The article argues that Trump's actions and words are more important than his denials of collusion. He has shown poor character in lying to the public, bullying law enforcement officials, and undermining the investigation into Russian interference in the 2016 election. The author suggests that the real scandal is not collusion, but rather Donald Trump himself.

The article concludes that it may be difficult to prove collusion, but Trump's behavior since the investigations began has made his possible guilt more believable. His lack of integrity and tendency to create his own reality have set a low example for the presidency.
