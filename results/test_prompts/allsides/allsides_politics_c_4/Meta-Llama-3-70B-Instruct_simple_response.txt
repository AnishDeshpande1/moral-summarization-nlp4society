SUMMARY:

The article critiques President Trump's repeated denials of collusion with Russia, despite mounting evidence of his wrongdoing. At a recent rally, Trump prodded the crowd to chant "Where is the collusion?" just hours after his former attorney Michael Cohen pleaded guilty to crimes including illegal campaign contributions, and his former campaign manager Paul Manafort was found guilty of felony tax and bank fraud charges. The author argues that Trump's denials are beside the point, as his behavior has shown him to be unfit for office. The article highlights Trump's numerous lies, including about his involvement in his son's statement about the Trump Tower meeting and his knowledge of Cohen's payments to Stormy Daniels. The author concludes that the real scandal is not collusion, but Trump's poor character and the low example he has set as President. His behavior has lent credence to his possible guilt, and he deserves zero credit for his denials.

END OF SUMMARY.
