SUMMARY:

US Democrats are seeking to bolster the case for impeaching President Donald Trump, describing his pressuring of Ukraine to investigate a political rival as a "clear and present danger" to free and fair elections and national security. They argue that Trump has put himself before his country, violating his most basic responsibilities to the people and breaking his oath. Republicans fired back, accusing Democrats of being desperate to impeach Trump and claiming that the inquiry has uncovered no direct evidence of impeachable offenses. The heart of the impeachment battle is whether Trump abused his power by pressuring Ukraine to investigate former Vice President Joe Biden and then obstructed Congress' investigation. Democrats believe Trump's actions are a "clear and present danger" to the electoral process and national security, while Republicans claim it's a "baloney" effort to unseat a president who has done nothing to merit removal from office. The House Judiciary panel is expected to vote on whether to send formal charges to the full House.

END OF SUMMARY.
