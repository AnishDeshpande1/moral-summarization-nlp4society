SUMMARY:

US Democrats continued their push for President Donald Trump's impeachment, calling his actions a "clear and present danger" to free and fair elections and national security. Representative Jerrold Nadler accused Trump of violating his oath by pressuring Ukraine to investigate a political rival, Joe Biden. Republicans fired back, arguing that the inquiry is unfair and lacks evidence.

The hearing focused on two articles charging Trump with abuse of power and obstruction of Congress. Democrats claim Trump withheld aid to Ukraine as leverage to pressure them into investigating the Bidens. However, some Democrats appeared to distance themselves from allegations of Russian interference in the 2016 election.

Representative Daniel Goldman warned that leaving Trump in office would undermine US elections and national security. Republican attorney Stephen Castor countered that there is no direct evidence of impeachable offenses and that Democrats are seeking to remove Trump due to policy disagreements.

END OF SUMMARY
