SUMMARY:
U.S. Democrats used a raucous House Judiciary hearing to argue that President Donald Trump subverted constitutional authority by pressuring Ukraine to investigate political rival Joe Biden, thereby cheating the nation out of free and fair elections and endangering national security. Representative Jerrold Nadler accused Trump of betraying his oath and putting himself before the country, while Democrats presented a 300‑page report alleging sweeping abuse of power and obstruction of Congress. Republicans countered with procedural objections and claimed the impeachment push was a partisan effort to punish a president they remain loyal to, labeling the accusations “baloney” and asserting no impeachable wrongdoing. The hearing also featured a disruptive protester accusing Democrats of treason, underscoring the deep loyalty divides. With the Democratic‑controlled House poised to vote on articles of abuse of power and obstruction, the clash pits claims of presidential betrayal against Republican defenses of loyalty to the office, setting the stage for a partisan showdown that could culminate in an impeachment vote before Christmas.

END OF SUMMARY.
