SUMMARY:

U.S. Democrats continued their push for impeaching President Donald Trump, arguing that his actions pose a clear and present danger to free and fair elections and national security. They presented extensive evidence of wrongdoing, claiming that Trump has put himself before the country and violated his most basic responsibilities as president. The top Democrat on the panel, Representative Jerrold Nadler, stated that Trump's behavior is a "clear and present danger" to the nation's electoral process.

Republicans countered with objections, arguing that there was no truth in the Democrats' claims and that they were seeking to impeach Trump simply because they disagree with his policies. They claimed that Trump had not committed any impeachable offenses, but rather engaged in a phone call where he sought investigations into former Vice President Joe Biden.

The impeachment battle has divided Americans, with Democrats arguing that Trump's actions are a threat to the nation's security and Republicans claiming that the process is unfair and motivated by partisanship. The full House is expected to vote on articles of impeachment before Christmas, but a conviction in the Republican-controlled Senate seems unlikely.
