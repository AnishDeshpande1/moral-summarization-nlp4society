SUMMARY:  
U.S. House Democrats held a heated Judiciary Committee hearing to strengthen a pending impeachment vote against President Donald Trump. They argued that Trump’s July 25 call with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy, in which he allegedly pressured Ukraine to investigate political rival Joe Biden and withheld $391 million in aid, constitutes an abuse of power and a threat to free elections and national security. Rep. Jerrold Nadler claimed the evidence shows Trump broke his oath, while Republicans, led by Rep. Doug Collins and attorney Stephen Castor, dismissed the case as partisan overreach and “baloney.” The hearing, disrupted by a protester, also featured testimony from intelligence officials and highlighted a 300‑page Democratic report accusing Trump of sweeping misconduct, countered by a 110‑page Republican report denying any impeachable offenses. The committee is expected to vote by week’s end on articles of impeachment, after which the full Democratic‑controlled House will likely vote before the holidays. If passed, the Senate, controlled by Republicans, would hold a trial where removal is unlikely.  

END OF SUMMARY.
