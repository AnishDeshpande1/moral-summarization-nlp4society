SUMMARY:
U.S. Democrats intensified their push to impeach President Donald Trump, arguing that his pressure on Ukraine to investigate political rival Joe Biden poses a “clear and present danger” to free elections and national security. In a heated Judiciary Committee hearing, Rep. Jerrold Nadler accused Trump of putting himself above the country, violating his oath and basic responsibilities. Republicans responded with procedural objections and dismissed the allegations as partisan “baloney,” insisting the inquiry is a partisan attempt to remove a president who has done nothing warrantable of impeachment. The hearing highlighted two proposed articles of impeachment—abuse of power and obstruction of Congress—centered on Trump’s withholding of aid to Ukraine and leveraging a White House meeting to demand investigations of the Bidens. Democrats warned that leaving Trump in office could further undermine the electoral process, while Republicans claimed no direct evidence of impeachable offenses. The committee is expected to vote soon on formal charges, setting the stage for a full‑House vote and a likely partisan clash with the Republican‑controlled Senate.

END OF SUMMARY.
