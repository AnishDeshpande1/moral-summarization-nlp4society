SUMMARY:  
In a heated House Judiciary Committee hearing, Democrats framed President Donald Trump’s pressure on Ukraine to investigate political rival Joe Biden as a “clear and present danger” to free elections and national security, accusing him of putting himself before the country, breaking his oath, and violating basic responsibilities to the American people. Rep. Jerrold Nadler presented extensive evidence of abuse of power and obstruction, while Republicans countered with procedural objections and claimed the impeachment drive was a partisan effort to remove a president who has done nothing warranting removal. The debate highlighted moral stakes: Democrats warned that allowing Trump to remain would further erode democratic norms, whereas Republicans dismissed the charges as “baloney” and a response to policy disagreements. The committee is poised to vote on articles of impeachment—abuse of power and obstruction—before the full, Democratic‑controlled House, setting up a likely partisan clash with a Republican‑controlled Senate where conviction is doubtful.  

END OF SUMMARY.
