SUMMARY:

In a raucous hearing, Democrats sought to bolster the case for impeaching President Donald Trump, describing his pressuring of Ukraine to investigate a political rival as a "clear and present danger" to free and fair elections and national security. Republicans fired back, accusing Democrats of desperation and treason. The hearing highlighted the divide between Democrats, who see Trump's actions as a violation of his oath and a threat to democracy, and Republicans, who believe the inquiry is a partisan attempt to unseat a president. The moral framing of this article suggests that Trump's actions are a subversion of authority, a betrayal of the public trust, and an unfair attempt to cheat his way to re-election. Democrats, on the other hand, are seen as loyal defenders of democracy and fairness. The article implies that Trump's refusal to participate in the hearings and his attacks on the inquiry are a further erosion of democratic norms and a threat to the rule of law.

END OF SUMMARY.
