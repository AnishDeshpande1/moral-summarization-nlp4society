SUMMARY:

Democrats in the House Judiciary Committee have made a strong case for impeaching President Donald Trump, accusing him of being a "clear and present danger" to free and fair elections and national security. They argue that Trump abused his power by pressuring Ukraine to investigate a political rival, former Vice President Joe Biden, and then obstructed Congress' investigation. Republicans, on the other hand, have fired back, claiming that the impeachment inquiry is a desperate attempt to unseat a president who has done nothing to merit removal from office. The hearing highlighted the deep divisions among Americans, with Democrats accusing Trump of violating his oath and Republicans claiming that the inquiry is based on presumption rather than truth. The committee is expected to vote on formal charges by the end of the week, which could lead to a trial in the Republican-controlled Senate.

END OF SUMMARY.
