SUMMARY:

U.S. Democrats have sought to bolster the case for impeaching President Donald Trump, describing his pressuring of Ukraine to investigate a political rival as a "clear and present danger" to free and fair elections and national security. The House Judiciary panel held a hearing on Monday, with top Democrat Representative Jerrold Nadler stating that there was extensive proof of Trump's wrongdoing. Republicans fired back, arguing that the inquiry had uncovered no direct evidence of impeachable offenses. The heart of the impeachment battle is whether Trump abused his power by pressuring Ukraine to investigate former Vice President Joe Biden and then obstructed Congress' investigation. If Trump is impeached, a trial would be held in the Republican-controlled Senate, where a two-thirds majority would be needed to vote to remove him from office. No current House Republican has come out in favor of impeaching Trump.

END OF SUMMARY.
