SUMMARY:

US Democrats are pushing for President Donald Trump's impeachment, citing his pressure on Ukraine to investigate a political rival as a "clear and present danger" to free and fair elections and national security. In a hearing, Democrats presented extensive proof of Trump's wrongdoing, accusing him of abusing his power and obstructing Congress. Republicans fired back, calling the inquiry a "hoax" and saying there's no evidence of wrongdoing. The hearing is a key step towards determining formal impeachment charges, which the full House is likely to vote on before Christmas. If Trump is impeached, a trial would be held in the Republican-controlled Senate, where a two-thirds majority would be needed to remove him from office. The impeachment battle centers around whether Trump abused his power by pressuring Ukraine to investigate former VP Joe Biden and then obstructed Congress' investigation.

END OF SUMMARY.
