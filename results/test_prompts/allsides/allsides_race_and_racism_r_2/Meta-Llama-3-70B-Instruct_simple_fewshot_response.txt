SUMMARY:

Attorney General William Barr has established a task force to combat "violent anti-government extremists of all persuasions." The task force aims to investigate and prosecute those who commit violent acts, prevent extremist violence, and ultimately eliminate it as a threat to public safety and the rule of law. Barr notes that while protests have been largely peaceful, some have descended into scenes of looting, arson, and violence. He cites attacks on police officers, destruction of property, and threats to innocent bystanders. The task force will provide training and resources to local law enforcement and work closely with the FBI to develop information about violent anti-government extremist individuals and networks. Barr's announcement comes as President Trump signed an executive order protecting American monuments, memorials, and statues from being destroyed. The move is seen as a response to recent violent acts during protests, with Barr warning that some extremists intend to sow more chaos and may be supported by foreign entities.

END OF SUMMARY.
