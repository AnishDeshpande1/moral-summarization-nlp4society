SUMMARY: Attorney General William Barr announced the creation of a DOJ task force to combat “violent anti‑government extremists” of all persuasions. In a June 26 memo, Barr said the unit will lead investigations, prosecutions and prevention efforts against individuals and groups committing violence, looting, arson, and threats against police, officials and the public during recent protests sparked by George Floyd’s death. The memo cites incidents in Minneapolis, Seattle’s “autonomous zone,” and attacks on journalists, and warns that some extremists may be aided by foreign actors. The task force will train local law‑enforcement, coordinate with the FBI, and share intelligence to stop extremist violence and protect public safety and the rule of law. The announcement coincided with President Trump’s executive order shielding American monuments from vandalism. END OF SUMMARY.
