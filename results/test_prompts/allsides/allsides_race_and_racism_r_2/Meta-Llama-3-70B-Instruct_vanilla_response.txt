SUMMARY:

Attorney General William Barr has established a task force to combat "violent anti-government extremists of all persuasions." The task force aims to investigate and prosecute those who commit violent acts, prevent extremist violence, and ultimately eliminate it as a threat to public safety and the rule of law. Barr cited recent protests that have turned violent, including attacks on police officers, destruction of property, and threats to innocent bystanders. The task force will provide training and resources to local law enforcement to arrest perpetrators of violence and work closely with the FBI to gather information about violent anti-government extremist individuals and networks. Barr noted that some extremist groups are supported by "foreign entities seeking to sow chaos and disorder." The announcement came on the same day President Donald Trump signed an executive order protecting American monuments, memorials, and statues from being destroyed.

END OF SUMMARY.
