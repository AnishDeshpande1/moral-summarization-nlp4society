SUMMARY: Attorney General William Barr announced a new DOJ task force to combat “violent anti‑government extremists of all persuasions,” framing the effort as a moral defense of the rule of law and constitutional rights. In a June 26 memo, Barr condemned groups that masquerade as champions of freedom while spreading “anarchy, destruction, and coercion,” and warned that some are backed by foreign actors seeking chaos. He linked the task force to recent unrest after George Floyd’s death—peaceful protests marred by looting, arson, attacks on police, and the Seattle “autonomous zone”—and asserted that such extremist violence threatens public safety and democratic values. The unit will coordinate with the FBI, train local law‑enforcement, and share intelligence to prevent and prosecute violent acts, targeting self‑identified Antifa members, “Bugaloo” supporters, and similar networks. The announcement coincided with President Trump’s executive order protecting monuments, underscoring a broader moral narrative that the government must protect national heritage and uphold law‑and‑order against lawless, anti‑constitutional forces. END OF SUMMARY.
