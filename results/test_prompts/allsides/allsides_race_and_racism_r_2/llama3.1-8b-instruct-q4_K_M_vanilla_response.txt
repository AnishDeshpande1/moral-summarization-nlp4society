SUMMARY:
Attorney General William Barr has established a task force aimed at combating violent anti-government extremists. In a memo, Barr stated that these groups are forces of anarchy, destruction, and coercion, and have been involved in acts of violence against police officers, government officials, and innocent bystanders during recent protests. The task force will provide training and resources to local law enforcement to investigate and prosecute those who commit violent acts, as well as prevent extremist violence before it happens. Barr noted that some extremists may be supported by foreign entities seeking to sow chaos and disorder.

The task force's efforts come on the same day President Donald Trump signed an executive order protecting American monuments, memorials, and statues from destruction. The announcement follows reports of armed activists patrolling a so-called "autonomous zone" in Seattle and extorting businesses. Barr emphasized that the goal of the task force is to eliminate violent anti-government extremism as a threat to public safety and the rule of law.

END OF SUMMARY.
