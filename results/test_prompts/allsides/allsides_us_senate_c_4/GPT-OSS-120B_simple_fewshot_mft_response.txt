SUMMARY:
Republican senators warned President Trump that attempting to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller would constitute a dangerous subversion of lawful authority and undermine the fair conduct of the Russia investigation. Senators Jeff Flake and Lindsey Graham cautioned that dismissing Mueller would signal “the beginning of the end of his presidency,” emphasizing the need to respect the investigative authority of the FBI and the special counsel. The GOP also voiced concern for the harm such a move could cause to the nation’s democratic institutions and to victims of potential collusion, urging that Mueller’s team be allowed to work without interference. At the same time, Republicans stressed the fairness of the probe, rejecting claims that it is a politically motivated witch hunt and condemning Trump’s attacks on the FBI, former Deputy Director Andrew McCabe, and former Director James Comey as defamatory and harmful. White House counsel denied any plans to fire Mueller, while Republican lawmakers pushed legislation to protect the special counsel’s independence, framing the issue as a fight against cheating and abuse of power. END OF SUMMARY.
