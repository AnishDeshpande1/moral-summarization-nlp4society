SUMMARY:

Republican senators have warned President Donald Trump not to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller, who is investigating Russian meddling in the 2016 US election. Trump has renewed his Twitter attacks on the FBI and Mueller's probe, sparking concerns that he may try to dismiss Mueller. Senators Jeff Flake and Lindsey Graham said that firing Mueller would be a major mistake, with Graham warning it would mark "the beginning of the end of his presidency." The White House has denied that Trump is considering firing Mueller. Trump's personal lawyer, John Dowd, has called for an end to the Russia collusion investigation, but Republican Representative Trey Gowdy criticized the move. Republicans have expressed support for Mueller's probe, with Senator Marco Rubio saying the decision to fire former FBI Deputy Director Andrew McCabe was made before the release of a Justice Department report.

END OF SUMMARY.
