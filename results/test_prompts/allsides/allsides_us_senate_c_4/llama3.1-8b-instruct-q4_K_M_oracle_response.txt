SUMMARY:

Republican senators are warning President Donald Trump not to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller, who is conducting an investigation into Russian meddling in the U.S. election. Trump has renewed his attacks on Mueller's probe and the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) since the firing of former FBI Deputy Director Andrew McCabe. Senators Jeff Flake and Lindsey Graham have expressed concerns that if Trump were to dismiss Mueller, it would be a sign of interference with the investigation.

The senators' comments underscored the risks for Trump if he continues to thwart the probe. Flake said he hopes it doesn't "go there" because Congress cannot accept such an action. Graham added that the only reason Mueller could be dismissed is for cause, and he sees none.

Trump's personal lawyer John Dowd has been urging the Justice Department to end the Russia collusion investigation, which Trump calls a "witch hunt." However, Republican Representative Trey Gowdy criticized Dowd's comments, saying they do not reflect an innocent client. The senators' warnings come as Trump continues to express frustration with the probe.

END OF SUMMARY.
