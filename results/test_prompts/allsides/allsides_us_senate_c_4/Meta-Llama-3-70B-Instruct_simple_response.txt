SUMMARY:

Republican senators warned President Trump not to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller, emphasizing that he must let federal investigators probe Russian meddling in the US election. Trump's renewed Twitter attacks on the FBI and Mueller's probe have raised concerns that he may try to dismiss Mueller. Republican senators, including Jeff Flake and Lindsey Graham, cautioned that firing Mueller would be a grave mistake, with Graham stating it would mark "the beginning of the end of his presidency." Despite White House denials, Trump's actions have sparked fears that he may try to thwart the federal probe. The president's personal lawyer, John Dowd, has also urged the Justice Department to end the Russia collusion investigation, which has been met with criticism from Republicans. The moral imperative is clear: Trump must allow the investigation to proceed without interference, and respect the integrity of the democratic process.
