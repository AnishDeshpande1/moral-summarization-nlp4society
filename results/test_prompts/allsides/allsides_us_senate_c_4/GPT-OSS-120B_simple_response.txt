SUMMARY:  
Republican senators warned President Trump that firing Special Counsel Robert Mueller would betray the rule of law and threaten the integrity of the Russia investigation. Jeff Flake and Lindsey Graham cautioned that dismissing Mueller could signal “the beginning of the end” of his presidency, while House Speaker Paul Ryan’s office reiterated that Mueller must be allowed to do his job. The White House denied any plan to fire Mueller, but Trump’s tweets continued to denounce the FBI, the probe as a “politically motivated witch hunt,” and to attack former officials Andrew McCabe and James Comey. Intelligence agencies still conclude Russia interfered in the 2016 election, and Mueller’s mandate remains to examine any collusion. Even some Republicans, including Marco Rubio and Rep. Trey Gowdy, rebuked Trump’s attacks and his personal lawyer’s attempts to shut down the probe. The episode underscores a moral conflict: the president’s impulse to silence an inconvenient investigation versus the democratic principle that independent inquiries must be protected from political interference.  

END OF SUMMARY.
