SUMMARY:

Republican senators warned President Donald Trump not to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller, as Trump's Twitter attacks on the FBI and Mueller's probe intensified. The president's comments appeared to be building towards dismissing Mueller, which would mark "the beginning of the end of his presidency," according to Senator Lindsey Graham. Trump's attacks on the FBI leadership, including fired officials James Comey and Andrew McCabe, were seen as an attempt to thwart the federal probe into Russian meddling in the 2016 election. The U.S. intelligence community has concluded that Moscow conducted an influence campaign to sway the election in Trump's favor. Mueller is investigating Russian meddling and possible collusion with the Trump campaign. Republican lawmakers urged Trump to let Mueller do his job without interference, warning that firing him would have severe consequences. Trump's personal lawyer, John Dowd, was criticized for urging the Justice Department to end the probe, which was seen as an attempt to corrupt the process.

END OF SUMMARY.
