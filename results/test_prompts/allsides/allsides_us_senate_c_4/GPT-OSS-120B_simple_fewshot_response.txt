SUMMARY:
Republican senators publicly warned President Donald Trump not to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller, insisting that federal investigators must be allowed to complete the Russia‑meddling probe without presidential interference. Jeff Flake and Lindsey Graham cautioned that dismissing Mueller would signal the “beginning of the end” of Trump’s presidency, while House Speaker Paul Ryan’s spokesperson affirmed Mueller’s right to work unimpeded. The White House denied any plans to remove Mueller, and Trump’s lawyer Ty Cobb reiterated that the president is not discussing a firing. Despite this, Trump continued to attack the FBI, the Mueller investigation and former officials such as James Comey and Andrew McCabe, calling the probe a “politically motivated witch hunt.” Other Republicans, including Marco Rubio and Trey Gowdy, rebuked Trump’s rhetoric and defended the special counsel’s independence. The moral framing underscores a bipartisan concern for upholding the rule of law and preventing executive overreach that could compromise the integrity of the investigation.  
END OF SUMMARY.
