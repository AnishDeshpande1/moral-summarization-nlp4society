SUMMARY: Republican senators warned President Trump not to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller, emphasizing that investigators into Russian election interference must be allowed to work. Senators Jeff Flake and Lindsey Graham warned that dismissing Mueller could end Trump’s presidency, while House Speaker Paul Ryan’s spokesperson affirmed Mueller’s right to do his job. The White House denied Trump was considering Mueller’s removal. Trump continued attacking the FBI, former Director James Comey, and ex‑deputy director Andrew McCabe, calling the Russia probe a “witch hunt.” His lawyer John Dowd urged Justice Department officials to end the investigation, prompting criticism from GOP Rep. Trey Gowdy. Republicans such as Senators Marco Rubio and Lindsey Graham expressed disapproval of McCabe’s firing and stressed that Mueller can only be removed for cause, which they see none. McCabe’s attorney condemned Trump’s tweets as defamatory. The article underscores intra‑party tension as Trump’s frustration with the prolonged probe clashes with Republican calls to preserve the special counsel’s independence. END OF SUMMARY.
