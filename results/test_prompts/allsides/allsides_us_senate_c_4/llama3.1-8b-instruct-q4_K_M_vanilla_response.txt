SUMMARY:

Republican senators, including Jeff Flake and Lindsey Graham, have warned President Trump not to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller, who is investigating Russian meddling in the 2016 US election. The senators said that firing Mueller would mark "the beginning of the end" of Trump's presidency. White House lawyer Ty Cobb also stated that Trump is not considering or discussing Mueller's removal.

Trump has been attacking the FBI and Mueller's probe on Twitter, calling it a "politically motivated witch hunt." However, his comments have drawn criticism from fellow Republicans, including Trey Gowdy and Marco Rubio, who said that Trump's attorney, John Dowd, was doing him a disservice by framing the investigation in a negative way.

The senators have urged Trump to let Mueller do his job, saying that any attempt to interfere with the probe would be unacceptable. The warning comes as Trump has renewed his attacks on Mueller and the FBI since the firing of former deputy director Andrew McCabe two days before he was eligible for retirement.

END OF SUMMARY.
