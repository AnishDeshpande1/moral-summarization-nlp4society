SUMMARY:

Republican senators are warning President Trump not to fire Special Counsel Robert Mueller, who is investigating Russian meddling in the 2016 US election. Trump has renewed his Twitter attacks on the FBI and Mueller's probe, sparking concerns that he may try to dismiss Mueller. Senators Jeff Flake and Lindsey Graham cautioned that firing Mueller would have severe consequences for Trump's presidency. The White House has denied that Trump is considering firing Mueller. The president's attacks on the FBI and Mueller's probe have been met with criticism from fellow Republicans, who argue that the investigation should be allowed to proceed without interference. The moral framing of this article suggests that Trump's actions are an attempt to undermine the rule of law and the integrity of the investigation, and that Republicans are pushing back against his efforts to discredit the probe.
