SUMMARY:
San Francisco’s newly elected sheriff, Paul Miyamoto, announced that the city’s police will no longer cooperate with Immigration and Customs Enforcement, refusing to honor ICE detainers and emphasizing that immigration enforcement is a federal matter. Miyamoto argues the policy protects public safety by encouraging undocumented residents to report crimes without fear of deportation, even as critics point to cases where released illegal‑aliens have reoffended. The move reinforces San Francisco’s long‑standing sanctuary stance, which is already supported by California’s 2018 Values Act that bars state and local agencies from complying with ICE requests. The Trump administration has singled out California as a “dangerous” sanctuary state, citing a high‑profile case in which an illegal‑alien suspect was released under the state law and later killed a man; the Justice Department has also sued a California law limiting private detention facilities. Mayor London Breed condemned the federal attacks, calling them unfair and pledging to continue protecting the city’s immigrant community.

END OF SUMMARY.
