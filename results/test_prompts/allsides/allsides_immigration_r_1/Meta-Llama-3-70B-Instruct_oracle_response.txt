SUMMARY:

San Francisco's newly elected sheriff, Paul Miyamoto, has declared that the city's law enforcement will no longer cooperate with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). This move cements San Francisco's status as a sanctuary jurisdiction, where community members are no longer afraid to report crimes. Miyamoto argues that public safety is compromised when individuals are fearful of coming forward, and that immigration enforcement is a federal matter. The department will not honor ICE detainer requests, even if an illegal alien is in local custody for an unrelated crime. This policy promotes public safety, despite concerns that it may lead to additional crimes being committed by released individuals. The California Values Act already prohibits state and local agencies from working with ICE, and the Trump administration has criticized the state's sanctuary policies, citing cases where illegal aliens have committed crimes after being released. San Francisco Mayor London Breed remains adamant about protecting the city's illegal aliens, vowing to fight against the Trump administration's attacks.

END OF SUMMARY.
