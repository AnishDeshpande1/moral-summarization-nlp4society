SUMMARY:

San Francisco's new sheriff, Paul Miyamoto, has announced that the city's law enforcement will no longer cooperate with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). This move cements San Francisco's status as a sanctuary jurisdiction, prioritizing public safety over immigration enforcement. Miyamoto argues that the policy promotes public safety, as community members may be afraid to report crimes if they fear deportation. Despite documented cases of illegal aliens committing additional crimes after release, the sheriff's department will not honor ICE detainer requests. This directive is in line with California's Values Act, which prohibits state and local agencies from working with ICE. President Trump has criticized California's sanctuary policies, citing the dangers they pose. However, San Francisco Mayor London Breed remains committed to protecting illegal aliens, vowing to fight against the Trump administration's attacks. The city's stance is rooted in a moral imperative to prioritize public safety and protect its community members.

END OF SUMMARY.
