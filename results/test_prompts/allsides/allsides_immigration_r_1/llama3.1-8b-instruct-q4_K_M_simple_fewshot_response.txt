SUMMARY:
San Francisco's newly elected sheriff, Paul Miyamoto, has announced that his department will no longer cooperate with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). This decision further solidifies San Francisco's status as a sanctuary jurisdiction, where local authorities refuse to honor immigration detainers lodged by federal agents. According to Miyamoto, this policy promotes public safety by allowing community members to report crimes without fear of deportation. However, critics argue that releasing undocumented immigrants back into the public can lead to additional crimes being committed. The Trump administration has taken a strong stance against sanctuary policies, including California's State Values Act and a recent complaint filed against a state law prohibiting private detention facilities. San Francisco Mayor London Breed maintains that the city will continue to protect its residents, regardless of immigration status.
