SUMMARY: San Francisco Sheriff Paul Miyamoto announced that his department will cease all cooperation with U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE), refusing to honor federal immigration detainers. Miyamoto argues the policy enhances public safety by encouraging community members to report crimes without fear of immigration repercussions. The move aligns with California’s 2018 Values Act, which already prohibits state and local agencies from complying with ICE detainer requests, reinforcing San Francisco’s status as a sanctuary city. President Donald Trump has criticized California’s sanctuary policies, citing a high‑profile case where an illegal immigrant released under the state law later committed murder. The Trump administration recently filed a DOJ complaint against a California law banning private detention facilities, further targeting the state’s immigration stance. San Francisco Mayor London Breed denounced the federal attacks, affirming the city’s commitment to protecting its residents, including undocumented immigrants. END OF SUMMARY.
