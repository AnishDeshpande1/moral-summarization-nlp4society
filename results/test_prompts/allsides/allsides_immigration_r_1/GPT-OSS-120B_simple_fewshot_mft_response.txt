SUMMARY:
San Francisco’s newly elected sheriff, Paul Miyamoto, announced that his department will cease all cooperation with Immigration and Customs Enforcement, refusing to honor ICE detainers and treating immigration enforcement as a purely federal matter. The move reinforces the city’s sanctuary stance, arguing that public‑safety depends on residents feeling safe to report crimes without fear of deportation—a care‑oriented justification that challenges the authority of the federal government. Critics, including President Trump and Attorney General Barr, portray the policy as subversive and unfair, claiming it lets criminal‑recorded illegal aliens back onto the streets and harms victims’ families. San Francisco officials, led by Mayor London Breed, counter that the federal attacks are unjust and that the sanctuary law upholds fairness by protecting vulnerable community members from a punitive federal agenda. The clash highlights a broader battle over who holds legitimate authority, how societies safeguard the vulnerable, and whether the sanctuary policy is a just defense or a cheating of immigration law.

END OF SUMMARY.
