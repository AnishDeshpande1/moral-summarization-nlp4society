SUMMARY:

San Francisco's newly elected Sheriff Paul Miyamoto has announced that his department will no longer cooperate with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). This decision further solidifies the city's status as a sanctuary jurisdiction, where local authorities do not assist in federal immigration enforcement. According to Miyamoto, this policy promotes public safety by allowing community members to report crimes without fear of deportation.

The California Values Act, which has been in effect since 2018, already prohibits state and local agencies from honoring ICE detainer requests. This means that illegal aliens cannot be directly transferred into ICE custody, even if they are arrested for unrelated crimes.

Mayor London Breed, a Democrat, defended the city's protection of illegal aliens, stating that San Francisco is being unfairly targeted by the Trump administration. The Department of Justice has filed a complaint against a state law that prohibits private detention facilities, further escalating tensions between California and the federal government.

END OF SUMMARY.
