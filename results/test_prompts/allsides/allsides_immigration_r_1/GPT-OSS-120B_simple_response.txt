SUMMARY:  
San Francisco’s newly elected sheriff, Paul Miyamoto, announced that the city’s police will no longer cooperate with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE), refusing to honor federal immigration detainers. Miyamoto argues that keeping local law enforcement focused on public safety—rather than immigration enforcement—encourages community members to report crimes without fear of deportation. The policy aligns with California’s 2018 Values Act, which already bars state and local agencies from transferring detainees to ICE. Critics, including former President Donald Trump, claim sanctuary policies endanger public safety, citing a high‑profile case in which an undocumented suspect released under the law later killed a victim. The Trump administration has intensified its challenge to California’s sanctuary stance, filing a DOJ complaint against a state law that bans private detention facilities. San Francisco Mayor London Breed denounced the federal attacks as unfair targeting, reaffirming the city’s commitment to protect all residents, including undocumented immigrants. The clash underscores a broader moral debate: whether prioritizing immigrant protection compromises community safety or upholds the city’s values of inclusivity and trust.  

END OF SUMMARY.
