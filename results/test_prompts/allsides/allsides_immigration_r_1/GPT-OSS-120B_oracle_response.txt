SUMMARY:  
San Francisco’s newly elected sheriff, Paul Miyamoto, announced that his department will no longer cooperate with Immigration and Customs Enforcement. He said the agency will ignore ICE detainer requests, arguing that forcing undocumented residents to stay in custody would make community members afraid to report crimes and would undermine public safety. The policy reinforces the city’s sanctuary stance, even though California’s Values Act already prohibits local agencies from honoring such detainers. President Trump has used California as an example of the dangers of sanctuary policies, citing a case in which an undocumented suspect was released and later killed a victim; the administration has even filed a federal complaint targeting state laws that limit private detention facilities. San Francisco Mayor London Breed condemned the federal attacks, declaring the city will fight to protect its residents and resist being targeted.  

END OF SUMMARY.
