SUMMARY:

San Francisco's newly elected sheriff, Paul Miyamoto, has announced that the city's law enforcement will no longer cooperate with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). This move further solidifies San Francisco's status as a sanctuary jurisdiction. Miyamoto argues that the policy promotes public safety, as it allows community members to feel comfortable reporting crimes without fear of deportation. However, critics argue that this policy puts public safety at risk, citing cases of illegal aliens committing additional crimes after being released from local custody. The directive is seen as redundant, given California's existing laws prohibiting cooperation with ICE. President Trump has highlighted California as an example of the dangers of sanctuary policies, and the Department of Justice has filed a complaint against a state law prohibiting private detention facilities. Despite opposition from the Trump administration, San Francisco Mayor London Breed remains committed to protecting illegal aliens in the city.

END OF SUMMARY.
