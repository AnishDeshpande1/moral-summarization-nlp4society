SUMMARY:

San Francisco's newly elected Sheriff, Paul Miyamoto, has announced that the city's law enforcement will no longer cooperate with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). This move solidifies San Francisco's status as a sanctuary jurisdiction. Miyamoto's department will not honor immigration detainers lodged by federal agents, citing public safety concerns and the need to build trust with the community. This policy is in line with California's Values Act, which prohibits state and local agencies from working with ICE. The Trump administration has criticized California's sanctuary policies, with President Trump highlighting the dangers of such policies in his State of the Union address. San Francisco Mayor London Breed has vowed to protect the city's illegal aliens, despite the Trump administration's opposition.

END OF SUMMARY.
