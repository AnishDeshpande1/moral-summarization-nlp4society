He argued that refusing to honor immigration detainers is a matter of public safety, saying “you can’t have a safe community if community members fear coming to us to report crimes.” Miyamoto framed the policy as a defense of care for residents and a rejection of federal authority that would subvert local safety. Critics, including President Trump, claim the sanctuary stance unfairly protects illegal aliens and targets the city, while Mayor London Breed called the federal attacks unfair and emphasized the city’s resilience and commitment to protect all people.
