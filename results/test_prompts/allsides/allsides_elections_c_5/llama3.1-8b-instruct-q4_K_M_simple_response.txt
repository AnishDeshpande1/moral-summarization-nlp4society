SUMMARY:

In the aftermath of Hurricane Sandy, President Obama emphasized the importance of the federal government's role in disaster relief, promising to do everything possible to help affected areas recover. In contrast, Mitt Romney has advocated for a more decentralized approach, arguing that local governments and voluntary efforts should take precedence over federal intervention. However, Romney's comments on reducing federal spending have raised questions about his commitment to funding disaster relief programs like FEMA. Republican Governor Chris Christie of New Jersey praised the federal response to the storm, but some Republicans are still seeking to reduce FEMA's budget.

The debate highlights the differing views between Obama and Romney on the role of government in times of crisis. Obama believes that a strong federal presence is essential for providing relief and support to affected areas, while Romney emphasizes individual action and local responsibility.
