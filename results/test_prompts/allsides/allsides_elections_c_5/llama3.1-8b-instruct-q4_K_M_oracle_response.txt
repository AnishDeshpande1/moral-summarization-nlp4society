SUMMARY: President Obama expressed his thoughts and prayers for those affected by Sandy, while also offering tangible help through the full resources of the federal government. He emphasized that America stands behind them and will do everything to help them get back on their feet. In contrast, Mitt Romney's approach focuses on individual action and voluntary efforts, as exemplified by a canned food drive in Ohio. However, during a Republican primary debate last year, Romney suggested decentralizing disaster relief and reducing federal spending. Despite this, state disaster officials have praised the Obama administration's response to Sandy, with New Jersey Governor Chris Christie expressing gratitude for the president's personal attention. The FEMA budget remains a target for Republicans seeking to reduce federal spending, but President Obama will continue to advocate for an active federal role in helping those affected by disasters. Borrowing $1.6 trillion more than taking in, Romney had previously argued that reducing unnecessary federal programs is crucial to securing the future for their children. END OF SUMMARY.
