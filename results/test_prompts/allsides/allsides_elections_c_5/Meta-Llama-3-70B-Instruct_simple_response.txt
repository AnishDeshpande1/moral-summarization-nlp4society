SUMMARY:

The debate over the government's role in disaster relief has been underscored by Hurricane Sandy. President Obama has offered the full resources of the federal government to affected areas, emphasizing the importance of government aid in times of crisis. In contrast, Republicans like Mitt Romney have advocated for a more decentralized approach, relying on local governments and voluntary efforts. Romney has suggested that disaster relief should be sent back to the states or even the private sector, sparking concerns about the adequacy of funding for FEMA. However, even Republican Governor Chris Christie of New Jersey has praised the federal response to Sandy, thanking President Obama for his personal attention to the disaster. The debate highlights the moral imperative of government responsibility in times of crisis, with Obama's approach emphasizing the importance of collective action and Romney's approach prioritizing individual initiative.

END OF SUMMARY.
