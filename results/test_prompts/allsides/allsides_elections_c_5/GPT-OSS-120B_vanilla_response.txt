SUMMARY: President Obama responded to Hurricane Sandy by offering prayers and mobilizing the full resources of the federal government, emphasizing that “America’s with you.” He highlighted FEMA’s proactive role under director Craig Fugate, promising to cut red tape and personally intervene if states encounter federal roadblocks. Republicans, exemplified by Mitt Romney, stressed local and private‑sector relief, citing a canned‑food drive and advocating for decentralized disaster aid. Romney has previously argued for shifting disaster responsibilities away from the federal level and cutting FEMA spending, though he avoided the topic during the recent event. Despite partisan differences, GOP Governor Chris Christie praised the federal response, and Obama plans to tour New Jersey to reinforce the case for strong federal involvement. The debate underscores the ongoing partisan split over the size and role of federal disaster assistance. END OF SUMMARY.
