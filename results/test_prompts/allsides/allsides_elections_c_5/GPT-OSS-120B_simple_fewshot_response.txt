SUMMARY:
The article frames the response to Hurricane Sandy as a moral test of government responsibility. President Obama’s offer of “thoughts and prayers” is followed by a concrete pledge to mobilize the full resources of the federal government, portraying a compassionate, collective effort to help victims get back on their feet. In contrast, Republican Mitt Romney emphasizes individual initiative and decentralized, voluntary aid, suggesting that disaster relief should be stripped back to the states or private sector. While Romney’s rhetoric praises personal responsibility, the piece highlights his reluctance to address FEMA funding and his broader call for cuts to federal disaster programs, casting his stance as morally indifferent to the urgent needs of storm‑hit communities. The article notes bipartisan praise from Republican Governor Chris Christie for the federal response, underscoring the effectiveness of Obama’s active federal role. By juxtaposing Obama’s decisive, government‑led assistance with Romney’s limited‑government approach, the piece morally endorses robust federal intervention over partisan calls for austerity in times of crisis.  
END OF SUMMARY.
