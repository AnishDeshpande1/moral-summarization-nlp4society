SUMMARY: President Obama's response to Hurricane Sandy highlights the debate over the government's role in disaster relief. He offered prayers and help to those affected, emphasizing the importance of an active federal presence. In contrast, Mitt Romney's approach focuses on local efforts and voluntary action, with a parable about cleaning one's lane contributing to the larger effort. While Romney did not suggest that individual action is a substitute for government support, he has previously advocated for decentralizing disaster relief and reducing federal spending. State officials have praised the federal response under Obama, who has promised to keep red tape from hindering recovery efforts. The FEMA budget remains an attractive target for Republicans seeking to cut federal spending, with Romney stating that the country cannot afford certain programs without jeopardizing its future. Obama's continued focus on delivering aid and attention to storm victims underscores his commitment to providing relief.

END OF SUMMARY.
