SUMMARY:
President Obama offered thoughts and prayers to Sandy victims and pledged the full help of the federal government, saying “America’s with you” and promising to keep red tape from blocking recovery. He named FEMA director Craig Fugate, a former state official, to lead the effort and told governors they could call him directly if they were getting a “no” from Washington. The administration’s response has been delivering aid quickly, earning praise from Republican Gov. Chris Christie. By contrast, Republicans such as Mitt Romney emphasized voluntary action and urged that disaster relief be as decentralized as possible, arguing that the federal government should keep spending down and avoid borrowing that could jeopardize future generations. Romney’s comments on cutting FEMA funding were ignored at his canned‑food drive. Obama will continue to make the case for an active federal role by touring hard‑hit New Jersey, while the debate over the proper balance of government and private help persists.  
END OF SUMMARY.
