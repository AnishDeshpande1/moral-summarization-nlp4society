SUMMARY: President Obama responded to Hurricane Sandy with both prayers and a promise of robust federal assistance, emphasizing that “America’s with you” and that the government will help citizens get back on their feet. He framed the federal response as a moral duty, appointing experienced FEMA chief Craig Fugate to cut red tape and offering direct presidential help to state leaders. In contrast, Republican candidate Mitt Romney highlighted the moral virtue of individual and local action, sponsoring a canned‑food drive and recalling a high‑school story about collective responsibility. While Romney praised volunteer effort, he reiterated his belief that disaster relief should be as decentralized as possible, urging a shift of resources from the federal level to states and the private sector. Even Republican Governor Chris Christie lauded the federal response, underscoring bipartisan appreciation of effective aid. Yet Romney’s earlier remarks about trimming FEMA spending reveal a tension between fiscal conservatism and the moral imperative to support disaster victims. Obama’s continued advocacy for an active federal role underscores the ethical stance that government must step in when communities are crippled by nature’s force. END OF SUMMARY.
