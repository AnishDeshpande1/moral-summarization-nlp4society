**SUMMARY:**
President Obama responded to Hurricane Sandy with prayers and concrete help, emphasizing that the federal government is a critical vehicle for relief. He pledged personal attention, cutting red tape and promising that mayors and governors could call him directly. Republicans, led by Mitt Romney, highlighted faith in voluntary action and the virtue of individual responsibility, citing a canned‑food drive and a high‑school story about cleaning a football field lane by lane. Romney argues that disaster relief should be as decentralized as possible, insisting that returning power to states—and even the private sector—is the right direction and “better” for the future of our kids. While some state officials have clashed with federal agencies, FEMA director Craig Fugate’s experience has eased tensions, earning praise from Gov. Chris Christie for the administration’s outstanding response. Nonetheless, Republicans remain focused on reducing the federal budget and questioning what can be afforded without jeopardizing future generations. Obama will continue to make the case for an active federal role by touring hard‑hit New Jersey.
