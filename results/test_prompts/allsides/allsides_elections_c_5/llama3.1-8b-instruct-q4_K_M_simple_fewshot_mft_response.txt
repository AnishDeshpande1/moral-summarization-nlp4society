SUMMARY:

President Obama's administration is emphasizing the importance of an active federal role in disaster relief, particularly in the aftermath of Hurricane Sandy. In contrast, Mitt Romney has expressed skepticism about the federal government's involvement, suggesting that disaster relief should be decentralized and privatized. Romney's comments are at odds with his current actions, as he sponsored a canned food drive for storm victims but ignored questions about FEMA funding. Obama's approach, on the other hand, prioritizes the federal government's role in providing aid and support to affected communities. The moral implications of this debate highlight the tension between different visions of governance: one that emphasizes individual responsibility and private initiative (Romney), and another that relies on the authority and resources of the federal government (Obama). As Obama tours hard-hit areas, his commitment to an active federal role in disaster relief underscores the importance of collective action and shared responsibility in times of crisis.

END OF SUMMARY.
