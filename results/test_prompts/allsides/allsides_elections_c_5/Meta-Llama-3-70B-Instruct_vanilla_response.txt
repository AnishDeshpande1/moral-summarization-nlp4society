SUMMARY:

President Obama has pledged the full resources of the federal government to help those affected by Hurricane Sandy, emphasizing the importance of government support in times of crisis. In contrast, Republicans, including Mitt Romney, have traditionally advocated for a more limited federal role, instead relying on local governments and voluntary efforts. Romney has previously suggested that disaster relief should be decentralized and that the federal government should focus on cutting spending. However, in the wake of Sandy, Romney has avoided discussing FEMA funding and has instead focused on individual efforts, such as a canned food drive. Meanwhile, Republican Governor Chris Christie of New Jersey has praised the federal response, thanking President Obama for his personal attention to the disaster. The debate highlights the differing views on the role of government in disaster relief.

END OF SUMMARY.
