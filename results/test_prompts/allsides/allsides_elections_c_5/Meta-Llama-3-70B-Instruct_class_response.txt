SUMMARY:

President Obama offered his thoughts and prayers to those affected by Hurricane Sandy, promising the full resources of the federal government to help them get back on their feet. In contrast, Republicans like Mitt Romney put more faith in local government and voluntary efforts, citing the virtue of individual action. Romney sponsored a canned food drive and told a parable about the importance of cleaning up one's own "lane" to contribute to the larger effort. However, Romney has previously suggested that disaster relief should be decentralized, and even considered cutting FEMA funding to reduce borrowing and avoid jeopardizing the future for our kids. Meanwhile, President Obama has promised to keep red tape from getting in the way of recovery, and has received praise from Republican Gov. Chris Christie for the federal response. The president will continue to make the case for an active federal role in disaster relief.

END OF SUMMARY.
