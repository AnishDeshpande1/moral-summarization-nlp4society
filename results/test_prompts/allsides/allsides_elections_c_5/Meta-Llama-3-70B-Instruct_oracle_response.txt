SUMMARY:

President Obama offered "thoughts and prayers" to those affected by Hurricane Sandy, along with the full resources of the federal government, emphasizing that "America's with you" and promising to "help" victims get back on their feet. In contrast, Republicans like Mitt Romney put more faith in local government and voluntary efforts, citing the virtue of individual action. Romney's past comments, however, suggest that he would consider cutting FEMA funding, which could "jeopardize the future for our kids." Despite this, Romney ignored questions about FEMA funding at a canned food drive, instead highlighting the importance of individual action. Meanwhile, Obama has promised to "keep red tape from getting in the way of recovery" and has been praised by Republican Gov. Chris Christie for the federal response. The debate highlights the role of government in disaster relief, with Obama advocating for an active federal role and Romney suggesting decentralization.

END OF SUMMARY.
