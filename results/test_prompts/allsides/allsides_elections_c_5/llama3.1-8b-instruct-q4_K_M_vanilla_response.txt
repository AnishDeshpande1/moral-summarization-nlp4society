SUMMARY:

President Obama offered his support for those affected by Hurricane Sandy, promising the full resources of the federal government. He emphasized that America is standing behind them and will do everything to help them recover. In contrast, Mitt Romney, during a Republican primary debate, argued that disaster relief should be decentralized as possible, with a focus on local governments and private sector efforts.

Romney's stance on FEMA funding is now being scrutinized in light of the storm. He suggested cutting federal programs to reduce spending, but ignored questions about FEMA funding at a canned food drive for storm victims. Republican Governor Chris Christie of New Jersey praised the federal response to the disaster, crediting President Obama and FEMA Administrator Craig Fugate with their outstanding efforts.

The debate over government's role in disaster relief is highlighting the differences between the two candidates' approaches. While Obama sees the federal government as a critical vehicle for help, Romney believes that local governments and private sector efforts can accomplish more.

END OF SUMMARY.
