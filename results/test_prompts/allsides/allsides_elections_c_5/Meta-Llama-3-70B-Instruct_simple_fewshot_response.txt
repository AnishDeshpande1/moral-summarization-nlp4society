SUMMARY:

The aftermath of Hurricane Sandy has highlighted the debate over the government's role in disaster relief. President Obama has emphasized the importance of the federal government in providing aid, stating that "America's with you" and promising to do everything possible to help those affected. In contrast, Republicans, including Mitt Romney, have historically advocated for a more decentralized approach, with a greater reliance on local government and voluntary efforts. While Romney did not suggest that voluntary efforts alone are a substitute for government aid, his past comments have implied that disaster relief should be as decentralized as possible. However, in the wake of Sandy, even Republican Gov. Chris Christie of New Jersey has praised the federal response, thanking President Obama for his personal attention to the crisis. The moral framing of this debate suggests that the government has a critical role to play in providing aid to those in need, and that a more decentralized approach may not be sufficient to address the scale of disasters like Sandy.

END OF SUMMARY.
