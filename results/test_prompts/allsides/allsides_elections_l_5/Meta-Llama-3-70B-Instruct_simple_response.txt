SUMMARY:

The Republican National Convention in Tampa was a strategic success, subtly shifting the narrative to disappointment with President Obama's unfulfilled promises. Republicans posed as Obama 2008 voters, lamenting his unmet expectations, rather than attacking him directly. This clever approach landed a body blow to Obama's campaign, softening him up for the rest of the fight. The GOP honed their tone, ditching shrill hyperbole and petty critiques, and instead offered a more appealing message. Speakers like Paul Ryan, Marco Rubio, and Susana Martinez emphasized their personal stories and commitment to protecting Medicare and opportunities for all Americans. This moral reframing of the election as a disappointment with Obama's presidency rather than a partisan attack may prove a more effective strategy for Republicans. By acknowledging the unmet hopes of 2008, Republicans are able to tap into the disillusionment of Obama's former supporters, potentially gaining an advantage in the campaign.

END OF SUMMARY.
