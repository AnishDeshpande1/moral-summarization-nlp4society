SUMMARY:
The Republican National Convention in Tampa was seen as a success by the GOP, but Obama's team is downplaying its impact. Republicans used a new strategy to attack Obama, focusing on his unfulfilled promises and disappointing voters who supported him in 2008. By posing as former Obama supporters, they aimed to shift momentum and soften up the opponent. The tone at the convention was more subdued than expected, with less hyperbole and petty criticism. Instead, Republicans used prime-time speeches to appeal to voters' emotions, highlighting their own personal stories and highlighting opportunities for social mobility in America.

Republican speakers like Paul Ryan, Marco Rubio, and Susana Martinez emphasized their own rags-to-riches stories, painting the GOP as a party that cares about its citizens. Mitt Romney's acceptance speech also focused on Obama's unfulfilled promises, rather than directly attacking him. The new strategy may be more effective in changing public opinion than previous attacks.

END OF SUMMARY.
