SUMMARY:

The Republican National Convention aimed to shift the narrative on President Barack Obama, framing him as a leader who has failed to live up to his promises from 2008. Republicans presented themselves as disappointed voters, lamenting Obama's unfulfilled potential and lack of progress in addressing key issues such as the economy and healthcare. By adopting this tone, they were able to subtly shift momentum and soften their opponent, creating an opportunity for Mitt Romney to present himself as a more effective leader who can help voters achieve their goals.

The convention was notable for its emphasis on protecting American values and institutions, with speakers such as Paul Ryan and Marco Rubio highlighting the opportunities that the US affords to its citizens. The tone of the convention was characterized by appeals to shared values and a focus on protecting vulnerable populations, including seniors and minorities. Overall, the Republican National Convention aimed to present Romney as a leader who can protect and help Americans achieve their full potential.

END OF SUMMARY.
