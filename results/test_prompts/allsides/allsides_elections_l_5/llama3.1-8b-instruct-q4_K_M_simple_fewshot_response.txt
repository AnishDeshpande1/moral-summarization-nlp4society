SUMMARY:
At the Republican National Convention, Republicans employed a subtle yet effective strategy against President Obama by posing as disappointed supporters of his 2008 message of hope. They framed their criticism not as attacks on Obama's policies, but rather as lamentations of his unfulfilled promises to heal the planet and mend the economy. By adopting this tone, they aimed to shift momentum and soften up Obama in a way that may not be immediately apparent to casual observers. Gone was the shrill hyperbole and petty critiques of Obama's every move; instead, Republicans presented themselves as advocates for the American Dream who have been disappointed by Obama's failure to deliver on his promises. This strategic approach may prove more damaging to Obama than direct attacks, as it speaks to voters' lingering hopes and expectations from 2008.

END OF SUMMARY.
