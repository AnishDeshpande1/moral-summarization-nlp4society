SUMMARY: The article critiques the Republican National Convention’s strategy, noting that while Obama’s campaign dismissed the event as a failure, the GOP employed a subtler, more effective message. Rather than overt attacks, Republicans framed the convention around “disappointment,” positioning themselves as former Obama supporters disillusioned by unfulfilled promises on the economy, healthcare, and climate change. Speakers like Mitt Romney, Marco Rubio, and Paul Ryan emphasized the perceived gap between Obama’s 2008 optimism and his record, portraying him as a “bad president” rather than a bad person. The convention softened its tone, avoiding hyperbole and instead highlighting personal stories and bipartisan‑styled appeals, which could resonate with swing voters. The piece warns that this nuanced shift may pose a greater challenge to Obama’s re‑election than the usual partisan attacks. END OF SUMMARY.
