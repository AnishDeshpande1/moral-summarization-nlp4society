SUMMARY:

The Republican National Convention in Tampa has been spun by Obama's team as a complete miss, but Republicans have subtly shifted their messaging to pose as disappointed 2008 Obama voters. They lamented Obama's unfulfilled expectations, highlighting the faded hope he offered for mending the economy, transforming politics, and saving the earth. Mitt Romney and Marco Rubio delivered strategic body blows, criticizing Obama's presidency without attacking him personally. The tone was honed, with shrill hyperbole and petty critiques disappearing. The convention featured an appealing young VP candidate, Paul Ryan, and diverse speakers like Marco Rubio and Susana Martinez, who spoke of the US's opportunities. While tough criticism was present, the convention sometimes resembled a Democratic gathering. Republicans' new strategy may not be evident to casual observers, but it could gain momentum and soften up Obama for the rest of the fight.

END OF SUMMARY.
