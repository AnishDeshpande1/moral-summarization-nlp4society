SUMMARY:  
The Republican National Convention in Tampa was framed by Obama’s team as a total failure, yet the GOP delivered a subtler, more dangerous message: disappointment with President Obama’s unfulfilled promises. Rather than the usual barrage of attacks, Republicans positioned themselves as former supporters who felt let down, suggesting that the hope Obama offered for the economy, politics, and the planet has faded. Mitt Romney pledged to “help you and your family,” while Sen. Marco Rubio warned that Obama is “a bad president,” and Paul Ryan vowed to “protect Medicare for seniors.” This shift from hyperbole to a quiet, moral appeal aims to erode Obama’s momentum and soften his base for the upcoming campaign months, with super‑PACs likely amplifying the theme. The convention’s tone, featuring diverse speakers who highlighted personal role models and the United States’ opportunity to “protect” and “help” its citizens, resembled a Democratic gathering more than a partisan rally.  

END OF SUMMARY.
