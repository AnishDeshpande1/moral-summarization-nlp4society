SUMMARY:  
The author frames the Republican National Convention as a moral rebuke to President Obama, casting the event not as a political loss for the GOP but as a calculated moral indictment of Obama’s broken promises. While Obama’s team dismisses the convention as a “miss,” the author argues that Republicans subtly shifted their rhetoric from petty attacks to a deeper narrative of disappointment, portraying Obama as the betrayer of the hopeful 2008 vision. By positioning themselves as former allies lamenting unfulfilled expectations, the GOP delivers a strategic “body blow” that attacks Obama’s moral credibility rather than his policies. The convention’s tone softened, with speakers like Paul Ryan, Marco Rubio, and Susana Martinez invoking personal stories of American opportunity, thereby reinforcing the moral theme that Obama has failed to deliver on his promises to the American people. This nuanced, morally charged messaging is presented as a more dangerous threat to Obama’s campaign than overt criticism, aiming to erode voter trust in his character and leadership.  

END OF SUMMARY.
