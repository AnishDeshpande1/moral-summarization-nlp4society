SUMMARY:

The Republican National Convention in Tampa was a strategic success, subtly shifting the momentum against President Obama. While Obama's team spun the convention as a complete miss, Republicans cleverly posed as Obama 2008 voters, lamenting his unfulfilled expectations. This "disappointment theme" taps into the power of Obama's failed promises to mend the economy, transform politics, and save the earth. Mitt Romney and Marco Rubio's speeches exemplified this approach, focusing on Obama's shortcomings as a president rather than attacking him personally. The convention's tone was honed, replacing shrill hyperbole with a more measured criticism. Even prime-time speeches resembled a Democratic gathering, with appealing young candidates and minority leaders sharing their stories of American opportunity. This strategic body blow may not be immediately apparent, but it softens up Obama for the rest of the fight. The moral framing of this issue is clear: Obama's failed promises have led to widespread disappointment, and Republicans are capitalizing on this sentiment to gain an advantage in the election.

END OF SUMMARY.
