SUMMARY:

The Alabama Senate has passed a bill banning nearly all abortions in the state, sending it to Governor Kay Ivey's desk for signature. The bill, which passed with a 25-6 margin, would ban abortions in all instances except when necessary to save a mother's life. It would also criminalize performing an abortion, punishable by 10 to 99 years in prison. The bill's proponents hope it will lead to a Supreme Court battle to overturn Roe v. Wade. The legislation is the most restrictive abortion law in the country, with no exceptions for rape and incest. Critics, including NARAL Pro-Choice America, have condemned the bill, arguing it puts women's lives at risk and threatens the landmark Roe v. Wade decision.

END OF SUMMARY.
