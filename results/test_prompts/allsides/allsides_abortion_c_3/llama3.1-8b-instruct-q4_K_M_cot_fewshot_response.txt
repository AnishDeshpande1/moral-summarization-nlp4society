The Alabama Senate has passed a "total ban" on abortion, sending it to Gov. Kay Ivey's desk for signature. The legislation would prohibit abortions in virtually all instances, including for victims of rape and incest, and only permit the procedure if necessary to save a mother's life. Wade decision, which was previously seen as "settled law." Pro-abortion rights groups have condemned the move, saying it would "criminalize" and "demean" women by punishing them for seeking an abortion. The Alabama bill is the most restrictive abortion law in the country, and its passage has raised concerns that the Supreme Court will soon be asked to rule on the constitutionality of such laws.
