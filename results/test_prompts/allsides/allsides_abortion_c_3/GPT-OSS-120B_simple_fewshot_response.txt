SUMMARY:
The Alabama Senate approved a near‑total abortion ban, sending a 25‑6 vote to Governor Kay Ivey’s desk. The bill bans abortions in virtually every circumstance—including rape and incest—and threatens doctors with 10 to 99 years in prison, while sparing patients from criminal charges. Lawmaker Terri Collins said the lack of exceptions was intentional, aiming to force a Supreme Court showdown that could overturn Roe v. Wade. Critics argue the measure is an overt attack on women’s autonomy, especially poor and vulnerable women, and a clear attempt to criminalize reproductive choice. NARAL’s Ilyse Hogue condemned the law as a “dangerous and demeaning” weapon that punishes both doctors and women, linking it to the broader right‑wing agenda that celebrated Brett Kavanaugh’s confirmation and threatens to erode settled abortion rights. The legislation, described as the nation’s most restrictive, is portrayed as a calculated move to cement a new, anti‑choice legal order rather than a genuine concern for fetal life.  
END OF SUMMARY.
