SUMMARY:

The Alabama Senate has passed a near-total ban on abortions, sending the bill to Governor Kay Ivey's desk to be signed into law. The legislation, which passed with a 25-6 margin, would ban abortions in virtually all instances, including for victims of rape and incest, and only permit the procedure if necessary to save a mother's life. Anyone performing an abortion could face up to 99 years in prison. The bill's proponents hope it will set up a Supreme Court battle to overturn Roe v. Wade. Critics argue that the law is a total ban on abortion, criminalizing the act and punishing women and doctors. The law is seen as a threat to women's reproductive health and autonomy, particularly for poor women who may not have access to abortions in other states. The passage of the bill is viewed as a chilling effect of Brett Kavanaugh's appointment to the Supreme Court.

END OF SUMMARY.
