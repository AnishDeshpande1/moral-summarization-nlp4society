SUMMARY:  
The Alabama Senate voted 25‑6 to adopt one of the nation’s most draconian abortion bans, sending the bill to Governor Kay Ivey for signature. The measure would outlaw virtually all abortions—including for rape and incest—allowing the procedure only to save a mother’s life, and imposes 10‑to‑99‑year prison terms on providers while sparing patients from criminal charges. Proponents, led by Rep. Terri Collins, argue the law is deliberately strict to force a Supreme Court showdown that could overturn Roe v. Wade. Critics warn the ban criminalizes medical care, punishes women and doctors, and betrays basic compassion for survivors of sexual violence. NARAL’s Ilyse Hogue condemned the vote as a “dangerous and demeaning” attack on women’s rights, recalling past battles over the Court’s composition and noting that Republican leaders now openly celebrate legislation that strips away legal protections for women. The vote reflects a broader national push by several states to impose “heartbeat” bans, but Alabama’s law stands out as the harshest, prompting many to consider leaving the state.  

END OF SUMMARY.
