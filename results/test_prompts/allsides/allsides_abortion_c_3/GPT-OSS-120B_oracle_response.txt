SUMMARY:  
The Alabama Senate **passed** a near‑total abortion ban that would **criminalize** the procedure and allow 10‑to‑99‑year prison terms for providers, while offering no criminal charges for women who undergo it. The bill **outlaws** abortions even for **victims** of **rape** and **incest**, permitting the practice only to save a mother’s life. State Rep. Terri Collins said she **empathized** with **rape** and **incest** **victims**, but removed those exceptions to create a law strong enough to force a federal court showdown aimed at overturning Roe v. Wade. During the heated debate, Republicans **stood** and **applauded** the measure, while opponents **wept** and condemned the **punishing** impact on women and doctors. NARAL’s Ilyse Hogue warned that the **promise** to **outlaw** abortion is being fulfilled, noting that anti‑choice Republicans no longer even **pretend** to respect women’s rights. The legislation now heads to Gov. Kay Ivey for a decision, amid concerns that it could trigger a Supreme Court challenge.
