SUMMARY:

The Alabama Senate has passed a draconian legislation that bans nearly all abortions in the state, sending it to Governor Kay Ivey's desk to be signed into law. This measure, which passed by a 25-6 margin, would criminalize abortions in virtually all instances, including for victims of rape and incest, and would only permit the procedure if necessary to save a mother's life. Anyone performing an abortion could be punished by 10 to 99 years in prison. The bill's proponents, who empathized with survivors of rape and incest, intentionally left out exceptions to force a federal court battle that could overturn the landmark Roe v. Wade decision. This law is the most restrictive abortion law in the country, and its passage has been met with outrage from pro-abortion rights groups, who condemn it as a "total ban on abortion, criminalizing the act and punishing women and doctors." The law's supporters, on the other hand, stood up and applauded, while women wept.

END OF SUMMARY.
