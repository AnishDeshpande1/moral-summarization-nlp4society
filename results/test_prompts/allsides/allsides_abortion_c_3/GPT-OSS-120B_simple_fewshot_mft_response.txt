SUMMARY:
The Alabama Senate voted 25‑6 to enact a near‑total abortion ban, sending the measure to Governor Kay Ivey for signature. The law would outlaw abortions in virtually all cases—including rape and incest—and punish providers with 10 to 99 years in prison, while leaving the woman uncharged. Proponents, led by Rep. Terri Collins, deliberately omitted rape‑incest exceptions to create a strong constitutional challenge aimed at overturning Roe v. Wade. Critics denounce the bill as an abuse of governmental authority that subverts women’s bodily autonomy, inflicts severe harm on survivors of sexual violence, and violates basic fairness by punishing doctors while leaving women defenseless. NARAL’s Ilyse Hogue condemned the measure as a “total ban” that criminalizes abortion, applauds Republican triumph, and underscores the betrayal of women’s rights. State Sen. Vivian Figures warned that such draconian policy will drive residents away. The legislation joins a wave of “heartbeat” bans, but stands out as the nation’s most restrictive, reflecting a strategic use of state power to undermine reproductive freedom and the equitable treatment of women. 
END OF SUMMARY.
