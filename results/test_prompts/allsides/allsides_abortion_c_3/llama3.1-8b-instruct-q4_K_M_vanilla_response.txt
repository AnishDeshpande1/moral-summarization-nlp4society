SUMMARY:
The Alabama Senate passed legislation banning nearly all abortions in the state, sending it to Gov. Kay Ivey's desk for signature. The bill would ban abortions in virtually all instances, including for rape and incest victims, with exceptions only for the mother's life. Anyone performing an abortion could face 10-99 years in prison, but not the person undergoing the procedure. The legislation aims to set up a Supreme Court battle to overturn the landmark Roe v. Wade decision that legalized abortion. Proponents hope other states' restrictive laws will lead to a federal challenge. The bill's passage has been met with criticism from pro-choice groups, who warn it could put women and doctors at risk of prosecution. With 16 states considering similar bans, NARAL Pro-Choice America President Ilyse Hogue expressed concern that the current Supreme Court makeup puts Roe v. Wade in jeopardy.

END OF SUMMARY.
