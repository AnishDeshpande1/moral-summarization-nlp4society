SUMMARY: The Alabama Senate voted 25‑6 to pass a near‑total abortion ban, sending the bill to Governor Kay Ivey for signature. The law would prohibit abortions in virtually all cases, including rape and incest, allowing the procedure only to save a mother’s life. Providers could face 10 to 99 years in prison, though patients would not be criminally charged. The measure, championed by Rep. Terri Collins, omitted rape‑incest exceptions to ensure a strong constitutional challenge aimed at overturning Roe v. Wade. The Senate’s debate was heated, with a prior shouting match when the exception was removed. Critics, including NARAM Pro‑Choice America, condemned the bill as a punitive, extremist move. Alabama’s ban is the most restrictive in the nation, surpassing the “heartbeat” laws in sixteen other states. Lawmakers and activists warned the legislation could drive residents out of the state and intensify the national abortion debate. END OF SUMMARY.
