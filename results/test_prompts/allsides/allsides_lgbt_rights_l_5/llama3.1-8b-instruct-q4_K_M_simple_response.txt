SUMMARY:

The Obama administration has announced that same-sex spouses of federal employees are now eligible for benefits, including health insurance and retirement accounts. This decision comes after the Supreme Court's ruling on the Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA), which declared that all married couples, regardless of their sexual orientation, should be treated equally under federal law. The Office of Personnel Management has issued guidance to executive departments and agencies, outlining access to benefits such as health, life, dental, vision, and long-term care insurance for same-sex spouses.

President Obama hailed the expansion of access to federal benefits as a "critical first step" in implementing the Supreme Court's decision. Attorney General Eric Holder also welcomed the announcement, stating that it will make a "meaningful, positive difference" in the lives of many Americans in same-sex marriages. The administration is working to implement the repeal of DOMA and ensure equal treatment for all married couples under federal law.

END OF SUMMARY.
