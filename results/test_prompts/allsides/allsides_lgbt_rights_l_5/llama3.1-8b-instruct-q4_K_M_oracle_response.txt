SUMMARY:

The Obama administration has announced that same-sex spouses of federal employees will now have access to benefits, including health insurance and retirement accounts, as a result of the Supreme Court's decision to overturn a key provision of the Defense of Marriage Act. This move is a critical step toward implementing equal treatment under federal law for all married couples, regardless of their sexual orientation. The administration is working to make this reality a tangible one, with President Obama directing his team to lead the implementation of the repeal. The expansion of benefits will be available to same-sex spouses who are legally married in any state, ensuring that they have the same access as heterosexual married couples. This change is a significant expansion of access and equality for thousands of gay and lesbian federal employees and their families, who deserve respect, protection, and treatment equal to all other families. The administration's efforts will make a meaningful difference in the lives of many, promoting a positive and inclusive environment for all.

END OF SUMMARY.
