SUMMARY:
The Obama administration announced that same‑sex spouses of federal employees can now apply for the same vision, health, life, dental, retirement and long‑term‑care benefits that opposite‑sex spouses receive. The Office of Personnel Management’s memo, signed by acting director Elaine Kaplan, states that all legally married couples—regardless of where they live—will have equal access to these federal benefits. President Obama praised the move as “a critical first step” toward fully implementing the Supreme Court’s decision that gay and straight marriages must be treated equally under federal law, emphasizing the respect and protection owed to thousands of LGBTQ federal workers and their families. Defense Secretary Chuck Hagel, Attorney General Eric Holder and the Justice Department also endorsed the expansion, calling it a concrete realization of the court’s ruling and a meaningful, positive change for same‑sex couples nationwide. The administration is working to resolve remaining legal details so that the benefits are applied uniformly across all states.

END OF SUMMARY.
