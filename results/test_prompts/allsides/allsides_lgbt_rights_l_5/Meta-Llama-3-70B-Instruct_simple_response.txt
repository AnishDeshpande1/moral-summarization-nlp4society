SUMMARY:

In a landmark move, the Obama administration has announced that same-sex spouses of federal employees can now apply for benefits, including health insurance and retirement accounts, effective immediately. This follows the Supreme Court's overturning of a key provision of the Defense of Marriage Act. The administration's guidance ensures that legally married same-sex spouses will have the same access to benefits as heterosexual married couples. While the memo does not address benefits for same-sex couples who marry in one state but live in another where their union is not legal, the administration appears to be making the case for equal treatment regardless of state laws. President Obama and Attorney General Eric Holder have welcomed the expansion of benefits, emphasizing that all married couples, gay and straight, deserve equal respect and protection under federal law. This move marks a critical step towards implementing the Supreme Court's decision and ensuring equal treatment for all families.

END OF SUMMARY.
