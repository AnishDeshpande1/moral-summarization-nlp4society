SUMMARY:
The Obama administration announced that same‑sex spouses of federal employees can now apply for vision, health, life, dental and long‑term care benefits, marking a **critical** step toward **equal** treatment under federal law. Acting OPM director Elaine Kaplan’s memo affirms that all legally married couples—regardless of state recognition—will receive the same benefits, reflecting the administration’s commitment to **respect** and **protection** for LGBTQ + federal workers and their families. President Obama called the move a **meaningful**, **positive** advance, noting that thousands of gay and lesbian employees deserve the same **entitlement** to benefits as opposite‑sex spouses. Attorney General Eric Holder echoed the sentiment, emphasizing that extending these benefits fulfills the **justice** promised by the Supreme Court’s decision and will make a **positive**, **meaningful** difference in the lives of many.
