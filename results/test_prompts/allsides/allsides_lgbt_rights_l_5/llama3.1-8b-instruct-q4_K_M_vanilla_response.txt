SUMMARY:
The Obama administration announced that same-sex spouses of federal employees can now apply for benefits, including health insurance and retirement accounts. This decision comes two days after the Supreme Court overturned a key provision of the Defense of Marriage Act (DOMA). The Office of Personnel Management has issued guidance to executive departments and agencies on providing access to various benefits, such as health, life, dental, vision, and long-term care insurance. President Obama praised the expansion of access to federal benefits, stating that it is a critical step towards implementing the Supreme Court's decision. Defense Secretary Chuck Hagel also announced that the Pentagon will move quickly to expand benefits to same-sex couples. Attorney General Eric Holder welcomed the announcement, saying that Americans in same-sex marriages are entitled to equal protection and treatment under the law.

END OF SUMMARY.
