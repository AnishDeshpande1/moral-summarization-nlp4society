SUMMARY: The Obama administration announced that same‑sex spouses of federal employees can now apply for the full range of federal benefits—health, vision, dental, life, and long‑term care insurance—as well as retirement accounts, following the Supreme Court’s decision to strike down a key part of DOMA. In a memo to agency heads, Acting OPM Director Elaine Kaplan affirmed that all legally married couples will receive the same benefits, implicitly supporting marriage equality across states. President Obama, emphasizing moral fairness, called the move a “critical first step” toward treating gay and straight couples equally under federal law, noting the service of thousands of LGBTQ + federal workers and their families. Defense Secretary Chuck Hagel and Attorney General Eric Holder echoed this sentiment, stressing that extending benefits fulfills the promise of equal protection and will positively impact many lives. The administration pledged swift, coordinated implementation of the ruling. END OF SUMMARY.
