SUMMARY:

The Obama administration has announced that same-sex spouses of federal employees can now apply for benefits, including health insurance and retirement accounts, effective immediately. This move follows the Supreme Court's decision to overturn a key provision of the Defense of Marriage Act. The Office of Personnel Management has provided guidance on the benefits available to legally married same-sex spouses, which are the same as those offered to heterosexual married couples. While the memo does not address the issue of same-sex couples who marry in one state but live in another where their union is not recognized, the administration appears to be taking a stance that benefits will be available regardless of the state of residence. The president has applauded the expansion of access to federal benefits, stating that all married couples, gay and straight, should be treated equally under federal law. This decision marks a significant step towards implementing the Supreme Court's landmark ruling and ensuring equal treatment for all families.

END OF SUMMARY.
