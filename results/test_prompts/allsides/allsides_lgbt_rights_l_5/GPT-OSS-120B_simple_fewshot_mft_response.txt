SUMMARY:
The Obama administration announced that same‑sex spouses of federal employees can now receive the full suite of federal benefits—health, vision, dental, life and long‑term‑care insurance—as well as retirement accounts, fulfilling the Supreme Court’s recent overturning of DOMA’s Section 3. By issuing an Office of Personnel Management memo that applies to “all legally married couples” without reference to conflicting state laws, the administration asserts federal authority over marital recognition and subverts previous discriminatory practices. The move is framed as a compassionate correction that prevents harm to LGBTQ+ federal workers and their families, ensuring they receive the same care and protection afforded to opposite‑sex couples. It is also presented as a matter of fairness, granting equal treatment and preventing cheating the system that had denied these benefits to gay and lesbian employees. Moreover, the announcement underscores loyalty to the nation’s diverse public‑service members, honoring their service and affirming that their families deserve the same respect and support as any other American family. President Obama, Defense Secretary Hagel, and Attorney General Holder all highlighted the moral imperative of equality, protection, and unity in implementing the ruling swiftly and smoothly.  
END OF SUMMARY.
