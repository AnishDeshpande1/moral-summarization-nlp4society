SUMMARY: The Obama administration announced that same‑sex spouses of federal employees can now apply for the same benefits as opposite‑sex spouses, including health, dental, vision, life, and long‑term care insurance, as well as retirement accounts. This guidance follows the Supreme Court’s decision overturning a key part of the Defense of Marriage Act, extending federal recognition to all legally married couples regardless of state law. Acting OPM director Elaine Kaplan issued a memo to agency heads confirming the benefits, while President Obama, Attorney General Eric Holder, and Defense Secretary Chuck Hagel praised the move as a crucial step toward equal treatment under federal law. The Justice Department will coordinate implementation of the ruling. END OF SUMMARY.
