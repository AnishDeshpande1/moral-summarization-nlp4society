SUMMARY:

The Obama administration has announced that same-sex spouses of federal employees can now apply for benefits, including health insurance and retirement accounts, effective immediately. This follows the Supreme Court's decision to overturn a key provision of the Defense of Marriage Act. The Office of Personnel Management has issued guidance detailing access to various benefits, including health, life, dental, vision, and long-term care insurance, for legally married same-sex spouses. The administration is working to ensure that couples married in one state but living in another where same-sex unions are not legal will also have access to these benefits. President Obama has applauded the expansion of access to federal benefits, stating that thousands of gay and lesbian federal employees and their families deserve equal respect and protection. The Justice Department is leading the implementation of the repeal of Section 3 of DOMA, with the goal of making the promise of equal treatment under the law a reality.

END OF SUMMARY.
