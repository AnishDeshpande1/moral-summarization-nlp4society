SUMMARY:

Jim Obergefell, the named plaintiff in the landmark Obergefell v. Hodges case, anxiously awaits a Supreme Court ruling on employment discrimination cases involving LGBTQ+ individuals. Despite the freedom to marry won four years ago, discrimination remains a threat, and many same-sex couples still lack explicit nondiscrimination protections in their home states. Obergefell has heard stories of gay, transgender, and bisexual individuals facing harassment, job rescission, and firing due to their identity. A win in these cases would protect people from harm and uphold the view that nearly 70% of Americans hold: LGBTQ+ individuals should be legally protected from discrimination. However, even with a victory, comprehensive protections are still needed in areas like housing, health facilities, and public spaces. Lawmakers have another chance to pass legislation prohibiting anti-LGBTQ discrimination, and it's up to all of us to push forward, educate, and share our stories to ensure everyone is treated with dignity and respect.

END OF SUMMARY.
