SUMMARY:

Jim Obergefell, the named plaintiff in the landmark civil rights decision Obergefell v. Hodges, reflects on his anxiety awaiting a Supreme Court ruling on employment discrimination cases against LGBTQ+ individuals. Despite the progress made in marriage equality, Obergefell notes that many same-sex couples still lack explicit nondiscrimination protections in their home states. He shares stories of people who have faced harassment and job loss due to their sexual orientation or gender identity.

Obergefell emphasizes that a win in these cases would be crucial in upholding the rights of LGBTQ+ individuals, but more work needs to be done to pass comprehensive protections in all areas of life. He appeals to lawmakers to take action and proposes a nondiscrimination bill in his home state of Ohio, which has been unsuccessful in passing similar legislation in the past.

The author concludes that everyone deserves dignity and respect regardless of their identity, and that it's up to all of us to push forward for equal rights by organizing, educating, and sharing our stories. END OF SUMMARY.
