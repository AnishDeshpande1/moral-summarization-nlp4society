SUMMARY:

Jim Obergefell, the named plaintiff in the landmark civil rights decision Obergefell v. Hodges, reflects on the anxiety he feels as the Supreme Court considers three cases brought by LGBTQ+ people who were fired from their jobs due to their identity or orientation. Despite the progress made with marriage equality, discrimination remains a threat and many states lack explicit nondiscrimination protections. A win in these employment cases would be a victory for the LGBTQ+ community, protecting them from harm and upholding a view held by nearly 70% of Americans. However, more work is needed to pass comprehensive protections in all areas of life. Ohio, Obergefell's home state, has proposed a nondiscrimination bill with bipartisan support, but lawmakers have failed to act in the past. It's essential to push forward every day, encouraging the Supreme Court and advocating for equal rights through activism and education.

END OF SUMMARY.
