SUMMARY:

Jim Obergefell, the named plaintiff in the landmark civil rights decision Obergefell v. Hodges, is anxiously awaiting a ruling from the Supreme Court on three cases involving LGBTQ+ individuals who were fired from their jobs due to their sexual orientation or gender identity. The cases aim to provide explicit nondiscrimination protections for LGBTQ+ people. Obergefell notes that despite marriage equality being achieved, many same-sex couples still face fears of discrimination in their home states.

A win in the employment discrimination cases would be a significant step forward, but Obergefell emphasizes that more work is needed to pass comprehensive protections for LGBTQ+ individuals in various areas of life, including housing and public spaces. Ohio, where Obergefell resides, lacks vital nondiscrimination protections, and he encourages lawmakers to take action.

Obergefell calls on the community to push forward every day by organizing, educating lawmakers, and sharing personal stories until everyone can thrive. He believes that with momentum building, together we can ensure that everyone is protected from discrimination, regardless of their identity or location.

END OF SUMMARY.
