SUMMARY: Jim Obergefell, the plaintiff who helped secure nationwide marriage equality, now faces another pivotal moment as the U.S. Supreme Court considers three cases of LGBTQ+ employment discrimination. He recounts the relief felt when marriage rights were affirmed and warns that, despite that victory, many LGBTQ+ individuals still endure job loss, harassment, and denial of basic dignity because many states lack explicit nondiscrimination laws. Obergefell argues that a Supreme Court win would reflect the moral consensus of roughly 70 % of Americans who believe LGBTQ+ people deserve legal protection, but stresses that judicial relief alone is insufficient. He urges Congress and state legislatures—highlighting a bipartisan Ohio bill—to enact comprehensive protections covering housing, health care, and public accommodations. Framed as a civil‑rights struggle, his message calls on citizens to rally, educate lawmakers, and share personal stories so that all people can live with dignity and respect, truly belonging to “We the people.” END OF SUMMARY.
