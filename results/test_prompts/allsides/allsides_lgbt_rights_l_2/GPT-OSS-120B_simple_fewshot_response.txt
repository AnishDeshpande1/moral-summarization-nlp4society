SUMMARY:
Jim Obergefell, the plaintiff whose case secured nationwide marriage equality, reflects on the triumph and the ongoing fight for LGBTQ + rights. He recalls the relief felt when the Supreme Court affirmed the right to marry and now awaits another pivotal ruling on three employment‑discrimination cases affecting LGBTQ + workers. Obergefell warns that, despite marriage equality, many LGBTQ + people still face job loss, harassment and a lack of explicit nondiscrimination laws in their states. He argues that a Supreme Court win would protect millions and align the Court with the roughly 70 % of Americans who believe LGBTQ + individuals deserve legal protection. Yet he stresses that judicial victories alone are insufficient; comprehensive statutes are needed to safeguard LGBTQ + people in housing, health care and public life. Returning to Ohio, he notes the state’s failure to enact broad nondiscrimination measures, but points to a new bipartisan bill as a hopeful opportunity. Obergefell calls on citizens to keep pressing lawmakers, sharing personal stories, and urging the Court to do the right thing, so that every person can live with dignity, respect and full equality under “We the People.”
END OF SUMMARY.
