SUMMARY: Jim Obergefell, the plaintiff in the Supreme Court’s 2015 same‑sex marriage ruling, reflects on his experience awaiting that decision and now awaits the Court’s ruling on three employment‑discrimination cases affecting LGBTQ+ workers. He notes that while marriage equality has helped many, the lack of explicit nondiscrimination laws in most states leaves LGBTQ+ people vulnerable to job loss, harassment, and other biases. Obergefell argues that a favorable Supreme Court outcome would protect millions, aligning with the roughly 70% of Americans who support LGBTQ+ nondiscrimination. He stresses that legislative action is also needed to extend protections to housing, health care, and public spaces. Returning to Ohio, he highlights the state’s failure to pass comprehensive LGBTQ+ nondiscrimination legislation, though a bipartisan bill was introduced in September. Obergefell calls for continued advocacy, community organizing, and education of lawmakers to achieve full equality nationwide. END OF SUMMARY.
