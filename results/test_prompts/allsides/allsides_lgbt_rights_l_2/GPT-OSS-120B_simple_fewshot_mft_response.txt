SUMMARY:
Jim Obergefell, the plaintiff who won nationwide marriage equality, reflects on the Supreme Court’s past victory and now anxiously awaits rulings in three new cases about LGBTQ+ workplace discrimination. He stresses that, while marriage rights have advanced, many LGBTQ+ people still face harmful bias—job offers withdrawn, transgender workers fired, and bisexual employees harassed—because state laws lack clear anti‑discrimination authority. Obergefell argues that upholding protections would align the Court with the overwhelming public support for fairness and care, preventing further harm. He also calls on legislators, especially in his home state of Ohio, to subvert the current neglect and enact comprehensive nondiscrimination statutes covering housing, health care and public spaces, framing this as a loyal duty to fellow citizens. Even a Supreme Court win would be only a step; lasting justice requires both institutional authority to protect the vulnerable and collective loyalty to ensure dignity for all LGBTQ+ individuals. END OF SUMMARY.
