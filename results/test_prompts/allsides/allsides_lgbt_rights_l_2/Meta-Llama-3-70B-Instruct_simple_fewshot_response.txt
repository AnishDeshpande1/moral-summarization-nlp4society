SUMMARY:

Jim Obergefell, the plaintiff in the landmark same-sex marriage case, reflects on the ongoing struggle for LGBTQ+ equality. Despite the Supreme Court's ruling in his favor, many states still lack explicit nondiscrimination protections, leaving LGBTQ+ individuals vulnerable to discrimination in employment, housing, and public spaces. Obergefell recounts personal stories of discrimination and harassment, emphasizing the need for comprehensive protections. He urges the Supreme Court to rule in favor of LGBTQ+ individuals in three employment discrimination cases, but notes that even a victory would not be enough. He calls on lawmakers to pass legislation prohibiting anti-LGBTQ discrimination, citing a proposed nondiscrimination bill in his home state of Ohio as an opportunity for progress. Obergefell's moral framing emphasizes the importance of dignity, respect, and equality for all individuals, regardless of their LGBTQ+ identity. He encourages community organizing, education, and storytelling to push forward the fight for equal rights. END OF SUMMARY.
