**SUMMARY:**  
Jim Obergefell, the named plaintiff in *Obergefell v. Hodges*, recalls the *justice* and *freedom* he felt when the Supreme Court granted marriage equality, a decision supported by a supermajority of Americans. He now faces *anxiety* as the Court hears three *LGBTQ+* employment‑discrimination cases, arguing that *discrimination* remains a *threat* to *dignity* and *respect* for millions. A victory would *protect* LGBTQ+ workers from *harm* and align the Court with the *rights* and *equality* that 70 % of the public endorse. Obergefell stresses that, regardless of the outcome, *fairness* demands comprehensive, *unfair*‑free protections in housing, health care, and public spaces. He urges lawmakers, especially in Ohio where *LGBTQ+* people lack explicit safeguards, to pass bipartisan *non‑discrimination* bills, emphasizing *loyalty* to the promise of “*We the people*” and warning that ignoring these issues would be a *betrayal* of that commitment. The call is for both the Court’s *authority* and legislative action to *subvert* ongoing bias and ensure all citizens are treated with *respect* and *dignity*.
