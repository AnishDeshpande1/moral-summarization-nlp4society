SUMMARY: The article condemns a growing Democratic stance that supports unrestricted abortion up to 40 weeks, citing Virginia legislator Kathy Tran and Governor Ralph Northam’s endorsement of a bill that would allow delivery of a full‑term fetus followed by its death. It notes New York’s law permitting abortions at any stage if a provider deems it necessary for a woman’s health, without requiring the pregnancy to threaten health. The author argues that such policies are designed to be unenforceable and misleading, and that leading Democratic presidential candidates back federal legislation that would eliminate state protections for late‑term fetuses, allowing bans only with vague health exceptions. The piece claims the public does not support late‑term abortions, yet Democrats justify them as medically necessary, despite evidence to the contrary. It asserts that terminating viable, healthy fetuses is morally unjust, equating it to killing a two‑month‑old infant, and calls on Republicans, journalists, Catholic bishops, and the Supreme Court to expose and reverse this position. END OF SUMMARY.
