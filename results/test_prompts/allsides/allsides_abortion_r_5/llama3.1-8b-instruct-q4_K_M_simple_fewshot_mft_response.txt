SUMMARY:

A growing number of Democratic politicians are embracing explicit support for abortion "all the way up to 40 weeks," including Virginia state legislator Kathy Tran, who introduced a bill allowing abortion until birth. Moderate Governor Ralph Northam defended the bill by suggesting that in some cases, a full-term child should be delivered and then allowed to die. Many leading Democratic presidential candidates have endorsed similar federal legislation, which would wipe away any state laws protecting unborn children late in pregnancy. These politicians claim that such abortions are only done for compelling medical reasons, but the available evidence does not bear out this contention. The author argues that treating unborn children as non-persons is manifestly unjust and lays moral obligations on all of us to expose the Democrats' indefensible position. Republicans, journalists, Catholic bishops, and even the Supreme Court should take action to reverse this policy of abortion-on-demand.

END OF SUMMARY.
