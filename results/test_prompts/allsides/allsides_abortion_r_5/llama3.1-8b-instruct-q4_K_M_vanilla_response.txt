SUMMARY:

Democratic politicians, including presidential candidates, are increasingly supporting abortion up to 40 weeks of pregnancy. This position is exemplified by Virginia state legislator Kathy Tran's bill, which has the backing of Governor Ralph Northam. Northam defended the bill by suggesting that a full-term child could be delivered and then allowed to die in certain circumstances. Many Democrats have endorsed similar federal legislation that would wipe away state laws protecting unborn children late in pregnancy.

Critics argue that this position is unjust and morally reprehensible, as it treats unborn children differently than two-month-old infants. They also point out that the available evidence does not support the contention that late-term abortions are only done for medical reasons such as severe fetal abnormality or unviability. The Democratic stance on abortion has sparked calls from Republicans, journalists, and Catholic leaders to reconsider and repudiate this extreme position.

END OF SUMMARY.
