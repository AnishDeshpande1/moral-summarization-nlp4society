SUMMARY:
The article denounces a growing Democratic push to legalize abortion “all the way up to 40 weeks,” citing Virginia legislator Kathy Tran and Governor Ralph Northam’s support for a bill that would allow full‑term fetuses to be delivered and then left to die. It points out similar legislation in New York that permits abortions at any stage on vague “health” grounds, and notes that leading Democratic presidential candidates back federal measures that would erase state protections for late‑term fetuses, leaving bans only where they contain unenforceable health exceptions. The author argues that the public does not share this “extreme” stance, that the laws are deliberately deceptive, and that treating viable, healthy fetuses as non‑persons is “manifestly unjust.” The piece calls on Democrats to abandon these positions, urges Republicans, journalists, and Catholic bishops to expose and condemn them, and demands that the Supreme Court overturn what it calls an unconstitutional, absurd endorsement of abortion‑on‑demand at any stage of pregnancy.  

END OF SUMMARY.
