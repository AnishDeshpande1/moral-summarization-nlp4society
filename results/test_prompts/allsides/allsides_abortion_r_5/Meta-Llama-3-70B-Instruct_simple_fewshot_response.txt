SUMMARY:

Explicit support for abortion up to 40 weeks is becoming a mainstream Democratic position. Virginia state legislator Kathy Tran's bill, supported by Governor Ralph Northam, suggests that in some circumstances, a full-term child should be delivered and then allowed to die. New York Democrats have passed a similar law, and many leading Democratic presidential candidates have endorsed federal legislation that would wipe away state laws protecting unborn children late in pregnancy. These Democrats claim that late-term abortions are only done for compelling medical reasons, but the available evidence does not support this contention. The laws they support do not require such reasons anyway. The author argues that allowing unborn children to be killed is manifestly unjust and lays moral obligations on all of us. Democrats who have taken this extreme position should reconsider, and those who have not should repudiate it. Republicans, journalists, and Catholic bishops should expose and condemn this indefensible position.

END OF SUMMARY.
