SUMMARY:

The nationwide reaction to the killing of George Floyd by a Minneapolis police officer is part of a larger trend of racial violence in America. Names like Emmett Till, Rodney King, Eric Garner, and Michael Brown have become markers of the struggles of specific periods in time. The combination of circumstances, timing, and context turned them into enduring figures. Floyd's death was sparked by a string of recent controversial killings, including Ahmaud Arbery, Breonna Taylor, and Tony McDade. The circumstance of his death, with an officer's knee pressed into his neck for nearly nine minutes, provided a detailed look at what happened. The incident cannot be separated from the larger context of health and economic crises caused by the coronavirus pandemic, which have affected black Americans disproportionately. The demonstrations in cities across the country are likely to continue as ongoing court cases keep the issue of police violence in the news.

The deaths of Floyd and others highlight the systemic racism that pervades American society, where black people are disproportionately affected by police brutality. The weight of this systemic racism became too much to bear, sparking a movement that will likely be remembered as one of the pivotal moments of the decade.

END OF SUMMARY.
