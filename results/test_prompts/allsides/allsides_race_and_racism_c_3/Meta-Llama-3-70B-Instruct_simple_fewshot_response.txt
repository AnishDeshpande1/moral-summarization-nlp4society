SUMMARY:

The killing of George Floyd by a Minneapolis police officer has sparked a nationwide movement, joining the ranks of Emmett Till, Rodney King, and Eric Garner as markers of racial violence in America. Floyd's death, caught on video, showed the senselessness of his killing, with an officer's knee pressed into his neck for nearly nine minutes. The incident was the tipping point of anger that had been building over recent controversial killings, including Ahmaud Arbery, Breonna Taylor, and Tony McDade. The coronavirus pandemic, which has disproportionately affected black Americans, and President Trump's incendiary tweets, may have contributed to the widespread protests. The officer who killed Floyd has been charged with second-degree murder and manslaughter, and three other officers face charges of aiding and abetting. The ongoing court cases will likely keep police violence in the news, and the movement sparked by Floyd's death will likely be remembered as a pivotal moment of the decade. 

END OF SUMMARY.
