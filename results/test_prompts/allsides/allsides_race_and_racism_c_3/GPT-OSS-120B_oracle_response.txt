SUMMARY:  
The article traces a line of American **violence** from historic **lynchings** and **brutality**—Emmett Till, Rodney King’s **beating** and **acquittal**, the 1992 **riots**—to recent **killings** that have become symbols of systemic **racism**. George Floyd’s **death**, caused by an officer **pressing** his knee on Floyd’s neck for nine minutes, was captured on multiple **videos**, making the **incident** starkly visible and turning it into a national **spark**. Earlier **incidents** such as Ahmaud Arbery being **gunned** down, Breonna Taylor **shot** after police **busted** into her home, and the shooting of Tony McDade added to the **crises**. The pandemic‑fuelled **inequalities**, **disproportionate** impacts on Black communities, and President Trump’s **incendiary**, **eager** rhetoric acted as **tinder**, making the country **combustible**. Ongoing **court cases** include a second‑degree **murder** and **manslaughter** charge against the officer who **kneeled**, **aiding** and **abetting** charges for three others, and **murder** charges for Arbery’s killers, while no charges yet exist for Taylor or McDade. The **spread** of protests reflects a nation on the brink of confronting entrenched **racism** and **inequality**.
