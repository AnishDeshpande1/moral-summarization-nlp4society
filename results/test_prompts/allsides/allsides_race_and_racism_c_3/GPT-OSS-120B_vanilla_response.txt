SUMMARY: The article examines why George Floyd’s death has become a defining moment in the United States’ ongoing struggle with racial violence and police brutality. It places Floyd alongside historic cases such as Emmett Till, Rodney King, Eric Garner, and Michael Brown, noting that while many Black victims have been killed, Floyd’s death—captured on multiple by‑stander videos showing a police officer kneeling on his neck for nine minutes—generated unprecedented nationwide outrage. The piece argues that the graphic footage, combined with a backdrop of other high‑profile killings (Ahmaud Arbery, Breonna Taylor, Tony McDade), pandemic‑exacerbated health and economic inequities, and political rhetoric, created a tipping point that ignited widespread protests. Ongoing legal actions include murder and manslaughter charges against the officers involved in Floyd’s case and murder charges for those in the Arbery case, while no charges have yet been filed for Taylor or McDade. Commentators quoted stress that Floyd’s death was the spark for a nation already on the brink, highlighting systemic racism and the power of visual evidence to mobilize public demand for change. END OF SUMMARY.
