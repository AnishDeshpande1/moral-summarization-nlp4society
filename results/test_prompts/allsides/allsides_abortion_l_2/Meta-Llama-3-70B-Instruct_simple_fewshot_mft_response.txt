SUMMARY:

The Alabama state senate's passage of a radical anti-abortion bill, banning abortion even in cases of rape and incest, is a blatant attempt to subvert women's autonomy and control over their own bodies. The bill's supporters, all 25 of whom are white male lawmakers, are driven by a desire to maintain patriarchal authority and suppress women's rights, particularly those of poor women who lack the means to access abortions elsewhere. This legislation is not about protecting the fetus, but about perpetuating a system of oppression that keeps women in their place. By criminalizing abortion, these lawmakers are willing to harm women, forcing them to carry unwanted pregnancies to term or seek dangerous illegal abortions. The bill's passage is a chilling consequence of Brett Kavanaugh's appointment to the Supreme Court, and its intention is clearly to overturn Roe v Wade. The author argues that this is not just about abortion, but about the elimination of women as a threat to the social order.

END OF SUMMARY.
