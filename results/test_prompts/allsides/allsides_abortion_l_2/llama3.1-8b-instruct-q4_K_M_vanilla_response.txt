SUMMARY:

Alabama's new anti-abortion law, which bans abortion at six weeks, has been widely criticized for its hypocrisy and targeting of poor women. Lawmakers who voted for the bill are mostly against banning guns, despite their argument that "banning things doesn't work". The author argues that the real intention behind the law is not to protect fetal life, but to control women's reproductive choices and keep them in a state of poverty.

The article suggests that the law will disproportionately affect poor women who lack the means to travel out of state for an abortion. It also implies that the Supreme Court's decision on Roe v Wade hangs in the balance, with overturning it being the ultimate goal. The author expresses frustration at the incremental approach to chipping away at Roe, which they believe only perpetuates the status quo.

END OF SUMMARY.
