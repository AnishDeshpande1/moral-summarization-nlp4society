SUMMARY:
The Alabama Senate, composed entirely of white male lawmakers, passed an extreme anti‑abortion bill that reveals a stark abuse of authority. By invoking “life” for six‑week embryos while disregarding the wellbeing of poor women who cannot afford travel or medical care, the legislators subvert the principle of caring for vulnerable citizens. The measure is framed not as a protection of fetal life but as a tool to control women’s bodies, keeping disadvantaged people poor and reinforcing a patriarchal social order—an injustice that violates fairness. The author argues that this draconian law, echoed by a Supreme Court increasingly hostile to reproductive rights, weaponizes state power to harm women, especially those without means, and exemplifies a broader pattern of elite authority subverting equitable care and fairness for the most vulnerable.

END OF SUMMARY.
