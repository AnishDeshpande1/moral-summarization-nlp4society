SUMMARY:

The recent anti-abortion bill passed by 25 white male lawmakers in Alabama's state senate is a thinly veiled attempt to control and oppress women, particularly poor women. The hypocrisy of these lawmakers is evident in their stance against banning guns while advocating for abortion bans, citing that "banning things doesn't work." The author argues that the focus is not on the fetus, but on restricting women's autonomy and perpetuating social inequality. The bill is designed to keep poor people poor and women in their place. The author condemns the lawmakers' actions as a threat to women's lives and reproductive health, and notes that the law will disproportionately affect women without means to travel for abortions. The bill's passage is a chilling effect of Brett Kavanaugh's appointment and a step towards overturning Roe v Wade.

END OF SUMMARY.
