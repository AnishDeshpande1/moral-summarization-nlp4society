SUMMARY:  
The article denounces the hypocrisies of Alabama’s 25 white male lawmakers who passed an extreme anti‑abortion ban, pointing out the inconsistency of Republicans who ban guns yet “ban” women’s reproductive rights. It argues that the law is not about protecting a six‑week‑old foetus but about the elimination of poor women as a social threat, ensuring that poverty and female subordination remain entrenched. By criminalising abortion, the bill forces women to bear unwanted pregnancies, stripping them of control over their own lives and forcing them to travel great distances for care—a costly emotional and financial investment. The piece likens the shame placed on abortion‑rights advocates to the shame once directed at mandatory‑minimum sentencing that led to mass incarceration of Black people, noting that the Supreme Court appointment of Brett Kavanaugh gave the ban a chilling, existential threat feel. While the law will likely linger in federal court, its intent is clear: overturning Roe and chipping away at reproductive freedom, keeping women “stay” where they are.  

END OF SUMMARY.
