SUMMARY: The article critiques Alabama’s all‑male Republican Senate for passing a near‑total abortion ban, highlighting the hypocrisy of lawmakers who oppose gun bans yet support restricting women’s reproductive rights. It argues the legislation is less about protecting fetal life and more about controlling poor women, reinforcing poverty and limiting women’s autonomy. The author likens the ban to other regressive policies that disproportionately harm marginalized groups, noting it will force women without resources to travel out of state or carry unwanted pregnancies, especially those with fetal abnormalities. While the law will likely linger in federal courts and may never reach the Supreme Court, its intent appears to be eroding Roe v. Wade. The piece warns that incremental attacks on abortion rights may be more effective than overt challenges and underscores the broader social impact of restricting reproductive freedom. END OF SUMMARY.
