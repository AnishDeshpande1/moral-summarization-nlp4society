SUMMARY:
Alabama’s all‑male Senate passed an extreme anti‑abortion law that, beyond its stated “protecting life,” reveals a deeper moral agenda: to keep poor women subjugated and maintain existing social hierarchies. The article points out the hypocrisy of Republican lawmakers who oppose gun bans while championing a ban on abortions, arguing that “banning things doesn’t work.” It notes that the law values a six‑week embryo over the life and wellbeing of a child born to a low‑income mother in a state that rejected Obamacare. By criminalising a woman’s choice, the bill effectively threatens women’s autonomy, especially those who cannot afford to travel out of state for care. The measure is framed not as a fetal‑rights issue but as an attack on women’s control over their bodies, reinforcing poverty and forcing women to bear the consequences of sex. While the law will likely linger in federal courts, its intent to overturn Roe v. Wade is clear, and its chilling effect mirrors broader attempts to marginalise disadvantaged groups. The piece condemns the legislation as a tool of oppression rather than a genuine moral crusade.

END OF SUMMARY.
