SUMMARY:  
The article condemns Alabama’s all‑male, Republican Senate for passing an extreme anti‑abortion bill, exposing their hypocrisy and the gendered oppression it enforces. It argues that these lawmakers claim to protect “life” while ignoring the lives of poor women, refusing gun bans, and rejecting healthcare reforms, revealing a selective moral agenda. The piece frames the ban not as a concern for fetuses but as a deliberate strategy to keep poor women subjugated, limiting their autonomy and reinforcing poverty. By likening the ban to policies that have mass‑incarcerated Black people, the author stresses that the law’s true purpose is to eliminate women’s reproductive freedom, especially for those who cannot travel to the few out‑of‑state clinics. The author warns that the law’s chilling effect signals a broader attack on Roe v. Wade and on women’s control over their bodies, and predicts it will languish in courts while incremental attacks continue. Ultimately, the bill is portrayed as a tool to force women to remain “where they are,” preserving a patriarchal social order.  
END OF SUMMARY.
