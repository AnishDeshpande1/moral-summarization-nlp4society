SUMMARY:

The article discusses the recent anti-abortion bill passed by 25 white male lawmakers in Alabama's state senate. Critics argue that these lawmakers are hypocrites, as they oppose banning guns but support banning abortion, and prioritize the "life" of a fetus over the life of a born child. The author suggests that the true intention behind the bill is to control women, particularly poor women, and maintain the social order. The bill's radical nature is seen as a chilling effect of Brett Kavanaugh's appointment to the Supreme Court. While the law may not reach the Supreme Court, its intention is clear: to overturn Roe v Wade. The author notes that people with means will still be able to access abortions by traveling out of state, but for others, the emotional and financial burden will be too great.

END OF SUMMARY.
