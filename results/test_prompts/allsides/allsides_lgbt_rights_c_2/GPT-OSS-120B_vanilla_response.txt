SUMMARY:
At the GOP convention, PayPal co‑founder Peter Thiel delivered a speech that highlighted the party’s internal clash over LGBT issues. Thiel proclaimed pride in being gay, Republican, and American, but warned that the focus on transgender bathroom debates distracts from larger national concerns. He did not endorse the party’s platform, which opposes same‑sex marriage, permits conversion therapy, allows businesses to refuse service to LGBT couples, and mandates bathroom use based on birth certificates. Donald Trump, speaking at the same convention, claimed he would protect LGBTQ citizens from “hateful foreign ideology,” yet his record is mixed: he describes himself as a friend of the gay community and cites gay friends, but supports the First Amendment Defense Act and opposes federal same‑sex marriage recognition, leaving decisions to states. His vice‑presidential pick, Indiana Gov. Mike Pence, holds especially hard‑line anti‑LGBT views, describing gay marriage as a sign of societal collapse. The article underscores the Republican Party’s shift toward an anti‑LGBT platform while some members, like Thiel, seek a more inclusive stance.

END OF SUMMARY.
