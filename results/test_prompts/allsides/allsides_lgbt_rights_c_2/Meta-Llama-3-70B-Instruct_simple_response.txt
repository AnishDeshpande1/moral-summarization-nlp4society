SUMMARY:

The Republican party has adopted an anti-LGBT platform, which contradicts the claims of some pro-LGBT Republicans that Donald Trump is the most supportive candidate in GOP history. Peter Thiel, PayPal co-founder, gave a speech at the GOP convention, where he declared his pride in being gay and American, but downplayed the importance of LGBT rights, calling the transgender bathroom debate a "distraction" from real problems. Trump has called himself a "friend of the gay community," but his party's platform opposes same-sex marriage, supports "conversion therapy," and allows businesses to refuse service to LGBT couples. Trump's own stance on LGBT issues is unclear, and his vice presidential pick, Mike Pence, holds extreme anti-LGBT views. The party's platform and Trump's ambiguous stance on LGBT issues raise concerns about the future of LGBT rights under a Republican administration.

END OF SUMMARY.
