SUMMARY:

The Republican party has adopted an anti-LGBT platform, which contradicts the views of some pro-LGBT Republicans who consider Donald Trump the most supportive candidate in GOP history. Peter Thiel, PayPal co-founder, proudly declared his gay identity and Republican affiliation, but failed to address the party's stance on LGBT rights, instead calling the transgender bathroom debate a "distraction" from real problems. Trump has called himself a "friend of the gay community," but his party's platform opposes same-sex marriage, supports "conversion therapy," and refuses services to LGBT couples. Trump's words on protecting LGBT citizens from "violence and oppression" are overshadowed by his support for the First Amendment Defense Act, which would allow individuals and businesses to oppose same-sex marriage. His vice presidential pick, Mike Pence, holds extreme views on LGBT rights, having opposed equal rights and called gay marriage a signal of "societal collapse." The party's stance raises questions about their commitment to protecting LGBT rights and opposing oppression.

END OF SUMMARY.
