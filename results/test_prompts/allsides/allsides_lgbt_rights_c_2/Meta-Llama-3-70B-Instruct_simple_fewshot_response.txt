SUMMARY:

The Republican party has adopted an anti-LGBT platform, but some pro-LGBT Republicans are praising Donald Trump as their most supportive candidate in GOP history. PayPal co-founder Peter Thiel, who is gay, gave a speech at the GOP convention, saying he's proud to be gay, Republican, and American. Thiel downplayed the importance of transgender rights, calling it a distraction from real problems. Trump has called himself a "friend of the gay community," but his party's platform opposes same-sex marriage, supports "conversion therapy," and allows businesses to refuse service to LGBT couples. Trump's own stance is unclear, but he has criticized transgender bathroom debates and supports the First Amendment Defense Act. His vice presidential pick, Mike Pence, holds extreme views on LGBT rights, opposing equal rights for LGBT people and calling gay marriage a signal of "societal collapse." Despite this, some pro-LGBT Republicans are optimistic about Trump's support for their community.

END OF SUMMARY.
