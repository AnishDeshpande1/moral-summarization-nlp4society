SUMMARY:
The GOP’s 2024 platform, described by the Log Cabin Republicans as the party’s most anti‑LGBT stance ever, sits alongside a surprising chorus of pro‑LGBT voices that praise Donald Trump as the most supportive Republican candidate on gay issues. At the convention, PayPal co‑founder Peter Thiel declared his pride as a gay Republican and an American, but stopped short of endorsing the party’s anti‑LGBT planks, dismissing the current focus on transgender bathroom debates as a distraction from “real problems.” Trump, meanwhile, labeled himself a “friend of the gay community” and pledged to protect LGBTQ citizens from “a hateful foreign ideology,” yet his record and the party platform oppose same‑sex marriage, endorse conversion therapy, and allow businesses to refuse service to LGBT couples. Both men have praised gay friends—Trump citing Elton John—and have criticized transgender‑related policies while supporting measures like the First Amendment Defense Act that protect anti‑LGBT beliefs. The contrast is starkly highlighted by vice‑presidential pick Mike Pence, whose long‑standing anti‑LGBT positions are deemed “more extreme than any vice‑presidential nominee in modern history” by Democratic Lt. Gov. Gavin Newsom. The article underscores the moral tension between the GOP’s official anti‑LGBT agenda and the individual claims of support from its leading figures.  
END OF SUMMARY.
