SUMMARY:

Peter Thiel, co-founder of PayPal, spoke at the GOP convention on Thursday night, expressing his pride in being gay and a Republican. However, he did not directly address the party's anti-LGBT platform. Donald Trump has been called out for his party's stance on LGBT issues, despite claiming to be a "friend of the gay community." The 2016 Republican Party Platform opposes same-sex marriage, supports "conversion therapy," and requires transgender individuals to use public bathrooms corresponding to their birth certificates.

Trump has not been clear on where he stands on LGBT issues, but his vice-presidential pick, Mike Pence, has a history of opposing equal rights for LGBT people. Pence has called gay marriage a signal of "societal collapse." The Log Cabin Republicans have criticized the GOP's platform as the most anti-LGBT in party history.

END OF SUMMARY.
