SUMMARY:

The Republican party has adopted a platform that is being called the most anti-LGBT in party history, but some pro-LGBT Republicans are calling Donald Trump the most supportive candidate in the GOP's history. PayPal co-founder Peter Thiel, who is gay, gave a speech at the GOP convention, saying he's proud to be gay and a Republican, but didn't address the party's platform on LGBT issues. Trump has called himself a "friend of the gay community," but his party's platform opposes same-sex marriage, supports "conversion therapy," and allows businesses to refuse service to LGBT couples. Trump's own stance on LGBT issues is unclear, but his vice presidential pick, Mike Pence, has a history of opposing equal rights for LGBT people. Despite this, some pro-LGBT Republicans believe Trump will protect LGBT citizens from violence and oppression.

END OF SUMMARY.
