**SUMMARY:**  
At the GOP convention, PayPal co‑founder Peter Thiel declared himself “proud to be gay, proud to be a Republican, and proud to be an American,” while cautioning that the party’s new platform is the most **anti‑LGBT** in its history. Thiel dismissed debates over transgender bathroom use as a **distraction**, recalling the Cold War’s “great debate.” Donald Trump, billed by some as the most **supportive** Republican for LGBT people, called himself a **friend** of the gay community but offered only vague promises to protect LGBT citizens from **violence** and **oppression** of a **hateful** foreign ideology. The 2016 Republican platform still **opposes** same‑sex marriage, **supports** “**conversion therapy**,” and allows businesses to **refuse** service to LGBT couples, while the First Amendment Defense Act would shield those who **oppose** same‑sex marriage. Vice‑presidential pick Mike Pence, described by California’s Gavin Newsom as **extreme**, has a long record of opposing LGBT rights, echoing past statements that gay marriage signals “societal collapse.”
