SUMMARY:

The Republican Party's platform on LGBT rights is being criticized as the most anti-LGBT in party history, but some pro-LGBT Republicans see Donald Trump as their most supportive candidate. PayPal co-founder Peter Thiel addressed this issue at the GOP convention, saying he was proud to be gay and a Republican, but acknowledging that not all party members agree with every platform plank. However, the platform itself opposes same-sex marriage, supports "conversion therapy", and allows businesses to discriminate against LGBT individuals.

Donald Trump has claimed to be a friend of the gay community, but his own words and actions suggest otherwise. He has criticized transgender bathroom debates and supports laws that restrict LGBT rights. His vice presidential pick, Indiana governor Mike Pence, is also known for his anti-LGBT views, with some calling them "more extreme than any vice-presidential nominee in modern history". This raises questions about Trump's true stance on LGBT issues.

END OF SUMMARY.
