SUMMARY:

The Republican party has adopted an anti-LGBT platform, but some pro-LGBT Republicans, including PayPal co-founder Peter Thiel, are supporting Donald Trump, who they claim is the most supportive candidate in GOP history. Thiel's speech at the GOP convention nodded to both sides, expressing pride in being gay, Republican, and American, while also downplaying the importance of transgender rights. Trump has called himself a "friend of the gay community," but his party's platform suggests otherwise, opposing same-sex marriage and supporting "conversion therapy." Trump's own stance is unclear, with some seeing him as more accepting of gay issues, while others point to his support for laws that discriminate against LGBT individuals. The actions of his vice presidential pick, Mike Pence, who has a history of opposing equal rights for LGBT people, may speak louder than Trump's words. The party's platform and Trump's stance raise concerns about harm and subversion of LGBT individuals' rights, as well as unfair treatment and cheating of their dignity.

END OF SUMMARY.
