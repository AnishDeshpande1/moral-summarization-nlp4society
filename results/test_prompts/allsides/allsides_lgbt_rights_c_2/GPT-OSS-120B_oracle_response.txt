SUMMARY:  
The Republican Party’s platform is being called the most anti‑LGBT in its history, yet some pro‑LGBT Republicans laud Donald Trump as the party’s most supportive candidate. At the GOP convention, PayPal co‑founder Peter Thiel declared he is “proud to be gay, proud to be a Republican, proud to be an American,” receiving a standing ovation. He admitted he does not agree with every plank of the platform but dismissed transgender bathroom debates as a distraction from “real problems.” Trump, who calls himself a “friend of the gay community,” offered vague protection for LGBTQ citizens, promising to shield them from “violence and oppression” while simultaneously backing the First Amendment Defense Act and opposing federal support for same‑sex marriage. His vice‑presidential pick, Indiana Governor Mike Pence, has a record of opposing LGBT rights, once describing gay marriage as a signal of “societal collapse.” The article’s moral framing highlights the tension between claimed friendship and support versus policies that oppose, refuse, and threaten rights, feeding fears of oppression and collapse.  

END OF SUMMARY.
