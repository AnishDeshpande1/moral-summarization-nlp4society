SUMMARY:

After months of fighting, the Senate finally approved a disaster aid package worth $19.1 billion, providing relief to millions of Americans affected by devastating hurricanes, wildfires, and floods. However, the process was marred by President Trump's interventions, which caused significant delays in approving aid. Trump initially balked at additional funding for hurricane-devastated Puerto Rico, spurring months of back-and-forth on the subject. He also requested $4.5 billion in aid for the border, but ultimately had his demands thrown out of the deal.

The final package allocates $1.4 billion in aid to Puerto Rico and strips funding for border aid altogether. The delay has left millions of people struggling to rebuild their lives, with many still waiting for help after disasters such as Hurricane Maria, Hurricane Michael, and flooding in Iowa and Missouri. The fight over disaster aid highlights the need for politicians to put aside partisan squabbles and provide assistance to those affected by natural disasters.

END OF SUMMARY.
