SUMMARY: After six months of fighting, the Senate finally approved a $19.1 billion disaster aid package, providing relief and help to millions of Americans hit by hurricanes, wildfires and floods. The deal was spurred by intense squabbles with President Trump, who balked at additional funding for Puerto Rico and threw in a $4.5 billion border request that limited the aid’s scope. Ultimately, border aid was stripped out and $1.4 billion was allocated to Puerto Rico, a modest increase from the $600 million Trump had previously made available. The delay caused by Trump’s finagling left many communities without assistance as destruction continued to damage homes, infrastructure and farms across the nation. Critics argue that the politicization of disaster relief slowed the response, leaving people waiting for care and reconstruction. The House is set to pass the legislation, and Trump has agreed to sign, ending the prolonged delay that had taken aid away from those most in need. END OF SUMMARY.
