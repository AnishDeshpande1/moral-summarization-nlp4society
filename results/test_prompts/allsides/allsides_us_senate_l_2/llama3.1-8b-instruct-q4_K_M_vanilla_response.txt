SUMMARY:

After six months of negotiations, the Senate has approved a $19.1 billion disaster aid package, which will provide relief to millions of Americans affected by hurricanes, wildfires, and floods. President Trump's involvement in the negotiations was a major obstacle, as he initially objected to additional funding for Puerto Rico and requested $4.5 billion in border aid. However, both of these demands were ultimately stripped from the final deal. The package includes $1.4 billion in aid for Puerto Rico and will now head to the House for approval, where it is expected to pass. Trump has agreed to sign the legislation. The delay in disaster aid has had significant consequences, with millions of people waiting on assistance after devastating natural disasters over the past year. The package's passage marks a rare instance of bipartisan cooperation in Washington.

END OF SUMMARY.
