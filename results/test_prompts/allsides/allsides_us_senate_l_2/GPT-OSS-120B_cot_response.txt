**SUMMARY:**  
After six months of fighting, the Senate finally approved a $19.1 billion disaster‑aid package, but the process was marked by delays and political flashpoints caused largely by President Trump’s aversion to funding Puerto Rico and his request for border aid. Trump’s baseless allegations about mismanagement in Puerto Rico and his balking at additional assistance turned the negotiations into a back‑and‑forth struggle, stripping border money from the deal and leaving only $1.4 billion for the island. The resulting package, now headed to the House, reflects a compromise that still leaves many communities— from Puerto Rico to Florida, Iowa, Missouri and beyond— waiting for essential relief. The politicization and squabbles over the aid have had staggering consequences, postponing rebuilding efforts and exposing how partisan battles can endanger Americans when natural disasters strike.
