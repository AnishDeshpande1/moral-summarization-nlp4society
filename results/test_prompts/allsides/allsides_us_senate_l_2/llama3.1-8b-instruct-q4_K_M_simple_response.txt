SUMMARY:

After six months of fighting, the US Senate has finally approved a $19.1 billion disaster aid package, providing relief to millions affected by hurricanes, wildfires, and floods. President Trump's involvement was a major roadblock, as he initially took issue with additional funding for hurricane-devastated Puerto Rico and requested $4.5 billion in aid for the border. However, neither of these demands were met in the final deal. The package allocates $1.4 billion to Puerto Rico and strips out funding for border aid. Trump's actions delayed progress on disaster aid, causing significant consequences: millions are still waiting for help after devastating disasters, including Hurricane Maria which hit over a year ago. The delay has left people's efforts to rebuild in limbo, highlighting the need for politicians to put aside partisan squabbles and prioritize helping those affected by natural disasters.

END OF SUMMARY.
