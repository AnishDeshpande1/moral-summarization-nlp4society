SUMMARY:

President Donald Trump's involvement in the disaster aid package negotiations has been a major obstacle, causing delays and ultimately stripping funding for border aid from the deal. Trump had requested $4.5 billion in aid for the border, which was met with opposition from Democrats. The final package allocates $1.4 billion to Puerto Rico, but does not address Trump's demands. This delay has had significant consequences, with millions of Americans waiting on aid after devastating hurricanes, wildfires, and floods. The need for aid touches every part of the country, with states like California, Iowa, and Florida still recovering from natural disasters. The politicization of disaster aid is a concern for future relief efforts. Trump's actions are seen as an attempt to subvert the democratic process, prioritizing his own agenda over the needs of affected communities. By doing so, he has caused harm to those who rely on aid after disasters. This raises questions about fairness and cheating in the allocation of resources.

END OF SUMMARY.
