SUMMARY:
President Trump's interference in disaster aid negotiations has delayed relief for millions of Americans affected by devastating hurricanes, wildfires, and floods. Despite initial opposition to additional funding for Puerto Rico, a deal was finally reached with $1.4 billion allocated to the island. However, Trump's request for $4.5 billion in border aid was completely stripped out of the package. The delay has had major consequences, with millions waiting on aid after disasters such as Hurricane Maria in 2017 and recent flooding in Iowa and Missouri. The politicization of disaster aid is concerning, particularly given that Americans nationwide are affected by these natural disasters. As one lawmaker noted, "the need for aid touches every part of the country - blue states and red states." The passage of this package is a welcome relief, but highlights the challenges posed by Trump's involvement in such negotiations.

END OF SUMMARY.
