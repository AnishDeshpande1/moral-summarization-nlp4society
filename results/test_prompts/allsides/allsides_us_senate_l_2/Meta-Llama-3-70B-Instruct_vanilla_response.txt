SUMMARY:

The US Senate has approved a $19.1 billion disaster aid package, which will provide relief to millions of Americans affected by hurricanes, wildfires, and floods. The passage of this package was delayed for six months due to President Donald Trump's demands, including additional funding for the border and limiting aid to Puerto Rico. However, the final package does not include funding for border aid and allocates $1.4 billion to Puerto Rico. Trump had previously sought to limit aid to Puerto Rico and had made unfounded allegations about the regional government mismanaging funds. The delay in disaster relief has had significant consequences, leaving millions of people waiting for aid in regions affected by natural disasters. The package is expected to pass the House and be signed by Trump.

END OF SUMMARY.
