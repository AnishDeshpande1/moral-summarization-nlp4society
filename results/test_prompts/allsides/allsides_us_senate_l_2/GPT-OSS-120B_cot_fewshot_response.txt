**SUMMARY:**  
After six months of stalled negotiations, the Senate finally approved a $19.1 billion disaster‑aid package, delivering much‑needed relief to millions affected by hurricanes, wildfires and floods. The fight was marked by Trump’s aversion to funding Puerto Rico and a sticking point over a $4.5 billion border request, both of which he balked on and framed with unfounded allegations of mismanaging. Those delays created a staggering problem, leaving communities waiting for aid, support and rebuilding assistance. While the final deal strips out the border money, it restores $1.4 billion for Puerto Rico and restores resiliency funding nationwide. The House is set to pass the bill, and Trump has agreed to sign, ending a period of politicization that delayed critical help. END OF SUMMARY.
