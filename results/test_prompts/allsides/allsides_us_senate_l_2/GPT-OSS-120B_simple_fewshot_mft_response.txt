SUMMARY:
After six months of gridlock, the Senate approved a $19.1 billion disaster‑aid package that finally reaches millions of Americans battered by hurricanes, wildfires, floods and other catastrophes.  The delay was largely blamed on President Trump, whose repeated objections and demands—first to slash Puerto Rico assistance and then to insert $4.5 billion for border security—subverted the normal authority of Congress and stalled relief for those in urgent need.  By refusing to support the island’s recovery and demanding unrelated border funds, Trump is portrayed as abusing his power for partisan goals, cheating the nation out of timely aid.  The final deal strips out the border request and provides $1.4 billion to Puerto Rico, yet the long‑awaited help still arrived after months of suffering, underscoring the human cost of the political power play.  Lawmakers now expect the House to pass the measure, and Trump has agreed to sign, but the episode highlights how authority can be misused, how vulnerable communities were harmed, and how the partisan subversion betrayed the principle of fairness.  
END OF SUMMARY.
