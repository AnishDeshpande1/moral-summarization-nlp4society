SUMMARY:

The US Senate has finally approved a $19.1 billion disaster aid package after six months of fighting, which will provide relief to millions of Americans affected by hurricanes, wildfires, and floods. The passage of this package was delayed due to President Trump's roadblocks, including his initial refusal to provide additional funding to Puerto Rico and his subsequent request for $4.5 billion in border aid. Despite Trump's efforts, the final package does not include funding for border aid and allocates $1.4 billion to Puerto Rico. The delay in disaster relief has had devastating consequences, leaving millions of people waiting for aid for over a year. The politicization of this fight has hindered the efforts of people trying to rebuild their lives, and it does not bode well for future natural disasters. Trump's actions have been morally reprehensible, causing unnecessary suffering and delay to those in need.

END OF SUMMARY.
