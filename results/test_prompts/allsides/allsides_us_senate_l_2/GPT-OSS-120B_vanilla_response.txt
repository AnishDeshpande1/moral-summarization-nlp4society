SUMMARY: After six months of debate, the Senate approved a $19.1 billion disaster‑aid package, pending House approval and a Trump signature. The bill funds relief for hurricanes, wildfires, floods and includes $1.4 billion for Puerto Rico, after President Trump initially opposed additional aid and demanded $4.5 billion for border assistance. Trump’s objections—particularly to Puerto Rico aid and the border request—delayed the legislation, leaving millions without assistance for events such as Hurricane Maria, Hurricane Michael, and recent floods and storms across the Midwest and South. The final package strips out the border funds and reduces Puerto Rico aid from Trump’s preferred level, but still provides substantial relief for housing, infrastructure, and nutrition. Lawmakers expect the House to pass the measure Friday, and Trump has agreed to sign. The prolonged fight highlights how politicization can hinder timely disaster response. END OF SUMMARY.
