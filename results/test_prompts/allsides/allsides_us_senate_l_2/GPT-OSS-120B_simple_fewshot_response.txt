SUMMARY:
After six months of partisan deadlock, the Senate finally approved a $19.1 billion disaster‑relief package, but the long‑standing obstructionism of President Donald Trump turned the process into a costly humanitarian crisis. Trump initially balked at modest aid for Puerto Rico and then hijacked negotiations with a $4.5 billion border‑fund request, forcing months of back‑and‑forth. In the final deal, border money was stripped out and only $1.4 billion—far less than the island’s needs—was allocated to Puerto Rico, despite desperate calls for infrastructure and medical assistance after Hurricane Maria. The delay left millions of victims of hurricanes, wildfires, floods and a “bomb cyclone” waiting for essential housing, food and rebuilding funds across both red and blue states. Lawmakers now expect the House to pass the bill, and Trump has agreed to sign, but his meddling caused needless suffering and exposed how politicizing disaster aid endangers Americans when nature strikes. The episode warns that future emergencies could be jeopardized whenever self‑interest overrides compassion and responsibility.  
END OF SUMMARY.
