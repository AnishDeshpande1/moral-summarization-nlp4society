SUMMARY: After six months of deadlock, the Senate approved a $19.1 billion disaster‑aid package, finally delivering relief to millions affected by hurricanes, wildfires, floods and other catastrophes. The author frames the struggle as a moral failure caused largely by President Donald Trump, whose objections to Puerto Rico assistance and demand for $4.5 billion in border aid stalled negotiations and left victims waiting. Trump’s unfounded accusations of mismanagement in Puerto Rico and his insistence on cutting aid reduced the island’s support to $1.4 billion—far less than Democrats argued was needed for infrastructure and medical care. Border‑aid requests were stripped from the bill altogether. The author stresses that these partisan roadblocks delayed critical help for disaster‑stricken communities across the nation, from Puerto Rico and Florida to Iowa, Missouri, Nebraska and California, exacerbating suffering and undermining resilience. By politicizing humanitarian aid, Trump’s interference is portrayed as a major moral lapse that jeopardized the nation’s ability to respond to future emergencies. The House is set to pass the bill, and Trump has agreed to sign it, ending a costly, ethically troubling impasse. END OF SUMMARY.
