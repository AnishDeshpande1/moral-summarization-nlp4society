SUMMARY:

A recent Pew Research Center study reveals a significant shift in American attitudes towards gun rights, with 52% of respondents prioritizing Second Amendment rights over gun control, a 7-point increase from 2012. This marks the highest approval rating in two decades. Notably, black Americans' views have dramatically changed, with 54% now believing firearms protect people from crime, up from 29% two years ago. Despite the Newtown school shooting, which sparked calls for stricter gun control, gun rights supporters have successfully blocked federal efforts to impose new restrictions. While Democrats remain committed to gun control, the survey highlights a sharp partisan divide and a growing trend towards prioritizing gun rights across demographic lines. The shift in public opinion raises questions about the effectiveness of gun control advocacy and the role of wording in shaping public perception.

END OF SUMMARY.
