SUMMARY:

According to a Pew Research Center survey, support for gun rights has increased significantly over the past two years, with 52% of Americans believing that Second Amendment rights are more important than gun control. This marks a 7-point increase since the 2012 Newtown school shooting. The shift is driven in part by changing attitudes among black Americans, who are now more likely to view guns as a means of public safety. The survey also found that 54% of blacks believe firearms protect people from crime, up from 29% two years ago. Despite this shift, gun control supporters remain committed to imposing new restrictions, citing strong support for specific changes such as universal background checks. The survey highlights a sharp partisan divide on the issue, with Republicans and independents more likely to support gun rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
