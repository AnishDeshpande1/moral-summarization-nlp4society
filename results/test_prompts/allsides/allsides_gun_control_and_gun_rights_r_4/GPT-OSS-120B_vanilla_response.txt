SUMMARY: New Pew Research data shows a sharp rise in U.S. support for gun rights, reaching 52%—the highest in two decades—just two years after the Sandy Hook shooting. Support grew across most demographics, notably among Black Americans, where 54% now view firearms as protective versus 41% who see them as a risk, a jump from only 29% seeing guns as beneficial two years earlier. Overall, preference for Second Amendment rights increased for men, women, all ages, whites, and Republicans/independents, while Hispanic support slipped slightly. Despite this shift, specific gun‑control measures still enjoy strong backing: about 90% favor universal background checks and just over 50% support limits on rifles and high‑capacity magazines. Critics note the survey’s wording may bias results toward “rights” over “control.” Democrats, including Nancy Pelosi, continue to push for stricter regulations, while gun‑rights advocates have blocked federal action since 2012. END OF SUMMARY.
