SUMMARY:

According to recent data from the Pew Research Center, support for gun rights in the US has reached its highest level in two decades, with 52% of Americans prioritizing Second Amendment rights over gun control. This shift is attributed to changing attitudes among black Americans, who are increasingly seeing guns as a means of public safety. In contrast, Democrats remain committed to imposing new restrictions on gun ownership, despite polling showing strong support for specific changes such as universal background checks.

The Pew research indicates that the shift in attitudes has occurred across nearly all demographic lines, with men and women, old and young, white and black, and Republican and Democrat all seeing their preference for Second Amendment rights increase over the last two years. While some critics have questioned the wording of the survey, the findings suggest a significant shift in public opinion on gun control.

END OF SUMMARY
