SUMMARY:
A recent Pew Research Center study found that support for gun rights in the US has reached its highest level in two decades, with 52% of Americans prioritizing Second Amendment rights over gun control. This shift is driven in part by changing attitudes among black Americans, who are increasingly likely to view guns as a means of public safety. The study also found that support for gun rights has increased across nearly all demographic lines, including men and women, old and young, white and black, and Republican and Democrat. While Democrats still generally back gun controls, majorities of both Republicans and independents say the Second Amendment trumps their desire for more controls. Gun control supporters have expressed concern about the findings, arguing that they reflect a partisan divide rather than a genuine shift in public opinion on gun safety.
