SUMMARY:

A new Pew Research Center study shows that support for gun rights is at its highest level in two decades, with 52% of Americans saying Second Amendment rights are more important than gun control. This is a significant increase from the 45% who said so after the Sandy Hook Elementary School shooting in 2012. The shift is driven by changing attitudes among black Americans, who are increasingly likely to view guns as a means of public safety. In fact, 54% of blacks now believe that firearms protect people from being victims of crimes, up from just 29% two years ago.

Despite the shift, gun control supporters remain committed to imposing new restrictions. However, specific polling still shows strong support for specific changes, such as universal background checks (90%) and limits on rifles and ammunition magazines (50%). The study suggests a sharp partisan divide on the issue, but also highlights a significant increase in support for Second Amendment rights across nearly all demographic lines.

END OF SUMMARY.
