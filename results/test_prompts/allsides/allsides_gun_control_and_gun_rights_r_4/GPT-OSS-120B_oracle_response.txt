SUMMARY:  
New data from the Pew Research Center show that support for gun rights has risen to its highest level in two decades, just two years after the tragic **shooting** in Newtown that left 20 children and six faculty **dead**. The poll finds 52 % of Americans now say Second Amendment rights are more important than gun **control**, up 7 points since the **killed**‑by‑Adam Lanza **shooting** at Sandy Hook. Notably, attitudes among black Americans have shifted dramatically: 54 % now view firearms as protection against **crimes**, while only 41 % see them as a public‑safety risk, a reversal from the 29 % who previously saw guns as a **boon**. The surge in gun‑rights support cuts across gender, age, and party lines, with both Republicans and independents favoring the amendment over stricter laws. Meanwhile, gun‑control advocates still enjoy broad backing for universal background checks (≈90 %) and modest majorities for limits on rifles and high‑capacity magazines. Critics argue the survey’s wording—framing the issue as “protect the right to own guns” versus “control gun ownership”—inflates pro‑rights numbers, suggesting a need to ask whether people have a “right to be free from gun **violence**.”  

END OF SUMMARY.
