**SUMMARY:**  
Pew Research shows a **stunning** reversal in public opinion on gun policy just two years after the Newtown tragedy. A **majority**—52 %—now says Second Amendment **rights** outweigh **gun control**, the highest **support** in two decades. The shift is especially **dramatic** among Black Americans, with 54 % viewing firearms as a **protect**ive tool against **victims** of crime, up from 29 % who saw them as a **risk**. While **universal background checks** still enjoy about 90 % **support**, overall **support** for **control** measures has fallen since the late‑1990s “Columbine” peak. Democrats, led by Nancy Pelosi, call the need for new restrictions **imperative**, yet Republican‑led Senate action has **blocked** federal reforms, leaving the issue **stalled** and pushing some states to act alone. Critics argue the poll’s wording—framing the choice as “protect the right” versus “control gun ownership”—may have inflated **rights** **support**. Across gender, age, and party lines, **support** for the Second Amendment has risen, while only Hispanic respondents showed a slight decline. The nation remains sharply divided, with **rights** advocates and **control** proponents battling over how to ensure **public safety**.
