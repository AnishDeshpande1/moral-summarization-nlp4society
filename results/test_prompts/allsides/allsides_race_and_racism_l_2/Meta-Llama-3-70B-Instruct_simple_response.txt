SUMMARY:

The author reflects on the 1960s civil rights movement, which he was a part of, and draws parallels with the current protests against racial injustice. He recounts his own experience of being arrested for violating segregation laws in Greenville, South Carolina, and how the movement eventually led to the desegregation of public facilities and the passage of the Voting Rights Act. However, he notes that despite these gains, racism and biased institutional structures persist. The author is heartened by the current generation of protesters, who are driving a new era of change, with demonstrations against racial injustice being the largest in US history. He acknowledges that the movement will face resistance from entrenched interests, but believes that moral force can overcome even the most powerful obstacles. He urges everyone with a conscience to join the movement, which has the potential to make America better and improve the lives of millions.

END OF SUMMARY.
