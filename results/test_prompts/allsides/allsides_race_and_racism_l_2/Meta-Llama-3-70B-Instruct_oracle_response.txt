SUMMARY:

The author reflects on the inspiring rise of a new generation protesting against racial injustice, reminiscent of the civil rights movement 60 years ago. The author recounts their own experience of violating segregation laws in 1960, which sparked a wave of protests and demonstrations that eventually led to the end of segregated public facilities and the Voting Rights Act. Despite these victories, the author notes that racism and biased institutional structures persist, and that Dr. King's drive for economic equality was cut short by his assassination. However, a new generation is emerging to challenge these injustices, with the largest demonstrations in history, including white participation, and politicians beginning to respond with reforms. The author emphasizes that this movement holds great promise for America, but will face resistance from entrenched interests. The author urges anyone with a conscience to join this extraordinary movement, as the lives and options of millions are at stake in their struggle for justice and equality.

END OF SUMMARY.
