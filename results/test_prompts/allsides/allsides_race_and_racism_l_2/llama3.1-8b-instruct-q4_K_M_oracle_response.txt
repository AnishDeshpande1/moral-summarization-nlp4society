SUMMARY:

A new generation of protesters is driving change in America, echoing the civil rights movement that emerged 60 years ago. This rising tide of protest against racial injustice is challenging entrenched interests and sparking reform. The protests have spread across the country, with demonstrations taking place in over 2,500 locations and involving millions of people. White participation has far exceeded that in the first civil rights movement, demonstrating a growing sense of urgency and moral outrage.

As this new generation takes on the injustices of our time – from police brutality to economic inequality – they are facing fierce resistance from those who benefit from the status quo. But history shows us that when people rise up against glaring injustice, change is possible. The movement's focus on reforming areas like criminal justice and economic inequalities threatens the privileged and powerful, but it also holds great promise for America.

This is a time for anyone with a conscience to join this extraordinary movement and challenge the injustices of our society. By working together, we can drive real change and create a more just and equitable future for all. END OF SUMMARY.
