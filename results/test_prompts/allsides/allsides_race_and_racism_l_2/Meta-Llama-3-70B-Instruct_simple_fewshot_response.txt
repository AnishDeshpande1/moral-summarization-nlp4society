SUMMARY:

The author, a veteran of the civil rights movement, reflects on the inspiring rise of a new generation protesting against racial injustice in America. He recounts his own experiences in the 1960s, including being arrested for violating segregation laws, and notes that despite the progress made since then, racial injustice persists. The current movement, sparked by the murder of George Floyd, has seen unprecedented participation, with 15-26 million Americans taking part in demonstrations across 2,500 locations. The movement has already led to reforms, such as banning choke holds and retiring the Confederate battle emblem from the Mississippi state flag. The author sees great promise in this new generation, but acknowledges that they will face resistance from entrenched interests. He urges anyone with a conscience to join the movement, which has the power to make America better and improve the lives of millions. The moral force of this generation can overcome the obstacles to justice and equality.

END OF SUMMARY.
