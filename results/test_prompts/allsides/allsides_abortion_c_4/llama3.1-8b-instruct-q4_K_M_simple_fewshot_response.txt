SUMMARY:
The Supreme Court delivered a major blow to California's law requiring anti-abortion pregnancy centers to inform women about publicly funded abortion and contraception services. In a 5-4 ruling, Justice Clarence Thomas stated that the law "likely" violates the First Amendment as compelled speech. The decision could have far-reaching implications, potentially threatening laws in more conservative states that require women seeking abortions to view ultrasounds or learn about fetal development. Liberal justices dissented, arguing that if a state can require doctors to inform women about adoption services, it should also be able to require medical counselors to inform women about childbirth and abortion services. The ruling has been met with criticism from reproductive rights advocates, who argue that the decision gives "fake health centers" a free speech right to deceive pregnant women.
