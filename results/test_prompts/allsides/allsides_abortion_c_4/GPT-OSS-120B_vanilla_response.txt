SUMMARY:
The U.S. Supreme Court struck down a California law that required anti‑abortion pregnancy centers to display state‑drafted notices about publicly funded abortion, contraception and other reproductive services. In a 5‑4 decision, Justice Clarence Thomas wrote that the mandate likely violates the First Amendment by compelling speech, a view supported by the conservative majority. Justice Anthony Kennedy concurred, calling the law a prime example of government overreach into individual expression. The law also forced unlicensed centers to disclose they are not medical facilities. Opponents, represented by the Alliance Defending Freedom, argued the rule forces pro‑life centers to promote abortion, while the state said the disclosures are needed to prevent deception by centers posing as medical clinics. Liberals warned the ruling could jeopardize similar “informed‑consent” requirements in other states, such as mandatory ultrasound viewing or counseling about pregnancy options. The decision reflects the Court’s recent trend of protecting free‑speech rights even as it has upheld abortion access nationally.

END OF SUMMARY.
