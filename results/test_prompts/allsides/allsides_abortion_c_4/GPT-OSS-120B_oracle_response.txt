SUMMARY:  
The Supreme Court struck down a California law that forced anti‑abortion pregnancy centers to post a government‑drafted script notifying women of state‑funded abortion and contraception services. In a 5‑4 decision, the conservative majority said the requirement likely violates the First Amendment as compelled speech, describing the law as a threat that imposes the government’s message on providers who oppose abortion. The justices warned that the ruling could put other required postings – such as first‑aid instructions or hand‑washing rules – on shaky legal ground. Liberals argued the state is justified in requiring disclosures to stop centers that deceive and misinform women by dressing up as medical clinics, but conservatives said the law forces pro‑life centers to give free advertising for abortion, a message they are forced to convey. The decision may also affect laws in conservative states that require women seeking abortions to view ultrasounds or learn about fetal development, raising concerns that the government is used to block alternative viewpoints and discriminate against certain speech.  

END OF SUMMARY.
