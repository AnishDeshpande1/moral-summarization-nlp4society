SUMMARY: Democratic front‑runner Joe Biden accused President Donald Trump of stoking white‑supremacy rhetoric that he says fuels U.S. mass shootings, referencing the recent attacks in El Paso, Texas, and Dayton, Ohio, which killed 31 people. Biden delivered the remarks in Burlington, Iowa, while Senator Cory Booker echoed the theme of white nationalism and gun violence in a speech at Charleston’s historic Emanuel AME Church. Both Democrats linked Trump’s anti‑immigrant language—such as calling Central American migrants an “invasion”—to the motives behind the El Paso shooter’s manifesto. Trump, denying any racist intent, condemned racism and proposed mental‑health reforms and limited gun‑access measures, but stopped short of major gun‑law changes. The incidents have intensified criticism of Trump’s rhetoric and highlighted the Democratic field’s push for stricter gun controls, including universal background checks and licensing. Biden and Booker framed the debate as a moral imperative to confront racism and white supremacy rather than merely labeling individuals. END OF SUMMARY.
