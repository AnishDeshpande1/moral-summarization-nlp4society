SUMMARY:
Democratic presidential front-runner Joe Biden accused President Donald Trump of fueling white supremacy beliefs, which are blamed for several U.S. mass shootings. Biden made the remarks in Iowa as Trump visited two cities where 31 people were killed in rampages last weekend. Another Democratic contender, Senator Cory Booker, echoed similar sentiments at a historically black church in Charleston, South Carolina.

Biden and Booker criticized Trump's rhetoric, including his description of Central Americans trying to enter the U.S. as an "invasion," which they say has emboldened white nationalists. Biden said that Trump's words are not just rhetoric, but have real consequences. The two Democrats called for stricter gun laws, including universal background checks and banning assault-style weapons.

Trump responded by proposing reforms to mental health laws and working with social media to detect possible mass shooters, but stopped short of calling for major gun law reforms. Biden and Booker's comments mark a new level of intensity in the debate over Trump's rhetoric and its impact on white nationalism and gun violence. END OF SUMMARY.
