SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential front-runner Joe Biden accused President Donald Trump of fueling white supremacy beliefs, which are blamed for several US mass shootings. Biden made the accusation in a speech in Iowa, where he said Trump's rhetoric has "fanned the flames of white supremacy" in the nation. Another Democratic contender, Cory Booker, also spoke out against white nationalism and gun violence in a speech at a historically black church in South Carolina. The speeches come after two mass shootings last weekend, which left 31 people dead. Trump has denied being a racist and called for condemning racism, bigotry, and white supremacy. However, many critics argue that his rhetoric, including calling immigrants an "invasion," has contributed to the problem. Trump has proposed reforming mental health laws and working with social media to detect potential mass shooters, but stopped short of calling for major gun law reforms.

END OF SUMMARY.
