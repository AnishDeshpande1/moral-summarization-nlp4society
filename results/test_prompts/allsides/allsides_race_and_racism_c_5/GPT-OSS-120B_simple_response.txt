SUMMARY: Democratic frontrunner Joe Biden denounced President Donald Trump for “fanning the flames of white supremacy,” linking Trump’s rhetoric to the recent mass shootings in El Paso and Dayton that left 31 dead. Biden argued that Trump’s anti‑immigrant language—calling Central Americans an “invasion”—directly fed the hatred expressed in the shooters’ manifestos. Senator Cory Booker echoed this moral charge, warning that white‑nationalist hate is “planted and then harvested,” and urged action against racism rather than debates over who is racist. While Trump condemned racism and proposed limited mental‑health and gun‑safety measures, he stopped short of broader gun‑control reforms. The Democrats, including Biden and Booker, are calling for stricter gun laws and licensing, framing the issue as a moral imperative to combat hate, white supremacy, and gun violence. They portray Trump as aligning with “the darkest forces” and a “toxic tongue” that spreads division, emphasizing that the crucial question is not labeling racism but confronting and ending it. END OF SUMMARY.
