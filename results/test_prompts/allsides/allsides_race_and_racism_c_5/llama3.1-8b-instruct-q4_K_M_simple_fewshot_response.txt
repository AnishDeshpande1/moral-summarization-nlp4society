SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential front-runner Joe Biden accused President Donald Trump of fueling white supremacy beliefs blamed for several U.S. mass shootings. Biden said in a speech that Trump's rhetoric has "fanned the flames of white supremacy" and exposed him to renewed condemnation following the El Paso shooting. Trump aides deny his rhetoric was a cause of the shootings, but Biden argued that it is not far from calling Central Americans trying to enter the U.S. an "invasion" to the shooter in El Paso declaring "his attack is a response to the Hispanic invasion of Texas." The weekend's back-to-back mass shootings have intensified criticism of Trump's incendiary rhetoric. Biden and other Democratic presidential candidates, including Cory Booker, are calling for stricter gun laws and addressing the issue of white nationalism.
