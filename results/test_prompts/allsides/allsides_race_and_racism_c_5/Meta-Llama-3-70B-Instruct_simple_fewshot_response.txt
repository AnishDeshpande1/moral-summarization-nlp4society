SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential front-runner Joe Biden has accused President Donald Trump of fueling white supremacy beliefs, which have led to several U.S. mass shootings. Biden argues that Trump's rhetoric, including calling immigrants an "invasion," has contributed to a climate of hate and racism. Trump's visit to the cities where the mass shootings occurred was met with protests, and his proposed solutions, such as reforming mental health laws and working with social media, were deemed insufficient by many Democrats. Biden and other Democratic candidates have called for stricter gun laws, including universal background checks and banning assault-style weapons. The debate has shifted from whether Trump is a racist to what actions can be taken to address the problem of white supremacy and racism. Biden's strong condemnation of Trump's rhetoric and policies highlights the moral imperative to address the root causes of hate and violence.

END OF SUMMARY.
