SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential front-runner Joe Biden accused President Donald Trump of fueling white supremacy beliefs, which are blamed for several U.S. mass shootings. Biden said that Trump's rhetoric has fanned the flames of hate and racism in the nation. Another contender, Senator Cory Booker, echoed similar sentiments, saying that acts of hatred don't happen in a vacuum but are harvested from seeds planted by leaders like Trump.

Booker rejected the debate over whether Trump is a racist, focusing instead on what people do to address the problem. Biden criticized Trump's immigration policies and language, pointing out the similarity between Trump's "invasion" rhetoric and the shooter's manifesto in El Paso. Both Biden and Booker called for stricter gun laws and addressing white supremacy.

Trump visited cities affected by mass shootings and proposed reforms, but stopped short of major gun law changes. The visits were met with protests, highlighting the ongoing debate over Trump's role in fueling hate and violence.
