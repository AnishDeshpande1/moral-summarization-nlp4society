SUMMARY: Democratic presidential front-runner Joe Biden accused Republican President Donald Trump of fueling white supremacy beliefs blamed for several U.S. mass shootings, including the recent El Paso and Dayton rampages that killed 31 people. Biden stated that Trump's rhetoric has "fanned the flames of white supremacy" and "embraced a political strategy of hate, racism, and division." Another contender, Cory Booker, also condemned Trump's rhetoric, saying it has "harvested" hatred and white nationalism. Trump's response to the shootings was criticized for not addressing the root causes of racism and bigotry. Instead, he proposed reforming mental health laws and working with social media to detect possible mass shooters. Biden and Booker, along with other Democratic candidates, have called for stricter gun laws, including universal background checks and banning assault-style weapons. The debate has highlighted the need to address racism, hatred, and division in the nation. END OF SUMMARY.
