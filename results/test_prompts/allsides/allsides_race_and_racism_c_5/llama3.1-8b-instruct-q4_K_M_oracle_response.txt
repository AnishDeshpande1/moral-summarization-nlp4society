SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential front-runner Joe Biden accused President Donald Trump of fueling white supremacy beliefs blamed for several U.S. mass shootings, which killed dozens of people in rampages over the weekend. Biden claimed that Trump's rhetoric has fanned the flames of hatred and racism, contributing to the violence. Another Democratic contender, Cory Booker, echoed similar sentiments, stating that such acts of hatred are harvested when planted by leaders like Trump.

Booker rejected the debate over whether Trump is a racist, instead focusing on addressing the problem. Biden also criticized Trump's immigration policies, saying that his rhetoric has led directly to hate-filled attacks, such as the El Paso shooting where 22 people were killed in response to an "invasion" of Hispanics.

The mass shootings have intensified criticism of Trump's incendiary language and policies. Democrats are calling for stricter gun laws, including universal background checks and banning assault-style weapons. Biden accused Trump of aligning himself with dark forces and embracing a toxic strategy of hate and division.

END OF SUMMARY.
