SUMMARY:

Democratic presidential candidates Joe Biden and Cory Booker have accused President Donald Trump of fueling white supremacy beliefs that have led to several mass shootings in the US. Biden stated that Trump's rhetoric has "fanned the flames of white supremacy" and that his words have been a catalyst for hatred and violence. Booker echoed this sentiment, saying that acts of hatred do not happen in a vacuum, but are instead "harvested" from the rhetoric and actions of leaders. Trump has denied being a racist and has called for condemnation of racism, bigotry, and white supremacy, but his critics argue that his words and actions have contributed to a culture of hate. The debate has highlighted the need for stricter gun laws and a more nuanced approach to addressing white nationalism. Ultimately, the candidates are calling for moral leadership that rejects hate and division, and instead promotes unity and understanding.

END OF SUMMARY.
