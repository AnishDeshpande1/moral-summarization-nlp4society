SUMMARY:

The revised travel ban by President Donald Trump has been criticized by the United Nations, which stated that it will increase the woes of refugees worldwide. The executive order blocks entry to the US for citizens from six Muslim-majority countries, including Iran, Somalia, and Sudan, for a period of 90 days and suspends the US refugee program for 120 days. UN High Commissioner for Refugees Filippo Grandi expressed concern that the decision may compound the anguish for those affected, highlighting the importance of providing protection for people fleeing deadly violence. The revised ban excludes Iraq, which has been fighting against Islamic State, but includes changes to make it less vulnerable to legal challenges. Critics argue that the measures are unnecessary, divisive, and wrong, and that they send a terrible message to the rest of the world on the refugee crisis. The US administration's decision is seen as an abdication of its responsibilities under international law.

END OF SUMMARY.
