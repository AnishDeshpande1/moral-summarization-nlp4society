SUMMARY:

The United Nations has expressed concern over Donald Trump's revised travel ban, which will increase the woes of refugees worldwide. The executive order blocks entry to the US for citizens from six Muslim-majority countries for 90 days and suspends the refugee program for 120 days. The UN High Commissioner for Refugees, Filippo Grandi, emphasized that refugees are "ordinary people forced to flee war, violence, and persecution in their home countries." The revised ban was met with disappointment from some affected countries, including Sudan and Iran, which had cooperated with US anti-terrorist efforts.

The executive order is seen as a selective and discriminatory measure by the Organisation of Islamic Cooperation. Grandi warned that the decision will compound the anguish for those it affects, saying "the imperative remains to provide protection for people fleeing deadly violence." The UN called on the US to promote humanitarian ideals and exemplify leadership in refugee protection, particularly at a time when forced human displacement is at record highs.

The revised ban still raises concerns about promoting global stability and exemplifying humane leadership. Despite some changes, the order remains vulnerable to legal challenges.

END OF SUMMARY.
