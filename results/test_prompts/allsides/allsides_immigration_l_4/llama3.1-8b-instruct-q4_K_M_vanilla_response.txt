SUMMARY:

The United Nations has expressed concern over Donald Trump's revised travel ban, which affects citizens from six Muslim-majority countries, including Iran, Somalia, Sudan, Yemen, Syria, and Libya. The UN High Commissioner for Refugees, Filippo Grandi, stated that refugees are not criminals but ordinary people forced to flee war, violence, and persecution in their home countries. The revised ban excludes Iraq, which was previously included, and makes some changes to make it less vulnerable to legal challenges.

The reaction from affected countries has been muted compared to the original ban, with Iran's deputy foreign minister saying that Tehran will wait and see the details of the new order before reacting. Other countries, such as Libya, have not made any statements regarding the revised ban. The UN remains concerned about the impact on refugees and has called for humane leadership in addressing the global refugee crisis.

END OF SUMMARY
