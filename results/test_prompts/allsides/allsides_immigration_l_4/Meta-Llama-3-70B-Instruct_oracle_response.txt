SUMMARY:

The United Nations has expressed concern that Donald Trump's revised travel ban will increase the woes of the world's refugees, who are "ordinary people forced to flee war, violence, and persecution" in their home countries. The ban blocks entry to the US for citizens from six Muslim-majority countries for 90 days and suspends the US refugee program for 120 days. The UN High Commissioner for Refugees, Filippo Grandi, emphasized that refugees are not criminals, but people in need of protection from deadly violence. The revised ban has been met with disappointment from affected countries, who claim to have fully cooperated with US anti-terrorist efforts. The ban's implementation has been criticized for being based on misleading data and for being selective and discriminatory. The UN Secretary-General, António Guterres, pointedly visited Somalia, where people are dying due to famine. The revised ban has been called a "retrograde step" that sends a "terrible message" to the world on the refugee crisis.

END OF SUMMARY.
