SUMMARY: The United Nations condemned Donald Trump’s revised travel ban, saying it will worsen the plight of refugees. The new executive order bars citizens of six Muslim‑majority countries—Iran, Somalia, Sudan, Yemen, Syria and Libya—from entering the U.S. for 90 days and suspends the refugee program for 120 days, though it exempts permanent residents, visa holders, existing refugees, asylum recipients and dual citizens. UN High Commissioner for Refugees Filippo Grandi stressed that refugees are “ordinary people” fleeing war and persecution, and warned the ban could deepen their suffering. Reactions from affected nations were mixed: Sudan’s foreign ministry expressed disappointment; Iran said it would “react in proportion” and keep its retaliatory measures; Iraq was omitted from the list and its prime minister was urged not to retaliate; Saudi Arabia and other Gulf states remained exempt and silent. The ban’s impact will be felt most by roughly 15,000 Somali refugees in Kenya. Critics—including the OIC, Britain’s shadow foreign secretary, and Australian PM Malcolm Turnbull—denounced the measure as discriminatory, contrary to international law, and unlikely to survive legal challenges, despite its more limited scope compared with the original order. END OF SUMMARY.
