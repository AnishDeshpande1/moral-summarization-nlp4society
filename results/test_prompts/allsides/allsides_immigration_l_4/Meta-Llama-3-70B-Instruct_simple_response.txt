SUMMARY:

The United Nations has expressed concern over Donald Trump's revised travel ban, which will worsen the plight of refugees worldwide. The ban blocks entry to the US for citizens from six Muslim-majority countries for 90 days and suspends the US refugee program for 120 days. The UN High Commissioner for Refugees, Filippo Grandi, emphasized that refugees are ordinary people fleeing war, violence, and persecution, not criminals. The revised ban has been met with disappointment from affected countries, including Sudan, Iran, and Somalia, which have cooperated with US anti-terrorist efforts. The UN and other countries have criticized the ban as unnecessary, divisive, and wrong, arguing that it will compound the anguish for those affected and send a terrible message to the rest of the world on the refugee crisis.

END OF SUMMARY.
