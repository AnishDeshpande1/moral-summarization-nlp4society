SUMMARY:

The United Nations has expressed concern over Donald Trump's revised travel ban, which affects citizens from six Muslim-majority countries. The ban blocks entry to the US for 90 days and suspends the refugee program for 120 days. The UN High Commissioner for Refugees, Filippo Grandi, emphasized that refugees are not criminals but ordinary people forced to flee war, violence, and persecution.

The revised ban excludes Iraq, a country currently fighting against Islamic State in Mosul, and clarifies that permanent residents and existing visa holders can enter the US. However, Grandi stated that the UN remains concerned about the impact on refugees and the need for protection from deadly violence.

Several countries, including Iran and Sudan, have expressed disappointment or muted responses to the revised ban. The Organization of Islamic Cooperation criticized the earlier order as "selective and discriminatory," and the UK's shadow foreign secretary described the ban as a "retrograde step" that abdicates US responsibilities under international law.

END OF SUMMARY
