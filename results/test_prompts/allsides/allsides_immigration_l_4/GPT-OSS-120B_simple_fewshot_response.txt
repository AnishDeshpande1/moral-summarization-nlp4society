SUMMARY:
The United Nations blasted President Trump’s revised travel ban, warning it will deepen the suffering of refugees who are “ordinary people forced to flee war, violence and persecution.” The order again bars citizens of six Muslim‑majority nations—Iran, Somalia, Sudan, Yemen, Syria and Libya—from entering the United States for 90 days and suspends the refugee program for 120 days, even as the UN stresses America’s historic role as a humanitarian leader. Though the revised decree is quieter than the chaotic original and includes limited exemptions for permanent residents and visa holders, officials from the affected countries expressed disappointment and vowed to watch the details closely. Somalia’s president urged the ban’s removal, noting his nation’s capacity to combat terrorism, while Iran and Sudan signaled continued diplomatic protest. Critics—from the OIC and Britain’s shadow foreign secretary to Australia’s prime minister—decry the measure as discriminatory, unnecessary and contrary to international law, warning it will compound the anguish of the world’s most vulnerable and undermine global stability. END OF SUMMARY.
