SUMMARY:  
The United Nations condemned Donald Trump’s revised travel **ban**, warning it will **compound** the **anguish** of refugees forced to flee **war**, **violence** and **persecution**. The new executive **order** still **blocks** entry for citizens of Iran, Somalia, Sudan, Yemen, Syria and Libya and **suspends** the U.S. refugee programme for 120 days, despite claims that the original **efforts** were based on **misleading** data. UN officials stressed that refugees are not criminals but ordinary people in need of **protection** and **support**, and that America’s historic **leadership** in **welcoming** displaced families sets a **gold standard** for humanitarian **exemplifying**. Critics called the **decision** **selective**, **discriminatory**, and an **abdication** of international responsibilities, saying it **promotes** division and **displaces** lives further. While Iraq is exempt and some **measures** were softened to reduce legal challenges, the **war‑torn** Somali community of 15,000 in Kenya remains at risk. The ban’s **impact** is seen as a retrograde step that **dies** the hope for safety and dignity for those already **dying** from famine and conflict.  

END OF SUMMARY.
