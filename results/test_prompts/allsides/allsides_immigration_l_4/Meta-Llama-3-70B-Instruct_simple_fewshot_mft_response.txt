SUMMARY:

The United Nations has criticized Donald Trump's revised travel ban, warning that it will increase the suffering of refugees worldwide. The ban, which blocks entry to the US for citizens from six Muslim-majority countries, is a "retrograde step" that abdicates the US's responsibilities under international law. The UN High Commissioner for Refugees, Filippo Grandi, emphasized that refugees are ordinary people fleeing war, violence, and persecution, not criminals. The ban is seen as a harmful and unfair measure that will compound the anguish of those affected. The exclusion of Iraq from the ban is a notable exception, likely due to the country's cooperation with the US in fighting Islamic State. The revised ban is still vulnerable to legal challenges and has been met with disappointment from affected countries, including Sudan, Iran, and Libya. The international community has condemned the ban as a violation of humanitarian ideals, with the UN Secretary-General António Guterres making an emergency visit to Somalia to highlight the famine and suffering there. END OF SUMMARY.
