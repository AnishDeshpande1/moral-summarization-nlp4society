SUMMARY:  
The United Nations condemned Donald Trump’s revised travel ban as a moral failure that will deepen the suffering of refugees, reminding the world that those fleeing war and persecution are “ordinary people,” not criminals. The new executive order bars citizens of six Muslim‑majority countries—Iran, Somalia, Sudan, Yemen, Syria and Libya—from entering the United States for 90 days and pauses the U.S. refugee program for 120 days, even as it carves out exemptions for permanent residents, visa holders and dual citizens. UN officials warned that the temporary measure could compound anguish for thousands of displaced people, especially the 15,000 Somalis in Kenya hoping to resettle in America. While some Muslim‑majority states expressed disappointment and hinted at reciprocal actions, the response was muted compared to the chaos of the original ban. Critics—from the OIC to Britain’s shadow foreign secretary—decried the policy as discriminatory, unlawful and morally wrong, urging the U.S. to uphold its humanitarian legacy. The ban’s limited scope and procedural tweaks may reduce legal challenges, but the ethical outcry remains: the world needs humane leadership, not policies that abandon the most vulnerable.  
END OF SUMMARY.
