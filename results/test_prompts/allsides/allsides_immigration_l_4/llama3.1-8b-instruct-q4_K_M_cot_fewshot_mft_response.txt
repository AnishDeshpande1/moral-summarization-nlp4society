SUMMARY:
The United Nations has expressed concern over Donald Trump's revised travel ban, which will increase the woes of the world's refugees. The executive order blocks entry to the US for citizens from six Muslim-majority countries for 90 days and suspends the US refugee programme for 120 days. The UN high commissioner for refugees, Filippo Grandi, said that refugees are not criminals but "ordinary people forced to flee war, violence and persecution in their home countries." He emphasized the need for protection and dignity for those fleeing deadly violence. The revised ban may compound the anguish for those affected, particularly nearly 15,000 Somalis in a Kenyan refugee camp who hoped to resettle in the US. The main Gulf states remain exempt from the ban, while Saudi Arabia has maintained its policy of discretion on the issue. Critics have described the ban as "retrograde step" and "unnecessary, divisive and wrong." END OF SUMMARY.
