SUMMARY:
The United Nations has expressed concern over President Trump's revised travel ban, which will increase the woes of the world's refugees. The ban blocks entry to the US for citizens from six Muslim-majority countries and suspends the US refugee program for 120 days. The UN High Commissioner for Refugees, Filippo Grandi, stated that refugees are not criminals but ordinary people forced to flee war, violence, and persecution in their home countries. The revised ban excludes Iraq, a country alongside which the US is fighting to defeat Islamic State, and clarifies that permanent residents and existing visa holders can enter the US. However, the UN remains concerned about the decision's impact on refugees, particularly the nearly 15,000 Somalis in Kenya who hoped to resettle in the US. The ban has been met with disappointment from some of the affected countries, including Iran and Sudan.
