SUMMARY:
The United Nations condemned President Trump’s revised travel ban as an abuse of executive authority that subverts international humanitarian norms. By blocking citizens of six Muslim‑majority nations and suspending the U.S. refugee programme, the order inflicts further harm on already vulnerable people fleeing war, famine and persecution, threatening the lives and dignity of thousands of Somalis, Sudanese, Iranians and others. Critics argue the ban is fundamentally unfair, targeting a specific religious group and cheating the world of the United States’ long‑standing role as a “gold standard” in refugee protection. The measure also degrades the moral sanctity of humanitarian aid, portraying refugees as security threats rather than ordinary civilians forced to flee violence. While the revised decree softens some legal loopholes—allowing permanent residents, visa holders and asylum seekers to enter—it does little to alleviate the anguish of those affected. International leaders from the UN, the OIC, Britain and Australia denounced the policy as discriminatory, divisive and contrary to global commitments to protect the displaced. END OF SUMMARY.
