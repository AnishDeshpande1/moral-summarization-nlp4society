**SUMMARY:**
The revised travel ban by Donald Trump has been met with disappointment from the United Nations, which says it will increase the woes of refugees. The UN high commissioner for refugees, Filippo Grandi, emphasizes that refugees are not criminals but ordinary people forced to flee war, violence, and persecution in their home countries. The new executive order blocks entry to the US for citizens from six Muslim-majority countries for a period of 90 days and suspends the US refugee programme for 120 days. Critics argue that this is an unnecessary and divisive measure that goes against humanitarian ideals. The UN remains concerned about the potential harm it may cause, saying it will further compound the anguish for those affected.
