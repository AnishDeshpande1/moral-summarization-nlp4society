SUMMARY:

The House of Representatives has passed a bill banning taxpayer funding for abortions, despite opposition from moderate Republicans and a veto threat from the White House. The bill's passage comes as anti-abortion rights activists gathered on the National Mall for the annual "March for Life." However, the real story is the internal spat among Republicans, who were forced to pull a more extreme bill banning "late-term" abortions due to concerns from female GOP members. The provision in question required women to file a police report in order to access an abortion in cases of rape, sparking outrage from those who argued it would further traumatize victims. The decision to pull the bill is a rare win for centrist members, who are pushing back against the party's focus on contentious social issues. The moral framing of this issue is clear: is it right for the government to dictate women's personal healthcare decisions, potentially causing harm to those who need access to abortion services? The bill's proponents argue it's a matter of protecting the unborn, while critics see it as an attack on women's autonomy and health.

END OF SUMMARY.
