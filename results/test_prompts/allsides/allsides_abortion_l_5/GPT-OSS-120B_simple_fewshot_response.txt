SUMMARY:
The GOP‑controlled House pushed a “no‑taxpayer‑funds for abortions” bill through on Thursday, but the more extreme “late‑term” abortion ban was abruptly withdrawn after moderate Republicans warned it was too harsh. The withdrawal was hailed by centrist GOP members as a rare concession to women’s health and political pragmatism, while anti‑abortion activists blasted the move as a betrayal of pro‑life principles. Democrats, led by Nancy Pelosi, seized on the episode to accuse Republicans of putting ideology ahead of women’s health and economic priorities. The episode exposed growing tension within the Republican conference between hard‑line conservatives and a growing bloc of female and swing‑district members who fear that symbolic, socially divisive votes damage the party’s broader appeal ahead of the 2016 election. While the anti‑abortion “no‑funding” measure passed 242‑179, the internal fight over the 20‑week ban underscores a moral dilemma for the GOP: whether to prioritize a strict pro‑life agenda at the cost of women’s rights and electoral viability, or to moderate its stance to avoid alienating voters.  
END OF SUMMARY.
