SUMMARY: The GOP‑controlled House passed a bill banning all taxpayer funding for abortions, a backup after a “late‑term” ban was pulled amid backlash from moderate Republicans. The vote (242‑179) saw three Democrats join Republicans, while Rep. Richard Hanna voted against it. The White House vowed to veto. The move came as tens of thousands marched for life on the 42nd anniversary of Roe v. Wade. The original “Pain‑Capable Unborn Child Protection Act,” which would have banned abortions after 20 weeks, was withdrawn after female GOP members objected to a rape exemption that required a police report—highlighting that most rapes and incest go unreported. Critics like Nancy Pelosi argued the ban “undermines” women’s health and is “worse” than the withdrawn bill, saying it “affects” millions and sends the “wrong” message. Centrist Republicans called the measure “symbolic” and urged the party to focus on “difference”‑making policies rather than “less” contentious social votes. The episode shows the GOP’s internal struggle over how far to push anti‑abortion bans. END OF SUMMARY.
