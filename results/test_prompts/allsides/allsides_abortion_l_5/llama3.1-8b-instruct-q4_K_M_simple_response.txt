SUMMARY:

In a significant move, the US House of Representatives passed legislation banning taxpayer money for abortions, with 242-179 votes in favor. However, the bill's passage was preceded by a contentious debate over another proposal to ban "late-term" abortions, which was withdrawn due to opposition from moderate Republicans who deemed it too extreme. The withdrawal sparked criticism from anti-abortion activists and some Republican leaders, highlighting the party's internal divisions on social issues. Female GOP members led by North Carolina Rep. Renee Ellmers opposed a provision that required women who were raped to provide evidence of filing a police report to access an abortion, citing concerns about unreported cases.

The debate comes as tens of thousands of anti-abortion activists gathered for the annual "March for Life" and on the 42nd anniversary of the Roe v. Wade decision, which upheld a woman's right to access abortion based on privacy rights. The White House has pledged to veto the legislation if it reaches President Obama's desk.

END OF SUMMARY.
