SUMMARY:

The US House of Representatives passed legislation banning taxpayer money for abortions, but the bill was initially intended as a backup after another proposal to ban late-term abortions was pulled due to opposition from moderate Republicans. The "Pain Capable Unborn Child Protection Act" aimed to ban abortion procedures beyond 20 weeks into pregnancy, but a provision requiring women who are raped to show evidence of filing a police report sparked controversy among female GOP members. The bill's author, Rep. Trent Franks, expressed disappointment at its non-passage, while Dr. Russell Moore, president of the Southern Baptist Convention's Ethics and Religious Liberty Commission, criticized the Republican leadership for not pursuing the vote on the more restrictive abortion measure.

The White House has vowed to veto the legislation if it reaches President Obama's desk. The vote comes as tens of thousands of anti-abortion rights activists gathered in Washington D.C. for the annual "March for Life" event commemorating the 42nd anniversary of the Roe v. Wade decision.

END OF SUMMARY.
