SUMMARY: The House GOP pushed a hard‑line anti‑abortion agenda, first pulling a 20‑week “late‑term” ban after moderate Republicans warned the measure was too extreme and would alienate women voters. Instead, they passed a symbolic bill that bans any federal funding for abortions, winning the support of almost all Republicans and three Democrats, while the White House vowed to veto it. The scramble revealed a moral clash within the party: pro‑life legislators argued they must protect unborn life, yet many centrist members and female GOP representatives, led by Rep. Renee Ellmers, condemned provisions that forced rape victims to prove a police report, arguing that such rules betray compassion and ignore the reality of unreported sexual assault. Democrats, led by Nancy Pelosi, seized on the discord to criticize the GOP for prioritizing punitive morality over women’s health, jobs, and economic policy. The episode underscores a growing tension between ideological purity and pragmatic, humane governance as the party eyes the 2016 election. END OF SUMMARY.
