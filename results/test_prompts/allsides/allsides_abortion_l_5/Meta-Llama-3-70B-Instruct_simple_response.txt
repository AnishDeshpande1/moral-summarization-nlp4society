SUMMARY:

The GOP-controlled House of Representatives passed a bill banning taxpayer funding for abortions, but only after a more extreme proposal to ban "late-term" abortions was scrapped due to backlash from moderate Republicans. The rejected bill would have required women to provide evidence of a police report to access an abortion in cases of rape, sparking criticism from female GOP members who argued it was too restrictive. The decision to pull the bill was seen as a rare win for centrist members, who feared it would send the wrong message to women and young people. The move was met with disappointment from anti-abortion rights activists, who accused the GOP of not doing enough to restrict abortion access. The debate highlights the ongoing struggle within the Republican party between conservatives and moderates on social issues.
