SUMMARY:

The GOP-controlled House of Representatives passed a bill banning taxpayer money for abortions, but only after a more extreme proposal to ban "late-term" abortions was yanked due to moderate Republicans' concerns. The original bill would have required women who were raped to show evidence of a police report to access an abortion, sparking outrage from female GOP members who argued it was too extreme. The provision was criticized for potentially undermining the health of millions of women and affecting their personal decisions. Religious activists were unhappy with the decision to cancel the vote, calling it a "wrong" message, especially on the day of the March for Life. The willingness of moderate Republicans to challenge their leaders may complicate future legislation, as they seek to focus on broader economic messages rather than symbolic votes. The bill banning taxpayer funds for abortions was approved, but the White House has promised to veto it.

END OF SUMMARY.
