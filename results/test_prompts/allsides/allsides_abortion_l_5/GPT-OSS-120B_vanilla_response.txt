SUMMARY:
The GOP‑controlled House passed a bill banning the use of federal taxpayer money for abortions, voting 242‑179 with three Democrats joining most Republicans; only Rep. Richard Hanna opposed it. The White House signaled it would veto the measure. Earlier, a more controversial “Pain Capable Unborn Child Protection Act” that would ban abortions after 20 weeks was withdrawn after moderate Republicans, especially female members, objected to a rape‑exception clause requiring a police report. The pull‑back was hailed by centrist Republicans as a strategic move ahead of the 2016 election, while anti‑abortion activists criticized the decision. House Democratic leader Nancy Pelosi condemned the abortion‑funding ban as harmful to women’s health and a distraction from economic issues. The episode highlights growing intra‑party tensions between conservative hard‑liners and moderates, potentially affecting future legislation. END OF SUMMARY.
