SUMMARY:

The GOP-controlled House of Representatives passed legislation banning taxpayer money for abortions, but only after a more extreme proposal was yanked due to backlash from moderate Republicans. The bill, which was approved in the last Congress, now heads to the White House, where it will likely be vetoed by President Obama. The decision to pull the more extreme bill was seen as a rare win for centrist members, who argued that it sent the wrong message to women and young people. Anti-abortion rights groups were unhappy with the leadership's decision, but some female Republicans, including Rep. Renee Ellmers of North Carolina, spoke out against the provision requiring evidence of police reports for raped women to access an abortion. The debate highlights the ongoing tension between social conservatives and more moderate Republicans in the party.
