SUMMARY:

Despite repeated predictions of his demise, Donald Trump remains a resilient figure in American politics. Critics have repeatedly written off his presidency as over, citing various events such as the COVID-19 crisis and the George Floyd protests as the final nail in the coffin. However, each time, Trump has proven them wrong, with his approval ratings remaining steady or even increasing in some areas. His durability is a testament to his ability to adapt and connect with his base. Critics' hyperbole, such as labeling him "dictator" or predicting that he will be responsible for civilian deaths, only serves to further embolden Trump's supporters and undermine the credibility of those who would seek to bring him down. As Trump continues to defy expectations, it seems prudent for critics to hold off on declaring his presidency over until after the 2024 election.
