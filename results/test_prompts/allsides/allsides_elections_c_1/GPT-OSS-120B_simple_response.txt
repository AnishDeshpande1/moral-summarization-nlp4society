SUMMARY: The author rebukes the persistent “Trump is finished” narrative that has haunted Donald Trump from the start of his campaign, arguing that every time pundits proclaimed his political death—whether over the COVID‑19 response, the economic downturn, or the George Floyd protests—they were proven wrong. Contrary to bleak forecasts, Trump’s approval ratings surged during the pandemic, his economic ratings stayed positive, the stock market rebounded, and unemployment fell dramatically. Even the most dramatic protest images and accusations of dictatorial intent have not shaken his base. Poll aggregators show his support remains above his lowest points and tracks closely with former presidents like Obama and Bush, outpacing one‑term predecessors. The piece claims the hyperbole of his critics fuels a backlash that actually benefits him, and warns that Democrats’ extreme narratives risk alienating voters. Concluding with a moral call for restraint, the author suggests the media should pause “Trump is finished” editorials until after the 2024 election, letting the American experiment decide its fate without premature condemnation. END OF SUMMARY.
