SUMMARY: The article argues that predictions of Donald Trump’s political demise have repeatedly proved false. From early campaign doubts to claims that the COVID‑19 response, the economic downturn, and the George Floyd protests would end his presidency, each forecast has been contradicted by rising approval ratings, a strong stock market rebound, record job gains, and polling that shows Trump remains comparable to past two‑term presidents and ahead of recent one‑term presidents. The piece criticizes media pundits for hyperbolic “Trump is finished” narratives, noting that such exaggerations often backfire and that Trump’s durability persists despite intense criticism. It suggests that critics should refrain from declaring Trump finished until after the 2024 election, after which they can revisit the narrative. END OF SUMMARY.
