SUMMARY:
The article argues that the relentless “Trump is finished” narrative is a politically motivated subversion of authority, repeatedly overstated by pundits who ignore the president’s actual performance. It highlights how critics claim Trump has abandoned his duties, yet his approval ratings on COVID‑19 response, the economy, and job creation have remained solid, suggesting a fair assessment of his leadership is being cheated by exaggerated doom‑saying. The piece also points out the careless, harmful portrayals of Trump during the George Floyd protests—doctored images, accusations of bunker‑hiding, and vilification of his support for law‑and‑order—while overlooking the harm inflicted on victims of looting and the deaths of police officers. By emphasizing the loyalty of Trump’s base, the author contends that the constant attacks betray a disloyal, partisan bias that distorts reality. The author calls for a moratorium on “Trump is finished” pieces until after the 2024 election, warning that continued hyperbole undermines fair discourse, harms democratic trust, and unjustly subverts legitimate authority. END OF SUMMARY.
