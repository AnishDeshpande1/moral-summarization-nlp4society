SUMMARY:
The article argues that the persistent narrative declaring Donald Trump “finished” is a recurring falsehood that has survived every major crisis—from early primary doubts and the bogus Russian‑collusion story to the COVID‑19 pandemic, the economic downturn, and the George Floyd protests. Each time pundits predicted his downfall, Trump’s approval ratings either surged or remained solid, the economy rebounded, the stock market hit new highs and unemployment fell dramatically. The piece highlights how media commentators have repeatedly exaggerated his supposed demise, portraying him as a hollow shell or a looming dictator, while ignoring the resilience of his support and the comparable or better polling numbers he holds against former presidents. The moral framing suggests that critics’ hyperbole fuels a self‑defeating prophecy; the author urges a pause on “Trump is finished” narratives until after the 2024 election, warning that such relentless negativity undermines the very democratic experiment it claims to protect. END OF SUMMARY.
