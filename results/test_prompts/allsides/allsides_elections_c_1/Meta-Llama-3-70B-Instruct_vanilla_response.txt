SUMMARY:

The article argues that despite numerous predictions of his downfall, Donald Trump's presidency has consistently defied expectations. Critics have written him off multiple times, from his presidential campaign to his handling of the COVID-19 crisis, the economy, and the George Floyd protests. However, Trump's approval ratings have remained strong, and the economy has rebounded strongly. The article suggests that the hyperbole of Trump's critics, who have predicted his demise and dictator-like behavior, has actually worked in his favor. It notes that Trump's approval numbers compare favorably with his predecessors, and that his critics' narratives have been consistently wrong. The article concludes that Trump's durability and his critics' track record of errors make it likely that he will be reelected.
