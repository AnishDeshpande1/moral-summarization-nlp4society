SUMMARY:

Donald Trump's critics have repeatedly predicted his downfall, but he continues to defy expectations. Despite numerous "obituaries" written about him, Trump remains a resilient figure in American politics. Recent crises, including the COVID-19 pandemic and the killing of George Floyd, were supposed to be the final nails in his coffin, but his approval ratings have remained relatively stable.

Critics such as Peter Wehner, Robert Reich, Jennifer Rubin, and Paul Krugman have been wrong time and again about Trump's fate. Their hyperbole and exaggerations often backfire, fueling Trump's base and making him more durable. In fact, Trump's numbers compare favorably to his predecessors, including Barack Obama and George W. Bush.

The author argues that Trump's critics should refrain from writing "he's finished" pieces until after the 2024 election, as their track record is dismal. By acknowledging Trump's resilience and the hyperbole of his critics, we can better understand the complexities of American politics and the factors contributing to Trump's enduring popularity.

END OF SUMMARY.
