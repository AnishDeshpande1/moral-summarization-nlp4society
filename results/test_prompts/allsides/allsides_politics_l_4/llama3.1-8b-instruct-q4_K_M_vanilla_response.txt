SUMMARY:

A scheme to fabricate sexual misconduct allegations against special counsel Robert Mueller appears to have been foiled, but not before it made headlines. Conservative activist Jacob Wohl, known for his pro-Trump tweets and dubious claims, suggested that a story about such allegations was coming. However, journalists quickly detected the plot, which involved a company called Surefire Intelligence, allegedly investigating the claims. Further investigation revealed that Surefire may be nothing more than a front for Wohl himself. The FBI is now investigating the matter after Mueller's office referred it to them.

Wohl's scheme may have been an attempt to smear Mueller and the investigation, but it also appears to have had the effect of distracting from other important stories just before the midterm elections. The scheme has been widely debunked by journalists, with many outlets devoting resources to exposing its flaws. The outcome of the FBI's investigation remains unclear, but Wohl may face consequences for his actions.

END OF SUMMARY.
