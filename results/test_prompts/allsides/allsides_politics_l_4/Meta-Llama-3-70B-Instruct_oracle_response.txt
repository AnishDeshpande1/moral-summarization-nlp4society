SUMMARY:

A suspicious attempt to smear Special Counsel Robert Mueller with phony sexual misconduct allegations has been exposed, and may have landed conservative activists in hot water with the FBI. The apparent plot involved offering women money to make false claims against Mueller. The key players, Jacob Wohl and Jack Burkman, have a history of making dubious claims and running shady operations. Wohl, a 20-year-old conservative, was cagey about his involvement, while Burkman insisted that the private intelligence company, Surefire, was "real" and run by Wohl. However, online sleuths discovered that Surefire was essentially a one-man show, with Wohl using different voices to convince others of his claims. The FBI is now investigating the allegations, which may have been a scam to troll journalists and steal the spotlight. Mueller's office referred the matter to the FBI, and several media outlets devoted resources to debunking Wohl's claims. The incident has raised questions about the motivations behind the attempt to smear Mueller.

END OF SUMMARY.
