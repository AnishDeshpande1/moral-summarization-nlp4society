SUMMARY:  
An alleged scheme to fabricate sexual‑misconduct accusations against Special Counsel Robert Mueller unraveled on Tuesday, prompting Mueller’s office to refer the matter to the FBI. The plot appears linked to conservative provocateur Jacob Wohl and Republican lobbyist Jack Burkman, who have a history of dubious political stunts. Wohl allegedly used a fictitious private‑intelligence firm called “Surefire Intelligence” to claim women were being paid to make false allegations. Burkman publicly defended Surefire as real, while journalists traced the operation back to Wohl himself. The far‑right site Gateway Pundit posted a heavily redacted document alleging rape by Mueller, then removed it amid criticism. Mueller’s spokesperson confirmed the FBI is now investigating the claim that women were offered money for false accusations. Whether the effort was a genuine smear attempt or a trolling stunt, it succeeded in drawing media attention away from other issues ahead of the midterm elections. Burkman has promised a press conference to reveal “the first” of Mueller’s alleged victims, but past similar events have proved empty. The FBI investigation will determine if any laws were broken and whether the conspirators face legal consequences.  
END OF SUMMARY.
