SUMMARY:

An apparent attempt by conservative activists Jacob Wohl and Jack Burkman to drum up phony sexual misconduct allegations against special counsel Robert Mueller has collapsed spectacularly, potentially landing them in hot water with the FBI. The scheme, which involved offering money to women to make false claims, was exposed after journalists reacted with alarm on Twitter. Wohl's involvement in the plot is unclear, but Burkman claimed that "Surefire," a supposed private intelligence company investigating the allegations, was real and run by Wohl. However, online researchers found that Surefire was likely just a front for Wohl's own activities. The far-right website Gateway Pundit posted a redacted document making a rape allegation against Mueller, but later took it down after Twitter mockery. Mueller's office has referred the matter to the FBI for investigation. This incident highlights the attempts by some conservative activists to undermine Mueller and the investigation, demonstrating a disregard for authority and fairness.

END OF SUMMARY.
