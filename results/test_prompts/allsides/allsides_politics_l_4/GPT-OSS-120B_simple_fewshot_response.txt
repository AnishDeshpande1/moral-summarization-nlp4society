SUMMARY:
A flimsy, likely fabricated scheme to accuse Special Counsel Robert Mueller of sexual misconduct collapsed, exposing a group of far‑right activists who appear to have tried to weaponize false allegations for political gain. Jacob Wohl, a Trump‑loyal Twitter provocateur, and Republican lobbyist Jack Burkman promoted a fake “Surefire Intelligence” operation that promised money to women willing to lie about Mueller. Their ruse spilled onto the internet when the Gateway Pundit posted a heavily redacted, dubious rape claim, only to delete it after journalists mocked the hoax. Mueller’s office referred the matter to the FBI, and investigators are now probing whether the plot was a deliberate smear or a trolling stunt. If the FBI finds wrongdoing, Wohl and possibly Burkman could face legal trouble; if it was merely a publicity stunt, they have already succeeded in diverting media attention from substantive election‑season issues. The episode underscores how extremist conspiracists weaponize fabricated sexual accusations to harass opponents, waste journalistic resources, and undermine democratic discourse.  

END OF SUMMARY.
