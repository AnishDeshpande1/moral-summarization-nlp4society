SUMMARY:

Conservative activists Jacob Wohl and Jack Burkman may have been involved in a scheme to drum up false claims of sexual misconduct against special counsel Robert Mueller, attempting to smear his reputation. The plot was uncovered when journalists noticed that the allegations were suspiciously thin and seemed to be an attempt to troll the media. An investigation by Mueller's office referred the matter to the FBI, who are now looking into it.

Wohl, a 20-year-old pro-Trump Twitter personality, was involved in the scheme through his company Surefire Intelligence, which appears to be a scam. Burkman, a Republican lobbyist and conspiracy theorist, was also involved and claimed that Surefire was a real company investigating the allegations. However, online sleuths revealed that Surefire was essentially Wohl himself, making dubious claims.

The attempt to smear Mueller's reputation has been debunked by journalists, but it may have had an unintended consequence: diverting attention away from more important stories just before the midterm elections.
