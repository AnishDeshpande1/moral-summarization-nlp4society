SUMMARY:

The Obama White House is suffering from the "60s disease," a terminal affliction that has led to a culture of contempt for institutions, particularly the military. The administration's handling of the Benghazi tragedy is a prime example of this disease. President Obama and his top aides peddled elaborate lies and evasions to cover up their betrayal of trust, including awarding medals for bravery in Benghazi as a "salve for conscience." The administration's original story about the tragedy was full of holes, plugged with bigger lies, and only now do they admit that parts of their stories were "misleading." Hillary Clinton must answer for her cold indifference to the plight of those fighting for their lives in Benghazi. The author argues that this lack of accountability is a result of the administration's values, which prioritize self-interest over sacrifice and loyalty.

END OF SUMMARY.
