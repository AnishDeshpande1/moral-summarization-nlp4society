SUMMARY:

The Obama administration is criticized for its handling of the Benghazi tragedy, with the author accusing them of betraying the trust of their own ambassador and others left to die. The president and his top aides, including Hillary Clinton, are said to have peddled lies and evasions to cover up their betrayal. The author argues that the administration's actions are rooted in the "60s disease" of selfishness, greed, and contempt for institutions, particularly the military. The president's past, including his associations with Bill Ayers and Marxist mentors, is seen as shaping his lack of understanding and appreciation for America. The article highlights the administration's changing versions of the Benghazi story, full of holes and lies, and their ultimate admission that parts of their stories were "misleading." The author condemns Hillary Clinton's cold indifference to the plight of those fighting for their lives in Benghazi, and the administration's lack of understanding of sacrifice and duty.
