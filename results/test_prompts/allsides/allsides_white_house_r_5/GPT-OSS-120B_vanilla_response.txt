SUMMARY: The article offers a highly critical, opinion‑laden view of the Obama administration, accusing senior staff of being “boomers” with a 1960s‑era cultural disdain for institutions, especially the military. It alleges that President Obama’s background and associations, including ties to Bill Ayers, left him ill‑prepared for leadership. The piece focuses on the 2012 Benghazi attack, claiming the administration deliberately misled the public, concealed the availability of rescue forces, and fabricated explanations linking the attack to a video of the Prophet Muhammad. It asserts that Hillary Clinton and other officials lied or were evasive about the incident, and that the White House later admitted parts of its narrative were “misleading.” The author concludes that the administration’s handling of Benghazi reflects broader moral failings rooted in a “‘60s disease.” END OF SUMMARY.
