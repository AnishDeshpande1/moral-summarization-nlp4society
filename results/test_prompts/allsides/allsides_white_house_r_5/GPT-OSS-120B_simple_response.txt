The article attacks the Obama administration as a product of the “‘60s disease,” portraying its senior staff as self‑absorbed boomers who inherited a culture of irresponsibility, contempt for institutions, and disdain for the military. It accuses Bill Clinton of disrespecting uniformed guards, and depicts Barack Obama as an outsider shaped by radical influences such as Bill Ayers, suggesting he lacks loyalty to America. The piece frames the Benghazi tragedy as a moral failure: the president allegedly ignored offers from elite Delta Force and Green Beret teams, allowed a limited rescue, and then spun a series of lies, “hoaxes,” and “disinformation” to hide the truth. It claims the administration’s false narrative about a Muhammad‑video protest was a deliberate cover‑up designed to damage Obama’s 2012 re‑election prospects, and that Hillary Clinton’s cold, indifferent responses betray a “faithful child of the ‘60s.” The author concludes that the White House’s deception and contempt for sacrifice must be exposed and that Clinton must answer for her “sins” of indifference.
