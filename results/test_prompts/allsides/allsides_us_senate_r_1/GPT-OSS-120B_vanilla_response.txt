SUMMARY: The Senate is set to consider the Born‑Alive Abortion Survivors Protection Act, which would require doctors to provide standard medical care to infants who survive an attempted abortion. The bill, reintroduced by Sen. Ben Sasse, aims to ensure that any newborn who is born alive receives the same level of care as any other child of the same gestational age. Democrats have opposed the measure, arguing that such “born‑alive” cases are rare or nonexistent and that existing laws already protect infants. Critics of the opposition claim that federal law does not currently mandate life‑saving care for these infants and that only a minority of states have such statutes. Proponents say the bill does not restrict abortion access but simply addresses the treatment of newborns who survive procedures. The debate highlights broader disagreements over abortion rights and the legal status of fetuses versus newborns. Democrats are expected to block the legislation again, while supporters view the vote as a test of the moral consistency of pro‑choice positions.

END OF SUMMARY.
