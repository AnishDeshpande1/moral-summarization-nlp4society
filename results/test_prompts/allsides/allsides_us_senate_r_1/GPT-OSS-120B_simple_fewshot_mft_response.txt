SUMMARY:
The Senate is set to debate the Born‑Alive Abortion Survivors Protection Act, a bill framed as a necessary assertion of moral authority over a Democratic agenda that, the author argues, subverts the duty to protect vulnerable life. The legislation would compel doctors to give newborns who survive an abortion the same medical care afforded to any child born alive, confronting what the author depicts as a Democratic pattern of neglect and cheating the most defenseless. While Democrats claim “born‑alive” infants do not exist and that existing laws already prevent infanticide, the article contends these arguments are false or evasive, pointing to cases like Kermit Gosnell and the lack of a federal requirement for life‑saving care. By labeling the bill “anti‑abortion,” opponents are said to misrepresent its purpose, which is simply to ensure care and fairness for infants, not to restrict women’s health choices. The author warns that supporting the bill would expose the inconsistency of granting rights only after birth, whereas opposing it continues a policy that permits the denial of care to a human being. The passage of the act would therefore reaffirm authority, protect the harmed, and restore fairness denied by partisan subversion. 
END OF SUMMARY.
