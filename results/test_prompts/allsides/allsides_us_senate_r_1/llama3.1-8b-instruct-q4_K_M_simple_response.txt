SUMMARY:

The US Senate is considering the Born-Alive Abortion Survivors Protection Act, which requires doctors to provide medical care to infants born alive after an attempted abortion. This legislation has been reintroduced by Senator Ben Sasse in response to comments made by Virginia governor Ralph Northam suggesting that newborns could be left to die if they survived an abortion. 44 Democratic senators have previously blocked the bill, with some arguing that "born-alive" infants don't exist and others claiming that laws already prevent infanticide.

However, evidence suggests that infants do survive late-term abortions, and only 33 states have federal protections in place. Proponents of the bill argue that it doesn't regulate abortion but rather requires doctors to provide medical care to newborns who survive an abortion, just as they would to any other infant. The debate highlights the moral implications of allowing a newborn to die after being targeted for abortion.

END OF SUMMARY.
