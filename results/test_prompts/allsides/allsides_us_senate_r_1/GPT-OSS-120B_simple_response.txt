SUMMARY: The Senate is set to debate the Born‑Alive Abortion Survivors Protection Act, a bill the author portrays as a moral test of Democratic commitment to unrestricted abortion. The legislation would obligate doctors to give newborns who survive an attempted abortion the same medical care as any other infant. The author accuses Democrats of denying the existence of “born‑alive” infants, ignoring evidence such as the Kermit Gosnell case, and of falsely claiming existing laws already prevent infanticide. He argues that only a minority of states require life‑saving care for these infants and that labeling the bill “anti‑abortion” is a deliberate mischaracterization. According to the author, opponents claim the bill interferes with women’s reproductive choices, yet the bill merely addresses post‑birth care, not abortion decisions. The piece frames support for the bill as a matter of decency that would expose the “dehumanizing” logic of allowing a fetus rights in utero while denying them after birth, whereas opposition allegedly upholds a right to let a newborn die through neglect. END OF SUMMARY.
