President Obama expressed deep disappointment over Uganda's plans to further criminalize homosexuality, calling it a "step backward" for all Ugandans. The Anti-Homosexuality Bill in Uganda prescribes life imprisonment for acts of "aggravated homosexuality", including sex with minors or the disabled, and has alarmed rights activists worldwide. Obama's statement reflects his commitment to protecting fundamental freedoms and universal human rights, and highlights the importance of equality and justice for all individuals regardless of their sexual orientation. The US is Uganda's largest donor, sending over $400 million in aid annually, and a review of its relationship with Uganda is expected if Museveni signs the bill.
