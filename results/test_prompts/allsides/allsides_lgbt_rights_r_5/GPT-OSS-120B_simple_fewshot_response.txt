SUMMARY:
President Obama issued a strong rebuke of Uganda’s pending Anti‑Homosexuality Bill, warning that the law would “complicate our valued relationship” and represent a “step backward” for all Ugandans and a setback for global commitments to freedom, justice and equal rights. He reiterated the United States’ support for universal human rights and the protection of fundamental freedoms, expressing deep disappointment that Uganda plans to criminalize homosexuality and impose life‑imprisonment for “aggravated homosexuality.” Obama noted that the U.S., Uganda’s largest aid donor, has conveyed its concerns to President Yoweri Museveni, and that the legislation could trigger a review of bilateral ties. The bill, already passed by Ugandan lawmakers and backed by many Christian leaders, would expand existing colonial‑era bans and target same‑sex activity, especially involving HIV‑positive individuals, minors or the disabled. The administration highlighted Obama’s broader record of defending gay rights at home and abroad, citing same‑sex marriage, the repeal of the gay‑service ban, and public support for openly gay athletes. The statement underscores a moral stance that equality and human dignity must be upheld worldwide.

END OF SUMMARY.
