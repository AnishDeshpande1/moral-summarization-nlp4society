SUMMARY:

President Obama strongly condemned Uganda's plans to further criminalize homosexuality, calling it a "step backward" for all Ugandans. He emphasized that the Anti-Homosexuality Bill would not only be an affront and danger to the gay community in Uganda but also reflect poorly on the country's commitment to protecting human rights. Obama stressed that enacting this legislation would complicate the valued relationship between the US and Uganda, and urged President Yoweri Museveni to reconsider signing the bill. The president emphasized that the US stands for the protection of fundamental freedoms and universal human rights, and believes people everywhere should be treated equally. He noted that the bill prescribes life imprisonment for acts of "aggravated homosexuality", which includes sex with minors or the disabled, and repeated sexual offenses among consenting adults. Obama's comments reflect his long-standing commitment to defending gay rights around the world, and his administration has taken several steps to support LGBTQ+ individuals.

END OF SUMMARY.
