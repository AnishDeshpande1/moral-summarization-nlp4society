SUMMARY:

President Obama has expressed deep disappointment and concern over Uganda's plans to further criminalize homosexuality, warning that it would "complicate our valued relationship" with the country. The proposed bill, which includes life imprisonment for "aggravated homosexuality," would mark a "step backward" for all Ugandans and reflect poorly on the country's commitment to human rights. Obama emphasized that the US stands for the protection of fundamental freedoms and universal human rights, and believes people everywhere should be treated equally. The President's statement is a strong defense of gay rights, which has been a hallmark of his presidency. The US has conveyed its concerns to Ugandan President Museveni, and is expected to review its relationship with the country if the bill is signed.

END OF SUMMARY.
