SUMMARY: President Obama warned that Uganda’s planned anti‑homosexuality legislation will “complicate our valued relationship” and represent a “step backward” for human rights. He expressed deep disappointment that President Yoweri Museveni intends to sign a bill that would criminalize homosexuality, including life imprisonment for “aggravated homosexuality” involving HIV, minors, the disabled, or repeat offenses. Obama reiterated U.S. commitment to universal human rights and equality, noting the United States is Uganda’s largest aid donor and may reassess aid if the law is enacted. U.S. officials, including National Security Adviser Susan Rice, have urged Museveni not to sign. The bill, passed by Ugandan lawmakers in December, has strong support from Christian leaders who claim it protects children from “Western” influence. The warning follows broader regional anti‑LGBT trends, such as Nigeria’s recent penalty increase, and aligns with Obama’s domestic record supporting gay rights, including same‑sex marriage and LGBTQ representation at high‑profile events. END OF SUMMARY.
