SUMMARY:
President Obama publicly rebuked Uganda’s pending anti‑homosexuality legislation, warning that signing the bill would “complicate our valued relationship” and represent a “step backward” for all Ugandans. Framing the issue as a clash of authority, he condemned the Museveni administration’s subversive move to deepen criminal penalties, including life imprisonment for “aggravated homosexuality,” as a harmful affront to basic human dignity. Obama emphasized the United States’ commitment to protect fundamental freedoms, arguing that the law would cause serious harm to Uganda’s gay community and betray the principles of fairness and equal rights. He reminded Museveni that U.S. aid—over $400 million a year—reflects a partnership built on shared values, and that a breach of these values could trigger a reassessment of that support. The statement highlighted loyalty to universal human rights and warned that the proposed legislation would betray both Ugandans and the international community committed to justice and care for the vulnerable.

END OF SUMMARY.
