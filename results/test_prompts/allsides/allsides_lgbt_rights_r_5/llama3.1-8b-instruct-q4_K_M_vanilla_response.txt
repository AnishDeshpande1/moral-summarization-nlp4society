SUMMARY:
President Obama expressed disappointment over Uganda's plans to further criminalize homosexuality, calling it a "step backward" for the country and its people. He warned that enacting the Anti-Homosexuality Bill would complicate the US-Uganda relationship. The bill prescribes life imprisonment for "aggravated homosexuality," which includes sex acts where one partner is infected with HIV or involves minors or disabled individuals. Obama said the US stands for protecting fundamental freedoms and universal human rights, and that people everywhere should be treated equally. He has made support for gay rights a hallmark of his presidency, supporting same-sex marriage and eliminating the ban on openly gay military service.

The US has conveyed its concerns to Ugandan President Yoweri Museveni, who plans to sign the bill despite heavy pressure from rights activists and Western countries. The US is Uganda's largest donor, sending over $400 million in aid annually. If the bill is signed, the US may review its relationship with Uganda and find ways to express its disappointment.

END OF SUMMARY.
