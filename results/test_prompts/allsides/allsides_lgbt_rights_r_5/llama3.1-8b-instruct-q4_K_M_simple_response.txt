SUMMARY:
President Obama condemned Uganda's plan to further criminalize homosexuality, calling it a "step backward" for all Ugandans and a reflection of the country's poor commitment to human rights. He expressed disappointment that President Yoweri Museveni has pledged to sign the Anti-Homosexuality Bill, which prescribes life imprisonment for acts of "aggravated homosexuality". Obama emphasized that the US stands for fundamental freedoms and universal human rights, and believes people everywhere should be treated equally. He warned that enacting this legislation would complicate the valued relationship between the two countries.

Obama's statement highlighted his long-standing support for gay rights, which he has championed throughout his presidency. He pointed out that Uganda is a major recipient of US aid, and that the US would review its relationship with the country if the bill becomes law. The President urged Museveni to reconsider signing the bill, echoing the concerns of human rights activists who argue that it will only perpetuate discrimination against the gay community in Uganda.
