**SUMMARY:**

President Obama expressed deep disappointment over Uganda's plans to further criminalize homosexuality, calling it a "step backward" for all Ugandans and a serious setback for those committed to freedom, justice, and equal rights. The Anti-Homosexuality Bill in Uganda would not only harm the gay community but also reflect poorly on the country's commitment to protecting human rights. Obama said that enacting this legislation will complicate the valued relationship between the US and Uganda. The bill prescribes life imprisonment for acts of "aggravated homosexuality", including sex with minors, the disabled, or those infected with HIV. Obama has consistently defended gay rights at home and abroad, supporting same-sex marriage and equal rights for all individuals.
