SUMMARY: President Obama publicly condemned Uganda’s pending Anti‑Homosexuality Bill, warning that its enactment would “complicate our valued relationship” and constitute a “step backward” for human rights, freedom, justice and equality. He emphasized that the United States stands for universal human rights and the equal treatment of all people, expressing deep disappointment that Uganda intends to criminalize homosexuality with penalties up to life imprisonment for “aggravated homosexuality.” Obama noted that the legislation would harm Uganda’s reputation and jeopardize U.S. aid, as Washington is the country’s largest donor. He and National Security Adviser Susan Rice have urged President Museveni not to sign the bill, stressing that such laws betray basic moral principles of dignity and protection for vulnerable groups. The article situates Obama’s stance within his broader record of championing gay rights domestically and internationally, citing his support for same‑sex marriage, the repeal of the gay ban in the military, and advocacy against anti‑LGBTQ laws elsewhere. END OF SUMMARY.
