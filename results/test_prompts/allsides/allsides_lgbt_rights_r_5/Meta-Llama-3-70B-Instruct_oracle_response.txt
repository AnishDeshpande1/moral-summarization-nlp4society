SUMMARY:

President Obama has warned Uganda against plans to further criminalize homosexuality, stating that it would "complicate our valued relationship" and mark a "step backward" for all Ugandans. The proposed bill, which President Yoweri Museveni has pledged to sign, would criminalize homosexuality and prescribe life imprisonment for "aggravated homosexuality" offenses. Obama defended gay rights, saying that the legislation would reflect poorly on Uganda's commitment to protecting the human rights of its people and represent a serious setback for anyone committed to freedom, justice, and equal rights. The US stands for the protection of fundamental freedoms and universal human rights, believing that people everywhere should be treated equally. Enacting this legislation would be an affront and a danger to the gay community in Uganda, and would deter progress for those committed to freedom, justice, and equal rights. The US has conveyed its disappointment to Museveni and may review its relationship with Uganda if the bill is signed.

END OF SUMMARY.
