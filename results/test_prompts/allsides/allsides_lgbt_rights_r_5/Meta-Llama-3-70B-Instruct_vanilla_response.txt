SUMMARY:

President Obama has warned Uganda's President Yoweri Museveni against signing a bill that would further criminalize homosexuality, stating that it would "complicate our valued relationship" between the two countries. Obama expressed deep disappointment over the bill, which would mark a "step backward" for all Ugandans and reflect poorly on the country's commitment to human rights. The bill, which prescribes life imprisonment for acts of "aggravated homosexuality", has alarmed rights activists. The US, Uganda's largest donor, may review its relationship with the country if the bill is signed. Obama has been a strong defender of gay rights, supporting same-sex marriage and eliminating the ban on openly gay service in the military. He has also spoken out against similar laws in Russia and Nigeria. The US national security adviser, Susan Rice, has also urged Museveni not to sign the bill.

END OF SUMMARY.
