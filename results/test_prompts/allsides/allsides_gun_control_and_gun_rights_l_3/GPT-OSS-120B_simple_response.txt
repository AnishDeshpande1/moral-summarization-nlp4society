SUMMARY: New details about the Sutherland Springs massacre reveal that the shooter, 26‑year‑old Devin Patrick Kelley, had a documented history of severe domestic violence that the Air Force failed to report to a federal database. Because of this omission, Kelley was able to purchase a Ruger AR‑556 rifle in 2016, which he later used to kill 26 people—including children, the elderly and a pregnant woman—at the First Baptist Church. Authorities say the attack was linked to a domestic dispute with his mother‑in‑law, a church member, not to race or religion. The case underscores a moral failing: institutions that ignored or concealed Kelley’s violent past enabled a tragedy that might have been prevented with proper reporting and stricter gun controls. Meanwhile, activists from Gays Against Guns were arrested during a Capitol Hill protest demanding tighter gun laws. END OF SUMMARY.
