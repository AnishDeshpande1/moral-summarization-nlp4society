SUMMARY: New details about the Sunday mass shooting at First Baptist Church in Sutherland Springs, Texas, reveal that 26 people—including children, elders and a pregnant woman—were killed by 26‑year‑old Devin Patrick Kelley. Kelley, a former Air Force member, was court‑martialed in 2012 for severe domestic violence, but the Air Force failed to enter his conviction into a federal database, allowing him to purchase a Ruger AR‑556 rifle in 2016. Authorities say the attack was linked to a domestic dispute with his mother‑in‑law, a church member who was not present during the shooting, and not motivated by race or religion. Texas Department of Public Safety spokesperson Freeman Martin emphasized the ongoing investigation into the family dispute. Meanwhile, seven activists from Gays Against Guns were arrested during a Capitol Hill die‑in protest demanding stricter gun control. END OF SUMMARY.
