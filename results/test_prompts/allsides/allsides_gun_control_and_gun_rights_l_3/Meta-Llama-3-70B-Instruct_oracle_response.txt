SUMMARY:

A devastating mass shooting occurred at the First Baptist Church in Sutherland Springs, Texas, resulting in the senseless killing of 26 innocent people, including children, elderly individuals, and a pregnant woman. The suspected shooter, 26-year-old Devin Patrick Kelley, had a history of domestic violence, having been court-martialed in 2012 for repeatedly hitting, kicking, and choking his wife, as well as pointing a loaded gun at her and brutally attacking his 18-month-old stepson, breaking the toddler's skull. Despite this, Kelley was able to purchase a Ruger AR-556 assault-style rifle in 2016 due to the Air Force's failure to report his domestic violence court-martial to a federal database. Authorities believe Kelley carried out the massacre as a result of a domestic dispute with his mother-in-law, who was a member of the church. This tragic event has sparked outrage, with activists demanding stricter gun control. The shooting is a stark reminder of the devastating consequences of unchecked domestic violence and the need for stricter gun laws.
