SUMMARY:

A mass shooting at the First Baptist Church in Sutherland Springs, Texas, left 26 people dead, including children, elderly individuals, and a pregnant woman. The suspected shooter, Devin Patrick Kelley, was identified as a 26-year-old white man from New Braunfels, Texas. Kelley had a history of domestic violence, having been court-martialed in 2012 for abusing his wife and stepson. Despite being discharged from the Air Force with a bad conduct discharge, Kelley was able to purchase an assault-style rifle due to the Air Force's failure to report his domestic violence conviction to a federal database.

According to authorities, Kelley's motive for the shooting appears to have been a domestic dispute with his mother-in-law, who attended the church but was not present on Sunday. A spokesperson for the Texas Department of Public Safety stated that the shooting was not racially motivated or related to religious beliefs.

END OF SUMMARY.
