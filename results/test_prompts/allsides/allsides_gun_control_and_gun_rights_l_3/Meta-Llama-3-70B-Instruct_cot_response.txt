A mass shooting at the First Baptist Church in Sutherland Springs, Texas, on Sunday resulted in the senseless deaths of 26 people, including children, elderly people, and a pregnant woman. The suspected shooter, Devin Patrick Kelley, a 26-year-old white man, had a history of domestic violence, having been court-martialed and confined for a year for repeatedly hitting, kicking, and choking his wife and attacking his stepson. Despite his bad conduct discharge from the Air Force, Kelley was able to purchase a gun due to the Air Force's failure to report his domestic violence court-martial to a federal database. The shooting appears to have been motivated by a domestic dispute with his mother-in-law, who was a member of the church. Authorities have emphasized that the massacre was not racially motivated, but rather a result of a threatening domestic situation. The tragedy has sparked calls for stricter gun control, with activists from Gays Against Guns being arrested during a die-in protest on Capitol Hill.
