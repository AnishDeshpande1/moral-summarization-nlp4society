SUMMARY:
New information about the Sutherland Springs, Texas, church shooting that left 26 dead, including children and a pregnant woman, points to a chain of institutional failures and a personal domestic‑violence motive. The gunman, 26‑year‑old Devin Patrick Kelley, had been court‑martialed in 2012 for repeatedly abusing his wife and stepson, an offense that led to a year‑long confinement and a bad‑conduct discharge from the Air Force in 2014. The Air Force later admitted it never entered Kelley’s court‑martial record into the federal background‑check database, allowing him to purchase a Ruger AR‑556 rifle in 2016. Authorities now say the massacre was likely triggered by a domestic dispute with his mother‑in‑law, a member of the First Baptist Church, rather than by race or religion. The tragedy underscores the deadly consequences of gaps in background‑check reporting and the need to address domestic‑violence warning signs before they erupt into mass violence. Activists from Gays Against Guns were arrested during a Capitol Hill protest demanding stricter gun‑control measures. END OF SUMMARY.
