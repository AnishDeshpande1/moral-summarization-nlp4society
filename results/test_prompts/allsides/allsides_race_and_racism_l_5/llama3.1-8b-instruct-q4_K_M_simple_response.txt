SUMMARY:

One year after the police shooting of Michael Brown in Ferguson, Missouri, the Black Lives Matter movement is gaining momentum. Recent surveys show that Americans are paying more attention to systemic racism in the criminal justice system, with a significant increase in people recognizing the issue as a "big problem" in society. Democratic presidential candidates have been forced to take stances on the issue, and some prosecutors have begun to hold police accountable for high-profile killings. The media is also giving more attention to police shootings, with several outlets tracking them in databases. While there is still much work to be done, the trend suggests that Black Lives Matter is winning its battle against anti-black law enforcement violence.

Lawmakers are taking notice, with President Obama and Democratic candidate Hillary Clinton calling for police to wear body cameras and proposing reforms to track police killings. The movement's efforts have also led to increased public awareness and interest in police shootings. While there is still a long way to go, the momentum suggests that Black Lives Matter is making progress towards its goal of "redressing the systemic pattern of anti-black law enforcement violence in the US."

END OF SUMMARY.
