SUMMARY:

The Black Lives Matter movement is making significant progress in raising awareness about systemic racism in the US criminal justice system. A year after the Ferguson protests, Americans are more likely to acknowledge that black people are unfairly treated by police and in society as a whole. Surveys show a 20-year high in recognizing racism as a "big problem" in society. The media is paying more attention, with databases tracking police shootings and public interest in the issue increasing. Prosecutors are taking police shootings more seriously, filing charges against officers involved in high-profile killings. Lawmakers, including President Obama and Hillary Clinton, are pushing for police accountability measures such as body cameras. While Republicans and white Americans remain skeptical, the trend is clear: Black Lives Matter is winning, and the American political system is slowly but surely reflecting this shift. The movement's mission to address anti-black law enforcement violence is far from over, but progress is being made.

END OF SUMMARY.
