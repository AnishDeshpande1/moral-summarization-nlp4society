SUMMARY:

A year after the Michael Brown shooting in Ferguson, Missouri, the Black Lives Matter movement is gaining momentum. Recent surveys show that Americans are paying more attention to systemic racism in the criminal justice system. Gallup and Pew Research Center polls found that a majority of Americans believe black people are unfairly treated by police and that racism is a significant problem in society. The media is also taking notice, with publications like The Washington Post and The Guardian tracking police shootings. Prosecutors are taking the issue more seriously, charging officers who have been involved in high-profile killings. Lawmakers, including President Obama and Democratic presidential candidate Hillary Clinton, are pushing for reforms such as body cameras and better tracking of police violence.

The movement still faces resistance from Republicans and white Americans, but the trend is clear: Black Lives Matter is winning. The shift in public opinion and media attention may take time to translate into policy changes, but it's a significant step towards addressing systemic racism in the US. END OF SUMMARY.
