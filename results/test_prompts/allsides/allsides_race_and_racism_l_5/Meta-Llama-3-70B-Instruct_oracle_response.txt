SUMMARY:

A year after the police shooting of Michael Brown in Ferguson, Missouri, the Black Lives Matter movement is winning. Americans are finally paying attention to systemic racism in the criminal justice system, and it's about time. The movement's efforts have led to a shift in public opinion, with more Americans acknowledging that black people are unfairly treated in all aspects of society, including police encounters. The media is also taking notice, with databases tracking police shootings and more interest in the issue. Prosecutors are taking police shootings more seriously, with some even calling them "unwarranted" and "senseless". Lawmakers are pushing for police accountability, with calls for body cameras and better tracking of police killings. While there is still much work to be done, the trend is clear: Black Lives Matter is winning, and it's about time we take racism in the US seriously. The notoriously slow American political system may take a while to reflect this, but it's really happening.

END OF SUMMARY.
