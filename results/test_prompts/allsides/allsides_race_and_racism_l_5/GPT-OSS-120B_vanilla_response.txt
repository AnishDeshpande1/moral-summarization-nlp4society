SUMMARY: A year after Michael Brown’s shooting sparked nationwide protests, the Black Lives Matter movement is gaining traction. Public opinion has shifted: Gallup and Pew surveys show more Americans believe Black people face systemic unfairness and view racism as a major problem. Media coverage has increased, with outlets like the Washington Post and the Guardian maintaining police‑shooting databases, and social media interest in Ferguson remains high. Prosecutors are now more willing to charge officers, as seen in cases involving Samuel DuBose, Walter Scott, Eric Harris, and Freddie Gray. Lawmakers, including President Obama and candidate Hillary Clinton, are pushing reforms such as body‑camera mandates and better federal tracking of police killings. While Black Americans still die disproportionately at the hands of police and many Republicans remain skeptical, the overall trend indicates that Black Lives Matter is influencing public attitudes and legal actions, even if political change is slow. END OF SUMMARY.
