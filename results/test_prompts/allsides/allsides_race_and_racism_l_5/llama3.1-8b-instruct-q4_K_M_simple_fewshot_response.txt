SUMMARY:

A year after the police shooting of Michael Brown in Ferguson, Missouri, the Black Lives Matter movement has made significant progress in raising awareness about systemic racism in the criminal justice system. Recent surveys show a shift in public opinion, with more Americans acknowledging that black people are unfairly treated by police and that racism is a "big problem" in society. The media is also paying more attention to police shootings, with both the Washington Post and the Guardian tracking these incidents. Prosecutors are taking the issue seriously, with several cases resulting in charges against police officers. Lawmakers, including President Obama and Democratic presidential candidate Hillary Clinton, have called for reforms such as body cameras for police and better data collection on police killings. While there is still much work to be done, the trend is clear: Black Lives Matter is winning. The movement has sparked a national conversation about racism and policing, and it will likely take time for the American political system to fully reflect these changes.
