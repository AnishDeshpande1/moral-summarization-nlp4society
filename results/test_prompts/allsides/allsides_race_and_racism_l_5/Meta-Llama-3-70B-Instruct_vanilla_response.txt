SUMMARY:

A year after the Ferguson protests, the Black Lives Matter movement is making progress. Americans are becoming more aware of systemic racism in the criminal justice system, and Democratic presidential candidates are being held accountable for their stance on the issue. Recent surveys show a shift in public opinion, with more Americans recognizing that black people are unfairly treated in society, including police encounters. The media is also paying more attention, with databases tracking police shootings. Prosecutors are taking police shootings more seriously, with several filing charges against officers involved in high-profile killings. Lawmakers, including President Obama and Hillary Clinton, are pushing for police accountability measures such as body cameras and better tracking of police killings. While there is still skepticism among Republicans and white Americans, the trend is clear: Black Lives Matter is winning.

END OF SUMMARY.
