SUMMARY:

The Black Lives Matter movement is making significant progress in raising awareness about systemic racism in the criminal justice system. A year after the police shooting of Michael Brown, Americans are paying more attention to the issue and are increasingly recognizing that black people are unfairly treated by law enforcement. Recent surveys show a shift in public opinion, with a 20-year high in the percentage of Americans calling racism a "big problem" in society. The media is also taking notice, tracking police shootings and providing a platform for discussion. Prosecutors are taking the issue more seriously, charging officers who have committed wrongdoing and acknowledging the need to hold police accountable. Lawmakers are pushing for reforms, including the use of body cameras to track police actions. While much work remains to be done, the trend is clear: Black Lives Matter is winning attention and sparking change.

END OF SUMMARY.
