SUMMARY:

New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg's gun control efforts have been dealt a blow as the City Council overrode his veto of two bills, creating a police inspector general and allowing more people to sue the city for racial profiling. Bloomberg claims this will put minorities at higher risk, despite his stop-and-frisk program being ruled unconstitutional and racist by a federal judge. The program has taken 8,000 illegal guns off the streets, but Bloomberg's critics argue that his gun control agenda is misguided and ineffective. The NRA, a frequent target of Bloomberg's criticism, has never taken an official position on stop-and-frisk and advocates for tougher sentencing for illegal gun possession. Bloomberg's efforts to link the NRA to the plaintiffs in federal lawsuits have been met with skepticism. The ruling has sparked debate about the balance between public safety and individual rights, with some arguing that Bloomberg's approach is heavy-handed and ineffective.

END OF SUMMARY.
