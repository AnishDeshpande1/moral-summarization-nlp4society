SUMMARY:
New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg, a longtime advocate of strict gun bans, has spent millions trying to export those policies nationwide, despite evidence that they do not stop criminals from obtaining firearms. To compensate, Bloomberg relied on aggressive policing tactics like stop‑and‑frisk, which he claims removed 8,000 illegal guns and halved the murder rate during his three terms. After a federal judge ruled the program unconstitutional and racially discriminatory, the City Council overrode Bloomberg’s veto to create an NYPD inspector general and expand civil suits for profiling. Bloomberg blamed the decision on the NRA and the ACLU, accusing them of putting ideology above public safety, while conservative commentator Mike Long defended stop‑and‑frisk as essential for protecting minority communities and warned that abandoning it would make New York resemble Chicago. The article argues that the NRA has no official stance on stop‑and‑frisk, that the tactic is a proven safety measure, and that Bloomberg’s broader gun‑control agenda—assault‑weapon bans, high‑capacity‑magazine limits, and private‑sale background checks—has never reduced crime. It calls for the appeals court to overturn the ruling and urges Bloomberg to stop vilifying law‑abiding gun owners.

END OF SUMMARY.
