SUMMARY:

New York City Mayor Michael R. Bloomberg is attempting to deflect blame for the city's decision to limit stop-and-frisk policing tactics, which have been found to disproportionately target minorities and violate their Fourth Amendment rights. Despite his claims that the tactic has led to a significant reduction in crime, Judge Shira A. Scheindlin ruled that it was a form of racial profiling. Bloomberg is attempting to shift the focus to the National Rifle Association (NRA), claiming they are opposed to his efforts to reduce gun violence. However, the NRA has never taken an official position on stop-and-frisk policies and advocates for tougher sentencing for possession of illegal guns. The article suggests that Bloomberg's true agenda is to restrict law-abiding citizens' Second Amendment rights, rather than addressing the root causes of crime. By blaming the NRA and attempting to link them to the plaintiffs in federal lawsuits, Bloomberg is trying to undermine the legitimacy of those who are fighting against his gun control measures.

END OF SUMMARY.
