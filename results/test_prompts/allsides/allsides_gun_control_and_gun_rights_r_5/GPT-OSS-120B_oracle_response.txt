SUMMARY:  
Mayor Michael R. Bloomberg has spent tens of millions trying to get his gun bans instituted nationwide, even though such bans have never stopped criminals from getting guns. To compensate, he has relied on policing tactics such as stop‑and‑frisk to get illegal firearms away from the bad guys. After the City Council overrode his veto and allowed a new police inspector‑general to oversee stop‑and‑frisk, Bloomberg warned that minorities could be put at higher risk, even though he claims the policy has led to a dramatic reduction in murders, with 8,000 illegal guns taken off the streets and the murder rate cut in half, making the city safer. Conservative Party chairman Mike Long defended the tactic, saying it has saved lives of people of color and is essential for making New York “like” the safest cities, warning that without it the city will be like Chicago. Bloomberg has blamed the NRA and the ACLU, linking them to the lawsuits and accusing them of protecting ideology rather than safety. Critics argue that stop‑and‑frisk is a racist ruling that must be overturned, while Bloomberg insists that enforcing gun laws and prosecuting illegal possession is the correct method, even if it means a harder fight for law‑abiding gun owners.  

END OF SUMMARY.
