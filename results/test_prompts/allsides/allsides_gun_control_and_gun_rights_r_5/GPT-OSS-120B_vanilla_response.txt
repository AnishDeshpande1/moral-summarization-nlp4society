SUMMARY: New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg has defended the city’s stop‑and‑frisk program, claiming it removed 8,000 illegal guns and cut the murder rate in half during his tenure. After a federal judge ruled the practice unconstitutional and racially discriminatory, the City Council overrode Bloomberg’s veto to create a police inspector general and expand civil suits for profiling. Bloomberg plans to appeal the decision, arguing the ruling ignores other courts that have upheld the tactic. Critics, including Conservative Party Chairman Mike Long, support stop‑and‑frisk as a safety tool, while opponents cite civil‑rights concerns. Bloomberg has also linked the New York Civil Liberties Union to the NRA, accusing both of undermining public safety, though the NRA has not taken an official stance on stop‑and‑frisk. The debate continues over the effectiveness of gun‑control measures versus policing tactics in reducing crime.  

END OF SUMMARY.
