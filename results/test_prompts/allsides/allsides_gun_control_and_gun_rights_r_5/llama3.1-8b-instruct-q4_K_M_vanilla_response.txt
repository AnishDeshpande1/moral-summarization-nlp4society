SUMMARY:

New York City Mayor Michael R. Bloomberg is frustrated that the City Council overrode his veto of two bills that would create an inspector general to oversee policing tactics, including stop and frisk. This comes after a federal judge ruled that the city's stop-and-frisk program was a violation of the Fourth Amendment and considered racial profiling. Bloomberg claims that stop and frisk has led to 8,000 illegal guns being taken off the streets in the past decade and reduced the murder rate by half. However, critics argue that this tactic is necessary for public safety, and without it, the city will become like Chicago.

Bloomberg has also been criticized for his attacks on the National Rifle Association (NRA), trying to link them to the plaintiffs in federal lawsuits against stop-and-frisk. The NRA advocates for tougher sentencing for possession of illegal guns and has never taken an official position on stop-and-frisk policies.

END OF SUMMARY.
