SUMMARY:

New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg's efforts to ban guns and implement stop-and-frisk policies have been met with resistance. The City Council recently overrode his veto of two bills that will create a police inspector general and allow more people to sue the city for racial profiling. Bloomberg claims this will put minorities at higher risk, but critics argue that his stop-and-frisk program has been deemed unconstitutional and targets blacks and Hispanics. Despite this, Bloomberg points to the program's success in reducing crime and taking 8,000 illegal guns off the streets. He has also blamed the National Rifle Association (NRA) for opposing his gun control measures, but the NRA has never taken a stance on stop-and-frisk policies. Bloomberg's efforts to restrict gun ownership have been ineffective in reducing crime, and his policies have been criticized as targeting law-abiding gun owners.

END OF SUMMARY.
