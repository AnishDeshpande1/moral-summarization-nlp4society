SUMMARY:

New York City Mayor Michael R. Bloomberg is upset that the City Council overrode his veto of two bills that would create an inspector general to oversee police tactics, including stop and frisk. Bloomberg claims that this move will lead to minorities being put at higher risk, as they have benefited most from crime reduction under the current policy. However, a federal judge had previously ruled that stop and frisk was a violation of the Fourth Amendment and considered racial profiling.

Critics argue that Bloomberg's tactics are misguided, pointing out that stop and frisk has resulted in 8,000 illegal guns being taken off the streets and a significant reduction in murder rates. They also accuse Bloomberg of unfairly blaming the National Rifle Association for his own failures to reduce crime through gun control measures.

The author suggests that Bloomberg is more interested in pushing an anti-gun agenda than in actually making New York City safer.

END OF SUMMARY.
