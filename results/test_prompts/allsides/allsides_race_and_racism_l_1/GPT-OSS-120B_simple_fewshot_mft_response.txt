SUMMARY:
The Hollywood Reporter columnist denounces recent anti‑Semitic outbursts by celebrities such as Ice Cube, DeSean Jackson and others, arguing that the muted public response undermines authority‑based norms against hate and fuels an “Apatholypse” of indifference. By allowing demeaning stereotypes to spread unchecked, these figures subvert the moral authority that should protect vulnerable groups, causing real harm to Jewish communities and eroding the fairness of public discourse. The columnist links the celebrities’ scapegoating of Jews to broader patterns of cheating the social contract—blaming “bad‑apple” groups for societal woes, echoing historic oppressors from Nazis to the KKK. Apologies from some offenders are portrayed as insincere spin, failing to restore trust or repair the damage inflicted on victims. Citing rising hate‑crime statistics and the historic lesson of Billie Holiday’s “Strange Fruit,” the piece calls for collective outrage that upholds caring responsibility toward all marginalized peoples and demands equitable accountability, insisting that justice for one group is essential for justice for all. END OF SUMMARY.
