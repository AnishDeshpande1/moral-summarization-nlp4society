SUMMARY:
A Hollywood Reporter columnist denounces the recent wave of anti‑Semitic remarks made by high‑profile Black entertainers—including Ice Cube, DeSean Jackson and Stephen Jackson—and warns that the muted public reaction fuels a broader “Apatholypse” of indifference to all injustice. The piece links their hateful posts, which revive classic conspiracies about Jews controlling finance and plotting world domination, to a dangerous logic that justifies discrimination against any group. It criticizes the superficial apologies and defensive spin that followed, noting that such behavior undermines genuine social‑justice advocacy and emboldens hate. By highlighting the rise in anti‑Jewish hate crimes and recalling historic protest songs like Billie Holiday’s “Strange Fruit,” the column stresses that true solidarity demands equal outrage toward every form of oppression. The author calls on celebrities and audiences alike to recognize their responsibility to speak accurately and compassionately, reminding readers that “injustice anywhere is a threat to justice everywhere” and that freedom for one is impossible without freedom for all. END OF SUMMARY.
