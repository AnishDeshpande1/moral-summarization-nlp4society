SUMMARY:
The Hollywood Reporter columnist denounces the recent anti‑Jewish outbursts by Ice Cube, DeSean Jackson and others, arguing that the muted response perpetuates racism and contributes to an “apatholypse” of apathy toward justice. The author notes a shocking lack of indignation as celebrities post hateful tweets and messages that discriminate and imply conspiratorial domination. Their agreeing talk and support for conspiracies undid previous advocacy and spread dehumanizing characterization, echoing the abuses that killed George Floyd. Even Jewish comedian Chelsea Handler posted videos that seemed to think anti‑Jewish sentiment is justified, while Trump’s campaign exploited anti‑Jewish bias, pandering to hate groups and emboldening attacks. The piece calls this disheartening, especially as marginalized communities repeat the same scapegoating logic, perpetuating prejudice. Apologies from DeSean Jackson, Stephen Jackson and Handler were seen as spin, lacking true contrition. Hate crimes against Jews have tripled, with murders and wounding illustrating the real impact of such rhetoric. The author invokes Billie Holiday’s “Strange Fruit,” Martin Luther King Jr.’s warning that injustice anywhere threatens justice everywhere, urging us to be outraged at any injustice and to love universal freedom. END OF SUMMARY.
