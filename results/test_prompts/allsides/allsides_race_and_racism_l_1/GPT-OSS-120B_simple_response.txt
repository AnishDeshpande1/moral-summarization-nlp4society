SUMMARY: The Hollywood Reporter columnist condemns recent anti‑Jewish outbursts by Ice Cube, DeSean Jackson and other celebrities, arguing that the muted public reaction fuels a broader “Apatholypse” of apathy toward all social‑justice causes. The piece links these hateful remarks to a dangerous logic that blames entire groups—whether Jews, Blacks, or others—and warns that tolerating one form of bigotry normalizes the rest, undermining the Black Lives Matter movement and broader anti‑racist efforts. It cites specific incidents (Ice Cube’s tweets, DeSean Jackson’s Hitler‑misquote, Stephen Jackson’s conspiracies, Chelsea Handler’s Farrakhan video, and Trump campaign rhetoric) and notes half‑hearted apologies that fail to convey genuine contrition. The columnist stresses that celebrities have a responsibility to speak accurately because words have real, destructive impact, as reflected in rising anti‑Jewish hate crimes. Drawing on Billie Holiday’s “Strange Fruit” and Martin Luther King Jr.’s warning that “injustice anywhere is a threat to justice everywhere,” the author calls for equal outrage against all oppression, insisting that freedom for any group requires freedom for all. END OF SUMMARY.
