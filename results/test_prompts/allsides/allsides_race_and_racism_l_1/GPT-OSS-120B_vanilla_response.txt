SUMMARY: A Hollywood Reporter columnist condemns recent anti‑Jewish remarks by celebrities such as Ice Cube, NFL player DeSean Jackson, former NBA player Stephen Jackson, and comedian Chelsea Handler, criticizing the muted public response as fostering an “Apatholypse” of social‑justice apathy. The piece details each figure’s offensive statements—Ice Cube’s tweets blaming Jews for Black oppression, DeSean Jackson’s false Hitler quote about Jewish world domination, Stephen Jackson’s endorsement of conspiracy theories about the Rothschilds and support for Louis Farrakhan, and Handler’s sharing of Farrakhan videos. It also links these incidents to broader political exploitation of anti‑Jewish tropes, citing Donald Trump’s campaign rhetoric. The columnist argues that half‑hearted apologies and defensive spin further legitimize prejudice, noting a rise in anti‑Jewish hate crimes from 751 in 2013 to 2,107 in 2019. By invoking historical resistance to racism, such as Billie Holiday’s “Strange Fruit,” the article urges consistent outrage against all forms of injustice, emphasizing that freedom is indivisible. END OF SUMMARY.
