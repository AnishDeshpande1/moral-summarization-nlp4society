SUMMARY:

The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) has filed a lawsuit against seven East Texas towns that have declared themselves "sanctuary cities" for unborn children, effectively outlawing abortion within city limits. The ACLU argues that the pro-life ordinances violate the rights to free expression and association of two pro-abortion groups, the Lilith Fund and the Texas Equal Access Fund (TEA Fund). The lawsuit claims that the ordinances mislead residents about the legality of abortion and designate abortion providers as "criminal organizations," hindering their ability to operate and provide services. The ACLU asserts that the ordinances are unconstitutional and that cities cannot punish pro-abortion organizations for carrying out their work. However, proponents of the ordinances argue that they are constitutional and necessary to protect unborn life, and are prepared to defend them in court.

END OF SUMMARY.
