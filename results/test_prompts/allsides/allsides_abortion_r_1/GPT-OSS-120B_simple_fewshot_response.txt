SUMMARY:
The American Civil Liberties Union has sued seven East Texas towns—Waskom, Naples, Joaquin, Tenaha, Rusk, Gary and Wells—over “unborn‑child sanctuary” ordinances that label abortion providers and pro‑choice groups as criminal organizations and seek to ban abortions if Roe v. Wade were ever overturned. The ACLU, representing the Lilith Fund and the Texas Equal Access Fund, argues the ordinances violate the plaintiffs’ First‑Amendment rights to free speech and association by preventing them from operating, speaking and assisting women seeking abortions, and by misleading residents about the legality of abortion services. ACLU attorney Anjali Salvador called the measures unconstitutional, noting that abortion remains legal nationwide and cities cannot punish pro‑abortion organizations for lawful work. The towns’ advocate, Mark Dickson of Right to Life East Texas, dismissed the lawsuit as meritless, insisting the ordinances are fully constitutional under Texas law and pledging free legal defense for any city that adopts similar measures.  

END OF SUMMARY.
