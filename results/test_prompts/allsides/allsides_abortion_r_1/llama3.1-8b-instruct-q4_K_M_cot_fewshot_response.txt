SUMMARY:
The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) has filed a lawsuit against seven East Texas towns that declared themselves "sanctuary cities" for unborn children. The ACLU argues that the pro-life ordinances are unconstitutional and violate the rights of pro-abortion groups to free expression and association. According to the lawsuit, the ordinances mislead residents into thinking that abortion is not available in these cities, which would be a violation of the First Amendment. The ACLU contends that the ordinances have dubbed abortion providers as "criminal organizations," preventing them from clearing up confusion and assisting people with accessing abortion services. Mark Dickson, the director of Right to Life East Texas, has vowed to defend the ordinances, saying they are entirely constitutional and consistent with the laws of Texas. The lawsuit aims to deter and intimidate cities from enacting these ordinances. END OF SUMMARY.
