SUMMARY: The ACLU has sued seven East Texas towns that have declared themselves “sanctuary cities” for unborn children, arguing that the local ordinances violate the First Amendment by branding abortion providers and pro‑abortion groups as criminal organizations and threatening to ban abortions if Roe v. Wade is ever overturned. Representing the Lilith Fund and the Texas Equal Access Fund, the ACLU claims the laws mislead residents, gag pro‑choice advocates, and prevent them from offering needed information and services. ACLU attorney Anjali Salvador calls the ordinances unconstitutional and an affront to legally protected abortion access. The pro‑life movement, led by Right to Life East Texas director Mark Dickson, defends the ordinances as a moral imperative to protect unborn life and vows to defend any city that adopts them at no cost to taxpayers, dismissing the lawsuit as a meritless attempt to intimidate. The clash highlights a broader moral battle over whether local governments can impose anti‑abortion measures that restrict free speech and association. END OF SUMMARY.
