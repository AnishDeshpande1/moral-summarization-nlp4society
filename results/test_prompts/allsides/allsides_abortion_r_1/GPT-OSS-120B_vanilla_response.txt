SUMMARY: The ACLU has sued seven East Texas towns—Waskom, Naples, Joaquin, Tenaha, Rusk, Gary, and Wells—over “sanctuary for unborn children” ordinances that label abortion providers and pro‑abortion groups as criminal organizations and would ban abortions if Roe v. Wade were overturned. Representing the Lilith Fund and the Texas Equal Access Fund, the ACLU argues the ordinances violate the First Amendment by restricting free speech, expression, and association, and mislead residents about abortion legality. ACLU staff attorney Anjali Salvador called the measures unconstitutional, noting abortion remains legal nationwide. The towns’ ordinance supporters, led by Right to Life East Texas director Mark Dickson, claim the laws are constitutional and aim to protect unborn life statewide, offering free legal defense to municipalities. The lawsuit seeks to block enforcement of the ordinances. END OF SUMMARY.
