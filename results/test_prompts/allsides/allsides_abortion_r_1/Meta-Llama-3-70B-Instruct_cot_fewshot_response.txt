SUMMARY:
The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) has filed a lawsuit against seven East Texas towns that declared themselves "sanctuary cities" for unborn children, arguing that their pro-life ordinances violate the rights to free expression and association. The ordinances, which outlaw abortion within city limits if Roe v. Wade is overturned, are deemed "unconstitutional" by the ACLU. The lawsuit claims that the ordinances mislead residents, dubbing abortion providers and pro-abortion entities as "criminal organizations," and hamper them from operating and speaking within the cities. However, pro-life advocates, such as Mark Dickson, believe the ordinances are "entirely constitutional" and are prepared to defend them at no charge to the cities. The lawsuit is seen as a "meritless" attempt to intimidate cities from enacting these ordinances. END OF SUMMARY.
