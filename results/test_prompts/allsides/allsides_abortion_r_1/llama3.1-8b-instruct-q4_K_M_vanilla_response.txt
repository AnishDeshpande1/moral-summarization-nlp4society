SUMMARY:

The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) has filed a lawsuit against seven East Texas towns that have declared themselves "sanctuary cities" for unborn children. The ordinances in question, passed by cities such as Waskom and Naples, aim to outlaw abortion if Roe v. Wade is overturned. The ACLU argues that these ordinances violate the rights of pro-abortion groups to free expression and association, and mislead residents about their right to access abortion. The lawsuit also claims that the ordinances label abortion providers and pro-abortion entities as "criminal organizations," preventing them from operating in these cities.

Mark Dickson, director of Right to Life East Texas, has expressed support for the ordinances and claims they are constitutional. He has vowed to defend other cities that enact similar laws without charging taxpayers. The ACLU argues that the ordinances are unconstitutional and aim to deter pro-abortion work.

END OF SUMMARY.
