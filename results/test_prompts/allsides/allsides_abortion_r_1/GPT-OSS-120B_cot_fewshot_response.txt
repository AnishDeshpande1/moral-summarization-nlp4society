SUMMARY:
The ACLU has filed a lawsuit against seven East Texas “sanctuary” towns that declared themselves sanctuaries for unborn children, arguing the ordinances violate the plaintiffs’ rights to free expression and association. The complaint says the pro‑abortion groups Lilith Fund and the Texas Equal Access Fund are mislabeled “criminal” and thus prohibited from operating, speaking, or assisting with abortions, which the ACLU calls unconstitutional and a mislead to residents. ACLU attorney Anjali Salvador called the ordinances “unconstitutional” and stressed that abortion is legal and that punishing pro‑abortion organizations infringes on important First‑Amendment rights. Right‑to‑Life East Texas director Mark Dickson dismissed the suit as merit‑less, claiming it aims to intimidate and that the ordinances are constitutional, consistent with Texas law, and represent a meaningful effort to protect unborn life. He added that the group is ready to defend any city that enacts such laws, viewing each town as valuable in the broader targeting of the state. END OF SUMMARY.
