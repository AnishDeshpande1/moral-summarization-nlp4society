SUMMARY: The American Civil Liberties Union has filed a lawsuit against seven East Texas towns that have enacted “sanctuary” ordinances for unborn children, alleging that the measures mislead residents and violate First‑Amendment rights. The ACLU argues the ordinances designate abortion providers and pro‑abortion groups as criminal organizations, thereby prohibiting them from operating, speaking, and associating within the cities and hampering their ability to counter and clarify the confusion created by the laws. ACLU attorney Anjali Salvador calls the ordinances unconstitutional, saying they deter and intimidate lawful pro‑abortion activity. Right‑to‑Life East Texas director Mark Dickson, a leader of the sanctuary movement, denounces the lawsuit as meritless and pledges to defend the ordinances at no cost to taxpayers, asserting the laws protect unborn life and are consistent with Texas law. The legal battle now centers on whether the cities’ targeting of abortion services can stand against claims of constitutional violations. END OF SUMMARY.
