SUMMARY:
The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) has filed a lawsuit against seven East Texas towns that declared themselves "sanctuary cities" for unborn children, outlawing abortion altogether within city limits if Roe v. Wade is ever overturned. The ACLU argues that the pro-life ordinances violate the rights to free expression and association of two pro-abortion groups, the Lilith Fund and the Texas Equal Access Fund (TEA Fund). The lawsuit contends that the ordinances mislead residents, dubbing abortion providers and pro-abortion entities as "criminal organizations," and prohibiting them from operating, speaking, and associating within these cities. The ACLU claims that these ordinances are "unconstitutional" and violate the First Amendment. In contrast, pro-life advocates, such as Mark Dickson, director of Right to Life East Texas, believe the ordinances are "entirely constitutional" and "valuable" in protecting unborn life.
