SUMMARY:

The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) has filed a lawsuit against seven East Texas towns that declared themselves "sanctuary cities" for unborn children, violating the rights of two pro-abortion groups. The towns' ordinances, which outlaw abortion if Roe v. Wade is overturned, mislead residents about their right to access abortion. The lawsuit contends that the ordinances dub abortion providers and pro-abortion entities as "criminal organizations," prohibiting them from operating, speaking, and associating within the cities. This designation hampers their ability to counter or clarify the confusion created by the ordinances. The ACLU argues that the ordinances are unconstitutional, violating the First Amendment, and punish pro-abortion organizations for carrying out their work. In response, pro-life advocates claim the lawsuit is meritless and aimed at deterring cities from enacting these ordinances, which they believe are entirely constitutional and consistent with Texas law.

END OF SUMMARY.
