SUMMARY:

The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) has filed a lawsuit against seven East Texas towns that declared themselves "sanctuary cities" for unborn children, alleging that their pro-life ordinances mislead residents and violate the rights of pro-abortion groups. The ordinances, which would outlaw abortion within city limits if Roe v. Wade is overturned, have dubbed abortion providers and pro-abortion entities as "criminal organizations," prohibiting them from operating, speaking, and associating within these cities. The ACLU argues that these ordinances are unconstitutional, as they hamper pro-abortion groups from countering and clarifying the confusion created by the ordinances. The lawsuit contends that cities cannot punish pro-abortion organizations for carrying out their work, especially when it violates the First Amendment. In response, the director of Right to Life East Texas, Mark Dickson, has derided the lawsuit as "meritless" and vowed to defend the ordinances, which he believes are entirely constitutional and consistent with Texas law.

END OF SUMMARY.
