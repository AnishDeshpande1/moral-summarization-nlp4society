SUMMARY:

The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) has filed a lawsuit against seven East Texas towns that have declared themselves "sanctuary cities" for unborn children. The lawsuit, brought on behalf of two pro-abortion groups, claims that the towns' pro-life ordinances violate their rights to free expression and association. The ordinances, passed by cities including Waskom, Naples, and Joaquin, contain language that would outlaw abortion within city limits if the Supreme Court's ruling in Roe v. Wade is ever overturned. The ACLU argues that the ordinances mislead residents about the legality of abortion and dub abortion providers as "criminal organizations," preventing them from operating and clarifying the confusion. The director of Right to Life East Texas, Mark Dickson, has vowed to defend the ordinances, calling the lawsuit "meritless" and offering to defend the cities at no charge.

END OF SUMMARY.
