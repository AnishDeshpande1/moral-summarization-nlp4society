SUMMARY:
The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) has filed a lawsuit against seven East Texas towns that declared themselves "sanctuary cities" for unborn children, claiming that their pro-life ordinances are unconstitutional and misleading. The ordinances, which would outlaw abortion altogether within city limits if Roe v. Wade is overturned, have been deemed "criminal" by the ACLU, hampering pro-abortion organizations from operating and speaking within these cities. The ACLU argues that these ordinances violate the First Amendment and are meant to deter and intimidate cities from enacting similar laws. However, Mark Dickson, director of Right to Life East Texas, calls the lawsuit "meritless" and claims that the ordinances are entirely constitutional and consistent with Texas laws. The case highlights the ongoing debate over abortion rights and the role of cities in protecting unborn life.
