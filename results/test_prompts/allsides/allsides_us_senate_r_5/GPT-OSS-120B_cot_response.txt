**SUMMARY:**  
Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham warned he would block the Senate confirmations of President Obama’s CIA and Defense Department nominees until the White House provides a full accounting of its actions on the night of the Sept. 11, 2012, Benghazi terror attacks that killed Ambassador Christopher Stevens and three other Americans. Graham called the administration’s silence “deceived, stonewalled and unacceptable,” demanding the president be held accountable for his leadership. He said no confirmation would occur without information, labeling the White House’s response “unprecedented” and “not appropriate.” While Graham has not threatened a filibuster, fellow Republican Sen. James Inhofe has, making the confirmation fight increasingly aggressive. Democrats support the nominees, but many Republicans remain critical, citing concerns over Hagel’s stance on Israel and Iran. Graham’s push for answers reflects a broader partisan clash over accountability for the Benghazi tragedy.
