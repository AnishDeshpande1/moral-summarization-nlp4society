SUMMARY:

Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham is demanding an "accounting" from the White House regarding the Benghazi terror attacks that killed four Americans, including Ambassador Christopher Stevens. Graham is threatening to hold up the nominations of Chuck Hagel for Defense Secretary and John Brennan for CIA director until the administration provides more details about the president's actions during the "incident". Graham wants to know if the White House "pressed" the Libyan government to allow a response team to reach the U.S. outpost under "attack" for eight hours. He claims the administration has "delayed, denied, deceived, and stonewalled" in providing information, and it's time for Obama to be "accountable" for his leadership during the "crisis". Democrats, including Sen. Jack Reed, have criticized Graham's actions as "unprecedented" and "not appropriate", saying he is using the "tragedy" to block nominations. END OF SUMMARY.
