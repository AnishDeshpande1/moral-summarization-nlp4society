SUMMARY: 

Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham is demanding more information about the White House's response to the Benghazi terror attacks, which killed four Americans, including Ambassador Christopher Stevens. Graham believes that until the White House provides a full accounting of what happened that night, he will block the confirmation of Defense Department nominee Chuck Hagel and CIA nominee John Brennan. He has accused the White House of stonewalling and called for President Obama to be accountable for his leadership during the incident. Graham wants to know if the White House ever pressed the Libyan government for help in responding to the attack, which lasted for eight hours. Democrats have criticized Graham's actions as unprecedented and inappropriate, but he remains firm in his stance that the White House must provide a full accounting of what happened. Graham has vowed to hold Obama "accountable" for his leadership during this national crisis.

END OF SUMMARY.
