SUMMARY:

Republican Senator Lindsey Graham has threatened to delay the confirmation of President Obama's nominees for the CIA and Defense Department until the White House provides more information about the president's actions during the Benghazi terror attacks. Graham is seeking details on whether the White House contacted the Libyan government to help the Americans under attack and why there were no follow-up calls after an initial 30-minute conversation between Obama and Defense Secretary Leon Panetta. Graham has been critical of the White House's handling of the attacks and has called for more accountability from the president. He has also threatened to delay the confirmation of Chuck Hagel as Defense Secretary and John Brennan as CIA Director until he receives the information he is seeking. Democrats have criticized Graham's actions as "unprecedented" and "not appropriate".

END OF SUMMARY.
