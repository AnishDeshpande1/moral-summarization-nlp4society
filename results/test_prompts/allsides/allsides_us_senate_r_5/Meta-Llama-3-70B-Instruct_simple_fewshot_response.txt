SUMMARY:

Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham is threatening to hold up the appointments of President Obama's nominees to run the CIA and Defense Department until the White House provides more details about the president's actions during the Benghazi terror attacks. Graham is seeking accountability from the administration, questioning why no follow-up calls were made after the initial phone conversation between Obama and Defense Secretary Leon Panetta. The senator is also asking whether the president pressed the Libyan government to allow a response team to reach the U.S. outpost under attack. Graham's actions have been criticized by Democrats, who argue that he is using the tragedy to block nominations. The senator's demands for transparency and accountability are morally justified, as the administration's handling of the Benghazi attacks has been marked by a lack of transparency and responsibility. By holding up the nominations, Graham is seeking to ensure that the administration is held accountable for its actions during a national crisis.

END OF SUMMARY.
