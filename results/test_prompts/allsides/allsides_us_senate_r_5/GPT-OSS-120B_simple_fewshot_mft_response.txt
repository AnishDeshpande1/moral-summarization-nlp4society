SUMMARY:
Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham warned that he will block the Senate confirmations of President Obama’s nominees for the CIA (John Brennan) and the Defense Department (Chuck Hagel) until the White House explains its actions on the night of the Sept. 11, 2012 Benghazi attacks that killed Ambassador Christopher Stevens and three Americans. Graham framed the issue as a challenge to presidential authority, demanding accountability for leadership that he says “delayed, denied, deceived and stonewalled” the nation during a crisis. By tying the nominations to a lack of information, he casts the administration’s silence as an unfair, deceptive practice that cheats both Congress and the public. The senator’s push also invokes the moral imperative of care, emphasizing the harm suffered by the victims and their families and insisting that the government must answer for its failure to protect them. While Democrats argue that using the tragedy to stall nominations is “unprecedented” and inappropriate, Graham maintains that fairness requires the president to disclose what, if any, assistance was offered to Libya that night, and that without such transparency, confirming the nominees would subvert both authority and justice.  
END OF SUMMARY.
