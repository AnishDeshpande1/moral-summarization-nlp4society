SUMMARY:

Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham is demanding more information from the White House about the night of the Benghazi terror attacks, which killed Ambassador Christopher Stevens and three other Americans, before confirming President Obama's nominees for the CIA and Defense Department. Graham is critical of the administration's handling of the crisis and wants to know what the president and top officials were doing during the attack. He questions why no follow-up calls were made after a 30-minute phone conversation between Obama and Defense Secretary Leon Panetta. Graham accuses the White House of "delayed, denied, deceived, and stonewalled" information, and wants to hold Obama "accountable" for his leadership during the incident. Democrats support the nominations, but Republicans are largely opposed, citing concerns over Hagel's support of Israel and stance on stopping Iran from producing a nuclear weapon. Graham's actions are deemed "unprecedented" by Democratic Sen. Jack Reed, who argues that dwelling on the tragedy to block nominations is "not appropriate."

END OF SUMMARY.
