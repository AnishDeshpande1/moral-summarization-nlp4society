SUMMARY: Republican Senator Lindsey Graham announced he will block the Senate confirmations of President Obama’s nominees for Secretary of Defense (Chuck Hagel) and CIA Director (John Brennan) until the White House provides a detailed account of its actions on the night of the September 11, 2012 Benghazi attacks. Graham criticized the administration’s response, questioning whether President Obama contacted the Libyan government or authorized a rescue team, and demanded accountability after Defense Secretary Leon Panetta and Joint Chiefs Chairman Martin Dempsey testified about limited communication with the president. While not threatening a filibuster, Graham has pressed for the withdrawal of Hagel’s nomination and forced Panetta’s testimony. Other Republicans, such as Sen. James Inhofe, have threatened filibusters, citing concerns over Hagel’s stance on Israel and Iran. Democrats support the nominees, and Sen. Jack Reed called Graham’s tactics “unprecedented” and inappropriate. END OF SUMMARY.
