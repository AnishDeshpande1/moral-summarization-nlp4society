SUMMARY:
Sen. Lindsey Graham (R‑SC) warned he will block the Senate confirmations of President Obama’s picks for Secretary of Defense and CIA Director unless the White House explains what Obama and senior officials did on the night of the Sept. 11, 2012 Benghazi attack. Graham argued that the administration “delayed, denied, deceived and stone‑walled” and that no follow‑up calls were made after the brief 30‑minute conversation Obama had with Defense Secretary Leon Panetta that evening. He demanded answers about whether Obama tried to contact the Libyan government or dispatch a rescue team, and he has already forced Defense Secretary Leon Panetta to testify before a vote on Chuck Hagel’s nomination. While Democrats view Graham’s tactics as “unprecedented” and inappropriate, Republicans see them as a necessary hold‑up to force accountability for what they portray as a leadership failure during a national crisis. The moral framing casts Graham’s obstruction as a principled stand for transparency and responsibility, contrasting it with a White House they allege is evasive and unaccountable.  

END OF SUMMARY.
