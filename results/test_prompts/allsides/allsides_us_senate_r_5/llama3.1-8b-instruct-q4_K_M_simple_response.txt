SUMMARY:

Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham is demanding more information from the White House about its response to the Benghazi terror attacks, which killed four Americans in 2012. He has vowed to hold President Obama accountable for his leadership during the incident and has suggested blocking the nominations of Defense Secretary nominee Chuck Hagel and CIA nominee John Brennan until he receives more details. Graham is concerned that the White House may have delayed or denied providing information about its response to the attacks, including whether it contacted the Libyan government for help.

Graham's actions are being criticized by Rhode Island Democratic Sen. Jack Reed, who called them "unprecedented" and "not appropriate." However, Graham believes that he has a duty to uncover the truth about what happened on the night of the Benghazi attacks and to hold those responsible accountable.

END OF SUMMARY.
