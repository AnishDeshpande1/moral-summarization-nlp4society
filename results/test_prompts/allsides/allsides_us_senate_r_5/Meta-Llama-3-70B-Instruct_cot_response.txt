SUMMARY:
Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham is demanding more information from the White House about the night of the Benghazi terror attacks, threatening to hold up the appointments of President Obama's nominees to run the CIA and the Defense Department. Graham is critical of the White House's handling of the attacks, which killed four Americans, and wants to know what the president and other top officials were doing during the crisis. He accuses the White House of "delayed, denied, deceived and stonewalled" and demands that Obama be held "accountable" for his leadership. Graham's actions have been called "unprecedented" by Democratic Sen. Jack Reed, who argues that dwelling on the tragedy to block nominations is "not appropriate." END OF SUMMARY.
