SUMMARY:

Republican Senator Lindsey Graham is threatening to hold up the confirmation of President Obama's nominees for CIA and Defense Department unless the White House provides more information about its response to the Benghazi terror attacks in September 2012. Graham, a critic of Defense nominee Chuck Hagel, wants to know if the White House contacted the Libyan government for help during the attack that killed four Americans. He also questioned why no follow-up calls were made with Obama after their initial phone conversation on the day of the attack. Graham has vowed to hold Obama "accountable" for his leadership during the incident and said he will not confirm Hagel or CIA nominee John Brennan without more information.

Oklahoma Senator James Inhofe has threatened to filibuster Hagel's nomination, and Rhode Island Senator Jack Reed called Graham's actions "unprecedented" and "not appropriate." The White House has denied any wrongdoing in its response to the attack. Graham is seeking answers about whether Obama pressed the Libyan government to let a response team leave a Libyan airport to get to the US outpost.

END OF SUMMARY.
