SUMMARY:

Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham is demanding more information from the White House about the night of the Benghazi terror attacks before confirming President Obama's nominees for the CIA and Defense Department. Graham is critical of the administration's handling of the attacks and wants to know what the president and top officials did during the crisis. He is particularly concerned about whether the White House contacted the Libyan government to help the Americans under attack. Graham's actions have been called "unprecedented" by Democratic Sen. Jack Reed, who argues that dwelling on the tragedy to block nominations is "not appropriate." However, Graham believes it is essential to hold the president accountable for his leadership during the incident. The senator's move is seen as a way to exert pressure on the administration to provide more transparency about the events of that night.

END OF SUMMARY.
