SUMMARY:  
Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham vowed to stall President Obama’s nominees for the CIA and Defense Department until the White House fully explains its actions on the night of the Sept. 11, 2012 Benghazi attacks that killed Ambassador Chris Stevens and three Americans. Framing the issue as a moral demand for accountability, Graham told “Face the Nation” that no confirmation will occur without a clear accounting of whether President Obama contacted the Libyan government or dispatched a rescue team. He has already forced Defense Secretary Leon Panetta to testify and urged Obama to withdraw the nominations, accusing the administration of “delayed, denied, deceived and stonewalled” behavior. While Graham has not threatened a filibuster, fellow GOP Sen. James Inhofe has signaled he will, and many Republicans oppose Chuck Hagel’s defense nomination over his Israel stance and Iran policy. Democrats, including Sen. Jack Reed, denounce Graham’s tactics as “unprecedented” and “inappropriate” for using a tragedy to block appointments. The clash underscores a partisan moral battle over how the government should answer for its leadership during a national crisis.  
END OF SUMMARY.
