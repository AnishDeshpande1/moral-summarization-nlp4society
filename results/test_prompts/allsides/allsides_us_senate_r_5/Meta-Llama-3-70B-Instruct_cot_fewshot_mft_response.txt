SUMMARY:
Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham has threatened to hold up the appointment of President Obama's nominees to run the CIA and the Defense Department until the White House provides more details about what the president and other top administration officials were doing the night of the Benghazi terror attacks. Graham is critical of the White House's handling of the attacks, which killed Ambassador Christopher Stevens and three other Americans. He demands an accounting of the president's leadership during the incident, and has threatened to filibuster the nomination of Chuck Hagel as Defense Secretary. Graham's actions have been called "unprecedented" by Democratic Sen. Jack Reed, who argues that dwelling on the tragedy to block nominations is "not appropriate." The senator's aggressive stance is seen as an attempt to hold the president accountable for his leadership during the crisis. END OF SUMMARY.
