SUMMARY:  
Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham warned he will try to hold up the confirmation of President Obama’s nominees to run the CIA and the Defense Department until the White House provides a full accounting of what the president and top officials did on the night of the Benghazi terror **attacks** that **killed** Ambassador Christopher Stevens and three other Americans. Graham told “Face the Nation” that he will not **allow** Chuck Hagel’s confirmation as secretary of Defense without answers, accusing the administration of having **delayed**, **denied**, **deceived**, and **stonewalled** the truth about the **crisis**. He also demanded the same scrutiny for John Brennan’s CIA nomination, saying “no confirmation without information.” While Graham has not threatened a filibuster, fellow Republican Sen. James Inhofe has, seeking a 60‑vote threshold. Democrats argue Graham’s tactics are “unprecedented” and that using the tragedy to block nominations is “not appropriate.” The dispute centers on whether the president **stopped** further calls or **producing** a coordinated response during the eight‑hour **attack**, and on the overall **leadership** shown in the national **crisis**.  

END OF SUMMARY.
