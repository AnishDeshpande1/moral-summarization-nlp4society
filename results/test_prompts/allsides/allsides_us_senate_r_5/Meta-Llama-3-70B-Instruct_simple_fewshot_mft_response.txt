SUMMARY:

Republican Sen. Lindsey Graham is demanding accountability from the White House over the Benghazi terror attacks, threatening to hold up the appointment of President Obama's nominees to run the CIA and Defense Department until more details are provided about the administration's response that night. Graham's concerns center on the lack of leadership and care for American lives, citing the administration's failure to prioritize security and protect those under attack. He questions why no follow-up calls were made after the initial phone conversation between Obama and Defense Secretary Leon Panetta, and whether the president pressed the Libyan government to allow a response team to reach the U.S. outpost. Graham's actions are seen as unprecedented by Democrats, who argue that dwelling on the tragedy to block nominations is not appropriate. However, Graham's moral framing emphasizes the importance of authority, care, and fairness in government actions, highlighting the administration's subversion of accountability and lack of transparency.

END OF SUMMARY.
