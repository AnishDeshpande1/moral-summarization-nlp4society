SUMMARY:  
A Democratic majority in the House is poised to introduce sweeping legislation to require background checks on all gun sales, framing the move as essential progress against a nation divided by gun violence. Senators Chris Murphy and Richard Blumenthal, whose state endured the 2012 Sandy Hook massacre that gunned down 20 children and six staff, will lead Thursday’s call to action alongside House Democrats, survivors, and bereaved families from Parkland, Las Vegas, Aurora, Charleston and Houston. The rally follows a national vigil at St. Mark’s Episcopal Church and features advocacy groups such as the Brady Campaign, Newtown Action Alliance and Giffords.  

Lawmakers highlight that existing federal background‑check laws are poorly enforced and often blocked, leaving many shootings unchecked. Even bipartisan efforts have languished in a Republican‑controlled Senate, where bills can be blocked despite occasional support from Republicans like Rep. Peter King.  

The push also underscores personal tragedies, including Lenny Pozner’s lawsuit against conspiracy theorists who claim the Sandy Hook massacre was a hoax; Pozner has faced relentless harassment and feared for his safety.  

Democrats aim to turn this moral urgency into legislative action before the 2020 election, hoping to curb future shootings, reduce killings and honor those who died.  

END OF SUMMARY.
