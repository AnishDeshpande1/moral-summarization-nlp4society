SUMMARY:

Prominent Democrats, including Senators Chris Murphy and Richard Blumenthal, are launching a new push for stronger gun control, citing the need to protect Americans from gun violence. The Democratic majority in the House of Representatives is expected to introduce legislation to impose background checks on all gun sales, a measure that has been stalled in Congress despite past tragedies like the Sandy Hook elementary school shooting. The event will feature shooting survivors, bereaved family members, and anti-gun violence advocacy groups, emphasizing the moral imperative to act. The current federal law requiring background checks is limited and poorly enforced, allowing loopholes that enable gun violence. Democrats are feeling emboldened to take a stronger stance on gun control, hoping to win Republican support and set the stage for the 2020 presidential election. The push for gun reform is framed as a matter of authority, with Democrats seeking to assert their power to protect citizens from harm, and fairness, as they argue that the current system allows cheaters to exploit loopholes and perpetuate gun violence.

END OF SUMMARY.
