SUMMARY:

Democratic leaders in the US House are planning to introduce legislation requiring background checks on all gun sales, a move aimed at reducing gun violence that has plagued the country. This push is led by Senators Chris Murphy and Richard Blumenthal, who represent Connecticut where the Sandy Hook elementary school shooting occurred in 2012. The event will coincide with the annual national vigil for victims of gun violence. The legislation comes as a result of the failure of previous bipartisan efforts to strengthen background checks, which have been blocked by Republican-controlled Congress. Democrats are feeling emboldened after their midterm election victories and plan to use their strengthened voice in Washington to push for gun control measures.

The issue has sparked emotional responses from families of victims of mass shootings, who are demanding change. Advocacy groups, including the Brady Campaign and Giffords, will join lawmakers on Capitol Hill to call for action. The introduction of this legislation marks a new strategy by Democrats to tackle the complex issue of gun control in the US.

END OF SUMMARY.
