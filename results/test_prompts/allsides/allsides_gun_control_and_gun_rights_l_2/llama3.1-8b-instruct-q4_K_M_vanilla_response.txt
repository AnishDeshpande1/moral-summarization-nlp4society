SUMMARY:

Democratic lawmakers plan to introduce legislation requiring background checks on all gun sales as one of their first priorities in the new Congress. Senators Chris Murphy and Richard Blumenthal, who represent Connecticut where the Sandy Hook elementary school shooting occurred, will lead demands for stronger gun control laws. The event will be joined by House Democrats, advocacy groups, and family members of victims from recent mass shootings.

The legislation aims to address the issue of stalled progress on gun violence in Washington, despite President Trump's promises to take action after the Parkland, Florida school shooting. Federal law requiring background checks is limited and often not enforced, and bipartisan efforts have failed to pass in the Republican-controlled Congress.

Democrats are feeling emboldened to push for stricter gun control laws, with 46 incoming House Democrats writing a letter demanding that gun reform be a top priority. The event on Capitol Hill will follow the annual national vigil for victims of gun violence and is expected to set out the Democratic Party's stance ahead of the 2020 presidential election.

END OF SUMMARY.
