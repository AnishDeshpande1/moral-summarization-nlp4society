SUMMARY:  
The article portrays a moral imperative for action against gun violence, highlighting how grieving families and survivors are demanding change. Democratic leaders—Senators Chris Murphy and Richard Blumenthal, along with House Democrats—are rallying to push sweeping universal background‑check legislation as a first step for the 2019 Congress. Their call is framed as a response to repeated tragedies—from Sandy Hook to Parkland, Las Vegas, Aurora, Charleston, and recent shootings in Texas and California—and to the ongoing harassment of victims’ families by conspiracy theorists. The piece underscores the ethical duty of elected officials to protect citizens, especially children, by closing loopholes in weak, poorly enforced federal background‑check laws. While Republicans still control the Senate and may block bills, the Democrats’ renewed, morally‑charged strategy seeks to build bipartisan support and honor the memory of the victims before the 2020 election. Advocacy groups such as the Brady Campaign, Newtown Action Alliance, and Giffords join the moral crusade, urging Congress to act decisively.  

END OF SUMMARY.
