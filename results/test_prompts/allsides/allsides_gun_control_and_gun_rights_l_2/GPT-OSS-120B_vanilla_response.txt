SUMMARY:  
Prominent Democrats, led by Senators Chris Murphy and Richard Blumenthal, will launch a renewed push for stricter gun control, beginning with a Capitol Hill rally on Thursday. The event will feature House Democrats, gun‑violence advocacy groups, and families of victims from mass shootings in Sandy Hook, Parkland, Las Vegas, Aurora, Charleston, Houston and others. The newly Democratic‑controlled House is expected to introduce comprehensive legislation mandating background checks for all gun sales in the 2019 Congress. While the GOP retains the Senate, Democrats hope to garner bipartisan support, citing Republican Rep. Peter King’s past backing of similar measures. The effort follows ongoing legal actions by Sandy Hook victim families against conspiracy theorists and reflects frustration with stalled federal action despite promises from the Trump administration after the Parkland shooting. Incoming House Democrats have urged Speaker‑designate Nancy Pelosi to prioritize gun reform ahead of the 2020 election. Advocacy groups such as the Brady Campaign, Newtown Action Alliance and Giffords will join the call for change.  

END OF SUMMARY.
