SUMMARY:

Democratic lawmakers are set to introduce legislation to impose background checks on all gun sales, a top priority for the incoming Congress. Senators Chris Murphy and Richard Blumenthal, who represent Connecticut, will lead the call for fresh action on Thursday, joined by House Democrats and anti-gun violence advocacy groups. The event follows the annual national vigil for gun violence victims and comes after a string of mass shootings, including the recent Thousand Oaks, California, shooting. Despite past pledges, there has been little progress on gun control from the White House or Congress. Democrats are feeling emboldened to push for stronger gun control, hoping to win Republican support and set out their stall ahead of the 2020 presidential election. A bipartisan bill to tighten background checks has previously languished in the GOP-controlled Congress, but some Republicans, like Peter King, have indicated support for fresh legislation.

END OF SUMMARY.
