SUMMARY:
Prominent Democrats and gun‑violence survivors are mobilizing a new, forceful campaign to push the Democratic‑controlled House to enact universal background checks on all gun sales in the 2019 Congress. Senators Chris Murphy and Richard Blumenthal, whose home state was the site of the Sandy Hook massacre, will lead the effort, joined by House members, advocacy groups (Brady, Newtown Action Alliance, Giffords) and families of victims from Parkland, Las Vegas, Aurora, Charleston and Houston. The push frames the issue as a moral imperative to protect citizens (Care/Harm) and to demand fair, consistent safety standards that prevent privileged loopholes (Fairness/Cheating). It also challenges the longstanding Republican‑led Senate obstruction, portraying the current Republican dominance as a subversive barrier to responsible governance (Authority/Subversion). By rallying victims’ voices and highlighting the inadequacy of existing, poorly enforced background‑check laws, Democrats aim to assert moral authority, demand equitable protection for all, and reshape the national dialogue ahead of the 2020 election.  
END OF SUMMARY.
