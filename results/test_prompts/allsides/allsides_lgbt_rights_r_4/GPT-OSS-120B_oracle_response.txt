SUMMARY: Christian leaders reacted to the Supreme Court’s historic ruling that same‑sex couples have a constitutional right to marry nationwide with a mix of love, concern, and warnings of civil disobedience. Critics like Mike Huckabee called the decision an out‑of‑control act of unconstitutional judicial tyranny, while Bob Vander Plaats said the Court succumbed to pressure to invent a new constitutional “right” never imagined by the Founders. Advocates for religious liberty, such as Austin Nimocks of the Alliance Defending Freedom and Rev. Albert Mohler, urged believers to speak the truth in love and warned that the ruling creates a restricted role for faith‑based voices. Jim Campbell argued the decision stripped Americans of the freedom to debate marriage policy through democratic means, a liberty he says is at the heart of freedom. Others, including Rev. Samuel Rodriguez and Liberty Counsel’s Mat Staver, claim the ruling puts religious believers at risk of marginalization and have even called for civil disobedience. Faith groups are studying the ruling to determine how best to respond while maintaining their moral convictions. END OF SUMMARY.
