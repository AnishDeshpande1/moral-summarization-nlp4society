SUMMARY:

Christian leaders are reacting to the Supreme Court's decision on same-sex marriage, expressing concern for religious liberty and warning of potential civil disobedience. Many leaders believe that the ruling will restrict the role of people of faith in American society, limiting their ability to participate in democratic debates about marriage policy. Rev. Albert Mohler emphasized the importance of speaking truth in love and speaking love in truth, while others, like Liberty Counsel's Mat Staver, have called for civil disobedience. The Southern Baptist Convention has partnered with the Alliance Defending Freedom to provide guidance on how churches can protect their ministries.

The decision is seen as a catalyst for the marginalization of Americans who hold a biblical worldview by Rev. Samuel Rodriguez. Many leaders are studying the ruling and considering the best ways to respond, with some calling it an "out-of-control act of unconstitutional judicial tyranny." The outcome has sparked warnings about the potential impact on religious liberty.

END OF SUMMARY.
