SUMMARY:
Christian leaders reacted to the Supreme Court’s historic ruling that same‑sex couples have a constitutional right to marry nationwide with a mix of love, concern and calls for resistance. They warned that the decision overturns the ability of states and citizens to debate marriage through democratic means and threatens religious liberty. Figures such as former governor Mike Huckabee denounced the ruling as “unconstitutional judicial tyranny,” while Alliance Defending Freedom’s Austin Nimocks and Jim Campbell argued it creates a “new constitutional right” never imagined by the Founders and strips Americans of democratic freedom. Southern Baptist president Albert Mohler urged believers to “speak the truth in love,” and Rev. Samuel Rodriguez warned the decision could marginalize those holding a biblical worldview. The Alliance Defending Freedom and the Southern Baptist Convention have already issued guidance for churches, and Liberty Counsel’s Mat Staver has called for civil disobedience. The overall moral framing emphasizes the need for Christians to protect religious freedom while responding to the ruling with love and principled resistance.

END OF SUMMARY.
