SUMMARY:
Christian leaders reacted to the Supreme Court’s historic ruling that same‑sex couples have a constitutional right to marry nationwide with a blend of love, alarm and calls for resistance. They framed the decision as a subversive act of “unconstitutional judicial tyranny,” accusing the Court of overruling the Founders and betraying the nation’s traditional authority on marriage. Figures such as Mike Huckabee and Bob Vander Plaats warned that the ruling cheats the public out of its democratic right to decide marriage policy, thereby harming religious liberty. Rev. Albert Mohler urged believers to speak “the truth in love,” emphasizing care for neighbors while refusing to lie about marriage. Alliance Defending Freedom attorneys described the decision as stripping Americans of the freedom to debate and decide social issues, a breach of fairness that endangers the faithful. Leaders like Rev. Samuel Rodriguez and Liberty Counsel’s Mat Staver called the ruling a catalyst for marginalizing biblical worldviews and even suggested civil disobedience, portraying the court’s action as a betrayal of loyal, faith‑based communities.  
END OF SUMMARY.
