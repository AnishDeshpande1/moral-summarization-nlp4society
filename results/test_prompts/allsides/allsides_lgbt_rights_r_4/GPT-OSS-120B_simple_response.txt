SUMMARY: Christian leaders reacted to the Supreme Court’s historic ruling that guarantees a constitutional right to same‑sex marriage nationwide with a mix of love, concern, and warnings of civil disobedience. While some, like former Gov. Mike Huckabee and activist Bob Vander Plaats, denounced the decision as “unconstitutional judicial tyranny” and an invented right contrary to the Founders, others, such as Rev. Albert Mohler, urged believers to “speak the truth in love” and refuse falsehoods about marriage. Many faith‑based attorneys and pastors argue the ruling threatens religious liberty by removing the democratic process for defining marriage, calling it a stripping of liberty and a catalyst for marginalizing those who hold a biblical worldview. Organizations like the Alliance Defending Freedom and the Southern Baptist Convention have issued guidance (e.g., “Protecting Your Ministry”) to help churches navigate the new legal landscape, while figures like Mat Staver of Liberty Counsel have already advocated civil disobedience. The overall moral framing presents the decision as a profound challenge to Christian conviction and religious freedom, urging a compassionate yet principled response. END OF SUMMARY.
