**SUMMARY:**  
In his Christmas Day address, Pope Francis called for **kindness** and **hope** toward migrants forced to flee by **injustice** and **suffering**. He prayed that God would bring **comfort**, **peace**, and **security** to those affected by wars in the Middle East, Ukraine, Africa and Latin America, and urged governments and the international community to act with **good will** and **compassion**. The pontiff highlighted the **love** and **tenderness** required to clothe the poor, feed the hungry, heal the sick, and reach out to the elderly, lonely, migrants and the marginalized. He asked that hearts be softened, turning them into channels of **solidarity** and **justice**, and that the world’s darkness be brightened by Christian **humility** and **compassion** on this joyful Christmas.
