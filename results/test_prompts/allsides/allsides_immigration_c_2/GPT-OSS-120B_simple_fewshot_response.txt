SUMMARY:
In his annual “Urbi et Orbi” Christmas address, Pope Francis appealed to the world’s conscience, urging kindness and concrete action for migrants and those suffering from war, poverty and religious persecution. He linked the plight of migrants to injustice, describing how it forces people to cross deserts and seas that become “cemeteries” and leaves them facing “walls of indifference.” The pontiff prayed for peace in the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine and Africa, and specifically for the Syrian people, asking governments and the international community to find humane solutions. He recalled a cross‑and‑life‑jacket memorial placed in the Vatican for migrants who drowned in the Mediterranean, and joined other religious leaders in pressing South Sudan’s leaders to honor their coalition‑government commitment. Concluding with a moral charge, Francis asked believers to soften selfish hearts, become channels of Christ’s love, and actively feed the hungry, clothe the naked, and care for the elderly, lonely, migrants and the marginalized this Christmas.  

END OF SUMMARY.
