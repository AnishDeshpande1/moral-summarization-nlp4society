SUMMARY: In his Christmas Day address, Pope Francis called for kindness and hope for migrants suffering around the world due to injustices that force them to emigrate in search of a secure life. He prayed for an end to conflicts in the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine, and Africa, and for those persecuted on the basis of religion. The pontiff urged governments and the international community to find solutions to ongoing humanitarian crises, allowing people to live together in peace and security. Francis also reflected on the suffering of migrants who drowned in the Mediterranean Sea as they sought refuge in Europe, placing a cross encircled by a life jacket inside the Vatican. He called for compassion, praying that God would soften hearts and make them channels of love, bringing tenderness to all and brightening the darkness of this world. The pope concluded his message with a call to recognize the suffering around us and act to help those in need. END OF SUMMARY.
