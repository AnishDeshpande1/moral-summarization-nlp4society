SUMMARY:

In his Christmas Day address, Pope Francis highlighted the suffering of migrants around the world, calling for kindness and compassion from governments and individuals alike. He prayed that God would comfort those who are suffering and urged an end to conflicts in various regions, including the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine, and Africa. The pontiff also expressed concern for children affected by war and persecution on the basis of religion.

Francis emphasized that it is injustice that drives migrants to cross deserts and seas in search of a secure life, but instead they often face walls of indifference. He called on people to recognize the suffering around them and act to help those in need. In his message, Francis also referenced his recent gesture of placing a cross inside the Vatican with a life jacket, remembering the migrants who drowned in the Mediterranean Sea.

The Pope concluded by calling for hearts to be softened and channels of God's love, bringing tenderness to all and brightening the darkness of the world. He urged governments and leaders to find solutions to ongoing humanitarian crises.

END OF SUMMARY.
