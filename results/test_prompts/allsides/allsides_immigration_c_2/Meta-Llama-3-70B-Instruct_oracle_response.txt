SUMMARY:

In his Christmas Day message, Pope Francis shone a light on the suffering of migrants around the world, calling for kindness and hope in the face of injustices. He prayed for God to comfort those who are suffering and urged people to show goodwill and find solutions to ongoing humanitarian crises. The pontiff lamented the injustices that force people to emigrate, often resulting in tragic consequences, such as drowning in the Mediterranean Sea. He called for an end to conflicts in various regions, including the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine, and Africa, and prayed for those who are persecuted on the basis of religion. Francis urged leaders to work towards peace and security, and for individuals to recognize the suffering around them and act to help those in need. He concluded by calling for people to show kindness and compassion to the marginalized, including migrants, the elderly, and the lonely.

END OF SUMMARY.
