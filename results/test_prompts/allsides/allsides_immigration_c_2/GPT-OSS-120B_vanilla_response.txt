SUMMARY: In his annual “Urbi et Orbi” Christmas Day address, Pope Francis called for compassion toward migrants, linking their suffering to global injustices that force people to cross deserts and seas that become “cemeteries.” He prayed for victims of wars and conflicts in the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine, and Africa, and urged governments and the international community to find peaceful solutions. The pope highlighted the recent Vatican memorial—a cross with a life‑jacket—honoring migrants who drowned in the Mediterranean. He also appealed to South Sudanese leaders to uphold their pledge to form a coalition government, which would enable a hoped‑for papal visit. Concluding, Francis asked believers to soften their hearts, aid the poor, feed the hungry, clothe the naked, and support the elderly, lonely, migrants, and marginalized, urging all to become channels of Christ’s love on Christmas. END OF SUMMARY.
