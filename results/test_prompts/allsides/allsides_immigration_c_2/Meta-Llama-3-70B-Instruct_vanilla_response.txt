SUMMARY:

In his annual Christmas Day address, Pope Francis highlighted the suffering of migrants and refugees around the world. He called for kindness and a end to conflicts in the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine, and Africa. The pontiff prayed for those persecuted for their religion and for the comfort of children suffering from war and conflicts. He also urged governments and the international community to find solutions to allow people to live together in peace and security. The pope's message was titled "Urbi et Orbi" and was delivered from the balcony of St. Peter's Basilica. He concluded by calling on people to recognize the suffering around them and act to help those in need, and to soften their hearts to become channels of love. The pope's message was one of hope and kindness, urging people to show goodwill and find solutions to ongoing humanitarian crises.

END OF SUMMARY.
