SUMMARY:
In his annual Christmas Day address, Pope Francis called for kindness and hope towards migrants around the world. He prayed that God would comfort those suffering from injustice and conflicts in various parts of the globe, including the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine, and Africa. The pontiff urged an end to ongoing humanitarian crises and encouraged governments and international leaders to find solutions to allow people to live together in peace and security.

Francis also highlighted the plight of migrants who are forced to cross deserts and seas due to injustice and persecution. He emphasized that it is our responsibility to recognize the suffering around us and act to help those in need. The Pope concluded his message by praying for a world where God's love would soften hardened hearts and bring comfort to those who suffer.

Francis also drew attention to specific regions, including Syria, South Sudan, and other countries affected by conflicts. He called on leaders to uphold their commitments and work towards forming a coalition government in South Sudan, which would allow him to visit the country in 2020.

END OF SUMMARY.
