SUMMARY: In his Christmas Day “Urbi et Orbi” address, Pope Francis framed the migrant crisis as a moral wound caused by injustice, urging the world to respond with compassion and concrete action. He prayed that God protect those forced to flee deserts and seas that become “cemeteries,” and called on individuals, governments, and the international community to open hearts, end indifference, and seek peaceful solutions to conflicts in the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine, Africa, and elsewhere. Highlighting the suffering of Syrian children and other war‑torn peoples, he asked that Christ’s light inspire leaders to create conditions for dignity and security. The pope recalled a cross with a life‑jacket placed in the Vatican to honor migrants who drowned in the Mediterranean, and he joined other religious leaders in urging South Sudan’s parties to honor their coalition pledge. Concluding with a plea for softened, self‑less hearts, he urged the faithful to clothe the naked, feed the hungry, heal the sick, and embrace migrants and the marginalized, so that Christmas joy may dispel the world’s darkness. END OF SUMMARY.
