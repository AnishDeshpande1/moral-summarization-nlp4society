SUMMARY:
In his Christmas Day “Urbi et Orbi” address, Pope Francis appealed to the world’s conscience to care for migrants suffering from injustice, war and indifference. He described how “injustice makes them cross deserts and seas that become cemeteries” and called for a fair response that does not treat displaced people as expendable. The pontiff prayed for the children and families torn by conflict in the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine and Africa, urging governments and the international community to act with compassion, to protect the vulnerable and to restore dignity to those denied basic rights. Highlighting the sanctity of human life, Francis recalled the cross‑and‑life‑jacket placed in the Vatican to honor migrants who drowned in the Mediterranean, urging believers to view every migrant as a sacred child of God. He asked that hearts soften from “stony and self‑centered” attitudes, that the faithful become channels of love, feeding the hungry, clothing the naked and comforting the lonely, especially migrants and the marginalized. The pope’s message framed the migrant crisis as a moral failure of care, fairness and respect for human sanctity that must be corrected.

END OF SUMMARY.
