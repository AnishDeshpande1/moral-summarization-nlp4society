SUMMARY:  
In his Christmas Day address, Pope Francis prayed for those suffering from injustice and urged kindness toward migrants forced to emigrate by wars, conflicts and other injustices. He asked that the Son of God protect those who cross deserts and seas that have become cemeteries, and called on governments and the international community to show solutions that end crises, hostilities and persecution. The pontiff highlighted the need for peace, security and hope for the Syrian people, for children in war‑torn regions, and for the abandoned and marginalized. He remembered the migrants who drowned in the Mediterranean, placing a cross and life jacket in the Vatican as a symbol of solidarity. Francis appealed for the world’s hearts to be softened, to give bread to the hungry, clothe those with nothing to wear, heal the sick, and draw close to the elderly, lonely, migrants and the persecuted. He concluded by urging all to act with love, turning indifference into compassionate action so that suffering may be relieved and humanity may live in peace.  

END OF SUMMARY.
