Pope Francis delivered his annual Christmas Day address, calling for kindness and compassion towards migrants around the world. He highlighted the suffering of those forced to emigrate due to injustice and persecution, and prayed that God would comfort them. The Pope also urged an end to conflicts in various regions, including the Middle East, Latin America, Ukraine, and Africa, and called on governments and the international community to find solutions to ongoing humanitarian crises. In a separate message, Francis and other religious officials appealed to leaders in South Sudan to uphold their commitment to form a coalition government next year. The Pope concluded his address with a call to recognize the suffering around us and act to help those in need, emphasizing that even small acts of kindness can bring joy and hope to those who are marginalized and vulnerable.
