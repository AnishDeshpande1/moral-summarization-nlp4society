SUMMARY: Veterans Affairs Secretary Robert Wilkie told a House appropriations subcommittee that the VA has nearly stopped using hydroxychloroquine, an unproven malaria drug, on veterans with COVID‑19. Initially the drug was used to “give them hope” when treatment options were scarce, but as studies showed limited benefit and safety concerns, prescriptions fell from a peak of 404 in March to just three last week. Of the 1,370 veterans who received it, 28% died compared with 11% who received standard care. The VA is shifting to evidence‑based therapies such as remdesivir and convalescent plasma and will keep hydroxychloroquine only in limited clinical‑trial settings, following guidance from Dr. Anthony Fauci. Lawmakers, including Rep. Nita Lowey, criticized the earlier reliance on the drug despite Fauci’s cautions. The FDA has warned that hydroxychloroquine should be used for COVID‑19 only in formal studies due to potential heart and other serious side effects. END OF SUMMARY.
