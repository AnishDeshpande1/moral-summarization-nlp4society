SUMMARY: Veterans Affairs Secretary Robert Wilkie announced that the VA has largely stopped using the unproven malaria drug hydroxychloroquine on veterans with COVID-19, citing studies that pointed to possible dangers and other treatments being brought online. Wilkie defended the initial use of the drug, saying it was justified "to give them hope" when few treatment options were available. However, he acknowledged that the VA has "ratcheted it down" to just three prescriptions in the last week, and expects this trend to continue. The VA is now turning to remdesivir, which has shown reduced recovery time, and convalescent plasma. Wilkie emphasized that the VA's mission is to "preserve and protect life," and that they are "all learning as we go in this crisis." The decision comes after major veterans organizations called on the VA to explain its use of hydroxychloroquine, citing an analysis that showed hundreds of veterans saw no benefit from the drug. Rep. Nita Lowey rebuked the VA for not listening to Dr. Anthony Fauci's counsel urging caution on the drug from the start. END OF SUMMARY.
