SUMMARY:

Veterans Affairs Secretary Robert Wilkie announced that his department will significantly reduce its use of hydroxychloroquine, an unproven malaria drug, on veterans with COVID-19. The decision comes after studies pointed to possible dangers and other treatments were brought online. Initially, the VA used the drug despite a lack of scientific evidence, citing President Donald Trump's promotion of it as a way to "give hope" to patients. However, Wilkie acknowledged that the VA has been listening to guidance from Dr. Anthony Fauci, who urged caution on the use of hydroxychloroquine due to its potential side effects. The decision is seen as a step towards prioritizing evidence-based medicine and protecting veterans' lives. As Rep. Nita Lowey noted, the VA should have listened to Fauci's counsel from the start, rather than relying on "wishful thinking" from the President. This move promotes fairness and care for veterans by prioritizing their health over unproven treatments.

END OF SUMMARY.
