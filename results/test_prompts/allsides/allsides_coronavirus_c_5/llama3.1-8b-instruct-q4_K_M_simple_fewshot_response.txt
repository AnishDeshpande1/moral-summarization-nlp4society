SUMMARY:
Veterans Affairs Secretary Robert Wilkie announced that the VA will significantly reduce its use of hydroxychloroquine, an unproven malaria drug, on veterans with COVID-19. Despite initial enthusiasm from President Donald Trump and some patients, studies have raised concerns about the drug's safety and effectiveness. The VA has reported 13,657 cases of COVID-19 among veterans, resulting in 1,200 deaths. Major veterans organizations had called for an explanation of the VA's use of hydroxychloroquine after analysis showed no benefit to treatment. Secretary Wilkie defended the initial decision to use the drug, citing a lack of treatment options at the time, but acknowledged that the trend would continue towards more evidence-based treatments. The move is seen as a shift away from Trump's enthusiastic promotion of the drug and towards following scientific guidance, including counsel from Dr. Anthony Fauci.

END OF SUMMARY.
