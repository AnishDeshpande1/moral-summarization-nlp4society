SUMMARY:  
Veterans Affairs Secretary Robert Wilkie told a House subcommittee that the VA has essentially stopped using the unproven malaria drug hydroxychloroquine on veterans with COVID‑19, reducing prescriptions to just three in the past week. He justified the early, widespread use as an effort to “give them hope” when few treatments existed, but acknowledged that emerging studies showed possible harms and that newer therapies such as remdesivir and convalescent plasma are now preferred. VA data revealed that 1,370 veterans received hydroxychloroquine, with a mortality rate of 28 % versus 11 % for those receiving standard care, prompting criticism from veterans groups and Rep. Nita Lowey, who urged the VA to follow Dr. Fauci’s cautions rather than the president’s wishful thinking. While the VA will keep the drug available only in tightly controlled clinical trials, Wilkie emphasized the agency’s moral duty to “preserve and protect life” by learning from science and abandoning ineffective, risky treatments.  

END OF SUMMARY.
