SUMMARY:

Veterans Affairs Secretary Robert Wilkie announced that the VA has almost stopped using the unproven malaria drug hydroxychloroquine to treat COVID-19 in veterans, citing studies that pointed to possible dangers and the emergence of other treatment options. Wilkie defended the initial use of the drug, saying it was justified to give patients hope when there were few treatment options available. However, he acknowledged that the VA has "ratcheted it down" to just three prescriptions in the last week. The department is now turning to remdesivir and convalescent plasma, which have shown promise in reducing recovery time. The VA has prescribed hydroxychloroquine to 1,370 veterans, but data shows that it did not provide any benefit for COVID-19. Major veterans organizations had called on the VA to explain its use of the drug, and lawmakers have expressed concerns about the VA's reliance on the drug despite a lack of scientific evidence.

END OF SUMMARY.
