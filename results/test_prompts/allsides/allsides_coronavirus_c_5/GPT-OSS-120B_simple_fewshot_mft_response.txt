SUMMARY:
Veterans Affairs Secretary Robert Wilkie told Congress that the VA has virtually ended the use of the unproven malaria drug hydroxychloroquine on veterans with COVID‑19, cutting prescriptions to just three in the past week. He defended the drug’s early rollout as a “hope‑giving” measure when options were scarce, but said the VA is now “ratcheting it down” as studies reveal safety risks and more effective treatments such as remdesivir and convalescent plasma become available. The shift reflects deference to scientific authority—particularly Dr. Anthony Fauci’s cautionary guidance—over the President’s unchecked promotion of the drug. Lawmakers highlighted the unfair harm to veterans: VA data showed a 28 % death rate among those given hydroxychloroquine versus 11 % for standard care, prompting criticism that the VA ignored evidence and politicized care. Rep. Nita Lowey urged the department to follow Fauci’s science rather than presidential wishful thinking, emphasizing the moral duty to protect veterans’ health and to apply treatments fairly, based on rigorous evidence rather than political pressure. The VA now limits the drug to clinical trials while focusing on proven therapies to preserve and protect life.

END OF SUMMARY.
