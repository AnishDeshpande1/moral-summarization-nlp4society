SUMMARY:

The US Department of Veterans Affairs has significantly reduced its use of hydroxychloroquine, an unproven malaria drug, to treat COVID-19 in veterans. Secretary Robert Wilkie defended the initial use of the drug, saying it was justified to give patients hope when few treatment options were available. However, as studies highlighted potential dangers and other treatments became available, the VA has "ratcheted down" its use of hydroxychloroquine. The department is now turning to remdesivir and convalescent plasma, which have shown promise in reducing recovery time. The VA's decision comes after major veterans organizations called for an explanation of its use of hydroxychloroquine, citing an analysis that showed no benefit for COVID-19 patients. The moral imperative is clear: the VA must prioritize evidence-based medicine and protect the lives of veterans, rather than relying on unproven treatments promoted by political leaders.

END OF SUMMARY.
