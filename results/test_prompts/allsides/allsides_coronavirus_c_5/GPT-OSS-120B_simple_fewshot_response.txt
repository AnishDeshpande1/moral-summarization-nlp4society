SUMMARY:
Veterans Affairs Secretary Robert Wilkie told Congress that the VA has virtually stopped giving the unproven malaria drug hydroxychloroquine to veterans with COVID‑19, cutting prescriptions to just three in the past week after studies showed it offered no benefit and could cause serious side effects. While Wilkie defended the drug’s early use as a way to give patients “hope” when options were scarce, the VA has now shifted toward evidence‑based treatments such as remdesivir and convalescent plasma. Data released to lawmakers revealed that 1,370 veterans received hydroxychloroquine, with a mortality rate of 28 % versus 11 % for those who received standard care alone. Critics, including Rep. Nita Lowey, warned the VA should heed Dr. Anthony Fauci’s cautions rather than the president’s unsubstantiated promotion of the drug. The VA maintains it prescribed hydroxychloroquine only after informed, doctor‑patient discussions, but the moral imperative highlighted is to follow rigorous science and protect veterans’ lives rather than cling to wishful‑thinking treatments.

END OF SUMMARY.
