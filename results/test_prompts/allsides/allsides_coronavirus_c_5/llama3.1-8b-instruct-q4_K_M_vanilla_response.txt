SUMMARY:

The US Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) has announced that it will significantly reduce its use of the unproven malaria drug hydroxychloroquine on veterans with COVID-19. VA Secretary Robert Wilkie stated that government-run hospitals have "ratcheted down" prescriptions to just three in the last week, as studies highlighted potential dangers and other treatments became available. The decision comes after President Donald Trump heavily promoted the drug without scientific evidence of its effectiveness. Preliminary results from a major study found reduced recovery time with remdesivir, which is now being used by the VA.

Major veterans organizations had called for an explanation of the VA's use of hydroxychloroquine, citing analysis showing no benefit and high mortality rates among those taking the drug. The FDA has warned against its use outside of formal studies due to potential side effects. Wilkie stated that the VA will continue using hydroxychloroquine in limited cases such as clinical trials.

END OF SUMMARY.
