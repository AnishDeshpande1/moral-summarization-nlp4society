SUMMARY:

Veterans Affairs Secretary Robert Wilkie has announced that the VA will largely discontinue the use of hydroxychloroquine on veterans with COVID-19, citing a lack of scientific evidence supporting its effectiveness. Despite initial enthusiasm for the drug, studies have revealed possible dangers and other treatment options have become available. The VA's decision is a welcome reversal, as the drug's use was previously justified as a means to "give them hope" despite the lack of evidence. The move is a victory for evidence-based decision-making and a rejection of wishful thinking. The VA's initial use of hydroxychloroquine, which was heavily promoted by President Trump without scientific backing, raises concerns about the subversion of authority and the potential harm to veterans. By prioritizing the well-being of veterans and adhering to scientific guidance, the VA is upholding its mission to "preserve and protect life." The decision also underscores the importance of fairness and accountability in healthcare decision-making.

END OF SUMMARY.
