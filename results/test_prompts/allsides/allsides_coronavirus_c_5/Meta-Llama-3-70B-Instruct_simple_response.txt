SUMMARY:

The Veterans Affairs Department has significantly reduced the use of unproven malaria drug hydroxychloroquine on veterans with COVID-19, from a peak of 404 prescriptions in mid-April to just three in the last week. Secretary Robert Wilkie defended the initial use of the drug, citing the need to "give them hope" when few treatment options were available. However, as studies pointed to possible dangers and other treatments emerged, the VA has "ratcheted it down". This decision is a step in the right direction, as the drug has been shown to have no benefit for COVID-19 and can cause serious side effects. The VA's decision to prioritize evidence-based treatments, such as remdesivir, aligns with its mission to "preserve and protect life". The moral imperative to prioritize the well-being and safety of veterans is clear, and it is encouraging to see the VA taking a more cautious and evidence-based approach.

END OF SUMMARY.
