SUMMARY:
The article argues that President‑elect Donald Trump will face constant attacks on his legitimacy and should meet them by exposing the extensive secrecy of the Obama administration. It frames transparency as a moral duty: just as Obama released Bush‑era torture memos, Trump ought to publish the classified legal papers that justified Obama’s drone program, the Syrian intervention, the 2011 Libya bombing, and other controversial actions. Revealing these “smoking‑gun” documents would vindicate Trump’s campaign warnings, punish former officials, and restore public trust. The piece also condemns Obama’s record of FOIA obstruction, citing delayed releases on Hillary Clinton’s emails, TSA “enhanced patdowns,” and secret OMB memos that underpinned the Affordable Care Act’s subsidies. By forcing disclosure of these hidden policies, the author claims Trump would demonstrate a commitment to democratic accountability, curb bureaucratic overreach, and give a “booster shot” to a populace that now trusts the federal government at historically low levels. In this moral framing, openness about past abuses is presented as the fair and necessary response to the anticipated “unprecedented and reckless” criticism Trump will face. END OF SUMMARY.
