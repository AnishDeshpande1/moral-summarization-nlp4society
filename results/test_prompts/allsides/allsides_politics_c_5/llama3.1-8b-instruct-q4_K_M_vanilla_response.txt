SUMMARY:

President-elect Donald Trump is advised to respond to expected criticism by revealing secret documents from the Obama administration, which commandeered more power than previously acknowledged. The article suggests that Trump follow President Obama's precedent in releasing Bush-era memos on torture and surveillance. This would include disclosing memos related to the drone assassination program, U.S. intervention in Syria, and the bombing of Libya in 2011.

The author argues that transparency is essential, as the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) has become largely ineffective during the Obama administration. Trump's White House should compel disclosures, which would help prevent similar mistakes in the future and boost public trust in government.

The article also mentions several specific areas where documents should be released, including:

* The secret memos underlying the drone assassination program
* Documents related to U.S. intervention in Syria
* Papers on the U.S. bombing of Libya in 2011
* FOIA requests related to Hillary Clinton's emails and the State Department

END OF SUMMARY.
