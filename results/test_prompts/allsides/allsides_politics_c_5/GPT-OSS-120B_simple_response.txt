SUMMARY: The author argues that President‑elect Donald Trump will face legitimacy attacks and should meet them by mirroring President Obama’s early‑term transparency push—by releasing the hidden legal memos and policy papers that underpinned Obama’s controversial actions. The piece frames this as a moral duty: exposing Obama‑era “secret” authorizations for torture, drone killings, Syrian interventions, the Libya bombing, TSA abuses, and the Affordable Care Act’s subsidies will vindicate Trump’s warnings, restore public trust, and protect democracy. It condemns the Obama administration for abusing executive power, stonewalling FOIA requests, and expanding intrusive regulations, portraying these as betrayals of civil liberties and accountability. By “opening the books,” Trump would demonstrate fairness, rectify past governmental overreach, and give Americans the truth needed to judge their leaders. The moral framing casts disclosure as a righteous corrective to Obama’s secrecy and a necessary step to heal a public that now distrusts the federal government. END OF SUMMARY.
