SUMMARY:
The author argues that President‑elect Donald Trump should confront doubts about his legitimacy by subverting the entrenched authority of the Obama administration and exposing its hidden abuses. By releasing the secret legal memos that justified torture, drone killings, and other aggressive policies, Trump would punish the cheating of past officials who concealed wrongdoing and restore fairness to the political system. Disclosing the undocumented decisions behind U.S. interventions in Syria, Libya, and the expansion of the TSA’s invasive searches would protect citizens from harm and demonstrate that the government cares for their safety and privacy. The piece also criticizes Obama’s record of FOIA stonewalling, portraying it as an unjust abuse of power that denied the public truthful information. By forcing transparency on these matters, Trump could rebalance authority, uphold fairness, and show a genuine concern for the well‑being of Americans, thereby strengthening democracy.  
END OF SUMMARY.
