SUMMARY: The article argues that President‑elect Donald Trump should pre‑empt legitimacy challenges by mirroring President Obama’s 2009 release of secret Bush‑era legal memos, but instead exposing Obama‑era documents. It calls for Trump to disclose classified memos on drone “targeted killing,” U.S. involvement in Syria, the 2011 Libya bombing, and other controversial Obama policies, claiming this would vindicate Trump’s campaign warnings and boost transparency. The piece criticizes the Obama administration’s poor FOIA record, alleging widespread censorship, delayed releases, and secretive practices by agencies such as the State Department, DHS, TSA, IRS, OMB, EPA, and others. It cites examples of concealed legal justifications for torture, warrantless searches, subsidies for the Affordable Care Act, and intrusive security measures. By opening these files, the author contends Trump could gain political goodwill, expose governmental overreach, and strengthen democracy, regardless of his policy choices. END OF SUMMARY.
