SUMMARY:  
Sondland testified that he told a senior Ukrainian official U.S. aid would be delayed until Ukraine took “action” on a public statement that had been discussed for weeks, and he conveyed to Vice President Pence that the aid was being held up pending investigations—though Pence’s chief of staff denied this. He recounted a call with President Trump in which Trump asked whether Ukraine would pursue investigations; Sondland said they would do whatever the president wanted. Sondland later told a colleague that Trump “did not give a [expletive] about Ukraine,” caring only about “big stuff” that would benefit the president. He explained that Trump cared not about actual investigations but about a public announcement that could **defame** Biden, **muddy** the waters about Russia’s role in the 2016 election, and provide material for a political **shakedown**. Republicans have tried to **massage** Trump’s claims of Ukrainian **interference** and **meddling**, but Sondland’s testimony shows Trump wanted an inquiry to support the false narrative that Ukraine, not Russia, **hacked** the D.N.C., thereby **attacking** the legitimacy of the 2016 **elected** outcome. Sondland’s credibility is questioned, yet he insists he inferred the aid‑investigation link on his own, “two plus two equals four.”  
END OF SUMMARY.
