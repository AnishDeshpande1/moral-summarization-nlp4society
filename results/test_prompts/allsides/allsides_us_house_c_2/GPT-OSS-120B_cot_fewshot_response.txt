**SUMMARY:**  
During the impeachment hearing, John Sondland testified that he told a senior Ukrainian official that the resumption of U.S. aid would likely be on hold until Ukraine took “some kind of action” on a public statement they had discussed for weeks. He said Vice President Mike Pence merely nodded when Sondland suggested the aid was being held for investigations, a claim Pence’s chief of staff denied. Sondland also recounted a call with President Trump in which Trump asked if Ukraine would pursue investigations; Sondland replied they would do whatever the president wanted, noting that Trump cared only about “big” political gains, not Ukraine itself. The testimony highlighted Trump’s desire for a public announcement of investigations to defame Biden and muddy the waters about Russia’s role in the 2016 election, a scheme the author describes as a political shakedown. Republicans, however, have tried to portray the alleged Ukrainian meddling as merely public statements, dismissing the more serious accusations as “preposterous” and “insane.” Sondland’s credibility is questioned, with his claims of ignorance about the Burisma‑Biden link deemed unreliable.
