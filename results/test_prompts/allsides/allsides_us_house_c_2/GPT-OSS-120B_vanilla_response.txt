SUMMARY:  
Former Trump envoy Richard Sondland testified that U.S. aid to Ukraine was effectively withheld until Kyiv agreed to take “some kind of action” on a public statement that had been discussed for weeks. He told Vice President Mike Pence the aid delay was tied to investigations, though Pence’s chief of staff disputed this. Sondland corroborated embassy official David Holmes’ account that, after Trump’s July 25 call to President Zelensky, Trump asked Sondland whether Ukraine would pursue investigations; Sondland replied they would do whatever the president wanted. He later said Trump cared less about actual investigations and more about a public announcement that could be used to attack Joe Biden and deflect blame for Russian election interference. Sondland emphasized that Zelensky only needed to announce investigations, not conduct them. The testimony highlighted Trump’s desire for a probe into the false claim that Ukraine, not Russia, hacked the D.N.C., aiming to exonerate Russia. While Sondland’s credibility is questioned—particularly his claim of ignorance about the Burisma‑Biden link—Republicans may focus on his statement that he never heard Trump directly link aid to investigations, concluding the connection was his own inference.  

END OF SUMMARY.
