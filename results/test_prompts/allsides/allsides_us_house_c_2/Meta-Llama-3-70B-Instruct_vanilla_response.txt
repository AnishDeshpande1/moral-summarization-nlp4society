SUMMARY:

Gordon Sondland, the US Ambassador to the EU, testified that he told a Ukrainian official that US aid would likely not be resumed until Ukraine made a public statement about investigations. He also testified that he told VP Mike Pence that aid was being held up pending investigations, but Pence's chief of staff denied this. Sondland confirmed a phone call with Trump, where Trump asked if Ukraine would pursue investigations, and Sondland replied that they would do what Trump wanted. Sondland testified that Trump didn't care about the investigations themselves, but about the public announcement, which would benefit him. The goal was to get Ukraine to announce investigations into the conspiracy theory that Ukraine, not Russia, hacked the DNC server, to exonerate Russia for its attack on America. Sondland's testimony revealed that Trump's demands were not about Ukraine's actions in 2016, but about using Ukraine to deflect blame from Russia.

END OF SUMMARY.
