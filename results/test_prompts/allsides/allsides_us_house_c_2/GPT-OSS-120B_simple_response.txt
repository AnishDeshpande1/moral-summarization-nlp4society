SUMMARY:  
The testimony of former Trump envoy Robert Sondland paints a picture of a president who weaponized U.S. aid to Ukraine as leverage for a political shakedown. Sondland recounts telling a senior Ukrainian official that aid would be delayed until Ukraine “took some kind of action” on a public statement that had been discussed for weeks, and he relayed to Vice‑President Pence that the aid hold‑up was tied to investigations—an assertion Pence’s chief of staff denies. Sondland corroborates embassy official David Holmes, who heard Trump ask whether Ukraine would pursue investigations; Sondland replied they would do “whatever the president wanted.” He later told Holmes that Trump “did not give a [expletive] about Ukraine,” caring only about “big stuff” that served his own interests. According to Sondland, Trump needed only a public announcement of investigations—particularly into the false claim that Ukraine, not Russia, hacked the D.N.C.—to generate damaging material against Joe Biden and obscure Russia’s role in his 2016 victory. The article condemns Sondland’s unreliable claims of ignorance about the Burisma‑Biden link and highlights Republican attempts to downplay the gravity of Trump’s quid‑pro‑quo scheme.  

END OF SUMMARY.
