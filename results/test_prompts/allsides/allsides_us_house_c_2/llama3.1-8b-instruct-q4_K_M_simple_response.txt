SUMMARY:

New testimony from Gordon Sondland, a key witness in the impeachment inquiry, reveals that President Trump's motivations for withholding aid to Ukraine were driven by his desire to obtain public announcements of investigations into conspiracy theories, rather than actual actions. Sondland testified that he communicated with Ukrainian officials that aid would not be released unless they publicly announced investigations, which would benefit Trump politically. This is in line with David Holmes' testimony that Trump only cared about the appearance of investigations, and did not actually want them to occur.

The investigation has also shed light on Trump's attempts to deflect blame from Russia for its role in interfering in the 2016 US election. Sondland's testimony confirms that Trump wanted Ukraine to investigate a conspiracy theory that they, rather than Russia, hacked the D.N.C. server.

This new evidence further highlights the morally dubious actions of President Trump and his administration, who sought to use public pressure to coerce a foreign government into advancing their own interests.

END OF SUMMARY.
