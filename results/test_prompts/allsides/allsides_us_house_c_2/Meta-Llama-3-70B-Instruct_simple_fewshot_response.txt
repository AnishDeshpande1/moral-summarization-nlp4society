SUMMARY:

Ambassador Gordon Sondland testified that he communicated to a Ukrainian official that US aid would likely not resume until Ukraine took action on a public statement regarding investigations. He also testified that Trump didn't care about the investigations themselves, but rather the public announcement of them, which would benefit him. Sondland's testimony revealed that Trump wanted Ukraine's help in exonerating Russia for its attack on America in 2016, and that he was seeking an inquiry into the conspiracy theory that Ukraine, not Russia, hacked the DNC server. Sondland's credibility was questioned, as he claimed to be ignorant of the connection between Burisma and the Bidens when he pressed Ukraine for investigations. Republicans may cling to the fact that Sondland never heard directly from Trump about a linkage between security aid to Ukraine and investigations, but Sondland's testimony suggests that he inferred this connection. The testimony sheds light on Trump's motivations and the extent of his pressure on Ukraine.

END OF SUMMARY.
