SUMMARY:

Over 100 people were arrested and 21 police officers injured in Minnesota on Saturday night during protests over recent police killings of black men. The protests, which started with a peaceful march, turned violent when demonstrators pelted police officers with rocks, rebar, and incendiary devices, including Molotov cocktails. Police used smoke bombs to clear demonstrators blocking Interstate 94, and over 100 troopers responded to the freeway. The injuries to officers were significant but not life-threatening. The protests were among several demonstrations nationwide following the deaths of Philando Castile and Alton Sterling. The riots came just days after five Dallas police officers were murdered by Micah Johnson. Authorities condemned the violence, with St. Paul Police Chief Todd Axtell saying he was "disgusted" by the acts of some people.

END OF SUMMARY.
