SUMMARY:

Over 100 people were arrested and 21 police officers injured in St. Paul, Minnesota on Saturday night during protests over the recent killings of black men by police. The protests turned violent, with protesters throwing rocks, rebar, and incendiary devices at officers. Police used smoke bombs to clear demonstrators blocking an interstate highway, but some protesters refused to leave. The violence was condemned by local officials, including St. Paul Mayor Chris Coleman and Police Chief Todd Axtell. The incidents follow the deaths of Philando Castile and Alton Sterling, who were shot by police in separate incidents. The protests were among several demonstrations nationwide following these killings. Minnesota Gov. Mark Dayton met with Castile's family on Saturday afternoon to offer condolences.

END OF SUMMARY.
