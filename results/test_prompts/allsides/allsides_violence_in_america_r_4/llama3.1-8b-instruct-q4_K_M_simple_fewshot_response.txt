SUMMARY:

Protests over recent police killings of black men turned violent in Minnesota on Saturday night, resulting in more than 100 arrests and 21 injured police officers. Demonstrators pelted police with rocks, rebar, and incendiary devices, including Molotov cocktails. The violence came just days after the murders of five Dallas police officers by a sniper targeting white cops. The families of Philando Castile and Alton Sterling denounced the attack on the Dallas officers, but some protesters turned violent in Minnesota, causing significant harm to law enforcement. Police used smoke bombs to clear demonstrators blocking Interstate 94, and over 100 troopers responded to the freeway. While some participants described the protest as mostly peaceful, others reported being pepper-sprayed by police while trying to move the crowd along.
