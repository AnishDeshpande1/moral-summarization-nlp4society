SUMMARY:  
On Saturday night in Minnesota, a peaceful march protesting the police killings of Philando Castile and Alton Sterling turned violent, resulting in more than 100 arrests and injuries to 21 officers. Mayor Chris Coleman called the episode an “extreme incident,” noting that protesters hurled rocks, rebar, Molotov cocktails and other incendiary devices at police, causing “significant” but non‑fatal injuries. Police chiefs expressed disgust and a zero‑tolerance stance toward the attacks, emphasizing that such violence against public servants is morally unacceptable. While earlier demonstrations across the country remained peaceful, this night’s crowd ignored police orders, blocked Interstate 94, and forced troopers and officers to use smoke bombs and force to restore order. Some participants, like 20‑year‑old Misty Macon, insisted most of the protest stayed peaceful, but the overall moral judgment presented in the report condemns the destructive actions that endangered officers and disrupted public safety. The incident underscores a stark moral contrast between lawful protest and violent rioting.  

END OF SUMMARY.
