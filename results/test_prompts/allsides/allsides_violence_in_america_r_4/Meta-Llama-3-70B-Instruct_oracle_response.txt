SUMMARY:

A violent incident unfolded in Minnesota on Saturday night, resulting in 102 arrests and 21 police officers injured. The protests, sparked by the recent police killings of two black men, Philando Castile and Alton Sterling, turned into rioting as demonstrators pelted police officers with rocks, rebar, and incendiary devices, including Molotov cocktails. The injuries to officers were significant, but none were life-threatening. The riots came just days after five Dallas police officers were murdered by Micah Johnson, who targeted white cops in a sniper attack. Sterling's and Castile's families denounced the attack on the Dallas officers. Police used smoke bombs to clear demonstrators blocking Interstate 94, but about 200 protesters refused to leave the roadway, leading to violent clashes. St. Paul Police Chief Todd Axtell expressed disgust at the acts of violence towards public servants, vowing that such behavior would not be tolerated. END OF SUMMARY.
