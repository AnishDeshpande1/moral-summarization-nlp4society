SUMMARY:
More than 100 people were arrested after a night‑time protest in St. Paul turned into rioting. Protesters pelted police officers with rocks, rebar and incendiary devices, causing injuries to 21 officers. The mayor called the episode an “extreme incident,” noting that the injuries were significant but not life‑threatening. Demonstrators had gathered to protest the killings of Philando Castile and Alton Sterling, whose deaths sparked nationwide protests. The unrest followed the murder of five Dallas officers, an attack that the victims’ families condemned. Police used smoke bombs to clear protesters blocking Interstate 94, but about 200 protesters refused to leave the roadway after midnight, prompting a response from troopers and riot‑gear‑clad officers. The police chief said he was disgusted by the acts of violence and would not tolerate such behavior, describing it as the worst level of violence he had seen in 28 years. Troopers unsuccessfully tried to keep the crowd from the freeway, and officers warned that anyone who did not disassemble would face arrest. The interstate reopened around 1:30 a.m. after the violence subsided.  
END OF SUMMARY.
