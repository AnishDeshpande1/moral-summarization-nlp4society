SUMMARY:
More than 100 people were arrested and 21 police officers injured during a violent turn of protests in St. Paul, Minnesota, on Saturday night. The demonstrations, sparked by the recent police shootings of Black men Philando Castile and Alton Sterling, escalated after a march from the governor’s mansion, with protesters hurling rocks, rebar, Molotov cocktails and other incendiary devices at officers. Police used smoke bombs and riot gear to clear demonstrators blocking Interstate 94; the freeway reopened around 1:30 a.m. St. Paul Mayor Chris Coleman called the incident “extreme,” noting the injuries were significant but not life‑threatening. Police chief Todd Axtell expressed disgust at the unprecedented violence, while State Patrol Colonel Matt Langer said earlier protests had been peaceful. Some participants, such as 20‑year‑old Misty Macon, described the overall protest as mostly peaceful despite isolated incidents. Governor Mark Dayton met with Castile’s family earlier in the day. The unrest follows the recent Dallas sniper attack that killed five white officers, an act condemned by the victims’ families.

END OF SUMMARY.
