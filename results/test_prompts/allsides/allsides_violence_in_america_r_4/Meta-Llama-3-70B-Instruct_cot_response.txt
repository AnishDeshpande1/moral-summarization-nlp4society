SUMMARY:
A violent riot broke out in Minnesota on Saturday night, resulting in the arrests of over 100 people and injuries to 21 police officers. The protests, sparked by the recent police killings of black men Philando Castile and Alton Sterling, turned extreme when demonstrators pelted police officers with rocks, rebar, and incendiary devices, including Molotov cocktails. St. Paul Police Chief Todd Axtell expressed disgust at the violent acts, calling them "disgusting" and vowing that the police would not tolerate such behavior. In contrast, previous protests had been peaceful, with marchers listening to police instructions. The riots came just days after the murder of five Dallas police officers, which was denounced by Castile's and Sterling's families. The police used smoke bombs to clear the demonstrators blocking Interstate 94, and eventually reopened the freeway at 1:30 a.m. Sunday.
