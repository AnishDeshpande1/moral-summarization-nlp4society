SUMMARY:
A volatile protest in St. Paul on Saturday night erupted into the most violent confrontation the city has seen in decades, resulting in more than 100 arrests and injuries to 21 police officers. Mayor Chris Coleman described the episode as an “extreme incident” after demonstrators, who had begun a march over the governor’s mansion, began hurling rocks, rebar, fireworks and Molotov‑style devices at law‑enforcement. Police, forced to use smoke bombs and riot gear, struggled to clear Interstate 94, which remained blocked until early Sunday morning. Police Chief Todd Axtell, visibly shaken, showed a bent badge as evidence of the assault and warned that such attacks on public servants would not be tolerated. While some participants, like 20‑year‑old Misty Macon, claimed the protest was largely peaceful, eyewitnesses recounted pepper‑spray, firework attacks and a general refusal to heed police orders. The unrest followed nationwide demonstrations over the police killings of Philando Castile and Alton Sterling, and came just days after the fatal shooting of five Dallas officers. Authorities emphasized the need for order and condemned the violent actions that endangered both officers and the community.  
END OF SUMMARY.
