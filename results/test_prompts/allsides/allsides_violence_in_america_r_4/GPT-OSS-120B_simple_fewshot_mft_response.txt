SUMMARY:
A “severe incident” erupted Saturday night in St. Paul when a march protesting the police killings of Philando Castile and Alton Sterling turned into a riot that assaulted law‑enforcement officers. Mayor Chris Coleman reported more than 100 arrests after demonstrators hurled rocks, rebar, fireworks and Molotov‑type devices at police, injuring 21 officers—injuries described as significant but not life‑threatening. Police chiefs emphasized that such violent attacks on public servants constitute an unacceptable subversion of legitimate authority, noting it was the worst level of aggression the officers had ever witnessed in their careers. At the same time, the article underscores the underlying care concern: the protests were sparked by outrage over the deaths of Black men at the hands of police, and families of the victims condemned the later violence against officers. Authorities used smoke bombs and a large police‑trooper response to restore order, reopening I‑94 after dawn. The piece frames the clash as a tension between protecting citizens from police harm and defending the authority of officers from unlawful aggression.

END OF SUMMARY.
