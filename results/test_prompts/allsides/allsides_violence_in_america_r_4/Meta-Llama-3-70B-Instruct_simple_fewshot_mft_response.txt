SUMMARY:

Protests in Minnesota turned violent on Saturday night, with over 100 arrests and 21 police officers injured. The protests, sparked by recent police killings of black men, began peacefully but escalated into rioting, with demonstrators pelting police officers with rocks, rebar, and incendiary devices. Authorities used smoke bombs to clear the crowd, and police in riot gear moved in to restore order. The violence was condemned by St. Paul Mayor Chris Coleman and Police Chief Todd Axtell, who expressed disgust at the attacks on law enforcement. The incident highlights the subversion of authority and the harm inflicted on police officers, who were simply doing their duty to maintain public safety. The protests, while initially peaceful, were hijacked by violent elements, undermining the legitimate concerns of those seeking justice and reform. The authorities' response was necessary to prevent further harm and restore order.

END OF SUMMARY.
