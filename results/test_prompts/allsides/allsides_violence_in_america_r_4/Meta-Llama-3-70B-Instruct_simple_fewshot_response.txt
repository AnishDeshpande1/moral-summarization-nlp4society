SUMMARY:

Protests in Minnesota turned violent on Saturday night, with over 100 arrests and 21 police officers injured. The protests, sparked by recent police killings of black men, began peacefully but escalated into rioting. Protesters pelted police officers with rocks, rebar, and incendiary devices, including Molotov cocktails. Police used smoke bombs to clear demonstrators blocking Interstate 94, and about 200 protesters refused to leave the roadway. The violence was condemned by St. Paul Mayor Chris Coleman and Police Chief Todd Axtell, who expressed disgust at the attacks on police officers. The protests were part of a nationwide movement following the deaths of Philando Castile and Alton Sterling, and came just days after five Dallas police officers were murdered in a sniper attack. Despite the violence, many protesters maintained that the movement was peaceful and aimed to bring attention to systemic racism and police brutality.

END OF SUMMARY.
