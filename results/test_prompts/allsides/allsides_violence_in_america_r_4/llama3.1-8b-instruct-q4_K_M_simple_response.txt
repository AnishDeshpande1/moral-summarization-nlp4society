SUMMARY:
Violence erupted in Minnesota Saturday night as protests over police killings turned into riots, resulting in 102 arrests and 21 injured police officers. Protesters threw rocks, rebar, and incendiary devices at police, including Molotov cocktails. The violence was condemned by local authorities, with St. Paul Mayor Chris Coleman calling it an "extreme incident." Police used smoke bombs to clear demonstrators blocking Interstate 94, but some protesters refused to leave. The riots followed the deaths of Philando Castile and Alton Sterling, two black men killed by police in recent days. Their families have denounced violence against law enforcement officers.

The moral framing presented in the original text highlights the condemnation of violent behavior towards law enforcement and the contrast between peaceful protests and destructive riots. The authorities' reactions emphasize their commitment to maintaining order and protecting public servants.
