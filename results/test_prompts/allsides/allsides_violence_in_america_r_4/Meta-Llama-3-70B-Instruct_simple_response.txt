SUMMARY:

Protests in Minnesota turned violent on Saturday night, resulting in 102 arrests and 21 injured police officers. The protests, sparked by the recent police killings of black men Philando Castile and Alton Sterling, began peacefully but escalated into rioting. Protesters pelted police officers with rocks, rebar, and incendiary devices, including Molotov cocktails. The police responded with smoke bombs and riot gear, and eventually cleared the demonstrators from Interstate 94. The violence was condemned by the families of Castile and Sterling, as well as by local authorities. The police chief expressed disgust and disappointment at the level of violence directed towards public servants. The incident marks a disturbing escalation of tensions between law enforcement and the communities they serve.

END OF SUMMARY.
