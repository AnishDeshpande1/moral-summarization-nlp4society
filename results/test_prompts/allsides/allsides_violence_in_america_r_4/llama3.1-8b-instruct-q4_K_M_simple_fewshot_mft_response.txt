SUMMARY:

Protests over the recent police killings of black men turned violent in Minnesota, resulting in over 100 arrests and 21 police officers injured. The protests started as peaceful marches but escalated into rioting, with protesters pelted police officers with rocks, rebar, and incendiary devices. The St. Paul Police Chief described the incident as an "extreme" one, while the Minnesota Governor expressed concern for the safety of both protesters and law enforcement. Despite efforts to de-escalate the situation, some protesters refused to leave the area, leading to confrontations with police. The violence has sparked debate about the legitimacy of the protests and the role of law enforcement in addressing social justice issues.

The moral framing of this article highlights the Authority/Subversion dimension, as it emphasizes the need for order and authority during times of protest and civil unrest. It also touches on the Care/Harm dimension, as it notes the harm caused to both protesters and police officers during the violence. The author's tone is critical of those who engage in violent behavior, while also acknowledging the underlying social justice concerns that sparked the protests.
