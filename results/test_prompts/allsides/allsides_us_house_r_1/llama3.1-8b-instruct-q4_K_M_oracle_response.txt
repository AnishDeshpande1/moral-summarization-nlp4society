SUMMARY:

The House Judiciary Committee, led by Chairman Jerrold L. Nadler, is proceeding with articles of impeachment against President Trump, citing his abuse of power for personal gain. Nadler argued that Trump put himself before the country, using his public office to advance private interests. The committee will focus on charges of abuse of power, bribery, and obstructing the congressional probe, all stemming from Trump's request for Ukraine to investigate Joe Biden and his son Hunter.

Trump has denied wrongdoing and labeled the impeachment drive a partisan "sham." However, Democrats argue that Trump's actions represent corruption at its core. They point to the president's withholding of $391 million in military aid as leverage, a clear example of using power for personal gain.

The Judiciary Committee is reviewing evidence gathered by an inquiry led by the Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence. The quid pro quo – a transaction of "this for that" – is at the heart of Democrats' case against Trump, who they accuse of engaging in a bribery or extortion scheme that warrants impeachment. END OF SUMMARY.
