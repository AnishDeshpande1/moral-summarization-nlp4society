SUMMARY:  
House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold L. Nadler told a hearing that President Trump “used” the presidency for private “gain,” putting himself before the country and violating the oath to serve the nation. He called the impeachment effort a “solemn and serious” response to threats to America’s institutions, condemning the president’s alleged “sham” of a foreign‑policy “shadow” campaign. The committee will draft articles of impeachment on abuse of power, bribery and obstruction, centered on Trump’s July 25 call to Ukraine’s Zelensky‑y asking for investigations into the Bidens and a missing DNC server—a theory the president “subscribes” to despite lacking evidence. Republicans argue the actions were unconventional but not criminal, pointing to Hunter Biden’s “corruption”‑laden Burisma job and the delayed $391 million aid. Witnesses, including Ambassador Gordon Sondland, described a possible quid‑pro‑quo, though Trump denied any such deal. The evidence gathered will determine whether the president truly put personal interests ahead of the country.  

END OF SUMMARY.
