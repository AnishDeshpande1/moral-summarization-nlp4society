SUMMARY:

House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold Nadler has outlined the Democrats' impeachment case against President Trump, arguing that the president put his own interests above the country's. Nadler stated that no public official, including the president, should use their office for personal gain. The committee will proceed to write articles of impeachment against Trump, citing evidence of abuse of power, bribery, and obstructing the congressional probe. The impeachment case stems from a July 25 phone call in which Trump asked Ukraine's president to investigate political rival Joe Biden and his son Hunter. Republicans argue that Trump's actions, though unconventional, did not cross the line into criminality. The Judiciary Committee is reviewing evidence gathered by the Intelligence Committee, including testimony from Gordon Sondland, who said he offered a quid pro quo of a White House visit for the investigations.

END OF SUMMARY.
