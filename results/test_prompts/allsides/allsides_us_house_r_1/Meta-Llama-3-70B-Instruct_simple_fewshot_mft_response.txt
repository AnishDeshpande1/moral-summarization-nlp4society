SUMMARY:

House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold Nadler argued that President Trump put himself before the country, abusing his power for personal gain. Nadler emphasized that no public official, especially the President, should use their office for private benefit. The impeachment case against Trump stems from his request to Ukraine to investigate political rival Joe Biden and his son Hunter, which Democrats argue is a clear example of abuse of power, bribery, and obstructing the congressional probe. Republicans, on the other hand, argue that Trump's actions, though unconventional, did not cross the line into criminality. The moral framing of this article suggests that Trump's actions subvert the authority of the presidency, cheat the country of its rightful leadership, and betray the trust of the American people. Nadler's committee will proceed to write articles of impeachment, emphasizing the seriousness of the threat to America's institutions, such as free and fair elections.

END OF SUMMARY.
