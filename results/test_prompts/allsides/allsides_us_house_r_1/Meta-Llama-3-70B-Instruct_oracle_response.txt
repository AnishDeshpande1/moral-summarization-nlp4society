SUMMARY:

House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold Nadler emphasized that President Trump put himself before the country, using his public office for private gain. Nadler argued that the president's actions were a serious threat to America's institutions, particularly "free and fair elections." Trump has denied wrongdoing, calling the impeachment drive a partisan "sham." However, Nadler maintained that the Constitution and the president's oath of office require him to put the country first, not engage in corruption for personal benefit.

The impeachment case revolves around Trump's July 25 phone call with Ukraine President Volodymyr Zelensky, where he pressed for an investigation into political rival Joseph R. Biden and his son Hunter. Democrats argue that Trump engaged in a bribery or extortion scheme, withholding $391 million in military aid as leverage. Republicans claim Trump's actions, though unconventional, did not cross the line into criminality.

The Judiciary Committee is reviewing evidence gathered by the Intelligence Committee, including testimony from Ambassador Gordon Sondland, who offered a quid pro quo of a White House visit for the investigations. Trump has acknowledged wanting an investigation into alleged corruption involving the Bidens, but his motives are disputed.

END OF SUMMARY.
