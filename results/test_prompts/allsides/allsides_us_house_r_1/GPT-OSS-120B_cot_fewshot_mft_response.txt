**SUMMARY:**  
House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold L. Nadler argued that President Trump placed himself before the country, violating the oath and public trust. He framed the impeachment inquiry as a “solemn and serious” effort to protect America’s institutions, free and fair elections, and to stop abuse of power, bribery, and obstruction. Nadler said no public official should use office for private gain and urged Republicans to remove their blinders and see the betrayal of loyalty to the nation. Democrats presented evidence of a quid‑pro quo scheme, personal gain, and leverage over Ukraine, labeling the president’s actions as an extortion‑type abuse and a sham. Republicans countered that the conduct was unconventional, not criminal, and defended the legitimacy of investigating the Bidens. The hearing highlighted the clash over authority, fairness, and loyalty in America’s highest office. END OF SUMMARY.
