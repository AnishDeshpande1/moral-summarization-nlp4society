SUMMARY: House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold L. Nadler opened an evidentiary hearing emphasizing that President Trump used his office for personal gain, urging Congress to draft articles of impeachment. Nadler described the process as a “solemn and serious undertaking” to protect democratic institutions, accusing Trump of putting himself before the country. The anticipated articles will charge abuse of power, bribery, and obstruction, stemming from Trump’s July 25 call to Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky, in which he allegedly pressed for investigations into political rival Joe Biden and his son Hunter. A whistleblower alleged Trump withheld $391 million in military aid as leverage. Democrats argue this constitutes a quid‑pro‑quo bribery scheme; Republicans claim the actions were unconventional but not criminal. Key testimony includes EU ambassador Gordon Sondland, who described a proposed White House visit as a possible quid‑pro‑quo, though Trump later denied any such deal. No witness linked the aid delay directly to the investigations. Trump denies wrongdoing, calling the impeachment a partisan sham, while defending his interest in probing alleged corruption involving the Bidens and a missing DNC server. The committee will now draft impeachment articles. END OF SUMMARY.
