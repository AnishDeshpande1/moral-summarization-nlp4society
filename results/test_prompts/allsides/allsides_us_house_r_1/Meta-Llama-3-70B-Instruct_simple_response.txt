SUMMARY:

House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold Nadler has made a strong case for impeaching President Trump, arguing that he prioritized his personal interests over the country's well-being. Nadler emphasized that no public official, especially the President, should use their office for private gain. The evidence presented suggests that Trump abused his power, engaged in bribery, and obstructed the congressional probe by pressuring Ukraine to investigate his political rival, Joe Biden, and his son Hunter. Republicans argue that Trump's actions, though unconventional, did not cross the line into criminality. However, Democrats contend that Trump's actions constituted a "shadow" foreign policy, with Trump's lawyer, Rudy Giuliani, exerting undue influence. The Judiciary Committee will proceed to write articles of impeachment, with Nadler calling the process a "solemn and serious undertaking" to protect America's institutions, including free and fair elections.

END OF SUMMARY.
