SUMMARY:
House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold L. Nadler warned that President Trump has placed his personal interests above the nation, violating the oath to put the country first. He framed the impeachment inquiry as a “solemn and serious undertaking” to protect America’s institutions, especially free elections, and urged Republicans to remove their “blinders.” The Democrats plan to draft articles of impeachment on abuse of power, bribery, and obstruction, centered on Trump’s July 25 call to Ukraine’s president asking for a political investigation into the Bidens while withholding military aid. A whistle‑blower’s allegation of a quid‑pro‑quo, testimony from Ambassador Gordon Sondland, and evidence gathered by the Intelligence Committee are presented as proof of Trump’s personal gain and shadow foreign policy. Republicans argue the actions were unconventional but not criminal and point to Hunter Biden’s lucrative Burisma role. The moral framing portrays Trump’s conduct as a dangerous abuse of presidential power that threatens democratic norms and justifies impeachment.

END OF SUMMARY.
