SUMMARY:

House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold Nadler argued that President Trump put himself before the country, using his public office for private gain. Nadler stated that the Constitution and Trump's oath of office require the president to put the country first. The impeachment case against Trump stems from his request to Ukraine to investigate political rival Joe Biden and his son Hunter, which Democrats argue is an abuse of power, bribery, and obstruction of the congressional probe. Republicans argue that Trump's action, though unconventional, did not cross the line into criminality. The Judiciary Committee is reviewing evidence gathered by the Intelligence Committee, including testimony from Ambassador Gordon Sondland, who said he offered a quid pro quo of a White House visit for the investigations. Trump has denied wrongdoing, calling the impeachment drive a partisan "sham." The moral framing of this article suggests that Trump's actions are a threat to America's institutions, particularly free and fair elections, and that his presidency is marked by a prioritization of personal gain over the country's interests.

END OF SUMMARY.
