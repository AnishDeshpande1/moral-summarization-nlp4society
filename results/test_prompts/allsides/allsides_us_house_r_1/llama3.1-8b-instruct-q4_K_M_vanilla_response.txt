SUMMARY:

House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold L. Nadler argued that President Trump put himself before the country, using his public office for private gain. Based on evidence presented, the committee will proceed to write articles of impeachment against Mr. Trump. The articles are expected to include charges of abuse of power, bribery, and obstructing the congressional probe, all stemming from Mr. Trump's request for Ukraine to investigate Joe Biden and his son Hunter.

Mr. Nadler stated that no public official should use their office for private gain, and that the Constitution requires the president to put the country first. Republicans argue that Mr. Trump's actions were unconventional but did not cross the line into criminality. The impeachment case centers around a July 25 phone call in which Mr. Trump pressed Ukraine President Volodymyr Zelensky for investigations into the Bidens and alleged Ukrainian meddling in the 2016 election.

The committee is reviewing evidence gathered by an inquiry led by the Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence, including testimony from Gordon Sondland, who said he offered a quid pro quo of a White House visit for the investigations.
