SUMMARY:

House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold L. Nadler argues that President Trump put himself before the country, using his public office for private gain. The committee will proceed to write articles of impeachment against Mr. Trump, citing evidence of abuse of power, bribery, and obstruction of the congressional probe. Mr. Trump has denied wrongdoing, calling the House impeachment drive a partisan "sham." Republicans argue that Mr. Trump's actions did not cross the line into criminality.

The case stems from a July 25 phone call in which Mr. Trump pressed Ukraine President Volodymyr Zelensky for investigations into the Bidens and Ukraine meddling in the 2016 election, allegedly withholding $391 million of U.S. military aid as leverage. The Democrats' quid pro quo argument suggests that Mr. Trump engaged in a bribery or extortion scheme.

Mr. Nadler emphasized that no public official should use their office for personal gain, citing the Constitution and the president's oath to put the country first. END OF SUMMARY.
