SUMMARY:  
House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold L. Nadler framed the impeachment effort as a moral imperative, insisting that a president must “put the country first” and never use public office for private gain. Citing the July 25 call with Ukrainian President Zelensky, Nadler argued that President Trump leveraged U.S. aid and threatened to withhold $391 million to force a “favor”—an investigation into the Bidens—constituting abuse of power, bribery, and obstruction. He called the proceedings a “solemn and serious undertaking” to protect America’s institutions, especially free and fair elections. Democrats contend the evidence, including testimony from EU ambassador Gordon Sondland, shows a quid‑pro‑quo scheme, while Republicans claim the actions were unconventional but not criminal. Trump denies wrongdoing and calls the impeachment a partisan sham. The committee will now draft articles of impeachment based on the evidence gathered.  

END OF SUMMARY.
