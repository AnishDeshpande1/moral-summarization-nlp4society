SUMMARY:
House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold L. Nadler framed the impeachment effort as a defense of constitutional authority and national fairness, accusing President Trump of subverting his oath by putting personal interests before the country. He warned that no public official should use office for private gain and called the inquiry a “solemn and serious undertaking” to protect free and fair elections. The Democratic case portrays Trump’s request to Ukraine for investigations into the Bidens as a cheating of democratic norms—an alleged quid‑pro‑quo that amounts to abuse of power and bribery. Republicans counter that the actions were unconventional but not criminal, insisting the focus on Hunter Biden’s Burisma role is legitimate. The narrative underscores loyalty and betrayal: Democrats argue Trump betrayed the nation’s institutions and the trust of the American people, while Republicans claim the impeachment is a partisan subversion of legitimate authority. The committee will now draft articles of impeachment on abuse of power, bribery and obstruction based on testimony from the intelligence probe and witnesses such as Ambassador Gordon Sondland.

END OF SUMMARY.
