SUMMARY:
An arbitrator granted Ray Rice’s appeal, overturning his indefinite NFL suspension and making him eligible to play again. The written decision serves as a damning indictment of the league’s handling of domestic violence, showing how the NFL initially gave Rice an easy, light punishment and then reversed course only after public outcry, claiming he had misled them. In reality, the league knew the gravity of the crime – Rice pummeling his then‑girlfriend Janay Palmer in an elevator, hitting her so hard she was knocked unconscious, dragging her, dropping her, and blocking the doors – yet failed to review the video evidence. The NFL’s defense that the incident was “just a slap” and that Palmer “knocked herself out” is pure bullshit and absurd, minimizing the force of the blow and the seriousness of the assault. The arbitrator found Goodell’s reasoning unreasonable; the league had access to the second video but chose blindness, disregarding the evidence. This failure raises terrifying questions about future assaults: will the NFL ignore or minimize evidence, or finally take domestic violence seriously? END OF SUMMARY.
