The NFL's defense arguments reveal a disturbing lack of understanding and response to domestic violence. They claimed Rice misled them about the assault, but the arbitrator saw through this excuse. The NFL's failure to take the assault seriously and their willful blindness to the evidence are alarming. The league's arguments, such as suggesting that a "slap" is morally superior to a "hit," are absurd and demonstrate a callous disregard for the seriousness of domestic violence. The decision raises questions about the NFL's ability to respond appropriately to future incidents of domestic violence. Will they review all available evidence and understand the seriousness of the situation, or will they continue to minimize and ignore it?
