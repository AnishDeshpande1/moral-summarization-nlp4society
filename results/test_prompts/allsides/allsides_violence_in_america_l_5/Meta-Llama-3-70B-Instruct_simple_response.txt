SUMMARY:

An arbitrator has overturned Ray Rice's indefinite suspension, allowing him to play in the NFL again. However, the decision is a scathing critique of the NFL's handling of domestic violence. The league initially gave Rice a light punishment, then increased it after public outcry, but the arbitrator found that the NFL was aware of the severity of the assault all along and simply didn't think it was worthy of serious punishment. The NFL's defense arguments revealed a disturbing lack of understanding and seriousness about domestic violence. They claimed Rice misled them about the assault, but the arbitrator saw through this excuse. The NFL's actions suggest a callous disregard for the seriousness of domestic violence and a willingness to ignore evidence. The league's handling of this case raises serious concerns about how they will respond to future incidents of domestic violence. The NFL still has much to learn about taking domestic violence seriously.

END OF SUMMARY.
