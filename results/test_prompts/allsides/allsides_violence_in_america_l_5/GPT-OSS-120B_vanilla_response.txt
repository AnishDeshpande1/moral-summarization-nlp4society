SUMMARY: An arbitrator has overturned the NFL’s indefinite suspension of former Baltimore Ravens running back Ray Rice, reinstating his eligibility to play. The arbitrator ruled that Commissioner Roger Goodell violated the collective bargaining agreement by imposing a second, harsher punishment after already knowing the facts of the 2014 domestic‑violence incident. Evidence shows the league was aware of the assault video well before it went public, yet relied on Rice’s limited description and failed to review the footage, reflecting a broader reluctance to treat spousal abuse seriously. The decision highlights systemic issues in the NFL’s handling of domestic‑violence cases, questioning whether the league will adequately investigate and punish future incidents. Critics argue the NFL’s defense—that Rice misled them—was disingenuous, and the arbitrator’s finding underscores lingering attitudes that downplay violent assaults against women. The case leaves Rice with only a modest penalty and exposes the league’s need to improve its response to domestic‑violence allegations. END OF SUMMARY.
