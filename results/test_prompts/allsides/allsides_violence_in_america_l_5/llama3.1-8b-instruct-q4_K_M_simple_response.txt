SUMMARY:

Ray Rice, former Baltimore Ravens player, had his indefinite suspension overturned by an arbitrator, allowing him to return to the NFL. This decision is seen as a damning indictment of the league's handling of domestic violence cases. The initial two-game suspension and fine were later increased to an indefinite ban after a second video of the assault was released. However, the arbitrator found that Commissioner Roger Goodell had already been aware of the facts at the time of the initial punishment and therefore could not impose a harsher penalty.

The decision raises concerns about the NFL's ability to understand and respond to domestic violence. The league's defense arguments suggest that they still don't take the assault seriously enough, with claims that Rice "slapped" his girlfriend rather than hit her. This dismissive attitude towards domestic abuse is worrying, especially given Goodell's failure to watch the second video of the incident despite having access to it.

END OF SUMMARY.
