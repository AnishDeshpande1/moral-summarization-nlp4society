SUMMARY:
An arbitrator overturned Ray Rice’s indefinite NFL suspension, allowing him to return to the league and exposing the NFL’s inadequate response to domestic violence. The league initially imposed a light two‑game ban, then doubled it after a second video surfaced, claiming Rice had misled officials. The arbitrator found Commissioner Goodell already knew the facts when the first sanction was issued, making the later punishment a violation of the collective‑bargaining agreement. The decision highlights the NFL’s willful blindness: officials had access to the second video but never reviewed it, relying on Rice’s down‑playing of the assault as a “slap.” This reflects a deeper problem—league leadership still treats severe spousal violence as a minor infraction, minimizing the victim’s injury and excusing the perpetrator. The ruling underscores the NFL’s failure to understand or take domestic abuse seriously, raising concerns about how future incidents will be handled if evidence is ignored or rationalized away. The case calls for a fundamental shift in the league’s attitudes and policies toward domestic violence. 
END OF SUMMARY.
