SUMMARY:

An arbitrator has granted Ray Rice's appeal of his indefinite suspension, making him eligible to play in the NFL again. The decision is a damning indictment of the NFL's handling of domestic violence. The league initially gave Rice a light punishment, then reversed itself after public outcry, but the arbitrator found that the NFL was well-informed about the incident all along and simply didn't think pummeling a woman was worthy of serious punishment. The NFL claimed Rice misled them about the nature of the assault, but the arbitrator rejected this argument, finding that the league didn't take the assault seriously enough. The NFL's defense arguments suggest that they still don't understand or take domestic violence seriously, and the league's failure to review all available evidence raises questions about their approach to handling such incidents in the future.

END OF SUMMARY.
