SUMMARY:

Ray Rice, a former Baltimore Ravens player, had his indefinite suspension lifted by an arbitrator due to the NFL's handling of domestic violence cases. The arbitrator found that Commissioner Roger Goodell was aware of the facts of the assault at the time of the initial 2-game ban and therefore couldn't impose a harsher punishment. This decision raises concerns about the NFL's understanding and response to domestic violence. The league initially punished Rice lightly, but revised its punishment after a public outcry when more video evidence surfaced. The arbitrator criticized the NFL for claiming that Rice misled them by downplaying the severity of the assault.

The case highlights the NFL's inconsistent approach to domestic violence, with some arguing that the league still doesn't take it seriously enough. Goodell and the team chose not to review additional footage, despite having access to it, suggesting a "willful blindness" to the assault's seriousness. The decision raises questions about what will happen in future cases of NFL players assaulting their romantic partners.
