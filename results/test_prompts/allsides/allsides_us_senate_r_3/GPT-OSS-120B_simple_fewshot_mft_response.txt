The author condemns Senate Democrats for subverting long‑standing authority and procedural norms by invoking the “nuclear option” to weaken the filibuster, arguing that this reckless breach of institutional authority harms the nation’s system of checks and balances. By recalling Sen. The narrative stresses that the abandonment of the filibuster caused real harm: it destabilized the Senate’s role as a moderating force, jeopardized careful deliberation, and set a dangerous precedent that fuels an arms‑race of procedural retaliation. The author laments that progressives’ contempt for the filibuster—and for federalism more broadly—reflects a willingness to cheat the democratic bargain, sacrificing long‑term institutional health for immediate partisan gain. Ultimately, the piece warns that this erosion of authority and fairness will continue to harm the Republic unless the entrenched norms are restored.
