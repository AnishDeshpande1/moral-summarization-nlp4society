SUMMARY:
The author revels in the fallout from the “nuclear option” that Senate Democrats used to gut the filibuster, viewing it as deserved schadenfreude. He argues that Democrats like Harry Reid, Chris Coons and Jeff Merkley sacrificed long‑standing norms for short‑term partisan gain, only to empower future presidents—whether Trump, Romney or any moderate Republican—to push through their nominees unchecked. The piece warns that this reckless abandonment of tradition has erased a vital “speed‑bump” that once protected the balance of power, and that the damage is likely irreversible because future majorities will have no incentive to restore the filibuster. By highlighting past liberal attacks on the filibuster and the success of Obama’s agenda despite the “myth” of gridlock, the author frames the Democrats’ rule‑changing as a moral failure: they chose power over principle, and now the nation must bear the consequences of their short‑sightedness. The overarching moral is that dismantling institutional safeguards for partisan advantage invites an endless arms race of procedural abuse, endangering the very checks and balances the Founders intended.

END OF SUMMARY.
