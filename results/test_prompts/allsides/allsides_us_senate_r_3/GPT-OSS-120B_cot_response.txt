**SUMMARY:**  
The author revels in schadenfreude over Senate Democrats’ regret for the “nuclear option” that weakened the fililbuster, noting their own recklessness now enables President‑elect Trump to push nominees with a simple majority. While Democrats claimed they were protecting checks and balances, the piece argues they pursued short‑term power grabs and majoritarianism, destroying longstanding norms and process. Figures such as Sen. Chris Coons and Harry Reid are portrayed as abandoning the Founding Fathers’ safeguards, trading stability for partisan gain. The author warns that this arms‑race of procedural abuse creates dangerous precedents, erodes federalism, and invites future parties to reverse any remaining protections. Ultimately, the loss of tradition and courageous restraint threatens the Senate’s role as a moderating force in American governance.
