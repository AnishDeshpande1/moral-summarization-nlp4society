SUMMARY:

The article critiques Senate Democrats who regret their decision to weaken the filibuster, allowing President-elect Donald Trump to easily appoint his Cabinet and judges. Democrats, such as Sen. Chris Coons, now lament the loss of a "speed bump" to slow down nominees, but the author argues that they only regret their decision because it will benefit Trump, not because they care about the integrity of the process. The author frames this as a betrayal of the Founding Fathers' vision of checks and balances, which Democrats like Sen. Harry Reid once revered but abandoned when it suited their partisan interests. The article also highlights the hypocrisy of liberal pundits like Paul Krugman, who previously claimed the filibuster would "destroy America" but now remain silent as Democrats reap the consequences of their actions. The author's moral framing emphasizes the importance of authority and process, the harm caused by reckless power plays, and the unfairness of exploiting rules for short-term gains.
