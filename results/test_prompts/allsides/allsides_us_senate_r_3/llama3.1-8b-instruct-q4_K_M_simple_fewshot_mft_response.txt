SUMMARY:

A recent CNN piece titled "Senate Dems, powerless to stop Trump nominees, regret 'nuclear option' power play" has sparked schadenfreude among some observers. Senate Democrats who previously weakened the filibuster now regret their actions as President-elect Donald Trump names his Cabinet and judges. Sen. Chris Coons expressed regret for voting to weaken the filibuster, stating that it would have been a "terrific speed bump" to slow down nominees. However, Democrats did not use the parliamentary procedure to change the rules for ideological purposes, but rather for short-term political gains.

The author argues that Democrats' actions were a betrayal of the principles of checks and balances, and that their contempt for federalism and the filibuster is enduring. The article highlights the hypocrisy of Democrats who now regret their decision, while others are trying to figure out ways to work Senate procedure to put Chief Judge Merrick Garland on the Supreme Court.

The moral framing of this issue is centered around the dimensions of Authority/Subversion (the undermining of institutional checks and balances), Care/Harm (the harm caused by weakening the filibuster), and Fairness/Cheating (the unfair use of parliamentary procedures for partisan gain). The author suggests that the nation should recognize the dangers of this precedent.

END OF SUMMARY.
