SUMMARY:

Some Senate Democrats are expressing regret over their decision to weaken the filibuster, which allowed President-elect Donald Trump to easily confirm his Cabinet nominees and judges. In 2013, they voted to change the rules to allow a simple majority vote on certain nominations, citing short-term political gains. However, now that Republicans are in power, these Democrats are lamenting the loss of the filibuster as a "speed bump" to slow down nominations.

The author argues that this shift in sentiment demonstrates how recklessness begets reckoning in Washington, D.C. Democrats had previously claimed that weakening the filibuster would allow them to function more effectively, but Obama's administration was able to pass major legislation and appoint liberal justices without it. The author suggests that this change sets a dangerous precedent for an arms race of partisan power grabs.

END OF SUMMARY.
