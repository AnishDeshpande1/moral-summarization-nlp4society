SUMMARY:
The author revels in the fallout from the Senate’s “nuclear option” that stripped the filibuster, viewing it as deserved schadenfreude for Democrats who once championed the change for short‑term partisan advantage. He argues that Senators like Chris Coons and Harry Reid now regret weakening a key procedural safeguard, not because they value democratic norms, but because it empowers a Trump (or any conservative) administration to confirm nominees with a simple majority. The piece frames the Democrats’ 2013‑14 rule‑busting as reckless self‑interest that eroded the Founders’ checks‑and‑balances, warning that such majoritarianism creates a dangerous arms race of procedural abuse. By abandoning tradition, the party sacrificed long‑term stability for fleeting power, and the author warns that restoring the filibuster is unlikely once the precedent is set. The moral judgment is clear: the Democrats’ willingness to discard institutional norms for political gain has backfired, endangering the very balance of government they once vowed to protect.  

END OF SUMMARY.
