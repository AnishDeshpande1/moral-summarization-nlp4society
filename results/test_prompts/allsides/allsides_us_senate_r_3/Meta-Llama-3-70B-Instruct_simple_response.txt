SUMMARY:

The article discusses how Senate Democrats, who previously weakened the filibuster to advance Obama's nominees, now regret their decision as it allows Trump to easily confirm his Cabinet and judges. Democrats like Sen. Chris Coons and Sen. Jeff Merkley, who voted to weaken the filibuster, now realize that it would have been a valuable "speed bump" to slow down Trump's nominees. The article argues that Democrats didn't make this change for ideological reasons, but for short-term political gains. It criticizes liberal pundits like Paul Krugman, who claimed that the filibuster would destroy America, and notes that Obama was able to pass significant legislation and appoint liberal Supreme Court justices without the filibuster. The article concludes that the changes will likely never be reversed and that the "recklessness" of Democrats has led to a dangerous precedent.

END OF SUMMARY.
