SUMMARY:

Some Democrats are expressing regret over their decision to weaken the filibuster in 2013, which allowed President Trump's nominees to be confirmed with a simple majority. Sen. Chris Coons said he regrets not having the "speed bump" of the filibuster to slow down nominees. However, it seems that these Democrats only regret the outcome when it benefits their opponents, and are willing to use similar tactics themselves when in power.

The author argues that this is an example of recklessness begetting recklessness in Washington D.C., where politicians prioritize short-term gains over long-term consequences. The filibuster was weakened for political gain, and now Democrats are lamenting the loss of a tool that could have slowed down Trump's nominees. The article suggests that this arms race will continue, with each party trying to outmaneuver the other in terms of nominations and confirmations.

END OF SUMMARY.
