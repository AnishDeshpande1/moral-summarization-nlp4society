SUMMARY:  
The author reflects on a CNN piece about Senate Democrats regretting the 2013 “nuclear option” that weakened the filibuster, arguing that their short‑term political gain now enables President‑elect Donald Trump to confirm nominees with a simple majority. He cites Senator Chris Coons’ admission of regret, noting that Democrats originally supported the change to advance Obama‑era appointments, not to undermine checks and balances. The piece criticizes liberal commentators like Jeff Merkley and Paul Krugman for exaggerating the filibuster’s threat, pointing out Obama’s major legislative achievements and unopposed Supreme Court appointments. It argues that once in power, Democrats like Harry Reid abandoned their earlier warnings about eroding constitutional safeguards, using the nuclear option to bypass the filibuster. The author warns that these procedural changes are unlikely to be reversed and may trigger a partisan “arms race” over Senate rules, threatening long‑standing norms and stability. He calls for recognition of the dangers of abandoning institutional checks in favor of partisan advantage.  
END OF SUMMARY.
