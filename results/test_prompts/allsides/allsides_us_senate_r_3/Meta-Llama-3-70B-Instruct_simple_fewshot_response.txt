SUMMARY:

Senate Democrats are now regretting their decision to weaken the filibuster three years ago, as it has allowed President-elect Donald Trump to easily nominate his Cabinet and judges. Democrats like Sen. Chris Coons of Delaware are lamenting that they didn't have a "terrific speed bump" to slow down Trump's nominees. However, they don't regret their vote for majoritarianism or power grabs, but rather that Trump will now be able to operate under the rules they set for themselves. The article argues that Democrats didn't use a parliamentary procedure to change the rules for some grand ideological purpose, but rather for short-term political gains. It also notes that liberal pundits and politicians have consistently shown contempt for federalism and the filibuster, and that this kind of arms race sets dangerous precedents. The moral framing of this issue is clear: the Democrats' recklessness and pursuit of power have led to a situation where they are now powerless to stop Trump's nominees.

END OF SUMMARY.
