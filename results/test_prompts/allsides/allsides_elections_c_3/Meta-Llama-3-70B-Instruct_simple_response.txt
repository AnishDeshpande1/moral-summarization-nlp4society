SUMMARY:

The unprecedented cyberattacks during this campaign cycle have already eroded trust in American democracy, with 38% of Americans concerned that the election could be hacked and 18% very concerned. These fears have led 1 in 5 respondents to consider not voting, potentially affecting 15 million people. The Obama administration has blamed Russia for the hacks, but even if voting booths aren't compromised, the internet campaign to spread fear may be enough to discredit the vote. Experts warn that mere claims of hacking could undermine the legitimacy of the election, and a disinformation campaign could be more effective than directly attacking election infrastructure. State officials are on high alert, but the mere threat of hacking has already had a profound impact on American democracy, undermining trust and potentially affecting voter turnout.

END OF SUMMARY.
