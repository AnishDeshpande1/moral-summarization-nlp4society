SUMMARY:

The recent cyberattacks on political organizations and warnings of possible Election Day digital fraud have already had a profound impact on American democracy. A survey found that 38% of Americans are concerned that the election could be hacked, leading 1 in 5 respondents to consider not voting. The Obama administration has blamed Russia for the hacks, and despite election and cybersecurity experts dismissing claims of Election Day tampering, the internet campaign to spread fear of vote hacking may be enough to undermine public trust. Even if hackers can't compromise voting systems, a credible claim of doing so could compel voters to cry foul and undermine the legitimacy of the vote. State officials are on high alert for any signs of tampering, and experts warn that mere reports of possible Election Day hacking could fuel post-election challenges to the results.

END OF SUMMARY.
