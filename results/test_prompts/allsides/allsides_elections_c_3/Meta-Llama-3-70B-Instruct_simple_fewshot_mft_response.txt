SUMMARY:

The unprecedented cyberattacks during this campaign cycle have already had a profound impact on American democracy, eroding trust in the electoral process. The Obama administration's accusation that senior Russian officials orchestrated the breach of the Democratic National Committee has fueled fears of digital sabotage, with 38% of Americans concerned that the election could be hacked. This has led 1 in 5 respondents to consider staying home on Election Day, potentially disenfranchising 15 million people. The hacking campaign's goal is not only to compromise voting systems but also to spread fear and undermine trust in the election. Even if hackers don't actually tamper with voting booths, the internet campaign to spread fear of vote hacking and manipulation may be enough to discredit the vote. The attack on authority and the perceived unfairness of the electoral process have already had a significant impact on public trust, potentially leading to post-election challenges to the results.

END OF SUMMARY.
