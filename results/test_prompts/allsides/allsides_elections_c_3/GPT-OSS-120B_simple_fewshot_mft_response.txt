SUMMARY:
A wave of cyber‑attacks and public warnings about possible Election‑Day hacking has already eroded confidence in American democracy. A survey by Carbon Black shows that 56 % of Americans are concerned or very concerned that the vote could be compromised, and 20 % say they might stay home—potentially 15 million voters—because of fear of digital fraud. The Obama administration, citing the DNC breach claimed by “Guccifer 2.0,” has accused senior Russian officials of orchestrating a disinformation campaign designed to undermine trust in the electoral process, a claim that authorities present as a serious subversive threat. Officials from DHS and state election boards have been mobilized to guard voting infrastructure, emphasizing the need for authority‑driven protection of the system. Experts stress that even without actual machine tampering, the mere suggestion of cheating can delegitimize results, prompting challenges and appeals that would threaten the fairness of the election. Thus, the article frames the cyber‑threat as both a subversive attack on legitimate authority and a potential cheating of the democratic process. END OF SUMMARY.
