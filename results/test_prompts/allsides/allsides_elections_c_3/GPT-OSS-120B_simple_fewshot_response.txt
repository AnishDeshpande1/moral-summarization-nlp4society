SUMMARY:
A wave of cyber‑attacks and relentless warnings about possible election‑day hacking have already shaken American democracy, even if no actual tampering occurs. A survey by Carbon Black shows 56 % of Americans are (very) concerned that the vote could be hacked, and 20 % say this fear might keep them from voting—potentially 15 million abstentions. The Obama administration blamed senior Russian officials for the DNC breach and for a broader disinformation effort aimed at eroding trust in the election. Hacker “Guccifer 2.0” has revived threats to monitor and expose alleged fraud, prompting experts to label the campaign a “last‑ditch effort” to influence or deter voters. Officials in all 50 states are on heightened alert, but experts stress that compromising the fragmented U.S. voting infrastructure would be extremely difficult; Russia’s likely goal is to spread doubt rather than directly alter results. The pervasive narrative of cyber‑threats risks fueling post‑election challenges, undermining confidence in the democratic process, and could even suppress turnout through fear.

END OF SUMMARY.
