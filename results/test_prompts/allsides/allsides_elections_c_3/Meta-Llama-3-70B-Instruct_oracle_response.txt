SUMMARY:

The unprecedented number of cyberattacks during this campaign cycle has already had a profound impact on American democracy. Despite no actual tampering, the fear of digital sabotage has led 1 in 5 respondents to consider not voting, potentially affecting 15 million people. The Obama administration has blamed Russia for orchestrating the breach, which aimed to sway American public opinion and undermine trust in the election. Guccifer 2.0, the unknown group behind the Democratic National Committee hack, has resurfaced, claiming to monitor the elections and hinting at more Election Day tampering. Experts believe this is a last-ditch effort to deter people from voting or sway the vote. The internet campaign to spread fear of vote hacking and manipulation may be enough to have a major impact on public trust. Even if voting booths aren't hacked, the mere claim of doing so could compel voters to cry foul and undermine the legitimacy of the vote.

END OF SUMMARY.
