SUMMARY:  
The Trump administration is weighing an executive order that would **bar** migrants – including those seeking **asylum** – from entering the United States at the southern border, citing **national security** concerns.  The proposal would **deny** asylum claims and give **broad** discretion to border officials, effectively **refusing** to **protect** people who can prove they are fleeing **persecution** or **torture**.  President Trump has **threatened** to cut **aid** to Guatemala, Honduras and El Salvador and has **sent** additional troops to **stop** the **crossing** of Central Americans, framing the **caravan** as a **disgraceful** and **cruel** crisis that he blames on Democrats.  Civil‑rights groups, including the National Immigration Law Center and the ACLU, call the plan a **moral failure**, **cruelty‑driven** and **undermining** the Constitution, and say they will **fight** any **ban** in court.  Legal scholars warn the measure will face intense **scrutiny** and **challenges**, though some predict courts might uphold it.  The administration also **threatens** to **instituting** a “Latin ban,” while Congress‑funded **anti‑corruption**, **good‑governance** **initiatives** for the region remain **appropriated** and under political **threat**.  
END OF SUMMARY.
