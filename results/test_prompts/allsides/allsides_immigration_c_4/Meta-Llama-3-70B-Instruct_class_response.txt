SUMMARY:

The Trump administration is considering an executive order to block migrants from entering the US at the southern border, effectively denying asylum claims based on national security concerns. This move would be a "racism-driven cruelty" and a "huge moral failure" according to critics. The order would give broad authority to border officials and would be met with legal challenges. The administration is also planning to send at least 800 additional troops to the border, which would be a "disgraceful" move, according to the ACLU. The troops would help with planning, engineering support, and providing protective equipment to border agents. The president has also threatened to cut off US aid to three Central American countries over the migrants heading towards the US. Critics argue that this move would be "stupid and cruel" and would undermine the Constitution and international laws.

END OF SUMMARY.
