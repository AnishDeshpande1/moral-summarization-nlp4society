SUMMARY:
The Trump administration is considering an order to block migrants at the southern border, using authority similar to its earlier travel ban. This would effectively bar asylum seekers from entering the country, citing national security concerns. Thousands of Central Americans are making their way toward the U.S., prompting President Trump to weigh options to prevent undocumented migration. The White House official confirmed that several remedies were on the table, including an executive order that would deny asylum claims based on national security concerns.

Critics have denounced this potential move as a "moral failure" and "racism-driven cruelty." The American Civil Liberties Union and National Immigration Law Center are prepared to fight against any new order, calling it a threat to international laws and the Constitution. Experts suggest that courts could uphold the president's actions, despite concerns about its policy implications. Meanwhile, Trump has threatened to cut off aid to Central American countries and deploy more troops to the border.

END OF SUMMARY.
