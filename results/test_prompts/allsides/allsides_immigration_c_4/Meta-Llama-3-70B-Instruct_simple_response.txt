SUMMARY:

The Trump administration is considering an executive order to block migrants from entering the US at the southern border, citing national security concerns. This move would effectively bar those seeking asylum from entering the country. The administration is also planning to send at least 800 additional troops to the border to support Customs and Border Protection agents. Critics have slammed the proposal, calling it "racism-driven cruelty" and a "moral failure" that would refuse to protect people fleeing persecution. The move is seen as a escalation of presidential power on immigration policy, and is likely to be met with legal challenges. The administration's actions have been criticized as "disgraceful" and "cruel", with some experts arguing that the restrictions make no sense from a policy perspective.
