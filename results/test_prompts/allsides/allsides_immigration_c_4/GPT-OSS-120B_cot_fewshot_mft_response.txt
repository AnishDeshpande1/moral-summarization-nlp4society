**SUMMARY:**  
The Trump administration is weighing an executive order that would block migrants—including asylum seekers—from entering the United States, using the same authority that created the 2017 travel ban. Officials say the plan would deny asylum claims on national‑security grounds and give broad power to border agents, a move critics label “racism‑driven cruelty” and “disgraceful.” Immigration‑rights groups warn it would be a “moral failure,” arguing that refusing protection to people who are fleeing persecution is both cruel and illegal. The president has blamed Democrats for the Central American caravan and threatened to cut aid to Guatemala, Honduras and El Salvador, framing the policy as a necessary response to a security threat. At the same time, Trump is sending additional troops to the border to support Customs and Border Protection, emphasizing the need to “protect” the nation. Legal challenges are expected, with advocates prepared to fight what they see as an unjust, subversive abuse of authority.
