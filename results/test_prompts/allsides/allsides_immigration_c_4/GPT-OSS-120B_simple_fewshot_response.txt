SUMMARY:
The Trump administration is weighing a sweeping executive order that would bar migrants—especially Central Americans seeking asylum—from entering the United States, using the same broad authority that powered the 2017 travel ban on several Muslim‑majority nations.  The plan would deny asylum claims on vague “national‑security” grounds, give border officials sweeping power, and be paired with a surge of troops and equipment to the southern border.  Officials say the move is a political gambit ahead of the November midterms, blaming Democrats for the migrant “caravan” and even threatening to cut U.S. aid to Guatemala, Honduras and El Salvador.  Critics, including the National Immigration Law Center and the ACLU, denounce the proposal as a “racism‑driven cruelty” and a “huge moral failure” that would violate the Constitution and international law.  While legal scholars warn the ban would be “stupid and cruel,” they note the Supreme Court’s recent willingness to uphold Trump’s travel restrictions, leaving the order’s fate uncertain but likely to face intense litigation.  
END OF SUMMARY.
