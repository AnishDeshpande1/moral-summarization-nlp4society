SUMMARY: The Trump administration is weighing a new executive order that would bar migrants, including asylum seekers, from entering the U.S. at the southern border by invoking national‑security grounds, similar to the 2017 travel ban. The move comes as thousands of Central American migrants travel northward and as Trump seeks to make immigration a key issue ahead of the November midterm elections. The White House is also planning to deploy at least 800 additional troops to support Border Patrol operations, joining existing National Guard forces. Trump has threatened to cut U.S. aid to Guatemala, Honduras and El Salvador in response to the migrant flow. Civil‑rights groups, including the National Immigration Law Center and the ACLU, say the proposal would violate constitutional and international law and vow legal challenges. Some legal scholars note the Supreme Court’s recent support for the original travel ban, suggesting the new order could survive judicial review despite widespread criticism that it is “cruel” and “policy‑wise nonsensical.”  

END OF SUMMARY.
