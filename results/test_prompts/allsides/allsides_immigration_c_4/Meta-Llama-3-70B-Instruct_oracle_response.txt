SUMMARY:

The Trump administration is considering an executive order to block migrants from entering the US at the southern border, effectively denying asylum to those seeking refuge. This move is seen as a "racism-driven cruelty" and a "moral failure" by critics, who argue it would be a "huge moral failure" to refuse protection to people fleeing persecution. The order would give broad authority to border officials and would be met with legal challenges. The administration is also planning to send additional troops to the border, which would include providing support for Customs and Border Protection agents. Trump has blamed Democrats for the migrant caravan and threatened to cut off aid to Central American countries. Critics have called the move "disgraceful" and "cruel", and have vowed to fight it in court. The administration's actions have been framed as an attempt to "undermine the Constitution and international laws" and to impose a "Latin ban".
