SUMMARY:

The Trump administration is considering an executive order to block migrants from entering the US at the southern border, effectively denying asylum claims based on national security concerns. This move would give broad authority to border officials and would be a significant escalation of presidential power on immigration policy. The plan is seen as a way to prevent undocumented migrants from crossing the border, with thousands of Central Americans making their way towards the US. Critics have denounced the move as "racism-driven cruelty" and a "moral failure" that would undermine the Constitution and international laws. Immigration advocates argue that the plan would be subject to intense legal scrutiny and would be a huge moral failure. However, some experts believe that courts could uphold the president's actions, citing the Supreme Court's previous decision to uphold Trump's travel ban. The move is seen as a way to appeal to Trump's base ahead of the midterm elections, with the president blaming Democrats for the migrant caravan.
