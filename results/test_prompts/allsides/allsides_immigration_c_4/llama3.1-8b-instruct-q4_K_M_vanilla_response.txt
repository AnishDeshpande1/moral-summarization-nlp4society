SUMMARY:

The Trump administration is considering an order to block migrants at the southern border, using authority similar to its earlier travel ban. This would effectively bar those seeking asylum from entering the country. President Trump is weighing options to prevent undocumented migrants from crossing the U.S.-Mexican frontier. The action could deny asylum claims based on national security concerns and give broad authority to officials at the border.

The move comes as thousands of Central Americans make their way toward the U.S., prompting Trump to blame Democrats for the "caravan" of migrants. He has also threatened to cut off aid to three Central American countries and is sending 800 additional troops to the border, joining 2,100 National Guard troops already deployed.

Critics have denounced the potential order as "racism-driven cruelty" and a "moral failure." The National Immigration Law Center and the American Civil Liberties Union have vowed to fight the move in court. Courts could potentially uphold the president's actions, but experts say it would be "stupid and cruel."

END OF SUMMARY.
