SUMMARY:
NFL quarterback Drew Brees was attacked by LGBTQ‑aligned critics after he promoted a modest “Bring Your Bible to School” program, urging children to express their Christian faith publicly. Brees, who frequently cites his faith as a moral compass, emphasized love, respect and acceptance in his Instagram response, denying any support for groups that discriminate or promote inequality. Critics on social media and outlets such as Deadspin labeled him a “bigot” and linked him to “gay‑conversion therapy,” framing his outreach as hateful and morally corrupt. The backlash was portrayed as a harmful assault on Brees’s right to share his religious convictions, portraying the accusers as unjustly cheating him of fair treatment and degrading the sanctity of sincere faith expression. Brees sought to restore the record, stressing that his message was simply about encouraging kids to bring their Bibles, not endorsing any discriminatory agenda, and called for the controversy to end so all parties could be treated with care, fairness, and respect.  
END OF SUMMARY.
