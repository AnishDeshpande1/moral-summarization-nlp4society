SUMMARY:
The San Francisco Board of Supervisors passed a resolution declaring the National Rifle Association (NRA) a "domestic terrorist organization" due to its opposition to gun control measures. The resolution connects the NRA to an "epidemic of gun violence" in the US, citing statistics on gun-related deaths and injuries. Supervisor Catherine Stefani, who sponsored the resolution, stated that the NRA's stance is "standing in the way of saving lives." The resolution urges San Francisco to distance itself from the NRA by examining local vendors' and contractors' ties to the organization. This move comes as national attention grows on the issue of gun violence following recent mass shootings. The NRA has condemned the resolution, calling it a "reckless assault" on the organization's members and freedoms. The designation is intended to pave the way for similar legislation at the local, state or federal level.

END OF SUMMARY.
