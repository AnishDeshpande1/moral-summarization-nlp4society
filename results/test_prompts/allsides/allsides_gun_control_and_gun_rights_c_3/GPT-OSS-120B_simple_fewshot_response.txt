SUMMARY:
San Francisco’s Board of Supervisors unanimously voted to label the National Rifle Association (NRA) a “domestic terrorist organization,” condemning the group’s propaganda and its obstruction of life‑saving gun‑control measures. The resolution, sponsored by Supervisor Catherine Stefani, ties the NRA to the nation’s “epidemic of gun violence,” noting that 100 Americans are killed daily and that the U.S. gun homicide rate far exceeds that of other wealthy nations. It calls for the city to audit local vendors and contractors for NRA ties and to refuse business with the NRA or affiliated entities, while urging other jurisdictions to adopt similar actions. The NRA blasted the measure as a “worthless…soundbite remedy” and an assault on law‑abiding citizens. The declaration comes amid renewed national focus on gun violence after recent mass shootings and ongoing debates over background‑check legislation, highlighting the moral urgency many see in confronting the NRA’s influence.  
END OF SUMMARY.
