SUMMARY: The San Francisco Board of Supervisors unanimously declared the National Rifle Association (NRA) a “domestic terrorist organization,” framing the designation as a moral condemnation of the group’s role in perpetuating an “epidemic of gun violence.” The resolution argues that the NRA spreads deceptive propaganda, blocks life‑saving gun‑control measures, and therefore endangers public safety. It calls for the city to audit and sever ties with any vendors or contractors linked to the NRA and to refuse future business with the organization or its affiliates, positioning this as a step toward broader local, state, or federal action. Supporters, led by Supervisor Catherine Stefani, portray the NRA as “absolutely disgraceful” and morally responsible for the daily loss of 100 American lives to gun violence—a rate 25 times higher than other high‑income nations. The NRA rebuked the resolution as a “worthless and disgusting ‘soundbite remedy’” and an assault on law‑abiding citizens’ freedoms. The move reflects heightened moral urgency amid recent mass shootings and ongoing national debates over gun control. END OF SUMMARY.
