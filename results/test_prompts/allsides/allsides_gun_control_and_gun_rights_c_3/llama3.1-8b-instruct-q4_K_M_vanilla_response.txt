SUMMARY:

The San Francisco Board of Supervisors passed a resolution declaring the National Rifle Association (NRA) a "domestic terrorist organization" due to its alleged role in perpetuating gun violence in the US. The resolution, which was approved unanimously, aims to distance San Francisco from the NRA by examining local vendors' and contractors' ties to the organization. It also urges the city to avoid doing business with the NRA or organizations associated with it. Supervisor Catherine Stefani sponsored the resolution, stating that the NRA's opposition to gun control is "standing in the way of saving lives." The NRA has condemned the resolution as a "reckless assault on law-abiding citizens and their freedoms." This move comes amid growing national attention on gun violence following recent mass shootings in California, Ohio, and Texas. The resolution aims to pave the way for similar legislation at the local, state or federal level.

END OF SUMMARY.
