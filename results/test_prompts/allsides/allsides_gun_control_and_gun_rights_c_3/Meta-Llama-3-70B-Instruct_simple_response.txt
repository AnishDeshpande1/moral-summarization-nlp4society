SUMMARY:

The San Francisco Board of Supervisors has passed a resolution declaring the National Rifle Association (NRA) a "domestic terrorist organization". The resolution connects the NRA to the "epidemic of gun violence" in the US, urging San Francisco to distance itself from the organization and avoid doing business with it. The resolution accuses the NRA of spreading propaganda that misinforms and deceives the public about gun violence, and Supervisor Catherine Stefani, who sponsored the resolution, argues that the NRA's opposition to gun control is "standing in the way of saving lives". The resolution highlights the alarming statistic that 100 Americans are killed daily by gun violence, making the US's gun homicide rate 25 times higher than any other high-income country. The NRA has condemned the resolution, calling it a "reckless assault" on a law-abiding organization. This move is seen as a step towards paving the way for similar legislation at the local, state, or federal level.

END OF SUMMARY.
