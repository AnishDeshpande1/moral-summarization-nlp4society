SUMMARY:  
The San Francisco Board of Supervisors unanimously passed a resolution labeling the National Rifle Association a “domestic terrorist organization,” accusing it of spreading propaganda that misinforms and deceives the public about the gravity of gun violence. The measure cites the nation’s epidemic of shootings, noting that 100 Americans are killed daily and that the U.S. homicide rate is 25 times higher than any other high‑income country, framing the NRA’s opposition to gun control as an assault on efforts to save lives. The resolution directs the city to examine local vendors’ and contractors’ ties to the NRA and to avoid doing business with the group or its affiliates, hoping to create a remedy that could inspire similar action at state or federal levels. NRA spokesperson Amy Hunter denounced the declaration as a reckless attack on a law‑abiding organization, calling it a “worthless and disgusting” sound‑bite amid the country’s gripping violence crisis. The debate follows recent mass shootings in California, Ohio, and Texas, which have intensified calls for stronger gun‑control measures.  

END OF SUMMARY.
