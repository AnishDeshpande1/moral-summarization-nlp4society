SUMMARY:

The San Francisco Board of Supervisors has declared the National Rifle Association (NRA) a "domestic terrorist organization" due to its role in perpetuating an "epidemic of gun violence" in the US. The resolution condemns the NRA for spreading "propaganda that misinforms and aims to deceive the public about the dangers of gun violence." By opposing gun control, the NRA is "standing in the way of saving lives," according to Supervisor Catherine Stefani. The resolution highlights the devastating impact of gun violence, citing that 100 Americans are "killed daily" and the US has a gun homicide rate 25 times higher than other high-income countries. The NRA has responded, calling the resolution a "reckless assault on a law-abiding organization" and a "worthless and disgusting'soundbite remedy' to the violence epidemic gripping our nation." The move is seen as a step towards paving the way for similar legislation at the local, state, or federal level.

END OF SUMMARY.
