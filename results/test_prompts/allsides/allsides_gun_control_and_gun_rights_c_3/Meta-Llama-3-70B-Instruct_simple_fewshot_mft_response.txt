SUMMARY:

The San Francisco Board of Supervisors has passed a resolution declaring the National Rifle Association (NRA) a "domestic terrorist organization" due to its role in perpetuating gun violence in the United States. The resolution condemns the NRA's opposition to gun control, which is seen as a major obstacle to saving lives. By spreading misinformation and propaganda, the NRA is deemed to be subverting the authority of the government and the public's right to safety. The resolution also highlights the devastating harm caused by gun violence, with 100 Americans killed daily, making the US's gun homicide rate 25 times higher than any other high-income country. Furthermore, the NRA's actions are seen as unfair and cheating, as they prioritize the interests of gun manufacturers over the lives of innocent people. The resolution aims to distance San Francisco from the NRA and pave the way for similar legislation at the local, state, or federal level. The NRA has condemned the resolution, calling it a "reckless assault" on their organization and its members.

END OF SUMMARY.
