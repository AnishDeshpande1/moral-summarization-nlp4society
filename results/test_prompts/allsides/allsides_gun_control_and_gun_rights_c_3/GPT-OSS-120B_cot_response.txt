**SUMMARY:**  
The San Francisco Board of Supervisors unanimously passed a resolution branding the National Rifle Association a “domestic terrorist” organization, linking the NRA to the nation’s gun‑related “epidemic.” The resolution condemns the NRA’s “propaganda” that “misinforms” and seeks to “deceive” the public about the dangers of gun “violence,” calling the group “absolutely disgraceful.” It urges the city to sever ties with the NRA, scrutinize local vendors and contractors for connections, and avoid any business with the organization or its affiliates. The measure is positioned as a model for broader legislation aimed at saving lives, noting that 100 Americans are “killed” daily and that U.S. gun homicide rates far exceed those of other high‑income nations. The NRA denounced the resolution as a “worthless, disgusting, reckless assault” on a “law‑abiding” group and its “freedoms.” The debate unfolds amid heightened national attention on gun control after recent mass shootings.
