SUMMARY: The San Francisco Board of Supervisors unanimously passed a resolution labeling the National Rifle Association (NRA) a “domestic terrorist organization,” citing its role in the U.S. gun‑violence epidemic. The measure urges the city to review and sever ties with vendors and contractors linked to the NRA and to avoid doing business with the group or its affiliates. Sponsored by Supervisor Catherine Stefani, the resolution frames the NRA’s anti‑gun‑control stance as obstructing lifesaving measures and calls the organization “absolutely disgraceful.” It also aims to inspire similar actions at higher government levels. The NRA denounced the resolution as a “worthless and disgusting ‘soundbite remedy’” and defended its advocacy. The move follows recent mass shootings in California, Ohio, and Texas, which have intensified national debate over gun control. END OF SUMMARY.
