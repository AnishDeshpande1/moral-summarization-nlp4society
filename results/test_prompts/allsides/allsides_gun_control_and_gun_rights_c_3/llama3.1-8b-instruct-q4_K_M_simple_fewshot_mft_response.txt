SUMMARY:

The San Francisco Board of Supervisors has declared the National Rifle Association (NRA) a "domestic terrorist organization" due to its alleged role in perpetuating gun violence in the US. The resolution, which passed unanimously, connects the NRA to an "epidemic of gun violence" and urges local vendors and contractors to distance themselves from the organization. Supervisor Catherine Stefani argued that the NRA's opposition to gun control is "standing in the way of saving lives." The resolution also highlights the high rate of gun-related deaths in the US compared to other developed countries, with 100 Americans killed daily by gun violence. The NRA has condemned the resolution as a "reckless assault on a law-abiding organization" and its members. This move is seen as part of a growing national debate on gun control, with Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell facing pressure to take up legislation.
