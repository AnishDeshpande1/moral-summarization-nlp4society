SUMMARY:

The city of Chicago has experienced one of its most violent years, with 762 homicides and 1,100 more shooting incidents compared to the previous year. This bloodshed has left the nation's third-largest city at the center of a national dialogue about gun violence. The statistics are staggering, with police recovering 8,300 illegal guns in 2016, a 20 percent increase from the previous year. Police Superintendent Eddie Johnson attributed the surge in violence to the flood of illegal guns into the city and the lack of deterrents for criminals. He noted that gang members have become emboldened by the perception that they have little to fear from the criminal justice system.

Johnson also pointed out that the price for being caught is small compared to other large cities, making it more likely for gang members to commit nefarious activity. The bulk of the deaths and shootings occurred in poor and predominantly black areas on the city's South and West sides. Johnson expressed hope that initiatives such as street cameras and gunshot-detection systems would lead to more arrests and drive down the violent crime rate.

END OF SUMMARY.
