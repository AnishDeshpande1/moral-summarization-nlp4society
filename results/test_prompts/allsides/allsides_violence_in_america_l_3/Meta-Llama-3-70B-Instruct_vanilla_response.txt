SUMMARY:

The city of Chicago ended 2016 with a record 762 homicides, the most in two decades, and a 20% increase in illegal guns recovered by police. The spike in violence has been attributed to a flood of illegal guns and a perceived lack of deterrent in the criminal justice system. Police Superintendent Eddie Johnson believes that gang members are emboldened to commit crimes due to lax gun laws and a perception that the court system is ineffective. The majority of the deaths and shootings occurred in five police districts on the city's South and West sides, predominantly black and poor areas where gangs are most active. The city has responded by adding 1,000 officers to the police department and implementing initiatives such as street cameras and gunshot-detection systems to drive down the violent crime rate.

END OF SUMMARY.
