SUMMARY:
Chicago experienced one of its most violent years, recording 762 homicides—the highest in two decades—and 1,100 more shootings than in 2015. Police recovered 8,300 illegal guns, a 20 % rise, underscoring officials’ claim that lax state gun laws and a weak criminal‑justice deterrent are emboldening gangs. Superintendent Eddie Johnson warned that anger toward police, heightened after the Laquan McDonald video, has eroded respect for authority and encouraged subversive behavior, while offenders perceive the courts as “a joke.” The surge was concentrated in five poor, majority‑Black districts where over 80 % of victims were known to police, highlighting the care/harm dimension of widespread community trauma. City leaders, citing fairness concerns, pointed to the disparity between harsh penalties for minor offenses and the light treatment of gun offenders, arguing that the system cheats victims of protection. In response, Mayor Rahm Emanuel pledged 1,000 new officers and expanded street cameras and gun‑shot detection, aiming to restore authority, protect citizens, and rebalance a justice system seen as unfairly lenient toward violent criminals.  
END OF SUMMARY.
