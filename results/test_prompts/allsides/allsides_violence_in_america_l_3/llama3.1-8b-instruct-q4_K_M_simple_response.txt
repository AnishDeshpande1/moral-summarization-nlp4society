SUMMARY:

Chicago experienced its deadliest year in two decades, with 762 homicides and over 1,100 more shooting incidents compared to 2015. The city's police superintendent, Eddie Johnson, attributed the spike in violence to a lack of deterrents for gun offenders, as well as anger towards police following high-profile cases like the fatal shooting of Laquan McDonald. Johnson noted that gang members consider the court system "a joke" and are becoming increasingly emboldened to commit violent crimes.

The bulk of deaths and shootings occurred in five predominantly black neighborhoods on the city's South and West sides, where gangs are most active. Mayor Rahm Emanuel has responded by increasing police presence, but critics argue that officers have become more cautious following the McDonald case, leading to a surge in gang violence. Johnson acknowledged this trend, citing concerns over viral videos and increased paperwork requirements as factors contributing to the decline in policing.

END OF SUMMARY.
