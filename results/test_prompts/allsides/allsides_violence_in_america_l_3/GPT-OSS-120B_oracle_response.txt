SUMMARY:  
Chicago endured one of its most violent years, recording 762 homicides – the highest in two decades and more than New York and Los Angeles combined – and 1,100 additional shooting incidents compared with 2015. Police recovered 8,300 illegal guns, a 20 % rise, as the flood of firearms fueled a surge in bloodshed. Superintendent Eddie Johnson warned that anger toward police, amplified by the viral video of a white officer shooting a black teen 16 times, has left criminals emboldened and convinced that the criminal‑justice system offers little deterrent. Most deaths and shootings clustered in five poor, predominantly Black districts on the South and West sides, where over 80 % of victims were linked to gangs. The city responded by adding 1,000 officers, expanding street cameras and gun‑shot detection systems, and increasing arrests and seizures. Yet officers have become more reluctant, fearing viral videos and new paperwork requirements, which critics say has caused a drop in proactive policing and allowed gangs to keep killing. Johnson argues that restoring fear of consequences is essential to stop the continued surge of crimes and killings.  
END OF SUMMARY.
