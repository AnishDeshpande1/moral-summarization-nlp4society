SUMMARY:

Chicago has ended one of its most violent years in history, with a staggering 762 homicides, the most in two decades. The city saw a 20% increase in illegal guns recovered, with 8,300 seized in 2016. Police Superintendent Eddie Johnson attributes the surge in violence to the flood of illegal guns and a lack of deterrent in the criminal justice system. He claims that criminals feel "emboldened" to commit violent crimes, including attacks on police, due to anger towards law enforcement and a perception that they have little to fear from the justice system. The majority of deaths and shooting incidents occurred in poor, predominantly black areas on the South and West sides, where gangs are most active. The city has scrambled to address the violence, with initiatives including more street cameras and gunshot-detection systems. However, critics argue that police have become more cautious, leading to a decline in arrests and allowing gangs to "kill each other."

END OF SUMMARY.
