SUMMARY:

The city of Chicago has experienced one of its most violent years in history, with 762 homicides and 1,100 more shooting incidents in 2016 compared to the previous year. The statistics are staggering, with a 20% increase in illegal guns recovered by police. Chicago Police Superintendent Eddie Johnson attributes the surge in violence to a lack of deterrent in the criminal justice system, allowing criminals to feel "emboldened" to commit violent crimes. The majority of deaths and shootings occurred in poor, predominantly black areas on the South and West sides, where gangs are most active. The city has scrambled to address the violence, adding 1,000 officers to the police department and implementing initiatives such as street cameras and gunshot-detection systems. However, critics argue that officers have become more cautious in their policing, leading to a decline in arrests and a perception of a weakened police force. The moral imperative is clear: the city must take immediate action to address the root causes of violence and restore public trust in the police department.

END OF SUMMARY.
