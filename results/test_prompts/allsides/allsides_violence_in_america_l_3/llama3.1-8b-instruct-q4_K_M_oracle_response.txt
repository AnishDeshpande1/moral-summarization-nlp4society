SUMMARY:

The city of Chicago has seen one of its most violent years, with 762 homicides and 1,100 more shooting incidents compared to 2015. This bloodshed has put the nation's third-largest city at the center of a national dialogue about gun violence. The statistics show that illegal guns are flooding into the city, with police recovering 8,300 illicit firearms in 2016, a 20 percent increase from the previous year. Police Superintendent Eddie Johnson blames the surge in violence on anger and attacks against law enforcement, which have emboldened criminals to commit crimes with little fear of repercussions. He points out that gang members are arming themselves due to the lack of a deterrent and the small price they pay for their actions. The city has scrambled to address the violence, but the problem persists in poor and predominantly black areas on the South and West sides.

END OF SUMMARY.
