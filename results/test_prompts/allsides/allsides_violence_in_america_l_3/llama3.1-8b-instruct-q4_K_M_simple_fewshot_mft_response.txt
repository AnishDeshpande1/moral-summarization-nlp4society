SUMMARY:

Chicago experienced its deadliest year in two decades, with 762 homicides and over 1,100 more shooting incidents compared to 2015. Police Superintendent Eddie Johnson attributed the surge in violence to a lack of deterrents, with gang members feeling emboldened by the leniency of the criminal justice system. He noted that police have become more cautious due to fear of becoming viral video targets and that a new state law requiring lengthy contact cards has resulted in fewer stops. Critics argue that this shift in policing strategy is contributing to the violence, allowing gangs to kill each other with impunity.

Johnson also pointed to the city's lax gun laws as a contributing factor, with 8,300 illegal guns recovered in 2016, a 20% increase from the previous year. Mayor Rahm Emanuel has announced plans to add 1,000 officers to the police department and implement new initiatives such as street cameras and gunshot-detection systems. However, the moral imperative to address the root causes of violence and restore trust in the police force remains unmet.

The city's poor and predominantly black areas are disproportionately affected by the violence, with over 80% of victims having previous gang ties or arrests. The situation highlights the need for a more comprehensive approach to addressing gun violence and restoring public safety.

END OF SUMMARY.
