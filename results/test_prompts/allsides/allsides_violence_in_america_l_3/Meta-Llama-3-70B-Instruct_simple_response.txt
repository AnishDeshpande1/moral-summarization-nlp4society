SUMMARY:

Chicago has ended one of its most violent years in history, with 762 homicides and 1,100 more shooting incidents compared to 2015. The city's police department attributes this surge in violence to the flood of illegal guns and a lack of deterrent in the criminal justice system. Police Superintendent Eddie Johnson notes that gang members feel emboldened to commit violent crimes due to lax gun laws and a perception that the court system is a "joke." The majority of the deaths and shootings occurred in poor, predominantly black areas on the South and West sides, where gangs are most active. The city has scrambled to address the violence, with initiatives such as adding 1,000 officers to the police department and expanding gunshot-detection systems. However, critics argue that officers have become more cautious and reluctant to do their jobs, fearing becoming the next "viral video." This moral crisis has led to a breakdown in trust between the police and the community, allowing criminals to take advantage of the situation.

END OF SUMMARY.
