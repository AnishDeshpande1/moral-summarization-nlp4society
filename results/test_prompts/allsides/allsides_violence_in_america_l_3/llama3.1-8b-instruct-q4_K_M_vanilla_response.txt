SUMMARY:

Chicago ended its most violent year on record, with 762 homicides and 1,100 more shooting incidents compared to 2015. The city's police superintendent, Eddie Johnson, attributed the increase in violence to the flood of illegal guns into Chicago and a lack of deterrent for criminals. He also stated that gang members are arming themselves due to the perceived leniency of the court system.

The majority of deaths and shootings occurred in five impoverished areas on the South and West sides, where gangs are most active. Police officials have scrambled to address the violence, adding 1,000 officers to the department and implementing initiatives such as street cameras and gunshot-detection systems.

Johnson pointed to several factors contributing to the increased violence, including the release of video showing a police officer shooting a black teenager 16 times, which left officers feeling fearful of becoming "viral videos" themselves. He also cited a state law requiring lengthy contact cards for officers when making stops, which has resulted in fewer stops and a perceived opportunity for criminals to commit nefarious activity.

END OF SUMMARY.
