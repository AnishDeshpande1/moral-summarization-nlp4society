SUMMARY:

The city of Chicago has witnessed one of its most violent years in history, with 762 homicides and 1,100 more shooting incidents in 2016 compared to the previous year. The staggering statistics underscore a story of bloodshed that has put Chicago at the center of a national dialogue about gun violence. The police department's data reveals a 20% increase in illegal gun recoveries, with 8,300 guns seized in 2016. Chicago Police Superintendent Eddie Johnson attributes the surge in violence to the lack of deterrent in the criminal justice system, emboldening criminals to commit violent crimes. He also points to the city's lax gun laws and the perception among gang members that the court system is a "joke." The bulk of the deaths and shootings occurred in predominantly black and poor areas on the city's South and West sides, where gangs are most active. The city has scrambled to address the violence, with initiatives such as street cameras and gunshot-detection systems aimed at driving down the violent crime rate.

END OF SUMMARY.
