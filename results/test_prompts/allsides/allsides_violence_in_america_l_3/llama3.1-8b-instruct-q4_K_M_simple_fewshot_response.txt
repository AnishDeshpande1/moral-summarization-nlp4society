SUMMARY:
Chicago's violent year ended with a record-breaking 762 homicides, surpassing New York and Los Angeles combined. The city saw 1,100 more shooting incidents in 2016 compared to 2015, with over 80% of victims having gang ties or past arrests. Police Superintendent Eddie Johnson attributed the surge in violence to lax gun laws and a lack of deterrents for criminals, who consider the court system "a joke." He also noted that officers have become more cautious due to fear of becoming viral video sensations. The police department has scrambled to address the issue with initiatives such as street cameras and gunshot-detection systems. However, critics argue that the increased violence is a result of a pullback by officers in enforcing minor laws, allowing gangs to kill each other without consequence. Johnson acknowledged this trend, stating that criminals are emboldened by their perception of little risk from the justice system.
