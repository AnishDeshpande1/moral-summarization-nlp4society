SUMMARY:  
Republican lawmakers stormed the secure SCIF where a deposition in the Trump impeachment probe was taking place, arguing that Democrats have denied them due‑process rights and are abusing impeachment power. They accused House Democrats of “bypassing constitutional norms” and shutting Republicans out of evidence, witnesses, and subpoena authority, while Democrats warned the breach endangered national‑security protocols and threatened ethics charges. Both sides framed the clash as a moral issue: Republicans claimed the inquiry is unfair and partisan, demanding equal access and “basic standards of due process,” whereas Democrats portrayed the Republican protest as a reckless violation of security rules that could invite foreign interference. The disrupted testimony of Defense Department official Laura Cooper eventually proceeded after a lengthy sit‑in, and the broader investigation continues amid disputed testimonies about a Ukraine aid quid‑pro quo, new document demands, and a court order for the State Department to release Ukraine‑related records. The episode underscores a deepening moral battle over procedural fairness versus security integrity in the impeachment process.  
END OF SUMMARY.
