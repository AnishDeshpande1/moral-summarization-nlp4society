SUMMARY:

House Republicans stormed a secure room, disrupting a deposition in the impeachment probe against President Trump, citing a lack of fairness from Democrats. They claimed Democrats have "bypassed" constitutional norms and "broken" the rules of fairness by shutting them out of the process. Republicans argued they have a "right" to see evidence and participate in the inquiry, but Democrats countered that they have "access" to transcripts and information. The protest was sparked by concerns that Trump's lawyers have been "denied" the chance to see evidence or call witnesses, and Republicans have been "restricted" from participating fully. Democrats accused the Republicans of creating a potential security lapse by bringing electronics into the secure area, which they said was an "outrage". The incident highlights the deepening partisan divide in the impeachment inquiry. END OF SUMMARY.
