SUMMARY:

A group of House Republicans stormed the secure room where lawmakers were conducting an impeachment probe against President Trump, demanding fairness from Democrats. They claimed that Democrats had broken rules of fairness by shutting them out of the process and denying them access to evidence and witnesses. The protest disrupted a scheduled deposition with Defense Department official Laura Cooper, who eventually testified after a five-hour delay.

Democrats responded by accusing Republicans of creating a security lapse by bringing electronics into the secure area. They threatened to bring ethics charges against the Republican protesters. Republicans argued that Democrats were bypassing constitutional norms and due process in their impeachment inquiry.

The protest came as Democrats issued new demands for State Department documents, including text messages from witnesses and communications between Rudy Giuliani and Secretary of State Mike Pompeo. A federal judge has ordered the release of Ukraine-related documents within 30 days.

END OF SUMMARY
