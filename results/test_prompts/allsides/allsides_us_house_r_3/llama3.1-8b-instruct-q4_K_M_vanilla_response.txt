SUMMARY:

House Republicans stormed the secure room where lawmakers are conducting the impeachment probe against President Trump, demanding fairness from Democrats. They claimed that the hearing they disrupted was not classified and that Democrats have broken rules of fairness by shutting them out of the process. The Republican protest, which included carrying electronics into the secure area, enraged Democrats who demanded ethics charges be brought against the protesters.

The impeachment inquiry is investigating Trump's communications with Ukraine over former Vice President Joe Biden, a political opponent. Republicans claim that Trump's lawyers have been denied access to evidence and the ability to call their own witnesses, while Democrats argue that Republican committee members can participate and ask questions. The protest delayed the testimony of Defense Department official Laura Cooper, who ultimately testified five hours after she was scheduled to begin.

The controversy comes as a former US diplomat to Ukraine testified that Trump made military aid to Ukraine contingent on investigating the Bidens and 2016 election interference. Republicans disputed this testimony, saying it was based on second-hand information.

END OF SUMMARY.
