SUMMARY:  
House Republicans stormed the secure SCIF where a scheduled deposition of Defense Department official Laura Cooper was to occur, claiming Democrats were **bypassing** constitutional norms and **breaking** due‑process rules in the impeachment probe. The protest, which **brought** unauthorized electronics into the room, was called an **outrage** by Rep. Debbie Lesko and prompted calls for ethics charges. Republicans argue they have a **right** under House rules to **access** the proceedings and that Democrats have **restricted** their participation, **denied** subpoena power, and **prevented** them from seeing evidence or **calling** witnesses. Democrats counter that committee members were **permitted** to ask questions and already have **access** to transcripts, and they accuse the GOP of **pushing** a partisan agenda and **press**uring the investigation. The incident delayed Cooper’s testimony by five hours, and the debate continues over procedural fairness as both sides accuse the other of **following** improper precedents.  

END OF SUMMARY.
