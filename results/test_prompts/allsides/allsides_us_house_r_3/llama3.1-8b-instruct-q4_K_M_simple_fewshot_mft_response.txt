SUMMARY:

House Republicans stormed the secure room where lawmakers have been conducting the impeachment probe against President Trump, upending a scheduled deposition as they demanded fairness from the Democrats running the show. Democrats accused Republicans of creating a potential security lapse by bringing electronics into the secure area and demanded ethics charges against the 30 or so Republican protesters. The Republicans claimed that Democrats are bypassing constitutional norms and basic standards of due process with their impeachment obsession, and that Trump is not above justice but neither is he below it. The Democrats' impeachment inquiry has been criticized for not following precedents and for restricting Republicans' access to evidence and witnesses.

The protest highlights the deep divisions between the two parties over the fairness and legitimacy of the impeachment proceedings. Republicans argue that they are being unfairly shut out of the process, while Democrats claim that Republicans have had ample opportunities to participate. The dispute raises questions about the balance of power in Congress and the proper conduct of an impeachment inquiry.

END OF SUMMARY.
