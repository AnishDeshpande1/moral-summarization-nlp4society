SUMMARY:

House Republicans stormed the secure room where lawmakers were conducting the impeachment probe against President Trump, demanding fairness from Democrats who they claim are bypassing constitutional norms and basic standards of due process. They argue that Democrats have broken the rules by shutting them out of the impeachment process and denying them access to evidence and witnesses. The protest was met with outrage from Democrats, who accused Republicans of bringing unauthorized electronic devices into a secure area, creating a potential security lapse. House Intelligence Committee Chairman Adam B. Schiff threatened to bring ethics charges against the Republican protesters. The controversy comes as the committees leading the impeachment probe issued new demands for State Department documents, including communications between Rudy Giuliani and Secretary of State Mike Pompeo. Democrats argue that Republicans have been restricted in their ability to participate in the impeachment process, while Republicans claim they are exercising their right to see what's going on.

END OF SUMMARY.
