SUMMARY:  
On Wednesday, a group of about 30 House Republicans stormed the secure SCIF where a deposition for the Trump impeachment inquiry was taking place, demanding what they called fairness from the Democratic‑led investigation. They entered with electronic devices, prompting Democrats to call for ethics charges and an investigation into the security breach. The disrupted hearing involved Defense Department official Laura Cooper, whose testimony was delayed by several hours. Republicans argued they were being shut out of the process and denied subpoena power, while Democrats said Republican committee members already have full access to transcripts and can ask questions. The protest turned into a sit‑in, after which Cooper finally testified. The hearing followed recent testimony by former U.S. diplomat William Taylor, who claimed Trump tied Ukraine aid to investigations of the Bidens, a claim Republicans say is contradicted by other witnesses. In response, impeachment committees have requested additional State Department records, and a federal judge ordered the State Department to release Ukraine‑related documents within 30 days, including communications between Rudy Giuliani and Secretary of State Mike Pompeo.  

END OF SUMMARY.
