SUMMARY:
House Republicans stormed the secure SCIF where a deposition for the Trump impeachment inquiry was taking place, arguing that Democrats running the investigation are violating constitutional norms and denying Republicans due‑process rights. They entered with electronic devices, prompting Democrats to accuse them of creating a security breach and to call for ethics charges. Republicans, including Rep. Mark Walker and Rep. Debbie Lesko, condemned the “impeachment obsession” as unfair and said the president should not be above justice. Democrats countered that the hearing was not classified, that Republican committee members could already ask questions, and that the protest disrupted the testimony of Defense Department official Laura Cooper. The incident occurred amid fresh testimony from a former U.S. diplomat alleging a Trump‑Ukraine quid pro quo, which Republicans dismissed as second‑hand. Both sides continue to clash over access to evidence, subpoena power, and the proper conduct of the impeachment probe.  
END OF SUMMARY.
