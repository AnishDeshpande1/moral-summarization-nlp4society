SUMMARY:
House Republicans stormed the secure SCIF where a deposition of Defense Department official Laura Cooper was taking place, accusing Democratic leaders of subverting constitutional authority and denying due‑process fairness in the impeachment inquiry into President Trump’s Ukraine dealings. Republicans framed the protest as a defense of loyalty to the president and a rebuke to what they called Democratic cheating—shutting Republicans out of evidence, refusing subpoenas and violating established impeachment precedents. Democrats responded by invoking authority, threatening ethics charges and a security breach for bringing unauthorized electronics into the classified room, and asserted that Republican members already have access to transcripts and can ask questions. The clash highlighted competing claims of legitimacy: Republicans decrying a betrayal of procedural norms and loyalty to the president, and Democrats asserting their rightful authority to conduct the investigation and protect national‑security protocols. Both sides accused the other of abusing power and undermining the rule‑of‑law foundations of the impeachment process.  
END OF SUMMARY.
