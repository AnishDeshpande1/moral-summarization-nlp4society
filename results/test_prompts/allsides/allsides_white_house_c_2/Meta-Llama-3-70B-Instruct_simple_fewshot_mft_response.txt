SUMMARY:

The Trump administration has imposed new economic sanctions on Iranian individuals and companies, citing Tehran's recent missile tests and support for rebels in Yemen. The sanctions, which affect 13 individuals and 12 companies, freeze any property or funds they may have under US jurisdiction. The administration's move is seen as a warning to Iran, with Trump signaling that he will not tolerate Iran's "bad behavior" and "provocations" that threaten US interests. The sanctions are outside the scope of the nuclear agreement, which the US and allies drew down sanctions on Iran in exchange for its agreement to give up the means to make nuclear weapons. The administration's actions are seen as a show of strength and authority, with Trump and his aides emphasizing the need to hold Iran accountable for its actions. However, some lawmakers have questioned the administration's sabre-rattling toward Iran, urging clarity and restraint in its approach.

END OF SUMMARY.
