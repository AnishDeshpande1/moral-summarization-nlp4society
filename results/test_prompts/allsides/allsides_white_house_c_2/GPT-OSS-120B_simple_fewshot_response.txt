SUMMARY:
The Trump administration announced fresh economic sanctions on 13 Iranian individuals and 12 companies, linking the measures to Tehran’s recent ballistic‑missile tests and its backing of the Houthi rebels in Yemen. Officials framed Iran as a “state sponsor of terrorism” whose missile program threatens the region, U.S. allies and America itself. Acting OFAC director John E. Smith emphasized that the United States will “actively apply all available tools, including financial sanctions,” to curb this behavior, while President Trump warned that Iran “doesn’t appreciate how ‘kind’ President Obama was to them.” The sanctions freeze any U.S.-jurisdiction assets and bar U.S. persons from dealing with the listed parties, but they do not touch the nuclear‑deal sanctions, which remain in place despite Trump’s campaign‑time criticism. The move was portrayed as a principled response to Iran’s “bad behavior,” with national‑security advisers warning that the international community has been too tolerant. Lawmakers generally supported the sanctions but urged the administration to clarify its overall Iran strategy and avoid reckless rhetoric.  
END OF SUMMARY.
