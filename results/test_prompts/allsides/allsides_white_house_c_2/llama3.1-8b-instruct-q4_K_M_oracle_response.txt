SUMMARY:

The Trump administration imposed new economic sanctions on Iranian individuals and companies, citing Iran's continued support for terrorism and development of its ballistic missile program as a threat to the region and global security. The sanctions target 13 Iranians and 12 companies, freezing any property or funds they may have under U.S. jurisdiction. The move reflects the United States' commitment to enforcing sanctions on Iran with respect to its destabilizing activities in the region. Trump described Iran's actions as "playing with fire" and accused them of not appreciating how "kind" President Obama was to them. The sanctions are outside the scope of the nuclear agreement, which dealt only with nuclear-related sanctions. The administration pledged to address Iran's behavior through continued application of financial sanctions and other tools. Lawmakers applauded the move but urged the administration to provide clarity on its overall strategy towards Iran and refrain from provocative rhetoric.

END OF SUMMARY
