SUMMARY:  
The Trump administration announced fresh economic sanctions targeting 13 Iranian individuals and 12 companies, condemning Tehran’s missile tests and alleged support for Yemen’s Houthi rebels as “terrorism” that threatens the region, U.S. allies, and America itself. Acting OFAC director John E. Smith framed the measures as a moral duty to punish Iran’s “bad behavior” and to “actively apply all available tools, including financial sanctions.” President Trump echoed this stance, tweeting that Iran “is playing with fire” and criticizing the “kind” approach of the Obama era. While the sanctions do not touch the nuclear deal, officials stressed they are a necessary response to Iran’s destabilizing activities, portraying the regime as the world’s leading state sponsor of terrorism. Iranian officials dismissed the accusations as baseless, warning that threats will not change Tehran’s conduct. Lawmakers praised the punitive step but urged the administration to clarify its overall strategy and avoid provocative rhetoric that could inflame tensions.  

END OF SUMMARY.
