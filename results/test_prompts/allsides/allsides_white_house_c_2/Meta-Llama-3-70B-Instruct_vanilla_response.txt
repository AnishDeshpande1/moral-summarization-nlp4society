SUMMARY:

The Trump administration has imposed new economic sanctions on 13 Iranian individuals and 12 companies, citing Iran's recent missile tests and support for rebels in Yemen. The sanctions freeze any US-held assets and prohibit US citizens from engaging in transactions with those on the list. The move is seen as a warning to Iran, with Trump stating that "Iran is playing with fire" and that the US will no longer tolerate its "bad behavior". The sanctions are separate from the Iranian nuclear deal, which Trump has criticized but has shown no signs of voiding. The Treasury Department said the sanctions reflect the US' commitment to enforcing sanctions on Iran's ballistic missile program and destabilizing activities in the region. Iranian officials have denied any wrongdoing and mocked Trump's criticism.

END OF SUMMARY.
