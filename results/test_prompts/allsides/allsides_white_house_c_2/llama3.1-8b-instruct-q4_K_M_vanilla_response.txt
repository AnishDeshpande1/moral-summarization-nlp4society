SUMMARY:

The Trump administration has announced new economic sanctions on Iranian individuals and companies, citing Iran's recent missile tests and support for rebels in Yemen. The sanctions affect 13 Iranians and 12 companies, freezing any property or funds they may have under US jurisdiction. The Treasury Department stated that the sanctions are meant to address Iran's "ballistic missile program and destabilizing activities in the region." The move is seen as a response to Iran's recent ballistic missile test and support for the Houthi rebel group in Yemen.

The sanctions do not affect the Iranian nuclear deal, which was negotiated by President Obama. Lawmakers have generally applauded the sanctions, but some have questioned the administration's strategy towards Iran. National security adviser Michael Flynn called Iran "the world's leading state sponsor of terrorism" and stated that the US will no longer tolerate its provocations.

The Trump administration has also signaled that it plans to continue applying pressure on Iran through financial sanctions. END OF SUMMARY.
