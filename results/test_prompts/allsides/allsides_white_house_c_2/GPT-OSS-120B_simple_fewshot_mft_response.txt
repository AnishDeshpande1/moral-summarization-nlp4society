SUMMARY:
The Trump administration announced new economic sanctions targeting 13 Iranian individuals and 12 companies for missile tests and alleged support for Yemen’s Houthi rebels, framing the move as a legitimate exercise of U.S. authority to protect regional and American security. Officials emphasized that Iran’s “continued support for terrorism and ballistic‑missile development” harms innocent populations, justifying the sanctions as a protective, caring response to prevent further danger. By freezing assets and prohibiting U.S. persons from dealing with the listed entities, the administration positions the sanctions as a fair corrective measure against Iran’s cheating of international norms—especially its disregard for United Nations regulations and the spirit of the nuclear‑deal‑related sanctions regime. While Trump’s blunt rhetoric and tweets signal a hard‑line stance, lawmakers call for clearer strategy, warning that provocative language could undermine the authority‑based, fair, and humanitarian objectives of the policy. The sanctions thus aim to assert U.S. authority, curb harmful Iranian behavior, and restore fairness by penalizing actions deemed deceitful and destabilizing.  
END OF SUMMARY.
