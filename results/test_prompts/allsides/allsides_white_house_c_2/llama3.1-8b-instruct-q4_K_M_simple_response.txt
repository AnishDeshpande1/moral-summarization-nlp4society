SUMMARY:
The Trump administration has imposed new economic sanctions on Iranian individuals and companies, citing Iran's recent missile tests and support for rebels in Yemen as threats to the region and US interests. The sanctions target 13 Iranians and 12 companies, freezing their assets under US jurisdiction. This move is seen as a continuation of the Obama-era policy, with Acting Secretary Adam J. Szubin leading the Treasury Department. National Security Adviser Michael Flynn described Iran as "the world's leading state sponsor of terrorism," and the administration vowed to hold Iran accountable for its behavior.

The sanctions are separate from the nuclear deal, which Trump attacked during his campaign but has not been voided. Lawmakers generally welcomed the move, but expressed concern over the administration's rhetoric towards Iran, with some urging clarity on their strategy towards the country. The US maintains that these sanctions address Iran's non-nuclear behavior and alleged support for terrorist groups.

END OF SUMMARY.
