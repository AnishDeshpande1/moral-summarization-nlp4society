SUMMARY:  
The Trump administration announced new sanctions targeting 13 Iranian individuals and 12 companies, citing Iran’s **continued** **support** for **terrorism**, its **development** of a ballistic‑missile **program**, and **threat**s to the region and U.S. interests. Acting OFAC director John E. Smith said the United States will **apply** all available tools, including **sanctions**, to **address** Iran’s **behaving**. President Trump warned Iran is **playing** with fire and vowed a tougher **action** and **commitment** to curb its destabilising **activities**. National security adviser Michael Flynn called Iran the world’s leading state **sponsor** of terrorism and said the U.S. will no longer **tolerate** its **provocations** or **threaten**‑filled **behavior**. Critics urged the administration to provide **clarity**, to **refrain** from ambiguous rhetoric that could **exacerbate** tensions, and to **confront** the challenges with a coherent strategy. The sanctions, which freeze U.S.‑jurisdiction assets, are separate from the nuclear deal and reflect a broader effort to **threaten** Iran’s illicit conduct without resorting to open conflict.  

END OF SUMMARY.
