SUMMARY: The Trump administration announced new U.S. economic sanctions targeting 13 Iranian individuals and 12 companies over recent missile tests and alleged support for Yemen’s Houthi rebels. Acting OFAC director John E. Smith said the sanctions freeze any U.S.-jurisdiction assets and prohibit U.S. persons from dealing with those listed. The measures, unrelated to the nuclear deal, focus on Iran’s ballistic missile program and destabilizing activities. President Trump criticized Iran on Twitter, while officials, including National Security Adviser Michael Flynn, warned of a tougher stance. Iran denied wrongdoing and dismissed the U.S. threats. The Treasury, led by acting Secretary Adam Szubin, implemented the sanctions after a review dating back to the Obama era; Treasury nominee Steve Mnuchin is still awaiting confirmation. Lawmakers praised the action but called for clearer overall strategy. END OF SUMMARY.
