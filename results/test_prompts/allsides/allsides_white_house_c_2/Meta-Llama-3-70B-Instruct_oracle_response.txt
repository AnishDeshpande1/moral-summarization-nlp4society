SUMMARY:

The Trump administration has imposed new economic sanctions on Iranian individuals and companies, citing Tehran's recent missile tests and support for rebels in Yemen as a threat to the region and the United States. The sanctions target 13 individuals and 12 companies, freezing their assets and prohibiting US persons from engaging in transactions with them. The administration claims that Iran's continued support for terrorism and development of its ballistic missile program poses a threat to global interests. Trump signaled the sanctions, saying Iran is "playing with fire" and doesn't appreciate the "kindness" of President Obama. The Treasury Department emphasized the US commitment to enforcing sanctions on Iran's destabilizing activities. Iran has denied violating UN regulations and mocked Trump's criticism, calling it "empty and baseless ranting." The sanctions are outside the scope of the nuclear agreement, which the US and allies drew down in exchange for Iran giving up its nuclear weapons program. Lawmakers have applauded the sanctions, but urged the administration to clarify its strategy towards Iran and refrain from provocative rhetoric.

END OF SUMMARY.
