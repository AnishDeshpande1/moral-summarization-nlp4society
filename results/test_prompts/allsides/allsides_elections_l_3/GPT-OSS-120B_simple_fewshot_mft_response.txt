SUMMARY:
Donald Trump rallied his core supporters in Wilkes‑Barre, Pennsylvania, while sidestepping the fresh “groping tape” that shows him bragging about sexually assaulting women—a scandal that critics frame as a serious harm to women’s dignity. He doubled down on his anti‑Clinton rhetoric, promising a special prosecutor and praising WikiLeaks, thereby subverting institutional norms and challenging the authority of the courts and the media. The speech highlighted a loyalty test: many Republican leaders, including House Speaker Paul Ryan and Sen. John McCain, publicly withdrew their support, accusing Trump of betraying party values and abandoning the standards of fairness that should apply to all candidates. Trump’s base, however, rejected these accusations, dismissing the tape as “locker‑room talk” and defending him against what they see as a double standard that punishes Trump while sparing rivals like Hillary Clinton for similar misconduct. The rally thus underscored a clash between loyalty to a charismatic leader and the broader moral expectations of care for victims, equal treatment under the law, and respect for democratic authority. END OF SUMMARY.
