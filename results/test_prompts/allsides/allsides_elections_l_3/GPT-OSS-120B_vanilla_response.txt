SUMMARY:  
Donald Trump rallied supporters in Wilkes‑Barre, Pennsylvania, ignoring the recent release of a 2005 “groping” tape and the wave of Republican defections. He repeated his “lock her up” chant against Hillary Clinton, praised WikiLeaks, and mischaracterized a Russian‑sourced report about Clinton aide Sidney Blumenthal and the Benghazi controversy. Trump dismissed the tape as “locker‑room talk,” while surrogate Rudy Giuliani turned it into a joke. He reiterated signature promises – a border wall, jobs, tax cuts – and even staged a stunt with a toddler onstage. Outside the rally, his campaign falters: House Speaker Paul Ryan refused to support him, polls show Clinton leading by 11 points (46%‑35%), and she is gaining ground in swing states like Ohio. Senior Republicans, including John McCain, publicly withdrew support, citing Trump’s offensive remarks toward women, minorities, and others. The fallout highlights deep divisions within the GOP and a challenging path ahead for Trump’s candidacy.  
END OF SUMMARY.
