SUMMARY:

Donald Trump's campaign is in trouble after a tape surfaced of him bragging about using his celebrity status to sexually assault women. Despite this, Trump made no reference to the scandal at a rally in Pennsylvania, instead firing up his supporters by repeating his threat to appoint a special prosecutor to investigate Hillary Clinton's alleged crimes. He praised WikiLeaks, which has been accused of hacking into Democratic emails, and adopted an erroneous report from a Russian government-sponsored news agency. Trump also made a joke about his previous comments, which many consider to be sexual assault, and his surrogate Rudy Giuliani turned the issue into a joke. Trump's supporters dismissed concerns about the tape as "political correctness." However, outside of the rally, Trump's campaign remains in trouble, with senior Republicans like Paul Ryan distancing themselves from him and Clinton solidifying her lead in key swing states. Trump's remarks have been widely condemned as mistreating people, and his candidacy has generated profound unease among Republicans. END OF SUMMARY.
