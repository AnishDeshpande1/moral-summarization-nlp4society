SUMMARY:

Donald Trump held a rally in Pennsylvania, ignoring the controversy surrounding a 2005 recording where he bragged about sexually assaulting women. Instead, he repeated his threat to appoint a special prosecutor to investigate Hillary Clinton's alleged crimes. He also praised WikiLeaks, which has been linked to Russian government-sponsored hacking, and adopted an erroneous report from a Russian news agency. Trump's supporters dismissed concerns about the recording as "political correctness." However, outside the rally, Trump's campaign remains in trouble, with senior Republicans like Paul Ryan distancing themselves from him. A new opinion poll shows Clinton leading Trump nationally by 11 points, and she is solidifying her lead in key swing states like Ohio. Trump's surrogates, like Rudy Giuliani, are trying to downplay the controversy, but it may backfire given Trump's praise for WikiLeaks and Russian leader Vladimir Putin.

END OF SUMMARY.
