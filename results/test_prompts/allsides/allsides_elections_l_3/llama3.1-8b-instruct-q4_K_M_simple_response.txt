SUMMARY:

US presidential candidate Donald Trump's campaign is facing significant trouble, despite his efforts to rally support from die-hard followers in Pennsylvania on Monday night. Trump made no reference to the recent groping tape scandal or Republican defections, instead focusing on his promise to build a wall along the southern border and cut taxes. His remarks were met with cheers from his loyal supporters, but the reality is that his campaign remains in trouble, with many senior Republicans distancing themselves from him.

The latest opinion polls show Clinton leading Trump by 11 points nationally, and she has solidified her lead in key swing states such as Ohio. Even some prominent Republican figures, including Senator John McCain, have publicly expressed their disapproval of Trump's behavior and pledged not to support him. Trump's recent praise for WikiLeaks and his adoption of a Russian government-sponsored news agency's report on the leaked emails only add to the controversy surrounding his campaign.
