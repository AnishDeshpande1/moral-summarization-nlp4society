SUMMARY:  
Donald Trump strutted before a loyal crowd in Wilkes‑Barre, ignoring the spiraling disaster of the groping tape that showed him bragged about using his celebrity status to sexually assault women. He dismissed the comments of defecting Republicans and the scandal’s impact, suggesting that grabbing women’s genitals was merely “locker‑room talk.” Trump repeated his threat to “lock” Hillary Clinton up, praised WikiLeaks as a conduit for leaked documents, and adopted a faulty Russian report that claimed Sidney Blumenthal had “something” to say about the Benghazi death. The candidate’s stance included a jabbed, interrupted debate performance and a taunted, disparaging attack on Clinton, while he joked and quipped about the tape, calling the accusations phony and dismissing them as political correctness. Rudy Giuliani turned the issue into a joke, and Trump even asked a toddler whether to stay with him, receiving a “Trump” answer. Despite the brash rhetoric, senior Republicans such as Paul Ryan and John McCain withdrew support, saying Trump’s remarks and attacks demean women and the party, and they could not part with a nominee they deemed unqualified.  

END OF SUMMARY.
