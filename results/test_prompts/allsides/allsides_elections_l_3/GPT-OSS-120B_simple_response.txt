SUMMARY:  
Donald Trump rallied his loyal base in Wilkes‑Barre, Pennsylvania, while ignoring the scandal that has shaken his campaign – an 11‑year‑old “groping” tape in which he bragged about sexually assaulting women. He dismissed the tape as “locker‑room talk,” and his surrogate Rudy Giuliani turned the accusation into a joke. Trump also ignored the wave of Republican defections, the loss of support from House Speaker Paul Ryan, and the surge in Hillary Clinton’s poll lead after the tape’s release. Instead, he repeated his usual incendiary promises – a border wall paid by Mexico, tax cuts, and a “lock her up” chant against Clinton – while praising WikiLeaks and echoing a Russian‑sponsored outlet’s inaccurate claim about Clinton’s aide Sidney Blumenthal. The rally featured theatrical moments, such as carrying a toddler on stage, but failed to stem the moral outcry: senior GOP figures like John McCain publicly withdrew their support, citing Trump’s long‑standing misogyny and other offensive remarks. The episode underscores a campaign built on deflection, denial, and appeals to a devoted base, while the broader electorate and many party leaders condemn the moral bankruptcy of his conduct.  

END OF SUMMARY.
