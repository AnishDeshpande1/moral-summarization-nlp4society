SUMMARY:
Donald Trump rallied his loyal base in Wilkes‑Barre, Pennsylvania, while ignoring the fallout from a newly released “groping tape” in which he bragged about sexually assaulting women and the wave of Republican defections that followed. He doubled down on attack rhetoric against Hillary Clinton, promising a special prosecutor to “lock her up,” praising WikiLeaks, and repeating a false claim about Clinton’s aide Sidney Blumenthal based on a Russian‑sponsored report. Trump dismissed the tape as “locker‑room talk,” and his surrogates, including Rudy Giuliani, turned the scandal into a joke. Meanwhile, senior Republicans such as House Speaker Paul Ryan and Sen. John McCain publicly withdrew their support, citing Trump’s repeated misogyny and incendiary remarks. Recent polls show Clinton leading Trump by double digits nationally and in key swing states. The article frames Trump’s behavior as reckless, morally bankrupt, and damaging to the party, emphasizing the need for accountability and the harmful impact of his contempt for women and democratic norms.
