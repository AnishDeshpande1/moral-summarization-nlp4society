**SUMMARY:**  
President Donald Trump erupted in *anger* after a wave of leaks—some *top‑secret*, some mere gossip—exposed private facts that painted his administration in a negative light. He ordered the FBI and his White House counsel to investigate, arguing that the unauthorized disclosure of classified material is a *felony* and that leaking is often done to *embarrass*, *control*, *intimidate* or *infuriate* him. The article notes that only a tiny fraction of NSA spies have access to the most sensitive data, yet a few chose to reveal it, violating their *duty* to inform the president. Selective concealment and *manipulating* intelligence, the piece argues, constitutes *interference* with presidential judgment—a serious offense. Meanwhile, White House staff leaks of internal conversations led to a seizure of government‑issued cellphones, raising Fourth Amendment concerns. The author warns that such *perilous* leaks could be the work of *unelected*, *unaccountable*, *unnamed* actors seeking to undermine the president’s constitutional authority. Although the leaks may be a nuisance, they are not *criminal*, and the press is protected by the First Amendment to publish material of public interest. As Thomas Jefferson warned, *accountability* and *transparency* demand a free press.
