SUMMARY:

President Trump's anger over recent "revelations" of private facts has led to FBI investigations and a White House counsel probe. However, the issue of "leaking" government information is complex, involving classified data, confidential communications, and publishing of leaked material. The National Security Agency employs 60,000 domestic spies, but only 100 have access to top-secret data. Some in the intelligence community have "leaked" information to embarrass, control, or intimidate the president, which is a "felony". On the other hand, selectively "concealing" and "revealing" intelligence data to manipulate presidential judgment is also a "felony". The White House staff has also been "leaking" Oval Office gossip to the press, which is not criminal but raises concerns about "unelected, unaccountable, and unnamed" individuals influencing national security decisions. The media is protected by the First Amendment to publish leaked data, and the president has taken an oath to "preserve, protect, and defend" these constitutional principles.

END OF SUMMARY.
