SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump’s fury over recent leaks—ranging from classified intel about alleged misconduct in Moscow to partial transcripts of Michael Flynn’s calls and internal White House gossip—has led him to order FBI investigations and a probe of staff cell‑phone use. The article argues that while leaking classified material is a felony that endangers national security and manipulates presidential judgment, the internal White House leaks are not criminal, and the government’s seizure of employees’ phones violates Fourth‑Amendment protections. It emphasizes that a free press, protected by the First Amendment, has the right to publish any material of public interest, including leaked information about the president, and that holding the president accountable through transparent reporting is a cornerstone of American democracy. The moral framing stresses the tension between protecting sensitive intelligence and upholding constitutional freedoms, warning that unchecked leaks by unelected actors threaten democratic accountability, yet also defending the essential role of the media in exposing government misconduct. END OF SUMMARY.
