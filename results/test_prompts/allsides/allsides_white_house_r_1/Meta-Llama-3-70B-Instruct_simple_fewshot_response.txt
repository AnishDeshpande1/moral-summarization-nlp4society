SUMMARY:

President Trump has been furious over recent leaks of private facts and classified information, ordering the FBI to investigate and his White House counsel to look into others. However, the leaks are a two-sided coin, with some revealing too much and others concealing too much. The duty to reveal intelligence data is a legal obligation, and selectively concealing it is a felony. The White House staff has also been leaking Oval Office gossip to the press, leading to the temporary seizure of government-issued cellphones. While these leaks may be a pain for the president, they are not criminal. The media is protected by the First Amendment to publish leaked data, even if it's top-secret or gossip. Trump's anger towards the press is understandable, but he has taken an oath to defend the constitutional principles that protect a free press. As Thomas Jefferson said, accountability and transparency in government are of overriding value.

END OF SUMMARY.
