SUMMARY:

The recent revelations of private facts about President Trump have led to his fury and orders for investigations into leaks. Leaking classified information is a felony, as seen in the case of top-secret data accessed by only 100 spies. This has raised concerns about the president's judgment being manipulated by unelected, unaccountable, and unnamed individuals within the intelligence community. However, not all leaks are criminal; some may be necessary to reveal crucial information about America's enemies. The media has a right to publish leaked data, as protected by the First Amendment, which insulates them from government retribution. Trump's predecessor, Thomas Jefferson, believed in accountability and transparency through a free press, even if it means publishing stolen or confidential information. By preserving this freedom of the press, the president is protecting the very principles that ensure his own judgment is not manipulated. Ultimately, revealing all information, rather than concealing or selectively leaking, is essential for good governance.

END OF SUMMARY.
