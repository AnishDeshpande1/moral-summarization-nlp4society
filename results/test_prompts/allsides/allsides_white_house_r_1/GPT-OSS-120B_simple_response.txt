SUMMARY:  
President Donald Trump’s furious reaction to a wave of leaks—ranging from top‑secret intelligence to White House gossip—highlights a clash between two moral duties: the government’s obligation to protect classified information and the democratic imperative of transparency. The author argues that leaking classified data is a felony that undermines the president’s ability to make informed, constitutionally‑mandated judgments, while the selective concealment of intelligence by spies is equally immoral because it sabotages the president’s duty to protect the nation. Conversely, the author stresses that the press’s right to publish leaked material is a cornerstone of accountability; the First Amendment shields media from prosecution for revealing information of public interest, even when that information is obtained unlawfully. The Fourth Amendment limits the government’s power to seize employees’ phones without a warrant, underscoring the need to respect individual rights even within the executive branch. Ultimately, the piece frames the leaks as either growing pains of an administration still staffed by former‑Obama loyalists or as dangerous meddling by unelected actors, and it reminds readers that preserving a free press is a constitutional oath Trump swore to uphold—echoing Thomas Jefferson’s warning that a government without newspapers is a tyranny.  

END OF SUMMARY.
