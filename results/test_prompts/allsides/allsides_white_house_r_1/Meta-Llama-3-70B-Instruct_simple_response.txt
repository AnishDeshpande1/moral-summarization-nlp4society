SUMMARY:

President Trump's anger over recent leaks of private facts and top-secret information is understandable, but it raises important moral questions. Leaking classified information is a felony, and concealing intelligence data can be a form of interference with the president's judgment, also a felony. However, the duty to reveal material information is crucial, and selectively concealing it can be perilous. The White House staff's leaking of Oval Office gossip is a pain for the president, but not criminal. The media has the right to publish leaked data, including top-secret information, as it is of material public interest. The First Amendment protects the media's publication of leaked data, and the president has taken an oath to defend these constitutional principles. The moral framing of this issue highlights the importance of accountability and transparency in government, as emphasized by Thomas Jefferson. The president's anger must be balanced with the need to protect and defend the free press.

END OF SUMMARY.
