SUMMARY:

President Trump is dealing with the fallout of leaked classified information, confidential communications, and Oval Office gossip that have painted him and his administration in a bad light. Leaks of this nature are a serious issue, as they can compromise national security and interfere with the president's judgment. However, not all leaks are created equal - those related to government business and national security may be protected by the First Amendment, which guarantees freedom of the press.

The article argues that the media has a responsibility to report on matters of public interest, including leaked information about the president. Trump's efforts to investigate and punish leakers may be seen as an overreach, given his oath to protect the principles of accountability and transparency in government. In fact, former President Thomas Jefferson believed that a free press is essential to holding those in power accountable.

END OF SUMMARY.
