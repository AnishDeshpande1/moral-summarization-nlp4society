**SUMMARY:**
President Donald Trump erupted in fury after a wave of leaks—some classified, some private White House gossip—exposed him and his administration to public embarrassment and intimidation. He ordered the FBI to investigate the alleged felony leaks and tasked his White House counsel with probing internal breaches. The article argues that selective concealment of intelligence, a violation of the duty to inform the president, constitutes interference with a governmental function and is a felony. Conversely, the internal leaks of confidential conversations, while a breach of privacy, are not criminal and fall under First Amendment protection of a free press and public’s material interest. The piece warns that unaccountable, unelected, and unnamed actors manipulating the president’s judgment create a perilous situation for constitutional governance. It underscores the president’s oath to protect and defend constitutional principles, including transparency, accountability, and the press’s right to publish, even when the information stems from secrecy or Fourth‑Amendment‑protected communications.
