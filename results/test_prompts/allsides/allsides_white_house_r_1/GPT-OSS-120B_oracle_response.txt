SUMMARY:  
Last week President Donald Trump erupted in fury over a cascade of **leaks** and **revelations** that exposed both **top‑secret** data and White House **gossip**, painting his administration in a negative light. He ordered the FBI to investigate the **leaking** and tasked his counsel with probing the **revealed** material. The author notes that **leaking** classified information is a **felony**, especially when it is used to **intimidate**, **control**, or **manipulate** the president. Only a tiny cadre of NSA spies actually accessed the **leaked** top‑secret files, yet their disclosures—ranging from alleged hotel misconduct to transcripts of Michael Flynn’s calls and an intelligence report contradicting Trump’s immigration stance—sparked justified presidential anger.  

Conversely, the author warns that **concealing** intelligence from the president is also a **felony**, as it **manipulates** the president’s judgment and undermines a lawful governmental function. Recent White House **leaks** of internal conversations led to a brief seizure of government‑issued cellphones, an act the author says is **unconstitutional** without a warrant because the Fourth Amendment **insulates** employees from unwarranted searches.  

The media, protected by the First Amendment, may freely publish any **leaked** material of public interest, even if **stolen**, because a free press is **preserved**, **protected**, **defended**, and **liberated** by the Constitution. The danger lies when **unelected**, **unaccountable**, **unnamed** actors pull the levers of power behind the scenes.  

END OF SUMMARY.
