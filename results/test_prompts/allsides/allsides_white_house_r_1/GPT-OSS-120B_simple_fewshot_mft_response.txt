SUMMARY:
President Donald Trump has reacted angrily to a wave of leaks that exposed private and classified information about him and his administration, prompting him to order FBI investigations and internal probes. The author frames these disclosures as harmful attacks that jeopardize the president’s ability to make informed national‑security judgments, and as unfair manipulations by unelected intelligence officials and White House staff who selectively conceal or reveal data to influence policy. While the leaks expose wrongdoing and gossip, the piece argues that the media’s right to publish such material is protected by the First Amendment, yet the damage caused to the president’s reputation and decision‑making underscores a moral concern for care—preventing harm to the office and the nation. At the same time, the author stresses the need for fairness: leaking classified or confidential information without accountability cheats the public and undermines the constitutional balance, allowing hidden actors to manipulate presidential judgment. The tension between protecting the president from harmful leaks and upholding a free press reflects a core debate over care for leadership and fairness in the handling of sensitive information.  
END OF SUMMARY.
