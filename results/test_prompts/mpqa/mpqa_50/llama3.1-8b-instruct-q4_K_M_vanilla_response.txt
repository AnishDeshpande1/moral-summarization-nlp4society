SUMMARY:

Zimbabwean President Robert Mugabe's reelection has been praised by African countries, including the Organization of African Unity (OAU) and Southern African Development Community (SADC), who describe the election as "free and fair". Tanzanian President Benjamin Mkapa called Mugabe a champion of democracy. However, some western countries have condemned the election, calling it flawed and unfair. The US Assistant Secretary for African affairs, Walter Kansteiner, said that the US would consider further sanctions against Mugabe and his government.

The European Union has also expressed concern over the election results, with Denmark's Foreign Minister Per Stig Moeller announcing that his country will cut aid to Zimbabwe and likely shut down its embassy in Harare. France, Germany, and the UK have also condemned the election, with the UK's Foreign Secretary Jack Straw saying that Mugabe "stole" Zimbabwe's vote.

The final election results show Mugabe winning 56.2% of the total valid votes, while Morgan Tsvangirai of the opposition Movement for Democratic Change (MDC) got 41.96%. END OF SUMMARY.
