SUMMARY:

The Organization of African Unity (OAU) praised Zimbabwean President Robert Mugabe's reelection as "free, fair, transparent, credible, and reflecting the will of the people". Tanzanian President Benjamin Mkapa described Mugabe as a champion of democracy who has defended the inalienable rights to freedom, democratic government, and sovereignty. The OAU observer team concluded that the election was a true reflection of the people's will.

However, some Western countries condemned the election as flawed, citing violence, intimidation, and lack of equal chances for candidates. US Assistant Secretary for African affairs Walter Kansteiner stated that the election was "flawed" and that further sanctions against Mugabe and his government may be considered. The European Union is also considering toughening sanctions.

Despite these criticisms, the Southern African Development Community (SADC) Council of Ministers viewed the vote as legitimate, while Kenyan President Daniel arap Moi congratulated Mugabe on his reelection. Zimbabwe's incumbent president won 56.2% of the valid votes, with Morgan Tsvangirai of the opposition Movement for Democratic Change (MDC) receiving 41.96%.

END OF SUMMARY.
