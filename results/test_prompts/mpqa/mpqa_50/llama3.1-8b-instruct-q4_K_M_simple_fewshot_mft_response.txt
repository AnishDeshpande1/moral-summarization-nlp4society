Zimbabwean President Robert Mugabe's reelection has been met with praise from African countries, but condemnation from some western nations. The Organization of African Unity (OAU) observer team described the election as "free and fair", while Tanzanian President Benjamin Mkapa hailed Mugabe as a champion of democracy. However, Western countries have criticized the election as flawed, citing allegations of violence and intimidation by Mugabe's government. US Assistant Secretary for African affairs Walter Kansteiner called the election "flawed" and threatened further sanctions against Mugabe's regime. The British Foreign Secretary Jack Straw accused Mugabe of "stolen" Zimbabwe's vote, while France's Foreign Ministry spokesman Francois Rivasseau deemed the election "not substantially free and fair". Denmark has announced it will cut aid to Zimbabwe and shut down its embassy in Harare, and the European Union is considering toughening sanctions on the Mugabe regime. The controversy highlights the differing views on democracy and fairness among African and Western nations.
