SUMMARY:

The Organization of African Unity (OAU) has praised Zimbabwean President Robert Mugabe's reelection as "free and fair," while some Western countries have condemned the election as flawed. Tanzanian President Benjamin Mkapa called Mugabe a champion of democracy, saying it was up to the people of Zimbabwe to decide their leader. The Southern African Development Community (SADC) also welcomed the results, stating that they reflected the will of the people.

However, several Western countries, including the US, UK, France, and Germany, have condemned the election as marred by violence and intimidation. They argue that Mugabe's ZANU-PF party had an unfair advantage over the opposition Movement for Democratic Change (MDC). The European Union is considering tougher sanctions on the Mugabe regime in response.

The final results showed Mugabe winning 56.2% of the vote, while Morgan Tsvangirai of the MDC received 41.96%. The election was considered Zimbabwe's closest-fought presidential election since independence 22 years ago.

END OF SUMMARY.
