**SUMMARY:**  
The Organization of African Unity, SADC and several African heads of state praised Zimbabwe’s March 9‑11 presidential election as “free and fair,” calling President Robert Mugabe a “champion of democracy” who earned a “deserved” mandate and defended the people’s “inalienable rights” to “freedom, democratic and sovereign” government. Tanzanian President Benjamin Mkapa warned it would be a “tragedy” for Europe to dictate African outcomes, while Kenya’s Daniel arap Moi and South Africa’s observers deemed the vote a “true reflection” of the people’s will and “legitimate.”  

In contrast, the United States, United Kingdom, France, Germany and Denmark condemned the poll as “flawed,” citing “violence,” “intimidation,” and a “stolen” vote, and threatened further “sanctions” and aid cuts. The election, the closest since independence, gave Mugabe 56.2 % of the vote against Morgan Tsvangirai’s 41.96 %, amid “controversial” proceedings and an extended voting period.
