SUMMARY: Japanese lawmaker Taro Kono, a Liberal Democratic Party member and son of former foreign minister Yohei Kono, criticized President George W. Bush’s speech to the Diet on 19 February in his daily e‑mail magazine “Gomameno no Hagishiri.” Kono said he considered heckling the president about the Kyoto Protocol and the Comprehensive Nuclear‑Test‑Ban Treaty (CTBT) but held back. He questioned Bush’s appeal to “common values,” arguing that Japan‑U.S. relations require “value sharing” rather than “burden” or “power sharing.” Kono listed policies he finds unacceptable: U.S. opposition to the CTBT, indifference to the Biological Weapons Convention, rejection of the Kyoto Protocol, the right of Americans to own handguns, support for land mines, refusal to revise the Status of Forces Agreement (SOFA) except in application, and calls for joint action against Iraq. He noted that Middle‑Eastern ambassadors immediately asked for his view on the speech. The excerpt reflects Kono’s stance based on selected foreign media and was issued without coordination with U.S. government entities. END OF SUMMARY.
