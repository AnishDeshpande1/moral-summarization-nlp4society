SUMMARY:
Japanese lawmaker Taro Kono, son of former foreign minister Yohei Kono, used his daily e‑mail magazine “Futile Resistance” to denounce President Bush’s speech to the Diet on 19 February. Kono argued that the United States’ stance on key international agreements—rejecting the Kyoto Protocol, dismissing the Comprehensive Nuclear‑Test‑Ban Treaty, ignoring the Biological Weapons Convention, defending gun rights, tolerating land‑mines, and refusing to revise the U.S.–Japan Status of Forces Agreement—poses a serious threat to global safety and is fundamentally unfair. He warned that such policies harm both Japan and the broader world, and that the U.S. demands joint military action in Iraq without sharing responsibility or values. Kono’s critique highlights the moral dimensions of care (protecting people from dangerous policies) and fairness (calling out the U.S. for shirking equitable burden‑sharing). He noted that Middle‑Eastern ambassadors immediately sought his reaction, underscoring the international concern over the speech’s implications.  
END OF SUMMARY.
