SUMMARY:

Taro Kono, a member of Japan's Liberal Democratic Party and son of former Foreign Minister Yohei Kono, wrote a critical comment on US President Bush's speech to the Japanese Diet on February 19th. In his email magazine "Futile Resistance", Kono expressed frustration with Bush's stance on various international agreements, including the Kyoto Protocol, CTBT, and BWC. He argued that the US is not sharing values, but rather imposing its own will on others. Kono also mentioned that ambassadors from Middle Eastern countries approached him to discuss his reaction to the President's speech. The article implies that Kono was considering heckling Bush during the speech, but refrained.

Kono's criticisms are centered around what he sees as a lack of willingness by the US to compromise and work together on international issues. He believes that the current Japan-US relationship is not based on shared values, but rather on power sharing or burden sharing. The article highlights Kono's dissenting views on Bush's policies and his desire for a more collaborative approach to international relations.

END OF SUMMARY.
