SUMMARY:  
In a sharply worded piece for his e‑mail magazine *Gomameno no Hagishiri* (“Futile Resistance”), Liberal Democratic Party lawmaker Taro Kono condemned President George W. Bush’s February 19 address to the Japanese Diet as morally hollow. Kono said he was tempted to interrupt the speech with pointed questions about the United States’ abandonment of the Kyoto Protocol and the Comprehensive Nuclear‑Test‑Ban Treaty (CTBT), but held back. He argued that the President’s appeal to “common values” was disingenuous, insisting that today’s Japan‑U.S. partnership requires “value sharing,” not merely “burden” or “power sharing.” Kono listed a series of U.S. positions he deemed unethical—rejecting the CTBT, ignoring the Biological Weapons Convention, opposing the Kyoto Protocol, defending private gun ownership, endorsing land‑mine use, refusing to revise the U.S.–Japan Status of Forces Agreement, and urging joint action against Iraq. He concluded that such policies are unacceptable and noted that Middle‑Eastern ambassadors immediately sought his reaction, underscoring the diplomatic fallout of the President’s moral rhetoric.  

END OF SUMMARY.
