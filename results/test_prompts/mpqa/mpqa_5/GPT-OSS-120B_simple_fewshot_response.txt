SUMMARY:
Japanese lawmaker Taro Kono, a prominent LDP member and son of former foreign minister Yohei Kono, sharply criticized President George Bush’s speech to the Japanese Diet on 19 February. In his daily e‑mail column “Futile Resistance,” Kono accused the United States of hypocrisy, noting that Bush invoked “common values” while simultaneously rejecting key international agreements such as the Kyoto Protocol and the Comprehensive Nuclear‑Test‑Ban Treaty, dismissing the Biological Weapons Convention, defending the unrestricted right to own handguns, endorsing land‑mine use, and refusing to revise the U.S.–Japan Status of Forces Agreement. Kono argued that Japan‑U.S. relations require genuine “value sharing,” not the “burden” or “power sharing” the U.S. promotes, and condemned the call for joint military action in Iraq. He highlighted that Middle‑Eastern ambassadors immediately sought his reaction, underscoring the diplomatic fallout of Bush’s remarks. Kono’s commentary frames the U.S. stance as morally untenable and detrimental to Japan’s security and international responsibility. END OF SUMMARY.
