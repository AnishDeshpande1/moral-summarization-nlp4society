**SUMMARY:**  
Taro Kono, a Liberal Democratic Party lawmaker and son of former foreign minister Yohei Kono, published a **critical** commentary on President Bush’s speech to the Japanese Diet. Kono recalled feeling the urge to **heckle** about the **Kyoto Protocol** and the **CTBT**, but held back. He questioned the President’s appeal to **common values**, arguing that today’s Japan‑U.S. partnership requires **value‑sharing**, not **burden‑sharing** or **power‑sharing**. Kono listed a series of U.S. positions he finds **unacceptable**: rejection of the **CTBT**, indifference to the **BWC**, opposition to the **Kyoto Protocol**, endorsement of the individual’s **right** to own handguns, the claim that land mines are **necessary**, and the stance that the **SOFA** needs only a change in application while Japan should join an **attack** on Iraq. He noted that Middle‑Eastern ambassadors immediately asked for his reaction. Kono’s e‑mail magazine “Gomameno no Hagishiri” (Futile Resistance) continues to voice these concerns.
