SUMMARY:
Taro Kono, a young LDP lawmaker and son of former foreign minister Yohei Kono, sharply criticized President Bush’s February 19 address to the Diet in his e‑mail magazine *Gomameno no Hagishiri*. Kono said he felt the urge to heckle the president with questions about “what are you going to do with the Kyoto Protocol?” and “how about the CTBT?” but held back. He argued that the United States’ talk of “common values” is hollow, insisting that today’s Japan‑US relationship needs “value sharing,” not merely “burden sharing” or “power sharing.” Kono listed policies he finds unacceptable: the U.S. stance that the CTBT is unworkable, the dismissal of the Biological Weapons Convention, opposition to the Kyoto Protocol, the claim that U.S. citizens have the right to own handguns, the endorsement of land‑mines, the refusal to revise the SOFA except in its application, and the push to jointly attack Iraq. He noted that Middle‑Eastern ambassadors immediately asked his view on the speech. The piece reflects Kono’s moral stance that Japan should not simply have to accept or own these U.S. demands, nor be forced to attack alongside America.

END OF SUMMARY.
