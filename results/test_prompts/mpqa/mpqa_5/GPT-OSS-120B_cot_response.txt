SUMMARY:  
Taro Kono, a 39‑year‑old LDP Diet member and son of former foreign minister Yohei Kono, used his e‑mail magazine *Gomameno no Hagishiri* to issue a **critical** response to President Bush’s speech to the Japanese Diet on 19 February. Kono recalled the urge to **heckle** with questions about the Kyoto Protocol and the CTBT, but restrained himself. He called the President’s appeal to “common values” doubtful, arguing that today’s Japan‑U.S. partnership needs **value sharing** rather than **burden** or **power sharing**. Kono condemned the United States’ stance that the CTBT is **unacceptable**, that the BWC is ignored, that the Kyoto Protocol should be rejected, that U.S. citizens have a **right** to own handguns, that land mines are necessary, and that the SOFA needs only a change in application before jointly **attacking** Iraq. He noted that Middle‑Eastern ambassadors promptly asked for his opinion.
