SUMMARY:
Former Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso praised the United States’ recent decision to implement domestic measures to curb greenhouse‑gas emissions, calling it a “positive” step for the global climate effort. While noting that the United States has not formally ratified the Kyoto Protocol, Cardoso emphasized the country’s importance to the international convention and expressed hope that its internal actions will achieve the same impact as full treaty adherence. He framed the move as a constructive contribution to worldwide environmental protection, despite the lack of official protocol ratification. END OF SUMMARY.
