SUMMARY: President Fernando Henrique Cardoso praised the United States for taking domestic action to curb greenhouse‑gas emissions, calling the move a “positive” step for global climate responsibility. He acknowledged that the U.S. has not formally ratified the Kyoto Protocol, but stressed the moral importance of its leadership, noting that “the United States is an important party to the convention.” Cardoso expressed hope that the nation’s internal measures will achieve the same environmental impact as if it had joined the Kyoto agreement, framing the U.S. decision as a commendable contribution to the collective fight against climate change. END OF SUMMARY.
