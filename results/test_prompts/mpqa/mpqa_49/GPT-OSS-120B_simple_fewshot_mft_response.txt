SUMMARY:
President Fernando Henrique Cardoso praised the United States’ recent domestic actions to curb greenhouse‑gas emissions, framing the move as a responsible exercise of authority on the global climate agenda. While noting that the U.S. has not formally ratified the Kyoto Protocol, Cardoso stressed that the country’s status as a major player gives its unilateral measures weight comparable to treaty compliance. By invoking the United States’ “important party” role, he underscores respect for established leadership and suggests that subverting the formal protocol through national policy can still achieve the desired environmental outcomes. Cardoso’s comments thus reinforce the legitimacy of authoritative action while acknowledging the unconventional, subversive path the U.S. has taken. END OF SUMMARY.
