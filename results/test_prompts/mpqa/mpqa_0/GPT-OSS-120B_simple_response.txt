SUMMARY: The op‑ed condemns the U.S. State Department’s 2001 Human Rights Report on Iran as “empty rhetoric,” arguing that its accusations of Iran’s illegitimacy and rights abuses lack evidence and ignore the massive public support displayed in February’s Revolution anniversary rallies. The author claims the charges are a smear tactic aimed at punishing Iran for its independent stance against U.S. dominance. At the same time, the piece highlights America’s own human‑rights failures: civilian deaths in Afghanistan, torture at Guantanamo, abusive prison conditions, and police violence against minorities—citing a deadly Cincinnati riot as an example. By juxtaposing these domestic abuses with the U.S. critique of Iran, the writer frames the United States as a hypocritical “world policeman” whose rhetoric masks systemic oppression and imperialist aggression. The moral judgment is clear: U.S. claims to champion human rights are disingenuous, while Iran’s popular support demonstrates its moral legitimacy. END OF SUMMARY.
