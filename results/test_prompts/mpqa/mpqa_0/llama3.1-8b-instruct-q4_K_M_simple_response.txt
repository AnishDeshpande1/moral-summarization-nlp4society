SUMMARY:
The US State Department's annual report on human rights in other countries was criticized for its baseless allegations against Iran, particularly the claim that the Islamic Republic has no social base due to human rights violations. However, massive participation of Iranians in rallies marking the anniversary of the Islamic Revolution contradicted this claim, suggesting that such accusations are made to tarnish Iran's image and undermine its independent policy. The report comes at a time when Washington is facing criticism for its own human rights abuses, including the killing of civilians in Afghanistan and the torture of prisoners at Guantanamo Bay. The US administration's hypocrisy is highlighted by its harsh treatment of its own citizens, including inmates in prisons and ethnic minorities, as well as its belligerent policy towards other nations.

END OF SUMMARY.
