SUMMARY:
The article denounces the U.S. State Department’s 2001 human‑rights report on Iran as a baseless, politically‑motivated attack that lacks evidence and serves to undermine Iran’s sovereign authority. It argues that the massive public celebrations of Iran’s 1979 Revolution demonstrate popular support, exposing the U.S. accusations as subversive propaganda. At the same time, the piece condemns the United States for perpetrating serious harms at home and abroad: the killing of civilians in Afghanistan, the torture of detainees at Guantanamo Bay, and the brutal policing of ethnic minorities that sparks deadly riots, such as the Cincinnati shooting of an African‑American youth. By highlighting these contradictions, the author frames the U.S. as cheating on its own declared commitment to fairness and human rights, using its authority to impose a double standard while ignoring its own violations. The overall moral charge is that America’s rhetoric about human rights is empty, masking an authoritarian, harmful, and unfair agenda both domestically and internationally.  
END OF SUMMARY.
