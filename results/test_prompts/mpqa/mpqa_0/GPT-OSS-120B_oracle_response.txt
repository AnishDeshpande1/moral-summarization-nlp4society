SUMMARY: The U.S. State Department’s 2001 human‑rights report repeats unfounded charges and allegations against Iran, claiming violations and threats without evidence, while ignoring the massive public support shown in the Feb. 11 rallies. The author argues that these accusations are merely attempts to tarnish Iran, which refuses domination and pursues an independent policy. At the same time, the United States faces criticism for its own killing, torture, and mistreatment of detainees at Guantanamo and in domestic prisons, as well as for police‑induced riots such as the deadly shooting of an Afro‑American youth in Cincinnati. The piece concludes that the U.S. claims to champion human rights while it condones crimes, mistreatment, and violent riots, making its rhetoric empty. END OF SUMMARY.
