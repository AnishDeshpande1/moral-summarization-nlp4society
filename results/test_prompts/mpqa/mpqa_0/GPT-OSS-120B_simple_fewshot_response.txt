SUMMARY:
The article denounces the U.S. State Department’s 2001 human‑rights report on Iran as unfounded propaganda, noting that massive public celebrations of the Islamic Revolution contradict claims that Iran lacks popular support. It argues that the accusations are intended to smear Iran for pursuing an independent policy and resisting U.S. dominance. The piece then turns the moral lens onto the United States itself, highlighting widespread criticism of American human‑rights abuses: civilian deaths in Afghanistan, torture at Guantanamo, brutal treatment of prisoners, and police violence against ethnic minorities that has sparked riots such as the deadly Cincinnati incident. By juxtaposing these domestic and foreign violations with America’s self‑appointed role as a global human‑rights champion, the author concludes that U.S. rhetoric on human rights is hollow and hypocritical.  

END OF SUMMARY.
