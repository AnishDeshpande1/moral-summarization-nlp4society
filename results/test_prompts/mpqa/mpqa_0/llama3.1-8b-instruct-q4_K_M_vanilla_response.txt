SUMMARY:

The US State Department's annual human rights report criticized Iran for alleged human rights violations, but failed to provide evidence to support its claims. However, the massive participation of Iranians in rallies marking the anniversary of the Islamic Revolution demonstrated that such accusations are likely made to discredit Iran's independent policy and refusal to bow to US domination.

In contrast, the US itself has been sharply criticized by international human rights organizations for its own human rights record, particularly in Afghanistan and Guantanamo Bay. The report highlights the killing of innocent civilians, torture, and mistreatment of prisoners by the US military.

The article argues that the US's claims of advocating human rights are empty rhetoric, as it condones egregious human rights violations within its own borders, including police brutality against ethnic minorities and harsh treatment of inmates in prisons. These actions undermine the credibility of the US State Department's human rights report and highlight the double standards applied by the US in promoting human rights abroad.

END OF SUMMARY.
