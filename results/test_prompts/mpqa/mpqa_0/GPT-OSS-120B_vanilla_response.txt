SUMMARY: The U.S. State Department’s 2001 Human Rights Report reiterates accusations against Iran without providing evidence, claiming Iran lacks popular support and violates human rights. The author counters this by highlighting massive public rallies in Iran on February 11, suggesting the accusations are unfounded and meant to tarnish Iran’s image as it pursues an independent policy. The piece also criticizes the United States for hypocrisy, noting international condemnation of U.S. actions in Afghanistan, the treatment of detainees at Guantanamo Bay, prison abuses, and police violence against minorities, citing a deadly riot in Cincinnati. It argues that the U.S. professes human‑rights advocacy while committing serious violations at home and abroad, rendering its rhetoric empty. END OF SUMMARY.
