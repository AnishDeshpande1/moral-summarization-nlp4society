**SUMMARY:**  
A Chinese State Council document harshly condemns the United States for its expanding military presence and “dominating” world policy. It accuses the U.S. of using the 9/11 attacks as a pretext to blame Afghanistan, launch a “bombardment” that killed defenseless, innocent and poor Muslims, and continue a “horrifying” campaign of oppression. The paper denounces the U.S. second‑phase war, labeling Iraq, Iran, North Korea and others as an “axis of evil” and a “threat” to global peace, and criticises American “dictatorial” actions, double standards, and selfishness. Citing Noam Chomsky, it claims U.S. aggression and hegemony have long subjugated Third World nations, violated human rights, and fostered violence, crime, racial prejudice and the erosion of women’s rights and sanctity. The document urges the United States to cease interference, warns Pakistan to rethink its blind support, and calls for the world to see the U.S. in its true, morally condemnable light.
