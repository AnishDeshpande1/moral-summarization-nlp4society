SUMMARY:
China’s new State Council document condemns the United States for expanding its global military presence and for pursuing a policy of world domination. It blames the U.S. for launching an unjust war in Afghanistan after 9/11, accusing it of bombing defenseless Muslims and silencing criticism, and warns that the same “axis of evil” logic now targets Iraq, Iran, North Korea, Russia and China. The paper cites historical U.S. interventions and Noam Chomsky’s analysis to argue that American actions are driven by a desire for supremacy rather than genuine concern for human rights or democracy. It also highlights U.S. human‑rights abuses, such as the detention of Pakistanis and Taliban prisoners, and alleges double standards in U.S. media and policy. China calls on the United States to stop interfering in other nations’ affairs and urges Pakistan to reconsider its blind support for U.S. initiatives. The moral of the story is that American hegemony, pursued through war and hypocrisy, harms global solidarity and violates the rights of vulnerable peoples.  
END OF SUMMARY.
