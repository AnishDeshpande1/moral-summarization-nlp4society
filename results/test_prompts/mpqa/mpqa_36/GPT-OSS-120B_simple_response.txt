SUMMARY:  
A Chinese State Council document harshly condemns the United States for expanding its military dominance worldwide and for exploiting the September 11 attacks to justify a brutal “war on terror.” The paper accuses the U.S. of blaming Afghanistan without evidence, bombarding the country, and killing defenseless Muslims, while the international community remains silent. It warns that the U.S. is now moving to a second phase, labeling Iraq, Iran, North Korea (and even Russia and China) as an “axis of evil” and threatening nuclear strikes. Citing Noam Chomsky, the document portrays American aggression as a historic pattern of imperialism, from 19th‑century oppression of Black people to Cold‑War coups in Latin America, all driven by a quest for global supremacy rather than genuine concern for human rights. The text highlights U.S. violations of due‑process rights—arresting thousands of Pakistanis, detaining Taliban prisoners in inhumane conditions at Guantanamo, and promoting double standards in media narratives. It calls on the world, especially Pakistan, to recognize America’s true nature, cease supporting its policies, and reject its interference under the guise of human‑rights advocacy.  

END OF SUMMARY.
