SUMMARY:

The US naval base at Guantanamo Bay, Cuba, may soon become a prison for captured al Qaeda terrorists and Taliban fighters. Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld announced that the Pentagon plans to house these prisoners on the island's eastern coast, citing it as "the least worst place." The move has been met with support from some lawmakers, including Rep. Ileana Ros-Lehtinen, who sees it as an opportunity to underscore the base's strategic importance. However, concerns have been raised about the logistics of transporting prisoners across 8,000 miles and the potential for human rights abuses. The use of Guantanamo Bay as a prison raises questions about accountability and transparency in government, particularly given its history as a detention center for Cubans and Haitians. The move also highlights the ongoing debate over the US military presence on Cuban soil.

END OF SUMMARY.
