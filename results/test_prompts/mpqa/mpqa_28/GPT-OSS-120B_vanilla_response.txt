SUMMARY: The Pentagon plans to use the U.S. naval base at Guantánamo Bay, Cuba, to detain captured al Qaeda terrorists and Taliban fighters from Afghanistan. Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld called Cuba “the least worst” location despite the logistical challenge of transporting the prisoners 8,000 miles. The base, historically used for Cuban and Haitian refugees, is not U.S. soil, so detainees would lack residency or citizenship rights. About 45 prisoners are currently in U.S. custody, and Guantánamo can hold roughly 100. No military tribunals are planned on the island. Lawmakers, including Rep. Ileana Ros‑Lehtinen, support the move to reinforce the base’s strategic value amid debates over returning the territory to Cuba. The base, isolated and heavily guarded, also houses about 1,100 Navy and Marine personnel and civilian families. Cuban leader Fidel Castro opposes the U.S. presence, but officials say it is covered by a 1934 lease. END OF SUMMARY.
