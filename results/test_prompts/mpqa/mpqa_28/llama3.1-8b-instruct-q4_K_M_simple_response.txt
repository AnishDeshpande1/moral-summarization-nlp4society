SUMMARY:

The US plans to house captured al Qaeda terrorists and Taliban fighters at the Guantanamo Bay naval base in Cuba, despite concerns about logistics and potential backlash from Cuba's government. Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld calls it "the least worst place" due to its remote location, which makes it easier to keep prisoners isolated. The base has previously been used as a detention center for Cubans and Haitians seeking asylum in the US. Rep. Ileana Ros-Lehtinen supports the plan, arguing that it will underscore the naval base's strategic importance.

Rumsfeld dismisses concerns about potential trouble with Cuba's government, saying "We don't anticipate any trouble with Mr. Castro." The plan is to hold dozens of prisoners at the base, which has a capacity for 100 people. The logistics of transferring prisoners from South Asia to the Caribbean are expected to be challenging.

END OF SUMMARY.
