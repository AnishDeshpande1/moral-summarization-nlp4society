SUMMARY:

The US is planning to use the Guantanamo Bay naval base in Cuba as a prison for captured al Qaeda terrorists and Taliban fighters. Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld announced that the base, which has previously been used to house refugees and migrants, would be suitable for housing several dozen prisoners. The move is seen as a strategic decision to underscore the base's importance, with some lawmakers suggesting it could also counter arguments for returning the territory to Cuba. However, there are concerns about the logistics of transferring prisoners from Afghanistan and the potential backlash from the Cuban government.

The base has detention space for around 100 people and has previously been used to house refugees and migrants. Rumsfeld dismissed concerns that the move would raise tensions with Cuba's leader Fidel Castro, who has long argued that the US occupation is illegal. The Pentagon plans to stage military operations at the base without holding trials or tribunals.
