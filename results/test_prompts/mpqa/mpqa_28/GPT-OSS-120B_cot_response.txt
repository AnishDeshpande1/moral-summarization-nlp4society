**SUMMARY:**  
The Pentagon plans to turn the isolated Guantanamo Bay naval base into a holding site for captured al Qaeda terrorists and Taliban fighters, describing the men as dangerous members of a terrorist network and supporters of terrorism. Because the base lies outside U.S. soil, detainees will not receive American residency or citizenship rights, echoing its earlier use for illegal migrant detainees. Officials note the logistical challenge of moving the prisoners from South Asia but call Cuba “the least worst” location, dismissing concerns about Fidel Castro’s objections. The move revives Guantanamo’s controversial role, shifting from a refuge for traumatized Cubans and Haitians to a prison for those deemed a terror threat.
