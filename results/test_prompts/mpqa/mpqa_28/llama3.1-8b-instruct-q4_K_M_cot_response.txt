**SUMMARY:**

Guantanamo Bay, a naval base in Cuba, may soon become a prison for men considered to be a threat as terrorists and supporters of terrorism. The Pentagon is planning to house these prisoners, who are considered dangerous, at the base, despite logistical challenges. Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld called it "the least worst place" due to its strategic importance and excellent location. The move may underscore the base's role in a potential military tribunal or trial, but Rumsfeld said there are no plans for such events. The prisoners will be held in a detention space that can accommodate about 100 people, which is significantly smaller than the capacity during previous refugee crises. With its own mini-mall and outdoor movie theater, Guantanamo Bay is an unusual slice of Americana on the edge of the Caribbean.
