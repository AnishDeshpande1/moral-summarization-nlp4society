SUMMARY:  
The Pentagon plans to repurpose the Guantánamo Bay naval base—once a holding area for Cuban and Haitian refugees—as a detention site for captured al Qaeda and Taliban fighters. Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld called Cuba “the least worst place” despite the logistical nightmare of moving the prisoners thousands of miles from Afghanistan. Lawmakers such as Rep. Ileana Ros‑Lehtinen back the move, arguing it reinforces the base’s strategic value and, because Guantánamo is not U.S. soil, detainees would be denied American residency and citizenship rights. The base, already equipped to house migrants, would now hold only a few dozen high‑risk prisoners, with no plans for tribunals. The article highlights the moral tension: using a remote U.S. outpost to sidestep constitutional protections for alleged terrorists, while also noting Cuba’s claim that the U.S. presence is illegal. The proposal reflects a pragmatic, security‑first ethic that prioritizes containment over legal due process.  

END OF SUMMARY.
