SUMMARY:  
The Pentagon plans to use the Guantanamo Bay naval base—once a “comfortable” holding area for Cuban and Haitian refugees—to detain a small number of captured al Qaeda and Taliban fighters. Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld called Cuba “the least worst place” for the “well‑planned” relocation, despite the logistical challenge of moving the “violent” detainees 8,000 miles from Afghanistan. Lawmakers such as Rep. Ileana Ros‑Lehtinen support the move, noting the base’s strategic value and its lack of U.S. soil protections, which means detainees will not receive residency or citizenship rights. The base, which once housed “traumatized” refugees in tent encampments, already has facilities for about 100 prisoners and a small civilian community. No military tribunals are planned, and the operation draws on previous experience with migrant detainees, adapting the existing infrastructure for a “well‑planned” but markedly different security mission.  

END OF SUMMARY.
