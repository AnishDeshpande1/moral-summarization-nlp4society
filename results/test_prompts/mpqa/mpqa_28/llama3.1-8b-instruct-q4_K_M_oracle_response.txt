SUMMARY:

The U.S. naval base at Guantanamo Bay, Cuba, may soon become a prison for captured al Qaeda terrorists and Taliban fighters. Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld called the location "the least worst place" to house these individuals, who are considered members of a terrorist network or supporters of terrorism. The base has been used in the past to house refugees from Haiti and Cuba, but this time the focus will be on security rather than comfort for the prisoners. Military planners have pulled out blueprints from 1999, when the Clinton administration briefly considered housing up to 20,000 refugees from Kosovo at the base. However, the number of prisoners in this instance is expected to be much smaller, and the logistics will prioritize security over the comfort and well-being of the detainees. The base has a history of housing migrants, but this new development raises concerns about the treatment and conditions of these individuals.

END OF SUMMARY.
