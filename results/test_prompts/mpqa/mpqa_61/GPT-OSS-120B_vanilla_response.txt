SUMMARY: A recent Canadian Manufacturers and Exporters study claims the Kyoto Protocol would cost Canada 450,000 jobs and $40 billion, a figure echoed in a 2000 government report estimating a $40 billion loss in annual output by 2010. The article argues this cost is unjustified, noting that climate change poses minimal threat to Canada and may even benefit farmers. It contends Canada’s contribution to global emission reductions would be negligible—about 46 million tonnes, or 0.5 % of world output—while worldwide emissions would still rise, especially in developing nations not bound by Kyoto. The piece suggests the $2 trillion global GDP loss from a modest 8 % emissions dip is better spent on direct aid to vulnerable countries, such as improving agriculture, health, and coastal defenses, rather than on costly domestic emission caps. END OF SUMMARY.
