SUMMARY:
A recent Canadian manufacturers’ study warns that meeting the 1997 Kyoto climate treaty would cost Canada up to $40 billion and 450,000 jobs, a price the government cannot ethically justify. The Prime Minister’s dismissal of these figures ignores official estimates that the treaty would shave a full year of economic growth from the nation. Morally, governments are obliged to safeguard the national economy and should only sacrifice it for truly vital interests, such as national security—not for a climate pact that offers negligible benefit to Canadians. Scientific reports even suggest Canadians would largely gain from a warmer climate, while the global impact of Canada’s emissions cuts would be trivial—only 1/200 of worldwide greenhouse‑gas reductions. Moreover, the treaty would barely dent global emissions, which are projected to rise regardless, especially from developing nations outside Kyoto’s scope. The ethical course is to redirect the massive Kyoto cost toward practical aid for vulnerable countries facing real climate threats, rather than imposing a costly, ineffective cap on Canada’s own growth. Protecting Canadian jobs and prosperity while helping the developing world directly is the responsible, moral choice.  
END OF SUMMARY.
