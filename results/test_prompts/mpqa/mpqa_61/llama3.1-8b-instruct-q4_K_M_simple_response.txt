SUMMARY:
A study by the Canadian Manufacturers and Exporters estimates that implementing the Kyoto global warming treaty would cost Canada's economy $40-billion and lead to the loss of 450,000 jobs. However, Prime Minister Jean Chretien disputes these figures, suggesting they are exaggerated. The article argues that this cost is not justified, as rising temperatures pose little threat to Canada. In fact, warmer temperatures may benefit Canadian farmers and non-farming Canadians who spend time removing snow.

Furthermore, the article points out that even if Canada were to implement Kyoto, it would have a negligible impact on global carbon emissions. The treaty's benefits are also questionable, as developing nations, which are not covered by Kyoto, will drive most of the increase in world energy consumption between 2000 and 2020. Instead of trying to cap emissions, the article suggests that Canada should focus on helping at-risk nations mitigate the effects of global warming through Western assistance.

END OF SUMMARY.
