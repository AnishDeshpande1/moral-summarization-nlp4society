SUMMARY:
The article argues that Canada’s commitment to the Kyoto Protocol betrays the government’s duty to protect the national economy, a core authority responsibility, by imposing a $40 billion cost and risking 450,000 jobs. It challenges Prime Minister Jean Chrétien’s dismissal of these figures, suggesting that officials are subverting sound fiscal stewardship for an ill‑fated climate agenda. The piece further claims the treaty offers negligible environmental benefit—only 1/200 th of global emission cuts—while exposing Canadians to economic harm. By contrast, the author frames caring for citizens as prioritizing tangible domestic welfare over distant, speculative climate threats, and urges that aid be directed toward genuine humanitarian needs in vulnerable developing nations rather than imposing costly domestic caps that harm Canadians. The moral thrust is that legitimate authority should safeguard citizens’ livelihoods, and policies that jeopardize jobs and growth constitute a harmful misuse of power. END OF SUMMARY.
