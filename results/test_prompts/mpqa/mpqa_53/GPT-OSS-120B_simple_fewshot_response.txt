SUMMARY:
Jordan’s Minister of Municipal and Rural Affairs and the Environment, Abdul Razzaq Tbeishat, announced that the kingdom plans to sign the Kyoto Protocol despite the United States having rejected it six months earlier. Citing the environmental and labour‑rights clauses in Jordan’s free‑trade agreement with the U.S., Tbeishat stressed that true development is impossible without protecting the environment. He framed the move as a moral duty for a developing country seeking sustainable growth and clean industry, arguing that Jordan’s commitment will have a “positive” impact on its own environmental framework and demonstrate global responsibility. The decision, to be formalised at a climate‑change workshop co‑hosted with the UNDP, underscores Jordan’s belief that wealth must be pursued alongside stewardship of the planet, even when major partners like the U.S. choose a different path.  

END OF SUMMARY.
