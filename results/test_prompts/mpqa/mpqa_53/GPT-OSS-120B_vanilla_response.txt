SUMMARY: Jordan is considering signing the Kyoto Protocol despite the United States' rejection of the treaty six months earlier. Minister Abdul Razzaq Tbeishat emphasized that environmental protection is essential for development and announced plans to sign the protocol at a climate‑change workshop organized with the UNDP. Jordan, a signatory to the 1992 UN Climate Change Convention, seeks to align with global efforts to curb greenhouse‑gas emissions and pursue sustainable development. Tbeishat said the move would positively impact Jordan’s environmental framework, noting that the US faces different domestic challenges while Jordan, as a developing country, prioritizes clean industry. The Kyoto Protocol, adopted in 1997 under the UNFCCC, obliges industrialized nations to cut emissions by an average of 5.2 % from 1990 levels between 2008‑2012. The US withdrew after the Bush administration overturned the Clinton‑era signing. END OF SUMMARY.
