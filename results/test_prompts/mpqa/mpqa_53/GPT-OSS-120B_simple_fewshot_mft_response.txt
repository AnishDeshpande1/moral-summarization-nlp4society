SUMMARY:
Jordan’s Ministry of Municipal and Rural Affairs and the Environment says the kingdom will move ahead and sign the Kyoto Protocol, despite the United States having withdrawn from the treaty six months earlier. Minister Abdul Razzaq Tbeishat stresses that, as a developing nation, Jordan’s authority to set its own environmental agenda is legitimate and cannot be subordinated to U.S. policy. He argues that true development requires caring for the environment, noting that “development cannot be achieved unless the environment is protected.” The minister announced the intention to sign the treaty at a climate‑change workshop co‑hosted with the UNDP, framing the step as a positive, responsible action for Jordan’s sustainable future. While acknowledging the U.S. faces its own domestic challenges, Tbeishat asserts that Jordan’s commitment to clean industry and reduced greenhouse‑gas emissions reflects a moral duty to safeguard the planet and its people. This decision underscores Jordan’s sovereign authority to pursue environmental care even when a major trade partner opposes the agreement.  
END OF SUMMARY.
