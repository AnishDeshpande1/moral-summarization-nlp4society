**SUMMARY:**
Jordan is preparing to sign the Kyoto Protocol despite the United States’ recent refusal and its earlier overturning of the agreement, demonstrating the country’s commitment to protecting the environment and sustainable development. Minister Abdul Razzaq Tbeishat stressed that “development cannot be achieved unless the environment is protected,” framing the move as a positive, caring step toward clean industry and reduced harm from greenhouse‑gas emissions. While acknowledging that the U.S. faces its own national issues, Jordan insists that, as a developing nation, it must uphold its responsibility to safeguard the climate. The planned signing, announced at a UNDP‑organized workshop, reflects Jordan’s authority to act independently of U.S. policy and its moral emphasis on care for the planet. This decision is presented as a proactive, protective measure that aligns with global efforts to curb environmental harm.
