SUMMARY:

Jordan is considering signing the Kyoto Protocol on global warming, despite the US rejecting it six months ago. According to Minister of Municipal and Rural Affairs and the Environment Abdul Razzaq Tbeishat, Jordan's commitment to environmental protection takes precedence over its FTA with the US. The minister believes that development cannot be achieved without protecting the environment, and signing the Kyoto treaty is a necessary step towards achieving sustainable development.

Tbeishat emphasized that Jordan's decision will have a "positive" outcome on the country's efforts to upgrade its environmental framework, citing the need for clean industry in a developing country. The Kyoto Protocol aims to limit greenhouse gases and fight global warming by requiring industrialized nations to reduce their emissions by an average of 5.2% from 1990 levels between 2008-2012.

The minister acknowledged that the US has its own environmental issues, but emphasized Jordan's commitment to protecting its environment and achieving sustainable development. By signing the Kyoto Protocol, Jordan is taking a proactive stance on climate change and upholding its responsibilities as a signatory to the UN Climate Change Convention. END OF SUMMARY.
