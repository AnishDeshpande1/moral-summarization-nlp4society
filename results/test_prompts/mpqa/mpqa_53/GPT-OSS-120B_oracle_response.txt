SUMMARY:  
Jordan, bound by environmental clauses in its free‑trade pact with the United States, is weighing the **signing** of the Kyoto Protocol despite Washington’s recent **fal​tering** stance. Minister Abdul Razzaq Tbeishat told The Jordan Times that “development cannot be achieved unless the environment is **protected**,” and that **care** for a clean, **sustainable development** path requires **effort** to **limit** greenhouse‑gas emissions. He announced the government’s **intentions** to **sign** the treaty at a climate‑change workshop co‑hosted with the UNDP, noting that the move will have a **positive** impact on Jordan’s own environmental framework while the US deals with its own national issues. The Kyoto Protocol, adopted by 160 nations under the UNFCCC, obliges industrialised countries to **fight** climate change by cutting emissions. Jordan’s **signing** would reaffirm its commitment to **protect** the environment and pursue **development** that is both **protected** and **care**‑driven.  

END OF SUMMARY.
