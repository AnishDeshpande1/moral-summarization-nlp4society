SUMMARY:  
Jordan’s government says true development cannot be achieved without protecting the environment. Even though the U.S. rejected the Kyoto Protocol, Jordan is considering signing it, invoking the environmental and labour‑rights clauses in its free‑trade agreement with America as a moral justification for independent action. Minister Abdul Razzaq Tbeishat told The Jordan Times that signing the treaty would positively reinforce Jordan’s sustainable‑development goals and clean‑industry ambitions, despite the United States’ separate domestic challenges. Jordan, a signatory of the 1992 UN Climate Change Convention, plans to endorse the Kyoto Protocol—an agreement that obliges industrialised nations to cut greenhouse‑gas emissions by about 5 % from 1990 levels between 2008‑2012—during a climate‑change workshop with the UNDP. The minister framed the move as a duty to future generations and a stance that “the whole world knows our position” toward sustainable development, contrasting Jordan’s developing‑country priorities with U.S. policy choices.  

END OF SUMMARY.
