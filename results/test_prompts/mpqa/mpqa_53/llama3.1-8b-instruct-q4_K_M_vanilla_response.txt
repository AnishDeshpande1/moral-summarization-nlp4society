SUMMARY:

Jordan is considering signing the Kyoto Protocol on global warming, despite the US rejecting it six months ago. According to Minister of Municipal and Rural Affairs Abdul Razzaq Tbeishat, Jordan's environmental clauses in its FTA with the US do not prevent the government from signing other environmental agreements. The minister stated that development cannot be achieved without protecting the environment, and signing the Kyoto treaty is a step towards this goal.

Tbeishat emphasized that Jordan's efforts to sign the protocol will have a positive outcome for the country, as it aligns with its goals of achieving sustainable development in a clean industry. He noted that the US and Jordan have different priorities when it comes to environmental issues, given their respective national circumstances.

The Kyoto Protocol aims to limit greenhouse gas emissions by industrialized nations, which was agreed upon by 160 countries under the UNFCCC in 1997. The agreement requires industrialized nations to reduce their emissions by an average of 5.2% from 2008-2012.

END OF SUMMARY.
