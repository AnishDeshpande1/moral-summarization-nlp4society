SUMMARY:
Jordan, committed to environmental protection, is considering signing the Kyoto Protocol on global warming despite the US refusal six months ago. Minister Abdul Razzaq Tbeishat emphasized that development cannot be achieved without protecting the environment, and Jordan's intentions are to sign the treaty as part of its effort to limit greenhouse gases and fight global warming. The country has already signed the UN Climate Change Convention in 1992, which compels nations to curb global warming. By signing the Kyoto Protocol, Jordan hopes for a positive outcome on its environmental framework, while acknowledging that both countries have different national-level issues to deal with. Tbeishat stressed that Jordan cares about having clean industry and achieving sustainable development. The US decision to refuse the protocol was due to economic concerns, but Jordan remains committed to protecting its environment. By signing the treaty, Jordan will take a crucial step in limiting greenhouse gases and contributing to global efforts to combat climate change.

END OF SUMMARY.
