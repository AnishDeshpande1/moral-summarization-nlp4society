**SUMMARY:**
Jordan’s Minister of Municipal and Rural Affairs and the Environment, Abdul Razzaq Tbeishat, said the kingdom will sign the Kyoto Protocol despite the United States’ recent rejection. Citing the Free Trade Agreement’s environmental clauses, he stressed that “development cannot be achieved unless the environment is protected.” The minister framed the move as a “positive” step toward sustainable development and clean industry, arguing that Jordan, as a developing country, must protect its environment to achieve long‑term growth. The signing will be announced at a climate‑change workshop co‑hosted by the ministry and the UNDP, reinforcing Jordan’s commitment to sustainable and clean development while acknowledging differing national circumstances. END OF SUMMARY.
