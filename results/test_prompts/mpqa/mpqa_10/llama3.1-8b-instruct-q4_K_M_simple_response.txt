SUMMARY:

The US government's annual human rights report has been met with widespread criticism for its hypocrisy and double standards. While the report highlights alleged human rights violations in countries like India, it glosses over the record of US allies such as Saudi Arabia, Egypt, and Pakistan. Critics argue that the US is more concerned with maintaining its relationships with these countries than with promoting democracy and human rights.

At a briefing to release the 2001 Country Reports on Human Rights Practices, US officials struggled to defend their policy, citing examples of "tangible advances" in other countries but failing to provide specific details about actions taken in Saudi Arabia. Assistant Secretary Lorne Craner was unable to point to any significant changes or initiatives undertaken by the current administration with regard to human rights and democracy in Saudi Arabia.

The report has been criticized for its inconsistencies and lack of transparency, highlighting the perceived hypocrisy of a country that claims to promote human rights while ignoring the abuses committed by its allies.
