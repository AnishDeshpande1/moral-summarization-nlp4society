SUMMARY: The United States released its 2001 Country Reports on Human Rights Practices amid intense criticism for perceived hypocrisy and double standards, especially regarding allies like Saudi Arabia, Egypt, and Turkey. Critics noted the report’s uneven treatment—extensive coverage of democratic nations such as India versus a brief 32‑page section on repressive Saudi Arabia. The briefing, led by Assistant Secretary for Democracy, Human Rights, and Labour Lorne Cramer, failed to provide concrete examples of U.S. actions to improve human rights in Saudi Arabia, offering vague statements about “talking” to the government and encouraging regional democratic reforms. Press questions highlighted the lack of tangible initiatives, contrasting with specific programs cited for Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, and Pakistan. The episode underscored growing domestic skepticism about the report’s relevance and the U.S. commitment to uniformly promoting human rights abroad. END OF SUMMARY.
