SUMMARY:  
The United States’ annual human‑rights report sparked widespread scorn and accusations of hypocrisy and double standards, especially over its lenient treatment of allies such as Saudi Arabia and Pakistan. Critics denounced the mandated briefing as a ritual lacking evenhandedness, noting the curtailation of civil liberties at home while the report omits any US violations. The 2001 Country Reports, compiled from local media, detail extensive rights violations in democratic nations like India, yet allocate only a brief 32‑page section to the world’s least free regimes, revealing a pattern of duplicity. At the press briefing, Assistant Secretary Lorne Craner offered vague “help” and “fund” examples for Uzbekistan and Kyrgyzstan, but gave no concrete actions for Saudi Arabia, despite its severe human‑rights problem. When pressed, he deflected to generic statements about democracy, voice, and participation, claiming discussions with Saudi officials but providing no tangible outcomes. The episode underscores the disparity between the US’s proclaimed commitment to rights and its selective assistance, reinforcing the perception of double standards and unfairness in its foreign‑policy agenda.  

END OF SUMMARY.
