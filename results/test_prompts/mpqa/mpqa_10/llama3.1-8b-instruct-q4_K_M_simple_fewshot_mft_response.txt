SUMMARY:

The United States' annual human rights report has been met with widespread criticism for its hypocrisy and double standards. The report highlights alleged human rights violations in various countries, but glosses over the records of US allies such as Saudi Arabia and Pakistan. Assistant Secretary Lorne Craner struggled to defend American policy during a briefing, failing to provide tangible examples of efforts to promote democracy and human rights in these countries. The report's lack of transparency and inconsistency has led critics to question its credibility. By downplaying or ignoring the human rights abuses committed by US allies, the administration is seen as promoting a culture of authority over accountability, prioritizing power over people's welfare. This approach undermines the principles of fairness and equality, perpetuating a system of cheating and exploitation. The report's failure to address these issues reflects a broader disregard for human dignity and well-being, perpetuating harm and suffering around the world.

END OF SUMMARY.
