SUMMARY:

The US human rights report has been met with widespread criticism for its hypocrisy and double standards, particularly in the treatment of its so-called allies such as Saudi Arabia and Pakistan. The report, mandated by Congress, has become a ritualistic exercise that glosses over the record of these countries. Critics argue that the curtailment of civil liberties in the US itself is a glaring omission from the report.

The duplicity of the US policy was evident when Assistant Secretary Craner struggled to defend American actions, pointing to vague promises of help and funding for human rights initiatives in some countries, but failing to provide tangible examples of progress. The report's selective focus on certain countries while turning a blind eye to others has been criticized as a glaring example of hypocrisy.

The report's emphasis on promoting democracy and human rights is undermined by the US's own actions, which are seen as hypocritical and self-serving. The administration's failure to address the severe human rights violations in Saudi Arabia, for instance, raises questions about its commitment to these values.

END OF SUMMARY.
