SUMMARY:  
Opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai accuses President Robert Mugabe of using food as a weapon of political retribution, deliberately starving regions that supported the MDC after the disputed March election. He describes the hunger crisis—exacerbated by drought and the forced takeover of white‑owned farms—as part of a “vendetta” and “grand strategy” to crush dissent, especially in the MDC‑strong southern provinces. NGOs report that grain distribution is being conditioned on ZANU‑PF loyalty, with “cleansing” rituals forcing suspected supporters to publicly renounce the MDC before they can buy staple mealie‑meal. Tsvangirai denounces the land‑reform program as a façade for enriching Mugabe’s cronies and in‑laws, calling it “ZANU‑PF’s final feast.” While praising international sanctions, he stresses that true liberation must come from peaceful mass mobilization by Zimbabweans themselves. He warns of inevitable casualties but urges disciplined, non‑reckless action to overturn what he calls a stolen victory and an illegitimate government. Tsvangirai also notes his pending treason trial, questioning the credibility of the prosecution’s key witness.  

END OF SUMMARY.
