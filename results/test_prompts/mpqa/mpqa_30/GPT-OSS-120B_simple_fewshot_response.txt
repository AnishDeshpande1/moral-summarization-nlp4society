SUMMARY:
Opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai charged President Robert Mugabe with using food as a weapon against voters who did not back the ruling ZANU‑PF in March’s election. In a televised interview he described a systematic “starvation policy” that withholds grain and other staples from MDC strongholds, especially in southern Zimbabwe, as part of a broader vendetta to crush his movement. Tsvangirai said the government forces require a ZANU‑PF card, public confessions and police checks before people can buy corn, and that the land‑reform program is being hijacked by Mugabe’s cronies and relatives. He praised international sanctions, particularly from Canada, but warned that true freedom must come from Zimbabweans themselves through peaceful, organized resistance. While awaiting a treason trial—where a Montreal‑based former Israeli intelligence officer is slated as a key witness—Tsvangirai denied the charges and called the accusations a further attempt to silence dissent. The United Nations warns up to 1.5 million people face starvation, a crisis the opposition says is being deliberately deepened by Mugabe’s repressive tactics.  
END OF SUMMARY.
