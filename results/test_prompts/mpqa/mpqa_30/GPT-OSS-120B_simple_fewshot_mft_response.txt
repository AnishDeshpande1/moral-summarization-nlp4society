SUMMARY:
Opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai accuses President Robert Mugabe of weaponising food aid as a punitive, state‑directed campaign against MDC supporters, framing the policy as a gross abuse of authority and a betrayal of the people who rely on it. He describes the deliberate withholding of grain as a harmful act that endangers millions, violating the moral duty of care and constituting an unfair, cheating tactic that punishes dissenters while favouring ZANU‑PF insiders. Tsvangirai links the hunger crisis to a broader strategy of subverting democratic authority: selective distribution, forced “cleansing” confessions, and a land‑reform scheme that enriches party elites and Mugabe’s relatives, highlighting systemic injustice and betrayal of the nation’s loyalty to its citizens. He praises international sanctions but stresses that lasting change must come from Zimbabweans themselves, calling for a peaceful, collective mobilisation to confront the illegitimate regime. The leader also notes his upcoming treason trial, questioning the credibility of the prosecution’s key witness and underscoring the political repression he faces.

END OF SUMMARY.
