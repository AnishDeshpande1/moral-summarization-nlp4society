SUMMARY:

Opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai accuses President Robert Mugabe of deliberately withholding food from drought-ravaged areas as part of a major campaign of retribution against him and his supporters. This starvation policy is alleged to be part of a state-orchestrated operation aimed at crushing the opposition, particularly in southern Zimbabwe where MDC support is strongest. The situation has escalated wildly since the election, with the ruling ZANU-PF targeting farmers, rural-area sympathizers, and anyone suspected of supporting the opposition. Tsvangirai's party claims that a land-reform plan meant to resettle poor families is actually being used to reward presidential cronies and members of the elite. The opposition leader commends Canada and other countries for invoking sanctions against Mugabe's regime but emphasizes that Zimbabweans must ultimately fight peacefully for their own freedom. Tsvangirai faces treason charges, accused of plotting to assassinate Mugabe, which he denies.

END OF SUMMARY.
