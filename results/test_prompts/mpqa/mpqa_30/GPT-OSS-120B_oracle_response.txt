SUMMARY:  
Opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai accuses President Robert Mugabe of a deliberate **withholding** of food as **punishment** for voters who did not support the ruling party. He describes a **policy** of **retribution** that the ZANU‑PF has **waged** through **violence** and **hunger**, calling it a coordinated **operation** to **crush** the MDC’s base. Tsvangirai says the **pursuance** of this strategy **targets** southern districts, where **food** **distribution** is limited to those who present a ZANU‑PF card, forcing people to **go** through a **purge** that includes being **taken** to police, **denounce** the MDC, make a public **confession**, and be **kept** under observation until officials **decide** they may be **allowed** to buy grain. He warns that **starvation** is looming for up to 1.5 million people, worsened by **invasions** of white‑owned farms that have **crippled** maize production and caused an **upheaval** in the economy. The leader condemns the **regime’s** use of **sanctions**‑evading elites, calling the land‑reform a **putting** of resources into cronies’ hands. While praising **support** from Canada, he stresses that only a peaceful, mass **action** can **confront** the **victory** of an **illegitimate** government, even if it brings **casualties** and **turmoil**.  

END OF SUMMARY.
