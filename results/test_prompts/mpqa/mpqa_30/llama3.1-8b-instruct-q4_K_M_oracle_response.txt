SUMMARY:

Opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai accuses President Robert Mugabe of withholding food from areas that did not vote for him in the March election, as part of a policy of retribution against his supporters. This operation is aimed at crushing the opposition and has led to widespread violence and hunger in Zimbabwe. The United Nations World Food Program warned that 1.5 million people face starvation due to drought and government-sponsored farm invasions, which have crippled agricultural production.

Tsvangirai alleges that the land-reform plan is a way for Mugabe's regime to seize control of commercial farms and distribute them among his cronies and supporters. The opposition leader commends Canada and other countries for invoking sanctions against Mugabe's regime, but emphasizes that Zimbabweans must fight peacefully for their own freedom.

The situation in Zimbabwe has escalated wildly since the election, with a vendetta being waged by the ruling party against Tsvangirai and his supporters. The burden of change lies with Zimbabweans themselves, who must confront the illegitimate government and its policies of repression and upheaval.

END OF SUMMARY.
