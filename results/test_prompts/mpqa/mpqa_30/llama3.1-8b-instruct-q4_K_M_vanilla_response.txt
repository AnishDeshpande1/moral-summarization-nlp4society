SUMMARY:

Opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai has accused Zimbabwean President Robert Mugabe of withholding food from areas that did not vote for him in the March election. Tsvangirai claims this is part of a campaign of retribution against his supporters, particularly in southern Zimbabwe where opposition to ZANU-PF is strongest. The United Nations World Food Program estimates 1.5 million people face starvation due to drought and government-sponsored farm invasions.

Non-governmental organizations have reported evidence of discriminatory food distribution, with people being forced to denounce the MDC or undergo a "cleansing" routine before buying corn. Tsvangirai also alleges that Zimbabwe's land-reform plan is being used to benefit presidential cronies and ruling party elite.

Tsvangirai thanked Canada and other countries for imposing sanctions on Mugabe's regime, but emphasized that the burden of change lies with Zimbabweans themselves. He is set to stand trial on treason charges next month, with his prosecution relying on a key witness from Montreal.

END OF SUMMARY.
