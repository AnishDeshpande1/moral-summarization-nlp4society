SUMMARY: Opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai accused President Robert Mugabe of deliberately withholding food from drought‑hit areas as retaliation for opposition support in the March election. He claimed the government’s “starvation policy” targets MDC supporters, especially in southern Zimbabwe, and described it as part of a broader vendetta that includes discriminatory grain distribution requiring ZANU‑PF cards and forced confessions. The UN World Food Programme warned that up to 1.5 million people face starvation, exacerbated by past farm invasions that crippled maize production. Human‑rights groups reported that access to grain is being conditioned on political loyalty, with alleged “cleansing” rituals in Matabeleland. Tsvangirai also said the land‑reform program benefits Mugabe’s cronies rather than the poor. He praised international sanctions, notably from Canada, but emphasized that change must come from Zimbabweans themselves. The leader is set to stand trial on treason charges on May 31, denying the accusations and questioning the reliability of the prosecution’s key witness. END OF SUMMARY.
