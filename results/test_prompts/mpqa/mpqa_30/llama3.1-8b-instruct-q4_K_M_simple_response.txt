SUMMARY:

Zimbabwean opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai accused President Robert Mugabe of withholding food from areas that did not vote for him in the March election, as part of a campaign of retribution against his supporters. Tsvangirai alleged that a starvation policy is being used to target farmers and rural sympathizers, exacerbating the country's drought-related hunger crisis. The United Nations World Food Program warned that 1.5 million Zimbabweans face starvation.

Human rights organizations have also reported evidence of discriminatory food distribution, with people required to denounce their opposition affiliations before buying grain. Tsvangirai condemned Mugabe's land reform plan, which he claims is being used to reward his cronies and allies rather than resettling poor families.

The opposition leader commended countries like Canada for imposing sanctions on Mugabe's regime, but emphasized that Zimbabweans themselves must lead the fight for change through peaceful mobilization. Tsvangirai also spoke about his upcoming treason trial, which he denies is a legitimate case against him.

END OF SUMMARY.
