SUMMARY:

Venezuelan government officials have reacted strongly to US Secretary of State Colin Powell's statement criticizing President Hugo Chavez's foreign policy, calling it an unacceptable interference in Venezuela's internal affairs. They argued that Venezuela's domestic policy is its own concern and that no country should request another's approval for its decisions. The opposition, however, sees the statement as a sign of growing international concern over Venezuela's human rights record and democratic institutions. US officials have expressed worries about Chavez's visits to countries on the US black list and his alleged ties with terrorist organizations. Venezuelan officials dismissed these concerns, accusing journalists of plotting against Chavez with foreign powers and calling for an investigation into their activities. The controversy highlights the complex relationship between Venezuela and its main trade partner, the United States.
