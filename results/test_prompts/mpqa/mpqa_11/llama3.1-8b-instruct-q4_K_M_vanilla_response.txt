SUMMARY:

US Secretary of State Colin Powell's statement on Venezuela's foreign policy and democratic nature has triggered reactions from the ruling party in Caracas. Members of the government have accused Washington of violating national sovereignty, with some calling for equal treatment for Venezuela's foreign policy. The opposition, however, sees Powell's remarks as a result of their own campaign to portray Chavez as an authoritarian leader who is befriending terrorist organizations.

Powell criticized Chavez's visits to countries on the US black list, including Cuba, Libya, and Iraq, and questioned his democratic credentials. Venezuelan officials responded that their domestic policy is none of Washington's concern, while some opposition deputies acknowledged that the government's actions had contributed to the concerns voiced by Powell.

The controversy has sparked debate over Venezuela's relations with its main trade partner, the US, and whether Chavez's policies are driving away investors. Some opposition leaders warned that if tensions continue, they could lead to an intervention or blockade, which would hurt the Venezuelan people.
