SUMMARY:

Venezuela's government reacted strongly to US Secretary of State Colin Powell's statement criticizing President Hugo Chavez's foreign policy and democratic nature. Members of the ruling party, including Guillermo Garcia Ponce and Cilia Flores, condemned Powell's comments as an interference in Venezuela's internal affairs and a violation of national sovereignty. They argued that Venezuela's domestic policy is its own concern and that other countries should not dictate its conduct.

Powell had criticized Chavez's visits to countries on the US list of terrorism supporters, including Cuba, Libya, and Iraq. The Venezuelan opposition, however, claimed that Powell's statement was motivated by a smear campaign against Chavez and that it suits their objectives to bring about an intervention or blockade in Venezuela. Some opposition deputies acknowledged that the government's actions were contributing to the concerns voiced by the US.

The incident highlights tensions between Venezuela and the US, with both sides accusing each other of interference in internal affairs. The situation is further complicated by reports of alleged ties between the Venezuelan government and terrorist organizations.

END OF SUMMARY.
