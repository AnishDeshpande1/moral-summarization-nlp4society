SUMMARY:

The Venezuelan government reacted strongly to US Secretary of State Colin Powell's statement criticizing President Hugo Chavez's actions and foreign policy decisions. The government accused Washington of meddling in Venezuela's internal affairs, violating its sovereignty, and trying to determine the country's conduct. They argued that Venezuela designs its own policies autonomously and demands respect for its sovereignty.

Powell's concerns centered on Chavez's visits to countries blacklisted by the US as supporters of terrorism, including Cuba, Libya, and Iraq. The Venezuelan government defended these trips, saying they aimed at strengthening their economy through trade relations with OPEC member states.

The opposition in Venezuela went further, accusing the government of instigating a smear campaign against Powell, which led to his comments. They also suggested that the government is trying to accomplish its objectives by portraying an image of Venezuela that scares away investors and triggers domestic turmoil.

The situation highlights the tension between Venezuela's foreign policy decisions and its relationship with its largest trade partner, the US. END OF SUMMARY.
