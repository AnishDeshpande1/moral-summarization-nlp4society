SUMMARY:  
U.S. Secretary of State Colin Powell’s remarks on President Hugo Chávez’s foreign policy sparked a wave of accusations of **interference**, **meddling** and **violations** of Venezuela’s sovereignty.  Chávez’s allies – Guillermo Garcia Ponce of the CPR, Deputy Cilia Flores of the MVR, and National Assembly Vice‑President Noheli Pocaterra – denounced the **concern** expressed by Washington as an unjustified **request** to **determine** and **adopt** Venezuelan policy.  They stressed that Venezuela **designs** and **decides** its own actions, that visits to Cuba, Libya and Iraq were meant to **strengthen** the economy, and that any foreign **approval** or **disapproval** of those trips **violates** the principle of non‑intervention.  

Opposition deputy Pedro Carreno linked Powell’s statements to a **waging** **war** of **smear** **sabotage**, claiming journalists fabricated a video to **portray** Chávez as linked to **terrorism** and to **trigger** **turmoil** that could justify **intervention** or a **blockade**.  He warned that such **conflict** and **threats** **hurt** the nation and constitute **treason** under Article 122.  

Meanwhile, a few opposition figures (Alejandro Arzola, Claudio Fermin, Julio Montoya) warned that the government’s **incompetent** **handling** of relations, **misguided** oil **cutting**, and ambiguous **readiness** to **establish** ties with “parasitic” economies **continue** to **scare** investors, **sell** less oil, and risk a diplomatic **breakdown**.  

END OF SUMMARY.
