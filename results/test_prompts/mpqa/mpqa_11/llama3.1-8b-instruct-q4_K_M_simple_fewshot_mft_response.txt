SUMMARY:

US Secretary of State Colin Powell's statement about Venezuela's government triggered reactions from members of the ruling party, who accused Washington of violating national sovereignty. They argued that Venezuela's domestic policy is its own concern and that no country should interfere in another's decisions. Deputy Pedro Carreno went further, accusing four journalists of plotting with powerful economic groups to portray an image of Venezuela that scares away investors and triggers domestic turmoil to request foreign intervention. He invited Powell to visit Venezuela to see what is really happening. The opposition seeks to portray the government as weak and incompetent, which would justify international intervention or a blockade.

The ruling party members criticized Powell's statement, saying it reflects the concerns voiced by the international community and Latin America over Venezuela's situation. They argued that the government's positions are harming the country domestically and internationally. Democratic Action leader Claudio Fermin warned that if there is no confidence in Venezuela's intentions regarding crude prices and the war on terrorism, investments will be lost.

END OF SUMMARY.
