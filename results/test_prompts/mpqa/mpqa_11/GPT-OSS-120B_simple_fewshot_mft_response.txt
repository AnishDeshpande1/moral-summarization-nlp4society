SUMMARY:
U.S. Secretary of State Colin Powell’s criticism of President Hugo Chávez’s foreign policy sparked an outcry from Venezuela’s ruling party, which framed the remarks as a blatant violation of national sovereignty and an illegitimate subversion of Venezuelan authority. Leaders such as Guillermo Garcia Ponce and Deputy Cilia Flores warned that external meddling threatens the country’s autonomy and endangers its people, stressing the need to protect Venezuelans from foreign harm. They portrayed Powell’s comments as unfair and deceitful, accusing the United States of cheating Venezuela of respect and equal treatment in international affairs. The officials also invoked loyalty, insisting that Venezuela’s policies must reflect the will of its citizens and that any foreign attempt to dictate or undermine those decisions constitutes a betrayal of the nation’s historic commitment to self‑determination. Opposition voices amplified the narrative, linking Powell’s statements to a broader smear campaign that aims to destabilize the government, harm the economy, and invite hostile intervention. Across the debate, the moral discourse centered on defending legitimate authority, safeguarding the populace, demanding fair treatment, and condemning perceived treachery. END OF SUMMARY.
