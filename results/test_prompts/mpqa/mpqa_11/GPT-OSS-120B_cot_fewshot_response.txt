**SUMMARY:**  
U.S. Secretary of State Colin Powell’s remarks on Venezuelan foreign policy provoked sharp accusations of **interference** and **violation** of national **sovereignty** from the ruling party. Guillermo García Ponce called the statement “unusual,” stressing that Venezuela’s **autonomous** domestic policy is a **constitutional** matter and that any **meddling** by another state contradicts **international law** and the principle of **non‑intervention**. Deputy Cilia Flores labeled the comments as **meddling** and demanded **respect** for Venezuela’s **sovereignty**, while Vice‑President Noheli Pocaterra warned the United States must respect Venezuela’s right to decide its own foreign policy. Opposition Deputy Pedro Carreño linked Powell’s criticism to a **smearing** campaign and alleged **sabotage** by journalists, even suggesting the journalists’ actions amounted to **treason**. Across the debate, the dominant moral framing centered on defending **sovereignty**, condemning **interference**, and accusing the U.S. of **disrespect** and **aggression** toward Venezuela.
