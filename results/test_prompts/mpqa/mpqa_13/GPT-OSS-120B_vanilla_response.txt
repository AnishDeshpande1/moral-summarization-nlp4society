SUMMARY: Representatives from Japan and the United States concluded a two‑day climate dialogue in Tokyo, focusing on cooperation in science and technology to combat global warming. The talks marked the first bilateral meeting since President George Bush announced a U.S. climate strategy that favors voluntary emission limits tied to economic growth rather than mandatory targets. The United States pledged to boost research and development on climate issues, while Japan reaffirmed its intention to ratify the Kyoto Protocol. Bush’s earlier rejection of the 1997 Kyoto agreement, citing concerns over U.S. economic growth, had sparked international criticism. END OF SUMMARY.
