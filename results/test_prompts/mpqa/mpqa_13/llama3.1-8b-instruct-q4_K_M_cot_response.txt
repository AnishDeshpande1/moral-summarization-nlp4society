SUMMARY: 
Representatives from the US and Japan met in Tokyo to discuss ways of co-operating on climate change, specifically through science and technology fields to prevent global warming. This meeting comes after President George Bush unveiled a new plan for limiting greenhouse gas emissions based on economic growth rather than fixed targets. The US emphasized its intention to promote research into climate change, whereas Japan reaffirmed its commitment to ratify the Kyoto protocol, which was rejected by President Bush last year due to claims that it would hinder US economic growth. However, the move has sparked an international outcry among countries concerned about global warming and the potential for environmental damage.
