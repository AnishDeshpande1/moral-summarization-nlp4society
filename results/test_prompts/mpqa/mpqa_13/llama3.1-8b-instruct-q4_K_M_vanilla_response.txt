SUMMARY:
US and Japan representatives concluded two days of climate talks in Tokyo on Tuesday, focusing on cooperation in science and technology to combat global warming. This meeting marked the first discussion between the two countries since President George Bush introduced a new US plan for voluntary greenhouse gas emission limits tied to economic growth. The US emphasized its commitment to researching climate change, while Japan reaffirmed its intention to ratify the Kyoto protocol, which President Bush rejected last year as detrimental to US economic growth.

The talks aimed to find common ground on addressing global warming, but significant differences between the two countries' approaches remain. President Bush's plan has been met with international criticism, and Japan's continued commitment to the Kyoto protocol suggests a divergence in climate policy between the two nations.

END OF SUMMARY.
