SUMMARY:  
Representatives from the United States and Japan concluded two days of climate‑change talks in Tokyo, focusing on joint scientific and technological cooperation to curb global warming. The dialogue marked the first high‑level encounter since President George Bush announced a new U.S. approach that favors voluntary, growth‑linked greenhouse‑gas limits rather than enforceable targets. The United States pledged to boost research and development on climate issues, while Japan reaffirmed its commitment to ratify the Kyoto Protocol. The article underscores the moral controversy surrounding President Bush’s earlier rejection of the 1997 Kyoto agreement, which sparked international condemnation for prioritising short‑term economic gains over global environmental responsibility.  

END OF SUMMARY.
