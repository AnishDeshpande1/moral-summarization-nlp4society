SUMMARY: Representatives from Japan and the United States concluded two days of climate talks in Tokyo, focusing on how to **prevent** global warming through joint science and technology cooperation. The meeting, the first since President George Bush announced a voluntary, growth‑based emissions plan, featured a U.S. pledge to **promote** research and development on climate change. Japan reaffirmed its intention to ratify the Kyoto Protocol, contrasting with Bush’s earlier rejection of the 1997 agreement, which he argued would harm U.S. economic growth. END OF SUMMARY.
