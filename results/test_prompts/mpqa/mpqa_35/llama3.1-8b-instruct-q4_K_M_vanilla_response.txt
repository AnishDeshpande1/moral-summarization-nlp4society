SUMMARY:

The US State Department's annual human rights report criticizes several countries, including those in the anti-terrorist alliance, for abusing human rights. However, it makes exceptions for Israel, citing its need to take counter-terrorism measures due to terrorist attacks. The report ignores similar abuses by other members of the coalition, such as Russia and China, in exchange for their support in the war on terror. This has led to concerns that the US is prioritizing its interests over human rights. The article argues that the US is using the "good vs. evil" narrative to justify its actions, rather than promoting universal values of democracy, human rights, and peace. It suggests that the international community should reconsider the role of the UN or learn from more collaborative approaches, such as those used by the European parliament.

END OF SUMMARY.
