SUMMARY:
The US State Department’s 2002 human‑rights report condemns “axis of evil” regimes but excuses abuses by members of the anti‑terrorist coalition, especially Israel, as inevitable counter‑terrorism measures. It omits any critique of post‑9/11 US restrictions on travel and civil liberties, and offers no corrective proposals. The author argues that the war on terror has become a tool for advancing US interests, forcing allies such as Russia, China and Israel to act with impunity while the US claims to champion democracy and human rights. By presenting its agenda as a moral crusade of good versus evil, the US masks a realist pursuit of power, which the author warns will only fuel terrorism in societies where rights are suppressed. The piece calls for the US to genuinely respect human rights, to avoid enabling allies’ abuses, and for the international community to rely on multilateral bodies like the UN or the European Parliament rather than a single nation’s agenda. The moral critique concludes that true peace and security require consistent, universal commitment to human rights, not selective, interest‑driven “two‑sided justice.”  
END OF SUMMARY.
