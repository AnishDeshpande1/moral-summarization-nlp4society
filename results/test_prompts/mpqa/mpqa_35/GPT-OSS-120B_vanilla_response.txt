SUMMARY: The U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report, released on March 4, condemns abuses in “axis of evil” states but also highlights violations by members of the U.S. anti‑terror alliance, notably Russia, China, and Israel. It justifies Israel’s harsh measures as counter‑terrorism responses and omits discussion of post‑9/11 U.S. restrictions on travel and civil liberties. While the report’s language is strong, it offers no corrective actions, reflecting a priority on consolidating the anti‑terror coalition and expanding the “war on terror” over human‑rights concerns. The author argues that U.S. policy prioritizes its own interests, using the good‑versus‑evil narrative to legitimize actions, and warns that supporting allies’ security measures without regard for rights could make the U.S. complicit in rights violations. The piece calls for the international community to reconsider the UN’s role and adopt more multilateral approaches, such as those of the European Parliament, to balance diverse interests and uphold universal human‑rights standards.  
END OF SUMMARY.
