**SUMMARY:**  
The U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report condemns “axis of evil” regimes while glossing over abuses by members of the anti‑terrorist alliance, notably Israel’s counter‑terrorism measures that are portrayed as understandable. Post‑Sept. 11 restrictions on travel and civil‑rights curtailments in the United States are omitted, and the report offers no corrective action. The author argues that the war on terror has become a justification for the naked pursuit of U.S. interests, turning the anti‑terrorist alliance into a two‑sided justice system where “if you’re not with us, you’re against us.” Suppression in Chechnya, Xinjiang, and against Palestinians is cited as evidence that the U.S. is tacitly accepting human‑rights violations. By demonizing enemies and ignoring the breeding ground for terrorism within its own coalition, the U.S. risks becoming an accomplice to oppression. The piece calls for a shift toward universal values—democracy, peace, and genuine human‑rights respect—suggesting that the international community look beyond a single nation’s interests.
