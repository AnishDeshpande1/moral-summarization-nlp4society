SUMMARY:
The U.S. State Department’s 2002 human‑rights report condemns a wave of **abuses** by the “axis of evil” and by members of the anti‑terrorist alliance, yet it **ignores** the **suppression** and **treatment** of Palestinians, Chechen rebels, Xinjiang’s independence advocates and Falun Gong practitioners. The document argues that Israel’s **measures** against terrorist **attacks** make its **abuses** somewhat understandable, while **curbing** civil liberties at home after Sept. 11 is left **un‑discussed**. By **overshadowing** the need for corrective action, the report reveals a **pursuit** of U.S. interests that **reflect** a “if you’re not with us, you’re against us” **demonizing** of foes, which **prevents** peace from being **gained**. It warns that where human rights are **repressed** and not **respected**, terrorists find support, so the U.S. must **attack** terrorism while **making** human‑rights protection a genuine part of its strategy. Expanding aid to allies risks **destruction** of rights and turns the U.S. into an accomplice. The author calls for **compromising** multilateral solutions, rather than **succumbing** to single‑nation dominance. END OF SUMMARY.
