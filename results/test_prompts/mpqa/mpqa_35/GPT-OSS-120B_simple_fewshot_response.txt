SUMMARY:
The U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report condemns “axis‑of‑evil” regimes while excusing abuses by members of the U.S. anti‑terror alliance, notably Israel, whose security measures are portrayed as understandable. The report omits any mention of post‑9/11 U.S. restrictions on travel and civil liberties, and offers no corrective steps, suggesting that the war on terror now outweighs concern for universal human rights. By overlooking violations in Russia’s Chechnya, China’s Xinjiang and Falun Gong repression, and Israel’s treatment of Palestinians, the United States appears to trade human‑rights standards for strategic support, embodying a “you’re with us or against us” double standard. The author argues this reflects a naked pursuit of U.S. interests dressed in moral rhetoric, warning that such hypocrisy fuels terrorist recruitment and jeopardizes global peace. The piece calls for a more multilateral approach—perhaps through the UN or European Parliament—to balance diverse interests and genuinely uphold democracy, human rights, and peace. END OF SUMMARY.
