SUMMARY:

The US State Department's human rights report highlights abuses in countries that are members of the anti-terrorist alliance, including Russia, China, and Israel. However, the report justifies Israel's actions as a response to terrorist attacks, while ignoring similar restrictions on civil liberties in the US post-9/11. The author argues that the US is prioritizing its own interests over human rights, and that the anti-terrorist alliance has become an embodiment of two-sided justice. By demonizing enemies and pursuing its own interests without regard for universal values like democracy and peace, the US risks becoming complicit in human rights abuses.

The report suggests that the US should instead focus on promoting human rights as a key aspect of the battle against terror. However, the author notes that this would require looking beyond US interests and practicing what it preaches. If not, the international community may need to reconsider the role of the UN or adopt more collaborative approaches like those used by the European parliament.
