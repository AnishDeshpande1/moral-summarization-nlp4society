SUMMARY:
The State Department’s annual human‑rights report condemns “axis‑of‑evil” regimes while excusing abuses by members of the U.S. anti‑terror alliance, especially Israel, on the grounds that counter‑terrorism measures are necessary. The article argues this double standard subverts the moral authority of the United States, betraying its duty of care by ignoring the harm inflicted on Palestinians, Chechen dissidents, Uyghurs and Falun Gong practitioners. By treating allied states as “good” and others as “evil,” the report undermines fairness, allowing cheating by powerful allies to go unchecked. The narrative also degrades the sanctity of universal human rights, suggesting that strategic interests justify the erosion of democratic and humanitarian values. The author warns that U.S. plans to fund allied militaries in anti‑terror operations risk making Washington an accomplice to rights violations, and calls for a more balanced, multilateral approach—such as that of the European Parliament—to restore legitimate authority, protect vulnerable peoples, and uphold a fair, sacred commitment to human dignity. END OF SUMMARY.
