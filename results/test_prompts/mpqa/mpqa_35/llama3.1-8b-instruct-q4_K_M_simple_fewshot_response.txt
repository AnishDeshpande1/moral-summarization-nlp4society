SUMMARY:
The US State Department's annual human rights report has sparked controversy by downplaying abuses in countries that are members of the anti-terrorist alliance, including Israel, Russia, and China. While criticizing "axis of evil" countries for abusing human rights, the report excuses similar abuses by its allies, citing counter-terrorism measures as justification. This double standard raises concerns about US hypocrisy and selective enforcement of human rights principles. The report's failure to propose corrective action or promote universal values such as democracy, human rights, and peace has been criticized by commentator Chien Hsi-chieh, who argues that the US is prioritizing its own interests over human rights. This approach risks making the US an accomplice in the destruction of human rights, particularly in countries where it provides military aid to suppress terrorism. The international community should reconsider the role played by the UN and learn from more inclusive models like the European parliament.
