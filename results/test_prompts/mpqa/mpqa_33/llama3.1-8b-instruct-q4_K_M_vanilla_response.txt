SUMMARY:

Many countries have strongly criticized the Annual Human Rights Report of the US State Department, calling it inaccurate and lacking goodwill. The report has been rejected by several countries in Asia, Europe, Africa, and Latin America, who consider it a distortion of reality and an interference in their internal affairs. A recent report on human rights in the United States by China's Information Office highlights various issues, including high murder rates, torture, and pressure to confess crime. The report also points out that many people are sentenced to death for crimes they did not commit, and over 12 million children live below the poverty line.

Critics argue that the US evaluation of human rights in other countries is influenced by its political interests and that it ignores its own human rights issues at home. Many Americans have spoken out against the report's inaccuracies, including an article in The Washington Times criticizing the US administration's judgment on human rights in Vietnam.

END OF SUMMARY.
