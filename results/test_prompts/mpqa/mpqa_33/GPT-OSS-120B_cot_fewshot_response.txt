**SUMMARY:**
The U.S. State Department’s 2001 Annual Human Rights Report has been widely condemned as inaccurate, fabricated, and a blatant distortion of reality. Nations across Asia, Europe, Africa and Latin America denounce it as a wrongful, illegitimate interference in internal affairs, while critics highlight its bias, politicized judgments and hypocrisy. The Chinese People’s Congress released a counter‑report exposing U.S. violations: high murder rates, systemic torture, pressure to confess, unjust death sentences, pervasive poverty affecting over 12 million children, and women beaten every 15 seconds. Observers note that the United States routinely ignores its own human‑rights abuses, focusing instead on politically convenient critiques of countries like North Korea and Iraq. Reuters pointed out the selective criticism of Egypt versus the U.S. plan to try al‑Qa’ida detainees in military courts. The report’s malicious agenda has sparked global protest, with American voices defending Vietnam’s religious freedom and rejecting the report’s falsehoods. Overall, the document is portrayed as an exploitative, oppressive tool that betrays fundamental principles of justice and truth.
