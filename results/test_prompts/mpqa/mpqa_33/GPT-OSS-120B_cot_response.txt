**SUMMARY:**  
The United States State Department’s Annual Human Rights Report has been **condemned** worldwide as **inaccurate**, **brazen**, and a **distortion** of reality, accused of **interference** in other nations’ internal affairs. Numerous Asian, European, African and Latin American governments have **rejected** its findings as **wrongful** and **illegitimate**. A Chinese parliamentary report highlighted severe **human‑rights violations** in the United States, citing high murder rates, **torture**, **unjust** legal practices, widespread **poverty**, and a woman being **beaten** every 15 seconds. Critics argue the U.S. ignores its own problems while publishing reports on 190 countries, tailoring judgments to **political** and **malicious** interests. Reuters noted the selective criticism of Egypt versus the treatment of al‑Qaida detainees, suggesting the report serves U.S. strategic goals rather than universal values. Domestic voices, such as Andrew Welsdan of the U.S. Fund for Reconciliation and Development, dispute allegations about Vietnam, but the overall consensus labels the report **fabricated** and lacking moral credibility.
