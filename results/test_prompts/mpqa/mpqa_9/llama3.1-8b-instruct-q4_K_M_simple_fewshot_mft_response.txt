SUMMARY:

The EU is concerned that the US proposals for climate change action will not effectively reduce emissions, as they allow for further increases in absolute emissions despite a decrease in intensity. The EU argues that this approach is insufficient to combat climate change and may even lead to an increase in greenhouse gas concentrations. The EU also criticizes the voluntary nature of the actions proposed by President Bush, which lacks mandatory monitoring and reporting of emissions at company level. This contrasts with the EU's approach, which includes a cap-and-trade system covering 46% of all CO2 emissions in 2010. The EU welcomes the US effort to address climate change but emphasizes the need for more stringent measures to meet the goals of the Climate Change Convention. The EU will continue on its current path of emission reduction and expects other major players, including Japan and Russia, to join them in these efforts.
