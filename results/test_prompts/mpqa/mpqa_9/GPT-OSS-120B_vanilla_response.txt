SUMMARY: The EU critiques the United States’ domestic climate plan, saying its “intensity target” allows absolute emissions to rise and falls short of the Kyoto Protocol’s absolute reduction requirements. While the U.S. aims to improve emissions intensity relative to GDP, projected emissions would still be 33‑39% above 1990 levels by 2012. The EU worries about the voluntary nature of the U.S. measures, the lack of a cap‑and‑trade system for power‑sector CO₂, and the delayed effectiveness review until 2012. The EU stresses that a mandatory, company‑level monitoring system is needed to gauge real effort. It remains committed to ratifying the Kyoto Protocol by June 2002 and to its own emission‑reduction path, hoping Japan and Russia will join. The EU will continue to assess the Bush administration’s proposals and keep dialogue open. END OF SUMMARY.
