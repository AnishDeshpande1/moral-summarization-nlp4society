SUMMARY:

The EU is concerned that the US proposals on climate change will not effectively reduce emissions, but rather lead to an increase in absolute levels. This contradicts the ultimate objective of the Climate Change Convention, which requires industrialised countries to stabilise their greenhouse gas emissions at 1990 levels. The Bush plan prolongs a trend of decreasing emission intensity, but this does not prevent a substantial increase in absolute emissions by 2012. The EU is also worried about the voluntary nature of the actions proposed and the lack of monitoring and reporting mechanisms. The EU believes that a cap-and-trade system is necessary to reduce emissions effectively and efficiently. They will proceed with ratification of the Kyoto Protocol and continue on their path of emission reduction, hoping that other major players will join them. The EU will further examine the US proposals and be willing to continue dialogue on this basis.

END OF SUMMARY.
