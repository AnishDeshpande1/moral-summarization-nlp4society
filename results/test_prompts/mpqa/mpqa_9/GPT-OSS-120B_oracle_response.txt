SUMMARY:  
The EU notes that US climate proposals are domestic and **allow** only modest **intensity** gains, while **leading** to a rise in absolute emissions. The EU **requires** absolute cuts, as set by the Kyoto Protocol and the 1992 Climate Change Convention, which aim to **prevent** dangerous warming by **reducing** greenhouse gases to 1990 levels. The Bush plan **extends** the current “business‑as‑usual” trend, **increasing** projected emissions by about 33 % above 1990 by 2012, despite modest **step** improvements in intensity. The EU criticises the **voluntary** nature of the US actions and the lack of an early **review**, and points out that a **cap‑and‑trade** system—seen as a cost‑effective **implement** to **reduce** CO₂—has not been **extended** to the power sector. While welcoming the US policy review, the EU stresses that mandatory monitoring is needed to **take** the US proposals to a level comparable with Kyoto. The EU remains committed to **ratification** of Kyoto by June 2002 and will **continue** dialogue, hoping Japan and Russia will **join** its **efforts**.  

END OF SUMMARY.
