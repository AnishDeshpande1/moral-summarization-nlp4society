SUMMARY:

The European Union (EU) expresses concerns over the proposed climate change plan by President Bush, which it believes will not reduce US emissions sufficiently to combat climate change effectively. The EU argues that the plan's "intensity target" allows for increased absolute emissions and is inconsistent with the Kyoto Protocol, which requires industrialized countries to achieve absolute emission reductions. The EU also criticizes the voluntary nature of the proposed actions and the lack of a cap-and-trade system for CO2 emissions from power plants. In contrast, the EU has implemented a cap-and-trade system that will cover 46% of all CO2 emissions in 2010 by 2005.

The EU welcomes President Bush's efforts to address climate change but notes that mandatory monitoring and reporting of emissions are necessary to assess its effectiveness. The EU is committed to reducing emissions and plans to proceed with ratifying the Kyoto Protocol, urging other major players like Japan and Russia to join them in this effort.

END OF SUMMARY.
