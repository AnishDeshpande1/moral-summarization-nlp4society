SUMMARY:

The European Union (EU) is concerned that the US proposals on climate change will not effectively reduce greenhouse gas emissions. The EU believes that the proposed "intensity target" allows for further increases in absolute emissions, which is insufficient to combat climate change. In contrast, the Kyoto Protocol requires industrialized countries to achieve absolute emission reductions. The EU notes that current emission projections indicate a 39% increase above 1990 levels by 2012, which will only be reduced to around 33% with the proposed measures. The EU also criticizes the voluntary nature of the actions and the lack of a cap-and-trade system for CO2 emissions from the power sector. While the EU welcomes the US's first step towards reducing emissions, it emphasizes the need for mandatory monitoring and reporting of emissions at company level. The EU will continue to proceed with ratification of the Kyoto Protocol and urges other major players, including Japan and Russia, to join in efforts to reduce emissions.

END OF SUMMARY.
