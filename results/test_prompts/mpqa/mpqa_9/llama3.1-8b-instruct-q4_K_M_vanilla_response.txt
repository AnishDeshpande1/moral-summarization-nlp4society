SUMMARY:

The European Union (EU) is concerned that the proposed climate change plan by President Bush will not effectively reduce US emissions. The EU believes that the plan's "intensity target" will allow for further increases in absolute emissions, which contradicts the Kyoto Protocol's requirement of absolute emission reductions. The EU also criticizes the voluntary nature of the actions proposed and the lack of a review mechanism to assess their effectiveness.

The EU is concerned about the increase in CO2 emissions projected by the US, which could reach 39% above 1990 levels in 2012. In contrast, the EU intends to implement a cap-and-trade system covering 46% of all CO2 emissions in 2010. The EU welcomes President Bush's proposal as a first step but emphasizes that mandatory monitoring and reporting of emissions at company level are necessary for adequate assessment.

The EU is committed to ratifying the Kyoto Protocol by June 2002 and will continue on its path of emission reduction, encouraging other major players to join them in these efforts. The dialogue with the US on climate change policy will continue based on the proposed plan.

END OF SUMMARY.
