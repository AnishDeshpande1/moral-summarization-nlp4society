SUMMARY: The EU warns that the United States’ new domestic climate plan is morally insufficient. While the US proposes an “intensity target” that allows absolute emissions to keep rising, the Kyoto Protocol and the 1992 Climate Change Convention obligate industrialised nations to cut emissions outright and stabilise them at 1990 levels—a duty the EU says the US is neglecting. The EU criticises the voluntary nature of the US measures, the lack of a cap‑and‑trade system for power‑sector CO₂, and the delayed effectiveness review until 2012, arguing that these choices betray a “business‑as‑usual” ethic rather than genuine responsibility. The EU pledges to ratify Kyoto by June 2002, continue its own emissions cuts, and urge Japan and Russia to join, while remaining open to dialogue and demanding mandatory company‑level monitoring to verify any US effort. The moral framing presents the US plan as a half‑hearted step that falls short of the collective ethical duty to prevent dangerous climate change. END OF SUMMARY.
