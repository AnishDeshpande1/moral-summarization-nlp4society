SUMMARY:
The European Union, invoking its authority under the 1992 Climate Change Convention and the Kyoto Protocol, warns that the United States’ proposed “intensity target” falls short of the obligations of industrialised nations to stabilise greenhouse‑gas emissions at 1990 levels. EU officials stress that the Bush administration’s voluntary, business‑as‑usual approach would actually increase absolute emissions, subverting the collective commitment to prevent dangerous climate change. They argue that only a binding, EU‑backed cap‑and‑trade system can guarantee the required environmental results and call on the United States to adopt mandatory monitoring and reporting to align with the authority of international climate accords. The EU reaffirms its resolve to ratify Kyoto by June 2002 and to continue reducing emissions, urging the US to move from subversive voluntary measures to compliance with established global authority. END OF SUMMARY.
