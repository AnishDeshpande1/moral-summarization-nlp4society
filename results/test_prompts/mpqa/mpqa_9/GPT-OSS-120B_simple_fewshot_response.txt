SUMMARY:
The European Union warns that the United States’ new “intensity‑target” climate plan is morally insufficient. While the proposal is framed as a domestic action, it would allow U.S. emissions to keep rising—projected to be 33‑39 % above 1990 levels by 2012—contrary to the 1992 Climate Change Convention’s duty for industrialised nations to stabilise greenhouse gases at 1990 levels and the Kyoto Protocol’s requirement for absolute cuts. The EU criticises the plan’s voluntary nature, the delayed effectiveness review, and the failure to apply cost‑effective cap‑and‑trade to power‑sector CO₂. Though it welcomes the Bush administration’s policy review as a possible first step, the EU insists that mandatory monitoring, reporting, and binding caps are needed to meet the ethical obligation of preventing dangerous climate change. The Union reaffirms its commitment to ratify Kyoto by June 2002, continue its own emissions reductions, and urges Japan, Russia and the U.S. to share the moral responsibility of safeguarding the planet.  
END OF SUMMARY.
