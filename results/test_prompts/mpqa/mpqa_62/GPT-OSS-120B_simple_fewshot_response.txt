SUMMARY:
Space shuttle Endeavour successfully docked with the International Space Station, delivering a fresh three‑person crew and relieving the long‑standing occupants. Commander Frank Culbertson, a retired Navy fighter pilot who had felt isolated after the September 11 attacks, welcomed the arrival, ringing the station’s bell in a maritime tradition as the shuttle approached within 1½ miles. The outgoing crew—Culbertson, Russian cosmonauts Vladimir Dezhurov and Mikhail Tyurin—will depart, making way for the new lineup: Russian commander Yuri Onufrienko and American astronauts Carl Walz and Daniel Bursch, who are slated to remain aboard until May. The event underscores the ongoing international cooperation and the human need for fresh support and camaraderie in the demanding environment of space.

END OF SUMMARY.
