SUMMARY:  
The shuttle Endeavour’s docking with the International Space Station is portrayed not just as a technical feat but as a moment of human solidarity and renewal. After a quiet, four‑month stint marked by isolation and frustration—especially for commander Frank Culbertson, who felt the weight of the September 11 attacks—he welcomes the fresh three‑person crew as a reminder that cooperation and fresh perspectives can lift morale in even the most remote outposts. The ritual of ringing the ship’s bell and announcing “Endeavour arriving,” echoing maritime tradition, underscores the shared values of duty and camaraderie that bind astronauts from the United States and Russia. The orderly hand‑over to the new crew—Yuri Onufrienko, Carl Walz, and Daniel Bursch—highlights the ongoing commitment to international partnership and the moral imperative of supporting one another in the pursuit of exploration.  

END OF SUMMARY.
