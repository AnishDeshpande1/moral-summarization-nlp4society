SUMMARY:
The shuttle Endeavour successfully docked with the International Space Station, delivering a fresh three‑person crew and relieving the current crew that had been aboard since August. Commander Frank Culbertson, a retired Navy fighter pilot, greeted the arriving shuttle with the traditional “bell‑ringing” signal, underscoring the disciplined hierarchy and ritual of spaceflight. Culbertson, who had felt isolated after the September 11 attacks, and his Russian crewmates Vladimir Dezhurov and Mikhail Tyurin will now step down, making way for the next appointed commander, Yuri Onufrienko, and his American teammates Carl Walz and Daniel Bursch. The orderly hand‑over highlights the authority structures that govern the station’s operations and the seamless subordination of individual crews to the established chain of command, ensuring continuous, orderly presence in orbit until May.  
END OF SUMMARY.
