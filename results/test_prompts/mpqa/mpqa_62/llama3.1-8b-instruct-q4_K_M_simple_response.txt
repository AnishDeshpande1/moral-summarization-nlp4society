SUMMARY:
Space shuttle Endeavour successfully docked with the international space station, bringing a new three-member crew to relieve the current occupants. The arrival was eagerly anticipated by Space Station Commander Frank Culbertson, who has been feeling isolated and frustrated since the Sept. 11 terrorist attacks. His four-month stay on the space station has been quiet, but he expressed relief at finally being able to leave.

The departing crew of three will be replaced by a new trio, including Russians Yuri Onufrienko and Americans Carl Walz and Daniel Bursch. This rotation is a testament to the dedication and sacrifice required for such extended stays in space. The Endeavour's successful docking marks another milestone in the ongoing collaboration between international partners on the International Space Station.

END OF SUMMARY.
