SUMMARY:  
Space shuttle Endeavour docked with the International Space Station, delivering a fresh three‑person crew and ending a two‑day chase over the Polish‑Ukrainian border. Station commander Frank Culbertson, a retired Navy fighter pilot, had been isolated and frustrated since the September 11 terrorist attacks, awaiting his ride home. He greeted the approaching shuttle, ringing the ship’s bell and announcing “Endeavour arriving,” echoing maritime tradition. Culbertson and his Russian crewmates, Vladimir Dezhurov and Mikhail Tyurin, will depart, making way for the incoming team of Russian commander Yuri Onufrienko and Americans Carl Walz and Daniel Bursch, who will stay aboard until May.  

END OF SUMMARY.
