SUMMARY:
Space shuttle Endeavour docked with the International Space Station (ISS) on Friday, delivering a new three-member crew to relieve the current residents. The shuttle arrived after a two-day chase, orbiting 250 miles above the Polish-Ukrainian border. Space station commander Frank Culbertson and his Russian crewmates were awaiting their ride home, which was delayed due to the September 11 terrorist attacks. They expressed frustration with being isolated during this time. Endeavour brought new crew members Yuri Onufrienko, Carl Walz, and Daniel Bursch, who will stay on the ISS until May. The handover of command took place after the arrival, with Culbertson and his team moving out of the station.

END OF SUMMARY.
