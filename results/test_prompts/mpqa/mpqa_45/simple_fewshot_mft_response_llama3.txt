SUMMARY:

Thirty-two NGOs have suspended all engagements with the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days due to concerns over its independence and commitment to protecting civil liberties and advancing human rights. The NGOs are protesting the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations, including the appointment of former attorney-general Abu Talib Othman as chairperson and the decision not to renew the appointments of three commissioners who were instrumental in producing critical reports. The NGOs argue that these moves undermine Suhakam's independence and erode public confidence in the commission. They also express concern over the appointment of new commissioners, mostly retired civil servants, and worry that they may prioritize policy implementation over human rights protection. The boycott aims to pressure Suhakam to improve its performance and fulfill its promise to protect and promote human rights in Malaysia.

END OF SUMMARY.
