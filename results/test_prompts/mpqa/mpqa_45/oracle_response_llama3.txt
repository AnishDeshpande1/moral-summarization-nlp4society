SUMMARY:

Thirty-two NGOs have suspended engagements with the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days, protesting the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations and decline of human rights in Malaysia. The NGOs are concerned that Suhakam has been ignored and failed to implement its own reports, including the Kesas report which found police guilty of using excessive force. They also lament the failure to respect or recognize Suhakam's recommendations, leading to the erosion of public confidence.

The NGOs are further disappointed with the government's refusal to ratify international human rights instruments and the appointment of commissioners who may not be committed to protecting civil liberties and advancing human rights. The suspension is a protest against the government's failure to show its commitment and respect for universal human rights principles.

END OF SUMMARY.
