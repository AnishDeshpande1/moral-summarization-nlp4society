SUMMARY:
Thirty-two non-governmental organizations (NGOs) have suspended their engagements with the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days, protesting the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations and the decline of human rights in Malaysia. The NGOs are concerned about the appointment of new commissioners, including former attorney-general Abu Talib Othman, who has a questionable track record on civil liberties and human rights. They also lament the lack of independence of Suhakam, which they believe will erode public confidence in the commission. The NGOs will not hold any dialogues or send memorandums to Suhakam during this period but will monitor its performance. Suhakam commissioner Prof Hamdan Adnan has expressed disappointment with the boycott, saying it is "unfair" and may create the impression that the NGOs are acting out of self-interest.

END OF SUMMARY.
