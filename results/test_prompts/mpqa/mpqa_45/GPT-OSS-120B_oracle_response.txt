SUMMARY:  
Thirty‑two NGOs announced a 100‑day boycott of Malaysia’s Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) to protest the government’s **ignored** recommendations and a **failure** to act on Suhakam’s maiden and freedom‑of‑assembly reports. The NGOs **denied** that the commission is anything more than a **public‑relations tool**, citing a **decline** in human‑rights conditions: increasing **detentions** without **trial**, a police **ban** on assemblies, **restrictions** on speech, and restrictive electoral **amendments**. They also **failed** to **show** commitment to international covenants. The boycott was triggered by controversial appointments, including former attorney‑general Abu Talib Othman as chair, and the **axing** of three commissioners who had **performed** their duties without fear or favour. The NGOs argue that dropping and **pleasing** the executive **undermines** Suhakam’s independence and that retired civil servants may **impinge** on objective oversight. While commissioner Prof Hamdan Adnan called the boycott **unfair**, the NGOs will continue to monitor **compliance** with past recommendations and judge Suhakam by **improvement** and **advancement** of human rights, not merely by reports.  

END OF SUMMARY.
