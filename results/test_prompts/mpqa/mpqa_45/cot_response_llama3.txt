SUMMARY:

Thirty-two non-governmental organizations have suspended all engagements with the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days, protesting the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations. The NGOs are concerned that the government has ignored Suhakam's reports and failed to respect or recognize its recommendations. This deep concern is rooted in the government's failure to adhere to Suhakam's suggestions, which creates the impression that Suhakam is nothing but a "mere public relations tool". The NGOs also lamented the decline of human rights in Malaysia, citing increasing numbers of people detained without trial and restrictions on freedom of speech and assembly.

The NGOs' promise to suspend engagements with Suhakam is aimed at highlighting the government's poor commitment to protecting civil liberties and advancing human rights. They will not hold any dialogues or send memorandums to Suhakam during this period, but will monitor the commission's performance in discharging its statutory duties.
