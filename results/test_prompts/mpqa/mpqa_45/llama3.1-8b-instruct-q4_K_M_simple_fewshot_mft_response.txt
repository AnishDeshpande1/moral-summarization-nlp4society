SUMMARY:

Thirty-two non-governmental organizations (NGOs) have suspended their engagement with the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days, citing the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations and the appointment of new commissioners who are perceived as not committed to protecting civil liberties. The NGOs express concern that the appointments undermine Suhakam's independence and erode public confidence in the institution. They also lament the decline of human rights in Malaysia, including increased detentions without trial and restrictions on free speech and assembly. The NGOs will monitor Suhakam's performance and review their position after 100 days. This boycott is a response to the government's failure to respect or recognize Suhakam's recommendations and its commitment to universal human rights principles.

The NGOs' action raises concerns about the authority of Suhakam to investigate human rights abuses, while the government's actions demonstrate a disregard for fairness and a lack of care for the well-being of citizens. The loyalty of Suhakam commissioners to their duties is also called into question by the appointment of new commissioners who are perceived as not committed to advancing human rights.
