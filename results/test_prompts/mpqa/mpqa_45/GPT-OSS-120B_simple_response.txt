SUMMARY:  
A coalition of 32 NGOs, led by Hakam president Ram Das Tikamdas, announced a 100‑day boycott of Malaysia’s Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) to protest the government’s disregard for the commission’s recommendations and to defend fundamental human‑rights values. The NGOs argue that the state’s failure to act on Suhakam’s maiden and freedom‑of‑assembly reports, its continued use of the Internal Security Act, bans on assemblies, curbs on free speech, and restrictive election‑law amendments betray democratic principles and reduce Suhakam to a “public‑relations tool.” They further condemn recent appointments—former attorney‑general Abu Talib Othman as chair and a slate of retired civil servants as commissioners—seeing these moves as undermining the commission’s independence and signaling that tenure depends on pleasing the executive. By suspending dialogue, memoranda and complaints, the NGOs intend to hold Suhakam accountable, monitoring whether it enforces past recommendations and genuinely improves human rights, rather than merely issuing statements. While Suhakam commissioner Prof Hamdan Adnan called the boycott “unfair,” the NGOs maintain that their moral duty is to demand genuine respect for universal human‑rights norms and to protect democratic space in Malaysia.  

END OF SUMMARY.
