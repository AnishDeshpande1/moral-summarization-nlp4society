SUMMARY:
Thirty‑two NGOs have suspended all engagement with Malaysia’s Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days to protest the government’s **disregard** of Suhakam’s reports and its **deteriorated** human‑rights record. The NGOs voiced **deep concern** that the government **dismissed** the maiden and freedom‑of‑assembly reports, **ignored** recommendations, and continued **detaining** people without trial, imposing a **ban** on assemblies, and imposing **restrictions** on free speech—actions they describe as **unfair** and a **betrayal** of democratic space. They condemned Prime Minister Mahathir’s **wrath** toward the Kesas report, which found **excessive** police force, and warned that the recent appointment of former attorney‑general Abu Talib Othman **undermines** Suhakam’s **independence**, **eroding** public confidence and turning the commission into a “**public‑relations tool**.” The NGOs argue the move is a **signal** that commissioners’ tenure depends on pleasing the executive, **degrading** the commission’s legitimacy. Suhakam commissioner Prof Hamdan Adnan called the boycott **unfair** and **disappointed**, urging NGOs to work **collaboratively** instead. The NGOs will review the boycott after the 100‑day period.
