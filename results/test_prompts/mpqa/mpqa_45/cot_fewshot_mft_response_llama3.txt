SUMMARY:

Thirty-two non-governmental organizations (NGOs) have suspended all engagements with the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days, citing concerns over the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations. The NGOs are protesting the government's disdain and wrath towards Suhakam's reports, which found excessive force used by police during a mass anti-government gathering. The boycott is also aimed at highlighting the deterioration of human rights in Malaysia, with increasing numbers of people detained without trial and restrictions on freedom of assembly and speech. The NGOs lamented the decline of human rights and the government's failure to respect or recognize Suhakam's recommendations, calling it a "public relations tool." The boycott aims to create pressure on the government to improve its human rights record and respect universal principles. END OF SUMMARY.
