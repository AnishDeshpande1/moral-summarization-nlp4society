SUMMARY:
Thirty-two Malaysian NGOs, led by human‑rights group Hakam’s president Ramdas Tikamdas, announced a 100‑day boycott of the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) to protest the government’s disregard for the commission’s recommendations on freedom of assembly and other rights. The NGOs criticize the government’s failure to act on Suhakam’s maiden and assembly reports, the continued use of the Internal Security Act, bans on public gatherings, restrictions on speech, and the lack of ratification of key international human‑rights treaties. They also question Suhakam’s independence after former attorney‑general Abu Talib Othman was appointed chair and three commissioners were removed, seeing the moves as political retaliation that undermines the commission’s credibility. The boycott, which includes groups such as MTUC, Suaram, Amnesty International Malaysia, JIM and Sisters in Islam, means NGOs will cease dialogues, memoranda or complaints to Suhakam while monitoring its performance. Suhakam commissioner Prof Hamdan Adnan called the boycott “unfair,” urging cooperation instead. The NGOs will reconvene after the 100‑day period to reassess their stance.

END OF SUMMARY.
