SUMMARY:
A coalition of 32 NGOs announced a 100‑day boycott of Malaysia’s Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) to protest what they see as government subversion of authority and a betrayal of democratic loyalty. They argue the state has abandoned care for its citizens, citing rising detentions without trial, police excesses, curbs on free speech and election‑law restrictions that degrade fundamental human dignity. The NGOs charge the government with unfairness and cheating, ignoring Suhakam’s maiden and freedom‑of‑assembly reports and dismissing the Kesas inquiry that condemned police brutality. Recent appointments—former attorney‑general Abu Talib Othman as chair and a slate of retired civil servants as commissioners—are portrayed as undermining the commission’s independence, signaling that tenure now depends on pleasing the executive, a clear subversion of institutional authority. The boycott is framed as a moral stand against the erosion of human‑rights sanctity and the betrayal of the commission’s mandate, while urging the state to honor international covenants and restore respect for universal rights. END OF SUMMARY.
