SUMMARY:

Thirty-two NGOs have suspended engagements with the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days due to the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations. The NGOs are protesting the appointment of new commissioners, including former Attorney-General Abu Talib Othman as chairperson, and the non-renewal of appointments for three previous commissioners who were instrumental in producing a scathing report on police brutality. The NGOs claim that the government has ignored Suhakam's reports and recommendations, leading to a decline in human rights in Malaysia. They are also concerned about the lack of commitment by the government to ratify international human rights instruments. The boycott will not include dialogues or submissions to Suhakam, but will instead monitor the commission's performance and compliance with previous recommendations.

END OF SUMMARY.
