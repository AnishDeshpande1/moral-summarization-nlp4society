SUMMARY:
Thirty-two NGOs have suspended all engagements with the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days, protesting the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations and concerns over the commission's independence. The NGOs are critical of the government's failure to respect and recognize Suhakam's recommendations, which has led to a decline in human rights in Malaysia. They also express concern over the appointment of former attorney-general Abu Talib Othman as chairperson and the decision not to renew appointments of three commissioners who were instrumental in producing critical reports. The NGOs will monitor Suhakam's performance and judge it based on improvement of human rights in Malaysia.

END OF SUMMARY.
