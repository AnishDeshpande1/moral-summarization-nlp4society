SUMMARY:

Thirty-two non-governmental organizations (NGOs) have suspended all engagements with the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days due to concerns over the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations. The NGOs are protesting the appointment of former attorney-general Abu Talib Othman as Suhakam's new chairperson and the decision not to renew the appointments of three commissioners who had been instrumental in producing critical reports. The NGOs claim that these developments undermine the independence of Suhakam and erode public confidence in the commission. They are also concerned about the appointment of five new commissioners, mostly retired civil servants, and worry that they may not be able to objectively assess and critique executive policies that impinge on human rights. The NGOs have vowed to monitor Suhakam's performance and will review their position after the 100-day period.

END OF SUMMARY.
