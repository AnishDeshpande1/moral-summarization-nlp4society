SUMMARY:

Thirty-two non-governmental organizations (NGOs) have suspended all engagements with the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days, citing poor government response to Suhakam's recommendations. The NGOs are protesting the government's failure to respect and recognize Suhakam's findings, which has led to a deep concern about the state of human rights in Malaysia. The government's excessive use of force during protests and restrictive amendments to election laws have further eroded democratic space. The NGOs' boycott is aimed at highlighting the government's poor record on human rights, including the deterioration of freedom of assembly and expression.

The Suhakam Act was touted as a positive development for protecting and promoting human rights in Malaysia, but the NGOs' disappointment with the commission's performance has led to this unprecedented action. The boycott is seen as unfair by one Suhakam commissioner, who urged the NGOs to work with the commission rather than resorting to self-interest. The NGOs' decision highlights the need for a more honest and transparent approach to human rights in Malaysia. END OF SUMMARY.
