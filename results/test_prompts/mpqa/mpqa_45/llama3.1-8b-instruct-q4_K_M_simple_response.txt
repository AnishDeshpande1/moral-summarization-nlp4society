SUMMARY:

Thirty-two Malaysian NGOs have suspended their engagement with the Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days, citing the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations and decline of human rights in Malaysia. The NGOs, including Hakam, MTUC, and Sisters in Islam, are protesting against the government's failure to respect Suhakam's recommendations, its treatment of critics, and the erosion of democratic space.

The NGOs have expressed concerns over the appointment of former Attorney-General Abu Talib Othman as Suhakam's new chairperson, which they perceive as a move to undermine the commission's independence. They also question his commitment to protecting civil liberties and advancing human rights, citing his involvement in the 1987 'Ops Lalang' operation.

The boycott is aimed at pressuring the government to take Suhakam seriously and adhere to its recommendations. The NGOs will review their position after 100 days and judge Suhakam's performance on its impact on human rights in Malaysia.

END OF SUMMARY.
