SUMMARY:

Thirty-two Malaysian NGOs have suspended their engagement with the Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days, starting from April 24. The boycott is in protest of the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations and the commission's perceived lack of independence. Hakam president Ramdas Tikamdas stated that the government has ignored Suhakam's reports on freedom of assembly and human rights, and that the commission's new chairperson, Abu Talib Othman, has a questionable track record on civil liberties.

The NGOs also expressed concerns over the appointment of five new commissioners, who are mostly retired civil servants. They worry that these individuals may not be able to objectively assess executive policies that impact human rights. The boycott will last for 100 days, after which the NGOs will review their position and decide whether to continue the boycott or engage with Suhakam again.

END OF SUMMARY.
