SUMMARY:

Thirty-two non-governmental organisations (NGOs) have suspended their engagements with the Malaysian Human Rights Commission (Suhakam) for 100 days, protesting the government's poor response to Suhakam's recommendations. The NGOs claim that the government has ignored Suhakam's reports and failed to show commitment to universal human rights principles. They lamented the decline of human rights in Malaysia, citing increasing numbers of people detained without trial under the Internal Security Act, blanket police bans on assemblies, and restrictions on free speech and expression.

The NGOs also expressed concern over the appointment of new commissioners, particularly Abu Talib Othman as chairperson, who has a history of defending government policies that impinge on human rights. They argue that this undermines the independence of Suhakam and erodes public confidence in the institution. The NGOs will monitor Suhakam's performance, judging it not on reports produced but on the improvement of human rights in Malaysia.

END OF SUMMARY.
