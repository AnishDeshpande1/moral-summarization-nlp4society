SUMMARY: President Robert Mugabe was sworn in for a fifth term in Zimbabwe amid widespread accusations of electoral fraud. Opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai and many Western governments denounced the vote as rigged, citing political violence, last‑minute legal changes, and the disenfranchisement of voters in Harare. While several African heads of state attended the ceremony, 57 opposition MPs and diplomats from the United States, EU, New Zealand and Canada boycotted it. Mugabe framed his victory as a strike against imperialism and pledged to accelerate land‑reform, seizing white‑owned farms for redistribution. He blamed criticism on racism and urged national reconciliation, though he stopped short of proposing a unity government. South African President Thabo Mbeki and Nigerian President Olusegun Obasanjo are urging Mugabe to form a coalition to ease sanctions and avoid suspension from the Commonwealth. A South African observer mission called the election “legitimate,” but Western leaders await Mbeki’s stance, fearing that support for Mugabe could signal a retreat from democratic norms and hinder economic revitalization in Africa. END OF SUMMARY.
