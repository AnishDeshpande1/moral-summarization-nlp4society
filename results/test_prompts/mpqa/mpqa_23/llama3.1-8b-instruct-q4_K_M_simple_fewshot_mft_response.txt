SUMMARY:

Zimbabwe's President Robert Mugabe was sworn in for his fifth term, despite allegations of election rigging and international condemnation. The opposition and Western governments denounced the election as rigged, but African heads of state attended the ceremony. However, 57 lawmakers from the opposition Movement for Democratic Change boycotted it, along with representatives from several countries. Mugabe blamed racism for the criticism and pledged to accelerate his land-reform program, which has led to widespread human rights abuses and economic decline. International donors have cut funding to Zimbabwe due to its poor governance record. South African President Thabo Mbeki is under pressure to denounce the results and promote a government of national unity to avoid deepening international sanctions. The situation highlights the tension between Mugabe's authoritarian rule and the demand for democratic principles and property rights in Africa.
