SUMMARY:
Zimbabwe's President Robert Mugabe was sworn in for his fifth term on Sunday, despite criticism from the opposition and Western governments that the election was rigged. Mugabe spoke publicly for the first time since his victory, blaming racism for the criticism and vowing to accelerate his land-reform program. He claimed his win was a triumph against imperialism and the West. The ceremony was attended by several African heads of state, but 57 lawmakers from the opposition Movement for Democratic Change boycotted it.

The election has been marred by months of political violence, intimidation, and last-minute changes to electoral laws. Tens of thousands of voters were disenfranchised in Harare, an opposition stronghold. The international community has condemned the election results, with many Western donors cutting funding to Zimbabwe due to its poor governance and human rights record.

South African President Thabo Mbeki is being urged to denounce the results and press Mugabe for a government of national unity to avoid deepening international sanctions. A South African observer mission has said the elections were "legitimate," but investors and Western diplomats are waiting for Mbeki's response before deciding on their next move.

END OF SUMMARY.
