**SUMMARY:**
President Robert Mugabe was sworn in for his fifth term in office, following a disputed election that Western governments denounced as rigged. He claimed victory as a triumph against imperialism, with several African heads of states attending the ceremony. However, the opposition Movement for Democratic Change boycotted it, along with representatives from Western countries, citing concerns over months of political violence and intimidation, electoral law changes, and disenfranchisement of voters in Harare. Mugabe blamed racism for the criticism, pledging to accelerate his land-reform program, which has been criticized for its lack of transparency and impact on the economy. The international community is pressuring him to accept a government of national unity to avoid deepening sanctions.
