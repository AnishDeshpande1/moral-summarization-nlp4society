SUMMARY:
Zimbabwe’s president Robert Mugabe was sworn in for a fifth term amid widespread accusations that the election was a rigged subversion of democratic authority. Opposition leaders and numerous Western governments denounced the vote as a cheat, citing political violence, last‑minute law changes and the disenfranchisement of tens of thousands of voters in the opposition stronghold of Harare. While African heads of state attended the ceremony, 57 opposition lawmakers and representatives from the United States, EU, New Zealand and Canada boycotted it in protest of the perceived fraud. Mugabe framed his victory as a triumph over imperialist authority, blaming criticism on racism and promising to accelerate land‑reform. International donors have responded to the alleged cheating by cutting aid, deepening Zimbabwe’s recession and food crisis. Regional leaders such as South African President Thabo Mbeki and Nigerian President Olusegun Obasanjo are urging Mugabe to consider a unity government to mitigate sanctions, while Western observers await a clear condemnation of the unfair electoral process.  

END OF SUMMARY.
