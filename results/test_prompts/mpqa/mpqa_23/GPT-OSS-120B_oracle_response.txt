SUMMARY:  
President Robert Mugabe was sworn in for a fifth term in a ceremony boycotted by 57 opposition lawmakers and Western delegations, who denounced the election as **rigged** and **tainted** by **violence**, **intimidation**, and last‑minute **changes** to the rules. Critics said tens of thousands of voters in Harare were **disenfranchised** after the government **reduced** polling stations, causing the opposition’s **failed** challenge. Mugabe framed his victory as a strike against **imperialism**, pledging to accelerate land **takeovers** despite the **lack** of transparency and a **short**‑term economic **legitimate** outlook. He urged the nation to **decide** its future, rejecting foreign criticism from Britain’s “10 Downing St.” and urging reconciliation, though he stopped short of proposing a unity government. Regional leaders, including South Africa’s Thabo Mbeki, are pressed to show the continent is **committed** to democratic **intent** and property rights, lest sanctions deepen.
