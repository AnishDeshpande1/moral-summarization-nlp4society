**SUMMARY:**  
Zimbabwe’s President Robert Mugabe was sworn in for a fifth term after an election the opposition and many Western governments denounced as **rigged** and **cheating**.  In a speech at the colonial‑style State House, Mugabe hailed his victory as a **blow to imperialism** and framed criticism as **racism** by the **whites** and the West.  Fifty‑seven opposition lawmakers **boycotted** the ceremony, citing **intimidation**, **violence**, and massive **disenfranchisement** that left tens of thousands of voters unable to cast ballots.  Mugabe pledged to accelerate land‑reform, declaring “Never again shall Zimbabwe be a **colon**y.”  Regional leaders, including South African President Thabo Mbeki, urged a **reconciliation** and a **government of national unity** to avoid deeper sanctions, while Western donors warned that support for the contested vote could signal a retreat from **fairness**, **property rights**, and democratic **authority**.  The ceremony highlighted the clash between claims of sovereign **subversion** of former colonial powers and accusations of undemocratic **cheating**.
