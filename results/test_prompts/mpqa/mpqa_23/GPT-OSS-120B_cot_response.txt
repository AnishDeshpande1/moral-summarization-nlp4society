**SUMMARY:**  
President Robert Mugabe was sworn in for a fifth term in a ceremony marked by a *boycott* from the opposition Movement for Democratic Change and absent Western delegations, who denounced the election as *rigged* and marred by *intimidation*, *violence* and *disenfranchisement*. Mugabe, a former *rebel* leader, framed his victory as a *triumph* over *imperialism*, *whites* and *racism*, declaring that Zimbabwe would no longer be a *colonial* outpost. He pledged to accelerate his land *reform* program, seizing farms from white owners to give to landless blacks. While African heads of state attended, the *opposition* and many Western governments protested, warning of *sanctions* and urging *reconciliation* and a government of national unity to address the deepening recession and food shortage. South African President Thabo Mbeki’s stance remains pivotal for regional credibility.
