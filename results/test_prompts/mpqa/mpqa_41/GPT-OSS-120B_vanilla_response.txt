SUMMARY:  
At the 56th UN General Assembly’s Third Committee, delegations highlighted that poverty, underdevelopment and health crises prevent people from fully enjoying human rights. The debate stressed that the right to development and international peace and security are essential preconditions for universal human rights protection. Thailand’s representative linked human rights and development as inseparable, urging people‑centered social and economic policies and protection from disease, drugs, conflict, terrorism and organized crime. Eritrea warned that globalization marginalizes developing nations and undermines their ability to exercise human rights. China’s delegate emphasized narrowing development gaps and noted that rapid globalization worsens conditions for poor countries. The United States argued that defeating terrorism requires safeguarding human rights and democracy, with no compromises allowed. Representatives from Cuba, Egypt, Morocco, Japan, Venezuela, Mali, Greece and Indonesia also contributed. The committee will reconvene Thursday to continue the debate.  
END OF SUMMARY.
