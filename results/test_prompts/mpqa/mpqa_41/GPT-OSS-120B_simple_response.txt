SUMMARY:  
At the UN General Assembly’s Third Committee, delegations warned that people trapped in poverty, hunger and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. They framed development as a moral prerequisite for universal human‑rights protection, alongside peace and security. Thailand’s delegate called human rights and development “two sides of the same coin” and urged that people be placed at the centre of social and economic progress, shielded from disease, drugs, conflict and organized crime. Eritrea warned that unchecked globalization marginalises the poor and erodes their right to development. China’s representative stressed that narrowing the development gap is essential for universal rights, noting that underdevelopment weakens states’ ability to protect their citizens. The United States linked the fight against terrorism to the safeguarding of human rights, insisting that no compromise or lapse in vigilance is acceptable. Representatives from Cuba, Egypt, Morocco, Japan, Venezuela, Mali, Greece and Indonesia also contributed. The committee will reconvene to continue debating how to ensure the effective enjoyment of human rights worldwide.  
END OF SUMMARY.
