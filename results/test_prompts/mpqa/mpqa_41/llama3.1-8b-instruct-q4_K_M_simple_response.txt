SUMMARY:

Delegates at the UN General Assembly's Third Committee emphasized that people suffering from poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. They stressed that the right to development is crucial for universal protection and promotion of human rights. Representatives highlighted the importance of addressing human rights and development in tandem, with people at the center of social and economic efforts.

Globalization was criticized for further marginalizing developing countries and hindering their ability to exercise human rights. The narrowing of the development gap between countries was seen as essential for realizing universal human rights. Delegates also emphasized the need to protect human rights during the war on terrorism, with no compromise or lapse in vigilance allowed.

Representatives from various countries, including Thailand, China, and the United States, shared their perspectives on the importance of promoting and strengthening human rights and democracy worldwide. The committee will continue its debate on Thursday, focusing on general questions related to human rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
