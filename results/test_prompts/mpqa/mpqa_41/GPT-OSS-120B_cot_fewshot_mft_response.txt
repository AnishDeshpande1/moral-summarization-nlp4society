**SUMMARY:**  
At the UN’s Third Committee debate, delegations warned that people suffering from **poverty**, **hunger**, and poor **health** cannot fully enjoy their **human rights** without addressing the **right to development**. They framed **development** as a precondition for **protection**, **security**, and **peace**, insisting that **terrorism**, **crime**, and other social ills must be combated without compromising **human rights** or **democracy**. Representatives highlighted how **globalization** can **marginalize** and **exploit** the developing world, widening **inequality** and threatening **fairness**. The United States stressed that the war on **terrorism** requires constant **vigilance** and cannot tolerate **compromise** that erodes **rights**. China, Eritrea, Thailand and others called for narrowing the **development** gap to ensure universal **freedom** and **protection**. The debate underscored that **authority** must serve the people, guarding against **oppression** and ensuring **care** for the most vulnerable. The committee will reconvene to continue this **fairness‑focused** discussion.
