SUMMARY:

Delegations at the Third Committee of the 56th UN General Assembly emphasized that people suffering from poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. Representatives stressed that the right to development is crucial for universal protection and promotion of human rights, along with maintaining international peace and security. Thailand's representative noted that human rights and development are interconnected and must be addressed together.

The Eritrea delegation warned that globalization is marginalizing the developing world and hindering its ability to exercise human rights. China's Shen Guofang emphasized the importance of narrowing the development gap between countries to realize universal human rights.

The United States representative stated that protecting human rights is essential in the war against terrorism, with no room for compromise or lapses in vigilance. Other delegations from Cuba, Egypt, Morocco, Japan, Venezuela, Mali, Greece, and Indonesia also shared their views on the importance of promoting and strengthening human rights worldwide.

END OF SUMMARY.
