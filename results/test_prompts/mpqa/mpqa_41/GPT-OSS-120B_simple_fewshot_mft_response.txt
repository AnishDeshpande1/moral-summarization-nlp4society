SUMMARY:  
During the UN General Assembly’s Third Committee debate, delegations warned that people living in poverty, hunger and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights, stressing the moral duty of the international community to protect the vulnerable. They framed the right to development as a **fair** prerequisite for universal human rights, arguing that narrowing the development gap is essential to prevent systemic injustice. Representatives highlighted that **care** for individuals must guide social and economic policies, insisting that development efforts place people at the centre and shield them from disease, drug abuse, armed conflict, terrorism and organized crime. The speakers also invoked **authority**, affirming the UN’s role and the need for coordinated, lawful action—without compromise or lapse in vigilance—to uphold human rights while combating terrorism. China, Thailand, Eritrea, the United States and other nations called for stronger, authoritative cooperation to ensure that globalization does not marginalize the poor and that development and security work together to secure human dignity worldwide. The committee will reconvene to continue this authoritative, caring, and fairness‑oriented discussion.  
END OF SUMMARY.
