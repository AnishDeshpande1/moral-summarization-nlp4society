**SUMMARY:**  
At the UN General Assembly’s Third Committee, delegations warned that people living in **poverty**, **hunger** and **poor** health cannot fully enjoy their **human rights**. They stressed that the **right to development** and **peace**‑**security** are essential preconditions for universal **human rights** protection. Representatives described **human rights** and **development** as two sides of the same coin, urging that people be placed at the centre of social and economic progress and shielded from **scourges** such as **disease**, **narcotic** abuse, **armed** conflict, **terrorism** and **organized crime**. The Eritrean delegate warned that **globalization** could **marginalize** and **alienate** the developing world, while China’s envoy said **underdevelopment** **hindered** human‑rights capacity and that the situation for poor nations was **deteriorating**. The U.S. representative linked the fight against **terrorism** to the protection of **human rights**, insisting there be no **compromise**, **expediency** or lapse in **vigilance**, and that promoting **democracy** is integral to the multilateral war on terror. The debate will continue the following day.
