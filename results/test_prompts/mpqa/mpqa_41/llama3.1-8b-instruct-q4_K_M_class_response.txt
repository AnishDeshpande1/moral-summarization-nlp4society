SUMMARY:

Representatives at the Third Committee of the 56th UN General Assembly emphasized that people suffering from poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. They stressed that the right to development is crucial for ensuring universal protection and promotion of human rights. Globalization was deemed a threat to further marginalize the developing world and alienate its masses, hampering their ability to exercise their rights. The representatives also highlighted the need to protect people from social ills like disease, narcotics, armed conflicts, terrorism, and transnational organized crime. Narrowing the development gap between countries is essential for realizing universal human rights. The representative of China noted that underdevelopment hinders developing countries' capacity to protect and promote human rights. The international community must work together to strengthen human rights and democracy, as this is integral to winning the war against terrorism.

The committee will continue its debate on Thursday, focusing on general questions related to human rights. Protecting people's enjoyment of their human rights requires sustained efforts, particularly in addressing the horrors caused by terrorism. By promoting and strengthening human rights, we can truly protect the most vulnerable populations.

END OF SUMMARY.
