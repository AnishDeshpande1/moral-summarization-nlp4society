SUMMARY:
The Third Committee of the 56th UN General Assembly debated human rights questions, emphasizing that people suffering from poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. The representatives stressed that the right to development is a crucial element for ensuring the universal protection and promotion of human rights, along with maintaining international peace and security. They highlighted that people should be at the center of social and economic development efforts, protected from scourges such as disease, narcotic drugs, armed conflicts, and terrorism. The representatives also noted that narrowing the development gap between countries is essential for realizing universal human rights. In this context, promoting and strengthening human rights and democracy are integral to winning the war against terrorism.
