SUMMARY:

Representatives at the Third Committee of the 56th UN General Assembly emphasized that people suffering from poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. They stressed that the right to development is crucial for ensuring universal protection and promotion of human rights. The committee also highlighted the importance of maintaining international peace and security in improving the effective enjoyment of human rights and fundamental freedoms.

The representatives noted that globalization is threatening to further marginalize developing countries, hindering their ability to exercise their rights and duties enshrined in international human rights instruments. They emphasized the need to address social ills such as disease, narcotics, armed conflicts, terrorism, and transnational organized crime.

The representative of China stressed that narrowing the development gap between countries is crucial for realizing universal human rights. The representative of the United States said that promoting and strengthening human rights and democracy throughout the world is an integral part of the multilateral war on terrorism.

END OF SUMMARY
