SUMMARY:
At the Third Committee (Social, Humanitarian, Cultural) of the 56th UN General Assembly, several delegations emphasized that people suffering from poverty, hunger, and poor health cannot fully enjoy their human rights. Representatives stressed that the right to development is a crucial element in ensuring universal protection and promotion of human rights, alongside maintaining international peace and security. They highlighted that globalization has threatened to marginalize developing countries and hinder their ability to exercise human rights. The representative of China noted that narrowing the development gap between countries is essential for realizing universal human rights. The US representative underscored the importance of protecting human rights in the war against terrorism, stating that there can be no compromise or lapse in vigilance.

The delegations called for addressing human rights and development in tandem, prioritizing people's well-being and protecting them from social ills such as disease, conflict, and crime. The committee will continue its general debate on human rights questions on Thursday. END OF SUMMARY.
