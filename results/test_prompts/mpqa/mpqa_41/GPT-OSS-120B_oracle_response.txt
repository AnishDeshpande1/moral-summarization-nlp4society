SUMMARY:  
At the UN’s Third Committee debate, delegations warned that people living in poverty, hunger and poor health cannot fully **enjoy** their human **rights** without the **protection** and **promotion** of development and **peace**. They argued that the **right to development** and **security** are pre‑conditions for the **realization** of universal **freedoms**. Representatives from Thailand, Eritrea, China, the United States and others stressed that **human rights** and development must be **addressed** together, with people placed at the centre of social and economic **improving** efforts and **protected** from **conflicts**, **terrorism**, narcotics and transnational **crime**. The speakers warned that **globalization** can **threaten**, **marginalize** and **alienate** the poor, **hampering** their ability to fulfil **duties** and enjoy **rights**. They called for **narrowing** development gaps, **ensuring** **security**, and avoiding any **expediency**, **compromise** or **lapse** in vigilance. The debate highlighted that the fight against terrorism must be coupled with **promoting** and **strengthening** human **rights** and democracy worldwide.  

END OF SUMMARY.
