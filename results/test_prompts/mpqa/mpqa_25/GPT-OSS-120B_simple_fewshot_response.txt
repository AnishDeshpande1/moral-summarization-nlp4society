SUMMARY:
Venezuela erupted in a day of violent upheaval that left at least 11 dead and over 80 injured, ending a three‑day general strike and ushering in a new government. Business leader Pedro Carmona declared the country’s name changed from the “Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela” to simply the “Republic of Venezuela,” positioning the rebranding as a break from Hugo Chávez’s legacy. After a massive, mostly middle‑class protest was brutally suppressed—snipers and Chávez supporters opened fire on demonstrators—military officers denounced the killings and surrounded the presidential palace. Chávez, who had just censored five TV channels, was forced to resign and was taken into custody at the Fuerte Tiuna army base. Carmona announced the end of the strike and the transition government’s takeover, while citizens gathered at the La Carlota air base hoping to see Chávez leave the country. The nation now faces a power vacuum and the daunting task of healing deep social divisions, with many expressing relief at Chávez’s fall but uncertainty about the country’s future direction.  
END OF SUMMARY.
