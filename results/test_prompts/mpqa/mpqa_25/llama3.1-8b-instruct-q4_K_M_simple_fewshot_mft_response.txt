SUMMARY:

Venezuela's new president, Pedro Carmona, announced the end of a general strike and oil industry stoppage that had threatened to cripple the country's economy. However, the name change from the Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela to simply the Republic of Venezuela was met with uncertainty among Venezuelans. The previous day, a peaceful rally turned violent when shots were fired into the crowd by armed supporters of former President Hugo Chavez. This led to the resignation of Chavez and his detention at Fuerte Tiuna army base in Caracas. The military's refusal to tolerate the violence marked a turning point in the crisis, with chief of the armed forces General Lucas Rincon stating that Chavez's resignation had been demanded and accepted. The country is now facing a power vacuum and deep social divisions, leaving many Venezuelans feeling uncertain about how their problems will be solved.

The change in government was met with a mix of emotions among Venezuelans, ranging from relief to uncertainty. While some saw it as an opportunity for the country to move forward, others were concerned about the future and how the new leaders would address the country's deep-seated issues. The crisis highlighted the need for greater accountability and respect for human rights in Venezuela.
