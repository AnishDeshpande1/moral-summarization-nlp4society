SUMMARY:
Venezuela woke to a **violent** 24‑hour upheaval that left at least 11 **dead** and more than 80 **injured**. A three‑day **strike** by oil workers and a **threatened**, **cripple**‑inducing shutdown of PDVSA ended when business leader Pedro Carmona declared the country no longer the Bolivarian Republic but the **Republic of Venezuela**. Carmona’s transition government halted the **protesting** managers’ **striking** and announced the end of the **general strike**, yet traffic remained light as many citizens stayed home, still reeling from the **bloodshed** of a previous rally where **shots** were **fired** at over 100,000 demonstrators. Eyewitnesses reported **snipers** and armed **Bolivarian** supporters **firing** indiscriminately, an act described as **horrible** **shooting** of defenseless people. President Hugo Chávez, who had just censored five TV channels for **lies**, was forced to resign after military officers condemned the **killings** as “not tolerable.” He was detained and may face **charges**. The nation now faces a power vacuum and must **heal** deep **divisions** while confronting uncertainty about its future.  
END OF SUMMARY.
