SUMMARY:
A violent and dramatic turn of events in Venezuela has led to the resignation of President Hugo Chavez, who was ousted by his own military after the deaths of at least 11 people and over 80 injuries during a peaceful rally against him. The country's economy had been crippled by a general strike called by business leaders, but this has now ended. A new president, Pedro Carmona, has taken office and announced a name change for the country from the Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela to simply the Republic of Venezuela. However, the transition government faces a power vacuum and deep social divisions that need to be addressed.

The events were sparked by Chavez's authoritarian actions, including filling the state oil company's board with his supporters, taking TV channels off the air for reporting "lies", and using violence against peaceful protesters. The military turned against him after the shootings, and he is now detained at an army base. The country is in shock, but many Venezuelans are hopeful that a new era of freedom and democracy will begin.
