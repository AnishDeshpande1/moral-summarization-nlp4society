SUMMARY:

A dramatic turn of events in Venezuela has led to the ousting of President Hugo Chavez, who was detained at an army base. A general strike and oil industry stoppage were called off by new president Pedro Carmona, but many Venezuelans did not return to work due to shock and uncertainty. At least 11 people died and over 80 were injured in clashes between protesters and government supporters on Thursday. The shootings sparked a military mutiny, with several officers announcing their support for the people and condemning the killings. Chavez's resignation was accepted, but his fate is uncertain, and he may face charges related to the killings. Venezuela now has a power vacuum and deep social divisions that need to be addressed. New president Carmona announced the end of the general strike and name change from the Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela to simply the Republic of Venezuela.

END OF SUMMARY.
