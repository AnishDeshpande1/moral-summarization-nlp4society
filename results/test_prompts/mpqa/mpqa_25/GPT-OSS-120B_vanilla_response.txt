SUMMARY: In a single day of upheaval, Venezuela experienced a violent climax that resulted in at least 11 deaths and over 80 injuries, ending a three‑day general strike and triggering a regime change. Business leader Pedro Carmona assumed control of a transition government, first renaming the nation from the “Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela” to the “Republic of Venezuela.” He also announced the cessation of the strike that had crippled the oil sector after managers protested President Hugo Chávez’s politicized appointments at PDVSA. The unrest followed a massive, mostly middle‑class rally of over 100,000 people demanding Chávez’s resignation, which turned deadly when armed pro‑Chávez groups opened fire near the Miraflores palace. Chávez, who had just censored five TV channels, was forced to resign after military officers condemned the killings. He was detained at the Fuerte Tiuna base, while his family fled to Barquisimeto. The power vacuum leaves Venezuela facing deep social divisions and uncertainty about future governance, though many citizens express relief at Chávez’s fall.  

END OF SUMMARY.
