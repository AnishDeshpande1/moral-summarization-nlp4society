SUMMARY:

US President Bush has ordered the CIA to assassinate opponents of the US worldwide, contradicting the government's claim to fight terrorism globally. This directive is reminiscent of Iran's response to Salman Rushdie's book insulting the Prophet Muhammad, where the Imam issued a fatwa and Islamic countries recalled their ambassadors from Tehran. The author argues that if European countries were sincere in condemning Iran for violating human rights, they should also recall their ambassadors from the US, given its alleged assassination order against millions of people without proof or reason. The article claims that the US is taking justice back to pre-historic times and using military force to silence the cries of the global community.

The author concludes that no country will tolerate this cruelty, and the world's human rights achievements should not be wasted due to US actions. The US believes it can justify its behavior with the sound of explosions from 7-ton bombs, but this is an attempt to silence justice-seeking voices worldwide.

END OF SUMMARY.
