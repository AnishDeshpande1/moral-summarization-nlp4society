SUMMARY: The author condemns President Bush’s alleged order for the CIA to assassinate perceived opponents worldwide, framing it as a gross violation of universal human rights. By contrasting this with the international outcry over the Iranian fatwa on Salman Rushdie—presented as a defense of religious respect—the piece argues that Western nations hypocritically claim to champion human rights while planning mass killings. It calls for Islamic countries to withdraw their ambassadors from the United States, just as European states recalled theirs from Tehran, asserting that the U.S. is now the true aggressor against countless innocent lives. The narrative portrays the U.S. response to 9/11 as a regression to “pre‑historic” cruelty, threatening the hard‑won, God‑given rights humanity has fought for over millennia. The author urges the global community to reject this “inhuman behavior” and defend the moral principle of protecting all human life. END OF SUMMARY.
