SUMMARY: The article claims that President George W. Bush ordered the CIA to assassinate America’s opponents worldwide, presenting this as a response to the United States’ anti‑terrorism stance after 9/11. It juxtaposes this alleged directive with the 1989 fatwa issued by Iran’s Supreme Leader against author Salman Rushdie, noting that European nations recalled their ambassadors from Tehran in protest. The piece argues that Iran’s fatwa was framed as defending the rights of Muslims, while the purported U.S. assassination order violates the rights of millions. It calls for Islamic countries to withdraw their ambassadors from the United States, accusing the U.S. of using military force to suppress human rights and “justice‑seeking cries” worldwide. The article presents a highly critical, alarmist view of U.S. foreign policy, without citing evidence. END OF SUMMARY.
