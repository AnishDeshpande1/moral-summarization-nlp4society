SUMMARY:
The article condemns President George W. Bush’s alleged order for the CIA to assassinate anyone who opposes the United States, portraying it as a gross violation of human rights and international law. It draws a parallel to the 1989 fatwa against author Salman Rushdie, arguing that while the Iranian clerics framed the edict as defending the Prophet and the rights of millions, the U.S. is now targeting countless individuals without evidence or due process. The piece calls on Islamic and other nations to withdraw their ambassadors from Washington in retaliation, accusing the United States of “pre‑historic” cruelty and of trying to silence global calls for justice with military force. By highlighting the contrast between the U.S. claim to lead the fight against terrorism and its purported secret assassination program, the article frames the policy as an immoral, inhuman abuse of power that threatens the very human‑rights principles it claims to protect.

END OF SUMMARY.
