SUMMARY: Jordan announced it will sign the Kyoto Protocol, a UN pact aimed at **reducing** global greenhouse‑gas emissions, after a Cabinet decision. Director General Faris Juneidi said Prince Zeid Ben Raad will be delegated to sign, though the exact date remains unset. While developed nations are the primary polluters, Jordan’s move underscores its “commitment towards the different international conventions” and hopes to attract funding for climate‑change mitigation. The signing follows recent EU ratifications and comes as the United States has **opposed** the treaty, claiming it harms its economic interests, while Canada has expressed similar concerns about potential GDP loss. By joining the agreement, Jordan aligns with international efforts to curb emissions that trap heat in the atmosphere. END OF SUMMARY.
