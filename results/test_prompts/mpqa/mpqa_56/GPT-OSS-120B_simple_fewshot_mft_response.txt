SUMMARY:
Jordan announced it will formally sign the Kyoto Protocol, delegating Prince Zeid Ben Raad to the United Nations as the country’s authorized representative. The decision, made by the Cabinet and communicated by GCEP director Faris Juneidi, underscores Jordan’s respect for international authority and its commitment to uphold globally‑agreed rules on greenhouse‑gas reductions. By joining the pact, Jordan positions itself on the side of fairness, signaling that it will honor shared environmental obligations while highlighting the reluctance of major emitters such as the United States and Canada, which have resisted or labeled the agreement as economically harmful. The move is presented as a principled effort to contribute to collective climate action and to attract funding, contrasting Jordan’s compliance with the perceived cheating of countries that sidestep the protocol’s duties.  
END OF SUMMARY.
