SUMMARY:
Jordan plans to sign the Kyoto Protocol, a UN agreement aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions, following a recent Cabinet decision. The country's representative, HH Prince Zeid Ben Raad, will be delegated to sign the protocol once an exact date is set. Jordan's decision is seen as a commitment to international environmental conventions and hopes to attract more funding for its efforts in reducing greenhouse gases. The General Corporation for Environment Protection (GCEP) is the country's point of reference for environmental pacts.

The Kyoto Protocol obliges developed countries to reduce emissions by 8% below 1990 levels between 2008 and 2012. So far, only European Union member states have agreed to be formally bound to the accord. The US administration has opposed the protocol in the past, citing economic interests, while other developed countries like Canada have expressed reluctance due to potential economic damage.

END OF SUMMARY.
