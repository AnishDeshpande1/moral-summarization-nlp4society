SUMMARY:  
Jordan announced it will join the Kyoto Protocol, the United Nations agreement that obliges developed nations to cut greenhouse‑gas emissions, signaling the country’s moral commitment to global environmental stewardship. Director‑General Faris Juneidi said Prince Zeid Ben Raad will be delegated to sign, though a precise date is pending. While the Protocol primarily targets industrialized states—whose historic pollution has driven climate change—Jordan’s participation aims to demonstrate solidarity with international conventions and to attract funding for climate‑mitigation projects. The move follows recent European Union ratifications and contrasts with the United States and Canada, which have resisted the pact on economic grounds, claiming it would harm growth and GDP. By signing, Jordan seeks to align itself with the ethical responsibility to protect the planet for current and future generations.  

END OF SUMMARY.
