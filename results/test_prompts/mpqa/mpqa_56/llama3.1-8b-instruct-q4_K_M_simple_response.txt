SUMMARY:

Jordan announced its decision to sign the Kyoto Protocol, a UN agreement aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions. The country's commitment to international environmental conventions is highlighted by this move. Jordan's General Corporation for Environment Protection (GCEP) hopes that signing the protocol will attract more funding to support global efforts in reducing climate change.

The Kyoto Protocol requires developed countries to reduce their emissions by 8% below 1990 levels between 2008 and 2012. While some developed countries, such as the US, have expressed reluctance or opposition to the protocol due to concerns over economic costs, others like the European Union are moving forward with ratification.

Jordan's decision is seen as a step towards international cooperation on environmental issues. By signing the Kyoto Protocol, Jordan aims to demonstrate its commitment to reducing greenhouse gas emissions and contributing to global efforts in combating climate change.

END OF SUMMARY.
