SUMMARY: Jordan announced plans to sign the Kyoto Protocol, the United Nations agreement that obliges developed nations to cut greenhouse‑gas emissions by 8 % below 1990 levels between 2008 and 2012. The decision follows a recent Cabinet approval, and the Jordanian government will delegate Prince Zeid Ben Raad to sign, though no exact date has been set. Director General Faris Juneidi of the General Corporation for Environment Protection (GCEP) said the move underscores Jordan’s commitment to international environmental conventions and aims to attract funding for climate‑change mitigation. GCEP, under the Ministry of Municipal and Rural Affairs and the Environment, serves as Jordan’s liaison for such pacts. The article also notes recent developments: fifteen EU member states have agreed to ratify the protocol by June 1, while the United States and some other developed countries, such as Canada, have resisted or opposed the agreement, citing economic concerns. END OF SUMMARY.
