SUMMARY:

Jordan has decided to sign the Kyoto Protocol, a pact aimed at impeding global greenhouse gas emissions. The country's decision is seen as a commitment towards international conventions and an effort to stress its dedication to reducing harmful pollutants. Jordan's General Corporation for Environment Protection (GCEP) hopes that by signing the protocol, it will attract more funding from the international community to contribute to efforts in reducing greenhouse gases, which trap heat in the atmosphere causing climate change. The Kyoto Protocol was legally obliged developed countries to reduce their emissions by 8% below 1990 levels between 2008 and 2012. Other developed countries like the US and Canada have shown reluctance towards signing the protocol, citing economic concerns, but Jordan's decision is seen as a step in the right direction for the international community.
