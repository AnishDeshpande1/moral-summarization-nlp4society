SUMMARY:

The ratification of the Kyoto Protocol by Russia is crucial for addressing global climate change, as it would give the country a controlling block of shares in controlling greenhouse gas emissions. However, experts disagree on the benefits of ratifying the protocol. Some argue that Russia can gain from perturbations of the climate, such as increased agricultural production, while others claim that this approach will not provide an impetus for high-tech development and may even harm the country's international reputation.

Professor Viktor Danilov-Danilyan emphasizes that the Kyoto Protocol is a complex issue that requires a careful balancing of economic and environmental considerations. He also highlights the importance of using scientific knowledge to inform decision-making, rather than relying on flawed models.

The article concludes that Russia's ratification of the Kyoto Protocol would be a significant step towards addressing global climate change, but it must be done in a way that balances competing interests and priorities. By doing so, Russia can demonstrate its commitment to environmental protection and assume a leadership role in international efforts to address this pressing issue.

END OF SUMMARY.
