SUMMARY:  
Tomorrow the Russian government will decide whether to ratify the Kyoto Protocol, an agreement that could curb greenhouse‑gas emissions worldwide. Scientists and journalists debated Russia’s role: some argue that a warming climate will turn permafrost into fertile land, giving Russia a “hot‑air” surplus it could sell for profit, while others warn that such benefits are illusory and that climate change will bring extreme weather, infrastructure damage, and ecological collapse across the country. Pro‑ratification voices stress that without Russia (and the United States) the treaty would collapse, and that selling emission credits could generate billions for the state. Opponents counter that relying on “hot‑air” trade would hinder technological development and isolate Russia from the EU’s greener future. Experts also question the severity of projected warming and call for deeper paleoclimatic research, noting that climate models are imperfect but human activity clearly contributes significantly to warming. The moral dilemma emerges: should Russia exploit short‑term economic gains from climate disruption, or accept responsibility by joining a global effort that, while costly, promotes sustainable development and avoids catastrophic environmental damage?  

END OF SUMMARY.
