SUMMARY:

The Russian government is set to ratify the Kyoto Protocol, an international document aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions. A group of scientists has discussed Russia's role in global climate strategy, highlighting its potential benefits and challenges. While some experts believe that Russia can gain from global warming, others argue that it poses a significant threat to the country's agriculture and ecosystems.

The Kyoto Protocol assigns countries quotas on their emissions, allowing them to buy and sell "hot air" credits if they exceed or fall short of their targets. Russia has signed the protocol but is yet to ratify it, despite having the largest share of emission reductions possible under the agreement. Ratification would allow Russia to benefit from increased demand for gas and become a dominant seller in the market.

However, not all experts agree that ratifying the Kyoto Protocol is in Russia's best interest. Some argue that it would lead to a lack of effort in reducing emissions and investing in nature-conservation technologies, ultimately knocking Russia out of the group of countries striving for rational development.

END OF SUMMARY.
