SUMMARY:
Russia is poised to decide whether to ratify the Kyoto Protocol, a key international treaty aimed at cutting greenhouse‑gas emissions. Scientists and officials debated the nation’s strategic position: some argue Russia’s excess “hot air” could turn it into a lucrative seller of emission credits, while others warn that treating climate policy as a profit‑making scheme undermines the moral imperative to combat global warming. Experts highlighted the uncertainty of climate models, the potential harms of warming for Russia’s permafrost, agriculture and infrastructure, and the broader worldwide risks of sea‑level rise and ecosystem disruption. The discussion underscored that genuine environmental stewardship—not short‑term economic gain—is essential; without Russia’s sincere participation, the Kyoto mechanism loses effectiveness, and the global community’s effort to safeguard the planet falters. The moral of the story is that Russia’s responsible ratification and active engagement in climate mitigation are crucial for collective survival and ethical responsibility toward future generations.  
END OF SUMMARY.
