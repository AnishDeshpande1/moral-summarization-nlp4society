SUMMARY:

Russia's ratification of the Kyoto Protocol, an international document aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions, has been put on the agenda for tomorrow's meeting of the Russian government. Experts from various fields have weighed in on the issue, with some arguing that Russia can gain from global warming due to its potential impact on agriculture. However, others have expressed concerns about the feasibility and effectiveness of this approach. A senior adviser to the Foreign Ministry has warned that relying solely on trading emissions quotas could hinder Russia's economic development and integration into the European Union. The Kyoto Protocol assigns countries quotas for their greenhouse gas emissions, with developed countries required to reduce theirs by 2008-2012. Russia's obligations under the protocol are relatively mild, requiring it only to maintain records of its emissions.
