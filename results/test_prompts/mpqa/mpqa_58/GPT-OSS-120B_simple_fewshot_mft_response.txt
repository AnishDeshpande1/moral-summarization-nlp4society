SUMMARY:
Russia is poised to decide whether to ratify the Kyoto Protocol, a key international treaty aimed at curbing greenhouse‑gas emissions. Experts at a closed meeting warned that global warming threatens both the planet and Russian citizens, citing extreme temperature swings, permafrost melt, and ecosystem collapse that could endanger lives, agriculture and infrastructure. They argued that Russia’s “hot‑air” surplus gives it a strategic advantage in the carbon‑credit market, but warned that exploiting this for short‑term profit would betray a broader responsibility to protect the nation and the world. Senior officials, invoking the authority of the Foreign Ministry, urged ratification, emphasizing that Russia’s cooperation is essential for the protocol’s success and for aligning with the EU and other allies. Critics within the scientific community cautioned against over‑optimistic claims of climate benefit for Russia, stressing that unchecked warming harms the vulnerable and undermines national loyalty to future generations. The debate frames the decision as a test of governmental authority, a moral duty to safeguard people from climate harm, and a question of loyalty to both domestic welfare and the international community.

END OF SUMMARY.
