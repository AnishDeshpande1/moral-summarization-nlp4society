SUMMARY:
NASA’s first permanent rail system in orbit hit a snag on its maiden run when the one‑ton railcar drifted slightly off its magnetic guide in zero‑gravity, causing the automatic latching system to fail. Engineers quickly shifted to manual control, and astronaut Carl Walz repeatedly commanded the car to move, allowing ground controllers to secure it each time. The incident highlighted the challenges of adapting terrestrial technology to the weightless environment, but also demonstrated the crew’s resourcefulness and teamwork in troubleshooting a $190 million asset. NASA officials said software tweaks may smooth future operations, and assured that safety rings prevent any danger of the car leaving the 44‑foot girder. The railcar is slated to transport the station’s 58‑foot robotic arm later this summer as additional girders are installed, with a final spacewalk planned to complete the girder’s installation. This episode underscores the perseverance and collaborative problem‑solving required to build and maintain humanity’s outpost in space.  
END OF SUMMARY.
