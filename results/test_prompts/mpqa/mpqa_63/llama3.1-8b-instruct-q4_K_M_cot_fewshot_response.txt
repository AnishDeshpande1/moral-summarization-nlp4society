SUMMARY:
The inaugural run of the international space station's first permanent railroad in orbit was interrupted due to a snag. The railcar, which is needed for transporting the space station's robot arm, floated off the rail and lost contact with the magnetic sensors on the bottom of the car. This problem arose because of weightlessness, causing the sensors to malfunction. NASA engineers suspect that this issue may become more frequent if not addressed through adjustments to the computer software controlling the railcar. Despite the snag, there is no danger of the $190 million railcar rolling off the track due to safety features installed on both ends of the girder. The first use of the railcar is set for late summer, and engineers are working to resolve this issue before then.
