SUMMARY: Iranian President Mohammad Khatami congratulated Venezuela on the restoration of President Hugo Chávez after a failed military coup. In a phone call, Khatami praised the return of the “legitimate government” as a triumph of justice and freedom and expressed hopes that Chávez could pursue his goals with popular support. Chávez thanked Khatami, noting that the coup attempt, which aimed to push Venezuela out of OPEC and increase oil production, was thwarted with divine help and popular resistance. He briefed Khatami on the situation and both leaders voiced a desire to meet soon. Iran had earlier condemned the coup, calling it an unacceptable use of force, and sent congratulations when Chávez was rescued from a Caribbean island and reinstated. Chávez, whose term runs until 2006, remains a strong advocate for OPEC oil production cuts and had hosted the 2000 OPEC summit in Caracas. END OF SUMMARY.
