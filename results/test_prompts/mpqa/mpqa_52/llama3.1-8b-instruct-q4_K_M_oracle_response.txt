SUMMARY: Iranian President Mohammad Khatami congratulated the Venezuelan nation on the reestablishment of their legitimate government, led by President Hugo Chavez, following a failed military coup attempt. Khatami referred to this event as a victory for justice and freedom, expressing hope that the Venezuelan government would achieve its objectives with the support of its people. In a telephone conversation, Chavez acknowledged the Almighty God's and the people's role in thwarting the anti-democratic attempts to topple his government. He also revealed that those behind the coup sought to undermine the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) by increasing oil production. The Iranian president condemned the ouster of the democratically elected Venezuelan president, stating that using force is not an acceptable method for bringing about political change and is incompatible with human society's norms. Chavez was detained after the failed coup attempt but managed to return to power, resuming his functions as president.

END OF SUMMARY.
