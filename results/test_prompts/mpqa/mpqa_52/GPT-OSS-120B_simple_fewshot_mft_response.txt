SUMMARY:
Iranian President Mohammad Khatami praised Venezuela’s “victory of justice and freedom” after the failed military coup that briefly ousted the democratically elected President Hugo Chávez. In a telephone call, Khatami affirmed the legitimacy of Chávez’s authority and condemned the subversive attempt to overthrow a lawful government, labeling the coup as a breach of human‑societal norms. Chávez, invoking divine assistance, thanked Khatami and highlighted the coup’s motive to pull Venezuela out of OPEC and undermine collective oil‑production fairness. Both leaders framed the episode as a triumph of care for the Venezuelan people, who, together with God, repelled the cheating forces that sought to degrade the nation’s sovereign order. The exchange underscored a shared commitment to uphold rightful authority, protect citizens from harm, enforce fair international cooperation, and preserve the sanctity of legitimate governance. Both presidents expressed a desire to meet soon to strengthen their alliance against future subversion.

END OF SUMMARY.
