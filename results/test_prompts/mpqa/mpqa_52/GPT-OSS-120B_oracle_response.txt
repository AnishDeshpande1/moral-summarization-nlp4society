SUMMARY: President Mohammad Khatami congratulated the Venezuelan nation on the **justice** and **freedom** achieved with the **reestablishment** of President Hugo Chávez’s legitimate government after a failed **coup**. In a telephone conversation, Khatami praised the **difficult** week Venezuela endured, calling the **attempts** to **topple** the elected leaders a defeat of **force** and an unacceptable **method** that violated the **conformity** of modern human society. Chávez, who had been **detained** and briefly **forced** out of office amid **violent** protests that resulted in **deaths**, thanked Khatami and noted that the conspirators sought to pull Venezuela out of OPEC by increasing oil output. Both presidents expressed hope that the restored government would pursue its **exalted** objectives with popular support and pledged to meet soon. Iran also condemned the **ouster** and the **collapsed** two‑day military takeover, reaffirming solidarity with Venezuela’s return to lawful rule. END OF SUMMARY.
