SUMMARY: President Mohammad Khatami praised the “victory of justice and freedom” achieved when Venezuela’s democratically elected leader Hugo Chávez was restored after a failed military coup. In a phone call, Khatami congratulated the Venezuelan people and expressed hope that Chávez’s return would enable the government to pursue its “exalted objectives” with popular support. Chávez, thanking Khatami, framed the thwarted coup as an anti‑democratic plot aimed at pulling Venezuela out of OPEC and undermining the organization, noting that divine help and the will of the people protected the nation. Iran reiterated its condemnation of the coup, calling the use of force “unacceptable” and contrary to contemporary human‑societal norms. Both presidents voiced a desire to meet soon, reaffirming mutual solidarity against attempts to subvert lawful, popular governance. END OF SUMMARY.
