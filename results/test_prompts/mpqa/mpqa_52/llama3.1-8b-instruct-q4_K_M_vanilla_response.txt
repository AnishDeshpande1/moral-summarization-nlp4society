SUMMARY:
Iranian President Mohammad Khatami congratulated Venezuelan President Hugo Chavez on his return to power after a failed military coup. In a telephone conversation, Khatami expressed hope that the Venezuelan government would achieve its objectives with the support of the people. Chavez thanked Khatami for the call and briefed him on the latest developments in Venezuela. He revealed that the attempted coup was aimed at having Venezuela leave OPEC and produce more oil to undermine the organization.

Iran had previously condemned the ouster of Chavez, calling it an unacceptable method of bringing about political change. The country sent congratulations to Venezuela after the return of the rule of law and reinstatement of the legitimate government. Chavez was brought back to power on Sunday by helicopter from a Caribbean island where he was detained. His mandate as president is due to end in 2006.

Khatami and Chavez expressed a mutual desire to meet in the near future, ending the conversation on a positive note. The events in Venezuela have significant implications for oil production and prices globally.

END OF SUMMARY.
