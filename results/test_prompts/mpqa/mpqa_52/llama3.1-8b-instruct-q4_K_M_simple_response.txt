SUMMARY:

Iranian President Mohammad Khatami congratulated Venezuelan President Hugo Chavez on his return to power after a failed military coup. Khatami described the event as a victory for justice and freedom, highlighting the importance of democratic rule. He expressed hope that with Chavez' reinstatement, the Venezuelan government would be able to achieve its objectives with the support of the people.

Chavez thanked Khatami for his congratulations and briefed him on the latest developments in Venezuela. He revealed that the coup plotters had aimed to undermine OPEC by increasing oil production and pushing Venezuela out of the organization. Iran had previously condemned the coup, stating that force is not an acceptable method of political change.

The two presidents expressed a mutual desire to meet soon, and Khatami reiterated Iran's support for Chavez' government. The event was seen as a significant victory for democracy in Venezuela, and Iran's strong stance against authoritarianism was reaffirmed.

END OF SUMMARY.
