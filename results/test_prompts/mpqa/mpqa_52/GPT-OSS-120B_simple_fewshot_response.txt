SUMMARY:
Iranian President Mohammad Khatami praised the restoration of Venezuela’s “legitimate” government after a failed military coup, calling it a triumph of justice and freedom. In a phone call with Hugo Chávez, Khatami expressed hope that the reinstated leadership would achieve its “exalted objectives” with popular support, while Chávez thanked Iran for its solidarity and condemned the coup plotters, accusing them of trying to pull Venezuela out of OPEC and undermine the oil cartel. Both leaders condemned the use of force to effect political change, emphasizing respect for democratic legitimacy and the rule of law. The two presidents pledged to meet soon and reaffirmed mutual support between Iran and Venezuela. Iran had earlier condemned the coup as unacceptable and congratulated Venezuela on the return of legal order. The episode underscores a shared moral stance against undemocratic power grabs and highlights the alliance of two oil‑rich nations defending each other’s sovereignty.

END OF SUMMARY.
