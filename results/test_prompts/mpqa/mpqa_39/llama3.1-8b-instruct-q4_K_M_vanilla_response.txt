SUMMARY:

The Islamic Human Rights Commission (IHRC) in Tehran expressed concern about the resurgence of force and violence in international relations, marking International Day for Human Rights on December 10. The IHRC stated that despite numerous documents signed to promote human rights, provisions of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights have not been implemented. The commission called for an end to discrimination, torture, and arbitrary detention, as well as respect for women's rights, political rights, and association rights.

The IHRC also condemned injustice against the Palestinian nation and violation of human rights in various parts of the world, including against journalists, religious minorities, children, and women. The commission urged statesmen to heed religious teachings on human rights and called for an end to military power and respect for civilians' rights.

Meanwhile, UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Mary Robinson urged Israel to stop bombing Palestinian targets in the West Bank and Gaza Strip and allow international monitors. She denounced Israeli attacks against Palestinian activists, calling for a halt to targeted killings of Palestinians.

END OF SUMMARY.
