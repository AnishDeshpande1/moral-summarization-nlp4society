SUMMARY:
The Islamic Human Rights Commission (IHRC) in Tehran expressed concern about the resurgence of force and violence in international relations, undermining human rights. Despite numerous documents signed to promote human rights, IHRC lamented that these provisions have not been materialized, leading to suffering and loss for humanity. The commission highlighted the importance of prohibiting discrimination, torture, arbitrary detention, and honoring human rights, including those related to women's rights and associations. IHRC also condemned injustice against the Palestinian nation and violation of human rights in various parts of the world, urging statesmen to heed religious teachings on respect for human rights.

In a separate development, United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights Mary Robinson called on Israel to stop bombing Palestinian targets and allow international monitors, denouncing Israeli attacks against Palestinian activists. Both IHRC and Ms. Robinson emphasized the need for honoring human rights to establish peace and security in the international community. They stressed that any progress depends on respecting human rights, which is a fundamental principle recommended by divine religions centuries ago.

END OF SUMMARY.
