SUMMARY:
The Tehran‑based Islamic Human Rights Commission (IHRC) used the International Day for Human Rights to warn that the world is slipping back into a “might makes right” era, where force overrides respect for human dignity. It highlighted the many international treaties and declarations that have been adopted over the past fifty years to outlaw discrimination, torture, arbitrary detention and to guarantee political, association, women’s and religious freedoms—principles rooted in the teachings of the world’s divine religions. Yet the IHRC lamented that these commitments remain largely unimplemented, citing the devastation of world wars, the 2001 Durban anti‑racism conference and ongoing atrocities against Palestinians, journalists, religious minorities, children and women. The statement condemned countries that resort to military power and violate civilian rights, urging leaders to heed religious imperatives for human‑rights respect and to recognize that peace cannot exist without it. In parallel, UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Mary Robinson called on Israel to cease bombings in the West Bank and Gaza, to end targeted killings of Palestinians and to allow international monitors. The IHRC’s message is a moral appeal for universal adherence to human‑rights standards and an end to violence that undermines them.  
END OF SUMMARY.
