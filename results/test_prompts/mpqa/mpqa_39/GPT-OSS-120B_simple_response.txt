SUMMARY:  
The Tehran‑based Islamic Human Rights Commission (IHRC) warned that the world is slipping back into an era where force, not law, dominates international relations. Marking December 10 as “International Day for Human Rights,” the IHRC highlighted the myriad treaties and declarations—rooted in both secular law and “divine religions” centuries ago—that pledge to end discrimination, torture, arbitrary detention, and to protect political, association, women’s, and children’s rights. It lamented that these commitments remain largely unfulfilled despite the catastrophic losses of the world wars, and praised the 2001 Durban anti‑racism conference as a pivotal moment for human‑rights advocacy. The IHRC condemned ongoing injustices, especially against Palestinians, political activists, journalists, religious minorities, children and women, and called out nations that resort to military force and civilian rights violations. It urged leaders to heed religious teachings that demand respect for human dignity, insisting that lasting peace is impossible without genuine respect for human rights. In parallel, UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Mary Robinson appealed to Israel to cease bombings in the West Bank and Gaza, allow international monitors, and stop targeted killings of Palestinians.  
END OF SUMMARY.
