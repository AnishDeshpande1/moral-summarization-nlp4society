SUMMARY: The Tehran-based Islamic Human Rights Commission (IHRC) issued a statement on December 10, International Human Rights Day, expressing concern that force and weapons are again dominating international relations. It highlighted that, despite hundreds of human‑rights documents and declarations over the past 50 years, many provisions remain unimplemented, leading to ongoing discrimination, torture, arbitrary detention, and violations of political, association, women’s, and minority rights. The IHRC cited the 2001 Durban conference as a pivotal moment for human‑rights advocacy, condemned injustices against Palestinians and other vulnerable groups, and warned that some countries continue to use military force that tramples civilian rights. It called on global leaders to follow religious teachings that promote respect for human rights, emphasizing that peace cannot be achieved without honoring them. In a related development, UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Mary Robinson urged Israel to cease bombings in the West Bank and Gaza, allow international monitors, and stop targeted killings of Palestinians. END OF SUMMARY.
