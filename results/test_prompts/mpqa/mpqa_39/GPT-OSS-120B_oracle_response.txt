SUMMARY:  
The Tehran‑based Islamic Human Rights Commission warned that the world is slipping back into an era where force and weapon dominate international relations. In a statement marking the International Day for Human Rights, the IHRC recalled how, after decades of bitter experience and massive losses, the global community formulated and signed hundreds of documents to honor and promote human rights, calling for the prohibition of discrimination, torture, arbitrary detention and other violations. It lamented that many provisions have not been materialized and highlighted the injustice suffered by Palestinians, political activists, journalists, religious minorities, children and women. The commission praised the 2001 Durban conference as a turning point and urged all nations, guided by religious teachings, to stop resorting to military power, trampling civilian rights, and bombing civilian areas. It called for an international effort to dry up the roots of terrorism, expand peace and security, and halt targeted killings. The statement echoed UN High Commissioner Mary Robinson’s appeal for Israel to cease attacks and allow monitors, stressing that true peace cannot be achieved without honoring human rights.  
END OF SUMMARY.
