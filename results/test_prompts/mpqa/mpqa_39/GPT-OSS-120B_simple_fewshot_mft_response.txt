SUMMARY:
The Tehran‑based Islamic Human Rights Commission (IHRC) warned that the world is reverting to a “might makes right” era, condemning the use of force and military power that subverts international law and authority. Emphasizing the moral duty to protect the vulnerable, the IHRC called for an end to discrimination, torture, arbitrary detention and attacks on women, children and religious minorities, stressing that failure to honor these rights causes real harm. It highlighted the unfairness of ongoing injustices—particularly the oppression of Palestinians, political activists and journalists—arguing that such cheating of basic human rights betrays the principles of equality enshrined in global declarations and religious teachings. The IHRC praised past collective efforts, such as the Durban anti‑racism conference, as examples of rightful authority guiding humanity toward peace. It urged leaders worldwide, including Israel after recent bombings in Gaza and the West Bank, to respect human dignity, uphold fair treatment, and refrain from subversive, violent policies that perpetuate suffering.  

END OF SUMMARY.
