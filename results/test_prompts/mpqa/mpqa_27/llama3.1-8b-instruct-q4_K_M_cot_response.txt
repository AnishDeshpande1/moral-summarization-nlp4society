**SUMMARY:**

Foreign multinational companies are furious about President Eduardo Duhalde's economic package, which aims to devalue the peso and scrap a decade-old currency parity with the US dollar. The move could result in billions of dollars in losses for foreign banks, telecommunications, transport, and energy companies. Analysts estimate that these companies stand to lose more than $3 billion, leading them to engage in intense lobbying efforts to reverse the package. Duhalde's proposal would help alleviate the pain on an increasingly angry middle class, who are struggling with debt, by converting mortgages and car loans into pesos. However, foreign banks want to maintain the dollar value of debt, despite Duhalde's resistance. The outcome of this crisis could have a ripple effect, as other nations may try to break promises made in contracts if Argentina is successful in devaluing its currency.
