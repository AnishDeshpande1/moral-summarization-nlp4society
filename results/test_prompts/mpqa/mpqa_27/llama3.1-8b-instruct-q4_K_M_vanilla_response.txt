SUMMARY:

Foreign multinational companies are frantically lobbying Argentine lawmakers and the government to reverse President Eduardo Duhalde's economic package, which aims to devalue the peso and end currency parity with the US dollar. If passed, the package would lead to billions of dollars in losses for foreign banks, telecommunications, transport, and energy companies. The most intense lobbying came from Spanish firms, which stand to lose over $3 billion.

The package seeks to ease the pain on Argentina's middle class by converting debt into pesos and devaluing the peso by about 30%. However, foreign banks want to maintain the dollar value of debt, while Duhalde plans to take gas exports to shore up banks. The crisis has sparked concerns that other nations may follow suit in breaking promises made in contracts.

Analysts warn that the ripple effect could impact suppliers throughout Latin America. U.S. companies have invested over $16 billion in Argentina through 1999, with a significant portion in petroleum and gas, telecommunications, and energy. The emergency package is expected to pass easily through the Senate today.

END OF SUMMARY.
