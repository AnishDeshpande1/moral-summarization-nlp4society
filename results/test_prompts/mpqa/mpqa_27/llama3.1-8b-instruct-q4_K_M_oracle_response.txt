SUMMARY:

Argentina is on the brink of a financial crisis, unable to pay its creditors $132 billion or even its pensioners and state workers. To prevent a run on the banking system, the government has imposed a cash withdrawal limit of $250 per week. Foreign companies, particularly from Spain, are frantically lobbying against President Eduardo Duhalde's economic package, which would devalue the peso by about 30%. The measure aims to ease the pain for an increasingly angry middle class, but foreign firms fear massive losses if it passes. Analysts estimate these companies could lose over $3 billion. The government plans to help banks make up losses by diverting gas exports, and Duhalde has resisted pressure from foreign companies to maintain the current exchange rate. If the package is passed, there are concerns about a return to street violence as companies seek to raise prices for food and medicine. The crisis in Argentina could have far-reaching consequences, with other countries potentially following suit if it sets a precedent.

END OF SUMMARY.
