SUMMARY:  
Foreign multinational firms are intensively lobbying Argentine legislators to block President Eduardo Duhalde’s emergency economic package, which would devalue the peso and end the long‑standing 1:1 convertibility with the U.S. dollar. U.S., Spanish and other foreign banks, telecoms, transport and energy companies fear combined losses exceeding $3 billion if the plan passes. The proposal also seeks to convert consumer debt under $100,000 from dollars to pesos, easing the burden on a middle class that holds over 80 % of personal debt in dollars. Duhalde, facing a fiscal crisis that leaves the government unable to pay pensions or salaries, intends to redirect gas‑export revenues to support banks and grant himself sweeping economic powers. Companies worry the break from dollar‑linked contracts could set a precedent for other nations, threatening broader investment and supply‑chain stability across Latin America. Despite fierce opposition, the lower house approved the legislation and it is expected to clear the Senate.  

END OF SUMMARY.
