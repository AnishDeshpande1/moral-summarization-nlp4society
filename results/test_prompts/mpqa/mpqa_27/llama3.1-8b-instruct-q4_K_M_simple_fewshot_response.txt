SUMMARY:
Foreign multinational companies are frantically lobbying Argentine lawmakers to reverse President Eduardo Duhalde's economic package, which would devalue the peso and end currency parity with the US dollar. The measures aim to ease the pain on Argentina's middle class, who hold most of their debt in dollars. However, foreign banks and companies stand to lose billions if the package is approved, as they will no longer be able to keep profits tied to the dollar. Duhalde has resisted intense lobbying efforts from these companies, which have invested heavily in Argentina. Analysts warn that a successful devaluation could set a precedent for other countries to break contract promises made in less dire times. The emergency package also proposes converting mortgages and consumer debt into pesos, effectively reducing their value. The fate of the package hangs in the balance as lawmakers debate its merits.
