SUMMARY:

Foreign companies, particularly from Spain, are frantically lobbying the Argentine government to reverse its economic package, which aims to devalue the peso and scrap currency parity with the US dollar. This move would result in billions of dollars in losses for these companies, who have invested heavily in Argentina's economy. The companies are concerned that this decision will set a precedent for other countries to renege on their contracts, causing a ripple effect throughout Latin America.

The Argentine government, led by President Eduardo Duhalde, has refused to back down despite intense lobbying efforts. Duhalde's proposal is aimed at easing the pain of the country's middle class, who hold most of their debt in dollars and stand to lose significantly if the peso is devalued. The government also plans to direct gas exports towards shoring up banks to help them cover losses.

The situation has raised concerns about a potential return to street violence when the devaluation measure passes, as companies may raise prices for essential goods to compensate for their losses.

END OF SUMMARY.
