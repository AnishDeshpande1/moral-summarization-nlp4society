SUMMARY:

In Zimbabwe's rigged election, South Africa and Nigeria took a stand against flawed governance by suspending the country from the Commonwealth. This courageous move will help uphold a promise made by leading industrialized nations to provide aid, trade, and investment if Africa shows better democracy and rule of law. The New Partnership for Africa's Development, initiated by Thabo Mbeki and Olusegun Obasanjo, aims to promote governance and democracy on the continent.

However, President Mugabe has responded with desperation, accusing opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai of trying to kill him. His actions are a sign of repression and intimidation, which must be condemned. The international community's isolation is cornering Mugabe, who must now agree to new, fair elections in order for the opposition to join a unity government.

South Africa's stand on Zimbabwe's future brings hope for African economic renewal and greater democracy. It remains to be seen if President Mbeki can work with Mugabe to prevent violence and find a political compromise that will keep the economy from collapsing.

END OF SUMMARY.
