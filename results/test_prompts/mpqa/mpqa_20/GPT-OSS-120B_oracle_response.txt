SUMMARY:  
South Africa and Nigeria took a courageous stand after Zimbabwe’s rigged, flawed presidential election, suspending the country from the Commonwealth to signal that aid, trade and investment will flow only with better governance and genuine democracy. Their decisive action reinforces the New Partnership for Africa’s Development and offers a model for the UN development conference in Mexico: without the rule of law, fair elections and an end to intimidation and manipulation, no aid can succeed. President Thabo Mbeki, moving beyond quiet diplomacy, urged Mugabe to halt repression, stop the violence, and compensate white farmers fairly, warning that continued collapse would damage the continent’s economic renewal. The opposition, demanding a new, better‑run election, will join a unity government only on those terms. Mugabe’s desperation, including the charge that opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai tried to kill him, underscores the urgency for African leaders to press for democratic reform. This joint African stand brings hope that the continent can avoid further collapse and achieve lasting peace and prosperity.  

END OF SUMMARY.
