SUMMARY:
Africa's two richest nations, South Africa and Nigeria, have taken a stand against Zimbabwe's flawed presidential election by suspending the country from the Commonwealth. This move is seen as a show of solidarity with the West and upholds the promise of aid, trade, and investment in exchange for better governance and democracy. The New Partnership for Africa's Development, initiated by South African President Thabo Mbeki and Nigerian President Olusegun Obasanjo, aims to reduce poverty on the continent.

The move is seen as a crucial step towards holding Zimbabwean leader Robert Mugabe accountable for his repressive tactics and land seizures. The international community has criticized Mugabe's actions, including voter intimidation and manipulation of polls. The opposition in Zimbabwe has called for a new election, while South Africa's trade unions have protested the government's tactics.

The suspension is seen as a moral stand against tyranny and corruption, and holds hope for African economic renewal and greater democracy.
