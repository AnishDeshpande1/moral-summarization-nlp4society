SUMMARY:

South Africa and Nigeria have taken a stand against Zimbabwe's flawed presidential election, suspending the country from the Commonwealth. This move is seen as a crucial step in upholding the "New Partnership for Africa's Development" (NEPAD), a pact aimed at promoting better governance and democracy on the continent. The two countries' leaders, Thabo Mbeki and Olusegun Obasanjo, have shown solidarity with the West by condemning Zimbabwean President Robert Mugabe's actions.

The suspension is seen as a blow to Mugabe, who has been accused of voter intimidation, manipulation of polls, and seizing farmland from whites without just compensation. The opposition in Zimbabwe has called for a new election, which would be a fair deal according to many observers. The international community has also condemned Mugabe's actions, with South Africa's leader Mbeki making his voice heard.

The move is seen as a hopeful sign for African economic renewal and greater democracy, and it may help to renew aid, trade, and investment in the continent.

END OF SUMMARY.
