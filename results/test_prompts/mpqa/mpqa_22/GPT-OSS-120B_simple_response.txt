SUMMARY:  
The article celebrates human ingenuity and collaborative perseverance in the harsh environment of space. After a six‑hour spacewalk, astronauts Steve Smith and Rex Walheim released the Mobile Transporter—a tiny rail car that will glide the Canadarm2 robot along a 120‑metre girder under construction on the International Space Station. Though its top speed is only a few centimetres per second, the trolley represents a vital step toward a “space railway” that will carry power‑generating trusses and eventually a hand‑car for crew movement. The 18‑metre Canadarm2, Canada’s flagship contribution, has performed “flawlessly” despite a recent joint anomaly that engineers promptly bypassed, illustrating the importance of diligent problem‑solving and resilience. Captain Marc Garneau’s remarks frame these technical feats as a testament to teamwork, ingenuity, and the willingness to confront and overcome obstacles in an extremely hostile setting. The successful deployment of the rail car and the arm’s continued reliability underscore a moral lesson: progress in exploration depends on careful preparation, international cooperation, and the steadfast resolve to fix problems when they arise.  

END OF SUMMARY.
