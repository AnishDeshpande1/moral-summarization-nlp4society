SUMMARY:
Astronauts Steve Smith and Rex Walheim completed a six‑hour spacewalk to release the restraints on the Mobile Transporter, the first rail car on the International Space Station. The tiny trolley, capable of moving at only a few centimetres per second, will shuttle Canadarm2 along the 13‑metre S‑0 truss, the first segment of a planned 120‑metre girder that will support vast solar‑panel arrays. Canadarm2, Canada’s flagship robotic arm, has performed “flawlessly” in lifting and installing the truss and in moving the spacewalkers to new work sites, even after engineers bypassed a joint anomaly. Captain Marc Garneau praised the arm as a “unique and highly complex robot working in an extremely hostile environment,” noting that problems are expected but were handled smoothly. Future missions will add eight more truss segments and a hand‑car, extending the rail system to over a football‑field length and further enhancing the station’s construction capability.  
END OF SUMMARY.
