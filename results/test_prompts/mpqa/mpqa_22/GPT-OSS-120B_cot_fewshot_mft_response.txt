Astronauts Steve Smith and Rex Walheim prepared the first outer‑space railway for its inaugural test, releasing launch restraints on the Mobile Transporter that will shuttle the Canadarm2 robotic arm along the S‑0 truss. The rail car, moving at just a few centimetres per second, is part of a 120‑metre girder that will eventually support thousands of square metres of solar panels. Canadarm2, described by Canadian Space Agency President Capt. Marc Garneau as a **unique** and **highly complex** robot operating in an **extremely hostile** environment, has performed **flawlessly** despite an earlier **anomaly** that created a **problem** in one joint—an issue engineers successfully **bound** and bypassed.
