SUMMARY:
Astronauts Steve Smith and Rex Walheim spent more than six hours on a spacewalk to release restraints on the Mobile Transporter, the first “rail car” ever installed on the International Space Station. Though it will crawl at only a few centimetres per second, the tiny trolley will soon ferry the Canadarm2 robotic arm along a 120‑metre truss that will support vast solar arrays and future construction. The successful deployment showcases international cooperation—NASA, the Canadian Space Agency and Brampton‑based MD Robotics—alongside human ingenuity in an extremely hostile environment. Even after a joint anomaly was detected weeks earlier, Canadian engineers swiftly bypassed the problem, underscoring a commitment to safety and reliability. Captain Marc Garneau praised Canadarm2’s flawless performance and highlighted that setbacks are a normal part of space work, yet the mission proceeded smoothly. The new rail system, soon to include a hand‑car for crew mobility, marks a modest but morally significant step toward expanding humanity’s ability to build and maintain a shared, sustainable outpost in space.

END OF SUMMARY.
