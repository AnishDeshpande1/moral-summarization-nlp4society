SUMMARY: A railway track has been installed on the International Space Station as part of the 13‑metre S‑0 truss, the first segment of a planned 120‑metre girder that will support extensive solar panels. Astronauts Steve Smith and Rex Walheim conducted a six‑hour EVA to release restraints on the Mobile Transporter, a small rail car that will move at about three centimetres per second. The transporter will carry the Canadian-built Canadarm2 robotic arm between construction sites during ongoing station assembly. Canadarm2, an 18‑metre arm capable of lifting entire modules, has performed flawlessly despite a recent joint anomaly that engineers bypassed, and has been rewired to draw power from the new truss. Future missions will add eight more truss segments and a hand‑car for astronaut mobility along the rail, which will eventually span the length of a football field. END OF SUMMARY.
