SUMMARY:
NASA’s first “space railway” was readied for its debut as astronauts Steve Smith and Rex Walheim completed a six‑hour spacewalk to release the Mobile Transporter—a tiny rail car that will ferry the Canadarm2 robotic arm along the International Space Station’s growing truss. Though its top speed is only a few centimetres per second, the rail system marks a crucial step toward a 120‑metre girder that will host massive solar arrays and a hand‑car for crew mobility. The successful deployment highlights the ingenuity and perseverance of international partners: Canada’s space agency and Brampton‑based MD Robotics overcame a joint‑arm anomaly, rewired the 18‑metre arm for power, and demonstrated flawless operation in the harsh environment of orbit. This achievement underscores the collaborative spirit and technical excellence that make humanity’s expansion into space possible, turning a complex, hostile setting into a laboratory of innovation and cooperation.

END OF SUMMARY.
