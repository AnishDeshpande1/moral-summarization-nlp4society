SUMMARY:
The Pakistani government rejected the US State Department's human rights report, terming it "factually inaccurate" and an unwarranted judgment. The report was deemed to have "failed" in acknowledging Pakistan's efforts to promote and protect human rights. The government highlighted its achievements, including the promulgation of laws such as the Juvenile Justice System Ordinance and the establishment of a permanent commission on women's status. These initiatives were aimed at eliminating child labour and ensuring equal remuneration for workers. The government also introduced joint electorates to respect the wishes of minority communities. Despite these efforts, the report claimed Pakistan's human rights record remained poor, calling it an "unacceptable misstatement". The Pakistani government urged other countries to address their own issues in promoting and protecting human rights, rather than making unfounded judgments about others.

END OF SUMMARY.
