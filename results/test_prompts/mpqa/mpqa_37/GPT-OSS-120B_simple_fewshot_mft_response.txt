SUMMARY:
The Pakistani government rejected the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights report as “factually inaccurate and unwarranted,” arguing that the assessment undermines the authority of a sovereign state that has taken concrete steps to protect its people. In its response, the Foreign Office highlighted a series of policy measures—such as the Juvenile Justice System Ordinance, a permanent women’s commission, police and jail reforms, a national plan to eliminate child labour, and amendments allowing women to pass citizenship to children of foreign husbands—as evidence of its care for vulnerable citizens. It also cited Pakistan’s ratification of ILO conventions on equal pay and child‑labour elimination, the signing of child‑rights protocols, and the introduction of joint electorates for minorities, portraying these actions as fair and just initiatives. The statement condemned the U.S. report as a biased subversion that unfairly judges Pakistan while ignoring its achievements, urging all states to cooperate rather than impose judgments. The government framed the dispute as a defense of legitimate authority, a commitment to human welfare, and a call for equitable treatment in the international arena.  
END OF SUMMARY.
