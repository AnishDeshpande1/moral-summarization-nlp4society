SUMMARY:
Pakistan’s foreign office rejected the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights report as “factually inaccurate and unwarranted,” accusing Washington of imposing its judgments on other nations. The statement highlighted a series of Pakistani reforms and initiatives that the government says the report ignored, including the Juvenile Justice System Ordinance, a permanent women’s commission, police and jail reforms, a national plan to eliminate child labour, a fund for rehabilitating working children, amendments to the Citizenship Act allowing women to pass nationality to children of foreign husbands, ratification of ILO conventions on equal pay and child‑labour eradication, signing of child‑rights optional protocols, and the introduction of joint electorates for minorities. While the U.S. report still characterizes Pakistan’s human‑rights record as poor, Islamabad argues that such assessments should be replaced by cooperative, respectful dialogue among states to genuinely advance human‑rights protections. The government maintains that its progress demonstrates a commitment to human dignity that the foreign report fails to acknowledge.  
END OF SUMMARY.
