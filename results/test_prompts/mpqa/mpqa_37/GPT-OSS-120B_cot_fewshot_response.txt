**SUMMARY:**
Pakistan’s Foreign Office rejected the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights report as “inaccurate” and “unwarranted,” condemning it as an “unacceptable” and “gross misstatement” that reflects a tendency of some states to pass judgment on others. The ministry highlighted the government’s “important” policy measures that have “achieved” the goals set at the 2000 human‑rights conference, noting reforms such as the Juvenile Justice System Ordinance, a permanent women’s commission, police and jail reforms, a national plan to eliminate child labour, a fund for rehabilitation of working children, and amendments to the Citizenship Act enabling women to pass nationality to their children. Pakistan also “ratified” ILO conventions on equal remuneration and the elimination of the worst forms of child labour, signed optional protocols on children’s rights, and introduced joint electorates respecting minority wishes. The statement called for “cooperation” and “understanding” among states in promoting and protecting human rights. **END OF SUMMARY.**
