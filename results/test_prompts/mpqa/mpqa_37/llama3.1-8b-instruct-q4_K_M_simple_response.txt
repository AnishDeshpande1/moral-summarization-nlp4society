SUMMARY:

The Pakistan government has rejected the US State Department's Human Rights Report, calling it "factually inaccurate and unwarranted". The report was criticized for failing to acknowledge Pakistan's efforts to promote and protect human rights. According to the government, Pakistan has made significant progress in areas such as juvenile justice, women's empowerment, police reforms, and child labor elimination. The country has also ratified several international conventions and protocols related to human rights.

The government feels that the report is biased and reflects a tendency of some states to judge others. It suggests that all countries should work together in promoting and protecting human rights, rather than sitting in judgment over each other. Pakistan's efforts have been recognized by the fact that all goals set at a 2000 human rights conference were achieved, but this has been ignored by the report.

The government feels that the report's assessment of its human rights record is unacceptable and a gross misstatement of facts. It believes that cooperation and understanding among countries would be more effective in promoting human rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
