SUMMARY:

The Pakistani government rejected the US State Department's human rights report, terming it "factually inaccurate and unwarranted". The report failed to acknowledge the country's efforts in promoting and protecting human rights. Pakistan has implemented various initiatives, including the establishment of a permanent commission on women's status, police reforms, and a plan for eliminating child labor. The government also ratified international conventions and signed optional protocols to ensure equal remuneration and protection of children's rights. Furthermore, measures were taken to promote transparency in elections, introducing joint electorates to respect minority communities' wishes. Despite these developments, the report claimed Pakistan's human rights record remained "poor", which the government deemed a "gross misstatement". The government feels constrained to reject the assessment and criticizes the tendency of some states to sit in judgment over others, urging cooperation and understanding instead.

END OF SUMMARY.
