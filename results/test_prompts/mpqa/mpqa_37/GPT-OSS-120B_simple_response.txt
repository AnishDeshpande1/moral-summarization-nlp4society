SUMMARY:  
The Pakistani government denounced the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights report as “factually inaccurate and unwarranted,” accusing it of moral hypocrisy and of other states judging Pakistan without understanding its efforts. In its rebuttal, the Foreign Office highlighted a series of moral initiatives the government claims demonstrate its commitment to human dignity: the Juvenile Justice System Ordinance, a permanent women’s status commission, police and jail reforms, a national plan to eliminate child labour, a rehabilitation fund for working children, amendment of the 1951 Citizenship Act to let women pass Pakistani nationality to children of foreign husbands, a mass awareness campaign marking the year 2000 as the Year of Human Rights and Human Dignity, ratification of ILO conventions on equal pay and the worst forms of child labour, signing of the Optional Protocols to the Convention on the Rights of the Child, and the introduction of joint electorates for minority communities. The statement argued that these achievements render the report’s claim of a “poor” human‑rights record a gross misstatement, urging all states to cooperate rather than judge each other.  
END OF SUMMARY.
