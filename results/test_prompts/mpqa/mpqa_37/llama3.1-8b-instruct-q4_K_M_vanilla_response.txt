SUMMARY:
The Pakistani government has rejected the US State Department's Report on Human Rights, calling it "factually inaccurate and unwarranted". The report was criticized for not acknowledging Pakistan's efforts to promote human rights, including initiatives such as promulgating the Juvenile Justice System Ordinance, establishing a permanent commission on women's status, and ratifying ILO Conventions. The government also pointed out that all goals set at a 2000 human rights conference had been achieved, but were ignored by the report. Pakistan has taken steps to address human rights issues, such as introducing joint electorates to respect minority communities' wishes and amending the Citizenship Act to allow women of Pakistani descent to claim nationality for their children. The government feels that the report reflects a tendency of some states to judge others, rather than working together to promote human rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
