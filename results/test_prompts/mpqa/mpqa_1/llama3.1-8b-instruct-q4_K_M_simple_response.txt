SUMMARY: 
Brazil's President Fernando Henrique Cardoso expressed optimism that the US will not hinder international efforts to control greenhouse gas emissions, despite their formal renunciation of the Kyoto Protocol. Cardoso believes the US is committed to joining other nations in fighting climate change and will implement domestic measures to reduce emissions. He also sent a message to Congress calling for Brazil's ratification of the Kyoto Protocol and committing the country to reducing its own greenhouse gas emissions by 5.2% by 2012.

It is worth noting that Cardoso's statements highlight the importance of international cooperation in addressing global environmental issues. His optimism about US participation underscores the potential benefits of working together towards a common goal, even when there are differing perspectives on how to achieve it. The commitment shown by Brazil and other countries serves as an example for nations with significant emissions to take responsibility for their impact on the environment.

END OF SUMMARY.
