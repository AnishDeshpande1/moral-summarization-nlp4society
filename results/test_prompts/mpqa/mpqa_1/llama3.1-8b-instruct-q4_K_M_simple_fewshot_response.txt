SUMMARY:
Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso expressed confidence that the United States will not interfere in international negotiations on controlling greenhouse gas emissions, despite its formal renunciation of the Kyoto Protocol. Cardoso believes that the US is committed to joining other nations in fighting climate change and will adopt domestic measures to reduce emissions. In a message sent to congress, Cardoso called for ratification of the Kyoto Protocol by Brazil and pledged to reduce greenhouse emissions by 5.2% by 2012. The move comes as the world's largest emitters of greenhouse gases are named, with the US leading the list at 36%. Cardoso's optimism is based on his confidence in the international community's commitment to addressing climate change, despite the challenges posed by the US withdrawal from the Kyoto Protocol.
