SUMMARY:
Former Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso expressed confidence that the United States will not sabotage upcoming international talks on curbing greenhouse‑gas emissions. He argued the U.S. remains committed to collaborating with other nations on climate action and will implement domestic measures despite having formally withdrawn from the Kyoto Protocol. In the same statement, Cardoso urged Brazil’s Congress to ratify the Kyoto Protocol and pledged a 5.2 % reduction in Brazil’s emissions by 2012. The report also listed the world’s largest emitters, with the United States responsible for 36 % of global greenhouse gases, followed by Russia, Japan, Germany, and others. Cardoso’s remarks frame U.S. participation as a moral responsibility to the global community and call for Brazil to lead by ratifying the treaty and cutting its own emissions. END OF SUMMARY.
