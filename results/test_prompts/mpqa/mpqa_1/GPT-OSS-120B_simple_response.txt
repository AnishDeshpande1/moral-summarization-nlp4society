SUMMARY: In a hopeful moral appeal, Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso expressed confidence that the United States will act responsibly and refrain from sabotaging global talks on greenhouse‑gas reductions. He stressed that the United States, despite formally withdrawing from the Kyoto Protocol, remains ethically obliged to join the international community in combating climate change and to implement its own domestic measures. Cardoso used this moment to urge Brazil’s Congress to ratify the Kyoto Protocol and pledged Brazil to cut its emissions by 5.2 % by 2012, framing the commitment as a matter of national integrity and global solidarity. He reminded readers that the world’s largest polluters—led by the United States (36 % of emissions) and followed by Russia, Japan, Germany, and others—must shoulder their moral responsibility to protect the planet for future generations. END OF SUMMARY.
