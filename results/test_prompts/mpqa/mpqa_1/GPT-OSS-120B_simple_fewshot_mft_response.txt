SUMMARY:
Former Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso asserted his authority on the global climate agenda, stating he does not expect the United States to undermine the upcoming greenhouse‑gas negotiations. He framed the United States as a potential ally rather than a subversive power, emphasizing its commitment to domestic climate action despite having withdrawn from the Kyoto Protocol. Cardoso used his presidential platform to issue a formal appeal to Brazil’s Congress, urging the ratification of the Kyoto Protocol and pledging a 5.2 % reduction in Brazil’s emissions by 2012. By highlighting the United States’ share of global emissions (36 %) alongside other major polluters, he positioned the U.S. as a key authority whose cooperation is essential, warning that any interference would be a subversive threat to collective climate efforts. The message calls for unified, authoritative action rather than disruptive dissent.

END OF SUMMARY.
