SUMMARY:
Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso expressed confidence that the US will not interfere in international negotiations on controlling greenhouse gas emissions. He believes the US is committed to addressing the issue and will adopt domestic measures, despite formally renouncing the Kyoto Protocol. However, the article notes that the US is one of the world's biggest sources of greenhouse gases, accounting for 36% of emissions.

Cardoso sent a message to Congress calling for ratification of the Kyoto Protocol in Brazil and committing the country to reducing its own greenhouse gas emissions by 5.2% by 2012. The protocol aims to limit global warming by controlling emissions from developed countries. Other major contributors to greenhouse gas emissions include Russia, Japan, Germany, and Great Britain.

Cardoso's comments come as international negotiations on climate change continue. His optimism about the US involvement is in contrast to some concerns that the country's formal rejection of the Kyoto Protocol could hinder progress in addressing global warming. END OF SUMMARY.
