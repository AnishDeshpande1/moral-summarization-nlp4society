SUMMARY:

British troops will not be sent to fight the Taliban, according to Robin Cook, Commons Leader. SAS soldiers and their American counterparts are fighting alongside the Northern Alliance in Afghanistan, but British troops remain on standby. The situation is "better than anybody could have hoped for," said Mr. Cook, who welcomed the fact that British troops' presence has not been needed.

The Taliban's control crumbled in Kunduz after mass surrender, leaving Kandahar as their last major stronghold. Talks are underway to surrender Kandahar, following calls from 70 Afghan tribal leaders to avert bloodshed. However, the situation remains fluid, and aid agencies have expressed concerns about security and instability across the country.

The Prime Minister's official spokesman said Tony Blair was pleased with progress made on military, diplomatic, and humanitarian fronts. The campaign shows that Alliance military success is key to getting food into the country. But Britain will need evidence before becoming involved in military action in other countries.

END OF SUMMARY.
