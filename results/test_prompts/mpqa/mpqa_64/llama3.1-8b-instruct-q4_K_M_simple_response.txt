SUMMARY:
British troops will not be sent to fight the Taliban, according to Commons Leader Robin Cook. This decision comes as the Northern Alliance gains ground in Afghanistan, with SAS soldiers and American counterparts fighting alongside them. Despite the rapid collapse of the Taliban regime, Cook emphasized that British troops would only be deployed if absolutely necessary. The situation is "better than anybody could have hoped for," he said, adding that it's an "advance" rather than a reverse.

The fall of Kunduz has left Kandahar as the Taliban's last major stronghold, but negotiations are underway for their surrender. Hundreds of foreign fighters were killed in a prison riot outside Mazar-e-Sharif, and aid agencies are still unable to operate due to insecurity. The Prime Minister's spokesman expressed optimism about progress on military, diplomatic, and humanitarian fronts, but emphasized that the campaign is far from over.

END OF SUMMARY.
