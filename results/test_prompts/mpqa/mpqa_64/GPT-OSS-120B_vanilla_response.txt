SUMMARY: British Foreign Secretary Robin Cook announced that regular British troops will not be deployed to fight the Taliban, noting that no ground troops have been used in the Afghan civil war and none are expected. While SAS and U.S. forces continue to operate alongside the Northern Alliance, Britain remains on a 48‑hour standby. The Taliban’s stronghold in Kandahar is under negotiation after the Northern Alliance captured Kunduz, the last major northern Taliban base, following mass surrenders and a deadly prison riot that killed many foreign fighters. Tribal leaders are urging a peaceful surrender of Kandahar. UN refugee chief Ruud Lubbers warned that Afghanistan is still unsafe for aid work and lacks sufficient food, a view echoed by Oxfam. Prime Minister Tony Blair’s spokesman praised progress on military, diplomatic and humanitarian fronts but stressed that any future British military action would require clear evidence. The campaign against terrorism continues, but Britain is not committing regular troops at this stage. END OF SUMMARY.
