SUMMARY:
British political leadership, represented by Commons Leader Robin Cook, reaffirmed the authority’s decision to keep regular British troops out of Afghanistan’s civil war, emphasizing a responsible, non‑military approach. Cook noted that the UK has not deployed ground forces in the recent conflict and sees no reason to change that stance, portraying the restraint as an “advance” rather than a retreat. This decision reflects a concern for the welfare of Afghan civilians, highlighting the “better than hoped for” situation and the desire to avoid further harm as the Taliban negotiate surrender in Kandahar after losing Kunduz. While Special Air Service units and U.S. forces continue to support the Northern Alliance, the UK maintains a 48‑hour standby, monitoring the fluid security environment. UN and humanitarian agencies warned that the country remains unsafe for aid work, underscoring the need for continued care and protection of vulnerable populations. The British government’s cautious stance underscores respect for legitimate authority and a commitment to minimizing human suffering amid ongoing negotiations and military actions.

END OF SUMMARY.
