SUMMARY:
British troops will not be sent to fight the Taliban, according to Commons Leader Robin Cook. Despite the rapid collapse of the Taliban regime across Afghanistan, British forces have not been deployed on the ground in the civil war. However, SAS soldiers and their American counterparts are fighting alongside the Northern Alliance as they sweep across the country. The situation is "better than anybody could have hoped for," according to Mr. Cook, but he warns that the campaign is not over yet. The fall of Kunduz has left Kandahar as the Taliban's last major stronghold, and negotiations are underway with the Taliban to surrender themselves. However, aid agencies are still hampered by instability across the country, and Ruud Lubbers, the UN Commissioner for Refugees, says Afghanistan is "not at all a secure environment." The Prime Minister's spokesman stresses that Britain will need to see evidence before becoming involved in military action elsewhere.
