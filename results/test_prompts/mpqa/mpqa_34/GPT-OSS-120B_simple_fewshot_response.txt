SUMMARY:
China’s State Council released its annual human‑rights report on the United States, accusing Washington of hypocrisy for spotlighting abuses abroad while ignoring serious problems at home. The Xinhua‑cited document lists U.S. police misconduct, poverty, racial discrimination and personal‑safety concerns, and condemns American military actions that “wantonly infringe” on other nations’ sovereignty. It also rebukes the Bush administration’s withdrawal from the Kyoto Protocol on greenhouse‑gas reductions. The Chinese report is issued in response to a U.S. State Department global‑human‑rights review that routinely cites China for widespread violations. Beijing argues that the United States positions itself as the world’s “human‑rights judge” while distorting conditions elsewhere, including in China, and turning a blind eye to its own human‑rights challenges. This reciprocal criticism underscores a broader moral dispute over which nation has the authority to judge human‑rights practices worldwide.  
END OF SUMMARY.
