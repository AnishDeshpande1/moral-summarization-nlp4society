SUMMARY:
China’s State Council released its annual human‑rights report, accusing the United States of hypocritical double standards. The document lists domestic problems in the U.S.—police abuse, poverty, racial discrimination and threats to personal safety—framing them as serious harms that the American government neglects, thereby violating the moral principle of caring for its citizens. It also condemns Washington’s “wanton infringement” on other nations’ sovereignty through overseas military deployments, portraying these actions as an abuse of authority that subverts international norms. By highlighting the U.S. withdrawal from the Kyoto Protocol, the report underscores what China sees as unfair, self‑serving behavior that cheats other countries out of collective climate responsibility. The Chinese narrative positions the United States as a self‑appointed world judge that distorts other nations’ human‑rights records while ignoring its own violations, casting the U.S. as both a perpetrator of harm and an illegitimate authority. This framing reinforces China’s claim to moral high ground by emphasizing fairness, care for the vulnerable, and respect for rightful authority.  

END OF SUMMARY.
