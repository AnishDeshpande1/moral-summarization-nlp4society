SUMMARY: China’s Information Office released its annual human‑rights report on the United States, accusing Washington of ignoring domestic abuses while condemning other nations. The report cites U.S. police misconduct, poverty, racial discrimination and personal‑safety concerns, and criticizes American military actions abroad as “wantonly infringing” on other nations’ sovereignty. It also denounces the Bush administration’s withdrawal from the Kyoto Protocol. The document is issued in response to a U.S. State Department global‑human‑rights review that routinely condemns China. China argues the United States acts as a “world judge of human rights,” distorting conditions elsewhere, including in China, while overlooking its own problems. END OF SUMMARY.
