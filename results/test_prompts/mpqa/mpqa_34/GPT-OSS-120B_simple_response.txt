SUMMARY:  
China’s State Council Information Office released its annual “U.S. Human Rights Report,” condemning Washington for hypocritical double standards. The report accuses the United States of ignoring its own serious problems—police misconduct, poverty, racial discrimination and threats to personal safety—while loudly criticizing other nations, including China, for human‑rights abuses. It also denounces U.S. military actions that “wantonly infringe” on other countries’ sovereignty and reproaches the Bush administration for abandoning the Kyoto Protocol on greenhouse‑gas reductions. Framed as a moral rebuke, the Chinese document claims the United States has once again assumed the role of “world judge of human rights,” distorting global conditions and turning a blind eye to its own failings. The release mirrors China’s customary response to the U.S. State Department’s global human‑rights report, which routinely highlights Chinese violations.  

END OF SUMMARY.
