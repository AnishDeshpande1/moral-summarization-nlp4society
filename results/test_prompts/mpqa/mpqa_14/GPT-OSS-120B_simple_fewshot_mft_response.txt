SUMMARY:
A Russian Progress supply ship successfully reached the International Space Station on Wednesday, but NASA later reported uncertainty about whether its docking hooks fully latched. The potential loose connection raised concerns that the shuttle’s docking forces could damage the orbiting laboratory, prompting extra caution. To investigate, the station’s steering system—normally disabled during docking—remained offline for an additional three hours, and non‑essential equipment was shut down to conserve power. Russian flight controllers worked through the night, and after thorough analysis they concluded the ship was securely attached enough to reactivate the steering system. The incident underscores the importance of meticulous safety checks in space operations, as any failure could jeopardize the crew and the costly infrastructure of the space station. NASA indicated the issue would be monitored to determine whether it might affect the planned launch of shuttle Endeavour later that night.

END OF SUMMARY.
