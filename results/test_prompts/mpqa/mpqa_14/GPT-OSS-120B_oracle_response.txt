SUMMARY:  
A Russian Progress supply ship docked with the International Space Station on Wednesday, but officials fear it may have **missed** a **proper** link‑up, leaving the **hooks** possibly **unlatched**. NASA warned that this **concern** could **damage** the orbiting complex if a shuttle’s docking forces act on a **loose** connection. The unmanned vessel, launched from Kazakhstan with more than a ton of food, fuel and other supplies, arrived on time and the initial **linkup** **appeared** successful. However, Russian flight controllers did not receive the expected signal confirming that the **hooks had closed**, prompting an extra three‑hour shutdown of the station’s steering system while engineers **studied** the problem. Power‑intensive laboratory equipment was turned off to **conserve** energy. After Russian engineers determined the ship was **securely** attached enough for the steering system to resume, systems were re‑activated. Russia’s Mission Control worked through the night to **assess** the situation, ensuring the station’s safety before tonight’s planned launch of shuttle Endeavour.  

END OF SUMMARY.
