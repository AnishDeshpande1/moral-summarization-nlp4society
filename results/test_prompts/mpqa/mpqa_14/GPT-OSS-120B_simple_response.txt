SUMMARY: A Russian Progress supply ship reached the International Space Station on schedule, delivering over a ton of food, fuel and other essentials. However, NASA later reported that the docking hooks may not have fully latched, raising concerns that a loose connection could jeopardize the station’s integrity when the shuttle Endeavour arrives later that night. In response, mission controllers prudently kept the station’s steering system offline for an additional three hours, conserving power and allowing Russian engineers to verify the ship’s attachment. After careful assessment, they concluded the link was secure enough to reactivate the steering system. The episode underscores the critical importance of vigilance, international cooperation, and responsible decision‑making in space operations, reminding us that even routine missions demand rigorous safety checks to protect shared assets and human lives. END OF SUMMARY.
