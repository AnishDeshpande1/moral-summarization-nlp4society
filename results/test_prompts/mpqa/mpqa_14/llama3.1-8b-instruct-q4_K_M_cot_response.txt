**SUMMARY:** A Russian supply ship, Progress, docked to the International Space Station on Wednesday, but there are concerns about a potentially improper linkup. NASA officials are unsure whether the issue will delay the launch of space shuttle Endeavour tonight. The concern is that a loose fit might cause damage to the orbiting complex due to the force it would impart. Russian flight controllers outside Moscow did not receive the expected indication that the hooks had closed and latched, prompting them to study the problem. Everything on the station was turned back on after Russian engineers determined the supply ship was attached securely enough for the steering system to operate. Russia's Mission Control worked through the night to assess the situation.
