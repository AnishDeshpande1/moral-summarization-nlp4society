SUMMARY:
A Russian supply ship, Progress, docked with the International Space Station on Wednesday, but there are concerns that it may not have latched properly into place. NASA officials are worried that a loose fit could cause damage to the space station due to the force exerted by a docking shuttle. The Progress ship, carrying over a ton of supplies, had launched from Kazakhstan on Monday and docked with the station as planned. However, Russian flight controllers did not receive the expected confirmation that the hooks had closed and latched, leading to a delay in turning back on some laboratory equipment. After an extended period of assessment, Russian engineers determined that the supply ship was securely attached, but the incident highlights the importance of meticulous attention to detail in space exploration.

END OF SUMMARY.
