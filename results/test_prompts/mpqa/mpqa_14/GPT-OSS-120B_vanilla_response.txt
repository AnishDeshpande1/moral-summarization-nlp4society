SUMMARY: A Russian Progress cargo vessel successfully rendezvoused with the International Space Station on Wednesday, but NASA reported uncertainty about whether the docking mechanisms fully latched. The unmanned ship, launched from Kazakhstan on Monday with over a ton of food, fuel and supplies, appeared to dock on schedule, yet Russian flight controllers did not receive the expected latch confirmation. As a precaution, the station’s steering system, which had been shut down for the docking, remained off for an additional three hours while engineers examined the issue, and some lab equipment was powered down to conserve energy. After Russian engineers concluded the connection was sufficiently secure, the steering system and other systems were reactivated. NASA said it was unclear if the docking anomaly would affect the planned launch of the Space Shuttle Endeavour later that night. END OF SUMMARY.
