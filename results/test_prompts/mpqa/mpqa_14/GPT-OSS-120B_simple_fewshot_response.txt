SUMMARY:
A Russian Progress cargo vessel successfully reached the International Space Station on Wednesday, but NASA later reported that the docking hooks may not have fully latched. The uncertainty raised concerns that a loose connection could jeopardize the station if the shuttle Endeavour attempts its scheduled launch tonight, due to possible forces from a docking maneuver. After the ship’s arrival, Russian flight controllers did not receive the usual confirmation signal that the hooks were secured, prompting them to keep the station’s steering system offline for an additional three hours while they investigated. To conserve power, some laboratory equipment was temporarily shut down. Once Russian engineers concluded the cargo ship was sufficiently attached, the steering system and other systems were re‑energized. Mission control in Moscow continued working through the night to verify the docking integrity, while officials assessed any potential impact on Endeavour’s upcoming mission. The incident underscores the critical importance of precise docking procedures for the safety of the orbiting complex.

END OF SUMMARY.
