SUMMARY:
Vietnam’s Minister of Science, Technology and Environment, Chu Tuan Nha, stressed at the Asia‑Europe Meeting in Beijing that protecting people and the planet is a top priority for Vietnam. He highlighted the country’s commitment to preventing pollution, safeguarding biodiversity, and improving environmental conditions in both industrial zones and rural‑urban communities, portraying these actions as essential to caring for citizens’ health and future generations. Nha announced that Vietnam has mobilized all available resources and deepened international cooperation to address these threats, calling for a new biennial Asia‑Europe environmental forum to share policies, expertise, and information. The meeting concluded with a joint statement urging prompt implementation of the Kyoto Protocol and continued dialogue, reinforcing a collective responsibility to nurture and protect the environment for all peoples involved. The moral emphasis is clear: collaborative, compassionate action is needed to prevent harm and ensure a healthier world.

END OF SUMMARY.
