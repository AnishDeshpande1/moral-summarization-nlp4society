SUMMARY:  
Vietnam’s Minister of Science, Technology and Environment, Chu Tuan Nha, used the first Asia‑Europe Meeting (ASEM) Environment Ministers’ Meeting in Beijing to reaffirm Vietnam’s moral commitment to safeguarding the planet. He highlighted the country’s dedication to preventing pollution, protecting biodiversity, and improving environmental conditions in both industrial zones and rural‑urban communities. Emphasising collective responsibility, the minister noted that Vietnam has marshaled all national resources and deepened international partnerships to confront environmental challenges. He called for stronger bilateral ties between Asia and Europe, proposing a biennial cooperation forum under the Asia‑Europe Environment Cooperation Program to enable regular policy dialogue, information sharing, and expert exchange. The meeting concluded with a Presidium Statement urging swift implementation of the Kyoto Protocol and continued cooperation among ASEM members.  

END OF SUMMARY.
