SUMMARY:
Vietnam’s Minister of Science, Technology and Environment, Chu Tuan Nha, used the Asia‑Europe Meeting (ASEM) Environment Ministers’ gathering in Beijing to stress that protecting the planet is a shared moral duty. He highlighted Vietnam’s long‑standing commitment to curb pollution, safeguard biodiversity and improve environmental conditions in both cities and rural areas, noting that the government has marshaled all available resources and deepened international partnerships to meet these challenges. The minister called for a new, biennial Asia‑Europe environmental cooperation forum under the ASEM Environment Cooperation Programme, arguing that regular dialogue, policy exchange and expert collaboration are essential for responsible stewardship of the Earth. The meeting concluded with a Presidium Statement urging prompt implementation of the Kyoto Protocol and reinforcing the need for continued cooperation and dialogue among ASEM members. The moral of the story is that collective, cross‑continental cooperation rooted in shared responsibility is vital for safeguarding the environment for current and future generations.  
END OF SUMMARY.
