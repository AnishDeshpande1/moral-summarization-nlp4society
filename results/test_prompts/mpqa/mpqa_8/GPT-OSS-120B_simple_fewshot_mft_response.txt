SUMMARY:
Former Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso warned that the chief test for Venezuelan President Hugo Chávez is to restore legitimate authority and normalcy after last week’s attempted coup. Cardoso urged Chávez to reopen communication channels with the military and to re‑establish social harmony, stressing that true leadership must subdue subversive forces rather than seek vengeance. Chávez replied that he will not punish the officers who backed the failed overthrow, pledging instead to set up dialogue tables. Former U.S. President Jimmy Carter backed Chávez’s promise of a conciliatory approach and announced plans to travel to Venezuela soon to support the restoration of orderly governance. The article frames the situation as a struggle between rightful authority and subversive disruption, highlighting the moral imperative to reaffirm legitimate power and avoid retaliatory cycles.

END OF SUMMARY.
