SUMMARY:
Former Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso warned that Hugo Chávez’s biggest obstacle is restoring normalcy after last week’s turmoil. Cardoso urged the Venezuelan leader to open communication channels and pursue social harmony, noting Chávez’s pledge not to retaliate against the military officers who backed the failed coup attempt. Former U.S. President Jimmy Carter backed Chávez’s commitment to dialogue and announced plans to travel to Venezuela soon to support the reconciliation effort. The emphasis is on restraint, constructive dialogue, and the moral responsibility of leaders to heal divisions rather than seek vengeance.  
END OF SUMMARY.
