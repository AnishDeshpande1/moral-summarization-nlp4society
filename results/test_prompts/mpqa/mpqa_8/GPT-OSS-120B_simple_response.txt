SUMMARY: Former Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso warned that Hugo Chávez’s foremost moral task is to restore normalcy after last week’s turmoil, emphasizing the need to reopen dialogue and foster social harmony. Chávez, in turn, pledged not to seek vengeance against the military officers who backed the failed coup, framing his stance as one of forgiveness and reconciliation. Former U.S. President Jimmy Carter endorsed Chávez’s commitment to dialogue and will travel to Venezuela to support these peace‑building efforts. The author presents the situation as a moral crossroads, urging leaders to choose forgiveness, communication, and unity over retaliation. END OF SUMMARY.
