Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso believes that Venezuelan President Hugo Chavez's main challenge is to restore normalcy in his country following last week's conflicts. To achieve this, Cardoso suggests establishing open communication channels and promoting social harmony. Notably, Chavez has stated his intention to forgive the military personnel who supported the failed coup attempt against him, a move endorsed by former US President Jimmy Carter, who plans to visit Venezuela soon. This willingness to reconcile and engage in dialogue is seen as a positive step towards healing and rebuilding.

It's worth noting that Cardoso's emphasis on reestablishing normalcy highlights the importance of prioritizing peace and stability over revenge or retribution. Chavez's commitment to forgiveness and dialogue demonstrates his commitment to working towards a more harmonious and inclusive society, which can only be beneficial for Venezuela's future.
