SUMMARY: A shuttle launch from Cape Canaveral sent Atlantis and a crew of seven astronauts toward the International Space Station, marking the first of six planned missions over the next seven months to advance the station’s construction. Despite a computer glitch that required a last‑minute software reload and a four‑day delay caused by a liquid hydrogen vent leak, Atlantis lifted off at 4:44 p.m. on time, powered by three new main engines. The flight is notable for mission specialist Jerry L. Ross becoming the first astronaut to fly seven space missions. During the 11‑day trip, the crew will perform four spacewalks to install the 44‑foot, 27,000‑pound “S‑Zero” keystone support unit, which will hold a new solar array the size of an acre. END OF SUMMARY.
