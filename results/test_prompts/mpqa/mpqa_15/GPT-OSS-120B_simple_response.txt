SUMMARY:  
The triumphant launch of shuttle Atlantis underscores the virtues of perseverance, teamwork, and human curiosity. Despite a four‑day delay caused by a hydrogen leak and a last‑minute computer glitch that was resolved with only seconds to spare, engineers and astronauts rallied together, demonstrating resolve and ingenuity. As NASA launch director Mike Leinbach urged the crew, “you spent a few extra days in Florida… but it’s time to take a ride,” reminding us that patience and dedication pay off. The mission also celebrates the spirit of experience and mentorship: veteran astronaut Jerry L. Ross became the first person to fly to space seven times, embodying the value of seasoned wisdom guiding future exploration. Over the next seven months, six more launches will continue this collective effort to complete the International Space Station, a testament to humanity’s commitment to cooperation and progress. The crew’s upcoming four spacewalks will install the critical “keystone” support unit and a massive solar array, symbolizing how diligent effort builds the foundations for a brighter, shared future in space.  
END OF SUMMARY.
