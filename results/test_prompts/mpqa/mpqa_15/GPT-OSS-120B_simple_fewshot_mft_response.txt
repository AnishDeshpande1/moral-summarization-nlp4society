SUMMARY:
The space shuttle Atlantis launched from Cape Canaveral at 4:44 p.m., beginning a seven‑mission series aimed at completing the International Space Station. Despite a computer glitch that required a last‑minute software reload and a four‑day delay caused by a hydrogen‑vent leak, engineers and launch staff overcame the challenges, reflecting a commitment to responsibility and perseverance. Atlantis rose on three new main engines, carrying seven astronauts and a “truckload” of hardware. Commander Mike Bloomfield and launch director Mike Leinbach highlighted the importance of staying the course despite setbacks. The mission marks a historic milestone: mission specialist Jerry L. Ross, a grandfather, becomes the first astronaut to fly seven times. Over an 11‑day stay, the crew will conduct four spacewalks to install the 44‑foot, 27,000‑pound “S‑Zero” keystone unit, which will support a massive new solar array for the station. The launch underscores the broader moral commitment to advancing human exploration, teamwork, and the diligent fulfillment of a national scientific goal.

END OF SUMMARY.
