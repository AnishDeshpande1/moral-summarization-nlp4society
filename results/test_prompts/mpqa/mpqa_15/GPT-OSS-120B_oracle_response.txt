SUMMARY:
The shuttle Atlantis roared into space from Cape Canaveral, carrying seven astronauts and a truckload of hardware destined for the International Space Station. After a four‑day delay caused by a liquid‑hydrogen vent leak and a last‑minute computer glitch that required engineers to reload software with only 11 seconds left, the launch proceeded at 4:44 p.m. aboard three new main engines that spewed an orange pillar of fire. NASA launch director Mike Leinbach reminded commander Mike Bloomfield that the extra days in Florida meant it was “time to take a ride.” The mission marked a milestone: veteran astronaut Jerry L. Ross, a grandfather, became the first person to fly to space seven times. Over the 11‑day flight, the crew will perform four spacewalks to install the 44‑foot, 27,000‑pound “S‑Zero” keystone support unit, which will anchor a massive solar array the size of an acre and longer than a football field, bringing the station closer to completion.

END OF SUMMARY.
