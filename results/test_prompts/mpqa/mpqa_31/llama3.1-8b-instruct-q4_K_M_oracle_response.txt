SUMMARY:

The Zimbabwean election is a test of patience for the country's citizens, who have been subjected to months of fear and intimidation by the ruling ZANU-PF Party. Despite this, many voters, like Martha, secretly supported the opposition MDC candidate Morgan Tsvangirai, seeking a better life with access to food, hospitals, school fees, and a chance at a dignified existence. However, attempts at rigging the election through voter intimidation, last-minute changes in election laws, and outright fraud have raised concerns about the legitimacy of the process. The slashing of polling stations in urban areas and boosting them in rural strongholds has further marred the integrity of the vote. With hopes for a free election now dead, many are left wondering if the rigging will succeed. Amidst this chaos, Zimbabweans are holding onto their faith in democracy, with some even risking their lives to cast their ballots. Despite everything, there is still hope that this election may bring change.

END OF SUMMARY.
