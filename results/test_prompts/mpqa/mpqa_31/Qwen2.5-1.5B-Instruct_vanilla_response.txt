SUMMARY: Zimbabwe held its first contested election in 22 years on Saturday, but it remains unclear if the vote will be free and fair. Voting was marred by intimidation and corruption, and many Zimbabweans expressed frustration with the electoral process. The opposition MDC won a majority of seats, but some rural polling stations remained largely unattended. International observers and local monitors raised concerns about the level of rigging and voter fraud. While many Zimbabweans supported the opposition, others feared for their safety and chose to remain silent during the vote. The outcome of the election remains uncertain, and many continue to express hope for change in the future.
END OF SUMMARY.
