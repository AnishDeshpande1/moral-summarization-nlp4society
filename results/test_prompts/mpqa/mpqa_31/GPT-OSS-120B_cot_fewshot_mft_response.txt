**SUMMARY:**  
The Zimbabwe presidential vote became a test of patience as voters faced fear and intimidation while trying to overcome a rigged, cheating system that many observers called unfair. In urban polling stations, long lines and tear‑gas, rubber‑bullets and other forms of harm were used to frustrate citizens, while in rural areas abuse and oppression by ZANU‑PF officials forced some to vote against their wishes. Despite the oppression, ordinary people like Martha, Janet and Edward expressed hope and happiness after finally casting a ballot, insisting they had made a difference. Opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai warned that the government’s attempts to move the goal‑posts were a betrayal of democracy, but the sheer number of voters waiting for results showed a deep desire for a fair, subversive break from the ruling authority. The election’s outcome remains uncertain, yet the collective resolve suggests a possible end to the long‑standing cheating and a move toward genuine democratic change.
