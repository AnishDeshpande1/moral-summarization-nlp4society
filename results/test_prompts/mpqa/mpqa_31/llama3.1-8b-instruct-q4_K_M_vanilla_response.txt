SUMMARY:

Zimbabweans have cast their votes in the country's first contested election since independence 22 years ago. Despite months of fear and intimidation, voters turned out in record numbers to choose between incumbent President Robert Mugabe and opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai. However, reports of voter intimidation, rigging, and attempts to disenfranchise opposition supporters have raised concerns about the fairness of the election.

Many polling stations were overwhelmed with long lines, and some voters waited for over 27 hours without food or water. Rural areas saw empty polling stations, while urban areas experienced long queues and clashes between police and protesters.

Despite these challenges, many Zimbabweans remain hopeful that this election may bring change. Voters like Martha, who secretly supported the opposition, say they are tired of living in poverty and want a better life. The country's 5.6 million voters are now waiting anxiously for the results, which may take days to be announced.

END OF SUMMARY.
