SUMMARY:

Zimbabwe's presidential election has been marred by widespread voter intimidation, attempts at rigging, and outright fraud. Despite this, many Zimbabweans have braved the odds to cast their votes, with some waiting for over 27 hours in the hot sun without food or water. The ruling ZANU-PF party has used various tactics to disenfranchise opposition voters, including slashing polling stations in urban areas and boosting them in rural strongholds. Local election monitors and international observers have condemned the vote rigging as "thorough" and say hopes for a free election are dead. However, there is still hope that this election may bring change to Zimbabwe, with many believing that democracy has the power to overcome even the most entrenched corruption. The outcome of the election remains uncertain, but one thing is clear: the people of Zimbabwe deserve a fair and transparent vote.
