**SUMMARY:**
Environmentalist groups in Japan staged a protest against the U.S. alternative to the Kyoto Protocol, which they deemed unacceptable and regrettable, as it would lead to a 30% increase in greenhouse gas emissions compared to 1990 levels by 2010. The protesters, who handed letters to President George W. Bush and Japanese officials, urged them to reconsider their climate policy plan and re-enter the Kyoto Protocol. They argued that this plan was not an alternative, but rather a negative step that would sacrifice the planet's future for economic growth. The protesters also called on the U.S. to take leadership in helping developing countries through peaceful dialogue and fair economic support, and to cease its war against terrorism, which they deemed unfair.
