SUMMARY:

Environmental groups staged a protest near the US Embassy in Tokyo on Sunday against the US alternative to the Kyoto Protocol, which aims to curb global warming. About 50 members of various NGOs, including Friends of the Earth Japan and Kiko Network, gathered with banners reading "Ratify the Kyoto Protocol" and handed letters to President Bush's guard. The groups expressed disappointment and dismay at the new plan, which calls for voluntary limits on greenhouse gas emissions instead of mandatory reduction targets.

The NGOs argued that the US alternative would allow greenhouse gas emissions to increase by 30% compared to 1990 levels in 2010, making it incompatible with the Kyoto Protocol. They demanded Bush reconsider his climate policy and re-enter the Kyoto Protocol. The protest came ahead of a meeting between Prime Minister Koizumi and President Bush on Monday, where they are expected to discuss measures to tackle global warming.

END OF SUMMARY.
