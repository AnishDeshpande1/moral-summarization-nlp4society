SUMMARY:
Environmental NGOs in Japan staged a protest outside the U.S. Embassy in Tokyo to denounce President George W. Bush’s “alternative” to the Kyoto Protocol, which they view as a morally unacceptable step that would let U.S. emissions rise by about 30 % by 2010. About 50 activists from groups such as Friends of the Earth Japan, Kiko Network and Peace Boat gathered with banners demanding that the United States ratify the Kyoto treaty and urging Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi to press Bush to reverse his climate policy. The demonstrators handed letters to embassy security, condemning the U.S. plan for relying on voluntary, growth‑linked limits rather than mandatory cuts, and calling the Kyoto Protocol the only viable framework to avert dangerous climate change. They also linked broader moral concerns, urging the U.S. to lead in fair support for developing nations, to end its “war on terrorism,” cease air raids in Afghanistan, halt nuclear experiments, and reduce military bases in Japan. The protest underscored a moral appeal for global cooperation on climate and peace ahead of the Bush‑Koizumi summit.

END OF SUMMARY.
