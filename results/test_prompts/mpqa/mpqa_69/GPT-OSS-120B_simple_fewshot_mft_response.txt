SUMMARY:
Environmental NGOs in Japan staged a protest outside the U.S. Embassy in Tokyo to confront President George W. Bush’s “alternative” to the Kyoto Protocol, accusing the United States of subverting international authority on climate policy and endangering global welfare. About 50 activists from groups such as Friends of the Earth Japan, Kiko Network and Peace Boat gathered near the embassy, brandishing banners demanding that Japan’s Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi reject Bush’s plan and uphold the Kyoto agreement. They handed a letter to a guard, urging Bush to abandon a voluntary, growth‑oriented emissions scheme that would let U.S. greenhouse‑gas output rise 30 % by 2010, a move they framed as harmful to vulnerable populations and the planet. The NGOs also appealed to Koizumi to reaffirm Japan’s commitment to ratify Kyoto and to press the United States to act responsibly, linking climate inaction to broader concerns about U.S. military actions and “war on terrorism.” The demonstration highlighted a moral challenge to U.S. authority while emphasizing the care‑based imperative to protect the environment from further harm.  
END OF SUMMARY.
