SUMMARY:
Environmental NGOs in Japan staged a protest near the U.S. Embassy in Tokyo to demand that President George W. Bush **reconsider** his climate **plan** and **re‑enter** the Kyoto Protocol. About 50 activists from Friends of the Earth, Kiko Network and Peace Boat gathered at Tameike‑Sanno Station, holding banners that **said** “Ratify the Kyoto Protocol” and urging Prime Minister Koizumi to **stick** to the treaty. They delivered letters **concerned** about the U.S. **stand** on **curbing** global warming, calling the U.S. **taken**‑by‑force approach a **dismayed** **increase** of emissions that would **allow** a 30 % rise by 2010. The NGOs **demand** that the United States **prevent** dangerous climate **change**, **tackle** it with genuine **leadership**, and provide **helping** **dialogue** and **support** to developing nations. The protest also referenced the September 11 **attacks**, urging an end to U.S. **raids**, nuclear tests and the “war against terrorism.” The demonstration came as Bush prepared to meet Prime Minister Koizumi, with Japan planning to **ratify** the Kyoto Protocol once the Diet approves it.  
END OF SUMMARY.
