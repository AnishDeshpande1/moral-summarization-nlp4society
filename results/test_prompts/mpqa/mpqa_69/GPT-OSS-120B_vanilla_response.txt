SUMMARY: Environmental NGOs in Japan protested the U.S. “alternative” to the Kyoto Protocol outside the U.S. Embassy in Tokyo on the day President George W. Bush arrived. About 50 activists from groups such as Friends of the Earth Japan, Kiko Network and Peace Boat gathered near Tameike‑Sanno Station, displaying banners demanding Japan and the U.S. ratify the Kyoto agreement. They handed Bush a letter condemning his voluntary‑limits climate plan, which they say would let U.S. emissions rise 30 % above 1990 levels by 2010, and urged him to re‑enter the treaty. The NGOs also urged Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi to press Bush on the issue and announced Japan’s intention to ratify Kyoto after Diet approval. Peace Boat’s separate letter also called for broader U.S. policy changes, including reduced military actions and support for developing nations. The protest coincided with heightened security for Bush’s meeting with Koizumi, where climate policy will be a key discussion point. END OF SUMMARY.
