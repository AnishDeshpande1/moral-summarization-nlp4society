SUMMARY:
Environmentalist groups staged a protest against the U.S. alternative to the Kyoto Protocol near the U.S. Embassy in Tokyo, opposing President George W. Bush's new climate change plan. The protesters carried banners and handed letters to the president, urging him to reconsider his policy and re-enter the Kyoto Protocol. They argued that the U.S. plan would allow greenhouse gas emissions to increase by 30% compared to 1990 levels, which they believe is unacceptable. The environmentalists see the Kyoto Protocol as a necessary measure to prevent dangerous climate change, and demand that the U.S. take leadership in addressing global warming. In their letters, they also called for peaceful dialogue and fair economic support for developing countries, while urging the U.S. to stop air raids on Afghanistan and cease nuclear experiments. The protest came ahead of Bush's meeting with Japanese Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi, who is expected to discuss measures to tackle global warming with the president.
END OF SUMMARY.
