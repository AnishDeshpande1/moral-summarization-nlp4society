SUMMARY:

Environmental groups staged a protest near the US Embassy in Tokyo against President George W. Bush's alternative plan for curbing global warming, which they claim would increase greenhouse gas emissions by 30% compared to 1990 levels. The protesters, representing various Japanese and US NGOs, handed a letter to Bush urging him to reconsider his climate policy and re-enter the Kyoto Protocol. They argue that the protocol is the only international framework to prevent dangerous climate change. Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi has been urged by environmental groups to call on Bush to return to the Kyoto Protocol during their meeting in Tokyo.

The protesters' concerns are driven by a sense of urgency and moral obligation to protect the planet from the devastating effects of global warming. They believe that the US, as one of the world's largest emitters of greenhouse gases, has a responsibility to take a leadership role in addressing this crisis. The protest highlights the need for countries to work together to address climate change and ensure a sustainable future.

END OF SUMMARY.
