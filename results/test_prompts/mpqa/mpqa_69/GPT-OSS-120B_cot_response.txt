**SUMMARY:**  
On Sunday, about 50 activists from Japanese and U.S. NGOs gathered near the U.S. Embassy in Tokyo to protest President George W. Bush’s “alternative” climate plan, which they called *regrettable* and *negative*. Speakers from Friends of the Earth, Kiko Network and Peace Boat held banners demanding the U.S. *ratify* the Kyoto Protocol and urged Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi to tell Bush to “*stick* to Kyoto.” In letters handed to embassy guards, the groups expressed that they were *dismayed* by the plan, which they warned would let U.S. greenhouse‑gas emissions rise 30 % by 2010 and thus pose a *dangerous* threat to the climate. They called for the United States to show *peaceful* and *fair* leadership, to *stop* air raids in Afghanistan, to *cease* nuclear experiments, and to reduce its military bases in Japan. The protest highlighted the moral urgency of re‑entering the Kyoto Protocol as the main international framework to avert catastrophic climate change.
