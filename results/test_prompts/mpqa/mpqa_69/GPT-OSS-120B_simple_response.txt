SUMMARY:  
Environmental NGOs in Japan staged a moral protest against President George W. Bush’s “alternative” to the Kyoto Protocol, condemning the U.S. plan as a betrayal of global climate responsibility. About 50 activists from Friends of the Earth Japan, Kiko Network, Peace Boat and other groups gathered near the U.S. Embassy in Tokyo, demanding that the United States honor its ethical duty to curb greenhouse‑gas emissions. They displayed banners urging the ratification of Kyoto and urged Prime Minister Koizumi to press Bush to reverse his rejection of the pact. In letters handed to embassy guards, the NGOs decried Bush’s voluntary, growth‑focused emissions plan, warning it would raise U.S. emissions by 30 % above 1990 levels by 2010 and would fail to prevent dangerous climate change. The protesters framed their appeal as a matter of global justice, urging the U.S. to lead with fair support for developing nations and to cease militaristic actions linked to the “war on terror.” Their moral stance highlighted the ethical imperative to prioritize environmental protection over short‑term economic gain.  

END OF SUMMARY.
