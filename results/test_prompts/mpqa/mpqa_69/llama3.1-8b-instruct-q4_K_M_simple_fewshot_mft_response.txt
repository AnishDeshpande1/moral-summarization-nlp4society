SUMMARY:
Environmental groups staged a protest against President George W. Bush's visit to Tokyo, opposing his administration's stance on global warming. The protesters, including members from Japanese and U.S. NGOs, gathered near the U.S. Embassy in Tokyo, carrying banners that read "Ratify the Kyoto Protocol" and "Koizumi say 'No' to Bush: Stick to Kyoto Protocol." They handed a letter to a guard at the embassy, urging Bush to reconsider his climate policy plan and re-enter the Kyoto Protocol. The NGOs argued that the U.S. alternative to the 1997 pact would allow greenhouse gas emissions to increase by around 30% compared to 1990 levels in 2010. The protesters also called on the U.S. to take leadership in helping developing countries through peaceful dialogue and fair economic support, and to refrain from pushing other governments to cooperate in its "war against terrorism." The demonstration came as Bush was set to meet with Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi to discuss measures to tackle global warming.
