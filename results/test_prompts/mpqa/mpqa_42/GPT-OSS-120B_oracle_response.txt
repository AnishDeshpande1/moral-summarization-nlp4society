SUMMARY:  
Former ISI chief and defense analyst Hameed Gul blasted the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights report as “a pack of blatant lies and propaganda.” He argued that any country that resists American hegemony is labeled a violator, because the United States uses force to subjugate the world and is unwilling to tolerate obstacles to its vested interests. Gul claimed the U.S. has committed unprecedented oppression, unleashing barbaric attacks on Afghan civilians and remaining silent while killings of Indian Muslims and rioting continue. He said America sees violations only when they hinder its goals, and that Israel’s abuses in Palestine are ignored, while U.S. support for Israeli attacks denies Palestinians a free state. Gul warned that America wants total submission of Muslims, and that the plight of Muslim nations will be crushed unless scholars and rulers forge unity to resist the ever‑increasing U.S. influence, which he described as led by imperialist forces that target them one by one.  
END OF SUMMARY.
