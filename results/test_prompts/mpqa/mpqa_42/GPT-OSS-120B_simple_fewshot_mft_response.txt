SUMMARY:
Former ISI chief Hameed Gul dismissed the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights report as “blatant lies and propaganda,” portraying the United States as an imperial authority that subjugates any nation that resists its dominance. He accused Washington of hypocrisy, claiming it ignores atrocities against Muslims—such as the killings of Indian Muslims and Israel’s actions in Palestine—while condemning others, thereby cheating the standards of fairness and betraying universal moral norms. Gul warned that the U.S. uses its power to inflict harm on vulnerable peoples, from Afghan civilians to Palestinians, and that this pattern reflects a broader strategy to suppress Muslim societies. He called for Muslim leaders to demonstrate loyalty to one another and unite in moral resistance, lest they be crushed one by one by the “imperialist” forces led by America. The interview frames the U.S. as an unjust, oppressive authority that harms innocent populations, cheats on human‑rights principles, and betrays any loyalty owed to the global Muslim community.  
END OF SUMMARY.
