SUMMARY:  
In a striking example of human responsibility and teamwork beyond Earth, two NASA astronauts—Linda Godwin and Daniel Tani—performed a crucial spacewalk to protect the International Space Station’s power system. By installing thermal covers over the motors that steer the station’s massive solar arrays, they acted to shield the equipment from harsh temperature swings, preventing power spikes and stalls that could jeopardize the station’s energy supply. Their careful maneuvering, using the shuttle Endeavour’s robotic arm for a 50‑foot ascent along the station’s side, underscores the moral imperative of diligent stewardship of shared scientific assets and the collaborative spirit that keeps humanity’s presence in space safe and sustainable.  

END OF SUMMARY.
