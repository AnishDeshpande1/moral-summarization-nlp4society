SUMMARY: Two astronauts, Linda Godwin and Daniel Tani, performed a spacewalk from shuttle Endeavour to service the International Space Station. They used the station’s robotic arm to reach the solar array wings and installed thermal covers over the motors that rotate the large solar panels. The covers are intended to protect the motors from extreme temperature fluctuations, preventing power spikes and stalls, and ensuring the panels stay correctly oriented toward the sun for optimal electricity generation. END OF SUMMARY.
