SUMMARY:
Two NASA astronauts, Linda Godwin and Daniel Tani, performed a critical space‑walk to protect the International Space Station’s power system. By installing thermal covers over the motors that steer the station’s massive solar arrays, they acted to prevent damaging temperature swings, power spikes and possible stalls that could jeopardize the crew’s safety and the mission’s success. The astronauts used the shuttle Endeavour’s robotic arm to safely reach the worksite, demonstrating careful planning, cooperation and a commitment to the well‑being of the station and all who depend on its power. Their hands‑on repair underscores a broader moral duty to safeguard shared scientific resources and protect the lives of those who rely on them. END OF SUMMARY.
