SUMMARY:
Two NASA astronauts, Linda Godwin and Daniel Tani, completed a spacewalk on Monday to repair the International Space Station's solar wings. They installed thermal covers around motors that control the direction of the solar panels, which generate electricity for the station. The repair aims to prevent power spikes and stalls caused by temperature fluctuations in space.

The astronauts used the shuttle Endeavour's robotic arm to transport themselves 50 feet up the side of the space station. Once at the work site, they secured themselves with tethers and completed their task. NASA hopes this repair will ensure the continued functionality of the solar wings and maintain a stable power supply for the International Space Station.

The mission is part of ongoing efforts to maintain and upgrade the space station's systems to extend its operational lifespan. The success of this repair will be crucial in ensuring the station remains operational for future research missions.

END OF SUMMARY.
