SUMMARY:
NASA astronauts Linda Godwin and Daniel Tani embarked on a crucial repair mission to protect the International Space Station's solar wings from extreme temperatures in space. The astronauts successfully wrapped thermal covers around motors that turn the solar panels, ensuring they generate electricity efficiently. This vital maintenance work aims to prevent power spikes and stalls, which could compromise the station's operations.

The endeavor demonstrates NASA's commitment to preserving and extending the life of this orbiting laboratory, a symbol of international cooperation in space exploration. The astronauts' skillful execution of the repair highlights their dedication to ensuring the continued success of the International Space Station project. By addressing potential issues proactively, NASA showcases its responsible stewardship of valuable resources and infrastructure.

END OF SUMMARY.
