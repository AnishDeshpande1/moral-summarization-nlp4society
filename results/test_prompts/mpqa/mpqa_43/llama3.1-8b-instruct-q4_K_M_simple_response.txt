SUMMARY:

A seminar by the China Society for Study of Human Rights (CSSHR) criticized the US State Department's annual report on human rights practices in world countries, specifically its portion on China. Experts claimed that the report is biased and contains many fabrications, exaggerations, and unsubstantiated stories. They argued that the US has no right to issue such a false report, as it violates the UN spirit of non-interference in other countries' internal affairs.

The seminar highlighted examples of human rights improvements in Tibet, including the restoration of monasteries and the protection of indigenous cultural heritage. Italian senator Renzo Gubert praised China's efforts in protecting religious freedom in Tibet. Experts also disputed the US report's claims about China restricting religious freedom, citing credible sources that contradict these assertions.

The seminar concluded that the US report uses human rights issues as a tool to exert political pressure on China, rather than genuinely promoting human rights. The experts called for an end to such biased and misleading reports.

END OF SUMMARY.
