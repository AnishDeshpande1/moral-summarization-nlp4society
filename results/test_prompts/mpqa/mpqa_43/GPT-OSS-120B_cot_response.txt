**SUMMARY:**  
The United States State Department’s 2001 human‑rights report on China is denounced by Chinese scholars and officials as a slandering, false document filled with absurdities, exaggerations and fabrications intended to tarnish China’s image and apply political pressure. Experts claim the report violates UN principles by interfering in internal affairs and presents unsubstantiated, reckless accusations, such as alleged violations of religious freedom in Tibet and a fabricated “crackdown” on a banned “heretical” sect. They argue the United States falsely portrays China’s anti‑terrorism actions in Xinjiang as oppression of ethnic groups, while actually targeting terrorists regardless of ethnicity. Visits by Western delegations, including an Italian senator, are cited as evidence of genuine religious freedom and development, contradicting the report’s claims. The authors assert that the United States has no right to issue such a report and that its allegations constitute a moral violation of international norms.
