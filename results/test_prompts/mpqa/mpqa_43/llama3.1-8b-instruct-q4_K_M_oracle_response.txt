SUMMARY:

The US State Department's annual human rights report on China contains numerous fabrications, exaggerations, and unsubstantiated stories that aim to tarnish China's image and exert political pressure on the Chinese Government. Human rights experts condemned the report as a form of slander, saying it violates the UN spirit of non-interference in other countries' internal affairs. The report is filled with incorrect information, using phrases like "according to credible reports" and "various sources" without providing any actual evidence.

Experts pointed out that China has made efforts to protect human rights, including repairing and renovating monasteries, protecting indigenous cultural heritage, and promoting religious freedom. In contrast, the US report restricts China's efforts by accusing it of restricting religious freedom in Tibet and Xinjiang. The report also uses anti-terrorism campaigns as a pretext to legitimize measures against ethnic minority groups.

The Chinese Government has refuted these claims, saying that whoever engages in terrorism should be brought to justice, regardless of their ethnicity. The US report's reckless conclusion is based on untruths and anecdotal evidence, demonstrating its lack of regard for the truth. END OF SUMMARY.
