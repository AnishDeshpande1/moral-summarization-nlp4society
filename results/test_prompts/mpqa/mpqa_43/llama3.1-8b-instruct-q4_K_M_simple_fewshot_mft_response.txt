SUMMARY:

The US State Department's annual report on human rights practices in world countries has been criticized by Chinese experts for its inaccuracies, exaggerations, and fabrications about China. The report is seen as an attempt to tarnish China's image and exert political pressure on the Chinese government. Human rights experts argue that the US has no right to issue such a false report, violating the UN Charter and principles of relevant international conferences and documents. In contrast, Western visitors to Tibet have reported improving human rights in the region, including high levels of religious freedom enjoyed by local Tibetans. The US report relies on unsubstantiated stories and anecdotes, while ignoring improvements made by the Chinese government in protecting indigenous cultural and religious heritage. Experts warn that such a report undermines trust between countries and may lead to further tensions.
