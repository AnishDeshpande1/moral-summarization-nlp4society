**SUMMARY:**
The United States State Department’s annual human‑rights report on China was denounced by Chinese scholars and foreign observers as a slandering, false, and politicized document riddled with absurdities, exaggerations and fabrications. Experts argued the report violates the UN spirit of non‑interference and constitutes an illegitimate, intrusive intrusion into China’s internal affairs. They highlighted unsubstantiated, reckless claims and blind accusations—such as alleged restrictions on religious freedom in Tibet and fabricated terrorism narratives in Xinjiang—as mischaracterizations that serve propaganda rather than truth. Visits by Tibetan scholars and Italian Senator Renzo Gubert were cited to show the report’s distorted and misleading portrayal of religious freedom and development. The condemnation stresses that the United States’ unfounded, biased narrative is harmful, deceptive, and unjustified. END OF SUMMARY.
