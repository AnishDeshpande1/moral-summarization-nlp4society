SUMMARY:
The U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report on China is portrayed as a politically motivated slur that violates international norms of non‑interference and undermines legitimate authority. Chinese scholars and former UN officials argue the report fabricates abuses, thereby harming China’s reputation and betraying the principle of fairness by presenting false accusations. They highlight genuine care for Tibetan and Uyghur peoples, citing improvements in religious freedom, cultural preservation, and economic development, and condemn the U.S. for degrading sacred traditions and spreading unsubstantiated claims. The article stresses loyalty to China’s sovereign authority, accusing the United States of subverting it for ulterior motives, and frames the U.S. actions as a betrayal of both moral integrity and international law. By contrasting Western allegations with on‑the‑ground testimonies of respect for religious practice and anti‑terrorism measures, the piece asserts that the U.S. report is an unjust, deceitful attack that degrades China’s sanctity and cheats the global community of a truthful assessment.  
END OF SUMMARY.
