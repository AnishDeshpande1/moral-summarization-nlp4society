SUMMARY:

A recent report by the US State Department on human rights practices in world countries has been criticized for its inaccuracies and biases against China. Experts from the China Society for Study of Human Rights (CSSHR) argued that the report is intended to tarnish China's image and exert political pressure on the Chinese government. The report allegedly uses unsubstantiated stories, exaggerations, and fabrications to accuse China of violating human rights in Tibet and Xinjiang.

However, experts pointed out that there are many instances where Western visitors have praised China's efforts to protect human rights in Tibet, such as Italian senator Renzo Gubert who visited the region twice. The report also contains procedural mistakes and incorrect facts. Critics argue that the US has no right to issue such reports and is violating the UN Charter by interfering with other countries' internal affairs.

The Chinese government has built and renovated many monasteries in Tibet, including the Potala Palace, and locals enjoy a high level of religious freedom. The report's accusations are also disputed by experts who argue that those accused of terrorism should be brought to justice regardless of their ethnicity.

END OF SUMMARY.
