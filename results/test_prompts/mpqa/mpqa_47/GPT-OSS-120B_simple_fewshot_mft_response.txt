SUMMARY:
The Russian Foreign Ministry blasted the U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report as “odious,” accusing Washington of undermining legitimate authority by reviving the “Chechen problem” and judging Russia’s anti‑terrorism efforts without proper context. It portrayed the U.S. criticism as a subversive move by “certain circles” intent on harming the growing Russian‑American partnership against terrorism. The ministry defended Russia’s actions in Chechnya as a lawful war against international terrorists, emphasizing that Russian security forces are protecting citizens from extremist violence. At the same time, it highlighted the care‑oriented argument that the United States should first address its own human‑rights shortcomings—such as incomplete treaty ratifications and the continued use of the death penalty—before accusing Russia of violating rights. By framing the U.S. report as unfair and hypocritical, the ministry suggested that the criticism amounts to cheating on the standards of mutual respect and equal treatment between nations. END OF SUMMARY.
