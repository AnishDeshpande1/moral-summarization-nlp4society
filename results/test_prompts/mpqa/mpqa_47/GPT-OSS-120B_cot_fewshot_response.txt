SUMMARY:
The Russian Foreign Ministry said it was “surprised, to put it mildly” by the U.S. State Department’s “odious” criticism of Russia’s human‑rights record, especially the section on Chechnya. The U.S. report labeled Russia’s record “poor,” noting that Russian security forces showed “little respect” for basic rights and were linked to “serious violations,” including “extrajudicial killings” by both government forces and Chechen fighters. While U.S. officials have also “criticized” Russian military and police “abuse” of civilians, the Ministry argued that this criticism is an “irritant” driven by “certain circles” seeking to revive the “Chechen problem” and undermine the new U.S.–Russian anti‑terrorism partnership. The ministry added that before judging others, the United States should address its own “failure” to ratify some human‑rights accords and its continued “death penalty,” despite a moratorium introduced in 1996.
