SUMMARY:  
The Russian Foreign Ministry blasted the U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report as “odious,” accusing Washington of relying on outdated drafts and ignoring recent events, including the post‑9/11 global fight against terrorism. While the U.S. report condemned Russia’s poor media freedom and alleged extrajudicial killings in Chechnya, Moscow retorted that the criticism serves “certain circles” in America eager to resurrect the “Chechen problem” and undermine the burgeoning U.S.–Russian anti‑terrorism partnership. The ministry warned that the United States should first examine its own record—its failure to ratify several human‑rights treaties and its continued use of the death penalty—before judging others. By framing the dispute as a moral double‑standard, Russia portrayed the U.S. attack as an unjust attempt to derail cooperation against international terrorism.  

END OF SUMMARY.
