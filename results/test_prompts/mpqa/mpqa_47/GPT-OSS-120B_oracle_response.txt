SUMMARY: The Russian Foreign Ministry blasted the U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report as “odious,” saying it was surprised by the criticism of Russia’s record, especially the section on Chechnya. Moscow accused U.S. writers of using outdated drafts, ignoring recent events such as the post‑9/11 “closed” “battle” against terrorism. The U.S. report condemned Russia for poor media independence and for “violations” in Chechnya, citing extrajudicial “killings,” police “abuse,” and a lack of “respect” for basic rights. While U.S. officials acknowledge that some Chechen rebels have ties to international terrorism, they also note that many lack such connections and have criticized Russian security forces. The Russian ministry claimed the U.S. criticism reflects “certain circles” that seek to “reanimate” the “Chechen problem” and “turn” it into a stumbling block in the new U.S.–Russian anti‑terrorism partnership, which includes intelligence “sharing,” the “opening” of airspace for humanitarian flights, and cooperation in Central Asia. It urged the United States to examine its own “failure” to ratify some human‑rights treaties and its continued use of the death penalty. END OF SUMMARY.
