SUMMARY: The Russian Foreign Ministry denounced the U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report, calling its criticism of Russia—especially the “odious” section on Chechnya—surprising and outdated. The report accused Russian security forces in Chechnya of serious human‑rights violations, including extrajudicial killings, while noting some rebel leaders’ links to international terrorism. Moscow responded that the U.S. narrative reflects certain American groups seeking to revive the “Chechen problem” and undermine the new U.S.–Russian anti‑terrorism partnership. The ministry also warned that the United States should address its own human‑rights issues, such as failure to ratify some treaties and the continued use of the death penalty, before judging others. END OF SUMMARY.
