SUMMARY:
The Russian Foreign Ministry denounced the U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report as “odious,” saying it showed a naïve reliance on outdated drafts that ignored recent events in both countries and the global fight against terrorism since 9/11. While the U.S. report accused Russia of media repression and serious violations in Chechnya—including extrajudicial killings by Russian forces and Chechen fighters—the ministry argued that such criticism serves “certain circles” in America that seek to revive the “Chechen problem” and undermine the growing U.S.–Russian anti‑terrorism partnership. Moscow highlighted that the United States itself has unresolved human‑rights issues, such as its failure to ratify several international accords and its continued use of the death penalty, noting Russia’s own moratorium on capital punishment since 1996. By framing the U.S. assessment as hypocritical and politically motivated, the ministry portrayed the criticism as a moral double‑standard that distracts from the shared fight against international terrorism and the cooperative security ties now binding the two nations. END OF SUMMARY.
