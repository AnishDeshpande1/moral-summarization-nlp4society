SUMMARY:
Russia's Foreign Ministry criticized the US State Department's annual human rights report, calling it "odious" for its section on Chechnya. The ministry expressed surprise at the report's tone and content, suggesting that it was based on outdated information and ignored recent developments, including the 9/11 attacks and Russia's cooperation with the US in the war against terrorism. The ministry also implied that the report reflected opposition to the growing partnership between the two countries.

The State Department's report highlighted abuses by Russian security forces in Chechnya, including extrajudicial killings, but also noted some rebel ties to international terrorist groups. Russia has portrayed the conflict as a fight against terrorists, and the US has partially endorsed this view while still criticizing Russian military and police abuse of civilians.

In response, the Foreign Ministry suggested that the US should investigate its own human rights record before judging others', citing issues such as unratified international agreements and the use of capital punishment.

END OF SUMMARY.
