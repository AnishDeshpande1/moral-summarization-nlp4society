SUMMARY:
Russia's Foreign Ministry criticized the U.S. State Department's annual human rights report, calling it "odious" for its section on Chechnya. The ministry accused the report of being outdated and biased against Russia. It claimed that the report reflected the efforts of certain circles in the U.S. who object to the new partnership between the two countries against international terrorism.

The ministry suggested that before judging others' human rights records, the U.S. should investigate its own situation, including its failure to ratify some international agreements and the use of capital punishment. Russia introduced a moratorium on capital punishment in 1996 as a prerequisite for joining the Council of Europe. The ministry implied that the criticism of Russia's human rights record was motivated by politics rather than a genuine concern for human rights.

The conflict in Chechnya has been portrayed by Russian officials, including President Putin, as a war against international terrorists, with some U.S. officials endorsing this view. However, the U.S. report also criticized Russian military and police abuse of civilians.

END OF SUMMARY.
