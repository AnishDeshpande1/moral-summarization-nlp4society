Rumsfeld Defends U.S. Treatment of Detainees in Cuba: Secretary of Defense Donald H. Rumsfeld defended the treatment of prisoners at Guantanamo Bay, Cuba, amid international outcry over photos showing detainees in restraints and masks. Critics accused the US of using sensory deprivation and psychological control techniques to weaken prisoners for interrogation. The International Committee of the Red Cross and Amnesty International called for prisoner of war status under the Geneva Convention, citing rights to protection from humiliation and unnecessary restraint. Rumsfeld argued that restraint was necessary to prevent escapes and attacks on American soldiers. However, officials have yet to classify the detainees as prisoners of war, leading some to question the US's adherence to international law. The issue raises concerns about the treatment of individuals held in detention without trial or access to lawyers, sparking debate over human rights and the rule of law.
