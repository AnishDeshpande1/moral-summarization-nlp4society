SUMMARY:

The Kyoto Protocol, signed by 150 countries in 1997, aims to slow global warming. However, the US has decided not to ratify it, causing irritation among its allies, particularly Japan, which had taken pride in hosting the accord's shaping process. The decision is seen as a turning point in US-Japanese relations, with many viewing it as part of a growing trend of unilateralism by the Bush administration. This trend includes pulling out of Kyoto, labeling certain countries as an "axis of evil," and forging its own path on climate change issues. Japan fears that this will lead to a two-tier system where some nations follow stricter limits while others choose their own targets. The US has proposed alternative initiatives, but environmental advocates argue they do not go far enough in reducing greenhouse gas emissions. This decision is seen as a slight to the international community and a setback for efforts to stop global warming.

END OF SUMMARY.
