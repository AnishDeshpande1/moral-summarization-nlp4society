SUMMARY:
Japanese officials and environmental groups denounce the United States for abandoning the Kyoto Protocol, portraying the move as a blatant subversion of international authority and a betrayal of the collective commitment to protect the planet. They argue that the U.S., the world’s largest emitter, is harming vulnerable ecosystems—coral reefs, polar bears and migratory birds—by refusing to join the agreement, thereby endangering global health and future generations. The Kiko Network’s president calls the decision “absolutely inadmissible,” emphasizing Japan’s loyalty to the climate‑change coalition and its pride in hosting the accord. The article links the U.S. stance to a broader pattern of unilateralism, suggesting that Washington’s “do‑it‑our‑way” approach undermines trusted alliances and threatens smaller nations that might follow Japan’s lead. While some Japanese business leaders warn that stricter emissions rules could hurt competitiveness, the dominant moral narrative stresses that the U.S. is betraying both international authority and the shared duty of care for the environment.  
END OF SUMMARY.
