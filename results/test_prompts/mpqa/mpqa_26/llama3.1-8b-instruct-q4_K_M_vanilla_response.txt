SUMMARY:

The Kyoto Protocol, signed by 150 countries in 1997, aims to slow global warming. However, the US has announced it will not ratify the protocol, citing concerns over its economic impact. This decision has been met with irritation from Japan and other US allies, who see it as a sign of Washington's growing unilateralism. The Bush administration is offering an alternative plan, which environmental advocates say will allow the US to continue increasing greenhouse gas emissions. Japan, in particular, is disappointed by the US stance, having played a key role in shaping the Kyoto Protocol on its soil.

The decision is seen as a blow to global efforts to combat climate change and may lead to a two-tier system where some countries opt out of stricter limits. The move has also strained US-Japanese relations at a time when Japan's economic influence is waning. While some Japanese industries see the alternative plan as a positive step, environmental groups are critical of its potential impact on global efforts to reduce emissions.

END OF SUMMARY.
