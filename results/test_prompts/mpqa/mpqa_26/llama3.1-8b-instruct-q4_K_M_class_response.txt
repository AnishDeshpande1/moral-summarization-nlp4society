SUMMARY:

The Kyoto Protocol, aimed at slowing global warming, is facing uncertainty due to President George Bush's decision not to ratify it. This move has been met with frustration from US allies, particularly Japan, which was proud to have hosted the shaping of the accord in 1997. The clash between economic growth and environmental protection is evident as countries struggle to find a balance between reducing greenhouse gas emissions and promoting industrial competitiveness.

The Bush administration's alternative plan, Clear Skies and Global Climate Change Initiatives, has been criticized for being too lenient, allowing the US to increase its gas emissions while smaller nations are forced to adhere to stricter limits. This could lead to a two-tier system, where some countries set their own targets while others follow the more stringent Kyoto Protocol.

Japan's industry is concerned that further reduction efforts will hurt their competitiveness, but environmental advocates argue that the US must take responsibility for its role in global warming and turn its back on polluting practices. The attacks on the Kyoto Protocol from Washington have left many wondering if countries can work together to protect the planet.

END OF SUMMARY.
