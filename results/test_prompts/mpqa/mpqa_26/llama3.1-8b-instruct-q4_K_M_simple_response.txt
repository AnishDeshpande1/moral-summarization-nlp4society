SUMMARY:

The US decision not to ratify the Kyoto Protocol, an international agreement aimed at reducing global warming, has sparked outrage among its allies, particularly Japan. Climate experts warn that failure to slow global warming will have dire consequences, including the extinction of polar bears and coral reefs. The Bush administration's alternative plan, while touted as more market-sensitive, is seen by environmental advocates as a means for the US to continue increasing greenhouse gas emissions. This decision has led to concerns among smaller nations that they may be left with stricter limits under the Kyoto Protocol, creating a two-tier system.

The situation reflects a growing trend in the Bush administration's unilateral approach to international relations, which has also been evident in its handling of post-Sept. 11 global issues. Japan and other US allies are increasingly frustrated by Washington's "do-it-our-way" decision-making style, which they see as disregarding global cooperation on critical issues.

END OF SUMMARY
