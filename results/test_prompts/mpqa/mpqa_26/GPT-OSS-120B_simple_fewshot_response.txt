SUMMARY:
Japanese environmentalists and officials expressed strong moral outrage at the United States’ decision not to ratify the Kyoto Protocol, calling it “absolutely inadmissible” for the world’s biggest greenhouse‑gas emitter to turn its back on a global climate pact. They portrayed President Bush as a unilateral “monster” who grabs a weeping globe, while Japan’s Prime Minister Koizumi modestly urged further U.S. effort. The article frames the move as a betrayal of collective responsibility, suggesting it signals a broader U.S. trend of acting first and consulting later on global issues. While Japan’s business lobby warns that stricter emissions rules could hurt competitiveness, most Japanese groups view the Bush administration’s alternative “Clear Skies” plan as a thinly‑veiled excuse for the U.S. to keep polluting and to encourage other nations to abandon Kyoto, creating a two‑tier climate regime. The overall moral tone condemns U.S. selfishness and praises Japan’s pride in supporting international cooperation on climate change.

END OF SUMMARY.
