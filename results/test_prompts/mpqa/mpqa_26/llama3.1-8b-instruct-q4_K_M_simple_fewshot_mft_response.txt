SUMMARY:

Japan is expressing indignation over the US decision not to ratify the Kyoto Protocol, an agreement aimed at slowing global warming. The country's environmental groups and leaders are criticizing President George Bush for turning his back on the accord, which was shaped in part to fit Washington's concerns five years ago. Japan's president, Junichiro Koizumi, gently balked at Mr. Bush's proposed alternative during his visit last month, saying that Japan would "like to see further efforts" from the US. Environmental advocates fear that the US alternative will allow it to continue increasing gas emissions, while smaller nations may be forced into a two-tier system with stricter limits. This decision is seen as another signal of the Bush administration's growing unilateralism in international relations. Japanese industry has already implemented energy-saving measures, but some business leaders worry that further policies to reduce emissions could hurt competitiveness.
