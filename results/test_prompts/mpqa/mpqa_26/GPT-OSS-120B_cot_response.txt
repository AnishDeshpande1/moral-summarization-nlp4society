**SUMMARY:**  
Tokyo is irked by the United States’ decision not to ratify the Kyoto Protocol, a move that many Japanese see as inadmissible for the world’s greatest greenhouse‑gas emitter. Environmental experts warn of dire consequences—dying coral reefs, shrinking polar‑bear habitats and starving birds—if global warming continues unchecked. Japanese leaders, proud of hosting the Kyoto talks, view President Bush’s “do‑it‑our‑way” stance as a unilateralist affront, symbolised in a Kiko Network cartoon where a fiendish, monster‑size Bush clutches a weeping globe while Prime Minister Koizumi defensively holds the treaty. The Bush administration proposes the Clear Skies initiative, claiming it will cut pollutants, but critics fear it lets the U.S. turn its back on real emission reductions and may encourage other nations to abandon Kyoto, creating a two‑tier system. While some Japanese industry voices concern that further cuts could hurt competitiveness, the overall mood in Japan is one of indignation toward Washington’s unilateral approach.
