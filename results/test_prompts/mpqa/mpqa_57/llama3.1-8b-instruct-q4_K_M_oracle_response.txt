SUMMARY:

The European Union's decision to ratify the Kyoto Protocol before June this year is a significant step towards combating climate change. In contrast, President Bush's US national climate protection program falls short of expectations, committing only to an 18% increase in energy efficiency by 2012, which will be cancelled out by economic growth. The warnings from climate experts have been unheeded, and the program does not meet the targets set by the Kyoto Protocol. Scientists estimate that industrialized countries must reduce their emissions to around 70% by 2020 to mitigate the effects of climate change.

The European Union has called on the United States to assume its responsibilities under the framework agreement on climate change, stabilizing greenhouse gas emissions at 1990 levels and strengthening domestic actions to bring them into line with the Kyoto Protocol. The US withdrawal from the Kyoto Protocol was a significant setback for international cooperation on climate change. However, it is not too late for the United States to return to the system.

The EU's commitment to climate protection has shown that an active policy can have positive effects on employment and the economy. We hope that the United States will strengthen its efforts and assume its responsibilities in combating global climate protection.

END OF SUMMARY.
