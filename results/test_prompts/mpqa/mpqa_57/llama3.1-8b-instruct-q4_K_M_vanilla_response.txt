SUMMARY:

In recent weeks, the European Union (EU) and the United States have made significant moves on climate change policy. The EU has reached a formal agreement to ratify the Kyoto Protocol by June, which sets binding targets for developed countries to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. In contrast, the US has announced a national climate protection program that falls short of expectations. While President Bush recognizes the need to fight climate change, the program's goals are considered weak and unlikely to meet international commitments.

The EU has called on the US to assume its responsibilities under the Kyoto Protocol, which requires developed countries to reduce emissions by 7-8%. Climate experts warn that without decisive action, the consequences of climate change will be dramatic, including rising global temperatures, sea levels, and extreme weather events. The EU hopes that the US will join them in implementing a strong climate protection policy, not only for environmental reasons but also to modernize industrial society and create new jobs.

END OF SUMMARY.
