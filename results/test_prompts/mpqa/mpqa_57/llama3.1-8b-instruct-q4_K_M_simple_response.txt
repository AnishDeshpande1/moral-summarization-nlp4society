SUMMARY:

Recent weeks have seen significant developments in the fight against climate change on both sides of the Atlantic. The European Union has made progress by ratifying the Kyoto Protocol, while the United States' national climate protection program falls short of expectations. President Bush's plan aims to reduce US emissions by 18% between 2002 and 2012, but this is considered inadequate compared to the EU's commitment to reduce its emissions by 8%. Climate experts warn that without rapid action, the consequences of climate change will be severe, including a 5.8°C increase in global temperature by 2100 and rising sea levels.

The EU has called on the US to assume its responsibilities under the Kyoto Protocol and stabilize greenhouse gas emissions at 1990 levels. The European Union's climate protection policy is not only necessary for environmental policy but also an opportunity to modernize industrial society, creating new jobs and promoting clean technologies. The door remains open for the US to return to the Kyoto process.

END OF SUMMARY
