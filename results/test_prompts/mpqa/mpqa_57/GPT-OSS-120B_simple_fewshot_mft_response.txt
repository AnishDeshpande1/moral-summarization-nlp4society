SUMMARY:
Recent European climate policy moves underscore the authority of the EU in upholding international environmental commitments, while the United States is portrayed as subverting them. EU environment ministers formally agreed on 4 March to ratify the Kyoto Protocol before 1 June, positioning Europe as a leader ready to enforce the treaty ahead of the Johannesburg summit. In contrast, President Bush’s 14 February climate program is depicted as a weak, sub‑standard response that falls far short of the obligations accepted by other industrialized nations. The article stresses that the US, the world’s largest emitter, must “assume its responsibilities” and stabilize emissions at 1990 levels, warning that its current policy merely masks a lack of genuine authority to act. EU officials argue that strong, authoritative climate action not only protects the environment but also drives economic growth and job creation, urging the US to re‑enter the Kyoto framework rather than continue its subversive retreat. The piece calls for the United States to align with the authoritative international effort, suggesting that only through such compliance can global climate protection succeed.

END OF SUMMARY.
