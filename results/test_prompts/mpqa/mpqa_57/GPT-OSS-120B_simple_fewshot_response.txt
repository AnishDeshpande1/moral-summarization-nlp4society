SUMMARY:
European ministers have secured a political agreement to ratify the Kyoto Protocol before June, positioning the EU to lead global climate action ahead of the August 2002 Johannesburg summit. In contrast, President Bush’s U.S. climate program is portrayed as woefully weak: it calls for only an 18 % improvement in energy efficiency by 2012, which, given projected economic growth, would still leave U.S. emissions 24.5 % above 1990 levels. The article argues that this falls far short of the commitments accepted by the EU, Japan and even the modest U.S. target under the Kyoto Protocol. Citing IPCC warnings and dire scientific forecasts, the author frames the U.S. inaction as a moral failing that endangers the planet and undermines global equity. The EU stresses that robust climate policy is not only environmentally necessary but also economically advantageous, creating jobs and fostering export markets for clean‑technology products. The piece calls on the United States to assume its responsibility, stabilize emissions at 1990 levels, and re‑enter the Kyoto process, presenting climate leadership as both a duty to humanity and an opportunity for economic modernization. END OF SUMMARY.
