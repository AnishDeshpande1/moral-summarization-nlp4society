**SUMMARY:**  
In March the EU environment ministers formally agreed to ratify the Kyoto Protocol before June, paving the way for its entry into force ahead of the August 2002 Johannesburg summit. By contrast, President Bush’s February U.S. climate program was condemned as “extremely weak” and “fundamentally flawed.” The U.S. pledged only an 18 % improvement in energy efficiency by 2012—far below the “ambitious” cuts required under Kyoto and insufficient to offset projected economic growth, leaving emissions 24.5 % above 1990 levels. European leaders warned that global climate protection cannot succeed unless the United States assumes its “responsibilities” and matches the “decisive” actions of Europe and Japan. Scientists’ warnings were “unheeded,” and without coordinated policy the world faces “dramatic” temperature rises, sea‑level rise, and more severe hurricanes. The EU argues that strong climate policy drives economic “positive” effects, creates jobs, and “modernizes” industry, and calls on the United States to re‑enter the Kyoto process. The door remains open, because the world’s biggest emitter “cannot” escape its duties.
