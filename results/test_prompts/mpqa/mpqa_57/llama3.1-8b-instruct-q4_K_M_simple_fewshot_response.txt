SUMMARY:

Recent weeks have seen significant developments in combating climate change, both in Europe and the United States. The European Union has ratified the Kyoto Protocol, a major step towards reducing greenhouse gas emissions. In contrast, President Bush's US national climate protection program falls short of expectations, aiming to reduce emissions by only 18% between 2002 and 2012, which is considered weak compared to other developed countries like Europe and Japan. Climate experts warn that without decisive action, the consequences of climate change will be severe, including rising temperatures, sea levels, and extreme weather events. The EU has called on the US to assume its responsibilities under the framework agreement on climate change, and for the US to stabilize greenhouse gas emissions at 1990 levels. Europe's dominant role in climate protection is driven by a commitment to reducing emissions, creating jobs, and modernizing industrial society. The return of the United States to the Kyoto process remains a crucial goal.

END OF SUMMARY.
