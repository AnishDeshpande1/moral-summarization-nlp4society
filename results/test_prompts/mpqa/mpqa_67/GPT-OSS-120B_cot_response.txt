**SUMMARY:**  
The Namibian government dismissed a U.S. human rights report that catalogued numerous **violations** by security forces, including **extrajudicial killings**, **torture**, **beatings**, **cruel**, **inhuman** and **degrading** treatment, **arbitrary arrests**, **deprivation** of life, **disappearances**, and **deaths in custody**. Information Permanent Secretary Shivute **mocked** the report, calling the United States the **worst** human‑rights violator because of its **detention** of 300 prisoners at Guantanamo Bay. He argued that similar **arbitrary** actions in the U.S. after 9/11 would be ignored. Shivute claimed the report was **unverified**, **unsubstantiated**, and full of **confusion**, citing factual errors about the ownership of *Namibia Today* and the affiliation of the NUNW. He said Namibia places **little value** on the report’s accusations. **END OF SUMMARY.**
