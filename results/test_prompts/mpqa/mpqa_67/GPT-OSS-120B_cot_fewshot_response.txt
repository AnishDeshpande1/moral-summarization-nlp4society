**SUMMARY:**
Namibia’s government dismissed a U.S. human‑rights report that documented numerous **violations** by security forces, including **extrajudicial killings**, **torture**, **disappearances**, **arbitrary** arrests, **deprivation** of life, and other **cruel**, **inhuman**, **degrading** **abuse**. The report accused the Special Field Force of unlawful **detention** and failure to bring detainees before a magistrate. Information Permanent Secretary M. Shivute **mocked** the report, calling the United States the **worst** human‑rights violator and pointing to Guantanamo Bay. He claimed the Namibian report was riddled with **unverified**, **unsubstantiated** **inaccuracies** and **confusion** over historical facts, asserting that the government placed **little value** on the findings. Shivute suggested that similar actions by the U.S. after 9/11 would not be labeled **abuse**, implying a double standard in the perception of **injustice** and **impunity**. **END OF SUMMARY.**
