Govt dismisses US rights report

The Namibian government has dismissed a US human rights report that lists several human rights violations in Namibia, including extra-judicial killings, torture, and arbitrary arrests by security forces. The report states that members of the Special Field Force committed numerous abuses during operations in the Kavango and Caprivi Regions. However, Information Permanent Secretary Mocks Shivute responded by accusing the US government of being the worst human rights violator in the world, citing the detention of 300 people at Guantanamo Bay without trial. Shivute also claimed that the report carried factual inaccuracies and was a "mere reproduction" of unverified reports.

The Namibian government's dismissal of the report raises concerns about its commitment to upholding human rights. While it is true that human rights abuses have occurred in many countries, including the US, this does not justify ignoring or downplaying such abuses in Namibia. The fact that Shivute chose to deflect attention from the report's findings by pointing out alleged flaws and comparing them to worse abuses committed elsewhere suggests a lack of accountability and transparency on the part of the government.
