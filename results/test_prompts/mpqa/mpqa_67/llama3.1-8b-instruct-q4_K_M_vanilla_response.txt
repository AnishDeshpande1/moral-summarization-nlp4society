SUMMARY:
The Namibian government has dismissed a US human rights report that lists several human rights violations in the country, including extra-judicial killings, torture, and arbitrary arrests by security forces. The report, released this week, cites cases of abuse committed by members of the Special Field Force (SFF) during operations in the Kavango and Caprivi Regions in 2001. Information Permanent Secretary Mocks Shivute responded that the US government is a worse human rights violator, citing the detention of 300 people at Guantanamo Bay without trial. Shivute also accused the report of factual inaccuracies and unsubstantiated claims. The Namibian government has expressed little value for the report, calling it a "mere reproduction" of unverified reports.

END OF SUMMARY.
