SUMMARY:  
The Namibian Government **trashed** a U.S. human‑rights report that documented numerous **violations** by security forces, including **killings**, **deaths** in custody, and the failure to **account** for the whereabouts of detainees. The report alleged that security members **tortured** and **beat** suspects, engaged in arbitrary **deprivation** of life, **disappearance** of persons, and other forms of **torture** and degrading **treatment**. It cited illegal **arrest**s, **denial** of a fair hearing, and widespread **abuses** such as not **informing** arrestees of the reason for their **arrest** and not bringing them before a magistrate within 48 hours. Many detainees were **arrested** on dubious links to UNITA or the Caprivi Liberation Army and later **disappeared**. Information Permanent Secretary Shivute retorted that the United States committed similar or worse **actions** after 9/11, citing Guantanamo Bay detentions, and questioned whether those would be labeled **abuses**. He dismissed the U.S. report as factually inaccurate, accusing it of **confusion** over historical data and of mischaracterising local media.  

END OF SUMMARY.
