SUMMARY: The Namibian government dismissed a U.S. human‑rights report that accused its security forces of extra‑judicial killings, torture, arbitrary arrests and disappearances in 2001, particularly in the Kavango and Caprivi regions. The report highlighted violations such as deaths in custody, failure to inform detainees of charges, and lack of timely magistrate hearings, attributing most abuses to the Special Field Force. Information Permanent Secretary Shalako Shivute rebuked the report as factually inaccurate, claiming it mischaracterized the state‑run newspaper Namibia Today and the affiliation of the National Union of Namibian Workers. He countered by labeling the United States as the world’s worst human‑rights violator, citing Guantanamo Bay detentions and post‑9/11 “special camps.” Shivute argued Namibia places little value on the U.S. report, calling it an unverified reproduction of unsubstantiated claims. END OF SUMMARY.
