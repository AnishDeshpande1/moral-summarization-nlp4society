SUMMARY:
The Korean government has responded to US President George W. Bush's new policy on controlling greenhouse gas emissions, which aims to reduce emissions relative to economic activities. In a statement, Foreign Minister Choi Sung-hong expressed appreciation for the US interest in climate change and acknowledged that the new initiative takes into consideration the concerns of developing countries. However, the Korean government also noted that greater elaboration is needed, including concrete methods to establish greenhouse gas intensity levels. This view is in line with the preparations being made by over 180 countries, including the US, for the ratification of the Kyoto Protocol, which requires signatories to reduce emissions.

The Seoul government will actively participate in international consultations to build understanding on the US initiative and ensure that it aligns with existing frameworks such as the Kyoto Protocol. This diplomatic effort is crucial given the global nature of climate change and the need for collective action to address this pressing issue.

END OF SUMMARY.
