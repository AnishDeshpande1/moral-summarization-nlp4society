SUMMARY:
South Korea’s government welcomed President George W. Bush’s new, flexible approach to cutting greenhouse‑gas emissions, praising U.S. interest in tackling what it called “one of the greatest challenges humankind faces.” Foreign Minister Choi Sung‑hong noted that the U.S. plan, which links reductions to economic activity and considers developing‑country concerns, is a step forward but still lacks concrete details on how emission‑intensity targets will be set. Seoul called for deeper international discussion and cooperation to flesh out the initiative, stressing the need for broad consultation as the world moves toward ratifying the Kyoto Protocol. While the United States has long resisted the Kyoto treaty, the Korean statement frames the new policy as a morally responsible, collaborative effort that could help bridge gaps between developed and developing nations in the global fight against climate change. The government pledged active participation in forthcoming talks to ensure the proposal is refined and aligned with the shared ethical duty to protect the planet.

END OF SUMMARY.
