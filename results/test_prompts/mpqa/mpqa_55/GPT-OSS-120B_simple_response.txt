SUMMARY:  
The South Korean government welcomed President George W. Bush’s new, “flexible and gradual” climate‑change initiative, praising U.S. interest in a problem the statement calls “one of the greatest challenges humankind faces in the 21st century.”  Foreign Minister Choi Sung‑hong noted that the plan, which ties emission cuts to economic activity and claims to consider developing‑country concerns, still lacks concrete details—especially on how greenhouse‑gas intensity levels will be measured.  Seoul therefore urges further international consultation and promises active participation, emphasizing the moral duty of all nations to build understanding and cooperate on climate action.  While the United States has withdrawn from the Kyoto Protocol, over 180 countries remain committed to its ratification, and other states such as Japan and Germany have responded to the U.S. shift in tone.  The Korean statement frames climate policy as a collective ethical responsibility that requires transparent, detailed, and globally coordinated efforts.  

END OF SUMMARY.
