SUMMARY: The South Korean government welcomed the United States’ new, flexible approach to greenhouse‑gas reduction announced by President George W. Bush, noting its consideration of developing‑country concerns. Foreign Minister Choi Sung‑hong said Korea appreciates U.S. interest in climate change and will engage in further international discussions, but called for more detail on how emission‑intensity targets will be set. With the Kyoto Protocol’s implementation rules recently adopted in Marrakesh and over 180 nations preparing for ratification, Seoul stresses the need for broader consultations to integrate the U.S. initiative into the global climate framework. While the Bush administration had previously rejected the Kyoto Protocol, the new policy—aimed at cutting emissions relative to GDP—has drawn varied reactions from other countries such as Japan and Germany. END OF SUMMARY.
