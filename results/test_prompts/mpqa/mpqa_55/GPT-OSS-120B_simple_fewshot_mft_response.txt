SUMMARY:
South Korea’s foreign ministry welcomed President George W. Bush’s new climate‑change initiative, praising the United States for taking “continued interest” in a problem that threatens humanity.  The statement framed the U.S. policy as a responsible, flexible step that respects the needs of developing nations, thereby upholding the authority of a major power while urging it not to subvert global cooperation.  At the same time, Seoul stressed that the proposal must be fleshed out with concrete measures to limit greenhouse‑gas intensity, warning that without clear safeguards the plan could harm vulnerable peoples and the planet.  By calling for broader international consultations and pledging active participation, Korea positioned itself as a supportive yet vigilant partner, seeking to balance respect for U.S. leadership with the moral duty to protect the climate and prevent harm to future generations.  
END OF SUMMARY.
