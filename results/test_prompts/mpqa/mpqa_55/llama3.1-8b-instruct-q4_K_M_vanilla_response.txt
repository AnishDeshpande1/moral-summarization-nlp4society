SUMMARY:

The Korean government has released a statement regarding US President George W. Bush's new policy on controlling greenhouse gas emissions. The statement, issued by Foreign Minister Choi Sung-hong, acknowledges the US interest in climate change and its efforts to address the issue. However, it notes that the new initiative requires greater elaboration, including concrete methods for reducing emissions, in order to be discussed internationally.

The Korean government believes that consultations among the international community are necessary to build understanding on the US initiative. The country will actively participate in this process. Over 180 countries have prepared to ratify the Kyoto Protocol, which aims to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. The US administration had previously rejected the Protocol but has recently announced a new policy focusing on reducing emissions relative to GDP.

The statement also notes that the Korean government appreciates the US efforts and acknowledges its flexibility in addressing climate change concerns. However, more details are needed for international discussions.

END OF SUMMARY.
