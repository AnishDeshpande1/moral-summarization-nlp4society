SUMMARY:  
The South Korean government praised the United States’ renewed **approach** to tackling climate **challenges**, noting that President George W. Bush’s flexible, gradual plan to cut greenhouse‑gas intensity shows sensitivity to developing‑country concerns. In a statement by Foreign Minister Choi Sung‑hong, Seoul welcomed U.S. interest in an international discussion that would complement the Kyoto Protocol, but warned that the initiative needs more concrete details—especially methods for measuring emissions intensity—before it can be fully debated in future fora. While the Korean side stresses the need for broader **consultations** to build understanding, it also recalls the U.S. **rejecting** the Kyoto Protocol earlier and the international criticism that followed, especially after the **attacks** of September 11, which had further strained global climate cooperation. Korea pledged active participation in shaping the dialogue, hoping the new U.S. policy will foster cooperation rather than division.  

END OF SUMMARY.
