SUMMARY:

US Secretary of State Colin Powell is attempting to temper President Bush's aggressive rhetoric towards countries in the "axis of evil," including Iran, Iraq, and North Korea. Despite Powell's claims that dialogue with these nations is possible, his administration's actions suggest otherwise. The hardline wing of the US administration, led by Vice President Cheney and Secretary Rumsfeld, appears to hold significant influence over policy decisions. This has raised concerns about the intentions behind Bush's threats, particularly given the close ties between the US administration and Israel.

The author argues that the current US administration is driven by a "Texan mentality" that prioritizes military might and war over dialogue and coexistence. This approach is seen as reckless and destabilizing, threatening international economic prosperity and global security. With President Bush facing an economic crisis, the author believes he may be seeking to trigger wars to revive the struggling arms industry.

END OF SUMMARY.
