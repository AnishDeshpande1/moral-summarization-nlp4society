SUMMARY:  
US Secretary of State Colin Powell, portrayed as one of the few enlightened officials in President Bush’s administration, tried to “patch up” Bush’s reckless threats against the “axis of evil” – Iran, Iraq and North Korea. Powell told the Senate Foreign Relations Committee that labeling these countries as an axis does not mean the US will **crush** them, and that Washington is **prepared to initiate a dialogue** and **consult** friends and allies on how to **deal** with such regimes. However, the author argues this rhetoric conflicts with the hard‑line wing of the administration (Vice President Cheney, Defense Secretary Rumsfeld, and National Security Adviser Rice), which has already decided to **mislead** and **target** these states, refusing dialogue until Iraq accepts UN inspectors. The piece claims the US, driven by the arms and Zionist lobbies, will **trigger** more **wars**, **engulf** the world in conflict, and **progress** its arms industry at the expense of global stability, especially as President Bush faces an economic crisis.  

END OF SUMMARY.
