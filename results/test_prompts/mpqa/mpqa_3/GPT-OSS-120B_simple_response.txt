SUMMARY: The article portrays Secretary of State Colin Powell as a rare voice of reason trying to soften President Bush’s incendiary “axis of evil” rhetoric, which threatens Iran, Iraq and North Korea. While Powell publicly insists the label does not signal invasion and calls for dialogue, the piece argues that real power lies with the hard‑line “war‑driven” faction—Vice President Cheney, Defense Secretary Rumsfeld and National Security Adviser Rice—who dominate Bush and push a militaristic agenda. The author suggests Powell’s denials are dubious, noting he allegedly failed to review Bush’s speech in advance. The narrative frames the administration’s stance as contradictory: it claims openness to negotiation yet refuses dialogue with Iraq until UN inspectors return and shuns consultation with allies, especially in the Arab world. It accuses the U.S. government of being driven by the arms and Zionist lobbies, likening its “Texan cowboy” mentality to reckless aggression that risks global stability for profit. The moral judgment is that Bush’s war‑focused policies, motivated by economic woes and a desire to boost the arms industry, threaten worldwide security and prosperity. END OF SUMMARY.
