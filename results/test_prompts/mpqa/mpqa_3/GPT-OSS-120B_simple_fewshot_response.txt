SUMMARY:
The article condemns the Bush administration’s “axis of evil” rhetoric, portraying it as reckless warmongering driven by a hard‑line coalition of Vice President Cheney, Defense Secretary Rumsfeld and National Security Adviser Rice. It casts Secretary of State Colin Powell as one of the few enlightened officials attempting to temper Bush’s threats, insisting the label does not signal an intent to invade and that dialogue is possible. However, the piece argues Powell’s statements are hollow, noting contradictions such as the refusal to negotiate with Iraq until UN inspectors return and the administration’s outright refusal to consult allies. The author blames a powerful alliance of the arms lobby and Zionist lobby, likening U.S. leadership to a “Texan cowboy” mentality that prioritizes war and arms sales over dialogue, stability, and global prosperity. It warns that Bush, facing recession and fearing electoral defeat, may provoke wars to boost the arms industry, a move that could trigger a worldwide catastrophe.  
END OF SUMMARY.
