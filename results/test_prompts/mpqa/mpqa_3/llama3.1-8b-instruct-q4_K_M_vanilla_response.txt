SUMMARY:

US Secretary of State Colin Powell is trying to clarify President Bush's recent statement on the "axis of evil" (Iran, Iraq, and North Korea), which some see as reckless threats. Powell stated that the US is prepared to initiate a dialogue with these countries, but this seems contradictory to previous statements that there would be no dialogue until they accept UN arms inspectors back in. The article suggests that the hard-line wing of the administration, led by Vice President Cheney and Secretary Rumsfeld, has significant influence over Bush's decisions. It also implies that Powell may not have been fully briefed on the speech before it was given, which raises questions about his role in the administration. The author believes that the US is driven by a alliance between the arms lobby and the Zionist lobby, leading to a "Texan mentality" that prioritizes war over dialogue and stability.

END OF SUMMARY.
