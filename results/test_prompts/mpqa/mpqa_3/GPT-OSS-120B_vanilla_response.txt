SUMMARY: The article portrays U.S. Secretary of State Colin Powell as a moderating voice trying to soften President Bush’s “axis of evil” rhetoric, which targeted Iran, Iraq, and North Korea. Powell told the Senate Foreign Relations Committee that labeling these states does not signal an intent to invade and emphasized a willingness to engage in dialogue with allies. However, the piece argues that real power lies with the hard‑line faction—Vice President Dick Cheney, Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld, and National Security Adviser Condoleezza Rice—who dominate policy and favor military action over diplomacy. It suggests Powell was not briefed on Bush’s speech beforehand and questions his credibility. The article claims the administration will not consult allies, will punish any perceived threat to Israel, and is driven by the arms and Zionist lobbies, using wars to boost the defense industry amid domestic economic woes. It warns that this “Texan cowboy” mentality threatens global stability and could lead to widespread catastrophe. END OF SUMMARY.
