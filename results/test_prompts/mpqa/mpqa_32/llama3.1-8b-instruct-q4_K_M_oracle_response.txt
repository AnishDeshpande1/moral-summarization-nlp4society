SUMMARY:

Zimbabwe's leader Robert Mugabe won a fifth term in office, but the victory was marred by allegations of violence, intimidation, and manipulation of electoral laws. The election was described as rigged, with opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai calling it "the biggest electoral fraud I have ever witnessed." The result was widely disputed, with many questioning the legitimacy of Mugabe's win. The US President George Bush condemned the outcome, stating that Washington would not recognize Mugabe's government due to its flawed nature.

In contrast, Zimbabwe's neighbors and regional superpower South Africa found little to object to in the election. However, domestic observers and opposition critics attacked the government for waging a systematic campaign of violence against Tsvangirai's supporters. The people are seething with anger, but Tsvangirai ruled out open rebellion, calling instead for a constitutionally acceptable manner of protest.

The country's economic situation is dire, with unemployment near 60% and inflation running at 112% a year. Many Zimbabweans are going hungry, and the situation is creating regional stability concerns. South Africa is trying to stem the flow of economic migrants fleeing war and political turmoil in neighboring countries.

END OF SUMMARY.
