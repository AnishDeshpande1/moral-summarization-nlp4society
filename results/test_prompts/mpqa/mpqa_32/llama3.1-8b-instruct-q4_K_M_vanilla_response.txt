SUMMARY:

Zimbabwe's President Robert Mugabe won a fifth term in office, defeating opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai in an election marred by violence, intimidation, and electoral manipulation. The outcome was widely condemned by Western leaders, with US President George Bush stating that Washington would not recognize the result. Britain's Foreign Secretary Jack Straw called for Mugabe to be held accountable for a "systematic campaign of violence." In contrast, African nations, including South Africa and regional superpower Nigeria, accepted the results as legitimate.

The opposition MDC party claimed the election was rigged, with many voters in urban areas unable to cast their ballots due to police intimidation. The government has responded by putting the military on alert and setting up roadblocks around major cities. Zimbabwe's neighbours are more concerned about regional stability than democratic niceties, given the country's economic crisis and potential for refugee flows into South Africa.

END OF SUMMARY.
