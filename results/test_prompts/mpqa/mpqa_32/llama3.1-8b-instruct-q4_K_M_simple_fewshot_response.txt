Mugabe's victory was seen as a consolidation of power, with the opposition calling for protests but ruling out open rebellion. The election highlighted the split between black African nations and the West on the issue of democracy in Zimbabwe. Mugabe's win also raised concerns about regional stability, as South Africa sought to stem the flow of economic migrants fleeing Zimbabwe's economic crisis.
