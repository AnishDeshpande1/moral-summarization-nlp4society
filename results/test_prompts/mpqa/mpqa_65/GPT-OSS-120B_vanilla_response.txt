SUMMARY:
A U.S. C‑17 cargo plane departed from Kandahar, Afghanistan, transporting 20 heavily guarded Taliban and al‑Qaida detainees to Guantanamo Bay’s Camp X‑Ray, marking the first wave of hundreds to be held there. Over 40 military police guarded the shackled, hooded prisoners on the 20‑hour flight, which may have included sedation, though details were unclear. The Pentagon stated the detainees are not POWs but will receive Geneva Convention protections. Camp X‑Ray, initially built for about 100 inmates, is being expanded to house up to 2,000. Simultaneously, the U.S. is preparing to send more than 100 troops, mainly Special Operations, to the Philippines to train forces against the Abu Sayyaf militants, with potential for direct combat if requested. Amnesty International criticized the shackling and possible sedation as violations of humane treatment standards. Additional news: a Marine tanker crash in Pakistan killed seven marines; U.S. bombings continued against al‑Qaida sites in Afghanistan; and President Bush signed a $318 billion defense spending bill for FY 2002.  
END OF SUMMARY.
