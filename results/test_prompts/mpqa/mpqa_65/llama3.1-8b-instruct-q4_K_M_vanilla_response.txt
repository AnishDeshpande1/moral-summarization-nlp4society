SUMMARY:

The United States began its first airlift of Taliban and Al Qaeda prisoners from Afghanistan to Guantanamo Bay, Cuba, on January 10. A C-17 Globemaster cargo plane carrying 20 heavily guarded prisoners left Kandahar Airport and is expected to arrive at Guantanamo Bay as early as Friday morning. The Pentagon has confirmed that some prisoners may be sedated during the flight, but it's unclear if this has happened. The prisoners will be held in a makeshift detention center known as Camp X-Ray, where they will await interrogation and possible trial before military tribunals.

Meanwhile, the US is preparing to send over 100 soldiers to the Philippines to help train Filipino troops against Muslim militants from the Abu Sayyaf group. Secretary of Defense Donald Rumsfeld defended the use of strong measures to restrain prisoners during transport, citing violent uprisings in Afghanistan and Pakistan. The US has also increased its military presence in the region, with B-1 and B-52 bombers dropping precision-guided weapons on Al Qaeda training camps in eastern Afghanistan.

END OF SUMMARY.
