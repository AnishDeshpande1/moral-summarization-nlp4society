**SUMMARY:**  
A U.S. C‑17 Globemaster lifted off from Kandahar carrying 20 heavily guarded Taliban and Al Qaeda detainees, whose hands and feet were shackled and heads hooded, toward Guantanamo Bay’s Camp X‑Ray. The Pentagon said the prisoners would receive Geneva Convention protections, though Amnesty International warned that sedating, shackling, and hooding them for the 20‑hour flight could be “cruel, inhuman or degrading.” Military police guarded the flight, and a brief skirmish with unknown snipers occurred near the runway. At Guantanamo, detainees will be placed in concrete cages for intensive interrogation and possible trial before military tribunals. Simultaneously, the U.S. prepared to send over 100 soldiers to the Philippines to train troops against the Abu Said faction, a group linked to Al Qaeda and holding American hostages. President Bush signed a $318 billion defense spending bill, emphasizing the need for resources to maintain “high state of readiness” against hostile, violent threats.
