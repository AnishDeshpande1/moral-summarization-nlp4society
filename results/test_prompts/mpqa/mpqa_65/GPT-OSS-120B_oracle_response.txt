SUMMARY:  
A U.S. C‑17 Globemaster lifted off from Kandahar carrying 20 heavily guarded Taliban and al‑Qaeda detainees toward Guantanamo Bay, where they will be placed in the makeshift Camp X‑Ray for interrogation and possible military tribunals. The prisoners were shackled, hooded and possibly sedated for the more‑than‑20‑hour flight, a practice Amnesty International warned could be “cruel, inhuman or degrading.” Shortly after take‑off, marines **came** under small‑arms fire near the runway, though no one was injured. The Pentagon stressed that, while the detainees are not classified as prisoners of war, they will receive Geneva‑convention protections. In related operations, the U.S. is preparing to **support** and possibly **clear** the Abu Said group in the Philippines, where militants are holding two American **hostages** for **ransom**. Meanwhile, American bombers continued strikes on al‑Qaeda camps in eastern Afghanistan. The administration **owes** its troops the resources to **maintain** **readiness** against enemies who rely on **deception**. Earlier this week, a Marine Corps tanker **crashed** in Pakistan, **killing** seven marines in the **accident**.  

END OF SUMMARY.
