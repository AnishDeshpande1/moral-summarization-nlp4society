SUMMARY:

The US has begun its first airlift of Taliban and Al Qaeda prisoners from Afghanistan to Guantanamo Bay, Cuba. A C-17 cargo plane carrying 20 heavily guarded prisoners took off from Kandahar Airport, with more than 40 military police officers on board. The Pentagon has defended the use of strong measures, citing violent uprisings by prisoners in other locations. Amnesty International has criticized the treatment of prisoners, saying it violates international standards.

Meanwhile, American forces are also involved in training Filipino troops to fight Muslim militants in the Philippines, and may become involved in direct military action if requested. The US is providing aid packages and equipment to the Philippine military. In Afghanistan, B-1 and B-52 bombers have dropped precision-guided weapons on an Al Qaeda training camp.

The number of prisoners held by American forces continues to rise, with 371 currently detained. Camp X-Ray in Guantanamo Bay has been expanded to accommodate up to 2,000 prisoners.

END OF SUMMARY.
