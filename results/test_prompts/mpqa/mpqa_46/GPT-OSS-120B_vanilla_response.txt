SUMMARY: Seyed Qamar Abbas, a senior PPP leader and former NWFP minister, dismissed the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights report that named Iran, Pakistan and others as violators. He argued the United States lacks moral authority, citing its own record of civilian deaths in wars, especially in Muslim countries, and accusing it of double standards by supporting Israel while condemning others. Abbas claimed that nations independent of U.S. influence are labeled as rights violators, whereas those aligned with U.S. policy receive favor. He suggested America’s anti‑terrorism and WMD rhetoric masks strategic and economic interests. Abbas concluded that global public opinion is increasingly skeptical of U.S. claims and policies. END OF SUMMARY.
