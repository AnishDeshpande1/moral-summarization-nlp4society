SUMMARY:  
Seyed Qamar Abbas, central leader of Pakistan’s People Party and former NWFP minister, dismissed the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights report as hypocritical, arguing that America itself perpetrates grave abuses. He accused the U.S. of killing thousands of civilians worldwide—especially in Muslim lands—under the pretext of a “war on terror,” citing the plight of Afghans and mistreated prisoners of war. Abbas condemned the double standard of a nation that condemns rights violations while backing Israel’s actions against Palestinians and Lebanese, and he claimed that any country that refuses U.S. dominance is branded a violator, whereas compliant states receive favors. He portrayed U.S. anti‑terrorism and anti‑WMD rhetoric as cover for strategic and economic self‑interest. Nonetheless, Abbas expressed hope that global citizens are awakening to America’s unjust and discriminatory policies and will no longer accept its narrative.  

END OF SUMMARY.
