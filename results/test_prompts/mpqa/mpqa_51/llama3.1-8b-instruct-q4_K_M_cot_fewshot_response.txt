US President George W. Bush presented a new plan to slow down global warming, which places no obligation on industrialists and relies on voluntary measures and tax incentives. Environmentalists immediately attacked this proposal, calling it a "Valentine's Day gift for the polluters." Bush's approach is considered unjust and counterproductive by environmentalists, who believe that binding measures are necessary to tackle global warming.
