SUMMARY:

US President George W. Bush presented an alternative plan to the Kyoto Protocol, aiming to slow down global warming. The plan is based on voluntary measures and tax incentives, placing no obligation on industrialists. Environmentalists immediately attacked the proposal, calling it "a Valentine's Day gift for polluters." Carl Pope of the Sierra Club described it as sticking to "polluting policies" instead of taking sensible steps to protect health.

The Bush Administration claims that the plan will reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 18% over 10 years and is comparable to the Kyoto Protocol. However, critics argue that this objective is unachievable through voluntary measures alone. The plan also calls on developing countries like India and China to join in reducing their emissions, but environmentalists see it as a way for the US to avoid responsibility.

The White House describes the plan as "the most audacious program in American history" aiming to reduce power station gas emissions, but critics disagree, calling it ineffective. The proposal is seen as a move away from the binding measures of the Kyoto Protocol, which was recently approved by over 170 countries.

END OF SUMMARY.
