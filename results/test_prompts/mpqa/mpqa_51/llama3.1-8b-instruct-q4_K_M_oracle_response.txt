SUMMARY:

US President George W. Bush presented an alternative plan to the Kyoto Protocol, which aims to slow down global warming through voluntary measures and tax incentives. However, environmentalists have condemned this approach as a "Valentine's Day gift for the polluters," arguing that it moves away from binding measures and imposes no obligations on industrialists. The plan advocates a voluntarist approach, linking greenhouse gas reduction objectives to US economic growth, which is seen as unsustainable by critics. Carl Pope of the Sierra Club stated that the Bush Administration is sticking to polluting policies rather than taking sensible steps to protect public health. Environmentalists have attacked this proposal for having little effect on emissions and moving in the opposite direction from the international community's efforts with the Kyoto Protocol.

The plan proposes tax incentives of $4.6 billion to encourage industry and consumers to adopt renewable sources of energy, but critics argue that it is irresponsible not to ask polluters to reduce their greenhouse gas emissions. President Bush called for India and China to recognize the need for shared obligations in fighting global warming, but environmentalists see this as an attempt to interfere with countries opting for the Kyoto Protocol.

END OF SUMMARY.
