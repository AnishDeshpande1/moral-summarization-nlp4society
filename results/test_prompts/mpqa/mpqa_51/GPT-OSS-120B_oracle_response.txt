SUMMARY:  
On 14 February President George W. Bush unveiled an alternative to the Kyoto Protocol that **moves** away from any binding **imposing** of limits on industry. The plan relies on voluntary measures and $4.6 billion in tax incentives, claiming it will **lower** the intensity of greenhouse‑gas emissions by 18 % in ten years while **sustaining** economic **growth**. The White House hails the proposal as the most **audacious** U.S. **program** to **stabilize** power‑station emissions and to **save** billions of dollars compared with mandatory rules. Critics label it a **gift** to polluters, arguing the U.S. is **sticking** to industry wishes instead of **taking** sensible steps to **protect** health. Environmental leaders warn the voluntary approach will have little **effect** and that it **condemns** developing nations by **insisting** they bear the burden while the U.S. seeks to **absolve** itself. Bush urges India and China to **ask** for shared responsibility, saying it would be **irresponsible** not to involve them, yet he also says the United States will not **interfere** with countries that choose to ratify Kyoto. The plan **recognizes** the right to development but is seen as **moving** opposite to global climate efforts.  
END OF SUMMARY.
