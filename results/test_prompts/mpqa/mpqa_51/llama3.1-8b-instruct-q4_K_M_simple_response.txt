SUMMARY:

US President George W. Bush presented an alternative plan to the Kyoto Protocol, which aims to slow down global warming but lacks binding measures. Instead, it relies on voluntary actions from industrialists and tax incentives worth $4.6 billion. Environmentalists have criticized this approach as a "Valentine's Day gift for polluters" that will not effectively reduce greenhouse gas emissions. The plan proposes to curb the growth of harmful gas emissions and potentially lower them by 18% over 10 years, but critics argue it is too weak compared to the Kyoto Protocol.

President Bush argued that his plan would give developing countries a "yardstick for progress on climate changes" while allowing them to pursue economic development. He also called on India and China to reduce their greenhouse gas emissions, which are already significant contributors to global warming. However, environmentalists see this as an attempt to shift responsibility away from the US and towards other nations.

END OF SUMMARY.
