SUMMARY:
President George W. Bush unveiled a voluntary, tax‑incentive alternative to the Kyoto Protocol, claiming it will cut U.S. greenhouse‑gas intensity by 18 % while preserving economic growth. The plan abandons binding international rules, relies on the goodwill of polluters, and seeks to shift responsibility onto developing nations such as China and India. Environmental groups denounce it as a subversion of legitimate global authority, arguing it sacrifices care for the planet and public health in favor of industry interests. They label the proposal a “gift for polluters,” accusing the administration of cheating fairness by avoiding equitable obligations and allowing the United States to escape its share of responsibility while demanding cuts from poorer countries. Critics warn the voluntary approach will do little to curb emissions and undermines the collective, fair effort needed to protect the climate and vulnerable populations.

END OF SUMMARY.
