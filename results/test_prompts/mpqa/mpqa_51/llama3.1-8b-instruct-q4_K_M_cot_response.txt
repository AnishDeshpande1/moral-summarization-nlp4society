**SUMMARY:**

US President George W. Bush unveiled a plan to combat global warming, but environmentalists are outraged by its voluntary measures and lack of binding procedures. The plan, presented on Valentine's Day, replaces the Kyoto Protocol, which was approved by over 170 countries in Marrakech. Bush's proposal relies on tax incentives and good will from polluters, who account for 36% of industrialized countries' greenhouse gas emissions. Environmentalists call it a "Valentine's Day gift for the polluters" and argue that it is an irresponsible approach to reducing greenhouse gas emissions. In contrast, the Kyoto Protocol was criticized by Bush as imposing unrealistic constraints on US industry. However, environmentalists maintain that the new plan will have little effect on emissions and that the US is moving in the opposite direction from the rest of the international community.
