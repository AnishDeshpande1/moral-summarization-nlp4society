**SUMMARY:**
On Valentine’s Day President George W. Bush unveiled a voluntary, tax‑incentive alternative to the Kyoto Protocol, branding it the “most audacious” U.S. program to cut power‑station emissions. The plan imposes no binding obligations on industry, relying on the goodwill of polluters and promising economic growth alongside a modest 18 % reduction in greenhouse‑gas intensity. Bush framed the approach as fair to developing nations, arguing that demanding Kyoto‑style cuts from China and India would be unjust, counterproductive and irresponsible, while the U.S. would not interfere with countries that ratify the treaty. Environmentalists reacted with outrage, calling the proposal a “Valentine’s Day gift for the polluters,” accusing the administration of siding with harmful industry interests, ignoring health protection, and delivering a toxic, false promise that would have little effect on emissions. Critics warned that the voluntary scheme subverts authority needed for real climate action and cheats future generations out of needed care and fairness. END OF SUMMARY.
