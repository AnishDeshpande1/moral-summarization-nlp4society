SUMMARY:
China's Foreign Ministry spokesman Kong Quan expressed disapproval of the term "axis of evil" used by the US leader to describe North Korea, Iran, and Iraq. According to Kong, such language is unacceptable in international relations as it can lead to serious consequences if followed logically. China advocates for anti-terrorism campaigns based on solid evidence, rather than arbitrary expansion. Kong emphasized that state-to-state relations should be handled according to the UN Charter, the Five Principles of Peaceful Co-existence, and the principle of equal treatment for all countries. This approach is essential for maintaining long-term peace and stability worldwide.

Kong's statement highlights China's concern about the impact of divisive language on international relations. By promoting a more nuanced understanding of terrorism and emphasizing cooperation over confrontation, China aims to foster a more stable global environment. The Chinese government's emphasis on equal treatment and respect among nations underscores its commitment to diplomacy and peaceful resolution of conflicts.

END OF SUMMARY.
