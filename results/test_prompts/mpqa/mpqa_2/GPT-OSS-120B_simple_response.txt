SUMMARY:  
Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesman Kong Quan publicly condemned the use of the phrase “axis of evil” to describe nations such as North Korea, Iran and Iraq. He argued that such labeling is morally irresponsible, noting that U.S. public opinion largely rejects the terminology and warning that adopting its logic would have serious consequences. Kong emphasized that anti‑terrorism actions must be grounded in solid evidence and should not be applied arbitrarily. He called for state‑to‑state relations to be guided by the United Nations Charter, the Five Principles of Peaceful Co‑existence, and the principle of equal treatment for all countries. According to Kong, adhering to these ethical standards is the only way to protect long‑term global peace and stability.  

END OF SUMMARY.
