SUMMARY:
China's Foreign Ministry spokesman Kong Quan has expressed disapproval towards the use of terms like "axis of evil" in international relations. Kong emphasized that anti-terrorism campaigns should be based on solid evidence and not arbitrarily expanded. He stressed that state-to-state relations should follow established principles, such as the United Nations Charter and the Five Principles of Peaceful Co-existence, where all countries are treated equally. By following these principles, it is possible to safeguard long-term peace and stability in the world and related regions.

Kong's comments come in response to a statement made by a U.S. leader describing North Korea, Iran, and Iraq as "axis of evil" nations. China has noted that public opinion on this issue varies widely, and that following such logic could have serious consequences.
