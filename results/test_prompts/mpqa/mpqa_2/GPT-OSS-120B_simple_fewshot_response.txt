SUMMARY:
China’s foreign ministry criticised the U.S. label “axis of evil” for North Korea, Iran and Iraq, saying such language is inappropriate in diplomatic discourse. Spokesman Kong Quan argued that the phrase provokes serious consequences and reflects a misuse of anti‑terrorism rhetoric that should be grounded in solid evidence rather than arbitrary labeling. He called for state‑to‑state interactions to adhere to the United Nations Charter, the Five Principles of Peaceful Co‑existence, and equal treatment of all nations, asserting that this approach best supports long‑term global peace and stability. END OF SUMMARY.
