SUMMARY:
China's Foreign Ministry spokesman, Kong Quan, stated that China disapproves of the use of terms like "axis of evil" in international relations. This comment was made in response to a statement by the US leader describing North Korea, Iran, and Iraq as such nations. Kong noted that Chinese officials have observed a divergence between public opinion and the wording used by the US government on this issue. He emphasized that anti-terrorism campaigns should be based on solid evidence and not expanded arbitrarily. According to Kong, state-to-state relations should follow UN Charter principles, the Five Principles of Peaceful Co-existence, and the principle of equal treatment for all countries. He stressed that these are essential for maintaining long-term peace and stability globally.

END OF SUMMARY.
