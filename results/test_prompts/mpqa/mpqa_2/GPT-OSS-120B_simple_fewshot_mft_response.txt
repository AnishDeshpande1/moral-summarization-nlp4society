SUMMARY:
China’s foreign ministry criticised the United States’ labeling of North Korea, Iran and Iraq as an “axis of evil,” calling the terminology a breach of proper diplomatic authority and a subversive act that undermines international order. Spokesman Kong Quan warned that such language, which lacks solid evidence, harms global stability and disrespects the equal‑rights principle enshrined in the UN Charter and the Five Principles of Peaceful Co‑existence. He argued that anti‑terrorism measures must be based on verifiable facts rather than arbitrary accusations, emphasizing fairness toward all nations and warning that the U.S. rhetoric could cause serious, harmful consequences for world peace. By urging states to treat each other on an equal footing, China frames the dispute as a matter of legitimate authority, the protection of peoples from harmful mischaracterisation, and the fair, just conduct of international relations. END OF SUMMARY.
