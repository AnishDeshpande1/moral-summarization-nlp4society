SUMMARY:

President George Bush's National Security Adviser, Condoleezza Rice, has defined the term "axis of evil" as countries that are repressive and gravely violating human rights, while also aggressively seeking weapons of mass destruction. However, this definition is problematic as it erred in excluding other nations that may not be pluralistic democracies but still have a relatively slow pace of acquiring such weapons. Furthermore, the mere possession or development of mass destruction weapons does not necessarily make a nation an evildoer; rather, it's the nature of the regime that matters. The US is the first and only nation to deploy nuclear weapons, yet its own democratic nature makes it more restrained in using them. If this is indeed the case, then Washington's concern should be with nations that are authoritarian or repressive, regardless of their pace of acquiring mass destruction weapons. Rice could have provided a clearer connection between regime type and weapon acquisition.

END OF SUMMARY.
