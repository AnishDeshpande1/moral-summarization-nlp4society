SUMMARY:
The article critiques Condoleezza Rice’s attempt to define the “axis of evil” that President Bush invoked for Iraq, Iran and North Korea. It argues that Rice’s criteria—repressive regimes that “aggressively” seek weapons of mass destruction—are inconsistently applied and morally questionable. By equating any non‑democratic state with human‑rights abuse, the definition would encompass dozens of nations, yet Rice limits the label to only three, ignoring many other authoritarian states that also pursue or possess such weapons at a slower pace. The piece further points out the hypocrisy of judging nations by their WMD programs while the United States, the original developer and sole user of nuclear weapons, is exempt. It suggests the real moral yardstick should be whether a regime is fully democratic, not merely its weapons ambitions, and calls for clearer, more precise standards to avoid double standards and unjust branding of countries as “evil.”  
END OF SUMMARY.
