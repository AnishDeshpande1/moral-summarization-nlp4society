SUMMARY:
Condoleezza Rice tried to justify President Bush’s “axis of evil” label by narrowing it to three states that are “repressive, grave human‑rights violators” and “aggressively seeking weapons of mass destruction.” The article argues that this authority‑driven definition is arbitrary and unfair: countless other nations lack pluralistic democracy and could be deemed oppressive, yet are omitted, revealing a double standard that cheats the principle of equal moral judgment. By focusing only on fast‑moving weapons programs, the criteria ignore slower‑developing states and overlook the fact that possessing such weapons is not itself evil—especially when the United States, the original nuclear power, is exempt. This selective subversion of authority undermines genuine care for human rights, because it treats the label as a political tool rather than a consistent safeguard against harm. The piece calls for clearer, more equitable standards that honestly link regime type with weapons ambition, warning that the current vague rubric betrays both fairness and concern for global safety.

END OF SUMMARY.
