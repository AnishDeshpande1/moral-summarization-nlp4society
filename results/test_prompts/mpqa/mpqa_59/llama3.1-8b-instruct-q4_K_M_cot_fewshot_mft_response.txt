SUMMARY:
President George Bush's National Security Adviser, Condoleezza Rice, has defined the "axis of evil" as countries that are repressive and gravely violating human rights, while aggressively seeking weapons of mass destruction. However, this criteria seems to err in its broad scope, as many countries could qualify under these grounds. The real issue may not be the acquisition of such weapons but rather the nature of the regime. Democratic nations, like the US, are more restrained and responsible when it comes to resorting to mass destruction weapons. Rice's definition appears to prioritize the connection between regime type and weapon development over other factors. This approach could lead to a flawed understanding of what constitutes an "evil" nation. The US seems particularly concerned about authoritarian regimes developing such capabilities, rather than the mere possession or development of weapons of mass destruction.
