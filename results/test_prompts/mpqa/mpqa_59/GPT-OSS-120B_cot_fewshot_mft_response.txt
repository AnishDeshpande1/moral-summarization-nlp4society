Condoleezza Rice tried to clarify President Bush’s “axis of evil” label for Iraq, Iran and North Korea, defining the trio as **repressive**, **grave** **human‑rights violators** that are **aggressively seeking** weapons of mass destruction. The article points out that many other nations could be deemed **oppressive** or **violating** human rights because they lack **pluralistic democracy**, suggesting Rice may have **erred** in drawing such a narrow line. It argues that **authoritarian** regimes are feared for their potential misuse of WMDs, while **democratic**, **restrained** states are seen as more **responsible**. The piece questions whether **evil** should be judged solely on **weapon acquisition** or on the **nature of the regime**, noting that the United States itself built and used nuclear arms. A clearer, **fairer** criterion linking **regime type** to **weapon pursuit** would reduce the moral ambiguity of the “axis of evil” designation.
