SUMMARY:  
Condoleezza Rice clarified the “axis of evil” label used by President George Bush, defining it as applying to Iraq, Iran and North Korea because they are repressive, violate human rights, and are aggressively pursuing weapons of mass destruction (WMD). The article critiques this definition, noting that many other nations also lack pluralistic democracy and could be deemed oppressive under a broad human‑rights standard. It also points out that the “aggressively seeking” qualifier excludes countries developing WMD more slowly, yet dozens of states are pursuing such weapons at varying paces. The piece argues that mere possession or development of WMD does not inherently make a state “evil,” citing the U.S. as the first nuclear power and the only one to use such weapons. Instead, the author suggests the true concern is the combination of authoritarian regimes with WMD ambitions, as democracies are perceived to be more restrained. Rice could have offered clearer criteria linking regime type to WMD acquisition to define the “axis of evil” more precisely.  

END OF SUMMARY.
