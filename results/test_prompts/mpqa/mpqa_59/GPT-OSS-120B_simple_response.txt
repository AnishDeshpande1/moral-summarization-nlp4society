SUMMARY:  
Condoleezza Rice tried to clarify Bush’s “axis of evil” label for Iraq, Iran and North Korea, saying the three are repressive human‑rights violators that are **aggressively** pursuing weapons of mass destruction. The article argues this definition is morally inconsistent: many non‑democratic states also violate human rights, and the “aggressive” qualifier excludes nations that develop such weapons more slowly. Moreover, possessing WMDs is not itself a moral failing—​the United States itself created and used nuclear bombs—​so the true moral judgment seems to hinge on a regime’s democratic nature. The piece suggests that Washington’s fear is less about weapons and more about authoritarian regimes wielding them, assuming democracies are more restrained. Consequently, Rice could have offered clearer moral criteria linking regime type to WMD ambition, rather than the vague “axis of evil” label.  

END OF SUMMARY.
