**SUMMARY:**  
Condoleezza Rice tried to clarify President Bush’s “axis of evil” label for Iraq, Iran and North Korea, defining the group by three criteria: they are **repressive**, **grave** human‑rights **violators**, and **aggressively** seeking weapons of mass destruction. By that logic, many other nations could be deemed **oppressive**, since any state lacking a fully **democratic** system is portrayed as violating human rights. Rice’s wording excludes countries that are **slow** in developing such arsenals, yet the article argues that **fear** of **authoritarian** regimes possessing nuclear, biological or chemical weapons fuels the U.S. stance, while **democratic** states are presumed more **restrained** and **responsible**. The piece suggests the criteria are vague and that labeling a nation **evil** merely for possessing or pursuing mass‑destruction weapons—especially when the United States itself pioneered nuclear arms—may be inconsistent. A clearer link between regime type and weapons acquisition would improve the definition of the “axis of evil.”
