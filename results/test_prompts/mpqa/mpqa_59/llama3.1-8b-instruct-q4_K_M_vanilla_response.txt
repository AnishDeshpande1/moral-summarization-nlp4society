SUMMARY:

Condoleezza Rice, President George Bush's National Security Adviser, has clarified the criteria for the "axis of evil" term used to describe Iraq, Iran, and North Korea. According to Rice, these countries are repressive human rights violators aggressively seeking weapons of mass destruction. However, critics argue that this definition is too broad and subjective, as many countries could be considered repressive due to their lack of pluralistic democracy.

Furthermore, the criteria for acquiring weapons of mass destruction seems arbitrary, as some countries may be developing such arsenal at a slower pace than others. The US itself developed nuclear weapons first and deployed them uniquely, raising questions about its own moral standing in this context. A more precise definition of the "axis of evil" could have been established by linking the nature of the regime to the acquisition of weapons of mass destruction.

The article suggests that Washington's primary concern is not just the possession of such weapons, but whether they are held by authoritarian or repressive regimes. This raises questions about the US' own national security and its motivations for labeling certain countries as "evil".
