SUMMARY:  
Condoleezza Rice tried to elaborate the “axis of evil” label by tying Iraq, Iran and North Korea together as repressive regimes that are violators of human rights and are aggressively seeking weapons of mass destruction. By her own basis, however, the criteria are shaky: countless other nations could be called oppressive for merely lacking a pluralistic democracy, and many states are only slowly acquiring such arsenals, so they escape the evildoer tag. The author points out that labeling a country an evildoer solely because it possesses or develops WMD ignores the fact that the United States itself was the first to create and even use nuclear weapons. The real moral question, the piece suggests, is whether the regime is democratic and therefore more restrained and responsible, or authoritarian and thus threatening. Rice could have been more precise, elaborating a clearer link between a regime’s democratic nature and its pursuit of mass‑destruction weapons, rather than relying on a vague “repressive” and “aggressive” basis.  

END OF SUMMARY.
