SUMMARY:
US National Security Adviser Condoleezza Rice clarified the criteria for the "axis of evil" label, which President George Bush applied to Iraq, Iran, and North Korea in his State of the Union address. According to Rice, these countries are repressive and aggressively seeking weapons of mass destruction. However, critics argue that this definition is too broad, as many other countries also violate human rights and seek such weapons at a slower pace. The author suggests that a more nuanced approach would consider not only the acquisition of WMDs but also the nature of the regime, particularly whether it is democratic. The US itself developed nuclear weapons first and deployed them exclusively, yet its own national security is only threatened when authoritarian regimes acquire such arms.

The article raises questions about the accuracy of Rice's criteria and suggests that a more precise definition would be necessary to avoid labeling some countries as "evil" simply because they possess WMDs.
