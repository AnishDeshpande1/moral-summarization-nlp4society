SUMMARY:

NASA is facing criticism over its soaring space-station costs, which have ballooned from $17.4 billion to over $30 billion. Administrator Daniel Goldin resigned after 10 years in office, and President Bush nominated Sean O'Keefe, a financial expert with no background in space exploration, to replace him. While some see O'Keefe's appointment as a necessary step to restore credibility to the agency, others worry that his focus on cost-cutting will undermine the agency's long-term vision. The International Space Station project has been particularly contentious, with estimates suggesting that it may not be completed within budget or meet international agreements.

O'Keefe's nomination comes at a time when NASA is struggling to balance its ambitions with fiscal realities. As Patricia Dasch of the National Space Society noted, "NASA has to clean up its act before we can talk about the neat stuff we'd like to see NASA do." The agency's credibility will be closely watched as O'Keefe takes the helm and attempts to steer the program back on track.

END OF SUMMARY.
