SUMMARY:

NASA administrator Daniel Goldin resigned after 10 years in office, and President Bush nominated Sean O'Keefe, the deputy director of the White House's Office of Management and Budget, to replace him. O'Keefe is known for his financial expertise, having served as chief financial officer for the Pentagon and Navy secretary. Under Goldin, NASA implemented a "faster, better, cheaper" approach, but the agency's credibility has been questioned due to soaring costs for the International Space Station. The project's budget has ballooned from $17.4 billion to over $30 billion, and a task force report concluded that NASA's cost estimate is not credible.

O'Keefe's appointment is seen as a message from the administration to clean up the agency's act before pursuing new projects. Some analysts believe O'Keefe's financial expertise could bring stability to the agency, while others worry that his focus on budget-cutting may stifle innovation and progress in space exploration.

END OF SUMMARY.
