SUMMARY:

NASA administrator Daniel Goldin resigned after 10 years in office, and President Bush nominated Sean O'Keefe to replace him. O'Keefe, a former Pentagon official and business-school professor, brings a focus on cost-cutting to the agency. His appointment has been met with mixed reactions, with some analysts seeing it as an opportunity for NASA to regain credibility and others expressing concern that it may indicate a shift away from visionary goals. The space station project, which has seen significant cost overruns, is a major challenge for O'Keefe. He will need to navigate complex international agreements and diplomatic relationships while addressing the agency's financial woes. Some compare his appointment to that of James Webb, who oversaw NASA during its Apollo-program heyday. However, others see it as a signal that the administration wants NASA to focus on "cleaning up its act" before pursuing more ambitious goals.
