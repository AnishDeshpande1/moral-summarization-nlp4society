SUMMARY:  
NASA administrator Daniel Goldin resigned after a decade, and President Bush nominated Sean O’Keefe—an experienced budget and finance official—to succeed him. Unlike Goldin, a former aerospace industry insider who championed the “faster, better, cheaper” mantra and expanded unmanned missions, O’Keefe is known as a “bean counter” with a background as Pentagon CFO and White House OMB deputy director. His primary challenge will be addressing the ballooning costs of the International Space Station, whose budget has risen from $17.4 billion to over $30 billion, and managing pressure from international partners who oppose scaling back the station. Analysts see O’Keefe’s fiscal expertise as potentially crucial for restoring NASA’s credibility and financial discipline, though the agency’s ambitious vision may be limited to cost‑cutting and station reform. The appointment signals the administration’s demand that NASA first “clean up its act” before pursuing new exploratory projects.  

END OF SUMMARY.
