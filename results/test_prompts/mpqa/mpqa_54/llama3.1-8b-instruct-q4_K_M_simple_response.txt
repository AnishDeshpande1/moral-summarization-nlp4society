SUMMARY:

Under the leadership of President Bush, the United States has adopted a policy of pragmatism and unilateralism in its foreign affairs. This approach prioritizes US interests over international cooperation and institutions. Despite opposition from the international community and even some US allies, the Bush administration has pushed forward with the development of the National Missile Defense System (NMD), which is seen as a means to maintain US dominance and security.

The article argues that this policy is driven by a desire for US exceptionalism and a disregard for international norms. The treatment of China in the plane collision incident and the handling of international treaties such as the Kyoto Protocol and the Comprehensive Test Ban Treaty are cited as examples of this approach. The author concludes that the Bush administration's actions undermine international cooperation and institutions, and prioritize US interests over global security.

END OF SUMMARY.
