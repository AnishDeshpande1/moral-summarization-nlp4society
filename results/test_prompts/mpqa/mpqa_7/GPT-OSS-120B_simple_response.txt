SUMMARY:  
A new poll by Alfredo Keller confirms the dramatic collapse of Hugo Chávez’s popular support, echoing earlier surveys. Only 33 % now approve of his rule, while 58 % disapprove, and more than half of respondents say the President must be removed to end the nation’s crisis. Of those, 49 % demand a constitutional, institutional transition and 5 % would accept force. Meanwhile, 43 % still prefer Chávez to finish his term, but the myth of a simple rich‑vs‑poor split has shattered: 78 % say many poor oppose him, while only 20 % view the elite as his enemies. A decisive 75 % back a referendum to rewrite the Constitution and rebalance power. Potential successors draw modest support—Chávez 25 %, Henrique Salas Romer 21 %, Julio Borges 16 %, Alfredo Peña 13 %, Francisco Arias Cárdenas 5 %.  

Commentators warn that Chávez now faces two classic symptoms of governmental decline—unresolved economic woes and eroding personal credibility—fueling pessimism and a growing extremist opposition (42 % vs. 18 % supporters). The moral imperative, as the poll suggests, is to restore democratic mechanisms before the crisis deepens.  

END OF SUMMARY.
