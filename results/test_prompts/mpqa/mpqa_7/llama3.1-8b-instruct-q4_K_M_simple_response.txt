SUMMARY:

A recent study by Alfredo Keller confirms the decline in President Hugo Chavez's approval rating, with over half of those polled believing it is necessary to remove him from office to solve the crisis. A significant portion of the population (54%) wants him out through institutional channels, while 5% advocate for forceful means. The study also reveals that Chavez's disapproval rating has surpassed his approval rating (58% vs. 33%), and his ability to address the country's problems is shrinking. Venezuelans' optimism levels are at a two-year low, with 80% believing unemployment can be solved, but four in ten blame Chavez for it. The study suggests that Chavez's popularity is declining due to unresolved major problems and a drop in his leader's image as capable.

The opposition still maintains a divided front, allowing Chavez to maintain a lead in voter preference (28%), but with a shrinking margin. The Consultores 21 polling firm notes that the number of extreme Chavez opponents has surpassed supporters, potentially leading to a confrontation for power.

END OF SUMMARY.
