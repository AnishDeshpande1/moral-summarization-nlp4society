SUMMARY:

A recent study by Alfredo Keller found that President Hugo Chavez' approval rating has dropped significantly, with only 33% of respondents approving of his presidency and 58% disapproving. The study also revealed that more than half of those polled believe it is necessary to remove Chavez from office to solve the crisis, with 49% supporting a peaceful transition and 5% advocating for forceful means.

The study's findings are consistent with other recent polls, which show a decline in Chavez' popularity and a drop in his lead over potential opponents. According to one poll, if elections were held tomorrow, Chavez would still win but his lead has collapsed from 40 to 24%.

Experts note that Chavez is facing several key challenges, including unresolved major problems, a drop in his leader's popularity, and the perception of him as an incapable leader. The study also suggests that the number of extreme Chavez opponents is larger than that of extreme Chavez supporters, which could lead to a confrontation for power.
