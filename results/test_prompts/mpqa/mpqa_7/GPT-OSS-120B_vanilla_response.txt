SUMMARY:  
A recent study by Alfredo Keller of 1,000 Venezuelans shows President Hugo Chávez’s popularity has sharply declined. Approval fell from 84% in April 1999 to 33% in November, while disapproval rose to 58%. More than half (54%) think Chávez should be removed, with 49% favoring constitutional means and 5% supporting force. Nonetheless, 43% still prefer he finish his term in 2007. The myth of a rich‑poor polarization has collapsed; only 20% see the rich opposing Chávez, while 78% say many poor oppose him. A strong majority (75%) backs a constitutional referendum to rebalance power. In hypothetical elections, Chávez leads with 25% support, followed by Henrique Salas Romer (21%) and Julio Borges (16%). Complementary polls cited by El Universal confirm rising dissatisfaction over the economy, insecurity and unemployment, with Chávez’s lead shrinking from 40 to 24 points and extreme opposition now outnumbering extreme support.  

END OF SUMMARY.
