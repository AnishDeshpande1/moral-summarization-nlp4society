SUMMARY:
A new poll by Alfredo Keller confirms the sharp decline of President Hugo Chávez’s popularity. Among 1,000 respondents across Venezuela’s major cities, more than half say Chávez must be removed to end the crisis—49 % favor a constitutional ouster, 5 % endorse force. Only 43 % want him to finish his term. Approval has fallen from 84 % in April 1999 to just 33 % in November, while disapproval has risen to 58 %. The once‑perceived divide between rich and poor has vanished; 78 % of the poor now oppose him, and only 20 % think the wealthy are against him. A decisive 75 % back a referendum to rewrite the Constitution and rebalance power. Though Chávez still leads in voter preference (25 % vs. 21 % for Salas Romer and lower figures for other candidates), the opposition is fragmented, and extreme opponents now outnumber extreme supporters. The data signal a collapsing mandate and growing demand for systemic change.  
END OF SUMMARY.
