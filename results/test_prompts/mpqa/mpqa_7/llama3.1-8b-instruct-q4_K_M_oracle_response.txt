SUMMARY:

A recent study by Alfredo Keller confirms the sustained decline of President Hugo Chavez's approval rating, with over half of those polled believing it is necessary to remove him from office to solve the crisis. His disapproval rating has reached 58 percent, while his approval rating stands at 33 percent. The collapse of the myth that Chavez is polarizing the rich and poor is evident, as only 20 percent believe the wealthy oppose him, whereas 78 percent think many poor people do. A significant majority (75 percent) agrees with calling a referendum to redraft the Constitution and restore balance in power. Chavez's edge in voter preference continues to shrink, but his lead remains due to the divided opposition. Analysts point out that unresolved major problems, such as unemployment and insecurity, combined with his drop in popularity and perception as an incapable leader, are key factors in his decline.

END OF SUMMARY.
