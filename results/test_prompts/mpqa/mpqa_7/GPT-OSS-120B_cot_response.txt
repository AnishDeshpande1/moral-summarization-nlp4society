**SUMMARY:**  
A new study by Alfredo Keller confirms a dramatic **collapse** in President Hugo Chávez’s **approval**, with disapproval now overtaking support. More than half of the 1,000 respondents say it is **necessary** to remove Chávez to end the **crisis**, 49 % favor an **institutional** replacement and 5 % endorse **forceful** means. While 43 % prefer he remain until 2007, the **opposition** remains divided, yet **radical** Chávez supporters still keep him in first place. The poll shows a shift in **polarization**: only 20 % see the **rich** as against him, while 78 % think many **poor** oppose him. A strong **referendum** call for a new Constitution aims to restore **balance** of **power**, with 75 % in favor. Venezuelans cite **misfortune**, **unemployment**, **insecurity**, and a loss of **hope** as reasons for **blaming** Chávez, and 42 % are now **extreme** opponents, raising the risk of **confrontation**.
