SUMMARY:
A new poll by Alfredo Keller shows a sharp erosion of President Hugo Chávez’s authority, with a majority of Venezuelans now urging his removal. Over half of the 1,000 respondents say Chávez must be ousted to end the crisis; 49 % favor a constitutional, institutional replacement while 5 % endorse forceful action. Disapproval has overtaken approval, falling from 84 % support in 1999 to just 33 % this November, and a growing segment of the poor—78 %—now opposes him, undermining the early‑stage “rich vs. poor” narrative. Seventy‑five percent back a referendum to rewrite the constitution and rebalance power, signaling a broad subversion of the existing regime. Although Chávez still leads in hypothetical elections (25 % vs. 21 % for Henrique Salas Romer), the divided opposition and rising extremist opposition (42 %) threaten his grip on authority. The data portray a populace increasingly willing to challenge and potentially overturn the current leadership.

END OF SUMMARY.
