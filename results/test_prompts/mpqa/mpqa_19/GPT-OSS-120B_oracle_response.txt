SUMMARY:  
Tony Blair expressed **disagreement** with Australia’s decision to sign a US‑backed “climate action partnership,” warning that the move **contradicts** the spirit of the Kyoto Protocol. The Australian Federal Government **denied** that the agreement signals a **dumping** of Kyoto obligations, insisting it remains **committed** to the treaty while engaging with the United States and the European Union. **Critics** argue that Australia is **cosying up** to a US that seeks to **dump** Kyoto, creating a **conflict** between its stated support for the protocol and its new partnership. Opposition environment spokesman Kelvin Thomson urged Australia not to **follow** the US position. Blair told the ABC that greenhouse gases are a point of **obvious disagreement** with the US and, while uncertain whether Kyoto goes far enough, he said the more he reads about climate change the more **concerned** he becomes. The future of Kyoto will be debated at the upcoming Johannesburg meeting.  

END OF SUMMARY.
