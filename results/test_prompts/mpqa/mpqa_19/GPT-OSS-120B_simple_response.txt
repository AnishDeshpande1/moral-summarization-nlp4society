SUMMARY:  
Tony Blair publicly expressed disappointment with Australia’s decision to sign a new climate‑action partnership with the United States, warning that the move undermines the moral imperative to keep the Kyoto Protocol alive. While the Australian government insists it remains committed to Kyoto, critics argue the partnership signals a dangerous alignment with the US, which has openly sought to discard the protocol. Blair highlighted the ethical clash over greenhouse‑gas policies, noting his growing personal concern about climate change and urging each nation to take responsibility rather than follow the US’s lax stance. The article frames the issue as a matter of global duty and moral leadership, casting Blair’s unease as a call for countries to honor their commitments to reduce pollution.
