SUMMARY:

British Prime Minister Tony Blair expressed disappointment over Australia's decision to sign a climate change deal with the US, which he believes undermines the Kyoto Protocol on greenhouse gases. Critics argue that Australia is compromising its commitment to Kyoto by cozying up to the US, which has stated that ratifying the protocol would harm its economy. The Australian government maintains that it remains committed to Kyoto, but Blair questioned whether the protocol goes far enough in addressing climate change. He emphasized that countries should state their own position on the issue rather than following others.

The deal between Australia and the US involves cooperation between governments, businesses, and research bodies on climate change action. However, Blair's comments suggest that this agreement may be seen as a shift away from international efforts to combat global warming. The future of the Kyoto Protocol will be decided at a meeting in Johannesburg later this year.
