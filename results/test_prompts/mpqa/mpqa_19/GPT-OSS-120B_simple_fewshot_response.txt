SUMMARY:
British Prime Minister Tony Blair expressed disappointment with Australia’s new “climate action partnership” with the United States, warning that the move signals a troubling alignment with a nation that seeks to abandon the Kyoto Protocol. While Australia insists it remains committed to Kyoto, critics argue the partnership undermines global climate responsibility by cozying up to U.S. policies that favor economic shortcuts over environmental stewardship. Blair emphasized that greenhouse‑gas mitigation is a moral issue demanding honest, independent national positions, and he admitted his own growing concern about the planet’s future. The dispute highlights a broader ethical clash: the duty of governments to protect the climate versus the temptation to prioritize short‑term economic interests. As the next international meeting in Johannesburg looms, the moral imperative remains clear—countries must choose genuine, ambitious action on climate change rather than convenient alliances that risk weakening global agreements.  

END OF SUMMARY.
