SUMMARY:

British Prime Minister Tony Blair expressed dissatisfaction with Australia's decision to sign a climate change deal with the US, despite Australia's previous commitment to the Kyoto Protocol. The Australian-US partnership aims to cooperate on climate change issues between governments, businesses, and research bodies. Critics argue that Australia is contradicting itself by cosying up to the US, which has opposed the Kyoto Protocol. Environment Minister David Kemp denied that Australia was abandoning its commitment to Kyoto, stating that it remains committed to reducing greenhouse gases.

Opposition environment spokesman Kelvin Thomson urged Australia not to follow the US position on climate change. Blair agreed with Thomson, stating that there is a clear disagreement between countries on this issue. Blair also expressed concerns about the effectiveness of the Kyoto Protocol in addressing climate change. The future of the protocol will be decided at a meeting in Johannesburg later this year.

END OF SUMMARY.
