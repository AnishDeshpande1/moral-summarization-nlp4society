SUMMARY:

British Prime Minister Tony Blair expressed his disagreement with Australia's decision to sign a climate change deal with the United States, which critics argue undermines its commitment to the Kyoto Protocol on greenhouse gases. While Australia claims it remains committed to Kyoto, its involvement has been in doubt since President George Bush announced that ratifying the protocol would harm the US economy. The future of the protocol will be decided this year at a meeting in Johannesburg. Blair stated that greenhouse gas emissions are an area where there is an obvious disagreement with the US, and also implied that Australia's decision to sign the climate action partnership with the US may not align with its own commitment to Kyoto. However, it remains unclear whether Australia has officially abandoned its commitment to the protocol.

END OF SUMMARY.
