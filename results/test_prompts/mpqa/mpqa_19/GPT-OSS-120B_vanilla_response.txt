SUMMARY:  
British Prime Minister Tony Blair criticized Australia’s recent climate‑change partnership with the United States, suggesting it conflicts with the Kyoto Protocol. Australia’s government denied that the deal signals a move away from Kyoto, emphasizing continued commitment while engaging with the EU and the US on climate initiatives. Critics argue the partnership aligns Australia with the US, which opposes Kyoto. The future of the protocol will be addressed at the upcoming Johannesburg meeting. Opposition environment spokesman Kelvin Thomson warned Australia not to follow the US stance. Blair expressed personal concern about climate change and uncertainty about Kyoto’s adequacy.  

END OF SUMMARY.
