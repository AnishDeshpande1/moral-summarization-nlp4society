SUMMARY:
British Prime Minister Tony Blair voiced criticism of Australia’s newly signed climate‑action partnership with the United States, warning that the move highlights a troubling split over global responsibility for greenhouse‑gas reductions. While the Australian government insists it remains committed to the Kyoto Protocol, critics argue the deal signals a softening of that commitment by aligning with a U.S. stance that favors abandoning Kyoto. Blair stressed that each nation must decide its own climate policy, yet expressed growing personal concern about the adequacy of existing agreements. The dispute underscores a broader moral question about collective duty to protect the environment versus national self‑interest, with opposition figures urging Australia not to follow the U.S. position. The future of Kyoto will be debated at the upcoming Johannesburg meeting.

END OF SUMMARY.
