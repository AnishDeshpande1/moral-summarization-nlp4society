SUMMARY:  
At a summit, Japanese Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi and U.S. President George W. Bush clashed over how to tackle global warming. Bush reiterated his rejection of the 1997 Kyoto Protocol, arguing it would damage the U.S. economy, and promoted a new voluntary‑limits plan that ties emissions cuts to economic growth. Koizumi, a Kyoto supporter, praised the U.S. proposal as “very constructive” but urged Washington to do more, stressing that economic growth and environmental protection can be pursued together. He called on the United States to increase its effort so both nations can move in the same direction. Despite their differences, the leaders agreed to cooperate on climate‑related technology development and to work with other, including developing, countries, hoping that joint innovation will benefit both the planet and their economies.  

END OF SUMMARY.
