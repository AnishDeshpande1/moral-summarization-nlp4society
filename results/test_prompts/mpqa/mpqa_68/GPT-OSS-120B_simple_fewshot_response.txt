SUMMARY:
Japanese Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi and U.S. President George W. Bush clashed over the Kyoto Protocol, with Bush reiterating his rejection of the 1997 treaty and promoting a voluntary, growth‑linked emissions plan that critics deem weak. Koizumi praised the U.S. proposal as “constructive” but urged Washington to do more, stressing that economic growth and environmental protection can be pursued together and calling for stronger U.S. effort. Despite their disagreement, the two leaders pledged to cooperate on global‑warming initiatives, including joint development of new technologies that could benefit both the environment and the economy, and to work with other nations, especially developing ones, on climate action. The exchange highlights a moral tension: Japan’s commitment to binding climate goals versus the U.S. preference for voluntary measures, yet both claim a shared responsibility to address global warming.  
END OF SUMMARY.
