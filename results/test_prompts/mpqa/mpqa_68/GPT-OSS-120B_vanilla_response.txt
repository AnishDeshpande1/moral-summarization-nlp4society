SUMMARY: Japanese Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi and U.S. President George W. Bush clashed over the Kyoto Protocol during a summit, but agreed to cooperate on climate‑change initiatives. Bush reiterated his rejection of the 1997 Kyoto treaty, arguing it would harm the U.S. economy, and promoted a new voluntary, growth‑linked emissions‑limit plan announced the previous week. Koizumi, a Kyoto supporter, praised the U.S. proposal as “constructive” but urged greater U.S. effort, emphasizing that economic growth and environmental protection can be pursued together. Both leaders committed to work with other nations, including developing countries, on global‑warming mitigation and to collaborate on new technologies that could benefit both the environment and economic development. END OF SUMMARY.
