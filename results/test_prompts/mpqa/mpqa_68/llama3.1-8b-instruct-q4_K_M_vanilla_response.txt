SUMMARY:

Japanese Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi and US President George W. Bush met to discuss global warming issues, but disagreed on the Kyoto Protocol. Bush rejected the protocol, saying it would hurt US economic growth, while Koizumi supports it and called the US alternative proposal "constructive." However, he urged the US to make more efforts to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. The US plan proposes voluntary limits on emissions based on economic growth, but environmentalists criticize it as ineffective in solving global warming.

Despite their differences, Bush and Koizumi agreed to cooperate with other countries, including developing nations, on the issue of global warming. They also agreed that cooperation can lead to the development of new technologies that benefit both the environment and the economy. The meeting highlighted the ongoing debate between the US and other countries over how to address global warming.

END OF SUMMARY.
