SUMMARY:

Japanese Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi and US President George W. Bush held talks on global warming, but disagreed over the Kyoto Protocol. Bush reiterated his rejection of the protocol, citing economic concerns, while Koizumi expressed support for it. The Japanese leader welcomed Bush's alternative proposal, which sets voluntary limits on greenhouse gas emissions, but urged the US to do more to address climate change. Environmentalists have criticized the plan as ineffective in solving global warming.

Despite their differences, both leaders agreed to cooperate on addressing global warming, including with developing nations. They acknowledged that cooperation can lead to economic and environmental benefits through the development of new technologies. The talks highlight the ongoing debate between countries with differing views on how to address climate change.

END OF SUMMARY.
