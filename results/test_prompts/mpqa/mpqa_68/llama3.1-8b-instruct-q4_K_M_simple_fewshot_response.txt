Instead, he proposed a voluntary plan for companies to limit greenhouse gas emissions in accordance with economic growth. Koizumi expressed support for the Kyoto Protocol and asked the US to make further efforts to reduce emissions. The two leaders agreed to cooperate on global warming issues, including developing new technologies that can benefit both the environment and economy.

Koizumi's stance is seen as a moral imperative to take action against climate change, contrasting with Bush's prioritization of economic growth over environmental concerns. The disagreement highlights the differing priorities between the US and Japan on this issue, with Koizumi pushing for more ambitious targets to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
