SUMMARY:  
Japanese Prime Minister Junichiro Koizumi and U.S. President George W. Bush met to discuss global warming, but they diverged on the Kyoto Protocol. Bush again rejected the 1997 Kyoto agreement, arguing it would damage the U.S. **economy**, and promoted a new voluntary plan that ties greenhouse‑gas limits to **economic** growth. Koizumi praised the U.S. proposal as “very constructive” yet urged more **economic** and **environment**al effort, insisting that the **economy** and **environment** can be improved together. He called for the United States to show greater commitment, warning that the voluntary scheme may be **ineffective** and allow emissions to keep rising. Both leaders agreed to cooperate on **environment**‑friendly technology development, believing such innovation can **help** both the **economy** and the **environment** while engaging other nations, including developing ones.  

END OF SUMMARY.
