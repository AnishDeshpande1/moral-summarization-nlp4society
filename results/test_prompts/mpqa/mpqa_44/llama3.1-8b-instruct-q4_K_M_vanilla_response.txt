SUMMARY:

Local authorities in Lebanon have expressed dissatisfaction with excerpts from the US State Department's annual human rights report, which criticizes the country for weakening its central government's prerogatives and subjecting the judiciary to political pressure. A source described the criticism as an "annual event" that targets several countries, not just Lebanon. The report also notes that intelligence agents monitor opposition politicians and tap phones of certain government leaders, which is seen as harming Lebanon's democratic image. On a positive note, the report praises the Lebanese press for its daily criticism of the government.

The Foreign Ministry has received a copy of the report and will relay its observations to the US State Department in Washington. The authorities have conveyed their dissatisfaction to US Ambassador Vincent Battle, who returned from a conference in Washington over the weekend. Lebanon sends an annual explanation to the State Department about factors outside of the government's control.
