SUMMARY: Lebanese officials expressed dissatisfaction—though not surprise—over the U.S. State Department’s annual Human Rights Report, which was delivered to the Foreign Ministry by the U.S. Embassy’s deputy chief of mission. The report criticizes Lebanon’s weakening central government authority, political pressure on the judiciary, intelligence monitoring of opposition politicians, and phone tapping of officials, portraying the country as undemocratic and harming its image. It also notes the Lebanese press’s role in daily government criticism. The ministry plans to circulate the report to relevant ministries, especially Interior and Municipalities, and will respond to Washington. Lebanon will submit its customary yearly explanation to the State Department, highlighting factors beyond its control. The concerns were conveyed to U.S. Ambassador Vincent Battle. The report also praised the Lebanese media’s critical stance. END OF SUMMARY.
