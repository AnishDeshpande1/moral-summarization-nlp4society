SUMMARY:
Lebanese officials expressed sharp dissatisfaction—though not surprise—at the U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report, which they view as a subversive attack on Lebanon’s sovereign authority. The deputy chief of mission handed the report to the foreign ministry, which will forward it to relevant ministries before replying to Washington. The document’s accusations—weakening central government prerogatives, political pressure on the judiciary, and intelligence agencies spying on opposition figures—are portrayed by the source as unfair, painting Lebanon as undemocratic and damaging its reputation. While the report commends the Lebanese press for its criticism of the government, officials argue that the broader narrative constitutes a cheating of Lebanon’s democratic credentials and an unjust challenge to its legitimate authority. The ministry plans to submit a formal rebuttal to U.S. Ambassador Vincent Battle, continuing the annual practice of defending Lebanon against what it sees as biased and subversive foreign judgments.  
END OF SUMMARY.
