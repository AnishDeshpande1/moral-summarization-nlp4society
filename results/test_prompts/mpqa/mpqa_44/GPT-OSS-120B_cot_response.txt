**SUMMARY:**  
Lebanese officials expressed dissatisfaction—but not surprise—at the U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report, which they say discredits Lebanon by portraying it as undemocratic, uncivilized and abusive of human rights. The report, delivered by Deputy Chief of Mission Carol Kalin to the foreign ministry, criticizes the weakening of central government prerogatives and notes “serious” political pressure on the judiciary, as well as intelligence agents monitoring opposition politicians and tapping leaders’ phones—actions that “harms” Lebanon’s image as a democratic country. While the report praises the Lebanese press for its daily criticism of the government, authorities plan to forward copies to relevant ministries and respond to Washington. The episode underscores ongoing tension between Lebanon and the United States over perceptions of governance and rights.
