SUMMARY:
Lebanese officials expressed their customary displeasure—though not surprise—over the U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report, which they say unfairly paints Lebanon as undemocratic and “uncivilized.” The report, delivered by Deputy Chief of Mission Carol Kalin to the Foreign Ministry, criticizes the weakening of central government authority, political pressure on the judiciary, and intelligence agencies spying on opposition figures and senior officials. While it lauds the Lebanese press for its daily government scrutiny, officials argue the accusations damage Lebanon’s democratic image. The ministry will circulate the document to relevant ministries and forward its rebuttal to Washington, as it has done each year, emphasizing that many cited issues lie beyond the government’s control. The moral takeaway: external assessments that single out a nation’s shortcomings without full context can undermine its reputation and diplomatic relations, underscoring the need for balanced, responsible reporting that respects sovereign challenges while encouraging genuine human‑rights improvements.  
END OF SUMMARY.
