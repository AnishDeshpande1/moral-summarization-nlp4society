SUMMARY:

Local authorities in Lebanon have expressed dissatisfaction with excerpts from the US State Department's annual human rights report, which they see as discrediting the country by portraying it as undemocratic or uncivilized and abusing human rights. The criticism is not unexpected, as it is an annual event that also targets other countries. However, officials are particularly concerned about the report's reference to the judiciary being subjected to political pressure and intelligence agents monitoring opposition politicians' activities, which they see as harming Lebanon's image as a democratic country. On the other hand, the report praises the Lebanese press for its critical coverage of the government. The ministry will soon deliver copies of the report to relevant ministries and relay their observations to the State Department in Washington. This incident highlights the challenges of balancing the need for transparency with the potential harm that criticism can cause to a country's international reputation.

END OF SUMMARY.
