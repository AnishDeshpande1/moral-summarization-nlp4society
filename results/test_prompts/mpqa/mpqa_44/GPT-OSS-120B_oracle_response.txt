SUMMARY:  
Local officials expressed dissatisfaction—though not surprise—at the U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report that portrayed Lebanon as **undemocratic** and **uncivilized**, accusing it of **abusing** rights and noting a **weakening** of central‑government prerogatives. The report, delivered by Deputy Chief of Mission Carol Kalin to the Foreign Ministry’s acting director‑general Naji Abi Assi, will be circulated to ministries, especially Interior and Municipalities, before Lebanon replies to Washington. Authorities condemned the claim that the judiciary is **subjected** to political pressure, albeit “independent in principle,” and decried references to intelligence agents **tapping** opposition politicians and government leaders’ phones as damaging to Lebanon’s democratic image. While the report praised the Lebanese press for daily government criticism, officials plan to send their rebuttal to U.S. Ambassador Vincent Battle.  
END OF SUMMARY.
