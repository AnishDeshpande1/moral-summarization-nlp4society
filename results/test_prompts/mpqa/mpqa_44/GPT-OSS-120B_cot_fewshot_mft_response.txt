**SUMMARY:**  
Lebanese officials expressed dissatisfaction – though not surprise – with the U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report, which they view as discrediting Lebanon by labeling the country “undemocratic” and “uncivilized.” The report criticizes the weakening of central‑government prerogatives and alleges that the judiciary is subject to political pressure, while intelligence agents are monitoring opposition politicians and tapping the phones of government leaders – a serious charge that harms Lebanon’s democratic image. The ministry will forward the report to relevant ministries and respond to Washington, emphasizing that the observations are not unique to Lebanon. Despite the criticisms, the report praised the Lebanese press for its daily criticism of the government. END OF SUMMARY.
