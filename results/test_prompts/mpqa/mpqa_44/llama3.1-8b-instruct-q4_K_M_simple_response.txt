SUMMARY:
Local authorities in Lebanon have expressed dissatisfaction with excerpts from the US State Department's annual human rights report, which they consider discrediting to the country's image. The report criticizes the weakening of the central government's authority and alleges that the judiciary is subjected to political pressure, despite being "independent in principle." It also mentions intelligence agents monitoring opposition politicians and tapping government leaders' phones. However, the report does praise the Lebanese press for its criticism of the government. An official source described the criticism as an annual event, not specific to Lebanon alone, but emphasized that it harms the country's image as a democratic state.

The authorities have conveyed their dissatisfaction to US Ambassador Vincent Battle and plan to deliver copies of the report to concerned ministries before responding to the State Department in Washington. Each year, Lebanon sends an explanation about external factors affecting its human rights situation.

END OF SUMMARY.
