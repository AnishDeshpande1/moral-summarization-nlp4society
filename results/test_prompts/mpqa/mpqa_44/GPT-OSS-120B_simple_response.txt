SUMMARY:  
Local officials in Lebanon reacted with displeasure—though not surprise—to the U.S. State Department’s annual human‑rights report, which they view as casting the country in an unfairly negative moral light. The report, delivered to the foreign ministry by the U.S. embassy’s deputy chief of mission, accuses Lebanon’s central government of eroding its own authority, depicts the judiciary as politically pressured despite nominal independence, and alleges that intelligence services spy on opposition figures and tap officials’ phones, thereby “damaging Lebanon’s image as a democratic country.” While the document also commends the Lebanese press for its daily criticism of the government, officials argue that the overall portrayal is discrediting, suggesting the nation is undemocratic and uncivilized. The ministry plans to circulate the report to relevant ministries and respond to Washington, continuing the yearly practice of explaining external factors that affect Lebanon’s human‑rights record.  

END OF SUMMARY.
