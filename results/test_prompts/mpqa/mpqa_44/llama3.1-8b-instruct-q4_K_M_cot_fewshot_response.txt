SUMMARY:
Local authorities in Lebanon have expressed their dissatisfaction with the US State Department's annual report on human rights, which they believe discredits the country by portraying it as undemocratic and uncivilized. The report criticizes the weakening of the central government's prerogatives and accuses intelligence agents of monitoring opposition politicians' activities and tapping government leaders' phones. The authorities see these observations as a serious matter that harms Lebanon's image as a democratic country. However, they did note that the report praises the Lebanese press for its daily criticism of the government and its practices. Officials have conveyed their concerns to US Ambassador Vincent Battle and will soon deliver copies of the report to relevant ministries before responding to the State Department in Washington. The annual report is seen as an opportunity for Lebanon to explain factors outside of its control, and this year's report has sparked a reaction from local authorities. END OF SUMMARY.
