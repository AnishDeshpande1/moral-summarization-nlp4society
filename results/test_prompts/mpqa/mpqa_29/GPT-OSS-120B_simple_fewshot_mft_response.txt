SUMMARY:
The article juxtaposes two moral debates in Canadian commentary. On the one hand, editorials invoke **care** and **fairness** by urging Canada to demand humane treatment and legal due process for al‑Qaeda detainees at Guantanamo, warning that ignoring Geneva‑convention standards would make Canada “behave like them.” On the other hand, the piece highlights the **authority/subversion** and **loyalty/betrayal** judgments leveled at former minister Brian Tobin. Columnists denounce his abrupt exit as a third betrayal of voters, branding him flighty, opportunistic and uncommitted, while praising John Manley as a trustworthy, competent authority figure who upholds national security. Through these contrasting lenses, the author frames the Guantanamo issue as a matter of moral **care** and **fairness**, and Tobin’s departure as a breach of **loyalty** and **subversion** of political authority, contrasting it with Manley’s loyal, authoritative stewardship. The moral narrative thus condemns mistreatment of detainees and condemns Tobin’s perceived disloyalty, while extolling Manley’s responsible authority.

END OF SUMMARY.
