SUMMARY:  
Ottawa’s editorial page has been busy weighing the **plight** of Guantanamo detainees and the **antics** of former minister Brian Tobin.  The Ottawa Citizen argues that even the harsh **treatment** of al‑Qaeda prisoners must be judged by law, urging a “competent tribunal” to **decide** their fate and **address** the issue before it **determines** future policy.  The Sudbury Star backs a UN‑run tribunal, while the Timmins Daily Press warns that any change in U.S. **treating** of the captives could **imperil** American lives.  

In contrast, Tobin’s abrupt exit has drawn scathing **vitriol**.  The Canadian Taxpayers Federation calls his resignation a **betrayal** of voters; Rosa Harris‑Adler mocks his claimed family motives with sarcastic diaper‑changing anecdotes; Le Droit labels his career “unstable” and full of **flighty**, **opportunistic** moves; and the Kingston Whig‑Standard says he is **lacking** commitment.  By comparison, John Manley is praised as **sensible**, **serious**, competent and “scrupulously honest,” a stabilising figure for national security.  

The moral framing highlights the tension between humane legal standards for detainees and the condemnation of Tobin’s perceived **betrayal** and **unstable** conduct.  

END OF SUMMARY.
