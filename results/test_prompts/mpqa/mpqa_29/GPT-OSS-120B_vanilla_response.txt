SUMMARY:  
Canadian editorialists are debating two major issues. First, they criticize the U.S. detention conditions at Guantanamo Bay, arguing that even terrorist detainees deserve humane treatment and urging Canada to push for a competent tribunal under the Geneva Convention, possibly via the UN, to determine their legal status. While the Ottawa Citizen and Sudbury Star call for legal oversight, the Timmins Daily Press defends the U.S. approach, warning that leniency could endanger lives.

Second, the media are harshly judging former cabinet minister Brian Tobin’s recent departure from politics. Columns in the Ottawa Sun, Ottawa Citizen, Le Droit, and Kingston Whig‑Standard label his exit a betrayal, describe him as erratic and opportunistic, and mock his claimed family reasons. In contrast, John Manley, newly appointed Deputy Prime Minister, receives unanimous praise as a competent, trustworthy manager of national security.

END OF SUMMARY.
