SUMMARY:

The departure of Brian Tobin from Canadian politics has been met with criticism, with pundits labeling him as "flighty, opportunistic and lacking in commitment" to his job as an MP. His sudden exit is seen as a betrayal by voters who feel he has let them down for the third time. In contrast, John Manley's appointment as Deputy Prime Minister has been widely praised, with commentators calling him "competent and scrupulously honest." Manley's leadership during the Sept. 11 crisis was seen as a sensible and serious voice in Cabinet, earning him trust from Canadians.

Meanwhile, the treatment of al-Qaeda fighters imprisoned at Guantanamo Bay remains a contentious issue. Editorial writers argue that even though the conditions may be harsh, they do not necessarily mean inhumane treatment. The Citizen calls for Canada to insist on a "competent tribunal" to determine the legal status of detainees, as required by the Geneva Convention. This would ensure that laws are followed and prevent the US from behaving like its enemies.

The conflict over Guantanamo Bay prisoners must move to its next stage, with a UN-formed tribunal addressing their fate. END OF SUMMARY.
