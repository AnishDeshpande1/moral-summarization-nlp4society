**SUMMARY:**  
Ottawa’s editorial pages are wrestling with two moral dilemmas: the treatment of al‑Qaeda detainees at Guantanamo Bay and the abrupt exit of former minister Brian Tobin. Columns in the *Ottawa Citizen* and *Sudbury Star* argue that even thugs deserve *humane* treatment and that *laws* and a UN‑backed *tribunal* are needed to ensure *justice* rather than *inhumane* abuse. By contrast, the *Timmins Daily Press* warns that softening U.S. policy could endanger lives, suggesting “civil *rights* advocates” may be overreacting. Meanwhile, Tobin’s departure provoked harsh moral condemnation. Critics labeled his actions a *betrayal* of voters, calling him *unstable*, *flighty*, and *opportunistic*, lacking *commitment* and *responsibility*. Praise was reserved for John Manley, described as *competent*, *honest*, and a *trustworthy* steward of national security. The debate underscores Canada’s struggle to balance *humane* principles with security imperatives.
