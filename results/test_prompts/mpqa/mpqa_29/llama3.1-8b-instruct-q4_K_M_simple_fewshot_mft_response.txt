SUMMARY:

Editorial writers across Canada are weighing in on the recent Cabinet shuffle, particularly the departure of Brian Tobin. While some pundits have expressed praise for John Manley's appointment as Deputy Prime Minister, others have criticized Tobin's sudden exit, labeling it a "betrayal" of voters. The Ottawa Citizen and Sudbury Star argue that Canada should insist on humane treatment of al-Qaeda fighters imprisoned at Guantanamo Bay, citing the Geneva Convention. However, The Daily Press in Timmins takes a more hawkish stance, suggesting that treating prisoners humanely might imperil American lives. Meanwhile, columnist Rosa Harris-Adler ridicules Tobin's assertion that he left politics for family reasons, while Michel Vastel writes a farewell to the former minister, calling him "unstable" and "flighty." The moral implications of these editorials lie in their emphasis on authority, fairness, and loyalty. While some writers call for respect for human rights and dignity, others prioritize national security over prisoner treatment. The debate highlights the complex moral landscape of Canadian politics.

END OF SUMMARY.
