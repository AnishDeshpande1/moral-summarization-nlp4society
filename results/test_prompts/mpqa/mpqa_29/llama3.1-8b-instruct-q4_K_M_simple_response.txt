SUMMARY:

Editorial writers in Ottawa have been weighing in on the recent Cabinet shuffle, with some criticizing Brian Tobin's departure as a betrayal of voters. Columnists describe him as "flighty," "opportunistic," and lacking commitment to his job. In contrast, John Manley has received praise for being a "sensible manager" who Canadians can trust. The Citizen and the Sun have both commended Manley's competence and honesty.

Meanwhile, there is also concern over the treatment of al-Qaeda fighters at Guantanamo Bay. Editorial writers argue that Canada should insist on humane treatment and a tribunal to determine their legal status, as required by the Geneva Convention. This view is shared by the Ottawa Citizen and the Sudbury Star, while the Daily Press in Timmins disagrees, suggesting that different treatment could imperil American lives.

The contrast between Tobin's criticism and Manley's praise highlights differing views on leadership and accountability. The emphasis on humane treatment for detainees underscores the importance of upholding international law and human rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
