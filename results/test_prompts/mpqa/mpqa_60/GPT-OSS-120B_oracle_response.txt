SUMMARY:
As Zimbabwe heads to the polls, fear of violence and a possible civil war looms. Surveys put opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai ahead of long‑time autocrat Robert Mugabe, but both sides are preparing for a contested result. Government officials have openly said they would not allow a Tsvangirai government and have hinted at a coup, while the military has been put on “high alert.” Recent attacks have already killed at least 34 people, almost all MDC supporters, and opposition agents have been abducted. ZANU‑PF is accused of rigging the vote by revising election laws, increasing polling stations in rural strongholds, reducing them in urban areas, and imposing a residency requirement that bans many young voters, effectively disenfranchising them. Critics say the regime is stealing the election through violations, edited evidence used to charge Tsvangirai with treason, and plans to bar independent monitors. If Mugabe announces a win, citizens may storm the castle; if the MDC wins, Mugabe may announce martial law, send the army to the streets and jail opposition leaders. The country faces corruption, food shortages and unemployment, and the specter of further bloodshed hangs over the vote.  
END OF SUMMARY.
