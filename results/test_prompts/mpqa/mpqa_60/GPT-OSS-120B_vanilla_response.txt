SUMMARY: Zimbabwe is heading to a highly tense election as citizens vote on whether to keep long‑time ruler Robert Mugabe or replace him with opposition leader Morgan Tsvangirai. Polls show Tsvangirai ahead, but the campaign has been marred by a surge in political violence, deaths of at least 34 MDC supporters, and accusations that ZANU‑PF has altered election laws to rig the vote. Government officials and military leaders have publicly stated they would not accept a Tsvangirai government and even hinted at supporting a coup. Mugabe’s regime has charged Tsvangirai with high treason, recalled troops from Congo, and imposed residency documentation requirements that could disenfranchise many young urban voters. ZANU‑PF plans to increase rural polling stations, limit independent monitoring, and use civil servants to oversee vote counting. Opposition supporters fear possible martial law, arrests, or a crackdown if they win, while Mugabe’s supporters fear a loss and possible civil unrest. International observers warn that both sides may reject the results, raising the specter of violence or a coup after the polls.  
END OF SUMMARY.
