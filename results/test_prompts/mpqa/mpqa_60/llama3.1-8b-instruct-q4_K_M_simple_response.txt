SUMMARY:

Tensions are high in Zimbabwe ahead of the presidential election between incumbent Robert Mugabe and challenger Morgan Tsvangirai. Many fear violence, especially if the outcome is disputed, given the surge in political violence and allegations that the governing party changed election laws to rig the vote. Senior government officials have vowed not to accept a Tsvangirai victory, with one saying they would support a coup if it meant preventing an MDC-led government.

At least 34 people have died in political violence this year, mostly opposition supporters. The State Department has accused Mugabe's government of human rights abuses during the election campaign, which ZANU-PF officials deny. Tsvangirai faces treason charges for allegedly plotting to kill Mugabe, with some analysts suggesting these could be a ploy to jail him if he loses.

The opposition and foreign diplomats warn that Mugabe is doing everything in his power to steal the election, including introducing new laws that could disenfranchise young voters. The outcome of the election remains uncertain, but one thing is clear: Zimbabwe's future hangs in the balance.

END OF SUMMARY.
