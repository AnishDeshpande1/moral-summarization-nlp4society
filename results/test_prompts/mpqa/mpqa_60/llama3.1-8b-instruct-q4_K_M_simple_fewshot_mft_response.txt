SUMMARY:

Tensions are running high in Zimbabwe as the country prepares for its presidential election, with many fearing violence regardless of who wins. Challenger Morgan Tsvangirai is ahead in polls, but surveys show that neither side is prepared to accept a loss. Senior government officials and military leaders have warned that they would not allow Tsvangirai's Movement for Democratic Change (MDC) party to take power. This has raised concerns about the use of force to maintain Mugabe's rule. The ruling ZANU-PF party has revised election laws and procedures, potentially disenfranchising hundreds of thousands of young opposition supporters. Independent observers have accused Mugabe's government of numerous human rights violations during the campaign. The situation is characterized by a struggle for authority between Mugabe, who has ruled Zimbabwe for decades, and Tsvangirai, who represents a new generation of leaders. The fear is that if Mugabe loses, he may resort to violence or martial law to maintain his grip on power.

END OF SUMMARY.
