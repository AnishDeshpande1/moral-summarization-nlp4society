SUMMARY:

As Zimbabweans head to the polls, fears of violence and war are escalating due to allegations that the governing party is trying to rig the election results. Senior government officials have announced that they will not allow a Movement for Democratic Change (MDC) victory, sparking concerns about potential civil unrest. At least 34 people have died in political violence this year, with most being MDC supporters. The opposition party accuses Mugabe's government of human rights violations and corruption, which has led to food shortages and unemployment.

The governing party has revised election laws and procedures, introducing a residency requirement that could disenfranchise hundreds of thousands of young opposition supporters. If the MDC wins, Mugabe may announce martial law or send the army into the streets. The MDC leader, Morgan Tsvangirai, has been charged with high treason for allegedly plotting to kill Mugabe, which some see as an attempt to jail him if he loses.

The international community is warning of a potential surge in violence and attacks on opposition supporters. The situation is dire, with many Zimbabweans fearing that the election will be stolen by the governing party at any cost. END OF SUMMARY.
