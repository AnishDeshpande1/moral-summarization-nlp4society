SUMMARY:

Zimbabwe is on edge as it prepares for elections this weekend between incumbent President Robert Mugabe and challenger Morgan Tsvangirai. While surveys show Tsvangirai ahead, fears of violence and a disputed outcome are growing. Senior government officials have threatened to support a coup if Tsvangirai wins, and Mugabe's party has been accused of rigging the vote through changes in election laws. At least 34 people have died in political violence this year, mostly opposition supporters.

Tsvangirai's Movement for Democratic Change (MDC) is concerned about the government's intentions to increase polling stations in rural areas where Mugabe's party is strong and reduce them in urban areas where MDC support is high. The opposition also fears that hundreds of thousands of young voters may be disenfranchised by a new residency requirement.

Analysts believe that Mugabe may try to jail Tsvangirai as a last-ditch effort to stay in power if he loses the election. Foreign diplomats and election observers are urging calm, but the situation remains volatile and potentially explosive.

END OF SUMMARY.
