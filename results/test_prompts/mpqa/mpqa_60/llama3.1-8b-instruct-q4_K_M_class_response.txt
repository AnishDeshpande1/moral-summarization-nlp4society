SUMMARY:

As Zimbabweans head to the polls, fears of violence and unrest are growing, with many expecting a civil war if Mugabe loses the election. The governing party has made pronouncements that they will not allow Tsvangirai's Movement for Democratic Change (MDC) to take power, with some officials even supporting a coup. The opposition party has accused the government of rigging the vote through revised election procedures and violent attacks on their supporters.

At least 34 people have died in political violence this year, mostly MDC supporters. Human rights violations have been rampant during the campaign, including abduction and attacks on opposition polling agents. Mugabe's government has also been accused of corruption, shortages, and unemployment, which has fueled widespread discontent among Zimbabweans.

The election outcome is far from certain, with some analysts predicting that Mugabe will steal the vote through manipulation of voting procedures. Tsvangirai has offered to pardon Mugabe for past human rights abuses if he wins the election, but this may not be enough to ease pressure on the 78-year-old autocrat.

END OF SUMMARY.
