**SUMMARY:**  
As Zimbabwe heads to the polls, many citizens **fear** a looming **civil war** sparked by escalating **violence**. Both the ruling ZANU‑PF and the opposition MDC accuse each other of rigging the vote, while senior officials warn that the **military** and **rioters** may take to the streets. A senior ZANU‑PF spokesman even threatened a **coup** if the MDC wins, calling a Tsvangirai government “intolerable.” So far, at least 34 **supporters** of the MDC have **died** in **violent** clashes, and dozens more have been **abducted**. The government has placed the armed forces on **high alert**, denied **human rights violations**, and introduced new residency rules that could disenfranchise many young voters. Opponents label Mugabe an **autocrat** responsible for past **corruption**, **unemployment**, and the 1980s Matabeleland massacres, while warning that a Mugabe victory could trigger **martial law**, **jail** for Tsvangirai, and a **storm** of protests. The opposition accuses the regime of trying to **steal** the election, while Mugabe’s side threatens **treason** charges and **prosecution** of any challenger.
