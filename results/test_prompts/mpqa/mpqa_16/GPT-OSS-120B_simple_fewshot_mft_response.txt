SUMMARY:
Thousands of Cubans gathered to honor Venezuelan President Hugo Chávez’s swift reinstatement after a brief coup, framing the event as a triumph over subversive forces. By likening the failed overthrow to the 1961 Bay of Pigs invasion that sought to topple Fidel Castro, the crowd highlighted loyalty to their revolutionary allies and condemned external attempts to undermine legitimate authority. Speakers emphasized the care owed to a fellow socialist leader, celebrating the “dignity and honor” of citizens who forced the coup‑plotters to retreat and warning of the harm caused by foreign meddling. The rally underscored collective loyalty to Chávez and Castro, portraying the U.S.‑backed plot as a betrayal of sovereign rule and a threat to the wellbeing of the peoples they claim to protect. This public affirmation of solidarity reinforces respect for established revolutionary authority while denouncing subversive interventions that endanger both leaders and their supporters.  
END OF SUMMARY.
