SUMMARY:
Thousands of Cubans celebrated Venezuelan President Hugo Chavez's return to power at a rally in Havana, comparing the failed coup against him to the Bay of Pigs invasion 41 years ago. About 4,000 Castro supporters gathered on the street corner where Fidel Castro declared Cuba socialist before the Bay of Pigs invasion. The commemoration was seen as a "beautiful historic coincidence" given the recent events in Venezuela, where loyalist military officers reinstated Chavez after demonstrations calling for his return. Cuba's close ties with Venezuela have rankled the US, and both countries' leaders are leftists who see themselves as revolutionary leaders. The Bush administration denied suggestions that it encouraged the removal of Chavez. The Bay of Pigs invasion, which attempted to overthrow Castro's government, failed miserably, resulting in 100 deaths and 1,000 captured invaders.

END OF SUMMARY.
