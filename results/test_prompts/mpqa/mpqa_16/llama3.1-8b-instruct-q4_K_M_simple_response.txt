SUMMARY:

Thousands of Cubans gathered to celebrate Venezuelan President Hugo Chavez's return to power, drawing parallels between the failed coup against him and the infamous Bay of Pigs invasion in 1961. The event was seen as a "beautiful historic coincidence" by Jorge Arias, a top local Communist Party official, given that it coincided with Venezuelans' successful resistance against the coup attempt. Castro supporters compared the two events, highlighting the US-backed invasion's failure and the bravery of Chavez's loyalists in thwarting the coup.

The celebration was also seen as a testament to the close ties between Fidel Castro and Hugo Chavez, who are both left-wing leaders in their respective countries. The event has further strained relations with the United States, which has been accused of backing the coup attempt against Chavez.

END OF SUMMARY.
