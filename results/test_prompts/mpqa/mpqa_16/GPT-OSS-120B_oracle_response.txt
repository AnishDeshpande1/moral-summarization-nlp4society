SUMMARY:  
Thousands of Cubans gathered to celebrate Venezuelan President Hugo Chávez’s reinstated return to power, likening the recent botched coup to the disastrous Bay of Pigs invasion that attempted to overthrow Fidel Castro in 1961. About 4,000 supporters—workers, students, housewives and retirees—met at the historic street corner where Castro declared Cuba socialist on the eve of that CIA‑trained effort. Communist Party official Jorge Arias called the commemoration a “beautiful historic coincidence,” noting that Chávez and Castro have “written a page of dignity and honor” by going out into the streets to defeat the coup attempt. The Bush administration brushed aside claims it quietly encouraged Chávez’s removal, while Cuba quickly denounced the ouster and praised the reversal that reinstated their ally. The original Bay of Pigs invasion saw roughly 1,500 exiles killed or captured, underscoring the legacy of foreign‑backed attempts to topple leftist governments.  

END OF SUMMARY.
