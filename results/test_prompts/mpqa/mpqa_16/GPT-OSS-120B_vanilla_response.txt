SUMMARY: Thousands of Cubans gathered in Havana to celebrate Venezuelan President Hugo Chávez’s reinstatement after a brief ouster, drawing parallels between the failed Venezuelan coup and the 1961 Bay of Pigs invasion that attempted to depose Fidel Castro. About 4,000 supporters—including workers, students, housewives, and retirees—met at the historic site where Castro announced Cuba’s turn to socialism. Communist Party official Jorge Arias called the event a “beautiful historic coincidence,” noting the shared revolutionary legacy of Castro and Chávez. Cuba quickly condemned the initial removal of Chávez, then praised his return after loyalist military officers responded to popular demonstrations. The U.S. Bush administration dismissed claims it had covertly supported Chávez’s removal. The Bay of Pigs, a CIA‑backed invasion by roughly 1,500 Cuban exiles, ended in failure with about 100 killed and 1,000 captured. END OF SUMMARY.
