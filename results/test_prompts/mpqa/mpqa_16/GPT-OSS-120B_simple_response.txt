SUMMARY:  
Thousands of Cubans took to the streets to celebrate Venezuelan President Hugo Chávez’s reinstatement after a brief ouster, drawing a moral parallel between the failed coup in Venezuela and the 1961 Bay of Pigs invasion that the United States backed against Fidel Castro.  Organizers—workers, students, housewives and retirees—framed the rally as a tribute to revolutionary solidarity, noting that both Chávez and Castro view themselves as defenders of socialist dignity against imperialist aggression.  Communist Party official Jorge Arias called the event a “beautiful historic coincidence,” emphasizing that the Venezuelan people “wrote a page of dignity and honor” by rejecting the coup.  The celebration underscored Cuba’s quick denunciation of Chávez’s removal and its criticism of U.S. interference, while the Bush administration denied any involvement.  By invoking the Bay of Pigs, the crowd cast the Venezuelan episode as a moral continuation of the struggle against foreign‑backed attempts to topple leftist governments.  

END OF SUMMARY.
