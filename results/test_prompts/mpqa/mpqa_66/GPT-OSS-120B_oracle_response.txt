SUMMARY:  
Three major bodies – the Council of Europe, the OS‑CE and the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights – issued a joint warning that the **fight** against **terrorism** must not become a pretext for **violations** of **fundamental freedoms**. Citing recent **attacks** on **human rights** after 9/11, they urged governments to avoid **excessive measures** that would **violate** the **right to life**, **freedom of expression**, **freedom of religion** and protection from **torture**. The statement condemned the **killing** of Taliban inmates in Afghanistan and called for an **inquiry** into the incident, while warning that **restrain**ed, law‑based action is essential. It stressed that states must **uphold** their international obligations, **strike** a balance between **legitimate** security concerns and **freedoms**, and avoid **hollowed‑out** rights or retroactive harsher **penalties**. The joint appeal follows Britain’s emergency **trial‑free** detention law and rising criticism of expanded U.S. **military** actions, urging **restraint** to prevent further **abuses** and a heavy **toll** on civil liberties.  

END OF SUMMARY.
