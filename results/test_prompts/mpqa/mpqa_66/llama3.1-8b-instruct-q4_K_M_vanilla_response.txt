SUMMARY:

Three major international organizations (Council of Europe, OSCE, and UN High Commissioner for Human Rights) issued a joint statement warning against the use of terrorism as a pretext to violate human rights. They expressed concern over recent developments in some countries that compromise civil liberties since the September 11 attacks. The organizations called on governments to balance national security concerns with fundamental freedoms, adhering to international law commitments.

The statement emphasized essential rights that cannot be compromised, including the right to life, freedom of expression and religion, and freedom from torture. They also urged against retroactive imposition of harsher penalties for crimes.

This warning comes after the British House of Commons passed emergency legislation allowing detention without trial and following reports of killings at a prison in Afghanistan. The United States has hinted at expanding its war on terrorism beyond Afghanistan to Iraq, drawing criticism from Germany and France who called for restraint.

END OF SUMMARY.
