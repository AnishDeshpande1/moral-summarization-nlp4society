SUMMARY:
Three leading international organizations - the Council of Europe, OSCE, and UN High Commissioner for Human Rights - have issued a joint statement warning against using the fight against terrorism as an excuse to violate human rights. They express concern over "worrying developments" in some countries, where governments are taking excessive measures that infringe on fundamental freedoms and legitimate dissent. The organizations emphasize the importance of striking a balance between national security concerns and human rights, and list essential rights that cannot be compromised, including the right to life, freedom of expression and religion, and protection from torture. They also urge against retroactive imposition of harsher penalties for crimes. This statement comes in response to recent events, such as the British House of Commons' emergency legislation allowing detention without trial, and the killing of Taliban inmates in Afghanistan.

END OF SUMMARY.
