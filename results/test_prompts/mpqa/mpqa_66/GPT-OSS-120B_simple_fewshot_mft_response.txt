SUMMARY:
Three leading international bodies – the Council of Europe, the OSCE and the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights – issued a joint warning that the fight against terrorism must not become a pretext for abusing state power. Citing recent “worrying developments” after 9/11, they urged governments to respect legitimate authority while refraining from excessive, unlawful measures that harm civilians and undermine dissent. The statement stresses the moral duty to protect fundamental freedoms – the right to life, free expression, religion and freedom from torture – and to avoid retroactive harsher penalties that would constitute unfair cheating of legal standards. It calls for a balanced approach that upholds security without subverting human‑rights obligations, condemning actions such as the UK’s emergency detention law and the mass killings of Taliban prisoners in Afghanistan. The appeal reflects a commitment to care for vulnerable populations, to hold authorities accountable, and to ensure fairness in the application of anti‑terrorism policies. END OF SUMMARY.
