SUMMARY:  
Three major human‑rights bodies – the Council of Europe, the OSCE and the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights – issued a joint warning that the global “war on terror” must not become an excuse to trample basic freedoms. Citing recent “worrying developments” after 9/11, they urged governments to avoid excessive measures that would undermine civil liberties, emphasizing that security actions must respect international obligations to protect the right to life, freedom of expression, freedom of religion and freedom from torture. The statement, delivered from Strasbourg, Geneva and Warsaw, condemned the British Parliament’s emergency law allowing detention without trial and called for an inquiry into the mass killing of Taliban prisoners in Afghanistan. It stressed that legitimate security concerns must be balanced with fundamental rights and warned against retroactive harsher penalties. The appeal reflects a moral stance that human dignity cannot be sacrificed in the name of counter‑terrorism, even as the United States considers expanding military action to Iraq, prompting criticism from Germany and France.  

END OF SUMMARY.
