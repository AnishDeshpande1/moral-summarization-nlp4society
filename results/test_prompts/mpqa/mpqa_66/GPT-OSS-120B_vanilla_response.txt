SUMMARY: The Council of Europe, the OSCE and the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights jointly warned that the fight against terrorism must not be used to breach human rights. In a statement issued from Strasbourg, Geneva and Warsaw, they condemned “worrying developments” in several countries after 9/11 and urged governments to avoid excessive measures that infringe fundamental freedoms, such as the right to life, freedom of expression, religion and protection from torture. The organisations called for a balance between legitimate security concerns and international law obligations, rejecting retroactive harsher penalties. The warning follows Britain’s passage of emergency detention legislation and recent killings of Taliban prisoners in Afghanistan, which prompted an Amnesty International inquiry. The United States’ hinted expansion of anti‑terror operations to Iraq also drew criticism from Germany and France. END OF SUMMARY.
