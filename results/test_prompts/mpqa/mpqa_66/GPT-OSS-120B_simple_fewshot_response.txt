SUMMARY:
Three major human‑rights bodies – the Council of Europe, the OSCE and the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights – issued a joint warning that the global war on terrorism must not become an excuse to trample civil liberties. Citing “worrying developments” after 9/11, they urged governments to avoid excessive, rights‑violating measures and to honor their international obligations. The statement emphasized that essential freedoms – the right to life, freedom of expression, religion and protection from torture – cannot be eroded, and that retroactive harsher penalties should be rejected. The appeal comes amid recent controversies: the UK Parliament’s emergency law permitting detention without trial, a deadly prison uprising in Afghanistan prompting Amnesty International’s call for an inquiry, and U.S. hints of expanding military action into Iraq, which drew criticism from Germany and France. The moral of the story is that security efforts must be balanced with, not at the expense of, fundamental human rights and the rule of law.

END OF SUMMARY.
