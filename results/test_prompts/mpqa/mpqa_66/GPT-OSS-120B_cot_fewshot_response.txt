**SUMMARY:**
Three leading international bodies – the Council of Europe, the OSCE and the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights – warned that the fight against terrorism must not become a pretext for **violations** of **human rights**. While acknowledging the **security** threat, they urged governments to avoid **excessive** measures that would erode **fundamental freedoms**, **civil liberties**, and the right to **dissent**. The joint statement stressed the **essential** need to **uphold** the **right to life**, **freedom of expression**, **freedom of religion**, and freedom from torture, and to **avoid** retroactive harsher penalties. Recent events – the UK’s emergency detention law and the mass killing of Taliban prisoners in Afghanistan – illustrate the **toll** on **rights** and have drawn **criticism** from Germany and France, who call for **restraint**. The organisations emphasized that any anti‑terrorism actions must be **consistent** with international **human rights** obligations, **protecting** and **safeguarding** liberty while combating terror. END OF SUMMARY.
