**SUMMARY:**  
Three major bodies – the Council of Europe, the OSCE and the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights – issued a joint warning that the fight against terrorism must not become a pretext for violating human rights. Citing “worrying developments” after 9/11, they urged governments to avoid excessive measures that would erode fundamental freedoms, legitimate dissent and civil liberties. The statement stressed the essential right to life, freedom of expression and religion, and the prohibition of torture, calling for a careful balance between security and these rights. It condemned recent actions such as the UK’s emergency detention law and the killing of Taliban inmates in Afghanistan, and warned against expanding the war without restraint. The three organisations reaffirmed that states must fully respect their international obligations while combating terrorism.
