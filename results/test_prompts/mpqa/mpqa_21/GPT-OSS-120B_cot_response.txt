**SUMMARY:**  
A NASA study shows that the world is already cooling a bit thanks to the 1987 Montreal Protocol, which phased out ozone‑depleting chlorofluorocarbons—powerful heat‑trapping gases. The researchers say the slowdown in greenhouse‑gas growth also reflects a modest decline in methane and carbon dioxide, though the reasons are not fully understood. While the Kyoto Protocol focuses on curbing CO₂ by limiting coal, oil, and gas use—an approach that can hinder booming economies—Hansen and Sato argue policymakers can make significant headway by also attacking other pollutants such as black carbon soot, low‑altitude ozone, and methane from waste and energy production. Cutting methane emissions by 30 % could deliver climate benefits comparable to the curbs on CO₂ envisioned for Kyoto’s first commitment period (2008‑2012). Their findings suggest a broader, less politically charged strategy may help protect the climate.
