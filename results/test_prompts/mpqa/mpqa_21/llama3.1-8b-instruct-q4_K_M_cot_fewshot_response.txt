A recent study suggests that the world has already stepped along the path to a future cooler than it might have been. Researchers at NASA's Goddard Institute for Space Studies link the slower rate to implementation of the Montreal Protocol, which phased out production of gases that trap heat thousands of times as effectively as carbon dioxide. The study shows that policymakers can make significant headway by attacking other pollutants such as black carbon soot and methane from landfills, mining, and oil and gas production, making it a more achievable goal to adjust the globe's thermostat in a politically charged effort.
