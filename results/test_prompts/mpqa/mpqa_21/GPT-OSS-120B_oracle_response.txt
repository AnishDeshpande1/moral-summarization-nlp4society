SUMMARY:  
A recent NASA GISS study shows that the world’s climate is already **adjusting** toward a cooler future thanks to the **implementation** of the 1987 Montreal **Protocol**, where nations **agreed** to **phase** out ozone‑depleting chlorofluorocarbons—powerful heat‑trapping gases. Researchers James Hansen and Makiko Sato **conducted** the analysis, finding that reduced emissions have slowed the atmosphere’s heat‑retention growth, partly due to slower rises in methane and carbon dioxide. They suggest that further **effort** should target other pollutants such as black carbon soot, lower‑atmosphere ozone, and methane from landfills and energy production. **Attacking** methane emissions by 30 % could yield climate benefits comparable to those expected from CO₂ cuts under the Kyoto Protocol’s first commitment period (2008‑2012). The study, published Dec. 18, 2001 in the *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, highlights alternative paths to tempering global warming beyond the politically charged Kyoto focus on CO₂.  

END OF SUMMARY.
