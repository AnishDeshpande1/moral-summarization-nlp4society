SUMMARY:  
The article stresses a moral imperative to act on climate change beyond the politically contentious Kyoto Protocol. NASA scientists report that reductions in greenhouse‑gas emissions—especially the phase‑out of ozone‑depleting CFCs under the 1987 Montreal Protocol—have already slowed the planet’s heat‑trapping capacity, averting a far hotter future. While Kyoto targets carbon dioxide, the authors argue that ethical climate policy should also curb other harmful pollutants such as black carbon, tropospheric ozone, and methane, which can deliver climate benefits comparable to Kyoto’s CO₂ cuts. They note that the observed slowdown in CO₂ and methane growth is not fully understood, urging further study. By expanding the moral scope of environmental action to include these additional pollutants, policymakers can make meaningful progress without the severe economic sacrifices tied solely to fossil‑fuel reductions.  

END OF SUMMARY.
