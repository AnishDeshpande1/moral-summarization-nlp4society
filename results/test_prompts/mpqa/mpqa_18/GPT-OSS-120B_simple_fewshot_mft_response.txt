SUMMARY:
A new multinational crew of two American astronauts and a Russian cosmonaut has taken up residence on the International Space Station for a six‑month expedition. After arriving on shuttle Endeavour, the team patiently completed essential tasks—installing custom seat liners, stowing suits, and transferring equipment—before officially opening the hatch. Russian Yuri Onufrienko eagerly assumed command from American Frank Culbertson, symbolizing trust and shared responsibility across national lines. Using Endeavour’s robotic arm, the crew attached the Italian‑built Raffaello cargo carrier, delivering three tons of food, clothing, experiments and spare parts, and later loading it with waste for return to Earth. Personal touches highlighted their humanity: Onufrienko brought fly‑tying gear, Bursch carried weaving supplies, and Walz installed a five‑octave keyboard he calls a “psychological support device.” The exchange underscores cooperative spirit, mutual care, and the moral commitment of astronauts to support one another and the broader scientific mission as the outgoing crew prepares for Earth after 128 days in orbit.  
END OF SUMMARY.
