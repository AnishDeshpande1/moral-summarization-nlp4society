SUMMARY:
Two American astronauts, Daniel Bursch and Carl Walz, and one Russian cosmonaut, Yuri Onufrienko, have settled into the International Space Station for a half-year stay. They arrived on space shuttle Endeavour on Saturday and took over from the previous crew, led by Frank Culbertson. The new residents brought their personal items, including Onufrienko's fishing gear, Bursch's weaving material, and Walz's keyboard, which he calls a "psychological support device." The cargo carrier Raffaello was also transferred to the space station, carrying three tons of food, clothes, science experiments, and spare parts. Once emptied, it will be filled with dirty laundry, trash, and used equipment for the return journey on Endeavour.

The previous crew, led by Culbertson, has been in space since August and will spend a total of 128 days in orbit. Onufrienko will remain on board until May, taking over as commander from Culbertson. The transition was smooth, with the new residents getting settled into their new home after the necessary preparations were made.

END OF SUMMARY.
