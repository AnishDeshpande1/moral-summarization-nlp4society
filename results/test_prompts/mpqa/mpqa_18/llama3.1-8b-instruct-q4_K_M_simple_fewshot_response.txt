SUMMARY:
A new crew of three astronauts, including two Americans and one Russian cosmonaut, has settled into the International Space Station for a half-year stay. Yuri Onufrienko took over as commander from Frank Culbertson, and will remain on board until May along with astronauts Daniel Bursch and Carl Walz. The new residents brought personal items to reflect their hobbies, including fishing gear, weaving material, and a keyboard. The crew is also preparing for the departure of the previous crew in another week, who have spent 128 days in space. The new cargo carrier, Raffaello, has been attached to the station and contains three tons of food, clothes, science experiments, and spare parts.

END OF SUMMARY.
