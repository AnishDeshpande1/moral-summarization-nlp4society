SUMMARY: Two American astronauts and a Russian cosmonaut have taken up residence on the International Space Station (ISS) for a six‑month expedition. Arriving on shuttle Endeavour, they had to transfer seat liners, stow spacesuits, and install equipment before moving into the station, now called Alpha. Russian Yuri Onufrienko assumed command from American Frank Culbertson, while crew members Daniel Bursch and Carl Walz will stay until May. Using Endeavour’s robotic arm, the shuttle delivered the Italian cargo module “Raffaello,” containing three tons of supplies and experiments; it will later be loaded with waste for return. The new crew brought personal items reflecting their hobbies—Onufrienko’s fly‑tying gear, Bursch’s weaving supplies, and Walz’s five‑octave keyboard for morale. The outgoing crew—Culbertson, Mikhail Tyurin, and Vladimir Dezhurov—have logged 128 days and will return to Earth in a week. END OF SUMMARY.
