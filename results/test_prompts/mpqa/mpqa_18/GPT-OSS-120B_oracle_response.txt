SUMMARY:  
Two American astronauts and a Russian cosmonaut **settled** into the International Space Station for a half‑year **stay**, arriving on shuttle Endeavour and quickly installing their **form‑fitting** seat liners and stowing their space suits before calling the station **home**. Russian Yuri Onufrienko eagerly awaited his turn as **commander**, watching through a small window until the hatch opened. The crew will remain on board until May alongside Daniel Bursch and Carl Walz. Using Endeavour’s robot arm, they lifted the Italian‑built cargo carrier **Raffaello**, loaded with three tons of **food**, clothing, science experiments and spare parts, to be later filled with **dirty laundry**, trash and used equipment for the return trip. Each astronaut brought personal items that reflect their **hobbies** and provide **psychological support**: Onufrienko’s fly‑tying gear, Bursch’s weaving supplies, and Walz’s five‑octave keyboard. The previous crew, led by Frank Culbertson, will depart in a week after logging 128 days in space.  

END OF SUMMARY.
