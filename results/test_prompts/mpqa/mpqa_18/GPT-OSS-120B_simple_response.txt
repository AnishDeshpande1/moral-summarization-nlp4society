SUMMARY:  
The arrival of two American astronauts and a Russian cosmonaut on the International Space Station underscores the moral virtues of international cooperation, perseverance, and human curiosity. After a hurried transfer from shuttle Endeavour, the new crew—Yuri Onufrienko, Daniel Bursch, and Carl Walz—took over command, symbolizing trust across former Cold‑War rivals. Their careful installation of seat liners and stowage of suits reflects disciplined responsibility, while the robotic arm’s handling of the Italian‑built Raffaello cargo carrier demonstrates collective ingenuity in sustaining life and research in orbit. Personal items—fly‑tying gear, weaving material, and a five‑octave keyboard dubbed a “psychological support device”—highlight the humane need for comfort, creativity, and mental health even in the most extreme environments. As the outgoing crew departs after 128 days, the seamless handover illustrates the ethical commitment of astronauts worldwide to share knowledge, support one another, and advance humanity’s reach into space.  

END OF SUMMARY.
