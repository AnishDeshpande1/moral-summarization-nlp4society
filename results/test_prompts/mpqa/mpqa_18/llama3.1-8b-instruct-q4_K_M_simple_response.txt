SUMMARY:

Three astronauts from the US and Russia have settled into the International Space Station, replacing the previous crew that had been on board since August. Russian cosmonaut Yuri Onufrienko has taken over as commander of the space station, where he will remain until May alongside American astronauts Daniel Bursch and Carl Walz. The new residents brought with them personal items reflecting their hobbies, including fishing gear for Onufrienko and a keyboard for musical expression from Walz.

The crew's arrival was facilitated by the transfer of equipment between Endeavour and the space station, including an Italian-built cargo carrier loaded with essential supplies such as food, science experiments, and spare parts. The previous crew will return to Earth in about a week after spending 128 days in space. This transfer marks a significant moment for the International Space Station, highlighting the collaboration and teamwork between nations in the pursuit of scientific discovery and exploration.

END OF SUMMARY.
