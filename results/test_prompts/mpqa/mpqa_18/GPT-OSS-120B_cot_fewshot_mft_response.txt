SUMMARY:
Two American astronauts and a Russian cosmonaut settled into the International Space Station, turning the orbiting laboratory into their new home for the next half‑year. Russian Yuri Onufrienko eagerly assumed the role of commander, while the crew packed personal items that reflected their hobbies—Onufrienko’s fly‑tying gear, Bursch’s weaving supplies, and Walz’s five‑octave keyboard, which he calls a “psychological support device.” As the shuttle Endeavour’s robot arm moved the Italian‑built Raffaello cargo carrier to the station, the crew loaded three tons of food, clothes, experiments and spare parts, and prepared to return waste and trash for disposal on the return flight. The smooth hand‑over of the crew and the careful management of supplies illustrate the cooperative spirit and mutual support that keep the space‑faring team thriving in the harsh environment of orbit.
