SUMMARY:
Two American astronauts and a Russian cosmonaut have begun a six‑month mission aboard the International Space Station, arriving on shuttle Endeavour and taking over from the crew that arrived in August. Russian Yuri Onufrienko eagerly assumed command from American Frank Culbertson, while fellow crew members Daniel Bursch and Carl Walz will remain on the station until May. The handover required moving seat liners, stowing suits and using Endeavour’s robotic arm to attach the Italian‑built cargo carrier “Raffaello,” loaded with three tons of supplies and later to be returned with trash and used equipment. The newcomers also brought personal items that reflect their hobbies—Onufrienko’s fly‑tying gear, Bursch’s weaving supplies, and Walz’s five‑octave keyboard, which he calls a “psychological support device.” The outgoing crew, after 128 days in orbit, will return to Earth in a week, leaving the station in the hands of the new international team, underscoring ongoing cooperation and shared purpose in space exploration.
END OF SUMMARY.
