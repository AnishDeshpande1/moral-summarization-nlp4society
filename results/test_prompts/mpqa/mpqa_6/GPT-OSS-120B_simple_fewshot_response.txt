SUMMARY:
Brazil and Germany have jointly issued an “Partnership Brazil‑Germany: Action Plan” urging that the Kyoto Protocol be fully implemented by the upcoming Sustainable Development Summit (Rio+10) in South Africa next September. The two nations appeal to the international community to ratify and enforce the treaty, framing climate action as a shared moral duty to protect the planet for current and future generations. They pledge to deepen cooperation on environmental and sustainable‑development issues and to present coordinated positions at Rio+10. The statement also highlights the significance of the Pilot Program for Protection of Tropical Forests (PPG‑7) and calls for continued German participation, underscoring a collective responsibility to safeguard tropical forests and combat climate change. By linking policy commitments to ethical stewardship of the Earth, Brazil and Germany seek to rally global support for a stronger, more just climate regime.  
END OF SUMMARY.
