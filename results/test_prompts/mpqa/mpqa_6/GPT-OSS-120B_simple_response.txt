SUMMARY: In a joint “Partnership Brazil‑Germany: Action Plan,” Brazil and Germany appeal to the world’s conscience, urging that the Kyoto Protocol be brought into force by the upcoming Sustainable Development Summit (Rio+10) in South Africa next September. They stress the moral duty of all nations to ratify and enforce the treaty, framing climate action as a shared responsibility for the planet’s health and future generations. The two countries also pledge deeper cooperation on environmental protection and sustainable development, promising to present a united front at Rio+10. Highlighted is the Pilot Program for Protection of Tropical Forests (PPG‑7), with Germany’s active participation underscoring a commitment to safeguard vital ecosystems. By calling on others to join this effort, Brazil and Germany position themselves as ethical leaders championing global stewardship and climate justice. END OF SUMMARY.
