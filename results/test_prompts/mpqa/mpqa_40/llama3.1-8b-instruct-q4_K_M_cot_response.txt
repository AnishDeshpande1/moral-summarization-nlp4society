Pakistan has rejected the US State Department's human rights report, calling it "factually inaccurate" and "unwarranted." The report allegedly portrays Pakistan's human rights record as "poor," which Islamabad finds to be an "unacceptable" misstatement. In contrast, Pakistan boasts of its initiatives to promote and protect human rights, including the establishment of a Permanent Commission on the Status of Women, police and jail reforms, and the ratification of ILO Conventions. The government claims that these efforts have gone unacknowledged by the US report, which they view as an attempt to sit in judgment over others. Pakistan remains committed to promoting human rights and welcomes cooperation from friendly countries.
