SUMMARY: Pakistan has dismissed the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights country report as “factually inaccurate and unwarranted.” A Foreign Office spokesman argued the report ignored numerous initiatives Pakistan claims to have implemented since an April 2000 human‑rights conference, including the Juvenile Justice System Ordinance, a Permanent Commission on the Status of Women, police and jail reforms, a national plan to eliminate child labour, a fund for rehabilitating working children, amendments to the Citizenship Act allowing women to pass nationality to children of foreign husbands, a mass awareness campaign for the Year 2000 as the Year of Human Rights and Dignity, ratification of ILO conventions on equal pay and child labour, signing optional protocols to the Convention on the Rights of the Child, and the introduction of joint electorates for minorities. The spokesman rejected the report’s conclusion that Pakistan’s human‑rights record remains poor, calling it a misstatement, and urged cooperative, non‑judgmental approaches to human‑rights promotion, while welcoming assistance from friendly nations. END OF SUMMARY.
