SUMMARY:
Pakistan’s foreign ministry dismissed the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights country report as “factually inaccurate and unwarranted,” arguing that the assessment subverts the legitimate authority of the Pakistani government and ignores its recent protective measures. The spokesman highlighted a series of reforms—such as the Juvenile Justice System Ordinance, a permanent commission on women’s status, police and jail reforms, a national plan to eradicate child labour, a fund for rehabilitating working children, amendment of the 1951 Citizenship Act to allow women to pass citizenship to children of foreign husbands, ratification of ILO conventions on equal pay and child‑labour elimination, and the introduction of joint electorates for minorities—framed as caring actions that safeguard vulnerable groups. By labeling the U.S. report a “gross misstatement of facts,” Pakistan accuses it of unfairly cheating the nation of due credit for these initiatives and urges a cooperative, respectful approach among states to address human‑rights challenges. The government reaffirmed its commitment to promote and protect human rights and welcomed constructive assistance from friendly countries.  
END OF SUMMARY.
