SUMMARY:
Pakistan's Foreign Office has rejected the US State Department's human rights report, calling it "factually inaccurate and unwarranted". The report was criticized for ignoring Pakistan's efforts to protect human rights, including the implementation of initiatives announced at a 2000 conference on human rights. These measures included amending the Citizenship Act to enable women of Pakistani descent to claim nationality for their children born abroad.

The Foreign Office highlighted several significant developments in promoting and protecting human rights, such as ratifying ILO Conventions on equal remuneration and the elimination of child labour, and introducing a system of joint electorates to respect minority communities. Despite these efforts, the report stated that Pakistan's human rights record remained poor, which was deemed a "gross misstatement" by the Foreign Office.

The Government of Pakistan remains committed to promoting and protecting human rights, and welcomes assistance from friendly countries towards this end. The Foreign Office expressed its hope for cooperation and understanding among states in addressing the challenges of promoting and protecting human rights.

END OF SUMMARY.
