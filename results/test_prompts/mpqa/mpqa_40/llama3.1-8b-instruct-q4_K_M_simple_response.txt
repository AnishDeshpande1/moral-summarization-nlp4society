SUMMARY:

Pakistan has rejected the US State Department's report on human rights in Pakistan, calling it "factually inaccurate and unwarranted". The government claims that the report fails to acknowledge their efforts to promote and protect human rights, including initiatives such as juvenile justice reforms, women's empowerment, and child labor elimination. They argue that despite these developments, the report still labels their human rights record as "poor", which they consider a gross misstatement of facts.

The Foreign Office spokesman emphasized that Pakistan is committed to promoting and protecting human rights, but feels constrained by the tendency of some states to judge others without cooperation or understanding. They urge friendly countries to provide assistance in addressing human rights challenges, rather than sitting in judgment. The government reiterates its commitment to human rights and welcomes international cooperation to address these issues.

END OF SUMMARY.
