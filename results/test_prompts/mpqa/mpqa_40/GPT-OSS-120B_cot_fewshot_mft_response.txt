**SUMMARY:**
Pakistan has **rejected** the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights **report**, calling it **factually inaccurate**, **unwarranted** and an **unacceptable misstatement** of its record. A Foreign Office spokesman argued the report **ignored** the **important policy** steps the government has taken to **protect** and **promote** **human rights**, including the **promulgation** of a Juvenile Justice Ordinance, **establishment** of a Permanent Commission on the Status of Women, police and jail **reforms**, a national **plan** to **eliminate** child labour, and a **fund** for the **rehabilitation** of working children. Pakistan also **amended** its Citizenship Act to **enable** women to **claim** nationality for children of foreign husbands, **ratified** ILO conventions on **equal** remuneration and the **elimination** of the **worst forms** of child labour, and **respecting** minority wishes through joint electorates. The government said it feels **constrained** by the foreign **judgement** and urged **cooperation** and **understanding** among states, stating the **cause of human rights** would be **better served** through **friendly** **assistance**. It remains **committed** to **promoting** and **protecting** **human rights**.
