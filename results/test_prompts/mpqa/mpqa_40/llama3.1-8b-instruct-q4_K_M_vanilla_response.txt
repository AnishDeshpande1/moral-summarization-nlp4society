SUMMARY:
Pakistan's Foreign Office has rejected the US State Department's country report on human rights in Pakistan, calling it "factually inaccurate and unwarranted". The report allegedly fails to acknowledge the government's efforts to promote and protect human rights, including initiatives such as police and jail reforms, national policy on child labor, and amendments to the citizenship act. The Foreign Office spokesman stated that despite these developments, the report claims Pakistan's human rights record remains poor, which is described as a "gross misstatement of facts". The government has reiterated its commitment to promoting and protecting human rights and welcomes assistance from friendly countries in this regard.

END OF SUMMARY.
