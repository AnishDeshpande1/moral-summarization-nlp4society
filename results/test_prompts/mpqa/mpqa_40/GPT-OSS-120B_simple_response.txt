SUMMARY: Pakistan’s Foreign Office denounced the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights country report as “factually inaccurate and unwarranted,” insisting that the critique ignores a long list of Pakistani initiatives aimed at safeguarding dignity and equality. The spokesman highlighted reforms such as the Juvenile Justice System Ordinance, the creation of a Permanent Commission on the Status of Women, police and jail improvements, a national plan to eradicate child labour, and a fund for rehabilitating working children. He also noted legal progress for women’s citizenship rights, a mass awareness campaign marking the Year 2000 as the Year of Human Rights and Human Dignity, ratification of ILO conventions on equal pay and the worst forms of child labour, and the adoption of optional protocols to the Convention on the Rights of the Child. Additionally, Pakistan introduced a joint‑electorate system to respect minority wishes. While the U.S. report still characterises Pakistan’s record as poor, Islamabad called this a “gross misstatement of facts” and urged a cooperative, non‑judgmental approach among nations to advance human rights, welcoming assistance from friendly countries. END OF SUMMARY.
