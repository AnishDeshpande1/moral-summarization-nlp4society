SUMMARY:
Pakistan rejected the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights country report as “factually inaccurate and unwarranted,” saying the document **fails** to **protect** the progress the government has made. A Foreign Office spokesman argued the report **ignored** the implementation of initiatives announced at an April 2000 human‑rights conference, including the **amended** Juvenile Justice System Ordinance, a **Permanent Commission on the Status of Women**, police and jail reforms, a national policy and Plan of Action for the **elimination** of child **labour**, and a fund for rehabilitating working children. The government also **enabled** women of Pakistani descent to secure citizenship for children of foreign husbands, launched a mass awareness campaign for the Year 2000 as the Year of Human Rights and Human Dignity, **ratified** ILO Conventions 100 and 182, and signed Optional Protocols to the Convention on the Rights of the Child. Recent **respecting** of minority wishes through joint electorates was highlighted. The spokesman called the report’s claim that Pakistan’s human‑rights record remains poor a **misstatement** and warned against some **states** **sitting** in judgement of others. He urged cooperation among **states** to better **protect** human rights and welcomed assistance from friendly nations.

END OF SUMMARY.
