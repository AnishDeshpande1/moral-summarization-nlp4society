**SUMMARY:**  
Pakistan has rejected the U.S. State Department’s human‑rights country report, calling it “factually inaccurate and unwarranted.” A Foreign Office spokesman argued that the report ignores the government’s **important** policy measures and **initiatives** launched since the April 2000 human‑rights conference, including the Juvenile **Justice** Ordinance, a Permanent Commission on the Status of Women, police and jail reforms, a national plan to eliminate child labour, and a fund for rehabilitating working children. Pakistan also highlighted amendments to the Citizenship Act, a mass awareness campaign for the Year of Human Rights and Human Dignity, ratification of ILO conventions on equal remuneration and child‑labour elimination, and the introduction of joint electorates for minorities. The spokesman said the report’s claim that Pakistan’s **human rights** record remains poor is an **unacceptable**, **gross misstatement of facts**. He urged all states to address **human rights** issues through **cooperation**, **understanding**, and the support of **friendly** nations, reaffirming Pakistan’s commitment to **promote** and **protect** human rights.
