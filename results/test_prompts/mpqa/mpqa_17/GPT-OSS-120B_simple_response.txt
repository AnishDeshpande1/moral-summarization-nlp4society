SUMMARY: The article condemns Hugo Chávez’s authoritarian drift in Venezuela, portraying his attempts to consolidate power—rewriting the constitution, planning to stay beyond 2020, and manipulating the state oil company—as betrayals of his popular mandate. It frames the brief ouster of Chávez as a moral victory for Latin America’s democratic values, emphasizing non‑violent, constitutional resistance led by business groups, labor unions, and sympathetic military officers. The violent crackdown on protesters, which killed dozens, forced Chávez back into office, but the piece argues that both domestic opposition and international pressure—despite the Bush administration’s opportunistic support for the coup—highlight the need for the region to reject tyrannical tactics and hold Chávez accountable. The moral message is clear: tyranny must be opposed through lawful, peaceful means, and the United States should repent for its role in encouraging the undemocratic episode. END OF SUMMARY.
