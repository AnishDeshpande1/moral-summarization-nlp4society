SUMMARY:
In Venezuela, President Hugo Chavez was removed from office for two days after he tried to stack the board of the state-owned oil company in his favor. This move sparked protests by business groups and labor unions, who were joined by parts of the military. Gunfire broke out during a march on the presidential palace, killing over a dozen people, prompting the generals to force Chavez's resignation. However, international pressure and renewed domestic support led to his reinstatement as president.

The incident has raised concerns about the Venezuelan government's authoritarian tendencies and its disregard for democratic principles. President Chavez's actions have been criticized as an abuse of power, and his economic promises to the poor have not been met. The US administration's initial welcoming of the coup has also come under scrutiny, with many questioning their role in undermining democracy in Latin America.
